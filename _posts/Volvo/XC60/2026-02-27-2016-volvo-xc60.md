# 2016 Volvo XC60 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2016 Volvo XC60, buyers will find a stylish, comfortable, and safety-oriented luxury crossover. However, owner complaints filed with federal regulators highlight a few recurring mechanical and electrical issues that deserve close attention during a pre-purchase inspection. Prospective owners should pay careful attention to engine oil consumption reports, electrical warnings, and proper functioning of driver-assist safety systems.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged / Supercharged 4-Cylinder

This 2.0-liter engine utilizes both a turbocharger and a supercharger to generate strong horsepower and torque for everyday driving. Buyers should verify service history and check oil levels regularly, as some owners have reported higher-than-average oil consumption.

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Low oil warning lights illuminating prematurely or drivers noticing they need to add engine oil frequently between routine services.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels regularly and consult a mechanic for diagnostics; piston ring replacement or specialized oil consumption services may be required in severe cases.

### 3.0L Turbocharged 6-Cylinder

This smooth 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine delivers robust acceleration and high performance for higher trims and all-wheel-drive configurations. It generally has a solid reputation, though routine maintenance is essential for longevity.

### 3.2L Naturally Aspirated 6-Cylinder

The 3.2-liter naturally aspirated inline-six offers linear power delivery and straightforward mechanical design without forced induction complexity. It is known as a relatively sturdy option when properly maintained.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

This multi-speed automatic transmission pairs with Drive-E powertrains to optimize both fuel efficiency and highway cruising capability. It shifts smoothly, though fluid changes are recommended to ensure long-term durability.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Used alongside select six-cylinder engine configurations, this traditional 6-speed automatic provides dependable power delivery. It offers straightforward operation and generally exhibits stable reliability.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Body Drains

Electrical architecture supports numerous advanced convenience and safety features, making preventative upkeep of body seals and drains vital.

**Clogged Sunroof Drains and Water Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Water accumulating in the cabin or damp carpets, potentially triggering warning lights or interfering with nearby electrical and airbag wiring harnesses.
- **Solution:** Clear sunroof drainage tubes periodically and inspect interior electronics for any signs of moisture intrusion.

## Safety

### SRS Airbag System

Comprehensive passive safety systems protect occupants, though occasional sensor or wiring faults can illuminate warning messages.

**SRS Airbag Service Warning**
- **Symptoms:** An urgent 'SRS Airbag Service' message appearing on the dashboard display without any preceding collision.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician scan diagnostic trouble codes to pinpoint faulty seat wiring harnesses, sensors, or seat movement contacts.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V378000: Seat Belts:Front](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The flexible steel cable that connects the seat belt to the front outboard seating positions may fatigue over time, causing the seat belt to not secure the occupant in the event of a crash. An unsecured occupant has an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will replace the seat belt anchor cable for both front seats. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V798000: Seat Belts:Front:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 210, "Seat Belt Assembly Anchorages" and number 209, "Seat Belt Assemblies." If the seat belt buckle separates from the bracket, the front seat passenger may not be adequately restrained in the event of a crash, increasing their risk of.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the front passenger seat belt buckle stud, replacing the seat belt buckle as needed, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V555000: Electrical System:Wiring:Fuses And Circuit Breakers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the vehicle is unexpectedly unable to be started in the middle of traffic, such as when stopped at an intersection, there is an increased risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will replace the existing starter motor fuse with a higher amperage fuse, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Volvo XC60 is a compelling luxury crossover choice praised for its safety credentials and comfortable cabin, but used buyers should remain vigilant. Prioritize vehicles with documented maintenance records, verify that all safety recalls have been addressed by a dealer, and carefully inspect for oil consumption tendencies or potential water leaks from sunroof drains during your test drive.
