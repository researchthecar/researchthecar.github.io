The 2019 Volvo XC60 is a popular compact luxury SUV known for its stylish design, comfortable ride, and focus on safety. Engine options include the T5 (2.0L turbocharged inline-4, 250 hp and 258 lb-ft of torque), the T6 (2.0L turbocharged and supercharged inline-4, 316 hp and 295 lb-ft of torque), and the T8 eAWD Plug-in Hybrid (2.0L turbocharged and supercharged inline-4 with electric motor, 400 hp and 472 lb-ft of torque combined). All engine options are paired with an Aisin-Warner 8-speed automatic transmission. Many owners appreciate the XC60's elegant interior, comfortable seats, and advanced safety features. Common complaints revolve around the sometimes-complex infotainment system and potential reliability issues.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged (T5)**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes.
    *   Solution: Replacing the piston rings
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged (T6)**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes.
    *   Solution: Replacing the piston rings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Plug-in Hybrid (T8)**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Aisin-Warner 8-Speed Automatic (All Engine Options)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, particularly between lower gears. Hesitation when accelerating from a stop.
    *   Solution: Software update to transmission control module (TCM) or complete transmission replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+ for software, $5,000 - $7,000+ for replacement
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Issues (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Air suspension malfunction leading to uneven ride height, error messages on the dashboard, or complete suspension failure.
    *   Solution: Replacing the air struts, air compressor, or ride height sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+ per component
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, slow response, or crashing of the Sensus infotainment system. Bluetooth connectivity problems.
    *   Solution: Software updates, system resets, or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,500+ (unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Battery draining quickly, especially when the vehicle is not in use.
    *   Solution: Software updates, replacement of faulty modules, or battery replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ for diagnosis and software, $200 - $400+ for battery replacement, variable for module replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Panoramic Sunroof Leaks/Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks around the panoramic sunroof seal, or the sunroof failing to open or close properly.
    *   Solution: Replacing the sunroof seal or repairing/replacing the sunroof motor and components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ (seal), $500 - $1,500+ (motor/components)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Summary**

The 2019 Volvo XC60 has some common issues to consider. Engine oil consumption (T5 & T6) can lead to substantial repair costs. Transmission issues, especially with the 8-speed automatic, can range from software fixes to expensive replacements. Electrical glitches, particularly with the infotainment system, are also prevalent. While not all XC60s will experience these problems, potential buyers should be aware and factor in potential repair costs. Overall, the severity and cost of these issues vary, but awareness is key for informed purchasing and maintenance.
