The 2016 Volvo XC60 is a compact luxury SUV known for its safety, comfort, and Scandinavian design. Engine options include a turbocharged 2.0L inline-4 (T5), a turbocharged and supercharged 2.0L inline-4 (T6), and a turbocharged 2.5L inline-5 (T5 AWD). The T5 engine produces 240 hp and 258 lb-ft of torque, the T6 engine produces 302 hp and 295 lb-ft of torque, and the T5 AWD produces 220 hp and 221 lb-ft of torque. All engine options are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the XC60's comfortable ride, spacious interior, and advanced safety features. Common complaints include the sometimes unintuitive infotainment system and higher maintenance costs compared to some competitors.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (T5)**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption requiring frequent topping off between oil changes. This can sometimes be indicated by a low oil warning light.
    *   Solution: Monitor oil levels and top off as needed. If consumption is excessive, a dealer inspection and potential repair (such as piston ring replacement) may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4 (T6)**

*   **No Issues Found**

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5 (T5 AWD)**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission (All Engine Options)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report experiencing harsh or delayed shifting, particularly between lower gears. This can manifest as a noticeable "clunk" or hesitation when accelerating.
    *   Solution: A software update from the dealer might resolve the issue. In some cases, a transmission flush and fluid change, or valve body replacement, is required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (software update) - $2,500+ (valve body replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Sensus infotainment system can sometimes freeze, crash, or become unresponsive. This can affect navigation, audio, and climate controls.
    *   Solution: Restarting the system may temporarily resolve the issue. Software updates from the dealer are often necessary. In some instances, the infotainment module may need replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,000+ (module replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain issues that cause the car to need to be jumped or replaced earlier than expected.
    *   Solution: Diagnose parasitic draw with a professional and address the underlying electrical fault.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the source of the drain.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sunroof may become stuck, fail to open or close properly, or produce excessive noise during operation.
    *   Solution: Inspect and lubricate the sunroof tracks and mechanism. In some cases, the sunroof motor or entire assembly may need replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (lubrication) - $1,000+ (motor/assembly replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Volvo XC60 generally exhibits a mix of minor and potentially costly issues. Electrical problems, particularly with the infotainment system, and transmission issues appear to be the most common concerns. Engine issues are less frequent but can be expensive to address if excessive oil consumption occurs. While the XC60 is known for its safety and comfort, potential buyers should be aware of these common issues and factor in potential repair costs when considering this vehicle.
