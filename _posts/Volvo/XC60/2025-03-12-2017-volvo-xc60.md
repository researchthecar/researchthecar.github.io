The 2017 Volvo XC60 is a compact luxury SUV that offered a blend of Scandinavian design, safety, and practicality. Engine options included the T5 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 (240 hp, 258 lb-ft of torque), the T6 2.0L turbocharged and supercharged inline-4 (302 hp, 295 lb-ft of torque), and the T6 Polestar 2.0L turbocharged and supercharged inline-4 (362 hp, 347 lb-ft of torque). All engines were paired with an Aisin 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally praised the XC60 for its comfortable ride, stylish interior, and comprehensive safety features. Common dislikes included the somewhat dated infotainment system, and some found the fuel economy to be less impressive than competitors.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (T5)**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners reported higher than normal oil consumption, requiring more frequent top-offs between oil changes.
    *   Solution: Some dealers recommend oil consumption tests, and in some cases, piston ring replacement.

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Leaks around the thermostat housing or water pump.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty thermostat housing or water pump and associated seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix:

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4 (T6)**

*   **Supercharger Clutch Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining or rattling noises from the engine, particularly at low speeds, indicating potential failure of the supercharger clutch. Reduced performance and potential warning lights.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the supercharger clutch or complete supercharger unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix:
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Faulty PCV Valve**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, rough idling, and potential oil leaks.
    * Solution: Replacement of the PCV valve.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix:

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4 (T6 Polestar)**

* **No issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Aisin 8-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifts, particularly between lower gears. Hesitation when accelerating from a stop.
    *   Solution: Software updates to the transmission control module (TCM) can sometimes resolve the issue. In more severe cases, valve body replacement or complete transmission rebuild/replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix:
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Air Suspension Failure (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sagging in the rear, air compressor running constantly, "Chassis Low" warning light.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the air springs, air compressor, or ride height sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix:

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Rebooting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Sensus infotainment system freezes, becomes unresponsive, or spontaneously reboots.
    *   Solution: Software updates can often resolve this issue. In some cases, replacement of the infotainment system module may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix:

* **Parking Sensor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Parking sensors failing intermittently or constantly, false alarms, or complete system failure.
    * Solution: Replacement of faulty sensors or repair of wiring harness.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix:

**Exterior**

*   **Tailgate Strut Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The power tailgate failing to stay open or closing unexpectedly.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the tailgate struts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix:

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof failing to open or close properly, binding, or making excessive noise.
    *   Solution: Lubricating the sunroof tracks. In more severe cases, replacement of the sunroof motor or entire assembly may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix:

*   **Driver's Seat Movement**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The driver's seat makes excessive noise and has excessive movement.
    *   Solution: Lubricating the seat rails.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix:

**Summary**

The 2017 Volvo XC60, while generally reliable, does have some common issues that potential buyers should be aware of. Engine issues such as oil consumption and supercharger problems (T6) can be costly to address. Transmission issues are also a concern, especially for rough shifting. Electrical problems with the infotainment system are frustrating, but often resolved with software updates. Overall, while some repairs can be expensive, the XC60 offers a comfortable and safe driving experience.
