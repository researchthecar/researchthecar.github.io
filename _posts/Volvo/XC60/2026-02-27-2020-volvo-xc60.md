# 2020 Volvo XC60 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Volvo XC60 offers a blend of Scandinavian luxury, advanced safety features, and striking design. As you shop for a pre-owned model, understanding its powertrain options and minor owner-reported tendencies can help ensure a confident purchase.

---

## Engine

### T5 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The T5 features a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing around 250 horsepower. It provides smooth, efficient power for daily driving with generally solid reliability.

### T6 2.0L Supercharged & Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The T6 utilizes a 2.0-liter engine that is both supercharged and turbocharged, generating roughly 316 horsepower. It delivers robust performance across the rev range, though buyers should ensure consistent maintenance history.

### T8 2.0L Plug-in Hybrid Turbo/Supercharged 4-Cylinder

The T8 plug-in hybrid combines the forced-induction 2.0-liter engine with electric motors for a total output of around 400 horsepower. It offers brisk acceleration and electric driving capability, requiring routine checks on both mechanical and hybrid systems.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission is paired across the XC60 lineup to deliver seamless gear shifts and optimized fuel economy. It is generally dependable when serviced according to manufacturer intervals.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V144000: Forward Collision Avoidance: Automatic Emergency Braking](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Due to a software/hardware incompatibility, the Automatic Emergency Brake (AEB) system may not detect obstacles and engage as intended. If the automatic emergency braking system does not engage as intended, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will update the Active Safety Domain Master (ASDM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V716000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Separation of the rotor and shaft may cause a reduction in braking assistance and a loss of the Antilock Brake System (ABS) and Electronic Stability Control (ESC), increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the hydraulic unit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V414000: Electrical System:Wiring:Fuses And Circuit Breakers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Failure of the low-pressure fuel pump can cause a stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuse, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V503000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The nuts that secure the front wipers may not have been sufficiently tightened, possibly causing the wipers to not function properly. The risk of the front wiper arms nut fixation to loosen and not holding the arm in correct position, the wiper function could be reduced and vision could be impaired, thus increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo Car will notify owners, and dealers will tighten the nuts holding the front windshield wiper arms in position, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V001000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A screw inside the gearbox housing can cause the steering wheel to lock up and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the steering gear, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V179000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The high-voltage battery may experience a short circuit within the battery module when the battery is fully charged and the vehicle is parked. A short circuit in a high-voltage battery increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the high-voltage battery module as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
