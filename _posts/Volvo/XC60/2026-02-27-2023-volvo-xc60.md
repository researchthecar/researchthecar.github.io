# 2023 Volvo XC60 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Volvo XC60 brings sleek Scandinavian styling, luxurious interior appointments, and advanced driver assistance technology to the compact luxury SUV segment. However, prospective buyers should pay close attention to reported electrical glitches, particularly involving the Google-based infotainment system and software updates, as well as occasional issues with driver-assist sensors and braking components. Overall, it remains a comfortable and sophisticated daily driver, but a thorough pre-purchase inspection of its electronics and safety systems is strongly recommended.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

This standard turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine (often paired with mild-hybrid B5 or B6 systems) delivers strong, smooth power and respectable efficiency for daily driving. It generally offers solid mechanical reliability, though owners should keep up with routine maintenance.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic

The smooth-shifting 8-speed automatic transmission routes power effectively to front- or all-wheel-drive configurations. It operates largely transparently in everyday driving conditions with minimal widespread mechanical complaints.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake system performance is generally robust, though intermittent pedal stiffness reports warrant inspection during a test drive.

**Brake Pedal Stiffness and Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported that the brake pedal can occasionally feel stiff and unresponsive when shifting into reverse, requiring the driver to release and reapply the pedal to restore normal operation.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the brake booster, vacuum or electronic braking actuators, and related software modules if braking delays occur.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Software

The heavy reliance on touchscreen controls and integrated software means occasional infotainment glitches or software updates can disrupt secondary cabin features.

**Loss of Audio and Warning Chimes**
- **Symptoms:** Software updates or system glitches can cause the vehicle's interior audio to fail completely, resulting in missing turn signal clicks, backup chimes, and infotainment sound.
- **Solution:** Reboot the infotainment system or visit a dealership to verify that the latest vehicle software patches have been correctly installed.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety suites provide great protection, but sensitive sensor calibration can occasionally lead to phantom braking events.

**Unexpected Automatic Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The advanced driver assistance system may occasionally trigger false-positive collision alerts or engage emergency braking unexpectedly when no obstacles are present.
- **Solution:** Check for available sensor calibrations and ensure camera and radar lenses are clean and unobstructed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V793000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Engine Control Module (Ecu/Ecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The vehicle may lose drive power as the high voltage battery is depleted, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the ECM software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V012000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A diagnostic error in the Brake Control Module 2 (BCM2) may cause a loss of Antilock Brake System (ABS), Electronic Stability Control (ESC), and traction control. A reduction in brake support functions can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V188000: Electrical System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The false warning message could prevent the detection of an actual e-call system failure, which could delay an emergency response during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the telematics and connectivity antenna module (TCAM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V282000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera image may not display when the vehicle is placed in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display reduces the driver's view of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V908000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2023)**

A rearview camera image that does not display reduces the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Volvo XC60 is a refined, comfortable luxury crossover that impresses with its premium cabin and high-tech features. While major mechanical failures are uncommon, buyers should carefully verify that all software updates have been applied and test drive the vehicle to ensure the infotainment and driver-assistance features operate smoothly.
