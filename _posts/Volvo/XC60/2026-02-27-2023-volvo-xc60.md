# 2023 Volvo XC60 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Volvo XC60 brings sleek Scandinavian design, advanced safety features, and a range of electrified powertrains to the luxury compact SUV segment. For used-car buyers, understanding the specific engine and transmission options available for this model year helps ensure you select a configuration that aligns with your performance and reliability expectations. While overall owner feedback highlights comfort and modern technology, prospective buyers should review available powertrain specs and remain mindful of common owner-reported complaints as gathered from real-world driving experiences.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (B5 AWD)

The B5 utilizes a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine with a mild-hybrid system, producing approximately 247 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. It offers a balanced blend of efficiency and everyday drivability for daily commutes.

### 2.0L Turbocharged & Supercharged Plug-In Hybrid (T8 AWD Recharge)

The T8 Recharge pairs a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine with an electric motor to deliver a robust 455 total horsepower and 523 lb-ft of torque. It provides brisk acceleration and electric-only driving capabilities, though its complex powertrain requires careful consideration of long-term upkeep.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission comes paired across the XC60 lineup, delivering smooth and responsive gear shifts matched to all-wheel-drive configurations. It generally maintains a reliable reputation under normal operating conditions.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V793000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Engine Control Module (Ecu/Ecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The vehicle may lose drive power as the high voltage battery is depleted, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the ECM software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V012000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A diagnostic error in the Brake Control Module 2 (BCM2) may cause a loss of Antilock Brake System (ABS), Electronic Stability Control (ESC), and traction control. A reduction in brake support functions can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V188000: Electrical System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The false warning message could prevent the detection of an actual e-call system failure, which could delay an emergency response during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the telematics and connectivity antenna module (TCAM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V282000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera image may not display when the vehicle is placed in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display reduces the driver's view of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V908000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC60&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera image may not display when the vehicle is placed in reverse. A rearview camera image that does not display reduces the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.
