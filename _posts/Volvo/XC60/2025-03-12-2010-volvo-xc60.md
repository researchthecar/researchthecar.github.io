The 2010 Volvo XC60 is a compact luxury SUV that was praised for its safety features, comfortable ride, and stylish design. Engine options include a 3.2-liter inline-6 producing 235 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque, and a turbocharged 3.0-liter inline-6 producing 281 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners often appreciate the XC60's comfortable interior, spacious cargo area, and excellent crash test ratings. Common dislikes include the somewhat dated infotainment system and occasionally higher repair costs compared to non-luxury brands.

**Engine**

**3.2L Inline-6**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from various locations, most commonly the valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket, or rear main seal. This can lead to oil consumption, low oil pressure, and potential engine damage.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking gasket or seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $800+ depending on the location of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **PCV (Positive Crankcase Ventilation) System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clogged or faulty PCV valve or system components can cause vacuum leaks, rough idling, oil leaks, and decreased engine performance.
    *   Solution: Replace the PCV valve and inspect/replace any damaged hoses or components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, whistling or whining noise from the turbocharger, and potential oil leaks.
    *   Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Similar to the 3.2L, oil leaks can occur from various seals and gaskets.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking gasket or seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $800+ depending on the location of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner AW TF-80SC 6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Abrupt or jerky shifting, especially between certain gears.
    *   Solution: Software updates, transmission fluid flush, or valve body repair/replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,500+ depending on the severity and required repairs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibration at low speeds, especially during acceleration.
    *   Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Strut Mounts Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the front suspension, especially when going over bumps. The strut mounts can wear out prematurely.
    * Solution: Replacing the strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Rear Suspension Bushing Wear**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rear suspension bushings can wear, leading to clunking noises or loose handling.
    * Solution: Replacing the rear suspension bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Software Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent issues with the infotainment system, warning lights, or other electronic features.
    *   Solution: Software updates or module replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The battery drains quickly, especially when the vehicle is not in use.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair the parasitic draw or replace the battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sunroof may leak or fail to open/close properly.
    * Solution: Repair or replace sunroof components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Seat Heater Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Seat heaters fail to operate or become excessively hot.
    *   Solution: Replace the seat heater element or control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2010 Volvo XC60 can present owners with a range of issues, from relatively minor electrical glitches to more significant engine and transmission problems. While the vehicle is generally reliable, potential buyers should be aware of these common complaints and have the vehicle inspected by a qualified mechanic before purchase. Turbocharger failure in the 3.0L engine and torque converter issues in the transmission can be particularly costly to repair, so paying close attention to these areas is essential.
