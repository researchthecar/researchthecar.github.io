**Introduction:**

The 2018 Volvo VNL 860 is a heavy-duty truck known for its robust construction, powerful engine options, and advanced safety features. It is available with several engine and transmission choices, including the D13 engines with 425-505 horsepower and 1250-1850 lb-ft of torque, and the I-Shift automated manual transmission. Owners appreciate the VNL 860's comfort, fuel efficiency, and reliable performance. However, like any vehicle, it has faced some reported issues.

**Engine**

**D13 Engine**
- **Turbocharger Failure:** 
    - Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, black smoke from the exhaust
    - Solution: Replacing the turbocharger
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $4,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**I-Shift Transmission**
- **Shifting Issues:** 
    - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, delayed gear changes
    - Solution: Reprogramming the transmission control module (TCM) or replacing sensors
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **Air Suspension Leaks:** 
    - Description/Symptoms: Deflated airbags, uneven ride height
    - Solution: Replacing air bags or air lines
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Battery Drain:** 
    - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the truck, loss of electrical power
    - Solution: Replacing the alternator or electrical components that are causing the drain
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Windshield Wipers:** 
    - Description/Symptoms: Wipers malfunction, obstructed view
    - Solution: Replacing wiper blades or wiper motor
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Climate Control Issues:** 
    - Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning air conditioning or heating, uneven temperatures
    - Solution: Replacing climate control module or other components
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    - Recalls: None Found

- **Dashboard Lights:** 
    - Description/Symptoms: Dimmed or malfunctioning dashboard lights
    - Solution: Replacing bulbs or electrical components
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2018 Volvo VNL 860 is a reliable vehicle, but it has some reported issues to be aware of. The most significant issues include turbocharger failure, shifting issues, and battery drain, which can lead to costly repairs. While some interior and electrical issues may be minor inconveniences, they can still impact the driver's comfort and safety. It's important to note that the severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific component affected and the labor rates in your area. Regular maintenance and prompt repairs can help minimize the risk of major problems and extend the lifespan of the VNL 860.
