The 2017 Volvo 9800 is a luxurious and spacious coach known for its comfortable ride, advanced safety features, and overall reliability. It often features a Volvo D13 engine paired with a Volvo I-Shift automated manual transmission, delivering a balance of power and fuel efficiency. The D13 engine can produce around 500 horsepower and 1850 lb-ft of torque. Owners appreciate the smooth ride, quiet cabin, and extensive list of standard features, but some have noted the complexity of the electronics and potential repair costs associated with high-end components.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaking from various locations including radiator hoses and the EGR cooler, resulting in low coolant levels, overheating, and potential engine damage.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking hoses or the EGR cooler.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idling, decreased fuel economy, and black smoke coming from the exhaust. Diagnosed through engine codes indicating fuel injector malfunction.
    *   Solution: Replacing faulty fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift**

*   **Shift Hesitation/Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Delayed shifts, jerking during gear changes, and difficulty finding the correct gear. Often accompanied by error codes related to the transmission control module (TCM) or solenoids.
    *   Solution: Updating the TCM software, replacing faulty solenoids, or in some cases, replacing the entire transmission.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $8,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clutch slippage, difficulty engaging gears, and unusual noises during clutch operation.
    *   Solution: Replacing the clutch assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The coach leans to one side, rides lower than usual, or fails to maintain proper ride height. Hissing sounds may be audible near the air springs or air lines.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing leaking air springs, air lines, or the air compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various warning lights illuminated on the dashboard, inaccurate readings from sensors (e.g., tire pressure, temperature), and potential malfunctions in other systems.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing and replacing the faulty sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, such as lights flickering, systems malfunctioning, or difficulty starting the engine. Often caused by chafed or damaged wiring harnesses.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the affected wiring harness.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Lighting Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Failure of headlights, taillights, or turn signals. Can be caused by blown bulbs, faulty wiring, or corroded connectors.
    *   Solution: Replacing bulbs, repairing wiring, or cleaning connectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent heating or cooling, failure of the air conditioning system, or unusual noises coming from the HVAC unit.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the HVAC components, such as the compressor, condenser, or blower motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Volvo 9800, while generally reliable, does exhibit certain common issues. Engine and transmission problems, such as coolant leaks, fuel injector failures, and shifting problems, can be costly to repair, ranging from a few hundred to several thousand dollars. Suspension and electrical issues, such as air suspension leaks and sensor failures, can also contribute to maintenance costs. While these issues are not necessarily prevalent in every vehicle, potential buyers should be aware of them and have the coach inspected thoroughly before purchase.
