The 2019 Volvo V90 is a luxurious and stylish wagon praised for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and advanced safety features. Engine options for the 2019 Volvo V90 include the T5, T6, and T8 variants. The T5 is a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing around 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. The T6 adds a supercharger to the 2.0-liter engine, boosting output to approximately 316 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. The T8 is a plug-in hybrid that combines the T6 engine with an electric motor, delivering a combined output of around 400 horsepower and 472 lb-ft of torque. All engine options are typically paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the V90's elegant design and comfortable interior but may find the infotainment system complex and some repair costs high.

**Engine**

**T5 2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

* **Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than expected oil consumption, requiring more frequent oil top-offs between scheduled oil changes.
    * Solution: Monitoring oil levels regularly and addressing any leaks or unusual consumption patterns promptly. In severe cases, piston ring replacement may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**T6 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Four-Cylinder**

* **Supercharger Clutch Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced issues with the supercharger clutch, resulting in a loss of supercharger boost, reduced acceleration, and potential engine hesitation.
    * Solution: Replacing the supercharger clutch or the entire supercharger assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**T8 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Four-Cylinder Plug-In Hybrid**

* **Battery Management System Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Problems with the battery management system can lead to reduced electric range, charging difficulties, or error messages related to the hybrid system.
    * Solution: Diagnosing and reprogramming the battery management system or replacing faulty components within the hybrid system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+ (depending on the specific component)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner 8-Speed Automatic (All Engine Variants)**

* **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners report experiencing rough or delayed shifting, especially between lower gears, and occasional hesitation during acceleration. This issue may be more apparent at lower speeds.
    * Solution: A transmission fluid flush and software update might resolve the issue. In more severe cases, valve body replacement or a complete transmission overhaul may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 (fluid flush) - $5,000+ (transmission overhaul)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Compressor Failure (If Equipped)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Models equipped with air suspension may experience compressor failure, resulting in a sagging suspension, ride height warnings, and a rough ride.
    * Solution: Replacing the air suspension compressor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Infotainment System Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The Sensus infotainment system can experience glitches, freezing, slow response times, and connectivity problems with Bluetooth and Apple CarPlay/Android Auto.
    * Solution: Software updates, resetting the system, or replacing the infotainment unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $2,000+ (unit replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Sensor Malfunctions (Various)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Failure of various sensors, such as parking sensors, blind-spot monitoring sensors, or adaptive cruise control sensors, can occur.
    * Solution: Diagnosing the faulty sensor and replacing it.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the sunroof, including difficulty opening or closing, unusual noises, or leaks.
    * Solution: Repairing or replacing the sunroof motor, seals, or the entire sunroof assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Seat Heater Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The seat heaters may fail to operate correctly, either not heating at all or overheating.
    * Solution: Replacing the heating element or the control module for the seat heaters.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Volvo V90 generally offers a comfortable and luxurious driving experience, but some owners have reported issues, particularly with the infotainment system, transmission, and potential oil consumption (T5). Models equipped with air suspension or the T6 engine may encounter specific component failures. While some issues can be resolved with software updates or minor repairs, others may require more costly component replacements. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and consider a pre-purchase inspection to identify any existing problems. The severity of these issues range from minor inconveniences to potentially expensive repairs.
