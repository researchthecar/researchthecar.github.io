**Introduction**

The 2016 Volvo 9800 is a luxury coach bus that offers both comfort and reliability. It is powered by a 7.7-liter engine that produces 350 horsepower and 1,350 lb-ft of torque. The 9800 comes standard with a 12-speed automated manual transmission.

People like the 9800 for its spacious interior, comfortable seats, and smooth ride. However, some people have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, exterior, and interior.

**Engine**

* **7.7-liter Engine**

 - **Oil Leaks:** Oil may leak from the engine's gaskets or seals. Symptoms include a burning smell, low oil levels, and oil spots on the ground. The fix involves replacing the leaking gaskets or seals. Estimated cost: $500 - $1,000+
 - **Turbocharger Failure:** The turbocharger may fail, causing a loss of power and black smoke from the exhaust. The solution is to replace the turbocharger. Estimated cost: $2,000 - $3,000+

**Transmission**

* **12-speed Automated Manual Transmission**

 - **Shifting Problems:** The transmission may have difficulty shifting gears, or it may shift gears too harshly. The fix involves reprogramming the transmission's computer or replacing the transmission's control module. Estimated cost: $1,000 - $2,000+
 - **Clutch Failure:** The clutch may fail, causing the transmission to slip or shudder. The solution is to replace the clutch. Estimated cost: $1,500 - $2,500+

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension:**

 - **Air Leaks:** The air suspension system may develop leaks, causing the vehicle to sag or ride rough. The fix involves finding and repairing the leak. Estimated cost: $500 - $1,500+
 - **Compressor Failure:** The air suspension compressor may fail, causing the vehicle to sag or ride rough. The solution is to replace the compressor. Estimated cost: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** The battery may drain quickly, even when the vehicle is not in use. The fix involves finding and fixing the source of the drain. Estimated cost: $100 - $500+
* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator may fail, causing the battery to not charge properly. The solution is to replace the alternator. Estimated cost: $500 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Windshield Wipers:** The windshield wipers may not work properly, or they may make a chattering noise. The fix involves replacing the wiper blades or the wiper motor. Estimated cost: $100 - $500+
 * **Headlight Failure:** The headlights may not work properly, or they may be dim. The fix involves replacing the headlight bulbs or the headlight assembly. Estimated cost: $100 - $500+

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Issues:** The climate control system may not work properly, or it may make a loud noise. The fix involves diagnosing and repairing the problem. Estimated cost: $200 - $800+
* **Infotainment System:** The infotainment system may not work properly, or it may freeze. The fix involves updating the software or replacing the infotainment system. Estimated cost: $500 - $1,500+

**Summary**

The 2016 Volvo 9800 is a reliable coach bus that offers a comfortable and spacious interior. However, some people have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, exterior, and interior. The severity of these issues can range from minor to major, and the cost to fix them can be significant.
