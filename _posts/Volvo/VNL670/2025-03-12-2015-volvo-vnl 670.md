The 2015 Volvo VNL 670 is a popular over-the-road semi-truck known for its comfortable cab, fuel efficiency, and reliability. Engine options for this model year include the Volvo D11, D13, and Cummins ISX15. The Volvo D13 engine, for example, produces between 405-500 horsepower and 1450-1850 lb-ft of torque, depending on the specific configuration. Transmission options include Volvo's I-Shift automated manual transmission and manual transmissions. Drivers often praise the VNL 670 for its spacious sleeper cab and smooth ride. Common complaints often revolve around the complexity of the Volvo electrical system and the cost of replacement parts.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**

*   **EGR Cooler Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Overheating, coolant loss, white smoke from the exhaust. Can lead to engine damage if not addressed promptly.
    *   Solution: Replace the EGR cooler.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Frequent regeneration cycles, reduced engine power, "check engine" light. Can become clogged, leading to engine shutdown.
    *   Solution: Cleaning or replacement of the DPF filter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Injector Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, reduced fuel economy, black smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Replacement of faulty injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ per injector
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Cummins ISX15**

*   **EGR Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, reduced power, "check engine" light, black smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the EGR valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke, unusual turbocharger noises.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idle, reduced fuel economy, white or black smoke from exhaust.
    *   Solution: Injector replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ per injector
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift**

*   **Shift Hesitation/Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Delays in shifting, jerky or harsh shifts, especially between certain gears.
    *   Solution: Software updates, clutch adjustment, or replacement of worn clutch components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $4,000+ (depending on severity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inability to shift, transmission stuck in gear, "check transmission" light.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the TCM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Actuator Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting, clutch slipping, transmission warning lights.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the clutch actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Airbag Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Truck leaning to one side, rough ride, hissing sound from leaking air.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty airbag.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800 per airbag
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Ride Height Sensor Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Incorrect ride height, rough ride, uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replacement or recalibration of the ride height sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sensor Failures (Various)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Multiple warning lights, inaccurate readings on the dashboard, performance issues. Volvo's electrical system is sensitive to sensor failures.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor (depending on the sensor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, lights flickering, random system failures.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the affected wiring harness section.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ (depending on the extent of damage)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Alternator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging, dimming lights, "check charging system" light.
    *   Solution: Alternator replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight/Taillight Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dim or non-functional lights, condensation inside the lens.
    *   Solution: Bulb replacement, lens replacement, or complete light assembly replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC System Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Insufficient heating or cooling, blower motor failure, unusual noises.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of faulty components such as the blower motor, blend door actuator, or compressor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Gauge Cluster Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or complete failure of gauges, incorrect readings, flickering lights.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the gauge cluster.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Volvo VNL 670, while generally reliable, can experience issues across various systems. Engine issues, particularly related to emissions components (EGR, DPF), are common and can be costly to repair. Transmission problems with the I-Shift can also be significant. Electrical issues, while potentially minor, can be frequent and require careful diagnosis. Suspension and interior issues are less common but still possible. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of the potential for moderate to high repair costs associated with this vehicle, especially as it ages.
