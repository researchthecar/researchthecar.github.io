The 2016 Volvo VNL 670 is a popular long-haul semi-truck known for its fuel efficiency, driver comfort, and safety features. Common engine options include the Volvo D13, Cummins ISX15, and Volvo D11. The D13 engine, a popular choice, offers horsepower ratings ranging from 405 to 500 and torque ranging from 1450 to 1850 lb-ft. Transmission options typically include Volvo I-Shift automated manual transmissions. Drivers often appreciate the VNL 670's spacious cab and smooth ride, but some common complaints revolve around electronic issues and the cost of repairs.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**

*   **EGR Cooler Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine overheating, coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, and potential engine damage.
    *   Solution: Replacing the EGR cooler with an updated design.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, reduced power, poor fuel economy, and diagnostic trouble codes related to fuel injectors.
    *   Solution: Replacing faulty fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Cummins ISX15**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke from the exhaust, whistling or grinding noises from the turbocharger, and elevated exhaust temperatures.
    *   Solution: Replacing the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **EGR Valve Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, reduced power, poor fuel economy, and diagnostic trouble codes related to the EGR valve.
    *   Solution: Cleaning or replacing the EGR valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Volvo D11**

* **Fuel Pump Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine Stalling, rough idling, or failure to start, accompanied by fuel system-related diagnostic trouble codes.
    * Solution: Replace the fuel pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800-$2,000
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Coolant Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Visible coolant leaks, low coolant levels, and engine overheating, often stemming from deteriorated hoses, connections, or the water pump.
    * Solution: Identify and repair coolant leaks, potentially involving hose replacements, tightening connections, or replacing the water pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift Automated Manual Transmission**

*   **Shift Actuator Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, erratic shifting behavior, and diagnostic trouble codes related to the shift actuator.
    *   Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the shift actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears, and unusual noises from the transmission.
    *   Solution: Replacing the clutch assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Truck leaning to one side, rough ride, and hissing sounds from air leaks in the suspension system.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing leaking air bags, airlines, or leveling valves.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncing or unstable ride, excessive body roll, and premature tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replacing worn or damaged shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, such as lights flickering, gauges malfunctioning, and warning lights illuminating without a clear cause. Often caused by chafing or corrosion.
    *   Solution: Inspecting and repairing damaged wiring harnesses, replacing corroded connectors, and ensuring proper grounding.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various performance issues, such as reduced power, poor fuel economy, and diagnostic trouble codes related to faulty sensors (e.g., wheel speed sensors, coolant temperature sensors).
    *   Solution: Replacing the defective sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Headlights dimming or failing.
    *   Solution: Replacing the headlight.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Mirror Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The power mirrors are not working.
    *   Solution: Replacing the mirror.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent heating or cooling, blower motor issues, and problems with temperature control.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing faulty HVAC components, such as the blower motor, compressor, or control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Seat Suspension Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Air ride seat not holding air, or not inflating to the right height.
    * Solution: Repairing or replacing the seat suspension system
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Volvo VNL 670 experiences a range of issues, from relatively minor electrical glitches to more significant engine and transmission problems. The cost of repairs can vary widely depending on the specific issue and the extent of the damage, but owners should be prepared for potential expenses ranging from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars. Addressing these issues promptly is essential to maintain the vehicle's performance, reliability, and safety.
