The 2006 Volvo XC90 is a mid-size luxury SUV known for its safety, comfort, and practicality. Engine options included a 2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5 producing 208 hp and 236 lb-ft of torque, a 3.2L Inline-6 producing 235 hp and 236 lb-ft of torque, and a 4.4L V8 producing 311 hp and 325 lb-ft of torque. All engines were paired with an Aisin automatic transmission. Owners often praised the XC90's spacious interior, comfortable ride, and reputation for safety. Common criticisms included the vehicle's fuel economy, repair costs, and some electronic issues.

**Engine**

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5**

* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can develop from various locations, including the rear main seal, valve cover gasket, and turbocharger lines. Symptoms include visible oil leaks, burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    * Solution: Replacing the leaking seals or lines.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+ depending on the leak location.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **PCV System Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) system can fail, leading to increased crankcase pressure. This can cause oil leaks, rough idling, and poor performance. A whistling sound may be heard.
    * Solution: Replacing the PCV valve and associated components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.2L Inline-6**

* **Coil Pack Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough running engine, check engine light.
    * Solution: Replace faulty coil pack(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ depending on how many need to be replaced
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Thermostat Housing Leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leak, overheating, low coolant level.
    * Solution: Replace thermostat housing.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**4.4L V8**

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging, electrical issues, dimming lights.
    * Solution: Replacing the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the valve cover onto the exhaust manifold, causing a burning smell.
    * Solution: Replacing the valve cover gaskets.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**All Transmissions (Aisin Automatic)**

* **Valve Body Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, slipping between gears, delayed engagement.
    * Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the valve body.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Torque Converter Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering at low speeds, stalling, poor acceleration.
    * Solution: Replacing the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Mount Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the front suspension, especially when turning or going over bumps.
    * Solution: Replacing the strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Rear Air Suspension Issues (if equipped)**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear of the vehicle sagging, the air compressor running constantly, or suspension malfunction warnings.
    * Solution: Replacing the air springs, compressor, or sensors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ depending on the component.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **DIM (Driver Information Module) Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Display fading, flickering, or completely failing.
    * Solution: Repairing or replacing the DIM.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Sunroof Motor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sunroof not opening or closing, motor grinding noise.
    * Solution: Replacing the sunroof motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

* **ABS Module Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: ABS and/or traction control lights illuminated, loss of ABS function.
    * Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the ABS module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Tail Light Bulb Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: One or more tail light bulbs not working.
    * Solution: Replacing the bulbs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $10 - $50
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Seat Heater Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Seat heaters not working.
    * Solution: Replacing the heating element or switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Climate Control Fan Motor Resistor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control fan only working on certain speeds, or not at all.
    * Solution: Replacing the resistor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2006 Volvo XC90 has a range of potential issues, from relatively minor electrical problems to more significant engine and transmission concerns. The severity and cost of repairs can vary widely depending on the specific issue and the engine/transmission configuration. Owners should be aware of the potential for these issues and budget accordingly for maintenance and repairs. The most expensive repairs tend to be those involving the transmission, air suspension, and some of the engine components.
