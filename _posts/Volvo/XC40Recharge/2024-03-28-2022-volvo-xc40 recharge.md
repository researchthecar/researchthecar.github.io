**Introduction**

The 2022 Volvo XC40 Recharge is an all-electric compact luxury SUV. It is powered by a dual-motor electric powertrain that produces 402 horsepower and 486 pound-feet of torque. The XC40 Recharge has a range of 223 miles on a single charge. It is praised for its stylish design, comfortable interior, and impressive performance. However, some owners have reported a few issues with the vehicle.

**Electric Motor**

**No Issues Found**

**Battery**

**Battery Degradation**
Description/Symptoms: Over time, the battery's capacity may decrease, reducing the vehicle's range.
Solution: Battery replacement
Estimated Cost to Fix: $15,000 - $20,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Touchscreen Malfunctions**
Description/Symptoms: The touchscreen may become unresponsive or freeze, making it difficult to use the vehicle's infotainment system.
Solution: Software update or touchscreen replacement
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 22V243000

**Exterior**

**Sunroof Leaks**
Description/Symptoms: Water may leak into the cabin through the sunroof.
Solution: Sunroof seal replacement
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 22V899000

**Interior**

**Steering Wheel Loose**
Description/Symptoms: The steering wheel may become loose or wobbly.
Solution: Steering wheel tightening
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Volvo XC40 Recharge is a generally reliable vehicle. However, some owners have reported issues with the battery, electrical system, and exterior. Overall, the XC40 Recharge is a good choice for those looking for an electric SUV, but it is important to be aware of the potential problems that may arise.
