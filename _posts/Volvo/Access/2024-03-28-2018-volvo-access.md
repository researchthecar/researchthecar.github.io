**2018 Volvo Access**

The 2018 Volvo Access is a compact luxury SUV available with two engine options: a 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 engine and a 2.0L supercharged and turbocharged inline-4 engine. Both engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. The 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 engine produces 248 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, while the 2.0L supercharged and turbocharged inline-4 engine produces 316 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. The Volvo Access has received praise for its comfortable interior, well-balanced handling, and advanced safety features. However, it has also been criticized for its high price tag and limited cargo space.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 Engine**

* **Intake Valve Deposits**
    * Description/Symptoms: Buildup of carbon deposits on the intake valves can lead to reduced engine performance and fuel efficiency.
    * Solution: Clean the intake valves using a chemical cleaning solution.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Supercharged and Turbocharged Inline-4 Engine**

* **Timing Belt Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: A faulty timing belt can cause the engine to lose power or stall.
    * Solution: Replace the timing belt and components as needed.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Shifting Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Issues with shifting gears, such as hesitation, slipping, or rough shifting.
    * Solution: Fluid changes, filter replacement, or transmission repair/replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Shock Absorber Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged shock absorbers can lead to a rough ride and reduced handling stability.
    * Solution: Replace the front shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Fuel Pump Control Module Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: A faulty fuel pump control module can cause the fuel pump to fail, resulting in engine stalling or no-starts.
    * Solution: Replace the fuel pump control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500
    * Recalls: NHTSA Recall #18V-412

* **Alternator Diode Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: A failed alternator diode can cause electrical issues, such as battery drain or dim headlights.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Release Button Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The tailgate release button may become inoperable or stuck.
    * Solution: Replace the tailgate release button assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: NHTSA Recall #18V-733

**Interior**

* **Heated Seat Control Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The heated seat controls may stop working or become unresponsive.
    * Solution: Replace the heated seat control module or wiring harness.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Rear Seat Backrest Mechanism Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear seat backrest may not recline or fold down properly.
    * Solution: Replace the rear seat backrest mechanism.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2018 Volvo Access has a few significant issues that can be expensive to repair, such as timing belt failure and transmission problems. Additionally, it has a handful of less common but still notable issues, such as tailgate release button failure and heated seat control failure. While the Access is a comfortable and well-rounded SUV, potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues before making a purchase.
