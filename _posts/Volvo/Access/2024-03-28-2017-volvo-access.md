**Introduction**

The 2017 Volvo Access is a mid-size hatchback that offers a blend of luxury, practicality, and safety features. It is powered by a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine that produces 240 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with either a 6-speed manual transmission or an 8-speed automatic transmission. The Access is known for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and strong performance. However, it has also been criticized for its uninspiring exterior design and lack of some advanced driver-assistance features.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Turbocharged Inline-4**

* **Cylinder Head Gasket Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: This issue occurs when the cylinder head gasket fails, causing coolant and oil to leak. Symptoms include white smoke from the exhaust, overheating, and a loss of power.
    * Solution: The cylinder head gasket must be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner may fail, causing the timing chain to skip and potentially damage the engine. Symptoms include rattling noises from the engine, hesitation, and a loss of power.
    * Solution: The timing chain tensioner and timing chain must be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Shifter Linkage Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The shifter linkage may fail, making it difficult or impossible to shift gears. Symptoms include sloppy shifting, difficulty engaging gears, and grinding noises.
    * Solution: The shifter linkage must be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Valve Body Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The valve body, which controls the flow of fluid in the transmission, may fail. Symptoms include slipping gears, harsh shifts, and a lack of power.
    * Solution: The valve body must be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Bearing Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The front strut bearings may fail, causing clunking or rattling noises over bumps. Symptoms include excessive vibration, poor handling, and premature tire wear.
    * Solution: The front strut bearings must be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Auxiliary Battery Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The auxiliary battery, which powers the vehicle's electrical systems, may fail. Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle, electrical system failures, and a variety of warning lights on the dashboard.
    * Solution: The auxiliary battery must be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control system may malfunction, causing the air conditioning or heating to not work properly. Symptoms include inconsistent temperatures, strange noises, and a musty odor.
    * Solution: The climate control system may need to be repaired or replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Infotainment System Freezing**
    * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may freeze or crash intermittently. Symptoms include a black screen, lost audio, and unresponsive controls.
    * Solution: The infotainment system may need to be updated or replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Volvo Access is a reliable vehicle overall, but it has some potential issues that owners should be aware of. The most common problems involve the engine, transmission, and electrical system. Repairs can be expensive, ranging from $200 to over $1,000. Overall, the Access is a well-built and durable vehicle that provides a comfortable and safe driving experience.
