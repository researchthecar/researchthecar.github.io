The 2015 Volvo XC60 is a compact luxury SUV known for its safety features, comfortable ride, and stylish design. Engine options include a turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder (T5) producing around 240 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, a supercharged and turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder (T6) generating about 302 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, and a turbocharged 2.5-liter five-cylinder (T5) that produces 250 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options are primarily an 8-speed automatic, with some earlier models potentially featuring a 6-speed automatic. Owners often praise the XC60 for its comfortable seats, upscale interior, and strong safety ratings. Common complaints include the infotainment system's complexity and some reported issues with reliability, particularly electrical glitches.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder (T5)**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. This may be indicated by the low oil level warning light illuminating.
    *   Solution: In some cases, Volvo has acknowledged the issue and offered a repair involving replacing the piston rings. Other solutions include more frequent monitoring of oil levels or, in severe cases, engine replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $6,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Supercharged and Turbocharged Four-Cylinder (T6)**

*   **Coolant Leak from Water Pump**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks originating from the water pump area. This may cause the engine to overheat.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the water pump and associated seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Turbocharged Five-Cylinder (T5)**

*   **Vacuum Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The vacuum pump can fail, resulting in reduced braking performance, especially at low speeds. The brake pedal may feel hard and require more force to stop the vehicle.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the vacuum pump.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner 8-Speed Automatic (TF-80SC)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing harsh or erratic shifting, particularly between lower gears. Hesitation when accelerating from a stop or during gear changes may also be present.
    *   Solution: A software update may resolve the issue in some cases. In other instances, valve body replacement or complete transmission replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (software update) - $4,000+ (transmission replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Suspension Air Spring Failure (Vehicles with Air Suspension)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicles equipped with rear air suspension may experience air spring leaks or failures, causing the rear of the vehicle to sag. This can result in a rough ride and reduced handling performance. An error message related to the suspension system may also appear on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty air spring(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+ per air spring
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Glitches/Freezing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system (Sensus) can experience frequent glitches, freezing, and slow response times. This may affect navigation, audio, and climate control functions.
    *   Solution: Software updates may resolve some issues, but in some cases, the infotainment unit may need replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1500+ (unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain can occur, particularly if the vehicle is not driven regularly. This can lead to difficulty starting the vehicle or a completely dead battery.
    *   Solution: Ensure all electrical accessories are turned off when the vehicle is not in use. Inspect the charging system for proper function. Replacement of the battery may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ (battery replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues (Leakage/Malfunction)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroofs can sometimes leak, particularly around the seals. Malfunctions such as failure to open or close properly can also occur.
    *   Solution: Cleaning and lubricating the sunroof seals may resolve minor leaks. Repair or replacement of the sunroof mechanism may be necessary for malfunctions.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Power Seat Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The power seats may experience malfunctions, such as failure to adjust in certain directions or complete failure of the power seat motor.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty seat motor or switch.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Volvo XC60, while praised for its safety and comfort, has some common issues that prospective buyers should be aware of. Engine issues, such as oil consumption and coolant leaks, can be relatively expensive to repair. Electrical issues, particularly with the infotainment system, are also frequently reported. Suspension problems with air springs can also be quite costly. While some issues may be minor and easily resolved with software updates, others can require significant repairs. The severity and cost of these potential issues should be considered when evaluating a used 2015 Volvo XC60.
