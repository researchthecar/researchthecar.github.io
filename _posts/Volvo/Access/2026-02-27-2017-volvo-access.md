# 2017 Volvo Access

### Overview
The 2017 Volvo XC90, often referred to as the 'Volvo Access' in some contexts, is a luxury SUV known for its safety features and refined interior. This model year generally exhibits strong reliability, particularly in its powertrain and core structural components. Volvo's commitment to quality engineering is evident, with most reported issues being minor or related to specific electronic features rather than fundamental mechanical failures. Owners generally appreciate the comfortable ride and advanced technology, though some electronic glitches can occasionally surface. Overall, the 2017 XC90 is considered a dependable choice within its segment, offering a good balance of performance, comfort, and safety.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged & Supercharged (T6)
**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, engine overheating, low coolant level warning light.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of leaking hoses, seals, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


### 2.0L Turbocharged Plug-in Hybrid (T8)
**Hybrid Battery Cooling System**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced electric-only range, battery temperature warnings, potential loss of hybrid functionality.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of the dedicated hybrid battery cooling system, which may involve pump or coolant line replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200

## Suspension

### Standard & Air Suspension
**Air Suspension Leaks (Air Suspension models)**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, uneven ride height, 'Air suspension service required' warning message.
- **Solution:** Leak detection and repair of air springs, lines, or compressor.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1500


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, system freezes, navigation or audio issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates or potential replacement of the infotainment control unit.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1000


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dies unexpectedly, difficulty starting the vehicle, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify parasitic draw from modules or accessories, followed by repair or replacement of faulty components.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Volvo XC90 demonstrates commendable reliability, with its core mechanical components, including the engine and transmission, performing robustly. The primary areas where minor issues have been noted are within the electrical system, specifically concerning the infotainment screen and occasional battery drain, and on models equipped with air suspension, where leaks can occur. These issues are generally addressable with software updates or component replacements and do not indicate systemic flaws. For owners seeking a safe, comfortable, and technologically advanced SUV, the 2017 XC90 remains a solid choice, provided potential electrical or air suspension quirks are considered.
