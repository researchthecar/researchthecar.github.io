The 2019 Volvo XC60 is a compact luxury SUV known for its stylish design, comfortable ride, and advanced safety features. Engine options for the 2019 XC60 include the T5, a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 producing 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque; the T6, a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged inline-4 producing 316 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque; and the T8 eAWD Plug-In Hybrid, a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged inline-4 combined with an electric motor, producing a total system output of 400 horsepower and 472 lb-ft of torque. All engine options are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally praise the XC60 for its elegant interior, comfortable seats, and smooth ride. Common complaints include the infotainment system's complexity and some reported issues with the vehicle's electronics.

**Electric Motor**

**T8 eAWD Plug-In Hybrid**
*   **Reduced Electric Range**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report that the actual electric range of the T8 hybrid is significantly lower than the manufacturer's advertised range, especially in colder weather or with frequent use of climate control.
    *   Solution: Check the battery health and consider software updates from the dealer. Adjust driving habits to maximize efficiency.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software updates may be free; battery diagnosis varies.

**Engine**

**T5 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**
*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report higher than expected oil consumption, requiring more frequent oil top-offs between scheduled oil changes.
    *   Solution: Monitor oil levels regularly. If consumption is excessive, consult with a Volvo technician for diagnosis, which may involve checking for leaks or internal engine wear.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the cause, ranging from minor repairs to more extensive engine work.

**T6 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4**
*   **Supercharger Clutch Noise**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A rattling or clattering noise from the supercharger, especially at idle or low speeds, indicating a potential issue with the supercharger clutch.
    *   Solution: Inspection and potential replacement of the supercharger clutch or the entire supercharger assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+

**T8 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4 Plug-In Hybrid**
*   **Coolant Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: External coolant leak is a common issue. The level of coolant will be consistently low.
    *   Solution: Have a mechanic inspect and replace any damaged parts
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**All Models with 8-Speed Automatic (Aisin Warner)**
*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported harsh or jerky shifting, particularly at lower speeds. This may be more noticeable when the transmission is cold.
    *   Solution: Check the transmission fluid level and condition. A software update may be available to improve shift quality. In some cases, a valve body replacement or complete transmission rebuild might be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software update may be free or low cost; valve body replacement $1500 - $3000+; transmission rebuild $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Rebooting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Sensus Connect infotainment system can freeze, become unresponsive, or randomly reboot. This can affect navigation, audio, climate control, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Try a system reset (holding the home button). Software updates from Volvo are frequently released to address these issues.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software updates may be free; hardware replacements can be more costly.
*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Parking sensors or blind spot monitoring systems may intermittently fail or provide false alerts.
    *   Solution: Check sensor cleanliness. Diagnose with a scan tool to identify the faulty sensor(s) and replace them.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per sensor.
*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain, especially if the vehicle is not driven frequently. This can lead to difficulty starting the car.
    *   Solution: Check for parasitic draws. Ensure the battery is in good condition. Software updates might address some battery management issues.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Battery replacement $200 - $400; diagnosis of parasitic drain varies.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Water Leaks from Tail Lights**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water can leak into the cargo area through the tail lights due to a faulty seal or design flaw.
    *   Solution: Replace tail light seals or the entire tail light assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sunroof might stick, fail to close properly, or make excessive noise during operation.
    *   Solution: Lubricate sunroof tracks. Inspect and replace damaged components (motor, cables, etc.).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Volvo XC60 presents a mix of potential issues, with electrical and infotainment system glitches being among the most commonly reported problems. While the engine and transmission issues are less frequent, they can be costly to repair. The T8 hybrid owners should be aware of potential reduced electric range, depending on environment and frequent coolant leaks. Overall, while these issues should be considered, many owners experience reliable and enjoyable ownership.
