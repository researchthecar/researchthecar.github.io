The 2016 Volvo XC90 is a luxury SUV that marked a new generation for Volvo, featuring a sleek design, a spacious interior, and advanced safety features. The XC90 offers a range of engine options, including a 2.0L turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine (T6) producing 316 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, as well as a 2.0L turbocharged four-cylinder engine (T5) producing 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. There's also a T8 Twin Engine plug-in hybrid variant combining the T6 engine with an electric motor, delivering a total output of 400 horsepower and 472 lb-ft of torque. All engines are paired with an Aisin-Warner 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the XC90 for its comfortable ride, elegant interior, and innovative technology. Common complaints often revolve around the complexity of the infotainment system and potential reliability issues, particularly with earlier model years.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged (T6)**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. May be related to piston ring design.
    *   Solution: Replacing the piston rings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Supercharger Clutch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining or rattling noise from the engine, reduced power, and potential check engine light. The supercharger clutch may fail prematurely.
    *   Solution: Replacing the supercharger clutch. In some cases, the entire supercharger assembly may need replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged (T5)**

*   **No Issues Found**

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged with Electric Motor (T8 Twin Engine)**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Aisin-Warner 8-Speed Automatic (All Engine Options)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or delayed shifts between gears, particularly at lower speeds. Hesitation when accelerating from a stop.
    *   Solution: Software update and/or valve body replacement. In more severe cases, a full transmission rebuild or replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 (software update) - $5,000+ (transmission replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Failure (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, air compressor running constantly, suspension fault warnings on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacing faulty air struts, air compressor, or air lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+ per strut, $500 - $1,500+ for the compressor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues (Sensus)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: System freezing, slow response times, Bluetooth connectivity problems, navigation errors, and unresponsive touchscreen.
    *   Solution: Software updates, resetting the system, or replacing the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,500+ (unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Battery draining overnight or after a short period of inactivity. May be caused by a faulty module or accessory.
    *   Solution: Identifying and replacing the parasitic drain source. Common culprits include the infotainment system, door modules, or alarm system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracking or splitting of the dashboard material, particularly in warmer climates.
    *   Solution: Replacing the dashboard or using a dashboard cover.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Volvo XC90, while lauded for its design and features, is prone to a few recurring issues. The T6 engine can experience oil consumption and supercharger problems, while the 8-speed transmission might exhibit harsh shifting. Electrical glitches with the infotainment system and battery drain are also concerns. If equipped, air suspension failure can be costly to repair. Dashboard cracking can also occur. While not every vehicle will experience these problems, potential buyers should be aware of these common issues. Repair costs can range from a few hundred dollars for software updates to several thousand for major engine or transmission repairs.
