The 2018 Volvo XC60 is a compact luxury SUV known for its stylish design, comfortable ride, and advanced safety features. Engine options include the T5 (2.0L turbocharged inline-4, 250 hp and 258 lb-ft of torque), the T6 (2.0L turbocharged and supercharged inline-4, 316 hp and 295 lb-ft of torque), and the T8 eAWD Plug-in Hybrid (2.0L turbocharged and supercharged inline-4 + electric motor, 400 hp and 472 lb-ft of torque). All engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the XC60's upscale interior, comfortable seats, and intuitive infotainment system. Common dislikes include the sometimes-complex infotainment interface and the higher price point compared to some competitors.

**Electric Motor**

**T8 eAWD Plug-in Hybrid**
*   **High Voltage Coolant Heater Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The vehicle may display a "Low Battery" or "Hybrid System Malfunction" warning. The heater, responsible for warming the cabin and maintaining battery temperature, fails prematurely.
    *   Solution: Replacing the High Voltage Coolant Heater.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**All Engine Options - 8-Speed Automatic (Aisin Warner TG-81SC)**
*   **Rough or Delayed Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifts, especially between 2nd and 3rd gear, or delayed engagement when shifting from Park or Neutral into Drive or Reverse.
    *   Solution: Software update, valve body replacement, or complete transmission replacement in severe cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (software update) - $4000+ (transmission replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Issues (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The vehicle may sag on one side or fail to raise to the proper ride height. The air compressor may run continuously, or error messages related to the suspension system may appear. Air leaks in the system can occur.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty air strut, compressor, or air lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+ (per strut), $500 - $1000+ (compressor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sensus Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen freezes, becomes unresponsive, or restarts randomly. This can affect navigation, audio, climate control, and other vehicle functions controlled through the system.
    *   Solution: Performing a software update, resetting the system, or replacing the Central Electronic Module (CEM) in severe cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1200+ (CEM replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The vehicle battery drains overnight or within a few days of non-use, leading to difficulty starting the engine or electrical system malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Identifying and repairing the parasitic draw (e.g., faulty module, improperly grounded circuit), replacing the battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+ (diagnostic and repair), $200 - $400+ (battery replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Cracked Tail Light Housings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks appear in the tail light housings, often around the edges or mounting points. This can lead to water ingress and damage to the bulbs or electrical components.
    *   Solution: Replacing the cracked tail light housing.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ (per tail light)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin, typically around the headliner or A-pillars, during rain or when washing the car. This is often due to clogged sunroof drain tubes.
    *   Solution: Clearing the sunroof drain tubes, repairing or replacing the sunroof seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ (drain cleaning), $300 - $800+ (seal replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2018 Volvo XC60 exhibits several potential issues, ranging from relatively minor infotainment glitches to more significant problems with the transmission, air suspension (if equipped), and hybrid components (for T8 models). The severity and cost of these issues vary widely, but some repairs, such as transmission or air suspension work, can be quite expensive. Potential buyers should be aware of these common complaints and thoroughly inspect the vehicle's service history and overall condition before making a purchase.
