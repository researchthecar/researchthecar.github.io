The 2021 Volvo XC60 is a compact luxury SUV known for its stylish design, comfortable interior, and emphasis on safety. Engine options include a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder (T5) producing 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder (T6) generating 316 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder with a plug-in hybrid system (Recharge/T8) producing 400 horsepower and 472 lb-ft of torque (later increased). All engines are paired with an Aisin-Warner 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the XC60's elegant interior, comfortable ride, and advanced safety features. Common complaints include the sometimes-complex infotainment system and the limited cargo space compared to some competitors.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder (T5)**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than expected oil consumption, requiring more frequent oil top-offs between scheduled services.
    *   Solution: Monitoring oil levels regularly and addressing any leaks or potential engine issues promptly. Performing an oil consumption test to determine if a problem exists. Some cases may require engine component replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$5000+ depending on the repair
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Four-Cylinder (T6)**

*   **Coolant Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaking from the water pump area or hoses. Low coolant light illumination and/or overheating.
    *   Solution: Replacing leaking hoses or the water pump.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Four-Cylinder Plug-in Hybrid (Recharge/T8)**

*   **Battery Degradation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced electric driving range and overall battery capacity over time.
    *   Solution: Battery management software updates. In severe cases, battery module replacement or full battery pack replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software update usually free. Battery module or pack replacement $2,000 - $20,000+ depending on components
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin-Warner 8-Speed Automatic (All Engines)**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or jerky gear changes, especially during low-speed acceleration or deceleration.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush. Software update. In severe cases, valve body replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$2500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Lagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Sensus infotainment system becomes unresponsive, freezes, or experiences noticeable lag when navigating menus or using features.
    *   Solution: Performing a system reset. Applying software updates. In some cases, replacing the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (reset) - $1500+ (unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erroneous alerts or non-functionality of parking sensors or blind spot monitoring systems.
    *   Solution: Cleaning the sensors. Replacing faulty sensors. Recalibrating the system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof motor failure, getting stuck open or closed, or excessive wind noise.
    *   Solution: Replacing the sunroof motor or repairing the sunroof mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Seat Heater Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Seat heaters not functioning or providing inadequate heat.
    *   Solution: Replacing the heating element or control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Volvo XC60 has some reported issues, some of which are more complex and expensive than others to address. Electrical issues, particularly with the infotainment system, are common annoyances. Engine issues can range from minor to more serious, and the plug-in hybrid models introduce potential battery-related concerns. Transmission issues are also not uncommon. The severity of these issues varies from minor inconveniences to potentially costly repairs. Overall, while the XC60 offers a luxurious and safe driving experience, potential buyers should be aware of these reported problems and factor them into their purchasing decision and budget.
