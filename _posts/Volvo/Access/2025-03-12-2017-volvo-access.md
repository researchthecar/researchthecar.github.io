The 2017 Volvo XC90 is a luxury SUV known for its stylish design, comfortable interior, and advanced safety features. Engine options for the 2017 XC90 include the T5 (2.0L Turbocharged I4, 250 hp and 258 lb-ft of torque), the T6 (2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged I4, 316 hp and 295 lb-ft of torque), and the T8 (2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged I4 Plug-in Hybrid, 400 hp and 472 lb-ft of torque combined). All engine options are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the XC90's elegant interior, comfortable ride, and advanced technology, while some criticisms include the complexity of the infotainment system and potential reliability concerns.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged (T5)**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. Can lead to engine damage if left unaddressed.
    *   Solution: Replacing the piston rings and potentially the pistons.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged (T6)**

*   **Supercharger Clutch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining or grinding noise from the engine, reduced power, and potential check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the supercharger clutch.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Plug-in Hybrid (T8)**

*   **High Voltage Battery Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced electric range, failure to charge, error messages related to the hybrid system.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and potential replacement of battery modules or the entire high-voltage battery pack.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $15,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner 8-Speed Automatic (All Engine Options)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifts between gears, hesitation when accelerating, particularly at lower speeds.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush and software update. In some cases, valve body replacement may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Failure (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, inability to adjust ride height, error messages related to the air suspension system, noise from the air compressor.
    *   Solution: Replacement of air struts, air compressor, or air lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,500+ per strut, $500 - $1,500+ for the compressor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System (Sensus) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: System freezing, slow response times, Bluetooth connectivity problems, navigation malfunctions, screen blackouts.
    *   Solution: Software updates, system resets, or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,500+ (depending on the severity and repair needed)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Parking Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: False alarms, failure to detect obstacles, error messages.
    *   Solution: Cleaning or replacement of faulty parking sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin, particularly after rain or car washes.
    *   Solution: Cleaning or replacement of sunroof seals and drainage tubes.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing on the dashboard, particularly in areas exposed to sunlight.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the dashboard (typically not cost-effective), or installation of a dashboard cover.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+ for replacement, $50 - $200 for a cover
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:** The 2017 Volvo XC90 presents a range of potential issues, varying in severity and cost. Engine issues, such as oil consumption and supercharger clutch failure, can be costly to repair. Transmission issues are prevalent across all engine options. Electrical problems, particularly with the infotainment system, are also common. Suspension and sunroof issues can add to the ownership costs. While not all vehicles will experience these problems, potential buyers should be aware of these common complaints and budget accordingly.
