The 2017 Volvo VNL is a popular heavy-duty truck often praised for its comfortable ride, spacious cabin, and fuel efficiency. Engine options for the 2017 VNL included the Volvo D11, D13, and Cummins ISX15. The Volvo D13 engine, a common choice, produces between 405 and 500 horsepower and between 1450 and 1850 lb-ft of torque, depending on the specific configuration. Common complaints about the VNL often relate to the complexity of its electrical systems and the cost of replacement parts.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**
*   **EGR Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, increased emissions, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace the EGR valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **DPF (Diesel Particulate Filter) Clogging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption, frequent regeneration cycles, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Clean or replace the DPF.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, reduced fuel economy, black smoke from exhaust.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,200+ per injector
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Cummins ISX15**
*   **EGR Cooler Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, engine overheating.
    *   Solution: Replace the EGR cooler.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke, unusual turbocharger noise.
    *   Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Pump Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hard starting, stalling, loss of power.
    *   Solution: Replace the fuel pump.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift**
*   **Shift Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, erratic shifting, transmission warning light.
    *   Solution: Replace the shift actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears, unusual noises during shifting.
    *   Solution: Replace the clutch assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sagging suspension, uneven ride height, air compressor running excessively.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace leaking air bags, lines, or valves.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ per air bag
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, excessive body roll.
    *   Solution: Replace shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per shock
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, malfunctioning lights, sensor failures.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace damaged wiring harnesses.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures (various)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, performance issues, inaccurate readings.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty sensors (e.g., NOx sensor, temperature sensor).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **ECU (Engine Control Unit) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various engine performance problems, difficulty starting, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Reprogram or replace the ECU.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside headlight housings, reduced visibility.
    *   Solution: Replace or reseal headlight housings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per headlight
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Mirror Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inability to adjust side mirrors.
    *   Solution: Replace the mirror motor assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per mirror
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent heating or cooling, blower motor failure, faulty blend doors.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace faulty HVAC components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Instrument Cluster Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent gauge failures, display issues, warning lights not functioning.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the instrument cluster.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2017 Volvo VNL, while generally a reliable truck, can experience issues with its engine (particularly EGR and DPF issues), transmission (I-Shift shift actuator), and electrical systems. Repair costs can range from a few hundred dollars for minor sensor replacements to several thousand dollars for major component replacements like turbos, ECUs, or transmissions. The severity of the issues varies, but electrical problems and emissions-related failures are the most frequently reported.
