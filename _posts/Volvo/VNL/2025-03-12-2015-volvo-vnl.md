The 2015 Volvo VNL is a popular choice for long-haul trucking, known for its comfortable cabin, fuel efficiency, and overall reliability. However, like any vehicle, it has its share of common issues and complaints. Engine options for the 2015 VNL typically include the Volvo D11, D13, and Cummins ISX15. The Volvo D13 produces around 405-500 horsepower and 1450-1850 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options commonly consist of the Volvo I-Shift automated manual transmission, Eaton Fuller manual transmissions, and Allison automatic transmissions. Owners often praise the VNL's spacious sleeper cab and smooth ride, while some common dislikes revolve around the cost of maintenance and the complexity of some of the electronic systems.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**

*   **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Frequent DPF regeneration cycles, reduced engine power, and DPF warning lights. May be caused by excessive soot buildup or faulty sensors.
    *   Solution: Forced DPF regeneration, DPF cleaning, or DPF replacement. Replacing faulty sensors such as differential pressure sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **EGR Valve Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, reduced engine power, increased emissions, and EGR valve-related diagnostic trouble codes.
    *   Solution: EGR valve replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, poor fuel economy, rough running, and difficulty starting.
    *   Solution: Fuel injector cleaning or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $2,000+ depending on how many injectors are replaced.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Cummins ISX15**

*   **Exhaust Manifold Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loud exhaust noise, reduced engine power, poor fuel economy, and soot buildup around the exhaust manifold.
    *   Solution: Exhaust manifold gasket replacement and/or manifold repair or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,200
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Injector Cup Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Fuel in the coolant, coolant loss, and engine misfires.
    *   Solution: Injector cup replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke, and turbocharger noise.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift**

*   **Shift Actuator Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, erratic shifting behavior, and transmission warning lights.
    *   Solution: Shift actuator replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clutch slippage, difficulty engaging gears, and clutch noise.
    *   Solution: Clutch adjustment, repair, or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Control Module (TCM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, failure to shift, and diagnostic trouble codes related to the TCM.
    *   Solution: TCM reprogramming or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Eaton Fuller Manual Transmissions**

*   **Shift Fork Damage**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting into certain gears, grinding noises during shifting, and potential gear lockout.
    *   Solution: Replacement of damaged shift forks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,500+ (depending on extent of transmission disassembly required)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Synchronizer Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Grinding or clashing noises during shifting, especially when downshifting, and difficulty engaging certain gears.
    *   Solution: Replacement of worn synchronizers. This often requires a full transmission rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+ (transmission rebuild)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Spring Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sagging suspension, uneven ride height, and air leaks.
    *   Solution: Air spring replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000 per air spring
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Shock Absorber Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, and excessive body roll.
    *   Solution: Shock absorber replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800 per shock absorber
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sensor Failures (Various)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various warning lights, engine performance issues, and inaccurate readings.
    *   Solution: Replacement of faulty sensors (e.g., coolant temperature sensor, oil pressure sensor).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500 per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Wiring Harness Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, short circuits, and corrosion.
    *   Solution: Wiring harness repair or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+ depending on the severity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Multiplexing Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic behavior of various electrical components, difficulty diagnosing problems due to the interconnected systems.
    *   Solution: Software updates, component replacement, or troubleshooting by a specialist.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Headlight condensation, dim lighting, and headlight failure.
    *   Solution: Headlight replacement or resealing.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent heating or cooling, blower motor failure, and refrigerant leaks.
    *   Solution: Component replacement (e.g., blower motor, compressor) or refrigerant recharge.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Gauge Cluster Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inaccurate readings, flickering displays, and complete gauge cluster failure.
    *   Solution: Gauge cluster repair or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Volvo VNL presents a range of potential issues, from engine and transmission problems to suspension, electrical, and interior malfunctions. The severity and cost of these issues can vary greatly depending on the specific problem and the extent of the damage. Engine and transmission repairs can be particularly expensive, while electrical and interior issues often involve lower costs. Regular maintenance and addressing problems promptly can help mitigate the risk of major repairs.
