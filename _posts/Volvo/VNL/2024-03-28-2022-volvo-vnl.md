**Introduction:**

The 2022 Volvo VNL is a heavy-duty truck known for its fuel efficiency, comfort, and safety features. It is available with a range of engine and transmission options, including:

* Engine: D11 (330-425 hp), D13 (405-500 hp)
* Transmission: I-Shift 12-speed, I-Shift 13-speed, I-Shift 14-speed

Customers appreciate the VNL's spacious cabin, user-friendly dashboard, and advanced safety systems. However, some owners have reported issues with certain components.

**Engine**

**D11 Engine**

* **EGR Valve Failure:** Symptoms include reduced engine power, increased emissions, and a check engine light. Solution involves replacing the EGR valve and cleaning the intake system. Estimated cost: $800 - $1,200+

**D13 Engine**

* **Exhaust Gas Temperature (EGT) Sensor Issues:** Symptoms include false readings, reduced engine power, and a check engine light. Solution involves replacing the EGT sensor and recalibrating the engine control module. Estimated cost: $400 - $600
* **Crankshaft Seal Leakage:** Symptoms include oil leaking from the engine. Solution involves replacing the crankshaft seal. Estimated cost: $600 - $800+

**Transmission**

**I-Shift 12-Speed**

* **Transmission Control Unit (TCU) Malfunction:** Symptoms include erratic gear shifting, loss of communication with the engine, and a check transmission light. Solution involves replacing the transmission control unit and reprogramming the transmission. Estimated cost: $1,000 - $1,500+

**I-Shift 13-Speed**

* **Shift Fork Failure:** Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises, and a check transmission light. Solution involves replacing the shift fork and rebuilding the transmission. Estimated cost: $2,000 - $3,500+

**I-Shift 14-Speed**

* **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Breakage:** Symptoms include sagging rear end, uneven ride height, and a rattling noise. Solution involves replacing the broken leaf spring. Estimated cost: $300 - $600 per spring
* **Airbag Suspension Leaks:** Symptoms include a drop in ride height, uneven loading, and air leaks. Solution involves repairing or replacing the airbags and associated components. Estimated cost: $500 - $1,000+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Symptoms include reduced battery voltage, dim lights, and a check charging system light. Solution involves replacing the alternator. Estimated cost: $500 - $800+
* **Wire Harness Damage:** Symptoms include electrical shorts, intermittent component failures, and check engine lights. Solution involves repairing or replacing the damaged wiring harness. Estimated cost: $200 - $600+

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:** Symptoms include moisture inside the headlight assemblies. Solution involves replacing the headlights or installing ventilation screens. Estimated cost: $200 - $400 per headlight
* **Windshield Cracks:** Symptoms include visible cracks or chips in the windshield. Solution involves replacing the windshield. Estimated cost: $300 - $600+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Panel Creaks:** Symptoms include annoying rattling or creaking noises coming from the dashboard. Solution involves tightening screws, replacing clips, or applying sound-dampening materials. Estimated cost: $100 - $300
* **Seat Heater Malfunction:** Symptoms include non-functioning seat heaters. Solution involves replacing the heating element or control module. Estimated cost: $200 - $500
* **Cup Holder Breakage:** Symptoms include broken or cracked cup holders. Solution involves replacing the cup holder assembly. Estimated cost: $50 - $150

**Recalls:**

- NHTSA Campaign Number 22V211000: Airbag may not deploy properly
- NHTSA Campaign Number 22V823000: Brake master cylinder may leak

**Summary:**

While the 2022 Volvo VNL offers many advantages, some owners have experienced issues with certain components, particularly in the engine, transmission, and suspension systems. The severity and cost of these issues vary, with some being relatively minor but others potentially requiring extensive and expensive repairs.
