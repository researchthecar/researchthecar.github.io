**2014 Volvo VNL**

The 2014 Volvo VNL is a heavy-duty truck known for its power, reliability, and comfort. It is powered by a range of engines, including the Volvo D11, D13, and D16, and offers transmission options including an I-Shift automated manual transmission and a manual transmission.

While the 2014 Volvo VNL is generally well-regarded, some owners have reported specific issues with their vehicles. Here is a comprehensive list of the problems commonly encountered with the 2014 Volvo VNL, organized by major component categories:

**Engine**

**Engine: Volvo D11**

* **Turbocharger failure**
    * Symptoms: Loss of power, increased exhaust smoke
    * Solution: Replace turbocharger
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **EGR valve issues**
    * Symptoms: Reduced engine performance, increased emissions
    * Solution: Clean or replace EGR valve
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Engine: Volvo D13**

* **Injector failure**
    * Symptoms: Engine misfire, rough idle, reduced power
    * Solution: Replace injector
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Oil leak from rear main seal**
    * Symptoms: Oil leak at the rear of the engine
    * Solution: Replace rear main seal
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Engine: Volvo D16**

* **Cylinder head gasket failure**
    * Symptoms: Coolant leak, overheating, white smoke from exhaust
    * Solution: Replace cylinder head gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Intake manifold failure**
    * Symptoms: Loss of power, reduced fuel efficiency
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Transmission: I-Shift automated manual transmission**

* **Transmission control module (TCM) failure**
    * Symptoms: Transmission slipping, gearshifting issues
    * Solution: Replace TCM
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Clutch failure**
    * Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears
    * Solution: Replace clutch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission: Manual transmission**

* **Synchronizer failure**
    * Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises
    * Solution: Replace synchronizer
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Transmission bearing failure**
    * Symptoms: Noisy transmission, loss of gears
    * Solution: Replace transmission bearings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Leaf spring failure**
    * Symptoms: Sagging suspension, uneven tire wear
    * Solution: Replace leaf springs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Air bag suspension leak**
    * Symptoms: Air leak, sagging suspension
    * Solution: Repair or replace air bag
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery failure**
    * Symptoms: Dead battery, electrical problems
    * Solution: Replace battery
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator failure**
    * Symptoms: Dimming headlights, electrical problems
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight condensation**
    * Symptoms: Foggy headlights
    * Solution: Replace headlight housing
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rust on frame**
    * Symptoms: Rust spots on the frame
    * Solution: Repair rust
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on severity
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Steering wheel cracking**
    * Symptoms: Cracks in the steering wheel material
    * Solution: Replace steering wheel
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Seat switch failure**
    * Symptoms: Seat adjustments not working
    * Solution: Replace seat switch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2014 Volvo VNL is generally a reliable and well-built truck, but there are some known issues that owners should be aware of. The most common engine problems are turbocharger failure and injector failure, while transmission issues include TCM failure and clutch failure. Suspension and electrical issues are relatively minor, but exterior and interior problems can be more cosmetic and less severe. Overall, the 2014 Volvo VNL is a solid truck with some potential issues that can be addressed with proper maintenance and repairs.
