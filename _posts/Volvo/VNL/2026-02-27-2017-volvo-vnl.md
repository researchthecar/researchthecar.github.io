# 2017 Volvo VNL Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Volvo VNL is a prominent long-haul semi-truck known for its comfortable cab and diverse powertrain configurations designed for highway efficiency. While commercial heavy-duty trucks typically log massive mileage and face heavy industrial use, prospective buyers should inspect powertrain components and electronic systems closely. Reviewing maintenance logs and conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help uncover hidden wear common to heavy-duty commercial fleets.

---

## Engine

### Volvo D13 12.8L Turbo-Diesel I6

The Volvo D13 is a 12.8-liter inline-six turbo-diesel engine typically outputting between 375 and 500 horsepower. It is a widely used commercial powerplant engineered for long-haul fuel economy and heavy freight duties.

### Volvo D16 16.1L Turbo-Diesel I6

The Volvo D16 is a heavy-duty 16.1-liter inline-six turbo-diesel engine offering higher horsepower and torque ratings up to 600 hp for extreme hauling. It provides robust pulling power for heavy-haul applications.

### Cummins ISX15 15.0L Turbo-Diesel I6

The Cummins ISX15 is a 15-liter heavy-duty diesel engine known for widespread parts availability and high torque output. It serves as a popular alternative powerplant in commercial highway tractors.

## Transmission

### Volvo I-Shift 12-Speed Automated Manual Transmission

The Volvo I-Shift is an advanced 12-speed automated manual transmission designed to optimize fuel efficiency and ease driver fatigue. Regular clutch calibration and fluid maintenance are vital for long-term operational reliability.

### Eaton Fuller 10/13/18-Speed Manual Transmission

Eaton Fuller manual transmissions offer traditional heavy-duty mechanical shifting preferred by many fleet operators for durability and straightforward service. Proper clutch adjustment and gear synchronization checks are recommended during routine inspections.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V668000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Diesel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNL&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The engine may also stall without warning, resulting in the vehicle's inability to restart, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Cummins will notify owners, and dealers will replace the single screen filter banjo bolt will be replaced with a dual screen filter banjo bolt, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V928000: Service Brakes, Air](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNL&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the brake system becomes contaminated, braking performance could be affected, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the vehicle control module and replace the air dryer filter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V927000: Suspension:Front:Hub](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNL&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Because of this, the driver may lose control of the vehicle or parts may be dropped on the road, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will replace the hub assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V393000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Diesel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNL&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Cummins will notify owners, and Cummins dealers will replace the existing ECM with a new ECM, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V097000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNL&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Also, the bolt connecting the upper steering shaft to the lower steering shaft may not have been properly tightened. Separation of the steering shaft will result in complete loss of steering which may lead to a vehicle crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.
