# 2016 Volvo VNL Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used heavy-duty commercial truck like the 2016 Volvo VNL, understanding potential mechanical trouble spots is essential for fleet owners and independent operators alike. Owner reports submitted to safety databases highlight several recurring areas of concern, particularly regarding drivetrain alignment and braking behavior. Reviewing these owner experiences can help buyers ask the right pre-purchase inspection questions.

---

## Engine

### Volvo D13 12.8L Diesel Engine

The Volvo D13 is a 12.8-liter inline-six turbocharged diesel engine, typically offering horsepower ratings ranging from 375 to 500 hp and robust torque output designed for heavy hauling. It serves as the primary powerplant for long-haul applications, balancing fuel efficiency with high mileage durability when properly maintained.

## Transmission

### Volvo I-Shift Automated Manual Transmission

The Volvo I-Shift is an automated manual transmission engineered to optimize fuel economy and smooth shifting in commercial operations. While generally dependable, owners have noted specific powertrain vibration concerns that require careful diagnostic checks.

**Driveshaft and Transmission Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience severe vehicle shaking, vibrations during acceleration, or issues related to out-of-phase driveshafts interacting with the transmission.
- **Solution:** Inspect driveshaft alignment and phasing, verify factory calibration specs, and ensure transmission output components are properly balanced.

## Suspension

### Cab Suspension and Bushings

Suspension components on long-haul trucks endure heavy stresses, and worn bushings can lead to noticeable cab sway and ride discomfort.

**Cab Sway and Excessive Bouncing**
- **Symptoms:** The cab sways back and forth or leans to the side while driving, or the vehicle exhibits abnormal hopping and shaking at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect cab bushings, shock absorbers, and suspension components for premature wear or failure.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The service brake system is crucial for heavy commercial vehicles, but some owners have reported abnormal shaking and stability concerns during braking events.

**Brake Pedal Vibration and Vehicle Pulling**
- **Symptoms:** Applying the brake pedal results in abnormal vibrations, violent shaking, or the vehicle independently veering to one side during stops, particularly in wet weather.
- **Solution:** Have a certified commercial truck technician inspect brake rotors, pads, alignment, and ABS sensors for wear or defects.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V668000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Diesel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNL&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The engine may also stall without warning, resulting in the vehicle's inability to restart, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Cummins will notify owners, and dealers will replace the single screen filter banjo bolt will be replaced with a dual screen filter banjo bolt, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V536000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNL&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The castellated nut on the steer axles may not be properly torqued, allowing the tie rod to loosen. If the tie rod loosens, it may disconnect from the steering knuckle, causing a complete loss of steering, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the torque of the castellated nut and tie rod, tightening it as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V928000: Service Brakes, Air](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNL&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the brake system becomes contaminated, braking performance could be affected, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the vehicle control module and replace the air dryer filter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V097000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNL&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Also, the bolt connecting the upper steering shaft to the lower steering shaft may not have been properly tightened. Separation of the steering shaft will result in complete loss of steering which may lead to a vehicle crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 15V026000: Parking Brake:Driveline:Hydraulic:Actuator](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNL&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** This air pressure in turn, if not vented, may prevent full release of the actuator unit, and may induce brake drag and may result in overheated wheelend service brakes, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the brake actuator and replace, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V095000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm:Upper Ball Joint](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=VNL&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Failure of the ball joint may cause separation of the socket and a complete loss of steering, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will replace the drag links, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Volvo VNL is a capable long-haul commercial truck, but prospective buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the drivetrain, driveshaft alignment, and braking systems. Paying close attention to reported vibration issues and suspension components can save buyers from unexpected post-purchase repair expenses.
