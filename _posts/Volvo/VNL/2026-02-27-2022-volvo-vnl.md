While specific issues can arise with any complex machinery, the 2022 model year generally upholds Volvo's reputation for building dependable trucks. ### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Volvo VNL demonstrates a high level of technical reliability, consistent with Volvo's reputation in the heavy-duty truck market. Major powertrain components, including the various Volvo diesel engines (D11, D13, D13 TC, D16) and the I-Shift transmission, show no widespread reported issues, indicating robust engineering and manufacturing. Similarly, suspension systems are generally reliable. The primary areas where minor issues have been noted are within the electrical system, specifically concerning wiring harness connections and control module software, and occasionally with the interior infotainment touchscreen. These are not systemic failures but rather isolated incidents that can occur in complex vehicle electronics. Overall, the 2022 VNL is a dependable choice for commercial fleets, with potential repair costs primarily associated with electrical gremlins rather than core mechanical failures.
