**Introduction:**

The 2018 Volvo S60 is a compact luxury sedan available with a range of powertrains. The T5 engine produces 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, while the T6 engine generates 316 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. The S60 is praised for its luxurious interior, responsive handling, and advanced safety features, but it has also received complaints regarding some specific components.

**Engine**

* **T5 Engine**
    * **Intake Manifold Issues:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Cracking or warping of the intake manifold, causing coolant leaks and reduced engine performance.
        * Solution: Replacement of the intake manifold.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

* **T6 Engine**
    * **Turbocharger Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Premature failure of the turbocharger, resulting in reduced power and increased emissions.
        * Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **8-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Shifting Problems:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Hesitation or jerking during gear changes, especially when upshifting.
        * Solution: Software updates or transmission fluid replacement.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Struts:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature wear of the front suspension struts, leading to rattling or clunking noises while driving.
    * Solution: Replacement of the suspension struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electrical Gremlins:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical issues, such as malfunctioning lights, sensors, or power windows.
    * Solution: Troubleshooting and repair of specific electrical components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Exterior Trim Pieces:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Fading or cracking of the exterior trim pieces, especially the chrome accents.
    * Solution: Replacement of the affected trim pieces.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Creaking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or rattling noises coming from the dashboard area, especially when driving over rough roads.
    * Solution: Removal of the dashboard and tightening of the mounting points.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2018 Volvo S60 has received some complaints regarding specific components, including the intake manifold, turbocharger, suspension struts, electrical issues, and exterior trim pieces. While these issues can be costly to fix, they do not appear to be widespread and the overall reliability of the vehicle is generally considered to be good. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues before purchasing a used S60.
