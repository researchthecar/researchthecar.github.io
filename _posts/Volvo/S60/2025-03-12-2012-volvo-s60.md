The 2012 Volvo S60 is a compact luxury sedan known for its safety, comfort, and Scandinavian design. Engine options included a 2.5L turbocharged inline-5 producing 250 hp and 266 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.0L turbocharged inline-6 producing 300 hp and 325 lb-ft of torque. All engine options were paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the S60 for its comfortable ride, supportive seats, and distinctive styling. Common dislikes include the sometimes-complicated infotainment system and higher maintenance costs compared to non-luxury brands.

**Engine**

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks commonly occur from the PCV (positive crankcase ventilation) system. Symptoms include oil consumption, oil smells, and visible oil leaks.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the PCV valve or entire PCV system assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Ignition Coil Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough running engine, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacement of faulty ignition coils.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+ depending on how many coils need replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6**

*   **Cracked Cylinder Head**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Overheating, coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, rough running engine.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the cracked cylinder head.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Similar to the 2.5L engine, oil leaks can occur from various locations, including the valve cover gasket and turbocharger oil lines.
    *   Solution: Replace gaskets or lines as necessary to stop the leaks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic (All Engine Options)**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, slipping between gears, delayed engagement, or failure to shift.
    *   Solution: Replacement or rebuild of the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering at low speeds, stalling, or inability to shift.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Strut Mount Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the front suspension, especially when going over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the strut mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+ for both sides.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rear Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, and leaking fluid from the shocks.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the rear shocks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+ for both sides.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Software Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent issues with the infotainment system, warning lights appearing for no apparent reason, or other electronic malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Software update at the dealership.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The battery frequently dies, especially if the car sits for a few days.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair of parasitic drain, which could be caused by a faulty module or accessory.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the cause
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Drain Clogs**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin, particularly in the headliner area.
    *   Solution: Cleaning the sunroof drains to remove debris.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $80 - $200+ (if performed by a shop; can be DIY)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Navigation Screen Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Screen goes black, unresponsive to touch, or flickering.
    * Solution: Replacement of the screen.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Seat Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power seats stop working or only move in certain directions.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the seat motor or switch.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2012 Volvo S60 can be a reliable and comfortable car, but potential buyers should be aware of the common issues. Engine oil leaks and transmission problems, particularly valve body issues, are relatively frequent and can be costly to repair. Electrical glitches and suspension component failures are also worth noting. While some issues are minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more significant. Overall, the severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to major repairs, with costs potentially ranging from a few hundred to several thousand dollars. Pre-purchase inspections and careful maintenance are recommended.
