The 2014 Volvo S60 is a compact executive sedan known for its safety features, comfortable ride, and understated luxury. Engine options for the 2014 S60 include a 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 (T5 Drive-E) producing around 240 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque and a 2.5L turbocharged inline-5 (T5) producing around 250 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque. There is also a 3.0L turbocharged inline-6 (T6) producing 300 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque, and a Polestar version of the T6 which produced 325 horsepower and 354 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 6-speed automatic and an 8-speed automatic (with the Drive-E engine). Owners often praise the S60 for its comfortable seats, quiet cabin, and responsive handling. Some common dislikes include the somewhat dated infotainment system and limited rear passenger space.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (T5 Drive-E)**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-ups between oil changes. Symptoms include low oil level warning lights and visible exhaust smoke.
    *   Solution: The fix varies. Some owners have reported success with software updates, while others have required piston ring replacement or engine replacement in severe cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5 (T5)**

*   **PCV System Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) system can fail, leading to vacuum leaks, rough idling, and potential oil leaks.
    *   Solution: Replacing the PCV valve and associated hoses.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (T6)**

*   **Accessory Belt Tensioner Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The accessory belt tensioner can fail, causing a squealing noise, especially at startup. If left unaddressed, the belt can break, leading to loss of power steering and alternator function.
    *   Solution: Replacing the accessory belt tensioner and belt.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner TF-80SC 6-Speed Automatic (Paired with Inline-5 and Inline-6 Engines)**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or delayed shifting, slipping between gears, and harsh engagement.
    *   Solution: Valve body replacement or rebuild. In some cases, transmission replacement may be recommended.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Aisin Warner TG-81SC 8-Speed Automatic (Paired with Drive-E Engines)**

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibrations at low speeds, especially during light acceleration.
    *   Solution: Torque converter replacement or transmission flush with updated fluid.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Strut Mount Noise**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clicking or popping noise from the front suspension when turning or going over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replacing the front strut mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sunroof Switch Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof not opening or closing properly, or switch being unresponsive.
    *   Solution: Replacing the sunroof switch.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Dimming Interior Lights**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Interior lights dim or flicker.
    *   Solution: Replacing the fuse.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $20 - $40
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Cracking Side Mirror Housings**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracking of side mirror housing
    * Solution: Replacing side mirror housing.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Center Console Button Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The labels on the buttons in the center console can wear off over time.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected buttons or the entire center console trim.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2014 Volvo S60 has some common issues, with engine and transmission problems being the most concerning due to their potential cost and severity. Oil consumption on the 2.0L engine and transmission valve body issues on the 6-speed can be expensive to address. Suspension and electrical issues are generally less severe and less costly to repair. Prospective buyers should be aware of these potential problems and have the vehicle inspected by a qualified mechanic before purchasing.
