# 2018 Volvo S60 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2018 Volvo S60, buyers will find a stylish and comfortable luxury sedan with European flair. While overall owner feedback is manageable, prospective buyers should inspect specific mechanical and electronic subsystems during a pre-purchase evaluation to ensure long-term satisfaction.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine typically produces around 240 horsepower, offering a balance of efficiency and punchy power. It serves as the standard powerplant for many models in this lineup, though buyers should watch for occasional electronic controls issues.

### 2.0L Supercharged and Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

Utilizing both a supercharger and a turbocharger, this engine pushes output to around 302 horsepower for strong, responsive acceleration across the rev range. It provides engaging performance but adds complexity under the hood for used vehicle shoppers.

### Engine Control Module

Owner reports indicate rare instances of early computer control failure that require professional diagnosis.

**Engine Control Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light and potential diagnostic trouble codes indicating computer failure at relatively low mileage.
- **Solution:** Have the diagnostic trouble codes read by a professional and replace the Engine Control Module if internal electronic failure is confirmed.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

This 8-speed automatic transmission channels power smoothly to the wheels while helping optimize highway fuel economy. It generally operates reliably when routine fluid maintenance is kept up to date.

## Brakes

### Brake Booster

Brake booster issues are uncommon but can severely impact stopping capability if they occur.

**Brake Booster Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A popping sound heard when pressing the brake pedal accompanied by a sudden loss of brake assist.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake booster assembly immediately and replace the failed component to restore proper braking pressure.

## Exterior

### Sunroof Glass

Isolated reports highlight rare occurrences of panoramic or sunroof glass failure without external impact.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** A sudden explosion-like sound while driving followed by glass falling into the vehicle cabin.
- **Solution:** Clear out broken glass fragments and replace the shattered sunroof assembly.

## Interior

### HVAC Blower Fan

Climate control fan failures can temporarily hinder front window defogging capabilities.

**HVAC Fan Inoperable**
- **Symptoms:** The climate control fan fails to blow air, preventing effective windshield defrosting and cabin climate control.
- **Solution:** Test the HVAC fan motor and resistor circuits, and replace the blower fan unit if it has failed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V378000: Seat Belts:Front](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=S60&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The flexible steel cable that connects the seat belt to the front outboard seating positions may fatigue over time, causing the seat belt to not secure the occupant in the event of a crash. An unsecured occupant has an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will replace the seat belt anchor cable for both front seats. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Volvo S60 offers a refined driving experience and solid performance options, but used-car buyers should perform a thorough mechanical inspection. Pay close attention to electronics, brake components, and climate control operation during a test drive to avoid unexpected repair bills.
