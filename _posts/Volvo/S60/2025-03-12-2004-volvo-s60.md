The 2004 Volvo S60 is a compact executive sedan known for its safety, comfort, and understated styling. Engine options include a 2.4L inline-5 producing 168 hp and 170 lb-ft of torque, a turbocharged 2.5L inline-5 producing 208 hp and 236 lb-ft of torque, and a high-performance 2.3L turbocharged inline-5 in the S60 T5 producing 247 hp and 243 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual and a 5-speed automatic. Owners generally appreciate the S60's comfortable ride, supportive seats, and solid build quality. Common complaints revolve around electrical issues, some engine-related problems, and the cost of maintenance.

**Engine**

**2.4L Inline-5**

* **Failed Electronic Throttle Module (ETM)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, limp mode, warning lights on the dash (especially the "throttle position" or "engine management" lights), rough idling, stalling.
    * Solution: Replacement or repair of the ETM.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5**

* **PCV System Clogging**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive crankcase pressure, oil leaks (rear main seal, camshaft seals), whistling noise from the engine, rough idle, Check engine light.
    * Solution: Replacing the PCV valve, breather box, and associated hoses.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Turbocharger Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whistling or whining noise from the turbocharger, excessive smoke from the exhaust, check engine light.
    * Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.3L Turbocharged Inline-5 (T5)**

* **Coil Pack Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough running, reduced power, check engine light.
    * Solution: Replacement of faulty coil pack(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ per coil pack
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **Solenoid Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hard shifting, slipping between gears, failure to shift into certain gears, transmission warning light.
    * Solution: Replacement of faulty solenoid(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Control Arm Bushings Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the front suspension, especially when going over bumps. Vehicle may wander or feel unstable.
    * Solution: Replacement of the control arm bushings or the entire control arm.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per side
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Dimming Instrument Cluster Lights**
    * Description/Symptoms: The instrument cluster lights gradually dim or flicker, making it difficult to see the gauges and information.
    * Solution: Replacement of the DIM (Driver Information Module) or repair of the DIM.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical issues affecting various systems (lights, wipers, power windows, etc.). Can be difficult to diagnose.
    * Solution: Replacement or reprogramming of the CEM.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Windshield Wiper Motor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Wipers stop working, work intermittently, or only work on certain settings.
    * Solution: Replacement of the wiper motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sunroof may become stuck, make grinding noises, or fail to open or close completely.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of sunroof motor, gears, or tracks.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2004 Volvo S60, while generally reliable, can suffer from a range of issues. The severity and cost vary widely, with electrical problems and engine issues (particularly related to the ETM, PCV system, and turbocharger) potentially leading to substantial repair bills. Suspension and interior issues are also common and can add to the overall ownership cost. Potential buyers should carefully inspect these areas and obtain a pre-purchase inspection from a qualified mechanic familiar with Volvo vehicles.
