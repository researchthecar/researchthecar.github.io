The 2006 Volvo S60 is a compact executive sedan known for its safety, comfort, and understated Scandinavian design. The base engine is a 2.4L inline-5 producing 168 horsepower and 170 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options include a 2.5L turbocharged inline-5 (S60 2.5T) and a high-performance 2.5L turbocharged inline-5 (S60 T5) which made 208 hp and 258 hp respectively. A 6-speed manual or 5-speed automatic transmission are available, depending on the trim. Drivers often appreciate the S60's comfortable ride, supportive seats, and safety features. Common complaints revolve around repair costs and some reliability issues, particularly as the cars age.

**Engine**

**2.4L Inline-5**

*   **PCV System Clogging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive crankcase pressure leads to oil leaks from various engine seals (rear main seal, camshaft seals), rough running, and potential damage to the engine.
    *   Solution: Replace the PCV system components including the oil trap/separator box and associated hoses. Regular oil changes can help prevent clogging.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Turbo Inline-5 (2.5T and T5)**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whistling or grinding noises from the turbocharger, oil leaks, and potentially a check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace the turbocharger. It is also recommended to inspect and replace oil lines and filters to the turbocharger to prevent future issues.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Vacuum Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Poor performance, rough idle, boost issues, and a check engine light. Often related to dry-rotted or cracked vacuum lines.
    *   Solution: Replace all damaged vacuum lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, slipping between gears, or complete failure to shift.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the valve body, or replace the entire transmission if the damage is extensive. Performing a transmission flush using the correct fluid is also recommended.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Control Arm Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the front suspension, especially when going over bumps. Poor handling and wandering steering.
    *   Solution: Replace the front control arm bushings, or the entire control arm assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Strut Mount Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noises when turning the steering wheel, especially at low speeds. Also, vibrations and poor handling.
    *   Solution: Replace the strut mounts. Often recommended to replace the struts/shocks at the same time.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Electronic Throttle Module (ETM) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, limp mode, and a check engine light. Can also cause erratic idle.
    *   Solution: Replace the ETM. Ensure the replacement is properly calibrated.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Driver Information Module (DIM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Display malfunctions, missing segments, flickering, or complete failure.
    *   Solution: Replace or repair the DIM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Drain Clogs**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin, particularly in the footwells.
    *   Solution: Clear the sunroof drains of debris.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (DIY) - $150 (Shop Labor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Climate Control Module (CCM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning climate control, including erratic temperature control, failure of the display, or blower motor issues.
    *   Solution: Replace or repair the CCM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2006 Volvo S60 is generally a reliable car, but like many vehicles of its age, it is prone to certain issues. The most common problems involve the engine, transmission, and electrical systems. Repairs can range from relatively inexpensive fixes like vacuum leaks and sunroof drain cleaning to more costly repairs like turbocharger replacement and transmission overhauls. Addressing these issues promptly can help maintain the vehicle's performance and longevity.
