# 2019 Volvo S60 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Volvo S60, prospective buyers will find a stylish and engaging luxury sport sedan that balances Scandinavian design with strong performance. However, owners have reported certain recurring mechanical and electrical concerns that warrant a careful pre-purchase inspection. Knowing what to look out for—ranging from minor electrical glitches to tire and headlight issues—helps ensure a smoother ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

The base 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine (often badged as T5) generates around 250 horsepower, offering a responsive and efficient daily driving experience. Regular maintenance is key to long-term reliability for this direct-injection power plant.

### 2.0L Supercharged and Turbocharged I4

The higher-output 2.0-liter engine (badged as T6) utilizes both a turbocharger and a supercharger to produce roughly 316 horsepower, delivering brisk acceleration. Buyers should ensure proper service records have been maintained due to the added mechanical complexity.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic

Paired across the lineup, the 8-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth and prompt gear changes suited to both relaxed cruising and spirited driving. Routine fluid checks support long-term durability.

## Suspension

### Tires and Wheels

Suspension and ride quality are generally comfortable, but original equipment tire foam separation has been a known source of sudden highway vibration.

**Foam Inlay Separation and Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Extreme highway vibrations and shaking can occur due to the internal sound-absorbing foam lining detaching and coming unglued inside original equipment tires.
- **Solution:** Inspect tires for internal separation and replace damaged tires, potentially switching to standard non-foam tires if recurring issues persist.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides strong stopping power, though a subset of owners have experienced premature wear on brake components.

**Premature Rotor Warping**
- **Symptoms:** Brake rotors may warp prematurely, leading to steering wheel vibrations or pulsations when applying the brakes within low mileage intervals.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake rotors and pads for excessive wear or warping, and replace or resurface them as necessary.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Core electrical components generally perform well, though occasional unexpected system shutdowns have been reported by owners.

**Unexpected Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly shut off during a drive, causing the dashboard to go blank and leaving only emergency blinkers operational.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose the main electrical harness, battery connections, and charging system components.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical complaints center primarily around exterior lighting components and intermittent module behavior.

**Headlight Failure and Flickering**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights or headlamps may flicker intermittently, fail to operate correctly, or trigger low beam malfunction alerts on the dashboard when turning at night.
- **Solution:** Inspect the headlight assemblies and wiring harnesses; faulty units may require complete headlamp assembly replacement.
- **Cost:** $3,400

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety driver-assistance features provide helpful protection, though sensor calibration is critical to avoiding phantom braking events.

**Inadvertent Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The automatic emergency braking system may occasionally engage unexpectedly at highway speeds without any visible obstacles or hazards present.
- **Solution:** Check the forward-facing camera and radar sensors for proper calibration, alignment, and cleanliness.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V144000: Forward Collision Avoidance: Automatic Emergency Braking](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=S60&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Due to a software/hardware incompatibility, the Automatic Emergency Brake (AEB) system may not detect obstacles and engage as intended. If the automatic emergency braking system does not engage as intended, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will update the Active Safety Domain Master (ASDM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V414000: Electrical System:Wiring:Fuses And Circuit Breakers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=S60&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Failure of the low-pressure fuel pump can cause a stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuse, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V327000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=S60&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The rear suspension toe link flange lock nuts may not have been properly tightened, possibly resulting in a sudden change in vehicle handling. A sudden change in vehicle handling can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rear toe link flange bolts and lock nuts, replacing them if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V780000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=S60&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The passenger air bag may not be secured properly, which can cause the air bag to deploy improperly. An air bag that does not deploy properly in a crash increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten the bolts securing the passenger air bag, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Volvo S60 offers an appealing blend of luxury, safety technology, and composed handling for used car buyers. While most mechanical systems prove dependable, shoppers should carefully inspect the vehicle for tire-related highway vibrations, lighting performance, and brake wear during a pre-purchase test drive. Ensuring past maintenance has been thoroughly completed will help secure a reliable and enjoyable driving experience.
