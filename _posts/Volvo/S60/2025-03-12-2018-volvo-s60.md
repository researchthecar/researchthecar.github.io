The 2018 Volvo S60 is a compact luxury sedan known for its safety, comfort, and distinctive Scandinavian design. The 2018 model year offered several engine options including the T5, T6, and Polestar variants. The T5 engine is a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing around 240 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. The T6 engine is a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine, delivering approximately 302 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. The Polestar variant, a performance-oriented version, also uses a 2.0-liter engine, but with higher output figures. All engine options typically came paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the S60's comfortable ride, stylish interior, and advanced safety features. Some common complaints include the infotainment system's complexity and the limited rear seat space compared to some competitors.

**Engine**

**T5 2.0L Turbocharged I4**

* **Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners report excessive oil consumption, requiring more frequent oil changes or topping off. This may manifest as low oil level warnings or visible blue smoke from the exhaust.
    * Solution: Monitoring oil levels regularly and addressing any leaks. In severe cases, piston ring replacement might be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**T6 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged I4**

* **Coolant Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, often originating from the water pump or hoses. Symptoms can include low coolant levels, overheating, and visible coolant puddles.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty water pump, hoses, or thermostat housing.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Polestar 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged I4**

*No Issues Found*

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (All Engines)**

* **Rough Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported rough or delayed shifting between gears, particularly at lower speeds. This can manifest as jerky movements or a noticeable "clunk" when shifting.
    * Solution: Performing a transmission fluid flush and potentially reprogramming the transmission control module (TCM). In some cases, valve body replacement may be required.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Infotainment System Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The Sensus Connect infotainment system can experience glitches, freezing, slow response times, and connectivity problems. This can affect navigation, audio, and climate controls.
    * Solution: Performing software updates, resetting the system, or, in some cases, replacing the infotainment unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain, especially in vehicles with a lot of electronic features. This can lead to difficulty starting the car or a completely dead battery.
    * Solution: Diagnosing the cause of the drain, which may involve a faulty module or accessory. Replacing the battery with an AGM battery is recommended.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The sunroof can become stuck or fail to open or close properly. This may be due to a faulty motor or issues with the sunroof track.
    * Solution: Replacing the sunroof motor or repairing the track mechanism.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2018 Volvo S60 has some reported issues with its engine (oil consumption and coolant leaks depending on engine variant), transmission (rough shifting), electrical system (infotainment glitches and battery drain), and interior (sunroof malfunctions). The severity and cost of repairs can vary widely, with engine and transmission issues potentially being the most expensive to address. Electrical and interior issues are usually less costly but can be frustrating. Overall, these issues should be considered when evaluating a used 2018 Volvo S60.
