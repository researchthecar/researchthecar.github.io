The 2015 Volvo S60 is a compact executive sedan that offers a blend of Scandinavian design, comfort, and safety. This model year came with a variety of engine options, including the T5 Drive-E 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 producing 240 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, the T5 2.5L turbocharged inline-5, the T6 Drive-E 2.0L turbocharged AND supercharged inline-4 producing 302 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, and the T6 3.0L turbocharged inline-6. Transmission options include an 8-speed automatic (Drive-E engines) and a 6-speed automatic (inline-5 and inline-6 engines). Owners often praise the S60 for its comfortable seats, refined ride, and distinctive styling. Common complaints include some infotainment system quirks and potentially higher repair costs compared to some competitors.

**Engine**

**T5 Drive-E 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. May be accompanied by blue smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Replacement of piston rings and/or pistons.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **PCV System Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whistling noise from the engine, rough idle, oil leaks.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the PCV valve/oil trap assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**T6 Drive-E 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. May be accompanied by blue smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Replacement of piston rings and/or pistons.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **PCV System Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whistling noise from the engine, rough idle, oil leaks.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the PCV valve/oil trap assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**T5 2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5**

* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from various locations, including the valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket, and turbocharger oil lines.
    * Solution: Replacing the leaking gaskets or lines.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+ depending on location
    * Recalls: None Found

**T6 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6**

* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from various locations, including the valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket, and turbocharger oil lines.
    * Solution: Replacing the leaking gaskets or lines.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+ depending on location
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (Aisin Warner TG-81SC) - Drive-E Engines**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, especially between certain gears. Hesitation when accelerating.
    *   Solution: Software update, transmission fluid flush, or valve body replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (Software Update) - $2,000+ (Valve Body)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Strut Mount Noise**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Creaking or popping noise from the front suspension, especially when turning the steering wheel at low speeds.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the front strut mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues (Sensus)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, slow response, glitches, or failure of the infotainment system screen.
    *   Solution: Software update, replacement of the infotainment module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (Software Update) - $1,000+ (Module Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain, requiring frequent jump starts.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair of parasitic drain, battery replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ (Diagnosis) - $200+ (Battery)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof malfunction, including failure to open or close, or excessive noise during operation.
    *   Solution: Sunroof track lubrication, motor replacement, or complete sunroof assembly replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Air Conditioning Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning air conditioning, including low airflow, failure to cool, or unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Recharge the AC system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Volvo S60, while a comfortable and stylish sedan, can experience a range of issues. Engine problems like oil consumption, especially in the Drive-E engines, and oil leaks in the inline-5 and inline-6 engines, can be costly to address. Transmission issues, particularly rough shifting in the 8-speed automatic, are another area of concern. Electrical issues related to the infotainment system and battery drain are also common. While some fixes are relatively inexpensive, others can run into the thousands of dollars. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems and have the vehicle thoroughly inspected before purchase.
