# 2021 Volvo S60

### Overview
The 2021 Volvo S60 is a compact luxury sedan known for its sophisticated design, advanced safety features, and comfortable interior. It offers a range of powertrain options, including turbocharged and mild-hybrid configurations, designed to balance performance with fuel efficiency. Reliability for the 2021 model year is generally strong, reflecting Volvo's commitment to quality and durability. Owners can expect a refined driving experience with a focus on passenger comfort and cutting-edge technology. While most systems perform admirably, like any vehicle, certain components may experience issues over time, though widespread problems are not a common theme for this model year.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Occasional screen freezing/unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, system reboots unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Software update or system reset. In rare cases, module replacement.
- **Cost:** $100 - $800


### 12V Battery
**Premature battery drain**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, electrical systems malfunctioning, battery warning lights.
- **Solution:** Check for parasitic draws, replace battery if faulty.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Volvo S60 demonstrates a commendable level of reliability, with most major systems performing as expected. The powertrain options, particularly the turbocharged and mild-hybrid variants, are generally robust. The primary areas where minor issues have been noted are within the electrical system, specifically occasional infotainment glitches and potential 12V battery drain, and in the plug-in hybrid variants concerning the battery cooling system. These issues are not pervasive across the model but are worth noting for potential owners. Overall, the S60 offers a dependable luxury sedan experience with a strong emphasis on safety and comfort.
