# 2021 Volvo S60 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Volvo S60 delivers a sophisticated blend of Scandinavian luxury, comfort, and advanced safety features, paired with a range of turbocharged and electrified powertrain options. For used-car buyers, navigating the market requires a balanced perspective: while owner complaints to safety regulators are relatively minimal for this model year, prospective buyers should still inspect complex electrical systems and powertrain operation closely during a pre-purchase test drive.

---

## Engine

### T5 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces around 250 horsepower and offers smooth, daily-driver power. It is generally reliable, though routine maintenance of its forced-induction and direct-injection systems is vital.

### T6 2.0L Turbocharged & Supercharged 4-Cylinder

Combining a turbocharger and a supercharger, the T6 outputs approximately 316 horsepower for brisk acceleration. Buyers should ensure proper service history to maintain the complex dual-forced induction system.

### T8 Recharge 2.0L Turbocharged/Supercharged Plug-in Hybrid

The T8 plug-in hybrid pairs forced induction with an electric motor to generate around 400 total system horsepower while offering electric-only driving range. Buyers looking at this option should thoroughly check both the traditional mechanical components and the hybrid battery health.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Geartronic Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission comes paired across the S60 lineup, delivering smooth and efficient gear shifts. Regular fluid changes will help ensure long-term durability across front-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V682000: Seat Belts:Front:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=S60&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the seat belt assemblies, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V780000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=S60&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The passenger air bag may not be secured properly, which can cause the air bag to deploy improperly. An air bag that does not deploy properly in a crash increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten the bolts securing the passenger air bag, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V179000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=S60&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The high-voltage battery may experience a short circuit within the battery module when the battery is fully charged and the vehicle is parked. A short circuit in a high-voltage battery increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the high-voltage battery module as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Volvo S60 is an appealing choice in the compact luxury sedan segment, offering stylish design, strong performance options, and robust safety credentials. Because widespread recurring mechanical defects are not heavily highlighted by owner complaints for this model year, a thorough inspection focused on electronics, powertrain refinement, and overall vehicle condition remains the best strategy for a confident purchase.
