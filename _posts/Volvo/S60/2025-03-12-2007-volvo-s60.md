The 2007 Volvo S60 is a compact executive sedan known for its safety, comfort, and understated style. Engine options included a 2.5L turbocharged inline-5 producing 208 hp and 236 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.5L high-output turbocharged inline-5 producing 257 hp and 258 lb-ft of torque. All engine options were paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praised the S60's comfortable seats, solid build quality, and distinctive Scandinavian design. Common complaints included electrical gremlins, some suspension issues, and higher repair costs compared to domestic vehicles.

**Engine**

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5 (208 hp)**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks, often from the turbocharger oil lines or the rear main seal. Can lead to low oil levels and potential engine damage if left unattended.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking oil lines, gaskets, or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the source of the leak and labor involved.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **PCV System Clogging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clogged PCV system leading to increased crankcase pressure, oil leaks, rough idle, and potential damage to seals and hoses.
    *   Solution: Replace the PCV valve, breather box, and associated hoses.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5 (257 hp)**

*   **Oil Leaks**

    *   Solution: Replace the leaking oil lines, gaskets, or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the source of the leak and labor involved.

*   **PCV System Clogging**

    *   Solution: Replace the PCV valve, breather box, and associated hoses.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, slipping between gears, or failure to shift altogether. Often caused by worn valves or solenoids within the valve body.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the valve body. In some cases, a full transmission rebuild or replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3000+, depending on the extent of the damage and the chosen repair method.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Control Arm Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the front suspension, especially when going over bumps. Poor handling and alignment issues.
    *   Solution: Replace the worn control arm bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Strut Mounts**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or creaking noises from the suspension, especially when turning the steering wheel. Can also cause rough ride and poor handling.
    *   Solution: Replace the worn strut mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A wide range of electrical problems, including intermittent failure of lights, wipers, power windows, and other accessories. Can also cause starting problems or prevent the car from starting altogether.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the specific problem with the CEM and repair or replace the module. This often requires specialized equipment and expertise.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Ignition Switch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the car, or the car stalling unexpectedly.
    *   Solution: Replace the ignition switch.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Lens Clouding/Yellowing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Headlight lenses become cloudy or yellowed, reducing visibility at night.
    *   Solution: Polish or replace the headlight lenses.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200 (polishing) or $200 - $500+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof failing to open or close properly, getting stuck, or leaking.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair the sunroof mechanism. This may involve replacing the motor, tracks, or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2007 Volvo S60, while generally a reliable vehicle, can suffer from several common issues. Engine oil leaks and PCV system problems are notable concerns. Transmission issues, particularly valve body problems, can be costly to address. Electrical problems with the CEM can be frustrating and difficult to diagnose. Suspension and interior issues also contribute to the overall ownership experience. While some issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be quite costly, making it important to have a pre-purchase inspection performed by a qualified mechanic.
