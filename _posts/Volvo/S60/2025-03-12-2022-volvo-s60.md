The 2022 Volvo S60 is a stylish and comfortable compact luxury sedan known for its safety features and Scandinavian design. Engine options for the 2022 model year include the B5 mild-hybrid 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine producing 247 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, and the T8 Recharge plug-in hybrid which combines a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine with an electric motor to produce 400 horsepower and 472 lb-ft of torque. All engines are paired with an Aisin AW TG-81 eight-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the S60 for its comfortable ride, elegant interior, and advanced safety technology. Common complaints include the infotainment system complexity and some concerns about long-term reliability.

**Engine**

**B5 Mild-Hybrid 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Four-Cylinder**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report higher than normal oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between scheduled oil changes. Symptoms can include the low oil level warning light illuminating, or noticing a burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Monitor oil levels regularly and consult with a Volvo dealer for diagnosis. This may involve checking for leaks, performing an oil consumption test, or potentially replacing piston rings or other engine components if the consumption is excessive.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+ depending on the cause and repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**T8 Recharge Plug-In Hybrid**

*   **Battery Management System (BMS) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the Battery Management System, leading to inaccurate state of charge readings, reduced electric driving range, or complete failure to charge.
    *   Solution: Software updates can sometimes resolve BMS issues. In more severe cases, battery module replacements or BMS unit replacement might be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (software update) - $5,000+ (battery module replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin AW TG-81 Eight-Speed Automatic (All Engines)**

*   **Harsh or Delayed Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing harsh or delayed shifting, particularly between lower gears. This can manifest as a noticeable "jerk" or hesitation during acceleration.
    *   Solution: A software update of the Transmission Control Module (TCM) may resolve the issue. If that does not work, a more in-depth inspection of the transmission could be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (software update) - $500+ (transmission inspection)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Lagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Sensus infotainment system can sometimes freeze, lag, or become unresponsive. This can affect navigation, audio, climate control, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Performing a system reset or software update often resolves these issues. In some cases, the infotainment module may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (system reset) - $100 (software update) - $1,000+ (infotainment module replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reports of parking sensors failing to detect obstacles or the blind spot monitoring system providing false warnings.
    *   Solution: Inspect the sensor and wiring for damage or debris. The sensor may need to be replaced. Recalibration of the system may also be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per sensor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof/Moonroof Issues (Leakage, Failure to Open/Close)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks around the sunroof/moonroof area, or the sunroof failing to open or close properly.
    *   Solution: Inspect and clean the sunroof drain tubes to ensure they are not clogged. If the motor is failing, replacement of the motor or sunroof assembly might be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Buzzing Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report experiencing rattling or buzzing noises emanating from the dashboard, door panels, or rear deck.
    *   Solution: Identify the source of the noise and secure any loose panels or components. Applying sound-deadening material can help reduce noise.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (DIY fix) - $200+ (professional repair)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Volvo S60 presents a mix of potential issues, ranging from minor infotainment glitches and interior rattles to more significant concerns such as excessive oil consumption, battery management problems, and transmission issues. The severity of these issues varies, and repair costs can range from a few dollars for a DIY fix to several thousand dollars for major component replacements. Addressing issues promptly and performing regular maintenance is essential to ensure the long-term reliability of the S60.
