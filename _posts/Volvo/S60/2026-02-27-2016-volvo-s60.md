# 2016 Volvo S60 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Volvo S60 combines Scandinavian luxury, distinctive styling, and strong safety credentials, but prospective buyers should research its mechanical history carefully. While it offers a comfortable ride and refined powertrain choices, owner reports highlight potential concerns regarding excessive oil consumption, accessory drive belt durability, and occasional electrical glitches. Thoroughly inspecting service records and verifying past maintenance can help mitigate these risks when shopping for a used model.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder (Drive-E)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder Drive-E engine produces around 240 horsepower, balancing efficiency with responsive power. Buyers should monitor oil consumption closely as these units age.

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers notice a burning oil smell and low oil level warnings requiring frequent quart additions.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels regularly and consult a technician for potential piston ring or internal engine inspections.

### 2.0L Supercharged and Turbocharged 4-Cylinder (Drive-E)

This dual-charged 2.0-liter engine pushes approximately 302 horsepower for robust acceleration. Regular inspection of the accessory belt drive system is vital for long-term reliability.

**Alternator and Serpentine Belt Failure**
- **Symptoms:** An alternator seizure can snap the serpentine belt, which may get drawn into the timing system and cause severe engine damage.
- **Solution:** Inspect the accessory belt and alternator condition periodically, and ensure timing guards are secure.

### 2.5L Turbocharged 5-Cylinder

The traditional 2.5-liter turbocharged five-cylinder engine delivers roughly 250 horsepower with a distinct exhaust note. It is generally regarded as a durable option within the lineup.

### 3.0L Turbocharged 6-Cylinder

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine generates up to 325 horsepower in higher-performance trims. It offers brisk performance but demands attentive maintenance.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with Drive-E engines to provide smooth, efficient gear changes. It generally operates reliably with routine fluid checks.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission is coupled with select inline-5 and inline-6 powertrains, offering proven mechanical operation. Routine servicing helps maintain crisp shift quality.

## Electrical

### Audio and Infotainment Module

electrical issues involving the infotainment and audio systems can occasionally frustrate owners.

**Audio Module Failure and Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment screen and audio system completely stop working while driving, occasionally causing parasitic battery drain.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose the audio module and update or replace faulty control units.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Sunroof

Exterior glass components, including windshields and sunroofs, have reported instances of spontaneous cracking.

**Spontaneous Glass Fracture**
- **Symptoms:** Sunroofs or windshields may develop stress fractures or shatter suddenly without direct impact from debris.
- **Solution:** Inspect glass for pre-existing stress cracks and replace damaged panels promptly through insurance or a professional glass shop.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V798000: Seat Belts:Front:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=S60&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 210, "Seat Belt Assembly Anchorages" and number 209, "Seat Belt Assemblies." If the seat belt buckle separates from the bracket, the front seat passenger may not be adequately restrained in the event of a crash, increasing their risk of.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the front passenger seat belt buckle stud, replacing the seat belt buckle as needed, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V555000: Electrical System:Wiring:Fuses And Circuit Breakers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=S60&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the vehicle is unexpectedly unable to be started in the middle of traffic, such as when stopped at an intersection, there is an increased risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will replace the existing starter motor fuse with a higher amperage fuse, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V378000: Seat Belts:Front](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=S60&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The flexible steel cable that connects the seat belt to the front outboard seating positions may fatigue over time, causing the seat belt to not secure the occupant in the event of a crash. An unsecured occupant has an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will replace the seat belt anchor cable for both front seats. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V849000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=S60&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A door that is not securely latched could open while the vehicle is in motion, increasing the risk of injury to a vehicle occupant.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will replace all four door latches, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Volvo S60 remains an appealing used luxury sedan option thanks to its safety pedigree and engaging performance. Prospective buyers should prioritize models with comprehensive service records, specifically checking for signs of oil consumption and verifying the condition of accessory drive belts. A pre-purchase inspection by a knowledgeable specialist is strongly recommended to catch minor electrical or mechanical issues before buying.
