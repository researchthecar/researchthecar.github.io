# 2017 Volvo S60

### Overview
The 2017 Volvo S60, known for its Scandinavian design and safety features, generally offers a solid ownership experience. Reliability data suggests that while most systems perform well, certain powertrain configurations and specific electronic components have seen more attention. Owners can expect a well-built vehicle with a focus on comfort and driving dynamics. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring the longevity of this premium sedan. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for prospective and current owners.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged (T5)
**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, low coolant level warning light, engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking hoses, seals, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger unit and associated plumbing.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500


### 2.0L Supercharged & Turbocharged (T6)
**PCV System Clogs**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, engine oil leaks, potential for increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve and associated hoses.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500

**Timing Chain Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup; check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,000


### 2.0L Turbocharged Hybrid (T8)
**Hybrid Battery Cooling System**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced electric range, battery warning lights, potential overheating.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair/replacement of cooling system components (pump, hoses, radiator).
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500


## Transmission

### 8-Speed Geartronic Automatic
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid from transmission pan or seals, rough shifting, slipping gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seals, gaskets, or transmission pan.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic shifting patterns, delayed engagement, occasional harsh shifts.
- **Solution:** Software update or recalibration of the transmission control module.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


## Suspension

### Standard & Four-C Chassis
**Worn Control Arm Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering feel, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of front or rear control arm bushings.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Strut Mount Bearing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Popping or creaking noise when turning the steering wheel, especially at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of front strut mount bearings.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment screen becomes unresponsive, freezes, or restarts randomly.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the infotainment control unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dies unexpectedly, car struggles to start, electrical components malfunction.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the source of the drain (e.g., faulty module, short circuit) and repair.
- **Cost:** $200 - $700


### Keyless Entry/Start System
**Key Fob Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Car fails to recognize key fob, difficulty starting the engine, intermittent door lock/unlock issues.
- **Solution:** Reprogramming or replacement of the key fob or related receiver module.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Volvo S60 is a generally reliable vehicle, particularly in its core mechanical components. The most frequently reported issues tend to be related to the more complex powertrain options, specifically the turbocharged and supercharged engines, where coolant leaks and turbocharger problems can arise. The automatic transmission is robust but can experience software glitches or fluid leaks. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment system unresponsiveness and battery drain, are also noted. Suspension components like control arm bushings and strut mounts may require attention over time. Overall, with diligent maintenance and prompt attention to any emerging issues, the S60 should provide a dependable luxury driving experience.
