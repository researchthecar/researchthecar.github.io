The 2009 Volvo S60 is a compact executive sedan known for its safety, comfort, and understated styling. Engine options for this year include a 2.5L turbocharged inline-5 producing 208 hp and 236 lb-ft of torque, a high-pressure turbocharged 2.5L inline-5 producing 257 hp and 258 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.0L inline-6 producing 281 hp and 295 lb-ft of torque. All engines are paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the S60's comfortable ride, supportive seats, and safety features. Common complaints often revolve around electrical issues, the cost of parts, and the age of the vehicle, which leads to increased maintenance.

**Engine**

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5 (B5254T2)**

*   **Failed Electronic Throttle Module (ETM)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine stalls, rough idle, reduced power, check engine light illuminated.
    *   Solution: Replace the ETM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Vacuum Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Poor fuel economy, rough idle, engine hesitation, check engine light. Common locations include vacuum hoses and the PCV system.
    *   Solution: Replace damaged hoses and PCV components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.5L High-Pressure Turbocharged Inline-5 (B5254T5)**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whistling noise from the engine bay, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Vacuum Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Poor fuel economy, rough idle, engine hesitation, check engine light. Common locations include vacuum hoses and the PCV system.
    *   Solution: Replace damaged hoses and PCV components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Inline-6 (B6304T2)**

*   **Coil Pack Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough running engine, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty coil pack(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**AW55-50/51SN 5-Speed Automatic**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, slipping between gears, delayed engagement, transmission warning light.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering at low speeds, transmission slipping, abnormal noises.
    *   Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Control Arm Bushing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise from the front suspension, poor handling, uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replace the front control arms or just the bushings (depending on condition).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per side.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Strut Mount Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or popping noises from the suspension, especially when turning, and poor handling.
    *   Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per pair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, including issues with lights, wipers, power windows, and door locks.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the CEM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Dimming Instrument Cluster**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The instrument cluster lights dim or flicker, making it difficult to read the gauges.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the instrument cluster.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Side Mirror Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The power side mirrors stop adjusting.
    *   Solution: Replace the side mirror assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof getting stuck, motor failing, or leaking.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the sunroof motor or assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2009 Volvo S60 is a generally reliable car, but some common issues exist, especially given its age. Engine issues like vacuum leaks and ETM failures can be costly to repair. Transmission problems related to the valve body are also potential concerns. Electrical issues, while intermittent, can also be difficult to diagnose and fix. Overall, prospective buyers should be aware of these potential problems and factor in repair costs when considering a purchase.
