**Introduction:**

The 2015 Volvo S60 is a compact luxury sedan that offers a balance of performance, comfort, and safety. It is available with three engine options: a 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 producing 240 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, a 2.5L turbocharged inline-5 producing 302 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.0L turbocharged inline-6 producing 325 horsepower and 354 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 6-speed manual, an 8-speed automatic, and a 6-speed dual-clutch automatic.

Drivers appreciate the S60's sporty handling, comfortable interior, and advanced safety features. However, some issues have been reported with various components of the vehicle.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**

* **Turbocharger Failure:** Symptoms include loss of power, increased oil consumption, and excessive exhaust smoke. Solution: Replace the turbocharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Intake Valve Deposits:** Carbon buildup on the intake valves can cause reduced engine performance and fuel economy. Solution: Clean the intake valves. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5**

* **Timing Belt Tensioner Failure:** A faulty timing belt tensioner can lead to timing belt failure, causing severe engine damage. Solution: Replace the timing belt tensioner. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
* **High-Pressure Fuel Pump Failure:** Symptoms include difficulty starting the engine, rough idling, and reduced power. Solution: Replace the high-pressure fuel pump. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800

**3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Master Cylinder Failure:** Symptoms include difficulty engaging gears and a spongy clutch pedal. Solution: Replace the clutch master cylinder. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500

**8-Speed Automatic**

* **Shift Solenoid Failure:** Malfunctioning shift solenoids can cause harsh shifting, slipping gears, and transmission overheating. Solution: Replace the shift solenoids. Estimated Cost to Fix: $700 - $1,200
* **Torque Converter Shudder:** Symptoms include vibration when accelerating from low speeds. Solution: Replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

**6-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic**

* **Clutch Pack Failure:** Worn or damaged clutch packs can cause slipping gears, shuddering, and loss of power. Solution: Replace the clutch pack. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,800 - $2,800+
* **Mechatronic Unit Failure:** The mechatronic unit controls the transmission's hydraulics and electronics. A faulty mechatronic unit can lead to various transmission problems. Solution: Replace the mechatronic unit. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $3,500+

**Suspension**

* **Front Control Arm Bushings Prematurely Wearing Out:** Symptoms include clunking noises, vibrations, and uneven tire wear. Solution: Replace the front control arm bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Parasitic battery drain can cause the battery to discharge over time. Solution: Diagnose and repair the cause of the drain. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
* **Infotainment System Glitches:** Symptoms include system freezes, unresponsive screens, and audio issues. Solution: Update the system software or replace the infotainment module. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:** Moisture can accumulate inside the headlights, causing fogging or reduced visibility. Solution: Replace the affected headlights. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunctions:** Symptoms include temperature fluctuations, blower motor noise, or air conditioning failure. Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty component. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Center Console Creaking:** Rattling or creaking noises can be caused by loose trim pieces or components in the center console. Solution: Tighten loose parts or replace damaged components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Summary**

While the 2015 Volvo S60 is a generally reliable vehicle, it has been known to experience various issues with its engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, and interior components. The severity and cost of these issues can range from minor inconveniences to major repairs. It is important to research specific issues and consider the potential costs before purchasing or owning a vehicle.
