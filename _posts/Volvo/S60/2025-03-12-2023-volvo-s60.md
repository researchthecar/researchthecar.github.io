The 2023 Volvo S60 is a luxury sedan that offers a blend of Scandinavian design, comfort, and technology. Engine options for the 2023 model year include the B5 mild-hybrid powertrain, which uses a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine producing 247 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. All engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. People often praise the S60 for its comfortable ride, stylish interior, and advanced safety features. Some common complaints include the infotainment system's complexity and limited rear passenger space.

**Engine**

**B5 (2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4 Mild Hybrid)**

*   **Engine Hesitation/Rough Idle**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing engine hesitation or a rough idle, especially when the engine is cold. This can manifest as a slight stumble or vibration felt through the car.
    *   Solution: Software update or replacement of faulty sensors (e.g., mass airflow sensor or oxygen sensor).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks originating from the valve cover gasket. This may manifest as oil spots on the ground or a burning smell.
    *   Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Abrupt or jerky gear changes, especially between lower gears.
    *   Solution: Transmission control module (TCM) software update or valve body replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Delayed Engagement**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Delay in gear engagement when shifting from Park or Neutral into Drive or Reverse.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush and filter change or valve body repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Bushing Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature wear of the control arm bushings. This can cause clunking noises and degraded handling.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the control arm bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Sensus infotainment system can freeze, crash, or become unresponsive, requiring a restart.
    *   Solution: Software update for the infotainment system. In some cases, the central control unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1200+ (control unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning parking sensors or blind spot monitoring system, giving false alerts or failing to detect obstacles.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty sensor(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling or squeaking noises emanating from the dashboard or door panels, especially on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Tightening of loose panels or application of felt tape to contact points to dampen vibrations.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof malfunctions such as getting stuck open or closed.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of sunroof motor or track assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2023 Volvo S60 experiences a range of issues, with electrical and transmission problems being among the most notable. While some issues, like rough shifting, can be addressed with software updates, others, such as infotainment malfunctions or sunroof issues, may require more costly repairs. Overall, owners should be aware of these potential problems and factor them into their ownership experience. The severity and cost of these issues vary, with electrical and transmission repairs potentially reaching upwards of $1000.
