The 2013 Volvo S60 is a compact executive sedan known for its safety features, comfortable ride, and Scandinavian design. Available engine options include a 2.5L turbocharged inline-5 producing 250 hp and 266 lb-ft of torque and a 3.0L turbocharged inline-6 producing 300 hp and 325 lb-ft of torque, as well as the 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 producing 240 hp and 258 lb-ft of torque. All engines were paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the S60 for its comfortable seats, responsive handling, and distinctive styling. However, some common complaints include the infotainment system complexity, limited rear seat space, and potential for costly repairs.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (T5)**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-ups between oil changes. May be indicated by the low oil pressure light illuminating.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace piston rings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5 (T5)**

*   **PCV System Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whistling noise from the engine, rough idle, check engine light, and potential oil leaks. Can lead to pressure buildup in the crankcase.
    *   Solution: Replace the PCV valve and potentially other components of the PCV system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Ignition Coil Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough running engine, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty ignition coil(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ per coil
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (T6)**

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks from various locations, including the water pump, thermostat housing, or radiator hoses. Can lead to overheating.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking component, such as the water pump, thermostat housing, or radiator hose.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ depending on the location and component.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining or whistling noises from the turbocharger, loss of power, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Inspect the turbocharger for damage or wear. Replace if necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner AW55-50/51SN 6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, slipping between gears, erratic shifting patterns, and the transmission entering limp mode.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Fluid Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Leaks around the transmission pan, seals, or cooler lines.
    *   Solution: Replace leaking seals or lines. Ensure the transmission pan is properly tightened.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the location of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Strut Mount Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the front suspension, especially when going over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replace the front strut mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rear Suspension Bushing Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vague handling, rear end wander, clunking or creaking noises from the rear suspension.
    *   Solution: Replace worn rear suspension bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Software Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic behavior of the infotainment system, warning lights illuminating for no apparent reason, issues with electronic features.
    *   Solution: Software update performed by a Volvo dealer or qualified mechanic.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The battery draining quickly, requiring frequent jump starts. Can be caused by parasitic drain from a faulty module or accessory.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair the source of the parasitic drain. Replace the battery if necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof not opening or closing properly, leaking water, making excessive noise.
    *   Solution: Clean and lubricate the sunroof tracks and seals. Repair or replace damaged components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Center Console Screen Delamination**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The LCD screen in the center console delaminates, causing distortion or discoloration.
    *   Solution: Replace the center console screen.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Door locks not functioning properly, making it difficult to lock or unlock the doors.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per door.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2013 Volvo S60, while generally reliable, does have some common issues. Engine issues can range from manageable oil consumption and PCV system problems to more costly repairs such as turbocharger or coolant leaks. Transmission issues, particularly with the valve body, can also be expensive. Suspension and electrical problems are also prevalent but are less common. Interior and exterior issues are less severe, but the center console screen delamination can be a significant expense. The severity and cost of these issues vary widely, but potential buyers should be aware of these common problems when considering a used 2013 Volvo S60.
