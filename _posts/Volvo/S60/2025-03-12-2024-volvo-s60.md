The 2024 Volvo S60 is a compact luxury sedan known for its stylish design, comfortable interior, and focus on safety. Engine options include the B5 mild-hybrid powertrain, which utilizes a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine paired with a 48-volt hybrid system. The B5 produces 247 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. All engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. The S60 is generally praised for its refined driving experience, upscale cabin, and advanced safety features. Some common dislikes include a somewhat less engaging driving experience compared to some rivals, and the infotainment system can be complex for some users.

**Electric Motor**

No Issues Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (All Engines)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rough or jerky shifts, particularly when the transmission is cold or during low-speed maneuvers. Hesitation when accelerating from a stop has also been noted.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a software update to the transmission control module (TCM) may resolve the issue. In more severe cases, replacement of valve body components within the transmission may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+ depending on the complexity
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Wear of Suspension Components**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported premature wear of suspension components, such as the struts/shocks and bushings. Symptoms include clunking noises over bumps, reduced handling precision, and uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the worn suspension components is the typical solution.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+ depending on the components replaced and labor costs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Lag**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The S60's infotainment system, powered by Google, has been reported by some owners to experience occasional freezing, lagging, or unresponsiveness.
    *   Solution: Restarting the system can sometimes temporarily resolve the issue. Software updates are often released to address these problems. In some rare cases, replacement of the infotainment system may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software updates are typically free. Replacement can cost $500 - $2000+.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported malfunctions with the parking sensors or blind spot monitoring system, resulting in false alarms or system inoperability.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the specific sensor causing the problem is required, followed by replacement of the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ depending on the sensor and labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Water Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reports exist of water leaks into the cabin, typically during heavy rain or after a car wash. The sunroof or door seals are often cited as the source.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the leak source. This might involve resealing the sunroof, replacing door seals, or addressing drainage issues.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the severity and source of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rattling or squeaking noises emanating from various areas of the cabin, such as the dashboard, door panels, or rear deck.
    *   Solution: Locating and isolating the source of the noise, then tightening or padding the affected area. This can sometimes be a time-consuming process.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+ depending on the source and complexity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2024 Volvo S60, while generally reliable, has some reported issues. The most concerning issues appear to be related to the infotainment system and potential transmission issues. The potential for water leaks is also something to consider. While the problems aren't universal, they should be considered when purchasing a used model. Costs to repair range widely, from relatively minor fixes to potentially more expensive transmission or electrical system repairs.
