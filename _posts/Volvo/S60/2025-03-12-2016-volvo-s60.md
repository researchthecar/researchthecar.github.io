The 2016 Volvo S60 is a compact executive sedan offering a blend of Scandinavian design, safety, and performance. Available engine options include the T5 Drive-E 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (240 hp and 258 lb-ft of torque), the T6 Drive-E 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4 (302 hp and 295 lb-ft of torque), and the T5 AWD 2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5 (250 hp and 266 lb-ft of torque). Transmission options include an 8-speed automatic (for the Drive-E engines) and a 6-speed automatic (for the 2.5L engine). Owners generally appreciate the car's comfortable ride, stylish interior, and safety features. Common dislikes include the somewhat complicated infotainment system and higher maintenance costs compared to some competitors.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (T5 Drive-E)**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. Can lead to engine damage if not monitored.
    *   Solution: Replacement of piston rings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **PCV Valve/Oil Separator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whistling noise from the engine, rough idle, oil leaks. Can lead to vacuum leaks and poor engine performance.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the PCV valve/oil separator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4 (T6 Drive-E)**

*   **Supercharger Clutch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of supercharger boost, reduced acceleration, potential error codes.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the supercharger clutch.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5 (T5 AWD)**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil spots under the car, burning oil smell, low oil level. Common leak areas include the valve cover gasket and rear main seal.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking gaskets or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ depending on the location of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (Aisin Warner TG-81SC)**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or jerky shifting, especially between lower gears. May be more noticeable when the transmission is cold.
    *   Solution: Software update or transmission fluid flush. In severe cases, valve body replacement might be needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ for software update or fluid flush; $800 - $2,000+ for valve body replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic (Aisin Warner AWF21)**

*No Issues Found*

**Suspension**

*   **Strut Mount Noise**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Creaking or clunking noise from the front suspension, especially when turning or going over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the strut mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600 per pair
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, slow response, intermittent reboots, Bluetooth connectivity problems.
    *   Solution: Software update, or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 for software updates at the dealership, $500 - $1500+ for unit replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Car battery drains quickly, especially when the car is not driven for a few days.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to identify the parasitic drain, followed by repair or replacement of the affected component (e.g., faulty module, relay, or wiring).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the complexity of the issue.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof sticking, making unusual noises, or failing to open or close properly.
    *   Solution: Lubrication of the tracks, motor replacement, or sunroof assembly repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+ depending on the nature of the issue.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing on the dashboard, especially in warmer climates.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the dashboard.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Volvo S60 presents a mix of potential issues. Engine problems, like oil consumption and PCV valve failure in the T5 Drive-E, and supercharger clutch failure in the T6 Drive-E can be costly. Transmission issues, mainly harsh shifting, also require attention. Electrical glitches with the infotainment system can be annoying. While some issues are minor and inexpensive to resolve, others, like dashboard cracking or engine repairs, can be quite costly. Overall, prospective buyers should be aware of these potential problems and factor them into their purchasing and maintenance plans.
