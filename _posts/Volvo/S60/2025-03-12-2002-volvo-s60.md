The 2002 Volvo S60 is a compact executive sedan known for its safety, comfort, and understated styling. It was available with a range of inline-5 cylinder engines, including a 2.4L naturally aspirated engine producing around 168 horsepower and 170 lb-ft of torque, a 2.4L light pressure turbo producing around 197 hp and 210 lb-ft of torque, and a high pressure 2.3L turbo producing around 247 hp and 243 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual and a 5-speed automatic. Owners generally liked the car's comfortable ride, supportive seats, and safety features. Dislikes often included higher maintenance costs compared to some competitors, and some quirky electrical issues.

**Engine**

**2.4L Naturally Aspirated Inline-5**

*   **Electronic Throttle Module (ETM) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, stalling, erratic idle, check engine light with codes related to the throttle position sensor.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the ETM with an updated version. Some owners opt for aftermarket rebuilds.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.4L Light Pressure Turbo Inline-5**

*   **PCV System Clogging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, pressure buildup in the crankcase, oil leaks from seals, and a whistling noise from the rear of the engine.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the PCV breather box, hoses, and possibly the oil trap.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whining noise from the turbocharger, excessive oil consumption, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.3L High Pressure Turbo Inline-5**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whining noise from the turbocharger, excessive oil consumption, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **PCV System Clogging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, pressure buildup in the crankcase, oil leaks from seals, and a whistling noise from the rear of the engine.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the PCV breather box, hoses, and possibly the oil trap.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic (AW55-50/51SN)**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, slipping between gears, delayed engagement, transmission fault messages.
    *   Solution: Valve body replacement or rebuild. Flushing the transmission fluid regularly can help prevent the issue, but not always.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering at low speeds, especially under light acceleration, poor fuel economy.
    *   Solution: Torque converter replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Lower Control Arm Bushing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise from the front suspension, especially when going over bumps, loose steering.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the lower control arms or just the bushings if possible.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Strut Mount Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or groaning noise from the front suspension, especially when turning.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the strut mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Dim Instrument Cluster Lighting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficult to see the gauges at night.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the instrument cluster or associated lighting components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, including issues with the windows, locks, and lights.
    *   Solution: CEM replacement or repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Drain Clogs**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin, wet carpets.
    *   Solution: Cleaning the sunroof drains.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ (depending on if water damage repair is needed)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Climate Control Blend Door Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inability to control the temperature on one or both sides of the car.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the blend door actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2002 Volvo S60 has some common issues, with the most prevalent ones relating to the engine's PCV system, the transmission's valve body, and electrical components like the ETM and CEM. Repair costs can range from a few hundred dollars for smaller fixes to over a thousand for more complex issues like transmission or turbocharger replacements. Addressing these common problems proactively can help extend the life of the vehicle.
