The 2008 Volvo S60 is a popular choice for those seeking a comfortable and safe sedan. Engine options include the 2.5T inline-5 (208 hp, 236 lb-ft of torque), the T5 inline-5 (257 hp, 266 lb-ft of torque), and the R trim's inline-5 (300 hp, 295 lb-ft of torque). All engines are paired with either a 5-speed automatic or a 6-speed manual (trim dependent). Owners often praise the S60 for its comfortable seats, safety features, and distinctive styling. Common complaints include electrical gremlins, some repair costs, and the turning radius.

**Engine**

**2.5T Inline-5**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks commonly occur from the valve cover gasket, PCV system, or rear main seal. Symptoms include oil spots under the car, burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking gasket or seal. In the case of PCV issues, the entire PCV system may need replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **EGR Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A faulty EGR valve can cause rough idling, poor acceleration, and a check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace the EGR valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

**T5 Inline-5**

*   **Coil Pack Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough running, and a check engine light can indicate a failing coil pack.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty coil pack(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced boost, whistling noises, or a sudden loss of power can point to turbocharger problems.
    *   Solution: Inspect the turbocharger for damage and repair or replace it as needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**S60R Inline-5**

*   **Angle Gear Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The angle gear, which transfers power to the rear wheels in the AWD system, is a common point of failure. Symptoms include clicking noises from the front of the car, loss of AWD, and potential vibrations.
    *   Solution: Replace the angle gear.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced boost, whistling noises, or a sudden loss of power can point to turbocharger problems.
    *   Solution: Inspect the turbocharger for damage and repair or replace it as needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner AW55-50/51SN 5-Speed Automatic**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, slipping between gears, or a complete loss of drive can indicate valve body problems.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Solenoid Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, failure to shift into certain gears, or transmission error messages can be caused by faulty solenoids.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty solenoid(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Strut Mount Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises when going over bumps, especially from the front suspension.
    *   Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$500
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Spring Seat Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms:  A broken or collapsed spring seat can cause noise, uneven ride height, and handling issues.
    *   Solution: Replace the spring seat.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Electronic Throttle Module (ETM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The ETM can develop problems, leading to reduced engine power, stalling, or a check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace or repair the ETM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The CEM controls many of the car's electrical functions. Failure can manifest in various ways, including intermittent electrical issues, non-starting, and malfunctioning lights.
    *   Solution: Replace or repair the CEM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **ABS Module Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: ABS light illumination, braking malfunctions, and speedometer issues can be caused by a faulty ABS module.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the ABS module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlight housings can reduce visibility and damage the bulbs.
    *   Solution: Reseal the headlight housings or replace the headlights.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The door lock actuator failing to automatically lock or unlock doors.
    *   Solution: Replace the door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sunroof motor failing or the sunroof getting stuck.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the sunroof motor and/or tracks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Climate Control Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The blend door failing which results in only hot or only cold air blowing out of the vents.
    *   Solution: Replace the blend door motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2008 Volvo S60, while generally a reliable vehicle, can suffer from several common issues. Engine oil leaks, turbocharger problems (especially in the T5 and R models), and transmission valve body issues are significant concerns that can lead to high repair costs. Electrical gremlins related to the ETM, CEM, and ABS modules can also be problematic and costly to address. While some issues are relatively minor, others require extensive repairs, potentially making ownership expensive. Overall, prospective buyers should be aware of these potential problems and have the car thoroughly inspected before purchase.
