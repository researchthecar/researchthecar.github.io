**Introduction:**

The 1996 Volvo FE is a versatile medium-duty truck available with various engine and transmission options. The D6B250 engine produces 250 horsepower and 710 lb-ft of torque. The FH12 engine offers 340 horsepower and 850 lb-ft of torque. Transmission choices include a 6-speed manual or a 6-speed automatic. The truck's durable construction and impressive payload capacity are well-regarded, but issues with certain components have been reported.

**Engine**

**D6B250 Engine**

* **Turbocharger failure:** Symptoms include excessive smoke, loss of power, and loud whistling sounds. The turbocharger and associated components need to be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
* **EGR valve issues:** The valve can become clogged or faulty, leading to engine performance problems. Cleaning or replacing the valve is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **Oil leaks:** Gaskets or seals may fail, causing oil leaks. Replacing the affected parts is required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**FH12 Engine**

* **Fuel injector failure:** Faulty injectors can cause engine misfires, poor fuel economy, and black smoke. Replacing the injectors is required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ per injector
* **Water pump failure:** Symptoms include overheating and coolant leaks. Replacing the water pump is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
* **Head gasket failure:** A blown head gasket can lead to oil leaks, coolant leaks, and engine overheating. Repair involves replacing the head gasket and associated components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Transmission**

**6-speed Manual Transmission**

* **Gear grinding:** Worn or damaged gears can cause grinding noises and difficulty shifting. Transmission rebuild or replacement may be necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
* **Clutch issues:** The clutch may slip or chatter, indicating worn components. Replacing the clutch assembly is required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**6-speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission fluid leaks:** Leaks can occur from seals or gaskets. Replacing the leaking components is required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **Solenoid failure:** Faulty solenoids can cause erratic shifting or transmission failure. Replacing the solenoids is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Suspension**

* **Leaf spring breakage:** Overloading or heavy use can cause leaf springs to break. Replacing the broken springs is required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per spring
* **Shock absorber failure:** Worn shock absorbers can compromise ride quality and handling. Replacing the shock absorbers is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250 per shock

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** A faulty alternator can lead to battery discharge and electrical system problems. Replacing the alternator is required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
* **Starter issues:** The starter may fail to engage the engine. Replacing the starter or starter solenoid is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Exterior**

* **Door handle issues:** Door handles may break or become loose. Replacing the handles is required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250 per handle
* **Rust:** Underbody or frame rust can occur over time, especially in areas with high salt exposure. Rust repair or replacement of affected components may be necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies greatly depending on the extent of rust

**Interior**

* **Seat adjustment failure:** Seat adjustment mechanisms may fail, preventing proper seat positioning. Replacing the mechanisms or motors is required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **Dashboard lights malfunction:** Lights or gauges on the dashboard may malfunction or fail. Replacing the affected components is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Summary:**

The 1996 Volvo FE offers reliable performance and high payload capacity, but it is not without issues. Engine problems, transmission issues, and suspension problems are the most frequently reported concerns. Electrical and interior issues are less common but can still arise. The severity and cost of the issues vary depending on the specific part and problem. Regular maintenance and proper upkeep can help minimize the likelihood of encountering major problems.
