The 2015 Volvo XC60 is a compact luxury SUV known for its safety, comfortable ride, and stylish design. Engine options include a 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (T5) producing 240 hp and 258 lb-ft of torque, a 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4 (T6) producing 302 hp and 295 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (T6) producing 300 hp and 325 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include an 8-speed automatic (T5 and T6 Drive-E) and a 6-speed automatic (T6 with the 3.0L engine). Owners generally appreciate the XC60's comfortable seats, quiet cabin, and abundance of safety features. Some common complaints include the infotainment system can be laggy, fuel economy is not outstanding, and the rear cargo area is smaller compared to competitors.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (T5 Drive-E)**
*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent oil top-offs between scheduled oil changes. Can lead to low oil levels and potential engine damage if not addressed.
    *   Solution: Updated piston ring design.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+ (depending on extent of damage and if an engine rebuild/replacement is necessary)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4 (T6 Drive-E)**
*   **Supercharger Clutch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining or grinding noise from the engine, especially during acceleration. Loss of power and reduced fuel economy.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the supercharger clutch.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (T6)**
*No Issues Found*

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic (Aisin AW TF-80SC)**
*   **Torque Converter Shudder**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibration during light acceleration, particularly at lower speeds.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the torque converter or complete transmission rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $3,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**8-Speed Automatic (Aisin AW TG-81SC)**
*   **Rough or Delayed Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hesitation when shifting gears, harsh shifting, or failure to shift properly.
    *   Solution: Software update. If the problem persists, valve body replacement or complete transmission rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (software update) - $3,500+ (transmission rebuild)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Air Suspension Failure (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sagging in the rear, air compressor running constantly, "Suspension Service Urgent" message.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the air springs, air compressor, or ride height sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ (depending on which components need replacing)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, slow response times, Bluetooth connectivity problems, navigation errors.
    *   Solution: Software update, or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle battery draining overnight or within a few days, requiring frequent jump-starts.
    *   Solution: Identifying and repairing parasitic drain, which could be caused by a faulty module or accessory.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+ (depending on the source of the drain)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the cabin during rain or car washes, often around the headliner.
    *   Solution: Cleaning and unclogging the sunroof drain tubes, or replacing the sunroof seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Cracked Dashboard**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracking or warping of the dashboard, particularly in areas exposed to direct sunlight.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the dashboard.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Volvo XC60 can experience a range of issues, with engine and transmission problems being potentially the most costly, especially concerning oil consumption and transmission shifting. Electrical glitches in the infotainment system and battery drain issues can be annoying but are typically less expensive to resolve. Suspension issues are likely with vehicles equipped with rear air suspension, while sunroof leaks and dashboard cracking are more cosmetic but still important to address. The severity and cost of these issues vary depending on the specific problem and the extent of the damage.
