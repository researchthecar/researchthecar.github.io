# 2019 Volvo S90

### Overview
The 2019 Volvo S90 is a luxury sedan known for its sophisticated design, advanced safety features, and comfortable ride. As a premium vehicle, it generally exhibits good build quality and reliability. However, like any complex automobile, specific components can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and prospective buyers. We will examine the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical systems, exterior, and interior, detailing any reported widespread issues and their associated repair considerations.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged (T5)
**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, low coolant level warning light, engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of leaking hoses, seals, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


### 2.0L Turbocharged & Supercharged (T6)
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement of the turbocharger unit or related plumbing.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500


### 2.0L Plug-in Hybrid (T8)
**Hybrid Battery Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced electric range, charging problems, hybrid system warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis by a qualified technician, potential battery module replacement or software update.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $5,000+

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, connectivity issues (Bluetooth, Apple CarPlay/Android Auto).
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential module replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery after short periods of inactivity, difficulty starting.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the source of the drain (e.g., faulty module, aftermarket accessory) and repair.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Volvo S90 demonstrates a generally strong reliability record, particularly in its transmission, suspension, and core exterior/interior components. The most frequently cited technical concerns tend to be related to the advanced powertrain options, specifically potential issues with the turbocharger on T6 models and hybrid battery components on T8 variants. Electrical systems, particularly the infotainment software and occasional battery drain issues, are also areas to be aware of. While these issues can be costly to repair, they are not universally reported across all vehicles. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to warning lights are crucial for maintaining the S90's long-term reliability and value.
