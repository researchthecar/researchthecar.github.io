# 2019 Volvo S90 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Volvo S90 offers a sophisticated blend of Scandinavian luxury, advanced safety features, and a selection of turbocharged and supercharged powertrains. As a used luxury sedan, buyers generally appreciate its striking design and comfortable ride, though prospective owners should review its complex mechanical and electronic systems carefully.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder (T5)

The T5 features a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing around 250 horsepower. It provides smooth power delivery for daily commuting with respectable efficiency, though buyers should ensure proper maintenance records.

### 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged 4-Cylinder (T6)

The T6 utilizes a 2.0-liter engine featuring both a supercharger and a turbocharged system to generate roughly 316 horsepower. It delivers robust performance across the rev range, though its mechanical complexity demands attentive upkeep.

### 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Plug-in Hybrid (T8)

The T8 plug-in hybrid pairs the turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine with an electric motor to output approximately 400 total system horsepower. It offers brisk acceleration and electric driving capability, but relies heavily on sophisticated electronics.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission handles power delivery across all standard configurations, paired with either front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive. It offers smooth and responsive gear shifts, contributing to overall driving refinement.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V220000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=S90&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The driver and passenger front seat rail rear flange nuts may not have been installed during assembly. If the nuts are missing, in the event of a crash, the front seat structure and position may be affected, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the front seat rails for missing flange nuts, installing them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V800000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=S90&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the GPS location is not provided to emergency personnel it can inhibit emergency response in the event of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will correct the software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V144000: Forward Collision Avoidance: Automatic Emergency Braking](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=S90&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Due to a software/hardware incompatibility, the Automatic Emergency Brake (AEB) system may not detect obstacles and engage as intended. If the automatic emergency braking system does not engage as intended, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will update the Active Safety Domain Master (ASDM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V414000: Electrical System:Wiring:Fuses And Circuit Breakers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=S90&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Failure of the low-pressure fuel pump can cause a stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuse, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Volvo S90 presents an attractive option for used car buyers seeking luxury, advanced technology, and distinctive styling. While its powertrain options are generally refined, prospective owners should thoroughly inspect the vehicle's service history and electronic features to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
