The 2023 Volvo S90 is a luxury sedan known for its elegant Scandinavian design, comfortable ride, and advanced safety features. Engine options include the B6 mild-hybrid engine. The B6 produces 295 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque. People often praise the S90 for its stylish interior, spacious cabin, and comfortable seats, but some find the infotainment system to be less intuitive than those of its competitors. Some potential buyers have also expressed concern about the reliability of certain Volvo systems.

**Engine**

**B6 Mild-Hybrid**

*   **Engine Stalling/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported instances of the engine stalling or hesitating, particularly during acceleration or when starting from a stop. This can be intermittent and may be accompanied by error messages related to the hybrid system.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis by a qualified Volvo technician is required, focusing on the mild-hybrid components (starter generator, battery, control module) and fuel delivery system. Software updates or component replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200+ depending on diagnosis

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have noticed harsh or jerky shifts, particularly between lower gears. This can be more pronounced when the transmission is cold.
    *   Solution: A software update may resolve the issue. In some cases, a transmission fluid flush or valve body replacement may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150+

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Malfunctions (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: For vehicles equipped with the optional air suspension, owners have reported issues with the system failing to maintain proper ride height, resulting in a harsh or uneven ride. This can be accompanied by warning lights on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Inspection of the air struts, compressor, and control module is necessary. Replacement of faulty components may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Sensus infotainment system has been known to occasionally freeze, crash, or become unresponsive. This can affect navigation, audio, climate control, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: A software update is often the first step. In some cases, a system reset or replacement of the infotainment unit may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Software Update) - $1,500+
*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced intermittent malfunctions with the parking sensors or blind spot monitoring system, resulting in false alerts or a complete failure of the system.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis with a scan tool is necessary to identify the faulty sensor. Replacement of the sensor is typically required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Rattling/Squeaking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported rattling or squeaking noises coming from the sunroof area, particularly when driving on uneven surfaces.
    *   Solution: The sunroof mechanism may need to be lubricated or adjusted. In some cases, replacement of worn components may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
*   **Speaker Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported issues with the sound quality from speakers, including buzzing and distortion.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis by a qualified Volvo technician to inspect the speaker to identify the location of the issue. Replacement of faulty speaker may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Summary**

The 2023 Volvo S90 can be a great choice for a luxury vehicle, but it is not without its potential issues. Electrical problems, particularly those related to the infotainment system, appear to be the most commonly reported, but other problems with the engine/transmission and suspension can occur. The severity and cost of repairs can vary significantly depending on the specific issue, so it's important to have any concerns diagnosed by a qualified Volvo technician.
