The 2018 Volvo S90 is a mid-size luxury sedan known for its elegant design, comfortable ride, and advanced safety features. Engine options include the T5, a 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 producing 250 hp and 258 lb-ft of torque; the T6, a 2.0L turbocharged and supercharged inline-4 producing 316 hp and 295 lb-ft of torque; and the T8 eAWD Plug-In Hybrid, which combines the T6 engine with an electric motor for a total output of 400 hp and 472 lb-ft of torque. All engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Some owners praise the S90 for its luxurious interior and comfortable seats, while others have criticized its infotainment system and reliability.

**Engine**

**T5 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than expected oil consumption between oil changes. This may be observed by the oil level decreasing more quickly than anticipated, requiring more frequent top-offs.
    *   Solution: Monitor oil levels regularly and address any leaks promptly. In cases of excessive consumption, consult a mechanic for further diagnosis (e.g., piston rings, valve seals).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies widely depending on the source of the leak or extent of the internal damage. $100 (simple oil leak) - $3,000+ (internal engine work).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**T6 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4**

*   **Coolant Leak From Water Pump**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leak resulting in loss of coolant. This leads to overheating if not caught quickly, and a low coolant warning light will come on.
    *   Solution: Replace the water pump and ensure proper sealing.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
    *   Recalls: None Found

**T8 eAWD Plug-In Hybrid (2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4 + Electric Motor)**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner 8-Speed Automatic (All Engine Options)**

*   **Rough or Delayed Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rough or delayed shifts, particularly between lower gears. This can manifest as a noticeable jerk or hesitation during acceleration.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a transmission fluid flush and software update can resolve the issue. More severe cases may require valve body repair or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 (fluid flush and software update) - $2,000+ (valve body replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Issues (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some S90s are equipped with air suspension. Potential issues include air leaks, compressor failure, or malfunctioning sensors, resulting in a sagging vehicle, uneven ride height, or error messages.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the source of the leak or component failure and replace the affected parts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ depending on the specific component.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Sensus infotainment system can be prone to freezing, crashing, or exhibiting slow response times. This can affect navigation, audio, climate controls, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Restart the infotainment system. Software updates can also resolve this issue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Free (software update) - $100-$200 (diagnostics) - $1,000+ (replacement screen or hardware)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the car battery draining prematurely, even when the vehicle is not in use. This can lead to difficulty starting the car.
    *   Solution: Identify the parasitic draw and repair or replace the faulty component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (new battery) - $100 - $500+ (diagnostics and repair of parasitic draw)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof becoming stuck, failing to open or close properly, or exhibiting unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Lubricate the sunroof tracks, replace damaged components, or reset the sunroof motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2018 Volvo S90 can have some frustrating issues for the owners. Some of the most commonly complained about issues are related to the infotainment system and the various engine components such as the coolant pump leaking, excessive oil consumption, and transmission issues. The cost for repairs can range from a few hundred dollars for a basic repair or maintenance item to a few thousand dollars for more extensive engine or transmission work. While the S90 offers a luxurious experience, potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and factor them into their purchasing decision.
