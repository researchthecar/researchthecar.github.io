# 2018 Volvo S90

### Overview
The 2018 Volvo S90 is a luxury sedan known for its sophisticated design, advanced safety features, and comfortable ride. As a premium vehicle, it generally exhibits good reliability, particularly in its core powertrain and structural components. However, like any complex automobile, specific areas can experience issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and prospective buyers. The S90's advanced technology, while offering convenience, can sometimes be a source of electrical or infotainment system glitches.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, connectivity issues (Bluetooth, Apple CarPlay/Android Auto), navigation system errors.
- **Solution:** Software updates or resets; in some cases, replacement of the infotainment module.
- **Cost:** $150 - $1000


### 12V Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical components malfunctioning or failing to operate, difficulty starting the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V battery.

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 Volvo S90 generally offers a reliable ownership experience, with its core mechanical components like the engine and transmission showing strong durability. The primary areas of concern tend to be related to its advanced technology and optional features. The hybrid variant's battery cooling system and optional air suspension can present repair costs. Electrically, infotainment system glitches and occasional premature 12V battery failure are noted. Exterior and interior components are largely robust, with minor issues like headlight condensation being infrequent. Overall, the S90 is a solid choice for those seeking luxury and safety, provided potential buyers are aware of and prepared for the costs associated with its sophisticated systems.
