The 2019 Volvo S90 is a mid-size luxury sedan known for its elegant Scandinavian design, comfortable ride, and advanced safety features. The S90 offers several engine options, including the T5, T6, and T8 plug-in hybrid. The T5 is a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. The T6 engine is a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine producing 316 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. The T8 Twin Engine plug-in hybrid pairs a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine with an electric motor for a combined output of 400 horsepower and 472 lb-ft of torque. All engines are paired with an Aisin 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally praise the S90 for its stylish interior, comfortable seats, and quiet cabin. Some common criticisms include the infotainment system's complexity and occasional reliability concerns.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged (T5)**
*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than expected oil consumption, requiring more frequent oil top-offs between scheduled oil changes. This can range from a quart every few thousand miles to more frequent consumption.
    *   Solution: Regularly monitor oil levels and address any potential leaks. If consumption is excessive, a dealer inspection may be necessary to determine the cause. Sometimes it's due to faulty PCV valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $1000+ depending on the cause and repair
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged (T6)**
*   **Coolant Leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported coolant leaks from the water pump or thermostat housing. Signs of a coolant leak include a low coolant level warning light, visible coolant under the car, or a sweet smell.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty water pump or thermostat housing
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Plug-in Hybrid (T8)**
*   **Battery Pack Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced electric range, failure to charge, or error messages related to the hybrid battery system.
    *   Solution: Requires diagnosis by a Volvo dealer to determine if individual modules need replacement or if the entire battery pack is failing.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $10,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin 8-Speed Automatic (All Engines)**
*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported harsh or jerky shifting, especially during low-speed acceleration or deceleration. Hesitation when accelerating from a stop has also been noted.
    *   Solution: A software update may resolve the issue in some cases. In more severe cases, valve body replacement or transmission replacement may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (software update) - $6,000+ (transmission replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*No Issues Found*

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Rebooting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Sensus Connect infotainment system can occasionally freeze, lag, or randomly reboot. This can affect navigation, audio, climate control, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Software updates are the most common solution. In some cases, a hardware replacement of the central control module may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1500+ (module replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Faulty sensors related to safety systems such as blind spot monitoring (BLIS), lane keep assist, or parking sensors. Symptoms include warning lights, error messages, or the system not functioning as intended.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Automatic Emergency Braking System Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Automatic Emergency Braking system may engage for no reason or it will not engage when there is a real hazard.
    *   Solution: Requires dealer recalibration and software updates.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$500+
    *   Recalls: Recall Number: 20200004V

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks around the sunroof area, potentially causing water damage to the headliner and interior.
    *   Solution: Cleaning and resealing the sunroof drain tubes or replacing the sunroof seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Volvo S90 has some common issues, including engine oil consumption and coolant leaks, as well as transmission shifting problems. Electrical issues are also prevalent, particularly with the infotainment system and sensor malfunctions. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to more significant mechanical failures, with repair costs varying accordingly. While some issues can be resolved with software updates, others may require more extensive and expensive repairs.
