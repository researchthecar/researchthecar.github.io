The 2020 Volvo S90 is a mid-size luxury sedan known for its elegant design, comfortable ride, and advanced safety features. Engine options for the 2020 S90 include the T5, a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque; the T6, a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine producing 316 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque; and the T8 eAWD Plug-in Hybrid, a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine paired with an electric motor, producing a combined 400 horsepower and 472 lb-ft of torque. All engine options are paired with an Aisin Warner 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally praise the S90 for its comfortable and quiet interior, stylish exterior, and advanced technology. Common complaints include the complexity of the infotainment system, some reliability concerns, and the firm ride quality on certain trims with larger wheels.

**Engine**

**T5 2.0L Turbocharged I4**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between scheduled oil changes. This can manifest as low oil level warnings or the need to add oil every few thousand miles.
    *   Solution: Monitoring oil levels regularly and addressing any leaks or unusual oil consumption with a mechanic. In severe cases, piston ring replacement might be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**T6 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged I4**

*   **Coolant Leakage**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks may develop from the water pump or thermostat housing, resulting in low coolant levels, overheating, and coolant smell.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty water pump and thermostat housing, ensuring proper sealing and torquing of bolts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200
    *   Recalls: None Found

**T8 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged I4 Plug-in Hybrid**

*   **High Voltage Coolant Heater Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Failure of the high voltage coolant heater used to heat the cabin in electric mode may lead to reduced cabin heating performance, especially in colder climates.
    *   Solution: Replacing the high voltage coolant heater unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner 8-Speed Automatic (All Engine Options)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rough or delayed shifts, particularly when shifting between lower gears. This can be accompanied by a noticeable "clunk" or hesitation.
    *   Solution: Performing a transmission fluid flush and software update may resolve the issue. In more severe cases, valve body replacement or complete transmission overhaul may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 (fluid flush) to $3,000+ (transmission overhaul)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Compressor Failure (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Models equipped with air suspension may experience compressor failure, leading to a sagging suspension, uneven ride height, and warning lights on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty air suspension compressor. Also, check for air leaks in the system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Sensus infotainment system can experience glitches, freezing, slow response times, and connectivity problems (Bluetooth, Apple CarPlay, Android Auto).
    *   Solution: Performing a software update can often resolve these issues. In some cases, a system reset or replacement of the infotainment unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software updates (typically free at dealer), Unit Replacement ($1,000+)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with excessive battery drain, particularly if the vehicle is left unused for extended periods. This can lead to difficulty starting the car.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing the source of the battery drain (e.g., faulty module, parasitic draw) and addressing it. Replacing the battery may also be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (battery replacement) to $500+ (diagnostics and repair)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the sunroof getting stuck, making excessive noise, or leaking.
    *   Solution: Cleaning and lubricating the sunroof tracks may resolve the issue. In more severe cases, sunroof motor replacement or complete sunroof assembly replacement may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2020 Volvo S90 can have some reliability concerns, mostly with electrical components such as the infotainment system and battery drain. The engine, regardless of the configuration, may experience oil consumption or coolant leaks. The transmission can have shifting issues that may require simple fluid changes or a complete overhaul depending on the severity. Air suspension may have compressor issues, as well. Depending on the issue, the cost to repair can range from a few hundred dollars to several thousand.
