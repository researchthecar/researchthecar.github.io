# 2021 Volvo S90

### Overview
The 2021 Volvo S90 is a luxury sedan known for its sophisticated design, advanced safety features, and comfortable ride. As a mid-size executive car, it aims to provide a premium ownership experience. Reliability data for the 2021 model year indicates a generally strong performance, with most reported issues being minor or related to specific, less common configurations. Volvo's commitment to quality and safety is evident in the S90's construction, though like any vehicle, certain components may experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond standard maintenance.

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Volvo S90 demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly with its standard T5 and T6 gasoline powertrains and the automatic transmission. The primary areas where owners might encounter issues are related to the optional Adaptive Air Suspension, specific electrical components like the infotainment system software and 12V battery, and the hybrid system's battery cooling on the T8 models. Exterior and interior components are generally robust. While some issues like LED headlight condensation or AC performance can occur, they are not indicative of systemic failure across the model range. Regular maintenance and addressing minor electrical glitches promptly are key to a trouble-free ownership experience.
