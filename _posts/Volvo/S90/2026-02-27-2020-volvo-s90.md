# 2020 Volvo S90 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Volvo S90 offers a sophisticated blend of Scandinavian luxury, advanced safety features, and a range of turbocharged and supercharged powertrains. As a used luxury sedan, buyers generally appreciate its serene ride quality and elegant interior appointments, though navigating its complex electronics and sophisticated engine options requires careful attention during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### T5 2.0L Turbocharged I4

The T5 features a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generating around 250 horsepower. It provides smooth power delivery for daily driving with generally solid reliability.

### T6 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged I4

The T6 utilizes a 2.0-liter engine that is both turbocharged and supercharged, producing approximately 316 horsepower. While brisk and responsive, its dual-forced induction system adds mechanical complexity.

### T8 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Plug-in Hybrid I4

The T8 plug-in hybrid combines the T6 forced-induction engine with an electric motor to deliver around 400 total system horsepower. Buyers should thoroughly evaluate the hybrid battery health and charging hardware when shopping used.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Geartronic Automatic Transmission

The standard 8-speed automatic transmission pairs across the S90 lineup to deliver smooth and prompt gear changes. It is generally robust, provided routine fluid maintenance has been performed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V144000: Forward Collision Avoidance: Automatic Emergency Braking](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=S90&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Due to a software/hardware incompatibility, the Automatic Emergency Brake (AEB) system may not detect obstacles and engage as intended. If the automatic emergency braking system does not engage as intended, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will update the Active Safety Domain Master (ASDM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V414000: Electrical System:Wiring:Fuses And Circuit Breakers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=S90&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Failure of the low-pressure fuel pump can cause a stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuse, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V179000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=S90&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The high-voltage battery may experience a short circuit within the battery module when the battery is fully charged and the vehicle is parked. A short circuit in a high-voltage battery increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the high-voltage battery module as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Volvo S90 is a striking and comfortable alternative to traditional German luxury sedans, supported by a refined lineup of powertrain options. While major mechanical failures are uncommon, prospective buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle's advanced electrical systems and hybrid components if considering the T8 model to ensure a worry-free ownership experience.
