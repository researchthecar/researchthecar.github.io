# 2019 Volvo XC40 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Volvo XC40 is a stylish and tech-forward luxury compact SUV, but prospective used-car buyers should review potential owner-reported trouble spots before purchasing. While it offers a premium cabin and solid performance, owner feedback highlights a few recurring mechanical and electrical concerns that warrant a careful pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine provides responsive power suited for daily driving. It generally runs smoothly, though routine maintenance is essential to avoid minor accessory or emissions-related complications.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic

The 8-speed automatic transmission delivers seamless gear changes and pairs well with both front-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations. Overall mechanical durability has proven dependable in everyday use.

## Brakes

### Brake Pedal and Rotors

Braking performance is generally robust, but owners have occasionally noted pedal firmness issues and premature rotor vibration.

**Brake Pedal Stiffness and Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported instances where the brake pedal became unusually hard to push or unresponsive, or where the front end shuddered during braking.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the brake pads, rotors, and brake booster assembly for wear or hydraulic faults.

## Steering

### Power Steering Assist

The electric power steering system provides light and accurate handling, though electrical voltage fluctuations can occasionally trigger assist warnings.

**Power Steering Assist Warning and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience warning messages related to the power steering system, sometimes linked to auxiliary battery health or sensor communication faults.
- **Solution:** Check the condition of the primary and secondary electrical batteries and have diagnostic trouble codes read by a specialist.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V535000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC40&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The rear seats may not have rear seat back lock strikers, allowing the seat back to move forward during certain hard braking conditions or a crash. If the seat back moves forward during hard braking or in a crash, it can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the vehicles and install the seat back lock strikers if they are missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V144000: Forward Collision Avoidance: Automatic Emergency Braking](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC40&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Due to a software/hardware incompatibility, the Automatic Emergency Brake (AEB) system may not detect obstacles and engage as intended. If the automatic emergency braking system does not engage as intended, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will update the Active Safety Domain Master (ASDM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V800000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC40&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the GPS location is not provided to emergency personnel it can inhibit emergency response in the event of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will correct the software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V554000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Pedals And Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC40&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The brake pedals may not have been correctly riveted, allowing the pedal to move out of position, possibly reducing braking performance. Reduced braking performance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used model, ensure a thorough inspection of the brakes, steering electronics, and general powertrain components. Paying attention to these key areas during a test drive will help ensure a safer and more reliable ownership experience.
