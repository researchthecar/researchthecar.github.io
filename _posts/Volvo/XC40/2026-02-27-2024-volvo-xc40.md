# 2024 Volvo XC40 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2024 Volvo XC40, buyers will find a stylish and technologically advanced compact luxury SUV. However, early owner feedback logged with safety authorities highlights a handful of software and electrical anomalies that prospective buyers should be aware of.

---

## Engine

### B4 Mild Hybrid 2.0L Turbocharged I4

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder mild-hybrid engine produces approximately 247 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. It provides smooth power delivery for daily commuting, though long-term reliability data for this newer powertrain variant is still unfolding.

### B5 Mild Hybrid 2.0L Turbocharged I4

Offering a higher output tune of the 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder mild-hybrid platform, this variant delivers around 295 horsepower. It pairs spirited performance with standard all-wheel drive, though buyers should verify routine maintenance history.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

This conventional 8-speed automatic transmission manages power smoothly across front-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations. It generally operates unobtrusively, matching well with the vehicle's luxury character.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera and Sensors

Advanced driver assistance and camera systems rely heavily on software stability to provide consistent visibility.

**Camera Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The backup camera screen displays a black feed with error icons instead of showing rear, front, or side video views.
- **Solution:** A system reboot or software update typically resolves camera feed interruptions.

## Exterior

### Interior Lighting

Cabin lighting controls utilize touch-sensitive technology that can occasionally exhibit erratic behavior.

**Map Light Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The passenger-side map light activates unexpectedly on its own and fails to respond to touch-sensitive controls.
- **Solution:** Owners report that cycling the main dome light manually can temporarily reset the system, though software updates may be required.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V010000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC40&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A diagnostic error in the Central Electronic Module (CEM) software may cause the left rear turn signal indicator to not function. A nonfunctioning exterior turn signal may not notify other drivers of a turning vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V282000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC40&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera image may not display when the vehicle is placed in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display reduces the driver's view of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V392000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist:Electric:Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC40&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A loss of brake function increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V908000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC40&modelYear=2024)**

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Volvo XC40 remains an appealing luxury choice with robust powertrain options, but used car shoppers should thoroughly check vehicle electronics, software versions, and infotainment systems during a pre-purchase test drive to ensure everything operates correctly.
