# 2023 Volvo XC40 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Volvo XC40 delivers contemporary styling, premium interior craftsmanship, and solid safety credentials. When shopping for a used model, prospective buyers should review owner-reported concerns regarding driver-assistance features, electronic systems, and occasional braking feedback.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

This mild-hybrid turbocharged four-cylinder engine delivers around 247 horsepower, providing brisk daily acceleration and smooth operation. It is generally reliable, though routine maintenance is essential to ensure long-term longevity.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic

Paired with standard front-wheel drive or available all-wheel drive, this conventional 8-speed automatic transmission offers refined, seamless gear shifts. It has a strong track record for overall durability in daily driving conditions.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides strong stopping power, but some owners have flagged rare electronic brake integration or pressure consistency issues.

**Brake System Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected brake pressure loss, intermittent freezing, or sudden unexpected vehicle acceleration while holding the brake pedal.
- **Solution:** Have the brake master cylinder, electronic parking brake, and related sensors thoroughly inspected by a qualified technician.

## Electrical

### Back Over Prevention

Advanced driver-assistance technologies offer robust security but rely heavily on software stability.

**Backup Camera Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview camera display may intermittently freeze, show a blank screen, or point downward toward the bumper instead of the normal viewing range.
- **Solution:** Install required software updates or have the camera module and wiring harnesses checked for faults.

## Safety

### Forward Collision

Collision mitigation features enhance security on paper, though occasional phantom braking events have been noted by owners.

**Unexpected Automatic Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The forward collision avoidance system may inadvertently trigger hard braking events when no obstacles or other vehicles are present on the road.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates relating to the radar and camera sensors or recalibrate the collision avoidance system.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V012000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC40&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A diagnostic error in the Brake Control Module 2 (BCM2) may cause a loss of Antilock Brake System (ABS), Electronic Stability Control (ESC), and traction control. A reduction in brake support functions can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V188000: Electrical System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC40&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The false warning message could prevent the detection of an actual e-call system failure, which could delay an emergency response during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the telematics and connectivity antenna module (TCAM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V282000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC40&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera image may not display when the vehicle is placed in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display reduces the driver's view of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V908000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC40&modelYear=2023)**

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Volvo XC40 remains a stylish and comfortable luxury subcompact SUV choice for used car shoppers. While the powertrain options are generally dependable, buyers should carefully test all driver-assistance technologies, infotainment displays, and brake responsiveness during a pre-purchase inspection.
