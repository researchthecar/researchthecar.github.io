# 2023 Volvo XC40 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Volvo XC40 brings distinctive Scandinavian styling, upscale interior touches, and refined performance to the compact luxury SUV segment. As buyers explore the used market for this model year, understanding its powertrain options and general owner-reported experiences can help guide a confident purchase. While the vehicle generally offers a comfortable and high-tech daily driving experience, reviewing specific mechanical and electrical traits ensures you select a well-maintained example.

---

## Engine

### B4 Mild Hybrid 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The B4 powertrain features a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder mild-hybrid setup producing around 194 horsepower. It delivers smooth everyday power while prioritizing efficiency for daily commuting.

### B5 Mild Hybrid 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The B5 powertrain utilizes a higher-output 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder mild-hybrid system generating roughly 247 horsepower. It provides brisk acceleration and confident highway passing capabilities.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission

This 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission pairs with mild-hybrid configurations to deliver quick gear shifts and optimized fuel economy. Overall mechanical reliability remains solid for routine driving.

### 8-Speed Geartronic Automatic Transmission

The traditional 8-speed torque-converter automatic transmission offers smooth, refined gear changes across various driving conditions. It is a dependable choice that complements the vehicle's luxury character.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V012000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC40&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A diagnostic error in the Brake Control Module 2 (BCM2) may cause a loss of Antilock Brake System (ABS), Electronic Stability Control (ESC), and traction control. A reduction in brake support functions can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V188000: Electrical System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC40&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The false warning message could prevent the detection of an actual e-call system failure, which could delay an emergency response during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the telematics and connectivity antenna module (TCAM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V282000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC40&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera image may not display when the vehicle is placed in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display reduces the driver's view of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V908000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC40&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera image may not display when the vehicle is placed in reverse. - **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.
