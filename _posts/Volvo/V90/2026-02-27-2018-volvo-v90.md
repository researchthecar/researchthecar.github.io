# 2018 Volvo V90

### Overview
The 2018 Volvo V90, a premium mid-size wagon, is generally recognized for its strong safety features, luxurious interior, and comfortable ride. As with any vehicle, specific components can experience issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance. The V90, particularly in its various powertrain configurations, aims to balance performance with efficiency. Owners often praise its refined driving dynamics and advanced technology. However, understanding potential weak points can help in long-term ownership and maintenance planning.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged (T5)
**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, low coolant level warning light, engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of leaking hoses, seals, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


### 2.0L Turbocharged & Supercharged (T6)
**Turbocharger/Supercharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement of the turbocharger or supercharger unit.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $3000


### 2.0L Turbocharged Diesel (D4)
**Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Regeneration Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, reduced engine performance, increased fuel consumption.
- **Solution:** Forced DPF regeneration or cleaning; replacement if severely clogged.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1200


### 2.0L Turbocharged Diesel (D5)
**AdBlue System Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** AdBlue warning light, reduced engine power, potential inability to start.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair or replacement of AdBlue pump, sensors, or injector.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, connectivity issues (Bluetooth, Wi-Fi).
- **Solution:** Software updates or system reset.
- **Cost:** $0 - $200 (for dealer updates)


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dies unexpectedly, car won't start.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify parasitic draw from modules or accessories, repair or replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 Volvo V90 demonstrates good overall reliability, with most reported issues being specific to certain powertrain options or optional features like air suspension. The gasoline engines (T5 and T6) and the automatic transmission are generally robust, though some owners have noted coolant leaks or turbocharger concerns. Diesel variants (D4 and D5) can encounter issues related to emissions systems (DPF, AdBlue), which are common in modern diesels. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system's software, are also a possibility. For the most part, the V90 offers a dependable ownership experience, provided potential issues are addressed promptly.
