# 2019 Volvo V90 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Volvo V90 offers a sophisticated blend of Scandinavian luxury, distinctive styling, and advanced safety features for wagon enthusiasts. As a used vehicle, buyers should evaluate its advanced turbocharged and supercharged powertrains alongside potential owner-reported electrical and mechanical quirks. While NHTSA complaints for this model year are limited, prospective owners are advised to conduct a thorough pre-purchase inspection to ensure all complex electronics and drivetrain components operate smoothly.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

This base 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 250 horsepower, delivering efficient daily performance. Buyers should ensure regular maintenance intervals have been followed to maintain long-term reliability.

### 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged 4-Cylinder

This higher-output 2.0-liter engine utilizes both a turbocharger and a supercharger to generate around 316 horsepower, offering brisk acceleration. While powerful, its complex forced-induction system requires diligent upkeep by previous owners.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with both engine options, delivering smooth gear shifts across front-wheel drive and all-wheel drive configurations. Overall, this transmission is generally robust when fluid changes are performed on schedule.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V800000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=V90&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the GPS location is not provided to emergency personnel it can inhibit emergency response in the event of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will correct the software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V144000: Forward Collision Avoidance: Automatic Emergency Braking](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=V90&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Due to a software/hardware incompatibility, the Automatic Emergency Brake (AEB) system may not detect obstacles and engage as intended. If the automatic emergency braking system does not engage as intended, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will update the Active Safety Domain Master (ASDM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V414000: Electrical System:Wiring:Fuses And Circuit Breakers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=V90&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Failure of the low-pressure fuel pump can cause a stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuse, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Volvo V90 remains an elegant and comfortable choice for used wagon buyers seeking premium features and distinctive design. Because owner reports and available data indicate a relatively quiet complaint profile, prioritizing a well-documented service history and a comprehensive mechanical inspection will help secure a dependable ownership experience.
