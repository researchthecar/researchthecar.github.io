# 2021 Volvo V90

### Overview
The 2021 Volvo V90, a premium estate car, is generally recognized for its strong safety features, comfortable ride, and sophisticated interior. As a model built on Volvo's Scalable Product Architecture (SPA), it benefits from modern engineering and a focus on reliability. While specific widespread mechanical failures are not commonly reported for this model year, like any vehicle, individual components can experience issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas and their associated costs for owners.

**Air Spring Leaks**

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Volvo V90 demonstrates a strong reliability record, with most major mechanical and electrical systems performing as expected. The primary areas where owners might encounter issues are related to the hybrid system's battery cooling on T8 models, potential software glitches within the infotainment system, and the possibility of premature 12V battery failure, which is not uncommon in modern vehicles with high electrical demands. Owners opting for the air suspension should be aware of potential leaks over time. Overall, the V90 is a dependable choice, but proactive maintenance and awareness of these specific potential issues are recommended.
