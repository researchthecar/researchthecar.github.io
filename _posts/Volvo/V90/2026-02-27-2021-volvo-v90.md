# 2021 Volvo V90 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Volvo V90 is a sophisticated luxury wagon that combines Scandinavian styling with advanced comfort features and a refined interior. Prospective buyers looking at the used market will find that while owner complaints are minimal, evaluating the vehicle's complex powertrain and electronic systems remains an important part of the pre-purchase inspection process.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder (T5)

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces around 250 horsepower and offers smooth power delivery for daily driving. It generally maintains a solid reliability record when routine maintenance schedules are strictly followed.

### 2.0L Supercharged and Turbocharged 4-Cylinder (T6)

Combining both a turbocharger and a supercharger, this engine generates approximately 316 horsepower for brisk acceleration. Buyers should ensure regular oil changes and system checks are documented to maintain long-term durability.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission provides seamless gear changes and optimizes fuel efficiency across various driving conditions. It is generally robust, though verifying smooth shifting performance during a test drive is always recommended.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V682000: Seat Belts:Front:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=V90&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the seat belt assemblies, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V001000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=V90&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The Supplementary Restraint System (SRS) control unit may not have been properly attached to the car body and may detach during a crash. A detached SRS control unit can decrease the performance of occupant protection systems such as air bags, seat belts, and high voltage battery disconnect, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo Car will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, as necessary, tighten the SRS control unit mounting screws, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Volvo V90 presents an attractive option for luxury wagon enthusiasts seeking style, comfort, and utility. With very few reported owner issues in standard tracking, a thorough test drive and a standard pre-purchase inspection will ensure the vehicle's electronics and mechanical systems are in top condition before purchase.
