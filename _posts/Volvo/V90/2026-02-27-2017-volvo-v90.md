# 2017 Volvo V90

### Overview
The 2017 Volvo V90, a premium estate car, is generally recognized for its strong safety features and comfortable ride. As a model that was relatively new to some markets in 2017, its long-term reliability data is still being compiled. However, initial reports and owner feedback suggest a solid build quality. Volvo's focus on robust engineering is evident, particularly in its powertrain and chassis design. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the V90 appears to offer a dependable ownership experience, with most reported concerns being minor or related to specific component failures rather than widespread systemic problems. Owners often praise its refined interior and advanced technology.

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Volvo V90 demonstrates good overall reliability, with its core mechanical components like the engine and transmission showing minimal reported issues. The primary areas where owners might encounter problems are with the optional air suspension, which can develop leaks, and occasional electrical gremlins, particularly concerning the infotainment system's software and potential battery drain issues. These are not typically indicative of systemic failures but rather isolated component or software-related concerns. With proper maintenance and attention to potential minor issues, the V90 should provide a dependable and luxurious ownership experience.
