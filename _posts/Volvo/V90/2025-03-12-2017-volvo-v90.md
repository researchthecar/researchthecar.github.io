The 2017 Volvo V90 is a stylish and comfortable wagon offering a blend of luxury, practicality, and Scandinavian design. Engine options include the T5, a turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder producing 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, the T6, a supercharged and turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder producing 316 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, and the T8 plug-in hybrid, which combines the T6 engine with an electric motor for a total system output of 400 horsepower and 472 lb-ft of torque. All engines are paired with an Aisin-Warner 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the V90's comfortable ride, spacious interior, and elegant styling. Some common complaints include the sometimes-complex infotainment system and occasional electrical glitches.

**Engine**

**T5 (2.0L Turbocharged)**

* **Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than expected oil consumption, requiring more frequent top-offs between oil changes.
    * Solution: In some cases, Volvo has addressed this issue with updated piston ring designs. Monitoring oil levels regularly is recommended.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+ depending on the severity and required repairs (e.g., piston ring replacement).
    * Recalls: None Found

**T6 (2.0L Supercharged and Turbocharged)**

* **Engine Stalling/Hesitation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported instances of engine stalling, hesitation upon acceleration, or rough idling.
    * Solution: Possible causes range from faulty sensors (e.g., mass airflow sensor) to fuel system issues or software glitches. Diagnosis by a qualified technician is essential. Updated ECU software or replacement of faulty components may be required.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+ depending on the cause and components.
    * Recalls: None Found

**T8 (2.0L Supercharged and Turbocharged Plug-In Hybrid)**

* **Battery Pack Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hybrid battery may have reduced capacity, failure to hold a charge, or error messages related to the hybrid system.
    * Solution: Diagnosis by a qualified technician is essential. In some cases, the battery management system (BMS) may need to be recalibrated or individual battery modules may need to be replaced. In severe cases, the entire hybrid battery pack may need replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $8,000+ depending on the severity and required repairs.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin-Warner 8-Speed Automatic (All Engines)**

* **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported harsh or jerky shifting, particularly between lower gears. Some report hesitation when accelerating from a stop.
    * Solution: A software update to the transmission control module (TCM) may resolve the issue. In some cases, a transmission fluid flush or replacement of faulty solenoids within the transmission may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $1,500+ depending on the cause and components.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Issues (if equipped)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vehicles equipped with the optional air suspension may experience issues such as sagging, uneven ride height, or failure of the air compressor.
    * Solution: Diagnosis by a qualified technician is essential. Leaks in the air struts or air lines, a faulty air compressor, or a malfunctioning suspension control module can cause these issues. Replacement of the faulty components is typically required.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ depending on the component.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Infotainment System Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The Sensus infotainment system can experience glitches, including freezing, slow response times, Bluetooth connectivity problems, and navigation errors.
    * Solution: Often, a software update can resolve these issues. However, in some cases, the infotainment unit may need to be reset or replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) to $1,500+ (unit replacement).
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Sensor Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: Various sensors, such as parking sensors or blind-spot monitoring sensors, may malfunction, leading to inaccurate readings or false warnings.
    * Solution: Diagnosis by a qualified technician is essential. Replacement of the faulty sensor is typically required.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per sensor.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported sunroof malfunctions, including difficulty opening or closing, or leaks.
    * Solution: Inspection and lubrication of the sunroof mechanism may resolve the issue. In some cases, the sunroof motor or seals may need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+ depending on the issue.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Seat Heater Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: The seat heaters may fail to heat properly or may overheat.
    * Solution: Diagnosis by a qualified technician is essential. Replacement of the heating element or the seat heater control module may be required.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per seat.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Volvo V90, while praised for its comfort and style, can present owners with various issues. Engine oil consumption and potential transmission problems could affect the longevity of the vehicle. Electrical glitches, particularly with the infotainment system, are also common. Suspension issues are also of concern. The severity and cost of repairs can vary widely, with some issues requiring relatively inexpensive fixes and others potentially costing thousands of dollars. It is advisable to obtain a pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic and inquire about the vehicle's service history.
