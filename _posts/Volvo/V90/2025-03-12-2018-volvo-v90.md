The 2018 Volvo V90 is a stylish and luxurious wagon known for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and advanced safety features. Engine options include the T5 (2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4), the T6 (2.0L Turbocharged AND Supercharged Inline-4), and the T8 (2.0L Turbocharged AND Supercharged Inline-4 Plug-in Hybrid). The T5 produces 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, while the T6 delivers 316 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. The T8 combines a gasoline engine and electric motor for a total output of 400 horsepower and 472 lb-ft of torque. All engine options are paired with an Aisin-Warner 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally praise the V90's elegant design, comfortable seats, and smooth ride, but some common complaints include the complex infotainment system and occasional electrical glitches.

**Engine**

**T5 (2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4)**

*   **No Issues Found**

**T6 (2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4)**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than expected oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. This can manifest as low oil level warnings, or visually noticing the oil disappearing off the dipstick.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a software update can help address the issue by adjusting the engine's oil consumption parameters. In more severe cases, piston ring replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix:

        *   Software Update: $150 - $300
        *   Piston Ring Replacement: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**T8 (2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4 Plug-in Hybrid)**

*   **Coolant Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaking from the coolant tank on the passenger side of the engine bay. Visible coolant dripping and low coolant level.
    *   Solution: Replace the coolant tank and related components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $700+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin-Warner 8-Speed Automatic (All Engine Options)**

*   **Rough or Delayed Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rough or delayed shifts, particularly when shifting between lower gears. This can manifest as jerky acceleration or a noticeable delay before the transmission engages.
    *   Solution: A software update to the transmission control module (TCM) can sometimes resolve shifting issues. In more severe cases, valve body replacement or a complete transmission rebuild may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix:

        *   Software Update: $150 - $300
        *   Valve Body Replacement: $1,200 - $2,500+
        *   Transmission Rebuild: $3,000 - $6,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Issues (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Models equipped with the optional air suspension system may experience issues with the air struts or compressor. Symptoms include the car sitting lower than normal, uneven ride height, or a warning light on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty air strut(s) or air compressor is typically required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix:

        *   Air Strut Replacement: $800 - $1,500+ per strut
        *   Air Compressor Replacement: $1,000 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Sensus infotainment system can experience glitches, such as freezing, slow response times, or connectivity issues with Bluetooth or Apple CarPlay/Android Auto.
    *   Solution: Restarting the system or performing a software update can sometimes resolve the issue. In some cases, replacement of the infotainment screen or control unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix:

        *   Software Update: $0 - $300 (depending on dealership policy)
        *   Infotainment Screen Replacement: $1,000 - $2,500+
        *   Control Unit Replacement: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with excessive battery drain, leading to difficulty starting the car, especially if it has been sitting for a few days.
    * Solution: Diagnosing and replacing the faulty component that is causing the parasitic drain. This could include modules that aren't properly shutting down, or a short circuit somewhere in the wiring.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof/Moonroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the sunroof or moonroof, such as getting stuck, making unusual noises, or leaking.
    *   Solution: The specific solution will depend on the nature of the problem. It may involve lubricating the tracks, replacing the motor, or resealing the glass panel.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2018 Volvo V90 is generally a reliable and well-regarded vehicle, but some common issues reported by owners include excessive oil consumption in the T6 engine, rough shifting with the 8-speed automatic transmission, air suspension problems (if equipped), and glitches with the infotainment system. While some issues can be resolved with software updates, others may require more extensive and costly repairs. The severity and cost of these issues vary depending on the specific problem and the extent of the damage. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and consider having a pre-purchase inspection performed by a qualified mechanic to identify any existing problems.
