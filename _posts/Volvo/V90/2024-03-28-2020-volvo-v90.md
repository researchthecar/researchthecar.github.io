**Introduction**

The 2020 Volvo V90 is a luxury station wagon that offers a spacious interior, a comfortable ride, and a variety of high-tech features. It is available with a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine that produces 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, or a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine that produces 316 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission. Critics praised the V90 for its comfortable interior and advanced safety features, but some complained about its high price and limited cargo space.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder Engine**

**Oil Consumption**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, resulting in low oil levels and potential engine damage.
Solution: Engine replacement or rebuild.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
Recalls: None Found

**2.0-liter Turbocharged and Supercharged Four-Cylinder Engine**

**No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Eight-Speed Automatic Transmission**

**Shifting Problems**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, delayed shifts, or transmission slipping.
Solution: Transmission fluid change or replacement of faulty transmission components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

**Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: Rapid battery discharge, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle or complete electrical failure.
Solution: Replacement of faulty battery or diagnosis and repair of electrical system.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Windshield Cracking**
Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing in the windshield without any apparent impact or damage.
Solution: Windshield replacement.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Infotainment System Malfunctions**
Description/Symptoms: Touchscreen freezing, navigation system errors, or audio system problems.
Solution: Software updates or replacement of infotainment unit.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2020 Volvo V90 has some issues that owners should be aware of, including oil consumption problems with the 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine, shifting problems with the eight-speed automatic transmission, and battery drain issues. However, the vehicle has also received praise for its comfortable interior and advanced safety features. The severity of the issues and the cost to fix them vary, but overall, the V90 is a well-regarded luxury station wagon.
