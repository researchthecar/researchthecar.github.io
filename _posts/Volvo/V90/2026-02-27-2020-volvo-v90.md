# 2020 Volvo V90

### Overview
The 2020 Volvo V90, a premium wagon known for its Scandinavian design and safety features, generally exhibits strong reliability. Volvo's commitment to quality is evident in many aspects of the V90's engineering. However, like any vehicle, specific components can experience issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns, excluding routine maintenance and normal wear and tear. We will examine the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical systems, exterior, and interior to provide a comprehensive overview of potential problem areas and their associated costs for owners.

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 Volvo V90 demonstrates a commendable level of reliability, particularly in its core mechanical components like the engine and transmission, with most variants reporting no significant widespread issues. The primary areas to monitor are the hybrid system's battery cooling on T8 models and potential leaks in the optional air suspension. Electrical gremlins, mainly related to infotainment software glitches and occasional 12V battery premature failures, are also noted but are generally manageable. Overall, the V90 offers a robust and dependable ownership experience, with most potential issues being relatively minor or specific to optional equipment.
