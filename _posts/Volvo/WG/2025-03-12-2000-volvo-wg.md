The 2000 Volvo V70 wagon, often referred to by enthusiasts as a "WG" (though not an official Volvo designation), is a popular vehicle known for its safety, practicality, and comfortable ride. Engine options include a 2.4L inline-5, a 2.4L turbocharged inline-5, and a 2.3L turbocharged inline-5 (T5). The naturally aspirated 2.4L produces around 168 hp and 170 lb-ft of torque. The common transmission choice is a 5-speed automatic. Owners generally praise the V70's spacious cargo area, comfortable seats, and robust build quality. Common complaints include electrical issues, suspension wear, and some engine-related problems, particularly with higher mileage examples.

**Engine**

**2.4L Inline-5 (B5244S)**

* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks are common, often originating from the valve cover gasket, rear main seal, or oil pan gasket.  Visible oil drips on the ground or a burning oil smell may be present.
    * Solution: Replace the affected gasket or seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $250+ (depending on the location of the leak and labor involved).
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Crank Position Sensor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent stalling, difficulty starting, or a complete no-start condition.  Check engine light may illuminate with a corresponding fault code.
    * Solution: Replace the crank position sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.4L Turbocharged Inline-5 (B5244T)**

* **Turbocharger Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke from the exhaust, whining or screeching noises from the turbocharger area.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Boost Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced power, hissing sound during acceleration, poor fuel economy.
    * Solution: Inspect and replace any damaged vacuum hoses or intercooler pipes.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.3L Turbocharged Inline-5 (B5234T3/B5234T8 - T5)**

* **Electronic Throttle Module (ETM) Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Erratic idle, stalling, reduced engine power (limp mode).
    * Solution: Replace the ETM or have it rebuilt by a specialist. Original Magneti Marelli ETMs are prone to failure.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Turbocharger Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke from the exhaust, whining or screeching noises from the turbocharger area.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner AW50-42LE 5-Speed Automatic**

* **Slipping or Harsh Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Delayed or abrupt shifts between gears, especially between 2nd and 3rd gear. This is often associated with high mileage.
    * Solution: Fluid Flush (may provide temporary relief). Transmission rebuild or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (fluid flush) $2000 - $4000 (rebuild or replacement).
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Torque Converter Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibration at low speeds, especially when accelerating from a stop.
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Worn Control Arm Bushings**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises when driving over bumps, wandering steering, uneven tire wear. The front lower control arm bushings are particularly prone to wear.
    * Solution: Replace the worn control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per side.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Failed Strut Mounts**
    * Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or groaning noises when turning the steering wheel, especially at low speeds. Excessive play in the steering.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per side
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems such as malfunctioning power windows, door locks, wipers, or interior lights.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the CEM.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **ABS Module Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: ABS light illuminated, loss of ABS functionality.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the ABS module. Some modules can be rebuilt.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Lens Clouding/Yellowing**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced headlight brightness due to oxidation of the plastic lens.
    * Solution: Polish the headlight lenses or replace the headlight assemblies.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $30 - $100 (polishing) $200 - $500+ (replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Rear Wiper Motor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rear wiper not functioning.
    * Solution: Replace the rear wiper motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning blend door actuators, resulting in uneven heating or cooling.
    * Solution: Replace the blend door actuators.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Pixel Loss in Instrument Cluster**
    * Description/Symptoms: Digits or segments of the digital display in the instrument cluster become unreadable.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the instrument cluster.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2000 Volvo V70 is generally a reliable vehicle, but owners should be aware of potential issues, particularly with higher mileage examples. Common problems include engine oil leaks, turbocharger issues (on turbocharged models), transmission slipping, worn suspension components, and electrical glitches. While some repairs are relatively inexpensive, others, such as turbocharger or transmission replacement, can be costly. The severity and cost of these issues vary depending on the vehicle's condition and maintenance history.
