**Introduction:**

The 1999 Volvo WG is a luxury station wagon that offers a spacious interior and a range of engine and transmission options. It is powered by a 2.5L inline-5 engine with 190 horsepower and 170 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission. The WG received praise for its comfortable ride, elegant styling, and advanced safety features, but some owners have reported various issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**2.5L Inline-5 Engine**

- **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaks, rough idling, poor performance
  - Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
  - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

- **Clutch Master Cylinder Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, clutch pedal feels spongy
  - Solution: Replace clutch master cylinder
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $350
  - Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

- **Torque Converter Shudder:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Vibration or shuddering while driving at low speeds
  - Solution: Replace torque converter
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500
  - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **Front Control Arm Bushings Wear:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or rattling noises while driving, uneven tire wear
  - Solution: Replace front control arm bushings
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
  - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Headlight Switch Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Headlights do not turn on, flickering or intermittent headlights
  - Solution: Replace headlight switch
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
  - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Door Handle Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Stiff or broken door handles, difficulty opening doors
  - Solution: Replace door handle
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
  - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Climate Control Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning or heating system not working properly, fan making noise
  - Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace faulty components (e.g., compressor, fan motor)
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500
  - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 1999 Volvo WG has some notable issues, but most are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix. The most severe issue is the torque converter shudder in the automatic transmission, which can be costly to repair. Other issues, such as intake manifold gasket failure, clutch master cylinder failure, and electrical problems, are more common but less expensive to address.
