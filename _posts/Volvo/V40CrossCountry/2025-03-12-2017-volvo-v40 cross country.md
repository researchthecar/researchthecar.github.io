The 2017 Volvo V40 Cross Country is a stylish and practical hatchback offering a blend of comfort and capability. Engine options include a range of petrol and diesel engines. For example, the T5 petrol engine produces around 245 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. Many owners appreciate the V40 Cross Country's comfortable ride, stylish interior, and safety features. However, some criticisms include limited rear passenger space and a somewhat dated infotainment system.

**Engine**

**T3 Petrol Engine**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption may occur, requiring frequent top-ups between service intervals.
    *   Solution: Replacing the piston rings
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**T5 Petrol Engine**

*   **Ignition Coil Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idling, and reduced engine power. Often accompanied by a check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty ignition coils.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Diesel Engines (D2, D3, D4)**

*   **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: DPF clogging, leading to reduced engine performance, increased fuel consumption, and warning lights. Common in vehicles used primarily for short trips.
    *   Solution: Forced DPF regeneration (if possible), DPF cleaning, or DPF replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **EGR Valve Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, stalling, poor acceleration, and excessive smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: EGR valve cleaning or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Automatic Transmissions (Geartronic)**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, slipping between gears, and occasional complete loss of drive.
    *   Solution: Valve body replacement or rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2500+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibrating during acceleration, especially at lower speeds, and poor fuel economy.
    *   Solution: Torque converter replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive bouncing, poor handling, and uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Rear shock absorber replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Front Control Arm Bushing Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the front suspension, especially when going over bumps.
    *   Solution: Front control arm bushing replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, slow response times, Bluetooth connectivity issues, and random restarts.
    *   Solution: Software update, infotainment system reset, or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Car won't start, requires frequent jump starts. Can be due to a faulty component not shutting down properly.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of parasitic draw, replacement of faulty component, battery replacement
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Central Locking System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Doors failing to lock or unlock remotely, requiring manual operation.
    *   Solution: Check and replace faulty door lock actuators or wiring.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Fog Light Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing on the fog light lenses, likely due to thermal stress or impact.
    *   Solution: Fog light replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Seat Heater Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Seat heater not working, either on one side or completely.
    *   Solution: Seat heater element replacement or wiring repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof failing to open or close properly, or leaking water.
    *   Solution: Sunroof motor replacement, track repair, or seal replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Volvo V40 Cross Country presents a mix of common issues. Engine and transmission problems, particularly with oil consumption, DPF clogging, and valve body issues, can lead to significant repair costs. Electrical glitches and suspension wear are also fairly common. While some issues are relatively minor, others can be costly to fix, so a thorough inspection is recommended before purchasing a used model.
