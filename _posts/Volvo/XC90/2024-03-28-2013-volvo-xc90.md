**Introduction to the 2013 Volvo XC90**

The 2013 Volvo XC90 is a luxury SUV known for its spacious cabin, safety features, and optional third row of seating. It is available with two engine options: a 3.2-liter inline-6 and a 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6. Both engines are paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. However, the 3.2-liter inline-6 engine has a horsepower of 240 and 236 lb-ft of torque, while the 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 engine has a horsepower of 300 and 325 lb-ft of torque.

Owners praise the XC90 for its comfortable ride, high safety ratings, and versatile interior. However, some have encountered issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical components.

**Engine**

3.2-liter inline-6
* **Leaking oil pan gasket:** Oil leakage from the oil pan gasket can cause decreased engine oil levels and potential engine damage. Replacement of the gasket is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6
* **Turbocharger failure:** The turbocharger may fail, resulting in reduced engine power and whistling or squealing sounds. Turbocharger replacement is typically required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
* **Intake manifold runner control failure:** Issues with the intake manifold runner control can cause rough idling, stalling, and decreased fuel economy. Replacement of the intake manifold runner control is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+

**Transmission**
6-speed automatic transmission
* **Hesitation and slipping:** The transmission may hesitate or slip when shifting gears, especially during acceleration. Transmission fluid changes and/or repairs may be required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **Clunking noises:** Noises such as clunking or grinding may occur during gear changes. This may indicate worn or damaged gears or bearings, requiring transmission repairs or replacement. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+

**Suspension**

* **Front strut mount failure:** Premature failure of the front strut mounts can lead to excessive noise, vibration, and poor handling. Replacement of the strut mounts is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Rear suspension sagging:** The rear suspension may sag over time, affecting handling and ride comfort. Replacement of rear suspension components, such as springs or shocks, may be required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Electrical**

* **Electrical gremlins:** Electrical issues such as flickering lights, malfunctioning gauges, or battery drain may occur. Diagnosis and repair of electrical components, such as wiring or control modules, may be necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
* **Blown fuse for rear climate control:** A blown fuse for the rear climate control system can prevent the rear AC/heater from functioning. Fuse replacement is typically sufficient. Estimated Cost to Fix: $10 - $50
* **Navigation system freezing:** The navigation system may freeze or malfunction, requiring a software update or replacement of the navigation unit. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Exterior**

* **Roof dents:** Some owners have reported dents or deformations on the roof panel.
* **Paint peeling:** The paint on the vehicle's exterior may peel or chip prematurely.

**Interior**

* **Plastic interior trim cracking:** Plastic interior trim pieces, such as door panels or dashboard components, may crack or break. Replacement of the trim pieces is typically necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
* **Heater/AC fan motor failure:** The heater/AC fan motor may fail, resulting in no airflow or reduced airflow through the cabin. Replacement of the fan motor is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Seat belt retractor failure:** The seat belt retractor may fail, preventing the seat belt from retracting properly. Replacement of the seat belt retractor is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Summary**

The 2013 Volvo XC90 is a generally reliable vehicle, but it has experienced some notable issues, particularly with the engine, transmission, and suspension. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem encountered. Regular maintenance and proper care can help prevent or minimize these issues.
