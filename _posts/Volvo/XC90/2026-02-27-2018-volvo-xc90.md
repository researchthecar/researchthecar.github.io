# 2018 Volvo XC90 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Volvo XC90 offers striking Scandinavian styling, a luxurious cabin, and advanced safety features, making it a popular choice for families looking for a used midsize luxury SUV. However, prospective buyers should inspect the vehicle carefully for potential mechanical and electrical complications. While many owners enjoy a comfortable and refined ride, recurring issues documented by drivers point to specific areas that require attention before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine delivers a balance of efficiency and power for daily driving. It forms the base of the vehicle's powertrain lineup.

### 2.0L Supercharged and Turbocharged I4

Featuring both a supercharger and a turbocharger, this dual-charged 2.0-liter engine provides robust horsepower and responsiveness across the power band. Routine maintenance is key to long-term reliability.

## Transmission

### Plug-In Hybrid Powertrain

Combining a supercharged/turbocharged four-cylinder engine with an electric motor, this plug-in hybrid option offers enhanced fuel economy and electric driving range. Buyers should verify the health of the hybrid propulsion components.

**Electric Rear Axle Drive (ERAD) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience a sudden loss of motive power accompanied by loud whining noises from the rear axle.
- **Solution:** Requires replacement of the ERAD propulsion system and internal bearings.

### 8-Speed Automatic

This smooth-shifting 8-speed automatic transmission routes power efficiently to the front wheels or all four wheels. Regular fluid changes help maintain optimal shift quality and longevity.

## Brakes

### Brake System

The braking system provides firm stopping power under normal conditions, though owners have reported occasional booster and caliper failures requiring expensive repairs.

**Brake Pedal Failure and Seized Calipers**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may hear a hissing noise and experience a sudden loss of brake pedal firmness, or encounter seized rear brake calipers.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace worn brake boosters, master cylinders, or seized brake calipers as necessary.
- **Cost:** $3,500

## Exterior

### Door Latches

Interior mechanisms generally offer high-end comfort, but door latches and exterior handles require careful inspection for wear and moisture-related sticking.

**Door Latch and Handle Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Rear doors fail to open from either the inside or outside, or exterior door handles break off during normal use.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty door latch assemblies and updated window channel parts to prevent moisture ingress.

## Interior

### Sunroof and HVAC Drainage

Electrical architecture relies heavily on centralized modules that can be sensitive to moisture intrusion if drainage paths become clogged.

**Panoramic Sunroof Water Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Water leaks through the panoramic sunroof or rearview mirror housing, collecting in cargo areas or dripping onto control displays.
- **Solution:** Clear blocked drain tubes or repair seals to prevent water intrusion into sensitive electrical compartments.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V273000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC90&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Liquid or condensation on the rear seat control display may cause the front passenger seat to move back as far as possible. The rear passenger may become trapped by the front seat without the ability to cancel the seat's movement, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Volvo Car will notify owners, and dealers will update the rear seat control display software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V800000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC90&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the GPS location is not provided to emergency personnel it can inhibit emergency response in the event of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will correct the software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V827000: Seat Belts:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC90&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The second-row seat belt buckle and lower belt anchor bolts may not be tightened properly. An improperly tightened seat belt buckle may not restrain an occupant as intended, increasing their risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten the second-row seat belt buckle anchorage bolts, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Volvo XC90 is an appealing used luxury SUV that rewards owners with comfort, safety, and performance, but it demands diligent pre-purchase inspection. Buyers should pay close attention to potential hybrid propulsion system concerns, brake component wear, and sunroof drainage issues. Securing a comprehensive service history and having a qualified mechanic inspect the vehicle can help avoid unexpected repair expenses.
