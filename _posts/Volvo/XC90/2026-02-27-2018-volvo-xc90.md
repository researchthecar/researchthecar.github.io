# 2018 Volvo XC90 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Volvo XC90 offers a luxurious interior, striking Scandinavian design, and advanced safety features that make it a compelling choice in the midsize luxury SUV segment. While it generally provides a comfortable ride and strong safety credentials, used-car buyers should review potential powertrain options and owner-reported concerns carefully before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged 4-Cylinder (T6)

The T6 engine is a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine producing approximately 316 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. It offers brisk performance for daily driving, though buyers should verify proper maintenance history given its complex forced-induction setup.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder (T5)

The T5 engine is a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generating around 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. It serves as the baseline powertrain option, delivering adequate power and respectable efficiency for everyday commuting.

### 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Plug-In Hybrid (T8)

The T8 plug-in hybrid combines a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine with an electric motor to generate around 400 total horsepower. It provides strong acceleration and electric-only driving capability, but prospective buyers should inspect the hybrid battery health and charging systems.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

This 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with all available engines across front-wheel and all-wheel drive configurations. It delivers smooth, seamless shifting under normal driving conditions.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V273000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC90&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Liquid or condensation on the rear seat control display may cause the front passenger seat to move back as far as possible. The rear passenger may become trapped by the front seat without the ability to cancel the seat's movement, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Volvo Car will notify owners, and dealers will update the rear seat control display software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V800000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC90&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the GPS location is not provided to emergency personnel it can inhibit emergency response in the event of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will correct the software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V827000: Seat Belts:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC90&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The second-row seat belt buckle and lower belt anchor bolts may not be tightened properly. An improperly tightened seat belt buckle may not restrain an occupant as intended, increasing their risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten the second-row seat belt buckle anchorage bolts, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Volvo XC90 remains a stylish and comfortable luxury family SUV with impressive safety ratings and appealing powertrain choices. Used-car buyers should perform a thorough mechanical inspection, test all onboard technology features, and review service records to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
