The 2014 Volvo XC90 is a luxury SUV known for its safety, comfort, and spaciousness. Engine options include a 3.2-liter inline-6 producing 240 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 producing 300 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the XC90 for its comfortable ride and family-friendly features, but some have expressed concerns about reliability and repair costs.

**Engine**

**3.2L Inline-6**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from various engine seals and gaskets, including the valve cover gasket, rear main seal, and oil pan gasket. Symptoms include visible oil leaks, oil spots on the driveway, and a burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking gaskets and seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+, depending on the location of the leak and the labor involved.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Thermostat Housing Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The thermostat housing, often made of plastic, can crack or warp over time, leading to coolant leaks. Symptoms include a coolant leak near the thermostat housing and overheating.
    *   Solution: Replace the thermostat housing, typically with an updated aluminum part.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The turbocharger can fail due to oil starvation, wear, or component failure. Symptoms include loss of power, excessive smoke from the exhaust, and unusual noises from the turbocharger.
    *   Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Wastegate Actuator Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The wastegate actuator controls boost pressure. Problems with this actuator can cause inconsistent power delivery, overboost conditions, or underboost conditions.
    *   Solution: Replace or repair the wastegate actuator. Sometimes the entire turbocharger needs to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2000, depending on whether the actuator can be replaced separately or the entire turbocharger is needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner AW TF-80SC 6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The valve body, which controls the flow of transmission fluid, can experience wear and tear, leading to shifting problems, harsh shifts, and transmission slipping.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The torque converter can fail, causing shuddering, stalling, or complete loss of drive.
    *   Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1800
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Compressor Failure (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: If equipped with air suspension, the compressor can fail, leading to a sagging or uneven ride height.
    *   Solution: Replace the air suspension compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rear Suspension Bushing Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature wear of the rear suspension bushings which causes clunking or rattling noises from the rear of the vehicle, especially when driving over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replace worn bushings with updated, more durable bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sunroof Drain Clogging**
    * Description/Symptoms: The sunroof drain tubes can become clogged with debris, leading to water leaks inside the cabin, often soaking the headliner or floors.
    * Solution: Clean out the sunroof drain tubes, and ensure they are properly routed.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+; higher if there is electrical damage caused by the water.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Software Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The XC90's electronic systems can experience software glitches that affect various functions, such as the infotainment system, climate control, or driver assistance features.
    *   Solution: Software updates from the dealership.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $200+; usually covered under warranty when applicable, but can incur diagnostic fees.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Tail Light Bulb Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The tail light bulbs have a tendency to fail prematurely, triggering a warning message on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty bulb.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $10 - $50
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Power Seat Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The power seat motors can fail, preventing the seat from adjusting properly.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty seat motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2014 Volvo XC90, while appreciated for its comfort and family-friendly features, exhibits several common issues that potential buyers should be aware of. Engine issues like oil leaks and turbocharger problems can be costly to address. Transmission issues can cause driveability issues. Suspension, electrical, and interior issues also contribute to the ownership experience and potential repair expenses. While some issues are minor, others can be more severe and lead to substantial repair bills.
