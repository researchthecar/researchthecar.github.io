The 2010 Volvo XC90 is a mid-size luxury SUV known for its safety features, comfortable ride, and spacious interior. Engine options for the 2010 XC90 include a 3.2L inline-6 producing 235 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque, and a 4.4L V8 generating 311 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners often appreciate the XC90's family-friendly design, comfortable seating, and Volvo's reputation for safety. Common complaints include issues with electrical components, higher repair costs compared to some competitors, and somewhat dated technology compared to newer vehicles.

**Engine**

**3.2L Inline-6**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur from various locations including the valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket, and rear main seal. Symptoms include visible oil leaks, burning oil smell, and low oil level.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected gasket or seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $600+ depending on the location of the leak.

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks from the water pump, radiator, or hoses. Symptoms include low coolant level, overheating, and visible coolant leaks.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking component (water pump, radiator, or hoses).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ depending on the component.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**4.4L V8**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption with the 4.4L V8. Symptoms include needing to add oil frequently between oil changes, blue exhaust smoke, and low oil level warnings.
    * Solution: The cause of excessive oil consumption can vary. Ring issues, valve stem seals, or PCV valve problems may be the cause. Repairs can range from replacing the PCV valve to a more extensive engine rebuild.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $4,000+ depending on the cause and repair needed.

*   **Thermostat Housing Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: The thermostat housing can develop leaks over time. Symptoms include coolant leaks near the thermostat housing, overheating, and low coolant level.
    * Solution: Replacing the thermostat housing.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

*   **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Alternators can fail prematurely. Symptoms include a dead battery, dimming lights, and electrical issues.
    * Solution: Replacing the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$600+

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic (Both Engines)**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, slipping between gears, or failure to shift. These issues are often caused by wear or failure within the valve body.
    *   Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering, stalling, or poor acceleration.
    *   Solution: Replacing the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Issues (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The rear air suspension (if equipped) can experience leaks in the air springs or compressor failure. This can lead to the vehicle sitting unevenly, a rough ride, or the suspension failing to adjust.
    *   Solution: Replacing the air springs or air suspension compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ per air spring, $500-$1000+ for compressor.

*   **Strut and Shock Absorber Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn struts and shock absorbers can lead to a bouncy ride, poor handling, and increased stopping distance.
    * Solution: Replacing the struts and shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400-$1000+

**Electrical**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof motor failure, leaks, or difficulty opening and closing.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the sunroof motor, cleaning drain passages, or replacing seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+ depending on the issue.
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Climate Control Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Problems with the climate control system, including failure to blow hot or cold air, or inconsistent temperature control. These issues may be caused by a faulty blend door actuator or a failing climate control module.
    *   Solution: Replacing the blend door actuator or the climate control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
     *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Central Electronic Module (CEM) can be prone to failure, causing a range of electrical problems including issues with power windows, door locks, lighting, and the immobilizer system.
    *   Solution: Replacing or reprogramming the CEM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Tail Light Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup or bulb failure in the tail lights.
    * Solution: Replacing the tail light assembly or bulb socket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Interior**

*   **Power Seat Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The power seat motors can fail, preventing the seat from adjusting properly.
    *   Solution: Replacing the seat motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Center Console Screen Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen can fail.
    * Solution: Replacing the unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+

**Summary**

The 2010 Volvo XC90, while a comfortable and safe SUV, can be prone to a range of issues. Engine problems, such as oil leaks and consumption, can vary in severity and cost. Transmission issues, particularly with the valve body, can be expensive to address. Electrical problems, including sunroof, climate control, and CEM issues, are also common. Suspension problems, especially with the optional air suspension, can add to the maintenance burden. While not every XC90 will experience all of these problems, potential buyers should be aware of these common issues and budget accordingly for potential repairs.
