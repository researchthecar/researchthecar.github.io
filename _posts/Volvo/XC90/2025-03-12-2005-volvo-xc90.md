The 2005 Volvo XC90 is a mid-size luxury SUV known for its safety, comfort, and practicality. Available engines include a 2.5L turbocharged inline-5 (208 hp, 236 lb-ft torque), a 2.9L twin-turbocharged inline-6 (268 hp, 280 lb-ft torque), and a 4.4L V8 (311 hp, 325 lb-ft torque). All engines were paired with an Aisin Warner automatic transmission. Owners often appreciate the XC90's spacious interior, comfortable ride, and reputation for safety. Common complaints include higher maintenance costs compared to non-luxury SUVs, electrical issues, and some reliability concerns as the vehicles age.

**Engine**

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5**

*   **Coil Pack Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough running engine, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty coil pack(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150 per coil pack, plus labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **PCV System Clogging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whistling noise from engine, rough idle, oil leaks from various seals (rear main seal, camshaft seals), potentially blowing out the dipstick.
    *   Solution: Replace the PCV valve, breather box, and associated hoses.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+, potentially more if oil leaks have caused further damage.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.9L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6**

*   **Turbo Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke (blue or white) from the exhaust, whining noise from the turbochargers.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty turbocharger(s). It is recommended to replace both turbos at the same time.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+ per turbo, plus labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coil Pack Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough running engine, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty coil pack(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150 per coil pack, plus labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Throttle Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, rough idle, stalling, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Clean or replace the throttle body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**4.4L V8**

*   **Alternator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging, warning lights on the dashboard (battery, ABS, etc.), vehicle may stall. This engine is notorious for this issue.
    *   Solution: Replace the alternator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Valve Cover Gasket Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks around the valve covers, burning smell, visible oil on the engine.
    * Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Thermostat Housing Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaking, overheating, low coolant level, coolant leak is visible near the thermostat housing.
    *   Solution: Replace the thermostat housing assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner Automatic (All Engines)**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, slipping between gears, failure to shift properly, transmission warning light.
    *   Solution: Valve body replacement or rebuild, transmission flush.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Fluid Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Visible leaks under the vehicle, especially near the transmission, difficulty shifting, transmission slipping.
    *   Solution: Replace seals and gaskets, check transmission cooler lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibrating at certain speeds, especially under light acceleration, stalling.
    *   Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Control Arm Bushing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise from the front suspension, especially over bumps, uneven tire wear, vague steering.
    *   Solution: Replace the control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per side.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rear Self-Leveling Suspension (if equipped) - Air Springs/Shocks Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear suspension, ride height is lower than normal, compressor running constantly, "suspension service required" message.
    *   Solution: Replace the air springs or shocks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200+ per side.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Driver Information Module (DIM) / Instrument Cluster Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or complete failure of the instrument cluster display, warning lights flickering or staying on, inaccurate readings.
    *   Solution: DIM repair or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Anti-lock Braking System (ABS) Module Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: ABS light on, traction control light on, braking performance may be affected.
    *   Solution: ABS module repair or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Random electrical problems, such as interior lights not working, windows not operating, alarm system malfunctions.
    *   Solution: CEM repair or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Tail Light Assembly Cracking/Water Intrusion**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks in the tail light lens, water accumulating inside the tail light housing, bulb failure.
    *   Solution: Replace the tail light assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400 per tail light.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof not opening or closing properly, sticking, motor failure, water leaks.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the sunroof motor or assembly. Cleaning and lubricating the tracks can also help.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Climate Control Module Display Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The display on the climate control module is dim or unreadable.
    *   Solution: Climate control module repair or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2005 Volvo XC90, while a comfortable and safe SUV, is prone to several issues typical of vehicles of this age and price range. The severity and cost of these issues can vary greatly. Engine problems such as coil pack failure, turbocharger issues (especially on the 2.9L), and alternator failure (on the V8) can be expensive. Transmission problems, particularly valve body issues, are also a significant concern. Electrical issues, such as those related to the DIM, ABS module, and CEM, are also common and can be difficult to diagnose. Suspension and interior issues add to the potential maintenance costs. Overall, prospective buyers should carefully inspect any 2005 XC90 and be prepared for potential repairs.
