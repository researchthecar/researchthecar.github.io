The 2004 Volvo XC90 was Volvo's first entry into the SUV market, offering a blend of safety, practicality, and Swedish design. Engine options for this year include a 2.5L turbocharged inline-5 (208 hp, 236 lb-ft torque), a 2.9L twin-turbocharged inline-6 (268 hp, 280 lb-ft torque), and a 4.4L V8 (311 hp, 325 lb-ft torque), all paired with an Aisin Warner automatic transmission. Owners often appreciate the XC90's comfortable ride, spacious interior, and Volvo's reputation for safety. Common complaints revolve around the vehicle's reliability, particularly with electrical systems and various engine components, as well as the cost of repairs.

**Engine**

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5**
*   **Coil Pack Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idling, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty coil pack.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **PCV System Clogging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whistling noise from the engine, rough idle, oil leaks, and a check engine light. This issue is exacerbated by Volvo's extended oil change intervals that they recommended when new.
    *   Solution: Replacing the PCV valve and cleaning the system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whistling or whining noise from the turbo, excessive smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Replacing the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.9L Twin-Turbocharged Inline-6**
*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whistling or whining noise from the turbo, excessive smoke from the exhaust. Since it is a twin turbo engine, both turbos need to be inspected.
    *   Solution: Replacing the turbocharger(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $5000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Throttle Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic idling, stalling, and hesitation upon acceleration.
    *   Solution: Cleaning or replacing the throttle body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**4.4L V8**
*   **Alternator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, battery warning light, difficulty starting the engine.
    *   Solution: Replacing the alternator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Balance Shaft Bearing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loud knocking noise from the engine, especially at idle. This is a serious issue that can lead to catastrophic engine damage.
    *   Solution: Engine removal and repair/replacement of the balance shaft and bearings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3000 - $7000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner Automatic Transmission (All Engines)**
*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, slipping between gears, erratic shifting behavior, and a "transmission service required" message on the dash.
    *   Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibrations at specific speeds, especially during acceleration. Can also cause transmission overheating.
    *   Solution: Replacing the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Lower Control Arm Bushing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise from the front suspension, especially when going over bumps. Poor handling and tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replacing the lower control arms or just the bushings if possible.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per side
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rear Suspension Airbag Failure (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sagging in the rear, the air compressor running constantly, and a "chassis settings service required" message.
    *   Solution: Replacing the air springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Driver Information Module (DIM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or complete failure of the instrument cluster. Can include loss of speedometer, tachometer, and other gauges.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the DIM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems affecting various systems, such as the headlights, wipers, power windows, and door locks. Can also cause starting problems.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the CEM. This often requires reprogramming.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Tail Light Assembly Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bulb sockets melting and causing lights to flicker or fail.
    *   Solution: Replacing the tail light assembly or repairing the bulb sockets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+ per side
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Drain Clogging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin, especially in the headliner and footwells. Can lead to mold and mildew.
    *   Solution: Cleaning the sunroof drains.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ (can be higher if water damage is extensive)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2004 Volvo XC90, while offering comfort and safety, is prone to various issues that can lead to significant repair costs. Engine problems, especially with the 2.9L turbo and the 4.4L V8, can be expensive to address. Transmission issues, electrical gremlins, and suspension problems further contribute to the overall cost of ownership. While some issues are relatively minor, others, like the balance shaft failure in the V8 engine, can be catastrophic. Potential buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle's maintenance history and consider a pre-purchase inspection to identify any existing or potential problems.
