# 2020 Volvo XC90 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Volvo XC90 offers a luxurious cabin, advanced safety features, and a range of turbocharged and supercharged powertrain options. While many owners enjoy its comfort and styling, used car buyers should carefully inspect electrical systems, door latches, and sunroof drainage to avoid costly surprises.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged I4 (T6)

The T6 powertrain uses a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine producing around 316 horsepower, offering brisk acceleration paired with smooth delivery. It generally provides strong performance, though proper maintenance is critical to avoid longevity concerns.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may suddenly lose power, hesitate, or drop to a low restricted speed while driving.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose the fuel delivery, throttle body, or engine management software.

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4 (T5)

The entry-level T5 option features a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine making roughly 250 horsepower. It provides balanced efficiency and adequate daily driving power for most users.

### 2.0L Turbocharged/Supercharged Plug-In Hybrid (T8)

The T8 Recharge plug-in hybrid pairs the 2.0L forced-induction engine with electric drive motors to push out a combined 400+ horsepower. It delivers swift performance and electric-only driving range, though hybrid electrical systems require thorough inspection.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic

An 8-speed automatic transmission comes standard across the XC90 lineup, seamlessly routing power to either the front wheels or the all-wheel-drive system. It is generally smooth-shifting and reliable when properly maintained.

## Exterior

### Door Latch

Interior door mechanisms can occasionally experience mechanical sticking or latch failure, requiring hardware replacement.

**Door Latch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rear doors may fail to latch properly when closing or refuse to open from either the inside or outside.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace faulty door latch assemblies or locking mechanisms as needed.

## Interior

### Sunroof and Drainage

Electrical and body accessory components are generally robust, but sunroof drainage issues can cause significant water damage if left unaddressed.

**Sunroof Water Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Water may pool inside the cabin or leak through the headliner during heavy rain or car washes, potentially affecting electronics.
- **Solution:** Clear blocked drain tubes or reseal the sunroof assembly to prevent interior water intrusion.
- **Cost:** $2,500 - $13,000

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V144000: Forward Collision Avoidance: Automatic Emergency Braking](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC90&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Due to a software/hardware incompatibility, the Automatic Emergency Brake (AEB) system may not detect obstacles and engage as intended. If the automatic emergency braking system does not engage as intended, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volvo will notify owners, and dealers will update the Active Safety Domain Master (ASDM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V716000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC90&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Separation of the rotor and shaft may cause a reduction in braking assistance and a loss of the Antilock Brake System (ABS) and Electronic Stability Control (ESC), increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the hydraulic unit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V414000: Electrical System:Wiring:Fuses And Circuit Breakers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC90&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Failure of the low-pressure fuel pump can cause a stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuse, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V273000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC90&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Liquid or condensation on the rear seat control display may cause the front passenger seat to move back as far as possible. The rear passenger may become trapped by the front seat without the ability to cancel the seat's movement, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Volvo Car will notify owners, and dealers will update the rear seat control display software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V001000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC90&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A screw inside the gearbox housing can cause the steering wheel to lock up and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the steering gear, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V827000: Seat Belts:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC90&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The second-row seat belt buckle and lower belt anchor bolts may not be tightened properly. An improperly tightened seat belt buckle may not restrain an occupant as intended, increasing their risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten the second-row seat belt buckle anchorage bolts, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V179000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC90&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The high-voltage battery may experience a short circuit within the battery module when the battery is fully charged and the vehicle is parked. A short circuit in a high-voltage battery increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the high-voltage battery module as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Volvo XC90 is a sophisticated and comfortable midsize luxury SUV, but prospective buyers should prioritize a pre-purchase inspection. Check the panoramic sunroof for any signs of past leaks, test all doors and latches thoroughly, and ensure the powertrain runs smoothly without hesitation.
