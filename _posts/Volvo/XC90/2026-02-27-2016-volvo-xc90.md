# 2016 Volvo XC90 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Volvo XC90 introduced a complete redesign featuring sleek Scandinavian styling, an advanced portrait touchscreen infotainment system, and efficient four-cylinder powertrain options. While praised for its luxurious interior and top-tier safety scores, used-car buyers should inspect prospective vehicles carefully. Early model-year production models have documented owner complaints regarding engine cooling leaks, electrical glitches, and rear door latch failures.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged I4

The standard 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine delivers ample power for daily driving while maximizing fuel efficiency, though early versions warrant careful pre-purchase inspection.

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant warning messages, visible fluid puddles under the vehicle, or engine overheating risks due to failing hoses or cooling components.
- **Solution:** Inspect coolant hoses and connections, and replace any compromised lines or leaking components promptly.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden reduction in vehicle speed, engine hesitation, or unexpected stalling while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Diagnose engine management codes and inspect internal components such as piston rings or fuel delivery systems.

## Transmission

### Plug-In Hybrid Powertrain (T8)

The T8 Twin Engine plug-in hybrid combines forced induction with an electric motor for strong performance and eco-friendly capability, but complex hybrid electronics require attentive maintenance.

**Electric Rear Axle Drive (eRAD) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden illumination of hybrid system warning lights and loss of electric propulsion functionality.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician or dealership inspect the hybrid drive unit and replace faulty electronic or mechanical components.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The eight-speed automatic transmission shifts smoothly across all driving modes and pairs well with the four-cylinder lineup, showing relatively few widespread mechanical complaints.

## Electrical

### Body Electrical and Infotainment

Advanced electronics power the vehicle's safety features and touchscreen interface, but water leaks from sunroof drains can occasionally disrupt electrical components.

**Water Intrusion and Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** Water leaking into the cabin during heavy rain, triggering random electrical warning lights and accessory malfunctions.
- **Solution:** Clear blocked sunroof drains or repair compromised weather seals to prevent moisture from reaching sensitive electronic modules.

## Exterior

### Door Latches and Locks

The cabin offers luxurious materials and thoughtful ergonomics, though owners have reported frustrating mechanical failures with rear door latches and power locking mechanisms.

**Door Latch Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rear doors failing to latch properly when closing, swinging open while driving, or getting permanently stuck shut.
- **Solution:** Replace the malfunctioning door latch actuator assembly.

## Safety

### Seat Belts and Airbags

Equipped with comprehensive active and passive safety systems, the vehicle generally provides excellent protection, though interior wiring harnesses can occasionally develop faults.

**Seat Harness Wiring Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Airbag warning lights illuminating on the dashboard due to misassembled or faulty wiring harnesses in the seat assembly.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the affected seat wiring harness to ensure proper airbag deployment readiness.

**[Recall 19V308000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Cooling System:Hoses/Lines/Piping/Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC90&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Exposure to heat and humidity may cause the engine coolant bleeder hose to crack, resulting in a coolant leak. If the coolant leak goes unnoticed, coolant may accumulate in the catalytic converter heat shield insulation and catch fire when the vehicle is stationary, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Volvo Cars will notify owners, and dealers will replace the engine side coolant bleeder hose, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V273000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC90&modelYear=2016)**

- **What to do:** Volvo Car will notify owners, and dealers will update the rear seat control display software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Volvo XC90 remains a compelling choice for used luxury SUV buyers thanks to its striking design, high-end interior, and stellar safety credentials. However, because it was the debut year for a complete generation redesign, it carries a higher incidence of growing pains. Buyers should prioritize finding a model with a comprehensive service history, verify that all open campaigns have been addressed, and perform a thorough check of the cooling system, door latches, and electronics before purchasing.
