The 2021 Volvo XC90 is a luxury SUV known for its elegant design, comfortable ride, and advanced safety features. Engine options include the T5, a turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine producing 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque; the T6, a turbocharged and supercharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine producing 316 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque; and the T8 eAWD plug-in hybrid, which combines the T6 engine with an electric motor for a total output of 400 horsepower and 472 lb-ft of torque. All engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally praise the XC90 for its stylish interior, comfortable seats, and long list of standard features. Common complaints include the complexity of the infotainment system, occasional software glitches, and higher than average repair costs.

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (All Engines)**
* **Harsh Shifting/Jerking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough or abrupt gear changes, especially noticeable at low speeds or during acceleration. Can be accompanied by a clunking noise.
    * Solution: Software update to the transmission control module (TCM). If the issue persists, valve body replacement or, in severe cases, full transmission replacement may be required.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
* **Transmission Slipping**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine RPMs increase without corresponding increase in vehicle speed. Hesitation during acceleration or difficulty maintaining speed on inclines.
    * Solution: Diagnostic check for error codes and fluid level inspection. Potential solutions include transmission fluid flush, valve body replacement, or complete transmission replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $7000+

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues (Sensus)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: System freezes, slow response times, screen blackouts, Bluetooth connectivity problems, navigation errors, and failure to save settings.
    *   Solution: Software updates from Volvo. In some cases, a system reset or replacement of the infotainment unit is necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,500+ (unit replacement)
*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: False alarms, non-functional sensors, error messages displayed on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Inspection and cleaning of sensors. Replacement of faulty sensors. Recalibration of the system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+ per sensor.
*   **Automatic Emergency Braking System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erroneous braking events when no obstacle is present, failure to activate when a collision is imminent.
    *   Solution: Recalibration of the system's sensors and cameras. Software updates. Replacement of faulty components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof fails to open or close properly, makes excessive noise during operation, leaks water.
    *   Solution: Inspection of the sunroof mechanism for debris or damage. Lubrication of moving parts. Replacement of damaged components, such as the motor or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
*   **Automatic Tailgate Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Tailgate fails to open or close, gets stuck mid-operation, beeps continuously, or gives an error message.
    *   Solution: Inspection and lubrication of hinges and struts. Replacement of faulty sensors, motors, or struts. Recalibration of the system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $700+

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing on the dashboard surface, often near the windshield or around air vents.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the entire dashboard.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
*   **Seat Motor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The power seats don't adjust properly or at all due to a bad motor.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the seat motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500+

**Summary**

The 2021 Volvo XC90 generally offers a luxurious and comfortable driving experience, but owners have reported issues ranging from minor software glitches to more significant problems with the transmission and electrical systems. The most concerning issues involve the automatic transmission and the potential for significant repairs. While some problems can be resolved with software updates or relatively inexpensive component replacements, others, such as transmission or dashboard replacements, can be costly. Potential buyers should be aware of these common issues and thoroughly inspect the vehicle's maintenance history before making a purchase.
