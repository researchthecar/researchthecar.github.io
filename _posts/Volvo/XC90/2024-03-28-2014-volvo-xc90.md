### Introduction to the 2014 Volvo XC90

The 2014 Volvo XC90 is a mid-size luxury SUV known for its safety features, spacious interior, and sleek design. It is powered by a 3.2-liter 240-horsepower engine and a six-speed automatic transmission. Other engine and transmission options include a 2.5-liter turbocharged 250-horsepower engine with a six-speed automatic transmission and a 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged 300-horsepower engine with an eight-speed automatic transmission. The XC90 received high praise for its comfortable ride, luxurious interior, and numerous safety features. However, it was criticized for its sluggish acceleration and high fuel consumption.

**Engine**

- 3.2-liter 240-horsepower engine with a six-speed automatic transmission

- **Engine Oil Consumption Issue**
  - Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption requiring frequent top-offs.
  - Solution: Replacement of piston rings and valve seals.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $3,500+
  - Recalls: Safety Recall 15V917 - Valve Spring Failure

- **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise on startup, loss of power, and potential engine damage.
  - Solution: Replacement of the timing chain tensioner.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
  - Recalls: Safety Recall 16V670 - Timing Chain Tensioner Failure

- 2.5-liter turbocharged 250-horsepower engine with a six-speed automatic transmission

- **Turbocharger Wastegate Actuator Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, hesitation during acceleration, and increased fuel consumption.
  - Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger wastegate actuator.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
  - Recalls: None Found

- 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged 300-horsepower engine with an eight-speed automatic transmission

- **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

- Six-speed automatic transmission

- **Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, difficulty shifting gears, and transmission slipping.
  - Solution: Replacement of the TCM.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Valve Body Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, delayed shifting, and loss of gears.
  - Solution: Replacement of the valve body.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **Rear Air Suspension Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear suspension, uneven ride height, and reduced handling.
  - Solution: Replacement of the air suspension struts.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+ per strut
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Front Control Arm Bushing Wear**
  - Description/Symptoms: Knocking or clunking noises during turning, vibrations in the steering wheel, and reduced handling.
  - Solution: Replacement of the worn bushings.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ per side
  - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Battery Drain Issue**
  - Description/Symptoms: Battery draining prematurely, difficulty starting the vehicle, and electrical malfunctions.
  - Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the underlying cause, such as faulty door switches or control modules.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the cause
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Electrical Harness Failures**
  - Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical issues, such as flickering lights, malfunctioning modules, and loss of accessories.
  - Solution: Identification and replacement of the affected harnesses.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ per harness
  - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Roof Rail Trim Peeling**
  - Description/Symptoms: Peeling or chipping of the chrome trim on the roof rails.
  - Solution: Replacement of the affected trim.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ per rail
  - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Climate Control Malfunction**
  - Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning climate control system, including incorrect temperature regulation, fan speed issues, and air distribution problems.
  - Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the underlying cause, such as faulty sensors or actuators.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Sunroof Drain Plugs Clogging**
  - Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the interior from the sunroof during heavy rainfall.
  - Solution: Cleaning and unclogging the sunroof drain plugs.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2014 Volvo XC90 has a number of potential issues, ranging from minor inconveniences to major repairs. Engine oil consumption, timing chain tensioner failure, and rear air suspension failure are the most severe and costly issues. While the XC90 offers a luxurious and safe driving experience, potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems before making a purchase.
