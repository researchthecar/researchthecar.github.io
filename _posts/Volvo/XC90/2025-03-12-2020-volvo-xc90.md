The 2020 Volvo XC90 is a popular luxury SUV known for its stylish design, comfortable ride, and advanced safety features. However, like all vehicles, it has its share of common issues. Engine options for the 2020 XC90 include the T5 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 (250 hp, 258 lb-ft of torque), the T6 2.0L turbocharged and supercharged inline-4 (316 hp, 295 lb-ft of torque), and the T8 2.0L turbocharged and supercharged inline-4 plug-in hybrid (400 hp, 472 lb-ft of torque combined). All engines are paired with an Aisin-Warner 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the XC90 for its luxurious interior and comfortable seats. Common complaints include issues with the infotainment system, electrical glitches, and occasional drivetrain problems.

**Engine**

**T5 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**

No Issues Found

**T6 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. Some owners have reported needing to add a quart of oil every 1,000-2,000 miles.
    *   Solution: Volvo released an updated PCV system design. Replacement of the PCV valve and related components to address potential vacuum leaks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**T8 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4 Plug-in Hybrid**

*   **Battery Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced range, failure to charge, or complete battery failure.
    *   Solution: Software update to manage battery charging and discharging, or battery replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) to $8,000+ (battery replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin-Warner 8-Speed Automatic (All Engines)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifts, particularly between lower gears. Hesitation or jerking when accelerating from a stop.
    *   Solution: Software updates to transmission control module (TCM), transmission fluid flush, or valve body replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) to $2,000+ (valve body replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Issues (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sagging on one side, suspension malfunction warnings, compressor failure, or uneven ride height.
    *   Solution: Replacement of air struts, compressor, or ride height sensors. Checking for and repairing air leaks in the system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+ per strut, $500 - $1,000+ for compressor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues (Sensus)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: System freezes, slow response times, Bluetooth connectivity problems, navigation malfunctions, and failure to update.
    *   Solution: Software updates, system reset, or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update/reset) to $1,500+ (unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Faulty readings from sensors such as parking sensors, blind spot monitoring, and lane keeping assist.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the affected sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **"Pilot Assist" System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent performance, phantom braking, or disengagement of the Pilot Assist system while driving.
    *   Solution: Recalibration of the system's sensors and software.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (covered under warranty or software update) to $300+ (sensor recalibration)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof failing to open or close, water leaks around the sunroof seal.
    *   Solution: Replacement of sunroof motor, track repair, or resealing of the sunroof assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Power Tailgate Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Tailgate failing to open or close automatically, getting stuck in the open or closed position.
    *   Solution: Replacement of tailgate struts, motor, or latch mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2020 Volvo XC90 exhibits a range of potential issues, from relatively minor infotainment glitches to more significant concerns like excessive oil consumption and air suspension failures. The severity of these issues varies, with electrical problems and sunroof issues being less costly to fix compared to engine or suspension repairs. While some problems can be addressed with software updates, others may require more extensive and expensive component replacements. Potential buyers should be aware of these common complaints and thoroughly inspect the vehicle's history and condition before purchasing.
