The 2023 Volvo XC90 is a luxury SUV known for its stylish design, comfortable interior, and advanced safety features. Engine options for the 2023 XC90 include the B5 mild-hybrid, a 2.0-liter turbocharged and supercharged four-cylinder engine producing 247 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, and the T8 Recharge plug-in hybrid, which combines a similar 2.0-liter engine with an electric motor for a total output of 455 horsepower and 523 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the XC90's elegant cabin, spaciousness, and smooth ride. Common complaints revolve around the infotainment system's complexity and some reliability concerns.

**Electric Motor**

**T8 Recharge Plug-in Hybrid**
* **Battery degradation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced all-electric range compared to initial performance. Range may diminish faster than expected.
    * Solution: Battery replacement or individual module replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $10,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (All Engine Options)**
* **Harsh Shifting/Jerking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noticeable harshness or jerking during gear changes, particularly at low speeds. Hesitation or delay when shifting.
    * Solution: Software update/reprogramming of the transmission control module (TCM). In some cases, replacement of valve body or complete transmission replacement may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300 for software updates, $1,000 - $3,000+ for valve body replacement, $5,000 - $8,000+ for transmission replacement.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    * Description/Symptoms: The Sensus Connect infotainment system freezes, becomes unresponsive, or crashes frequently. This can affect navigation, audio, climate controls, and other vehicle functions.
    * Solution: Software update or replacement of the central control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300 for software updates, $800 - $1,500+ for module replacement.
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Parking sensors give false alerts or fail to detect objects. Blind spot monitoring system malfunctions, providing incorrect warnings or no warnings at all.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of faulty sensors. Calibration of the system after sensor replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per sensor, plus labor.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Issues (Leaks, Failure to Open/Close)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaks around the sunroof. Sunroof fails to open or close properly, or makes unusual noises during operation.
    * Solution: Cleaning and lubricating the sunroof tracks. Replacement of seals or complete sunroof assembly replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 for cleaning and lubrication, $500 - $2,000+ for seal or assembly replacement.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Automatic Tailgate Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The automatic tailgate fails to open or close, or only opens/closes partially. It may also make unusual noises during operation.
    * Solution: Replacement of tailgate struts or the tailgate motor assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2023 Volvo XC90, while praised for its comfort and features, does have some reported issues. Electrical problems with the infotainment system and sensors seem to be the most commonly reported issues, and can be inconvenient. Transmission issues can potentially be more severe, but are less common. Battery issues on the plug-in hybrid can be costly. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to potentially expensive repairs, so being aware of these potential problems is essential when considering the purchase of a used 2023 Volvo XC90.
