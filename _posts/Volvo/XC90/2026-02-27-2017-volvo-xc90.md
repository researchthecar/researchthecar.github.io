# 2017 Volvo XC90

### Overview
The 2017 Volvo XC90 is a luxury mid-size SUV known for its sophisticated design, advanced safety features, and comfortable interior. It offers a range of powerful yet efficient powertrains, primarily focusing on its innovative turbocharged and supercharged engines. Reliability for the 2017 model year is generally considered good, with owners reporting satisfaction with its build quality and long-term performance. However, like any vehicle, specific components can experience issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas and their associated repair costs for various configurations.

### 2.0L T6 (Turbocharged and Supercharged)
**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Engine overheating, visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, sweet smell from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking hoses, seals, or potentially the water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Turbocharger/Supercharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine, check engine light illumination.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger or supercharger unit.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** The central touchscreen display may freeze, become unresponsive to touch inputs, or reboot unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of the infotainment module.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to start, battery warning light illuminates, electrical components malfunction.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify parasitic draw from modules or accessories, followed by repair or replacement of faulty components.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Volvo XC90 demonstrates solid overall reliability, particularly with its T5 engine and standard transmission. The T6 engine, while offering more performance, has a higher likelihood of encountering issues such as coolant leaks and turbocharger problems, which can be costly to repair. Electrical systems, especially the infotainment unit, can sometimes exhibit software-related glitches like screen unresponsiveness or battery drain, requiring software updates or component replacement. Other areas like suspension, exterior, and interior components are generally robust. Proactive maintenance and addressing early signs of potential issues, especially with the T6 engine, are recommended for optimal long-term ownership.
