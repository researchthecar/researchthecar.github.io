# 2017 Volvo XC90 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Volvo XC90 blends striking Scandinavian design, a luxurious interior, and advanced safety features, making it a popular choice in the midsize luxury SUV segment. However, used car buyers should approach this model year with careful consideration. While it offers impressive tech and comfortable seating, owner feedback highlights certain mechanical and electrical pain points that require attention.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine utilizes forced induction to deliver competitive horsepower and torque for everyday driving. Buyers should carefully check service records for previous cooling system maintenance and watch for any signs of fluid leaks.

**Coolant Leaks and Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant levels, sweet smells, visible fluid under the hood, and potential engine overheating or fire risks.
- **Solution:** Inspect coolant hoses, clamps, and connections for degradation; replace leaking components immediately.

**Reduced Performance and Acceleration Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected sluggishness, warning lights on the instrument cluster, and limited vehicle acceleration.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes, inspect spark plugs, fuel injectors, and oxygen sensors, and update software as needed.

### 2.0L Turbocharged/Supercharged I4

This dual-charged variant combines a turbocharger and a supercharger to provide enhanced low-end throttle response and robust power delivery. Regular maintenance of the complex induction and cooling systems is vital for long-term reliability.

### 2.0L Turbocharged/Supercharged I4 Plug-In Hybrid

The plug-in hybrid powertrain pairs a forced-induction four-cylinder engine with an electric drive system for efficient, high-output performance. Prospective buyers should have both the traditional mechanical systems and the hybrid electronics thoroughly inspected.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic

The eight-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth, refined gear changes across various driving conditions. Routine fluid checks and timely service help maintain optimal shifting performance.

## Suspension

### Suspension and Air Springs

Suspension components generally provide a smooth, composed ride quality, but inspecting control arms and air suspension hardware during a pre-purchase evaluation is recommended.

**Control Arm Cracks and Air Suspension Dust Cover Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Creaking noises when turning, cracked front control arms, and dislodged rear air spring dust covers.
- **Solution:** Inspect control arms, bushings, and air suspension components for physical damage or loose fasteners; replace worn items.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brakes

The braking system provides adequate stopping power for a heavy luxury SUV, though frequent users of adaptive cruise control may experience faster brake component wear.

**Brake Assist Loss and Component Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Firm brake pedal feel, hissing noises from under the dashboard, extended stopping distances, and accelerated pad/rotor wear.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake booster vacuum or hydraulic pressure lines, check pad thickness, and replace worn brake components.

## Electrical

### Vehicle Connectivity and Infotainment

Electrical architecture controls many comfort and safety features, making thorough pre-purchase diagnostics of the infotainment and connectivity systems essential.

**VCM and Infotainment Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Rearview camera freezing, Vehicle Connectivity Module (VCM) communication loss, and persistent warning lights.
- **Solution:** Check system software versions, inspect module wiring and voltage, and replace malfunctioning control modules if necessary.

## Exterior

### Door Latches and Locks

The cabin features premium materials and advanced conveniences, but door latch mechanisms have been a notable trouble spot for some owners.

**Door Latch and Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Doors failing to latch securely in cold weather, unlocking unexpectedly while driving, or refusing to open from the inside or outside.
- **Solution:** Inspect door latches and lock actuators for corrosion or mechanical binding; replace faulty latch mechanisms.

- **What to do:** Volvo Car will notify owners, and dealers will update the rear seat control display software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V800000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC90&modelYear=2017)**

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Volvo XC90 offers an appealing mix of luxury, advanced technology, and high safety standards. However, potential buyers should carefully verify the vehicle's service history, paying particular attention to cooling system integrity, door latch operation, and electrical module functionality. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a qualified technician is strongly recommended before finalizing any purchase.
