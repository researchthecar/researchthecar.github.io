The 2013 Volvo XC90 is a mid-size luxury SUV known for its safety, comfort, and spacious interior. Engine options for the 2013 model year include a 3.2L Inline-6 producing 240 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 producing 300 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque in the T6 trim. All models came standard with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the XC90's comfortable ride, roomy cabin, and reputation for safety. Some common dislikes include its fuel economy, somewhat dated technology, and relatively high maintenance costs.

**Engine**

**3.2L Inline-6**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks may occur from various locations including the valve cover gasket, rear main seal, or oil filter housing. Symptoms include visible oil drips, burning oil smell, and low oil level.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking gasket or seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $800+ depending on the location of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **PCV Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve can fail, leading to excessive crankcase pressure. This can cause oil leaks, rough idling, and potential damage to seals.
    *   Solution: Replace the PCV valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6 (T6)**

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The turbocharger may experience issues such as boost leaks, failure, or excessive noise. Symptoms include reduced engine power, whistling or whining sounds, and potential engine damage.
    *   Solution: Inspect and repair or replace the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks (Turbo Related)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur around the turbocharger oil lines or seals, leading to a burning oil smell and low oil level.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking oil lines or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner AW55-50/51SN 6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The valve body, responsible for controlling fluid flow within the transmission, can experience wear and sticking valves. Symptoms include harsh shifting, delayed engagement, slipping, or failure to shift.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The torque converter can fail, leading to slipping, shuddering, or complete loss of drive.
    *   Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Compressor Failure (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: If the vehicle is equipped with air suspension, the compressor may fail, leading to a saggy or uneven ride height.
    *   Solution: Replace the air suspension compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Strut/Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Worn or leaking struts/shock absorbers can cause a bouncy ride, poor handling, and increased stopping distance.
    *   Solution: Replace the struts/shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sunroof Drain Clogs**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clogged sunroof drains can cause water leaks into the cabin, potentially damaging electrical components.
    *   Solution: Clear the sunroof drains.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ (depending on damage caused by the leak)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The CEM can experience failures, leading to various electrical problems such as issues with the headlights, windshield wipers, or power windows.
    *   Solution: Replace or reprogram the CEM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Power Tailgate Failure (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The power tailgate may fail to open or close properly, or make unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Replace the tailgate struts or motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Climate Control Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning climate control systems can result in inconsistent temperatures, failure to blow hot or cold air, or unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace the faulty components, such as the blend door actuator or climate control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2013 Volvo XC90 can be a comfortable and safe SUV, but potential buyers should be aware of common issues, particularly those related to oil leaks, transmission valve body failures, and electrical system glitches. The severity of these issues can range from minor inconveniences to more significant repairs costing several thousand dollars. Addressing these issues promptly can help maintain the vehicle's reliability and prevent further damage. Pre-purchase inspections and thorough maintenance records are highly recommended.
