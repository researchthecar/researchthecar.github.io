# 2005 Volvo XC90

### Overview
The 2005 Volvo XC90, a luxury mid-size SUV, is generally known for its safety features and comfortable ride. However, like many vehicles of its age, it has specific areas that have seen more frequent issues than others. This analysis focuses on common mechanical and electrical problems reported by owners and technicians, excluding routine maintenance. Understanding these potential pitfalls can help prospective buyers and current owners budget for and address necessary repairs, ensuring the longevity and reliability of their XC90.

---


## Engine

### 2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5 (B5254T2)
**PCV System Clogging**
- **Symptoms:** Whining noise from engine, oil leaks, rough idle, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Replace or clean the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) system and associated hoses.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, blue smoke from exhaust, whistling noise.
- **Solution:** Replace the turbocharger unit.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $2500


### 4.4L V8 (B8444S)
**Timing Belt Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden engine shutdown, no start, rattling noise from engine.
- **Solution:** Replace timing belt, tensioners, and idler pulleys. If failure occurred, engine internal damage may require extensive repairs.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2000 (preventative), $4000+ (if engine damage)

**Camshaft Seal Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Oil leaks around the cylinder heads, burning oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replace camshaft seals.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900


## Transmission

### AW55-50SN (5-Speed Automatic)
**Solenoid Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, transmission warning light, slipping gears.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty shift solenoids or the entire valve body.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200

**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering at low speeds, slipping, rough idle when in gear.
- **Solution:** Replace the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $1800


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Sway Bar Links and Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or rattling noises over bumps, especially at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Replace worn sway bar links and bushings.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

**Control Arm Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Uneven tire wear, vague steering feel, clunking noises.
- **Solution:** Replace worn control arm bushings.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Electrical

### CEM Module (Central Electronic Module)
**Water Intrusion/Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical failures, warning lights, non-functioning accessories (wipers, lights, power windows).
- **Solution:** Repair or replace the CEM module and address the source of water intrusion.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500


### ABS Module
**Failure**
- **Symptoms:** ABS warning light illuminated, loss of ABS function.
- **Solution:** Replace the ABS control module.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200


## Exterior

### Standard Body
**No Major Issues**
- **Symptoms:** No widespread issues reported.
- **Solution:** No widespread issues reported.
- **Cost:** No widespread issues reported.


## Interior

### Standard Cabin
**Sunroof Drain Clogs**
- **Symptoms:** Water leaks into the cabin, musty smell, potential damage to electronics.
- **Solution:** Clear clogged sunroof drains.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250

**HVAC Blower Motor Resistor**
- **Symptoms:** HVAC fan only works on highest setting or not at all.
- **Solution:** Replace the blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2005 Volvo XC90 offers a blend of safety and luxury, but potential buyers should be aware of common issues. The 2.5L turbo engine can experience PCV system clogs and turbo issues, while the V8 variant has risks associated with its timing belt. Automatic transmission solenoids and torque converters are known failure points. On the electrical side, the CEM module is susceptible to water damage, leading to various electrical gremlins. Suspension components like sway bar links and control arm bushings can wear out. Interior issues often involve sunroof drains and HVAC components. Despite these potential problems, many XC90s remain reliable with proper maintenance and timely repairs.
