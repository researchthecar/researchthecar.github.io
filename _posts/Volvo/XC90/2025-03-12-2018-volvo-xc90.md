The 2018 Volvo XC90 is a luxurious and spacious SUV known for its comfortable ride, stylish interior, and advanced safety features. Engine options include the T5 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 producing 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, the T6 2.0-liter supercharged and turbocharged inline-4 generating 316 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, and the T8 2.0-liter supercharged and turbocharged inline-4 plug-in hybrid producing 400 horsepower and 472 lb-ft of torque. All engine options are paired with an Aisin Warner 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the XC90's elegant design, comfortable seats, and user-friendly infotainment system. Common complaints include issues with the infotainment system's responsiveness, electrical glitches, and occasional issues with the air suspension on models equipped with it.

**Engine**

**T5 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**

*   **No Issues Found**

**T6 2.0L Supercharged and Turbocharged Inline-4**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. Some owners report needing to add a quart of oil every 1,000 miles or less.
    *   Solution: Replacement of piston rings and potentially other engine components depending on the extent of the damage.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $7,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**T8 2.0L Supercharged and Turbocharged Inline-4 Plug-in Hybrid**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner 8-Speed Automatic (All Engine Options)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report harsh or jerky shifting, particularly between lower gears. Hesitation when accelerating from a stop is also a reported symptom.
    *   Solution: Transmission software update/reprogramming, valve body replacement, or, in some cases, complete transmission replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (software update) - $6,000+ (transmission replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Failure (Optional Feature)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle sagging on one or more corners, failure to maintain ride height, "Suspension Failure" warning lights.
    *   Solution: Replacement of air struts, air compressor, or ride height sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+ per strut, $500 - $1,500+ for compressor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues (Sensus System)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Screen freezes, slow response times, system crashes, Bluetooth connectivity problems, navigation errors, and failure of certain app functions.
    *   Solution: Software updates, system resets, or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update if covered under warranty) - $1,500+ (infotainment unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain, especially when the vehicle is not in use for extended periods.
    * Solution: Diagnosing parasitic drain, replacing the battery, or updating software.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin, particularly around the headliner or A-pillars during heavy rain.
    *   Solution: Cleaning or replacing sunroof drain tubes.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Dashboard Cracking/Delamination**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracking or delamination of the dashboard surface, particularly in areas exposed to direct sunlight.
    *   Solution: Dashboard replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2018 Volvo XC90, while a desirable luxury SUV, does have its share of potential issues. The most concerning is the potential for engine oil consumption in the T6 engine, which can be costly to repair. Electrical issues with the infotainment system are also a common complaint, although software updates may resolve some of these problems. Air suspension failures, while not as common, can also lead to significant repair bills. Overall, prospective buyers should be aware of these potential issues and carefully inspect the vehicle's service history before making a purchase.
