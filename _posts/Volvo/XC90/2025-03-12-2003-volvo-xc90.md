The 2003 Volvo XC90 marked Volvo's entry into the SUV market. It was available with a few engine options, including a 2.5L Turbocharged inline-5 producing 208 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque, a 2.9L Turbocharged inline-6 generating 268 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, and later a 4.4L V8. All engines were paired with automatic transmissions. Owners often praised the XC90 for its safety, comfort, and spacious interior, but some common complaints included electrical issues, suspension problems, and transmission concerns, particularly in the earlier model years.

**Engine**

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5**

*   **Coil Pack Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idling, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty coil pack(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Vacuum Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic idle, poor fuel economy, check engine light. Often occurs at the vacuum tree or associated hoses.
    *   Solution: Replace vacuum lines or vacuum tree
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.9L Turbocharged Inline-6**

*   **Turbo Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whining noise from the turbocharger, excessive oil consumption, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks (Valve Cover, Rear Main Seal)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Visible oil leaks, burning oil smell, low oil level.
    *   Solution: Replace valve cover gasket or rear main seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Valve Cover: $200 - $500+, Rear Main Seal: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**4.4L V8**

*   No Issues Found

**Transmission**

**All Transmissions (Aisin Warner)**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, slipping between gears, transmission failure.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering, stalling, or abnormal noises, especially at lower speeds, or when accelerating from a stop.
    *   Solution: Replace torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Lower Control Arm Bushing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise from the front suspension, especially when going over bumps, poor handling, uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replace front lower control arms.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rear Subframe Bushing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noise from the rear, vague handling, rear end instability.
    *   Solution: Replace rear subframe bushings. This job can sometimes be labor intensive.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **DIM (Driver Information Module) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or complete loss of instrument cluster functionality, warning lights appearing or disappearing erratically.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the DIM. Some shops specialize in repairing these units.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Repair $200 - $500+, Replacement $500 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **ETM (Electronic Throttle Module) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power message, limp mode, rough idling, stalling. These ETMs have a known issue with the throttle position sensor.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the ETM. Some shops specialize in rebuilding these units with updated sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Repair $300 - $700+, Replacement $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Tailgate Strut Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The tailgate will not stay open or closes unexpectedly.
    *   Solution: Replace the tailgate struts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof not opening or closing properly, motor failure, or track damage.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the sunroof motor or track.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2003 Volvo XC90, while offering a comfortable and safe driving experience, is prone to certain issues common in vehicles of its age. Engine issues, particularly with the 2.5L and 2.9L engines, can range from relatively minor coil pack failures to more serious turbocharger problems. Transmission issues, notably valve body problems, can be costly to address. Suspension and electrical issues, such as control arm bushings and DIM failures, are also common. The severity and cost of repairs can vary significantly, but owners should be prepared for potentially expensive maintenance and repairs, particularly as the vehicle ages.
