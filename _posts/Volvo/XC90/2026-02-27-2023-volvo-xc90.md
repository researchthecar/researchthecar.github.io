# 2023 Volvo XC90 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Volvo XC90 represents a modern luxury midsize SUV blending Scandinavian design with electrified powertrain technology. As a relatively recent model year, it benefits from contemporary styling and advanced driver-assistance systems, though shoppers on the used market should carefully evaluate early owner feedback regarding complex onboard electronics and powertrain performance.

---

## Engine

### B4 Mild Hybrid 2.0L Turbocharged I4

The B4 mild hybrid powertrain features a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing approximately 247 horsepower, paired with a 48-volt electrical system. It offers smooth everyday efficiency and standard front-wheel drive or optional all-wheel drive, representing a balanced entry point for daily commuting.

### B5 Mild Hybrid 2.0L Turbocharged I4

The B5 utilizes a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generating around 295 horsepower, integrated with a mild-hybrid system. Standard with all-wheel drive, it delivers confident acceleration and respectable fuel economy for a three-row luxury vehicle.

### T8 Plug-In Hybrid 2.0L Turbocharged/Supercharged I4

The T8 plug-in hybrid pairs a supercharged and turbocharged 2.0-liter engine with an electric motor to output a combined 455 horsepower. It provides swift acceleration and electric-only driving range, though its intricate dual-charging and hybrid architecture warrants thorough pre-purchase inspection.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission handles gear changes across the XC90 lineup, paired with either mild-hybrid or plug-in hybrid setups. It generally delivers refined and responsive shifting tailored to maintain a luxury driving dynamic.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V793000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Engine Control Module (Ecu/Ecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC90&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The vehicle may lose drive power as the high voltage battery is depleted, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the ECM software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V012000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC90&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A diagnostic error in the Brake Control Module 2 (BCM2) may cause a loss of Antilock Brake System (ABS), Electronic Stability Control (ESC), and traction control. A reduction in brake support functions can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V188000: Electrical System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC90&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The false warning message could prevent the detection of an actual e-call system failure, which could delay an emergency response during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the telematics and connectivity antenna module (TCAM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V827000: Seat Belts:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC90&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The second-row seat belt buckle and lower belt anchor bolts may not be tightened properly. An improperly tightened seat belt buckle may not restrain an occupant as intended, increasing their risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten the second-row seat belt buckle anchorage bolts, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V282000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC90&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera image may not display when the vehicle is placed in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display reduces the driver's view of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V908000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volvo&model=XC90&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera image may not display when the vehicle is placed in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display reduces the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Volvo XC90 remains a stylish and technologically advanced choice for used luxury SUV buyers, offering a range of efficient mild-hybrid and robust plug-in hybrid powertrains. While owner complaints for this model year are minimal, prospective buyers should verify all software updates and ensure the vehicle's complex electronics function properly before finalizing a purchase.
