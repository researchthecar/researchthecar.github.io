The 2017 Volvo XC90 is a luxury SUV known for its stylish design, comfortable interior, and advanced safety features. Engine options included the T5 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (250 hp, 258 lb-ft torque), the T6 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4 (316 hp, 295 lb-ft torque), and the T8 2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Inline-4 Plug-in Hybrid (400 hp, 472 lb-ft torque combined). All engine options were paired with an Aisin 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the XC90's elegant interior and comfortable ride, but some have expressed concerns about the complexity of the infotainment system and potential reliability issues.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged (T5)**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners reported excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent oil top-offs between scheduled services. Blue smoke may be visible from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: The solution varies; some owners reported success with using a heavier-weight oil or addressing PCV valve issues. In more severe cases, piston ring replacement may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+

*   **Coolant Leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks from the thermostat housing or water pump.
    * Solution: Replacement of the faulty thermostat housing or water pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged (T6)**

*   **Supercharger Clutch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining or grinding noise from the engine, especially during acceleration. Loss of supercharger boost. Diagnostic trouble codes related to supercharger performance.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the supercharger clutch.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks from the thermostat housing or water pump.
    * Solution: Replacement of the faulty thermostat housing or water pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**2.0L Turbocharged and Supercharged Plug-in Hybrid (T8)**

*   **Engine Stalling**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine stalls intermittently, especially at low speeds or when transitioning between electric and gasoline power.
    *   Solution: Software update or replacement of faulty sensors (crankshaft position sensor, mass airflow sensor).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks from the thermostat housing or water pump.
    * Solution: Replacement of the faulty thermostat housing or water pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Transmission**

**Aisin 8-Speed Automatic (All Models)**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or jerky shifting between gears, particularly at low speeds. Hesitation or delay when shifting.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush and software update. If the issue persists, valve body replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Complete transmission failure. Complete loss of power.
    *   Solution: Transmission replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $8,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Failure (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The rear of the vehicle sagging. Warning lights on the dash. Compressor not working.
    *   Solution: Replacement of faulty air struts, air compressor, or air lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues (Sensus)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: System freezes, crashes, or is unresponsive. Bluetooth connectivity problems. Navigation issues. Screen flickering.
    *   Solution: Software update, replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dead battery after short periods of inactivity. Difficulty starting the vehicle.
    *   Solution: Identify and repair parasitic drain (faulty module, short circuit). Battery replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sensor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Blind spot monitoring system not working, parking assist not working, etc.
    *   Solution: Replacement of sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the cabin from the sunroof area, especially during heavy rain.
    *   Solution: Cleaning the sunroof drain tubes, resealing the sunroof assembly, or replacing damaged sunroof components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Volvo XC90 has some notable issues that potential buyers should be aware of. Engine issues, particularly oil consumption and coolant leaks, can be costly to repair. Transmission problems, while less common, can also lead to significant expenses. Electrical issues with the infotainment system are frequent and can be frustrating. While the XC90 offers a luxurious driving experience, these potential problems should be considered when evaluating its overall reliability and cost of ownership.
