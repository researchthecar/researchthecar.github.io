The 2011 Volvo XC90 is a mid-size luxury SUV known for its safety, comfort, and spaciousness. Engine options include a 3.2L inline-6 producing 240 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque. Another engine option is a 4.4L V8 producing 311 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque. A common like about the vehicle is its comfortable ride, passenger space, and safety ratings. Common dislikes are its fuel economy, somewhat dated interior, and repair costs. Both engines were paired with an Aisin Warner 6-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**3.2L Inline-6**
*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks may occur from various locations, including the valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket, and rear main seal. Symptoms include visible oil leaks under the vehicle, burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gasket or seal is the common solution.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $800+ depending on the location of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **PCV System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) system can become clogged or malfunction, leading to increased crankcase pressure, oil leaks, rough idling, and potential damage to seals and gaskets.
    *   Solution: Replacing the PCV valve and cleaning the PCV system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    *   Recalls: None Found

**4.4L V8**

*   **Valve Cover Gasket Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can develop from the valve cover gaskets, leading to oil dripping onto the exhaust manifold and causing a burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Replacing the valve cover gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Thermostat Housing Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The thermostat housing is prone to leaks, causing coolant loss and potential overheating.
    *   Solution: Replacing the thermostat housing and gasket.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin Warner 6-speed Automatic**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The valve body can experience wear and tear, leading to rough or delayed shifting, slipping between gears, or failure to shift.
    *   Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The torque converter can fail, leading to shuddering, vibrations, or complete transmission failure.
    *   Solution: Replacing the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Air Suspension Problems (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicles equipped with rear air suspension can experience issues with the air springs leaking or the compressor failing. Symptoms include a sagging rear end, uneven ride height, and the air suspension warning light illuminating.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking air spring(s) or the faulty air compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+ per air spring or compressor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Worn Control Arm Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The front and rear control arm bushings can wear out over time, leading to clunking noises, vague steering, and uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600 per control arm.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sunroof Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clogged sunroof drains can cause water to leak into the cabin, leading to damp carpets and potential electrical issues.
    *   Solution: Cleaning the sunroof drains.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Tail Light Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bulbs frequently burn out and electrical connection issues.
    *   Solution: Replacing bulbs or fixing the electrical connection.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $20 - $100
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust on Rear Hatch**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicles in colder climates with road salt can experience rust forming on the rear hatch, particularly around the license plate area and near the bottom edge.
    *   Solution: Sanding, rust treatment, and repainting the affected area.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+ depending on the severity of the rust.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Power Seat Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The power seat motors can fail, causing the seats to become stuck in one position or only move intermittently.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty seat motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per seat motor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Center Console Cup Holder Breakage**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The center console cup holder is prone to breaking.
    *   Solution: Replacing the cup holder assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2011 Volvo XC90 is a comfortable and safe SUV, but owners have reported various issues, from oil leaks and transmission problems to suspension and electrical issues. The severity ranges from minor inconveniences like bulb failures to more significant repairs like valve body replacements or air suspension issues. Repair costs can vary widely depending on the issue and the chosen repair method. Potential buyers should be aware of these common problems and have the vehicle thoroughly inspected before purchasing.
