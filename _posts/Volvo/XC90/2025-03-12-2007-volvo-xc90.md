The 2007 Volvo XC90 is a popular SUV known for its safety, comfort, and practicality. Engine options include a 2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5 producing 208 hp and 236 lb-ft of torque, a 3.2L Inline-6 producing 235 hp and 236 lb-ft of torque, and a 4.4L V8 producing 311 hp and 325 lb-ft of torque. All engine options were paired with an Aisin AW55-50/51 five or six-speed automatic transmission depending on the engine. Owners appreciate the XC90's comfortable ride and spacious interior, but some common complaints include electrical issues, transmission problems, and higher repair costs.

**Engine**

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-5**

*   **Coil Pack Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough running engine, misfires, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty coil pack(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **PCV System Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whistling noise from the engine, rough idle, oil leaks, increased crankcase pressure.
    *   Solution: Replace the PCV valve or complete PCV system if heavily damaged.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.2L Inline-6**

*   **Timing Chain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise on startup, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace the timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**4.4L V8**

*   **Alternator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Battery light on, dimming headlights, difficulty starting, complete electrical failure.
    *   Solution: Replace the alternator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin AW55-50/51 (5 or 6-Speed Automatic)**

*   **Valve Body Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, slipping between gears, failure to shift, transmission error messages.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering at low speeds, stalling, poor acceleration, transmission overheating.
    *   Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Lower Control Arm Bushing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise from the front suspension, especially when going over bumps, wandering steering.
    *   Solution: Replace the front lower control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per side
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rear Air Suspension Compressor Failure (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rear of vehicle sagging, compressor not running, suspension error messages.
    *   Solution: Replace the air suspension compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Driver Information Module (DIM) / Instrument Cluster Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or complete loss of display, warning lights flickering, speedometer/tachometer malfunction.
    *   Solution: Replace or repair the instrument cluster.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Central Electronic Module (CEM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various electrical malfunctions including issues with power windows, locks, lights, and starting problems.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace the CEM.  This often requires dealer programming.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Tail Light Assembly Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks in the plastic of the tail light assembly, allowing moisture to enter.
    *   Solution: Replace the tail light assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400 per side
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Drain Clogging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin, wet headliner, musty smell.
    *   Solution: Clean the sunroof drains.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Seat Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Seat not moving or adjusting properly.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty seat motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2007 Volvo XC90, while generally a reliable and safe vehicle, has some common issues to be aware of. Engine problems, particularly with the timing chain on the 3.2L and alternator on the 4.4L can be expensive. Transmission issues are also a concern across all engine options. Electrical problems, particularly with the DIM and CEM, can cause a variety of frustrating issues. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to major repairs, with repair costs potentially reaching several thousand dollars. Careful inspection and maintenance are crucial for extending the life of a 2007 XC90.
