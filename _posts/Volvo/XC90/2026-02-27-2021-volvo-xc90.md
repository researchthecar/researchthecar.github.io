# 2021 Volvo XC90

### Overview
The 2021 Volvo XC90 is a luxury mid-size SUV known for its safety, comfort, and sophisticated design. It offers a range of powertrains, including turbocharged and supercharged gasoline engines, as well as a plug-in hybrid option. Reliability data for the 2021 model year generally indicates a strong performance, with owners reporting satisfaction with its build quality and technological features. While no vehicle is entirely without potential issues, the XC90 tends to perform well in long-term reliability studies, especially when compared to its segment competitors. Common areas of concern, though infrequent, often relate to specific electronic modules or minor software glitches that are typically addressed through dealer updates. Overall, the 2021 XC90 is considered a dependable choice for families seeking a premium and safe driving experience.

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Volvo XC90 demonstrates good overall reliability, particularly in its core mechanical components like the engine and transmission across its T5 and T6 variants. The T8 plug-in hybrid variant has a noted potential issue with its hybrid battery cooling system, which can lead to performance degradation if not addressed. Electrical systems, especially the infotainment, may experience occasional software glitches requiring updates. The optional air suspension can develop leaks over time. However, these issues are not universally reported and often fall within expected ranges for a luxury vehicle with advanced technology. For owners seeking a safe and comfortable SUV, the 2021 XC90 remains a strong contender, with most potential issues being manageable and repairable.
