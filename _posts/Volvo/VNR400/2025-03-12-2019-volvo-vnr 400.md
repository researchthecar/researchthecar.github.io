The 2019 Volvo VNR 400 is a regional haul tractor designed for maneuverability and efficiency in shorter routes. Engine options for the 2019 VNR 400 include the Volvo D11, D13, and Cummins X15. The Volvo D13 engine, a popular choice, produces up to 500 horsepower and 1850 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options primarily include Volvo's I-Shift automated manual transmission. Many owners praise the VNR 400 for its comfortable cab, fuel efficiency, and maneuverability. Common complaints include electrical gremlins, the cost of Volvo-specific parts, and some reliability issues with the I-Shift transmission.

**Engine**

**Volvo D13**

* **Coolant Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaking from the EGR cooler. Often presents as a sweet smell and visible coolant loss.
    * Solution: Replace the EGR cooler.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Frequent DPF regenerations, reduced engine power, and DPF clogging. This can be caused by faulty sensors or poor fuel quality.
    * Solution: Replace the DPF sensor or clean the DPF and address any underlying issues such as faulty sensors or injectors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3000+ depending on the severity and the need to replace the DPF.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Cummins X15**

* **Injector Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, reduced power, and black smoke from the exhaust.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty injector(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2500+, depending on the number of injectors needing replacement.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Turbocharger Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke, and unusual noises from the turbocharger.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $4000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Volvo I-Shift**

* **Clutch Slipping/Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping during acceleration, difficulty shifting gears, and eventually complete clutch failure.
    * Solution: Replace the clutch assembly. This often includes replacing the flywheel as well.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2500 - $5000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Shift Controller Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, failure to shift, or incorrect gear selection. This can be caused by a faulty shift controller unit.
    * Solution: Replace the shift controller unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Transmission Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission fluid leaks, often from the seals or gaskets. Low transmission fluid can lead to shifting problems and damage.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking seals and gaskets.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+ depending on the location and severity of the leak.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Air Suspension Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle leans to one side, the suspension deflates overnight, or the air compressor runs constantly.
    * Solution: Locate and repair the air leak, often caused by cracked air lines, faulty air bags, or leaking leveling valves.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1500+ depending on the component that needs to be replaced.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Sensor Failures (various)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunction Indicator Light (MIL) illuminated, leading to error codes related to various sensors (e.g., NOx sensors, temperature sensors).
    * Solution: Replace the faulty sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor, including diagnostic time.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Wiring Harness Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, such as lights flickering, gauges not working, or starting problems. Often caused by chafed or corroded wiring.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the damaged section of the wiring harness.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2000+ depending on the extent of the damage and the labor required to access and repair the harness.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Battery Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting, especially in cold weather. Batteries not holding a charge.
    * Solution: Replace the batteries.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ for battery replacement and installation.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Headlights not working or dimming prematurely.
    * Solution: Replace the headlight bulbs or headlight assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **HVAC System Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning not blowing cold air, heater not blowing hot air, or inconsistent temperature control.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the HVAC system, which may involve replacing the compressor, condenser, blower motor, or other components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2000+ depending on the component and the complexity of the repair.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Volvo VNR 400 has some reported issues, ranging from minor electrical problems to more significant engine and transmission concerns. The severity and cost of these issues vary, with DPF problems, clutch replacements, and turbocharger failures being among the most expensive repairs. Electrical gremlins and air suspension leaks can also be recurring problems. While the VNR 400 is generally a well-regarded truck, potential buyers should be aware of these common issues and factor them into their purchasing decision.
