**Introduction**

The 2016 Mini Cooper Countryman is a subcompact crossover SUV from Mini, BMW's British subsidiary. It is available with three engine options: a turbocharged 1.5-liter inline-three producing 134 horsepower and 162 lb-ft of torque, a turbocharged 2.0-liter inline-four producing 189 horsepower and 206 lb-ft of torque, and a turbocharged 2.0-liter inline-four producing 228 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. The Countryman can be equipped with either a six-speed manual transmission or an eight-speed automatic transmission.

The Countryman is praised for its fun-to-drive nature, stylish design, and comfortable interior. However, it has also been criticized for its high price, lack of cargo space, and cramped rear seats.

**Engine**

**1.5-liter Inline-Three Turbocharged**

* **Timing chain failure:** This issue can occur due to a faulty timing chain tensioner. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, rough idling, and poor performance. The solution is to replace the timing chain and tensioner. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Turbocharger failure:** This issue can occur due to a faulty wastegate actuator or a failed turbocharger bearing. Symptoms include a loss of power, blue smoke from the exhaust, and a whistling noise from the engine. The solution is to replace the turbocharger. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $1,500+

**2.0-liter Inline-Four Turbocharged (189 horsepower)**

* **Fuel injector failure:** This issue can occur due to a faulty fuel injector. Symptoms include a rough idle, poor performance, and increased fuel consumption. The solution is to replace the faulty fuel injector. Estimated cost to fix: $300 - $500+

**2.0-liter Inline-Four Turbocharged (228 horsepower)**

* **Water pump failure:** This issue can occur due to a faulty water pump. Symptoms include overheating, coolant leaks, and a whining noise from the engine. The solution is to replace the water pump. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $800+

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch failure:** This issue can occur due to a worn clutch disc or pressure plate. Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, slipping gears, and a burning smell. The solution is to replace the clutch. Estimated cost to fix: $800 - $1,200+

**Eight-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission fluid leak:** This issue can occur due to a faulty seal or gasket. Symptoms include a decrease in transmission fluid level, slipping gears, and rough shifting. The solution is to replace the faulty seal or gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+
* **Torque converter failure:** This issue can occur due to a faulty torque converter. Symptoms include shuddering during acceleration, slipping gears, and a loss of power. The solution is to replace the torque converter. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

**Suspension**

* **Strut failure:** This issue can occur due to a worn strut mount or a damaged strut. Symptoms include a rough ride, poor handling, and a clunking noise from the suspension. The solution is to replace the strut. Estimated cost to fix: $600 - $1,000+ per strut
* **Control arm bushing failure:** This issue can occur due to worn or damaged control arm bushings. Symptoms include a clunking noise from the suspension, poor handling, and a rough ride. The solution is to replace the control arm bushings. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400+ per side

**Electrical**

* **Battery failure:** This issue can occur due to a faulty battery or a faulty alternator. Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle, dim headlights, and a dead battery. The solution is to replace the battery or alternator. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **Electrical short:** This issue can occur due to a damaged wire or a faulty electrical component. Symptoms can vary depending on the location of the short. The solution is to locate and repair the short. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Roof rack failure:** This issue can occur due to a faulty roof rack crossbar or a damaged roof rack mounting point. Symptoms include a loose or rattling roof rack, and damage to the roof of the vehicle. The solution is to replace the faulty roof rack crossbar or repair the damaged mounting point. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+
* **Headlight failure:** This issue can occur due to a faulty headlight bulb or a damaged headlight housing. Symptoms include dim or flickering headlights, and a lack of illumination. The solution is to replace the faulty headlight bulb or repair the damaged housing. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $500+

**Interior**

* **Sunroof failure:** This issue can occur due to a faulty sunroof motor or a damaged sunroof panel. Symptoms include a sunroof that does not open or close properly, and a leaking sunroof. The solution is to replace the faulty sunroof motor or repair the damaged panel. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Dashboard rattle:** This issue can occur due to a loose dashboard panel or a damaged clip. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the dashboard while driving. The solution is to locate and tighten the loose panel or replace the damaged clip. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $300+

**Summary**

The 2016 Mini Cooper Countryman has a number of potential issues, including engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior problems. The severity of these issues ranges from minor annoyances to major failures that could require costly repairs. Overall, the Countryman is a reliable vehicle, but it is important to be aware of the potential issues that can occur.
