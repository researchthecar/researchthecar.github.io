# 2021 Mini Cooper Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Mini Cooper brings cheerful styling and nimble handling to the compact segment, backed by modern BMW-derived engineering. For used-car buyers, this model year generally benefits from improved overall refinement compared to earlier generations. Reviewing available owner reports helps highlight minor trouble spots to keep an eye on during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged 3-Cylinder

This base 1.5-liter turbocharged three-cylinder engine produces approximately 134 horsepower and 162 lb-ft of torque. It offers peppy performance for daily commuting paired with strong fuel efficiency, though buyers should verify regular oil maintenance.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine delivers roughly 189 horsepower in standard S trims and higher outputs in John Cooper Works variants. It provides brisk acceleration and robust overall mechanical durability when properly maintained.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic

This 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers rapid gear changes and efficient highway cruising. Routine fluid checks ensure smooth operation over long-term ownership.

### 6-Speed Manual

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission caters to enthusiast drivers seeking direct engagement. It features a solid mechanical design with a clutch lifespan heavily dependent on driving habits.

### 8-Speed Steptronic Automatic

The 8-speed conventional automatic transmission is paired with specific high-output variants, offering refined and responsive shifting. It is generally robust and well-matched to the vehicle's power delivery.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V554000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mini&model=Cooper&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these assemblies fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." During a crash, a seat belt retractor that does not function as intended increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the seat belt retractors (including the seat belts) and, if necessary, replace the retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V283000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mini&model=Cooper&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Due to a problem with the rollover sensor within the air bag control unit, in the event of a rollover crash, the head air bag, seat belt pretensioners and other safety systems, may not activate. If the safety systems do not deploy as intended in a roll over crash, it may increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the air bag control unit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Mini Cooper represents a solid and entertaining choice for used car shoppers, combining distinctive character with modern mechanical refinement. Because formal owner complaint data for this model year is sparse, prospective buyers should still perform a thorough test drive and a professional pre-purchase inspection to verify overall mechanical and cosmetic health.
