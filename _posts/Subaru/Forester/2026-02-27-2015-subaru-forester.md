# 2015 Subaru Forester Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2015 Subaru Forester is a popular compact crossover praised for its standard all-wheel-drive capability, commanding road visibility, and highly functional cabin space. For used-car buyers, it offers a versatile layout that handles bad weather and family duties with equal confidence. While many owners report a trouble-free ownership experience, potential buyers should recognize that owner-reported complaints indicate specific reliability trends. Knowing what failure points to watch out for—such as transmission durability, electrical system quirks, and occupant detection sensors—will help you make a more secure and informed purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Boxer (FB25)

The standard power plant for the 2015 Forester, delivering approximately 170 horsepower. It is a generally durable engine for daily commuting but has a history of high oil consumption in some units, meaning owners must closely monitor oil levels.

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Low oil pressure warnings, engine shuddering, or complete engine seizure if oil levels drop critically low between service intervals.
- **Solution:** Diligent, regular monitoring of the engine oil dipstick. If severe damage has occurred, a complete engine replacement may be required.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Boxer (FA20DIT)

Found exclusively in the Forester XT trims, this turbocharged engine boosts performance with roughly 250 horsepower. It offers a much more spirited driving experience but requires strict adherence to premium fuel specifications and rigorous routine maintenance.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)

The primary automatic option on the Forester, optimized to extract maximum fuel economy. While smooth in normal conditions, it has been subject to high-cost failure complaints near or past the 80,000-to-100,000-mile mark.

**CVT Component Failure and Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** A loud, high-pitched whining or humming noise that increases with vehicle speed, followed by shuddering, hesitation, stalling upon stopping, or transmission fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Valve body replacement or a complete transmission replacement.
- **Cost:** Approximately $2,200 for a valve body replacement, and $7,000 to over $9,100 for a full transmission replacement.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

A traditional manual option available on base trims. It is generally reliable, but has limited market availability and isolated reports of internal structural fatigue.

**Clutch Fork Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Clutch pedal drops to the floor or becomes impossible to shift into gear.
- **Solution:** Replace the clutch fork assembly.

## Suspension

### Standard Suspension and Wheel Hubs

**Premature Rear Wheel Bearing Wear**
- **Symptoms:** An increasingly loud grinding, humming, or roaring noise from the rear wheels, especially prominent at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the rear wheel bearing and hub assembly.
- **Cost:** Typically ranges from $500 to over $1,000 to replace.

**Broken Rear Coil Springs**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking, metal-on-metal popping, or grinding sounds over bumps, along with rear-end sagging.
- **Solution:** Replace the rear coil springs and associated struts.
- **Cost:** Ranges from $700 to $800 to replace coil springs, or up to $1,390 if the rear struts must be replaced simultaneously.

**Weak Wheel Studs**
- **Symptoms:** Wheel studs easily snapping or shearing off when removing or tightening lug nuts during routine tire rotations.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged wheel studs and lug nuts.
- **Cost:** Dealers and auto shops may charge upwards of $200 per occurrence to replace broken studs.

**Cracked Lower Control Arm Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Vibration or shaking felt through the steering wheel at highway speeds; creaking or knocking during braking and cornering.
- **Solution:** Replace the front lower control arm bushings.
- **Cost:** Typically costs around $800 at a dealership.

## Brakes

### General / Multiple Configurations

Reliability observations that apply broadly across the 2015 Forester's shared engine and fuel delivery systems.

**Sudden Unintended Acceleration or Surging**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle suddenly surges or accelerates rapidly without pressure being applied to the gas pedal, requiring immediate and firm braking to regain control.
- **Solution:** Dealer diagnostic check of throttle and brake override systems, though owners report dealerships often struggle to replicate the issue.

**Premature Stalling and Inaccurate Fuel Gauge**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle suddenly loses motive power or stalls completely while driving, even though the fuel gauge indicates several bars (40 to 70 miles) of range remaining.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the fuel pump assembly or the fuel level sensor.

### Brake System and Warning Switches

**Brake Light Switch and Sensor Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** The brake light, traction control, and hill assist emergency lights illuminate on the dashboard, occasionally disabling system functions.
- **Solution:** Replace the brake light switch or the ABS hydraulic unit module.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

**Premature Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle's battery repeatedly loses charge when parked overnight, requiring jump-starts and premature battery replacements.
- **Solution:** Frequent battery replacement and diagnosing electrical accessories for parasitic draw.

**Smoking Side Mirrors and Lock Failures**
- **Symptoms:** A strong burning odor or visible smoke coming from the exterior side mirrors (typically after washing or heavy rain), followed by failure of the door lock actuators, dome lights, clock, and TPMS system.
- **Solution:** Repair short-circuited wiring and replace the affected mirror or power lock components.

**Intermittent or Inoperable Horn**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel horn fails to sound or functions only intermittently.
- **Solution:** Replace the steering wheel roll connector (clockspring) assembly.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Tailgate Hardware

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** A large crack develops across the windshield glass without any visible impact from a rock or debris, frequently originating near the top edge.
- **Solution:** Windshield replacement, which requires expensive recalibration of the vehicle's EyeSight driver assist cameras.
- **Cost:** Ranges from $800 to over $1,000 due to glass replacement and camera calibration.

**Tailgate Lift Support Failures**
- **Symptoms:** The rear hatch door fails to stay open, or the structural pin attaching the hydraulic strut to the tailgate snaps.
- **Solution:** Replace the hydraulic tailgate lift supports.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Occupant Detection System

**Occupant Detection System (ODS) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard SRS airbag warning light illuminates, and the passenger airbag indicator status remains permanently 'off' even when a passenger is seated in the front passenger seat.
- **Solution:** Replace the passenger seat sensor harness, occupant detection sensor mat, or the entire front passenger seat bottom cushion assembly. Note that many owners report this safety issue recurring even after having the original manufacturer recall repair performed.
- **Cost:** Replacing the wiring harness costs approximately $440, while replacing the entire seat sensor cushion assembly can run between $1,000 and $1,700.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V149000: Exterior Lighting:Brake Lights:Switch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Forester&modelYear=2015)**
- **Issue:** Exposure to certain contaminants may cause the brake light switch to malfunction, preventing the brake lights from illuminating and also preventing keyless ignition vehicles from starting and CVT/automatic transmissions from being able to be shifted out of Park. Brake lights that do not illuminate properly will not alert other drivers that the vehicle is.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will replace the brake light switch, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V701000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Forester&modelYear=2015)**
- **Issue:** If the connection loosens, the front passenger airbag may deactivate even though the seat is occupied, increasing the risk of injury to the front passenger in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, as necessary, replace the ODS sensor mat harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V162000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Gasoline:Turbo/Supercharger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Forester&modelYear=2015)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the turbocharger air intake duct, and replace it as necessary, free of charge, depending on the lot number found on the duct. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2015 Subaru Forester remains a practical, highly capable used crossover option, but it requires a very selective eye when shopping on the secondary market. Buyers should prioritize vehicles with a clear and comprehensive service history. The most critical item to verify is whether the Lineartronic CVT was serviced or replaced under Subaru's extended warranty before it timed out, as out-of-pocket transmission repairs can easily write off the value of the car. Additionally, ensure that a pre-purchase inspection includes a verification of the passenger airbag occupant detection system, a physical check of the rear coil springs for rust or fractures, and a test drive to listen for humming rear wheel bearings. Selecting an example that has already had these common problem areas professionally addressed will yield a much safer, more reliable compact SUV.
