**Introduction**

The 2021 Subaru Forester is a compact SUV known for its reliability, versatility, and all-wheel drive capabilities. It comes with a choice of two engine and transmission options: a 2.5-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine with a continuously variable transmission (CVT) and a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine with a CVT or an eight-speed Lineartronic automatic transmission.

Despite its overall positive reputation, the 2021 Subaru Forester has received some complaints and reports of issues.

**Engine**

**2.5-liter Naturally Aspirated Four-Cylinder**

**Engine Oil Consumption**
Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-ups.
Solution: Engine repairs or replacement may be necessary.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
Recalls: None Found

**2.0-liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

**Turbocharger Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The turbocharger can fail prematurely, leading to reduced power, increased oil consumption, and unusual noises.
Solution: Turbocharger replacement is required.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $3,500
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT Transmission**

**Hesitation and Jerking**
Description/Symptoms: The CVT transmission may experience hesitation, jerking, or roughness during gear changes.
Solution: Transmission software updates or repairs may be necessary.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Lineartronic Automatic Transmission**

**No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

**Front Strut Mount Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The front strut mounts can fail prematurely, resulting in clunking noises, reduced ride quality, and misalignment.
Solution: Front strut mount replacement is required.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: The battery can discharge prematurely, leaving the vehicle unable to start.
Solution: Battery replacement or repairs may be necessary.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Wind Noise**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive wind noise can be noticed around the A-pillars and side mirrors.
Solution: Weatherstripping or mirror adjustments.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $300
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Climate Control Malfunctions**
Description/Symptoms: The climate control system may malfunction, leading to inconsistent temperatures or failure to circulate air.
Solution: HVAC system repairs or replacement may be necessary.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,200+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the issues reported for the 2021 Subaru Forester are not widespread and do not significantly affect the vehicle's overall reliability. The most common issues include engine oil consumption and CVT transmission hesitation, which can be resolved through repairs or software updates. While some issues, such as turbocharger failure and strut mount failure, can be more costly to fix, they are less frequent. The majority of the reported issues are relatively minor and do not pose major safety concerns.
