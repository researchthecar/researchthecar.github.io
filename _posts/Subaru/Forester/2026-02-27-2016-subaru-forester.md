# 2016 Subaru Forester Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Subaru Forester is a popular compact SUV known for its spacious interior, standard symmetrical all-wheel drive, and strong safety scores. However, used car buyers should carefully evaluate powertrain durability and electrical stability. While it offers practical utility, certain recurring owner-reported issues warrant a thorough pre-purchase inspection before signing on the dotted line.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine (FB25)

The standard 2.5-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine produces approximately 170 horsepower and 174 lb-ft of torque, delivering adequate daily acceleration and respectable fuel economy. Overall reliability is generally sound, though owners occasionally report stalling and throttle response concerns.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may occasionally stall or experience a rough idle and hesitation when accelerating from a complete stop.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fuel delivery and throttle body components; clean or replace faulty sensors as needed.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience sudden loss of motive power or hesitation while driving at highway speeds, often accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect the fuel system, mass airflow sensor, and powertrain control module for faults.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine (FA20F)

Available on the XT trim, the 2.0-liter turbocharged Boxer engine generates 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque for a much sportier driving experience. While offering brisk performance, it requires diligent maintenance and premium fuel.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Paired across all trims with Subaru's symmetrical all-wheel-drive system, the Lineartronic CVT provides smooth cruising efficiency. However, valve body and internal wear issues can emerge over higher mileage.

**Valve Body Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Multiple dashboard warning lights illuminate simultaneously, accompanied by rough idling, lurching at stops, or delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty transmission valve body assembly.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission was offered on base naturally aspirated models, providing direct gear control for enthusiast drivers. It is mechanically simple and generally robust, provided the clutch has been maintained.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

The electrical architecture handles numerous comfort and safety conveniences, but parasitic drains and infotainment glitches are known pain points for some owners.

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle battery frequently goes dead or drains unexpectedly, sometimes failing to start even after battery replacement.
- **Solution:** Diagnose parasitic electrical draws, including infotainment modules and accessory relays.

## Exterior

### Windshield Glass

Exterior styling and visibility are generally praised, though the factory windshield glass is prone to stress cracking under certain conditions.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Windshields develop stress cracks spontaneously across the glass without any clear point of impact from stones or debris.
- **Solution:** Replace the windshield with quality aftermarket or OEM glass.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Occupant Detection

Safety systems are comprehensive, but seat sensor malfunctions can trigger persistent warning lights and affect passenger airbag deployment readiness.

**Air Bag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** The SRS or passenger airbag warning light illuminates intermittently, indicating a fault within the passenger occupant detection system.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat sensors, wiring harnesses, and replace malfunctioning occupant detection components.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V149000: Exterior Lighting:Brake Lights:Switch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Forester&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Exposure to certain contaminants may cause the brake light switch to malfunction, preventing the brake lights from illuminating and also preventing keyless ignition vehicles from starting and CVT/automatic transmissions from being able to be shifted out of Park. Brake lights that do not illuminate properly will not alert other drivers that the vehicle is.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will replace the brake light switch, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V701000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Forester&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the connection loosens, the front passenger airbag may deactivate even though the seat is occupied, increasing the risk of injury to the front passenger in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, as necessary, replace the ODS sensor mat harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V162000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Gasoline:Turbo/Supercharger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Forester&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the turbocharger air intake duct, and replace it as necessary, free of charge, depending on the lot number found on the duct. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Subaru Forester remains a versatile, safe, and capable family crossover with its standard all-wheel drive. Buyers should verify the service history for any past transmission valve body repairs, check for battery drain issues, and ensure all safety recall work has been properly completed. A pre-purchase inspection by a knowledgeable mechanic is strongly recommended.
