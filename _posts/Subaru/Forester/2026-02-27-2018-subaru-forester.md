# 2018 Subaru Forester Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Subaru Forester, understanding common ownership feedback helps you weigh routine maintenance against potential repair visits. While many owners enjoy daily driving comfort and practical cargo space, specific recurring trouble spots have emerged across electrical, safety, and exterior glass categories. Reviewing these patterns allows you to inspect key areas prior to purchase and budget accordingly for upkeep.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The standard naturally aspirated 2.5-liter horizontally opposed four-cylinder engine produces around 170 horsepower, offering adequate daily power and standard symmetrical all-wheel drive. It is generally robust mechanically, though owners should routinely monitor fluid levels.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

The available 2.0-liter turbocharged Boxer engine boosts output to 250 horsepower for a significantly sportier acceleration response. While punchy and fun to drive, it requires strict adherence to maintenance schedules to ensure long-term turbocharger health.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic CVT Automatic Transmission

The Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission pairs with standard all-wheel drive across the lineup to optimize fuel economy. Smooth operation is typical when fluid changes are maintained, though prospective buyers should listen for unusual whines during a test drive.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Electrical complaints center primarily around parasitic battery drains and connectivity modules attempting to poll unsupported networks.

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle battery repeatedly goes dead after being parked for several days, requiring frequent jump-starts or premature battery replacement.
- **Solution:** Diagnose parasitic electrical draws, update software modules if applicable, and replace the battery with a robust unit.

## Exterior

### Visibility

Exterior complaints heavily feature windshield susceptibility to stress cracking, which can require unexpected glass replacement.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Windshields develop sudden, long stress cracks across the glass—often originating near the wiper area or edges—without any visible stone impact.
- **Solution:** Inspect the windshield thoroughly before purchase and replace it through an experienced glass technician if cracks compromise structural integrity.

## Safety

### Air Bag

Safety system grievances frequently involve passenger seat occupancy sensor faults that trigger warning lights and disable the airbag.

**Passenger Airbag Sensor Warning**
- **Symptoms:** The SRS or airbag warning light illuminates on the dashboard, and the passenger occupant detection system incorrectly indicates that the front passenger seat is empty when occupied.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified dealership or technician inspect the Occupant Detection System (ODS) sensor mat and perform necessary recalibrations or harness replacements.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V587000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Forester&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the low-pressure fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V701000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Forester&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the connection loosens, the front passenger airbag may deactivate even though the seat is occupied, increasing the risk of injury to the front passenger in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, as necessary, replace the ODS sensor mat harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V455000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Forester&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." Installing incorrect tires or applying incorrect tire air pressure may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will install a corrected label, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

Overall, this vehicle offers a comfortable ride, excellent outward visibility, and reliable traction through its all-wheel-drive system. Prospective buyers should carefully check for dashboard warning lights related to the occupant detection sensors, inspect the windshield for stress cracks, and verify that the battery holds a charge properly. Scheduling a pre-purchase inspection will help catch these common quirks before finalizing your deal.
