# 2023 Subaru Forester Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Subaru Forester brings back the traditional rugged capability, standard symmetrical all-wheel drive, and generous interior space that buyers expect from the brand. While it is generally regarded as a practical and safe compact crossover, owner reports highlight some recurring areas of concern regarding visibility glass, driver-assistance tech behavior, and early component wear. Prospective buyers should carefully inspect windshield conditions and test-drive the EyeSight safety system to ensure proper operation.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

Powered by a 2.5-liter horizontally opposed four-cylinder engine producing 182 horsepower and 176 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate power for daily commuting and mild adventuring, though owners should monitor fluid levels closely for potential leaks.

**Oil Leaks and Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences major oil leaks under moderate mileage, occasionally without triggering the low oil warning light immediately.
- **Solution:** Inspect engine bay during routine maintenance and replace worn oil filter housings, caps, or seals as needed.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic CVT

Paired with a Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) routing power to standard all-wheel drive. It offers smooth power delivery and optimized fuel efficiency for highway driving.

## Suspension

### Control Arm Bushings

The suspension system delivers a comfortable ride quality over rough terrain, though early wear on rubber bushings has been flagged in owner feedback.

**Premature Bushing Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Front control arm outer bushings show severe tearing and rubber degradation at relatively low mileages.
- **Solution:** Inspect front suspension components during routine services and replace torn bushings or control arm assemblies.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Glass

Exterior glass panels, including windshields and rear hatches, have been reported by some owners as prone to spontaneous cracking or shattering under normal environmental temperature swings.

**Spontaneous Glass Cracks and Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Front windshields develop stress cracks without impact, and rear tailgate glass can spontaneously fracture or shatter.
- **Solution:** Replace damaged glass and inspect for improper frame pressure or factory defect points.

## Safety

### Forward Collision & EyeSight System

Equipped with Subaru's EyeSight driver assist technology, designed to enhance road safety through adaptive cruise control and pre-collision braking.

**Unexpected Braking and Camera Deactivation**
- **Symptoms:** The EyeSight safety suite cameras randomly turn on and off while driving, or the forward collision system triggers unexpected automatic braking.
- **Solution:** Have dealership technicians recalibrate the camera sensors and check for software updates.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Subaru Forester remains a popular choice for compact SUV buyers valuing all-weather capability and passenger comfort. While major mechanical failures are infrequent, used-car shoppers should thoroughly check for exterior glass cracks, ensure the EyeSight driver-assistance electronics function correctly, and inspect the undercarriage for any early suspension or oil leak symptoms.
