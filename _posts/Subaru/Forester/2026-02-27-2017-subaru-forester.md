# 2017 Subaru Forester Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Subaru Forester offers practical cargo space, standard all-wheel drive, and strong safety scores, making it a popular choice for used SUV buyers. However, prospective buyers should review common owner-reported trouble spots, including electrical battery drain, persistent windshield cracking, and occupant detection system faults in the front passenger seat.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The standard 2.5-liter horizontally opposed four-cylinder engine produces 170 horsepower and 174 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting with respectable fuel economy. It generally proves dependable, though owners have reported occasional stalling incidents.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle unexpectedly stalls while driving or coming to a stop, causing a loss of power.
- **Solution:** Inspect throttle body, fuel system, and powertrain control modules for faults.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

Available on XT trims, the 2.0-liter turbocharged boxer engine bumps output to 250 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque for a much livelier acceleration experience. It requires attentive maintenance and quality fuel, but lacks widespread systemic complaint clusters in owner logs.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Both engine options are paired with a Lineartronic CVT that routes power to Subaru's symmetrical all-wheel-drive system. While early CVT iterations from Subaru faced scrutiny, this model year shows relatively few major internal transmission complaints in owner databases.

## Suspension

### Suspension & Wheel Bearings

Suspension and rolling components see higher-than-average rates of premature wheel bearing failures and control arm bushing wear, which can introduce steering play and unwanted noise.

**Wheel Bearing and Control Arm Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Premature humming or grinding noises from wheels indicating bearing failure, or loose front control arm bushings.
- **Solution:** Replace worn wheel hub assemblies and front lower control arm bushings.

## Electrical

### Electrical System & Ignition

Electrical concerns focus primarily on parasitic battery drains that leave vehicles with dead batteries, as well as gear selector microswitch failures that trap keys in the ignition.

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle battery repeatedly dies or loses charge over a short duration, preventing normal starting.
- **Solution:** Diagnose parasitic draws from infotainment or accessory modules and replace weak batteries.

**Stuck Ignition Key / Shifter Defect**
- **Symptoms:** Ignition key becomes stuck in the cylinder and cannot be removed after placing the vehicle in park.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the shift lock mechanism or key release assembly plates.

## Exterior

### Windshield & Visibility

Numerous owners report issues with windshield durability, noting cracks forming spontaneously or expanding rapidly from minor chips.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Cracks develop rapidly across the windshield, often originating from the edge without obvious impact from debris.
- **Solution:** Replace the windshield, ensuring proper calibration if equipped with EyeSight driver assist cameras.

## Safety

### Air Bag & Occupant Detection

Safety system grievances center heavily around front passenger seat occupant detection sensors failing, which can inadvertently deactivate the passenger airbag.

**Air Bag / ODS Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** Passenger airbag warning light illuminates or indicates the front passenger airbag is disabled even when an adult is seated.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace the Occupant Detection System (ODS) seat sensor components under applicable warranty or recall extensions.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V701000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Forester&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the connection loosens, the front passenger airbag may deactivate even though the seat is occupied, increasing the risk of injury to the front passenger in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, as necessary, replace the ODS sensor mat harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Subaru Forester remains an attractive option for used crossover shoppers seeking all-weather capability and spacious interiors. However, buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle for recurring trouble spots, such as front passenger seat airbag sensor faults, windshield integrity, battery drain issues, and worn wheel bearings or control arm bushings during a pre-purchase inspection.
