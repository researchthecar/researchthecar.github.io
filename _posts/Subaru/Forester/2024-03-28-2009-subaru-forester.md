**2009 Subaru Forester**

The 2009 Subaru Forester is a compact SUV that comes with a variety of engine and transmission options. Some of the popular engine options include a 2.5L 4-cylinder engine that produces 170 horsepower and 174 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.5L turbocharged 4-cylinder engine that produces 224 horsepower and 226 lb-ft of torque. The transmission options include a 4-speed automatic transmission and a 5-speed manual transmission. The Forester is known for its reliability, safety features, and spacious interior. However, some owners have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, suspension, and other components.

**Engine**

* **2.5L 4-cylinder engine**
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold gasket can fail, leading to an air leak and a loss of power.
        * Solution: The intake manifold gasket must be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Head gasket failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The head gasket can fail, leading to a loss of coolant and a loss of power.
        * Solution: The head gasket must be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
        * Recalls: None Found
* **2.5L turbocharged 4-cylinder engine**
    * **Turbocharger failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The turbocharger can fail, leading to a loss of power and an increase in fuel consumption.
        * Solution: The turbocharger must be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **4-speed automatic transmission**
    * **Transmission slipping**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip, causing the vehicle to hesitate or lose power.
        * Solution: The transmission must be rebuilt or replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **5-speed manual transmission**
    * **No issues found**

**Suspension**

* **Front struts**
    * **Strut failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The front struts can fail, leading to a loss of stability and control.
        * Solution: The front struts must be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear shocks**
    * **Shock failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The rear shocks can fail, leading to a loss of stability and control.
        * Solution: The rear shocks must be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator can fail, leading to a loss of electrical power.
    * Solution: The alternator must be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery can fail, leading to a loss of electrical power.
    * Solution: The battery must be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle may rust, especially in the underbody and around the wheel wells.
    * Solution: The rust must be repaired or removed.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Clearcoat peeling**
    * Description/Symptoms: The clearcoat on the paint may peel, leading to a loss of shine and protection.
    * Solution: The clearcoat must be repaired or replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Steering wheel peeling**
    * Description/Symptoms: The steering wheel may peel, exposing the material underneath.
    * Solution: The steering wheel must be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Dashboard cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard may crack, especially in the areas around the vents and airbag.
    * Solution: The dashboard must be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2009 Subaru Forester is a reliable and safe vehicle, but it has some common issues that owners should be aware of. The most severe issues include engine and transmission failure, which can be expensive to repair. However, many of the other issues are relatively minor and can be fixed for a reasonable cost. Overall, the Forester is a good choice for drivers who are looking for a reliable and affordable SUV.
