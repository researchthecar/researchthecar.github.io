# 2019 Subaru Forester Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Subaru Forester brings standard all-wheel drive and strong safety features to the compact SUV market, but used-car buyers should carefully evaluate a few recurring ownership complaints. While owners generally praise its cabin space and utility, vehicle data highlights notable problem areas including frequent windshield cracks, battery drain issues related to telematics, and occasional engine stalling tied to stop-start systems or thermal control valve failures.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

Powered by a 2.5-liter direct-injection 4-cylinder boxer engine producing 182 horsepower and 176 lb-ft of torque, this model provides steady acceleration for daily driving. Overall mechanical durability is solid, though buyers should verify the operational health of emissions and thermal management components.

**Engine Stalling and Auto Start/Stop Failures**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may stall or fail to restart automatically when transitioning out of the auto stop/start phase at traffic lights or stops, sometimes accompanied by illuminated warning lights.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle's starting and charging systems evaluated by a qualified technician, and check for relevant powertrain control module updates.

**Thermal Control Valve Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Failure of the thermal control valve can trigger multiple dashboard warning lights, disable driver assist features, and cause unexpected engine shutdown while driving.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty thermal control valve assembly.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Equipped with a Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission paired with Subaru's symmetrical all-wheel drive system, it delivers smooth power delivery and competitive fuel economy. Regular fluid maintenance is essential to ensure long-term reliability.

## Electrical

### Battery Drain and Charging System

Electrical system concerns typically revolve around parasitic draws causing premature battery failure and intermittent warning light illumination.

**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience repeated dead batteries or fail to start due to a parasitic electrical draw, often linked to telematics control modules attempting network communication.
- **Solution:** Inspect the Data Communication Module (DCM) and software status; update software or replace affected modules and the battery as needed.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Glass

Exterior complaints heavily feature reports of fragile windshield glass prone to chipping and expanding cracks.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Windshields may develop sudden cracks across the glass, often originating near the edges or lower ceramic frits, sometimes triggered by minor temperature shifts or wiper use.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged windshield with a quality replacement pane, ensuring proper recalibration of any attached camera-based safety systems.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V263000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Forester&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Missing bolts may allow the rear stabilizer bracket to shift, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will retorque and replace any missing bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V856000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Exhaust System:Emission Control:Crankcase (Pcv)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Forester&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An unexpected loss of power while driving can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the PCV valve, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V065000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Forester&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A connector inside the Electronic Power Steering (EPS) unit may short circuit resulting in a loss of electric power steering assistance. Loss of power steering assist would require higher steering effort, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru has notified dealers and they will inspect the steering gearbox, replacing it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Subaru Forester offers great outward visibility, comfortable seating, and confidence-inspiring traction, making it a popular pre-owned choice. However, prospective buyers should inspect the vehicle thoroughly for any history of windshield cracks, verify that battery and charging systems hold a proper charge, and ensure all engine management updates have been completed.
