**Introduction:**

The 2002 Subaru Forester is a compact SUV known for its reliability, all-wheel drive capability, and spacious interior. It offered two engine options: a 2.0-liter naturally aspirated engine with 125 horsepower and 135 lb-ft of torque and a 2.5-liter turbocharged engine with 165 horsepower and 166 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission.

People who like the 2002 Subaru Forester appreciate its practicality, ruggedness, and value for money. However, it has also been criticized for its unrefined interior and limited engine power, especially for the 2.0-liter option.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Naturally Aspirated Engine**
* **Head gasket failure:** Leaking coolant, oil consumption, overheating
  * Solution: Replace the head gasket
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**2.5-liter Turbocharged Engine**
* **Turbocharger failure:** Decreased engine performance, excessive oil consumption, smoke from the exhaust
  * Solution: Replace the turbocharger
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
  * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-speed Automatic Transmission**
* **Transmission slipping:** Difficulty changing gears, hesitation during acceleration
  * Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,800 - $3,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut mount bearing failure:** Clunking or rattling noises from the front suspension
  * Solution: Replace the strut mount bearings
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
  * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator problems:** Battery not charging, dim headlights
  * Solution: Replace the alternator
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
  * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust:**
  * Solution: Rustproofing or replacing affected panels
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of the rust
  * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate control issues:** Malfunctioning air conditioning, heater, or fan
  * Solution: Diagnose and repair the specific component(s)
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2002 Subaru Forester is a reliable vehicle, but it can be prone to certain issues, particularly with the head gasket, turbocharger, and transmission. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but some can be expensive to repair. It is important to factor in potential repair costs when considering the purchase of a used 2002 Subaru Forester. Regular maintenance and inspection can help prevent or minimize some of these issues.
