## 2013 Subaru XV Crosstrek

The 2013 Subaru XV Crosstrek is a compact crossover SUV that offers both comfort and off-road capability. It is powered by a 2.0-liter naturally aspirated flat-4 engine that produces 148 horsepower and 145 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with either a 5-speed manual transmission or a continuously variable transmission (CVT). The XV Crosstrek is known for its spacious interior, all-wheel drive capability, and good fuel economy. However, it has also had some issues reported by owners.

**Engine**

* **2.0L Flat-4 with 5-Speed Manual**
    * **Timing chain tensioner failure:** The timing chain tensioner can fail, resulting in a loss of power and potential engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **2.0L Flat-4 with CVT**
    * **CVT transmission failure:** The CVT transmission can fail, causing the vehicle to lose power or become stuck in gear.
        * Solution: Replace the CVT transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Manual**
    * **No Issues Found**
* **CVT**
    * **CVT transmission failure (Refer to Engine Section)**

**Suspension**

* **Rear suspension noise:** The rear suspension can make noise when going over bumps or rough roads.
    * Solution: Replace the rear suspension bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** The battery can drain quickly, especially in cold weather.
    * Solution: Replace the battery or check for parasitic battery drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Windshield cracking:** The windshield can crack easily from rocks or other debris.
    * Solution: Replace the windshield.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard rattling:** The dashboard can rattle over bumps or rough roads.
    * Solution: Tighten or replace the dashboard mounting screws.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Headliner sagging:** The headliner can sag and come loose from the roof.
    * Solution: Replace the headliner or use adhesive to reattach it.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2013 Subaru XV Crosstrek is a generally reliable vehicle, but it has had some reported issues. The most serious issue is the CVT transmission failure, which can be costly to repair. Other issues, such as suspension noise and interior rattling, are less severe but can still be annoying. Overall, the XV Crosstrek is a good choice for those looking for a compact SUV, but it's important to be aware of the potential issues before making a purchase.
