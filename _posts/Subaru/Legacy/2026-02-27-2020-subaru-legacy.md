# 2020 Subaru Legacy Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Subaru Legacy delivers standard all-wheel drive, spacious accommodations, and strong safety scores, making it an appealing midsize sedan choice. However, prospective buyers should review certain recurring mechanical and electrical concerns documented by owners. Paying close attention to the powertrain, cooling components, and electrical systems during a pre-purchase inspection can help avoid unexpected repair bills.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L Naturally Aspirated Boxer 4-Cylinder

This base 2.5-liter flat-four engine produces around 182 horsepower, offering adequate acceleration and commendable fuel economy for daily driving. It provides reliable performance overall, though buyers should verify the status of cooling system components like the thermo control valve.

**Thermo Control Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may overheat, illuminate warning lights, or trigger electrical shorts that can cause the car to become inoperable in traffic.
- **Solution:** Replace the defective thermo control valve and check associated electrical circuits for shorting.

### 2.4L Turbocharged Boxer 4-Cylinder

Found on higher trims like the XT, this turbocharged 2.4-liter engine generates roughly 260 horsepower for much punchier acceleration and highway passing power. It pairs standard all-wheel drive with smooth power delivery, though proper maintenance history is essential.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The Lineartronic CVT routes power to Subaru's symmetrical all-wheel-drive system across all trims. While it contributes to impressive fuel efficiency, buyers should watch for software-related shifting anomalies or drive chain concerns.

**Transmission Control Unit and Chain Slip Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated warning lights, an inoperable gear shifter, or hesitation and jerking under load while driving.
- **Solution:** Perform software updates via recall procedures or inspect transmission components for internal wear.

## Electrical

### Power Windows and Infotainment

Electrical accessories require a thorough checkout during a test drive to ensure the infotainment hub, driver assistance cameras, and power windows operate without intermittent glitches.

**Power Window and Head Unit Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows failing to roll up automatically in heavy rain or reversing into the fully open position; center console touchscreens freezing and rebooting.
- **Solution:** Inspect window regulators and switches or update/replace infotainment hardware modules.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Glass

Exterior glass visibility components require careful inspection, as several owners report unprovoked stress cracks forming on windshields.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Cracks developing across the windshield unexpectedly while parked or during temperature fluctuations without any impact from debris.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged windshield and recalibrate any associated Advanced Driver Assistance System (ADAS) cameras.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V766000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Legacy&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." The intermittent loss of the rearview image reduces the driver's visibility and increases the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the software and, if necessary, replace the Cockpit Control Module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V955000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Legacy&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** An improperly secured drive chain may slip and break, causing a loss of drive power and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the TCU, inspect TCU data for chain slip, and visually inspect the chain guide. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V587000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Legacy&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the low-pressure fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V664000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Pedals And Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Legacy&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A missing or loose bolt may cause the brake pedal mounting area to deform over time, which can reduce braking performance and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the brake pedal mounting bracket hardware and replace the hardware or brake pedal assembly, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V485000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Legacy&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** An improperly secured drive chain may slip and break, causing a loss of drive power and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the TCU, inspect TCU data for chain slip, and visually inspect the chain guide. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V227000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Legacy&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A capacitor in the sensors for the Occupant Detection System (ODS) may crack and short circuit, which can prevent the front passenger air bag from deploying in a crash. An air bag that does not deploy during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the ODS sensors on the front passenger seat, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Subaru Legacy is a comfortable and capable sedan, but prospective buyers should perform a thorough inspection focusing on known trouble areas. Ensure that engine cooling components like the thermo control valve have been monitored or serviced, check for proper operation of the CVT transmission, and inspect the windshield and electronics for any signs of prior trouble. Consulting vehicle service histories will help secure a reliable ownership experience.
