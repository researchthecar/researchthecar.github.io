# 2016 Subaru Legacy Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Subaru Legacy, buyers generally find a comfortable, sure-footed sedan with standard all-wheel drive. However, a review of owner complaints and technical feedback highlights a few recurring trouble spots, most notably electrical drains, ignition key extraction issues, and occasional windshield stress cracks. Being aware of these common pain points can help you thoroughly inspect a prospective vehicle before buying.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2.5-liter horizontally opposed four-cylinder engine produces approximately 175 horsepower, paired with a Lineartronic continuously variable transmission. It provides dependable daily efficiency and standard symmetrical all-wheel drive, though routine fluid and gasket checks are advised.

### 3.6L 6-Cylinder Engine

The 3.6-liter six-cylinder engine delivers a smoother, more robust 256 horsepower paired with a high-torque CVT. It offers strong highway acceleration and passing power, making it a desirable upgrade for drivers seeking extra performance.

### Ignition Switch and Key Cylinder

Steering column and ignition mechanism components can occasionally develop issues preventing proper key removal.

**Ignition Key Sticking**
- **Symptoms:** The ignition key becomes jammed or difficult to remove from the switch after placing the vehicle in park.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace the select lever assembly or ignition cylinder components.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic CVT Automatic Transmission

Subaru's Lineartronic continuously variable transmission routes power to all four wheels across both engine configurations. While designed for efficiency and smooth power delivery, buyers should test-drive the vehicle to ensure smooth engagement without hesitation or shuddering.

## Electrical

### Battery and Infotainment System

Electrical complaints largely center around parasitic battery drains often tied to vehicle infotainment and telematics software.

**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle battery frequently dies after sitting for short periods, requiring frequent jumping or premature battery replacement.
- **Solution:** Inspect for parasitic draws linked to telematics or infotainment modules, and update or replace faulty components as necessary.
- **Cost:** $300+

## Exterior

### Windshield Glass

Exterior glass reports indicate susceptibility to stress fractures originating near the lower edges of the windshield.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Cracks develop suddenly across the windshield without any visible impact point or stone chip.
- **Solution:** Replace the windshield glass, ensuring proper calibration if equipped with safety camera systems.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V794000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Legacy&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the drive shaft detaches from the rear differential, it may strike the fuel tank and result in a fuel leak, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Subaru has notified owners, and dealers will inspect the fastening nuts, and tighten or replace them, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V292000: Steering:Column](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Legacy&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The loss of steering ability would increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the lot number on the steering column, replacing the steering column, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V502000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Legacy&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the transmission oil leaks onto the exhaust pipe, there is a possible risk of fire.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will replace the propeller shaft, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Subaru Legacy is a practical and comfortable sedan option, particularly valued for its standard all-wheel-drive capability. Prospective buyers should carefully verify the health of the electrical system, ensure the ignition key releases smoothly from the switch, and inspect the windshield for any stress fractures. Overall, a thorough pre-purchase inspection will help secure a reliable model.
