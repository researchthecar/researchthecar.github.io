# 2016 Subaru Legacy

### Overview
The 2016 Subaru Legacy is a mid-size sedan known for its standard all-wheel drive and comfortable ride. Generally, it offers good reliability, with owners reporting satisfaction with its performance and longevity. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen more attention from repair shops. This analysis focuses on documented mechanical and electrical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers of the 2016 model year.

---


## Engine

### 2.5L Boxer (FB25)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning between oil changes, potentially leading to low oil levels.
- **Solution:** Engine inspection, potential piston ring replacement or PCV system check.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Head Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, overheating, milky oil.
- **Solution:** Replacement of head gaskets and related components.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $4,000

## Transmission

### CVT (TR580/TR690)
**Hesitation/Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission may hesitate or jerk during acceleration or gear changes.
- **Solution:** Software update, transmission fluid flush, or potential transmission replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $3,500

**Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible leaks from transmission seals or lines.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seals or lines.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Subaru Legacy generally presents a reliable package, especially with its standard AWD system. The most frequently cited issues revolve around the 2.5L engine's oil consumption and potential head gasket concerns, though these are not universal. The CVT transmission can sometimes exhibit hesitation or require fluid services. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system and occasional battery drain, are also noted. However, many owners experience trouble-free ownership, with the 3.6L engine and other systems showing strong reliability. Regular maintenance and addressing early signs of oil consumption can help mitigate potential long-term costs.
