# 2019 Subaru Legacy Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used midsize sedan, evaluating common trouble spots helps ensure a reliable ownership experience. This vehicle offers a comfortable ride and standard all-wheel drive, but buyers should be aware of a few recurring owner complaints involving electrical systems, engine oil leaks, and visibility concerns.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The standard 2.5-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces approximately 175 horsepower, offering balanced efficiency and daily drivability. It is generally dependable, though owners should routinely inspect for oil leaks around the timing covers and cam carriers.

**Cam Carrier Oil Leak**
- **Symptoms:** An oil leak can develop from the cam carrier area due to sealant failure, potentially dripping onto the exhaust and creating a burning smell or fire hazard.
- **Solution:** Reseal or replace the cam carrier gasket and apply fresh sealant.

### 3.6L 6-Cylinder Engine

The available 3.6-liter six-cylinder engine delivers a smoother, more robust 256 horsepower for stronger highway passing power. It pairs with symmetrical all-wheel drive and offers a sturdy mechanical design with fewer reported baseline complaints.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

A Lineartronic continuously variable transmission (CVT) comes standard across all trims, routing power smoothly to the symmetrical all-wheel-drive system while maximizing fuel economy. While generally adequate for daily commuting, a small number of owners have experienced premature wear and internal mechanical failure.

**CVT Failure and Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission may begin to slip, whine, or squeal, eventually leading to complete transmission failure.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace the transmission assembly.

## Electrical

### Battery and Charging System

The electrical architecture supports advanced safety and convenience features, but parasitic draws can occasionally deplete the battery.

**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle battery frequently goes dead, leaving the car unable to start after sitting for short periods.
- **Solution:** Diagnose parasitic electrical draws, update software, or replace faulty modules such as the Data Communication Module (DCM) fuse.

### Infotainment System

Infotainment and multimedia systems provide smartphone integration and navigation, though screen failures and software glitches are notable pain points.

**Touchscreen Delamination and Ghost Touching**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display develops bubbles under the screen, causing the radio, navigation, and Bluetooth controls to operate randomly on their own or become completely unresponsive.
- **Solution:** Replace the infotainment display unit.

## Exterior

### Windshield Glass

Exterior glass and trim are generally robust, though factory windshields have a documented history of cracking easily.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** The front windshield develops long cracks suddenly across the glass without any apparent impact from road debris.
- **Solution:** Replace the windshield glass and recalibrate safety camera systems if necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V587000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Legacy&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the low-pressure fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V218000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Legacy&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The low pressure fuel pump may become inoperative. If the low pressure fuel pump fails, the engine may stall without the ability to restart the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will replace the low pressure fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V493000: Structure](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Legacy&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the reduced strength of the vehicle body may increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the vehicles and repair or repurchase the vehicle, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

This vehicle is a practical and comfortable choice for used-car buyers seeking confident all-wheel-drive capability in a traditional sedan. However, prospective buyers should thoroughly test the infotainment touchscreen, verify battery health, inspect for engine oil leaks, and check service records for any history of windshield replacement or transmission trouble.
