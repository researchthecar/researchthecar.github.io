# 2019 Subaru Legacy

### Overview
The 2019 Subaru Legacy is a mid-size sedan known for its standard all-wheel drive and comfortable ride. It generally offers good reliability, with owners appreciating its practical features and Subaru's reputation for durability. While most owners report a positive experience, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen occasional reports of issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective buyers and current owners.

---


## Engine

### 2.5L Boxer (FB25)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning between oil changes, potentially leading to low oil levels.
- **Solution:** Engine inspection, potential piston ring replacement or PCV valve check.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant leaks, overheating, sweet smell from engine bay.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of leaking hoses, gaskets, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

## Transmission

### CVT (TR580)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent hesitation during acceleration or noticeable jerking when shifting.
- **Solution:** Software update for the transmission control module, or in severe cases, transmission fluid flush or replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $700

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing or Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, freezes, or restarts randomly.
- **Solution:** Software update, or in some cases, replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $100 - $1200


### Battery Drain
**Premature Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dies unexpectedly, requiring jump starts or replacement sooner than expected.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, check for faulty modules or accessories.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Subaru Legacy generally presents a reliable package, especially with its robust all-wheel-drive system. The most frequently cited technical concerns revolve around the 2.5L engine's potential for oil consumption and coolant leaks, and some CVT transmission units experiencing hesitation. Electrical issues, particularly with the infotainment system's responsiveness and occasional battery drain, are also noted. However, these issues are not universal, and many owners experience trouble-free operation. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to any warning signs are recommended to ensure long-term dependability.
