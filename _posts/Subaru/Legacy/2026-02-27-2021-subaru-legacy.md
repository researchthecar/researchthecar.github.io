# 2021 Subaru Legacy Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Subaru Legacy offers a comfortable ride, standard all-wheel drive, and modern safety tech, but prospective buyers should review a few known owner-reported issues. While overall owner satisfaction is generally positive, complaints logged with safety authorities highlight select areas such as electrical drains, visibility concerns, and powertrain symptoms that warrant careful inspection during a used car test drive.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L Horizontally-Opposed 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.5-liter naturally aspirated Boxer 4-cylinder engine produces approximately 182 horsepower and 176 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate daily efficiency and standard symmetrical all-wheel drive. Reliability is generally dependable, though rare owner reports of internal mechanical noise highlight the importance of checking service history.

**Internal Engine Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience sudden internal knocking or tapping noises from the engine compartment without prior warning lights or overheating.
- **Solution:** Requires professional mechanical inspection and diagnosis; severe cases may involve engine component repair or replacement.

### 2.4L Turbocharged Horizontally-Opposed 4-Cylinder Engine

The available 2.4-liter turbocharged Boxer 4-cylinder engine pushes output to 260 horsepower and 277 lb-ft of torque for a significantly more responsive driving experience. It pairs with all-wheel drive and delivers solid performance, provided routine oil maintenance schedules have been strictly maintained.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The Lineartronic CVT comes standard across all 2021 Legacy trims to optimize fuel economy and smooth power delivery. While most operate seamlessly, buyers should thoroughly test drive the vehicle to ensure smooth acceleration and prompt gear engagement.

**Powertrain Stuttering and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may intermittently stutter, hesitating to engage gear properly, which can cause sudden bogging, violent downshifts, and momentary loss of power to the wheels.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician inspect transmission control modules, fluid conditions, and software calibrations.

## Electrical

### Battery and Charging System

Electrical complaints center on premature battery drains and occasional infotainment screen freezes, so verifying electronics functionality during a used car walkthrough is recommended.

**Premature Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported batteries dying unexpectedly or failing to hold a charge, sometimes leaving the vehicle stranded with little to no prior warning.
- **Solution:** Test the battery and charging system health, and ensure any software updates or parasitic draw issues are addressed by a qualified technician.

## Exterior

### Windshield Glass

Exterior concerns are primarily focused on recurring windshield stress cracks reported by multiple vehicle owners.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Windshields may develop long stress cracks across the glass surface suddenly without any clear evidence of impact from outside objects.
- **Solution:** Inspect the windshield thoroughly for existing cracks or chips prior to purchase, and consult an auto glass specialist if replacement is necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V485000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Legacy&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An improperly secured drive chain may slip and break, causing a loss of drive power and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the TCU, inspect TCU data for chain slip, and visually inspect the chain guide. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V755000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Park/Neutral Start Interlock Switch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Legacy&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An inoperative inhibitor switch may prevent the reverse lights from illuminating and the rearview camera image from displaying, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the inhibitor switch, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V227000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Legacy&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A capacitor in the sensors for the Occupant Detection System (ODS) may crack and short circuit, which can prevent the front passenger air bag from deploying in a crash. An air bag that does not deploy during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the ODS sensors on the front passenger seat, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Subaru Legacy is a practical and safe midsize sedan choice, but used car buyers should perform targeted checks before finalizing a purchase. Pay close attention to battery health, inspect the windshield for stress cracks, and ensure the powertrain accelerates smoothly during a test drive. Verifying that all open safety campaigns have been completed by a dealer will help ensure a reliable ownership experience.
