**Introduction to the 2013 Subaru Legacy**

The 2013 Subaru Legacy is a midsize sedan available with three engine options: a 2.5-liter four-cylinder producing 173 horsepower, a 2.5-liter turbocharged four-cylinder producing 265 horsepower, and a 3.6-liter six-cylinder producing 256 horsepower. The 2.5-liter engine is paired with a six-speed manual or CVT, while the 2.5-liter turbo and 3.6-liter engine come with a CVT only.

Owners of the 2013 Legacy appreciate its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and all-wheel drive capability. However, some common issues have been reported.

**Engine**

**2.5-liter Four-Cylinder**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Description/Symptoms: The engine consumes excessive amounts of oil, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage. Solution: Repair or replace the piston rings and/or valve stem seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+ Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT**

* **Shuddering or Slipping:** Description/Symptoms: The CVT experiences shuddering or slipping during acceleration or shifting. Solution: Replace the CVT fluid or, in severe cases, the entire CVT transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+ Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Bushing Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The rear suspension bushings prematurely fail, causing excessive noise and vibration during turns or over bumps. Solution: Replace the failed bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Description/Symptoms: The battery drains prematurely without any apparent cause. Solution: Inspect and replace any components causing parasitic drain, such as the alternator or stereo system. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+ Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:** Description/Symptoms: Moisture accumulates inside the headlight assemblies, impairing visibility. Solution: Replace the affected headlights. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Fan Motor Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The climate control fan motor fails, resulting in reduced or no airflow from the vents. Solution: Replace the fan motor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400 Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2013 Subaru Legacy is generally a reliable vehicle, but it has some notable issues. Excessive oil consumption in the 2.5-liter engine and CVT transmission problems can be costly repairs. Other issues, such as suspension bushing failure and electrical problems, are less severe but can still impact the vehicle's performance and comfort.
