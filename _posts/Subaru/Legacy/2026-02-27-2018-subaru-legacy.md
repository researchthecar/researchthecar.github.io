# 2018 Subaru Legacy

### Overview
The 2018 Subaru Legacy is generally considered a reliable vehicle, offering a good balance of performance, safety, and practicality. It is known for its standard all-wheel-drive system, comfortable ride, and user-friendly interior. While most owners report a positive ownership experience, like any vehicle, there are a few areas where potential issues have been noted. This analysis focuses on common technical concerns reported by owners and automotive repair data, excluding routine maintenance and normal wear and tear. The data aims to provide a clear picture of the 2018 Legacy's technical reliability across its key systems.

---


## Engine

### 2.5L Boxer (FB25)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning, requiring frequent top-offs. May be accompanied by blue smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement of piston rings or valve stem seals. In some cases, engine replacement may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $4,000+

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, low coolant level, overheating.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of leaking hoses, gaskets, or the radiator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing or Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen may become unresponsive, freeze, or restart unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Software updates or a system reset. In some cases, replacement of the head unit may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $100 - $1,200


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dies unexpectedly, often after the vehicle has been parked for a period. May be related to parasitic draw from certain modules.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the source of the parasitic draw and repair or replace faulty components. Battery replacement may also be needed.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 Subaru Legacy is a solid choice for those seeking a reliable all-wheel-drive sedan. The 3.6L engine and manual transmission variants appear to be particularly robust with no significant widespread issues reported. The more common 2.5L engine has seen some reports of oil consumption and coolant leaks, which owners should monitor. The CVT transmission can occasionally exhibit hesitation, and the infotainment system may experience occasional glitches. Electrical issues like battery drain are also noted but often resolvable with diagnostics. Overall, the Legacy offers good reliability, but awareness of potential engine and electrical quirks is advisable for prospective buyers.
