# 2018 Subaru Legacy Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Subaru Legacy is a comfortable, all-wheel-drive midsize sedan known for its composed ride and standard safety features. While mechanically straightforward, prospective buyers should pay close attention to known owner-reported quirks—particularly surrounding interior electronics and exterior glass—to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

This naturally aspirated 2.5-liter horizontally opposed four-cylinder engine produces 175 horsepower and 174 lb-ft of torque, paired with symmetrical all-wheel drive. It offers adequate daily power and respectable fuel efficiency, representing the standard and most common powerplant for this model year.

### 3.6L 6-Cylinder Engine

The larger 3.6-liter six-cylinder boxer engine generates 256 horsepower and 247 lb-ft of torque, delivering smoother and significantly faster acceleration. It is a robust option for drivers seeking extra passing power, though less common in the used market.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

A continuously variable transmission is standard across all trims, engineered to optimize fuel economy and deliver seamless power delivery to the all-wheel-drive system. Routine fluid maintenance is key to ensuring long-term durability for this transmission type.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System

The Starlink infotainment touchscreen system is prone to delamination issues that can impair media controls and backup camera visibility.

**Screen Delamination and Ghost Touches**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen display develops internal bubbling or lamination separation, leading to erratic phantom inputs, unresponsiveness, or freezing without driver interaction.
- **Solution:** Requires replacement or professional repair of the head unit assembly.

### Battery and Charging

Electrical draws and battery health should be verified during a pre-purchase inspection to avoid unexpected starting failures.

**Premature Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle battery drains unexpectedly while parked, leaving the vehicle unable to start and stranding the driver.
- **Solution:** Diagnose parasitic electrical draws, update software modules, or replace the battery as needed.

## Exterior

### Windshield

Exterior glass visibility components are susceptible to unexpected cracking and should be closely inspected for prior replacement history.

**Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Windshields develop stress cracks or chip easily from minor impacts, often spreading across the driver's field of view without apparent severe trauma.
- **Solution:** Replace the windshield, ensuring proper recalibration of any attached EyeSight safety camera systems.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V587000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Legacy&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the low-pressure fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V935000: Visibility:Rearview Mirrors/Devices](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Legacy&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, a software issue may cause the audio display system to not initialize, resulting in the camera display remaining blank. As a result, the vehicles fail to comply with requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." Failure to display the rearview image while backing up increases the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru has notified owners, and dealers will reprogram the audio system display software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V773000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Legacy&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Due to a software error, the low fuel warning light may not illuminate at the intended remaining fuel level and the miles-to-empty display may incorrectly indicate a positive number despite the tank being empty. The inaccurate fuel display may cause a driver to unexpectedly run out of fuel and the vehicle to stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the combination meter software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Subaru Legacy provides a secure, all-wheel-drive driving experience with generally reliable core powertrains. Buyers should thoroughly test the infotainment touchscreen for proper responsiveness and inspect the windshield and battery health prior to purchase to ensure a smooth ownership experience.
