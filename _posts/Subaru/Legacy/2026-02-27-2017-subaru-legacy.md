# 2017 Subaru Legacy Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Subaru Legacy offers a comfortable ride, standard all-wheel drive, and solid safety ratings, making it an appealing choice for a used midsize sedan. However, prospective buyers should review common owner-reported trouble spots, including electrical battery drain issues, windshield durability concerns, and mechanical steering components.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.5-liter four-cylinder boxer engine produces around 175 horsepower, offering balanced everyday efficiency paired with Subaru's symmetrical all-wheel drive system.

**Fuel and Emissions Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Gasoline fumes inside or outside the vehicle, or reduced gas flow leading to engine stalling.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel lines and catalytic converter assembly; replace damaged components as necessary.

### 3.6L 6-Cylinder Engine

The available 3.6-liter six-cylinder boxer engine generates 256 horsepower for a smoother, more powerful acceleration profile and robust highway performance.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic CVT Automatic Transmission

A continuously variable transmission (CVT) comes standard across all trims, optimizing fuel economy while delivering smooth power delivery to all four wheels.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Column

Steering complaints center around front-end noise and eventual wear on the steering rack assembly.

**Steering Rattle and Rack Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or rattling noises from the front end when driving over rough roads, or electric power steering failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering rack and electric power steering components; replace the steering gear assembly if internal wear is found.

## Electrical

### Electrical System & Ignition

Owner complaints highlight recurrent electrical challenges, particularly related to battery longevity and ignition key release mechanisms.

**Battery Drain and Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to start unexpectedly or leaves the owner stranded due to a dead battery.
- **Solution:** Test charging system and battery draw; replace the battery and address underlying parasitic draws.

**Stuck Ignition Key**
- **Symptoms:** The key gets stuck in the ignition cylinder and cannot be easily removed without shifting the gear selector repeatedly.
- **Solution:** Inspect the shifter interlock switch and ignition cylinder assembly; replace faulty shift linkage or ignition parts.

## Exterior

### Windshield Glass

A number of owners have reported susceptibility to windshield stress cracks or quick damage from minor road impacts.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Cracks developing across the windshield from the lower edges or frame without any visible impact from road debris.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged windshield glass, ensuring proper calibration of any attached driver-assist camera systems.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V716000: Steering:Column](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Legacy&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, the knee guard bracket may not be properly attached to the steering beam assembly. If the knee guard is not properly attached and the driver is not wearing a seatbelt, the knee guard may not be able to properly restrain the driver's lower body in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the knee guard welding on each vehicle's steering beam, replacing the beam as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V292000: Steering:Column](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Legacy&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The loss of steering ability would increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the lot number on the steering column, replacing the steering column, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Subaru Legacy is a capable midsize sedan equipped with practical features and sure-footed all-wheel drive. While powertrain options generally hold up well, used-car shoppers should thoroughly test the battery, ignition key release, steering responsiveness, and inspect the windshield for stress fractures before finalizing a purchase.
