# 2017 Subaru Legacy

### Overview
The 2017 Subaru Legacy is a mid-size sedan known for its standard all-wheel-drive system and comfortable ride. Reliability for this model year is generally considered good, with owners reporting fewer significant mechanical issues compared to some competitors. Common areas of concern, while not widespread, tend to be minor electrical glitches and occasional transmission behavior. The powertrain options, including the naturally aspirated and turbocharged Boxer engines, have proven to be robust for their intended use. Overall, the Legacy offers a dependable choice for daily commuting and family transport, with a focus on safety and practicality.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing or Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen display may occasionally freeze, become unresponsive to touch inputs, or restart unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the infotainment system, or a system reset.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners have reported intermittent battery drain, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle. This can sometimes be linked to specific accessories or modules.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the parasitic draw, potentially involving checking modules, accessories, or replacing the battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Subaru Legacy presents itself as a reliable mid-size sedan, particularly noted for its standard all-wheel-drive. While most major mechanical components like the engines and suspension are robust, some minor issues have been reported. The CVT transmission can occasionally exhibit hesitation or shuddering, often resolvable with software updates or fluid changes. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment screen unresponsiveness and intermittent battery drain, are the most frequently cited concerns, though typically not critical. Overall, the Legacy is a solid choice for those seeking a dependable vehicle with good safety features and all-weather capability, provided potential minor electrical and transmission quirks are understood.
