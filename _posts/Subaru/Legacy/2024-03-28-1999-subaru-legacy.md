**Introduction:**
The 1999 Subaru Legacy is a mid-size sedan known for its reliability, spaciousness, and all-wheel drive capability. It was offered with a range of engine and transmission options. The 2.5L 4-cylinder engine produces 155 horsepower and 166 lb-ft of torque, while the 2.5L turbocharged engine generates 165 horsepower and 226 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual, a 4-speed automatic, and a continuously variable transmission (CVT).

**Engine**
- **2.5L 4-cylinder:**
    - **Head gasket failure:**
        - Description/Symptoms: Blown head gasket, coolant leaking into engine oil, overheating
        - Solution: Replace head gaskets
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
        - Recalls: None Found
- **2.5L turbocharged:**
    - **Turbocharger failure:**
        - Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, smoke from exhaust, rattling noise
        - Solution: Replace turbocharger
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        - Recalls: None Found
    - **Camshaft position sensor failure:**
        - Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, loss of power, check engine light
        - Solution: Replace camshaft position sensor
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
        - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
- **5-speed manual:**
    - **Clutch failure:**
        - Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears
        - Solution: Replace clutch
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        - Recalls: None Found
- **4-speed automatic:**
    - **Transmission slipping:**
        - Description/Symptoms: Gears slipping while driving
        - Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
        - Recalls: None Found
- **CVT:**
    - **Transmission slipping:**
        - Description/Symptoms: Gears slipping while driving
        - Solution: Replace CVT transmission fluid and filter
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
        - Recalls: None Found
    - **CVT belt failure:**
        - Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, grinding noise
        - Solution: Replace CVT belt and pulleys
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
        - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
- **Front strut mount failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise when turning or hitting bumps, uneven tire wear
    - Solution: Replace strut mounts
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
- **Alternator failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Dim headlights, battery draining, check engine light
    - Solution: Replace alternator
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
- **Climate control unit failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning or heating not working, fan not blowing
    - Solution: Replace climate control unit
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Window regulator failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Power windows not working, window falling off track
    - Solution: Replace window regulator
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
- **Sunroof leak:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the cabin through the sunroof
    - Solution: Replace sunroof seal
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**
The 1999 Subaru Legacy is a reliable and well-rounded vehicle with a spacious interior and capable all-wheel drive system. However, it does have some potential issues that can be expensive to repair. Head gasket failures, turbocharger failures, and CVT belt failures are among the most serious and costly issues to watch out for.
