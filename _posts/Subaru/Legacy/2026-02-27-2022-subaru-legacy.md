# 2022 Subaru Legacy

### Overview
The 2022 Subaru Legacy generally presents a strong profile for reliability, building on Subaru's reputation for durable vehicles. Owners often praise its standard all-wheel-drive system, comfortable ride, and practical interior. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the Legacy for this model year demonstrates a commendable track record. Common areas of concern, though infrequent, tend to be minor electrical glitches or occasional transmission behavior, rather than widespread mechanical failures. The powertrain options, particularly the naturally aspirated and turbocharged Boxer engines, are well-regarded for their longevity when properly maintained. Overall, the 2022 Legacy is positioned as a dependable choice in the mid-size sedan segment.

## Transmission

### CVT (Lineartronic)
**Hesitation or delayed engagement**
- **Symptoms:** Slight delay when shifting from Park to Drive or Reverse, or occasional hesitation during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Software update for the transmission control module, or in rare cases, transmission fluid flush and replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $350

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Occasional screen freezing or unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen may temporarily freeze, become unresponsive, or require a reset.
- **Solution:** Software update for the infotainment system. A hard reset by disconnecting the battery for a short period can also resolve temporary glitches.
- **Cost:** $0 - $150 (if software update needed outside warranty)


### Battery
**Premature battery drain**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the vehicle, especially after sitting for a few days, or warning lights related to battery voltage.
- **Solution:** Check for parasitic draws, ensure all accessories are off, and consider battery testing/replacement if it's old or faulty.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Subaru Legacy stands out as a reliable vehicle with a solid mechanical foundation. The engine options, both the standard 2.5L and the optional 2.4L turbo, are generally trouble-free. The CVT transmission, while occasionally exhibiting minor hesitation, is largely dependable. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system, are the most commonly cited, though usually resolved with software updates. Other systems like suspension, exterior, and interior components show minimal reported issues, indicating good build quality. Overall, the 2022 Legacy is a sensible choice for buyers prioritizing long-term dependability and standard AWD capability.
