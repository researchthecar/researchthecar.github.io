**Introduction:**

The 2019 Subaru Legacy is a midsize sedan that offers a comfortable ride and plenty of interior space. It comes standard with all-wheel drive, which provides excellent traction in all weather conditions. The Legacy is powered by a 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine that produces 182 horsepower and 176 lb-ft of torque. A six-speed manual transmission is standard, and a CVT is available as an option.

**Engine (2.5-liter Four-Cylinder)**

**No Issues Found**

**Transmission (Six-Speed Manual)**

**No Issues Found**

**Transmission (Continuously Variable Transmission)**

**CVT Slipping**
Description/Symptoms: The CVT slips when accelerating or shifting gears, causing a loss of power.
Solution: Replace the CVT transmission.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $6,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Strut Mounts Failing**
Description/Symptoms: The strut mounts fail, causing a clunking noise when driving over bumps.
Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: The battery drains quickly, even when the vehicle is not in use.
Solution: Replace the battery or diagnose and repair the cause of the drain.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**No Issues Found**

**Interior**

**Center Console Cracking**
Description/Symptoms: The center console cracks, causing a rattling noise.
Solution: Replace the center console.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2019 Subaru Legacy is a reliable vehicle with few reported issues. The most common issue is the CVT slipping, which can be expensive to repair. However, most other issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix. Overall, the Legacy is a good choice for a midsize sedan that offers all-wheel drive and a comfortable ride.
