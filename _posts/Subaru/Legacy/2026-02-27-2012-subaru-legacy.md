# 2012 Subaru Legacy

### Overview
The 2012 Subaru Legacy is a mid-size sedan known for its standard all-wheel-drive system and comfortable ride. It offers a range of engine options, from efficient four-cylinders to a more powerful six-cylinder. Generally, the 2012 Legacy is considered a reliable vehicle, with owners appreciating its practicality and Subaru's reputation for durability. However, like any vehicle, specific components can experience issues over time. This report details common technical reliability data for the 2012 model year, focusing on documented problems beyond routine maintenance.

---


## Engine

### 2.5L Naturally Aspirated (FB25)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning, requiring frequent top-offs. May be accompanied by blue smoke from the exhaust.
- **Solution:** Engine internal inspection, potential piston ring replacement or cylinder head gasket repair.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,500

**Head Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, overheating, white smoke from exhaust, milky oil, or external coolant leaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of head gaskets and related components.
- **Cost:** $1,800 - $3,000


### 2.5L Turbocharged (EJ25)
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, excessive smoke, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement or rebuild.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Head Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, overheating, white smoke from exhaust, milky oil, or external coolant leaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of head gaskets and related components.
- **Cost:** $1,800 - $3,000

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Subaru Legacy generally presents a solid reliability record, particularly with its standard all-wheel-drive system. The 3.6L engine and manual transmission variants are noted for fewer common issues. However, owners of models with the 2.5L naturally aspirated and turbocharged engines should be aware of potential oil consumption and head gasket concerns, which can be costly to repair. The CVT transmission, while common, can sometimes exhibit hesitation or shuddering. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment system glitches and battery drain, are also reported but are typically manageable. Overall, with proactive maintenance and awareness of these specific potential issues, the 2012 Legacy can be a dependable vehicle.
