# 2023 Subaru Solterra Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Subaru Solterra marks Subaru's entry into the all-electric vehicle market, bringing standard all-wheel drive and rugged styling to buyers. As a first-model-year EV co-developed with Toyota, it offers an appealing zero-emissions package, but used car shoppers should review early owner feedback regarding driving dynamics, cold-weather performance, and electrical systems.

---

## Engine

### Dual Electric Motor Powertrain

The Solterra utilizes dual AC synchronous electric motors generating an EPA-estimated 215 horsepower and 249 lb-ft of torque paired with symmetrical all-wheel drive. While providing smooth acceleration and traction, early models have prompted owner scrutiny regarding winter charging behavior and range efficiency.

**Sudden Unintended Acceleration or Low-Speed Brake Delay**
- **Symptoms:** Owners reported unexpected rapid acceleration during low-speed parking maneuvers or a perceived lack of braking power just before stopping, occasionally exacerbated by accidental paddle shifter activation.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle inspected by a certified dealer to check regenerative braking settings, sensor calibration, and software updates.

**Reduced Cold-Weather Driving Range and Slow DC Charging**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience significantly lower driving ranges than advertised during cold weather conditions alongside very slow DC fast-charging speeds.
- **Solution:** Pre-condition the battery when possible and plan trips with allowances for reduced winter range.

## Transmission

### Direct-Drive Single-Speed Transmission

Power is sent to all four wheels via a single-speed direct-drive transmission inherent to the dual-motor EV layout. This transmission setup is mechanically simple and generally reliable in day-to-day driving.

## Exterior

### Underbody Shields and Wheel Well Liners

Exterior panels and underbody aero shields protect mechanical components from road debris, though low-hanging plastic shields are vulnerable in high water.

**Undertray and Fender Liner Detachment**
- **Symptoms:** Front undertrays, wheel well liners, and mud guards can loosen, crack, or completely detach when driving through standing water or flooded roadways.
- **Solution:** Inspect and securely fasten underbody fasteners, or replace damaged plastic clips and liners.

## Interior

### Climate Control and Cabin Electronics

Onboard electronics manage vital functions including climate control, battery thermal regulation, and infotainment displays.

**Heating and Air Conditioning System Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The HVAC system may become inoperable or blow unheated air instead of warm air, leading to a loss of cabin heat.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle diagnosed by a technician to inspect compressor function, climate control modules, and refrigerant levels.
- **Cost:** $3000

## Safety

### Advanced Driver Assistance and Braking Sensors

A suite of electronic safety and collision avoidance features helps protect occupants, though sensor sensitivity in winter weather has drawn complaints.

**Sensor Faults and Overly Aggressive Collision Avoidance**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note dashboard warning lights for system malfunctions or emergency braking systems abruptly engaging at low speeds in snowy or slick conditions.
- **Solution:** Clear snow and debris from sensors, and visit a dealership if warning lights and error messages persist.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V444000: Wheels:Hub](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Solterra&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A wheel detaching from a moving vehicle can result in a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the hub bolts and wheels, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V064000: Wheels:Hub](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Solterra&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Wheel detachment while driving can result in a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten the hub bolts, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V577000: Visibility:Defroster/Defogger/Hvac System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Solterra&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** During certain HVAC system faults, such as electrical compressor failure, the defroster and defogger system may become inoperative. An inoperative defroster and defogger system can decrease the driver's visibility under certain driving conditions, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the HVAC control ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V744000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Solterra&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to freeze or display a blank screen when the vehicle is in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that fails to display an image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the parking assist software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Subaru Solterra offers eco-conscious buyers an all-electric crossover with standard all-wheel drive, but prospective owners should carefully inspect pre-owned models. Pay close attention to climate control functionality, software versions, underbody protective shields, and how the vehicle handles cold-weather charging and range before purchasing.
