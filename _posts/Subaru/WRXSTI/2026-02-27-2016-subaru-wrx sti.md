# 2016 Subaru WRX STI

### Overview
The 2016 Subaru WRX STI, a performance-oriented compact sedan, is known for its potent turbocharged engine, advanced all-wheel-drive system, and engaging driving dynamics. It appeals to enthusiasts seeking a capable daily driver with track-ready potential. While generally robust, like any performance vehicle, it can experience specific issues related to its high-strung powertrain and performance-oriented components. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of attention for owners and prospective buyers.

---


## Engine

### 2.5L Turbocharged Boxer (EJ257)
**Piston Ring Land Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil consumption, blue smoke from exhaust, loss of power, potential engine knock.
- **Solution:** Engine rebuild or replacement with forged pistons.
- **Cost:** $3,000 - $7,000+

**Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, engine overheating, milky oil.
- **Solution:** Replacement of head gaskets and potentially head machining.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of boost, whistling noises, smoke from exhaust, reduced engine performance.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement or rebuild.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Clutch replacement.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500

**Synchro Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Grinding when shifting into certain gears, difficulty engaging gears.
- **Solution:** Transmission rebuild with new synchronizers.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $4,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Subaru WRX STI is a highly capable performance car, but its high-performance nature means certain components are more susceptible to wear and potential failure under demanding use. The 2.5L turbocharged engine, while powerful, has documented issues with piston ring lands and head gaskets, particularly if not maintained meticulously or if subjected to aggressive driving without proper cooling. The manual transmission can also experience clutch and synchronizer wear. While other systems like suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior are generally reliable, owners should be aware of the potential for engine and transmission issues, especially on higher-mileage or heavily used examples. Regular maintenance and mindful driving are crucial for longevity.
