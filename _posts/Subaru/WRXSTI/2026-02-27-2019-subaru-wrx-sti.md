# 2019 Subaru WRX STI Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a used 2019 Subaru WRX STI means looking at a high-performance sport compact known for its rally heritage and engaging manual transmission. While these vehicles offer thrilling driving dynamics, prospective buyers should review available owner feedback and carefully inspect the vehicle's condition, as enthusiast-owned cars are often driven hard.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L Turbocharged Boxer 4-Cylinder (310 hp / 290 lb-ft)

The 2.5-liter turbocharged boxer 4-cylinder engine produces 310 horsepower and 290 lb-ft of torque, providing robust performance. Buyers should evaluate engine health and maintenance history closely due to the high-stress nature of turbocharged powertrains.

**Starter Engagement Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The starter motor may occasionally fail to engage on the first startup attempt, requiring a second try to successfully crank the engine.
- **Solution:** Inspect the starter motor, electrical connections, and ignition switch, and replace the starter assembly if internal winding or solenoid failure is confirmed.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The 6-speed manual transmission features driver-controlled center differential (CCD) integration suited for aggressive driving. Regular fluid changes are vital for long-term shifting smoothness and durability.
