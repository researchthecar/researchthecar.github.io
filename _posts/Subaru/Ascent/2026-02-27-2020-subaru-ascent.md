# 2020 Subaru Ascent Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Subaru Ascent is a popular three-row midsize SUV offering standard all-wheel drive and family-friendly utility. While it delivers strong utility and comfort, prospective buyers should review recurring owner complaints regarding electrical system glitches, transmission behavior, and exterior glass durability.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Turbocharged Boxer 4-Cylinder

The 2.4-liter turbocharged boxer 4-cylinder engine produces 260 horsepower and 277 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily driving and towing. Overall, the engine is generally robust, though minor fuel odor and stalling complaints have been documented by some owners.

**Fuel Odor or Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Occupants report noticing a distinct fuel smell inside or around the vehicle during operation.
- **Solution:** Have the fuel lines, rails, and pump connections inspected by a qualified technician to locate and resolve any leaks.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The Lineartronic CVT is paired exclusively with Subaru's symmetrical all-wheel-drive system across all trims. While it contributes to competitive fuel economy, some owners report drivetrain shudders and slipping that require professional diagnosis or component service.

**Transmission Slipping and Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a loss of power, a slipping sensation, chatter, or hesitation when accelerating or driving at cruising speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition, check for updated software calibrations, or consult a dealer regarding potential component wear.

## Electrical

### Electrical System & Battery

Electrical complaints center heavily around unexpected battery drains and starting issues, often requiring diagnostic checks on vehicle modules.

**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle's battery repeatedly loses charge or goes dead when parked for a period, making the vehicle difficult or impossible to start.
- **Solution:** Test the battery and charging system, check for parasitic electrical draws from accessories or control modules, and update software if applicable.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Sunroof Glass

Exterior glass components, including the windshield and panoramic sunroof, have generated owner reports regarding stress cracks occurring without clear impacts.

**Spontaneous Glass Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Windshields or panoramic sunroof glass develop large cracks without any apparent impact or visible point of origin.
- **Solution:** Have the affected glass panel thoroughly inspected and replaced by an auto glass specialist if structural integrity is compromised.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V955000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Ascent&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** An improperly secured drive chain may slip and break, causing a loss of drive power and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the TCU, inspect TCU data for chain slip, and visually inspect the chain guide. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V954000: Steering:Linkages:Tie Rod Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Ascent&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Due to an incorrect tapered hole in the housing, the front tie rod ends may not fit properly. The tie rods may separate from the housing, causing a loss of steering control and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the left and right front tie rod ends and housings, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V587000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Ascent&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the low-pressure fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V907000: Electrical System:Wiring:Interior/Under Dash](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Ascent&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The ground bolt that secures the ground terminal of the Positive Temperature Coefficient (PTC) heater may have been improperly fastened, which could result in melting of the ground terminal and surrounding components. A melting ground terminal increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the PTC heater ground bolts and if necessary, replace the ground wire and the connector holder, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V436000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Ascent&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) 110, "Tire selection and rims." An incorrect GAWR label may lead to an overloaded vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Subaru Ascent offers spacious family accommodation and standard all-wheel drive, but buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to transmission shift quality, electrical accessory functions, and the condition of exterior glass.
