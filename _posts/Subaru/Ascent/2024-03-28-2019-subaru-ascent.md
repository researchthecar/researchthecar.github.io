**Introduction:**

The 2019 Subaru Ascent is a three-row SUV powered by a 2.4-liter turbocharged boxer engine paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). It offers spacious seating, ample cargo room, and standard all-wheel drive, making it a popular choice for families. However, like any vehicle, it has received reports of certain issues.

**Engine**

**2.4L Turbocharged Boxer Engine**

* **Check Engine Light and Loss of Power:** The Check Engine Light may illuminate, and the vehicle may experience reduced power. The issue is often caused by a faulty knock sensor or wiring harness.
  * Solution: Replacing the knock sensor or repairing the wiring harness.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

* **Hesitation and Jerking:** The CVT may hesitate or jerk during acceleration or shifting. The issue is often caused by a faulty transmission control module (TCM) or worn-out clutch packs.
  * Solution: Updating the TCM software or replacing the clutch packs.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** The battery may drain prematurely, leaving the vehicle with insufficient power to start. The issue is often caused by a faulty alternator or parasitic draw.
  * Solution: Replacing the alternator or repairing the parasitic draw.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Infotainment System Glitches:** The infotainment system may experience glitches, such as freezing or displaying incorrect information.
  * Solution: Updating the system software or replacing the display unit.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Rear Seat Climate Control Issues:** The rear seat climate control system may malfunction, resulting in uneven or insufficient airflow.
  * Solution: Reprogramming the climate control module or replacing the actuators.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2019 Subaru Ascent has some reported issues, including engine and transmission problems, electrical glitches, and interior climate control issues. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but most can be resolved with proper maintenance and repairs. The Ascent remains a reliable and well-equipped family SUV, but potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems.
