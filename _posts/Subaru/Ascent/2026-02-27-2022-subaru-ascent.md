# 2022 Subaru Ascent

### Overview
The 2022 Subaru Ascent, a three-row SUV, generally presents a solid reliability record, particularly for its powertrain. Subaru's commitment to robust engineering is evident in its standard all-wheel-drive system and well-regarded turbocharged engine. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected repairs, like any vehicle, there are a few areas that have seen occasional attention. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective and current owners. The Ascent aims to balance family utility with Subaru's signature capability, and its reliability data reflects this focus.

---


## Engine

### 2.4L Turbocharged BOXER
**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, low coolant level warning light, engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of faulty hoses, seals, or the radiator.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement of the turbocharger or related components.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,500


## Transmission

### CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable hesitation during acceleration or a feeling of the transmission jerking.
- **Solution:** Software updates or, in severe cases, transmission fluid flush and internal component inspection.
- **Cost:** $150 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Subaru Ascent demonstrates good overall reliability, with its core powertrain components being a strong point. The 2.4L turbocharged engine and CVT transmission are generally dependable, though isolated reports of coolant leaks and transmission hesitation exist. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system, are the most frequently cited issues, often resolvable with software updates. Other systems like suspension, exterior, and interior components show minimal reported problems, indicating solid build quality. For potential buyers, addressing potential electrical quirks and ensuring proper maintenance of the powertrain are key considerations for a smooth ownership experience.
