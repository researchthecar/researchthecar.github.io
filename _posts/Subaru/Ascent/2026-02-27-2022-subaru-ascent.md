# 2022 Subaru Ascent Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Subaru Ascent is a practical three-row midsize SUV offering standard symmetrical all-wheel drive and a spacious cabin for families. While many owners appreciate its ride quality and utility, used-car buyers should pay close attention to recurring complaints involving transmission drivability, braking performance under heavy loads, and minor electrical or trim issues. Reviewing maintenance records and performing a thorough test drive can help ensure any potential mechanical or electronic issues have been addressed.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Turbocharged Boxer 4-Cylinder

The Ascent comes equipped with a 2.4-liter turbocharged horizontally-opposed (boxer) 4-cylinder engine producing 260 horsepower and 277 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate power for daily driving and towing, though some owners have noted minor exhaust component concerns.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The Lineartronic CVT features a high-torque design paired with standard all-wheel drive. While generally smooth, it is a frequent point of owner complaints regarding shudders and slip conditions.

**Transmission Shudder**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a noticeable shuddering or vibration sensation during acceleration, particularly between 45 and 60 mph.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the transmission software and drivetrain components; software updates or hardware replacement may be required.

**Transmission Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may fail to engage or slip when shifting into gear, particularly when operating in cold weather conditions.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid levels and condition, and consult a dealership for computer module diagnostics and potential transmission service.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brake System

The braking system provides stopping power for this large SUV, though some drivers note that factory components can feel prone to vibration under heavy descending loads.

**Brake Vibration and Chatter**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report pulsating, shaking, or rumbling noises from the brakes during regular stops or when descending mountain passes.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and calipers for premature wear or warping, and replace components as necessary.

## Electrical

### Battery and Charging System

Electrical complaints center primarily around early battery drains and related charging issues that can leave the vehicle immobilized.

**Premature Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to start or maintain charge, sometimes accompanied by electrical shorts or unresponsive electronics shortly after purchase.
- **Solution:** Test the battery and charging system output, and ensure all applicable electrical updates or service bulletins have been completed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V907000: Electrical System:Wiring:Interior/Under Dash](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Ascent&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The ground bolt that secures the ground terminal of the Positive Temperature Coefficient (PTC) heater may have been improperly fastened, which could result in melting of the ground terminal and surrounding components. A melting ground terminal increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the PTC heater ground bolts and if necessary, replace the ground wire and the connector holder, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V436000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Ascent&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) 110, "Tire selection and rims." An incorrect GAWR label may lead to an overloaded vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Subaru Ascent is a capable family SUV, but prospective buyers should inspect it carefully. Pay special attention to the CVT transmission for any signs of shuddering or slipping during a test drive, and verify that the brakes and electrical system are functioning properly. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection can help uncover these common pain points before finalizing a purchase.
