# 2019 Subaru Ascent Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Subaru Ascent is a popular three-row family SUV offering standard symmetrical all-wheel drive and a spacious cabin. However, as a first-model-year vehicle, it has accumulated a noticeable volume of owner complaints regarding electrical issues, transmission performance, and fragile windshield glass. Used-car shoppers should thoroughly inspect these specific areas and verify service history before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Turbocharged Boxer 4-Cylinder

The 2.4-liter turbocharged boxer 4-cylinder engine produces 260 horsepower and 277 lb-ft of torque, providing strong pulling power for a family crossover. While the mechanical core is generally robust, it shares vulnerability with associated electrical systems prone to premature battery drain.

**Battery Drain and Electrical Discharge**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle's battery repeatedly loses charge, often draining completely overnight or after a few days of sitting, leaving the car unable to start.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic testing to check for parasitic draws, updating software modules, and replacing the battery with a higher-capacity unit if necessary.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The Lineartronic CVT routes power to all four wheels, aiming to maximize fuel economy in a large vehicle format. Early production models, however, have been heavily scrutinized by owners for premature wear, slippage, and hesitation under load.

**Transmission Slipping and Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience hesitation during acceleration, sudden loss of power on the highway, and noticeable shuddering or shudder-like jerks during operation.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition, check for diagnostic trouble codes, and perform software updates or complete transmission replacement under warranty/extended coverage if severe.

## Exterior

### Windshield Glass

The factory glass fitted to early Ascent models has been widely reported by owners as susceptible to stress fractures and chips, prompting frequent replacements.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Windshields develop long cracks suddenly without any obvious impact from rocks, often starting near the edges or lower ceramic frit band.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the windshield with upgraded aftermarket or revised OEM glass, followed by recalibration of the EyeSight camera system if equipped.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V855000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Ascent&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If this occurs, the transmission drive chain may not have the proper tension, resulting in a loss of drive power and an increased risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the transmission control unit. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V955000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Ascent&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An improperly secured drive chain may slip and break, causing a loss of drive power and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the TCU, inspect TCU data for chain slip, and visually inspect the chain guide. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V587000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Ascent&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the low-pressure fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V218000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Ascent&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The low pressure fuel pump may become inoperative. If the low pressure fuel pump fails, the engine may stall without the ability to restart the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will replace the low pressure fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V856000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Exhaust System:Emission Control:Crankcase (Pcv)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Ascent&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An unexpected loss of power while driving can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the PCV valve, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V519000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Ascent&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the front end of the driveshaft separates, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driveshaft assembly, including the center support brace, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V508000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Ascent&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the missing spot welds may compromise the vehicle's strength, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the vehicle to check for missing spot welds. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V907000: Electrical System:Wiring:Interior/Under Dash](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Ascent&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The ground bolt that secures the ground terminal of the Positive Temperature Coefficient (PTC) heater may have been improperly fastened, which could result in melting of the ground terminal and surrounding components. A melting ground terminal increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the PTC heater ground bolts and if necessary, replace the ground wire and the connector holder, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Subaru Ascent offers compelling utility, comfort, and standard all-wheel drive, but its status as an introductory model year demands caution. Prospective buyers should prioritize models with documented dealer service records verifying that transmission concerns, battery draws, and windshield issues have been addressed.
