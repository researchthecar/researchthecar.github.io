# 2023 Subaru Ascent Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Subaru Ascent offers families a spacious, three-row crossover utility vehicle equipped with standard symmetrical all-wheel drive. While it provides strong utility and modern tech features, used car buyers should review specific owner-reported concerns regarding driver-assist quirks, windshield durability, and early brake wear.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Turbocharged Boxer 4-Cylinder

This 2.4-liter turbocharged direct-injection boxer engine produces 260 horsepower and 277 lb-ft of torque, providing ample power for daily driving and towing. It generally runs reliably, though some owners have experienced isolated electrical or battery draws impacting overall vehicle operation.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The Lineartronic CVT features an 8-speed manual mode with paddle shifters and transfers power smoothly to the standard all-wheel-drive system. It requires regular fluid maintenance but operates without widespread mechanical failure patterns in standard use.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake components can experience vibration or premature wear requiring service earlier than expected in standard driving conditions.

**Premature Brake Vibration and Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience steering wheel shake or vibration when applying the brakes at relatively low mileage, requiring early rotor resurfacing or pad replacement.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads and rotors for premature wear or warping, and consult your dealer regarding applicable technical service bulletins or updated components.

## Exterior

### Windshield Glass

Exterior complaints center primarily on fragile windshield glass that chips and cracks easily under minor road debris impacts.

**Excessive Windshield Susceptibility to Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Minor rock strikes or small chips quickly spread into long, unrepairable cracks across the windshield within minutes or days.
- **Solution:** Have the windshield replaced with OEM or aftermarket glass, and consider adding glass coverage to your auto insurance policy.

## Safety

### EyeSight Driver Assist System

Advanced safety features generally enhance protection but can occasionally generate false-positive warnings or sudden corrective actions.

**Unexpected Autonomous Braking or Steering Corrections**
- **Symptoms:** The forward collision avoidance or lane-keeping systems occasionally activate abruptly without any immediate hazard or obstruction on the road.
- **Solution:** Ensure camera and sensor lenses are clean and check with a dealership for software updates related to the EyeSight driver assist suite.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V258000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Ascent&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A damaged tire may experience a sudden loss of air pressure, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace all four tires, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V392000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm:Lower Arm](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Ascent&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Ball joint separation while the vehicle is in motion can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the lower control arm, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V647000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Ascent&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Separation of the front end of the driveshaft increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will clean the bolt mounting surfaces of the center support brace and install new bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V436000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Ascent&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) 110, "Tire selection and rims." An incorrect GAWR label may lead to an overloaded vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Subaru Ascent is a capable family hauler, but used car shoppers should perform thorough pre-purchase inspections. Pay close attention to brake pad and rotor condition, inspect the windshield for chips or cracks, and verify that all driver-assist electronics operate smoothly during a test drive.
