# 2021 Subaru Ascent Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Subaru Ascent, buyers will find a spacious three-row family SUV powered by a standard turbocharged boxer engine paired with a continuous variable transmission. While it offers strong utility and standard all-wheel drive, prospective owners should review common owner-reported complaints regarding electrical battery drains, transmission hesitation, and fragile windshields.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Turbocharged Boxer 4-Cylinder

The 2.4-liter turbocharged boxer 4-cylinder engine produces 260 horsepower and 277 lb-ft of torque, providing capable acceleration for a large crossover. Buyers should routinely inspect the engine bay for oil seepage and fluid leaks during pre-purchase evaluations.

**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Oil seepage or leaks developing around the upper and lower oil pans or timing cover areas.
- **Solution:** Requires professional inspection, resealing of the affected pan or cover surfaces, or replacing failed gaskets.
- **Cost:** $4800

## Transmission

### Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The Lineartronic CVT routes power to Subaru's standard symmetrical all-wheel-drive system across all trims. Buyers should carefully test-drive the vehicle to check for smooth acceleration and signs of shuddering or slipping.

**Transmission Slippage and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle hesitation in traffic, noticeable transmission slippage while in motion, or complete loss of drive capability.
- **Solution:** May require transmission replacement, rebuilding, or software updates depending on severity.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking hardware is tasked with stopping a heavy three-row crossover, and premature rotor wear or warping is a frequent point of frustration for owners.

**Brake Shudder and Rotor Warping**
- **Symptoms:** Vibration, shaking, or grinding noises when slowing down from highway speeds due to warped brake rotors.
- **Solution:** Resurface or replace brake rotors and pads, and consult dealer service bulletins regarding extended warranty coverage.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

The electrical system manages a wide array of cabin tech, driver aids, and convenience features, though parasitic draws and premature battery failures are reported by some owners.

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle battery repeatedly dying overnight or after sitting parked for short periods, requiring frequent jumps or replacements.
- **Solution:** Diagnose parasitic draws, check software calibration, and replace the battery if it fails load testing.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Visibility

Exterior glass and visibility components are generally robust, though spontaneous windshield cracking remains a notable owner complaint area.

**Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Windshields developing stress cracks spontaneously from the edge without impact from stones or road debris.
- **Solution:** Replace the windshield, ensuring proper recalibration of any attached safety and camera systems.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V907000: Electrical System:Wiring:Interior/Under Dash](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Ascent&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The ground bolt that secures the ground terminal of the Positive Temperature Coefficient (PTC) heater may have been improperly fastened, which could result in melting of the ground terminal and surrounding components. A melting ground terminal increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the PTC heater ground bolts and if necessary, replace the ground wire and the connector holder, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V436000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Ascent&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) 110, "Tire selection and rims." An incorrect GAWR label may lead to an overloaded vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Subaru Ascent is a practical and comfortable choice for families needing three rows of seating and standard all-wheel drive, but used-car buyers should perform a thorough inspection. Pay close attention to transmission shift behavior, verify the condition of the brakes and battery, and check service records for any history of oil leaks or windshield replacements.
