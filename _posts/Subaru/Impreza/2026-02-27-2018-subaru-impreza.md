# 2018 Subaru Impreza Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2018 Subaru Impreza, buyers will find a compact car celebrated for its standard all-wheel-drive capability and practical layout. However, reviewing common owner complaints reveals a few recurring trouble spots—most notably front windshield durability and electrical or lighting concerns. Being aware of these specific issues before purchasing can help you inspect prospective vehicles thoroughly and budget for potential upkeep.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2018 Subaru Impreza is powered by a 2.0-liter horizontally opposed (boxer) 4-cylinder engine producing approximately 152 horsepower. While generally dependable, buyers should watch for oil leaks and occasional cooling or stalling concerns.

**Engine Overheating and Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected engine overheating accompanied by temperature warning lights, as well as engine oil leaks from components like the cam carrier.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the engine bay for oil seepage around the cam carrier and check cooling system integrity.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Unexplained engine stalling while operating the vehicle, requiring multiple repair attempts or component replacements.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fuel delivery, ignition, and sensor systems to identify the root cause of unexpected stalls.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) / Manual

Equipped with either a 5-speed manual transmission or a Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) paired with standard symmetrical all-wheel drive, the powertrain provides smooth daily operation. Routine fluid changes are vital to ensure long-term longevity.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting and DCM Module

Electrical complaints primarily center around exterior lighting performance and battery drain issues linked to telematics modules.

**Headlight Failure and Flickering**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights may flicker, fail intermittently, or stop working altogether, raising safety concerns during nighttime driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight assemblies, wiring harnesses, and related switches or recall campaigns for necessary repairs.

**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The Data Communication Module (DCM) may fail to shut down properly, resulting in an ongoing parasitic drain on the battery system.
- **Solution:** Update or repair the DCM module software/hardware, or use a battery tender if parking for extended periods.

### Windshield Glass

Visibility components are frequently impacted by reports of fragile or cracking front windshield glass under normal driving or stationary conditions.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Front windshields have been reported to develop long, sudden cracks spontaneously without any obvious stone chips or impacts.
- **Solution:** Inspect the windshield thoroughly before purchase and verify if replacement under insurance or warranty is necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V587000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Impreza&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the low-pressure fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V264000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Impreza&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The Engine Control Module (ECM) may continue to power the ignition coil after the engine is shut off, causing a short circuit. A short circuit in the ignition coils may cause a stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will update the ECM software, replace the ignition coils and, if necessary, the front exhaust pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V744000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Exhaust System:Emission Control:Crankcase (Pcv)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Impreza&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** An unexpected loss of power while driving can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the PCV valve, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V743000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Impreza&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The Engine Control Module (ECM) may continue to power the ignition coil after the engine is shut off, which could result in a short circuit and blown fuse. If a short circuit occurs while driving, the vehicle may stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will update the ECM software and inspect and replace the ignition coil, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V624000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Impreza&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Reflectors that do not provide adequate illumination reduce the driver's ability to see and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front headlamp assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Subaru Impreza offers excellent safety credentials, standard all-wheel drive, and practical everyday usability. However, potential buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection paying close attention to windshield condition, engine oil leaks, and electrical systems. Addressing these key areas early ensures a more reliable ownership experience.
