**Introduction:**

The 1994 Subaru Impreza is a compact car available in sedan and wagon body styles. It offers a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 1.8-liter naturally aspirated engine with a 5-speed manual transmission, a 1.8-liter turbocharged engine with a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission, and a 2.2-liter naturally aspirated engine with a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Impreza is known for its reliability, affordability, and all-wheel-drive capability. However, some common issues have been reported with certain engine and transmission options.

**Engine**

**1.8-liter Naturally Aspirated Engine**

- **Head gasket failure:** A blown head gasket can cause coolant and oil to leak, leading to overheating and engine damage. **Symptoms:** White smoke from the exhaust, coolant or oil leaks, engine overheating. **Solution:** Replace the head gasket. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+
- **Crankshaft pulley failure:** The crankshaft pulley can fail, causing the timing belt to slip or break. **Symptoms:** Engine rattling or knocking, loss of power. **Solution:** Replace the crankshaft pulley. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+

**1.8-liter Turbocharged Engine**

- **Turbocharger failure:** The turbocharger can fail due to a variety of reasons, including oil leaks, worn bearings, or a faulty wastegate. **Symptoms:** Reduced boost pressure, whining noises from the engine, excessive oil consumption. **Solution:** Replace the turbocharger. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $3,000+
- **Head gasket failure:** Similar to the naturally aspirated engine, head gasket failure can also occur on the turbocharged engine. **Symptoms:** Same as 1.8-liter naturally aspirated engine. **Solution:** Same as 1.8-liter naturally aspirated engine. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+

**2.2-liter Naturally Aspirated Engine**

- **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

- **Clutch failure:** The clutch can wear out over time, causing slipping or difficulty shifting. **Symptoms:** Difficulty engaging gears, slipping clutch, shuddering during engagement. **Solution:** Replace the clutch. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+
- **Synchro damage:** Synchros can become worn, causing difficulty shifting gears. **Symptoms:** Grinding noises when shifting, difficulty getting into gear. **Solution:** Replace the damaged synchros. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $600+

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

- **Torque converter failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing shuddering or slipping. **Symptoms:** Shuddering when shifting gears, slipping transmission. **Solution:** Replace the torque converter. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+
- **Electrical problems:** The automatic transmission can experience electrical issues, such as faulty sensors or wiring problems. **Symptoms:** Transmission shifting erratically, check engine light. **Solution:** Diagnose and repair the electrical problem. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $1,000+

**Suspension**

- **Strut failure:** The struts can become worn or damaged, causing a loss of ride quality and handling. **Symptoms:** Rough ride, bouncing, difficulty steering. **Solution:** Replace the struts. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $400 - $800+
- **Control arm bushings:** Control arm bushings can deteriorate over time, leading to excessive play and noise. **Symptoms:** Squeaking or clunking noises, loose steering. **Solution:** Replace the control arm bushings. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+

**Electrical**

- **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the battery to discharge and the electrical system to malfunction. **Symptoms:** Dimming lights, difficulty starting the engine, battery warning light. **Solution:** Replace the alternator. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+
- **Starter failure:** The starter can fail, preventing the engine from starting. **Symptoms:** Engine won't crank, clicking noises from the starter. **Solution:** Replace the starter. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $700+

**Exterior**

- **Door latch failure:** The door latches can fail, preventing the doors from closing or opening properly. **Symptoms:** Difficulty opening or closing doors, doors popping open. **Solution:** Replace the door latch. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $200+
- **Window regulator failure:** The power window regulators can fail, causing the windows to become stuck or inoperable. **Symptoms:** Windows won't roll up or down, clicking noises. **Solution:** Replace the window regulator. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+

**Interior**

- **Airbag deployment failure:** The airbags may fail to deploy in a collision. **Symptoms:** Airbag warning light, no airbag deployment. **Solution:** Diagnose and repair the airbag system. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+
- **Seatbelt retractor failure:** The seatbelt retractors can fail, preventing the seatbelts from locking in an accident. **Symptoms:** Seatbelts won't retract, seatbelt warning light. **Solution:** Replace the seatbelt retractor. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+

**Summary:**

While the 1994 Subaru Impreza is generally a reliable vehicle, it can have some common issues. Engine problems, particularly with the 1.8-liter naturally aspirated and turbocharged engines, are the most significant concerns. Transmission issues, especially with the automatic transmission, can also be costly. Suspension, electrical, and interior issues are less severe but can still impact the ownership experience. The estimated cost to fix these issues can vary widely depending on the specific model and the severity of the problem. Overall, while the Impreza is a great value for the money, it's essential to be aware of the potential issues it may encounter.
