**Introduction**

The 2021 Subaru Impreza is a compact sedan and hatchback that combines affordability, practicality, and all-wheel drive. It is powered by a standard 2.0-liter flat-four engine that produces 152 horsepower and 145 lb-ft of torque. A continuously variable transmission (CVT) is the only transmission option. For 2021, the Impreza received a minor facelift and updated infotainment system.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Flat-Four**

- **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

- **Hesitation or Jerkiness**: Some owners have reported hesitation or jerkiness during acceleration or shifting. It is typically caused by software issues or a faulty valve body.
- Solution: Update the CVT control module software or replace the valve body.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
- Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 21V836000

**Suspension**

- **Strut Noise**: Some owners have reported a clunking or rattling noise from the front suspension, especially when driving over bumps or uneven surfaces. It is typically caused by a loose or worn strut mount.
- Solution: Inspect and tighten the strut mount bolts or replace the strut mounts.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+

**Electrical**

- **Infotainment System Glitches**: Some owners have reported occasional glitches with the infotainment system, such as freezing, crashing, or connectivity issues. It is typically resolved by software updates.
- Solution: Update the infotainment system software or visit a dealer for repairs.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue
- Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Rearview Camera Issues**: Some owners have reported problems with the rearview camera, such as blurry or distorted images, or a completely blank screen. It could be caused by a loose or faulty connection, or a defective camera unit.
- Solution: Inspect and tighten the camera connections or replace the camera unit.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
- Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Bluetooth Connectivity Issues**: Some owners have reported problems connecting their Bluetooth devices to the Impreza's audio system, such as intermittent connections or poor sound quality. It could be caused by software issues or a faulty Bluetooth module.
- Solution: Update the infotainment system software or visit a dealer for repairs.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue
- Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Subaru Impreza is a reliable and affordable vehicle, but it has a few notable issues. The most common problems are related to the CVT transmission, infotainment system glitches, and suspension noise. While most of these issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, they can be frustrating for owners.
