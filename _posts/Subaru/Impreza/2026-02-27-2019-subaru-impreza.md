# 2019 Subaru Impreza

### Overview
The 2019 Subaru Impreza is generally considered a reliable vehicle, offering a good balance of practicality, all-wheel-drive capability, and fuel efficiency. It is built on Subaru's Global Platform, which enhances rigidity and safety. Common trims include the 2.0i, Sport, Premium, and Limited, all typically powered by a 2.0-liter Boxer engine. While most owners report positive experiences, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen more attention from a reliability standpoint. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Boxer (FB20)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Slightly higher than average oil consumption, especially in older models or under hard driving. May require more frequent oil level checks.
- **Solution:** Regular oil checks and top-offs. In severe cases, internal engine inspection may be needed.
- **Cost:** $0 - $150 (for oil and filter if needed)

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking coolant from hoses, water pump, or head gaskets. Can lead to overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty hoses, water pump, or head gasket.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1200


## Transmission

### CVT (Lineartronic)
**Hesitation/Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional hesitation during acceleration or slight jerking sensations. Can be related to software or internal wear.
- **Solution:** Software update for the transmission control module. In some cases, internal transmission repair or replacement may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500 (for software update); $3000 - $6000 (for replacement)

**Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission warning light, slipping gears, or reduced performance under heavy load or prolonged high temperatures.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and replacement, potentially with an auxiliary cooler. Internal inspection and repair if necessary.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, freezes, or restarts unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system reset, or replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $100 - $800


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery drains prematurely, leading to starting issues. Can be caused by parasitic draw from various modules.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the source of the parasitic draw and repair or replace the faulty component.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Subaru Impreza is a solid choice for those seeking an affordable, AWD-equipped compact car. Its reliability is generally good, with the 2.0L Boxer engine and manual transmission proving quite robust. The CVT, while common, has seen some reports of hesitation or overheating, often addressable with software updates or maintenance. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system and occasional battery drain, are the most frequently cited non-routine issues. Owners should be mindful of oil levels and coolant system integrity. Overall, with regular maintenance and attention to these specific points, the 2019 Impreza should provide dependable service.
