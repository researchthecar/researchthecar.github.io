# 2019 Subaru Impreza Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Subaru Impreza offers a practical, compact package highlighted by standard symmetrical all-wheel drive and sensible everyday efficiency. However, used car buyers should note that owners have frequently reported issues related to spontaneous windshield cracking, exterior lighting concerns, and occasional electronic or powertrain glitches.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Boxer Engine

Powered by a 2.0-liter naturally aspirated flat-four engine producing 152 horsepower and 145 lb-ft of torque, this powerplant offers reliable day-to-day operation. Routine maintenance is key to longevity, though occasional electrical and sensor issues can crop up.

**Unexpected Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may suddenly roll to a stop or lose power while driving at various speeds with no warning lights illuminated.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic inspection of fuel delivery, sensors, and electrical connections; replace faulty components as needed.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The Lineartronic CVT routes power smoothly to all four wheels while maximizing fuel efficiency, though routine fluid checks are recommended to ensure long-term durability.

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 5-speed manual transmission is paired with base trims for a more connected driving experience, though isolated gear wear issues have been documented by owners.

**Gear Grinding During Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Grinding noise when shifting quickly into 4th gear at higher RPMs above 3,000.
- **Solution:** Inspect synchronizers and transmission fluid condition; repair or replace worn internal components.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical complaints center heavily on exterior lighting and infotainment stability, requiring careful pre-purchase verification of all lamps and display units.

**Headlight Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights fail intermittently or stop working entirely, creating dangerous night-driving visibility issues.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlamp assemblies, wiring harnesses, and bulbs; replace defective components or assemblies.

### Windshield Visibility

Owner reports frequently highlight recurring windshield stress cracks, which represent one of the most prominent body and visibility complaints for this model year.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Cracks appearing across the windshield without any clear impact from rocks or road debris, often starting near edges or wiper areas.
- **Solution:** Replace the windshield with a quality aftermarket or OEM glass unit, ensuring proper installation alignment.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V587000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Impreza&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the low-pressure fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V264000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Impreza&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The Engine Control Module (ECM) may continue to power the ignition coil after the engine is shut off, causing a short circuit. A short circuit in the ignition coils may cause a stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will update the ECM software, replace the ignition coils and, if necessary, the front exhaust pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V218000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Impreza&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The low pressure fuel pump may become inoperative. If the low pressure fuel pump fails, the engine may stall without the ability to restart the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will replace the low pressure fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V743000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Impreza&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The Engine Control Module (ECM) may continue to power the ignition coil after the engine is shut off, which could result in a short circuit and blown fuse. If a short circuit occurs while driving, the vehicle may stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will update the ECM software and inspect and replace the ignition coil, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V624000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Impreza&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Reflectors that do not provide adequate illumination reduce the driver's ability to see and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front headlamp assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Subaru Impreza remains a compelling choice for buyers seeking secure all-wheel-drive capability in a compact footprint. Prospective owners should thoroughly inspect the condition of the windshield, test all exterior lighting functions, and verify proper powertrain operation during a pre-purchase test drive.
