# 2024 Subaru Impreza Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Subaru Impreza introduces a redesigned hatchback lineup featuring standard all-wheel drive and updated technology. While early owner feedback highlights its improved handling and practical interior, shoppers should review initial owner-reported concerns ranging from driver-assistance quirks to exterior mirror vibrations. Most mechanical components remain fresh under factory warranties, but a thorough pre-purchase inspection helps catch any early production inconsistencies.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.0-liter horizontally opposed four-cylinder engine produces approximately 152 horsepower and 145 lb-ft of torque. It delivers reliable everyday efficiency paired with Subaru's signature symmetrical all-wheel drive.

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The larger 2.5-liter horizontally opposed four-cylinder engine boosts output to roughly 182 horsepower and 176 lb-ft of torque for quicker acceleration. It generally offers solid long-term performance, though minor sealing issues can occasionally appear early on.

**Valve Cover Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Oil leaks develop around the spark plug tube seals and valve cover area, sometimes appearing at relatively low mileage.
- **Solution:** Have the dealership inspect and reseal the affected valve covers and replace damaged spark plug tube seals under warranty.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The standard Lineartronic continuously variable transmission features a manual-mode paddle shifter setup across various trim levels to maximize fuel economy. It provides smooth power delivery for daily commuting, though routine fluid maintenance remains essential.

## Suspension

### Factory Tires

Suspension components absorb road impacts effectively, but original equipment tires have shown susceptibility to early structural wear.

**Premature Sidewall Cracking and Bubbling**
- **Symptoms:** Original equipment tires develop unexpected sidewall bulges, structural cracks, or sudden failures at very low mileage.
- **Solution:** Inspect tire sidewalls regularly for deformation and replace compromised tires promptly with quality replacements.

## Exterior

### Side Rearview Mirrors

Exterior panels and glass are generally well assembled, though mirror vibration is a recurring complaint among early owners.

**Driver Side Mirror Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** The driver-side rearview mirror vibrates noticeably at speeds over 25 to 50 mph, making the reflection blurry and difficult to use.
- **Solution:** Request dealership evaluation to determine if the mirror housing or glass insert requires replacement or adjustment.

## Safety

### EyeSight Pre-Collision Braking System

Advanced driver-assistance technologies offer helpful safety nets, but sensor misinterpretations can occasionally lead to phantom braking events.

**Unintended Forward Collision Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle unexpectedly triggers emergency pre-collision braking while traveling at moderate speeds when no imminent hazard or obstacle exists.
- **Solution:** Have a certified service center check for EyeSight camera calibration updates or sensor alignment adjustments.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V551000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Impreza&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Due to insufficient clearance, the instrument panel harness may contact the steering beam bracket, damaging the wire insulation and causing a short circuit. A short circuit may cause a loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the harness and install an additional clamp on the steering beam bracket. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V754000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Impreza&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Either of these scenarios can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the left and right front driveshafts as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Subaru Impreza is a capable and modern hatchback option, but buyers should test all driver-assistance systems and check for minor cosmetic or tire-related quirks during a pre-purchase inspection. Because many early vehicles remain covered by factory warranties, any initial production issues can often be addressed promptly by a dealer.
