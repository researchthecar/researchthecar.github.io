# 2021 Subaru Impreza Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Subaru Impreza is a practical compact car offering standard all-wheel drive, making it a popular choice for buyers seeking all-weather capability. While generally modern and well-equipped, owners have reported a handful of recurring mechanical and electronic concerns that potential buyers should keep in mind during an inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Horizontally-Opposed 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2.0-liter 4-cylinder boxer engine produces 152 horsepower and 145 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting. It delivers predictable performance, though owners should monitor routine maintenance and fluid levels carefully.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The Lineartronic continuously variable transmission is paired with Subaru's symmetrical all-wheel-drive system to maximize fuel efficiency. It operates smoothly in daily traffic, though routine fluid inspections are recommended to ensure long-term durability.

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission

The 5-speed manual transmission is available on select lower trims for drivers who prefer a traditional shift experience. It offers straightforward mechanical operation, though regular clutch checks are advised.

## Suspension

### Tires

Suspension and tire components should be inspected regularly to catch uneven wear or tire defects early.

**Premature Tire Bulges and Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected sidewall bulges, knots, or rapid tread wear on factory-equipped tires under normal driving conditions.
- **Solution:** Inspect tire tread depth and sidewalls for any structural abnormalities during routine maintenance.

## Steering

### Steering Column and Tracking

Steering performance is generally responsive, though alignment checks are recommended if tracking issues arise.

**Wandering Steering and Loose Feel**
- **Symptoms:** Some drivers notice that the vehicle tends to drift slightly to the left or right on the highway, lacking a firm center feel and requiring frequent steering corrections.
- **Solution:** Perform a professional wheel alignment and inspect the steering column components for play or looseness.

## Exterior

### Windshield

Exterior glass components require close inspection, as stress-related cracks have been noted by multiple drivers.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced sudden cracking across the front windshield, often originating near the defroster area or top center edge without any direct impact from debris.
- **Solution:** Inspect the windshield thoroughly before purchase and consult a dealer regarding potential coverage if stress cracks appear.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Subaru's EyeSight driver assist suite provides advanced safety features, but occasional sensor calibration issues can lead to unexpected system responses.

**EyeSight System False Braking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported instances where the adaptive cruise control or collision warning system suddenly applied hard braking when approaching slowing traffic or on clear stretches of highway.
- **Solution:** Have the dealer check for software updates or sensor calibration adjustments if erratic braking behavior occurs.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V675000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm:Lower Arm](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Impreza&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A separated control arm may cause the tire to contact the wheel well, resulting in a loss of control and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the control arm as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V024000: Power Train:Shift Linkage/Cable/Rod](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Impreza&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The continuously variable transmission (CVT) select lever cable nut may not have been tightened properly, allowing the nut to loosen. A loose nut can lead to a lost of retention in the CVT select lever cable and the gear selector not functioning properly, increasing risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will inspect, and, if necessary, tighten the nut, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Subaru Impreza remains an attractive option for used-car buyers looking for affordable all-wheel drive. While owners have reported specific complaints regarding windshield durability and driver-assist behavior, a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help ensure the vehicle you are considering is in solid condition.
