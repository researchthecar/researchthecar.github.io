# 2017 Subaru Impreza Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Subaru Impreza delivers practical all-wheel-drive capability, respectable fuel economy, and a redesigned platform that improved cabin space and ride comfort. However, used car buyers should approach this model year with careful consideration due to a cluster of reported owner grievances. Common trouble spots include fragile windshields, headlight and electrical concerns, and premature wear on components like wheel bearings.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

Equipped with a 2.0-liter direct-injection 4-cylinder boxer engine producing roughly 152 horsepower, this power plant provides standard symmetrical all-wheel drive. While generally efficient, buyers should check for historical ECM or ignition updates.

**Engine Stalling and Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience sudden hesitation or unexpected stalling while operating at low speeds or coming to a stop.
- **Solution:** Diagnostics may require checking the Engine Control Module (ECM), battery connections, or addressing related ignition coil service history.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic CVT Automatic / 5-Speed Manual

Power is routed through either a 5-speed manual transmission or a Lineartronic continuously variable transmission (CVT). The powertrain provides smooth power delivery for daily commuting, though manual models have occasionally reported clutch release bearing concerns.

## Suspension

### Wheel Bearings and Hubs

The suspension and wheel hub assemblies provide a composed and stable ride, though premature rear and front wheel bearing wear is a documented pattern among owners.

**Premature Wheel Bearing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers often notice a humming or roaring noise from the wheels that increases with vehicle speed, indicating worn bearing assemblies.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed wheel bearing hub assembly, ideally utilizing updated revised parts if available.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical system reports frequently highlight headlight performance issues, ranging from premature bulb burnout to total assembly failure.

**Headlight Assembly Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights may intermittently flicker, fail completely, or trigger dashboard warning messages regarding the headlight assemblies.
- **Solution:** Inspect wiring harnesses and replace faulty headlight bulbs or entire headlamp assemblies as required.

### Windshield Glass

Exterior panels are generally durable, though the front windshield is a frequent point of frustration for owners due to its susceptibility to cracking.

**Excessive Windshield Chipping and Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** The windshield is prone to accumulating micro-pits or developing long cracks rapidly after minor road debris impacts.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged windshield with a quality aftermarket or OEM glass. Vehicles equipped with EyeSight driver-assist technology will require professional camera recalibration.

## Safety

### Airbag System

Safety equipment includes modern airbag arrays and available driver-assist suites, though some owner complaints point toward sensor faults and SRS warning lights.

**Airbag Warning and Deployment Irregularities**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience persistent airbag warning lights on the dashboard or, in severe cases, failure of airbags to deploy during an impact.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician scan the Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) module, seat sensors, and wiring harness for fault codes.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V264000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Impreza&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The Engine Control Module (ECM) may continue to power the ignition coil after the engine is shut off, causing a short circuit. A short circuit in the ignition coils may cause a stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will update the ECM software, replace the ignition coils and, if necessary, the front exhaust pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V744000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Exhaust System:Emission Control:Crankcase (Pcv)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Impreza&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** An unexpected loss of power while driving can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the PCV valve, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V745000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Exhaust System:Emission Control:Crankcase (Pcv)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Impreza&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** An unexpected loss of power while driving can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will replace the engine, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V743000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Impreza&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The Engine Control Module (ECM) may continue to power the ignition coil after the engine is shut off, which could result in a short circuit and blown fuse. If a short circuit occurs while driving, the vehicle may stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will update the ECM software and inspect and replace the ignition coil, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V216000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Impreza&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the engine control unit to turn the radiator cooling fan on at a lower temperature, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V132000: Back Over Prevention: Camera System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Impreza&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Due to a software problem, the rearview camera display may not work properly. A rearview camera display that does not function as designed can reduce the driver's view of what is behind the car, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will update the infotainment software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V624000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Impreza&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Reflectors that do not provide adequate illumination reduce the driver's ability to see and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front headlamp assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Subaru Impreza offers great utility, confident handling in inclement weather, and a comfortable cabin, making it an appealing used car choice. However, prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle for known trouble areas such as wheel bearing noise, headlight operation, and windshield condition. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by an independent mechanic is strongly recommended to catch any lingering mechanical or electrical issues before purchase.
