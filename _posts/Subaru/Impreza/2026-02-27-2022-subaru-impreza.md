# 2022 Subaru Impreza Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2022 Subaru Impreza, buyers will find a practical compact car known for standard symmetrical all-wheel drive and everyday usability. While most owners enjoy a comfortable commute, a review of owner complaints reveals a few areas worth inspecting before purchase. Potential buyers should carefully evaluate the vehicle history and perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on visibility, electrical health, and powertrain performance.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Boxer Engine

The 2.0-liter horizontally opposed four-cylinder engine produces 152 horsepower and 145 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate power for daily commuting while maintaining decent fuel economy. Overall, this engine is generally reliable when properly maintained.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The Lineartronic continuously variable transmission is paired standard with Subaru's symmetrical all-wheel-drive system across the lineup. It delivers smooth power delivery during normal driving, though buyers should watch for any hesitation or unusual mechanical noises during a test drive.

**Transmission Failure and Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported sudden vehicle immobilization, burning smells, illumination of warning lights, or unexpected wheel lockups while driving.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnostic evaluation by a certified technician; repairs may involve component replacement or transmission overhaul depending on the severity.

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 5-speed manual transmission is available on base trim levels for drivers who prefer a direct shifting experience. It features standard clutch operation and is generally straightforward, though less common in the pre-owned market.

## Brakes

### Brake Lines

Brake system integrity is critical, and routine inspection of lines and pads will help catch potential hydraulic issues early.

**Brake Line Ballooning**
- **Symptoms:** Rear brake lines may exhibit abnormal swelling or ballooning under pressure, which can compromise braking performance if left unaddressed.
- **Solution:** Inspect flexible brake hoses and metal lines during routine maintenance and replace any compromised components immediately.

## Steering

### Steering Assembly

Steering feedback should be evaluated during a test drive to ensure the vehicle tracks straight and responds predictably to steering inputs.

**Over-Sensitive or Loose Steering Feel**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have noted that the steering can feel overly sensitive, disconnected, or loose, causing the vehicle to wander slightly at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Check alignment, steering rack components, and electronic power steering calibration during a test drive.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Battery System

Electrical complaints are infrequent but serious when they occur, underscoring the importance of checking battery health and electrical connections during an inspection.

**Battery Failure and Fire Risk**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced sudden electrical failures, battery malfunctions, and in rare instances, severe thermal events under the hood.
- **Solution:** Have the battery, charging system, and wiring harnesses inspected by a qualified professional to ensure proper electrical integrity.

## Exterior

### Windshield Glass

Owner reports highlight occasional spontaneous glass cracking that can impair visibility, making a careful visual check of the windshield essential during a used car inspection.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Windshields have been reported to develop sudden cracks—often starting near the base or middle—without any direct impact from road debris.
- **Solution:** Inspect the windshield thoroughly before purchase and replace the glass if cracks obstruct driver visibility.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V712000: Visibility:Windshield](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Impreza&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 212, "Windshield Mounting." An insufficiently bonded windshield can detach during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Subaru Impreza remains an attractive choice for used car buyers seeking secure all-wheel-drive capability and practical everyday styling. While owner reports point to potential concerns involving CVT operation, windshield durability, and rare electrical or brake line issues, a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection can help mitigate these risks. Prospective buyers should verify vehicle service records and test drive the vehicle carefully to ensure all systems operate as expected.
