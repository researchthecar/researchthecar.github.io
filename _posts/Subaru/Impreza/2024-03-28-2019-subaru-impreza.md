**The 2019 Subaru Impreza is a compact car that is available in sedan and hatchback body styles. It is powered by a 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine that produces 152 horsepower and 145 lb-ft of torque. The Impreza is available with either a five-speed manual transmission or a continuously variable transmission (CVT).**

**People like the Impreza for its affordability, fuel efficiency, and all-wheel drive capability. However, some people have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, and interior.**

### **Engine**
- **2.0-liter**

  - **Premature Engine Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: The engine may fail prematurely due to a manufacturing defect in the piston rings. This can cause the engine to lose compression and power, and eventually fail completely.
    - Solution: The engine will need to be replaced.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
    - Recalls: NHTSA Recall Number 19V-477

- **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    - Description/Symptoms: The engine may consume excessive amounts of oil, which can lead to low oil levels and engine damage.
    - Solution: The engine will need to be repaired or replaced.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

### **Transmission**

- **CVT**

  - **Slipping Gears**
    - Description/Symptoms: The CVT may slip gears, causing the vehicle to lose power and acceleration.
    - Solution: The CVT will need to be repaired or replaced.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

- **Manual Transmission**

  - **Grinding Gears**
    - Description/Symptoms: The manual transmission may grind gears when shifting, causing difficulty shifting and premature wear on the transmission components.
    - Solution: The transmission will need to be repaired or replaced.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

### **Suspension**

- **Front Suspension**

  - **Loose Control Arms**
    - Description/Symptoms: The front control arms may become loose, causing the vehicle to pull to one side and handle poorly.
    - Solution: The control arms will need to be replaced.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

### **Electrical**

- **Faulty Wiring Harness**
    - Description/Symptoms: The wiring harness may become damaged, causing electrical problems such as lights not working, the engine not starting, or the airbags not deploying.
    - Solution: The wiring harness will need to be repaired or replaced.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

### **Exterior**

- **Fog Lights**

  - **Condensation**
    - Description/Symptoms: The fog lights may become foggy or condensation may build up inside the lenses.
    - Solution: The fog lights may need to be replaced.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

### **Interior**

- **Climate Control**

  - **Stuck Vents**
    - Description/Symptoms: The vents may become stuck and unable to be adjusted, affecting the temperature and airflow inside the vehicle.
    - Solution: The vents will need to be repaired or replaced.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found

### **Summary**

The 2019 Subaru Impreza has been reported to have a number of issues, including engine failures, transmission problems, suspension issues, electrical problems, and interior issues. Some of these issues are more severe than others, and the cost to fix them can vary significantly. Overall, the Impreza is a reliable vehicle, but it is important to be aware of the potential issues that it may have.
