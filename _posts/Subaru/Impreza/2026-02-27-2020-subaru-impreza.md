# 2020 Subaru Impreza Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 Subaru Impreza, buyers will find a practical and efficient compact car featuring standard symmetrical all-wheel drive. While generally appreciated for its daily drivability and safety equipment, owners have reported a few notable recurring issues that prospective buyers should inspect carefully during a test drive.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

Equipped with a 2.0-liter horizontally opposed four-cylinder engine producing approximately 152 horsepower, this powertrain offers reliable efficiency for daily commuting. Routine fluid maintenance is key to long-term longevity.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Mathed with a continuously variable transmission, this setup prioritizes fuel economy across the model lineup. Smooth operation is expected, though buyers should verify seamless engagement during a test drive.

## Exterior

### Visibility

Exterior glass reports indicate potential susceptibility to stress-induced cracking along the lower windshield perimeter.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracks**
- **Symptoms:** Windshields develop long cracks originating near the bottom edges or wiper areas without any clear impact or debris strike.
- **Solution:** Inspect the windshield thoroughly before purchase and budget for professional glass replacement if cracks develop.

## Safety

### Forward Collision / Braking Control

Advanced safety and stability features generally enhance driver confidence, though occasional control unit sensor faults require professional diagnosis.

**ABS Control Unit and Pressure Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Activation of the EyeSight warning system, deactivation of vehicle dynamics control, and diagnostic trouble codes indicating internal pressure sensor failure.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician inspect the brake control module and replace faulty ABS hydraulic control units.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V587000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Impreza&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the low-pressure fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Subaru Impreza remains an appealing used compact option thanks to its standard all-wheel drive and balanced performance. Buyers should carefully check the condition of the windshield for stress cracks and verify that all electronic safety systems and brake modules operate without warning lights.
