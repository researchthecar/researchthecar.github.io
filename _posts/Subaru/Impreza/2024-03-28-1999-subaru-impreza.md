**Introduction:**

The 1999 Subaru Impreza is a compact car available in sedan and coupe body styles. It comes standard with a 2.2-liter naturally aspirated engine producing 142 horsepower and 152 pound-feet of torque. The 2.5-liter turbocharged engine is optional, producing 227 horsepower and 226 pound-feet of torque. The Impreza offers a variety of transmission options, including a 5-speed manual, 4-speed automatic, and 5-speed automatic.

Drivers appreciate the Impreza's all-wheel drive system, which provides excellent traction in all weather conditions. The car's handling is also praised, with sharp steering and a well-balanced chassis. However, some drivers have reported issues with the Impreza's interior quality, which is known to be sparse and lacking in features. Overall, the 1999 Subaru Impreza is a reliable and capable compact car that offers a good value for its price.

**Engine**

**2.2-Liter Naturally Aspirated Engine**

* **Head gasket failure:** Occurs when the head gasket fails to seal properly, allowing coolant and oil to mix. Symptoms include overheating, loss of power, and a white or milky substance in the oil. The solution is to replace the head gasket. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $1,500+
* **Timing belt failure:** Occurs when the timing belt breaks or slips, causing the engine to lose timing. Symptoms include a sudden loss of power, a ticking or rattling noise from the engine, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the timing belt and any damaged components. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+
* **Valve cover gasket leak:** Occurs when the valve cover gasket fails to seal properly, allowing oil to leak from the engine. Symptoms include an oil leak and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the valve cover gasket. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $300+
* **Intake manifold gasket leak:** Occurs when the intake manifold gasket fails to seal properly, allowing air to enter the engine. Symptoms include a rough idle, loss of power, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $400+
* **Crankshaft position sensor failure:** Occurs when the crankshaft position sensor fails, preventing the engine from starting. Symptoms include a no-start condition and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the crankshaft position sensor. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $300+

**2.5-Liter Turbocharged Engine**

* **Turbocharger failure:** Occurs when the turbocharger fails to operate properly, resulting in a loss of power. Symptoms include a lack of boost, a whistling or whining noise from the engine, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the turbocharger. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $2,000+
* **Intercooler leak:** Occurs when the intercooler leaks, preventing it from cooling the intake air. Symptoms include a loss of power, a knocking or pinging noise from the engine, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the intercooler or the leaking component. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+
* **Fuel injector failure:** Occurs when a fuel injector fails to deliver the correct amount of fuel to the engine. Symptoms include a rough idle, loss of power, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the failed fuel injector. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $300+
* **Knock sensor failure:** Occurs when the knock sensor fails to detect engine knock, resulting in a loss of power. Symptoms include a lack of power, a knocking or pinging noise from the engine, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the knock sensor. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $200+
* **Mass airflow sensor failure:** Occurs when the mass airflow sensor fails to measure the amount of air flowing into the engine. Symptoms include a rough idle, loss of power, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the mass airflow sensor. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $300+

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Transmission fluid leak:** Occurs when the transmission fluid leaks from the transmission. Symptoms include a loss of transmission fluid, difficulty shifting gears, and a whining or grinding noise from the transmission. The solution is to identify and repair the source of the leak. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500+
* **Synchronizer failure:** Occurs when the synchronizer fails to engage the gears properly, resulting in a grinding noise when shifting. The solution is to replace the synchronizer. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $500+
