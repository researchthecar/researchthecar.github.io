## 2011 Subaru Impreza

The 2011 Subaru Impreza is a compact car available in sedan and hatchback body styles. It offers a naturally aspirated 2.5-liter 4-cylinder engine that produces 170 horsepower and 170 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Impreza is known for its all-wheel-drive system, which provides excellent traction in all weather conditions. It also has a spacious interior and a comfortable ride. However, the Impreza has received some criticism for its lack of power and its outdated infotainment system.

**Engine**

* **2.5L 4-cylinder (manual transmission)**
    * **Oil leaks:** Leaks can occur from the valve cover gaskets or the oil pan gasket. Symptoms include low oil levels and oil spots under the car. The fix involves replacing the leaking gaskets. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400
    * **Timing belt tensioner failure:** The timing belt tensioner can fail prematurely, causing the timing belt to skip or break. This can lead to catastrophic engine damage. The solution is to replace the timing belt and tensioner. Estimated cost to fix: $800 - $1200
* **2.5L 4-cylinder (automatic transmission)**
    * **Transmission fluid leaks:** Leaks can occur from the transmission pan gasket or the axle seals. Symptoms include low transmission fluid levels and transmission slipping. The fix involves replacing the leaking gaskets or seals. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $300

**Transmission**

* **5-speed manual transmission:**
    * **Grinding gears:** Grinding gears can be caused by worn synchronizers or a faulty clutch. Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears or grinding noises when shifting. The fix involves replacing the worn parts. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1000
* **4-speed automatic transmission**
    * **Delayed shifts:** Delayed shifts can be caused by a faulty solenoid or a worn transmission filter. Symptoms include slow or delayed gear changes. The fix involves replacing the faulty solenoid or filter. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400

**Suspension**

* **Front struts:** The front struts can wear out prematurely, causing a loss of ride quality and handling. Symptoms include abouncy ride, excessive body roll, and uneven tire wear. The fix involves replacing the struts. Estimated cost to fix: $400 - $800
* **Rear shocks:** The rear shocks can also wear out prematurely, causing a similar loss of ride quality and handling. The fix involves replacing the shocks. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail prematurely, leading to a loss of electrical power. Symptoms include a dim dashboard, flickering lights, and a dead battery. The fix involves replacing the alternator. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $800
* **Battery drain:** The battery can drain prematurely if there is a parasitic draw on the electrical system. Symptoms include a dead battery after the car has been parked for a few days. The fi involves finding and fixing the source of the parasitic draw. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $300

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** The Impreza is susceptible to rust, especially in areas where salt is used on the roads. Symptoms include rust spots on the body panels, frame, and exhaust system. The fix involves repairing or replacing the rusted parts. Estimated cost to fix: Varies depending on the severity of the rust
* **Headlight condensation:** Condensation can form inside the headlights, which can reduce visibility at night. The fix involves replacing the headlights or repairing the seals. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400

**Interior**

* **Seat heater failure:** The seat heaters can fail prematurely, leaving you cold in the winter. Symptoms include a lack of heat from the seat heaters. The fix involves replacing the heating elements. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400
* **Dashboard rattles:** The dashboard can rattle over rough roads. Symptoms include a clicking or rattling noise coming from the dashboard. The fix involves tightening or replacing the loose components. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $200

**Summary**

The 2011 Subaru Impreza is a reliable and capable car, but it is not without its issues. The most common problems include engine oil leaks, timing belt tensioner failure, transmission fluid leaks, and suspension wear. These issues can be costly to fix, so it is important to factor them into your decision when buying an Impreza.
