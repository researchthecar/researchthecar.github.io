# 2016 Subaru Impreza Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Subaru Impreza, buyers generally find a practical compact car offering standard all-wheel drive and sensible fuel economy. However, reviewing owner-submitted complaints reveals a few recurring trouble spots that prospective buyers should inspect closely before purchasing, particularly related to electrical accessories, ignition key retention, and transmission performance.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L H4

The 2.0-liter horizontally opposed four-cylinder engine produces approximately 148 horsepower and 145 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate power for daily commuting paired with standard symmetrical all-wheel drive, though owners occasionally report rare stalling incidents.

### Ignition Lock Cylinder

Various convenience and control components can experience minor mechanical or electrical hitches, particularly regarding the key release mechanism.

**Stuck Ignition Key**
- **Symptoms:** The ignition key becomes stuck in the lock cylinder after placing the vehicle in park, refusing to turn to the off position or release.
- **Solution:** Inspect and adjust or replace the shift lock mechanism or ignition switch assembly.
- **Cost:** $700

## Transmission

### Lineartronic CVT

The continuously variable transmission (CVT) routes power to all four wheels. While generally smooth in everyday driving, some owners have reported unexpected powertrain hesitation and stalling concerns.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience sudden acceleration cutouts, jerking motions, or engine stalling while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis of the transmission system and software updates or component replacement where applicable.

### 5-Speed Manual

The traditional 5-speed manual transmission offers direct gear control for drivers who prefer a manual setup, typically demonstrating solid mechanical durability when maintained properly.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical system reports commonly highlight exterior lighting challenges, most notably short-lived headlight bulbs.

**Premature Headlight Bulb Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Headlight bulbs burn out repeatedly within short intervals, requiring frequent replacements.
- **Solution:** Inspect wiring harnesses and connectors for voltage spikes or moisture intrusion; replace bulbs with quality units.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V149000: Exterior Lighting:Brake Lights:Switch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Impreza&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Exposure to certain contaminants may cause the brake light switch to malfunction, preventing the brake lights from illuminating and also preventing keyless ignition vehicles from starting and CVT/automatic transmissions from being able to be shifted out of Park. Brake lights that do not illuminate properly will not alert other drivers that the vehicle is.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will replace the brake light switch, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The Subaru Impreza remains an appealing option for buyers seeking year-round traction and practical utility. However, used car shoppers should thoroughly check the operation of the ignition key release, test all exterior lights, and pay close attention to transmission smoothness during a test drive to ensure no hidden powertrain hesitation is present.
