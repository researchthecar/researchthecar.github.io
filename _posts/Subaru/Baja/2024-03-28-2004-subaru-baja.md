**Introduction:**

The 2004 Subaru Baja is a compact pickup truck that combines the versatility of a pickup with the agility of a car. It is available with a 2.5-liter naturally aspirated engine producing 165 horsepower and 166 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.0-liter naturally aspirated engine generating 220 horsepower and 214 lb-ft of torque. Both engines can be paired with either a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Baja is known for its rugged exterior, comfortable interior, and versatile cargo bed. However, it has also been associated with some common issues.

**Engine**

**2.5L Engine**

- **Head gasket failure:** Blown head gaskets can cause coolant leaks, overheating, and engine damage.
    - Solution: Replace the head gaskets.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
- **Oil consumption:** Some engines may consume excessive oil, leading to engine wear and failure.
    - Solution: Replace the piston rings or overhaul the engine.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
- **Timing chain tensioner failure:** A faulty timing chain tensioner can cause the timing chain to skip or break, resulting in engine damage.
    - Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**3.0L Engine**

- **No major issues found**

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

- **Clutch failure:** The clutch disc or pressure plate can fail, causing difficulty shifting or loss of power.
    - Solution: Replace the clutch components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

- **Shift solenoid failure:** Malfunctioning shift solenoids can cause shifting problems or transmission failure.
    - Solution: Replace the shift solenoids.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500

**Suspension**

- **Strut mount failure:** Worn strut mounts can cause noise, vibration, and unstable handling.
    - Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Electrical**

- **Alternator failure:** A faulty alternator can fail to charge the battery, leading to electrical problems.
    - Solution: Replace the alternator.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $700

**Exterior**

- **Tailgate handle failure:** The tailgate handle may break, making it difficult to open the tailgate.
    - Solution: Replace the tailgate handle.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300

**Interior**

- **Dashboard cracking:** The dashboard may develop cracks over time.
    - Solution: Replace the dashboard or use a dash cover.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000

**Summary**

The 2004 Subaru Baja is generally a reliable vehicle, but it is not without its issues. Engine problems, such as head gasket failure and oil consumption, can be serious and expensive to repair. Transmission issues, including clutch and shift solenoid failures, can also be costly. However, other issues, such as suspension and electrical problems, are typically less severe and less expensive to fix. Overall, the Baja is a capable and versatile vehicle, but potential buyers should be aware of the potential issues associated with it.
