# 2019 Subaru WRX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 Subaru WRX, buyers should weigh its performance credentials against reported owner concerns. While many drivers enjoy its engaging handling and turbocharged power, owner feedback highlights potential trouble spots ranging from electrical battery drains to engine and transmission hiccups. Reviewing maintenance records and performing a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help uncover hidden issues before you buy.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Boxer 4-Cylinder (FA20F)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder boxer engine (FA20F) produces 268 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, offering lively acceleration. Buyers should monitor for oil leaks and fuel system reliability during inspections.

**Oil Leaks from Cam Carriers**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note oil dripping or seeping from the cam carriers, typically around the 75k to 90k-mile mark.
- **Solution:** Have the cam carrier seals inspected and re-sealed by a qualified technician if leaks are detected.

**Fuel Pump Cutoff and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent fuel pump failure can lead to sudden hesitation or loss of motive power while driving.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the fuel pump and related electrical delivery components, replacing the faulty pump if necessary.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The standard 6-speed manual transmission provides a direct and engaging connection for enthusiast drivers. Buyers should thoroughly test-shift through all gears to ensure smooth engagement.

**Gear Engagement Difficulty**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional difficulty shifting into specific gears, such as second gear, while other gears engage normally.
- **Solution:** Inspect the transmission linkage, clutch assembly, and syncros for wear or misalignment.

### Sport Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The available Sport Lineartronic CVT features simulated gear steps and paddle shifters for a more automatic driving experience. Regular fluid maintenance is essential to ensure long-term durability.

## Steering

### Power Steering

Steering performance is generally sharp, though rare electronic glitches affecting power assist have been reported by owners.

**Intermittent Power Steering Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Power steering assist cuts out unexpectedly while driving, making the steering wheel very heavy until the car is restarted.
- **Solution:** Scan the steering control module for fault codes and inspect electronic power steering connections.

## Electrical

### Electrical System & Battery Drain

Electrical complaints primarily center around parasitic battery draws that can leave the vehicle dead if left parked for extended periods.

**Telematics and Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle experiences dead batteries after sitting for short periods due to excessive current draw from components like the DCM telematics system or dome lights.
- **Solution:** Test for parasitic electrical draws, update or disconnect faulty telematics modules, and replace weak batteries.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V587000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=WRX&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the low-pressure fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V522000: Exterior Lighting:Back Up Lights:Switch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=WRX&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Inoperative backup lights and an inoperative rearview camera can reduce vehicle visibility and driver awareness, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the backup light switch, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Subaru WRX offers compelling performance and handling, but prospective buyers should approach with eyes open regarding potential electrical drains and minor powertrain quirks. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection focusing on the engine bay, battery health, and transmission shift quality is strongly recommended to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
