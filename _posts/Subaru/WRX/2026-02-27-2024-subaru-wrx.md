# 2024 Subaru WRX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2024 Subaru WRX, buyers will find a sporty and engaging sedan powered by a turbocharged boxer engine. While owner complaints are relatively sparse, early reports highlight a few distinct electronic annoyances and mechanical concerns. Prospective buyers should carefully inspect powertrain components and test all interior features during a pre-purchase test drive.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Turbocharged Boxer 4-Cylinder

The 2.4-liter turbocharged horizontally opposed 4-cylinder engine produces approximately 271 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. It delivers punchy performance for the sport sedan segment, though buyers should monitor fluid levels closely during early ownership.

**Early Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** The low oil warning light illuminates unexpectedly during normal driving within the first few thousand miles.
- **Solution:** Check oil levels regularly and top off as needed while monitoring for any persistent consumption trends.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission pairs with symmetrical all-wheel drive to offer a direct, engaging driving experience for enthusiast drivers.

**Shifter Gate Misalignment or Internal Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The shifter may bypass expected gates—such as moving toward reverse from 6th gear—or suffer internal mechanical failure accompanied by grinding noises and loss of power.
- **Solution:** Inspect the shifter linkage and transmission assembly; damaged internal components require professional repair or replacement.

### Sport Performance Automatic (CVT)

The available automatic transmission uses a continuously variable design tuned for performance driving, offering paddle shifters and simulated gear steps.

## Brakes

### Anti-Lock Brakes and Stability Control

Advanced safety systems integrate with Subaru's EyeSight suite to protect occupants, though sensor calibration and brake logic must operate smoothly.

**Inappropriate ABS Activation**
- **Symptoms:** The anti-lock braking system or stability controls may independently engage during routine lane changes or low-speed maneuvers on slick surfaces.
- **Solution:** Have wheel speed sensors and stability control modules inspected by a qualified technician if unexpected braking occurs.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System

The large touchscreen infotainment system handles most cabin controls, though software interruptions can be a distraction for drivers.

**Intrusive Promotional Pop-Ups**
- **Symptoms:** Full-screen satellite radio advertisements take over the infotainment display while driving, blocking access to climate controls, navigation, and driver assist camera readouts until manually dismissed.
- **Solution:** Check for available software updates or infotainment settings that may reduce promotional notifications.

## Exterior

### Mirrors and Body Trim

Exterior styling features aggressive body cladding and sport mirrors, though minor quality control issues with glass and trim have been noted by owners.

**Distorted Side Mirror Glass**
- **Symptoms:** The driver-side mirror glass exhibits a wavy or distorted reflection resembling a funhouse mirror.
- **Solution:** Replace the affected mirror glass assembly under warranty if applicable.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Subaru WRX remains a compelling option for buyers seeking all-wheel-drive performance, but used-car shoppers should perform thorough inspections. Pay close attention to manual transmission shift gate operation, check fluid levels for early oil consumption tendencies, and verify that infotainment software operates without distracting pop-ups.
