# 2022 Subaru WRX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2022 Subaru WRX, buyers should review owner-reported experiences across various vehicle systems. While owner complaints highlight some isolated electrical, body, and safety-assist concerns, reviewing individual vehicle history and condition remains essential for any used purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Turbocharged Flat-4 (FA24F)

The 2.4-liter turbocharged horizontally opposed four-cylinder engine produces approximately 271 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. It provides strong standard performance across all trims, though proper maintenance and warm-ups are recommended for longevity.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission offers direct gear selection for enthusiast drivers. It is generally robust when not subjected to excessive abuse or modified power levels.

### Subaru Performance Transmission (CVT)

The optional automatic continuously variable transmission features manual-simulation shift paddles and sport-tuned logic. Buyers prioritizing an automatic should test drive to ensure the shift response meets their expectations.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System

Electrical complaints primarily center around intermittent screen blanking and infotainment responsiveness.

**Blank Infotainment Screen**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen display may periodically go blank while the audio system continues to play sound, temporarily restricting access to climate and camera controls.
- **Solution:** Restart the vehicle or perform an infotainment system reboot if unresponsive; dealer software updates may also address stability.

## Exterior

### Hood Panel

Body panel complaints are minimal, though minor exterior assembly quirks have been reported.

**Visual Hood Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** The hood panel may exhibit noticeable visual flutter or vibration when driving at highway speeds or traveling over minor road bumps.
- **Solution:** Inspect and adjust the hood latch stoppers to ensure the panel is tightly secured when closed.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Owner reports note occasional sensitivity with the EyeSight camera and driver-assist suites.

**EyeSight System False Activation**
- **Symptoms:** The automatic emergency braking or pre-collision system can occasionally trigger unexpectedly when encountering environmental elements such as heavy exhaust smoke in cold weather.
- **Solution:** Ensure camera lenses and sensors are clean and check with a dealer for any available software calibrations.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V016000: Equipment:Other:Owners/Service/Other Manual](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=WRX&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Incorrect instructions for adjusting the sensitivity of the High Beam Assist function contained in the owner's manual may cause the lights to not be adjusted as intended, increasing the risk.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Subaru WRX delivers engaging performance with its standard turbocharged boxer engine and standard all-wheel drive. While owner-reported complaints highlight minor grievances regarding infotainment stability and driver-assist sensor sensitivity, the vehicle exhibits no widespread catastrophic mechanical defects in owner data. Prospective buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection to verify proper mechanical and electronic operation.
