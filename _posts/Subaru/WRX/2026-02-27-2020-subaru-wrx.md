# 2020 Subaru WRX

### Overview
The 2020 Subaru WRX, a performance-oriented compact sedan, generally offers a robust and engaging driving experience. It is powered by a potent turbocharged boxer engine, paired with either a manual or a continuously variable transmission (CVT). While known for its sporty handling and all-wheel-drive system, like any performance vehicle, it can encounter specific issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and prospective buyers. The data aims to highlight common mechanical and electrical faults that have been reported, offering guidance on potential repair costs and solutions.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Boxer (FA20DIT)
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger unit.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Low oil level between changes, blue smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Engine rebuild or piston ring replacement (if severe).
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $5,000+

**Coolant Leaks (Hoses/Pipes)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant leaks, overheating, sweet smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking hoses or pipes.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Clutch kit replacement (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500

**Shifter Linkage Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loose or sloppy shifter feel, difficulty engaging gears.
- **Solution:** Adjustment or replacement of shifter cables/bushings.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400


### CVT (TR690)
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid from seals or lines, potential overheating.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seals, lines, or transmission cooler.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,000

**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual acceleration behavior, jerky shifts.
- **Solution:** Software update, fluid change, or internal transmission repair/replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $4,000+

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 Subaru WRX is a capable performance car with a generally good reliability record for its class. The most common reported issues tend to revolve around the powertrain, particularly concerning the turbocharger, oil consumption in the 2.0L engine, and potential wear on manual transmission clutches. CVT models may experience fluid leaks or operational hesitations. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment system glitches and battery drain, are also noted. While suspension, exterior, and interior components are largely free of widespread defects, owners should be mindful of the specific needs of a turbocharged, performance-oriented vehicle. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to any unusual symptoms are crucial for long-term reliability.
