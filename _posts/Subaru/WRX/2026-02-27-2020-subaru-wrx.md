# 2020 Subaru WRX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Subaru WRX delivers engaging sport-sedan performance with standard symmetrical all-wheel drive, but buyers should approach the used market with a clear picture of owner-reported issues. While many drivers enjoy its turbocharged performance and nimble handling, owner complaints highlight potential concerns ranging from electrical battery drains to occasional drivetrain and glass durability issues. Thoroughly inspecting a used model's service history and electrical system can help avoid unexpected headaches.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Boxer 4-Cylinder (FA20F)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged direct-injection flat-four engine produces 268 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. It provides brisk acceleration for the base and Premium trims, though buyers should listen closely for unusual noises during test drives.

**Engine Knock and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may unexpectedly stall, become difficult to restart, and subsequently develop a severe knocking noise.
- **Solution:** Requires professional mechanical diagnosis, which may involve internal engine inspection or replacement depending on component damage.

### 2.5L Turbocharged Boxer 4-Cylinder (EJ257)

Exclusive to the high-performance STI variant, this 2.5-liter turbocharged boxer engine churns out 310 horsepower and 290 lb-ft of torque. It offers aggressive power delivery and a robust track-ready feel for driving enthusiasts.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual gearbox pairs with both engine options, offering direct mechanical engagement. It is generally favored by enthusiasts seeking a connected driving experience.

### Sport Lineartronic CVT

The optional Sport Lineartronic continuously variable transmission is available on the standard WRX and features paddle shifters. While convenient for daily commuting, buyers should check the underside for signs of fluid seepage.

**CVT Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission fluid may begin leaking from the area between the engine and the automatic transmission housing, often discovered during routine maintenance.
- **Solution:** Requires inspection by a qualified technician to replace failed seals or gaskets and restore proper fluid levels.

## Electrical

### Battery and Charging System

Electrical system complaints indicate that some owners experience premature battery depletion or intermittent starting problems that require diagnostic troubleshooting.

**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle battery may repeatedly lose charge after sitting parked for a day or less, leaving the car unable to start.
- **Solution:** Diagnose abnormal parasitic draws in the electrical system and replace the battery or faulty modules as needed.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Glass

Exterior glass integrity is a notable owner complaint area, with reports of spontaneous cracking on both front and rear windshields.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracks**
- **Symptoms:** The front windshield or rear glass may develop sudden stress cracks or fractures without any apparent impact from outside objects.
- **Solution:** Have the affected glass inspected and replaced by an auto glass professional.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety component checks should include inspecting interior restraint systems, as some owners have reported premature wear on rear seatbelts.

**Fraying Rear Seatbelts**
- **Symptoms:** Rear passenger seatbelt webbing may begin to fray prematurely during normal use.
- **Solution:** Inspect seatbelt assemblies and replace frayed belts to ensure proper occupant safety.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V522000: Exterior Lighting:Back Up Lights:Switch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=WRX&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Inoperative backup lights and an inoperative rearview camera can reduce vehicle visibility and driver awareness, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the backup light switch, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Subaru WRX is a thrilling and capable daily driver, but used buyers should exercise due diligence before purchasing. Check the service records carefully, verify that the battery and charging system hold a proper charge, and inspect the exterior glass and interior safety belts for signs of wear. Selecting a well-maintained example will help ensure a more reliable and enjoyable ownership experience.
