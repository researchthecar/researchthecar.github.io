**Introduction:**

The 2020 Subaru WRX is a performance-oriented sedan powered by a 2.0-liter turbocharged boxer engine that produces 268 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. It is paired with either a 6-speed manual transmission or a Sport Lineartronic CVT. The WRX is known for its handling, performance, and affordability but has also been reported to have some issues.

**Engine**

* **2.0-Liter Turbocharged Boxer Engine**

    * **Head Gasket Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Blown head gasket, oil leaking, coolant leaks, overheating.
        * Solution: Engine rebuild or replacement.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $7,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Timing Chain Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Rattling or ticking sounds from the engine, loss of power, engine stalling.
        * Solution: Replacing the timing chain and components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Manual Transmission:**

    * **Input Shaft Bearing Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Grinding or chattering noises while shifting, difficulty shifting into gear.
        * Solution: Replace input shaft bearing and related components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

* **Sport Lineartronic CVT:**

    * **Overheating:**
        * Description/Symptoms: CVT overheating, loss of power, limp mode.
        * Solution: Cooling system flush, CVT oil replacement, or CVT replacement.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $4,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery dies prematurely even on new batteries.
    * Solution: Replacing battery or diagnosing for electrical drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Dashboard Lights Intermittent:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dashboard lights, especially the check engine light, come on and go off intermittently.
    * Solution: Troubleshooting electrical system, checking for loose connections or faulty sensors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Leaks:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the cabin through the sunroof.
    * Solution: Replacing or realigning sunroof components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **HVAC Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Climate control stops working or blows lukewarm air.
    * Solution: Diagnosing and replacing HVAC components such as actuators, blowers, or compressors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Infotainment System Freezing:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Touchscreen not responding or freezing, system reboots randomly.
    * Solution: Software updates or replacing the infotainment unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2020 Subaru WRX has received generally positive reviews, but it does have some known issues that can be costly to repair. The engine and transmission are the most common areas of concern, with potential failures leading to expensive repairs. Electrical and interior problems, while not as prevalent, can also be annoying and time-consuming to fix. Some issues can be resolved with relatively minor repairs, while others may require major overhauls. It is important to consider these potential issues when making a decision about purchasing a 2020 Subaru WRX.
