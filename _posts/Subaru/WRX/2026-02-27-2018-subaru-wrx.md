# 2018 Subaru WRX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Subaru WRX, prospective buyers will find a sporty and engaging vehicle known for its distinctive turbocharged performance and all-wheel-drive capability. However, used car shoppers should review potential owner-reported areas of concern, particularly regarding electrical draws, fuel components, and transmission hardware.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Boxer 4-Cylinder (FA20F)

The 2018 Subaru WRX is powered by a 2.0-liter turbocharged direct-injection horizontally opposed four-cylinder engine (FA20F) producing roughly 268 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. It delivers punchy performance paired with standard symmetrical all-wheel drive, though buyers should verify maintenance history.

**Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences fuel delivery issues, warning indicators flashing on the dashboard, and potential starting or running difficulties.
- **Solution:** Inspect the fuel pump assembly and replace failed components as necessary.

**Fuel Vapor Odor**
- **Symptoms:** A strong raw gasoline vapor odor enters the passenger cabin, particularly noticeable near the driver's side rear seat and wheel well.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel lines, seals, and connections for leaks or degradation.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The standard 6-speed manual transmission provides direct gear selection suited for enthusiast driving, though some owners have experienced premature clutch and bearing wear.

**Throw-Out Bearing and Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Abnormal noises from the transmission area, premature clutch burning, or failure of the throw-out bearing and release fork.
- **Solution:** Replace the defective throw-out bearing, clutch fork, and clutch assembly with updated parts.

### Sport Lineartronic CVT Automatic Transmission

An available Sport Lineartronic CVT automatic transmission with manual shift modes offers relaxed daily driving convenience alongside the manual gearbox option.

**Transmission Fluid Leak and Slippage**
- **Symptoms:** Fluid pooling under the vehicle, burning smells, and noticeable slippage or hesitation during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Inspect the transmission casing and seals; unit replacement may be required if leaks are unserviceable.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Column

Steering system reports highlight rare instances of stiffness or binding during turning maneuvers that warrant a thorough pre-purchase inspection.

**Steering Wheel Binding or Seizing**
- **Symptoms:** Steering wheel stiffens, locks up unexpectedly during low-speed turns, or fails to return to center.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering rack, electronic power steering components, and steering column linkages.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Electrical system complaints indicate occasional challenges with parasitic power drains that can deplete the battery when parked.

**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery loses charge repeatedly during periods of non-use, leaving the vehicle inoperable even after battery replacement.
- **Solution:** Diagnose unauthorized electrical draws on the circuit and repair or replace faulty modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V587000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=WRX&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the low-pressure fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V522000: Exterior Lighting:Back Up Lights:Switch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=WRX&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Inoperative backup lights and an inoperative rearview camera can reduce vehicle visibility and driver awareness, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the backup light switch, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Subaru WRX remains an appealing option for buyers seeking an all-weather sports sedan with strong turbocharged performance. While most vehicles perform reliably when maintained properly, prospective owners should carefully check for potential battery drain issues, fuel odor concerns, and manual transmission clutch wear during a pre-purchase test drive.
