# 2021 Subaru WRX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Subaru WRX, buyers are generally looking at a performance-oriented sedan with a loyal enthusiast following. While these cars offer engaging driving dynamics and standard all-wheel drive, owner reports submitted to federal safety regulators point to a few recurring mechanical and electrical concerns that prospective buyers should inspect closely before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Boxer 4-Cylinder (FA20F)

The standard WRX features a 2.0-liter turbocharged direct-injection boxer four-cylinder engine producing 268 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. It offers spirited acceleration paired with standard symmetrical all-wheel drive, though modified examples require careful PPI scrutiny.

**Excessive Blow-By and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Oil contamination on the MAP sensor can lead to incorrect air-fuel mixtures, causing engine hesitation, loss of power, and potential stalling.
- **Solution:** Inspect the intake and MAP sensor for oil residue; clean or replace affected sensors and address underlying PCV or blow-by issues.

### 2.5L Turbocharged Boxer 4-Cylinder (EJ257 - WRX STI)

Exclusive to the high-performance STI variant, the 2.5-liter turbocharged boxer engine delivers 310 horsepower and 290 lb-ft of torque. Known for its aggressive track-ready capability, it demands meticulous maintenance and is frequently prone to modification-related wear.

**Catastrophic Engine Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of motive power, engine knocking, oil starvation, or complete mechanical breakdown while driving.
- **Solution:** Thoroughly review service records for oil change frequency, verify the vehicle has not been subjected to improper aftermarket ECM tunes, and perform a comprehensive pre-purchase compression and leak-down test.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The standard 6-speed manual transmission provides a direct, engaging connection for enthusiast drivers. It is generally robust, though clutch components and shifter linkages can experience wear under aggressive driving.

**Clutch Release Bearing and Linkage Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty shifting gears, abnormal noises when pressing the clutch pedal, or a complete failure of the shifter linkage or clutch release mechanism.
- **Solution:** Inspect the clutch pedal assembly, shifter linkage pins, and release bearing for abnormal wear or binding; replace damaged mechanical components as needed.

### Sport Lineartronic CVT

Available exclusively on the standard WRX, this continuously variable transmission features manual shift modes via paddle shifters. It appeals to commuters looking for automatic convenience, though performance drivers typically prefer the manual.

## Electrical

### Data Communication Module (DCM)

Manages infotainment, telematics, and vehicle connectivity features. Faulty modules can occasionally cause parasitic battery drains.

**Battery Drain and Electrical Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Car battery repeatedly dying or failing to hold a charge, resulting in intermittent loss of vehicle electronics.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the DCM system for parasitic draw and update or replace faulty telematics hardware causing excessive battery drain.

## Exterior

### Windshield Glass

Factory glass assemblies that may occasionally be susceptible to stress cracking under extreme temperature changes or minor impacts.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Cracks developing across the windshield originating near the base, often expanding rapidly without significant initial impact.
- **Solution:** Inspect the windshield thoroughly for stress fractures or small chips; replace the windshield if cracks impair driver visibility.

## Interior

### Driver Seat Assembly

Manual seat adjustment hardware that can develop play or wear prematurely in high-mileage or heavily utilized vehicles.

**Loose or Rocking Seat Frame**
- **Symptoms:** Driver's seat shifting, rocking, or feeling loose during acceleration, braking, or cornering.
- **Solution:** Inspect mounting bolts and worn plastic washers within the manual seat track mechanism; tighten hardware or replace worn structural components.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V522000: Exterior Lighting:Back Up Lights:Switch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=WRX&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Inoperative backup lights and an inoperative rearview camera can reduce vehicle visibility and driver awareness, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the backup light switch, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Subaru WRX offers an exciting blend of AWD capability and turbocharged performance, but its reputation as an enthusiast favorite means careful inspection is mandatory. Buyers should prioritize finding un-modified examples, verify comprehensive service histories, and watch out for potential engine, clutch, and electrical issues. A professional pre-purchase inspection is strongly recommended before finalizing a deal.
