# 2017 Subaru WRX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Subaru WRX offers an engaging driving experience with standard all-wheel drive and turbocharged performance. However, prospective buyers should carefully review potential mechanical and electrical trouble spots. While many owners enjoy the sporty handling, common pain points include manual transmission clutch wear, electrical battery drains, and lighting switch failures.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Boxer 4-Cylinder (FA20F)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged direct-injection Boxer 4-cylinder engine produces approximately 268 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. It provides brisk acceleration and distinctive performance, though owners have occasionally reported unexpected stalling and sensor-related issues.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden engine stalling or power loss while driving at highway speeds, resulting in a loss of power steering.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fuel delivery and electronic throttle controls; inspect sensors and wiring harnesses for faults.

### 2.5L Turbocharged Boxer 4-Cylinder (EJ257 - STI)

Exclusive to the high-performance STI variant, the 2.5-liter turbocharged Boxer 4-cylinder pushes output to 305 horsepower and 290 lb-ft of torque. It delivers aggressive power delivery paired with a specialized drivetrain, though spirited driving histories demand careful inspection by a mechanic.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The 6-speed manual transmission is standard on the WRX and offers direct, mechanical gear engagement. However, early clutch assembly wear and noisy throw-out bearings are frequent complaints among owners.

**Clutch and Throw-Out Bearing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Premature rattling noises when depressing the clutch pedal in lower gears, difficulty shifting, and premature clutch disc wear.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty throw-out bearing, clutch release fork, and worn clutch components with updated aftermarket or revised OEM parts.

### Sport Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Available as an option on standard WRX trims, the Sport Lineartronic CVT features paddle shifters and simulated gear ratios for daily driving convenience. Prospective buyers should test the transmission thoroughly for smooth operation and fluid condition.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Column

The steering system provides sharp and communicative handling feedback typical of a sport sedan, though hydraulic or electronic assist components can develop seal leaks over time.

**Power Steering Fluid Leaks and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power steering assistance while driving at highway speeds or during low-speed parking maneuvers due to leaking gear seals.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering rack assembly for fluid leaks and replace damaged seals or the entire steering rack unit if necessary.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

The electrical architecture supports advanced infotainment and safety systems, but intermittent parasitic draws and battery degradation have been reported by some owners.

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle battery repeatedly drains or goes dead, requiring frequent jump starts or premature battery replacements.
- **Solution:** Test the charging system, check for parasitic draws from accessories or modules, and replace faulty components or batteries as needed.

## Exterior

### Windshield Glass

Exterior glass and body panels generally withstand daily use well, though windshield durability under certain climate conditions has been a recurring point of owner frustration.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Large cracks forming across the windshield suddenly without any identifiable impact marks or stone chips.
- **Solution:** Replace the windshield glass, ensuring proper installation alignment to minimize stress points on the frame.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V522000: Exterior Lighting:Back Up Lights:Switch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=WRX&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Inoperative backup lights and an inoperative rearview camera can reduce vehicle visibility and driver awareness, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the backup light switch, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Subaru WRX is a thrilling and capable sport sedan, but used buyers should proceed with a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to the manual transmission's clutch engagement and operation, verify that all electrical accessories and reverse lights function correctly, and check for any history of engine modifications or premature wear.
