# 2023 Subaru WRX Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2023 Subaru WRX, buyers generally find an engaging, performance-oriented sedan backed by standard all-wheel drive. While owner reports to NHTSA are relatively sparse for this model year, prospective buyers should still inspect specific areas highlighted by early adopters, such as potential engine sealant concerns and suspension noises.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Turbocharged Flat-4 (FA24F)

The 2.4-liter turbocharged flat-4 engine produces approximately 271 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, providing strong power delivery across the rev range. While generally robust, buyers should be aware of owner reports concerning excess factory sealant.

**Excess RTV Sealant on Oil Pickup Tube**
- **Symptoms:** Excess RTV silicone sealant used during factory oil pan assembly can break loose and potentially block the oil pickup tube, restricting oil flow.
- **Solution:** Inspect the oil pan sealing and oil pickup tube for excess silicone debris, especially if preventative maintenance is being performed.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission offers direct, engaging gear shifts paired with Subaru's symmetrical all-wheel drive system. It remains a fan-favorite option with no widespread mechanical failure patterns reported.

### Subaru Performance Transmission (CVT)

The automatic option is a CVT tuned specifically for performance driving, featuring paddle shifters and simulated gear steps. It provides daily driving convenience with no major recurring reliability complaints noted so far.

## Suspension

### Front Struts and Suspension

The suspension is tuned for sporty handling, though some owners have experienced minor noises during low-speed maneuvers.

**Popping and Clicking Noises During Turns**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may hear loud popping, clicking, or cracking noises from the front suspension and struts when navigating low-speed turns.
- **Solution:** Inspect front strut assemblies, mounts, and springs for proper seating and potential component wear or binding.

## Brakes

### Brake and Parking Brake System

Factory brakes provide adequate stopping power for daily commuting, though some enthusiasts opt for aftermarket pad and caliper upgrades for track use.

**Handbrake Assembly Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The manual handbrake may fail to hold the vehicle securely in place when parked.
- **Solution:** Inspect and, if necessary, replace the emergency brake assembly or adjust the cable tension.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Subaru WRX is an exciting and generally reliable sport sedan for enthusiasts looking for everyday practicality combined with turbocharged performance. Buyers should conduct a thorough pre-purchase inspection, keeping an eye out for suspension noises and verifying that routine maintenance has been properly executed.
