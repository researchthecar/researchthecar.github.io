**Introduction:**

The 2017 Subaru WRX is a performance-oriented sedan known for its sharp handling, turbocharged power, and all-wheel drive capability. It is offered with two engine and transmission options:

* 2.0L FA20DIT Boxer Engine with 6-Speed Manual Transmission (268 hp, 258 lb-ft)
* 2.0L FA20DIT Boxer Engine with CVT Automatic Transmission (268 hp, 258 lb-ft)

Owners generally appreciate the WRX's engaging driving experience, but some have encountered issues with certain components.

**Engine**

**2.0L FA20DIT Boxer Engine**

* **Intake Valve Coking**
  * Description/Symptoms: Buildup of carbon deposits on intake valves, causing reduced airflow and performance issues.
  * Solution: Cleaning or replacing intake valves.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Turbocharger Lag**
  * Description/Symptoms: Delayed response from the turbocharger, resulting in hesitation during acceleration.
  * Solution: Upgrading to a larger turbocharger or tuning the engine.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
* **Timing Belt Tensioner Failure**
  * Description/Symptoms: Worn or defective tensioner pulley, leading to improper timing belt tension and potential engine damage.
  * Solution: Replacing the timing belt tensioner.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
  * Recalls: NHTSA Recall ID: 18V-316

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Clutch Chatter**
  * Description/Symptoms: Vibrations and noises when engaging the clutch, particularly in cold temperatures.
  * Solution: Installing an upgraded or modified clutch.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
* **Gear Grinding**
  * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, accompanied by grinding sounds.
  * Solution: Repairing or replacing damaged gears or synchronizers.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500

**CVT Automatic Transmission**

* **Hesitation and Stuttering**
  * Description/Symptoms: Transmission hesitation or juddering during acceleration or shifting.
  * Solution: Updating the transmission software or replacing the CVT fluid.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
* **Premature Failure**
  * Description/Symptoms: Complete transmission failure at low mileage.
  * Solution: Replacing the entire transmission.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
  * Recalls: NHTSA Recall ID: 18V-198

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Noise**
  * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the suspension when driving over rough roads.
  * Solution: Replacing the strut mounts.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **Rear Sway Bar End Links**
  * Description/Symptoms: Wear or damage to the rear sway bar end links, causing a loose or rattling suspension.
  * Solution: Replacing the rear sway bar end links.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Electrical**

* **Headlight Condensation**
  * Description/Symptoms: Accumulation of moisture inside the headlight assemblies.
  * Solution: Replacing the headlight assemblies or addressing drainage issues.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
* **Battery Drain**
  * Description/Symptoms: Excessive power draw from the battery, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle.
  * Solution: Identifying and repairing the source of the power drain.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue and location

**Exterior**

* **Wheel Bearing Noise**
  * Description/Symptoms: Grinding or humming noises from the wheels at high speeds.
  * Solution: Replacing the damaged wheel bearings.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per wheel

**Interior**

* **Steering Wheel Vibration**
  * Description/Symptoms: Vibrations in the steering wheel during acceleration or braking.
  * Solution: Balancing the tires or replacing the brake pads.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
* **Door Lock Failure**
  * Description/Symptoms: Door locks not functioning properly or becoming stuck.
  * Solution: Repairing or replacing the door lock actuators.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Summary**

While the 2017 Subaru WRX offers an engaging driving experience, it has been known to encounter certain issues affecting its engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, and interior components. The severity and cost of these issues vary, and some may only affect specific versions of the vehicle. It is recommended to research the potential issues and consider preventive maintenance measures to minimize the chances of encountering major problems.
