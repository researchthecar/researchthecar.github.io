# 2014 Subaru BRZ

### Overview
The 2014 Subaru BRZ, a rear-wheel-drive sports coupe co-developed with Toyota, is known for its engaging driving dynamics and balanced handling. It features a naturally aspirated boxer engine, prioritizing agility and driver feedback over outright power. While generally considered a reliable vehicle, like any car, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners and mechanics. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective and current owners of the 2014 model year.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L FA20 Boxer (Naturally Aspirated)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Higher than expected oil level drops between changes, potentially leading to low oil warnings.
- **Solution:** Regular oil level checks and top-offs. In some cases, internal engine modifications or component replacements may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000+

**Valve Spring Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, check engine light, especially at higher RPMs.
- **Solution:** Replacement of affected valve springs. This can be a labor-intensive repair.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500+


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Clutch replacement (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Shifter Linkage Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Stiff or notchy gear changes, difficulty shifting into certain gears.
- **Solution:** Adjustment or replacement of shifter cables and bushings.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Subaru BRZ is generally a reliable sports car, particularly praised for its handling. The most commonly cited technical concerns revolve around the engine, specifically oil consumption and potential valve spring issues, which can be costly to repair. The manual transmission can experience normal wear-and-tear on the clutch and occasional shifter linkage stiffness. However, the automatic transmission and other major systems like suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components have not shown widespread, recurring technical failures beyond typical maintenance needs. Owners should be mindful of regular oil level checks and be prepared for potential engine-related repairs.
