**Introduction:**

The 2015 Subaru BRZ is a compact sports coupe known for its precise handling, agile performance, and affordable price point. It comes equipped with a standard 2.0-liter boxer engine that produces 200 horsepower and 151 lb-ft of torque. The BRZ is available with either a 6-speed manual transmission or a 6-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Boxer Engine**

* **Knocking Noise at Startup:**
    * Description/Symptoms: A noticeable knocking sound from the engine upon startup, particularly in cold temperatures.
    * Solution: Typically resolved by replacing the timing chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Valve Spring Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine performance, loss of power, and audible valve noise.
    * Solution: Requires valve spring replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Difficult Shifting:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty or grinding when shifting gears.
    * Solution: Usually corrected by adjusting the clutch pedal or replacing worn transmission fluid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Slipping Gears:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission hesitates or fails to engage gears properly.
    * Solution: May require replacement of transmission solenoids or fluid flush.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Subframe Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracking or bending of the rear subframe, affecting handling and stability.
    * Solution: Replacement of the rear subframe.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Front Control Arm Bushing Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Abnormal noises and vibrations from the front suspension under load.
    * Solution: Replacement of worn control arm bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery discharges quickly, particularly when parked.
    * Solution: Usually requires replacement of parasitic battery draw components, such as door switches or fuse box components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Starter Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the vehicle, or no response from the ignition system.
    * Solution: Replacement of the starter motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Trunk Lid Misalignment:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Gaps or uneven fitting of the trunk lid.
    * Solution: Adjustment of trunk lid hinges.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Headlight Condensation:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlight assembly.
    * Solution: Replacement of headlight housing or repair of condensation seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Climate control system fails to regulate temperature properly, or produces excessive noise.
    * Solution: Requires replacement of HVAC components, such as the compressor or evaporator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Instrument Cluster Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Gauges or displays on the instrument cluster stop functioning or display incorrect information.
    * Solution: Replacement of instrument cluster or faulty components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2015 Subaru BRZ has a good reliability record, but there are some potential issues that owners should be aware of, particularly with the engine and suspension. These issues range in severity and cost to repair, but most are not overly expensive or difficult to resolve.
