# 2018 Subaru BRZ Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Subaru BRZ continues to be a favorite among driving enthusiasts for its lightweight rear-wheel-drive platform and engaging handling. When evaluating a used model, prospective buyers should review available owner reports and inspect the powertrain thoroughly to ensure proper maintenance and care.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Boxer 4-Cylinder

This 2.0-liter horizontally opposed four-cylinder engine produces approximately 205 horsepower and 156 lb-ft of torque when paired with the manual transmission. It offers a low center of gravity that enhances vehicle handling, though buyers should verify regular oil maintenance and check for any signs of leaks.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The standard 6-speed manual gearbox provides short, direct throws suited for spirited driving. Buyers should test clutch engagement and shifter feel during a test drive.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The optional 6-speed automatic transmission features steering-wheel-mounted paddle shifters for manual control. Ensure smooth shifts across all gears during inspection.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V587000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=BRZ&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the low-pressure fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V935000: Visibility:Rearview Mirrors/Devices](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=BRZ&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, a software issue may cause the audio display system to not initialize, resulting in the camera display remaining blank. As a result, the vehicles fail to comply with requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." Failure to display the rearview image while backing up increases the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru has notified owners, and dealers will reprogram the audio system display software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Subaru BRZ offers engaging rear-wheel-drive dynamics for driving enthusiasts. Prospective used-car buyers should perform a thorough mechanical inspection, verify service history, and test all powertrain functions before purchasing.
