**Introduction:**

The 2018 Subaru BRZ is a sports car known for its balanced handling, responsive engine, and affordable price point. It is powered by a 2.0L 4-cylinder Boxer engine that produces 205 horsepower and 156 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 6-speed manual or a 6-speed automatic with paddle shifters.

People praise the BRZ for its sharp driving dynamics, engaging interior, and low cost of entry. However, critics lament its lack of rear-seat space, cramped rear seats, and modest fuel economy.

**Engine**

**2.0L 4-Cylinder Boxer Engine**

**Valve Spring Recall**
Description/Symptoms: Valve springs may break, causing engine damage and potential engine failure.
Solution: Replace affected valve springs.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000+
Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 19V520000

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

**Shifter Bushing Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Shifter bushings may wear out, resulting in imprecise shifting and increased shifter throw.
Solution: Replace worn shifter bushings.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

**Transmission Fluid Leak**
Description/Symptoms: Transmission fluid may leak from the transmission pan or lines, causing fluid loss and potential transmission damage.
Solution: Repair fluid leak or replace transmission.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Climate Control Fan Motor Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Climate control fan motor may fail, resulting in loss of climate control functionality.
Solution: Replace climate control fan motor.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2018 Subaru BRZ has relatively few reported issues. The most severe issue is the valve spring recall, which could potentially lead to engine failure if not addressed. Other issues, such as shifter bushing failure and climate control fan motor failure, are relatively minor and less costly to repair.
