**Introduction:**

The 2020 Subaru BRZ is the second generation of the automaker's rear-wheel-drive sports coupe. It is powered by a naturally aspirated 2.4-liter flat-4 engine that produces 235 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque. The BRZ is available with a six-speed manual transmission or a six-speed automatic transmission. It is praised for its handling and agility, but criticized for its lack of power and features.

**Engine**

* **2.4L Flat-4 Engine**

**Fuel Injector Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, rough idling, reduced power
Solution: Replace fuel injectors
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Manual Transmission**

**Input Shaft Bearing Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Grinding noise when shifting, difficult to shift gears
Solution: Replace input shaft bearing
Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1200
Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 20V312000

**Suspension**

**Rear Subframe Sagging**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive rear-end squat, misalignment
Solution: Replace rear subframe
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $3000
Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 20V312000

**Electrical**

**Starter Motor Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Engine fails to start, grinding noise
Solution: Replace starter motor
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500

**Exterior**

**Headlight Condensation**
Description/Symptoms: Moisture inside headlight assembly
Solution: Replace headlight assembly
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700

**Interior**

**Infotainment System Malfunction**
Description/Symptoms: Screen freeze, system crashes
Solution: Update software, replace infotainment system
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500

**Summary:**

The 2020 Subaru BRZ has several reported issues, including fuel injector failure, input shaft bearing failure, rear subframe sagging, starter motor failure, headlight condensation, and infotainment system malfunctions. While some of these issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more severe and costly. It is important for owners to be aware of these potential problems and address them promptly to avoid further damage or safety hazards.
