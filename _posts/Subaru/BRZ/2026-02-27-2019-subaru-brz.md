# 2019 Subaru BRZ

### Overview
The 2019 Subaru BRZ is a rear-wheel-drive sports coupe known for its engaging driving dynamics and affordable performance. It features a naturally aspirated boxer engine and a focus on handling rather than outright power. Reliability for the 2019 model year is generally considered good, with owners reporting satisfaction with its mechanical components. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen occasional reports of issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective and current owners.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Naturally Aspirated Boxer (FA20D)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Increased oil level checks required, potential for low oil levels if not monitored.
- **Solution:** Regular oil level checks and top-offs. In some cases, internal engine issues may require professional diagnosis and repair.
- **Cost:** $0 - $1500+

**Exhaust Manifold Cracks**
- **Symptoms:** Exhaust leaks, ticking or puffing noises from the engine bay, especially when cold.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the cracked exhaust manifold.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Subaru BRZ demonstrates solid overall reliability, particularly in its transmission and core chassis components. The primary areas of concern are related to the engine, specifically potential oil consumption and the possibility of exhaust manifold cracks, though these are not universally reported. Owners should be mindful of regular oil level checks. For those considering a used 2019 BRZ, a pre-purchase inspection focusing on these engine-related aspects is advisable. Despite these minor points, the BRZ remains a dependable choice for driving enthusiasts seeking an affordable and fun sports coupe.
