# 2013 Subaru BRZ

### Overview
The 2013 Subaru BRZ, a rear-wheel-drive sports coupe co-developed with Toyota, is known for its engaging driving dynamics and affordable performance. While generally considered a reliable vehicle, like any car, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners and mechanics. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential weak points and their associated repair considerations for the model year. Owners can use this data to anticipate and address common concerns, ensuring the longevity and optimal performance of their BRZ.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Naturally Aspirated Boxer (FA20)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Increased oil level checks required, potential for low oil levels if not monitored.
- **Solution:** Regular oil level checks, potential for engine rebuild or replacement in severe cases.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $4,000+

**Valve Spring Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, potential engine damage.
- **Solution:** Replacement of affected valve springs.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission
**Shifter Bushing Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Vague or sloppy shifter feel, difficulty engaging gears.
- **Solution:** Replacement of shifter bushings.

### Summary Verdict
The 2013 Subaru BRZ is a generally reliable sports car, with its primary concerns revolving around the engine, specifically oil consumption and potential valve spring issues. The manual transmission can experience minor wear in the shifter bushings. Other systems like the automatic transmission, suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior show no widespread significant reliability concerns. Regular maintenance and monitoring of oil levels are key to ensuring the long-term health of the BRZ. Most reported issues are manageable with proper repair, though engine-related problems can incur higher costs.
