# 2022 Subaru BRZ Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2022 Subaru BRZ, buyers get a lightweight, rear-wheel-drive sports car powered by a naturally aspirated 2.4-liter boxer engine. While early owner reports highlight a few specific mechanical and electrical quirks, the vehicle generally offers engaging driving dynamics. Prospective buyers should review service history and inspect specific components during a pre-purchase evaluation.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Naturally Aspirated Boxer 4-Cylinder

The 2.4-liter horizontally opposed four-cylinder engine produces approximately 228 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque, providing lively acceleration for the sports coupe. Overall, it is a spirited powerplant, though buyers should monitor for early manufacturing-related sealant concerns.

**Excess RTV Sealant in Oil Pan**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported discovering excess silicone or RTV sealant debris during oil checks, which can potentially restrict oil flow in the pickup tube and lead to motor concerns.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the oil pan and oil pickup tube during routine maintenance or pre-purchase inspection to clear any excess manufacturing sealant.

**Sudden Power Loss and Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual mechanical noises followed by multiple warning dashboard lights and a sudden inability to move the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnostic evaluation by a dealership or technician to determine the underlying mechanical or electrical fault.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission offers short throws and direct engagement, making it a favorite for driving enthusiasts seeking maximum control.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission with Paddle Shifters

The 6-speed automatic transmission provides smooth shifting and convenient daily drivability while retaining manual control via steering-wheel-mounted paddle shifters.

## Brakes

### Wheel Hubs and Service Brakes

Brake system hardware is robust, but verifying proper installation of wheel fasteners is critical for safe operation.

**Loose Wheel Bolts**
- **Symptoms:** Wheel bolts loosening shortly after purchase, potentially causing damage to surrounding brake components and wheel assemblies.
- **Solution:** Verify proper wheel torque specifications and have a professional inspect the wheel hub and brake hardware for secure fitment.

## Exterior

### Turn Signal and Exterior Lighting

Electrical reports focus primarily on exterior lighting anomalies that can affect visibility to other drivers.

**Rear Turn Signal Hyperflashing**
- **Symptoms:** Dash display indicates rapid blinking while rear turn signals fail to illuminate properly, particularly when bright sunlight faces the rear of the car.
- **Solution:** Inspect lighting modules and consult technical bulletins or dealer resources for potential lighting control updates.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V609000: Exterior Lighting:Turn Signal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=BRZ&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Inoperative rear turn signals may not notify other drivers of a turning vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both rear combination lamp assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Subaru BRZ is an engaging and enthusiast-focused sports car, but used buyers should remain thorough during inspections. Pay special attention to oil pan cleanliness regarding manufacturing sealant and verify that all basic safety items like wheel fasteners and lighting systems are fully secure and operational.
