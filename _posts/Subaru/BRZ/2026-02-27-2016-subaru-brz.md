# 2016 Subaru BRZ Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a used 2016 Subaru BRZ requires a look into owner-reported experiences alongside its engaging rear-wheel-drive performance. While the lightweight sports coupe is celebrated for its sharp handling, used buyers should inspect specific electrical and braking concerns reported by drivers to ensure a solid purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L H4 Boxer Engine

The 2016 Subaru BRZ comes equipped with a naturally aspirated 2.0-liter flat-four Boxer engine producing approximately 200 horsepower and 151 lb-ft of torque. This powerplant delivers high-revving sports car performance while maintaining a low center of gravity, though routine maintenance is essential for longevity.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The standard 6-speed manual transmission offers direct, mechanical shifting favored by driving enthusiasts. It is generally robust when not subjected to severe abuse.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission with Paddle Shifters

The optional 6-speed automatic transmission provides smooth daily driving convenience alongside steering-wheel-mounted paddle shifters. It offers a more relaxed commuting experience while retaining manual control.

## Brakes

### Brake Booster

Owner reports indicate occasional concerns regarding unexpected brake booster failure under heavy braking conditions.

**Brake Booster Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience a hissing sound from the brake area and a hard pedal feel immediately after heavy braking.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake booster and associated vacuum lines, and replace the failed brake booster component as necessary.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Electrical complaints highlight occasional issues with parasitic battery drains that persist even after replacing the battery.

**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle experiences unexpected battery discharge, causing a dead battery even after a direct OEM battery replacement.
- **Solution:** Perform a parasitic draw test to isolate the rogue electrical circuit and repair or replace the faulty module or wiring.

## Exterior

### Side Windows

Owner feedback points to occasional temperamental window operation that can cause driver distraction.

**Power Window Regulator Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The front passenger window fails to close smoothly, requiring the driver to inch it up carefully in increments.
- **Solution:** Inspect the window regulator mechanism, switch, and related wiring, and replace faulty components.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Subaru BRZ is a thrilling and capable sports coupe, but used buyers should verify proper electrical operation and test the braking system thoroughly during a pre-purchase inspection. Checking for battery drains and ensuring smooth window and brake booster performance will help secure a reliable example.
