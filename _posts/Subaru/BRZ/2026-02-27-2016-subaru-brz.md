# 2016 Subaru BRZ

### Overview
The 2016 Subaru BRZ is a rear-wheel-drive sports coupe known for its engaging driving dynamics and affordable price point. It was developed in collaboration with Toyota, sharing its platform with the Toyota 86. The BRZ is powered by a naturally aspirated Subaru Boxer engine, emphasizing balance and a low center of gravity. While generally considered a reliable vehicle, like any car, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners and mechanics. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective and current owners.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L FA20 Flat-4 (Naturally Aspirated)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Increased oil level checks required, potential for low oil levels if not monitored.
- **Solution:** Regular oil level checks and top-ups. In some cases, internal engine issues might be the root cause, requiring professional diagnosis.
- **Cost:** $0 - $200 (for oil) / Varies significantly if internal repairs are needed

**Valve Spring Issues (Early Models)**
- **Symptoms:** Potential for misfires, rough idling, or engine stalling, particularly at higher RPMs.
- **Solution:** Replacement of affected valve springs. This was more prevalent in earlier production years and may have been addressed by recalls or service bulletins.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Subaru BRZ is generally a reliable sports car. The most commonly cited technical concern is related to engine oil consumption, which requires diligent monitoring by the owner. Early production models may have had some valve spring issues, but these are less common in later production or have been addressed. Both the manual and automatic transmissions, as well as the suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components, have not shown widespread reliability concerns beyond typical wear and tear. Overall, the BRZ offers a good balance of performance and dependability for its class, provided routine maintenance is performed and potential engine oil levels are managed.
