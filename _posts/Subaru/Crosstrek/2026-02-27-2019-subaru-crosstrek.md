# 2019 Subaru Crosstrek Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Subaru Crosstrek is a popular subcompact crossover known for its standard symmetrical all-wheel drive, high ground clearance, and everyday utility. While it offers solid practicality, prospective buyers should review specific recurring owner complaints regarding electrical battery drains, spontaneous windshield cracking, and occasional powertrain hiccups. Keeping these potential trouble spots in mind can help you inspect a used model more thoroughly before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The standard 2.0-liter horizontally opposed 4-cylinder engine produces approximately 152 horsepower. It provides adequate efficiency and capability for daily commuting, though some owners report occasional driveability and stalling concerns.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may shake while idling at a stop light and completely stall out.
- **Solution:** Diagnose ignition and electrical components, and replace faulty ignition coils or sensors as necessary.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The Lineartronic CVT is paired with the standard all-wheel-drive system to maximize fuel economy. While generally smooth, major mechanical failures have been reported by a subset of owners.

**Transmission Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected transmission failure while driving up inclines, resulting in a loss of drive power.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace the transmission assembly if internal mechanical failure occurs.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

A traditional 6-speed manual transmission was available on base trims for drivers preferring a stick shift. It pairs with symmetrical all-wheel drive and has fewer reported complex electronic drivetrain complaints.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

The electric power steering system delivers light and responsive handling at low speeds, though rare assist failures can make steering heavy and difficult.

**Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of power steering assist while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by warning lights and a stiff steering wheel.
- **Solution:** Inspect electronic power steering control modules and wiring harnesses for faults.

## Electrical

### Battery and Charging System

The electrical architecture supports advanced safety suites and connectivity features, but parasitic drains linked to telematics modules can leave owners with dead batteries.

**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The battery drains completely even when the vehicle is fully turned off, preventing the car from starting after sitting for a few days.
- **Solution:** Inspect the DCM module and infotainment software, and replace depleted or damaged batteries.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Glass

Exterior visibility components are generally robust, though numerous owner complaints highlight a susceptibility to stress-induced windshield cracks.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Windshields develop long cracks across the glass suddenly without any noticeable impact from rocks or debris.
- **Solution:** Replace the windshield, ensuring proper calibration for any integrated EyeSight camera systems.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V404000: Visibility:Glass, Side/Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Crosstrek&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 205, "Glazing Materials." Improperly tempered glass can shatter into large pieces, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver's side rear-door window glass, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V189000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Crosstrek&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The seat belt webbing sensor locking mechanism may not lock as intended. In the event of a crash involving multiple impacts, the seat belt may not properly restrain the occupant, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will replace rear seat belt assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V065000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Crosstrek&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A connector inside the Electronic Power Steering (EPS) unit may short circuit resulting in a loss of electric power steering assistance. Loss of power steering assist would require higher steering effort, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru has notified dealers and they will inspect the steering gearbox, replacing it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V263000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Crosstrek&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Missing bolts may allow the rear stabilizer bracket to shift, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will retorque and replace any missing bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V264000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Crosstrek&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The Engine Control Module (ECM) may continue to power the ignition coil after the engine is shut off, causing a short circuit. A short circuit in the ignition coils may cause a stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will update the ECM software, replace the ignition coils and, if necessary, the front exhaust pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V856000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Exhaust System:Emission Control:Crankcase (Pcv)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Crosstrek&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An unexpected loss of power while driving can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the PCV valve, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V743000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Crosstrek&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The Engine Control Module (ECM) may continue to power the ignition coil after the engine is shut off, which could result in a short circuit and blown fuse. If a short circuit occurs while driving, the vehicle may stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will update the ECM software and inspect and replace the ignition coil, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Subaru Crosstrek remains an attractive used all-wheel-drive crossover, but buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to battery health, look for signs of previous windshield replacements, and test-drive the vehicle to ensure smooth engine and transmission operation.
