# 2018 Subaru Crosstrek Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Subaru Crosstrek is a popular subcompact crossover known for its standard all-wheel drive, raised ground clearance, and versatile hatchback design. While it offers strong utility and fuel efficiency, used-car buyers should be aware of several recurring owner complaints involving unexpected engine stalling, electrical battery drains, and a high incidence of spontaneous windshield cracking. Checking service history for completed recall work and past warranty replacements is strongly advised before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder (FB20D)

The 2.0-liter horizontally opposed four-cylinder engine produces approximately 152 horsepower and 145 lb-ft of torque. It balances everyday efficiency with standard symmetrical all-wheel drive, though owners have reported occasional ignition component failures leading to stalls.

**Engine Stalling and Ignition Coil Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may experience a sudden loss of motive power, violent shaking, or stalling while driving or stopped in traffic due to faulty ignition coils.
- **Solution:** Replace failing ignition coils and spark plugs. Verify if relevant ignition coil recalls or campaigns have been performed.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic CVT Automatic

The Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) is paired standard across trims to optimize fuel economy. While no prominent mechanical cluster dominated the complaints, buyers should test-drive thoroughly for smooth engagement and responsive acceleration.

### 6-Speed Manual

A 6-speed manual transmission was available on base trims for drivers preferring a traditional shifting experience. It generally offers simple mechanical reliability, though clutch wear depends heavily on prior driving habits.

## Suspension

### Rear Wheel Bearings

Suspension and wheel assembly components can experience premature rear wheel bearing wear, producing noticeable noise and requiring replacement.

**Premature Wheel Bearing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A loud, persistent howling or humming noise originating from the rear of the vehicle while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear wheel hubs and replace worn wheel bearings.

## Steering

### Steering Column

Steering components generally perform well, though rare reports note locking issues or power assist irregularities.

**Steering Wheel Lockup**
- **Symptoms:** Steering wheel becomes difficult to turn or locks up firmly after the vehicle is turned off in certain wheel positions.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering column and ignition lock mechanism, and consult a dealer if steering assist components fail.

## Electrical

### Electrical System & Battery

Electrical concerns include parasitic battery drains and occasional lighting or sensor warnings that require careful diagnostic testing.

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle battery loses charge rapidly when parked for a few days, leading to a dead battery and failure to start.
- **Solution:** Diagnose parasitic draws in the electrical system, test alternator output, and replace weak or discharged batteries.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Glass

Exterior complaints heavily feature recurring stress fractures on the front windshield glass, often appearing without external impacts.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracks**
- **Symptoms:** Front windshield develops long stress cracks suddenly without any apparent impact from rocks or debris.
- **Solution:** Replace the windshield with a quality aftermarket or OEM glass, ensuring proper calibration of any attached EyeSight safety cameras if equipped.

## Interior

### Driver Seat Tracks

Interior comfort is generally high, though structural wear on the driver seat track assembly has been noted by multiple owners.

**Loose or Rocking Driver Seat**
- **Symptoms:** Driver seat rocks back and forth during acceleration or braking, causing unintended shifts in seating position.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat track mechanisms and repair or replace loose mounting hardware and seat track components.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V744000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Exhaust System:Emission Control:Crankcase (Pcv)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Crosstrek&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** An unexpected loss of power while driving can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the PCV valve, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V263000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Crosstrek&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Missing bolts may allow the rear stabilizer bracket to shift, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will retorque and replace any missing bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V264000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Crosstrek&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The Engine Control Module (ECM) may continue to power the ignition coil after the engine is shut off, causing a short circuit. A short circuit in the ignition coils may cause a stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will update the ECM software, replace the ignition coils and, if necessary, the front exhaust pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V743000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Crosstrek&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The Engine Control Module (ECM) may continue to power the ignition coil after the engine is shut off, which could result in a short circuit and blown fuse. If a short circuit occurs while driving, the vehicle may stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will update the ECM software and inspect and replace the ignition coil, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V701000: Service Brakes](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Crosstrek&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the floor mat moves out of position and interferes with the accelerator or brake pedal operation, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru has notified owners, and dealers will inspect the floor mat retention bracket and replace it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Subaru Crosstrek remains an attractive used choice for buyers seeking capable all-weather traction and practical hatchback utility. Prospective owners should carefully inspect the vehicle for common pain points, including previous windshield replacements, rear wheel bearing noise, and battery health. Verifying that all safety recalls and ignition coil campaigns have been completed will help ensure a much more reliable ownership experience.
