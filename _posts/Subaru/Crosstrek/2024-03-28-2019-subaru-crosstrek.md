**About the 2019 Subaru Crosstrek:**

The 2019 Subaru Crosstrek is a compact crossover SUV known for its ruggedness and off-road capabilities. It comes with a choice of two engine options mated to either a manual or a continuously variable transmission (CVT). The 2.0-liter naturally aspirated engine produces 152 horsepower and 145 lb-ft of torque, while the 2.5-liter naturally aspirated engine produces 182 horsepower and 176 lb-ft of torque.

Owners generally appreciate the Crosstrek's spacious interior, good fuel economy, and excellent visibility. However, some reported concerns regarding certain components.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Engine**

* **Head gasket failure:** Premature failure of the head gasket can lead to oil and coolant leaks. Symptoms include a burning smell, overheating, and low coolant levels. The solution requires replacing the head gasket and associated components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500+
* **Timing chain tensioner failure:** The timing chain tensioner may fail, causing the timing chain to become loose and jump timing. This can result in engine damage. The solution involves replacing the timing chain tensioner and related parts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1200+

**2.5-liter Engine**

* **Intake valve carbon buildup:** Over time, carbon deposits can build up on the intake valves, reducing engine performance and fuel economy. Symptoms include rough idling, hesitation, and reduced power. The solution involves cleaning or replacing the intake valves. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+

**Transmission**

**CVT**

* **Jerking or hesitation during acceleration:** The CVT may experience occasional jerking or hesitation during acceleration. The issue can often be resolved by performing a transmission fluid change or updating the transmission control software. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+

**Suspension**

* **Strut mount bearing failure:** The strut mount bearings may fail, causing clunking or knocking noises from the front suspension. The solution involves replacing the strut mounts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** The alternator may fail, leading to a loss of electrical power or difficulty starting the vehicle. The solution involves replacing the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
* **Battery discharge:** The battery may prematurely discharge, leaving the vehicle unable to start. The issue can be caused by faulty electrical components or an excessive parasitic draw. The solution involves diagnosing and repairing the underlying cause. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate leak:** The tailgate may leak, allowing water to enter the cargo area. The solution involves sealing the tailgate or replacing the weatherstripping. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Interior**

* **Infotainment system freezing:** The infotainment system may occasionally freeze or become unresponsive. The solution involves resetting the system or updating the software. Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $200+
* **Climate control issues:** The climate control system may experience various issues, such as malfunctioning buttons or uneven temperature distribution. The solution may involve replacing components or updating the software. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+

**Summary**

The 2019 Subaru Crosstrek has several common issues across various components. Some issues, such as the head gasket failure, can be severe and costly to fix. However, the majority of issues are relatively minor and can be resolved at a reasonable cost. Overall, the Crosstrek remains a reliable and capable vehicle for its class.
