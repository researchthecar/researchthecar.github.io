# 2016 Subaru Crosstrek Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Subaru Crosstrek blends hatchback practicality with standard all-wheel drive and a rugged stance, making it a popular pick for commuters and light adventurers alike. However, prospective buyers should review some notable owner-reported trouble spots, including recurring ignition key entrapment, battery drain concerns, and occasional transmission or stalling complaints.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Boxer 4-Cylinder

The 2.0-liter horizontally opposed four-cylinder engine produces 148 horsepower and 145 lb-ft of torque, providing modest acceleration with standard all-wheel drive. While generally durable, some owners have reported intermittent stalling and rough idle issues.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle unexpectedly stalls while driving at highway speeds or coming to a stop, occasionally accompanied by rough idling.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the fuel system, throttle body, and engine management sensors; update software or replace failing components as needed.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic CVT Automatic

The continuously variable transmission (CVT) is paired with most Crosstrek models to optimize fuel efficiency. Buyers should verify proper CVT operation and check service history for any valve body or transmission replacements.

**Power Loss and Transmission Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent loss of power despite pressing the accelerator, illumination of warning lights (such as A/T Oil Temp), or complete internal transmission failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect valve body assembly and fluid condition; consult dealer regarding extended warranty coverage for CVT components.

### 5-Speed Manual

A 5-speed manual transmission was available on base trims for drivers preferring a traditional shift-it-yourself experience. Clutches are wear items and may require replacement depending on prior driving habits.

**Clutch Premature Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clutch pedal feels loose, slipping under acceleration, or premature failure of clutch components.
- **Solution:** Inspect the clutch assembly, master cylinder, and slave cylinder; replace worn clutch discs and pressure plates.

## Electrical

### Ignition and Shifter Switch

Electrical system concerns on this model frequently center around convenience electronics, parasitic battery drains, and ignition switch interlocks.

**Key Stuck in Ignition**
- **Symptoms:** The ignition key cannot be turned fully to the off position or removed after shifting the vehicle into park.
- **Solution:** Inspect the gear selector switch mechanism, microswitches, and lock cylinder; replace faulty shift lever assembly components.

**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle battery repeatedly goes dead overnight or after sitting for short periods due to an electrical draw.
- **Solution:** Diagnose parasitic draws, update DCM (Data Communication Module) software, and replace drained batteries or faulty fuses.

## Exterior

### Hood Latch and Hinges

Interior and body hardware components generally hold up well, though the hood latch assembly has experienced notable failure reports that can compromise forward visibility.

**Hood Latch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Hood safety latch fails while driving at highway speeds, causing the hood to fly open and strike the windshield.
- **Solution:** Inspect the primary and secondary hood latches for corrosion or binding; lubricate or replace the latch mechanism.

### Windshield and Glass

Exterior glass and sunroof components warrant a close inspection during a pre-purchase evaluation to check for stress cracks or prior replacements.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Windshield develops large cracks across the glass without any apparent impact from road debris.
- **Solution:** Replace the windshield with a quality aftermarket or OEM glass unit, ensuring proper installation alignment.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V149000: Exterior Lighting:Brake Lights:Switch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Crosstrek&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Exposure to certain contaminants may cause the brake light switch to malfunction, preventing the brake lights from illuminating and also preventing keyless ignition vehicles from starting and CVT/automatic transmissions from being able to be shifted out of Park. Brake lights that do not illuminate properly will not alert other drivers that the vehicle is.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will replace the brake light switch, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Subaru Crosstrek offers great versatility, but used-car buyers should thoroughly inspect key operational areas before purchasing. Pay close attention to how the ignition handles the key in park, test the transmission and engine for smooth power delivery without hesitation or stalling, and check for any history of electrical drains or windshield damage.
