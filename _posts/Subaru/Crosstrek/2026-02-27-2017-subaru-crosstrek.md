# 2017 Subaru Crosstrek Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Subaru Crosstrek blends hatchback practicality with standard all-wheel drive and elevated ground clearance, making it a popular choice for budget-conscious commuters and outdoor enthusiasts alike. While owners generally praise its versatility, used car shoppers should review reported trouble spots carefully before purchasing. Key areas of concern involve specific mechanical and electrical components, including ignition key retention issues, windshield durability, and powertrain-related quirks.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

Equipped with a 2.0-liter horizontally opposed (boxer) 4-cylinder engine producing 148 horsepower and 145 lb-ft of torque. This engine delivers reliable daily driving performance and decent fuel economy, though routine inspections for oil leaks are recommended.

**Electrical Warning Lights and Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Warning lights illuminate on the dashboard accompanied by potential oil leaks around the engine components.
- **Solution:** Inspect engine gaskets and seals for leaks; replace worn cam carriers or sensors as needed.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The standard automatic option is a Lineartronic CVT paired with Subaru's symmetrical all-wheel-drive system. It offers smooth acceleration for highway cruising, though prospective buyers should listen closely for unusual noises during test drives.

**Sudden Transmission Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loud noises such as a bang upon acceleration followed by a complete loss of forward propulsion or vehicle rolling backward.
- **Solution:** Have the transmission thoroughly inspected by a qualified technician; overhaul or replace the transmission assembly if internal failure is confirmed.

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission

A traditional 5-speed manual transmission was available on lower trims for drivers who prefer a hands-on shifting experience. While generally engaging, select owners have reported heavy internal wear requiring major repairs.

**Premature Shift Fork Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty shifting gears or complete loss of gear engagement resulting from internal component wear.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace damaged manual transmission components or perform a complete transmission assembly replacement.
- **Cost:** $10,000

## Electrical

### Ignition and Shifter Interlock

Electrical and ignition components generally function well, though recurring complaints highlight inconveniences with the key-removal interlock system.

**Ignition Key Stuck**
- **Symptoms:** The key becomes stuck in the ignition cylinder and cannot be removed after turning off the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Inspect the shifter microswitch and ignition lock mechanism; adjust or replace faulty shifter linkage components.

## Exterior

### Windshield Glass

Exterior body panels and lighting are generally robust, but the factory windshield has been reported by multiple owners as prone to stress cracking.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracks**
- **Symptoms:** Long cracks develop across the windshield surface seemingly out of nowhere without any obvious impact point.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged windshield with a quality aftermarket or OEM glass unit.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V149000: Exterior Lighting:Brake Lights:Switch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Crosstrek&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Exposure to certain contaminants may cause the brake light switch to malfunction, preventing the brake lights from illuminating and also preventing keyless ignition vehicles from starting and CVT/automatic transmissions from being able to be shifted out of Park. Brake lights that do not illuminate properly will not alert other drivers that the vehicle is.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will replace the brake light switch, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Subaru Crosstrek is a capable and practical compact crossover that offers great utility for everyday driving and light adventure. While most systems prove dependable, used car buyers should inspect the vehicle for common trouble spots such as ignition key retention quirks, windshield stress cracks, and proper powertrain operation. A thorough pre-purchase inspection by a trusted mechanic will help ensure you find a reliable model.
