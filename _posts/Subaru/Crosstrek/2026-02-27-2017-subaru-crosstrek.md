# 2017 Subaru Crosstrek

### Overview
The 2017 Subaru Crosstrek is generally recognized for its robust build and capability, particularly appealing to those seeking a versatile compact SUV with standard all-wheel drive. This model year benefits from Subaru's reputation for reliability, often translating into fewer major mechanical concerns compared to some competitors. Owners typically praise its practicality, fuel efficiency for its class, and its suitability for light off-roading and adverse weather conditions. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the Crosstrek has demonstrated a solid track record, with most reported problems being minor or related to typical usage rather than inherent design flaws. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Boxer (FB20)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Increased oil level checks required, potential for low oil warning lights.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels regularly. In severe cases, engine internal inspection and potential repair or replacement may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $100 - $150 (for monitoring/top-up), $1000+ (for internal repairs)

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, low coolant level warnings, overheating.
- **Solution:** Inspect hoses, radiator, water pump, and head gaskets for leaks. Replace faulty components.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800 (depending on the source of the leak)


## Transmission

### CVT (TR580)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable delay or abruptness during acceleration or gear changes.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the transmission control module are often the first step. Fluid changes and internal inspection may be required for persistent issues.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500 (for software update/fluid change), $2000+ (for internal repair/replacement)

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Subaru Crosstrek presents a generally reliable profile, with its 2.0L Boxer engine and available transmissions (CVT and manual) showing a good track record. The most commonly cited technical concerns revolve around potential oil consumption and occasional CVT hesitation, though these are not universal and often manageable with maintenance or software updates. Electrical systems, particularly the infotainment, can experience minor glitches. Suspension, exterior, and interior components are largely free from widespread issues. Overall, the Crosstrek is a dependable choice for its segment, with most potential problems being minor and repairable, contributing to its positive reputation for long-term ownership.
