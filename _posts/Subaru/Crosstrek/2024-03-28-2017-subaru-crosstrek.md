## 2017 Subaru Crosstrek

With its combination of rugged capability and everyday practicality, the 2017 Subaru Crosstrek appeals to buyers seeking a versatile and reliable compact crossover. Available engine options include a 2.0-liter naturally aspirated unit and a 2.5-liter direct-injected engine. Both engines can be paired with either a 6-speed manual transmission or a continuously variable transmission (CVT). The Crosstrek has received praise for its spacious interior, impressive fuel economy, and advanced safety features; however, it has also faced some criticism for its underwhelming engine performance and occasionally rough ride quality.

### **Engine**

**2.0-liter Naturally Aspirated Engine**

* **Intake Manifold Runner Issues**
  * Description/Symptoms: Faulty intake manifold runners can cause whistling noises, reduced engine performance, and increased fuel consumption.
  * Solution: Replacement of intake manifold runners.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $700 - $1,000
  * Recalls: None Found

**2.5-liter Direct-Injected Engine**

* **Piston Ring Problems**
  * Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged piston rings can lead to excessive oil consumption, blue smoke from the exhaust, and poor engine compression.
  * Solution: Engine rebuild or replacement.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

### **Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Grinding Gears**
  * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, particularly when cold.
  * Solution: Transmission fluid flush and adjustment, potential replacement of synchronizers.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
  * Recalls: None Found

**Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

* **Premature Wear**
  * Description/Symptoms: Slipping, shuddering, or hesitation during acceleration, especially at higher speeds.
  * Solution: Transmission fluid flush or replacement, potential rebuild or replacement.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

### **Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

### **Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
  * Description/Symptoms: Battery discharges quickly, especially when the vehicle is parked.
  * Solution: Testing and replacement of faulty electrical components, such as the starter, alternator, or battery.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500
  * Recalls: None Found

### **Exterior**

* **Wind Noise**
  * Description/Symptoms: Excessive wind noise around windows or door seals, especially at highway speeds.
  * Solution: Adjustment of window seals or replacement of defective parts.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
  * Recalls: None Found

### **Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunctions**
  * Description/Symptoms: Improper temperature or airflow control, intermittent operation.
  * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of faulty sensors, actuators, or control module.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
  * Recalls: None Found

* **Steering Wheel Controls Issue**
  * Description/Symptoms: Steering wheel controls may become unresponsive or intermittent.
  * Solution: Replacement of defective steering wheel control unit.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
  * Recalls: None Found

### **Summary**

While the 2017 Subaru Crosstrek offers a blend of versatility and functionality, it has faced some notable issues. Engine problems with the 2.5-liter engine and CVT transmission issues are among the most significant concerns. Other issues include electrical problems, wind noise, and interior malfunctions. The severity of the issues varies, with some being relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, while others can be more costly and disruptive. Overall, it is advisable for potential buyers to research these issues thoroughly and consider their impact on their purchase decision.
