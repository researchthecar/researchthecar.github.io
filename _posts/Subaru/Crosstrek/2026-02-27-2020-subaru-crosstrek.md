# 2020 Subaru Crosstrek Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Subaru Crosstrek is a popular compact crossover known for its standard all-wheel-drive capability, practical hatchback design, and friendly road manners. When shopping for a used model, buyers should be aware of a few recurring pain points reported by owners, notably concerning unexpected windshield cracking, electrical battery drains, and potential powertrain component durability issues.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

A 2.0-liter naturally aspirated boxer four-cylinder engine producing 152 horsepower and 145 lb-ft of torque, standard on base and premium trims. It offers dependable daily driving performance with respectable fuel efficiency.

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

A larger 2.5-liter naturally aspirated boxer engine generating 182 horsepower and 176 lb-ft of torque, introduced for upper trims. This option provides much-needed passing power and a more responsive feel on the highway.

### 2.0L Plug-In Hybrid Powertrain

A plug-in hybrid system pairing a 2.0-liter engine with dual electric motors for an EPA-estimated all-electric range. While efficient, the complex hybrid electronics see occasional owner complaints regarding warning lights and stalling.

**Engine Stalling and Electrical Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warning lights flash unexpectedly followed by an engine stall while driving, resulting in a temporary loss of power and steering control.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle inspected by a qualified technician or dealer to diagnose potential hybrid control module or valve body faults.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

A continuously variable automatic transmission paired across standard gas-powered trims to optimize fuel economy. Some owners have experienced premature mechanical wear, whining noises, or valve body failures.

**CVT Whining Noise and Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Low-speed moaning or whining noises from the transmission, accompanied by acceleration hesitation or total unit failure requiring replacement.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis by a Subaru dealer; failing transmissions typically need professional rebuild or complete replacement.

**Torque Converter and Valve Body Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Triggering of diagnostic trouble codes such as P2757 related to torque converter clutch pressure control solenoid performance.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the affected valve body assembly or torque converter components.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

A traditional 6-speed manual gearbox available on select lower trims for drivers preferring a direct shift connection. It generally presents fewer complex mechanical failure points than its CVT counterpart.

## Electrical

### Battery and Charging System

Manages vehicle power distribution, charging, and accessory integration. parasitic battery draws have been reported by some owners when vehicles sit unused for short periods.

**Premature Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to start after sitting parked for a few days due to an unexpected electrical drain, requiring a jump-start.
- **Solution:** Test the battery health and check for parasitic draw from onboard electronics or infotainment modules.

## Exterior

### Windshield Glass

Factory glass components and visibility aids. A notable volume of owner complaints highlights a tendency for windshields to crack spontaneously under normal driving or parking conditions.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Cracks developing across the windshield—frequently near the edges, defroster vents, or rearview mirror mount—without any visible impact or trauma from outside objects.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged windshield through an auto glass professional, ensuring proper recalibration of any attached EyeSight safety cameras.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Subaru Crosstrek remains a compelling choice for used-car buyers seeking confident all-weather capability and hatchback practicality. Prospective owners should thoroughly inspect the service history for any transmission or valve body repairs, check the condition of the windshield for stress cracks, and verify that the electrical system and battery hold a proper charge.
