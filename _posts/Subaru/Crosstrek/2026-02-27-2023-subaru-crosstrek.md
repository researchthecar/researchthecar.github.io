# 2023 Subaru Crosstrek Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Subaru Crosstrek is a popular compact crossover known for its standard all-wheel drive, rugged styling, and practical hatchback versatility. While generally well-regarded, used car buyers should inspect common owner-reported trouble spots, particularly regarding exterior glass durability and select electrical or braking concerns.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Boxer Engine

The standard 2.0-liter horizontally opposed four-cylinder engine produces approximately 152 horsepower and 145 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate daily driving power and respectable fuel economy, though some owners report minor gasket or acceleration quirks.

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Boxer Engine

The available 2.5-liter horizontally opposed four-cylinder engine delivers 182 horsepower and 176 lb-ft of torque, offering quicker acceleration for highway merging. It is a reliable powerplant widely utilized across Subaru's lineup.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

A continuously variable transmission paired with symmetrical all-wheel drive comes standard on most models, prioritizing fuel efficiency and smooth power delivery. While generally dependable with regular fluid maintenance, prospective buyers should test-drive to ensure smooth operation.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

A traditional 6-speed manual transmission was available on base trims, offering a more engaging driving experience for traditionalists. It features a straightforward mechanical design with a solid track record.

## Suspension

### Suspension and Struts

The suspension system delivers a comfortable, compliant ride suited for light off-pavement trails, though rare premature component failures have been reported.

**Premature Strut Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rear or front struts may experience structural breakage or fluid leaks under normal highway driving conditions.
- **Solution:** Inspect suspension dampers and replace damaged struts and supporting hardware.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Braking components provide strong stopping power under normal conditions, but a few owners have experienced unusual pedal behavior or rear brake engagement concerns.

**Brake Pedal Travel and Booster Issues**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal may feel soft or travel unexpectedly close to the floor, or the power brake booster may fail prematurely.
- **Solution:** Inspect the braking system for hydraulic pressure loss and replace defective brake boosters or worn components as necessary.

## Electrical

### Battery and Charging System

Electrical architecture supports the infotainment and safety suites, though isolated parasitic drain and infotainment glitches have been noted by owners.

**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle battery repeatedly loses charge, requiring frequent jump-starts or multiple battery replacements within a short timeframe.
- **Solution:** Diagnose stray electrical draws or faulty module software that may keep systems awake when the vehicle is turned off.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Glass

Exterior glass and visibility components generally function as expected, though multiple owners have flagged susceptibility to unexpected cracking.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report that the front windshield develops sudden cracks without any apparent stone impact or debris strike.
- **Solution:** Have the windshield inspected for stress fractures and replaced with an OEM or compatible aftermarket glass if compromised.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Subaru Crosstrek remains a compelling choice for used car buyers seeking all-weather capability and practical hatchback utility. While major mechanical failures are infrequent, prospective owners should thoroughly check the condition of the windshield, test the electrical accessories, and ensure the braking system operates smoothly during a pre-purchase inspection.
