# 2021 Subaru Crosstrek Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Subaru Crosstrek blends rugged hatchback styling, standard all-wheel drive, and practical ground clearance, making it a popular choice for commuters and weekend adventurers alike. While it generally offers dependable transportation and solid safety features, used car buyers should pay close attention to a few known trouble spots. Common owner complaints center around electrical battery drains, windshield durability concerns, and intermittent transmission or powertrain issues.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.0-liter horizontally-opposed four-cylinder engine produces 152 horsepower and 145 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate efficiency for daily driving. Overall, it is mechanically robust, though occasional sensor and internal mechanical complaints have been reported by owners.

**Electrical Warning Lights & Thermo Control Valve**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine lights accompanied by disabled driver-assist features like EyeSight.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the engine codes and replace faulty components such as the thermo control valve or related sensors.

**Engine Internal Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual engine knocking, sudden loss of power, or catastrophic internal component failure.
- **Solution:** Requires professional mechanical inspection; may require engine rebuild or replacement depending on damage severity.

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

Introduced for higher trims, the larger 2.5-liter boxer engine generates 182 horsepower and 176 lb-ft of torque, offering quicker acceleration and more confident highway passing. It maintains a solid reliability reputation with fewer reported drivetrain complaints.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Paired with standard symmetrical all-wheel drive, the automatic CVT maximizes fuel economy and provides smooth power delivery under normal conditions. Buyers should test-drive vehicles thoroughly at low speeds to check for differential binding or shudder.

**Transmission Shudder and Drivetrain Binding**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle shudders, jerks, or experiences binding sensations when executing sharp low-speed turns.
- **Solution:** Inspect the center differential and transmission assembly; fluid service or differential component replacement may be required.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission is available on select lower trims, offering a more engaging driving experience for traditionalists. It generally proves mechanically durable, though clutch wear depends heavily on prior driving habits.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

The electrical architecture supports modern infotainment and safety systems, but parasitic draws can occasionally drain the battery prematurely.

**Battery Parasitic Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to start unexpectedly after sitting overnight due to a drained battery, often linked to the DCM module.
- **Solution:** Update software or replace faulty telematics control modules and install a fresh battery.

## Exterior

### Visibility & Windshield

Exterior visibility is generally excellent thanks to large glass areas and slim pillars, though factory windshield glass is frequently cited by owners as prone to chipping and cracking.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracks**
- **Symptoms:** Windshields develop sudden stress cracks across the glass surface with little to no impact from road debris.
- **Solution:** Replace the windshield, ensuring proper calibration of any attached camera-based safety systems afterward.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Subaru's EyeSight driver assist suite provides robust collision protection, but sensitive sensor calibration can occasionally lead to false positive braking activations.

**Unexpected Automatic Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The EyeSight system occasionally triggers unprompted braking events or emergency stops while traveling at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Check for camera obstructions, ensure windshield glass is clean, and have dealership software calibrations performed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V755000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Park/Neutral Start Interlock Switch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Crosstrek&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An inoperative inhibitor switch may prevent the reverse lights from illuminating and the rearview camera image from displaying, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the inhibitor switch, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Subaru Crosstrek is a well-rounded and versatile compact crossover that offers standard all-wheel drive and strong safety ratings. While it is a compelling used vehicle purchase, buyers should verify that the battery and charging system hold a steady charge, inspect the windshield for existing stress cracks, and pay close attention to transmission behavior during tight low-speed turns. A thorough pre-purchase inspection by an independent mechanic will help ensure the vehicle has been properly maintained.
