# 2021 Subaru Crosstrek

### Overview
The 2021 Subaru Crosstrek is generally regarded as a reliable compact SUV, known for its standard all-wheel-drive system and practical design. Consumer feedback and reliability ratings suggest a solid performance for its model year. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected issues, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen occasional reports. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective buyers and current owners of the 2021 Crosstrek, covering its various powertrain configurations and key vehicle systems.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional reports of the infotainment screen freezing, becoming unresponsive, or restarting unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system reset, or potential replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500


### Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A small number of owners have reported batteries failing sooner than expected, particularly in colder climates.
- **Solution:** Battery replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


## Exterior

### Paint
**Chip Resistance**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners have noted that the paint can be susceptible to chipping from road debris.
- **Solution:** Paint touch-up or professional detailing/repainting.
- **Cost:** $50 - $500


### Headlights
**Condensation**
- **Symptoms:** Rare instances of condensation forming inside headlight assemblies.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential resealing or replacement of headlight unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Interior

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Subaru Crosstrek presents itself as a dependable choice in the compact SUV segment. Its powertrain options, both the 2.0L and the more potent 2.5L BOXER engines, are generally robust. The CVT transmission, while occasionally exhibiting minor hesitations, is largely reliable, and the manual transmission is a solid alternative. Electrical gremlins, primarily concerning the infotainment system's responsiveness, are the most frequently cited non-wear-and-tear issues, though often resolvable with software updates. Exterior paint susceptibility to chipping and minor interior wear on high-contact areas like seat bolsters are noted but not widespread critical failures. Overall, the Crosstrek offers good value with a strong reliability record for its class.
