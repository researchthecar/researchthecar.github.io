**Introduction to the 2021 Subaru XV**

The 2021 Subaru XV is a compact crossover SUV that offers a comfortable ride, excellent fuel economy, and standard all-wheel drive. It comes equipped with a 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine that produces 152 horsepower and 145 lb-ft of torque, paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). Other engine and transmission options are not available.

Owners of the 2021 Subaru XV generally praise its reliability, handling, and fuel efficiency. However, some have reported various issues with the vehicle, which are detailed below.

**Engine**

* **2.0L Four-Cylinder Engine**

  * **Oil leaks:** Some owners have reported oil leaks from the valve cover gasket or oil pan gasket.
    * Solution: Replacing the affected gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

  * **Failing spark plugs:** Spark plugs may prematurely fail, causing engine misfires or rough idling.
    * Solution: Replacing the spark plugs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

  * **Hesitation or shuddering:** During acceleration or gear changes, the CVT may hesitate or shudder.
    * Solution: Reprogramming the CVT or replacing the transmission oil.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Faulty battery sensor:** The battery sensor may malfunction, causing inaccurate battery readings or warning lights to illuminate.
    * Solution: Replacing the battery sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door lock malfunctions:** The door locks may fail to lock or unlock properly.
    * Solution: Replacing the door lock actuators.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Windshield wiper fluid reservoir sensor:** The sensor may fail, causing inaccurate readings or warning lights to illuminate.
    * Solution: Replacing the sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Rear seat latch failure:** The latch for the rear seats may fail to release, making it difficult to fold the seats down.
    * Solution: Replacing the latch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2021 Subaru XV has proven to be a reliable vehicle for many owners. However, some have experienced issues with the engine, transmission, and electrical systems. The severity of these issues can vary from mild (such as faulty sensors) to more serious (such as transmission problems). The cost to fix these issues can also vary significantly, but most can be resolved for under $500.
