# 2021 Subaru XV

### Overview
The 2021 Subaru XV (also known as Crosstrek in some markets) generally presents a strong reliability record, reflecting Subaru's reputation for building durable vehicles. Owners typically praise its all-wheel-drive system, practical interior, and comfortable ride. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the XV tends to avoid widespread, significant mechanical or electrical problems. Common owner feedback highlights satisfaction with its performance in various conditions and its suitability for an active lifestyle. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, aiming to provide a clear picture of potential concerns for prospective and current owners.

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Subaru XV demonstrates commendable reliability, with most major mechanical and structural components performing as expected. The primary area of concern, though not widespread, involves occasional glitches with the infotainment system, which can typically be resolved with software updates or, in rarer cases, unit replacement. Engine, transmission, suspension, exterior, and interior components generally receive positive feedback regarding their durability. Overall, the 2021 XV is considered a dependable choice, with a low probability of encountering significant, costly issues beyond standard wear and tear.
