## 2014 Subaru Tribeca

The 2014 Subaru Tribeca is a mid-size SUV that offers a spacious interior, standard all-wheel drive, and a variety of engine and transmission options. It is powered by a 3.6-liter V6 engine that produces 256 horsepower and 247 lb-ft of torque. The Tribeca is available with a 5-speed automatic transmission or a 6-speed manual transmission.

Some people love the Tribeca for its roomy interior and standard all-wheel drive. However, others find it to be outdated and thirsty for gas.

**Engine**

* **3.6-liter V6**

    * **Oil leaks:** Oil leaks may occur from the valve covers, oil pan, or oil filter housing. Symptoms include an oil leak under the vehicle and a low oil level on the dipstick. The solution is to replace the leaking gasket or seal. The estimated cost to fix is $150 - $300+.
    * **Timing chain noise:** A rattling or ticking noise may be heard from the engine when it is cold. This noise is caused by a loose timing chain. The solution is to replace the timing chain and tensioner. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1500+.

**Transmission**

* **5-speed automatic transmission:**

    * **Slipping gears:** The transmission may slip out of gear while driving. This can be caused by a faulty solenoid or a worn clutch pack. The solution is to replace the faulty component. The estimated cost to fix is $1000 - $2500+.
    * **Noisy bearings:** A whining or grinding noise may be heard from the transmission while driving. This noise is caused by worn bearings. The solution is to replace the bearings. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1500+.

* **6-speed manual transmission:**

    * **Difficult shifting:** The transmission may be difficult to shift into gear. This can be caused by a worn clutch or a faulty shift linkage. The solution is to replace the faulty component. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1500+.

**Interior**

* **Sunroof leaks:** Water may leak into the interior of the vehicle through the sunroof. This can be caused by a clogged sunroof drain or a damaged sunroof seal. The solution is to clean the sunroof drain or replace the sunroof seal. The estimated cost to fix is $100 - $500+.

**Summary**

The 2014 Subaru Tribeca is a reliable and practical SUV. However, it is not without its problems. Some of the most common issues include oil leaks, timing chain noise, slipping gears, noisy bearings, and sunroof leaks. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem. Overall, the Tribeca is a good choice for those who need a spacious and all-wheel drive SUV.
