# 2016 Subaru Outback

### Overview
The 2016 Subaru Outback is a popular crossover SUV known for its standard all-wheel-drive system and versatile cargo space. Generally, it's considered a reliable vehicle, especially for those who value its off-road capabilities and practicality. However, like any vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners and mechanics. This report focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers of the 2016 model year. We will examine common problems reported across its various engine and transmission configurations.

---


## Engine

### 2.5L Boxer (FB25)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning between oil changes, potentially leading to low oil levels and engine damage if not monitored.
- **Solution:** Engine internal inspection, piston ring replacement, or in severe cases, engine replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $4,000

**Head Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, engine overheating, milky oil.
- **Solution:** Replacement of head gaskets and related components.
- **Cost:** $1,800 - $3,500

## Transmission

### CVT (TR580)
**Hesitation/Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission may hesitate during acceleration or exhibit jerky movements.
- **Solution:** Software updates, transmission fluid flush and replacement, or transmission repair/replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $3,000

**Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission warning light, slipping gears, reduced performance, especially under load or in hot weather.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid cooler inspection and replacement, transmission fluid flush.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1,200

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, freezes, or restarts randomly.
- **Solution:** Software updates, infotainment unit reset, or replacement of the unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,000


### Battery Drain
**Premature Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery drains quickly, leading to starting issues, often related to parasitic draw.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, repair of faulty modules or wiring, battery replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Subaru Outback generally offers good reliability, particularly with the 3.6L engine and manual transmission. The most commonly cited issues revolve around the 2.5L engine's oil consumption and potential head gasket leaks, along with CVT transmission concerns like hesitation and overheating. Electrical issues, primarily with the infotainment system and battery drain, are also noted. While these problems can be costly to repair, they are not universal. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to early symptoms can help mitigate many of these potential issues, making the Outback a solid choice for many buyers.
