# 2016 Subaru Outback Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2016 Subaru Outback, buyers get a spacious, capable crossover with standard all-wheel drive and strong safety ratings. However, reviewing owner complaints highlights a few recurring trouble spots that prospective buyers should inspect closely. Most notably, electrical glitches—particularly parasitic battery drains tied to telematics modules—and transmission or shifter interlock concerns surface frequently in owner logs. Being aware of these patterns can help you spot potential issues during a test drive and negotiate a fair price.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder (FB25)

A 2.5-liter horizontally opposed (boxer) 4-cylinder engine producing approximately 175 horsepower and 174 lb-ft of torque. It offers reliable daily driving and competitive fuel economy for an all-wheel-drive crossover, though routine fluid monitoring is recommended.

### 3.6L 6-Cylinder (EZ36)

A smooth 3.6-liter horizontally opposed 6-cylinder engine generating roughly 256 horsepower and 247 lb-ft of torque. This option provides robust passing power and a more substantial towing feel, though fuel efficiency is lower than the base four-cylinder.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

A continuously variable transmission paired with standard symmetrical all-wheel drive across all trims. While it maximizes highway fuel efficiency, buyers should listen closely for whining noises or hesitation during test drives.

**Sudden CVT Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience an immediate and dangerous loss of propulsion while driving due to internal transmission failure.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis and potentially a complete CVT replacement or rebuild.

## Electrical

### Battery and Charging System

The electrical architecture is generally robust, but parasitic drains originating from infotainment and telematics components can frustrate owners if left unresolved.

**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle's battery repeatedly dies overnight or after being parked for short periods, requiring frequent jump-starts or multiple battery replacements.
- **Solution:** Inspect and update or replace faulty telematics or DCM (Data Communication Module) units causing continuous electrical draw.

### Shifter and Ignition Interlock

The gear selector and linkage assemblies connect the cabin shifter to the transmission, though microswitch failures can occasionally prevent key removal.

**Key Trapped in Ignition or Park Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The driver is unable to remove the key from the ignition even when the shifter is placed firmly in Park, or the vehicle fails to recognize that it is in Park/Neutral.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace the selector lever complete neutral/park sensor switch assembly or shifter interlock mechanism.

## Exterior

### Windshield Glass

Exterior panels and trim hold up well, though factory windshield glass has been a common point of owner frustration regarding stress fractures.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Cracks develop across the windshield—often originating near the lower deicing heating grid—frequently without clear impact from road debris.
- **Solution:** Replace the windshield, ensuring proper recalibration of any attached EyeSight safety cameras if applicable.

## Safety

### EyeSight Driver Assist

Subaru's camera-based safety suite adds great active protection, but owners occasionally report nuisance warnings or unexpected braking activations.

**Random System Deactivation and False Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The EyeSight safety suite turns off unexpectedly or triggers uncommanded sudden automatic emergency braking when no obstacles are present.
- **Solution:** Clean sensor lenses, check camera alignment, and have dealer software updates applied if available.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V910000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Outback&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A replacement air bag control module may have been installed that is not compatible with the passenger air bag module, possibly affecting air bag deployment. In the event of a crash necessitating passenger frontal air bag deployment, the air bag may not deploy properly, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will replace the passenger air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V292000: Steering:Column](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Outback&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The loss of steering ability would increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the lot number on the steering column, replacing the steering column, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V502000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Outback&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the transmission oil leaks onto the exhaust pipe, there is a possible risk of fire.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will replace the propeller shaft, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V794000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Outback&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the drive shaft detaches from the rear differential, it may strike the fuel tank and result in a fuel leak, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Subaru has notified owners, and dealers will inspect the fastening nuts, and tighten or replace them, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Subaru Outback remains a compelling choice for buyers seeking all-weather capability and generous cargo space. While powertrain options are generally dependable, used car shoppers should verify service history for transmission health, test the electrical system for battery drain issues, and check that the key removes cleanly from the ignition during pre-purchase inspections.
