# 2017 Subaru Outback Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Subaru Outback combines rugged wagon utility, standard symmetrical all-wheel drive, and comfortable road manners, making it a popular choice for used car buyers. However, shoppers should approach certain model-year quirks with caution, as owner complaints highlight a few persistent trouble spots. Reviewing vehicle history and inspecting common problem areas can help you find a reliable example.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.5-liter horizontally opposed four-cylinder engine produces 175 horsepower and 174 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate daily acceleration and respectable fuel economy. It generally proves dependable with regular fluid maintenance, though isolated electrical or sensor faults can occasionally trigger unexpected performance issues.

**Engine Misfire and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle suddenly misfires, rapidly loses power, and suffers power steering failure while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Diagnose ignition components, fuel delivery, or engine management sensors and replace faulty parts.

### 3.6L 6-Cylinder Engine

The optional 3.6-liter six-cylinder engine generates 256 horsepower and 247 lb-ft of torque, offering smoother, more robust power delivery for towing and highway passing. It is regarded as a robust powertrain option with few widespread mechanical complaints.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The Lineartronic CVT is paired with both engine options and features a manual shift mode via paddle shifters. While it aids fuel efficiency, owners have reported concerns regarding abnormal noises, hesitation, and premature internal wear.

**CVT Failure and Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission makes squeaking or whirring noises, engine RPMs climb without a corresponding increase in speed, or the drivetrain unexpectedly locks up at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission operation; severe internal failures typically require professional repair or full CVT assembly replacement.

## Electrical

### Battery and Charging System

The electrical system manages a wide array of comfort and safety features, but persistent battery drain issues are a frequent complaint among owners.

**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle battery drains completely after sitting for a few days, leading to a dead battery and failure to start.
- **Solution:** Perform a parasitic draw test to isolate rogue electrical loads, update software modules if applicable, and replace weak batteries.

### Ignition Key Interlock

Shifter and ignition interlock components can occasionally fail to register the 'Park' position correctly, preventing key removal.

**Key Stuck in Ignition**
- **Symptoms:** Ignition key will not release after turning the engine off and placing the vehicle in park, requiring multiple attempts to free.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace faulty gear selector switches or ignition cylinder mechanisms.

## Exterior

### Windshield Glass

Body visibility is generally good, though the factory windshield glass has been a frequent point of frustration for owners due to a perceived susceptibility to cracking.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Windshield develops large cracks across the glass surface spontaneously or following minor stress, often with little to no visible impact damage.
- **Solution:** Replace the windshield with quality replacement glass, ensuring proper calibration for eyesight safety camera systems if equipped.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V910000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Outback&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A replacement air bag control module may have been installed that is not compatible with the passenger air bag module, possibly affecting air bag deployment. In the event of a crash necessitating passenger frontal air bag deployment, the air bag may not deploy properly, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will replace the passenger air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V576000: Suspension](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Outback&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** These conditions would increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru has notified owners, and dealers will inspect the bolts for proper torque, and replace any loose bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V716000: Steering:Column](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Outback&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, the knee guard bracket may not be properly attached to the steering beam assembly. If the knee guard is not properly attached and the driver is not wearing a seatbelt, the knee guard may not be able to properly restrain the driver's lower body in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the knee guard welding on each vehicle's steering beam, replacing the beam as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the lot number on the steering column, replacing the steering column, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Subaru Outback offers solid utility, all-weather confidence, and comfortable passenger accommodations. However, prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle for known trouble spots, including potential CVT transmission concerns, chronic battery drain complaints, and a history of windshield replacement. Checking service records and ensuring all electrical and mechanical systems operate smoothly will go a long way toward securing a dependable used vehicle.
