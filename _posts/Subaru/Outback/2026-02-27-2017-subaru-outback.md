# 2017 Subaru Outback

### Overview
The 2017 Subaru Outback is a popular choice for those seeking a versatile wagon with standard all-wheel drive, offering a blend of car-like comfort and SUV capability. It generally presents a solid reliability record, particularly with its well-regarded powertrain options. Owners often praise its practicality, spacious interior, and comfortable ride, making it suitable for daily commuting and adventurous road trips. While most owners report a positive ownership experience, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen more attention from a reliability standpoint. This analysis focuses on documented issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective buyers and current owners.

---


## Engine

### 2.5L Boxer (FB25)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning between changes, potentially leading to low oil levels.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels regularly. In some cases, piston ring replacement may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Head Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, white smoke from exhaust, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of head gaskets.
- **Cost:** $1,800 - $3,500

## Transmission

### CVT (TR580)
**Hesitation/Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent hesitation during acceleration or a slight jerk when shifting.
- **Solution:** Software updates, transmission fluid flush, or in severe cases, transmission replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $3,500

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive or freezes, requiring a reboot.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,200


### Battery Drain
**Premature Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dies unexpectedly, often related to parasitic draw from modules.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw and repair of faulty modules or wiring.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Subaru Outback is generally a reliable vehicle, with its 3.6L engine and standard chassis components showing minimal issues. The most commonly reported concerns revolve around the 2.5L engine's potential for oil consumption and, less frequently, head gasket leaks. The CVT transmission can sometimes exhibit minor hesitation. Electrical issues, particularly with the infotainment system and occasional battery drain, are also noted. Despite these points, the Outback remains a strong contender in its segment, offering good value and capability. Addressing potential oil consumption early and ensuring proper maintenance of the CVT can contribute to a positive ownership experience.
