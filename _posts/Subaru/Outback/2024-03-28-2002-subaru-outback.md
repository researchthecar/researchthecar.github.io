**Introduction:**

The 2002 Subaru Outback is a versatile SUV that combines ruggedness and practicality. It comes with a standard 2.5-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine or an optional 3.0-liter H6 engine. Both engines are mated to a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Outback is known for its all-wheel-drive capability, spacious interior, and high ground clearance. However, some owners have reported issues with certain components of the vehicle.

**Engine**

**2.5-liter 4-Cylinder Engine**

* **Head Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking coolant, overheating, white smoke from the exhaust.
    * Solution: Replace head gaskets.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Belt Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine stalls while driving, rough idle, ticking sound from the engine.
    * Solution: Replace timing belt, tensioner, and water pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.0-liter H6 Engine**

* **Valve Cover Gasket Leaking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from the valve cover gaskets.
    * Solution: Replace valve cover gaskets.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Hesitation and Stalling:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission hesitates or stalls when shifting gears.
    * Solution: Replace transmission control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Top Plate Bearing Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Creaking or clunking sounds from the suspension, especially over bumps.
    * Solution: Replace strut top plate bearings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per side
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Differential Mount Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vibration or noise from the rear of the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace rear differential mount.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the vehicle, dim headlights, electrical system issues.
    * Solution: Replace alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Leakage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the vehicle through the sunroof.
    * Solution: Replace or repair sunroof seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Center Console Lid Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Center console lid breaks or becomes loose.
    * Solution: Replace center console lid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Overall Summary**

The 2002 Subaru Outback has mixed reliability ratings. While it offers some desirable features and capabilities, it is prone to certain issues, particularly with the head gasket and transmission. These issues can range from minor inconveniences to costly repairs. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems before making a purchase decision.
