**Introduction:**

The 2000 Subaru Outback is a midsize SUV that combines off-road capabilities with everyday practicality. It comes with a standard 2.5L H4 engine, but can be upgraded to a 3.0L H6 engine. The standard transmission is a 5-speed manual, but a 4-speed automatic is optional. The Outback is popular for its ruggedness, all-wheel drive system, and spacious interior. However, it has received some criticism for its lack of refinement and fuel economy.

**Engine**

**2.5L H4**

**Head Gasket Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Leaking coolant, overheating, loss of power
Solution: Replace head gaskets
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
Description/Symptoms: Oil leak from valve cover
Solution: Replace valve cover gasket
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

**3.0L H6**

**No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

**Grinding Gears**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, especially when downshifting
Solution: Replace synchronizers and/or gears
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic**

**Transmission Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Loss of gears, slipping, no movement
Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Front Struts**
Description/Symptoms: Worn struts result in a rough ride and reduced handling
Solution: Replace struts
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

**Rear Shock Absorbers**
Description/Symptoms: Worn shock absorbers result in poor handling and rear end sagging
Solution: Replace shock absorbers
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Alternator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Dead battery, flickering lights, warning lights
Solution: Replace alternator
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

**Starter Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the vehicle, no crank
Solution: Replace starter
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Rear Hatch Struts**
Description/Symptoms: Weak struts result in a difficult to open or close rear hatch
Solution: Replace rear hatch struts
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Power Window Switches**
Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning switches result in inoperable power windows
Solution: Replace power window switches
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ per window
Recalls: None Found

**Dashboard Panel**
Description/Symptoms: Cracking or fading dashboard panel
Solution: Replace dashboard panel
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2000 Subaru Outback is generally a reliable vehicle, but it has some common issues that owners should be aware of. The engine head gasket failure is a major concern, as it can be expensive to repair. Transmission problems can also be costly, and the suspension components can wear out prematurely. The electrical system and interior components are generally reliable, but there are some minor issues to watch out for. Overall, the Outback is a capable and practical vehicle, but it is important to be aware of the potential issues that it may encounter.
