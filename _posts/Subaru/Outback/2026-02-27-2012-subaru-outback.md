# 2012 Subaru Outback

### Overview
The 2012 Subaru Outback is a mid-size crossover SUV known for its standard all-wheel-drive system and comfortable ride. It offers a practical and versatile option for families and outdoor enthusiasts. This generation of the Outback generally presents a solid reliability record, with most owners reporting satisfaction with its performance and durability. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen more attention from owners and mechanics. Understanding these potential issues can help in maintenance and long-term ownership.

---


## Engine

### 2.5L Boxer (Naturally Aspirated)
**Head Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, overheating, milky oil.
- **Solution:** Replacement of head gaskets and associated seals.
- **Cost:** $1500 - $2500

**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Low oil level between changes, blue smoke from exhaust on startup.
- **Solution:** Can sometimes be related to PCV system or piston ring wear; may require engine rebuild in severe cases.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000+

## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Sway Bar Links**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or rattling noise over bumps, especially at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of sway bar links.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dies unexpectedly, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, replacement of faulty modules or wiring.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of head unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1000

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Subaru Outback is generally a reliable vehicle, particularly with the 3.6L engine and manual transmission. The most commonly cited issues revolve around the 2.5L engine's head gaskets and potential oil consumption, as well as some CVT transmission concerns like hesitation. Electrical gremlins, such as battery drain and infotainment glitches, can also occur. However, many owners experience trouble-free ownership, especially with diligent maintenance. Addressing potential issues like head gaskets proactively can prevent more significant problems down the line.
