**Introduction**

The 2023 Subaru Outback is a midsize crossover SUV known for its rugged capability, spacious interior, and advanced safety features. It is available with a choice of two engines and two transmission options. The base engine is a 2.5-liter flat-four that produces 182 horsepower and 176 lb-ft of torque, paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). The optional engine is a turbocharged 2.4-liter flat-four that produces 260 horsepower and 277 lb-ft of torque, paired with either a CVT or a six-speed manual transmission.

Drivers appreciate the Outback's versatility, comfort, and off-road capabilities. However, some owners have reported various issues with their vehicles.

**Engine**

**2.5-liter Flat-Four with CVT**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replacing the piston rings or engine block.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Head Gasket Failure:** A small number of owners have experienced head gasket failure, causing coolant leaks and overheating.
    * Solution: Replacing the head gasket and associated components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000
    * Recalls: N/A

**2.4-liter Turbocharged Flat-Four with CVT**

* **Turbocharger Failure:** Isolated cases of turbocharger failure have been reported, resulting in reduced engine performance.
    * Solution: Replacing the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.4-liter Turbocharged Flat-Four with Six-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Shudder:** Some owners have experienced clutch shudder during shifts, particularly when starting from a stop.
    * Solution: Replacing the clutch disk and pressure plate.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT**

* **Hesitation or Jerkiness:** Some CVT transmissions have exhibited hesitation or jerkiness during shifts.
    * Solution: Software updates or replacing the transmission control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Six-Speed Manual**

* **Shifting Difficulty:** A small number of owners have reported difficulty shifting gears, especially when the transmission is cold.
    * Solution: Adjusting or replacing the clutch linkage.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure:** Premature failure of the front strut mounts has been reported, causing noise and vibration.
    * Solution: Replacing the strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** A handful of owners have experienced excessive battery drain, leading to dead batteries.
    * Solution: Identifying and repairing the source of the drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Infotainment System Glitches:** Some owners have encountered glitches with the infotainment system, including freezing, slow response, or connectivity issues.
    * Solution: Software updates or replacing the infotainment unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Rust:** Isolated cases of premature rusting on the tailgate have been reported.
    * Solution: Replacing the tailgate.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunctions:** Some owners have reported issues with the climate control system, including uneven airflow, temperature fluctuations, or malfunctions.
    * Solution: Replacing or repairing the climate control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    * Recalls: NHTSA Recall No. 22V-010

**Summary**

The 2023 Subaru Outback is generally a reliable vehicle, but some owners have encountered issues. The severity and cost of these issues vary depending on the component and the specific problem. While some issues are isolated cases, others can be more widespread and potentially costly. Before purchasing a used 2023 Outback, it is recommended to have it thoroughly inspected by a reputable mechanic and to consider extended warranty coverage.
