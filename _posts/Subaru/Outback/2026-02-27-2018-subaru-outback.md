# 2018 Subaru Outback Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2018 Subaru Outback, buyers will find a versatile crossover known for standard all-wheel drive and a comfortable ride. However, prospective owners should review common owner-reported trouble areas, including electrical issues, infotainment display failures, and windshield durability concerns, before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.5-liter horizontally opposed four-cylinder engine produces 175 horsepower and 174 lb-ft of torque, offering adequate daily performance and reasonable fuel efficiency for everyday driving.

### 3.6L 6-Cylinder Engine

The available 3.6-liter six-cylinder engine provides a smoother, more robust 256 horsepower and 247 lb-ft of torque, though some owners have reported oil leaks requiring expensive repairs.

**Cam Carrier Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Engine oil leaking from the upper cam carrier seals, potentially resulting in burning smells or visible oil spots.
- **Solution:** Reseal or replace the leaking cam carrier gaskets.
- **Cost:** $5,700 plus tax

## Transmission

### Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Equipped across all trims with standard Symmetrical All-Wheel Drive, the Lineartronic CVT prioritizes fuel economy but requires regular fluid maintenance to ensure long-term durability.

## Electrical

### Battery and Charging System

Electrical complaints frequently center around unexpected battery drain and charging system faults that leave vehicles unable to start.

**Premature Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to start, requiring frequent jump-starts or premature battery replacement due to unexpected parasitic power draw.
- **Solution:** Diagnose parasitic electrical draws, update software modules if applicable, and replace the battery with an upgraded unit.

### Infotainment and Display Unit

Safety electronics are occasionally impacted by infotainment failures that can distract drivers or disable backup camera views.

**Screen Delamination and Phantom Touch**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen displays developing internal bubbling, opaque blotches, and erratic 'ghost touching' that changes radio stations or adjusts settings independently.
- **Solution:** Replace the infotainment head unit or display assembly.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Glass

Exterior complaints highlight vulnerability to windshield stress cracks, which can require frequent glass replacements.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Windshields developing large cracks across the glass suddenly without any clear impact from rocks or road debris.
- **Solution:** Replace the windshield, ensuring proper recalibration of any attached EyeSight safety cameras.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V935000: Visibility:Rearview Mirrors/Devices](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Outback&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, a software issue may cause the audio display system to not initialize, resulting in the camera display remaining blank. As a result, the vehicles fail to comply with requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." Failure to display the rearview image while backing up increases the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru has notified owners, and dealers will reprogram the audio system display software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V773000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Outback&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Due to a software error, the low fuel warning light may not illuminate at the intended remaining fuel level and the miles-to-empty display may incorrectly indicate a positive number despite the tank being empty. The inaccurate fuel display may cause a driver to unexpectedly run out of fuel and the vehicle to stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the combination meter software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V587000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Outback&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the low-pressure fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Subaru Outback is a capable and practical crossover, but buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle's electrical health, infotainment screen condition, and service records. Verifying whether the battery and touchscreen have been previously addressed can save new owners from frustrating and unexpected repair bills.
