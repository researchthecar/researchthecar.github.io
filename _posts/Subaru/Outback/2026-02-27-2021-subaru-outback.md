# 2021 Subaru Outback

### Overview
The 2021 Subaru Outback is a popular choice for those seeking a capable and versatile SUV with standard all-wheel drive. It generally offers a good balance of comfort, practicality, and off-road prowess. Reliability for the 2021 model year is considered strong, with most owners reporting a positive experience. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas where minor concerns have been noted, primarily related to infotainment and some powertrain aspects, though these do not appear to be widespread critical failures. The Outback's robust build and Subaru's reputation for longevity contribute to its overall favorable reliability standing.

---


## Engine

### 2.5L Boxer (FB25)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Slightly higher than average oil consumption noted by some owners, particularly during the initial break-in period. Typically resolves with normal driving.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels regularly and top up as needed. Consult dealer if consumption is excessive or persistent.
- **Cost:** N/A (Normal Operation)

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Rare instances of coolant leaks from hoses or seals reported.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty hose or seal.
- **Cost:** $150 - $350


### 2.4L Turbocharged Boxer (FA24)
**Turbocharger Lag**
- **Symptoms:** Some drivers report a slight delay in turbocharger response under certain acceleration conditions.
- **Solution:** Software updates or driving style adjustments can mitigate perceived lag.
- **Cost:** N/A (Characteristic)

**Carbon Buildup**
- **Symptoms:** Potential for carbon buildup on intake valves in direct-injection engines over time, though less common in this specific application compared to some others.
- **Solution:** Periodic intake valve cleaning service.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Transmission

### CVT (Lineartronic)
**Hesitation/Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Infrequent reports of minor hesitation or slight jerking during acceleration or gear changes.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the transmission control module. Fluid changes.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400

**Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Under extreme towing or heavy load conditions, some instances of transmission overheating have been noted.
- **Solution:** Ensure proper transmission fluid levels and cooling system maintenance. Avoid exceeding towing limits.
- **Cost:** N/A (User Dependent)

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Subaru Outback demonstrates solid reliability, aligning with Subaru's reputation. The powertrain, particularly the engines and CVT, is generally robust, though minor oil consumption on the 2.5L and potential for turbo lag on the 2.4T are noted characteristics rather than widespread failures. The most frequently reported, though still not common, issues tend to be with the infotainment system's software, such as screen unresponsiveness or connectivity glitches. These are often resolved with software updates. Electrical components like the battery have seen isolated premature failures. Overall, the Outback is a dependable vehicle, with most potential issues being minor and manageable through routine maintenance or software adjustments.
