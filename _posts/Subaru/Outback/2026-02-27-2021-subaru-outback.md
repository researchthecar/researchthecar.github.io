# 2021 Subaru Outback Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Subaru Outback offers standard symmetrical all-wheel drive, rugged wagon styling, and a comfortable ride, making it a popular choice for families and outdoor enthusiasts. However, prospective buyers should review several recurring owner-reported complaints. The most common areas of concern involve electrical system issues—particularly battery drain—and frequent reports of fragile windshield glass prone to spontaneous cracking.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The standard 2.5-liter horizontally opposed 4-cylinder engine produces 182 horsepower, providing adequate daily power and competitive fuel efficiency for a crossover wagon.

**Thermo Control Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light, disabled safety systems such as EyeSight, and potential engine overheating if not promptly addressed.
- **Solution:** Have the thermo control valve inspected and replaced under the manufacturer's extended warranty coverage.

### 2.4L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

The available 2.4-liter turbocharged boxer engine generates 260 horsepower, offering much stronger acceleration and passing power for highway driving and towing.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Paired with standard all-wheel drive across all trims, this CVT delivers smooth power delivery and optimizes fuel economy, though buyers should listen for unusual whines or hesitation during test drives.

## Electrical

### Battery and Charging System

Electrical complaints center heavily around parasitic power draws and unexpected dead batteries, often requiring dealer diagnostics.

**Premature Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to start after sitting, frequent dead batteries, or electrical drains linked to the Data Communication Module (DCM).
- **Solution:** Diagnose parasitic draws, update software modules, and replace the battery with an upgraded capacity unit if necessary.

### Infotainment System

The large touchscreen interface controls many core vehicle functions, but owners frequently report software instability and intermittent blackouts.

**Screen Freezing and Reboots**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment display goes completely blank or restarts spontaneously while driving, temporarily disabling climate and audio controls.
- **Solution:** Perform software updates or replace head unit components if hardware failure is diagnosed.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Glass

Windshield durability is a notable pain point for owners, with many complaints citing fragile glass that cracks easily under minor stress.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Small stone chips rapidly expanding into long cracks across the driver's field of vision, or cracks developing overnight without impact.
- **Solution:** Replace the windshield through an auto glass specialist familiar with recalibrating forward-facing safety cameras.

## Safety

### EyeSight Driver Assist

Advanced safety features generally enhance protection, though occasional false activations and sensor disengagements have been reported.

**Unexpected Automatic Braking**
- **Symptoms:** Forward collision avoidance system falsely triggers emergency braking when no obstructions are present.
- **Solution:** Check camera calibration, keep the upper windshield clean, and check for software updates at a dealership.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V024000: Power Train:Shift Linkage/Cable/Rod](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Outback&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The continuously variable transmission (CVT) select lever cable nut may not have been tightened properly, allowing the nut to loosen. A loose nut can lead to a lost of retention in the CVT select lever cable and the gear selector not functioning properly, increasing risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will inspect, and, if necessary, tighten the nut, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V485000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Outback&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An improperly secured drive chain may slip and break, causing a loss of drive power and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the TCU, inspect TCU data for chain slip, and visually inspect the chain guide. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V755000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Park/Neutral Start Interlock Switch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Outback&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An inoperative inhibitor switch may prevent the reverse lights from illuminating and the rearview camera image from displaying, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the inhibitor switch, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V227000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Outback&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A capacitor in the sensors for the Occupant Detection System (ODS) may crack and short circuit, which can prevent the front passenger air bag from deploying in a crash. An air bag that does not deploy during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the ODS sensors on the front passenger seat, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Subaru Outback is a versatile and capable used crossover, but buyers should perform a thorough inspection before purchasing. Pay close attention to battery health, electrical accessories, infotainment stability, and the condition of the windshield. Ensuring that any thermo control valve updates or warranty extensions have been applied will help secure a more dependable ownership experience.
