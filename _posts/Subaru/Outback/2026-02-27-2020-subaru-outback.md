# 2020 Subaru Outback Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Subaru Outback is a versatile and popular crossover known for its standard all-wheel drive and roomy interior, but buyers should approach used models with eyes wide open regarding known recurring issues. Owner complaints to federal safety regulators center around several distinct trouble spots, particularly persistent battery drain, fragile windshields, and potential transmission and electrical glitches. While it offers strong utility and rugged styling, checking service records for past warranty claims or prior component replacements is strongly advised.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L Naturally Aspirated H4

This base 2.5-liter horizontally opposed four-cylinder engine produces approximately 182 horsepower and 176 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting and routine driving with respectable fuel economy.

### 2.4L Turbocharged H4

The upgraded 2.4-liter turbocharged boxer engine bumps output to 260 horsepower and 277 lb-ft of torque, delivering much stronger acceleration and towing capability for XT and Onyx trims.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic CVT

Paired with standard symmetrical all-wheel drive across all trims, the Lineartronic continuously variable transmission (CVT) is designed for smooth power delivery and high efficiency, though some owners have reported premature internal failures.

**Transmission Slipping and Pressure Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a sudden loss of vehicle movement while in drive or reverse, illuminated check engine lights, and error codes such as P0867 indicating low transmission fluid pressure.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnostic inspection by a certified dealer, which may involve valve body replacement, software updates, or complete transmission repairs.

## Electrical

### Electrical System & Battery

The electrical architecture handles extensive safety and convenience tech, though it has generated a high volume of owner complaints regarding parasitic drains and starting troubles.

**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle fails to start intermittently after sitting, requiring frequent jump-starts or premature battery replacements.
- **Solution:** Inspect for parasitic draw from vehicle modules, update infotainment software, and replace the battery if depleted.

## Exterior

### Windshield Glass

The exterior styling incorporates a large glass greenhouse, but owners frequently report issues with windshield vulnerability and stress fractures.

**Spontaneous and Fragile Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Windshields develop long stress cracks spontaneously or from minor impacts that normally would not damage automotive glass.
- **Solution:** Replace the windshield, ensuring proper calibration of advanced driver assistance system (Eyesight) cameras mounted nearby.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V766000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Outback&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." The intermittent loss of the rearview image reduces the driver's visibility and increases the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the software and, if necessary, replace the Cockpit Control Module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V955000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Outback&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** An improperly secured drive chain may slip and break, causing a loss of drive power and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the TCU, inspect TCU data for chain slip, and visually inspect the chain guide. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V587000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Outback&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the low-pressure fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V664000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Pedals And Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Outback&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A missing or loose bolt may cause the brake pedal mounting area to deform over time, which can reduce braking performance and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the brake pedal mounting bracket hardware and replace the hardware or brake pedal assembly, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V485000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Outback&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** An improperly secured drive chain may slip and break, causing a loss of drive power and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the TCU, inspect TCU data for chain slip, and visually inspect the chain guide. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V227000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Outback&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A capacitor in the sensors for the Occupant Detection System (ODS) may crack and short circuit, which can prevent the front passenger air bag from deploying in a crash. An air bag that does not deploy during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the ODS sensors on the front passenger seat, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Subaru Outback is a comfortable and capable crossover, but used car buyers should verify that key trouble areas have been addressed. Pay close attention to battery health, electrical systems, windshield condition, and transmission performance during a pre-purchase inspection. Securing an extended warranty that covers CVT and electrical components can provide extra peace of mind.
