# 2019 Subaru Outback Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Subaru Outback offers rugged capability, standard symmetrical all-wheel drive, and generous cargo space, making it a popular choice for families and outdoor enthusiasts. However, prospective buyers should review a few well-documented recurring issues. Common owner complaints center around persistent electrical battery drains, spontaneous windshield cracking, and infotainment screen delamination.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Boxer Engine

The base 2.5-liter four-cylinder boxer engine produces 175 horsepower and 174 lb-ft of torque, offering adequate power for daily commuting and decent fuel economy. It is generally dependable, though owners should monitor for potential cam carrier oil leaks as the vehicle ages.

**Cam Carrier Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Oil spots under the vehicle or burning oil smells originating from the engine bay due to oil seepage from the cam carrier seals.
- **Solution:** Requires resealing the cam carrier housing, which is an intensive labor procedure.

### 3.6L 6-Cylinder Boxer Engine

The available 3.6-liter six-cylinder boxer engine generates 256 horsepower and 247 lb-ft of torque, providing much quicker acceleration and robust towing capability. This larger engine option is generally robust and smooth, though it consumes more fuel than the base four-cylinder.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic CVT Automatic Transmission

Equipped across all trims, the Lineartronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) pairs with Subaru's symmetrical all-wheel-drive system to maximize fuel efficiency. While generally smooth in everyday driving, buyers should test-drive thoroughly to ensure responsive power delivery without hesitation or slipping.

## Electrical

### Electrical System & Battery Drain

Electrical concerns are highlighted by numerous owner reports of premature battery failure and parasitic power drains.

**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to start or experiences a completely dead battery after sitting parked for a few days due to excessive parasitic draw, often linked to the DCM or infotainment modules.
- **Solution:** Diagnose parasitic draw, update software modules if applicable, and replace the battery as needed.

### Infotainment Head Unit

The Starlink touchscreen infotainment system can suffer from software glitches and hardware degradation over time.

**Screen Delamination and Ghost Touches**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment screen develops bubbles or internal bubbling (delamination), causing unresponsive controls, random screen freezing, or 'ghost touches' that change radio stations or settings automatically.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty infotainment head unit assembly.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Visibility

Exterior complaints frequently mention fragile windshield glass that appears prone to cracking spontaneously under normal ownership conditions.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Windshields develop long cracks suddenly without any apparent impact from road debris or stones, often spreading overnight.
- **Solution:** Replace the windshield, ensuring proper calibration of any associated EyeSight safety camera systems.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V493000: Structure](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Outback&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the reduced strength of the vehicle body may increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the vehicles and repair or repurchase the vehicle, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V587000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Outback&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the low-pressure fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V218000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Outback&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The low pressure fuel pump may become inoperative. If the low pressure fuel pump fails, the engine may stall without the ability to restart the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Subaru will notify owners, and dealers will replace the low pressure fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Subaru Outback remains a practical and highly capable used crossover option, backed by standard all-wheel drive and a comfortable ride. However, buyers should carefully check for electrical battery drains, history of infotainment screen delamination, and any pre-existing windshield cracks. A thorough pre-purchase inspection can help uncover hidden fluid leaks or electrical issues before finalizing your purchase.
