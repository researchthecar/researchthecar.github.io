# 2023 Subaru Outback Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Subaru Outback offers standard symmetrical all-wheel drive, generous ground clearance, and a practical wagon body style that appeals to everyday drivers and outdoor enthusiasts alike. While owners generally praise its comfort and capability, owner-submitted reports highlight several recurring areas of concern. Prospective buyers should carefully review electronics, driver-assistance features, and windshield durability during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.5-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder boxer engine produces 182 horsepower and 176 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting and routine driving tasks with reliable performance.

### 2.4L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

The turbocharged 2.4-liter boxer engine increases output to 260 horsepower and 277 lb-ft of torque, offering stronger acceleration and towing capability while maintaining standard all-wheel drive.

## Transmission

### Lineartronic CVT Automatic Transmission

All 2023 Outback trims utilize a Lineartronic continuously variable transmission paired with symmetrical all-wheel drive. Most drivers experience smooth operation, though mechanical concerns such as torque converter judder have been reported by some owners.

**Torque Converter Shaking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may begin to jolt or shake when coming to a stop, often linked to torque converter anomalies.
- **Solution:** Have the transmission evaluated by a qualified dealership or mechanic to determine if software resets or component replacement is necessary.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Exterior Lighting

Electrical complaints center around touch-screen infotainment software quirks, connectivity glitches, and exterior lighting visibility concerns.

**Infotainment Pop-Ups and Headlight Performance**
- **Symptoms:** Promotional pop-ups may take over the center screen blocking climate controls, while some drivers note visibility concerns with headlights at night.
- **Solution:** Check for system software updates at a dealership to resolve infotainment display glitches.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Glass

Exterior complaints frequently mention susceptibility to windshield chipping and progressive cracking from minor road debris.

**Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** The windshield may develop cracks rapidly after minor impacts from small road debris, or seemingly spontaneously across the glass.
- **Solution:** Inspect the windshield thoroughly for existing chips or stress cracks prior to purchase, and confirm your auto insurance covers glass replacement.

## Safety

### Forward Collision & Driver Assistance

Subaru's EyeSight driver assist suite enhances safety, but a subset of owners report unexpected system interventions or temporary deactivations.

**Unexpected Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The automatic emergency braking or collision avoidance systems may unexpectedly apply the brakes or deactivate during normal driving.
- **Solution:** Ensure camera and sensor arrays are clean and check with a dealer for any available software updates or sensor recalibrations.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V647000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Outback&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Separation of the front end of the driveshaft increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will clean the bolt mounting surfaces of the center support brace and install new bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V755000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Park/Neutral Start Interlock Switch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Subaru&model=Outback&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** An inoperative inhibitor switch may prevent the reverse lights from illuminating and the rearview camera image from displaying, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the inhibitor switch, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Subaru Outback remains a compelling choice for used-car buyers seeking versatility, safety, and all-weather traction. While powertrain performance is generally robust, shoppers should carefully inspect the vehicle for reported electrical glitches, advanced driver assistance behavior, and windshield condition. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a knowledgeable technician is strongly recommended to ensure all systems operate as intended.
