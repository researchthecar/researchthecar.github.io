# 2017 Fiat 500X Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a used 2017 Fiat 500X offers an Italian-styled compact crossover experience shared under the skin with Jeep. However, prospective buyers should inspect powertrain history and electronics carefully. While many owners enjoy its distinctive design and driving dynamics, owner reports indicate potential areas of concern regarding oil consumption, stalling, and complex electrical or transmission behavior.

---

## Engine

### 1.4L Turbocharged I4

The 1.4-liter turbocharged inline-four engine produces approximately 160 horsepower and is typically paired with a manual transmission. It provides an energetic drive, though routine maintenance and vigilant fluid monitoring are recommended.

### 2.4L Tigershark I4

The 2.4-liter Tigershark naturally aspirated inline-four engine generates 180 horsepower and 175 lb-ft of torque, usually paired with an automatic transmission. Buyers should thoroughly review service records for oil consumption habits and check for potential stalling symptoms.

**Excessive Oil Consumption and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported illuminated oil pressure lights, unexpected engine stalling while driving at various speeds, and high rates of oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels frequently between changes and consult manufacturer technical service campaigns regarding oil consumption evaluations and software updates.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The 6-speed manual transmission is paired exclusively with the 1.4L turbo engine and front-wheel drive. While simpler than its automatic counterparts, shifting mechanicals should be verified during a test drive.

**Difficulty Shifting Gears**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience resistance or an inability to shift into upper gears, sometimes requiring major transmission component replacement at relatively low mileage.
- **Solution:** Inspect manual shift linkage, clutch assembly, and transmission fluid condition; replacement of the transmission unit may be necessary if internal mechanical failure occurs.

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission is paired with the 2.4L engine across front-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations. Buyers should pay close attention to shift smoothness and warning indicators during a test drive.

**Transmission Warning and Engagement Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may encounter a 'Service Transmission' warning message accompanied by 'Drive Mode Unavailable,' effectively dropping the vehicle into neutral while driving.
- **Solution:** Check for diagnostic trouble codes, ensure software updates are applied, and inspect transmission control modules or internal hydraulic components.

## Steering

### Dynamic Steering Torque and Warning Lights

Steering assistance relies heavily on electronic sensors and integrated stability systems that require a healthy electrical system to operate reliably.

**Multiple Stability and Steering Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have noted recurring warning lights related to dynamic steering torque, anti-lock brakes, electronic parking brakes, and hill hold control.
- **Solution:** Scan the vehicle computer for diagnostic codes and check wheel speed sensors, wiring connections, and control modules.

## Exterior

### Liftgate and Body Control Modules

Electrical architecture governs numerous convenience and safety features, making thorough inspection of all switches and modules important before purchase.

**Unexpected Rear Liftgate Unlatching**
- **Symptoms:** The rear liftgate may unexpectedly unlatch while the vehicle is stationary or moving when passive locking features are active.
- **Solution:** Inspect liftgate latch mechanisms, wiring harnesses, and body control module programming for faults.

## Safety

### Seat Belts and Forward Collision Warning

Safety and driver-assist features should be fully tested during a pre-purchase inspection to ensure occupant protection systems function as intended.

**Seat Belt Buckle Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The seat belt may fail to physically latch properly while the vehicle indicator incorrectly shows that it is securely buckled.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat belt buckle assembly for mechanical obstruction or switch failure and replace the buckle mechanism if defective.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V146000: Tires:Pressure Monitoring And Regulating Systems](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Fiat&model=500X&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** When the vehicle has a tire with low tire pressure, the tire pressure monitoring system (TPMS) warning light may not illuminate. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 138, "Tire Pressure Monitoring Systems." Driving a vehicle with an underinflated tire may result in tire failure.
- **What to do:** Fiat will notify owners, and dealers will update the instrument panel software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Fiat 500X can be an appealing choice for buyers seeking distinctive European styling and crossover utility on a budget. However, because owner complaints highlight potential risks involving oil consumption, unexpected engine stalling, and transmission or electronic warnings, a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection and detailed service history review are strongly advised.
