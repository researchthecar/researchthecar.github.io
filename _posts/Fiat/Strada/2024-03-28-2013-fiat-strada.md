**2013 Fiat Strada**

The 2013 Fiat Strada is a compact pickup truck that is offered with a variety of engine and transmission options. The base engine is a 1.4-liter gasoline engine that produces 85 horsepower and 90 lb-ft of torque. A 1.6-liter gasoline engine that produces 110 horsepower and 112 lb-ft of torque is also available. Both engines are available with a five-speed manual transmission or a four-speed automatic transmission. The Strada is also available with a 1.3-liter turbodiesel engine that produces 95 horsepower and 140 lb-ft of torque, which is paired with a five-speed manual transmission.

Drivers appreciate the Strada's affordable price, nimble handling, and decent fuel economy. However, the vehicle has been criticized for its lack of power, cramped interior, and poor safety ratings.

**Engine**

* **1.4-liter Gasoline Engine**
    * **Valve Cover Gasket Failure:** Oil leaks from the valve cover gasket, causing a burning oil smell and potentially leading to engine damage.
      * Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
      * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
      * Recalls: None Found
    * **Timing Belt Failure:** The timing belt can fail prematurely, causing the engine to stall and potentially causing catastrophic engine damage.
      * Solution: Replace the timing belt and related components.
      * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000+
      * Recalls: None Found
* **1.6-liter Gasoline Engine**
    * **No Issues Found**
* **1.3-liter Turbodiesel Engine**
    * **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Problems:** The DPF can become clogged, causing a loss of power and increased fuel consumption.
      * Solution: Clean or replace the DPF.
      * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
      * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **Five-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **No Issues Found**
* **Four-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Transmission Fluid Leaks:** The transmission fluid can leak from the transmission pan or lines, causing the transmission to slip or fail.
      * Solution: Repair or replace the leaking component.
      * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
      * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Leaf Springs:** The rear leaf springs can sag or break, affecting the vehicle's handling and ride quality.
  * Solution: Replace the leaf springs.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
  * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** The battery can drain prematurely, leaving the vehicle unable to start.
  * Solution: Replace the battery or identify and fix the underlying cause of the drain.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
  * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Actuators:** The door lock actuators can fail, preventing the doors from locking or unlocking.
  * Solution: Replace the faulty actuators.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
  * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Air Conditioning Compressor Failure:** The air conditioning compressor can fail, leading to a loss of cooling.
  * Solution: Replace the compressor.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2013 Fiat Strada has several potential issues that can affect its engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, and interior components. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to major repairs. The cost of repairs can be significant, especially for engine and transmission problems. Overall, the Strada has a reputation for being a reliable vehicle, but it is important to be aware of the potential issues that it can experience.
