**2013 Fiat Palio Overview**

The 2013 Fiat Palio is a subcompact hatchback available with a range of engine and transmission options. The 1.2-liter Fire engine produces 68 horsepower and 74 lb-ft of torque, while the 1.4-liter Fire engine generates 85 horsepower and 90 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a 5-speed manual transmission, while the 1.4-liter engine can also be equipped with a Dualogic automated manual transmission.

The Palio has received positive reviews for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and fuel efficiency. However, it has also drawn criticism for its lackluster performance and dated interior design.

**Engine Options**
* 1.2-liter Fire - 68 horsepower, 74 lb-ft of torque
* 1.4-liter Fire - 85 horsepower, 90 lb-ft of torque

**Transmission Options**
* 5-speed manual transmission
* Dualogic automated manual transmission (with 1.4-liter Fire engine only)

**Engine**

**1.2-liter Fire**
* **Faulty camshaft sensor**
Description/Symptoms: The camshaft sensor may fail, causing the engine to misfire, idle roughly, or stall.
Solution: Replace the camshaft sensor.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
Recalls: 15V617

**1.4-liter Fire**
* **Intake manifold gasket failure**
Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold gasket may fail, causing a loss of engine power, rough idle, and excessive exhaust emissions.
Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
Recalls: 14V555

**Transmission**

**5-speed manual transmission**
* **Difficulty shifting gears**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission may become difficult to shift gears, especially when cold.
Solution: Replace the transmission fluid and inspect the shift linkage.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250+
Recalls: None Found

**Dualogic automated manual transmission**
* **Jerky shifts**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission may shift gears jerkily or hesitate when changing gears.
Solution: Update the transmission software and inspect the clutch system.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
Recalls: 13V364

**Suspension**
* **Rear suspension noise**
Description/Symptoms: A clunking noise may be heard from the rear suspension when driving over bumps or rough roads.
Solution: Replace the rear shock absorbers or springs.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
* **Battery drain**
Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain quickly, leading to starting problems or electrical failures.
Solution: Check for parasitic draw on the electrical system and replace the battery if necessary.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
Recalls: 13V029

**Exterior**
* **Rust on rear hatch**
Description/Symptoms: Rust may form on the rear hatch, especially around the license plate area.
Solution: Repair the rust and apply anti-rust treatment.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
* **Hard-to-use climate controls**
Description/Symptoms: The climate controls may be difficult to use, especially when driving.
Solution: None known.
Estimated Cost to Fix: N/A
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2013 Fiat Palio has some significant issues with its engine and transmission, including camshaft sensor failures, intake manifold gasket failures, and shifting problems. The suspension and electrical systems may also experience issues, but these are less common. The interior and exterior have some minor issues, but they are not as severe as the mechanical problems. Overall, the Palio's issues are moderate in severity and can be expensive to repair, but the vehicle still offers a spacious interior and comfortable ride.
