**Introduction:**

The 2012 Fiat 500 is a subcompact hatchback that is available in four trim levels: Pop, Sport, Lounge, and Abarth. The Fiat 500 is powered by a 1.4L MultiAir I4 engine that produces 101 horsepower and 98 lb-ft of torque. This engine is paired with either a 5-speed manual transmission or a 6-speed automatic transmission.

People like the Fiat 500 for its stylish design, fuel efficiency, and affordability. However, some people dislike the Fiat 500 for its small size, lack of power, and poor reliability.

**Engine**

**1.4L MultiAir I4 Engine**

* **Intake Manifold Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold may crack, causing a loss of power and fuel economy.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner may fail, causing the timing chain to skip and damage the engine.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Shifter Linkage Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The shifter linkage may break, making it difficult to shift gears.
    * Solution: Replace the shifter linkage.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Slipping:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip gears, causing a loss of power.
    * Solution: Replace the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Mount Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The front strut mount may fail, causing a clunking noise and a loss of stability.
    * Solution: Replace the front strut mount.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Shock Absorber Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear shock absorber may fail, causing a bouncy ride and a loss of stability.
    * Solution: Replace the rear shock absorber.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain quickly, leading to a dead battery.
    * Solution: Replace the battery or find the cause of the drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
* **Electrical System Malfunctions:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Electrical components may malfunction, such as the headlights, taillights, or power windows.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty electrical component.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The door handle may break, making it difficult to open the door.
    * Solution: Replace the door handle.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Panel Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard panel may crack, causing a cosmetic issue.
    * Solution: Replace the dashboard panel.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Power Window Switch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The power window switch may fail, making it difficult to operate the power windows.
    * Solution: Replace the power window switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2012 Fiat 500 is a popular subcompact hatchback that offers style, fuel efficiency, and affordability. However, the Fiat 500 has a number of potential issues, including engine problems, transmission problems, suspension problems, electrical problems, and interior issues. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the extent of the damage.
