# 2017 Fiat 500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 Fiat 500, buyers will find a compact city car with distinct Italian styling and efficient powertrain options. While generally fun to drive and easy to park, prospective owners should review owner-reported complaints carefully, as some models experience minor electrical glitches, body hardware wear, and occasional mechanical quirks. A thorough pre-purchase inspection can help catch any underlying issues before bringing one home.

---

## Engine

### 1.4L MultiAir Naturally Aspirated I4

The 1.4-liter naturally aspirated MultiAir four-cylinder engine produces approximately 101 horsepower and 97 lb-ft of torque, offering modest power well-suited for city commuting.

**Unexpected Engine Shutdown**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may shut down unexpectedly while driving if heavy items on the key ring are inadvertently bumped.
- **Solution:** Avoid hanging heavy key rings or fobs from the ignition key to prevent accidental contact while operating controls.

### 1.4L MultiAir Turbocharged I4

The 1.4-liter turbocharged MultiAir four-cylinder engine boosts output to around 135 or 160 horsepower depending on the trim level, providing a more spirited driving dynamic for sportier models.

## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional five-speed manual transmission pairs with base engines, offering direct driver engagement and generally reliable operation when maintained properly.

### 6-Speed AISIN Automatic Transmission

The six-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth daily shifting in city traffic, representing a common choice for buyers seeking two-pedal convenience.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake pedal feel can occasionally exhibit jerky engagement during low-speed stopping maneuvers.

**Abrupt Brake Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** Brakes may grab or jerk abruptly just before coming to a complete stop, or feel overly sensitive.
- **Solution:** Have the brake system inspected for pad wear, rotor condition, and ABS module operation.

## Electrical

### Odometer Display

Owners have reported minor digital display anomalies that typically require software recalibration.

**Flashing Odometer**
- **Symptoms:** The mileage indicator or odometer display flashes continuously on the instrument cluster.
- **Solution:** Have a dealer or qualified mechanic perform a proxy alignment procedure using diagnostic software to sync body control modules.

## Exterior

### Doors and Windows

Body hardware components such as door handles and window regulators can experience premature mechanical wear.

**Door Handle Fracture and Window Drop**
- **Symptoms:** Interior door handles may fracture and detach, or side windows may slip out of alignment and fall into the door panel.
- **Solution:** Inspect door hardware for wear and replace broken door handle assemblies or window regulators as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19E044000: Power Train:Manual Transmission:Floor Shift Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Fiat&model=500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the shifter breaks while the vehicle is in motion, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V474000: Tires:Pressure Monitoring And Regulating Systems](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Fiat&model=500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The tire pressure monitoring system (TPMS) warning light may not illuminate as intended. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 138, "Tire Pressure Monitoring Systems." A TPMS warning light that fails to illuminate can result in the vehicle being driven with improperly inflated tires.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the TPMS electronic control unit module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Fiat 500 remains an appealing option for city dwellers looking for efficiency and iconic styling, but buyers should check for minor electrical quirks, door handle wear, and brake responsiveness during a test drive. Prioritizing a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection helps ensure that any small recurring complaints have been addressed.
