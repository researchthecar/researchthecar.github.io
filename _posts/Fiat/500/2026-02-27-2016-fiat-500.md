# 2016 Fiat 500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Fiat 500 offers distinct European styling and nimble handling, making it a popular choice for urban commuters looking for efficiency and character. However, prospective buyers should review owner-reported mechanical feedback carefully, particularly concerning powertrain operation and electrical management. While many owners enjoy their daily drives, researching specific maintenance histories can help avoid unexpected headaches.

---

## Engine

### 1.4L MultiAir Inline-4

The standard naturally aspirated 1.4-liter MultiAir inline-4 produces roughly 101 horsepower and 97 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for city driving. It offers straightforward mechanical operation, though regular maintenance is vital for overall longevity.

### 1.4L Turbocharged Inline-4

Found in sportier trims like the Abarth, this turbocharged 1.4-liter engine cranks out up to 160 horsepower and 170 lb-ft of torque. It delivers a much punchier driving dynamic, though it requires diligent upkeep and premium fuel.

## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission

The conventional 5-speed manual transmission pairs with select naturally aspirated models, offering a direct and engaging gear-shifting experience. It is generally robust, though clutch wear depends heavily on driver habits.

**Clutch and Shifting Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The manual clutch may fail unexpectedly while driving, preventing the driver from changing gears or maintaining vehicle momentum.
- **Solution:** Inspect the clutch assembly, hydraulic linkage, and pedal box, and replace worn components as necessary.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

Exclusive to high-performance variants, the 6-speed manual handles increased torque loads from the turbocharged engine. It offers a sporty feel with short throws, requiring standard clutch maintenance over time.

### 6-Speed Aisin Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission provides smooth shifts for daily commuting, though some owners have reported erratic shifting behaviors. Routine fluid checks help maintain long-term reliability.

**Unexpected Shifting to Neutral**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may shift into neutral on its own while driving, causing a temporary loss of motive power and illuminating warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the powertrain control module, wiring harness, and transmission shift solenoids for faults or software updates.

## Brakes

### Brake System

Brake components provide adequate stopping power for this lightweight car, though electronic sensor calibration issues can occasionally disrupt normal pedal feel.

**Unpredictable Braking Response**
- **Symptoms:** Braking behavior can occasionally feel unexpectedly hard, sudden, or inconsistent until the brake controller sensor is recalibrated.
- **Solution:** Perform a diagnostic brake controller sensor recalibration using appropriate scan tools.

## Exterior

### Keyless Entry and Body Electronics

Body electronics manage convenience features like keyless entry and power accessories, with minor remote connectivity glitches reported by some drivers.

**Key Fob Door Unlock Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle doors may fail to unlock when using the key fob transmitter.
- **Solution:** Inspect key fob batteries, receiver modules, and door actuator mechanisms for potential faults.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19E044000: Power Train:Manual Transmission:Floor Shift Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Fiat&model=500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the shifter breaks while the vehicle is in motion, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 16V667000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Fiat&model=500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." If the spare tire is used while overinflated, the tire may suddenly fail, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Fiat will notify owners, and dealers will add an overlay on the tire placard to the correct tire inflation information, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V302000: Power Train:Manual Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Fiat&model=500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the clutch diaphragm spring fails, the driver may not be able to shift gears or the vehicle may lose motive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V474000: Tires:Pressure Monitoring And Regulating Systems](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Fiat&model=500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The tire pressure monitoring system (TPMS) warning light may not illuminate as intended. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 138, "Tire Pressure Monitoring Systems." A TPMS warning light that fails to illuminate can result in the vehicle being driven with improperly inflated tires.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the TPMS electronic control unit module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2016 Fiat 500, checking the service history and verifying proper transmission and electrical operation is essential. While many drivers experience trouble-free commuting, attention to owner-reported concerns regarding unexpected shifting or sensor behavior can save buyers from future inconvenience. Take a thorough test drive before purchasing to ensure all systems perform as expected.
