# 2019 Fiat 500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Fiat 500 brings Italian flair and compact dimensions to the city car segment, anchored by its turbocharged MultiAir powertrain. While stylish and easy to park, used car buyers should approach with a measure of caution given limited owner feedback data and historical concerns associated with early-generation automated manual transmissions and complex electronics. Reviewing maintenance records and performing a thorough pre-purchase inspection are strongly advised.

---

## Engine

### 1.4L Turbocharged MultiAir 4-Cylinder

This 1.4-liter turbocharged inline-four engine produces approximately 135 horsepower and 150 lb-ft of torque. It offers punchy performance for the lightweight city car, though proper oil maintenance is critical for the hydraulic MultiAir valve system's longevity.

## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 5-speed manual transmission pairs well with the turbo engine, offering a direct driving feel and generally solid mechanical durability when the clutch is maintained properly.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The conventional 6-speed automatic provides smooth daily commuting shifts, replacing the problematic automated manuals found in earlier model years.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19E044000: Power Train:Manual Transmission:Floor Shift Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Fiat&model=500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the shifter breaks while the vehicle is in motion, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V474000: Tires:Pressure Monitoring And Regulating Systems](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Fiat&model=500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The tire pressure monitoring system (TPMS) warning light may not illuminate as intended. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 138, "Tire Pressure Monitoring Systems." A TPMS warning light that fails to illuminate can result in the vehicle being driven with improperly inflated tires.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the TPMS electronic control unit module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Fiat 500 remains an appealing, fuel-efficient choice for urban drivers seeking distinctive style. Prospective buyers should prioritize models with a complete service history and inspect the mechanical and electrical systems carefully before finalizing a purchase.
