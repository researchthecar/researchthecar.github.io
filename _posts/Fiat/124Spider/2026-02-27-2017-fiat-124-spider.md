# 2017 Fiat 124 Spider Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used roadster, checking out owner feedback and known mechanical trends helps ensure a smooth buying experience. The Fiat 124 Spider shares its platform with the Mazda MX-5 Miata, giving it a nimble character, but it features distinct Italian powertrain and electrical architecture. Reviewing potential trouble spots across the engine, transmission, and electrical systems helps prospective buyers identify what to inspect during a pre-purchase evaluation.

---

## Engine

### 1.4L MultiAir Turbo Inline-4

The 1.4-liter MultiAir turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 160 to 164 horsepower depending on the trim level. It offers lively turbocharged performance, though owners should monitor oil consumption and MultiAir system health closely.

**Engine Stalling and Oil Pressure Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may stall out during driving or fail to start after sitting for extended periods due to loss of oil pressure in the MultiAir valve actuation system, sometimes compounded by oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Check oil levels frequently, inspect the turbo and MultiAir brick assembly, and consult a qualified technician if stalling or low pressure occurs.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission pairs directly with the turbocharged engine for an engaging driving dynamic. Buyers should test all gears thoroughly during a test drive to ensure smooth engagement.

**Jumping Out of Gear**
- **Symptoms:** The manual transmission may unexpectedly pop or shift out of 4th gear while driving in traffic or at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission shift linkages, fluid condition, and internal synchronizers for wear.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

An available 6-speed automatic transmission provides relaxed cruising capability. It generally operates reliably when routine fluid maintenance is observed.

### Alarm System and Electronics

electrical issues such as erratic security alarms can occasionally plague the cabin electronics, requiring sensor calibration or module checks.

**Random Alarm Activation**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle security alarm, horn, and emergency flashers may activate spontaneously while driving at various speeds and gear selections.
- **Solution:** Inspect door latches, hood switches, body control modules, and security sensors for faults.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Occupant safety systems generally function well, though mechanical wear on seat belt retractors should be checked during a used car inspection.

**Retraction Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Seat belts may fail to retract smoothly or secure properly, posing potential safety concerns.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt assemblies, guides, and retractors, and replace faulty components as necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V694000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Fiat&model=124+Spider&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A software error in the air bag control module may cause the front air bags to deploy with excessive force during a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An air bag that deploys with excessive force can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the air bag control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The Fiat 124 Spider combines sporty roadster handling with distinctive styling, but prospective buyers should perform a thorough inspection. Pay close attention to engine oil levels, test all manual gear shifts carefully, and verify that the electrical alarm system and seat belts operate without anomalies before finalizing a purchase.
