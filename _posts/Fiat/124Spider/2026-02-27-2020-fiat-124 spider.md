# 2020 Fiat 124 Spider

### Overview
The 2020 Fiat 124 Spider, a stylish and agile roadster, generally presents a respectable reliability profile. Built on a platform shared with Mazda's MX-5 Miata, it benefits from proven engineering. The primary powertrain is a turbocharged 1.4-liter MultiAir engine, known for its peppy performance. While most owners report a positive experience, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen occasional attention. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective buyers and current owners. The focus is on specific component failures rather than general wear and tear.

---


## Engine

### 1.4L Turbo MultiAir
**Turbocharger Lag/Performance Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced acceleration, hesitation, or a noticeable delay in turbo boost.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of boost leaks, wastegate issues, or sensor malfunctions. May require turbocharger repair or replacement.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil spots under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of seals, gaskets, or oil pan.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Connectivity Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Bluetooth connectivity issues, screen freezing, or unresponsive touch controls.

### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the vehicle, dead battery without apparent cause.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw from electrical components or faulty alternator.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 Fiat 124 Spider is generally considered a reliable vehicle, especially given its shared platform with the Mazda MX-5. The powertrain, a 1.4L turbocharged engine, is robust but can occasionally exhibit turbocharger lag or minor oil leaks, which are typically manageable repairs. Transmissions, both manual and automatic, have shown good durability. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system's connectivity and occasional battery drain issues, are the most frequently cited non-routine problems. Suspension and exterior components, including the convertible top, have largely been trouble-free. Overall, the 124 Spider offers a fun driving experience with a reasonable expectation of reliability, provided potential electrical and minor engine concerns are addressed.
