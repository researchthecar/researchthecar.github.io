# 2017 Fiat 124 Spider

### Overview
The 2017 Fiat 124 Spider, a collaboration with Mazda based on the MX-5 platform, generally presents a positive reliability picture for its inaugural year. It benefits from a shared platform with a proven track record, though it incorporates Fiat's own powertrain. Owners often praise its engaging driving dynamics and stylish Italian design. While no vehicle is entirely without potential concerns, the 124 Spider has demonstrated a commendable level of dependability, particularly in its core mechanical components. Attention to specific areas, such as the turbocharger and certain electronic features, can help ensure a smooth ownership experience. Overall, it offers a compelling blend of sportiness and practicality for its segment.

---


## Engine

### 1.4L Turbocharged MultiAir
**Turbocharger Lag/Boost Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced power, hesitation during acceleration, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of wastegate actuator, boost leaks, or turbocharger itself. Repair or replacement of faulty components.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

**MultiAir System Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, misfires, poor performance, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential cleaning or replacement of the MultiAir solenoid or related hydraulic components.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Connectivity Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Bluetooth pairing issues, touchscreen unresponsiveness, freezing.
- **Solution:** Software updates, hard resets, or potential replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $100 - $700


### Keyless Entry/Start
**Intermittent Operation**
- **Symptoms:** Key fob not recognized, difficulty starting the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Battery replacement in key fob, reprogramming of fobs, or diagnosis of receiver module.
- **Cost:** $50 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Fiat 124 Spider generally exhibits good reliability, especially considering its shared platform with the Mazda MX-5. The primary areas of concern tend to be related to the turbocharged MultiAir engine, specifically potential issues with the turbocharger and the MultiAir system itself, which can manifest as performance problems or warning lights. Electrical components, particularly the infotainment system and keyless entry, have seen some reports of glitches or intermittent operation, often resolvable with software updates or minor repairs. The manual and automatic transmissions, along with the suspension and exterior/interior components, have largely proven robust with no widespread issues reported. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to any engine-related warning signs are recommended for optimal long-term ownership.
