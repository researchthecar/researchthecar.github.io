**Introduction to the 2008 Fiat Panda**

The 2008 Fiat Panda is a compact city car available in two trim levels: Dynamic and Emotion. It comes with a 1.2-liter gasoline engine mated to a 5-speed manual transmission, producing 69 horsepower and 75 lb-ft of torque. The Panda is known for its spacious interior, maneuverability, and fuel efficiency. However, it has received criticism for its lack of power and refinement.

**Engine**

**1.2-liter Gasoline**

**Fuel Leak**
* Description/Symptoms: Gasoline leaks from the fuel tank or fuel lines. This can cause a strong gasoline odor, difficulty starting the engine, or engine stalling.
* Solution: Replace the leaking part (fuel tank, fuel lines)
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* Recalls: NHTSA ID: 10039898

**Ignition Coil Failure**
* Description/Symptoms: The ignition coil fails, preventing the spark plugs from receiving the necessary voltage to ignite the air-fuel mixture. This can cause engine misfires, rough idling, and difficulty starting the engine.
* Solution: Replace the ignition coil
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
* Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

**Shifting Difficulties**
* Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, especially into first or second gear. This can be caused by worn or damaged shift linkages or synchronizers.
* Solution: Replace the worn or damaged components
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Front Suspension Strut Failure**
* Description/Symptoms: The front suspension struts wear out prematurely, leading to excessive noise, vibration, and reduced handling performance.
* Solution: Replace the front suspension struts
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
* Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Battery Drain**
* Description/Symptoms: The battery drains quickly, even when the vehicle is not in use. This can be caused by faulty electrical components or a weak battery.
* Solution: Inspect and replace the faulty electrical components or replace the battery
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
* Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Wiper Motor Failure**
* Description/Symptoms: The wiper motor fails, resulting in the wipers not functioning properly. This can pose a safety hazard in inclement weather.
* Solution: Replace the wiper motor
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250
* Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Plastic Trim Cracking**
* Description/Symptoms: The plastic trim pieces in the interior, such as the dashboard or door panels, crack or break over time.
* Solution: Replace the cracked or broken trim pieces
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
* Recalls: None Found

**Seat Adjustment Lever Failure**
* Description/Symptoms: The seat adjustment levers fail, making it difficult or impossible to adjust the front seats.
* Solution: Replace the seat adjustment levers
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
* Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2008 Fiat Panda has some common issues, with the most severe being the fuel leak, which can pose a safety hazard. Other issues, such as the shifting difficulties, suspension strut failure, and interior problems, can impact the driving experience and comfort. The estimated cost to fix these issues varies, with some repairs being relatively inexpensive and others requiring more significant investment. Overall, the Panda is a reliable and practical city car, but owners should be aware of these potential problems and address them promptly to ensure the vehicle's safety and performance.
