# 2008 Fiat Punto

### Overview
The 2008 Fiat Punto, a popular supermini, generally offers a decent ownership experience with a focus on practicality and fuel efficiency. While it avoids major widespread reliability crises, like many vehicles of its era, certain components and engine/transmission combinations have been noted for specific issues. Owners should be aware of potential problems, particularly concerning diesel particulate filters on certain engines and some electrical gremlins. The interior and exterior are typically robust for its class, though age-related wear is expected. Overall, the Punto can be a reliable choice if well-maintained, with a focus on addressing known potential weak points.

### 1.3L JTDm Diesel
**DPF Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated DPF warning light, reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption.
- **Solution:** Forced regeneration or DPF replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, black smoke from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger repair or replacement.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2000


### 1.9L JTDm Diesel
**DPF Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated DPF warning light, reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption.
- **Solution:** Forced regeneration or DPF replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

**Injector Problems**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, difficulty starting, engine misfires, increased emissions.
- **Solution:** Injector cleaning or replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800 per injector

### Summary Verdict
The 2008 Fiat Punto presents a mixed reliability profile. Petrol engines, particularly the 1.2L and 1.4L Fire variants, are generally robust with minimal reported issues beyond standard maintenance. Diesel engines, especially the 1.3L and 1.9L JTDm, are more prone to issues, primarily concerning the Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) and turbocharger problems, which can be costly to repair. The manual transmission is typically reliable, but the automated Dualogic transmission has been known to suffer from actuator and hydraulic pump failures. Electrical gremlins, such as window regulator faults and BCM glitches, are not uncommon. Suspension and interior/exterior components are generally solid for the car's class. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to warning lights, especially for diesel models, are crucial for ensuring long-term reliability.
