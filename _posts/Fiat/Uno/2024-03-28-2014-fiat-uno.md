**Introduction to the 2014 Fiat Uno**

The 2014 Fiat Uno is a compact hatchback available in Brazil. It is powered by a 1.4-liter Fire Evo petrol engine mated to a 5-speed manual transmission. The Uno is praised for its fuel efficiency, maneuverability, and affordability. However, it has received criticism for its lack of standard features and dated interior design.

**Engine**

- **1.4-liter Fire Evo**
    - **Oil Leak from Oil Pan Gasket**
        Description/Symptoms: Oil leak from the bottom of the engine, visible as dark fluid spots under the car
        Solution: Replacement of oil pan gasket
        Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150
    - **Coolant Leak from Radiator**
        Description/Symptoms: Overheating engine, coolant level drop, visible coolant leak from the front of the engine
        Solution: Replacement of radiator or related hoses
        Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    - **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
        Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, loss of power, engine misfires
        Solution: Replacement of intake manifold gasket
        Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
    - **Ignition Coil Failure**
        Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, loss of power, no spark
        Solution: Replacement of ignition coil
        Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250

**Transmission**

- **5-speed Manual**
    - **Gear Shift Issues**
        Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises, stuck gears
        Solution: Replacement of gear shifter assembly or internal transmission parts
        Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Suspension**

- **Front Suspension Strut Failure**
    Description/Symptoms: Knocking noises from the front end, uneven tire wear, poor handling
    Solution: Replacement of front suspension struts
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
- **Rear Suspension Spring Sag**
    Description/Symptoms: Lowered rear end, poor handling, reduced ground clearance
    Solution: Replacement of rear suspension springs
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250

**Electrical**

- **Battery Drain**
    Description/Symptoms: Dead battery after sitting overnight, dim headlights
    Solution: Identification of electrical component causing the drain and replacement if necessary
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500
- **Alternator Failure**
    Description/Symptoms: Warning light on dashboard, dimming headlights, battery not charging
    Solution: Replacement of alternator
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600

**Exterior**

- **Fog Light Malfunction**
    Description/Symptoms: One or both fog lights not working
    Solution: Replacement of fog light bulbs or wiring harness
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200
- **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    Description/Symptoms: Difficulty locking or unlocking doors, doors not unlocking using remote
    Solution: Replacement of door lock actuators
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Interior**

- **Dashboard Cracking**
    Description/Symptoms: Cracks on the plastic dashboard
    Solution: Replacement of dashboard or repair using epoxy or other adhesives
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
- **Air Conditioner Blower Motor Failure**
    Description/Symptoms: No air flow from vents, intermittent air flow
    Solution: Replacement of blower motor
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Summary**

The 2014 Fiat Uno has several potential issues affecting various components, including the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, and interior. While some issues, such as oil leaks and electrical problems, can be relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others, like intake manifold gasket failure, can be more severe and require significant repairs. Overall, the cost of these issues can vary widely depending on the severity of the problem and the specific parts that need to be replaced.
