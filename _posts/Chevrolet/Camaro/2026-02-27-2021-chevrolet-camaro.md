# 2021 Chevrolet Camaro Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a pre-owned 2021 Chevrolet Camaro, prospective buyers will find a muscular sports car that offers thrilling performance and iconic styling. However, a review of owner feedback reveals certain mechanical and powertrain areas that warrant careful inspection during a pre-purchase evaluation.

---

## Engine

### 6.2L V8

The 6.2L V8 engine delivers robust horsepower and torque, providing exceptional straight-line performance. Buyers should verify proper maintenance history and check for any signs of fuel delivery or internal mechanical issues.

**Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience sudden hesitation, vehicle shaking, and a loss of engine power while driving at various speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the faulty fuel pump or associated control modules as necessary.

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

The 2.0L turbocharged four-cylinder engine offers a balance of efficiency and spirited acceleration for everyday driving. It is generally reliable when maintained properly with regular oil changes.

### 3.6L V6

The 3.6L V6 provides a smooth and responsive mid-tier power option for the Camaro lineup. This engine option is straightforward to maintain, though buyers should ensure consistent service records.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic

The 10-speed automatic transmission delivers quick and responsive gear changes across various driving conditions. Buyers should thoroughly test-drive automatic models to check for smooth shifting behavior and unusual noises.

**Loss of Motive Power and Transmission Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported excessive transmission whine, harsh shifting, and rough engagement into Drive or Reverse.
- **Solution:** Consult a qualified technician to inspect internal components, such as the fluid pump, and perform necessary transmission repairs or replacements.
- **Cost:** $8,263.46

### 6-Speed Manual

The 6-speed manual transmission provides an engaging, hands-on driving experience for enthusiasts. It remains mechanically straightforward, though clutch wear should be assessed during a pre-purchase inspection.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V811000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Camaro&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** If an incorrect bolt was used, the seat belt may not be properly attached to the vehicle. If a seat belt assembly is not properly attached to the vehicle, the seat belt may not properly restrain an occupant in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the suspect bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V188000: Power Train:Axle Assembly:Axle Shaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Camaro&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The left or right axle shaft may be missing a retaining ring, which could allow the axle shaft to separate while driving. A separated axle shaft can cause a loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the left or right axle shaft as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V211000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Camaro&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The plastic "Camaro" emblem, located on the steering wheel air bag cover, may separate during deployment of the frontal driver air bag. In the event of a crash, plastic pieces of emblem may act as projectiles from deployment of the driver air bag can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the frontal driver side air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V148000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Camaro&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The transmission may become damaged internally and cause the wheels to lock-up. Wheel lock-up while driving increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install transmission control module monitoring software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Chevrolet Camaro is an exciting performance vehicle, but potential buyers should perform a thorough mechanical inspection focusing on powertrain and transmission performance. Verifying service history and checking for any signs of rough shifting or fuel system hesitation will help ensure a more confident ownership experience.
