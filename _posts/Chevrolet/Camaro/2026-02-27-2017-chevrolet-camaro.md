# 2017 Chevrolet Camaro Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Chevrolet Camaro delivers aggressive styling and sharp handling, but prospective used-car buyers should pay close attention to several recurring mechanical and electrical concerns. Owner complaints submitted to safety agencies highlight notable trouble spots, particularly involving automatic transmission behavior and power steering assist systems. Being aware of these common pain points can help you inspect a used model more thoroughly before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbo Inline-4

This entry-level turbocharged engine produces around 275 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, offering solid fuel economy and decent pep for daily driving.

### 3.6L V6

The naturally aspirated V6 pumps out approximately 335 horsepower, providing a balanced blend of traditional muscle-car sound and everyday drivability.

### 6.2L LT1 V8

Generating a robust 455 horsepower, the V8 transforms the Camaro into a true performance machine, though it demands careful inspection of maintenance history.

### Cooling System

Cooling components manage operating temperatures across all engine trims, though occasional premature thermostat failures have been reported by owners.

**Thermostat Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The engine thermostat may fail prematurely, leading to fluctuating engine temperatures or overheating warnings well before typical mileage intervals.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty thermostat with an updated OEM or quality aftermarket unit.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The standard manual gearbox pairs well with enthusiast driving, but manual models can experience premature clutch or synchronizer wear if driven hard.

**Synchronizer and Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty shifting smoothly into specific gears, particularly second gear, or experiencing reduced clutch performance during normal or spirited driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid, clutch assembly, and synchronizers; repair or replace worn internal components as necessary.

### 8-Speed / 8L45 Automatic Transmission

Available automatic units offer quick gear changes but are subject to well-documented torque converter and fluid-related shudder issues requiring specialized service.

**Transmission Shudder and Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable shuddering, vibration, or harsh shifting at highway speeds, often accompanied by RPM fluctuations.
- **Solution:** Flush and replace transmission fluid using updated specifications, or service the torque converter as recommended by technical service guidelines.

## Steering

### Electronic Power Steering

The electronic power steering system provides responsive handling, though intermittent assist failures can abruptly make the vehicle difficult to maneuver.

**Loss of Power Steering Assist**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden hardening of the steering wheel accompanied by dashboard warning messages indicating power steering failure, requiring an engine restart to temporarily restore function.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the electronic power steering module and wiring harness, and replace faulty steering gear assemblies if electronic failure persists.

## Exterior

### Convertible Top and Rear Window

Convertible models require careful inspection of the soft top and rear glass bonding points to prevent costly weatherstripping and interior water damage.

**Rear Window Separation**
- **Symptoms:** The glass rear window begins ungluing or separating entirely from the surrounding convertible top fabric, potentially allowing water leaks into the cabin.
- **Solution:** Re-secure or replace the convertible top assembly according to manufacturer service bulletins.

## Interior

### Cabin Electronics

Interior electrical accessories generally function well, but localized actuator failures can occasionally disrupt climate control and visibility.

**HVAC Blend Door Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Airflow control doors stick or close completely, causing persistent windshield fogging and erratic temperature shifts between hot and cold.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed HVAC blend door actuator motor.

## Safety

### Restraints and Airbags

Safety restraint systems are vital to occupant protection, though owners have reported occasional sensor warnings and hardware wear on seat belt tethers.

**Airbag Warning and Seat Belt Cable Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illumination of the airbag service light or physical disconnection of the driver seat belt retention cable.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace damaged seat belt anchors or faulty airbag diagnostic modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V086000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Camaro&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Loss of power steering assist would require a higher steering effort, especially at lower speeds, which may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the power steering gear assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V211000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Camaro&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The plastic "Camaro" emblem, located on the steering wheel air bag cover, may separate during deployment of the frontal driver air bag. In the event of a crash, plastic pieces of emblem may act as projectiles from deployment of the driver air bag can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the frontal driver side air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V175000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Camaro&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A loss of power steering assist can require greater steering effort, especially at low speeds, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the power steering gear assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used Chevrolet Camaro, prioritize models with a documented service history, paying special attention to how the automatic transmission shifts and whether any power steering warning lights appear during a test drive. Checking convertible top seams and verifying proper engine cooling operation will also save you from unexpected repair bills. With proper maintenance and a thorough pre-purchase inspection, this sports car can offer an exciting and rewarding ownership experience.
