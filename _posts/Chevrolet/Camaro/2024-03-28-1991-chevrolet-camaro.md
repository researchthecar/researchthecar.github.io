**1991 Chevrolet Camaro**

The 1991 Chevrolet Camaro was a popular sports car that was offered with a variety of engine and transmission options. The base engine was a 3.1-liter V6 that produced 140 horsepower and 180 lb-ft of torque. The optional engines were a 5.0-liter V8 that produced 220 horsepower and 275 lb-ft of torque, and a 5.7-liter V8 that produced 240 horsepower and 300 lb-ft of torque. The Camaro was available with a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission.

People who liked the 1991 Chevrolet Camaro praised its stylish design, powerful engines, and fun-to-drive handling. However, some people complained about the car's cramped interior and poor fuel economy.

**Engine**

* **3.1-liter V6**
    * **Piston ring failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The piston rings can fail, causing the engine to burn oil excessively. The symptoms of piston ring failure include blue smoke coming from the exhaust pipe, a decrease in engine power, and an increase in oil consumption.
    * Solution: The solution to piston ring failure is to rebuild the engine.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **5.0-liter V8**
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing coolant and/or oil to leak. The symptoms of intake manifold gasket failure include coolant or oil leaking from the engine, a decrease in engine power, and an increase in fuel consumption.
    * Solution: The solution to intake manifold gasket failure is to replace the gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **5.7-liter V8**
    * **Valve stem seal failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The valve stem seals can fail, causing the engine to burn oil excessively. The symptoms of valve stem seal failure include blue smoke coming from the exhaust pipe, a decrease in engine power, and an increase in oil consumption.
    * Solution: The solution to valve stem seal failure is to replace the valve stem seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-speed manual transmission**
    * **Synchronizer failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The synchronizers can fail, making it difficult to shift gears. The symptoms of synchronizer failure include grinding noises when shifting gears, difficulty shifting gears, and a popping out of gear.
    * Solution: The solution to synchronizer failure is to replace the synchronizers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **4-speed automatic transmission**
    * **Torque converter failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The torque converter can fail, causing the transmission to slip or shudder. The symptoms of torque converter failure include slipping or shuddering when shifting gears, a decrease in acceleration, and an increase in fuel consumption.
    * Solution: The solution to torque converter failure is to replace the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator can fail, causing the battery to not charge. The symptoms of alternator failure include a dim or flickering headlights, a dead battery, and a warning light on the dashboard.
    * Solution: The solution to alternator failure is to replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Starter failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The starter can fail, preventing the engine from starting. The symptoms of starter failure include a clicking sound when trying to start the engine, no sound when trying to start the engine, and a warning light on the dashboard.
    * Solution: The solution to starter failure is to replace the starter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust**
    * Description/Symptoms: The body panels can rust, especially around the wheel wells and rocker panels. The symptoms of rust include bubbling or peeling paint, holes in the body panels, and a decrease in the car's value.
    * Solution: The solution to rust is to repair the rusted areas. This can involve welding in new metal, applying a rust converter, or painting over the rusted areas.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **HVAC system failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The HVAC system can fail, causing the car to not heat or cool properly. The symptoms of HVAC system failure include no air coming from the vents, cold air coming from the vents when the heat is on, and hot air coming from the vents when the AC is on.
    * Solution: The solution to HVAC system failure is to repair the HVAC system. This can involve replacing the blower motor, the evaporator core, or the condenser.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Power window failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The power windows can fail, preventing them from opening or closing. The symptoms of power window failure include the windows not moving when the switch is pressed, the windows moving slowly, and the windows making a grinding noise.
    * Solution: The solution to power window failure is to repair the power window system. This can involve replacing the window motor, the window regulator, or the window switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1991 Chevrolet Camaro is a popular sports car that is known for its stylish design, powerful engines, and fun-to-drive handling. However, the car has some potential issues that can be expensive to fix. The most common issues include piston ring failure, intake manifold gasket failure, valve stem seal failure, synchronizer failure, torque converter failure, alternator failure, starter failure, rust, HVAC system failure, and power window failure. If you are considering buying a 1991 Chevrolet Camaro, be sure to have it inspected by a qualified mechanic to check for any potential issues.
