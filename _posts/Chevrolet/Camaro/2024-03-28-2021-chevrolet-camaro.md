**Introduction**

The 2021 Chevrolet Camaro is a muscular sports car with a sleek design and powerful performance. It's available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 2.0L turbocharged inline-four, 3.6L V6, and 6.2L V8. The Camaro is known for its handling and acceleration, as well as its stylish interior. However, it also has some issues that owners have reported.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-Four**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Some owners have reported that their 2.0L engines are consuming excessive amounts of oil. Symptoms include low oil levels on the dipstick and blue smoke from the exhaust.
* **Solution:** GM has issued a technical service bulletin (TSB) that recommends replacing the piston rings and valve seals.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2,000 - $4,000+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**3.6L V6**

* **No Issues Found**

**6.2L V8**

* **Valve Spring Failure:** Some owners have reported that their 6.2L engines have experienced valve spring failure. Symptoms include loud ticking noises and reduced engine power.
* **Solution:** Replace the affected valve springs.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Recalls:** NHTSA Campaign ID: 22V323000

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

* **Difficult Shifting:** Some owners have reported that their 6-speed manual transmissions are difficult to shift. Symptoms include grinding gears and difficulty engaging gears.
* **Solution:** GM has issued a TSB that recommends replacing the shift forks.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**8-Speed Automatic**

* **Delayed Shifts:** Some owners have reported that their 8-speed automatic transmissions are experiencing delayed shifts. Symptoms include slow or sluggish gear changes.
* **Solution:** GM has issued a TSB that recommends updating the transmission software.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $300+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **Squeaky Rear Suspension:** Some owners have reported that their rear suspension squeaks when driving over bumps or uneven surfaces.
* **Solution:** Replace the rear suspension bushings.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electrical System Malfunction:** Some owners have reported electrical system malfunctions, including issues with the battery, alternator, and starter. Symptoms vary depending on the issue.
* **Solution:** Diagnose and repair the specific electrical component that is causing the issue.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** Varies depending on the issue
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

* **Cracked Windshield:** Some owners have reported that their windshields have cracked or chipped easily.
* **Solution:** Replace the windshield.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $600+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Interior**

* **Rattling Dashboard:** Some owners have reported that their dashboards rattle when driving over bumps or uneven surfaces.
* **Solution:** Tighten or replace the dashboard components that are causing the rattle.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $200+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Chevrolet Camaro is a powerful and stylish sports car, but it has some issues that owners should be aware of. The most severe issues include excessive oil consumption in the 2.0L turbocharged inline-four engine, valve spring failure in the 6.2L V8 engine, and difficult shifting in the 6-speed manual transmission. The cost of repairs for these issues can be substantial, so it's important to factor this into the cost of ownership.
