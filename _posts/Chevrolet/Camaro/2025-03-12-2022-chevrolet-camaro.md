The 2022 Chevrolet Camaro represents the final year of the sixth-generation Camaro. It offers a range of powertrains to suit different driving preferences. Engine options include a 2.0L Turbo I4 producing 275 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, a 3.6L V6 making 335 horsepower and 284 lb-ft of torque, a 6.2L LT1 V8 generating 455 horsepower and 455 lb-ft of torque, and a supercharged 6.2L LT4 V8 found in the ZL1, boasting 650 horsepower and 650 lb-ft of torque. Transmission choices typically include a 6-speed manual (depending on the engine), an 8-speed automatic, and a 10-speed automatic (depending on the engine). Many drivers appreciate the Camaro's sharp handling, aggressive styling, and powerful engine options. Some common criticisms include limited rear visibility, a cramped rear seat, and some interior materials that don't match the car's sporty character.

**Suspension**

*   **Magnetic Ride Control (If Equipped) - Damper Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature failure of the magnetic ride control dampers, leading to a bouncy or unstable ride, reduced handling performance, and potential warning lights.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the affected damper(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000 per damper, depending on location and labor rates.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Malfunctioning**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Infotainment screen freezing, unresponsive touch inputs, Bluetooth connectivity issues, navigation problems, and/or audio system glitches.
    *   Solution: Software updates, replacement of the head unit if the problem persists.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,500+ (head unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain, requiring frequent jump starts or battery replacements.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair of parasitic drains, battery replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (depending on the cause and required repairs)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Tail Light Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Condensation forming inside the tail light housings.
    *   Solution: Replacement of tail light assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ (per tail light assembly)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Seat Heater Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Seat heaters failing to operate or overheating.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the heating element or control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2022 Chevrolet Camaro can experience a range of issues, from minor infotainment glitches to more significant concerns like potential piston failure in modified 2.0L turbo engines and suspension component issues. The severity and cost of these issues vary considerably. Infotainment and electrical problems can usually be resolved with software updates or component replacements, while engine or suspension repairs could be much more expensive. Before purchasing a used 2022 Camaro, a thorough inspection by a qualified mechanic is highly recommended.
