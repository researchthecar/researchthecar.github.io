**Introduction:**

The 2002 Chevrolet Camaro is a formidable muscle car that boasts a powerful engine, capable handling, and a sleek design. It is available with two engine options: a 3.8L V6 producing 200 horsepower and 225 lb-ft of torque, and a 5.7L V8 delivering 305 horsepower and 360 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Camaro's responsive steering, precise handling, and strong brakes make it a thrill to drive. Owners praise its classic good looks, comfortable seats, and impressive performance, while some criticize its limited visibility and lack of modern conveniences.

**Engine**

**3.8L V6**
* **Exhaust Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    * **Symptoms:** Loss of power, engine noise, decreased fuel economy
    * **Solution:** Replace exhaust manifold gasket
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $150 - $300+
* **Intake Manifold Failure:**
    * **Symptoms:** Reduced power, stalling, rough idling, decreased fuel economy
    * **Solution:** Replace intake manifold
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $400 - $800+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**5.7L V8**
* **Oil Pan Leaks:**
    * **Symptoms:** Oil leaks under the car, reduced oil levels
    * **Solution:** Replace oil pan gasket
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $250+
* **Valve Cover Gasket Failure:**
    * **Symptoms:** Oil leaks from the top of the engine, reduced oil levels
    * **Solution:** Replace valve cover gasket
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $150 - $300+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**
* **Slipping Gears:**
    * **Symptoms:** Transmission slips or hesitates when shifting
    * **Solution:** Replace transmission solenoids
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Failure:**
    * **Symptoms:** Reduced handling, clunking or rattling noises
    * **Solution:** Replace struts
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $500+ per wheel

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * **Symptoms:** Dim or flickering lights, difficult starting, battery warning light illuminated
    * **Solution:** Replace alternator
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $600+
* **Starter Failure:**
    * **Symptoms:** Engine won't start, clicking noise when turning the key
    * **Solution:** Replace starter
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Failure:**
    * **Symptoms:** Door handle breaks or sticks
    * **Solution:** Replace door handle
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $200+ per door

**Interior**

* **Window Switch Failure:**
    * **Symptoms:** Power windows don't work, window switches damaged
    * **Solution:** Replace window switch
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $50 - $100+ per switch

**Summary:**

While the 2002 Chevrolet Camaro is a powerful and fun-to-drive muscle car, it is not without its issues. Engine problems, transmission issues, suspension failures, and electrical malfunctions are among the most common. The estimated cost to fix these issues can range from a few hundred dollars to over a thousand dollars, depending on the severity of the problem. While not all Camaros will experience these issues, it is important to be aware of the potential problems and consider the cost of repairs before purchasing one.
