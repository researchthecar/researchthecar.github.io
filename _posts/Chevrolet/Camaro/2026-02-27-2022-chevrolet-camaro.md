# 2022 Chevrolet Camaro Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Chevrolet Camaro offers an exciting blend of iconic styling and robust performance options, ranging from efficient turbocharged engines to roaring V8s. While overall owner satisfaction is high among driving enthusiasts, used car buyers should review specific mechanical and electrical owner complaints. Potential shoppers should carefully inspect powertrain operation, convertible top integrity, and electronic systems during a pre-purchase test drive.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces 275 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, serving as the entry-level powerplant for the Camaro. It provides brisk acceleration and balanced handling due to its lighter weight, making it a reasonable choice for daily driving.

### 3.6L V6

The 3.6-liter naturally aspirated V6 pushes out 335 horsepower and 284 lb-ft of torque, offering a traditional muscle car exhaust note and strong mid-range power. It strikes a popular middle ground for buyers seeking V6 performance without stepping up to V8 fuel costs.

### 6.2L V8 (LT1 / LT4)

The 6.2-liter V8 engine delivers 455 horsepower in standard SS trim and up to 650 horsepower in supercharged ZL1 form, offering blistering acceleration. Buyers should look out for rare but serious internal component complaints reported by some owners.

**Main Bearing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A severe mechanical failure occurring at low mileage, resulting in engine seizure and complete engine replacement requirements.
- **Solution:** Requires professional mechanical inspection and potentially a complete engine rebuild or replacement if bearing damage occurs.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission features Active Rev Matching and gives purists direct control over gear changes. It is widely regarded as engaging and generally robust when properly maintained.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission is paired with specific engine configurations to offer quick, seamless shifts. Owners should test drive thoroughly to ensure smooth operation across all driving modes.

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission delivers rapid gear changes for V8 and select V6 models, enhancing performance on track and street. Some drivers have reported shift delay complaints and component backorders that require professional attention.

**Delayed Shifting and Valve Body Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough or delayed shifting between first and second gears, accompanied by failure to properly engage park or shifting delays.
- **Solution:** Consult a certified dealer for transmission diagnostics, software updates, or potential replacement of faulty valve body components.

## Suspension

### Factory Tires

Performance-tuned suspension setups provide exceptional cornering grip but can consume aggressive factory rubber quickly depending on use.

**Premature Tread or Belt Separation**
- **Symptoms:** High-performance factory tires exhibiting signs of tread separation or belt issues at very low mileage intervals.
- **Solution:** Inspect tire production dates and tread condition closely; replace compromised high-performance tires with quality replacements.

## Exterior

### Convertible Top and Rear Window

Exterior styling is a key highlight, but convertible models warrant a close inspection of soft-top seams and glass bonding areas.

**Rear Window Separation**
- **Symptoms:** The glass rear window begins to peel or detach from the surrounding convertible fabric top material, usually starting at the bottom corners.
- **Solution:** Requires professional top repair or complete replacement of the convertible top assembly by an upholstery specialist.

## Safety

### Airbags

Safety systems generally perform as expected, though occasional electronic glitches in the restraint monitoring network have been noted by owners.

**Intermittent Airbag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** Random airbag malfunction warnings appearing on the driver information center, particularly after the vehicle sits parked for a few days.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician scan the supplemental restraint system codes and inspect wiring harnesses or sensor connections.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V148000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Camaro&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The transmission may become damaged internally and cause the wheels to lock-up. Wheel lock-up while driving increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install transmission control module monitoring software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Chevrolet Camaro remains a thrilling performance car with strong engine choices and sharp handling characteristics. While most owner complaints are isolated, buyers should thoroughly test the transmission shift quality, inspect convertible top window seals, and verify proper electronic and safety system function before finalizing a purchase.
