**Introduction:**

The 2004 Chevrolet W5500 Tiltmaster is a heavy-duty commercial truck designed for hauling heavy loads. It is available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 5.7L V8 gasoline engine, a 6.5L V8 Vortec diesel engine, and a 7.8L V8 Vortec diesel engine. The 5-speed Allison 1000 automatic transmission is standard on all models.

Owners of the 2004 Chevrolet W5500 Tiltmaster generally praise its powerful engines, sturdy construction, and spacious cabin. However, there have been some reported issues with the vehicle, including problems with the engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical system.

**Engine**

* **5.7L V8 Gasoline Engine:**
    * **Head gasket failure:** Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, white smoke from exhaust. Solution: Replace head gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **6.5L V8 Vortec Diesel Engine:**
    * **Injection pump failure:** Description/Symptoms: Stalling, poor engine performance, black smoke from exhaust. Solution: Replace injection pump. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
* **7.8L V8 Vortec Diesel Engine:**
    * **Turbocharger failure:** Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, increased fuel consumption, black smoke from exhaust. Solution: Replace turbocharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+

**Transmission**

* **Allison 1000 Automatic Transmission:**
    * **Shifting problems:** Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, slipping gears, clunking noises. Solution: Overhaul or replace transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+

**Suspension**

* **Leaf spring failure:** Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, uneven tire wear, rough ride. Solution: Replace leaf springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Shock absorber failure:** Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, bottoming out over bumps. Solution: Replace shock absorbers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** Description/Symptoms: Battery dies quickly, electrical components malfunctioning. Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical system. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
* **Alternator failure:** Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, electrical components not functioning, battery not charging. Solution: Replace alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** Description/Symptoms: Corrosion on body panels, frame, and other metal components. Solution: Repair or replace rusted parts. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies greatly depending on severity of rust

**Interior**

* **Climate control issues:** Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning or heater not working properly. Solution: Diagnose and repair climate control system. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
* **Power window failure:** Description/Symptoms: Power windows not functioning. Solution: Replace power window motor or switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Other**

* **Oil leaks:** Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from engine or transmission. Solution: Repair or replace seals or gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **Braking issues:** Description/Symptoms: Brake pads worn, rotors warped, brake lines leaking. Solution: Diagnose and repair braking system. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies greatly depending on severity of issue
* **Fuel system issues:** Description/Symptoms: Fuel pump failure, fuel filter clogged, fuel injectors malfunctioning. Solution: Diagnose and repair fuel system. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies greatly depending on severity of issue

**Summary**

The 2004 Chevrolet W5500 Tiltmaster is a versatile commercial truck that has received positive reviews from owners. However, it has also been plagued by a number of issues, including engine problems, transmission failures, suspension problems, and electrical issues. The severity and cost of these issues can vary greatly depending on the specific part that has failed and the extent of the damage.
