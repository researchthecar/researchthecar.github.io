# 2016 Chevrolet City Express Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Chevrolet City Express is a compact cargo van mechanically identical to the Nissan NV200, offering solid utility for urban delivery and trades. Because it serves primarily as a commercial workhorse, prospective buyers should inspect used models carefully for signs of heavy wear, suspension fatigue, and past cargo area damage.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder

This 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces 131 horsepower and 139 lb-ft of torque. It prioritizes fuel efficiency for urban delivery routes and generally proves dependable under normal maintenance schedules.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The continuously variable transmission transfers power to the front wheels, maximizing fuel economy in stop-and-go driving. Buyers should test-drive thoroughly, as early CVT designs require strict fluid maintenance to avoid long-term durability concerns.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V244000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=City+Express&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As a result, the passenger frontal air bag may be turned off and not deploy in the event of a crash. If the passenger frontal air bag does not deploy as intended in the event of a crash, the passenger is at an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the Air Bag Control Unit (ACU) and OCS Electronic Control Unit (ECU) in Altima, Maxima, Murano, Rogue, and Sentra vehicles, and replace the OCS ECU in LEAF, NV200, NV200 Taxi, Pathfinder, Infiniti Q50, JX35, and QX60. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V676000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump:Control/Drive Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=City+Express&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The Fuel Tank Temperature (FTT) sensor harness may have been incorrectly routed, damaging wires and causing a short circuit in the fuel pump fuse. A blown fuel pump fuse can cause the engine to stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and reroute the FTT sensor harness or replace the fuel pump assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Chevrolet City Express provides practical, fuel-efficient capability for small businesses and urban commuters. While powertrain options are straightforward, shoppers should conduct a thorough mechanical inspection and verify maintenance records, particularly regarding transmission fluid changes, before purchasing.
