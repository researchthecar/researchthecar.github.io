# 2017 Chevrolet City Express Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Chevrolet City Express is a compact cargo van built on a Nissan-shared platform, designed primarily for urban delivery and commercial utility. While owner reports to federal regulators are relatively scarce for this model year, prospective buyers should inspect used models carefully for standard wear items and any reported cabin odors or mechanical quirks.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder

Equipped with a 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine making around 131 horsepower, this powertrain prioritizes fuel efficiency for urban delivery routes. It generally offers dependable daily operation when routine maintenance schedules are followed.

**Gasoline Odor**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may occasionally notice an abnormal gasoline odor inside the cabin while executing sharp turns.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the fuel system lines, connections, and evaporative emissions components for leaks.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The continuously variable transmission routes power to the front wheels, maximizing fuel economy in stop-and-go city driving. Buyers should test drive the vehicle to ensure smooth acceleration and verify that the transmission engages properly without slipping or hesitation.

## Steering

### Grab Handle Placement

The cabin prioritizes utilitarian cargo hauling, though ergonomic quirks such as grab handle placement should be checked during personal inspection.

**Impaired Steering Clearance**
- **Symptoms:** The positioning of the interior grab handle can obstruct or impair the driver's hand clearance when attempting complete steering maneuvers.
- **Solution:** Evaluate driver seating position and steering clearance during a test drive to ensure comfortable operation.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V551000: Electrical System:Ignition](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=City+Express&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In these models, over time, the weight on the key ring and road conditions or some other jarring event may cause the ignition switch to move out of the run position to the accessory position, turning off the engine. If the vehicle stalls, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the production information on the ignition switch, and replace it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V676000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump:Control/Drive Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=City+Express&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The Fuel Tank Temperature (FTT) sensor harness may have been incorrectly routed, damaging wires and causing a short circuit in the fuel pump fuse. A blown fuel pump fuse can cause the engine to stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and reroute the FTT sensor harness or replace the fuel pump assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Chevrolet City Express serves as a practical, fuel-efficient option for small business owners and delivery drivers. Overall owner complaint volume is low, but buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the fuel system, cabin ergonomics, and standard mechanical components.
