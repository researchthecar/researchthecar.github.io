# 2018 Chevrolet City Express Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Chevrolet City Express is a compact cargo van mechanically identical to the Nissan NV200, designed primarily for urban commercial use and delivery fleets. Used-car buyers evaluating this model should inspect functional areas like seating electronics and occupant sensors, as minor electrical glitches can occasionally surface in commercial duty cycles. Overall, it remains a straightforward and utilitarian choice for small businesses seeking nimble cargo space.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder

The 2.0-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine produces 131 horsepower and 139 lb-ft of torque, paired with a continuously variable transmission. It provides adequate power for city deliveries and generally dependable mechanical performance under normal commercial maintenance.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

This front-wheel-drive commercial van utilizes a continuously variable transmission focused on optimizing urban fuel economy. Buyers should test-drive thoroughly and check service records, as CVTs require diligent fluid maintenance to ensure long-term durability.

## Safety

### Passenger Presence System

Electrical components governing cabin accessories and safety indicator modules should be verified during a pre-purchase inspection to ensure warning lights operate properly.

**Blinking Airbag Readiness Light**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning light begins flashing on the instrument cluster, indicating a potential fault in the supplemental restraint system.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning may be required; however, owner reports note that discontinued components within the seat assembly can complicate official dealer repairs.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V676000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump:Control/Drive Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=City+Express&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The Fuel Tank Temperature (FTT) sensor harness may have been incorrectly routed, damaging wires and causing a short circuit in the fuel pump fuse. A blown fuel pump fuse can cause the engine to stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and reroute the FTT sensor harness or replace the fuel pump assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Chevrolet City Express offers strong utility and maneuverability for buyers in need of a compact work van. While major powertrain mechanical failures are uncommon in owner reports, shoppers should thoroughly check all dashboard warning lights, safety sensors, and interior electronics before finalizing a purchase.
