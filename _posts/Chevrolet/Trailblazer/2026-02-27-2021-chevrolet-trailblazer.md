# 2021 Chevrolet Trailblazer Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Chevrolet Trailblazer brings sharp styling and agile handling to the subcompact SUV segment, but prospective buyers should carefully review its mechanical and electrical reliability profile. Owner reports highlight a noticeable concentration of recurring concerns—most notably persistent 'Shift to Park' errors and select powertrain or fuel system issues. While it offers efficient packaging and modern features, thorough pre-purchase inspections are strongly recommended for used shoppers.

---

## Engine

### 1.2L Turbocharged I3

A 1.2-liter turbocharged inline-three engine producing approximately 137 horsepower, paired primarily with front-wheel drive and a continuously variable transmission (CVT). It offers adequate efficiency for daily commuting, though long-term durability warrants careful inspection.

### 1.3L Turbocharged I3

A slightly larger 1.3-liter turbocharged inline-three producing around 155 horsepower, available with all-wheel drive and a 9-speed automatic transmission. This option provides livelier acceleration than the base engine but shares common subcompact mechanical considerations.

**Fuel Odor and Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** A strong raw fuel smell noticeable around the vehicle or coming from the engine bay, frequently linked to fuel rail or line concerns.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel lines, connections, and injectors for leaks, and replace damaged components as necessary.

## Transmission

### CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)

A continuously variable transmission paired exclusively with front-wheel-drive 1.2L models. Drivers should test thoroughly for engagement delays or unusual slipping during pre-purchase test drives.

**Severe Transmission Slippage and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of power, severe transmission slipping, diagnostic trouble codes such as P2714, or prolonged delays when engaging forward or reverse gears.
- **Solution:** Professional diagnostic testing by a certified transmission specialist; may require complete transmission replacement.

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

A traditional 9-speed planetary automatic transmission coupled with all-wheel-drive 1.3L configurations. Generally offers more conventional shift characteristics than the CVT variant.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brake and Assist System

Integrates traditional hydraulic braking with electronic stability and assist features. While rare, electronic assist failures can require heightened braking effort.

**Brake Assist Malfunction and Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden illumination of brake service warnings, accompanied by reduced vehicle speed limits or loss of electronic power brake assist.
- **Solution:** Check brake control modules, fluid levels, and associated electronic harnesses for faults.

## Electrical

### Shifter and Ignition Switch Assembly

Manages gear selection signals and ignition power cycles. Microswitch wear in this assembly represents one of the most frequently reported owner complaints.

**'Shift to Park' Warning and Power-Off Failure**
- **Symptoms:** An error message displaying 'Shift to Park' even when the vehicle is parked, accompanied by persistent chimes and an inability to completely shut off electrical accessories or lock the car.
- **Solution:** Inspect the shifter microswitch or replace the shift lever assembly as required.

### Infotainment and Display Systems

Handles multimedia, connectivity, and rearview camera display functions. Software glitches and module failures can temporarily disable convenience features.

**Infotainment Screen Freeze and Reboot**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent freezing, sudden resetting, or complete failure of the central touchscreen controlling the radio, backup camera, and vehicle safety features.
- **Solution:** Perform software updates or replace faulty infotainment control modules.

## Safety

### Advanced Driver Assistance (ADAS) Cameras

Relies on windshield-mounted cameras and modules to govern active safety features. Failures disable critical collision avoidance systems.

**Front Camera and ADAS Module Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of access to forward collision avoidance, lane keep assist, and related driver aids due to internal module or front-view camera faults.
- **Solution:** Inspect internal power and ground connections to the camera module; replace faulty camera units if internal faults are diagnosed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V537000: Seats:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Trailblazer&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The rear attachment bolts of one or both front seats may not have been installed. If an occupied front seat is missing the rear attachment bolts, it may move during a crash, increasing risk of injury to the occupant.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect seat attachments for both front seats and install attachment bolts as needed, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V295000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Trailblazer&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard Number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." If drivers are uninformed and overinflate or underinflate their tires, this may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 20V588000: Service Brakes](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Trailblazer&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Sensor connection contamination in the electronic brake boost system may cause an interruption of communication between the sensor and the brake boost system. If communication with the sensor is interrupted, the electronic brake boost assist could be lost.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the electronic brake boost module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V440000: Equipment:Mechanical:Accessory Scissor/Screw/Bottle Jack/Lift:Stand](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Trailblazer&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A fractured jack can cause the vehicle to collapse, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the jack, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Chevrolet Trailblazer offers a practical and stylish choice for used SUV buyers, but it demands careful evaluation before purchase. Prospective owners should pay close attention to operation of the gear shifter, ensuring the vehicle recognizes the 'Park' position properly without electrical glitches. Furthermore, buyers considering front-wheel-drive models with the CVT should carefully test transmission engagement and acceleration response, while all-wheel-drive models should be inspected for fuel system or electronic stability warnings. A comprehensive pre-purchase mechanical inspection remains essential.
