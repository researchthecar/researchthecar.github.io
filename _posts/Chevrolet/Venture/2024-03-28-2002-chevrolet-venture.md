The 2002 Chevrolet Venture is a popular minivan that is known for its spacious interior and affordable price tag. It is powered by a 3.4L V6 engine that produces 185 horsepower and 210 lb-ft of torque. The Venture is available with either a four-speed automatic transmission or a five-speed manual transmission.

Some of the things that people like about the Venture include its comfortable ride, roomy interior, and easy-to-use dashboard. However, some people have also complained about the Venture's lack of power, poor fuel economy, and cheap interior materials.

Here is a comprehensive list of the specific issues that people are having with the 2002 Chevrolet Venture, separated into categories of the major components:

**Engine**

* **3.4L V6**
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil to leak. This can lead to overheating and engine damage.
        * Solution: The intake manifold and gasket need to be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls:
            * NHTSA Campaign Number: 03V063000
    * **Valve cover gasket failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The valve cover gasket can fail, causing oil to leak. This can lead to engine damage if not repaired.
        * Solution: The valve cover and gasket need to be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **Four-speed automatic**
    * **Shifting problems**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may have difficulty shifting gears or may slip out of gear.
        * Solution: The transmission may need to be rebuilt or replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000
        * Recalls:
            * NHTSA Campaign Number: 05V134000
* **Five-speed manual**
    * **No known issues**

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension**
    * **Strut failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The struts can fail, causing the front end of the vehicle to sag and making it difficult to control.
        * Solution: The struts need to be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear suspension**
    * **No known issues**

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator can fail, which will prevent the battery from charging. This can lead to the vehicle stalling or not starting.
    * Solution: The alternator needs to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls:
            * NHTSA Campaign Number: 04V053000

**Exterior**

* **Headlight failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The headlights may fail, making it difficult to see at night.
    * Solution: The headlights need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
        * Recalls:
            * NHTSA Campaign Number: 02V068000

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard can crack, making the interior look unsightly.
    * Solution: The dashboard needs to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Door panel failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The door panels can fail, causing the door to open or close improperly.
    * Solution: The door panels need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2002 Chevrolet Venture is a reliable vehicle that is relatively inexpensive to maintain. However, there are some issues that can be problematic, such as the intake manifold gasket failure and the transmission shifting problems. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the severity of the problem and the cost of parts and labor in your area.
