# 2022 Chevrolet Bolt EUV Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Chevrolet Bolt EUV, prospective buyers will find a modern, all-electric crossover that generally offers efficient daily driving and practical cargo space. While powertrain technology has evolved rapidly, owner reports highlight a few specific areas to check before purchasing, particularly concerning steering mechanics and occasional software or electrical quirks. Reviewing these known owner concerns can help you conduct a thorough pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### Permanent Magnet Electric Motor

The Bolt EUV utilizes a single front-mounted permanent magnet synchronous electric motor producing 200 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque, powered by a 65 kWh lithium-ion battery pack. This electric propulsion setup delivers punchy acceleration and strong everyday efficiency, though buyers should verify that all battery-related recall software updates have been properly installed.

**Propulsion and Battery Management**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected battery failure alerts detected by onboard software, which can significantly reduce propulsion power and disable charging capabilities.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic evaluation by a certified dealership to inspect the battery management system and apply software updates or component replacements as needed.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

Power is routed to the front wheels via a simple single-speed direct-drive automatic transmission. This configuration lacks traditional multi-gear shifting, resulting in seamless power delivery and very minimal mechanical maintenance.

## Steering

### Steering Gear / Rack

Owner reports indicate potential steering rack wear that can cause binding and failure to center.

**Steering Rack Binding and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Steering can become stiff at lower speeds, fail to return to center automatically after a turn, and exhibit knocking or clunking noises over bumps.
- **Solution:** Dealers typically diagnose the issue and replace the steering gear assembly to restore normal steering return and eliminate binding.

## Electrical

### Camera and Infotainment System

Electrical accessories generally function well, though intermittent display and camera operation have been noted by some owners.

**Intermittent Camera Operation**
- **Symptoms:** The front-facing and rear backup cameras may occasionally fail to display or operate intermittently.
- **Solution:** Inspect wiring harnesses and software calibration; software resets or module replacements may be required.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V517000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EUV&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** An improperly installed air bag may not deploy properly in a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace as necessary, the driver-side air bag fasteners. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V650000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EUV&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The high voltage battery could catch fire when charged to full or nearly full capacity. A battery fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V481000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EUV&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The advanced diagnostic software may have been incorrectly installed and fail to detect defective battery modules, possibly resulting in a battery fire. A battery fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reinstall the advanced diagnostic software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V812000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EUV&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, the high voltage battery could catch fire when charged to full or nearly full capacity. A battery fire increases the risk of injury.

## Summary Verdict

The Chevrolet Bolt EUV is an appealing all-electric option for commuters seeking practical space and zero tailpipe emissions. While its electric motor and single-speed transmission offer straightforward reliability, buyers should carefully check for potential steering rack binding and ensure all battery and software campaigns are up to date during a pre-purchase test drive.
