# 2023 Chevrolet Bolt EUV Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Chevrolet Bolt EUV is an all-electric crossover that offers practical range, compact utility, and an affordable entry point into EV ownership. While it generally delivers efficient everyday driving, used-car buyers should review potential trouble spots reported by owners, particularly concerning steering mechanics, driver-assistance system behavior, and electrical issues.

---

## Engine

### Permanent Magnet Synchronous Electric Motor

The Bolt EUV is powered by a single permanent magnet synchronous electric motor producing 200 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque, driving the front wheels. It provides punchy acceleration and overall robust reliability, though rare propulsion control issues have been documented.

**Propulsion Power Reduction**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle suddenly loses power, displaying a propulsion power reduced warning on the dash, and speed is restricted to roughly 30 mph.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle diagnosed by a dealer or EV technician to check for diagnostic trouble codes such as P2797 and update or repair powertrain control modules.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

Power is sent to the front wheels via a simple single-speed direct-drive automatic gearbox. This transmission design has very few moving parts and typically requires minimal maintenance throughout the vehicle's lifespan.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Column

The steering system utilizes an electric power-steering rack. Owners have noted concerns with steering returnability and mechanical noises from the intermediate shaft area.

**Steering Wheel Return Failure**
- **Symptoms:** When completing left or right turns, the steering wheel fails to return automatically to the center position, requiring manual adjustment.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering gear, rack, and U-joint area for lack of factory lubrication or mechanical binding, and replace components if necessary.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Rear Camera

Electrical architecture manages cabin displays, vehicle connectivity, and safety cameras. While generally functional, software glitches affecting screen reboots and camera feeds have been reported by some owners.

**Backup Camera and Infotainment Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The rear backup camera feed fails to display on the infotainment screen or cuts out intermittently while driving in reverse.
- **Solution:** Perform software updates at a dealership, or inspect and replace the camera module and associated wiring harnesses if glitches persist.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Driver assistance features like adaptive cruise control and automatic emergency braking enhance safety but can occasionally trigger false warnings or unexpected deceleration.

**Phantom Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The automatic emergency braking or forward collision system engages abruptly and unexpectedly when no obstacles or vehicles are present.
- **Solution:** Have dealership technicians check for software updates or recalibrate the radar and camera sensors responsible for advanced driver assistance.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Chevrolet Bolt EUV is generally well-regarded for its everyday drivability and electric efficiency. Prospective buyers should thoroughly check the steering responsiveness during test drives, verify that all driver-assistance safety features operate smoothly without unwarranted braking, and ensure infotainment and backup camera systems function without interruption.
