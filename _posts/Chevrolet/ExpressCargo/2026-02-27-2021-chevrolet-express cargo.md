## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Intermittent Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard lights flickering, radio cutting out, or temporary loss of power to accessories.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of wiring harness, grounds, or potential issues with the Body Control Module (BCM).
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dies prematurely, requiring frequent jump starts or replacement.
- **Solution:** Identification of parasitic draw from modules or accessories, or faulty alternator.
- **Cost:** $100 - $400


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Doors
**Door Latch/Lock Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Doors not locking or unlocking properly, difficulty opening from the inside or outside.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty door latch or lock actuator assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


### Lighting
**Headlight/Taillight Condensation**
- **Symptoms:** Moisture or fogging inside the headlight or taillight housings.
- **Solution:** Sealing of housing or replacement of the light assembly if severe.
- **Cost:** $100 - $400


## Interior

### HVAC System
**Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air coming from vents, or air only blowing at certain speeds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor or blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Chevrolet Express Cargo van demonstrates a generally solid reliability record, particularly concerning its core powertrain components. The V6 and V8 engines, along with the 6-speed automatic transmissions, are robust and designed for longevity in commercial applications. However, like many vehicles, it is not immune to electrical gremlins, with intermittent power loss and battery drain being the most commonly cited issues. Exterior components like door latches and lighting can also present minor problems. Interiorly, the HVAC blower motor is a component that may require attention. Overall, for a commercial van, the Express Cargo offers dependable performance, with most reported issues being manageable and not indicative of systemic design flaws.
