## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Intermittent Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard lights flicker, radio cuts out, or engine may stall unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Inspect and repair loose or corroded wiring harnesses, check battery terminals and connections, and test the alternator.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

**Faulty Alternator**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light illuminates, dimming headlights, and difficulty starting the engine.
- **Solution:** Replace the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Doors
**Door Latch Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Doors may not open or close properly, or may not latch securely.
- **Solution:** Lubricate or replace the door latch mechanism.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300

**Rusting on Lower Body Panels**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust formation, particularly in areas prone to road salt and moisture exposure.
- **Solution:** Rust treatment and repair, potentially involving panel replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1000+


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, or intermittent fan operation.
- **Solution:** Replace the HVAC blower motor and resistor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

**Seat Upholstery Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Tearing or excessive wear on driver and passenger seats due to frequent use.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace seat covers/cushions.
- **Cost:** $150 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Chevrolet Express Cargo is generally considered a reliable vehicle for commercial applications, with its powertrain options (4.3L V6, 5.3L V8, 6.0L V8, and 6-speed automatic transmissions) showing few widespread mechanical failures. The primary areas where owners might encounter issues are within the electrical system, specifically concerning intermittent power loss and potential alternator failures. Exterior components like door latches and susceptibility to rust in certain climates can also require attention. Interiorly, the HVAC blower motor and seat upholstery are noted for potential wear. Overall, with diligent maintenance and addressing these specific potential issues, the Express Cargo should continue to serve effectively as a dependable work van.
