**Introduction:**

The 2019 Chevrolet Express Cargo is a full-size commercial van known for its spacious interior and powerful engine options. It offers a choice of two gasoline engines: a 4.3L V6 and a 6.6L V8, both paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Despite its capabilities, the vehicle has received its fair share of complaints.

**Engine**

**4.3L V6**

* **Intake manifold failure:** Cracks in the intake manifold, causing rough idling, stalling, and reduced power. Manifold replacement is required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Valve train noise:** Excessive noise from the valve train, indicating worn or damaged components. Replacement of lifters, rockers, or camshaft may be necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Oil leaks:** Leaks from the valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, or rear main seal. Replacement of gaskets and seals is necessary to prevent further leaks. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**6.6L V8**

* **No significant issues reported**

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Shifting problems:** Hesitation or slipping during gear changes, caused by worn transmission components. Transmission fluid change, solenoid replacement, or transmission rebuild may be required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
* **Overheating:** Transmission fluid overheating, leading to transmission damage. Flushing or replacing the fluid, along with repairing cooling lines or cooler, may be necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension noise:** Squeaking or rattling from the front suspension, caused by worn or damaged ball joints, bushings, or shocks. Replacement of affected components is needed. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
* **Rear leaf spring breakage:** Breakage of the rear leaf springs, leading to sagging and reduced load capacity. Replacement of the leaf springs is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** Failure of the alternator, causing loss of electrical power and battery discharge. Replacement of the alternator is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
* **Wiring harness issues:** Faulty wiring harnesses can cause electrical malfunctions, such as flickering lights, power loss, or instrument panel failures. Repair or replacement of the wiring harness is needed. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Door handle breakage:** Breaking of the door handles, making it difficult to open the doors. Replacement of the handles is required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
* **Rust:** Rust formation on the undercarriage, body panels, and exhaust system. Rust removal and treatment, or replacement of affected parts, is necessary to prevent further damage. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies greatly

**Interior**

* **HVAC system failure:** Malfunctions of the heating, ventilation, and air conditioning system, resulting in poor climate control. Component replacement, such as the blower motor, condenser, or evaporator, may be necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **Instrument cluster issues:** Faulty instrument clusters, displaying incorrect readings or causing gauges to stop functioning. Cluster replacement or repair is required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**Summary:**

The 2019 Chevrolet Express Cargo has been plagued by a range of issues across various components. While some issues are minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be severe and costly. Engine and transmission problems, such as intake manifold failure and shifting issues, are among the most concerning. Suspension and electrical issues are also prevalent, leading to reduced drivability and safety concerns. The cost of repairs can vary significantly depending on the severity of the issue and the components involved.
