**Introduction: 2022 Chevrolet Express Cargo**

The 2022 Chevrolet Express Cargo is a full-size cargo van that offers a powerful V8 engine and a comfortable interior. It is available in both regular and extended wheelbase configurations, and with a choice of two V8 engines: a 6.6-liter V8 producing 401 horsepower and 464 lb-ft of torque, and a 6.0-liter V8 producing 341 horsepower and 373 lb-ft of torque. The Express Cargo comes standard with a 6-speed automatic transmission, and rear- or all-wheel drive is available.

People like the Express Cargo for its spacious cargo area, comfortable interior, and powerful engine. However, some people have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, exterior, and interior.

**Engine**

**6.6-liter V8**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil to leak. This can lead to overheating and engine damage. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. The estimated cost to fix is $1,000 - $2,000+.
* **Valve cover gasket failure:** The valve cover gasket can fail, causing oil to leak. This can lead to reduced oil pressure and engine damage. The solution is to replace the valve cover gasket. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,000.

**6.0-liter V8**

* **No issues found.**

**Transmission**

**6-speed automatic**

* **Slipping gears:** The transmission may slip gears, causing the vehicle to lose power. This can be caused by a variety of problems, including worn clutches, a faulty torque converter, or a problem with the transmission control module. The solution is to rebuild or replace the transmission. The estimated cost to fix is $2,000 - $4,000+.

**Suspension**

* **Leaf spring failure:** The leaf springs can fail, causing the vehicle to sag and lose stability. This can be caused by overloading the vehicle or by driving on rough roads. The solution is to replace the leaf springs. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,000 per leaf spring.
* **Shock absorber failure:** The shock absorbers can fail, causing the vehicle to bounce and lose stability. This can be caused by wear and tear or by driving on rough roads. The solution is to replace the shock absorbers. The estimated cost to fix is $200 - $400 per shock absorber.

**Electrical**

* **Battery failure:** The battery can fail, causing the vehicle to lose power. This can be caused by a variety of problems, including a faulty alternator, a loose connection, or a dead battery. The solution is to replace the battery. The estimated cost to fix is $100 - $200.
* **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the battery to lose power. This can be caused by a variety of problems, including a faulty voltage regulator, a loose connection, or a worn-out alternator. The solution is to replace the alternator. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,000.
* **Starter failure:** The starter can fail, preventing the engine from starting. This can be caused by a variety of problems, including a faulty solenoid, a loose connection, or a worn-out starter. The solution is to replace the starter. The estimated cost to fix is $300 - $500.

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** The vehicle may develop rust on the body panels, frame, and suspension components. This can be caused by exposure to moisture and salt. The solution is to repair or replace the rusted parts. The estimated cost to fix varies depending on the extent of the damage.

**Interior**

* **HVAC system failure:** The HVAC system may fail, causing the vehicle to lose heat or air conditioning. This can be caused by a variety of problems, including a faulty compressor, a loose connection, or a clogged filter. The solution is to repair or replace the failed component. The estimated cost to fix varies depending on the problem.
* **Power window failure:** The power windows may fail to operate, causing the windows to become stuck in the up or down position. This can be caused by a variety of problems, including a faulty switch, a loose connection, or a worn-out motor. The solution is to repair or replace the failed component. The estimated cost to fix varies depending on the problem.
* **Radio failure:** The radio may fail to operate, causing the vehicle to lose audio. This can be caused by a variety of problems, including a faulty antenna, a loose connection, or a burned-out fuse. The solution is to repair or replace the failed component. The estimated cost to fix varies depending on the problem.

**Summary**

The 2022 Chevrolet Express Cargo is a reliable and capable cargo van. However, some people have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, and interior. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem.
