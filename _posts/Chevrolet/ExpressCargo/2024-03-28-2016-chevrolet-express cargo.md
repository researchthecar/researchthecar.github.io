**Introduction:**

The 2016 Chevrolet Express Cargo is a full-size van offered with three engine options and two transmission options. The base engine is a 4.8L V8 that produces 285 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. The next step up is a 6.0L V8 producing 342 horsepower and 373 lb-ft of torque. The top-of-the-line engine is a 6.6L V8 that produces 401 horsepower and 464 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 6-speed automatic and an 8-speed automatic.

The Express Cargo is praised for its spacious interior, comfortable seats, and powerful engines. However, it is criticized for its poor fuel economy, outdated interior design, and lack of modern safety features.

**Engine**

**4.8L V8**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks from the intake manifold gaskets, causing overheating and engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gaskets.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain stretches or breaks, causing engine failure.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain, guides, and tensioners.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6.0L V8**

* ** Piston Ring Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The piston rings fail, causing excessive oil consumption and blue smoke from the exhaust.
    * Solution: Replace the piston rings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Valve Spring Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The valve springs weaken or break, causing valve float and engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the valve springs and related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6.6L V8**

* **Fuel Injector Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The fuel injectors fail, causing rough idling, loss of power, and increased emissions.
    * Solution: Replace the fuel injectors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **EGR Valve Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The EGR valve fails, causing increased emissions and decreased engine performance.
    * Solution: Replace the EGR valve.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Shudder:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The torque converter shudders during acceleration or shifts, causing vibration.
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Transmission Fluid Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission fluid leaks from seals or gaskets, causing transmission slippage and damage.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking seals or gaskets.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**8-Speed Automatic**

* **Shift Solenoid Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The shift solenoids fail, causing delayed or missed shifts and transmission damage.
    * Solution: Replace the shift solenoids.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Valve Body Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The valve body fails, causing erratic shifting, transmission slipping, and loss of power.
    * Solution: Replace the valve body.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Leaf Spring Failure:**

**Summary**

The 2016 Chevrolet Express Cargo has a number of known issues that can be costly to repair. These issues range from minor annoyances to major mechanical failures. Owners should be aware of these potential problems and factor in the cost of repairs when considering purchasing this vehicle.
