**2007 Chevrolet Suburban 2500**

The 2007 Chevrolet Suburban 2500 is a full-size SUV that is available with two engine options: a 6.0L V8 and a 8.1L V8. The 6.0L V8 produces 320 horsepower and 360 lb-ft of torque, while the 8.1L V8 produces 320 horsepower and 450 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. The Suburban 2500 has a spacious interior and a towing capacity of up to 11,800 pounds. It is also known for its reliability and off-road capabilities. However, there are some common issues that owners have reported with this vehicle.

**Engine**

* **6.0L V8**
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure**
        * **Description/Symptoms:** This issue can cause coolant leaks, overheating, and a loss of power.
        * **Solution:** Replace the intake manifold gaskets.
        * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $1,500+
        * **Recalls:** None Found
* **8.1L V8**
    * **Valve seat recession**
        * **Description/Symptoms:** This issue can cause a loss of compression, misfires, and a drop in fuel efficiency.
        * **Solution:** Replace the affected valve seats.
        * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $2,000+
        * **Recalls:** None Found

**Transmission**

* **6-speed Automatic**
    * **Slipping gears**
        * **Description/Symptoms:** This issue can cause the transmission to slip out of gear, especially when shifting from 1st to 2nd gear.
        * **Solution:** Replace the transmission's valve body.
        * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+
        * **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension noise**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** This issue can cause a clunking noise from the front suspension when driving over bumps.
    * **Solution:** Replace the upper control arms.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+
        * **Recalls:** None Found

**Electrical**

* **Headlight switch failure**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** This issue can cause the headlights to turn off while driving.
    * **Solution:** Replace the headlight switch.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $300+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rear bumper separation**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** This issue can cause the rear bumper to separate from the body of the vehicle.
    * **Solution:** Reattach the bumper using bolts.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard vibration**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** This issue can cause the dashboard to vibrate excessively while driving.
    * **Solution:** Replace the dashboard mounting brackets.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $800+
    * **Recalls:** None Found
* **Rear seat headrest release failure**
    * **Description/Symptoms:** This issue can cause the rear seat headrests to become stuck in the up position.
    * **Solution:** Replace the headrest release cables.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $300+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary**

The 2007 Chevrolet Suburban 2500 is a reliable and capable SUV that has some known issues. The most common engine issues are intake manifold gasket failure and valve seat recession. The transmission can also experience slipping gears. The suspension may make noise, and the electrical system can have issues with the headlight switch. The exterior may have rear bumper separation, and the interior may have dashboard vibration or rear seat headrest release failure. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but some of them can be expensive to repair.
