The 2007 Chevrolet Suburban 2500 is a heavy-duty, full-size SUV known for its towing capacity and spacious interior. Engine options include the 6.0L Vortec V8 (352 hp, 382 lb-ft of torque), and the 8.1L Vortec V8 (320 hp, 440 lb-ft of torque). Both engines are paired with the 4L80E four-speed automatic transmission. Owners appreciate the Suburban's ability to haul large families and cargo, and its ruggedness. Common dislikes include its poor fuel economy, large size making it difficult to park, and the age-related issues that come with a vehicle of this vintage.

**Engine**

**6.0L Vortec V8**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, leading to the need for frequent oil top-offs between oil changes. May be accompanied by blue smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Addressing the issue might involve replacing valve stem seals or piston rings. A complete engine rebuild might be necessary in severe cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Exhaust Manifold Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A ticking noise that is louder on cold starts and decreases as the engine warms up. Sometimes a visible crack on the exhaust manifold can be seen.
    *   Solution: Exhaust manifold replacement
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400-$800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**8.1L Vortec V8**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent oil top-offs between oil changes. May be accompanied by blue smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Addressing the issue might involve replacing valve stem seals or piston rings. A complete engine rebuild might be necessary in severe cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Exhaust Manifold Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A ticking noise that is louder on cold starts and decreases as the engine warms up. Sometimes a visible crack on the exhaust manifold can be seen.
    *   Solution: Exhaust manifold replacement
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400-$800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4L80E Four-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or jerky shifting between gears, especially when cold.
    *   Solution: Could be caused by a faulty shift solenoid, worn valve body, or internal damage. Start by checking fluid levels and condition. A valve body replacement or transmission rebuild might be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The engine revs up, but the vehicle does not accelerate properly. Can occur in one or more gears.
    *   Solution: Often caused by worn clutch packs or internal damage. Requires a transmission rebuild or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Leaf Spring Sagging/Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rear end sagging, reduced load capacity, and potentially clunking noises from the rear suspension.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaf spring pack.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,000+ per side
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Front End Component Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loose steering, uneven tire wear, and clunking noises from the front suspension.
    *   Solution: Inspect and replace worn ball joints, tie rod ends, pitman arm, and idler arm.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ (depending on the number of components needing replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Instrument Cluster Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Gauges failing to read correctly or intermittently, complete failure of the instrument cluster.
    *   Solution: Instrument cluster replacement or repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Body Control Module (BCM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems such as power windows not working, door locks malfunctioning, or issues with the lights.
    *   Solution: BCM replacement or reprogramming.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust on Rocker Panels/Wheel Arches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust developing along the rocker panels and wheel arches.
    *   Solution: Rust repair, panel replacement, and painting.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500-$2,000+ per area depending on severity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Cracked Dashboard**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracking and splitting of the dashboard, particularly in warmer climates.
    *   Solution: Dashboard replacement or installation of a dashboard cover.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2007 Chevrolet Suburban 2500, while robust, exhibits common issues related to its age and heavy-duty nature. Engine problems like oil consumption and exhaust manifold cracks are prevalent, potentially leading to moderate to high repair costs. Transmission problems such as harsh shifting and slipping can also require expensive repairs. Suspension wear is typical in a vehicle of this class and age, and electrical issues like instrument cluster and BCM failures can be frustrating. Exterior rust is also common. Overall, prospective buyers should be aware of these potential issues and factor them into their purchase decision, including budgeting for potential repairs.
