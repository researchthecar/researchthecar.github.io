**Introduction:**

The 1999 Chevrolet C2500 is a heavy-duty pickup truck offered with a choice of three engines and two transmissions. The base engine is a 5.7L V8 that produces 255 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque. The other two engine options are a 6.0L V8 and a 7.4L V8, both of which produce more power and torque. The C2500 is available with either a four-speed automatic transmission or a five-speed manual transmission.

Overall, the 1999 Chevrolet C2500 is a capable and reliable pickup truck. However, there are a few common issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

**5.7L V8**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** This is a common problem on the 5.7L V8 engine. The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil to leak. Symptoms include a coolant leak, overheating, and a loss of power. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,000.
* **Head gasket failure:** This is another common problem on the 5.7L V8 engine. The head gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil to leak into the combustion chambers. Symptoms include a coolant leak, overheating, and a loss of power. The solution is to replace the head gasket. The estimated cost to fix is $1,000 - $2,000.
* **Piston ring failure:** This is a less common problem on the 5.7L V8 engine. The piston rings can fail, causing oil to leak into the combustion chambers. Symptoms include a blue smoke coming from the exhaust, a loss of power, and an increase in oil consumption. The solution is to replace the piston rings. The estimated cost to fix is $2,000 - $3,000.

**Transmission**

**Four-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission slipping:** This is a common problem on the four-speed automatic transmission. The transmission may slip when shifting gears, causing a loss of power. The solution is to rebuild or replace the transmission. The estimated cost to fix is $1,500 - $3,000.

**Suspension**

* **Leaf spring failure:** The leaf springs on the C2500 can fail, causing the rear of the truck to sag. Symptoms include a sagging rear end, a rough ride, and a decrease in handling. The solution is to replace the leaf springs. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,000.
* **Shock absorber failure:** The shock absorbers on the C2500 can fail, causing a rough ride and a decrease in handling. Symptoms include a bouncing ride, a lack of stability, and a decrease in braking performance. The solution is to replace the shock absorbers. The estimated cost to fix is $200 - $400.

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** The battery on the C2500 can drain prematurely, leaving the truck stranded. Symptoms include a dead battery, a loss of power, and a lack of starting power. The solution is to find and fix the source of the drain. The estimated cost to fix varies depending on the cause of the drain.
* **Alternator failure:** The alternator on the C2500 can fail, causing the battery to not charge. Symptoms include a dead battery, a loss of power, and a lack of starting power. The solution is to replace the alternator. The estimated cost to fix is $300 - $500.

**Exterior**

* **Door handle failure:** The door handles on the C2500 can fail, making it difficult to open the doors. Symptoms include a loose or broken door handle, a difficult-to-open door, and a lack of access to the cab. The solution is to replace the door handle. The estimated cost to fix is $100 - $200.
* **Tailgate lock failure:** The tailgate lock on the C2500 can fail, making it difficult to open or close the tailgate. Symptoms include a stuck tailgate, a difficult-to-open tailgate, and a lack of access to the bed. The solution is to replace the tailgate lock. The estimated cost to fix is $100 - $200.

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cluster failure:** The dashboard cluster on the C2500 can fail, causing the gauges to not work. Symptoms include a blank or flickering dashboard, a lack of instrument readings, and a lack of warning lights. The solution is to replace the dashboard cluster. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,000.
* **HVAC system failure:** The HVAC system on the C2500 can fail, causing the heating and cooling system to not work. Symptoms include a lack of heat or cold air, a lack of airflow, and a lack of climate control. The solution is to diagnose and repair the HVAC system. The estimated cost to fix varies depending on the cause of the failure.

**Summary**

Overall, the 1999 Chevrolet C2500 is a capable and reliable pickup truck. However, there are a few common issues that owners should be aware of. These issues range in severity from minor inconveniences to major repairs. The estimated cost to fix these issues varies depending on the severity of the issue.
