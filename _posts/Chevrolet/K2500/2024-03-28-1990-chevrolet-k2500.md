**Introduction**

The 1990 Chevrolet K2500, a heavy-duty pickup truck, was known for its durability and off-road capabilities. Available with various engine and transmission options, it offered a range of horsepower and torque outputs. Some drivers praised its towing and hauling capacity, while others found its handling and fuel economy lacking.

**Engine**

**6.2L Diesel**
- **Fuel Injector Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Engine misfiring, rough idle, reduced power
  - Solution: Replace faulty fuel injectors
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Injection Pump Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling, hard starting, loss of power
  - Solution: Replace injection pump
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Manual**
- **Transmission Gearbox Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises, loss of power
  - Solution: Replace gearbox
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Clutch Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears
  - Solution: Replace clutch assembly
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
  - Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Manual**
- **Synchronizer Wear**
  - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises
  - Solution: Replace worn synchronizers
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700
  - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **Leaf Spring Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Sagging or broken leaf springs, rough ride
  - Solution: Replace leaf springs
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Shock Absorber Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling
  - Solution: Replace shock absorbers
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
  - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Alternator Regulator Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, battery discharge, electrical system issues
  - Solution: Replace alternator regulator
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Ignition Coil Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Engine misfiring, reduced power
  - Solution: Replace ignition coil
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
  - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty locking or unlocking doors, door stuck closed or open
  - Solution: Replace door lock actuator
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
  - Recalls: 98V236000

**Interior**

- **Power Window Regulator Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Power windows stuck open or closed, grinding noises
  - Solution: Replace window regulator
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
  - Recalls: None Found
- **HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: No airflow from vents, weak airflow
  - Solution: Replace blower motor
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
  - Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1990 Chevrolet K2500 experiences a range of issues that vary in severity and cost. Fuel injector and injection pump failure are major concerns with the diesel engine, while gearbox failure and clutch failure can significantly impact the driving experience. In terms of suspension, leaf spring and shock absorber failure can compromise handling and ride quality. Electrical system issues such as alternator regulator and ignition coil failure can affect the overall functionality of the vehicle. Exterior issues like door lock actuator failure can cause inconvenience, while power window regulator and HVAC blower motor failure impact interior comfort. Overall, some issues are relatively minor and affordable to fix, while others can be costly and require extensive repairs.
