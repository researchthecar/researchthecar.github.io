# 2021 Chevrolet Onix Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Chevrolet Onix offers modern styling and efficient powertrain options suited for everyday commuting. Prospective buyers should review available vehicle history and inspect key functional areas during a test drive to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 1.0L Turbocharged 3-Cylinder

This 1.0-liter turbocharged three-cylinder engine typically produces around 116 horsepower, providing responsive acceleration for city driving. It is generally regarded as efficient, though buyers should verify regular oil maintenance.

### 1.2L Naturally Aspirated 3-Cylinder

A basic 1.2-liter naturally aspirated three-cylinder engine delivering modest power outputs suited for relaxed daily commuting. Routine fluid checks and timely servicing help maintain its longevity.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A traditional six-speed automatic transmission paired with front-wheel drive configurations. It delivers smooth gear shifts under normal driving conditions when maintained properly.

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission

A conventional five-speed manual transmission offering direct gear selection and straightforward mechanical operation. Clutch wear depends largely on previous driving habits.

## Safety

### Side Air Bags

Owner reports highlight occasional concerns regarding restraint system performance during specific collision angles.

**Side Air Bag Non-Activation**
- **Symptoms:** An owner reported that side air bags failed to deploy during a collision involving a rear and side impact.
- **Solution:** Have the Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) inspected and diagnosed by a qualified repair facility to ensure sensor functionality.
