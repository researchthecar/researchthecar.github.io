# 2022 Chevrolet Bolt EV Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Chevrolet Bolt EV offers an affordable, all-electric driving experience with an EPA-estimated range of around 259 miles. As a dedicated battery-electric vehicle, it utilizes a single electric motor setup. While many owners enjoy its efficiency and brisk acceleration, used-car buyers should review potential electrical, steering, and powertrain concerns reported to federal safety authorities.

---

## Engine

### Permanent Magnet Electric Motor

The Chevrolet Bolt EV is powered by a permanent magnet synchronous AC electric motor producing approximately 200 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque. This powertrain provides instant torque and strong daily drivability, though prospective buyers should verify that all battery and propulsion recall services have been properly completed.

**Propulsion Power Reduction and Speed Limiting**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported sudden dashboard alerts for reduced propulsion power, forcing the vehicle to limit speeds sequentially down to lower maximums on the highway.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified EV technician diagnose diagnostic trouble codes (DTCs) in the propulsion control module and update or replace faulty inverter/battery components.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

The vehicle uses a single-speed direct-drive automatic gearbox paired with a front-wheel-drive layout. It generally provides smooth, seamless acceleration without traditional gear shifting, though some owners have flagged electronic shifter glitches.

**Unexpected Shifter and Park Defects**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced sudden messages to service the transmission or unexpected locking into the Park position while operating at low speeds or on the highway.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic shifter assembly, control modules, and transmission wiring harness; software updates or hardware replacement may be necessary.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

The electric power steering system provides speed-sensitive steering assist, but a subset of owners has reported premature rack wear and binding issues.

**Steering Assistance Reduction and Wheel Binding**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience heavy steering feel, warning messages regarding reduced power steering assistance, or the steering wheel failing to return smoothly to center.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electric power steering rack and control module; replacement of the steering rack assembly may be required for severe binding.

## Electrical

### Charging and Display System

The electrical system manages high-voltage battery charging, propulsion, and cabin displays, and requires careful verification of all active electrical recalls.

**Charging System Failure and Screen Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners note that the vehicle fails to charge adequately or experiences intermittent digital dashboard and infotainment screen blackouts while driving.
- **Solution:** Check charging port pins, onboard charger software, and main electrical harnesses for loose connections or software bugs.

## Exterior

### Service Brakes and Rear Lighting

The braking system features regenerative braking integrated with hydraulic friction brakes, though exterior lighting placement has drawn owner complaints.

**Bumper-Mounted Brake Light Visibility Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers following the vehicle may not immediately notice braking because the primary brake lights are mounted low on the rear bumper rather than integrated with the upper tail lights.
- **Solution:** Be aware of following distances and verify that all rear lighting functions correctly per factory specifications.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V517000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** An improperly installed air bag may not deploy properly in a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace as necessary, the driver-side air bag fasteners. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V650000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The high voltage battery could catch fire when charged to full or nearly full capacity. A battery fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 22V930000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** After a crash with seat belt pretensioner deployment, the pretensioner exhaust may ignite carpet fibers near the B-pillar, causing a fire. A vehicle fire can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install metal foil at the carpet near the pretensioner exhaust, and install a pretensioner cover as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V567000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The instrument panel may be missing the perforation on the underside of the panel cover that allows the passenger-side air bag to deploy properly. An air bag that does not deploy properly in a crash increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the instrument panel, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V845000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** After a crash with seat belt pretensioner deployment, the pretensioner exhaust may ignite carpet fibers near the B-pillar, causing a fire. A fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect both front seat belt pretensioners and, if necessary, install metal foil at the carpet near the pretensioner exhaust. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V481000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The advanced diagnostic software may have been incorrectly installed and fail to detect defective battery modules, possibly resulting in a battery fire. A battery fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reinstall the advanced diagnostic software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V812000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, the high voltage battery could catch fire when charged to full or nearly full capacity. A battery fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reinstall advanced diagnostic software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Chevrolet Bolt EV is an attractive, highly efficient option for budget-conscious electric vehicle shoppers. While its powertrain delivers brisk acceleration, buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle history for completed safety recalls, check for any active electric power steering complaints, and ensure the high-voltage battery system has a clean service record.
