**Introduction:**

The 2017 Chevrolet Bolt EV is an electric vehicle that offers a range of up to 238 miles on a single charge. It is powered by a single electric motor that produces 200 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque. The Bolt EV has received praise for its affordability, practicality, and electric range, but some owners have experienced issues with various components of the vehicle.

**Electrical**

* **Charging System Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle may not charge properly or may lose power while driving.
    * Solution: Replace the charging system components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 21V-695
* **Battery Pack Degradation:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery pack may lose capacity over time, reducing the vehicle's range.
    * Solution: Replace the battery pack.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Windshield Wiper Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The windshield wipers may not work properly or may become inoperable.
    * Solution: Replace the wiper motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 22V-148
* **Rearview Camera Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rearview camera may not display or may provide a distorted image.
    * Solution: Replace the rearview camera.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 21V-504

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Panel Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The plastic dashboard panel may crack or become brittle.
    * Solution: Replace the dashboard panel.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found
* **HVAC System Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The HVAC system may not blow air properly or may malfunction.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the HVAC components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

While the Chevy Bolt EV has proven to be a popular electric vehicle, it is not without its issues. The most common problems reported by owners include charging system malfunctions, battery pack degradation, and various electrical issues. While the majority of these issues are not considered severe, some can be expensive to repair and may impact the vehicle's overall functionality. It is important for owners to be aware of these potential issues and to address them promptly if they occur.
