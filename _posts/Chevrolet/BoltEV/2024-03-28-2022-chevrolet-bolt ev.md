**Introduction:**

The 2022 Chevrolet Bolt EV is an electric vehicle with a single-speed transmission. It is praised for its affordability, roomy interior, and impressive range. However, some owners have reported issues with the vehicle.

**Electric Motor**

**No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Single-Speed Transmission**

**Issue:** Transmission failure
Description/Symptoms: The transmission may fail, causing the vehicle to lose power or become inoperable.
Solution: Replace the transmission.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $7,000+
Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 21V571000

**Suspension**

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

**High-Voltage Battery**

**Issue:** Battery pack fire risk
Description/Symptoms: The high-voltage battery pack may overheat and catch fire.
Solution: Replace the battery pack with a new design.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $10,000 - $15,000+
Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 22V127000

**Other Electrical Components**

**Issue:** Electrical system failures
Description/Symptoms: The electrical system may fail, causing various issues such as loss of power, inoperable electronics, or flickering lights.
Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty electrical component.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Body Panels**

**Issue:** Rust and corrosion
Description/Symptoms: The body panels may rust and corrode prematurely.
Solution: Replace the affected body panels.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Infotainment System**

**Issue:** Infotainment system malfunctions
Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may freeze, crash, or become unresponsive.
Solution: Update or replace the infotainment system software.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Other Interior Components**

**Issue:** Seat adjustment issues
Description/Symptoms: The power seat adjusters may malfunction, causing the seats to move unexpectedly or become stuck.
Solution: Replace the faulty seat adjuster motor or switch.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2022 Chevrolet Bolt EV has had a number of issues, including battery pack fire risk, transmission failure, and electrical system failures. The battery pack fire risk is a serious safety concern and requires immediate attention. The other issues can be costly to fix, but the severity of the issues varies.
