# 2021 Chevrolet Bolt EV

### Overview
The 2021 Chevrolet Bolt EV is a fully electric compact hatchback known for its impressive range and affordability. As an electric vehicle, its mechanical complexity is significantly lower than that of internal combustion engine cars, leading to generally high reliability. The primary focus for potential issues shifts from traditional powertrain components to battery health, charging systems, and the integrated electronics. Owners generally report a positive experience with the Bolt EV's day-to-day operation, with most concerns revolving around specific software updates or battery performance in extreme temperatures. Regular maintenance is minimal, primarily involving tire rotations and cabin air filter replacements.

## Electrical

### Battery Pack
**Battery Fire Risk (Recall)**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden battery fires, smoke emanating from the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Software update to limit charging to 90% and specific diagnostic checks. In some cases, battery module replacement or full pack replacement.
- **Cost:** $0 (Recall)


### Charging System
**Intermittent Charging Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to charge, charging port not recognized, error messages during charging.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of charging port, onboard charger, or software glitches. May require software updates or component replacement.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500


### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, connectivity issues (Apple CarPlay/Android Auto).
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $100 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Chevrolet Bolt EV generally exhibits good reliability, particularly due to its simpler electric powertrain. The most significant issue reported was the battery fire risk, which led to a major recall and has been addressed through software updates and potential battery replacements. Beyond this critical recall, other electrical components like the charging system and infotainment can experience occasional glitches, but these are typically resolved with software updates or minor repairs. The mechanical aspects of the vehicle are robust, and owners can expect minimal issues outside of the battery recall and standard EV maintenance.
