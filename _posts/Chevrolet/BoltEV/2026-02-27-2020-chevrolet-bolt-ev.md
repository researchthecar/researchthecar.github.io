# 2020 Chevrolet Bolt EV Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Chevrolet Bolt EV is an affordable, all-electric hatchback offering practical daily driving range and peppy performance. While it is generally well-regarded for its low operating costs and efficient electric powertrain, prospective buyers should carefully review potential issues related to battery health, electric drive components, and known mechanical issues such as steering rack stiffness. Evaluating the status of past battery recalls and service history is critical when shopping for a used model.

---

## Engine

### Electric Motor / Propulsion System

The Bolt EV utilizes a single permanent-magnet synchronous electric motor producing approximately 200 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration. The propulsion system is generally reliable, though owners have reported occasional electronic and power-delivery glitches.

**Engine Stalling / Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have experienced a sudden loss of motor power while driving at highway speeds or through intersections, accompanied by illuminated warning lamps and the "Ready" light extinguishing.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic testing by a certified dealer to check propulsion system software, wiring harnesses, and battery cell health.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct Drive Transmission

The vehicle uses a single-speed fixed-gear automatic transmission paired with front-wheel drive, offering seamless, shift-free power delivery that requires very little maintenance.

## Brakes

### One-Pedal Driving Brake Lights

Regenerative braking systems allow for efficient energy capture and single-pedal operation, though software calibration issues can occasionally affect rear illumination timing.

**Brake Lights Fail to Illuminate During One-Pedal Stops**
- **Symptoms:** When coming to a complete stop using One-Pedal Driving mode, the rear brake lights may fail to illuminate, creating a rear-end collision hazard for following traffic.
- **Solution:** Check for available software patches or sensor recalibrations designed to correct brake light activation thresholds during regenerative deceleration.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Column

The electronic power steering setup provides light and direct handling, though some owners experience mechanical binding or stiffness within the steering rack over time.

**Steering Wheel Fails to Return to Center**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel may feel stiff or sticky, failing to automatically return to center after executing turns, which requires constant manual correction by the driver.
- **Solution:** Inspection of the electric power steering assist and replacement of the steering rack assembly if a mechanical binding fault is confirmed.

## Electrical

### Charging System and Battery Software

Electrical architecture centers around a high-voltage lithium-ion battery pack. While capable of long driving ranges, early model years have been heavily scrutinized for battery safety recalls and charging software interventions.

**Charging Failures and Limited Range**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may fail to accept a charge from multiple charging stations or experience a severe limitation in usable driving range due to battery monitoring software flags.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis by a dealership to determine if software updates or high-voltage battery module replacements are required under applicable warranty extensions.

### Backup Camera and Visibility

Exterior electronics and safety peripherals generally function well, but rearview camera glitches are a frequent minor annoyance reported by owners.

**Intermittent Backup Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display shows a black screen or a crossed-out camera icon when shifting into reverse, frequently exacerbated by moisture or rain.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera wiring harnesses, connectors, or replace the backup camera module if moisture intrusion has caused permanent failure.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V184000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A damaged cable may cause the rear door to open unintentionally when the rear window is opened. Additionally, it may cause the rear inside door handle to not work.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rear inside door-handle cables, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V808000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** If a brake caliper fractures and brake fluid is lost, the vehicle may experience reduced brake performance, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the casting lot number on the front left brake caliper and replace it if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V811000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** If an incorrect bolt was used, the seat belt may not be properly attached to the vehicle. If a seat belt assembly is not properly attached to the vehicle, the seat belt may not properly restrain an occupant in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the suspect bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V650000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The high voltage battery could catch fire when charged to full or nearly full capacity. A battery fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 22V930000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** After a crash with seat belt pretensioner deployment, the pretensioner exhaust may ignite carpet fibers near the B-pillar, causing a fire. A vehicle fire can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install metal foil at the carpet near the pretensioner exhaust, and install a pretensioner cover as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V845000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** After a crash with seat belt pretensioner deployment, the pretensioner exhaust may ignite carpet fibers near the B-pillar, causing a fire. A fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect both front seat belt pretensioners and, if necessary, install metal foil at the carpet near the pretensioner exhaust. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V481000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The advanced diagnostic software may have been incorrectly installed and fail to detect defective battery modules, possibly resulting in a battery fire. A battery fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reinstall the advanced diagnostic software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V812000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, the high voltage battery could catch fire when charged to full or nearly full capacity. A battery fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reinstall advanced diagnostic software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Chevrolet Bolt EV remains a compelling choice for budget-conscious used EV buyers due to its solid range and low running costs. However, shoppers should prioritize finding a vehicle with documented battery recall compliance and thoroughly test both the steering feel and the charging system before purchasing. Checking service records for any past high-voltage battery repairs or software updates will provide valuable peace of mind.
