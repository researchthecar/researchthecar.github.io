# 2021 Chevrolet Bolt EV Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Chevrolet Bolt EV is an all-electric compact hatchback offering snappy acceleration and an impressive driving range for its price point. While generally well-regarded for daily commuting and efficiency, prospective buyers should review important owner-reported complaints concerning high-voltage propulsion warnings, steering rack concerns, and complex electrical faults.

---

## Engine

### Permanent Magnet Electric Motor

Powered by a single permanent magnet synchronous electric motor producing roughly 200 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk and immediate acceleration. The electric drive unit is fundamentally durable, though high-voltage battery software and monitoring notices have drawn owner scrutiny.

**Propulsion System Warning / Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a sudden 'Reduced Propulsion Power' warning or unexpected power loss while driving, occasionally accompanied by the vehicle stalling or failing to maintain speed.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic scanning by a qualified technician to check high-voltage battery status, software logic, or related wiring harnesses.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

Equipped with a fixed-ratio single-speed direct-drive automatic transmission paired with front-wheel drive. It offers smooth, shift-free operation and has a minimal history of mechanical failure.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Gear

Complaints indicate potential binding and stiffness within the steering assembly that can affect handling confidence.

**Steering Rack Binding and Stiffness**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel becomes increasingly stiff or binds at highway speeds, fails to return naturally to the center after a turn, and may produce a clunking sound when hitting bumps.
- **Solution:** May require replacement of the steering rack assembly if internal binding or mechanical failure is confirmed.

## Electrical

### Electrical System and Wiring

Electrical architecture governs charging, propulsion monitoring, and cabin accessories, with select owner reports highlighting wiring and warning light anomalies.

**Battery Warning Lights and Wiring Harness Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Illumination of 12V or high-voltage battery warning indicators, intermittent infotainment glitches, or sudden stalling linked to compromised wiring harnesses.
- **Solution:** Inspection of vehicle wiring, software updates, or replacement of affected wiring components.
- **Cost:** ~$3,600 total parts and labor for wiring harness replacement

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V650000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The high voltage battery could catch fire when charged to full or nearly full capacity. A battery fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 22V930000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** After a crash with seat belt pretensioner deployment, the pretensioner exhaust may ignite carpet fibers near the B-pillar, causing a fire. A vehicle fire can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install metal foil at the carpet near the pretensioner exhaust, and install a pretensioner cover as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V845000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** After a crash with seat belt pretensioner deployment, the pretensioner exhaust may ignite carpet fibers near the B-pillar, causing a fire. A fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect both front seat belt pretensioners and, if necessary, install metal foil at the carpet near the pretensioner exhaust. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V481000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The advanced diagnostic software may have been incorrectly installed and fail to detect defective battery modules, possibly resulting in a battery fire. A battery fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reinstall the advanced diagnostic software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V812000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, the high voltage battery could catch fire when charged to full or nearly full capacity. A battery fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reinstall advanced diagnostic software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Chevrolet Bolt EV is an appealing and cost-effective option for EV shoppers prioritizing range and utility, but buyers should perform a thorough inspection. Pay close attention to steering feel during a test drive to rule out rack binding, and verify that all high-voltage battery updates and electrical diagnostics are up to date.
