# 2023 Chevrolet Bolt EV Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Chevrolet Bolt EV offers an affordable, all-electric driving experience with practical range, but prospective buyers should review known owner complaints carefully. While generally praised for its urban maneuverability and efficiency, certain recurring issues involving the steering system, infotainment electronics, and powertrain components have surfaced in owner reports.

---

## Engine

### Permanent Magnet Electric Motor

The Bolt EV is powered by a single permanent magnet synchronous electric motor producing 200 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque, drawing energy from a 65 kWh lithium-ion battery pack. This powertrain option provides lively acceleration and strong overall reliability with minimal moving parts compared to internal combustion engines.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

Power is sent to the front wheels via a fixed-ratio, single-speed direct-drive transmission. This unit is structurally simple and generally dependable, though isolated electronic push-button shifter faults have been reported.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Column

Owner reports indicate occasional steering binding and a failure for the wheel to return to center, which can pose safety concerns at higher speeds.

**Steering Return-to-Center Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel fails to return to center automatically after completing a turn, requiring the driver to manually steer the wheel back into position.
- **Solution:** Requires inspection of the steering assembly and potential replacement of the steering rack under warranty or out-of-pocket.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Display System

Electrical and screen failures can temporarily disable convenience features and critical rear-visibility camera systems.

**Infotainment Screen Blanking**
- **Symptoms:** The central infotainment screen completely blacks out or stops functioning, leading to a loss of the backup camera display and associated safety warnings.
- **Solution:** Requires dealership diagnostic assessment and potential replacement of the infotainment display hardware or software module.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V930000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** After a crash with seat belt pretensioner deployment, the pretensioner exhaust may ignite carpet fibers near the B-pillar, causing a fire. A vehicle fire can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install metal foil at the carpet near the pretensioner exhaust, and install a pretensioner cover as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V567000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The instrument panel may be missing the perforation on the underside of the panel cover that allows the passenger-side air bag to deploy properly. An air bag that does not deploy properly in a crash increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the instrument panel, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V845000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** After a crash with seat belt pretensioner deployment, the pretensioner exhaust may ignite carpet fibers near the B-pillar, causing a fire. A fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect both front seat belt pretensioners and, if necessary, install metal foil at the carpet near the pretensioner exhaust. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Chevrolet Bolt EV is a compelling, budget-friendly electric vehicle, but used car shoppers should thoroughly inspect the steering feel during a test drive and ensure all electronic systems function correctly. While major mechanical failures are uncommon, verifying dealership service records for any past electrical or steering repairs can help mitigate ownership risks.
