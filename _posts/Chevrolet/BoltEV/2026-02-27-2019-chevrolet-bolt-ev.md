# 2019 Chevrolet Bolt EV Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Chevrolet Bolt EV offers practical all-electric driving with impressive range for its class, but prospective buyers should carefully review several documented owner complaints. While the electric powertrain generally delivers quick acceleration and low ongoing operating costs, specific areas such as steering components and high-voltage battery charging systems warrant a thorough pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### Permanent Magnet Electric Motor

The Bolt EV uses a permanent magnet synchronous electric motor producing 200 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque, delivering brisk acceleration. Overall reliability is generally solid, though isolated incidents of unexpected motive power loss have been reported by owners.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected deceleration or sudden loss of propulsion while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by reduced propulsion warnings.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic scanning by a certified technician to identify software or inverter faults, which may involve software updates or component replacement.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

The vehicle utilizes a single-speed reduction gear transmission paired with front-wheel drive, offering smooth and seamless power delivery without traditional gear shifts. This direct-drive setup features very few mechanical moving parts and typically requires minimal maintenance.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Column

The electric power steering system provides light and responsive handling, but some owners experience mechanical binding or failure within the steering rack over time.

**Steering Wheel Fails to Return to Center**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel sticks or fails to return to the center position automatically after completing a turn, requiring manual physical correction.
- **Solution:** Often requires replacement of the steering rack assembly.
- **Cost:** $3,000 - $4,000

## Electrical

### Charging System and Battery Interface

The electrical system manages high-voltage power distribution, regenerative braking, and onboard charging. While efficient, complex charging hardware interfaces can occasionally experience latching or communication errors.

**Charging Port Locking and Connection Failures**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle displays charging port error messages, fails to lock the charging plug securely, or refuses to charge after plug insertion.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the charge receptacle actuator solenoid or update vehicle charging software.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V184000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A damaged cable may cause the rear door to open unintentionally when the rear window is opened. Additionally, it may cause the rear inside door handle to not work.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rear inside door-handle cables, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V560000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The high voltage battery could catch fire when charged to full or nearly full capacity. A battery fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 20V701000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The high voltage battery could catch fire when charged to full or nearly full capacity. A battery fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and as an interim repair, beginning on November 17, 2020, dealers will reprogram the hybrid propulsion control module 2 (HPCM2) to limit the full charge to 90%, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V576000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A reduction of braking performance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will bleed the vehicle's brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V930000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** After a crash with seat belt pretensioner deployment, the pretensioner exhaust may ignite carpet fibers near the B-pillar, causing a fire. A vehicle fire can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install metal foil at the carpet near the pretensioner exhaust, and install a pretensioner cover as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V845000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** After a crash with seat belt pretensioner deployment, the pretensioner exhaust may ignite carpet fibers near the B-pillar, causing a fire. A fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect both front seat belt pretensioners and, if necessary, install metal foil at the carpet near the pretensioner exhaust. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Chevrolet Bolt EV is an appealing choice for buyers seeking affordable, long-range electric mobility. However, used car shoppers should verify that all applicable battery and safety recalls have been properly completed and pay close attention during a test drive to ensure the steering returns smoothly to center without binding.
