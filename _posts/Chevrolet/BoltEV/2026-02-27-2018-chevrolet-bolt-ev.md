# 2018 Chevrolet Bolt EV Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Chevrolet Bolt EV is an all-electric hatchback known for its impressive driving range and peppy performance. For used-car shoppers, it offers an accessible entry point into long-range electric vehicle ownership, though buyers should carefully evaluate battery health and historical service records regarding past recall campaigns.

---

## Engine

### Permanent Magnet Electric Motor

The 2018 Bolt EV utilizes a single permanent magnet synchronous AC motor producing approximately 200 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque. Power delivery is smooth and immediate, and the electric drive unit generally proves robust with few mechanical complaints.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

Power is sent to the front wheels via a fixed-ratio single-speed gearbox. This transmission setup is mechanically simple, highly efficient, and typically requires very little maintenance over the vehicle's lifespan.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V560000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The high voltage battery could catch fire when charged to full or nearly full capacity. A battery fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 20V701000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The high voltage battery could catch fire when charged to full or nearly full capacity. A battery fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and as an interim repair, beginning on November 17, 2020, dealers will reprogram the hybrid propulsion control module 2 (HPCM2) to limit the full charge to 90%, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V576000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A reduction of braking performance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will bleed the vehicle's brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V930000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** After a crash with seat belt pretensioner deployment, the pretensioner exhaust may ignite carpet fibers near the B-pillar, causing a fire. A vehicle fire can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install metal foil at the carpet near the pretensioner exhaust, and install a pretensioner cover as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V845000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Bolt+EV&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** After a crash with seat belt pretensioner deployment, the pretensioner exhaust may ignite carpet fibers near the B-pillar, causing a fire. A fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect both front seat belt pretensioners and, if necessary, install metal foil at the carpet near the pretensioner exhaust. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Chevrolet Bolt EV is a practical and efficient choice for daily commuting, backed by a simple electric powertrain. Prospective buyers should verify that all battery-related recall remedies have been properly completed and check the remaining health of the battery pack before purchasing.
