**Introduction:**
The 2017 Chevrolet Caprice is a full-size sedan that was discontinued after the 2017 model year. It was powered by a 6.0L V8 engine that produced 360 horsepower and 380 lb-ft of torque. The Caprice was available with a 6-speed automatic transmission. It was known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and powerful engine. However, there have been some reported issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**
* **6.0L V8**
    * **Intake manifold cracking:** Intake manifold cracking can cause coolant leaks, engine overheating, and loss of power. The intake manifold needs to be replaced. - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
    * **Water pump failure:** Water pump failure can cause coolant leaks and overheating. The water pump and associated gaskets need to be replaced. - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * **Oil leaks:** Oil leaks can occur from the oil pan gasket, valve cover gaskets, or rear main seal. The leaking gasket or seal needs to be replaced. - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
* **6-speed automatic**
    * **Transmission slipping:** The transmission may slip or jerk when shifting gears. The transmission may need to be rebuilt or replaced. - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * **Solenoid failure:** Solenoid failure can cause shifting problems and transmission failure. The solenoids need to be replaced. - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
* **Front strut failure:** Front strut failure can cause a knocking noise, poor handling, and uneven tire wear. The front struts need to be replaced. - Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500
* **Rear shock failure:** Rear shock failure can cause a bouncy ride, poor handling, and uneven tire wear. The rear shocks need to be replaced. - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
* **Battery drain:** Battery drain can occur due to faulty wiring or electrical components. The faulty component needs to be repaired or replaced. - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000
* **Electrical shorts:** Electrical shorts can cause various electrical issues, such as flickering lights, inoperative components, and battery drain. The electrical short needs to be located and repaired. - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
* **Rust:** Rust can occur on the body panels, frame, and exhaust system. The affected parts need to be replaced or repaired. - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
* **Climate control issues:** The climate control system may not work properly or may make strange noises. The climate control components need to be serviced or replaced. - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000
* **Window regulator failure:** The window regulator may fail, causing the window to become stuck or inoperable. The window regulator needs to be replaced. - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
* **Seat adjustment issues:** The seat adjustment mechanisms may fail, making it difficult or impossible to adjust the seat. The seat adjustment components need to be repaired or replaced. - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2017 Chevrolet Caprice is a spacious and powerful sedan, but it has been reported to have some issues. The most severe issues include engine intake manifold cracking, transmission slipping, and suspension component failure. These issues can be expensive to fix, costing upwards of $4,000. However, many of the other issues are relatively minor and can be fixed for a few hundred dollars. Overall, the Caprice is a reliable vehicle, but it is important to be aware of the potential issues before purchasing one.
