# 2016 Chevrolet Impala Limited Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Chevrolet Malibu presents a mix of modern styling and efficiency, but prospective used-car buyers should carefully inspect certain mechanical and electrical systems before purchasing. Based on owner feedback and federal safety complaints, the model experiences several notable recurring issues—most prominently a persistent "Shift to Park" electrical defect, occasional brake vacuum pump failures, and power steering assist warnings. While many owners enjoy its comfortable ride and fuel economy, addressing these common issues can lead to unexpected repair bills.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbo 4-Cylinder

The standard 1.5-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces around 160 horsepower and is paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. It offers solid fuel economy for daily commuting, though routine maintenance of the turbo and cooling systems is essential.

### 2.0L Turbo 4-Cylinder

The upgraded 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 generates approximately 250 horsepower and comes mated to an 8-speed automatic transmission. It provides brisk acceleration for highway passing, though buyers should watch for signs of heavy wear on higher-mileage examples.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The standard 6-speed automatic transmission provides smooth shifts paired with the base engine option. Routine fluid changes help maintain long-term durability.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission handles higher torque loads from the optional turbo engine. Smooth operation is typical, though shift quality should be verified during a test drive.

## Brakes

### Vacuum Pump & Mechanical Brakes

Engine-driven vacuum pumps supply necessary assist for braking functions. Failures can compromise stopping power and lead to secondary mechanical damage if left unaddressed.

**Brake Vacuum Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal becomes exceptionally hard to press, requiring significantly more effort to stop the vehicle, and may trigger reduced braking capability codes.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed mechanical vacuum pump and inspect internal camshaft components for oil contamination or wear.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

Electric power steering systems provide adaptive feel but can occasionally suffer from intermittent module or sensor faults.

**Steering Assist Loss**
- **Symptoms:** A warning message stating 'Steering Assist Reduced, Drive with care' appears on the dashboard, making the steering wheel significantly heavier to turn.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the electric power steering module and wiring harness, and replace faulty sensors or steering gear components as needed.

## Electrical

### Shifter Switch and Ignition Interlock

The electronic shifter sensing mechanisms frequently lose calibration or fail, resulting in persistent warning messages and accessory power drain.

**Persistent 'Shift to Park' Warning**
- **Symptoms:** Even after placing the vehicle fully in park, the instrument cluster displays a 'Shift to Park' message and the vehicle refuses to shut off completely or drains the battery.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty shifter microswitch or assembly inside the center console.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used model, verify that all electrical features—especially the shifter sensor and ignition shutoff—operate correctly to avoid frustrating battery drains or warning messages. Check service records for past vacuum pump replacements and ensure the brakes and steering feel responsive during your test drive. Choosing a well-maintained example with verified service history significantly reduces the risk of encountering these common trouble spots.
