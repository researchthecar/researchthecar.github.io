Here is a breakdown of the common issues and complaints associated with the 2016 Chevrolet Impala Limited. The 2016 Chevrolet Impala Limited was primarily sold to fleet customers, being a slightly restyled version of the previous-generation Impala. It came standard with a 3.6L V6 engine, producing 305 horsepower and 264 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. One of the main pros of the vehicle is that they tend to be cheaper to buy used as opposed to the regular Impala. The main con of the vehicle is that it is outdated compared to the standard Impala due to being a continuation of the last generation of the vehicle.

**Engine**

**3.6L V6**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report higher than expected oil consumption, requiring frequent topping off between oil changes. This can sometimes be related to issues with the valve seals or piston rings.
    *   Solution: The best solution is to replace the valve seals and piston rings. Some owners opt to switch to higher-viscosity oil to help mitigate the issue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Timing Chain Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The timing chain can stretch or wear prematurely, leading to a check engine light and potential engine performance issues. The engine may also produce a rattling noise, especially upon startup.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the timing chain, tensioners, and guides is required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic (6T70)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing harsh or erratic shifting, especially between certain gears. Transmission slipping may also occur.
    *   Solution: Replacing the valve body is the common solution, but depending on the severity of the symptoms, the transmission may need to be rebuilt or replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+ depending on the repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Strut Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature wear or failure of the front struts can cause a bouncy ride, poor handling, and unusual noises (clunking or rattling) when driving over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the affected struts is necessary, typically done in pairs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+ per pair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report experiencing unexplained battery drain, leading to a dead battery and difficulty starting the vehicle. This can be caused by a faulty sensor, relay, or module.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of the faulty component causing the battery drain.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the component.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Faulty Mass Air Flow Sensor (MAF)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Can cause drivability issues like a loss of power, stalling, or rough running.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the Mass Air Flow Sensor (MAF).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlight housings, reducing visibility.
    *   Solution: Replacing the headlight assembly. Some owners try to seal the housing themselves, but this is only a temporary solution.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per headlight assembly.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Radio/Infotainment System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, crashing, or unresponsive touchscreen.
    *   Solution: Updating the software or replacing the radio head unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+ depending on the severity and solution.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Air Conditioning Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning failure and not being able to get cold.
    *   Solution: Check Freon levels. If Freon levels are good, then look to replace the compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1200+ depending on the repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Chevrolet Impala Limited, while offering a comfortable ride and ample space, has some common issues that potential buyers should be aware of. The engine, particularly the 3.6L V6, can experience oil consumption and timing chain wear, which can lead to costly repairs. The 6-speed transmission can also suffer from harsh shifting and slipping issues. Other issues include suspension problems, electrical malfunctions, headlight condensation, and infotainment system problems. The severity of these issues ranges from minor annoyances to major mechanical failures, and the cost to fix can vary significantly. Therefore, a thorough inspection and maintenance history review are recommended before purchasing this vehicle.
