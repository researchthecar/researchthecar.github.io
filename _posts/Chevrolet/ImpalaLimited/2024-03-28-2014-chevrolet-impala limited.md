## 2014 Chevrolet Impala Limited

The 2014 Chevrolet Impala Limited is a full-size sedan that offers a comfortable ride and a spacious cabin. It is powered by a 3.6-liter V6 engine that produces 305 horsepower and 264 lb-ft of torque, paired with a six-speed automatic transmission. Other engine and transmission options include a 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine with a six-speed automatic transmission and a 3.6-liter V6 engine with a nine-speed automatic transmission.

People like the Impala Limited for its comfortable ride, spacious cabin, and powerful engine. However, some owners have reported issues with the vehicle, including engine and transmission problems.

### Engine

**3.6-liter V6 Engine**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing a vacuum leak. This can lead to reduced engine power, rough idling, and increased fuel consumption. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,000+.
* **Timing chain failure:** The timing chain can fail, causing the engine to lose power and stall. The solution is to replace the timing chain and related components. The estimated cost to fix is $2,000 - $3,000+.

**2.5-liter Four-Cylinder Engine**

* **No Issues Found**

### Transmission

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission slipping:** The transmission may slip gears, causing the vehicle to hesitate or lose power. The solution is to replace the transmission. The estimated cost to fix is $2,000 - $3,000+.
* **Transmission fluid leak:** The transmission may leak fluid, causing a loss of power and overheating. The solution is to replace the transmission fluid and fix the leak. The estimated cost to fix is $100 - $500+.

**Nine-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **No Issues Found**

### Suspension

* **Strut failure:** The struts can fail, causing the vehicle to bounce and handle poorly. The solution is to replace the struts. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,000+.
* **Sway bar links failure:** The sway bar links can fail, causing the vehicle to lean excessively when cornering. The solution is to replace the sway bar links. The estimated cost to fix is $100 - $200+.

### Electrical

* **Electrical system failure:** The electrical system can fail, causing a variety of problems, such as the vehicle not starting, the lights not working, or the air conditioning not blowing. The solution is to diagnose and repair the electrical system. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $2,000+.
* **Battery drain:** The battery can drain excessively, causing the vehicle to not start. The solution is to diagnose and fix the battery drain. The estimated cost to fix is $100 - $500+.

### Exterior

* **No Issues Found**

### Interior

* **Instrument cluster failure:** The instrument cluster may fail, causing the gauges to stop working. The solution is to replace the instrument cluster. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,000+.
* **Infotainment system failure:** The infotainment system may fail, causing the screen to go black or the system to stop responding. The solution is to update the software or replace the infotainment system. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $1,000+.

### Summary

The 2014 Chevrolet Impala Limited is a comfortable and powerful sedan, but it has been plagued by a number of issues, including engine and transmission problems. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but some can be expensive to fix. If you are considering buying a 2014 Chevrolet Impala Limited, be sure to do your research and be aware of the potential issues that you may encounter.
