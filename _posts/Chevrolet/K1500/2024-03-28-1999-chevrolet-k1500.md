**1999 Chevrolet K1500**

The 1999 Chevrolet K1500 is a full-size pickup truck that was offered with multiple engine and transmission options. The base engine is a 4.3L V6 that produces 190 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options include a 5.0L V8 that produces 230 horsepower and 290 lb-ft of torque, and a 5.7L V8 that produces 255 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual, a 4-speed automatic, and a 6-speed manual.

People like the K1500 for its powerful engines, spacious interior, and comfortable ride. However, some people have experienced issues with the vehicle's reliability.

**Engine**

**4.3L V6**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil to leak. Symptoms include overheating, low coolant levels, and oil leaks. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+.
* **Valve cover gasket failure:** The valve cover gasket can fail, causing oil to leak. Symptoms include oil leaks and a burning oil smell. The solution is to replace the valve cover gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400.
   * NHTSA Recall Number: 99V139000

**5.0L V8**

* **No Issues Found**

**5.7L V8**

* **Oil pump failure:** The oil pump can fail, causing the engine to lose oil pressure. Symptoms include knocking sounds from the engine, reduced oil pressure, and engine damage. The solution is to replace the oil pump. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+.
   * NHTSA Recall Number: 99V139001

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **No Issues Found**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Torque converter failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing the transmission to slip and lose power. Symptoms include slipping gears, loss of power, and a burning smell. The solution is to replace the torque converter. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500 - $2,500+.
   * NHTSA Recall Number: 99V139002

**6-Speed Manual**

* **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Leaf spring breakage:** The leaf springs can break, causing the rear of the vehicle to sag. Symptoms include a sagging rear end, a rough ride, and uneven tire wear. The solution is to replace the leaf springs. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000 per side.
* **Shock absorber failure:** The shock absorbers can fail, causing the ride to become bouncy and unstable. Symptoms include abouncy ride, poor handling, and uneven tire wear. The solution is to replace the shock absorbers. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400 per shock.

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the battery to drain and the vehicle to lose power. Symptoms include dimming lights, a dead battery, and a warning light on the dashboard. The solution is to replace the alternator. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000.
* **Starter failure:** The starter can fail, preventing the engine from starting. Symptoms include a clicking noise when trying to start the engine, a dead battery, and a warning light on the dashboard. The solution is to replace the starter. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400.

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** The body panels can rust, especially on vehicles that are exposed to salt or moisture. Symptoms include rust spots, holes in the body panels, and bubbling paint. The solution is to repair or replace the rusted panels. Estimated cost to fix: Varies depending on the extent of the rust.

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracking:** The dashboard can crack, especially on vehicles that are exposed to sunlight. Symptoms include cracks in thedashboard, a faded dashboard, and a peeling dashboard. The solution is to replace the dashboard. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000.
* **Seat switch failure:** The seat switch can fail, preventing the seat from moving. Symptoms include a seat that does not move, a warning light on the dashboard, and a buzzing sound from the seat. The solution is to replace the seat switch. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400.

**Summary**

The 1999 Chevrolet K1500 is a powerful and spacious truck that is popular with many drivers. However, the vehicle has experienced some reliability issues, including engine and transmission problems. The severity of the issues varies, and the cost to fix them can be significant.
