**Introduction:**

The 2012 Chevrolet Suburban 1500 is a full-size SUV available in two trim levels: LS and LT. It offers a spacious interior, ample cargo space, and towing capacity of up to 8,500 pounds. The Suburban 1500 is powered by a 5.3L V8 engine with 320 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission.

People appreciate the Suburban's comfort, power, and functionality. However, complaints have been raised regarding its fuel economy and reliability.

**Engine**

**5.3L V8**

* **Intake Manifold Issue:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking coolant, overheating, rough idling
    * Solution: Intake manifold replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 14V871000
* **Active Fuel Management System Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, loss of power, increased fuel consumption
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of AFM lifters and other components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Valve Cover Leakage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the valve covers
    * Solution: Valve cover replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Harsh Shifting:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Abrupt or delayed gear changes, clunking sounds
    * Solution: Transmission fluid change, TCM update, or transmission rebuild
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Premature Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slips, fails to engage gears
    * Solution: Transmission replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,500 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Control Arm Bushings:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or clunking noises from the front suspension
    * Solution: Control arm replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Leaf Springs:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear suspension, reduced ride height
    * Solution: Leaf spring replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electrical System Issues:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Electrical failures, loss of power, battery drain
    * Solution: Varies depending on the specific issue
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust Issues:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust developing on the undercarriage, body panels, or exhaust system
    * Solution: Rust repair or replacement of rusted components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Depends on the extent of the damage
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Steering Wheel Controls Intermittent Function:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Buttons on the steering wheel intermittently fail to work
    * Solution: Replacement of steering wheel controls
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Air Conditioning Inoperative:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning system does not cool the cabin
    * Solution: Compressor, condenser, or evaporator replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2012 Chevrolet Suburban 1500 can be a reliable vehicle but has faced issues with its engine, transmission, and other components. While some issues are relatively inexpensive to fix, others can be quite costly. It's essential to consider these potential repair expenses when evaluating the overall value and longevity of the vehicle.
