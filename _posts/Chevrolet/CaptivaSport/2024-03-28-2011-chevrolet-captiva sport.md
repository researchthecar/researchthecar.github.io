**Introduction:**

The 2011 Chevrolet Captiva Sport is a midsize SUV known for its versatility and family-friendly features. It's powered by a 3.0L V6 engine and offers a choice of a 6-speed automatic transmission or a 5-speed manual transmission. The Captiva Sport has received generally positive reviews for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and decent fuel economy. However, it has also been criticized for its unrefined engine and lack of standard features.

**Engine**

**3.0L V6**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:**
  * Description/Symptoms: Owners report high oil consumption, leading to frequent oil changes and potential engine damage.
  * Solution: Replace piston rings and oil pan gasket.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
  * Recalls: #16V342000 (2016), #18V843000 (2018)
* **Timing Chain Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain can stretch or fail prematurely, resulting in a loss of engine power or catastrophic engine damage.
  * Solution: Replace timing chain, guides, and tensioners.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Slipping Transmission:**
  * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip or hesitate during gear changes, causing erratic acceleration or loss of power.
  * Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Differential Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: The differential may wear out or fail, resulting in a grinding or humming noise, vibration, and loss of power.
  * Solution: Replace differential assembly.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: The front strut mounts may wear or break, leading to a clunking or rattling noise over bumps.
  * Solution: Replace front strut mounts.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Control Arm Bushing Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: The control arm bushings may deteriorate or fail, causing a loose steering feel, vibration, or tire alignment issues.
  * Solution: Replace control arm bushings.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
  * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
  * Description/Symptoms: The battery may discharge rapidly, requiring frequent charging or replacement.
  * Solution: Identify and fix parasitic drain sources, such as faulty electrical components or accessories.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: The alternator may fail, resulting in a lack of power to electrical systems and a warning light on the dashboard.
  * Solution: Replace alternator.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
  * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Undercarriage:**
  * Description/Symptoms: The undercarriage components, including the frame and suspension parts, may exhibit rust and corrosion, especially in areas with high humidity or exposure to salt.
  * Solution: Apply anti-rust coatings or replace affected parts.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Power Window Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: The power windows may become inoperable, leaving the windows stuck in the open or closed position.
  * Solution: Replace window motor or switch.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
  * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Air Conditioner Failure:**
  * Description/Symptoms: The air conditioner may stop cooling effectively or fail to blow air, causing discomfort in hot weather.
  * Solution: Diagnose and repair refrigerant leak, replace compressor or expansion valve.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Dashboard Rattling:**
  * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard may rattle or make noises when driving over rough roads.
  * Solution: Apply sound dampening materials or secure loose panels.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2011 Chevrolet Captiva Sport has some notable issues that can impact its reliability and driving experience. The excessive oil consumption and timing chain failure are significant concerns for the engine. The transmission issues, such as slipping and differential failure, can also lead to costly repairs. While the suspension and electrical issues are generally less severe, they can still affect the vehicle's performance and comfort. The Captiva Sport's rust problem on the undercarriage is a particular concern for owners in humid or salty environments. However, it's important to note that these issues do not affect all vehicles, and many owners have reported good experiences with the Captiva Sport.
