**Introduction**

The 2015 Chevrolet Captiva Sport is a compact SUV powered by a 3.0L V6 engine that produces 281 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque. It comes standard with a 6-speed automatic transmission. The Captiva Sport is praised for its spacious interior, good fuel economy, and comfortable ride. However, it has received some criticism for its dated exterior design and lack of advanced safety features.

**Engine**

**3.0L V6**

* **Engine Knock**
    * Description/Symptoms: A knocking sound coming from the engine under acceleration or load.
    * Solution: Replace piston rings or engine bearings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from the engine gaskets or seals.
    * Solution: Replace or reseal gaskets or seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission slips when shifting gears or accelerates poorly.
    * Solution: Replace or repair transmission components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Shift Solenoid Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission does not shift properly or gets stuck in gear.
    * Solution: Replace shift solenoids.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Bearing Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: A clunking or rattling noise coming from the front suspension when driving over bumps or turning.
    * Solution: Replace strut mount bearings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Rear Sway Bar Links Worn**
    * Description/Symptoms: A clunking or rattling noise coming from the rear of the vehicle when driving over bumps.
    * Solution: Replace sway bar links.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery drains quickly even when the vehicle is not in use.
    * Solution: Check for parasitic drain on electrical system and repair or replace faulty components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200
* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle loses power or the battery light comes on.
    * Solution: Replace or repair alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000
* **Child Safety Lock Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The child safety lock does not engage or disengage properly.
    * Solution: Replace child safety lock mechanism.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Housing Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Condensation builds up inside the headlight housings, reducing visibility.
    * Solution: Replace or repair headlight housings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Switch Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The sunroof does not open or close properly.
    * Solution: Replace sunroof switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Instrument Cluster Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The instrument cluster does not display correctly or intermittently loses power.
    * Solution: Replace instrument cluster or repair electrical connections.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Summary**

Overall, the 2015 Chevrolet Captiva Sport has a moderate number of issues that range in severity and cost. Some issues, such as engine knock and transmission slipping, can be costly to repair. However, other issues, such as strut mount bearing failure and child safety lock malfunctions, are relatively inexpensive to fix.
