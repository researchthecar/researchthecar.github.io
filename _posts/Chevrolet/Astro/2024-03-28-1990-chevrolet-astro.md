**Introduction:**

The 1990 Chevrolet Astro was a compact van available with two engine options: a 4.3L V6 producing 160 horsepower and 230 lb-ft of torque, and a 5.7L V8 producing 210 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options included a 4-speed automatic and a 5-speed manual. The Astro was praised for its spacious interior and versatility, but criticized for its poor fuel economy and lack of refinement.

**Engine**

**4.3L V6**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** Warping or cracking of the intake manifold gasket, causing coolant and oil leaks, and reduced engine performance.
* Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
* Recalls: NHTSA Campaign ID Number: 96V017000

**5.7L V8**

* **Valve Lifter Failure:** Worn or damaged valve lifters, resulting in noisy engine operation, reduced power, and potential engine damage.
* Solution: Replace the valve lifters.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200
* Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Failure:** Failure of the torque converter clutch, causing slipping, shuddering, and transmission overheating.
* Solution: Replace the torque converter.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,800
* Recalls: NHTSA Campaign ID Number: 93V061000

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Failure:** Worn or damaged clutch disc or pressure plate, causing slipping and difficulty shifting gears.
* Solution: Replace the clutch assembly.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000
* Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Sway Bar Link Failure:** Corroded or weakened sway bar links, resulting in excessive body roll and reduced handling.
* Solution: Replace the sway bar links.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Faulty alternator, causing insufficient charging of the battery and electrical system failure.
* Solution: Replace the alternator.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
* Recalls: NHTSA Campaign ID Number: 90V117000

* **Battery Drain:** Parasitic battery drain due to faulty electronic components or wiring issues.
* Solution: Identify and fix the source of the parasitic drain.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
* Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rusty Rocker Panels:** Corrosion of the rocker panels, leading to structural weakness and unsightly appearance.
* Solution: Repair or replace the rusted rocker panels.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200
* Recalls: None Found

* **Door Hinge Sagging:** Loosening or worn door hinges, causing doors to sag or become difficult to open/close.
* Solution: Adjust or replace the door hinges.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dash Panel Fade:** Fading or cracking of the dashboard panel over time.
* Solution: Replace the dashboard panel.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
* Recalls: None Found

* **Air Conditioning Compressor Failure:** Malfunction of the air conditioning compressor, resulting in insufficient cooling.
* Solution: Replace the air conditioning compressor.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
* Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 1990 Chevrolet Astro is a reliable and versatile vehicle but has some known issues to watch out for. The cost of repairs can range from minor to moderate, but potential issues such as engine or transmission failures can be costly. Regular maintenance and inspections are crucial to prevent or mitigate these problems.
