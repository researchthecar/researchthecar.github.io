**Introduction:**

The 1996 Chevrolet Corvette is a high-performance sports car known for its sleek design, powerful engine, and exceptional handling. It was offered with two engine options: a 5.7L LT1 V8 and a 6.2L LT4 V8. Both engines were mated to a standard 6-speed manual transmission or an optional 4-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**5.7L LT1 V8**

**Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Coolant and oil leaks, rough idling, reduced engine power
Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

**6.2L LT4 V8**

**None Found**

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

**Synchro Gear Wear**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting into specific gears, grinding noises
Solution: Replace synchro gears
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

**Torque Converter Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, shuddering during acceleration, overheating
Solution: Replace torque converter
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Rear Axle Seal Leak**
Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from rear axle
Solution: Replace rear axle seal
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Ignition Coil Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idling, loss of power
Solution: Replace ignition coil
Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Headlight Switch Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Headlights not working, intermittent electrical problems
Solution: Replace headlight switch
Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Power Window Motor Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Windows not operating, grinding noises
Solution: Replace power window motor
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 1996 Chevrolet Corvette is a reliable and enjoyable vehicle with few significant issues. The most common issues include intake manifold gasket failure, synchro gear wear, and torque converter failure. While these issues can be costly to fix, they are relatively rare and typically do not affect the overall performance or longevity of the vehicle.
