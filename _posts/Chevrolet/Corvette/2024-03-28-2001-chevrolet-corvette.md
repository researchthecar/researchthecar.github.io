**Introduction**

The 2001 Chevrolet Corvette is a highly acclaimed sports car known for its stunning design, powerful performance, and impressive handling. It features a front-mounted engine that drives the rear wheels. The available engine options include the LS1 5.7L V8 and the LS6 5.7L V8. Transmission options include a 4L60E 4-speed automatic and a Tremec T56 6-speed manual. With 350 horsepower and 360 lb-ft of torque, the LS1 engine delivers an exhilarating driving experience.

Despite its overall excellence, the Corvette has encountered some issues over the years. While most are minor, some can be more severe and expensive to repair.

**Engine**

**LS1 5.7L V8**

**Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaking from under the intake manifold, overheating, loss of coolant.
Solution: Replace the intake manifold gaskets.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

**LS6 5.7L V8**

**No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**4L60E 4-Speed Automatic**

**Torque Converter Shudder**
Description/Symptoms: Vibration or shuddering during acceleration, especially when the transmission is cold.
Solution: Replace the torque converter.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
Recalls: None Found

**Tremec T56 6-Speed Manual**

**Clutch Slave Cylinder Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, clutch pedal feels spongy or goes to the floor.
Solution: Replace the clutch slave cylinder.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

**ECM Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Engine performance issues, stalling, difficulty starting.
Solution: Replace the Engine Control Module (ECM).
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Rear Spoiler Issue**
Description/Symptoms: Rear spoiler may detach from the vehicle at high speeds.
Solution: Install a reinforcement bracket to secure the spoiler.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
Recalls: 01V156000

**Interior**

**Radio Buttons Sticking**
Description/Symptoms: Radio buttons become unresponsive or difficult to press.
Solution: Clean or replace the radio buttons.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

While the 2001 Chevrolet Corvette is a highly capable and desirable sports car, it is not without its issues. Some of the more common problems include intake manifold gasket failure, torque converter shudder, clutch slave cylinder failure, ECM failure, and rear spoiler issues. Most of these issues can be resolved with relatively minor repairs, but some, such as ECM failure, can be more expensive. Overall, the severity and cost of the issues associated with the 2001 Chevrolet Corvette are generally moderate.
