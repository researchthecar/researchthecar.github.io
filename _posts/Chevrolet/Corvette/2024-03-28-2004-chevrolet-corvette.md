**Introduction:**

The 2004 Chevrolet Corvette is a high-performance sports car. It is available with several engine and transmission combinations, including a 5.7L V8 paired with a 6-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission, and a 6.0L V8 paired with a 6-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission. The Corvette is praised for its powerful engines, agile handling, and sleek design, but criticized for its limited practicality and high maintenance costs.

**Engine**

**5.7L V8**
- **Excessive Oil Consumption:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Vehicles may consume excessive oil, causing low oil levels and engine damage.
    - Solution: Replace piston rings and valve seals.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Head Gasket Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Blown head gaskets can lead to coolant and oil leaks, overheating, and engine damage.
    - Solution: Replace head gaskets and associated components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    - Recalls: None Found

**6.0L V8**
- **Valve Spring Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Broken valve springs can cause lifter noise, engine misfires, and reduced power.
    - Solution: Replace valve springs and lifters.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    - Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 08V414000

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**
- **Clutch Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged clutches can cause difficulty shifting gears, slipping, and loss of power.
    - Solution: Replace clutch assembly.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,500+
    - Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic**
- **Transmission Slippage:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Vehicles may experience slipping or flaring gears, resulting in loss of power and transmission damage.
    - Solution: Overhaul or replace transmission.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $3,500+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **Rear Shock Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Worn or malfunctioning rear shocks can lead to decreased handling and stability, as well as excessive tire wear.
    - Solution: Replace rear shock absorbers.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Battery Drain:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Vehicles may experience a rapid battery drain, leaving them unable to start.
    - Solution: Diagnose and replace faulty components, such as the alternator, battery, or wiring.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Rear End Leaks:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Vehicles may experience leaks from the rear differential, causing fluid loss and damage to components.
    - Solution: Repair or replace rear differential seals.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Instrument Panel Malfunctions:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Various issues with the instrument panel, including faulty gauges, warning lights, and LCD display.
    - Solution: Replace or repair instrument panel components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2004 Chevrolet Corvette is a powerful and agile sports car, but it is not without its issues. The engine, transmission, and suspension can all experience problems that can be costly to repair. Additionally, electrical and interior components can also malfunction, leading to further inconveniences and expenses. Overall, the severity and frequency of the issues can vary from vehicle to vehicle, but potential owners should be aware of these potential concerns before purchasing.
