# 2017 Chevrolet Corvette

### Overview
The 2017 Chevrolet Corvette, a performance icon, generally exhibits strong reliability, particularly given its high-performance nature. Owners often praise its exhilarating driving dynamics and sophisticated engineering. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the Corvette's primary concerns tend to be concentrated in areas related to its powerful powertrain and advanced electronic systems. Routine maintenance is crucial for preserving its performance, but widespread, recurring mechanical failures are uncommon for this model year. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond standard wear and tear, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and prospective buyers.

---


## Engine

### 6.2L V8 LT1
**Oil Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil leaks around the oil cooler lines, potential for oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking oil cooler lines or fittings.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Active Fuel Management (AFM) Lifter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Engine misfires, rough idle, ticking noises from the engine, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty lifters, often requiring cylinder deactivation system disabling.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000


## Transmission

### 7-Speed Manual Tremec TR-6070
**Clutch Actuator Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty shifting gears, stiff clutch pedal, potential for clutch not disengaging.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the clutch master or slave cylinder.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


### 8-Speed Automatic 8L90
**Torque Converter Shudder**
- **Symptoms:** Vibration or shuddering during acceleration, especially at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Software update for transmission control module, or torque converter replacement.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2,000


## Suspension

### Magnetic Ride Control (Z51 Performance Package)
**Sensor Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Stiff ride, suspension warning light illuminated, inconsistent damping.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty suspension sensors or control module.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,200

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, system freezes or reboots randomly.
- **Solution:** Software updates, or replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500


### Battery Drain
**Parasitic Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dies unexpectedly, difficulty starting the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of faulty modules or circuits causing drain.
- **Cost:** $200 - $700

**Accelerated Wear on Seat Bolsters**

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Chevrolet Corvette is a highly capable sports car with a generally good reliability record. The most frequently cited issues revolve around the powerful 6.2L V8 engine's Active Fuel Management system and potential leaks from the oil cooler. Transmission issues, particularly torque converter shudder in the automatic and clutch actuator problems in the manual, are also noted. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment system glitches and parasitic battery drain, can occur. While interior wear on seat bolsters is a common observation for performance seats, major structural or widespread component failures are rare. With diligent maintenance and addressing specific known issues, the 2017 Corvette can provide a thrilling and dependable ownership experience.
