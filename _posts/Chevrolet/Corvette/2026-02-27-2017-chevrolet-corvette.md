# 2017 Chevrolet Corvette Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Chevrolet Corvette delivers world-class performance and striking design, making it an enticing choice for sports car enthusiasts. However, prospective buyers should carefully evaluate known mechanical and structural pain points before purchasing, particularly concerning wheel durability and steering assist functionality.

---

## Engine

### 6.2L V8 LT1

The standard naturally aspirated 6.2L V8 LT1 engine produces 455 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, providing exhilarating acceleration and strong overall reliability for everyday driving and track use.

### 6.2L Supercharged V8 LT4

The high-performance supercharged 6.2L V8 LT4 engine powers the Z06 trim, churning out a massive 650 horsepower and 650 lb-ft of torque with robust engineering suited for high-speed capability.

### Fuel/Propulsion System

Issues with the fuel system are relatively isolated but can involve fuel leaks around the tank filler neck or fuel pump module, posing potential fire or odor risks.

**Fuel Pump Flange Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Fuel odors around the vehicle and noticeable loss of fuel pooling on garage floors due to leaks at the primary fuel pump module flange.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the leaking fuel pump module or associated seals.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Manual Transmission

The 7-speed manual transmission features Active Rev Matching and offers an engaging, direct-connection driving experience with generally solid long-term mechanical durability.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed paddle-shift automatic transmission provides quick gear changes, though some owners report harsh shifting characteristics under specific driving conditions.

## Steering

### Wheels & Rims

Factory wheel and rim integrity is a heavily reported concern for this model year, with numerous owners experiencing cracking or bending on rear wheels even during standard road use.

**Factory Wheel Cracking and Bending**
- **Symptoms:** Slow tire pressure leaks, sudden flat tires, visible cracks on the inner barrel of the wheel, or steering vibrations caused by bent rims under normal driving conditions.
- **Solution:** Have the wheels thoroughly inspected for hairline fractures and replace damaged factory rims with stronger aftermarket or updated replacement wheels.

### Steering System

Electronic power steering assist failures can occur unexpectedly, requiring drivers to exercise caution if power assistance cuts out while driving.

**Power Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of electronic power steering assist accompanied by a dashboard warning message, making the steering wheel significantly heavier to turn.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the electronic power steering module and replace faulty components or steering racks as required.

## Electrical

### Infotainment & Display Screen

Electrical complaints center primarily around intermittent failures of the central infotainment and navigation display screen, which impacts visibility when reversing.

**Infotainment Screen Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The central infotainment touchscreen goes completely black or blank without warning, causing a loss of audio, radio controls, and the crucial rearview camera display.
- **Solution:** Inspect infotainment software, fuses, or replace the display unit module if internal hardware fails.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V086000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Corvette&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Loss of power steering assist would require a higher steering effort, especially at lower speeds, which may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the power steering gear assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V651000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Corvette&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test. During this test, deployment of the frontal air bags and the seat belt pretensioners would not occur in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V437000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Corvette&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** While being previously remedied for recall 16V-651, the affected vehicles may not have received the complete software update necessary to remedy the recall condition. Without the update, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V175000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Corvette&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A loss of power steering assist can require greater steering effort, especially at low speeds, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the power steering gear assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Chevrolet Corvette is an exceptional performance car that offers immense thrills for the money, but buyers should conduct a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to the condition of the factory wheels for hidden inner-barrel cracks, verify that the electronic power steering operates smoothly, and check for any fuel odors or infotainment glitches.
