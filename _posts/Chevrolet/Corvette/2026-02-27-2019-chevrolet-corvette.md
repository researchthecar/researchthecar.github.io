# 2019 Chevrolet Corvette Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Chevrolet Corvette represents the final model year of the iconic front-engine C7 generation, offering world-class performance and striking design. While its powertrain options deliver exhilarating acceleration and track-ready capability, prospective buyers should review common owner-reported issues regarding wheel durability and transmission behavior.

---

## Engine

### 6.2L LT1 V8

A naturally aspirated 6.2-liter V8 engine producing approximately 455 to 460 horsepower, serving as the standard power plant for Stingray models. It provides strong low-end torque and traditional American V8 performance with generally solid mechanical reliability.

### 6.2L LT4 Supercharged V8

A supercharged 6.2-liter V8 engine generating 650 horsepower, equipped on the Z06 model for extreme track and street performance. Buyers should ensure proper cooling maintenance on heavily tracked examples.

### 6.2L LT5 Supercharged V8

A massive supercharged 6.2-liter V8 pushing 755 horsepower, exclusive to the high-performance ZR1 flagship. This powerhouse delivers incredible top-end speed but requires meticulous inspection of its complex cooling and fuel systems.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Manual Transmission

A traditional seven-speed manual transmission featuring Active Rev Matching for seamless downshifts. It offers an engaging driver experience with relatively few widespread mechanical complaints reported by owners.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An eight-speed paddle-shifted automatic transmission available across the lineup. While it offers quick gear changes, certain models can develop torque converter shudder issues requiring a fluid exchange.

**Transmission Shudder**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience an abnormal shaking, shuddering, or vibration sensation during acceleration, often mimicking driving over a rough washboard surface.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified service center perform a complete transmission fluid flush using the updated specification fluid to clear out contaminants and eliminate torque converter shudder.

## Suspension

### Wheels

The factory wheel assemblies on certain performance trims are prone to cracking or bending under normal road impacts, leading to persistent air pressure loss.

**Cracked or Bent Rims**
- **Symptoms:** Owners notice slow air leaks, low tire pressure warning lights, or visible structural fractures and bends on the inner barrels of the wheels, particularly on rear rims.
- **Solution:** Inspect all wheels thoroughly for inner barrel cracks or structural damage, and replace damaged factory rims with stronger aftermarket alternatives if necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V360000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Corvette&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Hard braking or acceleration may cause the sensing diagnostic module (SDM) to enter a fault state. As a result, the SDM will not provide crash sensing or deploy the necessary air bags in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the SDM with updated software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Chevrolet Corvette is a thrilling high-performance sports car, but buyers should carefully inspect vehicle history and perform a pre-purchase inspection. Particular attention should be paid to the wheels for structural cracks and to the automatic transmission for any signs of shuddering during test drives.
