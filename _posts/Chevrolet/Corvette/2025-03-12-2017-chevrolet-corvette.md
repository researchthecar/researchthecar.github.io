Here's a breakdown of common issues reported for the 2017 Chevrolet Corvette, formatted for your website, separated by category and configuration where applicable. The 2017 Chevrolet Corvette is a popular sports car known for its powerful engines, sharp handling, and stylish design. It offers a thrilling driving experience that many enthusiasts appreciate. However, some common complaints include issues with the infotainment system, specifically the touchscreen, and the occasional electrical glitch. The 2017 Corvette came with three engine options: a 6.2L LT1 V8 (455 hp, 460 lb-ft of torque), a supercharged 6.2L LT4 V8 (650 hp, 650 lb-ft of torque) for the Z06 model, and a 6.2L LT1 V8 (460 hp, 465 lb-ft of torque) for the Grand Sport model. Transmission options included a 7-speed manual and an 8-speed automatic.

**Engine**

**6.2L LT1 V8**

*   **Active Fuel Management (AFM) Lifter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Ticking or knocking noise from the engine, often misfires, and potential engine damage. Caused by failure of the AFM lifters, which can collapse and damage the camshaft.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the failed lifters and potentially the camshaft. Some owners opt to delete the AFM system entirely.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+ depending on the extent of the damage and the repair shop.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Crankshaft Pulley Wobble/Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Wobbling crankshaft pulley that can lead to belt misalignment and failure, potentially causing damage to other engine components.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the crankshaft pulley.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6.2L LT4 Supercharged V8**

*   **Supercharger Intercooler Brick Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaking into the supercharger, causing reduced performance, misfires, and potential engine damage. Due to cracks in the intercooler bricks within the supercharger.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the supercharger intercooler brick(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (8L90)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Harsh Engagement**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hesitation, hard shifts, and clunking noises, especially during low-speed driving. Often reported as a general lack of smoothness in shifting.
    *   Solution: Some have had success with transmission fluid flushes using an updated fluid formulation specifically designed to address this issue. More severe cases may require valve body replacement or transmission rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Fluid flush: $300 - $500, Valve body replacement: $1,500 - $3,000, Transmission rebuild: $3,000 - $6,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Magnetic Ride Control (MRC) Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Fluid leaks from the Magnetic Ride Control shocks, leading to diminished suspension performance and a rougher ride.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking Magnetic Ride Control shocks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+ per shock.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen freezes, becomes unresponsive, or crashes completely.  This can affect navigation, audio, and climate control functions.
    *   Solution: Software updates sometimes resolve the issue. In other cases, the infotainment unit needs to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software update: $100 - $200, Infotainment unit replacement: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The battery drains prematurely, especially if the car sits for extended periods.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of parasitic drain and repair of the affected circuit or component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the complexity of the drain.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Condensation buildup inside the headlight housings.
    * Solution: Replacing the headlight housing.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Delamination**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Separation or bubbling of the dashboard material, particularly around the instrument cluster or vents. This is often exacerbated by heat and sunlight.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the dashboard.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Chevrolet Corvette, while a desirable sports car, can suffer from several common issues. Engine problems, particularly with AFM lifters and supercharger intercoolers, can be costly. Transmission issues with the 8-speed automatic are also a significant concern. Electrical glitches and interior delamination can detract from the ownership experience. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to major repairs costing several thousand dollars. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems and carefully inspect any used Corvette before purchase.
