The 2004 Chevrolet Express 2500 is a full-size van known for its durability, practicality, and versatility. It's popular among tradespeople, large families, and those needing a workhorse vehicle. Engine options include a 4.3L Vortec V6 (200 hp and 260 lb-ft of torque), a 5.0L Vortec V8 (220 hp and 290 lb-ft of torque), a 5.7L Vortec V8 (255 hp and 330 lb-ft of torque), and a 6.0L Vortec V8 (300 hp and 360 lb-ft of torque). Transmission options are primarily the 4L60E and 4L80E 4-speed automatic transmissions, depending on the engine. Owners often appreciate its spaciousness, towing capacity, and robust build. Some common dislikes include its fuel economy, ride quality, and dated interior.

**Engine**

**4.3L Vortec V6**

*   **Intake Manifold Gasket Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks externally from the intake manifold gasket area, leading to overheating and potential engine damage.
    *   Solution: Replace the intake manifold gaskets with updated versions.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4L60E 4-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic or harsh shifting, slipping between gears, or failure to shift at all. This is often due to wear and tear on internal components and can be exacerbated by high mileage or heavy use.
    *   Solution: Transmission rebuild or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1800 - $3500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**4L80E 4-Speed Automatic**

*No Issues Found*

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Ball Joints**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or popping noises from the front suspension, uneven tire wear, and loose steering.
    *   Solution: Replace worn ball joints.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per side
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Worn Leaf Spring Bushings**
    * Description/Symptoms: Squeaking from the rear suspension.
    * Solution: Replace the bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Fuel Gauge Inaccuracy**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Fuel gauge reading inaccurately or not at all, leading to potential fuel starvation.
    *   Solution: Replace the fuel level sending unit in the fuel tank.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Blower Motor Resistor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Blower motor only working on certain speeds or not at all.
    *   Solution: Replace the blower motor resistor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Hinge Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sagging doors, difficulty closing doors, and clicking or creaking noises when opening or closing doors.
    *   Solution: Replace the door hinge pins and bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 per door
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Cracked Dashboard**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracking of the dashboard, especially in sun-exposed areas.
    *   Solution: Dashboard replacement or overlay.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2004 Chevrolet Express 2500 presents a mix of potential issues, ranging from relatively minor electrical problems to more significant drivetrain concerns. While some engines appear more reliable, transmission issues, particularly with the 4L60E, can be costly. Suspension wear is typical for a vehicle of this age and usage, requiring attention to ball joints and potentially leaf spring bushings. Interior issues such as a cracked dashboard are common. Overall, prospective buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle, particularly the transmission, and budget for potential repairs.
