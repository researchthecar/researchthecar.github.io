The 2007 Chevrolet Express 2500 is a full-size van known for its utility and spaciousness, making it a popular choice for commercial use and large families. For the 2007 model year, it offered several engine options including a 4.3L V6 (around 195 hp and 260 lb-ft of torque), a 4.8L V8 (around 295 hp and 305 lb-ft of torque), a 5.3L V8 (around 301 hp and 325 lb-ft of torque), and a 6.0L V8 (around 300 hp and 360 lb-ft of torque). Transmission options primarily included a 4-speed automatic. Owners often appreciate the van's robust build and cargo capacity, but common complaints revolve around fuel economy and certain reliability issues, especially with age and high mileage.

**Engine**

**4.3L V6**

*   **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leak, engine overheating, milky oil.
    *   Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**4.8L V8**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, blue smoke from exhaust, low oil level warning.
    *   Solution: Often requires replacing valve stem seals or piston rings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Lifter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Ticking noise from the engine, especially when cold, potential loss of power.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty lifters.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**5.3L V8**

*   **Active Fuel Management (AFM) Lifter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough running engine, check engine light with codes related to cylinder deactivation.
    *   Solution: Replace AFM lifters, potentially disabling the AFM system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, blue smoke from exhaust, low oil level warning.
    *   Solution: Often requires replacing valve stem seals or piston rings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6.0L V8**

*   **Head Gasket Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Overheating, coolant loss, white smoke from exhaust, coolant mixing with oil.
    *   Solution: Replace head gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $4000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic (4L60E)**

*   **Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Transmission slips between gears, delayed engagement, RPM flares without acceleration.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the transmission. Could involve valve body repair or replacement, clutch pack replacement, or full rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or jerky shifts between gears.
    *   Solution: Check and replace transmission fluid, valve body repair/replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Leaf Spring Sagging/Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rear of the van sags, reduced load capacity, potential cracking of leaf springs.
    *   Solution: Replace leaf springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Ball Joint Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or popping noises from the front suspension, loose steering.
    *   Solution: Replace ball joints.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Fuel Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling, difficulty starting, no fuel pressure.
    *   Solution: Replace fuel pump.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power door locks not working, intermittent operation.
    *   Solution: Replace door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust on Rocker Panels and Wheel Arches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust formation on lower body panels.
    *   Solution: Rust repair and painting or replacement of rusted panels.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2000+ (depending on severity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Blower motor stops working, no air from vents.
    *   Solution: Replace blower motor or blower motor resistor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Seat Frame Cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks forming in the driver and passenger seat frames
    * Solution: Remove the seat from the vehicle and weld new plates into the cracked sections of the frame. After welding, the frame should be repainted to prevent corrosion
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2007 Chevrolet Express 2500, while a dependable workhorse, can be prone to certain issues as it ages. Engine problems like oil consumption and lifter failure, particularly in the V8 engines, are common and can lead to significant repair costs. Transmission issues such as slipping and harsh shifting also contribute to potential expenses. Suspension components like leaf springs and ball joints can wear out, affecting ride quality and safety. Electrical problems with fuel pumps and door lock actuators are also noted. Rust is a concern on the exterior, especially in areas prone to road salt. Overall, potential buyers should carefully inspect these areas and be prepared for potential maintenance costs associated with an older vehicle.
