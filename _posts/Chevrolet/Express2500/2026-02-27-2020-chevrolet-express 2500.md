### Overview
The 2020 Chevrolet Express 2500 is a workhorse van known for its utility and robust construction. Owners often praise its straightforward mechanicals and durability, making it a popular choice for commercial fleets and tradespeople. ## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Intermittent Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard lights flickering, radio cutting out, or engine stalling unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of wiring harness issues, faulty grounds, or alternator problems. May require harness repair or replacement of electrical components.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Doors
**Door Latch Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Doors failing to open or close properly, or not latching securely.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the door latch mechanism. Lubrication may be a temporary fix.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500


## Interior

### HVAC System
**Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air or reduced airflow from vents, regardless of fan speed setting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor or blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $450

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 Chevrolet Express 2500 demonstrates solid reliability, particularly in its powertrain. The V6 and V8 engines, along with the 6-speed automatic transmissions, are generally robust and rarely present widespread issues. The primary areas where owners might encounter problems are in the electrical system, specifically intermittent power issues, and exterior components like door latches. The interior HVAC system can also experience blower motor failures. These issues are not endemic to all vehicles but are documented concerns that may require attention. Overall, the Express 2500 remains a dependable choice for demanding applications, with most potential problems being repairable without excessive cost.
