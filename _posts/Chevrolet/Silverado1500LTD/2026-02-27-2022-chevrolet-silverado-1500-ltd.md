# 2022 Chevrolet Silverado 1500 LTD Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2022 Chevrolet Silverado 1500 LTD, buyers will find a capable full-size pickup truck designed to handle everyday towing, hauling, and commuting duties. While the truck offers strong engine options and modern technology, owners have reported several recurring mechanical and electrical concerns. Prospective buyers should inspect the vehicle thoroughly, paying close attention to powertrain performance and electrical accessories before finalizing a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.7L Turbo I4

A 2.7-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing around 310 horsepower and 430 lb-ft of torque, offering strong low-end responsiveness for daily driving and light utility tasks.

### 5.3L V8

A traditional 5.3-liter V8 engine delivering approximately 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, known for balanced capability across work and travel needs.

### 6.2L V8

A high-output 6.2-liter V8 engine generating 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, providing robust towing capacity but requiring careful mechanical vetting.

**Sudden Engine Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a sudden and complete loss of propulsion or mechanical failure at highway speeds without prior warning lights or abnormal sounds.
- **Solution:** Requires professional inspection and potential engine replacement or internal component repair.

### 3.0L Turbo-Diesel I6

A 3.0-liter Duramax turbocharged inline-six diesel engine producing around 277 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, prized by buyers for strong towing torque and highway fuel efficiency.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An eight-speed automatic transmission paired with select gas engines, delivering smooth daily gear selection when functioning properly.

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

A ten-speed automatic transmission designed to optimize power delivery and fuel economy across heavy loads, though select units exhibit internal sensor and shifting irregularities.

**Output Shaft Speed Sensor and Shifting Faults**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission may enter limp mode, shift violently, or experience sudden gear loss and hesitation during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning for trouble codes followed by replacing faulty internal speed sensors, wiring harness components, or valve body modules.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brake System

The hydraulic service brakes provide stopping power, though some owners report issues with brake warning notifications and hardware wear.

**Brake Pedal Firmness and Warning Messages**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers encounter dashboard brake monitor alerts, increased pedal effort required to stop the vehicle, and broken brake pad retaining clips.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and hardware clips, replacing damaged components and bleeding the hydraulic system as necessary.

## Exterior

### Tailgate Latches and Switches

**Unexpected Tailgate Opening**
- **Symptoms:** Water intrusion into the tailgate switch can cause the tailgate to unlatch and drop open independently while driving or parked, potentially leading to a dead battery.
- **Solution:** Inspect the tailgate switch for moisture damage and replace the switch assembly or wiring harness.
