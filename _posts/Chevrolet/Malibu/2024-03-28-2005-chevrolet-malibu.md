**2005 Chevrolet Malibu**

The 2005 Chevrolet Malibu is a midsize sedan offered in LS, LT, and LTZ trim levels. It came with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 2.2-liter Ecotec I4 engine with 144 horsepower and 155 lb-ft of torque, a 3.5-liter V6 engine with 201 horsepower and 222 lb-ft of torque, a 3.9-liter V6 engine with 240 horsepower and 250 lb-ft of torque, a 4-speed automatic transmission, and a 5-speed manual transmission. The Malibu was praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and good fuel economy, but it was criticized for its lack of performance and bland styling.

**Engine**

**2.2-liter Ecotec I4**
- **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** The intake manifold gasket may fail, causing coolant and oil leaks. This can lead to overheating, engine damage, and reduced performance. The gasket must be replaced. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $400 - $600.
- **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** The timing chain tensioner may fail, causing the timing chain to skip or break. This can lead to catastrophic engine damage. The timing chain and tensioner must be replaced. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $1,000 - $2,000.

**3.5-liter V6**
- **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** The intake manifold gasket may fail, causing coolant and oil leaks. This can lead to overheating, engine damage, and reduced performance. The gasket must be replaced. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $400 - $600.
- **Valve Cover Gasket Leaks:** The valve cover gaskets may fail, causing oil leaks. This can lead to engine damage and reduced performance. The gaskets must be replaced. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $200 - $300.

**3.9-liter V6**
- **Oil Pressure Sensor Failure:** The oil pressure sensor may fail, causing a false reading on the oil pressure gauge. This can lead to engine damage if the oil pressure is not monitored. The sensor must be replaced. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $100 - $200.

**Transmission**

**4-speed Automatic**
- **Transmission Slipping:** The transmission may slip or shudder when shifting gears. This can lead to reduced performance and transmission failure. The transmission may need to be repaired or replaced. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $1,500 - $3,000.

**5-speed Manual**
- **Clutch Failure:** The clutch may fail, making it difficult or impossible to shift gears. The clutch must be replaced. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $500 - $800.

**Suspension**

- **Strut Mount Failure:** The strut mounts may fail, causing the struts to make noise and vibrate. This can lead to reduced handling and stability. The strut mounts must be replaced. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $300 - $500.
- **Sway Bar Link Failure:** The sway bar links may fail, causing the sway bar to make noise and vibrate. This can lead to reduced handling and stability. The sway bar links must be replaced. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $200 - $400.

**Electrical**

- **Battery Drain:** The battery may drain prematurely, even when the car is not running. This can be caused by a faulty alternator, starter, or other electrical component. The faulty component must be repaired or replaced. The estimated cost to fix this issue varies depending on the component that is causing the issue.
- **Headlight Failure:** The headlights may fail to turn on or may flicker. This can be caused by a faulty headlight switch, wiring, or ballast. The faulty component must be repaired or replaced. The estimated cost to fix this issue varies depending on the component that is causing the issue.

**Exterior**

- **Door Handle Failure:** The door handles may fail, making it difficult or impossible to open the doors. The door handles must be replaced. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $100 - $200.

**Interior**

- **Power Window Failure:** The power windows may fail to operate. This can be caused by a faulty switch, motor, or wiring. The faulty component must be repaired or replaced. The estimated cost to fix this issue varies depending on the component that is causing the issue.

**Summary**

The 2005 Chevrolet Malibu is a reliable vehicle, but it is not without its issues. The most common problems include intake manifold gasket failure, timing chain tensioner failure, transmission slipping, and clutch failure. These issues can be costly to fix, but they are not typically major safety concerns. Overall, the Malibu is a good value for its price and offers a comfortable and spacious ride.
