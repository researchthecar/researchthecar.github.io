# 2018 Chevrolet Malibu Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Chevrolet Malibu is a stylish and generally comfortable midsize sedan, but used car buyers should pay careful attention to a few prominent mechanical and electrical quirks. The most widely reported issue across owner complaints involves the gear shifter failing to register when the car is placed in park, leading to frustrating battery drain and ignition shut-off troubles. Additionally, certain powertrain configurations have experienced intermittent engine performance and braking concerns.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbo 4-Cylinder

This base turbocharged 4-cylinder engine produces around 160 horsepower and provides adequate power for daily commuting with competitive fuel economy. Buyers should inspect for any history of rough idling or warning lights.

### 2.0L Turbo 4-Cylinder

The larger 2.0L turbo engine delivers a peppier 250 horsepower, paired typically with a 9-speed automatic transmission. It offers stronger acceleration performance for highway driving.

### 1.8L Hybrid 4-Cylinder

The hybrid option pairs a 1.8L 4-cylinder engine with an electric drive unit to maximize fuel efficiency. It shares many of the general electrical quirks found across the standard Malibu lineup.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The standard 6-speed automatic transmission shifts smoothly under normal driving conditions, though some owners have reported torque converter wear or shifting hesitation over higher mileages.

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Mated primarily to the 2.0L turbo engine, this 9-speed unit offers refined gear selection. Maintenance history is key to ensuring long-term longevity.

## Brakes

### Vacuum Pump and Booster

The brake hydraulic system generally performs well, but isolated vacuum pump failures can severely compromise brake assist.

**Brake Pedal Stiffness and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal becomes extremely stiff and hard to depress, leading to significantly reduced stopping power and potential near-misses.
- **Solution:** Inspect the vacuum pump and brake booster assembly; replace damaged components if internal mechanical failure has occurred.

## Steering

### Power Steering Assist

Steering assist issues are less common but represent a critical safety concern when electronic faults occur while driving.

**Power Steering Assist Fault**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel jerks unexpectedly or displays a 'Power Steering Assist Fault' warning message, making the vehicle difficult to control at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Have a certified mechanic run diagnostic trouble codes on the electronic power steering module and replace faulty sensors or wiring harnesses.

## Electrical

### Shifter Microswitch and Sensor

Electrical sensors within the center console and ignition logic frequently trigger false warnings regarding gear selection.

**Shift to Park Error Message**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle fails to recognize when the shifter is placed in park, displaying a persistent 'Shift to Park' warning message on the dashboard and preventing the car from turning off completely or draining the battery.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty shifter assembly or internal microswitch component responsible for gear detection.
- **Cost:** $1087

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V400000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Malibu&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As a result, the PPS may not properly identify an adult passenger from a child passenger in the front passenger seat, potentially causing the air bag to not deploy when it should, or causing the air bag to deploy when it shouldn't. In the event of a crash, improper air bag deployment can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front passenger PPS seat service kit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V576000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Malibu&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A reduction of braking performance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will bleed the vehicle's brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V358000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Malibu&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A damaged fuel line can create a fuel leak, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the high pressure fuel pump, and high pressure fuel pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V642000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Malibu&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** An error in the Engine Control Module (ECM) software may result in the fuel injectors being disabled. Disabled fuel injectors would prevent the engine from starting and may cause a stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the ECM software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V668000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Malibu&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Missing bolts on the start-stop accumulator endcap could result in a transmission oil leak and may progress to a loss of propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash. A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the start-stop transmission accumulator and replace it if any bolts are missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V649000: Seat Belts:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Malibu&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** An improperly secured seat belt retractor may not function properly in a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten the rear seat belt retractors, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Chevrolet Malibu is an appealing used sedan choice thanks to its comfortable ride, modern tech features, and efficient engine lineup. However, buyers should thoroughly test the gear shifter to ensure it doesn't suffer from the widespread 'Shift to Park' defect, which can drain batteries and frustrate daily use. A pre-purchase inspection focusing on the electronics, brakes, and steering components will help ensure a reliable ownership experience.
