# 2022 Chevrolet Malibu Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a 2022 Chevrolet Malibu, buyers will find a stylish and efficient midsize sedan that generally offers a comfortable ride. However, reviewing owner-submitted complaints highlights several persistent areas of concern—most notably a recurring glitch with the gear shifter failing to register the park position. Prospective owners should carefully inspect these known trouble spots during a test drive to ensure everything is in proper working order.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged I4

The standard 1.5-liter turbocharged inline-four engine produces approximately 163 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque, paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). It delivers solid fuel economy for daily commuting, though drivers should watch for occasional power delivery hiccups.

**Reduced Engine Power**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle unpredictably enters a reduced power state, causing sudden and dangerous drops in vehicle speed while driving in traffic.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the engine management system, throttle body, or turbocharger components; software updates or hardware replacement may be required.

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

The available 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine generates 250 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, mated to a 9-speed automatic transmission. This option offers much livelier acceleration and passing power for highway driving.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) / 9-Speed Automatic

The transmission options—a CVT on 1.5L models and a 9-speed automatic on 2.0L models—generally transfer power smoothly, though the mechanical linkage or sensor communicating the 'park' position is a known failure point.

**Shift to Park Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle fails to recognize when the gear selector is placed in the park position, preventing the driver from fully turning off the vehicle and causing battery drain or dashboard chimes.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the shifter assembly microswitch or related shifter components.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

The electric power steering system provides light, easy maneuverability at low speeds, though intermittent assist warnings have been reported by some owners.

**Reduced Power Steering Assist**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel jerks coming from a stop, accompanied by a dashboard warning message stating that power steering assist is reduced.
- **Solution:** Check the electronic power steering connections and sensor inputs; modules or steering gear components may need replacement.

## Exterior

### Body Control Module and Telematics

The electrical architecture governs cabin tech, safety systems, and core vehicle modules, with occasional reports of module failure leading to erratic warning lights.

**Multiple Warning Lights and Electrical Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Faulty body control modules can cause dashboard gauges to flutter, warning lights for ABS and power steering to illuminate, and the vehicle to unexpectedly shut down.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician diagnose the body control module (BCM) and update or replace faulty electronic units.

### Rear Glass and Body Seals

Exterior body seals and glass installations generally keep cabin weather-tight, but seal defects can occasionally lead to water intrusion.

**Rear Window Water Leak**
- **Symptoms:** During inclement weather, water leaks through the rear glass area, resulting in visible stains on the vehicle's interior ceiling.
- **Solution:** Inspect and reseal or replace the rear windshield weatherstripping.

## Safety

### Seatbelt Tensioners and Restraints

Safety restraint systems are critical for occupant protection, and recurring sensor or tensioner faults require prompt dealer attention.

**Seatbelt Tensioner Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The safety restraint system repeatedly fails and displays warning lights indicating a fault with the seatbelt tensioner.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seatbelt assembly and wiring harness; faulty tensioners or sensors must be replaced.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V923000: Structure:Frame And Members](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Malibu&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The front impact bar, a structural portion of the vehicle frame, may not be properly welded to the front frame rail. An improperly welded front impact bar may result in the front crash sensors not performing correctly, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the right-hand and left-hand side of the vehicle's motor rail for an incomplete weld. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Chevrolet Malibu is a comfortable daily driver with appealing styling, but used-car buyers should thoroughly check for the notorious 'Shift to Park' glitch during a test drive. Verifying that all electrical systems, steering components, and safety restraints operate without warning lights will help ensure a dependable ownership experience.
