# 2017 Chevrolet Malibu Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Chevrolet Malibu offers a sleek design and comfortable ride, making it a popular choice in the midsize sedan market. However, prospective buyers should review recurring owner complaints, particularly concerning electrical issues, shifter mechanisms, and potential braking issues. While many drivers enjoy its fuel efficiency and interior tech, paying close attention to its mechanical history will help ensure a dependable purchase.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbo 4-Cylinder

The 1.5-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 160 horsepower and is paired with a stop-start system. It delivers decent fuel economy for daily commuting, though some owners report reliability hiccups related to stalling and sensors.

**Engine Stalling and Throttle Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may experience unexpected engine stalling, jerking while driving, or illuminated warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the throttle body position sensor or associated wiring and replace components as necessary.

### 2.0L Turbo 4-Cylinder

The larger 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates around 250 horsepower, offering considerably stronger acceleration and highway passing power. It is generally regarded as robust, though proper maintenance is critical.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission comes paired with the base engine option. It offers smooth everyday shifting, though proper fluid maintenance is recommended to prevent premature wear.

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission is paired with higher-trim engines. While it enhances fuel efficiency and highway cruising, a subset of drivers experience engagement delays.

**Intermittent Engagement Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission may intermittently fail to engage forward or reverse gears from a stop, requiring a vehicle restart.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission control module and internal components; software updates or valve body service may be required.

## Brakes

### Vacuum Pump and Brake Booster

The braking system generally provides confident stopping power under normal conditions, but mechanical vacuum pump issues can occasionally lead to a hard pedal and reduced assist.

**Hard Brake Pedal and Brake Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal suddenly becomes extremely stiff and difficult to depress, severely reducing braking power.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the engine-driven vacuum pump and brake booster.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

The electric power steering system provides light, maneuverable handling at low speeds, though intermittent assist failures have been reported by some owners.

**Steering Assist Loss**
- **Symptoms:** A 'Steering Assist Reduced' message appears on the display, and the steering wheel becomes heavy or difficult to turn.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electric power steering motor, electronic control modules, and wiring connections for faults.

## Electrical

### Shifter Microswitch and Infotainment

Electrical complaints on this model frequently center around shifter sensors failing to register the correct gear, which can prevent proper vehicle shutdown and lead to battery drains.

**Shift to Park Warning and Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard displays a 'Shift to Park' message even when the vehicle is in park, preventing complete ignition shutdown and draining the battery.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty shifter microswitch assembly or internal metal activation arm.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V400000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Malibu&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As a result, the PPS may not properly identify an adult passenger from a child passenger in the front passenger seat, potentially causing the air bag to not deploy when it should, or causing the air bag to deploy when it shouldn't. In the event of a crash, improper air bag deployment can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front passenger PPS seat service kit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V358000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Malibu&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A damaged fuel line can create a fuel leak, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the high pressure fuel pump, and high pressure fuel pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V870000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Malibu&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Insufficient welds could cause the inflator to separate and propel air bag debris into the cabin during a crash. Also, the inflator could fail to inflate during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rear side air bag modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V649000: Seat Belts:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Malibu&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** An improperly secured seat belt retractor may not function properly in a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten the rear seat belt retractors, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Chevrolet Malibu is an appealing used sedan with comfortable road manners and modern features, but buyers should conduct a thorough inspection before purchasing. Pay close attention to the shifter mechanism to check for any 'Shift to Park' warnings, and verify that the brakes and steering operate smoothly without warning lights. Selecting a well-maintained model with documented service history will greatly minimize the risk of unexpected repair bills.
