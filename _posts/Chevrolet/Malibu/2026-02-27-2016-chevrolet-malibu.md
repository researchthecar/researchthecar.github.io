# 2016 Chevrolet Malibu Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2016 Chevrolet Malibu, buyers will find a modern midsize sedan that offers comfortable ride quality and efficient powertrain choices. However, owners have reported several recurring mechanical and electrical concerns that prospective buyers should inspect closely before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 1.5-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 160 horsepower paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission, offering standard efficiency for daily commuting.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The larger 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder delivers around 250 horsepower mated to an 8-speed automatic transmission, providing punchy acceleration for highway driving.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A conventional 6-speed automatic transmission paired with base engines, generally providing smooth shifting under normal operation.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission utilized with higher-output engine options to maximize highway efficiency.

## Brakes

### Vacuum Pump and Brakes

Engine-driven vacuum pump issues can compromise braking assistance and lead to heavy pedal feel.

**Brake Vacuum Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal becomes exceptionally hard and difficult to depress, severely reducing stopping capability.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed mechanical vacuum pump and inspect the internal camshaft components for potential debris damage.

### Electronic Brake Control Module

Brake module issues can create premature pad wear, excessive heat buildup, and handling instability.

**EBCM Drag and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Brakes drag unexpectedly, causing overheating, reduced stopping efficiency, and unstable vehicle handling.
- **Solution:** Inspect the Electronic Brake Control Module and replace faulty components or modules as needed.

## Electrical

### Shifter Microswitch and Park Sensor

Electrical sensors within the shifter assembly frequently fail to recognize the park position, leading to accessory drain and warning alerts.

**Shift to Park Warning Message**
- **Symptoms:** A persistent 'Shift to Park' message displays on the dashboard even when the vehicle is fully in park, preventing the car from turning off completely and draining the battery.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace the shifter assembly or internal microswitch component.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V400000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Malibu&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As a result, the PPS may not properly identify an adult passenger from a child passenger in the front passenger seat, potentially causing the air bag to not deploy when it should, or causing the air bag to deploy when it shouldn't. In the event of a crash, improper air bag deployment can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front passenger PPS seat service kit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V358000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Malibu&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A damaged fuel line can create a fuel leak, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the high pressure fuel pump, and high pressure fuel pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V272000: Electronic Stability Control (Esc)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Malibu&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to conform to Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 126, "Electronic Stability Control Systems." If the EBCM fails the primary braking system will still function, however, the loss of ABS and ESC increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will install a replacement EBCM, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V781000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Malibu&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The fabric of the side-impact air bag cushion may tear during deployment. If the air bag tears during deployment, the air bag may not perform as designed, increasing the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, as necessary, replace the air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V502000: Electrical System:Ignition](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Malibu&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 114, "Theft Protection and Rollaway Prevention." If the key is removed without the transmission in PARK, the vehicle may rollaway as occupants are exiting, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and if necessary replace the key cylinder lock housing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V084000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Malibu&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (FMVSS) number 114, "Theft Protection", and/or 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unbelted driver is at a greater risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will update the radio software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V151000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Malibu&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The two weld studs that mount the front and rear side impact air bags may fracture and separate from the air bag during deployment. 214, "Side Impact Protection." Fractured weld studs may allow the side air bag to move out of position during deployment, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the side air bag modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V125000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Malibu&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The driver frontal air bag may improperly inflate during second-stage deployment in the event of a high speed crash. An improperly inflated air bag increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver side frontal air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Chevrolet Malibu presents attractive styling and comfortable road manners, but buyers need to perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to the shifter mechanism for 'Shift to Park' glitches and verify that the brake system and vacuum pump operate properly without pressure loss.
