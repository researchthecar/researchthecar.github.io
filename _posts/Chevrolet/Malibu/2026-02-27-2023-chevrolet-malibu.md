# 2023 Chevrolet Malibu Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Chevrolet Malibu continues a long-running nameplate tradition as an affordable, stylish midsize sedan. For used car buyers, it offers a comfortable ride and modern cabin tech, but prospective owners should review key owner complaints concerning powertrain hesitation, electronics, and visibility systems before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged I4

The standard 1.5-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 160 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque, paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). It provides adequate daily efficiency, though buyers should watch for occasional throttle hesitation or limp mode warnings reported by some drivers.

**Acceleration Hesitation and Limp Mode**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle hesitates after starting or when accelerating from a stop, and may occasionally enter limp mode displaying a reduced power warning.
- **Solution:** Have a certified mechanic or dealership inspect powertrain control modules, throttle body assembly, and turbocharger wastegate operation.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The front-wheel-drive CVT is designed to maximize fuel economy across daily commutes. While generally smooth in operation, high-mileage examples should be thoroughly test-driven for shifting delays or slipping.

**Transmission Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of drive functionality, unusual whining noises, or complete transmission failure at higher mileage intervals.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition and level; severe mechanical failures require professional transmission rebuild or replacement.

## Suspension

### Tires

Suspension and rolling stock components generally hold up well, though tire-related incidents emphasize the importance of monitoring rubber condition.

**Premature Tread Separation and Blowouts**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected tire tread separation or rapid deflation while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect factory-equipped tires for tread wear patterns, age, and proper inflation pressure; replace damaged or defective tires immediately.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake system complaints highlight rare but severe concerns regarding physical pedal resistance and electronic module communication failures.

**Brake Pedal Sticking and Module Communication Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal intermittently becomes stuck in the fully raised position and resists depression, or drivers experience a total loss of brakes accompanied by brake system module communication faults.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pedal linkage, electronic brake modules, and wiring harnesses for loose connections or internal faults.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera and Vision System

Safety equipment includes standard driver assists and a rearview camera display. The most notable pattern of concern involves backup camera system failures and associated recall campaigns.

**Rearview Camera Blank Screen**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview camera intermittently flickers, displays a distorted image, or goes completely black with a 'Service Vision System' or 'Backup Camera Unavailable' warning message.
- **Solution:** Check for open safety recalls related to the rearview camera system and have software updates or camera hardware replaced by a certified dealer.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V923000: Structure:Frame And Members](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Malibu&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The front impact bar, a structural portion of the vehicle frame, may not be properly welded to the front frame rail. An improperly welded front impact bar may result in the front crash sensors not performing correctly, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the right-hand and left-hand side of the vehicle's motor rail for an incomplete weld. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V212000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Malibu&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A rearview image that does not display correctly reduces the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect, and if necessary, replace the rearview camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Chevrolet Malibu is an appealing everyday sedan with an attractive design and comfortable road manners. Used car shoppers should prioritize verifying that all safety recalls—particularly those concerning the rearview camera—have been completed, and perform a careful inspection of the engine and braking systems during a test drive.
