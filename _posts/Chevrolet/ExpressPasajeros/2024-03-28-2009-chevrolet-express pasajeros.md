**2009 Chevrolet Express Passenger**

The 2009 Chevrolet Express Passenger van is a full-size van that is available in both cargo and passenger configurations. It is powered by a variety of gasoline and diesel engines, and offers a choice of two transmissions. The Express Passenger is known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and powerful engines. However, it has also been plagued by a number of issues, including:

**Engine**

* **4.3L V6**
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil to leak. This can lead to overheating and engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Camshaft position sensor failure:** The camshaft position sensor can fail, causing the engine to run erratically or stall.
        * Solution: Replace the camshaft position sensor.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **4.8L V8**
    * **Valve lifter failure:** The valve lifters can fail, causing the engine to make a ticking noise and lose power.
        * Solution: Replace the valve lifters.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $800+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Timing chain failure:** The timing chain can fail, causing the engine to skip time and lose power.
        * Solution: Replace the timing chain.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **6.0L V8**
    * **Head gasket failure:** The head gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil to leak. This can lead to overheating and engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace the head gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Crankshaft position sensor failure:** The crankshaft position sensor can fail, causing the engine to stall.
        * Solution: Replace the crankshaft position sensor.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **6.6L Duramax Diesel**
    * **Fuel injector failure:** The fuel injectors can fail, causing the engine to run rough and lose power.
        * Solution: Replace the fuel injectors.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Turbocharger failure:** The turbocharger can fail, causing the engine to lose power.
        * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **4L60E**
    * **Torque converter failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing the transmission to slip and lose power.
        * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Transmission filter failure:** The transmission filter can fail, causing the transmission to slip and lose power.
        * Solution: Replace the transmission filter.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **4L80E**
    * **Sun gear failure:** The sun gear can fail, causing the transmission to lose power.
        * Solution: Replace the sun gear.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Input shaft bearing failure:** The input shaft bearing can fail, causing the transmission to make noise and lose power.
        * Solution: Replace the input shaft bearing.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension:** The front suspension can wear out prematurely, causing the vehicle to handle poorly and ride rough.
    * Solution: Replace the worn suspension components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear suspension:** The rear suspension can also wear out prematurely, causing the vehicle to handle poorly and ride rough.
    * Solution: Replace the worn suspension components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery failure:** The battery can fail, causing the vehicle to lose power.
    * Solution: Replace the battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the battery to drain and the vehicle to lose power.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Starter failure:** The starter can fail, causing the vehicle to not start.
    * Solution: Replace the starter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door locks:** The door locks can fail, causing the doors to not open or close properly.
    * Solution: Replace the door locks.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Window motors:** The window motors can fail, causing the windows to not open or close properly.
    * Solution: Replace the window motors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard:** The dashboard can crack or warp, causing it to look unsightly.
    * Solution: Replace the dashboard.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Seats:** The seats can wear out prematurely, causing them to become uncomfortable.
    * Solution: Replace the seats.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2009 Chevrolet Express Passenger is a capable van with a comfortable interior and powerful engines. However, it has been plagued by a number of issues, including engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, and interior problems. Many of these issues can be expensive to fix, and they can significantly impact the reliability of the vehicle. As a result, potential buyers should carefully consider the potential costs of ownership before purchasing a 2009 Chevrolet Express Passenger.
