The 2019 Chevrolet Express Pasajeros is a full-size passenger van known for its durability and cargo/passenger capacity. It caters to large families, shuttle services, and commercial transportation needs. While valued for its robust build and simple mechanicals, it does have some common issues reported by owners. Engine options include the 4.3L V6 (276 hp and 298 lb-ft of torque), the 6.0L V8 (341 hp and 373 lb-ft of torque), and the 2.8L Duramax Turbo-Diesel inline-4 (181 hp and 369 lb-ft of torque). Transmission options include a 6-speed automatic and an 8-speed automatic. Some owners appreciate its reliability and utilitarian nature, while others dislike its outdated design and fuel economy.

**Engine**

**4.3L LV1 V6**

*   **Active Fuel Management (AFM) Lifter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, rough idling, check engine light, and potential damage to the camshaft. This is due to the AFM system which shuts down cylinders to improve fuel economy.
    *   Solution: Replace the lifters and potentially the camshaft. AFM can also be disabled with a tune.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6.0L L96 V8**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption requiring frequent topping off, blue smoke from the exhaust, and potential fouling of spark plugs.
    *   Solution: Piston ring replacement, valve stem seal replacement, or engine replacement in severe cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $6,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.8L LWN Duramax Turbo-Diesel Inline-4**

* **Diesel Exhaust Fluid (DEF) System Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, reduced engine power, error messages related to DEF level or quality. The DEF system is responsible for reducing NOx emissions.
    * Solution: Diagnosis of the DEF system including the DEF injector, DEF pump, and NOx sensors. Replacement of faulty components may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6L90 6-Speed Automatic Transmission (paired with the 6.0L V8 and 2.8L Diesel)**

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibrations during shifting, especially at lower speeds, potential stalling, and transmission slipping.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Harsh or Delayed Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Abrupt or delayed gear changes, often accompanied by a "clunking" sound. This is usually caused by worn valve body components or solenoid issues.
    *   Solution: Valve body rebuild or replacement, solenoid replacement, transmission flush, and adaptation reset.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**8L90 8-Speed Automatic Transmission (paired with the 4.3L V6)**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: This transmission is known to sometimes have harsh or erratic shifting, especially when cold. This can be due to a faulty torque converter or solenoids within the transmission.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the torque converter or the solenoids.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Leaf Spring Sagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rear of the vehicle sitting lower than the front, reduced ride height, potential bottoming out under heavy loads.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the rear leaf springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Faulty Body Control Module (BCM)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical issues such as malfunctioning lights, power windows, door locks, and other accessories. The BCM controls many of the vehicle's electrical functions.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the BCM and reprogramming.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sliding Door Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening or closing the sliding side door, sticking, unusual noises, and potential motor failure in power sliding door models.
    *   Solution: Lubrication of the door tracks and rollers, adjustment of the door alignment, or replacement of the door motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Seatbelt Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Seatbelts that retract slowly or not at all.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the seatbelt assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Chevrolet Express Pasajeros, while generally reliable, has common issues across different engine options and components. Engine problems like AFM lifter failure and oil consumption can be costly, but they are not present in every vehicle. Electrical and suspension issues are also possible, though not as prevalent. Transmission issues are common, especially with the 6L90 transmission. Severity ranges from minor inconveniences like a slow retracting seatbelt to major repairs like engine and transmission replacements. Repair costs can vary widely depending on the issue and the extent of the damage. It is advisable to have the vehicle inspected by a qualified mechanic before purchase.
