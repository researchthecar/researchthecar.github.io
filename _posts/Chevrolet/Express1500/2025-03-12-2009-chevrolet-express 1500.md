The 2009 Chevrolet Express 1500 is a full-size van known for its versatility, cargo capacity, and towing capability. Engine options included the 4.3L V6 (195 hp, 260 lb-ft of torque), the 5.3L V8 (310 hp, 338 lb-ft of torque), and the 6.0L V8 (300 hp, 360 lb-ft of torque). A 4-speed automatic transmission was the only transmission offered. Owners often appreciate the Express's ruggedness and dependability for work purposes, as well as its spacious interior. Common complaints include the vehicle's relatively poor fuel economy, dated interior design, and basic handling characteristics compared to more modern vans.

**Engine**

**4.3L V6**

*   **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, coolant leaks, rough idle, possible overheating.
    *   Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks (Rear Main Seal, Valve Covers)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Visible oil leaks, oil consumption, burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking seals and gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $800 (depending on location and severity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**5.3L V8**

*   **Active Fuel Management (AFM) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, engine misfires, rough idle, reduced fuel economy. (Although AFM was more prevalent in later models, some 2009 5.3L engines were equipped with it. Confirm the presence of AFM before diagnosing this issue).
    *   Solution: Replace the AFM lifters, replace the VLOM (Valve Lifter Oil Manifold), disable AFM with a tuner, or perform a complete AFM delete.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (Tuner) - $3,000+ (AFM Delete)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Pressure Sensor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erroneous oil pressure readings on the dashboard, "Check Engine" light.
    *   Solution: Replace the oil pressure sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Exhaust Manifold Bolts Breakage**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Ticking noise from the engine, especially when cold; exhaust leak.
    *   Solution: Replace broken exhaust manifold bolts. Sometimes the manifold itself can be warped and will need replacement as well.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6.0L V8**

*   **Oil Leaks (Rear Main Seal, Valve Covers)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Visible oil leaks, oil consumption, burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking seals and gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $800 (depending on location and severity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Exhaust Manifold Bolts Breakage**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Ticking noise from the engine, especially when cold; exhaust leak.
    *   Solution: Replace broken exhaust manifold bolts. Sometimes the manifold itself can be warped and will need replacement as well.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4L60E (All Engine Options)**

*   **Slipping or Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Transmission slips between gears, harsh or delayed shifts, "Check Engine" light.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the transmission. Could be caused by a failing valve body, worn clutch packs, or other internal damage.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering at low speeds, stalling, transmission overheating.
    *   Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Ball Joints and Tie Rod Ends**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loose steering, clunking noises from the front end, uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replace the worn ball joints and/or tie rod ends.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600 per side
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Leaf Spring Sagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rear end sagging, reduced load-carrying capacity, rough ride.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaf springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1000+ (pair)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Power Window Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power windows not working, intermittent operation.
    *   Solution: Replace the power window motor or regulator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400 per window
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Door locks not working, intermittent operation.
    *   Solution: Replace the door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 per door
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust (Wheel Arches, Rocker Panels)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Visible rust on wheel arches and rocker panels.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace rusted body panels.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+ (depending on severity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Headliner Sagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Headliner detaching from the roof.
    *   Solution: Replace the headliner or have it reupholstered.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing on the dashboard surface, especially in sun-exposed areas.
    *   Solution: Replace the dashboard or use a dashboard cover.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+ (replacement); $20 - $100 (cover)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2009 Chevrolet Express 1500, while a workhorse, has its share of common issues. Engine-related problems, such as oil leaks and, in some cases, AFM issues with the 5.3L V8, can lead to moderate to high repair costs. Transmission problems with the 4L60E can also be costly. Suspension wear is typical for a vehicle of this age and type. Interior issues, like headliner sagging and dashboard cracking, are mostly cosmetic but can affect the vehicle's overall appeal. Rust can be a major concern depending on location and maintenance history. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and factor in the costs of repairs or maintenance when considering purchasing this vehicle.
