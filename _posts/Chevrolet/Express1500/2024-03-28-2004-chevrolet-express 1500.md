**2004 Chevrolet Express 1500**

The 2004 Chevrolet Express 1500 is a full-size van offered in various configurations, including cargo, passenger, and cutaway chassis. It features a 5.3L V8 engine and a 4-speed automatic transmission, producing 290 horsepower and 320 lb-ft of torque. The Express 1500 is known for its spacious interior, powerful engine, and versatility. However, owners have reported several issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

* **5.3L V8**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, rough idle
        * Solution: Replace intake manifold gaskets
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Valve Train Noise**
        * Description/Symptoms: Tapping or clicking sounds from the engine
        * Solution: Adjust or replace valve lifters
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **4-Speed Automatic (4L60E)**
    * **Shift Solenoid Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission shifts erratically or fails to engage gears
        * Solution: Replace shift solenoids
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Vehicle leans to one side, rough ride, broken leaf springs
        * Solution: Replace leaf springs
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
        * Description/Symptoms: Battery loses charge quickly, even when the vehicle is not in use
        * Solution: Determine and fix the source of the battery drain (e.g., faulty alternator, parasitic draw)
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Headlight Switch Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Headlights turn on/off intermittently or do not work at all
        * Solution: Replace headlight switch
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Undercarriage**
        * Description/Symptoms: Rust or corrosion on the undercarriage components
        * Solution: Apply rust inhibitor or replace affected parts
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of rust
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **HVAC System Malfunction**
        * Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning or heating system not working properly
        * Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty component (e.g., compressor, evaporator coil)
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2004 Chevrolet Express 1500 can be a reliable vehicle, but it has a history of some common issues. The most severe problems include intake manifold gasket failure, valve train noise, and transmission shift solenoid failure, which can all be expensive to fix. Overall, the cost of repairs can vary widely depending on the issue and the severity of the damage.
