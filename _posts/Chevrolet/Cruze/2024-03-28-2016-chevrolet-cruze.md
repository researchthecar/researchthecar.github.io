**Introduction:**

The 2016 Chevrolet Cruze is a compact sedan known for its affordability, fuel efficiency, and ample interior space. It offers a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 1.4L turbocharged engine with a 6-speed automatic or 6-speed manual transmission, and a 1.6L naturally aspirated engine with a 6-speed automatic transmission. The Cruze receives praise for its comfortable ride, spacious cabin, and user-friendly infotainment system, but some owners have encountered various issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**1.4L Turbocharged**
* **Intake Valve Deposits:** Buildup of carbon deposits on the intake valves can cause reduced engine performance, rough idling, and increased fuel consumption.
    * **Solution:** Cleaning or replacing the intake valves.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $800+
    * **Recalls:** N/A

* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** The timing chain tensioner can fail, leading to noisy engine operation and potential engine damage.
    * **Solution:** Replacing the timing chain tensioner and related components.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+
    * **Recalls:** N/A

**1.6L Naturally Aspirated**

* **No Major Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic (1.4L Turbocharged)**
* **Shifting Issues:** Some owners have reported rough or delayed shifts, as well as shuddering or slipping.
    * **Solution:** Updating the transmission control module software or replacing the transmission fluid.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $600+
    * **Recalls:** N/A

* **Premature Failure:** In some cases, the transmission has experienced premature failure, requiring a full replacement.
    * **Solution:** Replacing the transmission.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $3,000 - $5,000+
    * **Recalls:** N/A

* **No Major Issues Found**

**6-Speed Automatic (1.6L Naturally Aspirated)**

**Suspension**

* **Rear Shock Absorber Failure:** The rear shock absorbers can fail prematurely, causing a rough ride and potential handling issues.
    * **Solution:** Replacing the shock absorbers.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $400 - $600+
    * **Recalls:** N/A

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Some owners have experienced excessive battery drain, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle.
    * **Solution:** Identifying and addressing the source of the drain, such as faulty wiring or accessories.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $300+
    * **Recalls:** N/A

* **Headlight Malfunction:** In certain cases, the headlights have failed or malfunctioned, affecting visibility.
    * **Solution:** Replacing the headlights or associated electrical components.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500+
    * **Recalls:** N/A

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Failure:** The exterior door handles can break or become loose, making it difficult to open the doors.
    * **Solution:** Replacing the door handles.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $150 - $300
    * **Recalls:** N/A

* **Trunk Lid Malfunction:** The trunk lid latch can fail, preventing the trunk from opening or closing properly.
    * **Solution:** Replacing the latch or associated components.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400
    * **Recalls:** N/A

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Issues:** The climate control system may malfunction, causing insufficient heating or cooling.
    * **Solution:** Diagnosing and repairing the faulty component, such as the thermostat or blend door actuator.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500+
    * **Recalls:** N/A

* **Infotainment Screen Glitches:** The infotainment system may experience glitches or freezes, affecting its functionality.
    * **Solution:** Updating the software or replacing the infotainment unit.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $800+
    * **Recalls:** N/A

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2016 Chevrolet Cruze has received mixed feedback from owners. While its affordability and spacious interior are often appreciated, it has been plagued by several issues that can be costly to repair. The most significant problems include premature transmission failure, intake valve deposits, and rear shock absorber failure. These issues can affect the vehicle's performance, reliability, and comfort. It is important for potential buyers to be aware of these potential issues before making a purchase decision.
