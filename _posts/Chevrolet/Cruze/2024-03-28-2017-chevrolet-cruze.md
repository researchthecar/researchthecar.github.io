**Introduction:**

The 2017 Chevrolet Cruze is a compact sedan praised for its stylish design, affordable price, and efficient performance. It features a range of engine and transmission options, including a 1.4L turbocharged 4-cylinder engine with 153 horsepower and 177 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed manual or 6-speed automatic transmission, and a 1.6L diesel engine with 137 horsepower and 240 lb-ft of torque, mated to a 9-speed automatic transmission. Despite its appeal, the Cruze has been plagued by a number of reported issues across various components.

**Engine**

**1.4L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine**

**Coolant Leak**
Description/Symptoms: Noticeable loss of coolant, overheating, steam or coolant smell from engine bay
Solution: Replace leaking component (e.g., radiator, gasket)
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Oil Consumption**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage
Solution: Check for leaks, replace piston rings if necessary
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

**1.6L Diesel Engine**

**No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

**Gear Synchronization Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises, and potential lock-up of transmission
Solution: Replace synchronizer rings and other damaged components
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

**Transmission Slipping**
Description/Symptoms: Loss of power during acceleration, delayed gear shifts, or shuddering
Solution: Flush transmission fluid, replace faulty sensors or solenoids, or rebuild transmission
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
Recalls: None Found

**9-Speed Automatic Transmission**

**Transmission Shudder**
Description/Symptoms: Vibration or shuddering felt during acceleration or gear shifts
Solution: Update software, replace transmission fluid, or rebuild transmission
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Strut Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Noisy suspension, uneven tire wear, poor handling
Solution: Replace struts and associated components
Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Battery Discharge**
Description/Symptoms: Rapid battery drain, difficulty starting the vehicle
Solution: Test and replace faulty components (e.g., alternator, battery, wiring)
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Infotainment System Glitches**
Description/Symptoms: Random freezing, crashing, or performance issues with the infotainment system
Solution: Update software, replace malfunctioning components
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**No Issues Found**

**Summary**

The 2017 Chevrolet Cruze has been plagued by a variety of issues, ranging from coolant leaks and oil consumption to transmission failures and electrical problems. The severity and cost of these issues can vary significantly, depending on the specific part that needs repair or replacement. Some issues have been addressed through recalls, but others remain unresolved. It is important for owners to be aware of these potential problems and to have the vehicle inspected and repaired as needed to ensure safe and reliable operation.
