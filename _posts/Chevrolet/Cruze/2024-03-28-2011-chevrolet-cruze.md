# 2011 Chevrolet Cruze Issues

The 2011 Chevrolet Cruze is a compact sedan that offers a choice of two engines: a 1.8L inline-4 with 138 horsepower and 125 lb-ft of torque, or a 1.4L turbocharged inline-4 with 138 horsepower and 148 lb-ft of torque. The 1.8L engine is paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission, while the 1.4L engine can be paired with either a 6-speed automatic or a 6-speed manual transmission.

The Cruze is known for its fuel efficiency, with the 1.8L engine achieving up to 26 mpg in the city and 39 mpg on the highway, and the 1.4L engine achieving up to 28 mpg in the city and 42 mpg on the highway. It also has a spacious interior and a comfortable ride.

However, the Cruze has also been plagued by a number of issues, which we will discuss in detail below.

**Engine**

* **1.8L Engine**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause coolant or oil leaks, overheating, and reduced engine performance.
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
        * Recalls: 14V373000
    * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause a rattling noise from the engine, reduced engine power, and eventually engine failure.
        * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **1.4L Engine**
    * **Turbocharger Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause a loss of power, a whistling noise from the engine, and increased oil consumption.
        * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Solenoid Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause shifting problems, delayed engagement, and slipping gears.
        * Solution: Replace the solenoid.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
        * Recalls: 14V373000
* **6-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **Synchro Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises, and popping out of gear.
        * Solution: Replace the synchro.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause the battery to drain quickly, even when the car is not running.
    * Solution: Check for any parasitic drains on the battery and repair or replace the faulty component.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Headlight Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause one or more headlights to fail.
    * Solution: Replace the headlight bulb or assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Door Lock Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause the door locks to fail to lock or unlock, or to lock or unlock intermittently.
    * Solution: Replace the door lock actuator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Power Window Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause one or more power windows to fail to operate.
    * Solution: Replace the power window motor or switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Overall**

The 2011 Chevrolet Cruze has been plagued by a number of issues, including engine problems, transmission problems, electrical problems, and interior problems. The severity of these issues varies, but some of them can be quite costly to repair. As a result, it is important to be aware of these potential issues before purchasing a Cruze.
