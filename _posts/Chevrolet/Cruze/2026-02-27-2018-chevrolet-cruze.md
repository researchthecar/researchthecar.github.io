# 2018 Chevrolet Cruze Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Chevrolet Cruze offers efficient daily driving dynamics and a comfortable cabin, available in both sedan and hatchback body styles. However, prospective used-car buyers should carefully evaluate potential mechanical issues, particularly regarding powertrain reliability and electronic accessories. While many owners enjoy its fuel economy, reviewing known owner-reported trouble spots can help prevent unexpected repair bills.

---

## Engine

### 1.4L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 1.4-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 153 horsepower and 177 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate pep for daily commuting. Buyers should watch for occasional turbocharger or fuel-delivery anomalies reported by some owners.

**Engine Stalling and Misfire**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may experience unexpected engine stalling when coming to a stop or hesitation during acceleration, often accompanied by rough idling or misfire codes.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace worn spark plugs, clean the throttle body, or service components such as the PCV valve as needed.

### 1.6L Turbo-Diesel 4-Cylinder

The 1.6-liter turbo-diesel four-cylinder generates around 137 horsepower and a robust 240 lb-ft of torque, delivering exceptional highway fuel economy. It requires careful maintenance of emissions systems and fuel delivery components to remain trouble-free.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The conventional 6-speed manual transmission offers direct driver engagement primarily paired with lower trim levels. Proper upkeep of the clutch hydraulic system is important to avoid shifting problems.

**Clutch Pedal Pressure Loss**
- **Symptoms:** The clutch pedal may feel loose, sink to the floor, or lose hydraulic pressure, making it difficult or impossible to shift gears.
- **Solution:** Inspect the hydraulic clutch lines for air or leaks, and replace failing master or slave cylinders.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth gear changes during daily city and highway driving. Routine fluid checks help maintain long-term durability.

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission is paired with diesel models to optimize highway efficiency. Buyers should test-drive thoroughly to ensure smooth shifting across all gears.

**Internal Transmission Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission may slip, fail to engage higher gears like ninth gear, or display harsh shifting behavior due to internal component wear.
- **Solution:** Consult a qualified transmission specialist for diagnostic evaluation; internal component replacement or professional rebuild may be required.

## Electrical

### Infotainment Touchscreen

The cabin technology suite includes touchscreen infotainment with smartphone integration, though software glitches can occasionally hinder usability.

**Screen Freezing and Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display freezes on startup screens or becomes completely unresponsive to touch inputs, locking out features like Apple CarPlay and Android Auto.
- **Solution:** Perform a system reset, update the software at a dealership, or replace the infotainment control module if hardware failure persists.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Seat Belts

Passive safety systems include standard airbags and seat belt pretensioners designed to protect occupants in a collision.

**Restraint System Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Warning lights for the airbag or safety restraint system illuminate, potentially disabling occupant detection or deployment functions.
- **Solution:** Have the diagnostic trouble codes read by a technician to inspect seat sensors, wiring harnesses, or faulty restraint modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V668000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Cruze&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Missing bolts on the start-stop accumulator endcap could result in a transmission oil leak and may progress to a loss of propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash. A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the start-stop transmission accumulator and replace it if any bolts are missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V576000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Cruze&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A reduction of braking performance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will bleed the vehicle's brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V304000: Fuel System, Gasoline](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Cruze&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The vehicles may leak gasoline following a rear-impact crash and a rollover. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 301, "Fuel System Integrity." A fuel leak, in the presence of an ignition source, increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will install a lock-ring on the fuel tank that will shield the fuel tank vapor pressure sensor from damage in a rear-impact crash, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Chevrolet Cruze can be an economical and practical used car, but buyers should conduct a thorough inspection before purchasing. Pay close attention to engine idle quality, manual transmission clutch feel, and infotainment functionality during a test drive. Verifying service records and checking for unresolved NHTSA complaints will help ensure a more reliable ownership experience.
