# 2017 Chevrolet Cruze Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Chevrolet Cruze is a compact car offered with efficient engine options and practical everyday drivability, but prospective buyers should carefully review common mechanical trouble spots. While it delivers respectable fuel economy and a comfortable ride, owner complaints highlight recurring powertrain quirks, electrical glitches, and specific mechanical vulnerabilities that require attention.

---

## Engine

### 1.4L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 1.4-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 153 horsepower and 177 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate pep for daily commuting. While generally fuel-efficient, this powerplant is associated with owner reports of rough running, misfires, and stalling issues.

**Engine Stalling and Misfires**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles experience unexpected engine stalling, rough idling, illumination of the check engine light, and a 'Service Stabilitrak' warning message.
- **Solution:** Diagnose ignition components, replace worn spark plugs or ignition coils, and inspect internal components if misfires persist.

### 1.6L Turbo-Diesel 4-Cylinder

The 1.6-liter turbo-diesel inline-4 engine generates around 137 horsepower and a robust 240 lb-ft of torque, maximizing highway cruising range and fuel efficiency. It offers strong low-end pulling power, though buyers should verify proper emissions and fuel system maintenance.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The 6-speed manual transmission is paired with a hydraulic clutch setup that has proven prone to premature slave cylinder failure, resulting in a lost pedal feel.

**Clutch Pedal Failure and Slave Cylinder Leak**
- **Symptoms:** The clutch pedal sinks straight to the floor without springing back, leaving the driver unable to shift gears or disengage the clutch.
- **Solution:** Replace the failing hydraulic slave cylinder, master cylinder, and associated hydraulic lines.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The conventional 6-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth gear shifts and pairs well with the standard gas engine. It generally experiences fewer widespread owner complaints than its manual counterpart.

## Suspension

### Suspension System

The suspension provides a compliant daily ride quality, though minor mechanical noises from bushings and mounts can develop over time.

**Rear Suspension Knocking**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or knocking noises emanating from the rear suspension when driving over small bumps, potholes, or uneven road surfaces.
- **Solution:** Inspect suspension bushings, shocks, and mounts, and replace worn components.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The service brake system provides adequate stopping power under normal conditions, but hydraulic leaks linked to shared master cylinder setups can compromise performance.

**Brake Fluid Loss and Pedal Travel**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated low brake fluid warning lights accompanied by excessive brake pedal travel and reduced braking responsiveness.
- **Solution:** Inspect the braking system for fluid leaks, bleed the lines, and replace damaged master cylinders or hydraulic components.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

The electrical system manages the cabin electronics, though owners report occasional screen blackouts and camera failures.

**Infotainment and Backup Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The radio screen goes blank or stops functioning entirely, rendering the backup camera display inoperable.
- **Solution:** Inspect software, fuses, and display modules; update or replace faulty infotainment hardware.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V304000: Fuel System, Gasoline](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Cruze&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The vehicles may leak gasoline following a rear-impact crash and a rollover. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 301, "Fuel System Integrity." A fuel leak, in the presence of an ignition source, increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will install a lock-ring on the fuel tank that will shield the fuel tank vapor pressure sensor from damage in a rear-impact crash, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V057000: Seats:Front Assembly:Head Restraint](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Cruze&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A bracket used in the driver or front passenger seat-back recliner mechanism may have been incorrectly welded to the seat-back frame, as a result, in the event of a crash, the head-restraints may not function properly. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 202a, "Head.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the front passenger seats, replacing any with seat-back frames that are incorrectly welded, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Chevrolet Cruze presents a mixed picture for used-car shoppers, combining attractive fuel economy and modern styling with notable mechanical pain points. Buyers interested in manual transmission models should thoroughly check the clutch hydraulic system for spongy pedal feel or leaks, while prospective owners of the gas engine should listen for rough idling or misfire symptoms during a test drive. Conducting a pre-purchase inspection helps catch hidden leaks and electrical glitches before sealing a deal.
