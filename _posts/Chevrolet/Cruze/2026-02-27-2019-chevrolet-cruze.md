# 2019 Chevrolet Cruze Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 Chevrolet Cruze, buyers will find a stylish and fuel-efficient compact car available in both sedan and hatchback body styles. While it offers a comfortable ride and modern technology features, prospective owners should review owner-reported feedback concerning various mechanical and electrical systems. Taking the time to thoroughly inspect powertrain options and accessory systems during a test drive can help ensure a dependable ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 1.4L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 1.4-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 153 horsepower and 177 lb-ft of torque, offering a balance of efficiency and pep. It serves as the primary gasoline powerplant for this model year.

**Timing Chain Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported hearing a rattling noise from the timing chain area during early morning start-ups, often attributed to oil drainage from the tensioner.
- **Solution:** Inspect the timing chain tensioner and associated components for wear; replace parts as necessary.

### 1.6L Turbodiesel 4-Cylinder

The 1.6-liter turbodiesel four-cylinder engine generates around 137 horsepower and a robust 240 lb-ft of torque. It is designed for maximum highway fuel economy and long-distance cruising.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with the diesel engine variant, providing smooth gear changes suited for highway driving.

**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice fluid pooling under the vehicle or experience shifting irregularities due to leaks originating from the main transmission seal.
- **Solution:** Inspect the transmission seals and housing areas, and replace worn seals or gaskets to restore proper fluid retention.

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission is paired with the 1.4L gasoline engine, offering optimized gear ratios for city and highway efficiency.

**Shifting Hesitation and Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Some drivers experience rough shifting behavior, jerking, or operational concerns potentially related to transmission mounting or accumulator hardware.
- **Solution:** Check transmission mounting bolts and related hydraulic components; ensure any applicable service bulletins or hardware updates are addressed.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The standard disc braking system provides reliable stopping power, though proper maintenance of hydraulic assist components is essential.

**Stiff Pedal and Reduced Stopping Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported instances where the brake pedal feels unusually hard to push, requiring significantly more effort to bring the vehicle to a stop.
- **Solution:** Have the brake booster, vacuum lines, and hydraulic components inspected by a qualified technician to verify proper brake assist function.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

The vehicle's electrical architecture manages numerous comfort and safety accessories that should be fully tested during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Accessory and Defroster Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent failure of the front window defroster or infotainment radio may occur, sometimes requiring a full vehicle restart to restore function.
- **Solution:** Inspect electrical connections, fuses, and software modules associated with the body control and climate systems.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Defroster

Exterior visibility components require regular upkeep to ensure clear sightlines in varying weather conditions.

**Windshield Fogging and Visibility Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note that the front windshield can become foggy or develop an interior film due to defroster performance issues or glass lamination concerns.
- **Solution:** Check the defroster blower and climate control electronics for proper operation, and clean or replace the cabin air filter as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V668000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Cruze&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Missing bolts on the start-stop accumulator endcap could result in a transmission oil leak and may progress to a loss of propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash. A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the start-stop transmission accumulator and replace it if any bolts are missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V576000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Cruze&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A reduction of braking performance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will bleed the vehicle's brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Chevrolet Cruze is an appealing compact car that combines modern styling with respectable fuel economy options. While most owners enjoy a comfortable driving experience, buyers should carefully evaluate the vehicle's service history, paying particular attention to potential brake feel anomalies, transmission operation, and electrical accessory functions. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a trusted mechanic remains the best way to verify vehicle condition before making a purchase.
