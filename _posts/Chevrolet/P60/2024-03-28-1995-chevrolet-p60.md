**Introduction to the 1995 Chevrolet P60**

The 1995 Chevrolet P60 is a heavy-duty commercial truck available with rear-wheel drive only. It comes equipped with a 7.4L gasoline engine mated to a 4-speed automatic transmission. The P60 boasts impressive specs, producing 210 horsepower and 410 lb-ft of torque. Owners appreciate its reliability and durability, but some have reported issues across various components.

**Engine**

**7.4L Gasoline Engine**

**Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
* Description/Symptoms: Coolant leak around the intake manifold, overheating, rough idling, loss of power
* Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
* Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

**Transmission Slipping**
* Description/Symptoms: Transmission fails to engage properly, slipping gears, delayed shifting
* Solution: Repair or rebuild transmission
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Leaf Spring Failure**
* Description/Symptoms: Broken leaf springs, sagging suspension, uneven ride height
* Solution: Replace leaf springs
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+ per axle
* Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Alternator Failure**
* Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging, dim headlights, electrical system issues
* Solution: Replace alternator
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
* Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Corrosion on Frame**
* Description/Symptoms: Rust on the frame, compromising structural integrity
* Solution: Repair or replace frame
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
* Description/Symptoms: Blower motor not working, lack of air flow
* Solution: Replace blower motor
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1995 Chevrolet P60 is a reliable workhorse, but it is not without its issues. The most common problems reported by owners include intake manifold gasket failure, transmission slipping, suspension issues, and electrical problems. The severity of these issues varies, and repair costs can range from minor to major. While the P60 has its shortcomings, its durability and towing capabilities make it a popular choice for commercial applications.
