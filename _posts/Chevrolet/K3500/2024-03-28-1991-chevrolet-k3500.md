**Introduction:**

The 1991 Chevrolet K3500 is a heavy-duty pickup truck available with a variety of engine and transmission options. It is powered by a choice of gasoline or diesel engines, and available with either a manual or automatic transmission. The K3500 is known for its rugged construction, impressive towing and hauling capabilities, and comfortable cabin. However, like any vehicle, it does have its share of potential issues.

**Engine**

**5.7L V8 Gasoline**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, rough idling, and reduced engine power.
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

* **Distributor Cap and Rotor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfire, hard starting, and stalling.
    * Solution: Replace distributor cap and rotor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250

**7.4L V8 Gasoline**

* **Connecting Rod Bearing Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Knocking noises from engine, loss of oil pressure, and engine seizure.
    * Solution: Replace connecting rod bearings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000

**6.2L V8 Diesel**

* **Injection Pump Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling, loss of power, and white smoke from exhaust.
    * Solution: Replace injection pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000

* **Turbocharger Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, black smoke from exhaust, and whistling noises.
    * Solution: Replace turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000

* **Fuel Injectors Clogging:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hard starting, poor fuel economy, and engine misfire.
    * Solution: Clean or replace fuel injectors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Synchronizer Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises, and popping out of gear.
    * Solution: Replace synchronizers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, delayed gear engagement, and shuddering.
    * Solution: Overhaul or replace transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000

* **Torque Converter Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, slipping gears, and overheating.
    * Solution: Replace torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, uneven tire wear, and reduced handling.
    * Solution: Replace leaf springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500

* **Shock Absorber Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough ride, excessive bouncing, and reduced handling.
    * Solution: Replace shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $600

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dim headlights, battery not charging, and warning lights on dashboard.
    * Solution: Replace alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800

* **Battery Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting, flickering lights, and reduced electrical power.
    * Solution: Replace battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Body Panels:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust spots, blistering paint, and holes in body panels.
    * Solution: Repair or replace affected panels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on extent of damage

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks and splitting on dashboard surface.
    * Solution: Replace dashboard.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

* **Instrument Cluster Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning gauges, dim or flickering displays, and warning lights not illuminating.
    * Solution: Replace instrument cluster.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Summary**

The 1991 Chevrolet K3500 is a capable workhorse that offers impressive towing and hauling capabilities. However, it is not without its potential issues. The most severe and costly issues typically involve engine or transmission failures, which can be expensive to repair. Other issues, such as rust on body panels or electrical problems, are more manageable and less expensive to fix. Overall, the K3500 is a reliable vehicle, but as with any used vehicle, it is important to be aware of potential issues and factor in the cost of repairs when considering a purchase.
