**Introduction:**

The 2005 Chevrolet Cobalt is a compact sedan known for its sporty handling and affordable price. It was available with a range of engine and transmission options, including a 2.2L Ecotec inline-4 engine and a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission. The Cobalt also impressed with its roomy interior and comfortable seats. However, some owners have reported issues with specific components.

**Engine**

**2.2L Ecotec Inline-4 Engine**

* **Head Gasket Failure:** Premature gasket failure leading to coolant leaking into the cylinders or oil passages. Symptoms include overheating, white smoke from the exhaust, and oil consumption. **Solution: Replace the head gasket.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,500+
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:** Failure of the gasket between the intake manifold and the cylinder head, causing vacuum leaks and rough idle. **Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $600
* **Timing Chain Failure:** Premature failure of the timing chain, potentially leading to engine damage. Symptoms include rattling noises from the engine and difficulty starting. **Solution: Replace the timing chain.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $3,000+
* **Camshaft Position Sensor Failure:** Malfunctioning sensor can cause inaccurate camshaft timing, leading to engine performance issues and possible stalling. **Solution: Replace the camshaft position sensor.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Shifter Linkage Failure:** Loose or broken linkage can cause difficulty shifting or prevent the transmission from engaging gears. **Solution: Repair or replace the shifter linkage.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $600
* **Clutch Failure:** Premature wear or failure of the clutch disc or pressure plate, resulting in slipping or difficulty engaging gears. **Solution: Replace the clutch components.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $800 - $1,500+

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Shift Solenoid Failure:** Malfunctioning solenoid can cause issues with gear shifting, leading to hesitation or transmission not shifting properly. **Solution: Replace the shift solenoid.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500
* **Transmission Fluid Contamination:** Internal fluid contamination can cause slipping, delayed shifts, or transmission failure. **Solution: Flush the transmission fluid and replace the filter.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400

**Suspension**

* **Lower Control Arm Bushing Failure:** Premature wear of the lower control arm bushings, leading to excessive tire wear, vibration, and instability. **Solution: Replace the lower control arm bushings.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $400 - $800+
* **Strut Mount Noise:** Worn strut mounts can cause clunking or squeaking noises, especially over bumps. **Solution: Replace the strut mounts.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Excessive battery drain, potentially caused by a faulty alternator, parasitic draw, or failing battery. **Solution: Diagnose and rectify the source of the battery drain.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $500+
* **Electrical Short:** Short circuit in the electrical system, leading to blown fuses, flickering lights, or other electrical malfunctions. **Solution: Locate and repair the short circuit.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Front Bumper Crack:** Prone to cracking due to sudden impacts or low-speed collisions. **Solution: Replace the front bumper.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+

**Interior**

* **Power Window Failure:** Motor or switch failure can cause the power windows to become inoperable. **Solution: Replace the faulty window motor or switch.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500+
* **Door Lock Actuator Malfunction:** Failure of the actuator can prevent the door locks from engaging or disengaging properly. **Solution: Replace the door lock actuator.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400
* **Seat Fabric Tearing:** Premature tearing of the seat fabric, especially on the driver's seat. **Solution: Repair or replace the seat fabric.** **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $600+

**Summary:**

The 2005 Chevrolet Cobalt has been praised for its affordability and handling. However, some owners have experienced issues with specific components, including engine gaskets, transmission sensors, and suspension components. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the extent of the damage. It's recommended to have the vehicle inspected regularly and to address any issues promptly to prevent further damage and costly repairs.
