## 2018 Chevrolet Tornado

The Chevrolet Tornado is an excellent choice for families looking for a reliable and affordable sedan. With its spacious interior and impressive fuel economy, it's no surprise that it has become a popular pick. It is available with a choice of two engines: a 1.4-liter turbocharged four-cylinder that produces 153 horsepower and 177 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder that produces 163 horsepower and 148 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a six-speed automatic transmission.

Pros:
- Fuel-efficient
- Affordable
- Spacious interior

Cons:
- Underpowered engine options
- Bland exterior styling

### Engine

**1.4L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

- **Oil Leak from Valve Cover Gasket:** Oil leaks from the valve cover gasket, causing oil to accumulate on the engine and under the vehicle. The valve cover gasket needs to be replaced. $200 - $400+
- **Timing Chain Failure:** The timing chain can stretch or break, causing the engine to lose timing and potentially catastrophic engine damage. The timing chain, tensioner, and guides need to be replaced. $1,500 - $2,500+

**2.0L Naturally Aspirated Four-Cylinder**

- **No Issues Found**

### Transmission

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

- **Hesitation and Slipping:** Transmission hesitates or slips when shifting gears. Transmission fluid and filter need to be replaced. $200 - $400
- **Delayed Engagement:** Transmission delays before engaging into gear when shifting from Park or Neutral. Transmission control module needs to be updated or replaced. $500 - $1,000+

### Suspension

**Front Suspension**

- **Strut Mount Failure:** Strut mounts can wear out and fail, causing noise and vibration when driving over bumps. The strut mounts need to be replaced. $400 - $600+

### Electrical

- **Battery Drain:** Battery drains quickly when the vehicle is parked. The alternator or battery may need to be replaced. $300 - $800+
- **Electrical Shorts:** Electrical shorts can occur due to faulty wiring or components. The affected wiring or components need to be repaired or replaced. $300 - $1,000+

### Exterior

- **No Issues Found**

### Interior

- **Climate Control Malfunction:** Climate control system may not function properly, resulting in inconsistent temperature or airflow. The climate control module or actuators may need to be replaced. $400 - $800+
- **Seat Belt Buckle Failure:** Seat belt buckle may not latch or release properly. The seat belt buckle or pretensioner may need to be replaced. $200 - $400+

### Summary

The 2018 Chevrolet Tornado has several potential issues to be aware of, including engine oil leaks, timing chain failure, and transmission problems. While some of these issues can be costly to fix, others are relatively affordable. Overall, the Tornado is a reliable and economical choice, but it's important to be aware of these potential problems before purchasing one.
