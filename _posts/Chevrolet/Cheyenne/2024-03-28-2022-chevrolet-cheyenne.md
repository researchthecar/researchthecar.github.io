The 2022 Chevrolet Cheyenne is a full-size pickup truck known for its impressive power, capability, and towing capacity. It comes with various engine and transmission options, including a 5.3L V8 gas engine, a 6.2L V8 gas engine, and a 3.0L diesel I6 engine paired with a 6-speed automatic or 8-speed automatic transmission. The truck has a solid reputation for reliability and performance, but like any vehicle, it has some reported issues.

**Engine**
**5.3L V8 Gas Engine**
* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and reduced engine performance. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
* **Piston ring failure:** Symptoms include excessive oil consumption and blue smoke from the exhaust. Solution: Replace the piston rings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500+
**6.2L V8 Gas Engine**
* **Cam phaser failure:** Symptoms include rough idle, reduced engine performance, and a rattling noise from the engine. Solution: Replace the cam phaser. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $1500
* **Fuel injector failure:** Symptoms include hard starting, rough idle, and a check engine light. Solution: Replace the faulty fuel injector(s). Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per injector
**3.0L Diesel I6 Engine**
* **Diesel particulate filter (DPF) clogging:** Symptoms include reduced engine performance, increased fuel consumption, and a check engine light. Solution: Clean or replace the DPF. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200
* **Turbocharger failure:** Symptoms include loss of power, excessive smoke from the exhaust, and a check engine light. Solution: Replace the turbocharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500+

**Transmission**
**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**
* **Torque converter clutch failure:** Symptoms include slipping, shuddering, or overheating transmission. Solution: Replace the torque converter clutch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
* **Transmission fluid leak:** Symptoms include a leak underneath the transmission and slipping or jerking gears. Solution: Replace the faulty seal or gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
**8-Speed Automatic Transmission**
* **Shift solenoid failure:** Symptoms include delayed or rough shifting, slipping gears, and a check engine light. Solution: Replace the faulty shift solenoid. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
* **Transmission cooler line leak:** Symptoms include transmission fluid leak and overheating transmission. Solution: Replace the faulty transmission cooler line. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Suspension**
* **Front suspension noise:** Symptoms include a clunking or rattling noise from the front suspension when driving over bumps. Solution: Replace the worn or damaged suspension components (e.g., ball joints, control arms). Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600 per component
* **Rear suspension sag:** Symptoms include a noticeable drop in the rear of the truck. Solution: Replace the worn or damaged rear leaf springs or air springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200
* **Steering wheel vibration:** Symptoms include a vibration in the steering wheel at highway speeds. Solution: Balance and rotate the tires and/or replace any bent or damaged suspension components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200 per tire

**Electrical**
* **Battery drain:** Symptoms include difficulty starting the truck or a dead battery without any accessories turned on. Solution: Diagnose and repair the electrical system to identify the source of the drain (e.g., faulty alternator, parasitic draw). Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Headlight condensation:** Symptoms include moisture buildup inside the headlight assembly. Solution: Replace the headlight assembly or repair the seal. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per headlight

**Exterior**
* **Rust on the frame:** Symptoms include visible rust spots on the truck's frame. Solution: Clean and repaint the affected areas or replace the rusted frame section. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+ per section
* **Tailgate latch failure:** Symptoms include difficulty opening or closing the tailgate. Solution: Replace the tailgate latch or cable. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Interior**
* **Climate control system failure:** Symptoms include malfunctioning air conditioner, heater, or ventilation. Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty component or replace the climate control module. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
* **Instrument cluster failure:** Symptoms include flickering or blank gauges, or inaccurate readings. Solution: Replace the instrument cluster. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
* **Rear seat release failure:** Symptoms include difficulty folding or lowering the rear seats. Solution: Replace the rear seat release cable or handle. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Summary**
The 2022 Chevrolet Cheyenne has a mixed record regarding reliability. While it offers some desirable features and capabilities, it has been plagued by several reported issues across its various components. The most common problems are related to the engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical systems. Some issues, like intake manifold gasket failure and cam phaser failure, can be expensive to fix. However, many of the reported issues are relatively minor and can be addressed for a reasonable cost. Overall, the severity and cost of repairs for the Cheyenne vary depending on the specific issue encountered.
