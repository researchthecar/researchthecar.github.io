**Introduction:**

The 2012 Chevrolet Cheyenne is a full-size pickup truck that comes standard with a 5.3L V8 engine and a 6-speed automatic transmission. Other engine options include a 6.2L V8 and a 6.6L Duramax diesel V8. Transmission options include an 8-speed automatic and a 10-speed automatic. The Cheyenne is known for its powerful engines, spacious cabin, and comfortable ride. However, it has also been plagued by a number of issues.

**Engine**

**5.3L V8**

* **AFM lifter failure:** Lifters in the engine can fail, causing a ticking noise and reduced engine power. The issue is covered by a GM recall (#14367) and can be fixed for free.
* **Intake manifold gasket leak:** The intake manifold gasket can leak, causing a vacuum leak and reduced engine performance. The gasket can be replaced for around $500 - $1,000.
* **Timing chain failure:** The timing chain can fail, causing catastrophic engine damage. The chain can be replaced for around $2,000 - $4,000.

**6.2L V8**

* **Piston ring failure:** Piston rings can fail, causing excessive oil consumption and reduced engine power. The engine may need to be rebuilt or replaced, costing around $5,000 - $10,000+.

**6.6L Duramax Diesel V8**

* **Injector failure:** Injectors can fail, causing rough idling and reduced engine power. Injectors can be replaced for around $500 - $1,000 each.
* **Turbocharger failure:** Turbochargers can fail, causing reduced engine power and increased exhaust smoke. Turbochargers can be replaced for around $2,000 - $4,000.

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Torque converter shudder:** The torque converter can shudder, causing vibrations and reduced acceleration. The torque converter can be replaced for around $1,000 - $2,000.
* **Shifting problems:** The transmission may have difficulty shifting gears or may slip out of gear. The transmission may need to be rebuilt or replaced, costing around $2,000 - $5,000.

**8-Speed Automatic**

* **Jerking:** The transmission may jerk or hesitate when shifting gears. The issue can be resolved with a software update.
* **Delayed engagement:** The transmission may take a long time to engage when the vehicle is put into gear. The issue can be resolved with a software update.

**10-Speed Automatic**

* **No issues found**

**Suspension**

* **Rear axle seals:** The rear axle seals can leak, causing a loss of fluid and damage to the rear axle. The seals can be replaced for around $500 - $1,000.
* **Leaf spring failure:** The leaf springs can fail, causing the rear of the vehicle to sag and reduce its stability. The leaf springs can be replaced for around $1,000 - $2,000.

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** The battery may drain prematurely, leaving the vehicle unable to start. The issue can be caused by a variety of factors, including faulty wiring, parasitic drains, and a weak alternator. The cost to fix the issue will vary depending on the cause.
* **Headlight failure:** The headlights may fail to turn on or may flicker. The issue can be caused by a faulty headlight switch, wiring, or bulbs. The cost to fix the issue will vary depending on the cause.

**Exterior**

* **Bedliner peeling:** The bedliner may peel or crack, exposing the metal bed to the elements. The bedliner can be replaced for around $500 - $1,000.
* **Clear coat failure:** The clear coat on the paint may fail, causing the paint to fade and chip. The clear coat can be reapplied for around $500 - $1,000+.

**Interior**

* **Climate control issues:** The climate control system may not work properly, causing the vehicle to be too hot or too cold. The issue can be caused by a variety of factors, including faulty sensors, actuators, and wiring. The cost to fix the issue will vary depending on the cause.
* **Instrument cluster failure:** The instrument cluster may fail, causing the speedometer, tachometer, and other gauges to stop working. The instrument cluster can be replaced for around $500 - $1,000.
* **Seat belt retractors:** The seat belt retractors may not work properly, causing the seat belts to become loose or jammed. The retractors can be replaced for around $200 - $500 each.

**Overall Summary:**

The 2012 Chevrolet Cheyenne has a number of serious issues that can affect its reliability, performance, and safety. These issues can be costly to fix, and some may require major repairs. While the Cheyenne is a popular vehicle, it is important to be aware of its potential problems before purchasing one.
