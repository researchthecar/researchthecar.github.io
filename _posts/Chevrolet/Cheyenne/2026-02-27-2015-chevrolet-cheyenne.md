# 2015 Chevrolet Cheyenne

### Overview
The 2015 Chevrolet Cheyenne, a robust full-size pickup truck, generally exhibits good reliability, reflecting Chevrolet's commitment to durability in its truck lineup. Owners often praise its strong performance, comfortable ride for its class, and capable hauling and towing features. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected repairs, like any vehicle, certain components can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems reported by owners and technicians, providing insights into potential areas of concern and their typical resolutions. The Cheyenne's mechanical robustness is a key selling point, and this data aims to offer a balanced perspective on its long-term dependability.

## Engine

### 5.3L EcoTec3 V8
**Active Fuel Management (AFM) Lifter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Engine misfires, rough idle, reduced power, ticking or knocking noise from the engine.
- **Solution:** Replacement of lifters, camshaft, and potentially the oil pump. Some solutions involve disabling AFM.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (6L80/6L90)
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering or slipping during acceleration, delayed engagement, harsh shifting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the torque converter and potentially transmission fluid flush.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Touchscreen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment screen becomes unresponsive, freezes, or restarts intermittently.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,000


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dies unexpectedly, requiring jump starts; electrical components may malfunction.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to find parasitic draw, repair or replace faulty module (e.g., BCM, radio).
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Tailgate Handle
**Broken Tailgate Handle**
- **Symptoms:** Tailgate handle becomes loose or breaks, making it difficult to open the tailgate.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the tailgate handle assembly.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300


## Interior

### Seat Adjusters
**Power Seat Adjuster Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Power seat motors fail to move the seat forward, backward, or adjust height.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the seat motor or switch.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


### HVAC Blower Motor
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air coming from vents, or air only comes out at high speeds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor or resistor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2015 Chevrolet Cheyenne is a generally reliable truck, especially considering its heavy-duty nature. The powertrain, particularly the 6.2L V8 and the 6-speed automatic transmission, shows good durability. The most commonly cited issues revolve around the Active Fuel Management system in the 5.3L engine, which can lead to costly repairs if not addressed promptly. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment system glitches and intermittent battery drain, are also noted but are not pervasive. Minor issues with exterior components like tailgate handles and interior parts like power seat adjusters and HVAC blowers are present but typically straightforward to fix. Overall, with proper maintenance and awareness of potential AFM concerns, the 2015 Cheyenne offers a dependable ownership experience for truck buyers.
