**About the 2019 Chevrolet Cheyenne**

The 2019 Chevrolet Cheyenne is a full-size pickup truck that is offered with a variety of engine and transmission options. Engine choices include a 5.3L V8, a 6.2L V8, and a 3.0L Duramax turbo-diesel V6. Transmission options include a 6-speed automatic, an 8-speed automatic, and a 10-speed automatic.

The Cheyenne is praised for its powerful engines, spacious cabin, and comfortable ride. However, it is also criticized for its high fuel consumption and lack of some advanced safety features.

**Engine**

**5.3L V8**

**Dynamic Fuel Management (DFM) Lifter Failure**
* Description/Symptoms: This issue affects engines equipped with Dynamic Fuel Management (DFM). The lifters can fail, causing a ticking noise and reduced engine power.
* Solution: The lifters need to be replaced.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**6.2L V8**

**No Issues Found**

**3.0L Duramax Turbo-Diesel V6**

**Fuel Injector Failure**
* Description/Symptoms: This issue affects engines equipped with the 3.0L Duramax turbo-diesel V6. The fuel injectors can fail, causing rough idling, reduced power, and increased emissions.
* Solution: The fuel injectors need to be replaced.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
* Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

**Transmission Slipping**
* Description/Symptoms: This issue affects vehicles equipped with the 6-speed automatic transmission. The transmission may slip when shifting gears, causing a loss of power and acceleration.
* Solution: The transmission may need to be rebuilt or replaced.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**8-Speed Automatic**

**Torque Converter Lockup Issue**
* Description/Symptoms: This issue affects vehicles equipped with the 8-speed automatic transmission. The torque converter may not lock up properly, causing a vibration and reduced fuel efficiency.
* Solution: The torque converter may need to be replaced.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**10-Speed Automatic**

**No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

**Rear Leaf Spring Failure**
* Description/Symptoms: This issue affects vehicles equipped with rear leaf springs. The rear leaf springs can fail, causing the vehicle to sag and lose stability.
* Solution: The rear leaf springs need to be replaced.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Battery Drain**
* Description/Symptoms: This issue affects vehicles equipped with a specific version of the electrical system. The battery can drain quickly, even when the vehicle is turned off.
* Solution: The electrical system may need to be reprogrammed or a new battery may need to be installed.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Headlight Condensation**
* Description/Symptoms: This issue affects vehicles equipped with certain headlights. Condensation can form inside the headlights, reducing visibility.
* Solution: The headlights may need to be replaced.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Climate Control Malfunction**
* Description/Symptoms: This issue affects vehicles equipped with the automatic climate control system. The system may not function properly, causing the temperature to fluctuate or the air to not blow.
* Solution: The climate control module may need to be replaced.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
* Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2019 Chevrolet Cheyenne is a capable and comfortable full-size pickup truck. However, it is important to be aware of the potential issues that have been reported with the vehicle. Some of the issues, such as the fuel injector failure and transmission slipping, can be serious and costly to fix. It is recommended that potential buyers research these issues thoroughly before purchasing a 2019 Chevrolet Cheyenne.
