# 2018 Chevrolet Cheyenne

### Overview
The 2018 Chevrolet Cheyenne, a robust full-size pickup truck, generally exhibits strong reliability, reflecting Chevrolet's commitment to durability in its truck lineup. Owners often praise its capable powertrains and solid construction. While no vehicle is entirely without potential concerns, the Cheyenne for this model year has demonstrated a good track record. Common areas of attention tend to be related to specific powertrain configurations or electronic features, rather than widespread systemic failures. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring the longevity and optimal performance of this workhorse, which is designed for demanding tasks and everyday utility.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, freezes, or restarts randomly.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system reset, or replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery dies unexpectedly, often traced to parasitic draw from certain modules.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the source of the draw, repair or replacement of faulty modules or wiring.
- **Cost:** $150 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 Chevrolet Cheyenne generally offers good reliability, particularly with its V8 engine options. The most frequently cited issues revolve around the 8-speed automatic transmission, which can sometimes exhibit harsh shifting, and occasional electrical glitches with the infotainment system or battery drain. These issues, while not universal, are worth noting for potential buyers. The suspension, exterior, and interior components have proven to be quite durable. With proactive maintenance and addressing any early signs of transmission or electrical anomalies, the 2018 Cheyenne remains a dependable choice for a full-size pickup.
