**Introduction**

The 2015 Chevrolet Cheyenne is a full-size pickup truck offered with an array of engine and transmission options. The base engine is a 4.3L V6 with 285 horsepower and 305 lb-ft of torque. There are two available V8 engine options: a 5.3L with 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, and a 6.2L with 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 6-speed automatic, an 8-speed automatic, and a 10-speed automatic.

Owners of the Cheyenne appreciate its spacious cabin, powerful engine options, and impressive towing and hauling capabilities. However, some have encountered issues with various components of the vehicle.

**Engine**

**4.3L V6**

* **Oil Consumption:** Excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Piston ring replacement or engine overhaul
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: #15V702000
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** The timing chain tensioner may fail, causing the timing chain to skip or break.
    * Solution: Timing chain replacement and tensioner upgrade
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**5.3L V8**

* **Active Fuel Management (AFM) Lifter Failure:** The AFM lifters may fail, causing engine noise and decreased performance.
    * Solution: Lifter replacement and AFM deactivation
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Fuel Pump Failure:** The fuel pump may fail, causing engine stalling and loss of power.
    * Solution: Fuel pump replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**6.2L V8**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Lockup Clutch Failure:** The torque converter lockup clutch may fail, causing transmission shuddering and slippage.
    * Solution: Torque converter replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800
    * Recalls: None Found

**8-Speed Automatic**

* **Shift Hesitation and Jerking:** The transmission may exhibit hesitation and jerking during shifts.
    * Solution: Transmission software update
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Free of charge if under warranty
    * Recalls: None Found

**10-Speed Automatic**

* **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Front Control Arm Bushing Wear:** The front control arm bushings may wear prematurely, leading to excessive noise and vibration.
    * Solution: Control arm replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500 per side
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Leaf Spring Breakage:** The rear leaf springs may break, causing the vehicle to sag or limit towing capabilities.
    * Solution: Leaf spring replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000 per side
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** The battery may drain excessively, especially when the vehicle is parked.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of electrical system
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator may fail, causing the vehicle to lose power and stall.
    * Solution: Alternator replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Actuator Failure:** The door lock actuator may fail, causing the doors to become inoperable.
    * Solution: Actuator replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per door
    * Recalls: #15V350000, #15V701000

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Squeaks and Rattles:** The dashboard may develop squeaks and rattles over time.
    * Solution: Inspection and tightening of loose components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue
    * Recalls: None Found
* **HVAC Malfunction:** The HVAC system may experience issues such as fan noise, temperature fluctuations, or blower motor failure.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair or replacement of affected components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, there are a range of issues reported by owners of the 2015 Chevrolet Cheyenne. While some issues are minor, others can be more severe and expensive to repair. It's important to note that not all vehicles will experience these issues, and the frequency and severity of problems can vary.
