**1995 Chevrolet S10**

The 1995 Chevrolet S10 is a compact pickup truck that was available in regular cab, extended cab, and crew cab configurations. It was offered with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 2.2L four-cylinder, a 4.3L V6, and a 5.7L V8. The S10 was praised for its versatility, affordability, and towing capacity, but it was also criticized for its lack of interior space and refinement.

**Engine issues**

**2.2L four-cylinder engine**
* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and reduced engine performance. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $200-$400. Recalls: None found.

**4.3L V6 engine**
* **Head gasket failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and white smoke from the exhaust. Solution: Replace the head gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000-$2,000. Recalls: None found.

**5.7L V8 engine**
* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and reduced engine performance. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $200-$400. Recalls: None found.

**Transmission issues**

**5-speed manual transmission**
* **Shifting problems:** Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises, and popping out of gear. Solution: Repair or replace the transmission. Estimated cost to fix: $500-$1,500. Recalls: None found.

**4-speed automatic transmission**
* **Slipping gears:** Symptoms include loss of power, delayed engagement, and shuddering. Solution: Repair or replace the transmission. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000-$2,500. Recalls: None found.

**Suspension issues**
* **Front ball joint failure:** Symptoms include clunking noises, loose steering, and uneven tire wear. Solution: Replace the ball joints. Estimated cost to fix: $200-$400 per side. Recalls: None found.

**Electrical issues**
* **Faulty ignition switch:** Symptoms include difficulty starting the engine, flickering lights, and dead battery. Solution: Replace the ignition switch. Estimated cost to fix: $100-$200. Recalls: None found.

**Exterior issues**
* **Rust on the body panels:** Symptoms include unsightly rust spots and holes. Solution: Repair or replace the affected panels. Estimated cost to fix: Varies depending on the extent of the damage. Recalls: None found.

**Interior issues**
* **Cracked or broken dashboard:** Symptoms include unsightly cracks or breaks in the dashboard. Solution: Replace the dashboard. Estimated cost to fix: $300-$600. Recalls: None found.

**Summary**

The 1995 Chevrolet S10 is a versatile and affordable pickup truck, but it is not without its issues. The most common problems include engine gasket failures, transmission shifting problems, suspension ball joint failure, electrical issues, rust on the body panels, and a cracked or broken dashboard. These issues can be costly to repair, but they are not typically severe enough to cause major safety concerns.
