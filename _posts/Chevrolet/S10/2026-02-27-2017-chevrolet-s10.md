# 2017 Chevrolet S10 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a used Chevrolet S10 requires keeping in mind that the model year referenced falls outside of the standard North American production run, meaning buyers should carefully verify vehicle history and specifications. While compact trucks offer great utility, evaluating individual mechanical health remains paramount when considering older or internationally-marketed configurations.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder

This standard four-cylinder engine provides basic power suited for light utility tasks. It is generally straightforward to maintain, though buyers should check for standard signs of oil leaks and cooling system wear.

### 2.8L Duramax Turbo-Diesel

Offering increased torque for towing and hauling, this diesel option is appreciated for its pulling capability. Buyers should verify proper maintenance history, especially regarding the emissions equipment and turbocharger.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual

The manual transmission offers direct gear control and is typically durable. Prospective buyers should test the clutch engagement and ensure smooth shifts across all gears.

### 6-Speed Automatic

This automatic transmission provides relaxed daily driving convenience. A thorough test drive is recommended to check for delayed engagement or rough shifting behavior.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system is critical for safety, and any reports of pedal softness require immediate inspection.

**Brake Pedal Fade**
- **Symptoms:** An owner reported that the brake pedal traveled all the way to the floor and failed to stop the vehicle within a safe distance at low speeds, with pumping having no effect.
- **Solution:** Inspect the master cylinder, brake lines, and fluid condition for leaks or internal failure, and replace components as necessary.
