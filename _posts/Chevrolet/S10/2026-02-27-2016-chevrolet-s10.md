# 2016 Chevrolet S10 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Chevrolet S10, buyers should approach the market with careful inspection habits, particularly regarding mechanical systems and safety equipment. While owner experiences vary, understanding the common trouble spots for this model year can help you navigate a purchase with greater confidence.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L I4

This 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine delivers modest horsepower and torque well-suited for light-duty tasks. It generally offers straightforward mechanical reliability for everyday commuting and basic utility.

### 2.8L Duramax Turbo-Diesel I4

The 2.8-liter turbo-diesel engine provides strong low-end torque and enhanced towing capability for its class. Routine maintenance and fuel system inspections are vital for long-term ownership satisfaction.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual

The six-speed manual transmission offers direct gear selection and a traditional driving feel. Overall durability is generally solid provided the clutch has been maintained properly.

### 6-Speed Automatic

This six-speed automatic transmission is paired with various engine options to balance highway efficiency and utility work. Routine fluid changes help preserve smooth shifting behavior over high mileage.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Braking components require regular inspection to ensure stopping power remains consistent under both normal and low-speed driving conditions.

**Low-Speed Brake Pedal Hardness**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported that while parking or maneuvering at low speeds, the brakes suddenly lose power, the pedal becomes very hard to press, and the braking effectiveness is severely reduced accompanied by an ABS-like noise.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the brake booster, vacuum lines, and anti-lock braking system components for faults.
