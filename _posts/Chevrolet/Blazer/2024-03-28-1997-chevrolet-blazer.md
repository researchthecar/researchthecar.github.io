**Introduction:**

The 1997 Chevrolet Blazer was a popular mid-size SUV available with three engine options and two transmission choices. The lineup included a 4.3L V6, a 5.7L V8, and a 6.5L V8. The 4.3L V6 produced 190 horsepower and 250 lb-ft of torque, while the 5.7L V8 generated 255 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque. The 6.5L V8 offered 270 horsepower and 385 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options included a 4-speed automatic and a 5-speed manual. The Blazer was known for its robust construction, spacious interior, and off-road capabilities. However, it also had its share of issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

**4.3L V6**
**Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Leaking coolant, overheating engine, poor performance
Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**5.7L V8**
**Vortec Engine Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the valve covers, causing a burning oil smell and ticking noise
Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**6.5L V8**
**Fuel Injector Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Engine misfiring, poor acceleration, increased fuel consumption
Solution: Replace the faulty fuel injectors
Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
Recalls: #97V180000

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**
**Transmission Slipping**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, loss of power under acceleration
Solution: Repair or replace the transmission
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,500+

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**
**Clutch Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, slipping clutch
Solution: Replace the clutch
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Suspension**

**Front Ball Joints**
Description/Symptoms: Loose steering, clunking noise when turning, uneven tire wear
Solution: Replace the ball joints
Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+

**Electrical**

**Alternator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, battery not charging, electrical problems
Solution: Replace the alternator
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**Exterior**

**Door Lock Actuator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty locking or unlocking doors, stuck doors
Solution: Replace the door lock actuators
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Interior**

**Window Regulator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Windows not functioning properly, stuck or broken windows
Solution: Replace the window regulators
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Summary:**

The 1997 Chevrolet Blazer had some common issues that could impact its reliability. Engine problems, transmission issues, and suspension problems were the most prevalent. The severity of the issues varied, with some being relatively minor and inexpensive to fix. However, more significant problems like engine failure or transmission replacement could result in substantial repair costs. Overall, the Blazer was a capable vehicle, but potential buyers should be aware of these known issues to make informed decisions about ownership.
