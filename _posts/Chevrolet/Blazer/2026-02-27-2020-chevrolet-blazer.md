# 2020 Chevrolet Blazer Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Chevrolet Blazer combines sharp, aggressive styling with sporty handling, making it an appealing midsize crossover option on the used market. However, prospective buyers should review common owner-reported complaints carefully, particularly regarding electrical glitches and powertrain behavior. While it offers an engaging drive and comfortable cabin, checking service history for unresolved thermostat, steering assist, and shifter sensor issues is strongly recommended.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

A naturally aspirated 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine producing approximately 193 horsepower and 188 lb-ft of torque, serving as the base power plant for front-wheel-drive models.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

A turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine delivering around 230 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, offering a stronger blend of acceleration and efficiency for mid-tier trims.

### 3.6L V6 Engine

A robust 3.6-liter V6 engine pushing 308 horsepower and 270 lb-ft of torque, providing strong performance for higher trims and towing duties, though tied to recurring thermostat sensor complaints.

**Thermostat and Cooling System Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report the temperature gauge dropping and staying at the 'cold' position, accompanied by dashboard warnings such as 'Steering Assist Reduced' and loss of proper cabin heating or air conditioning.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty thermostat and associated cooling components to restore proper engine temperature regulation and clear related electronic warnings.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

A 9-speed automatic transmission paired across the Blazer lineup, designed to balance highway efficiency with responsive gear selection, though some owners report early mechanical and shifting faults.

**Transmission Slipping and Hard Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience rough shifts, unexpected jerking during acceleration, sudden drops in power, or noticeable transmission slipping while navigating traffic.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the transmission control module, fluid condition, and internal components for wear or mechanical failure.

## Electrical

### Shift-to-Park Sensor

Handles internal electronic communication, gear selector sensing, and accessory power management, with recurring sensitivity in park-detection circuitry.

**False Shift-to-Park Warnings and Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle displays a persistent 'Shift to Park' message even when the gear selector is fully in the park position, often preventing the vehicle from shutting off completely and draining the battery.
- **Solution:** Replace the defective shifter microswitch or sensor assembly responsible for detecting the park position.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V115000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Blazer&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Over cured tires may develop a break in the sidewall resulting in sudden air loss or a belt edge separation which could lead to a tread/belt loss. Either condition can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the tires, replacing them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V446000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain:Inflator](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Blazer&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The diffuser component of the Roof-Rail Air Bag (RRAB) inflator may not have been properly crimped to the inflator and could separate from the inflator during air bag deployment. If the diffuser separates from the inflator during deployment, RRAB performance may be degraded, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the suspect RRAB modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V668000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Blazer&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Missing bolts on the start-stop accumulator endcap could result in a transmission oil leak and may progress to a loss of propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash. A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the start-stop transmission accumulator and replace it if any bolts are missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Chevrolet Blazer is a stylish and generally capable crossover, but buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to whether the vehicle has experienced cooling system faults, steering assist warnings triggered by thermostat failures, or annoying 'Shift to Park' electrical glitches. Securing a detailed maintenance history will help ensure these common issues have been properly addressed.
