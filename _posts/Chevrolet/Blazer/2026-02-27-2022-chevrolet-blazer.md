# 2022 Chevrolet Blazer Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Chevrolet Blazer offers bold styling, agile handling, and comfortable daily driving dynamics. However, prospective buyers should review common owner-reported issues affecting select powertrain components, electronics, and cooling systems.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4 Engine

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine produces approximately 228 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, providing a solid balance of efficiency and power for daily driving.

### 3.6L V6 Engine

The 3.6-liter naturally aspirated V6 engine delivers around 308 horsepower and 270 lb-ft of torque, offering robust acceleration and strong towing capability for the midsize crossover.

### Engine Cooling

Cooling system components manage operating temperatures for both available engine configurations.

**Radiator Coolant Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report smelling engine coolant and discovering leaks near the top seams where the aluminum meets the plastic tank on the radiator.
- **Solution:** Inspect the radiator for cracks or seam separation and replace the radiator assembly if a leak is confirmed.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The nine-speed automatic transmission pairs with both engine options to distribute power smoothly across front-wheel or all-wheel drive systems.

**Transmission Slippage and Internal Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience severe gear slipping, failure to shift past third gear, loss of reverse gear, or complete transmission failure while driving.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the transmission, replace damaged valve body components, or rebuild/replace the transmission assembly as needed.

## Suspension

### Suspension and Tires

Suspension components and wheels maintain ride comfort and road contact across varying driving surfaces.

**Premature Tire Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Original equipment tires exhibit rapid tread wear down to the wear bars well before expected mileage intervals.
- **Solution:** Check alignment settings regularly, rotate tires frequently, and replace worn tires with durable aftermarket alternatives.

## Steering

### Power Steering Assist

Electronic power steering systems provide adaptive steering resistance based on vehicle speed.

**Loss of Power Steering Assist**
- **Symptoms:** A 'Service Power Steering' warning message appears on the dash, and steering effort suddenly becomes heavy and less responsive.
- **Solution:** Scan the vehicle for diagnostic trouble codes, check steering module connections, and replace faulty electronic steering components.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Instrument Cluster

Cabin electronics manage infotainment, driver information displays, and vehicle safety features.

**Screen Freezing and Dashboard Blackout**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display freezes, volume controls malfunction, the backup camera stays active while driving forward, or the digital dashboard goes completely black.
- **Solution:** Perform a software update or replace the failing display module and control units.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V359000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Blazer&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** An improperly welded seat frame may not adequately restrain the driver in a crash, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the driver's seat and replace the cushion frame, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used model, buyers should thoroughly inspect the radiator for coolant leaks, test all infotainment and digital dash functions, and verify proper transmission operation during a test drive. Paying close attention to these specific areas can help secure a reliable vehicle.
