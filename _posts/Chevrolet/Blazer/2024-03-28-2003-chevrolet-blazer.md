**Introduction**

The 2003 Chevrolet Blazer is a mid-size SUV that was available with a variety of engine and transmission options. The 4.3L V6 engine produced 190 horsepower and 250 lb-ft of torque, while the 5.3L V8 engine produced 290 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were available with a 4-speed automatic transmission or a 5-speed manual transmission. The Blazer was praised for its spacious interior and comfortable ride, but it was also criticized for its poor fuel economy and handling.

**Engine**

* **4.3L V6**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaks from the intake manifold, causing overheating or engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Cylinder Head Gasket Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks into the cylinders, causing overheating and loss of power.
        * Solution: Replace the cylinder head gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **5.3L V8**
    * **Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
        * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the valve cover gaskets, causing engine oil loss.
        * Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Timing Chain Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain stretches or breaks, causing the engine to lose power or run rough.
        * Solution: Replace the timing chain and related components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **4-Speed Automatic**
    * **Slipping or Jerking**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission slips or jerks when shifting gears, causing difficulty accelerating or maintaining speed.
        * Solution: Overhaul or replace the transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **5-Speed Manual**
    * **Grinding Noise When Shifting**
        * Description/Symptoms: A grinding noise is heard when shifting gears, indicating worn synchronizers.
        * Solution: Replace the synchronizers.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
