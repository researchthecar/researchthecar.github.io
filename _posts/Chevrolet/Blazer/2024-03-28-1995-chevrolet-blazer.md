**1995 Chevrolet Blazer**

The 1995 Chevrolet Blazer was a popular mid-size SUV that offered a range of engine and transmission options. It was known for its rugged design, comfortable interior, and impressive off-road capabilities.

However, like any vehicle, the Blazer had its share of issues. Here is a comprehensive list of the specific problems reported by owners:

**Engine**
* **4.3L V6**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, rough idle, loss of power
        * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Oil Pressure Sensor Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Low oil pressure warning light, engine damage in severe cases
        * Solution: Replace oil pressure sensor
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150
        * Recalls: None Found
* **5.7L V8**
    * **Head Gasket Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, white smoke from exhaust, overheating
        * Solution: Replace head gaskets
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Transmission Cooler Line Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission fluid leaks, overheating, loss of gears
        * Solution: Replace transmission cooler lines
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
* **4L60E Automatic Transmission**
    * **Sun Shell Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Delayed engagement, slipping gears, no gears
        * Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
        * Recalls: None Found
* **NV3500 Manual Transmission**
    * **Clutch Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting, slipping gears, grinding noise
        * Solution: Replace clutch
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Input Shaft Bearing Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Whining noise, difficulty shifting, loss of gears
        * Solution: Replace input shaft bearing
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
* **Front Suspension Control Arm Bushing Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise, loose steering, uneven tire wear
    * Solution: Replace control arm bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Suspension Leaf Spring Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, harsh ride, loss of control
    * Solution: Replace leaf springs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dim headlights, difficulty starting, battery warning light
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Starter Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine clicks but won't start, no cranking noise
    * Solution: Replace starter
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
* **Rear Liftgate Hinge Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loose or sagging liftgate, difficulty opening or closing
    * Solution: Replace liftgate hinges
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
* **Dashboard Cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks on the dashboard, especially in hot or cold climates
    * Solution: Replace dashboard
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Cruise Control Button Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cruise control intermittently or not working
    * Solution: Replace cruise control button
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 1995 Chevrolet Blazer is a reliable and capable SUV. However, it does have some common issues that owners should be aware of. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but most can be repaired for a reasonable price.

The most common engine and transmission problems involve gaskets and sensors, while suspension issues may require more expensive repairs. Electrical and interior issues are generally less severe. Exterior issues may not be as crucial but can still be inconvenient.

Potential buyers should consider the age and mileage of the vehicle and have it inspected by a qualified mechanic before making a purchase. Regular maintenance and timely repairs will help keep the Blazer running smoothly for many years to come.
