**Introduction:**

The 1996 Chevrolet Blazer is a mid-size SUV known for its ruggedness and off-road capabilities. It was offered with various engine and transmission options, including:

* 4.3L V6 (190 hp, 250 lb-ft)
* 4.3L Vortec V6 (200 hp, 260 lb-ft)
* 5.7L V8 (255 hp, 330 lb-ft)
* 6-speed manual transmission
* 4-speed automatic transmission

Drivers appreciate the Blazer's spacious interior, powerful engines, and reliable performance. However, certain issues have been reported with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**4.3L V6**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** **Description/Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, engine overheating. **Solution:** Replace intake manifold gasket. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $600
* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** **Description/Symptoms:** Oil leaks from under valve covers. **Solution:** Replace valve cover gaskets. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $350

**4.3L Vortec V6**

* **Oil Consumption:** **Description/Symptoms:** Excessive oil consumption, blue smoke from exhaust. **Solution:** Replace piston rings, valve seals, or engine rebuild. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $3,000+
* **Knock Sensor Failure:** **Description/Symptoms:** Engine knocking, reduced performance. **Solution:** Replace knock sensor. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $200
* **Fuel Injector Failure:** **Description/Symptoms:** Engine misfire, rough idle. **Solution:** Replace fuel injectors. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500

**5.7L V8**

* **Head Gasket Failure:** **Description/Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, loss of power, overheating. **Solution:** Replace head gaskets. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $2,500

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Slipping Transmission:** **Description/Symptoms:** Transmission slipping gears, loss of power. **Solution:** Rebuild or replace transmission. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Solenoid Failure:** **Description/Symptoms:** Transmission shifting problems, erratic behavior. **Solution:** Replace solenoids. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $500

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Wear:** **Description/Symptoms:** Noises, vibrations, poor handling. **Solution:** Replace worn components (ball joints, bushings, struts). **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** **Description/Symptoms:** Battery not charging, electrical problems. **Solution:** Replace alternator. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $500
* **Starter Failure:** **Description/Symptoms:** Engine not starting, clicking noises. **Solution:** Replace starter. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Body Panels:** **Description/Symptoms:** Rust spots, peeling paint. **Solution:** Rust repair or panel replacement. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $2,000+

**Interior**

* **Window Switch Failure:** **Description/Symptoms:** Power windows not working, intermittent operation. **Solution:** Replace window switch. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $200
* **HVAC Blower Motor Failure:** **Description/Symptoms:** No air flow from vents, overheating. **Solution:** Replace blower motor. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $350

**Summary:**

Overall, the 1996 Chevrolet Blazer has mixed reliability ratings. Some issues, such as engine leaks and transmission problems, can be severe and costly to repair. However, the vehicle offers good performance and off-road capabilities.
