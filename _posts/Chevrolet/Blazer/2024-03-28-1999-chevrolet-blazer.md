**Introduction**

The 1999 Chevrolet Blazer was a mid-size SUV offered with a choice of two engines and two transmissions. The standard engine was a 4.3L V6 that produced 190 horsepower and 250 lb-ft of torque, while the optional engine was a 5.7L V8 that produced 250 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were paired with a four-speed automatic transmission. The Blazer was available in three trim levels: Base, LS, and LT.

People who like the 1999 Chevrolet Blazer appreciate its spacious interior, powerful engines, and off-road capabilities. However, some people have expressed concerns about its fuel economy, reliability, and outdated design.

**Engine**

**4.3L V6**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaking from the intake manifold, rough idle, decreased engine performance
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from the valve covers, burning oil smell
    * Solution: Replace valve cover gaskets
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5.7L V8**

* **Oil Pressure Sensor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Low oil pressure light illuminated, engine knocking or ticking
    * Solution: Replace oil pressure sensor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Crankshaft Position Sensor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine stalls or hesitates, difficult to start
    * Solution: Replace crankshaft position sensor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slippage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping out of gear, delayed gear engagement, shuddering
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Torque Converter Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission shuddering, slipping, or overheating
    * Solution: Replace torque converter
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Worn Front Control Arm Bushings:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noise over bumps, uneven tire wear
    * Solution: Replace control arm bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Leaking Rear Shock Absorbers:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking fluid, reduced shock absorption, bouncy ride
    * Solution: Replace rear shock absorbers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Faulty Ignition Lock Cylinder:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the vehicle, key getting stuck or breaking off
    * Solution: Replace ignition lock cylinder
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Defective Turn Signal Switch:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Turn signals not working or blinking erratically
    * Solution: Replace turn signal switch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Frame and Undercarriage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust forming on the metal components beneath the vehicle
    * Solution: Clean and treat rust, apply protective coatings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ depending on severity
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Cracked Windshield:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Crack or damage to the windshield
    * Solution: Replace windshield
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ depending on size and type of windshield
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Broken Window Regulator:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Window not rolling up or down properly
    * Solution: Replace window regulator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Faulty Dashboard Cluster:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Gauges not working or displaying incorrect information
    * Solution: Replace dashboard cluster
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1999 Chevrolet Blazer has some potential issues that buyers should be aware of. The engine and transmission issues can be costly to repair, while the suspension and electrical issues are less severe but still require attention. The rust issue is particularly concerning and can affect the overall safety and value of the vehicle. Overall, the Blazer is a capable SUV but it may require some maintenance and repairs to keep it in good condition.
