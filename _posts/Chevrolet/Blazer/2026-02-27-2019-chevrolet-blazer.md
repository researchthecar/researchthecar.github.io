# 2019 Chevrolet Blazer Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Chevrolet Blazer brings sharp styling and engaging driving dynamics to the midsize crossover segment. However, prospective buyers should carefully review known owner complaints concerning electronic quirks and specific mechanical annoyances before purchasing a used model. While its powertrain options generally offer a solid daily driving experience, being aware of recurring pattern failures can save you from unexpected repair bills and frustration.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L I4

The base 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine produces 193 horsepower and 188 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate efficiency for daily commuting. It offers straightforward, conventional reliability for buyers prioritizing fuel economy over spirited performance.

### 3.6L V6

The robust 3.6-liter V6 engine generates 308 horsepower and 270 lb-ft of torque, giving the Blazer swift acceleration and confident highway passing power. This engine is generally durable, though owners should monitor routine maintenance and cooling system components.

### Thermostat and Cooling System

Engine auxiliary systems operate reliably overall, but cooling system sensor anomalies can unexpectedly trigger secondary driver-assist warning messages.

**Thermostat Failure Triggering Steering Assist Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant temperature gauge reads cold, OBD-II codes such as P0128 appear, and the dash displays a 'steering assist reduced, drive with care' warning message alongside stiffer steering.
- **Solution:** Replace the electronically controlled thermostat and associated coolant temperature sensors.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic

Paired across the lineup, the nine-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth shifts and highway cruising efficiency. While generally capable, early production models require careful inspection for fluid leaks or torque converter performance concerns.

**Transmission Fluid Leaks and Shifting Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Fluid pooling under the vehicle, difficulty engaging reverse, or hesitation and slippage during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission seals, lines, and fluid levels; repair leaking components or service the transmission as required by a qualified mechanic.

## Electrical

### Shift-to-Park Sensor

electrical issues in this vehicle frequently center around cabin electronics and sensor miscommunications, most notably involving the gear selector switch.

**Persistent 'Shift to Park' Warning and Ignition Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle displays a 'Shift to Park' message on the dash despite being fully in park, preventing the car from shutting off completely and draining the battery.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty shifter assembly or microswitch responsible for detecting the gear position.
- **Cost:** $600+

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V115000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Blazer&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Over cured tires may develop a break in the sidewall resulting in sudden air loss or a belt edge separation which could lead to a tread/belt loss. Either condition can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the tires, replacing them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V668000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Blazer&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Missing bolts on the start-stop accumulator endcap could result in a transmission oil leak and may progress to a loss of propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash. A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the start-stop transmission accumulator and replace it if any bolts are missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Chevrolet Blazer is an attractive used crossover with distinct styling and capable engine options, but buyers should perform a thorough inspection prior to purchase. Pay close attention to whether the vehicle properly registers park mode and test all climate control and steering functions to rule out common sensor and thermostat faults. Securing a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection can help verify that these common electronic and cooling system issues have been addressed.
