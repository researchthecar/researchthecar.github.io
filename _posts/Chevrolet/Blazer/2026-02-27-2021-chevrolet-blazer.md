# 2021 Chevrolet Blazer Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Chevrolet Blazer offers bold styling and a comfortable cabin, but prospective used-car buyers should carefully review potential mechanical and electrical trouble spots. Owner complaints to federal safety regulators highlight recurring issues ranging from cooling system malfunctions and fuel pump failures to intermittent brake and steering concerns. Inspecting service records and verifying whether powertrain or fuel system components have been addressed is recommended before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder

A naturally aspirated 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine making approximately 193 horsepower, serving as the base powerplant for lower-tier front-wheel-drive trims with dependable daily efficiency.

### 2.0L Turbo 4-Cylinder

A turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine producing around 227 horsepower, offering a strong balance of low-end torque and efficiency for mid-level trims.

### 3.6L V6

A robust 3.6-liter V6 engine generating 308 horsepower, providing brisk acceleration and strong towing capability for higher trims, though some owners have reported cooling and fuel module concerns.

**Thermostat and Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** A malfunctioning thermostat can trigger temperature gauge failures, cause cooling fans to run continuously after the engine is shut off, and lead to coolant leaks.
- **Solution:** Inspect the cooling system for leaks, replace faulty thermostats, and ensure sensors are functioning correctly.

**Fuel Pump Control Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may crank but fail to start without prior warning, often leaving drivers stranded and requiring a tow to a repair facility.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fuel system error codes and replace the failed fuel pump control module.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

A nine-speed automatic transmission paired across all engine options, designed to optimize fuel economy and highway cruising, though a subset of owners have experienced rough shifting and engagement delays.

**Harsh Shifting and Engagement Delay**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience delayed engagement when shifting into reverse, harsh jerking during acceleration, or instances where the vehicle operates as if stuck in neutral.
- **Solution:** Perform transmission diagnostics, check fluid condition, and consult a dealer for software updates or mechanical valve body repairs.

## Brakes

### Brake System

The service and parking brake components provide adequate stopping power under normal conditions, but isolated reports of brake booster and pedal failures warrant a careful pre-purchase inspection.

**Brake Pedal Stiffness and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal can suddenly become very stiff, lose responsiveness, or travel all the way to the floor, significantly increasing stopping distances.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake booster and hydraulic system thoroughly, and replace failed brake boosters or master cylinders.

## Steering

### Power Steering

The electronic power steering system delivers light and responsive handling at low speeds, though some owners have encountered unexpected reduced-assist warnings.

**Steering Assist Reduced Warning**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may receive a dashboard warning stating 'Steering Assist is Reduced' accompanied by heavier steering effort, often linked to electronic or thermostat-related faults.
- **Solution:** Scan diagnostic trouble codes to determine if the issue stems from an electrical sensor, thermostat fault, or electronic power steering rack failure.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V811000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Blazer&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** If an incorrect bolt was used, the seat belt may not be properly attached to the vehicle. If a seat belt assembly is not properly attached to the vehicle, the seat belt may not properly restrain an occupant in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the suspect bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2021 Chevrolet Blazer, prioritize models with documented maintenance records and verified service history. Pay close attention to cooling system performance, transmission shift quality, and proper brake responsiveness during a test drive. Checking for completed recall or bulletin repairs will help mitigate potential ownership surprises.
