**Introduction:**

The 1991 Chevrolet W6500 Tiltmaster is a heavy-duty dump truck with a 6.6L V8 diesel engine producing 190 horsepower and 420 lb-ft of torque. It is paired with a 6-speed manual transmission. Known for its durability and hauling capabilities, the W6500 is favored by construction and landscaping professionals. However, some issues have been reported by owners and mechanics over the years.

**Engine**

**6.6L V8 Diesel**

* **Fuel Injector Failure:** Injectors can become clogged or fail, causing rough idling, loss of power, and poor fuel economy.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty injectors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

* **Shift Linkage Malfunction:** The shift linkage can wear out or become misaligned, making it difficult or impossible to shift gears.
    * Solution: Adjust or replace the shift linkage components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Breakage:** The leaf springs can weaken over time, especially if overloaded or driven on rough roads.
    * Solution: Replace the broken leaf springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Shock Absorber Failure:** The shock absorbers can fail to dampen road vibrations, resulting in a rough and bouncy ride.
    * Solution: Replace the worn-out shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Problems:** The alternator can fail to charge the battery properly, leading to electrical issues and difficulty starting the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Wiring Harness Damage:** The wiring harness can become brittle or damaged, causing electrical shorts and various electrical malfunctions.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the damaged wiring.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of damage
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** The body panels and frame can rust over time, especially in areas with high moisture or road salt exposure.
    * Solution: Remove the rust and repair or replace the damaged panels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity of the rust

* **Headlight Failure:** The headlights can fail due to burnt-out bulbs or faulty wiring.
    * Solution: Replace the bulbs or repair the wiring.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Seat Belt Issues:** The seat belts can become frayed or fail to retract properly.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty seat belts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Dashboard Warning Lights:** The dashboard warning lights may malfunction or fail to illuminate when there is an actual issue with the vehicle.
    * Solution: Diagnose the cause of the malfunction and repair or replace the faulty components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue

**Summary:**

The 1991 Chevrolet W6500 Tiltmaster is generally a reliable vehicle, but like all vehicles, it can experience various issues over time. Some of the most common issues include fuel injector failure, shift linkage malfunction, leaf spring breakage, alternator problems, and rust. The severity and cost of repairs can vary depending on the issue and the extent of damage or malfunction.
