**2004 Chevrolet T7500**

The 2004 Chevrolet T7500 is a heavy-duty pickup truck that offers a combination of power, capability, and comfort. It is available with a range of engine and transmission options, including gasoline and diesel engines, and manual or automatic transmissions. Some of the popular options include:

* 6.0L L96 Vortec V8 gasoline engine (300 horsepower, 360 lb-ft of torque)
* 8.1L L18 Vortec V8 gasoline engine (340 horsepower, 455 lb-ft of torque)
* 6.6L LBZ Duramax V8 diesel engine (310 horsepower, 620 lb-ft of torque)
* 10L V10 Allison MD3560 6-speed automatic transmission
* 6-speed NV5600 manual transmission

**Engine**

**6.0L L96 Vortec V8**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Coolant and/or oil leak, reduced engine power, rough idle
Solution: Replace intake manifold gaskets
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Piston Slap**
Description/Symptoms: Knocking or rattling noise from the engine
Solution: Replace affected pistons
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000+
* **Engine Misfire**
Description/Symptoms: Rough running, loss of power, increased fuel consumption
Solution: Replace spark plugs, ignition coils, or fuel injectors
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**8.1L L18 Vortec V8**

* **Cylinder Head Gasket Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Coolant and/or oil leak, overheating
Solution: Replace cylinder head gaskets
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
* **Timing Chain Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Noisy engine, reduced power, check engine light
Solution: Replace timing chain and related components
Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
* **Fuel Pump Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Engine stalls, difficulty starting
Solution: Replace fuel pump
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700

**6.6L LBZ Duramax V8**

* **Injector Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Rough running, reduced power, increased fuel consumption
Solution: Replace affected injectors
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Turbocharger Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Reduced power, black smoke from exhaust
Solution: Replace turbocharger
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000
* **Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) Valve Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, increased emissions
Solution: Replace EGR valve
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Transmission**

**10L V10 Allison MD3560 6-speed Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Slipping or shuddering during gear changes
Solution: Replace torque converter
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
* **Transmission Fluid Leak**
Description/Symptoms: Low transmission fluid level, slipping gears
Solution: Repair or replace leaking component
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Solenoid Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Intermittent gear engagement, delayed shifts
Solution: Replace affected solenoid
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700

**6-speed NV5600 Manual**

* **Clutch Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, slipping
Solution: Replace clutch
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200
* **Transmission Bearing Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Noisy transmission, difficulty shifting
Solution: Replace affected bearing
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
* **Synchronizer Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Grinding noise when shifting gears
Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Ball Joint Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Clunking or popping noise when turning, uneven tire wear
Solution: Replace ball joints
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per side
* **Rear Suspension Leaf Spring Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, reduced load-carrying capacity
Solution: Replace leaf springs
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000 per side
* **Steering Rack and Pinion Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty steering, loose steering feel
Solution: Replace steering rack and pinion
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging, dim lights
Solution: Replace alternator
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700
* **Battery Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Vehicle fails to start, electrical problems
Solution: Replace battery
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
* **Wiring Harness Damage**
Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical issues, check engine light
Solution: Repair or replace damaged wiring
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Rusty Frame**
Description/Symptoms: Corrosion on the vehicle frame
Solution: Replace affected frame section or repair corrosion
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $5,000+
* **Paint Peeling**
Description/Symptoms: Clear coat or paint peeling off the vehicle exterior
Solution: Repaint affected areas
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Door Handle Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening or closing doors
Solution: Replace door handles
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per door

**Interior**

* **Seat Bolster Wear**
Description/Symptoms: Worn or torn upholstery on the seat bolsters
Solution: Repair or replace seat upholstery
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **Air Conditioning Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Weak or no air conditioning
Solution: Repair or replace air conditioning compressor or other components
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Dashboard Rattles**
Description/Symptoms: Noises from dashboard or interior panels
Solution: Tighten or replace loose components
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300

**Summary**

The 2004 Chevrolet T7500 is a generally reliable vehicle, but like all vehicles, it has some potential issues. The most common issues are related to the engine, transmission, and suspension. The severity of these issues varies depending on the specific issue and how well the vehicle has been maintained. Overall, the cost of repairs for the T7500 can range from minor to significant, depending on the issue.
