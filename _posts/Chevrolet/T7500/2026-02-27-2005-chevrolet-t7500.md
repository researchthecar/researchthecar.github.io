## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Alternator Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Battery not charging, dimming lights, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator unit.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Wiring Harness Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical faults, non-functioning accessories, dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspection and repair or replacement of damaged wiring sections.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Trim
**Corrosion on Frame and Body**
- **Symptoms:** Rust formation, particularly in areas exposed to salt and moisture; potential weakening of structural components.
- **Solution:** Rust treatment, panel replacement, or undercoating applications.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000+


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC System Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Poor heating or cooling performance, unusual noises from the blower motor, leaks.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of blower motor, heater core, evaporator, or control modules.
- **Cost:** $300 - $900

### Summary Verdict
The 2005 Chevrolet T7500 demonstrates good overall reliability, especially within its powertrain and suspension systems, which are built for demanding commercial use. The primary areas where owners might encounter issues are the electrical system, specifically alternator failures and potential wiring harness problems, and exterior corrosion, a common concern for heavy-duty vehicles operating in varied climates. Interior components, particularly the HVAC system, can also require attention. Regular maintenance and prompt addressing of early signs of corrosion or electrical anomalies are recommended to ensure the longevity and operational efficiency of the T7500.
