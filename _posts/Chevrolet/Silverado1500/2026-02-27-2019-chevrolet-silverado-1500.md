# 2019 Chevrolet Silverado 1500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Chevrolet Silverado 1500 offers a broad variety of robust powertrain configurations and impressive towing capabilities, making it a popular choice in the full-size truck market. However, used-car buyers should approach this model year with careful consideration due to a notable volume of owner-reported complaints regarding electrical issues, steering assist failures, and transmission hesitations. Taking time to inspect service records and verifying that past warranty or campaign updates have been completed will help ensure a more dependable ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.7L Turbo I4

A turbocharged 4-cylinder engine producing around 310 horsepower and 348 lb-ft of torque, designed to provide efficient daily drivability and respectable light-duty capability.

### 4.3L EcoTec3 V6

A traditional V6 engine offering roughly 285 horsepower and 305 lb-ft of torque, serving as a durable base option for standard utility tasks.

### 5.3L EcoTec3 V8

A widely selected V8 engine delivering approximately 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, balancing strong towing performance with active fuel management technology.

**Electrical Warning Lights and Lifter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illumination of warning lights, sudden loss of motive power, and potential engine misfires or lifter ticking noise.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning by a qualified mechanic; replacement of failing valve lifters or faulty sensors as needed.

### 6.2L EcoTec3 V8

A high-output V8 powerplant generating 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, paired with high-end trims for maximum towing and hauling performance.

### 3.0L Duramax Turbo-Diesel I6

A smooth diesel option delivering around 277 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, tailored for long-distance cruising and superior fuel efficiency.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A traditional automatic transmission paired with select standard engine options, offering proven mechanical simplicity.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission configured across many popular trims, providing balanced gear ratios for everyday driving and towing.

**Transmission Shudder and Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle shudders during acceleration, harsh shifting between first and second gear, or hesitation when cold.
- **Solution:** Flushing and replacing transmission fluid with updated specifications, or servicing internal components as advised by a technician.

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

A heavy-duty multi-speed automatic transmission paired with premium engine options to maximize efficiency and power delivery under load.

## Suspension

### Wiring and Grounding System

Manages power distribution and module communication throughout the vehicle, requiring careful inspection for harness wear on pre-owned models.

**Chafing Harness and Ground Strap Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Multiple sudden error codes, loss of power, electrical accessory failure, or corrosion on chassis grounding straps.
- **Solution:** Inspect wiring harnesses for chafing points, repair damaged wires, and replace or service corroded grounding straps.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

A robust four-wheel disc brake system designed to handle heavy stopping loads, though rare electronic glitches can trigger unexpected behavior.

**Unexpected Brake Locking**
- **Symptoms:** Brakes unexpectedly lock up while driving at speed accompanied by stability control warning messages.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake hydraulic lines, anti-lock brake modules, and electronic stability sensors for faults.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

An electronic steering setup that provides light effort at low speeds and stable feedback on the highway, though sudden assist failures have been reported.

**Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Complete loss of power steering assist without prior warning, making the steering wheel exceptionally heavy to turn.
- **Solution:** Inspect steering gear assembly and electrical connections; replace faulty steering gear units or sensors.
- **Cost:** $2183

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V115000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Over cured tires may develop a break in the sidewall resulting in sudden air loss or a belt edge separation which could lead to a tread/belt loss. Either condition can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the tires, replacing them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V889000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Abs Warning Light](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Due to an Electronic Brake Control Module (EBCM) software error, the vehicle's Electronic Stability Control (ESC) and Antilock Brake System (ABS) may become disabled. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 126, "Electronic Stability Control" and 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems.".
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the EBCM, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V888000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery:Cables](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An intermittent electrical connection between the B+ battery cable and the alternator could cause the vehicle to stall, increasing the risk of a crash. The intermittent connection could also lead to electrical arcing, which could generate sufficient heat to damage surrounding material, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect for the presence of glue or other contamination at the B+ cable attachment to the alternator and clean the connection and reattach the B+ cable connection, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V792000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The seat belt brackets may not be secured to the seat frame in the front-row center seating position. If a seat-belt bracket is not secured, the seat belt may not properly restrain an occupant in the front-row center seat position, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect left-side and right-side front-row center seat-belt bracket attachments and reassemble it correctly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V055000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** This software has an error, and as a result, the vehicle's electronic brake assist may be disabled. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 126, "Electronic Stability Control" and 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." A loss of electronic brake assist can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the EBCM, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V814000: Seat Belts:Pretensioner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** When the front seat belt pretensioners deploy, hot gas may vent through an opening in the pretensioner bracket, possibly igniting the carpet. A vehicle fire could result if materials ignite inside the vehicle.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will close off the opening in the pretensioner bracket, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V423000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Over cured tires may develop a break in the sidewall resulting in sudden air loss or a belt edge separation which could lead to a tread/belt loss. Either condition can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 21V985000: Tires:Temporary/Emergency Spare Tire](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An ABS system that does not function properly can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will provide a new 17" spare tire and wheel assembly that is compatible with the accessory wheels, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Chevrolet Silverado 1500 is a capable and versatile full-size truck, but prospective buyers should conduct a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to how the 8-speed transmission shifts during a test drive, and verify that all electrical components and steering systems operate smoothly. Choosing a well-maintained vehicle with documented service records can help mitigate potential repair risks.
