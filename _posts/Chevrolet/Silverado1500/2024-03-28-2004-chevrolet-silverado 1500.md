## 2004 Chevrolet Silverado 1500

The 2004 Chevrolet Silverado 1500 is a full-size pickup truck that was offered with a variety of engine and transmission options. The standard engine was a 4.8L V8 that produced 285 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options included a 5.3L V8 (295 horsepower, 335 lb-ft of torque), a 6.0L V8 (300 horsepower, 360 lb-ft of torque), and a 8.1L V8 (340 horsepower, 455 lb-ft of torque). Transmission options included a 4-speed automatic, a 5-speed automatic, and a 6-speed manual.

The Silverado 1500 was praised for its powerful engines, spacious cabin, and comfortable ride. However, it was also criticized for its poor fuel economy and outdated interior.

### Engine
**4.8L V8**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil to leak. Symptoms include overheating, coolant loss, and a rough idle. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Timing chain failure:** The timing chain can fail, causing the engine to lose power and stall. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, a loss of power, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the timing chain. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500 - $2,500

**5.3L V8**

* **Valve cover gasket leaks:** The valve cover gaskets can leak, causing oil to leak onto the engine. Symptoms include an oil leak from the engine, a burning oil smell, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the valve cover gaskets. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400
* **Piston ring failure:** The piston rings can fail, causing the engine to lose compression and burn oil. Symptoms include blue smoke from the exhaust, a loss of power, and a check engine light. The solution is to rebuild the engine. Estimated cost to fix: $2,000 - $3,000

**6.0L V8**

* **Head gasket failure:** The head gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil to leak. Symptoms include overheating, coolant loss, and a rough idle. The solution is to replace the head gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500 - $2,000
* **Throttle position sensor failure:** The throttle position sensor can fail, causing the engine to run poorly. Symptoms include a rough idle, a loss of power, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the throttle position sensor. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $200

### Transmission

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission slipping:** The transmission can slip, causing the vehicle to lose power or stall. Symptoms include a loss of power, a slipping feeling when shifting gears, and a check engine light. The solution is to rebuild or replace the transmission. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500 - $2,500

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **Shift solenoid failure:** The shift solenoid can fail, causing the transmission to shift hard or not shift at all. Symptoms include hard shifting, a loss of power, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the shift solenoid. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400

**6-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch failure:** The clutch can fail, causing the vehicle to lose power or stall. Symptoms include a slipping clutch, a burning smell, and a difficult time shifting gears. The solution is to replace the clutch. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000

### Suspension

* **Front suspension:** The front suspension can wear out, causing the vehicle to ride rough and handle poorly. Symptoms include a rough ride, poor handling, and a clunking noise from the front end. The solution is to replace the worn suspension components. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000

### Electrical

* **Battery failure:** The battery can fail, causing the vehicle to lose power. Symptoms include a dead battery, a weak battery, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the battery. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $200
* **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the vehicle to lose power. Symptoms include a weak battery, a loss of power, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the alternator. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400
* **Starter failure:** The starter can fail, causing the vehicle to not start. Symptoms include a clicking noise when trying to start the vehicle, a weak battery, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the starter. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400

### Exterior

* **Tailgate latch failure:** The tailgate latch can fail, causing the tailgate to open while driving. Symptoms include a loose tailgate, a rattling noise from the tailgate, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the tailgate latch. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $200
* **Headlight failure:** The headlights can fail, causing the vehicle to not be able to see at night. Symptoms include dim headlights, flickering headlights, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the headlights. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $200

### Interior

* **HVAC system failure:** The HVAC system can fail, causing the vehicle to not be able to heat or cool the interior. Symptoms include a lack of heat or cool air, a strange noise from the HVAC system, and a check engine light. The solution is to repair or replace the HVAC system. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Power window failure:** The power windows can fail, causing the windows to not be able to open or close. Symptoms include a stuck window, a slow window, and a check engine light. The solution is to repair or replace the power window motor. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400
* **Cruise control failure:** The cruise control can fail, causing the vehicle to not be able to maintain a set speed. Symptoms include a non-functioning cruise control, a check engine light, and a loss of power. The solution is to repair or replace the cruise control system. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400

### Summary

The 2004 Chevrolet Silverado 1500 is a reliable and capable pickup truck. However, it is not without its problems. The most common issues include intake manifold gasket failure, timing chain failure, valve cover gasket leaks, piston ring failure, and head gasket failure. These issues can be expensive to fix, but they are generally not major problems. Overall, the Silverado 1500 is a good choice for those who need a powerful and reliable pickup truck.
