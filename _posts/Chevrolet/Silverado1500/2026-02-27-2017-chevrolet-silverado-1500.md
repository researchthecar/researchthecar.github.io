# 2017 Chevrolet Silverado 1500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 Chevrolet Silverado 1500, buyers will find a capable full-size truck known for strong towing power, comfortable ride quality, and a practical interior. However, owner complaints filed with safety authorities highlight several recurring mechanical and electrical concerns that prospective buyers should inspect closely. Key areas to watch include transmission operation, brake performance, and certain engine reliability items.

---

## Engine

### 4.3L EcoTec3 V6

The base 4.3L V6 engine produces 285 horsepower and 305 lb-ft of torque, offering solid utility for light-duty tasks with decent reliability.

### 5.3L EcoTec3 V8

The popular 5.3L V8 generates 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, balancing everyday towing capability with fuel management systems, though some owners experience valvetrain issues.

**Lifter Failure and Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report engine ticking noises, sudden stalling, and mechanical failure of the valve lifters leading to rough running.
- **Solution:** Diagnose faulty lifters and replace the affected valve train components or camshaft as necessary.

### 6.2L EcoTec3 V8

The high-output 6.2L V8 delivers 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque for maximum towing and hauling performance.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission pairs with standard V6 and smaller V8 configurations, but early shudder and torque converter complaints warrant a thorough test drive.

**Transmission Shudder and Torque Converter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a noticeable shudder or vibration during acceleration, harsh shifting, and transmission slipping.
- **Solution:** Flush transmission fluid or replace the failing torque converter and worn internal components.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission offers refined gear spacing for improved highway efficiency, though some owners report shifting hesitation and rough engagement.

**Harsh Shifting and Gear Hunting**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission hesitates, hunts for gears during deceleration, or slips out of gear unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Reprogram the transmission control module or service internal clutches and fluid.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake system complaints emphasize soft pedals and unexpected loss of hydraulic pressure, requiring careful inspection of lines and calipers.

**Brake Pedal Softness and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal travels all the way to the floorboard with reduced stopping power, or brakes fail to release fully.
- **Solution:** Bleed the brake lines, service sticking calipers, or replace the master cylinder and vacuum pump components.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

Electric power steering assists low-speed maneuvering, but sudden loss of assist can create significant handling difficulties.

**Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers encounter a 'Service Power Steering' warning message accompanied by extremely stiff steering wheel operation.
- **Solution:** Inspect power steering electrical connections, electronic control modules, or replace the steering rack assembly.

## Interior

### Rear Defroster and Cabin Electronics

Cabin electrical systems generally function well, though high-current circuits like the rear defroster have generated severe owner complaints.

**Rear Window Defroster Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** A burning smell, smoke, or sudden shattering of the rear window glass when activating the rear defroster.
- **Solution:** Disconnect damaged defroster wiring and replace the rear window assembly.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Restraint systems are critical for occupant protection, making physical inspection of seat belt latches essential during a pre-purchase exam.

**Seat Belt Pretensioner Fracture**
- **Symptoms:** The driver-side seat belt retainer or pretensioner cracks, snaps, or fails to latch securely.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged seat belt buckle and pretensioner assembly.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V645000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist:Vacuum](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+1500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As the vacuum level drops, the brake assist decreases, increasing braking effort, extending the distance required to stop the vehicle, thereby increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the Electronic Brake Control Module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V761000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Wheel Speed Sensor/Tone Ring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+1500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Unintended activation of the driveline-protection system will cause unintended braking on the wheel on the opposite side of the failed sensor, causing the vehicle to pull to one side unexpectedly, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the EBCM, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V651000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+1500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test. During this test, deployment of the frontal air bags and the seat belt pretensioners would not occur in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V487000: Child Seat:Tether: Connector/Clip](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+1500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 225, "Child Restraint Anchorage Systems." If the instructions are missing from the owner's manual, the child seat may be installed incorrectly and not be properly secured, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will provide a corrected insert for the owners manual, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V256000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+1500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Inadequate welds may allow the control arm to separate from the bushing, compromising steering and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the left and right front upper control arms and realign the vehicle, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V437000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+1500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** While being previously remedied for recall 16V-651, the affected vehicles may not have received the complete software update necessary to remedy the recall condition. Without the update, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Chevrolet Silverado 1500 remains a popular used truck choice due to its robust engine lineup and towing capacity, but buyers should proceed with diligence. Prioritize a thorough test drive to check for transmission shudder, smooth gear shifts, and firm brake pedal feel. Verifying service records for past warranty repairs or component replacements can help mitigate potential ownership risks.
