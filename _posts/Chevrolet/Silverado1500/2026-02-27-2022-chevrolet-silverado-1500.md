# 2022 Chevrolet Silverado 1500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Chevrolet Silverado 1500 is a capable modern full-size pickup truck offering a blend of utility, technology, and diverse powertrain configurations. However, prospective buyers should carefully review owner-reported issues regarding engine performance, electrical glitches, and transmission operation. While many owners enjoy trouble-free driving, a subset of vehicles has experienced notable mechanical and electrical hiccups that require attention.

---

## Engine

### 2.7L Turbocharged I4

A high-output turbocharged four-cylinder engine generating approximately 310 horsepower and 430 lb-ft of torque, balancing everyday efficiency with respectable pulling power.

### 5.3L V8 Ecotec3

A traditional small-block V8 producing around 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, known for robust towing capabilities and widespread parts availability.

### 6.2L V8 Ecotec3

A high-performance V8 delivering 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, offering strong acceleration but occasionally subject to severe internal component complaints.

**Sudden Mechanical Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Complete loss of propulsion and engine shutdown while driving at highway speeds without prior warning lights.
- **Solution:** Requires professional mechanical inspection and potentially major engine repair or replacement.

### 3.0L Duramax Turbo-Diesel I6

A smooth and efficient inline-six diesel engine offering strong low-end torque and impressive fuel economy, ideal for frequent towers.

**Loss of Motive Power and Fuel System Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected loss of vehicle power, hesitation, or fuel pump module performance issues during operation.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic inspection of the fuel delivery system and replacement of failing pumps or modules.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired primarily with the 2.7L engine, this transmission provides responsive gear selection for daily commuting and light utility use.

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

A multi-speed automatic transmission matched with V8 and diesel engines, designed to optimize fuel efficiency and towing performance.

**Output Shaft Speed Sensor and Limp Mode**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden violent shifting, entry into limp mode, and diagnostic trouble codes due to internal sensor failure.
- **Solution:** Replace the internal output shaft speed sensor and associated lead frame assembly.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brake and Trailer System

Manages stopping power for both the truck and towed loads, requiring routine monitoring of pads, rotors, and integrated towing electronics.

**Trailer Brake Connection Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warnings stating trailer brakes are connected and disconnected repeatedly, eventually leading to brake system failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect trailer wiring harnesses, brake controllers, and vehicle fuses.

## Exterior

### Tailgate Switch and Latches

Controls power-release tailgates and exterior locking assemblies, which can experience moisture-related electrical faults.

**Unexpected Tailgate Opening**
- **Symptoms:** Tailgate drops open independently while parked or while driving at highway speeds due to moisture intrusion.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the tailgate electronic switch or latch mechanism.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V463000: Exterior Lighting:Brake Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+1500&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The accessory sport bar contains a high-mounted brake light that may not function. As such, these vehicles may fail to conform to Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, "lamps, reflective devices, and associated equipment." An inoperative high-mounted brake light can reduce visibility to other drivers and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the installation of the accessory bar and provide the correct vehicle wiring, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V873000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+1500&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An SDM in manufacturing mode will not deploy the vehicle's air bags in a crash, increasing the risk of occupant injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 22V903000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+1500&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The daytime running lights (DRLs) may not deactivate when the headlights are on. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." DRLs that do not deactivate as intended may result in a glare, reducing visibility, and increasing the risk.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V797000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+1500&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Rear wheel lock-up can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install new transmission control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V274000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+1500&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Engine failure increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the engine, and as necessary, repair or replace the engine. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V083000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+1500&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Rear wheel lock up increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the vehicles and update the transmission control software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Chevrolet Silverado 1500 offers impressive capability and diverse engine choices, but prospective buyers should perform thorough pre-purchase inspections. Pay close attention to transmission shifting behavior, electrical accessories like the tailgate switch, and service records. Selecting a well-maintained model with remaining powertrain coverage can provide greater peace of mind.
