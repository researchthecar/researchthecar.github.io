**Introduction**

The 1998 Chevrolet Cavalier is a compact car available in sedan, coupe, and convertible body styles. It came with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 2.2L Ecotec inline-four (115 hp, 140 lb-ft torque), a 2.4L LD9 inline-four (150 hp, 160 lb-ft torque), a 3.1L LG6 V6 (160 hp, 185 lb-ft torque), and a 3.4L L36 V6 (215 hp, 230 lb-ft torque). Transmission options included a 5-speed manual, a 4-speed automatic, and a 5-speed automatic. The Cavalier was praised for its affordability, fuel efficiency, and sporty handling, but was criticized for its cramped interior, noisy cabin, and lack of safety features.

**Engine**

**2.2L Ecotec Inline-Four**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking coolant and oil, engine overheating, rough idle
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking oil, visible oil streaks on engine
    * Solution: Replace valve cover gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.4L LD9 Inline-Four**

* **Head Gasket Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Mixing of coolant and oil, overheating, decrease in engine performance
    * Solution: Replace head gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.1L LG6 V6**

* **No Major Issues Found**

**3.4L L36 V6**

* **Crankshaft Sensor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling, trouble starting, engine misfires
    * Solution: Replace crankshaft sensor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Synchronizer Wear**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises during shifting
    * Solution: Replace worn synchronizers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Fluid Leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking transmission fluid, slipping gears, overheating transmission
    * Solution: Repair or replace leaking component
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noise during suspension travel, excessive body roll
    * Solution: Replace strut mounts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500 per axle
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Ball Joint Wear**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises during suspension travel, loose or unstable steering
    * Solution: Replace worn ball joints
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per side
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, battery not charging, electrical system failure
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Starter Motor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine cranks slowly or fails to start, grinding noises during starting
    * Solution: Replace starter motor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Door locks not working properly, doors not opening or closing
    * Solution: Replace door lock actuator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per door
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Window Regulator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Power windows not working properly, stuck or slow-moving windows
    * Solution: Replace window regulator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500 per window
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks and fading on the dashboard
    * Solution: Replace or repair dashboard
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Seat Fabric Staining**
    * Description/Symptoms: Stains on the seat fabric
    * Solution: Clean or replace seat fabric
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per seat
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1998 Chevrolet Cavalier is a reliable and affordable compact car with a few common issues. The most serious issue is the head gasket failure on the 2.4L LD9 engine, which can be costly to repair. Other common issues include intake manifold gasket failure on the 2.2L Ecotec engine, transmission fluid leaks on the 4-speed automatic transmission, and strut mount failure in the suspension. Overall, the Cavalier's issues are relatively minor and can be fixed for a reasonable cost.
