## 2019 Chevrolet Cavalier

The 2019 Chevrolet Cavalier is a compact sedan that offers a comfortable ride and a spacious interior. It comes standard with a 1.4-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine that produces 153 horsepower and 177 lb-ft of torque. A 1.5-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine is available, which produces 177 horsepower and 207 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a six-speed automatic transmission.

People like the Cavalier for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and affordable price. However, some people have experienced problems with the vehicle's engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical system.

## Engine

**1.4-Liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder Engine**

- **Stalling**
- Description/Symptoms: The engine may stall while driving, especially when coming to a stop.
- Solution: Reprogram the engine control module (ECM).
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
- Recalls: None Found

- **Rough Idle**
- Description/Symptoms: The engine may idle roughly or shake excessively.
- Solution: Replace the engine mounts.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
- Recalls: None Found

**1.5-Liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder Engine**

- **Turbocharger Failure**
- Description/Symptoms: The turbocharger may fail, causing a loss of power.
- Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
- Recalls: None Found

- **Timing Chain Failure**
- Description/Symptoms: The timing chain may fail, causing the engine to skip time and potentially causing catastrophic damage.
- Solution: Replace the timing chain and related components.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000
- Recalls: None Found

## Transmission

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

- **Shifting Problems**
- Description/Symptoms: The transmission may shift harshly or may not shift gears properly.
- Solution: Reprogram the transmission control module (TCM) or replace the transmission.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000
- Recalls: None Found

- **Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- Description/Symptoms: The transmission may leak fluid, causing the transmission to overheat and fail.
- Solution: Repair or replace the leaking component.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
- Recalls: None Found

## Suspension

- **Strut Failure**
- Description/Symptoms: The struts may fail, causing the vehicle to ride rough and handle poorly.
- Solution: Replace the struts.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
- Recalls: None Found

## Electrical

- **Battery Drain**
- Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain quickly, even when the vehicle is not in use.
- Solution: Diagnose and repair the source of the battery drain, such as a faulty alternator or parasitic draw.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
- Recalls: None Found

## Exterior

- **Headlight Condensation**
- Description/Symptoms: Condensation may form inside the headlights.
- Solution: Replace the headlights.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
- Recalls: None Found

## Interior

- **Infotainment System Malfunctions**
- Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may crash, freeze, or display incorrect information.
- Solution: Update the software or replace the infotainment system.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
- Recalls: None Found

Overall, the 2019 Chevrolet Cavalier has some reliability issues that can be costly to fix. However, it is important to note that not all Cavaliers will experience these problems. If you are considering purchasing a Cavalier, be sure to do your research and factor in the potential costs of repairs.
