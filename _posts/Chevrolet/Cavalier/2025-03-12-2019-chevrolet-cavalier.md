Here is a comprehensive breakdown of common issues and complaints associated with the 2019 Chevrolet Cavalier, categorized by component and engine/transmission configuration. The 2019 Chevrolet Cavalier is primarily marketed towards budget-conscious buyers. It offers a comfortable ride and decent fuel economy at an affordable price point. The 2019 Cavalier comes with a 1.5L I4 engine which produces 107 horsepower and 104 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with either a 5-speed manual or a 6-speed automatic transmission. Common dislikes include a lack of advanced safety features, bland styling, and subpar interior quality compared to competitors.

**Engine**

**1.5L I4**

*   **Engine Misfires**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, decreased power, check engine light illumination, diagnostic trouble codes (DTCs) related to misfires on specific cylinders.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis often involves checking spark plugs, ignition coils, fuel injectors, and compression. Replacing faulty components resolves the issue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the component that needs to be replaced and the number of parts to be replaced.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Low oil level between oil changes, blue smoke from the exhaust, and a burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Requires investigation into potential causes such as worn piston rings or valve stem seals. Repair may involve replacing these components or performing an engine rebuild, depending on the severity.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3000+ depending on the severity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant may leak externally and internally leading to overheating.
    *   Solution: Requires investigation into potential causes such as the water pump. Repair may involve replacing the damaged components or performing an engine repair, depending on the severity.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $3000+ depending on the severity and component that needs to be replaced.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Jerky or delayed shifts, slipping between gears, and unusual noises during gear changes.
    *   Solution: Could be due to low transmission fluid, a faulty valve body, or internal transmission damage. Diagnosis and repair may involve fluid replacement, valve body replacement, or transmission rebuild/replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $4000+ depending on the severity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen freezes, becomes unresponsive, or restarts unexpectedly.
    *   Solution: The issue might be software related and resolved by a software update, but can be resolved by replacing the head unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (for software update) - $500+ (for head unit replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Creaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling or creaking noises coming from the dashboard, door panels, or other interior trim pieces while driving.
    *   Solution: Identifying the source of the noise and tightening or replacing loose fasteners or trim pieces. Applying felt tape or other sound-dampening materials to eliminate contact points.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (DIY fix) - $200+ depending on the source of the rattle and the labor involved.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Chevrolet Cavalier presents a mix of potential issues ranging from minor inconveniences to more significant mechanical problems. Engine misfires and excessive oil consumption on the 1.5L engine are potential concerns. Additionally, the 6-speed automatic transmission can exhibit harsh shifting or slipping. Electrical glitches with the infotainment system and interior rattling contribute to the overall ownership experience. The severity and cost of these issues vary significantly, with engine repairs potentially reaching several thousand dollars, while other fixes are more affordable. Careful inspection and preventative maintenance can help mitigate some of these issues.
