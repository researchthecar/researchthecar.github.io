## Suspension

### Heavy-Duty Independent Front Suspension / Solid Rear Axle
**Ball Joint Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises, steering looseness, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Leaf Spring Sagging**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced load capacity, rear end sitting low, potential for bottoming out.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn or broken leaf springs.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900

### Summary Verdict
The 2010 Chevrolet Express 4500 demonstrates good overall reliability, especially concerning its core powertrain components. The available V8 engines and robust automatic transmissions are generally dependable for heavy-duty applications. The primary areas that may require attention are related to suspension components, such as ball joints and leaf springs, which can experience wear under significant load or mileage. Electrical issues like alternator failure and power window regulator problems are also noted, though not excessively widespread. With proper maintenance and timely repairs, the Express 4500 remains a capable and reliable work vehicle.
