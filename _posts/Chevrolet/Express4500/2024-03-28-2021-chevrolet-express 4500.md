The 2021 Chevrolet Express 4500 is a full-size passenger van that offers a range of engine and transmission options. It's known for its spacious interior, powerful engines, and reliable performance. However, like any vehicle, it may encounter some common issues.

**Engine**

**6.0L V8 L86**

* **Engine Stalling**
    * Description/Symptoms: The engine shuts off unexpectedly while driving or idling.
    * Solution: Replace the fuel pump or ignition coil.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: N/A

**6.6L V8 L96**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant seeps from the intake manifold, causing a coolant leak and overheating issues.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: N/A

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic 6L90**

* **Transmission Slipping**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission hesitates or slips when shifting gears, causing rough shifts or a loss of power.
    * Solution: Replace the valve body or rebuild the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: N/A

**Suspension**

* **Leaking Shock Absorbers**
    * Description/Symptoms: Fluid leaks from the shock absorbers, leading to reduced ride quality and poor handling.
    * Solution: Replace the shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: N/A

* **Excessive Body Roll**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle leans excessively when cornering or turning.
    * Solution: Install stiffer sway bars or upgrade the suspension system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: N/A

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery drains prematurely, even when the vehicle is parked and turned off.
    * Solution: Identify and replace the faulty component causing the drain (e.g., alternator, starter motor, or parasitic draw).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: N/A

* **Malfunctioning Sensors**
    * Description/Symptoms: Various sensors (e.g., ABS, TPMS, or engine sensors) malfunction, causing warning lights to illuminate and potential performance issues.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+
    * Recalls: N/A

**Exterior**

* **Rust Around Wheel Wells**
    * Description/Symptoms: Corrosion forms around the wheel wells, compromising the structural integrity of the body.
    * Solution: Treat and repair the rust, or replace affected body panels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: N/A

* **Paint Peeling on Door Handles**
    * Description/Symptoms: The paint on the exterior door handles chips or peels, revealing bare metal.
    * Solution: Repaint the door handles or replace them.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: N/A

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The HVAC system fails to regulate temperature or airflow, causing discomfort for passengers.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty component (e.g., blower motor, AC compressor, or actuators).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: N/A

* **Seat Cushion Sagging**
    * Description/Symptoms: The seat cushions lose support and become uncomfortable to sit on.
    * Solution: Replace the seat cushions.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: N/A

**Summary**

Overall, the 2021 Chevrolet Express 4500 is a reliable vehicle with few significant issues. The common problems it faces are generally manageable and do not affect the overall dependability of the van. The estimated cost of repairs can vary depending on the severity of the issue and the location of the repairs. While some issues may be minor inconveniences, others can be more costly and require professional attention.
