**Introduction:**

The 2006 Chevrolet Meriva is a subcompact MPV (multi-purpose vehicle) known for its spacious interior, compact size, and versatile seating configuration. It features a single engine option paired with multiple transmission options.

**Engine**

* **1.4L ECOTEC Inline-4** (100 hp, 130 lb-ft torque)

**Transmission**

* **5-speed Manual Transmission**

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Water Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaking from the sunroof during heavy rain or car washes.
    * Solution: Replace sunroof seals or drainage tubes.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Dashboard Squeaks and Rattles:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noises and vibrations coming from the dashboard while driving over uneven roads.
    * Solution: Tighten loose bolts or panels, or apply sound-dampening foam.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
* **Infotainment System Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Display freezing, slow response, or audio issues.
    * Solution: Software update or hardware replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+

**Other**

* **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary:**

The 2006 Chevrolet Meriva has a few common issues, primarily related to sunroof leaks and interior noise. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but most are relatively minor and affordable to repair. Overall, the Meriva remains a reliable and practical subcompact MPV for those seeking a versatile and spacious vehicle.
