# 2021 Chevrolet Equinox Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Chevrolet Equinox is a compact crossover SUV that offers comfortable daily driving manners and modern connectivity features. However, prospective buyers should review owner-reported reliability data closely, particularly regarding engine and fuel system performance, electrical glitches, and interior water leaks.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

A 1.5-liter turbocharged 4-cylinder engine producing 170 horsepower and 203 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard base powerplant for most models. It balances everyday efficiency with modest acceleration, though buyers should watch for fuel pump and stalling complaints.

**Fuel Pump and Engine Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may suddenly stall while driving, fail to restart, or experience severe engine seizure due to fuel pump issues.
- **Solution:** Inspect the high-pressure fuel pump and replace damaged fuel system components or the engine assembly if internal damage occurred.
- **Cost:** Varies by repair extent; potential major out-of-pocket costs if outside warranty.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

A more powerful 2.0-liter turbocharged 4-cylinder engine generating 252 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission. It offers stronger towing capability and faster passing power, though it was dropped or modified in later production cycles.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A 6-speed automatic transmission paired primarily with the 1.5L engine, providing routine gear changes for front-wheel or all-wheel drive models.

**Hard Shifting and Drivetrain Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may shift harshly, exhibit front-end vibrations, or require propeller shaft service.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission control modules, update software, or replace worn mechanical driveline components.
- **Cost:** $859 for propeller shaft replacement.

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

A 9-speed automatic transmission paired with the 2.0L turbo engine, offering a broader gear ratio spread for enhanced highway cruising efficiency.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Audio System

Controls cabin entertainment, hands-free calling, and vehicle acoustic alerts.

**Loss of Sound and Warning Chimes**
- **Symptoms:** Audio system cuts out completely, including turn signal clicks, warning indicators, radio sound, and phone call audio.
- **Solution:** Perform software updates or replace the radio receiver and amplifier modules.

## Exterior

### Windshield Wipers

Exterior components responsible for clearing rain, snow, and debris from the windshield.

**Wiper Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Windshield wipers suddenly stop working during active weather events, resulting in a total loss of forward visibility.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the wiper motor linkage or control module.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Occupant restraint systems designed to secure passengers during sudden deceleration or impacts.

**Inertia Locking Defect**
- **Symptoms:** Driver or passenger seat belt inertia locking mechanisms fail to lock during sudden stops, allowing occupants to move forward abruptly.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace defective seat belt assembly and locking retractors.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V811000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Equinox&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** If an incorrect bolt was used, the seat belt may not be properly attached to the vehicle. If a seat belt assembly is not properly attached to the vehicle, the seat belt may not properly restrain an occupant in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the suspect bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V612000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Equinox&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The tires may have been manufactured with an insufficient amount of curative agent, which could cause tread separation. Tread separation can result in a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the tire DOT numbers, and replace any affected tires, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V013000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump:Control/Drive Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Equinox&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The fuel pump module may not consistently provide sufficient fuel to the engine, resulting in an engine stall. An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V339000: Child Seat:Vehicle Latch Anchor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Equinox&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The four rear-seat lower child seat LATCH anchorage bars may have excessive powder coating, which could prevent a child seat from being installed with the anchorage bars. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 225, "Child Restraint Anchorage Systems." The inability to install a child seat.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the latch anchorage bars, and remove and replace the finish, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Chevrolet Equinox provides practical family transportation and a comfortable ride, but used car shoppers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to engine performance, potential fuel pump or stalling histories, infotainment electronics, and interior water intrusion.
