# 2017 Chevrolet Equinox Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Chevrolet Equinox is a compact crossover that offers a comfortable ride and spacious cabin, making it a popular choice for families and commuters. However, used-car buyers should thoroughly inspect prospective models due to recurring owner-reported complaints concerning engine reliability, fluid consumption, and electrical or transmission quirks.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine produces 182 horsepower and 172 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting but requiring careful monitoring of oil consumption during used-vehicle inspections.

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Oil levels drop significantly between regular oil changes, frequently triggering low oil pressure warning lights and potential engine stalling if left unchecked.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels weekly and consult a mechanic for diagnosis; repairs may require piston ring replacement or engine overhaul in severe cases.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle suddenly loses power or stalls while driving, accompanied by electrical warning lights or dashboard alerts.
- **Solution:** Have the powertrain control module, sensors, and electrical grounds inspected by a qualified technician.

### 3.6L V6 Engine

The optional 3.6-liter V6 engine delivers a robust 301 horsepower and 272 lb-ft of torque, offering much stronger acceleration and towing capability compared to the base four-cylinder option.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with both engine options in front-wheel or all-wheel drive configurations, but a subset of owners have reported harsh shifting and slip symptoms.

**Transmission Shifting Delays and Slippage**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission hesitates, jolts, or slips during gear changes, particularly between lower gears or when coming to a stop.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition and level; software updates or component rebuilds may be necessary if internal wear is present.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Body Electrical

Electrical systems control cabin accessories and driver assistance features, with occasional reports of screen blackouts and accessory glitches.

**Infotainment System Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment screen, backup camera, climate controls, and audio systems intermittently lose power or shut down completely.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuses, software calibration, and wiring harnesses; replace faulty display modules or radio units as needed.

## Safety

### Air Bags

Safety components include standard multi-stage airbags and seat belt systems, though a few owners have experienced critical deployment failures during impacts.

**Air Bag Deployment Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Air bags fail to deploy during a frontal or side collision, followed by a warning light indicating an airbag system malfunction.
- **Solution:** Have the airbag control module and impact sensors thoroughly inspected and replaced if diagnostic trouble codes are present.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used model, prioritize vehicles with comprehensive service history records, verified oil maintenance intervals, and a clean pre-purchase inspection. While the Equinox offers great utility and comfort, keeping a close eye on oil levels and transmission behavior will help avoid costly unexpected repairs.
