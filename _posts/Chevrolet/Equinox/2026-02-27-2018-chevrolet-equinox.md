# 2018 Chevrolet Equinox Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Chevrolet Equinox represents the first model year of the crossover's third generation, featuring a lighter platform, a more compact footprint, and an updated lineup of turbocharged engines. It offers a comfortable ride, a spacious cabin, and modern driver-assistance options, making it a highly practical choice for family duty. However, potential buyers should be aware of several reliability trends reported by owners. While these complaints reflect real-world experiences, they are not guaranteed to occur on every vehicle, and finding a well-maintained model with a documented service history remains the key to a satisfying purchase.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder (LYX)

This base 1.5-liter engine generates roughly 170 horsepower and 203 lb-ft of torque. It is designed to balance everyday fuel economy with modest performance, though some owners have reported typical small-displacement turbo sensitivities like low-speed pre-ignition if maintenance is neglected.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder (LTG)

A more robust 2.0-liter engine option producing approximately 252 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque. It offers significantly stronger acceleration and higher towing capability, generally presenting a durable option for buyers seeking more power.

### 1.6L Turbo-Diesel 4-Cylinder (LH7)

An uncommon 1.6-liter turbo-diesel engine producing 137 horsepower and 240 lb-ft of torque. Highly specialized for highway fuel efficiency, it provides exceptional cruising range but may carry higher long-term maintenance costs and harder-to-find parts.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (6T40/6T45)

Paired with the 1.5-liter gasoline and 1.6-liter diesel engines, this transmission is geared toward maximizing fuel economy during standard driving duties.

### 9-Speed Automatic (9T50)

Exclusively paired with the 2.0-liter turbocharged engine to manage its higher torque output and deliver smoother shift transitions.

### General / Multiple Configurations

Covers transmission linkage, gear selectors, and structural driveshafts. While the core gearboxes are generally durable, electronic sensor switches and AWD mechanical driveshafts have drawn frequent owner reports.

**'Shift to Park' Sensor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The shifter is placed in Park, but the dashboard displays a 'Shift to Park' warning, preventing the driver from turning off the vehicle and draining the accessory battery.
- **Solution:** Replace the shifter microswitch assembly or install an updated inline wire harness connector as detailed in GM Service Bulletin 19-NA-206.

**All-Wheel Drive (AWD) Driveshaft Fracture**
- **Symptoms:** Loud popping or whining sounds from under the chassis upon acceleration, severe vibrations, or a complete loss of propulsion if the shaft snaps.
- **Solution:** Replace the fractured front intermediate driveshaft or prop shaft assembly.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Tires

Covers the factory-installed wheels, tire pressure monitoring sensors, and original equipment tires. Multiple owners have noted rapid tread wear on factory-supplied rubber.

**Premature Tire Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive or uneven tread wear on original equipment tires (such as Michelin Premier LTX or Hankook models) requiring replacement at very low mileage (under 18,000 miles), accompanied by chronic tire pressure loss.
- **Solution:** Replace the worn tires with higher-durability aftermarket alternatives and perform a four-wheel alignment.

## Brakes

### General / Multiple Configurations

Covers shared accessory systems, emissions controls, and software that affect various engine options. While most auxiliary components serve standard lifespans, some shared fuel and vacuum parts have been prone to localized failures.

**Brake Vacuum Pump Mechanical Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A sudden loss of braking power, an extremely stiff brake pedal, and the appearance of a check engine or 'Service Brake Assist' warning light.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed brake vacuum pump. If the pump's internal components shattered, a mechanic must inspect the engine, camshaft, and oil supply for metal shrapnel to prevent complete engine failure.

**Fuel Injector Rail Assembly Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** A strong raw fuel odor coming through the cabin air vents, visible fuel leaks near the high-pressure fuel pump under the engine, and the vehicle entering 'Reduced Engine Power' or limp mode.
- **Solution:** Replace the high-pressure fuel injector rail assembly. GM addressed this under Customer Satisfaction Program N242489010 on some units.

**Emissions Software and Calibration Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle fails state emissions or SMOG inspections due to an incorrect CVN error code, even when no check engine light is illuminated.
- **Solution:** Reprogram and recalibrate the Engine Control Module (ECM) at an authorized dealer, often covered under Emissions Recalls N212345540 or N202298780.
- **Cost:** $289.99 for diagnostics

### Brake System

Covers hydraulic lines, calipers, rotors, and the vacuum assist system. Mechanical pump failures in this system can directly degrade braking performance.

**Brake Vacuum Booster Loss of Assist**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal suddenly becomes extremely stiff, firm, and hard to push down, resulting in significantly increased stopping distances and making deceleration difficult.
- **Solution:** Replace the brake vacuum pump and brake booster assembly to restore full power-assist functions.

## Steering

### Steering Gear and Assist

Covers the steering column, electronic power steering assist motor, and steering rack. Some drivers have reported momentary stiffness or loss of steering assist.

**Steering Wheel Sticking and Power Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel intermittently 'sticks' or resists driver input at highway speeds, requiring sudden extra physical effort to steer; dashboard warning lights indicating disabled steering assist.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the electronic power steering module and replace the steering rack or gear assembly if mechanical binding is present.

## Exterior

### Cabin Electronics and Lighting

Includes the wiring harnesses, fuse panels, power accessories, and exterior illumination. Water ingress from clogged sunroof drains can severely impact electrical components in the rear of the cabin.

**Sunroof Drain Clog and Fuse Box Water Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Water dripping directly from the rear driver-side fuse panel, standing water in the spare tire compartment, strong mold or mildew odors, and sunroof motor or sunshade malfunction due to moisture damage.
- **Solution:** Clear debris from the sunroof water drainage tubes and blow out the lines. Dry the cabin and inspect/replace any water-damaged fuse panels or wiring harness connectors.

**Headlight Wiring and Ballast Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A single headlight bulb repeatedly failing or burning out, even after installing a brand-new bulb.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the headlight assembly and replace the faulty headlight ballast or repair damaged wiring connections.
- **Cost:** Over $400 for dealership bulb and assembly diagnostic/repair

### Sunroof and Glass

Covers windshield glass, panoramic sunroof panels, and the wiper systems. Structural glass tension issues and wiper motor longevity have been highlighted by some owners.

**Spontaneous Panoramic Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** A sudden loud popping sound resembling a gunshot, followed by the panoramic sunroof glass cracking, buckling outward, or completely shattering with no impact from external debris.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged panoramic sunroof glass panel and inspect the surrounding frame alignment.

**Windshield Wiper Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The front or rear windshield wipers unexpectedly freeze, stop working entirely, or fail to respond to switch inputs.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed front or rear wiper motor and service the wiper transmission linkage.

## Safety

### Driver Assistance and Airbag Systems

Includes automated safety alerts, forward-facing radar and cameras, and the supplemental restraint system (SRS). Sensor calibration issues have been reported, occasionally triggering system alerts under specific weather conditions.

**Forward Collision and Emergency Braking System Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent 'Forward Collision System Unavailable' warnings appearing in high-temperature or humid environments, or a failure of the automatic emergency braking system to engage prior to a front-end impact.
- **Solution:** Inspect, clean, or replace malfunctioning forward-facing sensors or cameras, and perform any pending driver assistance software updates.

**Airbag Sensor Faults**
- **Symptoms:** The SRS airbag warning light illuminates intermittently or continuously on the driver instrument panel.
- **Solution:** Replace the malfunctioning front or rear passenger-side airbag sensors.
- **Cost:** Approximately $1,000 for repair ($180 diagnostic fee)

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V576000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Equinox&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A reduction of braking performance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will bleed the vehicle's brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V358000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Equinox&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A damaged fuel line can create a fuel leak, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the high pressure fuel pump, and high pressure fuel pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V252000: Visibility](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Equinox&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The driver and front passenger door window glass may not be properly tempered. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 205, "Glazing Materials." If the glass is not tempered properly, and the window is broken, the glass can break into large pieces, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the glass in the front doors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V516000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Equinox&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the front right intermediate drive shaft assembly separates while driving, the vehicle will lose propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash. If the assembly separates while parked on a grade, without the park brake engaged, the vehicle may move unintentionally, increasing the risk of a vehicle roll-away.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the right front intermediate drive shaft assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V668000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Equinox&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Missing bolts on the start-stop accumulator endcap could result in a transmission oil leak and may progress to a loss of propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash. A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the start-stop transmission accumulator and replace it if any bolts are missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Chevrolet Equinox remains a versatile and highly affordable choice in the competitive compact crossover segment, notable for its comfortable ride and sensible cabin dimensions. When shopping for a used model, it is highly recommended to seek out examples that come with a complete, detailed service history showing regular maintenance. During a pre-purchase inspection, ensure your mechanic checks the brake vacuum pump for secure operation and verifies that the sunroof drain tubes are entirely clear of blockages. Models equipped with the 2.0L turbocharged engine and the 9-speed automatic transmission often represent the most appealing balance of performance and drivetrain durability.
