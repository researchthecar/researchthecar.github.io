**Introduction:**

The 2007 Chevrolet Equinox is a compact crossover SUV powered by either a 3.4L V6 engine or a 3.6L V6 engine. Both engines are paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. The Equinox received praise for its spacious cabin, comfortable ride, and strong V6 engines, but it was criticized for its lack of fuel efficiency and dated interior design.

**Engine**

**3.4L V6**

* **Oil Leak from Oil Pressure Sending Unit:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leak from the oil pressure sending unit located on the back of the engine.
    * Solution: Replace the oil pressure sending unit and gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine makes a rattling noise at startup or acceleration.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.6L V6**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leak from the intake manifold gasket.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Mount Bearing Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Knocking or clunking noise from the front suspension.
    * Solution: Replace the front strut mount bearings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Shock Absorber Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking or worn rear shock absorbers.
    * Solution: Replace the rear shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Power Window Module Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Power windows do not operate properly or become inoperable.
    * Solution: Replace the power window module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Clock Spring Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Airbag and cruise control warning lights illuminate, horn and audio controls may not work.
    * Solution: Replace the clock spring.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rear Bumper Cover Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks in the rear bumper cover.
    * Solution: Replace the rear bumper cover.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Fade:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dashboard material fades or discolors.
    * Solution: Replace the dashboard.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Headliner Sagging:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Headliner material sags or detaches from the roof.
    * Solution: Replace the headliner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2007 Chevrolet Equinox has a mixed reputation for reliability. While it has received praise for its spacious cabin and comfortable ride, it has also been criticized for its dated interior design and lack of fuel efficiency. The major issues experienced with this vehicle include oil leaks, intake manifold gasket leaks, and timing chain tensioner failure. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific part that fails and the severity of the problem.
