# 2020 Chevrolet Equinox Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Chevrolet Equinox is a compact crossover offering comfortable daily driving and modern tech features. However, used car buyers should carefully review owner-reported reliability trends, particularly concerning brake vacuum pump performance and engine driveability. While many owners enjoy trouble-free operation, paying close attention to service history and potential mechanical hiccups will ensure a more confident purchase.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbo I4

The base 1.5-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces 170 horsepower and 203 lb-ft of torque, paired with a smooth automatic transmission. It offers adequate efficiency for daily commutes, though some owners have reported intermittent hesitation or stalling concerns.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly stall while driving or experience delayed acceleration from a stop.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fuel delivery and electronic control modules; replace failing components as necessary.

### 2.0L Turbo I4

The available 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine delivers a much peppier 252 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, providing stronger passing power and increased towing capacity. It generally offers robust performance when properly maintained.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with the 1.5L engine, this conventional 6-speed automatic provides reliable and predictable gear changes for everyday driving.

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Matched with the 2.0L engine, the 9-speed automatic transmission keeps the engine in its optimal power band while contributing to highway cruising efficiency.

## Brakes

### Brake Vacuum Pump

Owners have reported rare instances where the mechanical brake vacuum pump fails internally, resulting in a sudden loss of power brake assist and a hard pedal feel.

**Brake Pedal Stiffness and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a sudden hard brake pedal with severely reduced braking power, often without advance warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the internally damaged brake vacuum pump and associated components.
- **Cost:** $950

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Accessories

Electrical accessories, including infotainment displays and auxiliary sensors, occasionally exhibit glitches or complete unresponsiveness.

**Infotainment Screen and Radio Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The center touchscreen or audio system becomes completely inoperable or experiences intermittent resets.
- **Solution:** Have the dealer perform a diagnostic scan and update or replace the infotainment module if required.
- **Cost:** $950

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V668000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Equinox&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Missing bolts on the start-stop accumulator endcap could result in a transmission oil leak and may progress to a loss of propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash. A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the start-stop transmission accumulator and replace it if any bolts are missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V667000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Equinox&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of the ignition source can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel tank, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V704000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Equinox&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Brake drag may result in overheating of the brake pads, affecting the braking ability and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rear brake calipers, including the brake hose gasket, brake fluid and wheel to hub lubricant, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V339000: Child Seat:Vehicle Latch Anchor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Equinox&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The four rear-seat lower child seat LATCH anchorage bars may have excessive powder coating, which could prevent a child seat from being installed with the anchorage bars. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 225, "Child Restraint Anchorage Systems." The inability to install a child seat.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the latch anchorage bars, and remove and replace the finish, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Chevrolet Equinox is a versatile compact SUV, but prospective buyers should inspect the vehicle thoroughly for potential brake vacuum pump issues and electrical issues. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection and a verified service history will go a long way toward securing a reliable daily driver.
