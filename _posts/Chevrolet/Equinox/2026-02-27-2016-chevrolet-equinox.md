# 2016 Chevrolet Equinox Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Chevrolet Equinox is a compact crossover that offers a comfortable ride and spacious cabin, making it a popular choice for families and daily commuters. However, prospective buyers should approach with caution due to notable powertrain and electrical complaints. Engine oil consumption and unexpected stalling are among the primary issues reported by owners, requiring careful inspection and consistent maintenance monitoring.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder

The 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine produces 182 horsepower and 172 lb-ft of torque, offering adequate power for daily driving. It is the more common engine option but requires close monitoring for oil consumption issues.

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Engine rapidly burns through oil between changes without visible external leaks, potentially leading to internal engine knocking or failure if left unchecked.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels weekly and consult a mechanic for piston ring inspection or service bulletins if oil loss is severe.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle unexpectedly loses power or stalls while driving, often accompanied by warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fuel system and electronic throttle body components; update powertrain control module software if applicable.

### 3.6L V6

The 3.6-liter V6 engine delivers a robust 301 horsepower and 272 lb-ft of torque, providing strong acceleration and a higher towing capacity. It generally avoids the severe oil consumption complaints associated with the smaller four-cylinder option.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission pairs with both engine options, delivering smooth gear changes in front-wheel or all-wheel-drive configurations. Overall, it tends to be a relatively dependable mechanical unit when properly serviced.

## Steering

### Power Steering

Power steering failures, though less frequent, present significant safety risks if steering assist is lost entirely.

**Loss of Power Steering Assist**
- **Symptoms:** Steering becomes stiff or completely locks into one position, making the vehicle extremely difficult to maneuver.
- **Solution:** Inspect power steering electronic components, wiring harnesses, and steering rack assembly for faults.

## Exterior

### Windshield Wipers

Issues with the wiper linkage can lead to sudden loss of visibility during severe weather events.

**Wiper Linkage Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Windshield wipers suddenly stop working or freeze in the middle of the windshield during heavy rain or snow storms, severely limiting visibility.
- **Solution:** Replace the wiper motor linkage assembly if the ball joints pop out or strip.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V449000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Equinox&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." If the information on the certification labels is incorrect, the operator may install incorrectly sized tires or rims or may improperly inflate the tires, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the certification label, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2016 Chevrolet Equinox, buyers should prioritize models equipped with the 3.6L V6 engine or ensure the 2.4L engine has a well-documented maintenance history regarding oil consumption. A thorough pre-purchase inspection should check the windshield wiper linkage and electronic power steering systems. Keeping up with fluid checks and addressing dashboard warnings promptly will help maintain vehicle reliability.
