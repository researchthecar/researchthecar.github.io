# 2023 Chevrolet Equinox Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Chevrolet Equinox is a compact crossover SUV offering a comfortable ride, practical interior space, and modern technology features. While many owners enjoy its daily drivability and fuel economy, used car shoppers should review reported owner complaints regarding fuel system concerns, electrical glitches, and specific towing behaviors. Taking time to inspect a used model thoroughly can help ensure a reliable ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged Inline-4

The standard 1.5-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 175 horsepower and 203 lb-ft of torque, paired with front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive. It provides adequate power for daily commuting, though some owners have reported fuel delivery and stalling concerns.

**Fuel Pump and Injector Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may enter a low power mode, display reduced power warnings, stall, or fail to restart.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the fuel pressure and replace failing fuel pumps or injectors as necessary.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth shifts and works efficiently with the 1.5L engine. It generally offers dependable daily operation, though proper maintenance is recommended.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The standard braking system provides reliable stopping power under normal conditions, but rare hydraulic failures require prompt attention.

**Brake Pedal Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal travels all the way to the floor during operation, resulting in a severe reduction in braking performance.
- **Solution:** Inspect the braking hydraulic system immediately and replace any defective master cylinders or components.

## Steering

### Steering and Flat Towing Mechanics

Steering components provide responsive handling for daily driving, though owners planning to flat-tow the vehicle should note specific handling behaviors.

**Tire Binding During Flat Towing**
- **Symptoms:** Tires lock up, squeal, or drag when executing tight turns while flat towing behind a motorhome.
- **Solution:** Verify alignment with owner's manual instructions or consult a dealer regarding specific dinghy towing setup requirements.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Body Control

Electrical systems control convenience features and driver aids, though occasional cabin water leaks and touchscreen glitches have been documented.

**Touchscreen and Water Leak Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment screen freezes or produces loud audio noise; water leaks into the rear passenger compartment and fuse panel.
- **Solution:** Check rear window and pillar seals for proper water drainage, and update or reset infotainment software.

## Exterior

### Body Structure and Glass

Exterior styling and body panels are generally robust, though rare glass durability incidents have been reported.

**Rear Window Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Rear liftgate glass shatters unexpectedly when closing the door.
- **Solution:** Replace the shattered rear glass and inspect the liftgate alignment and latch pressure.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V724000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Equinox&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A seat with a missing or loose bolt may not properly restrain the occupant in a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the driver's seat and replace the seat cushion frame, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V929000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Equinox&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The Occupant Classification System may not properly disable the front passenger air bag when there is a small child or child restraint detected in the seat. In the event of a crash, a front passenger air bag that deploys with a child or infant in the front passenger seat can increase their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the passenger presence system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V339000: Child Seat:Vehicle Latch Anchor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Equinox&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The four rear-seat lower child seat LATCH anchorage bars may have excessive powder coating, which could prevent a child seat from being installed with the anchorage bars. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 225, "Child Restraint Anchorage Systems." The inability to install a child seat.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the latch anchorage bars, and remove and replace the finish, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Chevrolet Equinox is a versatile compact SUV that appeals to daily drivers and small families alike. While most vehicles provide trouble-free service, prospective buyers should verify proper functioning of the fuel system, check for any signs of cabin water leaks, and ensure all electrical features operate correctly. A thorough pre-purchase inspection will help secure a dependable used vehicle.
