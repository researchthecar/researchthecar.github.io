# 2016 Chevrolet Silverado 2500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Chevrolet Silverado 2500 is a heavy-duty workhorse capable of handling demanding towing and hauling tasks. Buyers exploring the used market should carefully evaluate the service history and perform thorough pre-purchase inspections, as owner complaints highlight notable mechanical and electrical concerns that can surface over time.

---

## Engine

### 6.0L Vortec V8 Gasoline

The standard 6.0-liter V8 gasoline engine produces approximately 360 horsepower and 380 lb-ft of torque, providing reliable baseline performance for everyday work duties without complex diesel emission systems.

### 6.6L Duramax V8 Diesel

The optional 6.6-liter Duramax turbocharged V8 diesel engine delivers massive torque for heavy towing, though buyers should monitor the high-pressure fuel pump for potential wear issues.

**Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Engine may fail to start, lose fuel pressure, or show metal shavings in the fuel system due to pump wear.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the high-pressure fuel pump and clean or replace affected fuel system components.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (Hydra-Matic 6L90)

Paired with the gasoline engine, this 6-speed automatic transmission offers proven durability under standard commercial and recreational workloads.

### 6-Speed Heavy-Duty Automatic (Allison 1000)

Coupled with the Duramax diesel engine, the Allison 6-speed automatic is renowned for heavy-duty capability, though sporadic reports of torque converter wear and harsh shifting require attentive maintenance checks.

**Torque Converter and Shifting Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Hard downshifts, transmission entering limp mode, check engine lights, or shuddering and overheating when towing.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace damaged internal components such as the torque converter, or rebuild/replace the transmission unit.

## Brakes

### Trailer Brake System

The integrated trailer brake controller is vital for towing safety, but electrical module glitches can trigger nuisance warnings or intermittent faults.

**Trailer Brake Fault Messages**
- **Symptoms:** Warning chimes and error messages stating 'Service Trailer Brake System' repeatedly illuminate on the instrument cluster.
- **Solution:** Diagnose trailer brake wiring, modules, and connections; replace faulty control modules if necessary.

## Steering

### Steering Gear Box and Linkage

Steering linkage and gear box wear can develop over time, potentially leading to loose handling and tracking difficulties.

**Excessive Play and Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Loose steering feel with significant play in the wheel, sudden pulling to either side, and fluid leaks from the steering gear box.
- **Solution:** Inspect steering components for wear and replace the steering gear box or worn linkage parts.

## Electrical

### Instrument Cluster and Accessories

Cabin electronics generally hold up well, though auxiliary add-ons or heavy-duty accessory packages can occasionally expose wiring vulnerabilities.

**Electrical System Cutout**
- **Symptoms:** Instrument cluster, HVAC controls, radio, and auxiliary electrical systems intermittently lose power or fail to function.
- **Solution:** Inspect accessory wiring harnesses, grounds, and upfitter connections for secure installation and sealing.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety restraints are critical for occupant protection, and proper mechanical operation of belt retractors should be verified during inspection.

**Seat Belt Tensioner Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Seat belt retractors or pretensioners fail to operate correctly, triggering warning lights or loosening passenger restraint.
- **Solution:** Inspect front seat belt assemblies and replace malfunctioning tensioners or sensors.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V578000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Pedals And Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+2500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Either condition may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will add adhesive to the nut and reinstall the nut with increased tightness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V651000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+2500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test. During this test, deployment of the frontal air bags and the seat belt pretensioners would not occur in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V437000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+2500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** While being previously remedied for recall 16V-651, the affected vehicles may not have received the complete software update necessary to remedy the recall condition. Without the update, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V069000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Pedals And Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+2500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Either condition may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will add adhesive to the brake pedal pivot nut and reinstall with an increased torque, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V504000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain:Inflator](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+2500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A separated inflator end cap or inflator rupture can allow compressed gas to escape, resulting in the end cap or other components being propelled into the vehicle, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the left and right side RRAB modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Chevrolet Silverado 2500 offers impressive capability for hauling and towing, but used-car buyers should perform a rigorous mechanical inspection. Pay close attention to the health of the diesel fuel system, transmission shifting performance, steering gear box play, and trailer brake electronics before finalizing a purchase.
