## 2005 Chevrolet Tracker

The 2005 Chevrolet Tracker is a compact SUV that offers a combination of practicality and affordability. It is available with two engine options: a 2.0-liter inline-four (I4) and a 2.5-liter V6. Transmission options include a five-speed manual and a four-speed automatic. The Tracker is praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and affordable price point. However, it has also been criticized for its lackluster performance and poor fuel economy.

**Engine**

* **2.0L I4**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil leaks. Symptoms include overheating, reduced engine power, and a check engine light. The repair involves replacing the gasket and typically costs $500 - $700.
    * **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** The valve cover gasket can also leak, causing oil to seep out. Symptoms include a burning oil smell, decreased oil levels, and a check engine light. The repair involves replacing the gasket and usually costs $150 - $250.
* **2.5L V6**
    * **Piston Ring Failure:** The piston rings can fail, causing excessive oil consumption. Symptoms include blue or white smoke from the exhaust, low oil levels, and a reduction in engine power. The repair involves replacing the piston rings and can cost $1,000 - $1,500.

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Manual**
    * **Synchronizer Failure:** The synchronizers in the manual transmission can wear out, making it difficult to shift gears. Symptoms include grinding noises when shifting, difficulty engaging gears, and a check engine light. The repair involves replacing the synchronizers and can cost $500 - $800.
* **4-Speed Automatic**
    * **Transmission Fluid Leak:** The transmission fluid pan gasket can leak, causing transmission fluid to leak out. Symptoms include low transmission fluid levels, slipping gears, and a check engine light. The repair involves replacing the gasket and typically costs $150 - $250.
    * **Valve Body Failure:** The valve body in the automatic transmission can fail, causing shifting problems. Symptoms include erratic shifting, slipping gears, and a check engine light. The repair involves replacing the valve body and can cost $800 - $1,200.

**Suspension**

* **Rear Leaf Spring Failure:** The rear leaf springs can break or sag, affecting ride quality and causing alignment issues. Symptoms include a rough ride, uneven tire wear, and a misaligned steering wheel. The repair involves replacing the leaf springs and can cost $300 - $500 per side.
* **Front Strut Failure:** The front struts can wear out, causing a loss of ride comfort and reduced handling. Symptoms include excessive bouncing, noise when driving over bumps, and uneven tire wear. The repair involves replacing the struts and typically costs $400 - $600 per side.

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, preventing the battery from charging. Symptoms include dim headlights, difficulty starting the vehicle, and a check engine light. The repair involves replacing the alternator and typically costs $300 - $500.
* **Starter Failure:** The starter can fail, preventing the engine from starting. Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle, grinding noises when starting, and a check engine light. The repair involves replacing the starter and can cost $250 - $400.
* **Battery Drain:** The vehicle may experience a battery drain, causing the battery to discharge quickly. Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle, dim headlights, and a check engine light. The repair involves identifying and fixing the cause of the drain, which can vary in cost.

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Failure:** The door handles can break or become loose, making it difficult to open or close the doors. Symptoms include loose handles, difficulty opening or closing the doors, and a rattling noise. The repair involves replacing the door handles and typically costs $100 - $200 per handle.

**Interior**

* **Power Window Failure:** The power window motors or switches can fail, preventing the windows from working properly. Symptoms include inoperative windows, slow window operation, and a clicking noise. The repair involves replacing the motor or switch and typically costs $150 - $300 per window.
* **Dashboard Cracking:** The dashboard material can crack or fade over time, affecting the aesthetics of the vehicle. Symptoms include visible cracks or discoloration on the dashboard. The repair involves replacing the dashboard, which can cost $500 - $800.

**Summary**

The 2005 Chevrolet Tracker has several common issues, including intake manifold gasket failure, piston ring failure, transmission fluid leaks, valve body failure, rear leaf spring failure, alternator failure, and power window failure. While some repairs can be relatively inexpensive, others can be costly, especially the engine and transmission repairs.
