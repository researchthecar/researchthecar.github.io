## 2021 Chevrolet Tracker

The 2021 Chevrolet Tracker is a subcompact SUV that offers a combination of affordability, practicality, and a comfortable ride. It comes standard with a 1.4-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing 155 horsepower and 177 lb-ft of torque, paired with a six-speed automatic transmission. The Tracker is also available with a turbocharged 1.3-liter four-cylinder engine that produces 155 horsepower and 174 lb-ft of torque, paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). 

Despite its strengths, the 2021 Chevrolet Tracker has several reported issues that owners have encountered.


### Engine

#### 1.4-liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder Engine

**Faulty Turbocharger**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, reduced engine performance, turbocharger whine or whistling noises.
Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
Recalls: None Found

#### 1.3-liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder Engine

**No major issues reported**

### Transmission

#### Six-Speed Automatic Transmission

**Delayed Gear Engagement or Shifting**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, transmission slipping or jerking during gear changes.
Solution: Transmission fluid flush or replacement, potential software updates or repairs.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

#### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

**No major issues reported**

### Suspension

**No major issues reported**

### Electrical

**Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: Rapid battery discharge, difficulty starting the vehicle.
Solution: Battery replacement, electrical system inspection to identify potential parasitic draw.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

**Infotainment System Issues**
Description/Symptoms: Screen freezing, unresponsive touchscreen, audio system malfunctions.
Solution: Software updates, hardware repairs or replacements.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

### Exterior

**Sunroof Leaks**
Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the cabin through the sunroof.
Solution: Replace or reseal the sunroof.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

### Interior

**Seat Adjustment Issues**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty adjusting the driver's seat, loose or broken seat components.
Solution: Replace or repair seat components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

### Summary

The 2021 Chevrolet Tracker has a mix of reported issues that range from minor inconveniences to more serious mechanical problems. Some owners have experienced electrical gremlins and sunroof leaks, while others have encountered engine and transmission issues. The severity and cost of repairs can vary depending on the nature of the problem. It's important for potential buyers to be aware of these issues and consider whether they are acceptable risks before purchasing a Tracker.
