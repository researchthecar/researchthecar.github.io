The 2001 Chevrolet Tracker is a compact SUV that gained popularity for its maneuverability, affordability, and fuel efficiency. It came with two engine options: a 2.0L and a 2.5L, paired with either a 5-speed manual or a 4-speed automatic transmission. While it offered a solid driving experience, the vehicle has also been plagued by several issues over the years.

**Engine**

**2.0L Engine**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** This gasket can fail, causing vacuum leaks and rough idle.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Belt Tensioner Failure:** The timing belt tensioner can fail, causing the timing belt to slip or break, leading to catastrophic engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the timing belt and tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Engine**

* **Head Gasket Failure:** This gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil to leak and leading to engine overheating.
    * Solution: Replace the head gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
    
**Transmission**

**5-speed Manual Transmission**

* **Transmission Fluid Leaks:** The transmission may develop leaks from various seals and gaskets.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking seals or gaskets.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Synchronizer Failure:** The synchronizers can wear out, causing difficulty shifting gears or grinding noises.
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**4-speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Torque Converter Failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing loss of power and transmission shudder.
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure:** The TCM can fail, causing transmission malfunction and shifting problems.
    * Solution: Replace the TCM.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Failure:** The struts can wear out, leading to a rough ride, poor handling, and uneven tire wear.
    * Solution: Replace the struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Control Arm Bushing Failure:** The control arm bushings can fail, causing clunking noises and vibration.
    * Solution: Replace the control arm bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, leading to a loss of electrical power and potential battery failure.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Drain:** The Tracker can experience battery drain due to faulty components or a parasitic draw.
    * Solution: Test the electrical system to identify and fix the source of the drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Body Panels:** The body panels, particularly the rear wheel wells and undercarriage, can develop rust over time.
    * Solution: Apply rust protection or repair rusted areas as needed.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Door Panel Rattling:** The door panels may rattle due to loose screws or clips.
    * Solution: Tighten loose screws or replace damaged clips.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
* **Dashboard Cracking:** The dashboard can develop cracks or warping due to sun exposure.
    * Solution: Replace the dashboard or apply a dashboard cover.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Summary**

While the 2001 Chevrolet Tracker is known for its affordability and maneuverability, it has faced its fair share of mechanical and electrical issues. Some of the more severe and costly problems include head gasket failure, timing belt tensioner failure, and transmission control module failure. Other common issues include leaks, worn suspension components, and interior rattles. Overall, the reliability and longevity of the Tracker can be a concern for potential buyers, especially if they are not prepared for the potential costs of repairs.
