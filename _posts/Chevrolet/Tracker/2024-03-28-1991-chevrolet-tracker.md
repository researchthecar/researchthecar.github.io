**Introduction**

The 1991 Chevrolet Tracker is a compact SUV that was available with two engine options. The 1.6L inline-4 produced 80 horsepower and 98 lb-ft of torque, while the 1.8L inline-4 made 127 horsepower and 135 lb-ft of torque. Transmission offerings included a 5-speed manual or a 3-speed automatic. The Tracker was praised for its low price and versatility, but criticized for its lack of power and poor handling.

**Engine**

**1.6L Inline-4**

* **Head Gasket Failure:** Description: Blowing of the head gasket leading to coolant leaking, overheating, and potential engine damage. Solution: Replace the head gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+ Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Belt Failure:** Description: Breakage of the timing belt leading to catastrophic engine damage. Solution: Replace the timing belt according to the recommended maintenance schedule. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+ Recalls: None Found

**1.8L Inline-4**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **No Issues Found**

**3-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping:** Description: Transmission failing to engage gears properly, resulting in loss of power and difficulty shifting. Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+ Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Wear:** Description: Worn or damaged ball joints, tie rods, and/or control arms leading to loose handling, vibration, and uneven tire wear. Solution: Replace the affected components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Suspension Sag:** Description: Sagging rear suspension due to worn or broken coil springs or shock absorbers. Solution: Replace the springs and/or shocks. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+ Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Description: Failure of the alternator to charge the battery, leading to electrical issues and potential stalling. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+ Recalls: None Found
* **Starter Problems:** Description: Failure of the starter to engage or turn over the engine. Solution: Replace the starter or repair electrical connections. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** Description: Rust developing on body panels, particularly around the wheel wells, undercarriage, and roof. Solution: Treat and prevent rust by applying rust inhibitors or repairing affected areas. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity of the rust Recalls: None Found
* **Cracked Tail Lights:** Description: Tail lights breaking or cracking due to impacts or exposure to elements. Solution: Replace the tail lights. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:** Description: Cracking of the dashboard due to exposure to sunlight or temperature fluctuations. Solution: Replace or repair the dashboard. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ Recalls: None Found
* **Seat Fabric Tearing:** Description: Tearing or fraying of the seat fabric due to wear and tear. Solution: Repair or replace the affected seat covers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1991 Chevrolet Tracker has some common issues, particularly with the engine, transmission, and suspension. The cost of repairs can vary significantly depending on the severity of the problem. While the Tracker is a reliable vehicle overall, it is essential to be aware of potential issues before purchasing one.
