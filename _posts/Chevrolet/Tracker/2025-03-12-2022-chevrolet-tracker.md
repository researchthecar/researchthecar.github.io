The 2022 Chevrolet Tracker is a subcompact SUV designed for affordability and fuel efficiency. In many markets, it is sold as the Chevrolet Seeker. It's generally liked for its low price point, relatively spacious interior for its class, and decent fuel economy. Common complaints often revolve around its somewhat underpowered engine and basic interior features. In most markets, it is only offered with the 1.2L Turbocharged Inline-3 engine, producing around 130-137 horsepower and 148 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**1.2L Turbocharged Inline-3 (LIH)**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, check engine light illuminated, whistling noise from the engine. Potentially oil leaks.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1200 - $2500+
	*   Recalls: None Found

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Low oil level warning light illuminates frequently, needing to add oil between scheduled oil changes, blue smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to determine the cause (e.g., piston rings, valve stem seals). Repair or engine replacement depending on the severity.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 (minor repairs) - $5000+ (engine replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic (6T40/MH8)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or delayed shifts between gears, feeling the transmission "slip" or hesitate during acceleration.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to identify the root cause (e.g., valve body issues, worn clutch packs). Could involve valve body replacement, transmission rebuild or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 (valve body) - $4000+ (transmission rebuild/replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Wear of Stabilizer Bar Links**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the front or rear suspension, especially when driving over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the stabilizer bar links.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen freezes, becomes unresponsive, or restarts randomly.
    *   Solution: Software update to the infotainment system or replacement of the infotainment unit if the problem persists.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $800+ (infotainment unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The car is difficult to start, especially after sitting for a few days. The battery may need frequent recharging.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair the source of the parasitic drain (e.g., faulty module, aftermarket accessory).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (depending on the source of the drain)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Poor Quality Door Seals**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive wind noise inside the cabin, water leaking into the cabin during rain or car washes.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the door seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ (per door)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises from Dashboard**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Annoying rattles or squeaks emanating from the dashboard, particularly when driving on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Identifying and securing the loose components within the dashboard.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ (depending on the complexity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Chevrolet Tracker can have some notable issues. The most concerning are potential engine problems like turbocharger failure and excessive oil consumption, and transmission issues such as harsh shifting. Electrical glitches with the infotainment system and battery drain can be frustrating, while suspension and interior noise issues impact overall driving enjoyment. The cost to fix these problems ranges from a few hundred dollars for minor repairs to several thousand dollars for major engine or transmission work, making it important to thoroughly inspect and address any potential issues when considering this vehicle.
