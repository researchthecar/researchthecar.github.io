# 2024 Chevrolet Trax Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Chevrolet Trax offers a stylish exterior and attractive pricing, but early adopters have highlighted several recurring trouble spots. Owners frequently report electrical glitches, particularly involving infotainment screen reboots and malfunctioning instrument clusters. Additionally, powertrain complaints point to transmission hesitation and occasional jerky shifting.

---

## Engine

### 1.2L Turbocharged Inline-3

The 1.2-liter turbocharged inline-3 engine produces approximately 137 horsepower and 162 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting. While generally fuel-efficient, some early models exhibit concerning electrical and stalling issues that require careful diagnostic evaluation.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences sudden electrical cutoffs and engine stalling while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by loss of steering assist.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose the electrical and fuel delivery systems; software updates or sensor replacements may be required.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission pairs with the front-wheel-drive powertrain to deliver smooth operation under normal conditions. However, owner reports highlight intermittent shift quality concerns and hesitation.

**Transmission Slipping and Jerky Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Hard gear shifts at lower speeds, intermittent surging, and a sensation of slipping or unexpected braking pulsations at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition and consult a dealer for potential control module software updates or mechanical inspections.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Instrument Cluster

Electrical system complaints center around software instabilities in the central display, digital instrument panel, and exterior lighting controls.

**Screen Freezing and Glitching**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display and speedometer screen blink on and off, go completely blank, or freeze while driving.
- **Solution:** Dealer software flashes or module replacements may resolve persistent screen blackouts and connectivity dropouts.

**Headlight and Sensor Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights flicker or fail intermittently, and driver assistance sensors falsely trigger warning lights during rain or wind.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight wiring harnesses, clean sensor lenses, and check for diagnostic trouble codes.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V744000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Trax&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The software in the Virtual Cockpit Unit Module (VCU) may cause the instrument panel display to intermittently go blank at vehicle startup or while driving. A blank instrument panel will not show critical information, such as vehicle speed and warning lights, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used Chevrolet Trax, prospective buyers should thoroughly test all electronics, infotainment functions, and transmission shift quality during a test drive. Verifying that all software updates have been performed by a dealer can help mitigate some of the most common early-model complaints.
