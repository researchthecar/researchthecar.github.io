Here's a comprehensive breakdown of common issues and complaints for the 2017 Chevrolet Trax, formatted for your website.

The 2017 Chevrolet Trax is a subcompact SUV known for its maneuverability and fuel efficiency. It comes standard with a 1.4L turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing 138 horsepower and 148 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. While owners appreciate its ease of parking, nimble handling, and available all-wheel drive, some common complaints revolve around its relatively small cargo space, somewhat underwhelming acceleration, and potential reliability issues.

**Engine**

**1.4L Turbocharged Engine**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whistling or whining noise from the engine, check engine light illuminated.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Low coolant level, overheating, sweet smell coming from the engine, visible leaks. Often stems from the water outlet.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty water outlet or other leaking component and topping off or replacing coolant.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150-$500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **PCV (Positive Crankcase Ventilation) Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, check engine light, oil leaks, increased oil consumption. Often caused by a cracked or damaged PCV valve diaphragm, which is integrated into the valve cover in some models.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the valve cover assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Frequent need to add oil, blue smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Depending on the cause, solutions may include replacing valve seals, piston rings, or even a complete engine rebuild in severe cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3000+ depending on the severity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission (6T40)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Jerky shifts, hesitation when accelerating, transmission slipping out of gear, check engine light on.
    *   Solution: Could require a transmission flush, repair or replacement of solenoids, valve body work, or in severe cases, a complete transmission rebuild or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (flush) - $3500+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibration at highway speeds, stalling, poor fuel economy.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Wear of Struts/Shocks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, excessive body roll, poor handling, clunking noises over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replacement of worn struts and/or shocks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per axle
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Wheel Bearing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Humming or grinding noise that changes with vehicle speed, wheel play when checked by hand.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty wheel bearing.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per wheel
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting, slow cranking, dead battery, electrical system malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Battery replacement, check for parasitic draws.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Infotainment System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, unresponsive touchscreen, Bluetooth connectivity issues, audio problems.
    *   Solution: Software update, resetting the system, or replacing the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (update) - $800+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failure (O2, MAF, etc.)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, poor fuel economy, rough idling, stalling.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis with a scan tool and replacement of the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust on Underbody Components**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Visible rust on suspension components, exhaust system, and other underbody parts, especially in areas with road salt.
    *   Solution: Rust removal, application of rust inhibitors, and replacement of severely corroded parts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+, depending on the extent of the rust.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Seatbelt Latch Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty latching or unlatching the seatbelt, seatbelt not staying latched.
    * Solution: Replacement of the faulty seatbelt latch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$300+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Air Conditioning Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning not blowing cold, weak airflow, strange noises from the AC system.
    *   Solution: Recharge the AC system. Repair AC compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1200+ depending on issue
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Chevrolet Trax, while offering fuel efficiency and maneuverability, is prone to several issues. Engine problems, particularly turbocharger and PCV valve failures, can be costly. Transmission issues, while not always catastrophic, can lead to expensive repairs or replacements. Electrical and infotainment system glitches can also be frustrating for owners. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to major mechanical failures, with repair costs varying accordingly. Potential buyers should be aware of these common problems and consider a pre-purchase inspection to identify any existing or potential issues.
