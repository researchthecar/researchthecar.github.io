# 2018 Chevrolet Trax Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Chevrolet Trax, prospective buyers will find a compact crossover designed primarily for urban commuting and easy maneuverability. While it offers a budget-friendly entry point into the SUV market, review of owner complaints highlights several areas requiring careful inspection, particularly concerning powertrain accessories, suspension components, and electrical systems.

---

## Engine

### 1.4L Turbocharged I4

Equipped with a 1.4-liter turbocharged inline-four engine producing around 138 horsepower, this powerplant provides adequate pep for city driving but requires routine maintenance and diligent monitoring of its forced-induction components.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected engine stalling, activation of traction control warning lights, and sudden losses of motive power while driving.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the engine control modules, wiring harnesses, and related sensors for faults or damage.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with front-wheel or all-wheel drive setups, the six-speed automatic transmission generally delivers smooth daily shifts, though prospective buyers should still test-drive thoroughly to ensure responsive gear engagement.

## Suspension

### Control Arms

The suspension system manages daily road impacts well, though front control arms and associated joints are known wear items that warrant a pre-purchase inspection.

**Control Arm and Joint Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note clunking noises when hitting bumps or turning, loose suspension feel, and premature component wear.
- **Solution:** Inspect control arms, bushings, and ball joints for looseness or structural cracking, and replace worn parts as necessary.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Service Brakes

Standard disc and drum brake setups provide sufficient stopping power under normal conditions, but any reports of stiff pedals or warning lights require prompt professional diagnosis.

**Stiff Pedal and Reduced Stopping Response**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience unusually hard brake pedals or a temporary failure of the vehicle to slow down promptly when the pedal is depressed.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake booster, vacuum lines, and hydraulic fluid condition immediately if pedal firmness or stopping delay is observed.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

Electric power steering provides light effort at low speeds, though unexpected loss of assist has been flagged in owner reports as a safety concern.

**Power Steering Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of power steering assist accompanied by warning lights, making the steering wheel very heavy and difficult to turn.
- **Solution:** Check power steering electronic modules, fuses, and steering rack components for faults or electrical connection issues.

## Interior

### Blower Motor and Wiring

The electrical architecture handles basic cabin accessories adequately, though high-draw circuits like the HVAC blower motor can experience localized component degradation.

**Blower Motor Resistor and Wiring Failures**
- **Symptoms:** The HVAC blower motor stops working or operates intermittently, sometimes accompanied by melting wires or burning smells near the resistor.
- **Solution:** Replace the blower motor, resistor, and any damaged wiring harnesses to restore proper climate control function and eliminate fire risks.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V312000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm:Lower Ball Joint](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Trax&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A partial separation of the lower-control arm from the vehicle could cause the attached front wheel to toe outward, impacting the vehicle's handling and steering and increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the left and right front lower-control arms, replacing them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Chevrolet Trax offers practical dimensions and an accessible price point for used car buyers seeking a small crossover. However, prospective owners should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection paying close attention to suspension control arms, brake responsiveness, electrical accessories, and steering functionality to avoid unexpected repair bills.
