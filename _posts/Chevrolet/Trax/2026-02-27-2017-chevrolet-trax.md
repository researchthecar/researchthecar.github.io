# 2017 Chevrolet Trax Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Chevrolet Trax offers nimble handling and compact dimensions that appeal to urban drivers, but used car buyers should approach with careful inspection. While it provides decent fuel economy and standard tech features, owner reports highlight recurring concerns with turbocharger reliability, cooling system leaks, and suspension wear. Potential buyers should thoroughly evaluate the vehicle's maintenance history and powertrain health before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 1.4L Turbocharged I4

The 1.4-liter turbocharged inline-four engine produces 138 horsepower and 148 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate pep for city commuting. However, owners have reported recurring issues concerning turbocharger performance and coolant leaks requiring careful monitoring.

**Engine Stalling and Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences sudden engine stalling, a illuminated check engine light with code P0299, and a loss of motive power while driving.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic inspection of the turbocharger system; replacement of failing turbocharger components or sensors.

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Abnormal ticking noises, smoke coming from the engine compartment, and low coolant levels.
- **Solution:** Inspect cooling lines and components for leaks, and replace damaged valves or seals.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission delivers power smoothly to the front wheels or available all-wheel-drive system, though routine fluid changes are recommended to ensure longevity.

## Suspension

### Suspension and Control Arms

Suspension components such as control arms and sway bar links have been noted by owners to wear out prematurely, requiring mechanical attention.

**Worn Sway Bar Links and Control Arms**
- **Symptoms:** Premature wear, uneven tire wear, and clunking or knocking noises from the front suspension during cornering.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace worn sway bar links, ball joints, or lower control arms.

## Exterior

### Windows and Sunroof

Exterior complaints center occasionally on tempered glass fragility, with a small number of reports highlighting spontaneous shattering of windows and sunroofs.

**Spontaneous Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Rear side windows or sunroof glass shattering unexpectedly with a loud popping noise.
- **Solution:** Clear out broken glass fragments and replace the shattered window or sunroof panel.

## Interior

### HVAC Blower Motor and Controls

Electrical system reports commonly involve cabin climate control glitches, specifically related to blower motor resistors and manual control cables.

**Blower Motor Resistor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Failure of the heating and AC fan, burning smells, or smoke coming from the dashboard area due to a short circuit.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty blower motor resistor and wiring harness.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V312000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm:Lower Ball Joint](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Trax&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A partial separation of the lower-control arm from the vehicle could cause the attached front wheel to toe outward, impacting the vehicle's handling and steering and increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the left and right front lower-control arms, replacing them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V652000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm:Lower Ball Joint](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Trax&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A partial separation of the lower-control arm from the vehicle could cause the attached front wheel to toe outward, impacting the vehicle's handling and steering and increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the left and right front lower-control arms, replacing them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V651000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Trax&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test. During this test, deployment of the frontal air bags and the seat belt pretensioners would not occur in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Chevrolet Trax can be an affordable and practical choice for budget-conscious buyers seeking a small crossover, but it requires diligent pre-purchase screening. Buyers should pay close attention to turbocharger operation, verify the condition of the cooling system, and inspect the suspension and HVAC components during a test drive. Partnering with a trusted mechanic for a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection is strongly advised to uncover potential hidden repair costs.
