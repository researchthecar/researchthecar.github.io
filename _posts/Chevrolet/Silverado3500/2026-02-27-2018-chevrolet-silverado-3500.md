# 2018 Chevrolet Silverado 3500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a 2018 Chevrolet Silverado 3500, prospective buyers are looking at a heavy-duty truck designed for serious towing and hauling. While these trucks are built for commercial-grade workloads, owners have reported various mechanical and electrical quirks. Reviewing specific powertrain options and known trouble spots can help you navigate your used truck purchase.

---

## Engine

### 6.0L V8 Vortec Gasoline Engine

The 6.0-liter V8 gasoline engine produces around 360 horsepower and 380 lb-ft of torque, offering a traditional, simpler alternative for heavy-duty fleets. It generally provides dependable service, though buyers should watch for standard maintenance wear.

### 6.6L V8 Duramax Diesel Engine

The 6.6-liter Duramax V8 turbo-diesel pumps out roughly 445 horsepower and a massive 910 lb-ft of torque, paired with robust towing capabilities. While powerful, diesel systems require close attention to fuel and emissions maintenance.

**Engine Misfire and Knocking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported the engine suddenly misfiring, producing abnormal knocking sounds, and causing vehicle shuddering while traveling at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the fuel injectors, glow plugs, and internal components to isolate the source of the misfire.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission works alongside the gasoline engine to manage heavy loads. It is generally sturdy, though proper fluid maintenance is critical for longevity.

### Allison 6-Speed Heavy-Duty Automatic Transmission

The Allison 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with the Duramax diesel to handle high torque outputs. It is renowned for durability, but electronic sensors and control modules can occasionally cause shifting anomalies.

**Hard Shifting and Powertrain Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note harsh gear engagement and intermittent transmission slipping accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning is recommended to check transmission control modules and fluid condition.

## Electrical

### Starting and Accessory Systems

electrical issues in heavy-duty trucks can range from stubborn starter relays to complex wiring harnesses.

**Intermittent No-Start and Starter Click**
- **Symptoms:** When attempting to restart the vehicle after it has been turned off, the truck may fail to crank immediately, producing only a clicking noise from the starter area.
- **Solution:** Test the battery, starter motor, and ignition switch connections for corrosion or voltage drop.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V567000: Seat Belts:Front:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+3500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 209, "Seat Belt Assemblies." The seat belt retractors may not perform as intended during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace both front seat belt retractor assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V328000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+3500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The engine block heater cord or the terminals that connect the heater cord to the block heater may short circuit and fail. A short-circuit may damage the engine components and increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** GM will contact owners, dealers will replace the engine block heater, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V088000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+3500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the front tank overfills, the excess pressure may cause the front tank to expand and contact the driveshaft, possibly resulting in a hole in the tank. The hole will leak fuel, which in the presence of an ignition source, can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM has notified owners, and dealers will replace the rear-tank fuel-pump module, inspect the front tank and, if necessary, replace the front tank, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V496000: Equipment:Electrical:Engine Block Heater](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+3500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** An electrical short-circuit can increase the risk of a fire. Vehicles that have this feature disabled as a remedy to the safety condition will be contacted and offered replacement block heaters and heater cords under a separate customer satisfaction program, free of charge.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace engine block heaters and heater cords. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V047000: Fuel System, Diesel:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+3500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the fuel tank and fuel pump module, replacing them as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V756000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain:Inflator](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+3500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A separated inflator end cap or inflator rupture can allow compressed gas to escape, resulting in the end cap or other components being propelled into the vehicle, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the left and right side RRAB modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Chevrolet Silverado 3500 remains a capable workhorse for heavy hauling, especially when equipped with the Duramax diesel and Allison transmission. Buyers should thoroughly inspect the electrical system, test all powertrain functions during a pre-purchase inspection, and verify service history to ensure long-term reliability.
