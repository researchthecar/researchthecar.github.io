# 2016 Chevrolet Silverado 3500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used heavy-duty truck, evaluating past owner feedback is essential to understanding long-term ownership dynamics. This vehicle offers heavy-duty towing capability and robust utility, but buyers should carefully review potential trouble spots reported by previous owners. Familiarizing yourself with these common areas helps you plan for future maintenance and negotiate a fair price.

---

## Engine

### 6.0L V8 Vortec Gasoline Engine

The 6.0-liter V8 gasoline engine produces around 360 horsepower and 380 lb-ft of torque, offering a traditional, simpler powertrain choice for heavy-duty tasks. It is generally regarded as dependable for fleet and work use, though fuel economy is lower compared to diesel alternatives.

### 6.6L V8 Duramax Turbo-Diesel Engine

The 6.6-liter Duramax turbo-diesel V8 delivers high torque output (often exceeding 765 lb-ft depending on the exact model year) paired with an Allison transmission for heavy towing. Buyers should verify maintenance history, as fuel system repairs can be exceptionally costly if neglected.

**Fuel System and Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported sudden loss of engine power, entering low-power mode, or complete engine stalling related to fuel delivery components.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential replacement of high-pressure fuel pumps and contaminated fuel system components.
- **Cost:** $6,500 to $9,000

## Transmission

### Allison 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Matched primarily with the Duramax diesel engine, this heavy-duty 6-speed automatic transmission is widely praised for its durability under heavy towing loads. Routine fluid and filter changes are key to maintaining long-term reliability.

### 6-Speed Heavy-Duty Automatic Transmission (Gas)

Paired with the 6.0L gasoline engine, this conventional heavy-duty automatic transmission handles everyday work tasks and moderate hauling reliably. Regular service intervals help prevent premature wear.

## Brakes

### Trailer Brake and Hydraulic System

Braking systems on dual-rear-wheel and heavy-duty trucks require careful inspection of pads, rotors, and electronic trailer integration modules.

**Trailer Brake Controller Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard alerts indicating trailer brakes are disabled or service trailer brake warnings appearing intermittently.
- **Solution:** Check trailer wiring harnesses, clean corrosion from plugs, and diagnose integrated trailer brake control modules.

## Steering

### Steering Gear and Linkage

Steering components on heavy-duty trucks endure high stress, and owners frequently highlight play or looseness over time.

**Excessive Play and Wandering**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note loose steering feel, causing the vehicle to wander on the highway or pull inconsistently, particularly when towing heavy trailers.
- **Solution:** Inspect steering linkage, pitman arms, idler arms, and the steering gear box for wear, replacing worn components as needed.

## Exterior

### Wiring and Lighting Harnesses

Electrical accessories and exterior lighting connections can be vulnerable to moisture and corrosion, especially in regions that use road salt.

**Wiring Harness and Connector Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Burning odors, smoke from the dash, or exterior running and turn signal lights failing due to moisture and road salt accumulation.
- **Solution:** Inspect, clean, or replace corroded electrical pins, bulb sockets, and damaged wiring harnesses.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V578000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Pedals And Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+3500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Either condition may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will add adhesive to the nut and reinstall the nut with increased tightness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V088000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+3500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the front tank overfills, the excess pressure may cause the front tank to expand and contact the driveshaft, possibly resulting in a hole in the tank. The hole will leak fuel, which in the presence of an ignition source, can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM has notified owners, and dealers will replace the rear-tank fuel-pump module, inspect the front tank and, if necessary, replace the front tank, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V651000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+3500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test. During this test, deployment of the frontal air bags and the seat belt pretensioners would not occur in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V437000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+3500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** While being previously remedied for recall 16V-651, the affected vehicles may not have received the complete software update necessary to remedy the recall condition. Without the update, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V069000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Pedals And Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+3500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Either condition may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will add adhesive to the brake pedal pivot nut and reinstall with an increased torque, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V504000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain:Inflator](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+3500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A separated inflator end cap or inflator rupture can allow compressed gas to escape, resulting in the end cap or other components being propelled into the vehicle, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the left and right side RRAB modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

This heavy-duty truck is a capable workhorse equipped for demanding towing and hauling duties. While many owners experience dependable service, prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the steering linkage for excessive play, verify proper trailer brake controller operation, and carefully review the fuel system history on diesel variants to avoid unexpected repair expenses.
