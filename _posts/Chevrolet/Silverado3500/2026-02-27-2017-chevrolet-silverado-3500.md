# 2017 Chevrolet Silverado 3500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Chevrolet Silverado 3500 is a heavy-duty workhorse designed for demanding towing and hauling jobs. While its robust capability makes it a popular choice for commercial and heavy-duty personal use, prospective buyers should review known owner complaints concerning braking systems, transmission shifts, and electrical components. Understanding these common trouble spots can help you evaluate a used model's condition before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 6.0L V8 Vortec Gasoline Engine

The 6.0-liter V8 gasoline engine produces approximately 360 horsepower and 380 lb-ft of torque, offering reliable daily performance for standard heavy-duty tasks without the complexity of a diesel system.

### 6.6L V8 Duramax Diesel Engine

The 6.6-liter Duramax turbo-diesel V8 generates around 445 horsepower and a massive 910 lb-ft of torque, making it the preferred option for heavy towing, though fuel system components require careful inspection.

**Fuel Injector Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Engine may experience rough running, misfires, or fail to start due to fuel injector degradation.
- **Solution:** Diagnose faulty injectors using scan tools and replace failing injector units as necessary.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission (Gas)

Paired with the 6.0L gas engine, the 6-speed automatic transmission delivers dependable shifting under load, though regular fluid maintenance is recommended for longevity.

### Allison 6-Speed Heavy-Duty Automatic Transmission (Diesel)

The Allison 6-speed automatic transmission is engineered specifically for heavy-duty commercial towing and high-torque diesel applications, offering durable performance with proper upkeep.

**Transmission Slipping and Shifting Delays**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may hesitate, shudder during acceleration, or experience rough and delayed gear engagement.
- **Solution:** Perform a diagnostic check, flush fluid, or rebuild/replace internal components if excessive wear is found.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brake System

The heavy-duty braking system is designed to handle high loads, but owners have reported occasional vacuum assist and pedal stiffness failures.

**Brake Pedal Stiffness and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal can suddenly become extremely hard to depress, significantly reducing stopping power and increasing stopping distance.
- **Solution:** Inspect vacuum pump, brake booster, and hydraulic lines, and replace failed components immediately.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

Steering components generally withstand heavy use, though intermittent losses of power assist have been documented by owners.

**Loss of Power Steering Assist**
- **Symptoms:** Steering wheel becomes unexpectedly stiff and difficult to turn while maneuvering at low speeds or making turns.
- **Solution:** Inspect steering pump, electrical connections, and assist modules, and replace faulty components.

## Exterior

### Fuse Panel and Lighting

Electrical architecture supports heavy towing and complex electronics, though occasional fuse panel and lighting glitches require attention.

**Melted Fuse Panel and Starting Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may fail to start, experience electrical accessory failures, or show signs of melting around the fuse panel.
- **Solution:** Inspect wiring harnesses and fuse blocks, and replace damaged panels or wiring.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V567000: Seat Belts:Front:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+3500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 209, "Seat Belt Assemblies." The seat belt retractors may not perform as intended during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace both front seat belt retractor assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V328000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+3500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The engine block heater cord or the terminals that connect the heater cord to the block heater may short circuit and fail. A short-circuit may damage the engine components and increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** GM will contact owners, dealers will replace the engine block heater, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V088000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+3500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the front tank overfills, the excess pressure may cause the front tank to expand and contact the driveshaft, possibly resulting in a hole in the tank. The hole will leak fuel, which in the presence of an ignition source, can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM has notified owners, and dealers will replace the rear-tank fuel-pump module, inspect the front tank and, if necessary, replace the front tank, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V651000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+3500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test. During this test, deployment of the frontal air bags and the seat belt pretensioners would not occur in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V496000: Equipment:Electrical:Engine Block Heater](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+3500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** An electrical short-circuit can increase the risk of a fire. Vehicles that have this feature disabled as a remedy to the safety condition will be contacted and offered replacement block heaters and heater cords under a separate customer satisfaction program, free of charge.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace engine block heaters and heater cords. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V047000: Fuel System, Diesel:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Silverado+3500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the fuel tank and fuel pump module, replacing them as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Chevrolet Silverado 3500 is a formidable heavy-duty truck capable of tackling strenuous commercial and towing tasks. While its engines and transmissions are generally built to last, used-car buyers should thoroughly inspect the braking system, power steering functionality, and electrical connections during a pre-purchase test drive. Addressing these potential wear points early ensures safer and more reliable operation.
