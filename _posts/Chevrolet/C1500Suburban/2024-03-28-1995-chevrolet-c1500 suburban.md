The 1995 Chevrolet C1500 Suburban is a full-size SUV that was available with a variety of engine and transmission options. The most popular engine choice was the 5.7L V8, which produced 200 horsepower and 300 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options included the 4.3L V6, the 5.0L V8, and the 6.5L V8 diesel. The C1500 Suburban was also available with a variety of transmission options, including a four-speed automatic, a five-speed manual, and a six-speed manual.

Owners of the 1995 Chevrolet C1500 Suburban have reported a number of different issues with their vehicles. These issues range from minor annoyances to major mechanical failures.

**Engine**

* **5.7L V8**
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing a loss of coolant and engine overheating.
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **4.3L V6**
    * **Timing chain failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain can fail, causing the engine to lose power and eventually stall.
        * Solution: Replace the timing chain.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **4-speed automatic**
    * **Transmission slipping**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip when shifting gears, causing a loss of power and acceleration.
        * Solution: Replace the transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **5-speed manual**
    * **Clutch failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The clutch may fail, causing the transmission to slip and the vehicle to lose power.
        * Solution: Replace the clutch.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension**
    * **Ball joint failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The ball joints can fail, causing the front wheels to lose alignment and the vehicle to pull to one side.
        * Solution: Replace the ball joints.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear suspension**
    * **Leaf spring failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The leaf springs can fail, causing the rear of the vehicle to sag and the ride quality to suffer.
        * Solution: Replace the leaf springs.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery**
    * **Battery failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The battery may fail, causing the vehicle to lose power and the electrical system to malfunction.
        * Solution: Replace the battery.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Body panels**
    * **Rust**
        * Description/Symptoms: The body panels can rust, causing the vehicle to look unsightly and the paint to peel off.
        * Solution: Repair the rust damage.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard**
    * **Cracking**
        * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard may crack, causing the vehicle to look unsightly.
        * Solution: Replace the dashboard.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 1995 Chevrolet C1500 Suburban is a reliable vehicle that is well-suited for a variety of purposes. However, it is not without its issues. The most common issues reported by owners include intake manifold gasket failure, timing chain failure, transmission slipping, ball joint failure, and rust. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the condition of the vehicle.
