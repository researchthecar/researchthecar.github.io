**1995 Chevrolet Impala**

The 1995 Chevrolet Impala is a full-size sedan offered with a range of engine and transmission options. Its spacious interior and comfortable ride make it a popular choice for families and those who value practicality. The Impala also boasts a powerful engine lineup, delivering impressive performance for its size. However, like any vehicle, the 1995 Impala has its share of issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

* **3.8L V6**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing a vacuum leak and loss of engine power. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * **Throttle Position Sensor (TPS) Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The TPS can fail, leading to rough idling, stalling, and poor fuel economy. Solution: Replace the TPS. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
* **5.7L V8**
    * **Distributor Cap and Rotor Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The distributor cap and rotor can wear out, causing ignition problems and engine misfires. Solution: Replace the distributor cap and rotor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * **Intake Valve Carbon Buildup:** Description/Symptoms: Carbon buildup on the intake valves can reduce engine performance and fuel economy. Solution: Clean the intake valves. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Transmission**

* **4L60E Automatic Transmission**
    * **Overdrive Band Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The overdrive band can fail, resulting in slipping and shuddering when shifting into overdrive. Solution: Replace the overdrive band. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    * **Torque Converter Clutch (TCC) Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The TCC can fail, causing the transmission to slip or shudder during lockup. Solution: Replace the TCC. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
* **700R4 Automatic Transmission**
    * **Speed Sensor Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The speed sensor can fail, leading to incorrect speedometer readings and transmission shifting issues. Solution: Replace the speed sensor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * **Valve Body Leaks:** Description/Symptoms: The valve body can develop leaks, causing transmission fluid loss and shifting problems. Solution: Repair or replace the valve body. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Suspension**

* **Front Lower Control Arms:** Description/Symptoms: The front lower control arms can wear out, causing clunking noises and poor handling. Solution: Replace the lower control arms. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Rear Leaf Springs:** Description/Symptoms: The rear leaf springs can sag or break, resulting in a lowered ride height and poor handling. Solution: Replace the leaf springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The alternator can fail, causing the battery to discharge and the vehicle to stall. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Starter Solenoid Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The starter solenoid can fail, preventing the starter from engaging. Solution: Replace the starter solenoid. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Actuators:** Description/Symptoms: The door lock actuators can fail, causing the doors to lock or unlock intermittently. Solution: Replace the door lock actuators. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Interior**

* **Instrument Cluster Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The instrument cluster can fail, causing the gauges to malfunction. Solution: Replace the instrument cluster. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Power Window Switches:** Description/Symptoms: The power window switches can fail, preventing the windows from being raised or lowered. Solution: Replace the power window switches. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Summary**

The 1995 Chevrolet Impala is a reliable and comfortable full-size sedan that offers plenty of space and performance. However, like any vehicle, it has its share of issues that owners should be aware of. While some of these issues are minor and relatively inexpensive to fix, others can be more serious and costly. It is important to note that the severity and frequency of these issues can vary depending on individual driving habits and maintenance practices.
