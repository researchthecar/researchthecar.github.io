# 2018 Chevrolet Impala Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Chevrolet Impala is a spacious, comfortable full-size sedan known for its smooth ride and pleasant highway manners. While it generally serves as a reliable daily driver or fleet vehicle, used car buyers should pay attention to a few recurring owner complaints involving the transmission, steering, and electrical components. Reviewing service history and performing a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help uncover potential underlying issues before buying.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder

The 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine produces 197 horsepower and 191 lb-ft of torque, offering adequate efficiency for daily commuting. This engine option is generally dependable, provided routine oil changes and scheduled maintenance have been maintained.

### 3.6L V6

The 3.6-liter V6 engine delivers a more robust 305 horsepower and 264 lb-ft of torque for stronger acceleration. It is a widely used GM powerplant with a solid overall reputation, though buyers should watch for signs of cooling system or accessory wear.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The six-speed automatic transmission pairs with the 2.5L engine to deliver smooth power delivery, though higher-mileage units can occasionally exhibit shifting delays or internal wear.

**Transmission Shifting and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report harsh gear changes, hesitation, shuddering during acceleration, or unexpected gear skipping, sometimes requiring major transmission replacement.
- **Solution:** Diagnose mechanical wear or valve body faults; repair or replace failing internal components or the entire transmission unit if necessary.
- **Cost:** $8,200

### Hydra-Matic 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The eight-speed automatic transmission is paired with the 3.6L V6 engine, offering responsive gear selection to balance performance and efficiency.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

The electric power steering system provides light, easy maneuverability at low speeds, but unexpected failures of the power assist mechanism have been reported by some owners.

**Power Steering Assist Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a sudden loss of electronic power steering assist while driving or maneuvering, making the steering wheel exceptionally heavy and difficult to turn.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic power steering module, wiring harness, and steering rack assembly, and replace faulty electrical sensors or components as needed.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting Housing

Exterior lighting systems generally perform well, though owners have occasionally noted moisture buildup inside the front headlight lenses.

**Headlight Condensation and Water Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Water accumulates inside the headlight housings, leading to heavy fogging, condensation, and reduced nighttime illumination.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight seals and housing assemblies for cracks or gaps, and replace the affected headlight units if moisture intrusion persists.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Seat Sensors

Safety systems include a comprehensive airbag network, though electrical wiring connections under the front seats can occasionally trigger warning lights.

**Passenger Air Bag Warning and Sensor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning light illuminates on the dashboard, or the passenger seat occupancy sensor intermittently fails to recognize an occupant.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat sensor wiring harnesses, connectors, and control modules under the seat, and repair or replace faulty components.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V576000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Impala&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A reduction of braking performance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will bleed the vehicle's brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Chevrolet Impala is a comfortable and appealing used sedan option for buyers seeking ample interior space and a smooth highway ride. While most vehicles provide trouble-free service, prospective buyers should carefully check for potential transmission shifting issues, verify the proper operation of the electronic power steering system, and inspect exterior lighting for moisture accumulation. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection can provide valuable peace of mind before finalizing a purchase.
