# 2017 Chevrolet Impala Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Chevrolet Impala is a spacious and comfortable full-size sedan known for its smooth ride and modern styling. While it offers a pleasant driving experience and roomy interior, prospective buyers should review common owner-reported reliability concerns spanning electrical, steering, and powertrain systems.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine produces around 196 horsepower and serves as the standard entry-level powerplant. It prioritizes fuel efficiency for daily driving, though some owners opt for the larger V6 for heavier acceleration demands.

### 3.6L V6 Engine

The 3.6-liter V6 engine delivers a strong 305 horsepower, providing brisk acceleration and robust performance for highway cruising. It is generally dependable, though occasional exhaust and emissions component issues have been reported.

**Catalytic Converter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The check engine warning light illuminates on the dashboard, often associated with a cracked flange weld or exhaust leak near the cylinder head.
- **Solution:** Inspect the exhaust and catalytic converter assembly; replace damaged components as necessary.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission pairs with both engine options to deliver smooth gear changes under normal conditions. However, a subset of drivers has experienced harsh shifting behavior and delayed engagement over time.

**Harsh Shifting and Lag**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission hesitates or delays when shifting into gear, experiences harsh jerking between 1st and 2nd gears, or momentarily loses forward momentum under acceleration.
- **Solution:** Update control module software if applicable or have a qualified technician inspect internal mechanical components for wear.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

The electronic power steering system provides light and responsive handling at low speeds. Owners should verify proper system operation during a pre-purchase test drive, as assist failures can occur.

**Power Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A 'power steering assist drive with care' warning message appears on the instrument cluster, accompanied by a sudden loss of power steering assistance.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic power steering gear and control modules, and replace faulty steering components.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting and Trunk

Electrical accessories and exterior lighting generally function well, though water sealing around the rear trunk gaskets remains a known trouble spot that can lead to lighting malfunctions.

**Trunk Water Leaks and Lighting Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Water leaks into the trunk compartment—particularly around the spare tire well and brake light gaskets—causing brake light failures and moisture accumulation.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace worn trunk lid seals and taillight gaskets to prevent water intrusion.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Safety equipment is robust, though sensor-related warning lights in the supplemental restraint system require professional diagnosis if they persist.

**Air Bag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning light illuminates intermittently on the dashboard, or the passenger presence sensor falsely indicates the seat is unoccupied.
- **Solution:** Run diagnostic scans to identify faulty sensors or wiring harnesses, and repair or replace affected components.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Chevrolet Impala remains an appealing choice for buyers seeking a spacious, comfortable sedan with composed road manners. While major mechanical components are generally solid, prospective owners should thoroughly check for potential transmission harshness, power steering assist warnings, and trunk seal leaks during an inspection. A comprehensive pre-purchase evaluation will help ensure these common issues are identified before purchase.
