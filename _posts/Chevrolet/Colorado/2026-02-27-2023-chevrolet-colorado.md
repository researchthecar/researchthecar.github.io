# 2023 Chevrolet Colorado Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Chevrolet Colorado arrives as a fully redesigned midsize truck boasting rugged styling and modern cabin technology. Under the hood, it features General Motors' 2.7-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine mated to an 8-speed automatic transmission. While initial impressions are favorable, early-production models have accumulated notable owner complaints concerning advanced safety features, powertrain hiccups, and electrical glitches.

---

## Engine

### 2.7L Turbocharged L3B I4

The 2.7-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine provides strong output (ranging from 237 up to 310 horsepower depending on the tune) and robust towing capability for a midsize truck. Buyers should monitor fluid levels closely as early reports highlight potential cooling and lubrication concerns.

**Coolant Leak and Upper Radiator Hose Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The check engine light illuminates, and the cooling fan runs on high due to a leaking or failed upper radiator hose, potentially causing engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Replace the defective radiator hose and refill the coolant system.
- **Cost:** $850

**Sudden Oil Loss Without Warning**
- **Symptoms:** Complete loss of engine oil during normal operation without the low-oil-pressure warning light illuminating.
- **Solution:** Inspect engine integrity, repair any leaks or component failures, and replenish engine oil.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission pairs across the Colorado lineup to optimize fuel economy and power delivery. While generally smooth, some owners experience erratic shifting behavior and temporary limp mode activations.

**Hard Shifting and Limp Mode**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission may shift very harshly between first and second gear or drop into limp mode, restricting vehicle speed to around 45 mph.
- **Solution:** Reprogram the transmission control module or diagnose set diagnostic trouble codes at a dealership.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides firm stopping power under normal conditions, though owners have noted occasional premature component wear and intermittent warning alerts.

**Premature Rear Brake Wear and Squeal**
- **Symptoms:** A persistent squealing noise develops from the rear brakes when reversing, eventually requiring early replacement of brake pads and rotors.
- **Solution:** Replace worn rear brake pads and rotors.

**Spongy Pedal and Brake Failure Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal suddenly becomes very spongy, and the dashboard displays a brake failure warning message restricting top speed.
- **Solution:** Have the brake hydraulic system inspected immediately by a qualified technician.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Gateway Module

Electrical architecture controls a wide array of cabin features and driver aids, though early models can suffer from software bugs and minor switch failures.

**Outdated Firmware and Software Update Blocks**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle is unable to receive over-the-air software updates due to outdated gateway module firmware.
- **Solution:** Perform a vehicle-wide programming update at the dealership per service bulletins.

**Turn Signal Stalk Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Activating a right turn signal causes the system to automatically click for a left turn after completing the maneuver, or the stalk sticks entirely.
- **Solution:** Replace the turn signal switch assembly.

## Exterior

### Roof Structure

The exterior styling is modern and aggressive, though owners should be aware of structural sensitivity on select early-production roof panels.

**Roof Panel Denting During Car Washes**
- **Symptoms:** High-pressure blowers or car wash equipment cause the roof panel to buckle, dent inward, or make a loud popping sound.
- **Solution:** Apply factory-approved structural reinforcement per manufacturer service bulletins.

## Safety

### Forward Collision and Driver Assist

Advanced driver assistance systems add an extra layer of security, but sensitive sensors can occasionally trigger false warnings or unexpected braking events.

**Unexpected Automatic Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle aggressively engages automatic emergency braking without any obstacles or vehicles present in front.
- **Solution:** Have the collision avoidance sensors and software calibrated or updated by a dealer.

**Random Blind Spot Indicator Activation**
- **Symptoms:** The blind spot monitoring warning lights illuminate randomly even when no other vehicles are in adjacent lanes.
- **Solution:** Inspect and recalibrate the blind spot radar sensors.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V133000: Electrical System:Adas:Driver Monitoring:Camera/Sensor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Colorado&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The front camera module (FCM) system may falsely detect an obstacle, causing the automatic emergency braking (AEB) to engage unexpectedly. Unexpected automatic emergency braking can cause the vehicle to abruptly stop or slow down, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the front camera module (FCM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V237000: Wheels:Lugs/Nuts/Bolts/Studs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Colorado&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Damaged bolts may loosen or break, which can result in a loss of vehicle control and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the left and right front wheel hub bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V703000: Seat Belts:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Colorado&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A loose seat belt may not properly restrain an occupant in a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will tighten the left and right front seat belt buckle attachment bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Chevrolet Colorado is an impressive midsize pickup with strong performance and updated technology, but as a first-model-year vehicle, it comes with notable growing pains. Used-car buyers should thoroughly check service history for completed software updates, cooling system integrity, and transmission behavior. A pre-purchase inspection by a trusted mechanic is strongly recommended before finalizing a deal.
