# 2016 Chevrolet Colorado Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Chevrolet Colorado offers versatile midsize truck utility with a choice of capable gasoline and diesel engines. However, used car buyers should carefully review potential trouble spots, most notably steering assist complaints and transmission shifting concerns.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine produces approximately 200 horsepower and 191 lb-ft of torque, serving as an efficient option for light-duty commuting and basic utility tasks.

### 3.6L V6 Engine

The 3.6-liter V6 engine generates around 305 horsepower and 269 lb-ft of torque, providing stronger towing and acceleration capabilities for daily driving and hauling.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may unexpectedly lose power or stall while driving, accompanied by dashboard warnings or software faults.
- **Solution:** Reprogram or update powertrain control modules and inspect engine sensors for proper operation.

### 2.8L Duramax Turbo-Diesel 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2.8-liter Duramax turbo-diesel delivers 181 horsepower and a robust 369 lb-ft of torque, offering excellent low-end pulling power and high fuel economy.

**Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Issues with the fuel delivery system can cause the truck to fail to start, run rough, or experience fuel pump degradation.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel lines and replace failing high-pressure fuel pumps or clogged fuel filters.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with select gas engines, this 6-speed automatic transmission handles everyday shifting duties, though some units exhibit hesitation or shudder over time.

**Transmission Shudder and Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice a noticeable shudder or vibration at low speeds, rough gear shifts, or intermittent transmission slipping.
- **Solution:** Perform a transmission fluid flush with updated fluid specifications or repair internal components and valve bodies as needed.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The available 8-speed automatic transmission provides tighter gear spacing for improved highway efficiency and smoother power delivery across various driving conditions.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The 6-speed manual transmission offers direct driver engagement, though it is typically restricted to base 2.5L rear-wheel-drive configurations.

## Brakes

### Brake System

The hydraulic braking system provides reliable stopping power under normal conditions, though occasional vacuum assist issues require prompt attention.

**Brake Pedal Stiffness and Vacuum Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal can suddenly become very stiff, requiring excessive physical force to depress and resulting in extended stopping distances.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace failing vacuum pumps, brake boosters, or compromised vacuum lines.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering System

The electric power steering system provides light effort at low speeds but has generated notable owner complaints regarding intermittent loss of assist.

**Steering Assist Loss and Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel suddenly becomes extremely stiff and difficult to turn, accompanied by 'Service Power Steering' and StabiliTrak warning messages on the dash.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electric power steering motor, wiring harnesses, and battery voltage drops, or replace the steering rack assembly if electronic failure occurs.

## Electrical

### Body Electrical and Infotainment

Cabin electronics and wiring require close inspection during a pre-purchase evaluation to ensure moisture has not compromised internal harnesses.

**Water Intrusion and Wiring Chafing**
- **Symptoms:** Cab water leaks from roof seals can corrode wiring harnesses, damage interior electronics, cause third brake light failures, or cause infotainment screens to malfunction.
- **Solution:** Reseal leaking roof body seams, repair or replace damaged wiring harnesses, and address damaged electronic modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V621000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Colorado&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The driver's frontal air bag may improperly inflate during second-stage deployment in the event of a high speed crash. An improperly inflated air bag increases the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver's frontal air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V358000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Colorado&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A damaged fuel line can create a fuel leak, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the high pressure fuel pump, and high pressure fuel pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V807000: Latches/Locks/Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Colorado&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles have a console compartment lid latch assembly that may fail to keep the compartment door closed in the event of a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of the Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 201, "Occupant Protection in Interior Impact." If the console compartment door does not remain.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the console compartment door latch assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V125000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Colorado&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The driver frontal air bag may improperly inflate during second-stage deployment in the event of a high speed crash. An improperly inflated air bag increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver side frontal air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used model, verify the service history for any past transmission fluid updates or power steering repairs. A thorough test drive checking for smooth gear engagement and responsive steering will help ensure you select a reliable truck.
