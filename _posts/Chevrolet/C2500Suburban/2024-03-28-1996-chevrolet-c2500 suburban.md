**Introduction:**

The 1996 Chevrolet C2500 Suburban, part of the fifth generation of the Suburban line, is a rugged and versatile full-size SUV. It comes equipped with a powerful 5.7L V8 engine, delivering 255 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 4L60E four-speed automatic transmission. Known for its spacious interior, towing capacity, and off-road capabilities, the Suburban is a popular choice for families and outdoor enthusiasts alike. However, like all vehicles, it has its share of common issues that owners may encounter.

**Engine**

**5.7L V8**

* **Vortech Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Causes coolant and oil leaks, resulting in overheating and reduced engine performance.
    * Solution: Replacement of the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Throttle Body Position Sensor (TPS) Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Causes erratic acceleration, stalling, and poor fuel economy.
    * Solution: Replacement of the TPS.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4L60E Four-Speed Automatic**

* **Overdrive Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power in overdrive gear, resulting in reduced fuel economy and performance.
    * Solution: Replacement of the overdrive solenoid or transmission rebuild.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **TCC Solenoid Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Causes transmission shuddering or slipping, particularly when the transmission is warm.
    * Solution: Replacement of the TCC solenoid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front End Sway Bar End Links Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Produces clunking noises when turning or hitting bumps, resulting in reduced handling and stability.
    * Solution: Replacement of the sway bar end links and bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, flickering electronics, and difficulty starting the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replacement of the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Terminal Corrosion:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Causes poor electrical connections, resulting in difficulty starting the vehicle or electrical system malfunctions.
    * Solution: Cleaning or replacing the battery terminals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Handle Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Release mechanism fails, making it difficult or impossible to open the tailgate.
    * Solution: Replacement of the tailgate handle assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Window Seal Leaks:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the interior of the vehicle through the rear window seal.
    * Solution: Replacement of the rear window seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Power Window Motor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: One or more power windows stop working or become intermittent.
    * Solution: Replacement of the power window motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per window
    * Recalls: None Found
* **HVAC Blend Door Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty regulating temperature inside the vehicle, causing excessive heat or cold.
    * Solution: Replacement of the HVAC blend door actuator or blend door itself.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 1996 Chevrolet C2500 Suburban is a reliable and capable vehicle, but it is not without its potential issues. Engine and transmission problems can be expensive to repair, while suspension and electrical issues are generally less severe and costly. Exterior and interior problems tend to be more related to wear and tear than major mechanical failures. It's important for owners to be aware of these potential issues and to address them promptly, as neglect can lead to more significant and costly repairs down the road.
