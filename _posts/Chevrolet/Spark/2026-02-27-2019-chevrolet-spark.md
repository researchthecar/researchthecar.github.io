# 2019 Chevrolet Spark Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

If you are shopping for a used 2019 Chevrolet Spark, you are looking at one of the most affordable and fuel-efficient subcompact hatchbacks on the market. While its small footprint makes it a popular commuter and city car, used-car buyers should carefully evaluate powertrain durability and electrical or safety system operation during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 1.4L I4 LV1 Engine

The 2019 Chevrolet Spark comes standard with a 1.4-liter naturally aspirated inline-4 engine producing 98 horsepower and 94 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate power for basic urban commuting while delivering impressive fuel economy, though owners have occasionally noted internal mechanical concerns like piston wear.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Power is routed to the front wheels via a Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) or an optional 5-speed manual. The CVT is essential for maximizing the small engine's efficiency, but buyers should listen closely for unusual whines or slipping during a test drive.

**Transmission Control Module and Failure Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported vehicle hesitation from a complete stop, unexpected locking up, illuminated check engine lights, and transmission control module codes.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes and replace the transmission control module or damaged internal components as necessary.
- **Cost:** $800

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission

A traditional 5-speed manual transmission is also available on base trims, offering a more direct mechanical feel and avoiding the complexity associated with the automatic CVT.

## Suspension

### Tires

Suspension and wheel components are generally standard for a light car, though owners should keep an eye on tire longevity depending on the brand installed.

**Rapid Aftermarket Tire Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Certain replacement tires, such as specific budget-friendly all-season radial tires, have worn down significantly faster than expected within 30,000 miles.
- **Solution:** Inspect tire tread depth closely during routine maintenance and replace prematurely worn tires with quality replacements.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides stopping power suited to the car's light weight, but noisy operation or spongy pedal feel requires prompt inspection.

**Premature Brake Noise and Feel**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have experienced very loud noises when applying the brakes and a concerning pedal feel suggesting reduced stopping power.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the brake pads, rotors, and calipers for premature wear or defect.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Basic electrical accessories function reliably, though occasional electronic glitches like random alarm triggers have been documented by owners.

**Random Alarm Activation**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle security alarm may trigger spontaneously while parked with no apparent cause or nearby activity.
- **Solution:** Inspect door switches, hood latches, and the security system module for faulty signals.

## Safety

### Air Bags and Restraints

Safety equipment includes standard airbags and passive restraints, but proper diagnostic checks are vital to ensure restraint warning lights and crash sensors function correctly.

**Airbag Non-Deployment and Code 95**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported dashboard warning lights like Code 95 accompanied by ABS and airbag lights, as well as instances where airbags failed to deploy during frontal collisions.
- **Solution:** Scan the Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) for diagnostic trouble codes and repair faulty sensors, wiring modules, or clock springs.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V774000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Spark&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The Sensing Diagnostic Module (SDM) on these vehicle may not have been turned off of "manufacturing mode," preventing the air bags from deploying in the event of a crash. If the air bags do not deploy in the event of a crash, it can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the SDM module to the correct mode, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Chevrolet Spark can serve as an economical daily driver, but used car buyers should proceed with due diligence. Pay close attention to CVT operation, verify that all safety and airbag warning indicators function normally, and check service records to ensure the vehicle has been properly maintained.
