**Introduction:**

The 2017 Chevrolet Spark is a subcompact hatchback available with two engine and transmission options:

* 1.4L ECOTEC 4-cylinder engine with 98 horsepower and 94 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed manual or a CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission).
* 1.4L ECOTEC 4-cylinder engine with 98 horsepower and 94 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission (only available in the ACTIV trim).

The Spark is praised for its fuel efficiency, maneuverability, and affordable price. However, it is criticized for its lack of power, cramped interior, and limited features.

**Engine**

**1.4L ECOTEC 4-cylinder Engine**

* **Oil Consumption:** Excessive oil consumption leading to oil starvation and engine damage.
    * **Symptoms:** Blue smoke from the exhaust, low oil levels, illuminated oil pressure warning light.
    * **Solution:** Engine rebuild or replacement.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2,000 - $4,000+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Shifting Issues:** Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises, or gears popping out of place.
    * **Symptoms:** Inability to shift gears smoothly, grinding noises during shifting, gears disengaging unexpectedly.
    * **Solution:** Transmission rebuild or replacement.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $3,000+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)**

* **Slipping or Jerking:** The transmission experiences slippage or jerking during acceleration or gear changes.
    * **Symptoms:** Decreased acceleration, hesitation upon acceleration, rough shifting.
    * **Solution:** Transmission fluid replacement or rebuild.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,500+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge:** Premature battery discharge leading to starting problems.
    * **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the vehicle, dead battery, warning lights on the dashboard.
    * **Solution:** Battery replacement or alternator repair/replacement.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $800+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rear Bumper Cracking:** Stress cracks developing in the plastic rear bumper.
    * **Symptoms:** Visible cracks or splits in the rear bumper.
    * **Solution:** Bumper replacement.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $500+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Interior**

* **Central Console Rattle:** Excessive rattling and vibration from the central console area.
    * **Symptoms:** Noisy rattling or buzzing coming from the dashboard area.
    * **Solution:** Tightening loose components or adding sound-dampening materials.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $300+
    * **Recalls:** None Found
* **Air Conditioning Malfunction:** Insufficient cooling or complete failure of the air conditioning system.
    * **Symptoms:** Weak or no airflow, warm air blowing from vents, no cooling at all.
    * **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of the air conditioning system (e.g., refrigerant leak repair, compressor replacement).
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,500+
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary:**

The 2017 Chevrolet Spark has several potential issues, including excessive oil consumption, transmission problems, rear bumper cracking, and interior rattles. While the majority of these issues are relatively minor in nature, the oil consumption issue can lead to catastrophic engine damage if not addressed promptly. The cost of repairs can vary significantly depending on the nature of the problem and the location of the repairs. Overall, the Spark's issues are generally not severe and can be addressed without causing substantial financial burden.
