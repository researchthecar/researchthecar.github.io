# 2016 Chevrolet Spark Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Chevrolet Spark offers buyers an affordable, highly maneuverable city car available in both traditional gasoline and battery-electric configurations. While it is praised for fuel efficiency and tight parking capabilities, used car shoppers should review owner feedback concerning potential mechanical and electrical hiccups. A careful pre-purchase inspection is recommended to ensure powertrain health and electrical system integrity before buying.

---

## Engine

### 1.4L 4-Cylinder Engine

The standard gasoline powertrain features a 1.4-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine producing approximately 98 horsepower and 94 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate motivation for urban commuting paired with commendable fuel economy. Overall mechanical reliability for this engine is generally solid when properly maintained.

### Permanent Magnet Electric Motor

The Spark EV utilizes a permanent magnet electric motor generating 140 horsepower and a robust 327 lb-ft of torque for punchy acceleration. While EV versions offer spirited city performance, shoppers should verify battery health and propulsion system software status.

**Propulsion and Acceleration Loss**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle suddenly loses all ability to accelerate or completely shuts down while driving without prior warning.
- **Solution:** Have the propulsion system and inverter modules diagnosed by a qualified dealership or EV technician.

## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission

The conventional 5-speed manual transmission offers a direct connection and straightforward operation for base trims. Owners occasionally report premature clutch wear requiring component replacement.

**Clutch Wear and Slippage**
- **Symptoms:** The clutch wears out prematurely, leading to slipping, elevated engine RPMs, and difficulty changing gears.
- **Solution:** Inspect clutch disc thickness and pressure plate condition; replace worn clutch components.

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The automatic CVT optimizes fuel delivery for the gasoline engine across varying speeds. Proper maintenance and software update history are key to ensuring long-term CVT dependability.

**Transmission Control Module and Power Train Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle experiences unexpected stalling in traffic, loss of gear engagement, or transmission slippage following module updates.
- **Solution:** Check for proper Transmission Control Module operation and software updates performed by a certified dealer.

### Single-Speed Direct Drive

The Spark EV relies on a single-speed direct-drive gearbox that delivers power seamlessly without traditional gear shifts. This unit is mechanically simple and generally robust.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes and Brake Assist

Brake components require diligent inspection, especially on EV models where regenerative braking integrates with traditional hydraulic hardware.

**Brake System Malfunction and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Random brake stuttering, illuminated warning lights, loss of brake assist, and intermittent complete brake failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake master cylinder, ABS module, and regenerative braking sensors, and consult dealer service bulletins.

## Steering

### Power Steering

The electric power steering system provides light, maneuverable handling at low speeds but must be checked for potential electronic assist failures.

**Sudden Loss of Power Steering**
- **Symptoms:** Power steering assists fails completely while driving at highway speeds without prior warning signs.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic power steering motor, wiring connections, and steering control module.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

The electrical architecture manages cabin electronics and safety features, though intermittent display glitches have been reported by some owners.

**Dashboard and Infotainment Blackout**
- **Symptoms:** The entertainment screen and instrument panel lighting fail intermittently or stop working entirely without warning.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuses, wiring harnesses, and instrument cluster connections for loose or failing components.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Restraint systems and air bags are critical safety features that should be thoroughly inspected for warning lights or fault codes prior to purchase.

**Air Bag Malfunction and Code 95**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated dashboard error codes (such as Code 95) indicating an air bag fault, or inadvertent curtain air bag deployment.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to a certified dealer to diagnose sensor faults and replace defective air bag modules.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Chevrolet Spark remains a budget-friendly option for city commuters, but prospective buyers should perform a detailed inspection focusing on the brakes, electrical accessories, and transmission performance. Verifying service histories and checking for unresolved recall or complaint items will help ensure a dependable ownership experience.
