**1990 Chevrolet C60 Kodiak**

The 1990 Chevrolet C60 Kodiak is a medium-duty pickup truck that offers a variety of engine and transmission options. The standard engine is a 6.2L naturally aspirated V8 producing 230 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque. Optional engines include a 6.5L turbocharged V8 diesel and a 7.4L V8. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual and a 4-speed automatic. The C60 Kodiak is known for its rugged construction, powerful engines, and high payload capacity. However, it has also been plagued by a number of issues.

**Engine**

**6.2L V8**

* **Valve Train Noise:** Excessive noise from the valve train, particularly at idle.
    * Solution: Replace the hydraulic valve lifters.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** Failure of the intake manifold gasket can cause coolant leaks, reduced engine performance, and increased emissions.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 91V070006

**6.5L V8 Diesel**

* **Injection Pump Failure:** Failure of the injection pump can cause the engine to stall or lose power.
    * Solution: Replace the injection pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 97V074000

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Grinding When Shifting:** Difficulty shifting gears, accompanied by a grinding noise.
    * Solution: Replace the worn synchronizers or shift forks.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **No Forward Gears:** The transmission may not engage in any forward gears.
    * Solution: Overhaul or replace the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Leaf Spring Failure:** Failure of the front leaf springs can result in a loss of ride height and handling.
    * Solution: Replace the leaf springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Axle Seal Leak:** Leakage of oil from the rear axle seals can cause the seals to fail and result in a loss of rear axle fluid.
    * Solution: Replace the rear axle seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Failure of the alternator can result in a loss of electrical power.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Starter Failure:** Failure of the starter can prevent the engine from starting.
    * Solution: Replace the starter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Corrosion on Body and Frame:** Corrosion can occur on the body and frame, particularly in areas exposed to salt or moisture.
    * Solution: Clean and protect the affected areas from further corrosion.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of corrosion.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **HVAC System Malfunction:** The HVAC system may fail to provide heat or air conditioning.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the HVAC system as needed.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Cruise Control Failure:** The cruise control system may fail to engage or maintain a set speed.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the cruise control system as needed.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1990 Chevrolet C60 Kodiak is a capable and durable pickup truck, but it has been plagued by a number of issues. The most common problems include engine valve train noise, intake manifold gasket failure, injection pump failure, transmission grinding, and suspension failures. Electrical issues and interior malfunctions have also been reported. The severity and cost of repairs for these issues can vary greatly, but some can be quite expensive. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems before purchasing a C60 Kodiak.
