## 2019 Chevrolet Tahoe

The 2019 Chevrolet Tahoe is a full-size SUV that offers a combination of power, space, and comfort. It is powered by a standard 5.3L V8 engine with 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, but a 6.2L V8 with 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft is also available. The 5.3L V8 comes paired with a six-speed automatic transmission and the 6.2L V8 is paired with a 10-speed automatic transmission.

Some of the strengths of the Tahoe include its spacious and comfortable interior, impressive towing capacity, and powerful engine options. However, it also has some drawbacks, such as a lack of fuel efficiency, dated interior design, and sluggish handling.

### **Engine**

**5.3L V8 (355 horsepower)**
- **Intake Manifold Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Cracking or warping of the intake manifold, leading to coolant leaks and engine overheating.
  - Solution: Replacement of the intake manifold.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
  - Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 20V484000

**6.2L V8 (420 horsepower)**
- **No Issues Found**

### **Transmission**

**Six-Speed Automatic**
- **Transmission Slippage**
  - Description/Symptoms: Loss of power or hesitation during acceleration, accompanied by a burning smell.
  - Solution: Repair or replacement of the transmission.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+
  - Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 20V764000

**Ten-Speed Automatic**
- **Delayed Gear Shifts**
  - Description/Symptoms: Hesitation or jerking during gear changes, especially when shifting from low to high gears.
  - Solution: Software update or replacement of the transmission control module.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200

### **Suspension**
- **Rear Shock Absorber Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged rear shock absorbers, leading to a rough ride and poor handling.
  - Solution: Replacement of the rear shock absorbers.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

### **Electrical**
- **Battery Discharge**
  - Description/Symptoms: Premature battery discharge, often caused by a drain in the electrical system.
  - Solution: Inspection and repair of the electrical system, potentially involving replacement of faulty components.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
- **Headlight Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning or dim headlights.
  - Solution: Replacement of the headlight bulbs or repair of the headlight wiring.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+

### **Exterior**
- **Hood Latch Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Faulty hood latch, causing the hood to pop open unexpectedly.
  - Solution: Replacement of the hood latch assembly.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250+
- **Power Liftgate Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning power liftgate, preventing it from opening or closing properly.
  - Solution: Repair or replacement of the liftgate motor or control module.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

### **Interior**
- **Dashboard Rattle**
  - Description/Symptoms: Rattling or buzzing noise coming from the dashboard area.
  - Solution: Inspection and tightening of loose components or replacement of the dashboard trim.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
- **Climate Control Malfunction**
  - Description/Symptoms: Improper functioning of the climate control system, such as ineffective cooling or heating.
  - Solution: Inspection and repair of the climate control system, potentially involving replacement of the blower motor or temperature sensor.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+

## Summary

The 2019 Chevrolet Tahoe has a range of issues affecting various components, including the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, and interior. While some issues, such as dashboard rattle and battery discharge, are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others, such as intake manifold failure and transmission slippage, can be more severe and costly to repair. Overall, the severity and cost of the issues vary, and it is advisable for potential buyers to thoroughly research and consider the potential repair costs before making a decision.
