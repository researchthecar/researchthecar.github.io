# 2022 Chevrolet Tahoe Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Chevrolet Tahoe continues the lineage of this full-size, three-row American SUV, offering generous passenger and cargo space alongside strong towing capabilities. However, used-car buyers should review owner complaints and technical concerns carefully, particularly regarding powertrain behavior, electrical issues, and specific component issues reported to federal safety regulators.

---

## Engine

### 5.3L V8 (L84)

The standard 5.3-liter EcoTec3 V8 engine produces 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, offering robust performance and everyday capability for towing and family hauling.

### 6.2L V8 (L87)

The optional 6.2-liter EcoTec3 V8 delivers a more robust 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, providing extra muscle for heavy loads but carrying occasional reports of sudden propulsion loss.

**Loss of Propulsion / Engine Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may enter limp mode unexpectedly or experience sudden engine failure while driving at highway speeds, displaying warning messages.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle inspected by a qualified technician or dealership to diagnose internal engine faults or sensor failures.

### 3.0L Duramax Turbo-Diesel I6 (LZ0/LM2)

The 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six diesel engine balances solid low-end torque with excellent highway fuel economy, making it a popular choice for long-distance drivers.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission is paired across the engine lineup to maximize efficiency and smooth power delivery, though select owners have experienced valve and shifting complications.

**Transmission Control Valve / Shifting Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission control valve failure can cause unexpected shifting behavior, warning messages regarding immediate service, or inability to select gears properly.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace faulty transmission control valves or sensors as indicated by diagnostic trouble codes or active recalls.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides adequate stopping power for a large SUV, though minor noise complaints like squealing have been noted by owners.

**Brake Squeal**
- **Symptoms:** Brakes produce audible squealing noises during normal application.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and hardware for wear, glazing, or improper seating.

## Electrical

### Battery and Charging System

Electrical architecture supports advanced infotainment and driver assistance features, though battery drain and module glitches can occasionally surface.

**Sudden Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences repeated unexpected battery drain and no-start events without prior warning.
- **Solution:** Perform a parasitic draw test and check charging system components to isolate faulty modules or wiring.

## Exterior

### Windshield Wipers

Exterior visibility components are generally reliable, but occasional failures of the wiper motor have compromised driver visibility during inclement weather.

**Wiper Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Windshield wipers stop working entirely during rainstorms, severely limiting visibility.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed wiper motor or faulty switch assembly.

## Interior

### Sunroof and Trim

The cabin offers spacious comfort and modern amenities, though occasional reports of sunroof drainage issues leading to interior leaks warrant attention during inspection.

**Water Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Water pools or leaks through overhead lights and trim pieces after rainfall.
- **Solution:** Clear or repair clogged sunroof drain tubes and check roof seals.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V617000: Seat Belts:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A seat belt with a deformed rivet may not properly restrain an occupant, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rivet head formation on both the left and right side third-row seat belt buckle assemblies and replace seat belt buckle assemblies, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V903000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The daytime running lights (DRLs) may not deactivate when the headlights are on. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." DRLs that do not deactivate as intended may result in a glare, reducing visibility, and increasing the risk.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V274000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Engine failure increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the engine, and as necessary, repair or replace the engine. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V085000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Rear wheels that lock up while driving increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install new transmission control module (TCM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2022 Chevrolet Tahoe, buyers should perform a thorough mechanical and electronic inspection, paying close attention to transmission shift quality, battery health, and potential water leaks. While the vehicle offers unmatched utility and comfort, verifying service history and addressing any outstanding campaigns will help ensure a more dependable ownership experience.
