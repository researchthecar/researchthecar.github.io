# 2023 Chevrolet Tahoe Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Chevrolet Tahoe is a full-size SUV offering spacious seating, robust towing capability, and three distinct engine choices. However, used car buyers should approach with caution due to a notable volume of owner complaints concerning engine performance, unexpected electrical warnings, and transmission drivability concerns.

---

## Engine

### 5.3L V8 Engine

The standard 5.3-liter V8 engine produces 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, providing reliable baseline performance for daily driving and moderate towing, though owners have reported occasional internal mechanical complaints.

### 6.2L V8 Engine

The larger 6.2-liter V8 engine generates 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque for stronger acceleration and towing capacity, but has drawn owner reports regarding internal component wear and failure.

**Catastrophic Engine Failure and Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil consumption followed by sudden engine failure, illuminated warning lights, and power loss.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis and potentially a complete engine replacement.

### 3.0L Duramax Turbo-Diesel I6

The 3.0-liter turbocharged diesel inline-six produces 277 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, offering excellent fuel economy and strong low-end torque for long-distance hauling.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission is paired across all engine options to maximize fuel efficiency and power delivery, though some drivers report rough shifting and valve body concerns.

**Transmission Slipping and Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle jerks during gear changes, hesitation when accelerating, or unexpected deceleration at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Dealer inspection may require valve body replacement, fluid service, or control module reprogramming.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides stopping power suited for a heavy full-size SUV, but intermittent complaints warn of sudden pedal stiffness or reduced responsiveness.

**Unresponsive Brakes and ABS Lock**
- **Symptoms:** Spongy or hard brake pedal feel, delayed stopping response, or sudden anti-lock braking system lockups.
- **Solution:** Have the brake hydraulic system, ABS module, and master cylinder thoroughly inspected by a qualified technician.

## Exterior

### Wiper and Lighting Controls

The electrical system manages various convenience and safety features, with specific owner complaints highlighting glitchy software updates affecting wiper operations.

**Inoperable Windshield Wipers**
- **Symptoms:** Windshield wipers become stuck in the active position or fail completely during rainstorms, severely reducing visibility.
- **Solution:** Requires software reset or replacement of faulty wiper control modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V903000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The daytime running lights (DRLs) may not deactivate when the headlights are on. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." DRLs that do not deactivate as intended may result in a glare, reducing visibility, and increasing the risk.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V642000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The rear suspension outer control arm bolts may not have been properly heat-treated, which can cause the bolts to break. A broken suspension bolt can result in misalignment of the rear wheel, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both rear suspension outer control arm bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V674000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Brake Fluid Low Warning: Lamp](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Vehicle Safety Standard number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." Without a warning light, the vehicle may be driven with low brake fluid, which can reduce braking performance and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V274000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Engine failure increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the engine, and as necessary, repair or replace the engine. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V619000: Fuel System, Diesel:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The original fuel tank may have been replaced with an incorrect fuel tank, which can allow fuel to leak in a rollover crash. A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a vehicle fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect for the correct fuel tank and replace it, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2023 Chevrolet Tahoe, carefully inspect the service history, powertrain operation, and brake responsiveness. While it offers impressive passenger space and utility, verifying that any open recalls or software updates have been performed by a certified dealer can help mitigate potential ownership headaches.
