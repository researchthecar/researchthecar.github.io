# 2021 Chevrolet Tahoe Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Chevrolet Tahoe introduces a fully redesigned generation featuring independent rear suspension, a much more spacious cabin, and several advanced powertrain choices. While buyers appreciate its comfort and utility, owner reports to federal safety regulators highlight notable reliability concerns. Common trouble spots include fuel pump failures, unexpected engine stalling, valvetrain and lifter issues, and automatic transmission or valve body malfunctions.

---

## Engine

### 5.3L V8 (EcoTec3)

The standard 5.3-liter V8 engine produces 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, offering robust towing capability for everyday family needs. Reliability can be compromised by reports of DFM lifter sticking and sudden stalling incidents.

**Dynamic Fuel Management (DFM) Lifter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The engine experiences a sudden loss of motive power, misfires, and unusual ticking or tapping noises, often leading to camshaft damage.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed DFM lifters and damaged camshaft components, or service the valve train.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle unexpectedly shuts off while driving or turning, occasionally with little to no prior warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the fuel control module, fuel pump, or electrical harness and replace faulty components.

### 6.2L V8 (EcoTec3)

The optional 6.2-liter V8 delivers 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque for stronger acceleration and high-end performance. Buyers should watch for similar cylinder deactivation and valvetrain complications reported in large-displacement GM V8s.

**Engine Misfire and Lifter Stick**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light, engine vibration, and diagnostic trouble codes indicating cylinder misfires.
- **Solution:** Inspect valvetrain components and replace failed lifters or software control modules as needed.

### 3.0L Duramax Turbo-Diesel I6

The 3.0-liter turbo-diesel inline-six generates 277 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, providing exceptional fuel economy for a full-size SUV. It is generally praised for highway cruising range, though routine maintenance of emissions equipment is key.

### Fuel System

Supplies pressurized fuel to the direct-injection system. Failures in the pump or associated control modules can cause unexpected engine shutdowns.

**Fuel Pump and Control Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to start, stalls mid-drive, or displays powertrain and fuel system warning messages.
- **Solution:** Replace the defective fuel pump assembly or fuel pump control module.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission distributes power smoothly across all engine options. While it helps maximize highway efficiency, some owners experience premature valve body wear and engagement failures.

**Valve Body and Gear Engagement Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission refuses to shift into reverse or specific forward gears, jerks harshly during shifting, or completely fails to go into gear.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the transmission valve body or internal pistons; complete transmission replacement may be required in severe cases.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Responsible for slowing and stopping the heavy SUV. Premature wear or hydraulic pressure issues can impact stopping confidence.

**Brake Pedal Ineffectiveness and Grinding**
- **Symptoms:** Depressing the brake pedal results in extended stopping distances, grinding noises, or a lack of expected braking response.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, hydraulic lines, and electronic brake control modules.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering

Provides speed-sensitive power assistance for effortless maneuverability. System resistance or locking issues can compromise vehicle control.

**Steering Wheel Binding or Locking**
- **Symptoms:** Steering wheel becomes very difficult to turn or temporarily locks up at low speeds, accompanied by grinding noises.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electric power steering motor and steering column shafts; update software or replace failing components.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Manages forward illumination and lighting controls. Internal mechanical adjusters can fail prematurely.

**Headlight Adjuster Breakage**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights drop unexpectedly and point downward toward the ground, severely reducing nighttime visibility.
- **Solution:** Replace the broken headlight adjuster mechanism or headlight assembly.

## Safety

### Air Bag

Monitors and deploys supplemental restraint systems in a collision. Electronic warnings can indicate sensor or wiring faults.

**Service Restraints Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** Airbag warning light illuminates intermittently on the dashboard, accompanied by 'Service Restraints System' alerts.
- **Solution:** Inspect wiring harnesses, sensors, and airbag modules, and repair or replace faulty components.

### Seat Belt

Provides occupant restraint across all three seating rows. Latch mechanisms require secure operation to ensure passenger safety.

**Third-Row Seat Belt Latch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rear seat belts fail to latch securely or do not lock up properly during sudden movement.
- **Solution:** Replace the malfunctioning seat belt buckle assembly.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V455000: Steering:Steering Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A software issue may cause a loss of power steering assist at startup or while driving. Increased steering effort, particularly at lower speeds, increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 21V579000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist:Electric:Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Overall braking performance may be compromised, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will recalibrate the brake system control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V036000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Driveshaft failure can cause a sudden loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the left and/or right rear driveshaft assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V792000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The seat belt brackets may not be secured to the seat frame in the front-row center seating position. If a seat-belt bracket is not secured, the seat belt may not properly restrain an occupant in the front-row center seat position, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect left-side and right-side front-row center seat-belt bracket attachments and reassemble it correctly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V811000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** If an incorrect bolt was used, the seat belt may not be properly attached to the vehicle. If a seat belt assembly is not properly attached to the vehicle, the seat belt may not properly restrain an occupant in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the suspect bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V064000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel tank, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V735000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The attachment bolts for the front seats may not have been properly reinstalled after the repair was completed. If an occupied front seat has loose or missing attachment bolts, it may move during a crash, increasing the risk of injury to the seated occupant.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the seat attachments for both front seats and install attachment bolts as needed, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V739000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump:Control/Drive Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The fuel pump power control module may cause the fuel pump to fail or operate intermittently, which can interrupt fuel flow to the engine, and cause an unexpected engine stall. An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump power control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Chevrolet Tahoe is a capable and spacious full-size SUV that appeals to families needing substantial cargo and towing capacity. However, prospective buyers should thoroughly evaluate the vehicle's service history, paying close attention to potential engine valvetrain issues, fuel pump reliability, and transmission valve body performance. A pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic is strongly advised to catch any hidden mechanical or electrical concerns before buying.
