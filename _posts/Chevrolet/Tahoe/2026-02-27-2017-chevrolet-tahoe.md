# 2017 Chevrolet Tahoe Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Chevrolet Tahoe offers robust utility, three rows of seating, and strong towing capabilities, making it a popular choice for families. However, prospective buyers should carefully evaluate potential mechanical and electrical issues. While the full-size SUV provides plenty of capability, owner reports highlight several recurring problem areas ranging from transmission and brake concerns to active fuel management system hiccups.

---

## Engine

### 5.3L EcoTec3 V8

The 5.3-liter EcoTec3 V8 produces approximately 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, providing standard motivation for the Tahoe. It features direct injection and Active Fuel Management cylinder deactivation, though shoppers should watch out for occasional lifter durability issues.

**Active Fuel Management (AFM) Lifter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Severe engine misfires, rough idling, shaking while stopped, and a noticeable loss of power when driving uphill.
- **Solution:** Requires replacing the failed valve lifters and inspecting the valvetrain for further damage.

## Transmission

### 6L80 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6L80 6-speed automatic transmission pairs with the standard V8 engine across various trims and drivetrain configurations. Buyers should check for smooth shifting behavior during a test drive, as mechanical wear and shudder are known owner complaints.

**Transmission Slipping and Shudder**
- **Symptoms:** Jerking during takeoff, delayed or slow gear shifts, whining noises, and occasional transmission slipping while driving.
- **Solution:** Professional diagnostic testing is required; solutions range from fluid flushes and torque converter replacement to a complete transmission rebuild.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brake System

The service brake system provides adequate stopping power for a full-size SUV under normal conditions, but certain owners report troubling vacuum loss symptoms.

**Brake Pedal Stiffness and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal becoming excessively hard to press at low speeds, leading to significantly increased stopping distances.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake booster and vacuum pump components, and verify whether recall remediation efforts fully resolved the mechanical failure.

## Interior

### Wheels and Rims

Suspension and wheel assemblies generally handle road imperfections well, though factory wheel finishes are susceptible to inner rim corrosion.

**Wheel Corrosion and Air Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Pitting and peeling on the interior of factory wheels, causing tires to slowly lose air pressure.
- **Solution:** Wheels may need to be resurfaced, cleaned, or replaced entirely if severe corrosion compromises the bead seal.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V645000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist:Vacuum](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As the vacuum level drops, the brake assist decreases, increasing braking effort, extending the distance required to stop the vehicle, thereby increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the Electronic Brake Control Module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V761000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Wheel Speed Sensor/Tone Ring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Unintended activation of the driveline-protection system will cause unintended braking on the wheel on the opposite side of the failed sensor, causing the vehicle to pull to one side unexpectedly, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the EBCM, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V651000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test. During this test, deployment of the frontal air bags and the seat belt pretensioners would not occur in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V289000: Power Train:Transfer Case (4-Wheel Drive)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A component missing from the drivetrain transfer case may cause the front and/or rear wheels to lock up without warning to the driver. Front or rear wheel lockup increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the transfer case assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Chevrolet Tahoe is a capable and spacious family transporter, but a thorough pre-purchase inspection is highly recommended. Buyers should pay close attention to transmission shift quality, engine performance under load, and brake pedal responsiveness during a test drive to avoid unexpected repair bills.
