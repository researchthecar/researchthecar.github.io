# 2016 Chevrolet Tahoe Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Chevrolet Tahoe remains a popular choice for families and buyers needing full-size utility, offering robust towing capacity and generous passenger space. However, prospective buyers should approach this model year with care due to a notable volume of owner complaints involving major mechanical and electrical systems. Key areas requiring attention include brake assist failures, transmission shudder, and occasional powertrain hiccups.

---

## Engine

### 5.3L EcoTec3 V8

The standard 5.3-liter EcoTec3 V8 engine produces 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, providing strong towing capability and confident highway acceleration. Equipped with Active Fuel Management, it balances utility with decent fuel economy, though buyers should watch out for potential valvetrain wear.

**Lifter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of engine power, severe engine vibration, and loud ticking noises.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace failed valve lifters and associated valvetrain components; in severe cases, engine replacement may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $8,000+

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle unexpectedly stalls while driving at low speeds, accompanied by a stiff or seized steering wheel.
- **Solution:** Diagnose electronic throttle body, fuel delivery, or sensor faults; update software or replace failing engine management components.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The Hydra-Matic 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with the V8 engine in both rear-wheel drive and four-wheel drive configurations. While generally capable of handling heavy loads, higher-mileage units can experience torque converter wear and internal clutch degradation.

**Transmission Shudder and Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable shuddering during acceleration, harsh gear engagement, delayed shifting, or complete transmission failure at higher mileages.
- **Solution:** Flush fluid, replace the torque converter, rebuild clutch packs, or perform a complete transmission replacement depending on damage extent.

## Suspension

### Shocks and Struts

The suspension setup is designed for a plush ride and heavy hauling, but factory dampers can wear out or seize over time.

**Suspension Stiffening and Premature Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Shocks lock up or fail prematurely, causing a harsh, bouncy ride and reduced high-speed vehicle control.
- **Solution:** Inspect suspension dampers and replace worn or seized struts and shocks.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brake and Vacuum Pump

The braking system provides firm stopping power under normal conditions, but a pattern of vacuum pump and booster complaints requires thorough pre-purchase inspection.

**Brake Assist Failure and Soft Pedal**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal feels spongy and travels close to the floorboard, or the brake assist suddenly stops working, making it difficult to stop.
- **Solution:** Inspect the vacuum pump, brake booster, and hydraulic lines, and replace malfunctioning components as needed.

## Steering

### Electronic Steering Assist

Power steering assist relies heavily on electrical stability systems, making proper battery and sensor health critical to avoid sudden warnings.

**Steering Assist Loss and Stabilitrak Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of power steering assist, illuminated Stabilitrak warning lights, and difficulty turning the wheel.
- **Solution:** Check wheel speed sensors, electronic power steering connections, and related wiring harnesses for faults.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Body Control

Electrical accessories provide advanced connectivity and convenience features, though software bugs and touchscreen glitches are frequently reported by owners.

**Touchscreen Glitches and Radio Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment screen freezes, radio channels change randomly, and navigation systems wander off-route.
- **Solution:** Perform software updates for the MyLink system or replace unresponsive display modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V291000: Seat Belts:Front](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the pretensioner cable becomes fatigued or breaks, the driver may not be properly restrained in the event of a crash, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the pretensioner cable and seat side-shield, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V645000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist:Vacuum](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As the vacuum level drops, the brake assist decreases, increasing braking effort, extending the distance required to stop the vehicle, thereby increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the Electronic Brake Control Module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V761000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Wheel Speed Sensor/Tone Ring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Unintended activation of the driveline-protection system will cause unintended braking on the wheel on the opposite side of the failed sensor, causing the vehicle to pull to one side unexpectedly, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the EBCM, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V651000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test. During this test, deployment of the frontal air bags and the seat belt pretensioners would not occur in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V437000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** While being previously remedied for recall 16V-651, the affected vehicles may not have received the complete software update necessary to remedy the recall condition. Without the update, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V256000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Inadequate welds may allow the control arm to separate from the bushing, compromising steering and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the left and right front upper control arms and realign the vehicle, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V245000: Seat Belts:Front](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A broken pretensioner cable reduces the effectiveness of the driver's seat belt, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver seat belt lap pretensioner, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V289000: Power Train:Transfer Case (4-Wheel Drive)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Tahoe&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A component missing from the drivetrain transfer case may cause the front and/or rear wheels to lock up without warning to the driver. Front or rear wheel lockup increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the transfer case assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Chevrolet Tahoe is a capable and spacious full-size SUV that excels at family hauling and towing, but used-car buyers should proceed with caution. Pre-purchase inspections should specifically check for transmission shudder, brake assist function, and any signs of engine valvetrain tick or lifter issues. Securing a comprehensive service history and an extended warranty can help mitigate unexpected repair expenses.
