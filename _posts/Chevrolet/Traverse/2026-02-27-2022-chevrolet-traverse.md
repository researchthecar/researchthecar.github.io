# 2022 Chevrolet Traverse Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Chevrolet Traverse offers spacious three-row family hauling and practical capability, but prospective buyers should review recurring owner complaints closely. Across model-year owner data, the most prominent concern centers around electrical and shifter communication glitches, particularly persistent 'Shift to Park' warnings that can prevent the vehicle from shutting off completely. Powertrain and transmission issues also appear in smaller numbers, making a thorough pre-purchase inspection and test drive essential.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 Engine

Equipped with a 3.6-liter V6 engine producing approximately 310 horsepower, this powertrain provides robust acceleration for a large crossover. While generally capable, owners have flagged intermittent stalling, hesitation, and low oil warnings.

**Engine Stalling and Limp Mode**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected hesitation, bucking, hesitation when shifting gears in traffic, or sudden drops into limp mode accompanied by a check engine light.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician pull diagnostic trouble codes and inspect throttle body, ignition, or powertrain control modules.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly in normal conditions, though higher-mileage examples can develop noticeable shuddering or hesitation under load.

**Transmission Shudder and Acceleration Delay**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences severe shuddering across multiple gears or delayed acceleration and sluggish response during everyday driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition, check for internal wear, and consult a dealer regarding potential fluid flushes or software updates.

## Electrical

### Shifter Sensor and Parking Switch

Electrical architecture supports advanced convenience features, but cabin electronics and selector sensors are prone to frustrating communication bugs.

**'Shift to Park' Error Message**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle displays a 'Shift to Park' warning on the dashboard and chimes persistently even when the gear selector is fully in park, preventing the driver from turning off the ignition or locking the doors.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty shifter microswitch assembly or gear selector sensor.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V210000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Traverse&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The harness connector to the left or right side roof rail air bag may have incompatible electrical terminals, which could prevent the air bags from deploying in a crash. Roof rail air bags that do not deploy as intended increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the harness connector to the roof rail air bags, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used Chevrolet Traverse, pay close attention to how the gear selector and electronics behave during a test drive, ensuring the vehicle recognizes the park position without recurring warning messages. Verify that the transmission shifts smoothly without shuddering, and confirm that all maintenance records are up to date. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by an independent mechanic will help safeguard against hidden electrical or powertrain faults.
