# 2022 Chevrolet Traverse

### Overview
The 2022 Chevrolet Traverse is a popular three-row SUV known for its spacious interior and comfortable ride. From a technical reliability standpoint, it generally performs well, with most owners reporting a satisfactory ownership experience. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen more attention than others. This analysis focuses on documented issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers. We will examine the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical systems, exterior, and interior components to offer a comprehensive reliability picture.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic (9T65E/9T65)
**Hesitation/Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners report occasional hesitation or shuddering during gear shifts, particularly at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the transmission control module (TCM) have been known to resolve some of these issues. In more severe cases, internal transmission repairs may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $150 - $1,200

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional reports of the infotainment screen freezing, becoming unresponsive, or restarting unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the infotainment system are often effective. A hard reset of the system may also temporarily resolve the issue.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners have experienced premature battery drain, leading to starting issues. This can sometimes be linked to parasitic draw from certain modules.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the source of the parasitic draw is crucial. Replacing a faulty battery or module may be required.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Chevrolet Traverse generally presents a solid reliability record, particularly concerning its powertrain (3.6L V6 engine and 9-speed automatic transmission), which is largely free of widespread major defects. The most commonly cited issues tend to be related to the infotainment system's software and occasional transmission hesitation, both of which often have software-based solutions. Battery drain is another area to monitor, though not pervasive. Overall, for a vehicle in its class, the Traverse offers a dependable ownership experience, with most potential issues being manageable through software updates or relatively straightforward repairs. Routine maintenance remains key to ensuring long-term reliability.
