# 2019 Chevrolet Traverse Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a used 2019 Chevrolet Traverse offers buyers a spacious and family-friendly midsize SUV with generous cargo capacity and comfortable ride dynamics. However, prospective buyers should review common owner-reported complaints, particularly regarding electrical and drivetrain anomalies that can affect daily usability. While many drivers enjoy its passenger space and capability, checking service history for transmission performance and electrical updates is strongly advised.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6

The 3.6-liter V6 engine produces approximately 310 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque, providing strong acceleration for this three-row SUV. It is generally robust, though minor accessory component and gasket leaks have been documented by some owners.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic

The 9-speed automatic transmission pairs with the V6 engine to optimize highway efficiency and power delivery. Some owners report shift quality issues and torque converter shudder over higher mileages.

**Transmission Shudder and Hard Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a shudder or vibration during acceleration, along with harsh or delayed gear engagement.
- **Solution:** Inspection by a qualified technician may require transmission service, torque converter evaluation, or fluid flushing.

### Shift-to-Park Microswitch

Electrical complaints for this model frequently center around cabin convenience features and shifter sensor connectivity. The most widely reported glitch involves false park-position detection.

**Shift-to-Park Warning and Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard displays a 'Shift to Park' warning message even when the vehicle is fully in park, sometimes causing continuous beeping or a dead battery.
- **Solution:** Replace the shifter assembly or internal microswitch sensor responsible for detecting the gear position.

## Suspension

### Rear Suspension

The suspension provides a compliant ride, though isolated reports indicate that rear suspension components like tow links can experience premature wear.

**Premature Tow Link Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, wheel misalignment, or rear tire handling imbalance.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear tow links and suspension bushings, replacing worn components as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V668000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Traverse&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Missing bolts on the start-stop accumulator endcap could result in a transmission oil leak and may progress to a loss of propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash. A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the start-stop transmission accumulator and replace it if any bolts are missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V246000: Air Bags:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Traverse&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The side curtain air bags may not be properly attached. Unsecured side curtain air bags may not perform as intended, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, if necessary, realign or replace the weld nuts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V774000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Traverse&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The Sensing Diagnostic Module (SDM) on these vehicle may not have been turned off of "manufacturing mode," preventing the air bags from deploying in the event of a crash. If the air bags do not deploy in the event of a crash, it can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the SDM module to the correct mode, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Chevrolet Traverse is a practical choice for families needing expansive seating and cargo room, but used-car buyers should perform a thorough inspection prior to purchase. Pay close attention to the operation of the shifter to ensure the 'Shift-to-Park' defect is not present, and test-drive the vehicle carefully to check for smooth transmission operation without shuddering. Verifying past maintenance records can help mitigate potential repair surprises.
