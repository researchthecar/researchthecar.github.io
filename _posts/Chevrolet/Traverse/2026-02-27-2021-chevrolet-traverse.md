# 2021 Chevrolet Traverse Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Chevrolet Traverse, buyers will find a spacious and family-friendly midsize SUV with generous passenger and cargo room. However, reviewing owner feedback reveals several recurring mechanical and electrical concerns that prospective owners should inspect closely before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 Engine

The 3.6-liter V6 engine produces approximately 310 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque, providing strong acceleration for a large crossover. While generally capable for daily driving duties, buyers should look out for minor coolant and gasket leaks.

**Cooling System and Thermostat Failures**
- **Symptoms:** The check engine light illuminates, and the cooling fan may continue running continuously for several minutes even after the vehicle has been turned off.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the diagnostic trouble codes and replace the faulty thermostat or cooling component as needed.

**Oil Pan Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Unexplained oil spots or puddles under the vehicle, often discovered during routine maintenance or independent mechanical inspections.
- **Solution:** Inspect the gasket condition and replace a leaking oil pan gasket.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive, the 9-speed automatic transmission offers smooth cruising ratios on the highway. Some owners report hesitation and shudder issues that require professional diagnosis.

**Transmission Shudder and Acceleration Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may shake or shudder noticeably, particularly when accelerating from a complete stop or when merging between 25 and 35 mph.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the transmission fluid condition and software calibration, or overhaul/replace internal components if mechanical failure is present.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake system feedback highlights premature squeaking and rare structural suspension-link concerns near the brakes that warrant a thorough pre-purchase undercarriage inspection.

**Premature Brake Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Brakes emit loud squeaking noises prematurely at low mileage levels, even when pad thickness appears adequate.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads and rotors for glazing or abnormal wear, and replace hardware if necessary.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

Power steering reliability is critical, and intermittent loss of electronic steering assist represents a notable safety concern raised by multiple owners.

**Steering Assist Reduction**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warnings indicate reduced or lost power steering assist, making the steering wheel suddenly very heavy and difficult to control.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic power steering rack and control modules, and replace the steering rack assembly if failure is confirmed.

## Electrical

### Charging System

Electrical system complaints indicate that charging components like alternators can fail prematurely, leading to warning lights and stranded vehicles.

**Alternator and Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warning messages such as 'Service Battery Charging System' appear while driving, potentially resulting in a dead battery or sudden electrical shutdown.
- **Solution:** Test the alternator output and battery health, and replace faulty charging components.
- **Cost:** Varies by repair scope

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V817000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Traverse&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A transmission fluid leak can cause a loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash. In addition, a leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the screw and clip, and inspect for signs of contact with the transmission, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V472000: Air Bags:Sensor:Frontal Impact](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Traverse&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** During service by dealers, certain vehicles may have received incorrect air bag calibration software that may affect frontal airbag deployment. Air bags that do not deploy as intended can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the air bag calibration software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V210000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Traverse&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The harness connector to the left or right side roof rail air bag may have incompatible electrical terminals, which could prevent the air bags from deploying in a crash. Roof rail air bags that do not deploy as intended increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the harness connector to the roof rail air bags, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Chevrolet Traverse offers impressive interior space and utility, but prospective buyers should conduct a rigorous inspection before finalizing a purchase. Pay close attention to the health of the electrical charging system, transmission shift quality, and steering components to avoid unexpected repair bills.
