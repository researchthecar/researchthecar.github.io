**Introduction:** The 2012 Chevrolet Traverse is a midsize crossover SUV powered by a 3.6L V6 engine, producing 281 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque. It comes with a 6-speed automatic transmission. The Traverse is known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and strong towing capabilities. However, it has faced some specific issues reported by owners.

**Engine**

**3.6L V6**

* **Oil Consumption:** Some engines consume excessive oil, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
  * Solution: Replace piston rings or perform other repairs to address oil leaks.
  * Estimated Cost: $1,500 to $3,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

* **Camshaft Actuator Failure:** The camshaft actuator may fail, causing a loss of power or engine stalling.
  * Solution: Replace the camshaft actuator.
  * Estimated Cost: $500 to $800
  * Recalls: 13V429000

* **Timing Chain Issues:** The timing chain may stretch or break prematurally, leading to engine damage.
  * Solution: Replace the timing chain, gears, and associated components.
  * Estimated Cost: $2,000 to $4,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping:** The transmission may slip from one gear to another, causing a loss of acceleration or transmission failure.
  * Solution: Replace or repair the transmission.
  * Estimated Cost: $2,500 to $5,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

* **Torque Converter Problems:** The torque converter may develop issues, leading to vibration, shuddering, or reduced fuel economy.
  * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
  * Estimated Cost: $1,000 to $1,500
  * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Failure:** The front struts may fail, causing abnormal noises, decreased stability, and poor ride quality.
  * Solution: Replace the struts.
  * Estimated Cost: $600 to $1,200
  * Recalls: None Found

* **Sway Bar Link Issues:** The sway bar links may wear or break, affecting stability and handling.
  * Solution: Replace the sway bar links.
  * Estimated Cost: $200 to $400
  * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** The battery may drain prematurely or fail due to electrical system issues.
  * Solution: Diagnose and repair the underlying electrical problem.
  * Estimated Cost: $150 to $500+
  * Recalls: None Found

* **Headlight Failure:** The headlights may fail due to faulty bulbs or electrical issues.
  * Solution: Replace the headlights or repair the electrical system.
  * Estimated Cost: $100 to $500
  * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Issues:** The exterior door handles may become loose or break.
  * Solution: Replace the door handles.
  * Estimated Cost: $100 to $200 per handle
  * Recalls: None Found

* **Rear Bumper Impact Bar:** The rear bumper impact bar may detach due to corrosion.
  * Solution: Replace the rear bumper impact bar.
  * Estimated Cost: $500 to $800
  * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:** The dashboard may develop cracks or become faded due to sunlight exposure.
  * Solution: Replace the dashboard.
  * Estimated Cost: $1,000 to $1,500
  * Recalls: None Found

* **Climate Control Issues:** The climate control system may malfunction, resulting in improper airflow or temperature regulation.
  * Solution: Diagnose and repair the climate control system.
  * Estimated Cost: $500 to $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:** The 2012 Chevrolet Traverse has faced specific issues, primarily related to the engine, transmission, and suspension systems. Some issues, such as oil consumption and timing chain problems, can be severe and costly to repair. However, other issues, like exterior door handle problems and dashboard cracking, are generally less severe and more affordable to fix. Overall, the Traverse is a reliable and spacious vehicle but may require attention to potential issues before they become major problems.
