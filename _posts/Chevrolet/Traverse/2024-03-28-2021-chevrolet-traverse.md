**Introduction**

The 2021 Chevrolet Traverse is a popular midsize SUV that offers ample space, versatility, and a powerful engine. It is available with a 3.6L V6 engine producing 310 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission. Other engine and transmission options include a 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 engine with 252 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission. The Traverse is praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and strong engine performance, but criticized for its subpar fuel economy and infotainment system.

**Engine**

**3.6L V6 Engine**

* **Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, resulting in low oil levels and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace piston rings and valve stem seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 21V235000
* **Intake Manifold Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intake manifold cracking, causing vacuum leaks and engine performance problems.
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Fuel Pump Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature fuel pump failure, leading to stalling, reduced engine power, and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace fuel pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**9-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Slipping**
    * Description/Symptoms: Gears slipping or hesitating during acceleration or gear changes, resulting in poor performance.
    * Solution: Replace or rebuild transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **TCM Software Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Faulty transmission control module software, causing shifting problems, engine stalling, and other transmission issues.
    * Solution: Update TCM software.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Torque Converter Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Faulty torque converter, resulting in shuddering, slipping, and transmission overheating.
    * Solution: Replace torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Mount Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature failure of front strut mounts, causing clunking noises, poor handling, and premature tire wear.
    * Solution: Replace strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Shock Absorber Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking rear shock absorbers, resulting in reduced ride comfort and handling issues.
    * Solution: Replace shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rapid battery drain, even when the vehicle is parked and turned off.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical faults causing the drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Headlight Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: Headlights flickering, dimming, or failing completely.
    * Solution: Replace headlight bulbs or repair electrical connections.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Infotainment System Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Infotainment system freezing, unresponsive, or displaying errors.
    * Solution: Update software or replace infotainment module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sunroof allowing water to leak into the vehicle's interior.
    * Solution: Replace or repair sunroof seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Hatch Struts**
    * Description/Symptoms: Weak or failing rear hatch struts, causing the hatch to open slowly or not stay open.
    * Solution: Replace struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Climate control system not functioning properly, resulting in poor temperature or airflow control.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical or mechanical faults causing the issue.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Interior Trim Noise**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattles or squeaks from interior trim panels, especially from the dashboard or door panels.
    * Solution: Replace or adjust trim panels to eliminate noise.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Chevrolet Traverse has some moderate to severe issues, particularly with its engine, transmission, and electrical systems. Some of these issues can be costly to repair, exceeding $2,000 in some cases. While the vehicle offers many attractive features and is popular among families, it is important to be aware of these potential problems before making a purchase decision. Regular maintenance and inspections can help mitigate some of these issues, but it is recommended to research specific issues and recalls related to the year and mileage of the vehicle you are considering.
