**Introduction:**

The 2019 Chevrolet Traverse is a three-row SUV that offers seating for up to eight passengers. It comes with a standard 3.6-liter V6 engine that produces 310 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque. A 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine is also available, which produces 255 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a nine-speed automatic transmission.

The Traverse is a popular choice for families due to its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and strong performance. However, there have been some issues reported with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**3.6-liter V6**

* **Oil Consumption:** Excessive oil consumption has been reported in some 3.6-liter V6 engines. Symptoms include low oil levels and a burning oil smell. The issue is caused by faulty piston rings. The solution is to replace the piston rings. Estimated cost to fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
* **Timing Chain Failure:** Timing chain failure has also been reported in some 3.6-liter V6 engines. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, loss of power, and engine stalling. The issue is caused by a defective timing chain. The solution is to replace the timing chain. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Intake Valve Carbon Buildup:** Excessive carbon buildup can occur on the intake valves of some 3.6-liter V6 engines. Symptoms include reduced power, poor fuel economy, and increased emissions. The issue is caused by a combination of factors, including the engine's direct injection system. The solution is to clean the intake valves. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Nine-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Shifting Problems:** Some 2019 Traverses have experienced shifting problems, such as harsh shifts, delayed shifts, and slipping gears. The issue is caused by a software glitch in the transmission control module. The solution is to update the software. Estimated cost to fix: $0 - $100+
* **Transmission Failure:** In some cases, the nine-speed automatic transmission has failed completely. Symptoms include a loss of power, grinding noises, and the inability to shift gears. The issue is caused by a variety of factors, including faulty internal components. The solution is to replace the transmission. Estimated cost to fix: $5,000 - $7,000+

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure:** The strut mounts in some 2019 Traverses have failed prematurely. Symptoms include excessive noise from the front suspension, a rough ride, and uneven tire wear. The issue is caused by a defective design. The solution is to replace the strut mounts. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Some 2019 Traverses have experienced a battery drain issue. Symptoms include a dead battery after the vehicle has been parked for a period of time. The issue is caused by a faulty electrical component, such as a parasitic drain. The solution is to identify and replace the faulty component. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $500+
* **Headlight Malfunction:** Some 2019 Traverses have experienced headlight malfunctions. Symptoms include flickering headlights, dim headlights, and headlights that turn off unexpectedly. The issue is caused by a faulty headlight switch or a wiring problem. The solution is to replace the faulty component. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Leak:** Some 2019 Traverses have experienced a sunroof leak. Symptoms include water leaking into the cabin through the sunroof. The issue is caused by a faulty seal around the sunroof. The solution is to replace the seal. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+
* **Rear Liftgate Latch Failure:** The rear liftgate latch in some 2019 Traverses has failed prematurely. Symptoms include the inability to open the rear liftgate. The issue is caused by a faulty latch mechanism. The solution is to replace the latch mechanism. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattles:** Some 2019 Traverses have experienced dashboard rattles. Symptoms include a rattling noise coming from the dashboard while driving. The issue is caused by a loose component in the dashboard. The solution is to locate and tighten the loose component. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $200+
* **Center Console Lid Malfunction:** The center console lid in some 2019 Traverses has malfunctioned. Symptoms include the inability to open or close the center console lid. The issue is caused by a faulty latch mechanism. The solution is to replace the latch mechanism. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $200+

**Summary**

The 2019 Chevrolet Traverse is a popular choice for families, but it has experienced some issues. The most severe issues include excessive oil consumption and timing chain failure in the 3.6-liter V6 engine, transmission failure, and strut mount failure. The cost to fix these issues can be high, so it is important to be aware of them before purchasing a 2019 Traverse.
