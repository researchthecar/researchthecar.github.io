# 2023 Chevrolet Traverse Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Chevrolet Traverse, prospective buyers will find a spacious three-row crossover equipped to handle family hauling duties. While many owners enjoy its comfort and utility, owner feedback submitted to federal safety regulators highlights several recurring mechanical and electrical concerns worth noting during your inspection.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 Engine

The 3.6-liter V6 engine produces approximately 310 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque, providing confident acceleration for highway merging and towing. Overall, the engine is generally robust, though buyers should verify routine maintenance history.

**Engine Misfire and Cooling Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual mechanical noises under acceleration, potential misfires, or cooling system performance alerts.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the cooling system, fuel injectors, and ignition components for proper operation.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with the V6 engine, the 9-speed automatic transmission distributes power smoothly across front-wheel or all-wheel-drive configurations. However, some owners have reported shifting anomalies requiring professional diagnosis.

**Harsh Shifting and Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission hesitation, harsh engagement when shifting into reverse or drive, unexpected jerking, or loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition and consult a transmission specialist to diagnose solenoid or torque converter performance.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides adequate stopping power for a large crossover, though proper maintenance is critical to prevent premature wear or caliper sticking.

**Brake Dragging and Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Vibration during highway driving, burning metallic odors, and excessively hot wheels due to locked brake pads or sticking caliper seals.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and caliper slider pins for proper movement and replace seized components.

## Electrical

### Charging System and Infotainment

Electrical architecture governs modern safety and comfort features, but intermittent glitches involving charging warnings and screen blackouts have been documented by vehicle owners.

**Battery Saver and Stalling Alerts**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warnings indicating 'battery saver active' or prompting the driver to service the charging system, occasionally accompanied by engine stalling.
- **Solution:** Test the battery, alternator, and charging circuit voltage under load to ensure electrical stability.

**Infotainment Screen Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Center display turns black, temporarily disabling the rearview camera, navigation, and audio controls.
- **Solution:** Perform a system reboot or have the infotainment module and software inspected by a dealer.

## Summary Verdict

The Chevrolet Traverse offers substantial passenger room and capable performance, making it a popular choice for families. While most owners experience trouble-free driving, used car buyers should carefully test drive the vehicle to check for transmission shifting smoothness, inspect the electrical charging system, and ensure the brakes operate without dragging. A pre-purchase inspection by a trusted mechanic is strongly recommended.
