# 2020 Chevrolet Traverse Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 Chevrolet Traverse, prospective buyers will find a spacious three-row midsize crossover powered by a standard V6 engine. While it offers strong family-hauling utility and comfortable highway manners, a review of owner feedback reveals several recurring mechanical and electrical pain points that shoppers should inspect closely.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 Engine

The 3.6-liter V6 engine produces 310 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque, providing robust acceleration for daily driving and towing. Overall, it is generally dependable, though specific exhaust and manifold weld concerns have been flagged by owners.

**Exhaust Component and Bracket Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report loud rattling noises, exhaust leaks, or fractured welds near the catalytic converter and exhaust brackets, occasionally leading to a loss of pressure or engine stalling.
- **Solution:** Inspect the exhaust system and bracket welds for cracks or pinholes. Professional re-welding or replacement of affected exhaust components may be necessary.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly in most driving conditions, paired with either front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive. However, potential buyers should thoroughly test-drive the vehicle to check for early signs of shuddering or harsh low-gear shifts.

**Transmission Shudder and Torque Converter Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a noticeable shudder, shaking, or hard shifting—particularly between low gears or around 25 to 30 mph—which can progress to severe component wear.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the transmission fluid and torque converter. Repairs often involve flushing fluid, replacing the valve body, or rebuilding/replacing the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $8,500

## Electrical

### Shifter and Ignition Switch

The electrical architecture handles convenience and safety features well, though sensor and shifter microswitch glitches can cause frustrating auxiliary power issues.

**"Shift to Park" Error Message**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle displays a persistent "Shift to Park" warning message on the dashboard even when the gear selector is fully in park, preventing the ignition from turning off properly and draining the battery.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace the microswitch inside the shifter assembly that detects park engagement.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety restraints are vital for occupant protection, making it important to verify that all seat belt buckles latch firmly during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Seat Belt Latch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Front or second-row seat belt buckles intermittently fail to click securely into place or pop out unexpectedly during use.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt buckles for internal jams or wear, and replace the faulty buckle assembly as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V115000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Traverse&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Over cured tires may develop a break in the sidewall resulting in sudden air loss or a belt edge separation which could lead to a tread/belt loss. Either condition can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the tires, replacing them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V817000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Traverse&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A transmission fluid leak can cause a loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash. In addition, a leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the screw and clip, and inspect for signs of contact with the transmission, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V639000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Traverse&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Insufficient fuel supply can result in an engine stall without warning, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump module. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V684000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Traverse&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Overcured tires may experience a carcass break in the sidewall resulting in a sudden air loss or could develop a belt edge separation which could lead to partial or full tread/belt loss. Either condition increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect vehicles and replace tires that have DOT number and production mold number combinations identified by Continental, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V446000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain:Inflator](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Traverse&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The diffuser component of the Roof-Rail Air Bag (RRAB) inflator may not have been properly crimped to the inflator and could separate from the inflator during air bag deployment. If the diffuser separates from the inflator during deployment, RRAB performance may be degraded, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the suspect RRAB modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V668000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Traverse&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Missing bolts on the start-stop accumulator endcap could result in a transmission oil leak and may progress to a loss of propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash. A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the start-stop transmission accumulator and replace it if any bolts are missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V643000: Structure:Frame And Members](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Traverse&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A crack in the frame rail may reduce crash performance, increasing the risk of an injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, as necessary, replace the vehicle, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V210000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Traverse&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The harness connector to the left or right side roof rail air bag may have incompatible electrical terminals, which could prevent the air bags from deploying in a crash. Roof rail air bags that do not deploy as intended increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the harness connector to the roof rail air bags, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Chevrolet Traverse offers generous cabin space and strong V6 performance, making it a popular choice for large families. However, used-car buyers should carefully evaluate the vehicle for common mechanical vulnerabilities—specifically transmission shudder and torque converter durability, exhaust bracket weld longevity, and the irritating "Shift to Park" electrical glitch. A thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the transmission and exhaust system is strongly recommended before finalizing a purchase.
