# 2016 Chevrolet Traverse Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Chevrolet Traverse is a spacious, family-friendly three-row crossover SUV known for its generous cargo room and comfortable ride quality. However, used-car buyers should approach this model year with care, as owner complaints highlight notable recurring mechanical and electrical concerns. Particular attention should be paid to the powertrain and steering systems, where owners have reported unexpected issues that can impact drivability and safety.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 Engine

Equipped with a 3.6-liter V6 engine producing around 281 to 288 horsepower, this powerplant offers strong acceleration suitable for hauling families and cargo. While generally capable, this engine option is associated with occasional reports of hesitation, stalling, and timing chain wear in higher-mileage examples.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience sudden engine stalling, jerking during acceleration, or a complete loss of motive power while driving.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fuel system components, throttle body, or timing components; replace faulty sensors or failed parts as needed.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission comes paired with front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive. Buyer feedback indicates that the transmission and torque converter are potential weak spots, requiring careful pre-purchase inspection for smooth shifting.

**Transmission Slipping and Torque Converter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Unsafe vibrations between gears, rough gear shifting, slipping, and sudden loss of transmission power at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission solenoids and torque converter; rebuild or replace the transmission if internal wear is severe.

## Steering

### Steering System

Issues involving steering effort and pump longevity can compromise maneuverability and require costly component replacements.

**Power Steering Stiffening and Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Abnormal moaning sounds when turning the steering wheel, heavy steering effort, or feeling like the steering is locking up.
- **Solution:** Inspect the power steering pump and rack and pinion assembly; flush fluid or replace failing steering components.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Lighting

electrical issues involving exterior lighting and driver-assist safety modules can be frustrating and recurrent nuisances.

**Headlight Bulb Burnout and Side Detection Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Frequent burning out of low-beam headlight bulbs due to overheating sockets, alongside warning messages for blind-spot and cross-traffic systems.
- **Solution:** Replace damaged headlight sockets and bulbs; diagnose and replace faulty blind-spot detection modules.

## Safety

### Air Bags and Seat Belts

Restraint system warnings and seat belt latch complaints highlight areas requiring functional checks before purchasing.

**Air Bag Warnings and Seat Belt Latch Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Air bag warning lights illuminating on the dashboard or seat belt buckles becoming stuck and difficult to latch.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician scan supplemental restraint system codes and inspect seat belt assemblies for mechanical jams.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V609000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Motor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Traverse&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A wiper motor that overheats increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** GM has notified owners, and dealers will replace the windshield wiper motor cover assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V833000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Traverse&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the seat frame is not welded in the correct places, the seat may not perform as intended, and in the event of a crash, the seat occupant could be at an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the third row left side lower seat frame, replacing it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V782000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Traverse&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The driver's air bag inflator may explode during deployment, due to a manufacturing defect. An inflator explosion may result in sharp metal fragments striking the driver or other occupants, resulting in serious injury or death.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver's air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V246000: Air Bags:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Traverse&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The side curtain air bags may not be properly attached. Unsecured side curtain air bags may not perform as intended, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, if necessary, realign or replace the weld nuts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V334000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Traverse&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The driver's air bag inflator may explode during deployment, due to a manufacturing defect. An inflator explosion may result in sharp metal fragments striking the driver or other occupants, resulting in serious injury or death.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver's air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Chevrolet Traverse offers undeniable utility and space for growing families, but used buyers should proceed with caution. Due to reported concerns regarding transmission durability, torque converter performance, and power steering reliability, a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a trusted mechanic is essential. Verifying that all open recalls have been completed will also help ensure a safer ownership experience.
