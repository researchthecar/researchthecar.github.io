# 2017 Chevrolet Traverse Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Chevrolet Traverse is a spacious, family-friendly three-row crossover SUV, but prospective buyers should carefully review its history of mechanical and safety concerns. While it offers generous cargo capacity and a comfortable ride, owner reports indicate potential trouble spots ranging from transmission issues to electrical and steering glitches. Being well-informed on these common problem areas can help you inspect a used model more thoroughly before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 Engine

Equipped with a 3.6-liter V6 engine producing approximately 281 to 288 horsepower, this model provides ample power for highway cruising and towing. However, owners have occasionally reported driveability hiccups and timing-related concerns.

**Engine Stalling and Rough Running**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences unexpected engine stalling, loud noises upon startup, and erratic RPM fluctuations while idling or driving.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes, inspect electronic sensors, and replace worn engine components or timing components as necessary.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive. While generally adequate for daily commuting, a notable portion of owners experience premature shuddering, slipping, and mechanical wear.

**Transmission Shudder and Shifting Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle shudders during acceleration, hesitates before engaging gears, or has trouble shifting smoothly into higher gears like third gear.
- **Solution:** Flush or service transmission fluid, update software control modules, or rebuild/replace worn transmission units in severe failure cases.
- **Cost:** $8,500

## Steering

### Steering Pump and Assembly

Power steering assist failures can severely compromise vehicle control and safety during low-speed maneuvers.

**Stiff or Inoperable Steering**
- **Symptoms:** Steering wheel suddenly becomes very tight, difficult to turn, or completely inoperable accompanied by loud clanking noises during turns.
- **Solution:** Inspect power steering pump, fluid levels, and steering linkage components, and replace faulty pumps or damaged steering shafts.

## Electrical

### Dashboard and Infotainment Electronics

electrical issues involving interior screens and accessory power can cause intermittent frustrations for drivers.

**Dashboard Blackout and Backup Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Entire dashboard display goes dark while driving, backup camera shows a distorted or blank screen, and phone connectivity drops.
- **Solution:** Check battery connections, inspect body control modules, and update or replace faulty infotainment/display hardware.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Air bag system warnings and sensor failures can deactivate vital supplemental restraint systems, posing a significant safety risk.

**Air Bag Warning Light and Sensor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Air bag warning light stays illuminated on the dashboard due to faulty passenger presence sensors or internal module failures.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes at a dealership or certified repair shop and replace faulty passenger presence sensors or airbag modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V782000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Traverse&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The driver's air bag inflator may explode during deployment, due to a manufacturing defect. An inflator explosion may result in sharp metal fragments striking the driver or other occupants, resulting in serious injury or death.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver's air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V246000: Air Bags:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Traverse&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The side curtain air bags may not be properly attached. Unsecured side curtain air bags may not perform as intended, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, if necessary, realign or replace the weld nuts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V334000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Traverse&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The driver's air bag inflator may explode during deployment, due to a manufacturing defect. An inflator explosion may result in sharp metal fragments striking the driver or other occupants, resulting in serious injury or death.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver's air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Chevrolet Traverse presents a mixed ownership picture. While its expansive cabin and smooth ride make it practical for families, used-car buyers should thoroughly inspect the transmission for signs of shudder or slipping, test all electrical accessories, and verify that safety recalls and air bag sensors have been properly addressed. A pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic is strongly recommended.
