# 2018 Chevrolet Traverse Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Chevrolet Traverse offers family-friendly three-row space and comfortable driving dynamics, but potential buyers should approach used models with caution. A significant cluster of owner complaints points toward recurring electrical and powertrain snags that can cause frustration and unexpected repair bills. Inspecting service history and thoroughly testing major mechanical systems during a test drive is essential.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 Engine

The standard 3.6-liter V6 engine produces approximately 310 horsepower and provides confident acceleration for everyday driving and highway merging. While generally robust, a small number of owners have experienced unexpected stalling and fuel system component concerns.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report random dashboard warning lights and occasional engine stalling while driving.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes, inspect electronic throttle and fuel system components, and replace failed sensors or purge valves.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

The available 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine delivers strong low-end torque and competitive fuel economy, though it is less common in the used marketplace. Routine maintenance and cooling system checks are vital for long-term health.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission helps optimize highway fuel efficiency and delivers smooth gear changes under light loads. However, torque converter wear and shudder issues represent some of the most notable mechanical concerns reported by owners.

**Transmission Shudder and Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle shudders during acceleration, particularly between 18 and 40 mph, or exhibits delayed engagement and slipping.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis; torque converter failure often necessitates a complete transmission overhaul or replacement.

## Electrical

### Shifter Microswitch

The electrical architecture supports advanced cabin convenience and safety features, but persistent microswitch failures in the shifter assembly are a widespread nuisance.

**Shift to Park Defect**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to recognize when it is placed in park, displaying a persistent 'Shift to Park' message and preventing the ignition from turning off completely.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty shifter assembly or internal microswitch.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety restraint components generally hold up well, but rare failures in rear passenger buckles require immediate attention to ensure proper passenger safety.

**Inoperable Seat Belt Buckle**
- **Symptoms:** Second-row seat belt buckles fail to latch securely or become entirely inoperable.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the defective seat belt buckle assembly.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V668000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Traverse&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Missing bolts on the start-stop accumulator endcap could result in a transmission oil leak and may progress to a loss of propulsion, increasing the risk of a crash. A transmission fluid leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the start-stop transmission accumulator and replace it if any bolts are missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V246000: Air Bags:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Traverse&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The side curtain air bags may not be properly attached. Unsecured side curtain air bags may not perform as intended, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, if necessary, realign or replace the weld nuts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Chevrolet Traverse is appealing for families needing three rows of seating and generous cargo capacity. Prospective buyers should thoroughly check for the notorious 'Shift to Park' electrical glitch and listen closely for transmission shudder during a test drive. Verifying service records can help avoid purchasing a vehicle with hidden drivetrain wear.
