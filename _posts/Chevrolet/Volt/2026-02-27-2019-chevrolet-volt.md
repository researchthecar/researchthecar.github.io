# 2019 Chevrolet Volt Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Chevrolet Volt is a popular plug-in hybrid electric vehicle known for its efficient daily electric driving range backed by a gasoline engine. Prospective used car buyers should carefully review owner feedback and common mechanical tendencies, particularly surrounding drivetrain electronic sensors and electrical system modules, to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L 4-Cylinder Range Extender Engine

The 1.5-liter four-cylinder engine acts primarily as a generator to extend driving range once the high-voltage battery is depleted. It provides seamless transitions between electric and hybrid operation, though hybrid control modules require periodic attention.

**Propulsion Power Reduced / High-Voltage Disconnect**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle displays a 'Propulsion Power Is Reduced' or 'Engine Not Available' warning message, and drivers may experience unexpected loss of power.
- **Solution:** Have the high-voltage battery disconnect relay and propulsion control modules inspected and replaced by a qualified technician if faulty.

## Transmission

### Voltec Electric Drive Unit / Automatic Transmission

The multi-mode electronic drive unit manages power delivery from both electric motors and the gasoline engine. It is generally robust, though minor shifter sensor faults can occur.

**Shift to Park Warning Defect**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle fails to register that it has been placed in park, resulting in a persistent 'Shift to Park' message and preventing the vehicle from shutting off.
- **Solution:** Replace the shifter microswitch assembly or related selector components.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Regenerative braking system blends friction brakes with electric motor resistance to recapture energy during deceleration.

**Brake Pedal Stiffness**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal feels frozen or extremely stiff and difficult to depress during stop-and-go driving conditions.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake pump, booster assembly, and vacuum or electronic assist systems.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

Electric power-assist steering system provides light and responsive handling under normal conditions.

**Power Steering Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power steering assist suddenly stops working while driving, displaying a warning message and making the steering wheel very heavy to turn.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electric power steering motor, wiring harnesses, and control module for faults.

## Electrical

### Battery Control Module

Manages high-voltage battery charging, discharging, and thermal regulation. Proper module communication is critical for vehicle operation.

**Battery Control Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle abruptly loses power or shuts down while driving due to a failing battery control module, potentially leaving the driver stranded.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the electrical control modules and replace defective battery control components.

## Interior

### Seats

Interior cabin seating designed for daily commuter comfort.

**Driver Seat Frame Squeak**
- **Symptoms:** A loud squeak emanates from the driver seat frame during normal vehicle operation.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat frame welds and lubrication points, and replace or repair components as necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V576000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Volt&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A reduction of braking performance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will bleed the vehicle's brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V673000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Volt&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Certain second-row or third-row rear seatbelts retractor assemblies may not automatically lock when the seatbelt is fully pulled out of the retractor, possibly preventing a child seat from being properly secured. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rear seatbelt retractors and replace them if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Chevrolet Volt offers an impressive electric driving experience and strong overall efficiency, making it an appealing choice for used car buyers. While most complaints center around minor electronic gear selector glitches and occasional high-voltage sensor warnings, a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help verify that all powertrain and electrical modules are operating correctly.
