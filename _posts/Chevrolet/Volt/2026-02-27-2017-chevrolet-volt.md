# 2017 Chevrolet Volt Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Chevrolet Volt is an innovative plug-in hybrid that pairs an efficient electric drive system with a range-extending gasoline engine, offering compelling daily electric range for commuters. However, used-car buyers should carefully evaluate its complex electrical architecture and potential high-voltage battery management concerns. While it delivers impressive fuel efficiency and smooth performance, several recurring owner-reported issues warrant a thorough pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L 4-Cylinder Range Extender Engine

The 1.5-liter four-cylinder engine acts primarily as a generator to extend driving range once the high-voltage battery is depleted. It integrates closely with the dual-motor drive unit to maintain overall system output. While generally durable mechanically, its reliance on complex electronic controls makes electrical diagnostics critical.

**Reduced Propulsion Mode and Motive Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a sudden loss of motive power accompanied by a 'Propulsion Power Reduced' warning message on the dashboard, leading to an unexpected drop in vehicle speed.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic scanning at a dealership or qualified hybrid specialist to isolate battery energy control module (BECM) or powertrain software faults.

## Transmission

### Voltec Continuously Variable / Two-Motor Transmission

The advanced Voltec electric-drive transmission seamlessly blends electric motor output and gas-engine power to drive the front wheels efficiently. It is generally robust mechanically, though shifter switch failures frequently frustrate owners.

**Shift to Park Defect**
- **Symptoms:** Upon parking, the vehicle fails to recognize that it has been shifted into Park, triggering a persistent 'Shift to Park' dashboard message and preventing the vehicle from fully shutting off.
- **Solution:** Replacing the faulty microswitch or gear shifter assembly within the center console.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering System

Provides electronic assist for steering inputs. Failures can lead to heavy steering effort and compromised vehicle control.

**Power Steering Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report sudden steering chattering, erratic behavior, and complete loss of power steering assistance accompanied by a dashboard warning message.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential replacement of the electric power steering assembly or column components.

## Electrical

### Battery Energy Control Module (BECM) & High-Voltage Systems

Manages high-voltage battery charging, power distribution, and thermal regulation. electrical issues in this subsystem can disable driving functions entirely.

**BECM Failure and Charging Interruptions**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may refuse to take a charge, display sudden power loss warnings, or fail to start due to internal control module failure.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic evaluation and replacement of the battery energy control module or related high-voltage components.

## Safety

### Occupant Detection & Airbag Systems

Monitors cabin occupants to ensure proper supplemental restraint deployment in the event of a collision.

**Airbag Warning Light and Passenger Sensor Fault**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning light illuminates on the dashboard because the system fails to register a passenger sitting in the front seat.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning and replacement of faulty occupant detection system sensors or wiring harnesses.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Chevrolet Volt is a highly efficient and rewarding plug-in hybrid for daily commuting, provided its high-voltage systems and electronics are in healthy working order. Prospective buyers should thoroughly test all charging functions, check for any active 'Shift to Park' or propulsion warnings, and verify service history regarding battery control modules. Securing a professional pre-purchase inspection by a technician familiar with plug-in hybrids is strongly recommended.
