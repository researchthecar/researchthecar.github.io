# 2016 Chevrolet Volt Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Chevrolet Volt is a popular plug-in hybrid hatchback known for offering an impressive all-electric driving range paired with a reliable gasoline generator for extended trips. While many owners praise its fuel efficiency and smooth driving dynamics, used car buyers should pay careful attention to a few recurring electrical and powertrain issues. Most notably, issues surrounding hybrid battery management and shifter sensors have been widely reported by owners, making a thorough pre-purchase inspection crucial.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L 4-Cylinder Plug-In Hybrid

The 2016 Chevrolet Volt features a 1.5-liter four-cylinder engine paired with a dual-motor electric drive system, producing an estimated 149 horsepower. This plug-in hybrid setup delivers exceptional efficiency, though prospective buyers should verify the health and history of the high-voltage battery components.

**Battery Energy Control Module (BECM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience sudden loss of propulsion, reduced power warnings, or a complete failure of the vehicle to start or move.
- **Solution:** Requires replacement of the Battery Energy Control Module (BECM) by a qualified service center.
- **Cost:** $2,000

## Transmission

### Two-Motor Split-Power Electronic Continuously Variable Transmission (eCVT)

The advanced two-motor electronic continuously variable transmission seamlessly blends electric motor torque with gasoline engine power. It is generally robust, though electronic shifter communications can occasionally trigger dashboard errors.

### Shift-to-Park Microswitch

The electrical architecture governs critical vehicle functions, including infotainment, driver assists, and gear selection sensors.

**'Shift to Park' Dashboard Error and Power-Off Failure**
- **Symptoms:** When parking and placing the shifter into park, the vehicle fails to recognize the gear position, displaying a persistent 'Shift to Park' warning and preventing the driver from turning the car off.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the shifter assembly or internal microswitch responsible for detecting the park position.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V621000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Volt&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The driver's frontal air bag may improperly inflate during second-stage deployment in the event of a high speed crash. An improperly inflated air bag increases the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver's frontal air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Chevrolet Volt presents a compelling option for buyers seeking high fuel economy and daily electric driving, but it is not without potential pitfalls. The two most prominent issues to watch out for are failures of the Battery Energy Control Module (BECM) and the pesky 'Shift to Park' sensor glitch. When shopping for a used model, ensure that the high-voltage hybrid components have been inspected, verify if extended warranty coverages apply to the BECM, and test the shifter thoroughly before purchasing.
