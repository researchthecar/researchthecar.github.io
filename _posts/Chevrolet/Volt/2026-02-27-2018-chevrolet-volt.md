# 2018 Chevrolet Volt Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Chevrolet Volt is widely praised for its efficiency, offering an impressive all-electric range paired with a reliable gasoline generator for seamless long-distance travel. However, prospective buyers should inspect specific recurring electrical and drivetrain quirks, such as the widely documented 'Shift to Park' microswitch failure and Battery Energy Control Module (BECM) concerns, which can disrupt daily driving if left unaddressed.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L 4-Cylinder Plug-In Hybrid Engine

The 1.5-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine acts primarily as a range-extending generator for the Volt's plug-in hybrid system, producing roughly 149 horsepower. It generally proves dependable, though supporting electrical control hardware warrants close inspection.

**Battery Energy Control Module (BECM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly lose motive power, display reduced propulsion warnings, or trigger a check engine light accompanied by sudden erratic battery state-of-charge readings.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic evaluation and replacement of the faulty BECM unit by a certified service center.
- **Cost:** $3,500

## Transmission

### Voltec Two-Motor Extended Range Electric Drive

The advanced two-motor planetary hybrid transmission seamlessly blends electric motor output with gasoline engine power to drive the front wheels efficiently. It operates smoothly and generally proves durable, though owners must monitor shifter-related microswitch sensors.

### Shifter Assembly / Park Switch

Electrical architecture supports the vehicle's complex hybrid components and interior electronics, though minor sensor and switch glitches have been noted by owners.

**"Shift to Park" Error Message**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard continuously displays a "Shift to Park" warning even when the gear selector is placed firmly in park, preventing the vehicle from turning off completely.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty microswitch inside the shifter assembly or follow updated dealer service bulletins.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The regenerative braking system works hand-in-hand with traditional friction brakes to recapture energy and slow the vehicle down smoothly.

**Sticking Brake Calipers**
- **Symptoms:** Front brake calipers may seize or stick, causing brake pads to drag against the rotors, leading to excessive heat buildup and accelerated wear.
- **Solution:** Clean, lubricate, or replace the brake calipers and slide pins as necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V576000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Volt&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A reduction of braking performance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will bleed the vehicle's brake system, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V673000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Volt&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Certain second-row or third-row rear seatbelts retractor assemblies may not automatically lock when the seatbelt is fully pulled out of the retractor, possibly preventing a child seat from being properly secured. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rear seatbelt retractors and replace them if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Chevrolet Volt remains a compelling used plug-in hybrid choice for commuters seeking substantial electric-only driving range. While the powertrain and hybrid architecture offer great efficiency and overall durability, buyers should verify whether the BECM and shifter microswitch assemblies have been inspected or repaired, as these represent the most common pain points.
