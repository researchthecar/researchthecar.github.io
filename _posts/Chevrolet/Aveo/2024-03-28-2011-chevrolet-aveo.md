**Introduction**

The 2011 Chevrolet Aveo is a subcompact sedan that was available with a choice of two gasoline engines and two transmissions. The 1.6-liter engine produced 103 horsepower and 107 lb-ft of torque, while the 1.8-liter engine produced 122 horsepower and 127 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were available with either a five-speed manual transmission or a six-speed automatic transmission.

The Aveo was praised for its affordability, fuel efficiency, and stylish design. However, it was also criticized for its cramped interior, lack of features, and poor crash test ratings.

**Engine**

**1.6-liter Engine**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: The engine burns excessive oil, causing the oil level to drop and the engine to run low on oil. This can lead to engine damage if not addressed.
    * Solution: Replace the piston rings and valve seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 15V659000: PISTON RINGS AND VALVE SEALS INSTALLED
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner fails, causing the timing chain to skip or break. This can lead to catastrophic engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain and tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 17E043000: TIMING CHAIN TENSIONER FAILURE
