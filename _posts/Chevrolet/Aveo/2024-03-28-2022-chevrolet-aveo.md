## 2022 Chevrolet Aveo

The 2022 Chevrolet Aveo is a subcompact sedan that offers a single engine and transmission option. The 1.5L inline-four engine produces 103 horsepower and 108 lb-ft of torque and is paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). The Aveo has received praise for its affordability, fuel efficiency, and spacious interior, but has been criticized for its lack of power and uninspired driving dynamics.

### Engine

**1.5L Inline-Four**

- **Excessive Oil Consumption**
Description/Symptoms: Vehicles may experience excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
Solution: Repair or replace the piston rings or oil control valves.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
Recalls: None Found

### Transmission

**CVT**

- **Jerking or Shuddering**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission may jerk or shudder during acceleration or shifting.
Solution: Replace the transmission fluid or reprogram the transmission control module.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
Recalls: None Found

### Suspension

- **No Issues Found**

### Electrical

- **Alternator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The alternator may fail to charge the battery, leading to electrical system problems and potential stalling.
Solution: Replace the alternator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
Recalls: None Found

### Exterior

- **Headlight Condensation**
Description/Symptoms: Moisture may accumulate inside the headlight assemblies, causing fogging or reduced lighting output.
Solution: Seal the headlight housings or replace the headlights.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 22V456

### Interior

- **Loose Interior Trim Panels**
Description/Symptoms: The interior trim panels, such as the door panels or dashboard, may become loose or rattle.
Solution: Reinstall or tighten the trim panels.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
Recalls: None Found

### Summary

The 2022 Chevrolet Aveo has a limited number of reported issues, with the most common problems being excessive oil consumption, transmission jerking, alternator failure, and headlight condensation. While these issues can be frustrating, they are generally not major or expensive to fix. Overall, the Aveo is a reliable and affordable subcompact sedan that offers good fuel efficiency and a spacious interior.
