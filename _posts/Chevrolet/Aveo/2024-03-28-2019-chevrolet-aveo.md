**Introduction:**

The 2019 Chevrolet Aveo is not sold in the United States or Canada. It is a subcompact sedan available in Mexico and other countries. The Aveo comes with a 1.5-liter naturally aspirated engine that produces 103 horsepower and 108 lb-ft of torque paired with a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic.

**Engine**

**1.5L Naturally Aspirated**

* **Loss of power:** Failure of the throttle position sensor. Symptoms include engine hesitation, reduced power, and stalling. Solution involves replacing the throttle position sensor. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+
* **Check engine light:** Faulty oxygen sensors. Symptoms include illuminated check engine light, reduced fuel efficiency, and performance issues. Solution involves replacing the affected oxygen sensors. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+
* **Oil leaks:** Worn valve cover gaskets. Symptoms include oil leaks from the valve cover. Solution involves replacing the valve cover gaskets. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $200+
* **Camshaft position sensor failure:** Malfunctioning camshaft position sensor. Symptoms include engine stalling, rough idling, and difficulty starting. Solution involves replacing the camshaft position sensor. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $300+

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Difficulty shifting:** Worn or damaged shift linkage. Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises, and popping out of gear. Solution involves inspecting and repairing the shift linkage. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500+
* **Clutch slipping:** Worn clutch disc. Symptoms include slippage when accelerating, difficulty climbing hills, and burning smell. Solution involves replacing the clutch disc and pressure plate. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $800 - $1,200+

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **No issues found.**

**Suspension**

* **Strut mounts:** Worn strut mounts. Symptoms include clunking noises when going over bumps, reduced handling, and uneven tire wear. Solution involves replacing the strut mounts. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+
* **Stabilizer bar links:** Loose or damaged stabilizer bar links. Symptoms include rattling noises when going over bumps, reduced stability, and uneven tire wear. Solution involves replacing the stabilizer bar links. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $200+

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** Faulty alternator. Symptoms include starting problems, dim lights, and battery warning light. Solution involves replacing the alternator. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $600+
* **Electrical shorts:** Damaged wiring harness. Symptoms include blown fuses, flickering lights, and intermittent electrical problems. Solution involves diagnosing and repairing the damaged portion of the wiring harness. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $800+

**Exterior**

* **Minor body panel misalignment:** Gaps or misalignment of body panels. Solution involves adjusting the body panels. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $300+

**Interior**

* **Rattling noises:** Loose interior trim or components. Symptoms include rattling or buzzing sounds when driving. Solution involves identifying and tightening the loose components. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $50 - $200+
* **HVAC blower motor failure:** Malfunctioning blower motor. Symptoms include reduced or no airflow from the vents. Solution involves replacing the blower motor. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+

**Summary:**

The 2019 Chevrolet Aveo has some common issues, ranging from engine performance problems to suspension and electrical faults. While the cost of repairs can vary, many of the issues are relatively minor and can be resolved for under $500. However, potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues before purchasing an Aveo.
