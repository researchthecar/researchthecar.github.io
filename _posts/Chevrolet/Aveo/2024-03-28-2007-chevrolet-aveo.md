**Introduction**

The 2007 Chevrolet Aveo is a subcompact sedan offered with two engine options: a 1.6L inline-4 producing 103 hp and 107 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.0L inline-4 producing 122 hp and 129 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual and a 4-speed automatic. The Aveo received mixed reviews, with praise for its affordability and fuel economy, but criticism for its lackluster performance and handling.

**Engine**

- **1.6L Inline-4**
    - **Knocking Sound on Startup**
        Description/Symptoms: A loud knocking noise coming from the engine upon startup, especially when cold.
        Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
        Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
        Recalls: None found
    - **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
        Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaks from the intake manifold, accompanied by a drop in engine performance.
        Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
        Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
        Recalls: None found

- **2.0L Inline-4**
    - **Cylinder Head Gasket Failure**
        Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, and loss of power, often accompanied by white smoke from the exhaust.
        Solution: Replace the cylinder head gasket.
        Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        Recalls: None found

**Transmission**

- **5-Speed Manual**
    - **Grinding Gears**
        Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, accompanied by a grinding noise.
        Solution: Replace the damaged gears or synchronizers.
        Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000+
        Recalls: None found
- **4-Speed Automatic**
    - **Shifting Delay**
        Description/Symptoms: Hesitation or delay in shifting gears, especially when accelerating or downshifting.
        Solution: Replace or repair the transmission solenoids or torque converter.
        Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        Recalls: None found

**Exterior**

- **Body Panel Rust**
        Description/Symptoms: Rust formation on the body panels, especially around the wheel wells and rear fenders.
        Solution: Repair or replace the affected body panels.
        Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of the damage
        Recalls: None found

**Interior**

- **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
        Description/Symptoms: Difficulty or inability to lock or unlock the doors using the power locks.
        Solution: Replace the faulty door lock actuators.
        Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
        Recalls: None found
- **Dashboard Squeaks and Rattles**
        Description/Symptoms: Annoying squeaks and rattles coming from the dashboard, especially on rough roads.
        Solution: Locate and isolate the source of the noise and apply sound deadening material or tighten loose components.
        Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity of the issue
        Recalls: None found

**Summary**

The 2007 Chevrolet Aveo has several potential issues that can vary in severity and cost. Engine problems, such as knocking sounds and intake manifold gasket failures, can be costly to repair. Transmission issues, including grinding gears and shifting delays, can also lead to significant expenses. However, the lack of reported issues in other areas, such as suspension, electrical components, and interior features (excluding door lock actuators and dashboard noises), indicates that the Aveo's reliability is generally acceptable.
