# 2010 Chevrolet HHR

### Overview
The 2010 Chevrolet HHR is a compact car known for its retro styling and versatile cargo space. As with any vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding reliability. This analysis focuses on common reported issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential mechanical and electrical concerns that may arise. Understanding these potential problem areas can help owners anticipate and address issues proactively, ensuring a smoother ownership experience. The HHR, particularly in its later model years, generally offered a decent balance of features and practicality for its segment.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged (LSJ)
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, excessive smoke
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level
- **Solution:** Replacement of seals and gaskets
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


### 2.4L (LE5)
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level
- **Solution:** Replacement of seals and gaskets
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Timing Chain Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, check engine light
- **Solution:** Timing chain and tensioner replacement
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500


### 2.2L (L61)
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level
- **Solution:** Replacement of seals and gaskets
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Transmission

### 4-Speed Automatic (4T45-E)
**Harsh Shifting / Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Jerking during gear changes, delayed engagement, inability to hold gears
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush, solenoid replacement, or full transmission rebuild/replacement
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $3,000


### 5-Speed Manual (MV5)
**No widespread issues reported.**
- **Symptoms:** N/A
- **Solution:** N/A
- **Cost:** Varies


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Ball Joints and Control Arms**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, uneven tire wear, vague steering
- **Solution:** Replacement of ball joints and control arms
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Strut Mount Bearing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Popping or creaking noise when turning the steering wheel
- **Solution:** Replacement of strut mount bearings
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Electrical Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not working, interior lights flickering, dashboard warning lights illuminating randomly
- **Solution:** Reprogramming or replacement of the BCM
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,000


### Alternator
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, difficulty starting
- **Solution:** Alternator replacement
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Trim
**Rust on Lower Body Panels**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust spots or bubbling paint, especially around wheel wells and rocker panels
- **Solution:** Rust repair and repainting
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,000+


### Headlight Lenses
**Clouding/Yellowing**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced headlight visibility, hazy appearance
- **Solution:** Headlight restoration or replacement
- **Cost:** $100 - $300


## Interior

### HVAC System
**Blend Door Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to control temperature or direct airflow, clicking noises from dashboard
- **Solution:** Replacement of blend door actuator
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


### Seat Fabric Durability
**Excessive Wear and Tear**
- **Symptoms:** Fraying or tearing of seat fabric, especially on driver's side
- **Solution:** Seat cover installation or professional upholstery repair
- **Cost:** $100 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2010 Chevrolet HHR, while offering a unique style and practicality, presents a few common areas of concern for owners. The engines, particularly the turbocharged variant, can experience issues like turbocharger failure and oil leaks. Automatic transmissions may develop problems with harsh shifting or slipping. On the suspension, worn ball joints and strut mounts are noted. Electrical gremlins, often linked to the Body Control Module or alternator, can also occur. Exterior rust and interior HVAC blend door actuator failures are other points to consider. Despite these potential issues, many HHRs remain reliable with proper maintenance and timely repairs of these specific components.
