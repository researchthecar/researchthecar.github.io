## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Intermittent Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional failure of accessories, dashboard lights, or engine not starting.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of wiring harnesses, grounds, or faulty relays/fuses.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery depletes prematurely, leading to starting issues.
- **Solution:** Identify parasitic draw from accessories or faulty charging system components.
- **Cost:** $100 - $400


## Exterior

### Body and Panels
**Door Latch/Lock Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Doors may not lock or unlock properly, or may not open from the inside or outside.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty door latch or lock actuator.
- **Cost:** $250 - $600


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air or intermittent airflow from the vents, regardless of fan speed setting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the HVAC blower motor or blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2023 Chevrolet Express 3500 demonstrates a generally solid reliability record, especially concerning its powertrain options (4.3L V6 and 6.6L V8 Duramax Diesel) and the standard 6-speed automatic transmission. These core components are well-proven and typically do not present widespread issues. The primary areas where minor concerns have been noted are within the electrical system, specifically intermittent power loss and battery drain, which can often be traced to wiring or charging system components. Exterior issues like door latch failures and interior concerns such as HVAC blower motor malfunctions are also reported, though not to a degree that suggests systemic design flaws. Overall, the Express 3500 remains a dependable choice for demanding applications, with most potential issues being repairable and not prohibitively expensive.
