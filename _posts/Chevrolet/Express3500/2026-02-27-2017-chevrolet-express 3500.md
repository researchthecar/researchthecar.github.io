# 2017 Chevrolet Express 3500

### Overview
The 2017 Chevrolet Express 3500 is a robust full-size van designed for commercial and heavy-duty use. Its utilitarian nature prioritizes durability and functionality over luxury. Reliability is generally considered good, especially for its intended purpose, with a focus on sturdy mechanical components. However, like any vehicle, specific areas can experience issues over time, particularly with high mileage and demanding operational conditions. This analysis focuses on documented common problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential repair needs for different configurations.

---


## Engine

### 4.8L V8 Vortec
**No Major Issues**
- **Symptoms:** No widespread issues reported.
- **Solution:** No widespread issues reported.
- **Cost:** N/A


### 6.0L V8 Vortec
**Intake Manifold Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, engine misfires, rough idle, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


### 6.6L Duramax V8 Turbo-Diesel
**Glow Plug Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Hard starting in cold weather, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty glow plugs and potentially the glow plug module.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Fuel Injector Problems**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, loss of power, black smoke, poor fuel economy.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per injector


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (RPO MYD)
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering, slipping, delayed engagement, overheating.
- **Solution:** Rebuild or replacement of the torque converter and potentially transmission fluid flush.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2500

## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Leaf Spring Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Sagging rear end, clunking noises over bumps, reduced load capacity.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn or broken leaf springs.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming lights, battery warning light, dead battery, electrical system malfunctions.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Wiring Harness Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical problems, non-functioning accessories, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair or replacement of damaged wiring.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Chevrolet Express 3500 is a workhorse van with a generally solid reputation for reliability, especially when equipped with the gasoline V8 engines. The 6.6L Duramax diesel, while powerful, can present more complex issues like glow plug and injector problems, which are common for diesel engines of this era. Transmission issues, particularly with the torque converter, can be a concern, especially on higher-mileage units. Suspension components like leaf springs may require attention due to the van's load-carrying nature. Electrical issues, such as alternator failure, are not uncommon. Overall, the Express 3500 is a dependable choice for commercial applications, but owners should be aware of potential diesel-specific engine repairs and the general wear and tear associated with heavy use.
