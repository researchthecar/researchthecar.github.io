**Introduction to the 1996 Chevrolet Express 3500:**

The 1996 Chevrolet Express 3500 is a full-size van that offers a spacious interior and powerful engine options. It is commonly used for commercial and personal transportation purposes. The vehicle is available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 5.7L V8 engine with 255 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque, mated to a 4-speed automatic transmission. Other engine options include a 6.0L V8 and a 7.4L V8.

Owners generally appreciate the Express 3500's powerful performance, versatility, and spacious cargo area. However, some potential issues and concerns have been reported.

**Engine**

* **5.7L V8**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant and oil leaks, engine overheating, rough idle
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Knock Sensor Issues:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Engine knocking, poor fuel economy, reduced power
        * Solution: Replace the faulty knock sensor
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
        * Recalls: None Found

* **6.0L V8**
    * **Head Bolt Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, loss of power
        * Solution: Replace the head bolts
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Fuel Injector Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, poor fuel economy, decreased power
        * Solution: Replace the faulty fuel injector
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200 per injector
        * Recalls: None Found

* **7.4L V8**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **4-Speed Automatic**
    * **Transmission Slippage:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Failure to engage gears, erratic shifting, loss of power
        * Solution: Repair or replace the transmission
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Torque Converter Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping, overheating, shuddering
        * Solution: Replace the torque converter
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension:**
    * **Ball Joint Wear:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Loose steering, clunking noises when turning
        * Solution: Replace the ball joints
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per side
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Suspension:**
    * **Leaf Spring Breakage:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, uneven tire wear
        * Solution: Replace the broken leaf spring
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+ per side
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Battery dies quickly, electrical components malfunctioning
        * Solution: Test and replace any faulty electrical components
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the specific component
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, electrical components not working, battery not charging
        * Solution: Replace the alternator
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Corrosion on body panels, especially in areas like wheel arches and undercarriage
        * Solution: Rust removal and prevention measures
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity of the rust
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Fade:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Cracking or fading of the dashboard
        * Solution: Replace or repair the dashboard
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Seatbelt Issues:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Seatbelt not retracting properly, buckle malfunctioning
        * Solution: Inspect and repair or replace the faulty seatbelt components
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**
The 1996 Chevrolet Express 3500 is a powerful and versatile vehicle, but it has some potential issues and concerns. The most severe issue is head bolt failure in the 6.0L V8 engine, which can lead to expensive repairs. Other common issues include transmission slippage, ball joint wear, and electrical problems. While these issues can be costly to fix, they are not widespread and can be mitigated with regular maintenance and repairs. Overall, the Express 3500 is a reliable vehicle with a comfortable interior and ample cargo space, making it a solid choice for commercial or personal use.
