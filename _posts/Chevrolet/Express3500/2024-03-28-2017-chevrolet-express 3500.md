**Introduction**

The 2017 Chevrolet Express 3500 is a full-size van used for commercial and personal use. It is powered by a 6.0L V8 engine and comes with a 6-speed automatic transmission. It is praised for its spacious interior, towing and payload capacities, but criticized for its outdated design and poor fuel economy.

**Engine**

- **6.0L V8**
    - **Intake manifold gasket failure**
        - Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, rough idle
        - Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

- **6-speed automatic transmission**
    - **Torque converter shudder**
        - Symptoms: Vibration during acceleration
        - Solution: Replace torque converter
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
        - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

- **Battery drain**
        - Symptoms: Battery dies prematurely
        - Solution: Inspect and repair or replace electrical components as needed
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the source of the drain
        - Recalls: None Found
- **Alternator failure**
        - Symptoms: Dim headlights, difficulty starting, battery warning light
        - Solution: Replace alternator
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

- **HVAC system failure**
        - Symptoms: Blower motor stops working, weak airflow, temperature control issues
        - Solution: Inspect and repair or replace HVAC components as needed
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue
        - Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2017 Chevrolet Express 3500 has some common issues with the engine and transmission, as well as electrical problems. The severity of these issues can range from minor annoyances to major repairs. The cost of repairs can be significant, especially for transmission or engine issues.
