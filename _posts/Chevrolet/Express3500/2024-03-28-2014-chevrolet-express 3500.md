**Introduction:**

The 2014 Chevrolet Express 3500 is a versatile full-size van offered with various engine and transmission options. It is known for its towing and hauling capabilities, but it also has a spacious interior and comfortable ride quality. However, it received mixed reviews due to its outdated interior and limited cargo capacity compared to larger vans.

**Engine**

* **6.0L V8 L96 Gas**
    * **Piston Ring Failure:** Excessive oil consumption, blue smoke from the exhaust, and loss of power.
        * Solution: Replace piston rings and other affected components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $4,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Intake Manifold Failure:** Coolant leaks, overheating, and rough idle.
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
        * Recalls: None Found
* **6.6L V8 VVT Gas**
    * **AFM System Failure:** Engine vibration, reduced fuel economy, and potential engine damage.
        * Solution: Disable AFM system by installing a device or reprogramming the ECU.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Automatic 6L90**
    * **Overheating:** Transmission slipping, shuddering, or refusing to shift gears.
        * Solution: Flush transmission fluid, replace filter, and install additional transmission cooler.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200
        * Recalls: None Found
* **6-Speed Automatic 6L80**
    * **Torque Converter Failure:** Whining or grinding noise, shuddering, and lack of power.
        * Solution: Replace torque converter and transmission fluid.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension:** No Issues Found
* **Rear Suspension:** No Issues Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Dimming lights, battery warning light, and difficulty starting.
        * Solution: Replace alternator.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Starter Failure:** Difficulty starting or no starting at all.
        * Solution: Replace starter.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Power Door Lock Failure:** Doors may not lock or unlock electronically.
        * Solution: Replace actuator or door latch.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Rust:** Excessive rust on undercarriage, door panels, and other areas.
        * Solution: Repair rust and apply anti-corrosion measures.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on severity
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:** AC or heater may not work properly.
        * Solution: Repair or replace climate control module, blower motor, or other affected components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Power Window Failure:** Windows may not open or close electronically.
        * Solution: Replace window motor or switch.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2014 Chevrolet Express 3500 has several common issues that can be costly to repair. Engine problems, such as piston ring failure and intake manifold failure, are significant concerns. Transmission overheating and torque converter failure can also be expensive to fix. Electrical issues, such as alternator and starter failures, are relatively minor but can cause inconvenience. Rust is a potential problem, especially in areas with harsh weather conditions. Overall, the severity and cost of these issues vary, and some models may experience more problems than others.
