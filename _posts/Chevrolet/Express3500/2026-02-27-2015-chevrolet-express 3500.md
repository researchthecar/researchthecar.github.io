### 2.8L Duramax Turbo-Diesel I4
**Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Regeneration Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine light illuminated, reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption, exhaust smell.
- **Solution:** Forced regeneration, DPF cleaning, or replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

**Glow Plug Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Hard starting, especially in cold weather, Check Engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty glow plugs.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, electrical system malfunctions, engine stalling.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to start after sitting, rapid battery discharge.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of parasitic draw, battery replacement if necessary.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2015 Chevrolet Express 3500 demonstrates solid reliability, particularly with its V8 gasoline engines and standard automatic transmissions. The 2.8L Duramax diesel, while offering efficiency, has shown some susceptibility to DPF regeneration issues and glow plug failures, which are common for modern diesel powertrains. Electrical components like the alternator and potential battery drain are the most frequently cited non-routine issues across all models. Suspension and exterior/interior components are generally robust for this workhorse vehicle. Overall, the Express 3500 is a dependable choice for demanding applications, with most potential issues being manageable and repairable.
