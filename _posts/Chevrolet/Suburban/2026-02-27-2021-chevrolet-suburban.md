# 2021 Chevrolet Suburban Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Chevrolet Suburban offers expansive passenger and cargo room, modern technology, and a choice of potent gas or diesel engines. However, used-car buyers should review owner complaints regarding drivetrain behavior, electrical accessories, and select mechanical components before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 5.3L EcoTec3 V8

The standard 5.3-liter V8 engine produces 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, providing capable towing and daily driving performance for the full-size SUV.

**Lifter and Misfire Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine lights, rough idling, engine misfires, and a sudden loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnostic evaluation; may require replacing failed valve lifters or related valvetrain components.

### 6.2L EcoTec3 V8

The optional 6.2-liter V8 engine outputs 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, delivering robust acceleration and enhanced towing capability.

### 3.0L Duramax Turbo-Diesel I6

The 3.0-liter turbo-diesel inline-six generates 277 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, prioritizing high torque output and improved fuel economy for long-distance cruising.

### Fuel Pump

Components responsible for fuel delivery and pressure regulation across the engine lineup.

**Fuel Pump and Control Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Engine stalling while driving, unexpected shifting into neutral, and failure to start.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel pressure and control modules, and replace faulty fuel pumps or system wiring as needed.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission pairs with all available engines to optimize fuel efficiency and power delivery, though some owners have reported shifting irregularities.

**Transmission Shudder and Locking Issues**
- **Symptoms:** High-speed jerking, harsh shifting, low-RPM shuddering resembling driving over a rumble strip, and transmission locking completely in neutral.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to a dealer or specialist to inspect the valve body, torque converter, and control systems.
- **Cost:** $1,000

## Suspension

### Wheels and Suspension

Suspension setup and factory wheels designed to support ride comfort and handling.

**Wheel Cracking and Air Suspension Leveing**
- **Symptoms:** Rapid loss of tire pressure due to interior rim cracking, or warning lights for the leveling system dropping the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace cracked wheels or service faulty air suspension components.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Primary braking hydraulics and pedal assembly.

**Brake Pedal Hardness and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal becomes excessively hard to press and experiences reduced stopping responsiveness.
- **Solution:** Inspect master cylinder, brake booster, and hydraulic lines.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Lighting systems and exterior illumination controls.

**Headlight Adjuster and Water Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights pointing downward due to broken internal adjusters or water pooling inside the lens housing causing failure.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged headlight assembly or seals.
- **Cost:** $1,000

### Seats

Cabin seating, folding mechanisms, and occupant comfort features.

**Headrest Latch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Second-row seat headrest fails to stay in the upright position.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace the defective seat headrest latching mechanism.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V455000: Steering:Steering Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Suburban&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A software issue may cause a loss of power steering assist at startup or while driving. Increased steering effort, particularly at lower speeds, increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 22V036000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Suburban&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Driveshaft failure can cause a sudden loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the left and/or right rear driveshaft assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V792000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Suburban&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The seat belt brackets may not be secured to the seat frame in the front-row center seating position. If a seat-belt bracket is not secured, the seat belt may not properly restrain an occupant in the front-row center seat position, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect left-side and right-side front-row center seat-belt bracket attachments and reassemble it correctly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V811000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Suburban&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** If an incorrect bolt was used, the seat belt may not be properly attached to the vehicle. If a seat belt assembly is not properly attached to the vehicle, the seat belt may not properly restrain an occupant in the event of a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will replace the suspect bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V735000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Suburban&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The attachment bolts for the front seats may not have been properly reinstalled after the repair was completed. If an occupied front seat has loose or missing attachment bolts, it may move during a crash, increasing the risk of injury to the seated occupant.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the seat attachments for both front seats and install attachment bolts as needed, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V739000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump:Control/Drive Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Suburban&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The fuel pump power control module may cause the fuel pump to fail or operate intermittently, which can interrupt fuel flow to the engine, and cause an unexpected engine stall. An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump power control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V421000: Air Bags:Srs Malfunction Warning Lamp/Light](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Suburban&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The air bag malfunction indicator light may fail to warn the driver of a problem with the air bag, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 21V190000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Suburban&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The third-row outboard seat belts may have been entrapped or misrouted behind the outboard seat-folding mechanism, which could result in damaged seat belts. A damaged seat belt can break during a crash, increasing the risk of injury to the occupant.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the third-row outboard seat belts, replacing any damaged seat belts, and rerouting them if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2021 Chevrolet Suburban, conduct a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on transmission shift quality, powertrain operation, and electrical accessories. Ensuring that previous service campaigns and updates have been performed can help mitigate potential ownership issues.
