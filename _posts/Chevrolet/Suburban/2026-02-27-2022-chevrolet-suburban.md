# 2022 Chevrolet Suburban Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Chevrolet Suburban remains a top choice for large families seeking unmatched cargo and passenger space, but prospective buyers should approach used models with a discerning eye. While it offers versatile powertrain options and modern technology, owner complaints and technical bulletins highlight potential areas of concern—particularly involving engine driveability, electrical glitches, and safety restraint systems. Evaluating service records and ensuring outstanding updates have been addressed will be crucial for a smooth ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 5.3L V8 Engine

The standard 5.3-liter V8 engine produces approximately 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, providing capable towing and daily driving performance for this full-size SUV.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may unexpectedly stall while driving at highway speeds or experience a sudden loss of motive power without prior warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis by a qualified technician is required to identify faulty sensors, fuel delivery components, or internal mechanical wear.

### 6.2L V8 Engine

The optional 6.2-liter V8 engine delivers a robust 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, offering stronger acceleration and pulling power at the expense of fuel economy.

**Engine Failure and Propulsion Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported illuminated check engine lights, sudden entry into limp mode, or complete engine seizure.
- **Solution:** Requires professional inspection; severe mechanical failures may necessitate extensive engine repairs or complete replacement.

### 3.0L Duramax Turbo-Diesel I6

The 3.0-liter turbo-diesel inline-six generates 277 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, praised for its impressive fuel efficiency and low-end pulling strength.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission pairs across all engine choices, delivering smooth shifting and optimized gear ratios for highway cruising and towing.

**Transmission Malfunction and Limp Mode**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden illumination of warning lights, entry into limp mode, and loss of normal gear operation including reverse.
- **Solution:** Have the transmission control module scanned for diagnostic trouble codes and consult a certified powertrain specialist.

## Suspension

### Suspension Components

The suspension is engineered to provide a smooth, controlled ride for a large vehicle, though physical wear items should be routinely inspected during maintenance intervals.

**Ball Joint Separation**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected separation or failure of front suspension components such as the upper ball joint while driving at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect front suspension components for wear and replace damaged or compromised joints immediately.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Infotainment System

The electrical architecture supports extensive cabin tech and driver aids, though occasional software glitches and module failures have been documented by owners.

**Telematics and Instrument Cluster Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Failure of GPS and OnStar emergency services following software updates, alongside instrument clusters displaying distorted lines or blank screens.
- **Solution:** Software reflashing or module replacement by a dealership may be necessary to restore full functionality.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Seat Belts

Safety restraint systems are vital for occupant protection, but intermittent sensor warnings can cause frustration and require targeted wiring repairs.

**Service Safety Restraint System Warning**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent airbag warning lights and dashboard alerts indicating a fault in the safety restraint system.
- **Solution:** Inspection of vehicle wiring harness connections; dealerships have referenced technical service guidance regarding overly short wiring sections.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V617000: Seat Belts:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Suburban&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A seat belt with a deformed rivet may not properly restrain an occupant, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rivet head formation on both the left and right side third-row seat belt buckle assemblies and replace seat belt buckle assemblies, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V903000: Exterior Lighting:Lighting Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Suburban&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The daytime running lights (DRLs) may not deactivate when the headlights are on. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." DRLs that do not deactivate as intended may result in a glare, reducing visibility, and increasing the risk.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V274000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Suburban&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Engine failure increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the engine, and as necessary, repair or replace the engine. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V085000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Suburban&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Rear wheels that lock up while driving increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install new transmission control module (TCM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Chevrolet Suburban is a highly capable full-size SUV that excels in hauling people and gear, but buyers should exercise caution and verify vehicle history. While many owners enjoy trouble-free driving, reports concerning engine driveability, electrical bugs, and restraint system warnings underscore the importance of a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Prioritizing models with documented service records and verified updates will help mitigate unexpected repair expenses.
