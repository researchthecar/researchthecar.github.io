# 2024 Chevrolet Suburban Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used full-size SUV, the model provides generous passenger and cargo space alongside capable towing options. However, buyers should review owner-reported concerns regarding powertrain operations, electrical glitches, and body components before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 6.2L V8 (L87)

The 6.2-liter V8 engine produces approximately 420 horsepower and 460 lb-ft of torque, providing robust acceleration and heavy towing capability. Buyers should verify maintenance records and listen closely for unusual noises during test drives.

**Sudden Engine Seizure and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report complete and sudden engine failure while driving at highway speeds, resulting in a seized motor that will not restart.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnostic evaluation and potentially a complete engine replacement.

**Unexpected Engine Shutdown**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may suddenly shut off without warning while operating, while instrument panel lights remain illuminated.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic testing by a qualified technician to identify electrical or fuel delivery faults.

### 5.3L V8 (L84)

The standard 5.3-liter V8 delivers around 355 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, serving as the dependable workhorse for everyday driving and moderate hauling.

### 3.0L Duramax Turbo-Diesel I6

The 3.0-liter Duramax turbo-diesel offers high torque and excellent fuel economy for long road trips and frequent towing duties.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission is paired across the engine lineup to optimize fuel efficiency and power delivery, though some owners have experienced premature internal component failures.

**Internal Component Failure and Valve Body Issues**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission can fail prematurely, rendering the vehicle completely undrivable, often linked to faulty valve body components.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the defective valve body or transmission assembly, which may be subject to parts availability delays.

**Unintended Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly shift into neutral while driving or when applying the brakes, displaying prompts to press the start button again.
- **Solution:** Software updates or diagnostic inspection of the electronic shifter and transmission control module.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

Issues with electronic power assist can temporarily compromise steering control.

**Power Steering Loss and Wheel Lockup**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel may lock at an angle while driving at moderate speeds, accompanied by a 'Power Steering Not Active' warning message.
- **Solution:** Inspection of the electric power steering assist motor, wiring harness, and related control modules.

## Exterior

### Sunroof

Body glass and exterior fixtures generally hold up well, though occasional spontaneous sunroof breakage has been reported.

**Spontaneous Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The sunroof glass may suddenly shatter or explode outward while driving at highway speeds with no apparent impact from outside objects.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the sunroof glass assembly and clearing of any remaining glass debris.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Driver assistance features occasionally produce false positive alerts or unexpected autonomous braking events.

**Phantom Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** Cruise control or collision avoidance systems may suddenly slam on the brakes without any preceding vehicles or obstacles.
- **Solution:** Reprogramming or sensor recalibration by a dealership service center.

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V274000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Suburban&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Engine failure increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the engine, and as necessary, repair or replace the engine. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The vehicle remains a dominant choice for families needing expansive space and substantial towing capability. Buyers should thoroughly inspect the powertrain, test all driver assistance and electrical systems, and confirm that all open manufacturer recalls have been addressed.
