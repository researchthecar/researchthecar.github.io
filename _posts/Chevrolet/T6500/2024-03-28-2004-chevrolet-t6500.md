## 2004 Chevrolet T6500 Issues

The 2004 Chevrolet T6500 is a heavy-duty pickup truck with gasoline or diesel engine options and various transmission options. It offers impressive towing and hauling capabilities, making it popular for commercial and personal use.

Despite its rugged construction, the T6500 is not without issues. Here's a comprehensive list of the specific problems reported by owners, categorized into major components:

**Engine**

**6.0L Vortec V8**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** Leaks coolant, causing overheating and performance issues. Requires replacement of the gasket and associated components. **$1,500 - $2,500+**
* **Piston Ring Wear:** Excessive oil consumption and engine smoke. May require engine rebuild or replacement. **$5,000 - $10,000+**

**8.1L Vortec V8**

* **Valve Seat Recession:** Premature wear of valve seats, leading to reduced engine compression and performance. May require cylinder head replacement. **$3,000 - $5,000+**

**Transmission**

**4L80E 4-Speed Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Failure:** Vibration, shuddering, and loss of power. Requires replacement of the torque converter and transmission service. **$1,500 - $2,000+**

**6L90E 6-Speed Automatic**

* **Solenoid Failure:** Erratic gear shifting and transmission slipping. May require replacement of solenoids and transmission service. **$1,000 - $1,500+**

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Breakage:** Failure of the leaf springs, resulting in sagging or uneven ride height. Requires replacement of the affected leaf springs. **$500 - $1,200+**
* **Shock Absorber Failure:** Worn or damaged shock absorbers reduce ride comfort and stability. Requires replacement of the shocks. **$300 - $600+**

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Loss of charging power, causing battery drain. Requires replacement of the alternator. **$500 - $800+**
* **Starter Failure:** Difficulty starting the vehicle. May require replacement of the starter or related components. **$300 - $600+**
* ****Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust Prone:** Certain areas of the body, especially the undercarriage, are prone to rust. Requires regular rust prevention measures. **Varies based on severity**
* **Headlamp Condensation:** Moisture buildup inside the headlamps. May require resealing or replacement of the headlamps. **$200 - $500+**

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:** Formation of cracks on the dashboard. Requires replacement or repair of the dashboard. **$500 - $1,000+**
* **Air Conditioning Failure:** Loss of cooling or heating. May require repairs to the compressor, condenser, or other components. **$500 - $1,500+**

**Summary**

The 2004 Chevrolet T6500 is a capable heavy-duty pickup truck plagued by several significant issues that can affect its reliability and performance. Some of these issues, particularly with the engine and transmission, can be expensive to repair. It's essential for potential owners to be aware of these problems and factor in the potential costs of maintenance and repairs.
