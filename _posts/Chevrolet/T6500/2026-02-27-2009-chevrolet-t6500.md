# 2009 Chevrolet T6500

### Overview
The 2009 Chevrolet T6500, a commercial truck designed for medium-duty applications, generally exhibits a robust build suitable for its intended purpose. Reliability data suggests that while the core mechanical components are sound, specific areas may require attention over time. Owners and fleet managers should be aware of potential issues related to exhaust systems, particularly the Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) on certain diesel engines, and occasional electrical gremlins. The transmission options, while generally dependable, can also present isolated problems. Overall, the T6500 is considered a workhorse, but proactive maintenance and awareness of common problem areas are key to maximizing its lifespan and minimizing unexpected downtime.

## Engine

### 5.2L Duramax Diesel (LG4)
**DPF Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine light illumination, reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption, regeneration failures.
- **Solution:** Forced regeneration, DPF cleaning, or replacement of the DPF unit.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2500

**EGR System Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, black smoke from exhaust, reduced power, Check Engine light.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of EGR valve and cooler.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1200

## Transmission

### Allison 1000 (Automatic)
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, shuddering during acceleration, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $3000

**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible fluid leaks, low transmission fluid level, potential overheating.
- **Solution:** Inspection and repair of seals, gaskets, or cooler lines.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2009 Chevrolet T6500 is a capable commercial vehicle with a generally solid reliability record. The Duramax diesel engine, while powerful, can be susceptible to DPF and EGR system issues, which are common in many modern diesel applications and require specific maintenance. The gasoline engine option is typically more straightforward. Automatic transmissions are generally robust, though torque converter and fluid leak issues can arise. Electrical components, particularly the alternator and wiring, are areas to monitor. For its class, the T6500 offers a reasonable balance of performance and durability, with most reported issues being manageable with proper servicing and timely repairs.
