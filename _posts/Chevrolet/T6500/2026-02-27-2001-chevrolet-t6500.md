# 2001 Chevrolet T6500

### Overview
The 2001 Chevrolet T6500, a commercial truck designed for medium-duty applications, generally exhibits a robust build suitable for its intended purpose. Reliability is a key factor for fleet operators, and the T6500, while not without its potential issues, offers a generally dependable platform. This analysis focuses on common technical concerns reported by owners and technicians, excluding routine maintenance. Understanding these specific points can help in proactive maintenance and informed purchasing decisions for this workhorse vehicle.

---


## Engine

### 7.2L Caterpillar 3126B Diesel
**Injector Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, loss of power, engine stalling, black smoke.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the fuel injection pump.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, excessive smoke, whistling noises from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger unit.
- **Cost:** $1,000 - $2,000


### 7.2L Caterpillar 3126E Diesel
**EGR Valve Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light, rough idle, reduced engine performance, increased emissions.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of the Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) valve.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Transmission

### Allison 2000 Series Automatic
**Torque Converter Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, shuddering during acceleration, delayed engagement.
- **Solution:** Rebuild or replacement of the torque converter.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,800


### Allison 3000 Series Automatic
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Low transmission fluid level, difficulty shifting, overheating.
- **Solution:** Inspection and repair of seals, lines, or transmission pan gasket.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600


## Suspension

### Standard Leaf Spring Suspension
**Leaf Spring Breakage**
- **Symptoms:** Sagging rear end, uneven ride height, clunking noises over bumps.
- **Solution:** Replacement of broken leaf springs.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, electrical system malfunctions.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Wiring Harness Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical issues, non-functioning lights or accessories, corrosion on connectors.
- **Solution:** Cleaning and sealing of corroded connectors, or replacement of affected harness sections.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


## Exterior

### Standard Body Panels
**Rusting**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust formation, particularly on wheel arches, frame rails, and cab corners.
- **Solution:** Rust treatment, panel repair, or replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $1,000+


## Interior

### Standard Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, or intermittent airflow.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2001 Chevrolet T6500 is a capable commercial vehicle with a generally solid mechanical foundation. The most common areas for concern revolve around the diesel engines, particularly the fuel injection system and turbochargers, and the automatic transmissions, with torque converter issues being notable. Suspension components like leaf springs can wear and break under heavy use. Electrical issues, such as alternator failure and wiring corrosion, are also reported. Exterior rust can be a concern depending on climate and maintenance. Overall, with diligent maintenance and addressing specific known issues, the T6500 can remain a reliable asset for commercial operations.
