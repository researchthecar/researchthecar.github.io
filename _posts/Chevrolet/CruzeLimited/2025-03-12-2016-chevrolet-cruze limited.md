The 2016 Chevrolet Cruze Limited is a compact sedan known for its fuel efficiency and comfortable ride. It was available with a 1.4L turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing 138 horsepower and 148 lb-ft of torque. This engine was paired with either a 6-speed manual or a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the Cruze Limited for its gas mileage and quiet cabin, but some common complaints include issues with the emissions system, the infotainment system, and certain engine components. Reliability can be a concern for some owners.

**Engine**

**1.4L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder (LUJ/LUV)**

*   **PCV Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The PCV valve diaphragm in the valve cover can crack or tear, leading to a vacuum leak. This can cause rough idling, poor fuel economy, and a check engine light with codes related to lean conditions (e.g., P0171). A whistling noise may also be heard coming from the engine.
    *   Solution: Replace the valve cover assembly, as the PCV valve is often integrated into it.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the turbocharger, including boost leaks, excessive noise, and complete failure. Symptoms include decreased acceleration, a whining sound from the engine, and a check engine light.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks can occur from various points in the cooling system, including the water outlet, thermostat housing, and radiator hoses. This can lead to overheating and engine damage.
    *   Solution: Identify and repair the source of the leak by replacing the faulty component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the location and severity of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic (6T40)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing harsh or delayed shifting, as well as slipping between gears. This can be caused by worn or damaged internal components, such as valve body issues, or low transmission fluid.
    *   Solution: A transmission fluid flush, valve body repair, or complete transmission replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (fluid flush) to $3000+ (transmission replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The MyLink infotainment system can experience issues such as freezing, slow response times, Bluetooth connectivity problems, and screen malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Software updates may resolve some issues. In other cases, the infotainment unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+ depending on the severity and solution.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing a parasitic battery drain that can leave the car unable to start. This can be caused by a faulty module or a wiring issue.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the source of the battery drain and repair or replace the faulty component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the source of the drain.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Chevrolet Cruze Limited has several potential issues owners may encounter, ranging from relatively minor electrical glitches to more serious engine and transmission problems. PCV valve failure and coolant leaks are common concerns, while turbocharger issues and transmission problems can be more costly to repair. Infotainment system problems are also a frequent source of frustration. Overall, the severity and cost of these issues can vary, but potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems and have the vehicle thoroughly inspected before purchasing.
