**Introduction**
The 2006 Chevrolet Zafira is a compact MPV available with several engine and transmission options. The 1.6-liter ECOTEC I4 gasoline engine generates 105 horsepower and 150 Nm of torque, while the 1.8-liter ECOTEC I4 gasoline engine produces 140 horsepower and 175 Nm of torque. Transmission choices include a 5-speed manual, a 4-speed automatic, and a 6-speed automatic. The Zafira is praised for its spacious interior, versatility, and affordability. However, it has also received criticism for its unreliability and subpar build quality.

**Engine Issues**

**1.6-liter ECOTEC I4 Gasoline Engine**
- **Intake Manifold Runner Failure:** Failure of the intake manifold runners leads to decreased engine performance, stalling, and difficulty starting. The faulty manifold may need to be replaced.
- **Camshaft Sensor Failure:** A faulty camshaft sensor can cause the engine to run rough, stall, or not start at all. The sensor may need to be replaced.
- **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** The timing chain tensioner can fail, leading to a rattling noise from the engine. The tensioner and timing chain may need to be replaced.

**Transmission Issues**

**5-speed Manual Transmission**
- **Shifting Problems:** Difficulty shifting gears may indicate worn or damaged synchronizers or shift forks. The transmission may need to be rebuilt or replaced.

**4-speed Automatic Transmission**
- **Slipping Gears:** The transmission may slip out of gear, especially under load. The issue may be caused by worn clutch packs or a faulty solenoid. The transmission may need to be rebuilt or replaced.

**6-speed Automatic Transmission**
- **Shift Delay:** The transmission may experience hesitation or delay when shifting gears. The issue may be caused by faulty solenoids or a malfunctioning torque converter. The transmission may need to be serviced or replaced.

**Electrical Issues**

- **Alternator Failure:** The alternator may fail, causing the battery to discharge and the vehicle to lose power. The alternator may need to be replaced.
- **Battery Drain:** A parasitic drain on the battery can cause the battery to discharge even when the vehicle is turned off. The faulty component that is causing the drain may need to be identified and replaced.
- **Faulty Wiring Harness:** Electrical problems, such as intermittent lights, may be caused by a faulty wiring harness. The wiring harness may need to be repaired or replaced.

**Interior Issues**

- **Climate Control Malfunction:** The climate control system may fail to regulate temperature or air flow. The faulty component may need to be diagnosed and replaced.
- **Power Window Failure:** Power windows may fail to operate or become stuck. The window motor or switch may need to be replaced.
- **Dashboard Rattle:** The dashboard may rattle or make noise while driving. The issue may be caused by loose components or a faulty mounting bracket. The dashboard may need to be disassembled and the loose components tightened or repaired.

**Summary**

The 2006 Chevrolet Zafira can experience a variety of issues, ranging from minor inconveniences to major mechanical failures. The cost of repairs can vary significantly depending on the severity of the issue and the cost of labor in the local area. Engine problems can be costly, especially if the timing chain tensioner fails. Transmission issues can also be expensive, particularly if the transmission requires rebuilding or replacement. While some electrical issues can be minor and inexpensive to fix, others may require extensive troubleshooting and repairs. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues before purchasing a Zafira and factor these into their decision-making.
