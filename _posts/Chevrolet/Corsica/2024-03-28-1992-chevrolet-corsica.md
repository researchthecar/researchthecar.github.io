**Introduction:**

The 1992 Chevrolet Corsica is a mid-size sedan that came with a choice of two engine and transmission options:

- 2.2L inline-4 engine (110 hp, 145 lb-ft torque) with 3-speed automatic transmission
- 3.1L V6 engine (140 hp, 185 lb-ft torque) with 4-speed automatic transmission

The Corsica was praised for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and affordable price. However, it was also criticized for its lackluster performance and poor fuel economy.

**Engine**

- **2.2L Inline-4 Engine**

    - **Oil leaks:** Oil leaks are a common problem on the 2.2L inline-4 engine. The oil pan gasket is known to fail, causing oil to leak out of the engine.
        - Solution: Replace the oil pan gasket.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
        - Recalls: None Found
    - **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail on the 2.2L inline-4 engine, causing a loss of power and fuel efficiency.
        - Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400
        - Recalls: None Found

- **3.1L V6 Engine**

    - **Valve cover gasket leaks:** Valve cover gasket leaks are a common problem on the 3.1L V6 engine. The gaskets can fail, causing oil to leak out of the engine.
        - Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $350
        - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

- **3-Speed Automatic Transmission**

    - **Slipping or shuddering:** The 3-speed automatic transmission is known to slip or shudder when shifting gears. This is often caused by a worn-out torque converter or transmission fluid that is low or dirty.
        - Solution: Replace the torque converter or transmission fluid.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
        - Recalls: None Found

- **4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

    - **No issues found.**

**Suspension**

    - **Front strut mounts:** The front strut mounts can wear out on the Corsica, causing the struts to make noise and the vehicle to handle poorly.
        - Solution: Replace the front strut mounts.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
        - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

    - **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail on the Corsica, causing the battery to not charge and the vehicle to stall.
        - Solution: Replace the alternator.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
        - Recalls: None Found
    - **Power window failure:** The power windows can fail on the Corsica, preventing them from being opened or closed.
        - Solution: Replace the power window motor or switch.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400
        - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

    - **Door handles:** The door handles can break on the Corsica, making it difficult to open the doors.
        - Solution: Replace the door handles.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
        - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

    - **Dashboard cracking:** The dashboard can crack on the Corsica, especially in hot climates.
        - Solution: Replace the dashboard.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        - Recalls: None Found
    - **Seat foam failure:** The seat foam can fail on the Corsica, causing the seats to become uncomfortable.
        - Solution: Replace the seat foam.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
        - Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1992 Chevrolet Corsica is a reliable and affordable mid-size sedan. However, it does have some common issues that owners should be aware of. The most serious issues include oil leaks, intake manifold gasket failure, and transmission problems. The estimated cost to fix these issues ranges from $150 to $1,500.
