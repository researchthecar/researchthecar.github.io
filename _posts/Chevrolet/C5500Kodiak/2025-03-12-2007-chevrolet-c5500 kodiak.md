The 2007 Chevrolet C5500 Kodiak is a medium-duty truck designed for commercial use, offering a robust platform for various applications like towing, hauling, and utility work. Engine options include the Duramax 6.6L diesel V8 and the 8.1L Vortec gasoline V8. The Duramax 6.6L produces around 330 horsepower and 620 lb-ft of torque. Transmission choices primarily consist of Allison automatic transmissions. Owners often praise the Kodiak's high towing capacity and durability, but some dislike its fuel economy and cumbersome size.

**Engine**

**Duramax 6.6L Diesel V8 (LMM)**

*   **Fuel Injector Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough running, misfires, white smoke, decreased fuel economy, and potential engine damage due to improper fuel delivery.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty fuel injectors with updated versions. Recalibrate the ECM.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **EGR Cooler Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant loss, white smoke, engine overheating. The EGR cooler can crack and leak coolant into the exhaust system.
    *   Solution: Replace the EGR cooler.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke, unusual noises from the turbocharger.
    *   Solution: Replace the turbocharger. Check and clean oil supply lines to the turbo.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**8.1L Vortec Gasoline V8**

*   **Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, often requiring frequent topping off of oil levels. May be related to valve stem seals or piston rings.
    *   Solution: Depending on the cause, solutions can range from replacing valve stem seals to a complete engine rebuild or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 (valve stem seals) - $5,000+ (engine rebuild/replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Exhaust Manifold Bolts Breaking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Exhaust leak noise, decreased fuel economy. The bolts securing the exhaust manifold can corrode and break, leading to leaks.
    *   Solution: Replace broken exhaust manifold bolts. May require manifold replacement if the surface is warped.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Allison 1000 Series Automatic Transmission**

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering during acceleration, slipping between gears, overheating transmission.
    *   Solution: Replace the torque converter. Check and flush the transmission cooler.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Shift Solenoid Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, hard shifts, or failure to shift. Can trigger transmission fault codes.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty shift solenoids. Inspect wiring harness for damage.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Leaf Spring Sagging/Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced ride height, poor ride quality, vehicle leaning to one side. Leaf springs can weaken or crack over time, especially with heavy loads.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaf springs. Consider upgrading to heavier-duty springs if the vehicle is consistently used for heavy hauling.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ per axle
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Ball Joint Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the front end, loose steering, uneven tire wear. Ball joints can wear out due to age and heavy use.
    *   Solution: Replace the worn ball joints.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per side
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Wiring Harness Corrosion**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, including sensor failures, lighting issues, and starting problems. Corrosion can occur, especially in areas exposed to road salt and moisture.
    *   Solution: Inspect and repair damaged wiring harnesses. Clean corroded connectors. Use dielectric grease to prevent future corrosion.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+ (depending on the severity and location of the damage)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Glow Plug Failure (Diesel Only)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hard starting in cold weather, excessive white smoke on startup, engine misfires.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty glow plugs. Check glow plug relay and wiring.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Cab Mount Corrosion**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Squeaking noises from the cab, visible rust or damage to the cab mounts.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace corroded cab mounts. Treat surrounding areas to prevent further rust.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: No air blowing from the vents, weak airflow, or noisy blower motor.
    *   Solution: Replace the HVAC blower motor. Check the blower motor resistor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2007 Chevrolet C5500 Kodiak, while a capable work truck, can suffer from several common issues related to its engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical systems. The Duramax diesel engine is prone to fuel injector and EGR cooler failures, while the 8.1L Vortec gasoline engine may experience oil consumption and exhaust manifold bolt breakage. The Allison transmission can have torque converter and shift solenoid problems. Suspension issues include leaf spring sagging and ball joint failure. Electrical problems are often related to wiring harness corrosion. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to major repairs, with costs varying accordingly. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems and thoroughly inspect any used C5500 Kodiak before purchasing. Addressing these issues promptly can help maintain the vehicle's reliability and longevity.
