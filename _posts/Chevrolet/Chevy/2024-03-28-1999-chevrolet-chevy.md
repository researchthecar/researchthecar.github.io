**Introduction**

The 1999 Chevrolet Chevy is a full-size pickup truck that was available with a variety of engine and transmission options. The most popular engine was the 5.7L V8, which produced 255 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options included a 4.3L V6, a 6.0L V8, and a 7.4L V8. Transmission options included a 5-speed manual, a 4-speed automatic, and a 6-speed automatic.

People who like the 1999 Chevrolet Chevy praise its powerful engines, spacious interior, and comfortable ride. However, some people complain about its poor fuel economy and lack of modern features.

**Engine**

**5.7L V8**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaks from the intake manifold, engine overheating, loss of coolant
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Valve lifter failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Tapping or clicking noises from the engine, loss of power, engine misfire
    * Solution: Replace the valve lifters
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Piston ring failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, blue smoke from the exhaust, loss of power
    * Solution: Overhaul the engine
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**4.3L V6**

* **Timing chain failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling or grinding noises from the engine, loss of power, engine misfire
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain and gears
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Head gasket failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaks from the head gasket, engine overheating, loss of coolant
    * Solution: Replace the head gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Intake manifold runner control failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, engine misfire, rough idle
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold runner control
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Summary**

The 1999 Chevrolet Chevy is a reliable and capable pickup truck. However, it is not without its issues. Some of the most common problems owners experience include engine intake manifold gasket failure, valve lifter failure, transmission shifting problems, ball joint failure, and alternator failure. The severity of these issues varies, but some can be expensive to fix. Overall, the 1999 Chevrolet Chevy is a good choice for people who need a powerful and spacious pickup truck. However, potential buyers should be aware of the common problems associated with this vehicle before making a purchase.
