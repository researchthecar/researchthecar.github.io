## 2016 Chevrolet SS

The 2016 Chevrolet SS is a high-performance sedan that was offered with a single engine and transmission option. The 6.2L LS3 V8 engine produces 415 horsepower and 415 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed manual transmission. The SS was praised for its powerful engine, comfortable interior, and sporty handling. However, some owners have reported various issues with their vehicles.

**Engine**

- **6.2L LS3 V8 Engine**
    - **Rod Bearing Failure**: The rod bearings in the LS3 engine can fail prematurely, leading to catastrophic engine damage. Symptoms include knocking sounds, reduced engine power, and seized pistons. The solution is to replace the affected rod bearings. Estimated cost to fix: $5,000 - $10,000+.
    - **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak**: The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing a vacuum leak and reduced engine performance. Symptoms include poor idle, reduced fuel efficiency, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000.

**Transmission**

- **6-Speed Manual Transmission**
    - **Clutch Failure**: The clutch can wear out prematurely, leading to slipping or difficulty shifting gears. Symptoms include difficulty engaging or disengaging gears, grinding noises, and a burning smell. The solution is to replace the clutch. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500 - $2,500.
    - **Transmission Gear Grinding**: The gears in the transmission can grind when shifting, indicating worn synchros or other internal issues. Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, loud grinding noises, and a check engine light. The solution is to rebuild or replace the transmission. Estimated cost to fix: $2,000 - $5,000+.

**Suspension**

- **Front Lower Control Arms**: The front lower control arms can develop cracks or become damaged, affecting the vehicle's alignment and handling. Symptoms include poor handling, uneven tire wear, and a rattling noise. The solution is to replace the affected control arms. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+.

**Electrical**

- **Battery Discharge**: The battery can discharge prematurely, leaving the vehicle unable to start. Symptoms include difficulty starting, reduced electrical power, and a dead battery. The solution is to replace the battery and diagnose the underlying cause of the discharge. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500+.

**Exterior**

- **Cracked Windshield**: The windshield can crack or chip easily due to its large size and thin construction. Symptoms include a compromised view, reduced structural integrity, and potential injuries. The solution is to replace the windshield. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+.

**Interior**

- **Infotainment System Glitches**: The infotainment system can experience various glitches, including freezing, unresponsive touchscreens, and audio issues. The solution is to update or replace the infotainment system. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,500+.

**Summary**

While the 2016 Chevrolet SS offers a thrilling driving experience, it is not without its issues. The engine and transmission components have been known to experience severe and expensive problems. Suspension and electrical faults can also arise, along with miscellaneous exterior and interior concerns. Before committing to a used SS, it is crucial to carefully consider the potential costs associated with these issues.
