Okay, here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints associated with the 2006 Chevrolet C6500 Kodiak, formatted for your website.  The 2006 Chevrolet C6500 Kodiak is a medium-duty truck known for its hauling capability and commercial applications. Engine options included the Duramax 6.6L diesel (300hp/520lb-ft torque) and the Vortec 8.1L gasoline engine.  Transmission options were typically Allison automatics. Owners often appreciate the Kodiak's robust build and towing capacity but sometimes find the ride quality and fuel economy to be less desirable. The information below is based on reported issues and repair data available for this model year.

**Engine**

**6.6L Duramax Diesel (LLY)**

*   **Injector Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough running, white smoke from exhaust, decreased fuel economy, engine knocking. Can lead to engine damage if unaddressed. Often linked to overheating and fuel quality.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty injectors. Verify proper cooling system function.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $4000+ (depending on number of injectors and shop labor rates)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Overheating**
    *   Description/Symptoms: High coolant temperature gauge reading, coolant loss, steam from the engine compartment. Can cause head gasket failure and engine damage. Often caused by plugged radiators or a faulty fan clutch.
    *   Solution: Inspect and clean or replace the radiator. Test and replace the fan clutch if necessary. Pressure test the cooling system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive black smoke, whistling sound from the turbocharger.
    *   Solution: Replace the turbocharger. Inspect oil supply lines to the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**8.1L Vortec Gasoline**

*   **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leak, rough idle, engine misfires, possible overheating.
    *   Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Exhaust Manifold Bolt Breakage**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Exhaust leak, ticking noise from the engine, reduced fuel economy.
    *   Solution: Replace broken exhaust manifold bolts. May require manifold replacement if bolts are seized.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Allison Automatic (1000/2000 Series)**

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering during acceleration, slippage between gears, transmission overheating.
    *   Solution: Replace the torque converter. Flush the transmission cooler lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $4000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Shift Solenoid Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, harsh shifting, failure to shift into certain gears, trouble codes related to shift solenoids.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty shift solenoids.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Internal Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Leakage of the transmission fluid
    *   Solution: Replace seals, inspect for cracks in the transmission
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Leaf Spring Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, rough ride, clunking noises from the suspension, visible cracks or breaks in the leaf springs.
    *   Solution: Replace damaged leaf springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+ per spring
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Worn Ball Joints/Tie Rod Ends**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loose steering, wandering on the road, tire wear, clunking noises from the front end.
    *   Solution: Replace worn ball joints and/or tie rod ends. Get a wheel alignment.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per side
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Glow Plug Issues (Diesel)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hard starting in cold weather, excessive smoke on startup. Often related to faulty glow plugs or the glow plug control module.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty glow plugs or the glow plug control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Wiring Harness Chafing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical problems, check engine lights, sensor malfunctions.
    *   Solution: Inspect and repair chafed wiring harnesses. Protect harnesses from further damage.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (depending on the severity and location)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust (Frame/Body)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Corrosion on the frame and body panels, particularly in areas exposed to road salt.
    *   Solution: Treat rust with rust converter, repair or replace rusted panels. Apply rust prevention coatings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $5000+ (depending on severity and extent of rust)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Door Handle Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The door handle won't open the door.
    *   Solution: Replace the broken door handle.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ (depending on the severity and location)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: No air blowing from the vents, weak airflow, unusual noises from the blower motor.
    *   Solution: Replace the blower motor. Inspect the blower motor resistor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2006 Chevrolet C6500 Kodiak, while a capable truck, can experience several issues, particularly related to its diesel engine, transmission, and suspension. The most severe and costly problems typically involve injector failure, turbocharger problems, and transmission issues. Rust is also a significant concern, particularly in areas with harsh winters. While many of these issues can be addressed, potential buyers should be aware of these potential maintenance costs when considering this model.
