**Introduction:**

The 2007 Chevrolet C6500 Kodiak is a heavy-duty pickup truck that offers impressive towing and hauling capabilities. It is available with various engine and transmission options, including a 6.6L Duramax Diesel engine with 350 horsepower and 660 lb-ft of torque. The Kodiak boasts spacious interiors with comfortable seating, while its exterior features a robust and utilitarian design. However, like any vehicle, it has its share of reported issues.

**Engine**

**6.6L Duramax Diesel Engine**

* **Cracked Cylinder Heads:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking coolant, overheating, white smoke from exhaust
    * Solution: Replacement of cylinder heads
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
* **Injector Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hard starting, rough idle, loss of power
    * Solution: Replacement of injectors
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 14V-267

**Transmission**

**Allison 1000 6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Overheating:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slipping gears, shuddering, burning smell
    * Solution: Cooler upgrade, fluid change
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Torque Converter Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slipping gears, shuddering, loss of power
    * Solution: Replacement of torque converter
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 14V-224

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Breakage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, noise and vibration
    * Solution: Replacement of leaf springs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Shock Absorber Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Poor ride quality, handling issues
    * Solution: Replacement of shock absorbers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, battery not charging
    * Solution: Replacement of alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dead battery after short periods of inactivity
    * Solution: Identification and repair of parasitic draw
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Rear Bumper Corrosion:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Corrosion and holes on the rear bumper
    * Solution: Replacement of rear bumper
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Paint Peeling (Hood)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Flaking and peeling paint on the hood
    * Solution: Repainting of the hood
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks and splits on the dashboard panel
    * Solution: Replacement or repair of dashboard
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Door Lock Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inability to lock or unlock doors using power locks
    * Solution: Replacement of door lock actuators
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Summary**

Overall, the 2007 Chevrolet C6500 Kodiak has received positive feedback for its durability, towing capacity, and spacious interiors. However, like any vehicle, it is not immune to issues. The reported problems are mainly related to the engine, transmission, and suspension. While some of these issues can be costly to fix, such as cracked cylinder heads or transmission failure, others are more minor and affordable. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and factor them into their purchasing decision.
