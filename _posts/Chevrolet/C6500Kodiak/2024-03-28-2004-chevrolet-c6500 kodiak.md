**Introduction**

The 2004 Chevrolet C6500 Kodiak is a heavy-duty truck known for its robust construction and powerful Duramax diesel engine. It offers various engine and transmission options, including a 7.8L V8 Duramax diesel engine with 300 horsepower and 660 lb-ft of torque, paired with an Allison 6-speed automatic transmission. Other available engines include a 5.2L V8 gasoline engine and a 6.6L V8 Duramax diesel engine.

**Engine**

* **7.8L V8 Duramax Diesel Engine**
    * **Fuel Injector Failure:** Worn or leaky fuel injectors can cause poor engine performance, decreased fuel economy, and engine misfires.
        * Solution: Replace faulty fuel injectors.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **5.2L V8 Gasoline Engine**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** A faulty intake manifold gasket can lead to coolant or oil leaks, engine overheating, and performance issues.
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **Allison 6-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Torque Converter Clutch Failure:** A worn or malfunctioning torque converter clutch can cause shuddering, slipping, or difficulty shifting.
        * Solution: Replace the torque converter clutch.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Manual Transmissions:**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension:**
    * **Ball Joint Failure:** Worn or damaged ball joints can lead to clunking noises, uneven tire wear, and decreased handling.
        * Solution: Replace failing ball joints.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 (per joint)
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Suspension:**
    * **Leaf Spring Breakage:** Heavy loads or rough use can cause leaf springs to break, leading to a saggy rear end and reduced stability.
        * Solution: Replace broken leaf springs.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Premature Failure:** Some models may experience premature battery failure due to manufacturing defects or excessive electrical load.
        * Solution: Replace the faulty battery.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator Failure:** A faulty alternator can fail to properly charge the battery, leading to electrical issues or vehicle stalling.
        * Solution: Replace the alternator.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Frame and Body:** Some models may exhibit premature rust or corrosion on the frame and body panels.
        * Solution: Repair or replace rusted areas as needed.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity of rust
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Cruise Control Switch Failure:** The cruise control switch may fail, making it impossible to engage or disengage the cruise control feature.
        * Solution: Replace the faulty switch.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2004 Chevrolet C6500 Kodiak is a reliable and capable truck. However, it may experience certain issues, including fuel injector failure on the 7.8L Duramax engine, torque converter clutch failure on the Allison transmission, ball joint failure in the front suspension, and premature electrical component failures. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the severity of the problem and the specific parts that need to be replaced. It's recommended to have regular inspections and maintenance to address any potential issues early on.
