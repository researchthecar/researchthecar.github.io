# 2016 Chevrolet Spark EV Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Chevrolet Spark EV offers an efficient and punchy electric hatchback ride, making it an attractive option for budget-conscious city commuters. However, prospective buyers should review potential trouble spots, particularly concerning intermittent brake assist failures, electrical anomalies, and airbag warning systems.

---

## Engine

### Permanent Magnet Electric Motor

Powered by a permanent magnet synchronous electric motor producing 140 horsepower and an impressive 327 lb-ft of torque, the Spark EV delivers snappy acceleration. While generally robust, electrical and high-voltage component failures can occasionally cause unexpected shutdowns.

**Unexpected Vehicle Shutdown**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle suddenly shuts down while driving on major roads without any prior warning lights or symptoms.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle's high-voltage battery management and drive inverter systems inspected by a certified EV technician.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct Drive Transmission

The single-speed coaxial planetary gearbox transfers power smoothly to the front wheels. It is mechanically simple compared to traditional gearboxes, though software updates and module checks remain critical for reliability.

**Transmission Control Module and Power Interruption**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle stalls or shuts off in traffic following transmission control module updates or experiences sudden drive system faults.
- **Solution:** Verify that all open transmission control module updates have been properly performed by a Chevrolet dealer, and inspect wiring harnesses.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes and Brake Assist

The braking system integrates regenerative braking with conventional hydraulic components. Owners have reported intermittent loss of brake boost and warning lights related to stability and brake assist.

**Brake Assist and StabiliTrak Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illumination of 'Service StabiliTrak' and 'Service Brake Assist' warning lights accompanied by a sudden loss of power brake assist, requiring heavy pedal effort.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the electronic brake control module and replace failing hydraulic pumps or sensors as indicated by diagnostic trouble codes.

**Intermittent Brake Stuttering and Loss of Stopping Power**
- **Symptoms:** Random brake stuttering or severe reduction in braking efficiency, potentially leading to extended stopping distances.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pressure sensors, anti-lock braking system (ABS) modules, and regenerative braking actuators.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Instrument Cluster

Electrical architecture controls the vehicle's infotainment, gauges, and accessory systems. Screen blackouts and intermittent accessory power issues have been documented by owners.

**Instrument Panel and Screen Blackout**
- **Symptoms:** The entertainment screen and instrument panel lighting fail intermittently while driving without prior warning.
- **Solution:** Inspect the body control module (BCM), display fuses, and internal wiring connections for loose or corroded pins.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

The safety restraint system includes multiple airbags, though occasional electrical faults like the Code 95 warning may require module or sensor replacements.

**Code 95 Air Bag Error Message**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard displays a Code 95 error message, indicating a fault within the Supplemental Restraint System (SRS).
- **Solution:** Have a dealership or qualified technician diagnose the airbag module wiring harness or replace faulty passenger airbag modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V651000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Spark+EV&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test. During this test, deployment of the frontal air bags and the seat belt pretensioners would not occur in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V437000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Chevrolet&model=Spark+EV&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** While being previously remedied for recall 16V-651, the affected vehicles may not have received the complete software update necessary to remedy the recall condition. Without the update, certain driving conditions may cause the air bag sensing and diagnostic module (SDM) software to activate a diagnostic test.
- **What to do:** GM will notify owners, and dealers will reflash the SDM software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Chevrolet Spark EV is a fun and economical city runabout, but buyers should conduct a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the braking system, electrical accessories, and any history of warning lights. Ensuring that all relevant software updates have been completed will help mitigate potential drivability and safety concerns.
