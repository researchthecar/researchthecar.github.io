# 2017 Chevrolet LCF 3500

### Overview
The 2017 Chevrolet LCF 3500 is a commercial truck chassis designed for various upfitting needs, often used for delivery vans, shuttle buses, and specialized work vehicles. As a platform vehicle, its reliability is heavily influenced by the specific powertrain and the quality of the upfit. Generally, the LCF 3500 is built for durability and heavy-duty use. However, like any vehicle, certain components can experience issues. This analysis focuses on documented common problems reported by owners and technicians, excluding routine maintenance and normal wear and tear. The data aims to provide a clear picture of potential reliability concerns for prospective buyers and fleet managers.

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Chevrolet LCF 3500 generally presents as a robust platform for commercial applications. The gasoline 6.0L Vortec V8 engine and the 6-speed automatic transmissions (6L90 and Allison 1000) show good reliability with no widespread issues reported. The Duramax 6.6L diesel engine, while powerful, is more prone to specific issues like Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) and Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) system problems, which are common in many diesel vehicles of this era and can incur moderate repair costs. Electrical systems can occasionally exhibit intermittent power issues, requiring diagnostic attention. Suspension, exterior, and interior components are largely free of significant widespread complaints. Overall, the LCF 3500 is a dependable chassis, with the diesel powertrain requiring more vigilance regarding emissions-related components.
