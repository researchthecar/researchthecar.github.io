**Introduction**

The 2007 Chevrolet Silverado 2500 HD Classic is a heavy-duty pickup truck that was available with a variety of engine and transmission options. The base engine was a 6.0-liter gasoline V8 that produced 300 horsepower and 360 lb-ft of torque. A 6.6-liter Duramax turbodiesel V8 was also available, producing 360 horsepower and 650 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options included a 6-speed manual, a 5-speed automatic, and a 6-speed automatic.

The Silverado 2500 HD Classic was praised for its strong towing and hauling capabilities, as well as its comfortable interior. However, it was also criticized for its poor fuel economy and rough ride.

**Engine**

**6.0-Liter Gasoline V8**

* **Intake Manifold Runner Control Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold runner control system fails, causing a loss of power and decreased fuel economy.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold runner control system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Exhaust Manifold Bolt Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The bolts that hold the exhaust manifold to the engine can break, causing an exhaust leak.
    * Solution: Replace the exhaust manifold bolts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6.6-Liter Duramax Turbodiesel V8**

* **Turbocharger Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The turbocharger can fail, causing a loss of power and increased fuel consumption.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 10V512000

* **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) System Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: The DPF system can become clogged, causing a loss of power and decreased fuel economy.
    * Solution: Clean or replace the DPF system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2007 Chevrolet Silverado 2500 HD Classic has a number of potential issues that can be costly to repair. Some of the most severe and expensive issues include turbocharger failure, transmission slippage, and wiring harness damage. However, many of the issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, such as intake manifold runner control failure, exhaust manifold bolt failure, and door handle failure. Overall, the Silverado 2500 HD Classic is a reliable vehicle with a good track record, but it is important to be aware of the potential issues that can arise.
