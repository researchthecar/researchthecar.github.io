# 2022 McLaren Artura Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 McLaren Artura introduces an advanced high-performance hybrid powertrain combining a twin-turbocharged V6 with an electric motor. As a relatively new and complex exotic supercar, prospective buyers should approach used models with careful inspection and an understanding of its sophisticated hybrid architecture.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged V6 Hybrid

The 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 paired with an axial flux electric motor produces a combined 671 horsepower and 531 lb-ft of torque. This lightweight internal combustion engine delivers high-revving performance, though its intricate hybrid plumbing and electronics require specialist maintenance.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission

An 8-speed dual-clutch transmission manages power delivery to the rear wheels while omitting a traditional mechanical reverse gear to save weight. It provides rapid gear shifts suited for track and street driving, though long-term durability relies heavily on proper software calibration.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V216000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=McLaren&model=Artura&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A rear wheel locking condition may cause the driver to lose control of the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and correct the brake line connections as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 McLaren Artura offers cutting-edge hybrid supercar performance, but its complex powertrain and technology mean used buyers should prioritize vehicles with documented dealer service history and remaining warranty coverage where applicable.
