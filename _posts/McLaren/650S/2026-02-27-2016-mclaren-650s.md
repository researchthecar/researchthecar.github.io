# 2016 McLaren 650S

### Overview
The 2016 McLaren 650S, a potent supercar, generally exhibits strong mechanical and electrical reliability, reflecting its high-performance engineering. While exotic cars inherently require specialized maintenance and can incur higher repair costs than mainstream vehicles, the 650S has a reputation for robust construction. Owners typically report satisfaction with its performance and build quality. However, as with any high-performance vehicle, potential owners should be aware of the specific components that may require attention and the associated costs for upkeep and potential repairs. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond routine maintenance.

### Proactive Control Chassis
**Hydraulic Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Potential for fluid leaks from hydraulic lines or actuators, leading to reduced suspension performance or stiffness.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of leaking seals, hoses, or actuators. System flush and refill may be required.

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 McLaren 650S demonstrates a commendable level of reliability for a supercar, with its core powertrain components (engine and transmission) showing no significant widespread issues. The primary areas where minor concerns have been noted are within the suspension's hydraulic system, potential electrical gremlins like infotainment glitches and battery drain, and specific exterior components such as the dihedral door hinges. Interior issues are minimal, with climate control performance being the most common, albeit not widespread, concern. Overall, the 650S is a robust machine, but owners should budget for specialized maintenance and be prepared for potential costs associated with its advanced systems, particularly the suspension and electrical components.
