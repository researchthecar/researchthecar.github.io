The 2018 McLaren 720S, a pinnacle of supercar engineering, generally exhibits strong reliability, reflecting its premium construction and advanced technology. Owners typically report satisfaction with its performance and build quality. However, as with any high-performance machine, potential owners should be aware of the specialized nature of repairs and the associated costs. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into the 720S's robust engineering.

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 McLaren 720S demonstrates a high level of technical reliability for a supercar. The core powertrain components, including the 4.0L twin-turbo V8 engine and the 7-speed dual-clutch transmission, are exceptionally robust with no widespread issues reported. Similarly, the advanced suspension system and exterior elements are generally trouble-free. Minor electrical gremlins, such as occasional infotainment glitches or intermittent sensor warnings, are the most commonly cited issues, though these are typically resolved with software updates or component replacement and are not indicative of systemic failures. Overall, the 720S offers a reliable supercar experience, provided it receives specialized maintenance and care.
