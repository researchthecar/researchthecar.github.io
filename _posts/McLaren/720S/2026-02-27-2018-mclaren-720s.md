# 2018 McLaren 720S Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 McLaren 720S is a powerhouse exotic that delivers blistering performance and striking design, making it a dream car for many enthusiasts. While supercar ownership always demands a careful eye on maintenance and upkeep, understanding potential powertrain traits and rare owner-reported issues can help buyers navigate the pre-owned market with confidence.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L Twin-Turbocharged V8

The 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 produces an incredible 710 horsepower and 568 lb-ft of torque, providing breathtaking acceleration. While generally robust under high-performance conditions, buyers should carefully inspect intake and turbo components during pre-purchase evaluations.

**Turbo Inlet Baffle Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A loose baffle inside the turbo inlet can detach, enter the turbocharger, cause severe internal damage, and potentially project debris into the engine leading to failure or fire risks.
- **Solution:** Inspect the turbo inlet components for security and replace damaged turbocharger assemblies or intake parts as necessary.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic

The 7-speed dual-clutch transmission delivers lightning-fast gear changes tailored to aggressive driving and track use. It offers smooth operation when properly maintained, though prospective buyers should verify clutch health and shift performance during a test drive.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V231000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=McLaren&model=720S&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** McLaren will notify owners, and dealers will remove the NVH foam pad from the vehicle and inspect the fuel tank. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

Shopping for a pre-owned 2018 McLaren 720S requires diligence, professional pre-purchase inspections, and a thorough review of service history. By staying aware of owner-reported engine intake concerns and ensuring the powertrain has been meticulously maintained, buyers can better enjoy the uncompromising performance this supercar offers.
