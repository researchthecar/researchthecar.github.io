# 2016 Ram 1500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Ram 1500 remains a popular light-duty full-size truck choice thanks to its smooth ride quality, versatile engine lineup, and practical interior. However, used car buyers should be aware of several recurring owner complaints reported to federal safety regulators. Key areas of concern involve anti-lock braking system module failures, electronic power steering issues, and fuel system or stalling troubles. Thoroughly inspecting these specific components before purchasing can help avoid unexpected repair bills.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 24V VVT Engine

The standard 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 produces 305 horsepower and 269 lb-ft of torque, offering solid everyday efficiency and capability for daily driving tasks. It is generally regarded as a reliable base powerplant with fewer major mechanical complaints compared to its diesel counterpart.

### 5.7L V8 HEMI MDS VVT Engine

The legendary 5.7-liter HEMI V8 delivers 395 horsepower and 410 lb-ft of torque, making it a favorite for towing and heavy-duty use. While robust, some owners have reported intermittent stalling and fuel system concerns worth noting during an inspection.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle unexpectedly stalls while driving at various speeds, occasionally accompanied by warning lights, requiring the driver to coast to a stop and restart.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fuel delivery and powertrain control systems; inspect and replace faulty components such as the fuel pump or sensors as needed.

### 3.0L V6 Turbo Diesel Engine

The 3.0-liter EcoDiesel V6 prioritizes fuel economy and low-end torque (420 lb-ft), appealing to long-distance drivers. Buyers should verify maintenance records, as diesel-specific systems require attentive care.

**Coolant Leaks and Fuel Pump Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant levels, visible fluid leaks under the hood, or sudden fuel pump failure leaving the vehicle stranded.
- **Solution:** Inspect cooling system hoses and fittings; replace failed high-pressure fuel pumps or coolant components as necessary.

## Transmission

### TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The ZF-sourced 8-speed automatic transmission is paired with both gasoline engines, delivering smooth, responsive shifts and optimized fuel economy. It generally performs reliably with proper fluid maintenance.

## Suspension

### Factory Air Suspension

The optional four-corner air suspension offers adjustable ride heights for improved aerodynamics and off-road clearance, though mechanical and electrical glitches can arise as these systems age.

**Air Suspension Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warning messages regarding the air suspension system, failure to maintain ride height, or broken suspension components.
- **Solution:** Inspect air lines, compressor, and control modules; replace damaged struts, airbags, or elbows as needed.

## Brakes

### Anti-lock Braking System (ABS) Module

The braking system provides adequate stopping power under normal conditions, but the widespread ABS module failure represents a notable complaint trend for this model year.

**ABS Module and Brake Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illumination of the ABS, traction control, and brake warning lights, accompanied by inoperable cruise control and potential wheel lock-up during hard braking.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty ABS hydraulic control unit/module assembly. Note that replacement parts may experience backorders.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering (EPS) System

The electric power steering system delivers nimble handling for a large pickup, but intermittent assist failures and rack wear have been highlighted by multiple vehicle owners.

**Steering Assist Failure and Stiffness**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of power steering assist, heavy steering feel during low-speed cornering or highway driving, and illuminated warning alerts.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electric power steering rack and control modules; replace faulty EPS assemblies if steering assist is lost.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V475000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Diesel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** FCA US will notify owners, and dealers will update the powertrain control module software to maintain vehicle propulsion by reading the camshaft position signal in the event that the crankshaft position signal is lost, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V347000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Tailgate:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the tailgate opens while the vehicle is being driven, cargo may fall out the vehicle and create a road hazard, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will repair the tailgate latch, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V486000: Structure:Body:Tailgate](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the tailgate latch releases and the tailgate opens while driving, cargo may fall out, creating a road hazard and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will repair the vehicle, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V632000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain:Inflator](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A separated inflator end cap or inflator rupture can allow compressed gas to escape, resulting in the end cap or other components being propelled into the vehicle, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace as necessary, the SABIC assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V757000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Exhaust System:Emission Control:Gas Recirculation Valve (Egr Valve)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Combustion in the intake manifold can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the EGR cooler, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V821000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Lever And Linkage:Column Shift](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Pushing the brake pedal for prolonged periods when the vehicle is running and in PARK may cause the Brake Transmission Shift Interlock (BTSI) pin to stick in the open position. Being able to shift the transmission without pushing the brake pedal and/or without a key in the ignition can increase the risk of an unintended vehicle rollaway that may result in.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will update the vehicle software, inspect the BTSI, and, as necessary, replace it, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V198000: Power Train:Driveline:Differential Unit](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Either scenario increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the differential retaining pin, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2016 Ram 1500, buyers should prioritize models with thorough service histories and verify that major powertrain and safety recalls have been addressed. Pay close attention during test drives to the braking feel, steering responsiveness, and overall electrical function. While the truck offers great comfort and capability, checking for ABS module and power steering longevity can save you from unexpected headaches down the road.
