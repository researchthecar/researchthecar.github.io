# 2021 Ram 1500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a used 2021 Ram 1500 offers access to one of the most comfortable and capable half-ton full-size trucks on the market. However, prospective buyers should review common owner-reported reliability patterns before purchasing. While overall driving dynamics and powertrain performance receive high marks, owner feedback highlights recurring issues involving electrical faults, fuel pump failures, and stability control or braking anomalies.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 eTorque

The base 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 features a mild-hybrid eTorque system producing 305 horsepower and 269 lb-ft of torque, offering responsive city driving and improved efficiency for everyday tasks.

### 5.7L V8 HEMI

The legendary 5.7-liter HEMI V8 pushes out 395 horsepower and 410 lb-ft of torque, delivering strong towing capability and robust acceleration with or without the optional eTorque assist system.

### 3.0L V6 EcoDiesel

The 3.0-liter EcoDiesel V6 generates 260 horsepower and an impressive 480 lb-ft of torque, tailored for buyers prioritizing long-distance fuel economy and substantial low-end pulling power.

### 6.2L Supercharged V8 HEMI (TRX)

Exclusive to the high-performance TRX off-road trim, the supercharged 6.2-liter HEMI churns out a massive 702 horsepower and 650 lb-ft of torque for extreme high-speed performance.

### Fuel System

Owner reports indicate occasional fuel delivery issues and sudden stalls, which can compromise drivability until the underlying pump or electrical connections are serviced.

**Fuel Pump Failure and Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience unexpected engine stalling while operating the vehicle at various speeds, often resulting in extended crank times or a complete failure to restart.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic inspection and replacement of the faulty fuel pump assembly or related fuel delivery components.

## Transmission

### TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic

Paired across all engine options, the ZF-sourced TorqueFlite 8-speed automatic transmission delivers seamless gear changes and efficient highway cruising, though electronic shifter behavior warrants attention.

**Shifter or Park Defect and Unintended Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may display gear shift anomalies, warning messages regarding the electronic shifter, or fail to securely hold the park position.
- **Solution:** Inspect software calibration, shift linkages, and electronic control modules; apply applicable manufacturer updates or replace failing switch assemblies.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brake System

The braking system provides confident stopping power, but electronic module glitches and warning alerts can disable secondary driving aids until serviced.

**Electronic Brake System and ABS Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated ABS, check electronic brake system, and stability control warning lights, occasionally accompanied by a temporary loss of cruise control or traction management features.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician scan for diagnostic trouble codes and inspect hydraulic control units or wheel speed sensors.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Assistance

Steering responsiveness is generally praised for vehicle size, but rack wear and electronic sensor faults require careful pre-purchase inspection.

**Steering Rack and Assistance Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Stiff steering feel, mechanical binding, or electronic warning messages related to power steering assistance and trailer steering systems.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electric power steering rack and pinion assembly, wiring connections, and update software modules as needed.

## Electrical

### Cabin Wiring and Infotainment

Electrical architecture supports advanced comfort and safety features, though environmental leaks can create persistent issues within interior wiring and infotainment systems.

**Water Intrusion and Electrical Shorting**
- **Symptoms:** Water leaks entering through the rear window frame can saturate internal wiring harnesses and damage critical electronics, such as the radio frequency hub.
- **Solution:** Reseal or replace the rear window assembly and inspect, repair, or replace damaged electrical modules and wiring harnesses.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V438000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera image may not display. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not display an image reduces the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the TRSCM software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V698000: Tires:Temporary/Emergency Spare Tire](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection And Rims." A driver that relies on the incorrect spare tire inflation pressure on the tire placard label may inflate the spare tire incorrectly, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the spare tire assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V925000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The side air bag inflatable curtain (SABIC) assemblies may have been manufactured with the incorrect amount of compressed gas, which can result in an improper air bag deployment. An air bag that deploys improperly can increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the SABIC assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V037000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Master Cylinder](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A separated brake pedal can result in the driver being unable to apply the service brakes, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** FCA US will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and install the push rod clip, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not display an image reduces the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash.

**[Recall 22V767000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the HPFP and inspect and replace additional fuel system components, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V904000: Structure:Body:Tailgate:Hinge And Attachments](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The tailgate may not latch properly due to misaligned tailgate strikers, which could result in the tailgate opening while driving. An unintended tailgate opening while driving may result in a loss of unsecured cargo, possibly creating a road hazard and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the tailgate striker alignment to the box latch and adjust, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Ram 1500 stands out as a highly capable and refined choice in the used truck market, combining strong powertrain variety with a plush interior. Prospective buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle for common trouble spots, including rear-window water leaks that can impact electrical components, fuel pump reliability, and electronic brake or shifter warnings. Conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection will help ensure you select a well-maintained truck free of hidden problems.
