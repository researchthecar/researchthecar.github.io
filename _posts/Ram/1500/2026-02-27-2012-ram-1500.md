# 2012 Ram 1500

### Overview
The 2012 Ram 1500 is a capable full-size pickup truck known for its robust build and towing capacity. Reliability for this model year is generally considered good, with most owners reporting satisfaction. However, like any vehicle, certain components and systems have shown a propensity for issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems across key categories, providing insights into potential repair needs and associated costs for different engine and transmission configurations.

- **Cost:** N/A


### 4.7L V8
**Cylinder Head Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Misfires, rough idling, potential coolant loss.
- **Solution:** Replacement of affected cylinder head(s).
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500


### 5.7L HEMI V8
**Lifter/Camshaft Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Ticking noise from the engine, reduced power, misfires.
- **Solution:** Replacement of lifters and potentially camshaft.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000


## Transmission

**Torque Converter Issues**

## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Ball Joint Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises, uneven tire wear, steering looseness.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**TIPM (Totally Integrated Power Module) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical issues, non-starting, warning lights, malfunctioning accessories.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the TIPM module.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1,200


## Exterior

### Body and Trim
**Tailgate Latch Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty opening or closing tailgate, spontaneous opening.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of tailgate latch mechanism.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Ram 1500 offers a generally reliable ownership experience, particularly with the 3.7L V6 and the 6-speed automatic transmissions. The 4.7L V8 and 5.7L HEMI V8 engines, while powerful, have documented issues with cylinder heads and camshaft/lifter wear, respectively, which can be costly to repair. The 4-speed automatic transmission has also seen reports of torque converter problems. Common non-powertrain issues include potential TIPM failures and tailgate latch problems. Suspension components like ball joints can also require attention. Overall, routine maintenance and addressing these specific potential issues proactively can contribute to long-term reliability.
