# 2015 Ram 1500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2015 Ram 1500 remains a popular option in the used half-ton truck market, praised for its smooth ride, comfortable cabin, and diverse powertrain lineup. Buyers can choose from the efficient Pentastar V6, the muscular HEMI V8, or the torque-rich EcoDiesel V6, paired with a modern eight-speed automatic transmission. However, prospective owners should be aware of several widely reported issues that can affect reliability and ownership costs. While many trucks provide dependable service, certain components—particularly the electronic power steering, the air suspension system, and the anti-lock braking module—have generated recurring complaints. Understanding these potential weak points and knowing what to look for during a pre-purchase inspection can help buyers find a dependable model while avoiding costly, lingering repairs.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L Pentastar V6 Engine

This 3.6-liter dual-overhead-cam V6 engine produces approximately 305 horsepower and 269 lb-ft of torque. It is widely recognized as a versatile and generally reliable workhorse across the Chrysler lineup, offering a solid balance of everyday utility and fuel efficiency for buyers who do not require heavy-duty towing capacity.

**Oil Pump and Engine Ticking Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported hearing a persistent ticking noise from the engine bay, which may be accompanied by warning lights and a faulty oil pump.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic testing of the oil pump and replacement of defective internal oil system components.

### 5.7L HEMI V8 Engine

The 5.7-liter HEMI V8 delivers 395 horsepower and 410 lb-ft of torque for robust towing and hauling performance. It features cylinder deactivation (MDS) to save fuel during light cruising, though it is best known for its classic exhaust note and strong power delivery.

**Broken Exhaust Manifold Bolts ("Hemi Tick")**
- **Symptoms:** A loud ticking or clicking noise from the engine bay, particularly during cold starts, alongside exhaust odors inside or around the cabin.
- **Solution:** Replacing the broken manifold bolts and, if necessary, resurfacing or replacing the warped exhaust manifold.

### 3.0L EcoDiesel V6 Engine

The 3.0-liter turbocharged EcoDiesel V6 offers an appealing combination of 240 horsepower and a stout 420 lb-ft of torque, prioritizing highway fuel economy and low-end pulling power. While highly efficient on paper, this engine has faced significant scrutiny due to complex emissions equipment and critical mechanical vulnerabilities.

**Crankshaft Tone Wheel Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden engine stalling, loss of motive power while driving, "limp mode" capping speeds, and diagnostic trouble codes indicating a crankshaft position sensor circuit range/performance issue.
- **Solution:** Dealers initially addressed this with a Powertrain Control Module (PCM) software flash, but a physical repair requires dropping the transmission to install a new physical tone wheel on the crankshaft.
- **Cost:** Up to $8,745.75 if not covered under warranty/campaign support.

**Exhaust Manifold Warping and Soot Accumulation**
- **Symptoms:** Heavy soot buildup inside the intake, limited throttle response or loss of power, and potential fire hazards.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the exhaust manifold and associated EGR components.

### General / multiple configurations

**High-Pressure Fuel Pump Failures and Fuel Line Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Engine stalling or loss of motive power, accompanied by fuel leaks or an atomized fuel mist spray in the engine compartment.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the high-pressure fuel pump and ensuring the single-use, torque-to-yield fuel lines are replaced rather than reused.

**Fuel Tank Strap Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The fuel tank sagging beneath the vehicle or the front strap breaking entirely.
- **Solution:** Replacing the fuel tank support straps.

## Transmission

### TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

This transmission is a licensed variation of the highly regarded ZF 8-speed automatic, standard on most 2015 configurations to optimize gear ratios and fuel economy. Controlled via a rotary shift dial on the dashboard, it generally provides smooth shifting and strong durability.

**Shifter Malfunction and Rollaway Risk**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle rolling backward or forward after being shifted into Park, or the rotary shifter failing to properly secure the vehicle in a parked state.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic inspection of the electronic shifter module and transmission control software to ensure correct park engagement.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

This traditional six-speed automatic transmission was offered primarily on base-trim models equipped with the 5.7L V8 engine. While simpler in design, it lacks the refined gear-spacing and efficiency benefits of the newer eight-speed unit.

## Suspension

### Standard and Air Suspension

**OEM Air Suspension Failures in Cold Weather**
- **Symptoms:** Severe loss of ride quality with the vehicle dropping completely to the bump stops, warnings stating "air suspension not available," and extreme bouncing or wandering on uneven roads.
- **Solution:** Replacement of air springs, compressor, and system lines, or installation of an aftermarket coil-spring conversion kit to delete the air ride entirely.
- **Cost:** OEM replacement parts can cost up to $3,000 for standard repairs, with complete system overhauls exceeding $8,000.

**Cracked Sway Bar and Weld Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Popping or clunking noises from the front end, particularly during turns or over bumps, and visible cracks on the ends of the sway bar near the welds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the stabilizer sway bar.

## Brakes

### Brake System and ABS Module

**ABS Module Failure and Nationwide Part Backorder**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warning lights for ABS, traction control, and brakes remain illuminated; cruise control, 4WD, and trailer braking systems become completely inoperable; in severe cases, a failing module can short the CAN bus, disabling the throttle control, power steering, and gear shifter while driving.
- **Solution:** Replacing the ABS control module, although sourcing the part has proven exceptionally difficult for owners.
- **Cost:** Replacement costs are quoted between $1,400 and $1,579, though parts have been on indefinite backorder.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and EPS Assist

**Intermittent or Sudden Loss of Power Steering Assist**
- **Symptoms:** Steering abruptly becomes extremely stiff and heavy while driving or at startup, sometimes jerking unexpectedly or pulling hard in one direction.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the electric power steering (EPS) unit and control module. Some models are covered under recalls (e.g., Campaign 16V167000), but many owners find their VIN is excluded.
- **Cost:** Sellers or mechanics have quoted $3,000 to $4,000 for a steering rack and EPS module replacement.

## Electrical

### Electrical Harnesses and Accessories

**Headlight Flickering and Melted Wiring Sockets**
- **Symptoms:** Headlight and fog light bulbs burning out prematurely, dim low-beam operation, flickering every minute, and melted electrical sockets or wiring harnesses in the headlight assembly.
- **Solution:** Replacing the wiring harnesses and socket connectors, and updating the vehicle's voltage settings if necessary.

**Backup Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rearview camera feed intermittently cutting out or screen displaying a blank or erratic picture.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace failing wires or the display head unit.

**Cabin Water Leaks and TIPM Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Rainwater leaking into the cabin via grab handles or roof pillars, which can seep into the dashboard and cause the Totally Integrated Power Module (TIPM) to malfunction, leading to ghost operations like windows rolling down, horns blowing, and door locks cycling randomly.
- **Solution:** Locating and sealing cabin water leaks (often from the third brake light or windshield) and replacing the damaged TIPM.

**Dashboard Electrical Fire Hazard**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden dashboard flickering or wild instrument cluster fluctuations, followed by a fire starting underneath the passenger's side dashboard.
- **Solution:** Demands complete wiring harness inspection and immediate attention if dashboard flickering occurs.

## Safety

### Airbags and Restraints

**Airbag Failure to Deploy or Spontaneous Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** Airbags failing to inflate during severe front-end collisions or deploying unexpectedly without a collision while the truck is parked.
- **Solution:** Dealer inspection of the airbag control modules and crash sensors. Some vehicles are affected by Recall Campaign 21V632000.

**Unlatching Seat Belts**
- **Symptoms:** The driver's seat belt buckle spontaneously unlatching or releasing on its own while driving.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the seat belt buckle assembly.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V475000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Diesel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2015)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** FCA US will notify owners, and dealers will update the powertrain control module software to maintain vehicle propulsion by reading the camshaft position signal in the event that the crankshaft position signal is lost, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V347000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Tailgate:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2015)**
- **Issue:** If the tailgate opens while the vehicle is being driven, cargo may fall out the vehicle and create a road hazard, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will repair the tailgate latch, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V486000: Structure:Body:Tailgate](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2015)**
- **Issue:** If the tailgate latch releases and the tailgate opens while driving, cargo may fall out, creating a road hazard and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will repair the vehicle, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2015)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V632000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain:Inflator](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2015)**
- **Issue:** A separated inflator end cap or inflator rupture can allow compressed gas to escape, resulting in the end cap or other components being propelled into the vehicle, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace as necessary, the SABIC assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V757000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Exhaust System:Emission Control:Gas Recirculation Valve (Egr Valve)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2015)**
- **Issue:** Combustion in the intake manifold can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the EGR cooler, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V821000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Lever And Linkage:Column Shift](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2015)**
- **Issue:** Pushing the brake pedal for prolonged periods when the vehicle is running and in PARK may cause the Brake Transmission Shift Interlock (BTSI) pin to stick in the open position. Being able to shift the transmission without pushing the brake pedal and/or without a key in the ignition can increase the risk of an unintended vehicle rollaway that may result in.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will update the vehicle software, inspect the BTSI, and, as necessary, replace it, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V824000: Equipment](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2015)**
- **Issue:** Chrysler (FCA US LLC) is recalling various Dodge, Chrysler, and RAM vehicles equipped with Kidde Plastic-Handle or Push Button 'Pindicator' Fire Extinguishers. These extinguishers may become clogged, preventing the extinguisher from discharging as expected or requiring excessive force to activate the extinguisher.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2015 Ram 1500 remains a highly capable, comfortable truck with great curb appeal, but used buyers must shop with extra diligence. A well-maintained model with the 5.7L V8 or the fuel-efficient 3.6L V6 can still serve as an excellent daily driver or work truck. Buyers should heavily favor examples with a documented service history showing that critical safety components—such as the Electronic Power Steering rack, the ABS control module, and any applicable EcoDiesel recalls—have already been addressed. Avoid models equipped with the factory air ride suspension unless you are prepared to budget for an aftermarket coil-spring conversion, particularly if you live in a cold climate. Additionally, a thorough pre-purchase inspection should prioritize checking for cab water leaks, inspecting the front sway bar welds, and verifying the operation of the rotary shifter to ensure a safe, long-lasting purchase.
