# 2019 Ram 1500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Ram 1500 delivers a smooth ride, upscale interior, and strong towing capabilities, making it a popular choice in the full-size truck market. However, prospective buyers should review recurring owner complaints regarding electrical anomalies, steering assist issues, and powertrain quirks. Being aware of these areas can help you inspect a used model thoroughly before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L Pentastar V6 with eTorque

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 engine produces approximately 305 horsepower and 269 lb-ft of torque, aided by a mild-hybrid eTorque system for improved low-end responsiveness. It offers a balanced blend of everyday drivability and efficiency.

### 5.7L HEMI V8 (with or without eTorque)

The legendary 5.7-liter HEMI V8 pushes out 395 horsepower and 410 lb-ft of torque, providing robust towing capacity and strong acceleration. While generally durable, buyers should keep an eye out for occasional fuel delivery and electrical accessory issues.

**Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience sudden engine stalling, difficulty starting, or failure to run properly due to fuel delivery loss.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the faulty fuel pump or associated fuel system components.

## Transmission

### TorqueFlite 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The TorqueFlite 8-speed automatic transmission delivers crisp, smooth shifts across all trims and pairs effectively with both V6 and V8 engines. It is generally robust, though occasional electronic shifter complaints have been noted by owners.

**Shifter or Park Defect**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may encounter issues where the electronic rotary shifter fails to engage the correct gear or unexpectedly shifts into park or neutral while slowing down.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic inspection of the gear selector module, software updates, or replacement of faulty shifter components.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides confident stopping power under normal conditions, though occasional sensor faults and premature pad wear have been cited in owner feedback.

**Brake System Warning and Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Illumination of ABS, traction control, and brake warning lights, or premature wear of brake pads and rotors.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake hydraulics, wheel speed sensors, and replace worn pads and rotors as necessary.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

The electric power steering system provides light and manageable handling at low speeds, but intermittent electrical assistance failures have been reported by some owners.

**Steering Assist Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience sudden loss of power steering functionality accompanied by warning lights on the dashboard, making the steering wheel significantly heavier to turn.
- **Solution:** Inspect electric power steering components, wiring harnesses, or replace the steering rack assembly if faulty.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Infotainment Systems

Cabin electronics and the available large touchscreen display offer modern connectivity, but prospective buyers should check for signs of water intrusion around the rear window that can impact onboard electronics.

**Water Intrusion and Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Water leaks from the rear window frame can pool onto electrical modules like the RF Hub, triggering erratic electrical behavior or instrument cluster failures.
- **Solution:** Seal or replace the rear window frame assembly and inspect/replace affected electrical modules.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Restraints

Safety systems include advanced airbag deployment setups and pretensioners, though intermittent warning lights related to seat sensors and harnesses have been documented.

**Air Bag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** Illumination of the airbag warning light due to seat belt retractor circuit issues or seat occupancy sensor faults.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning of the SRS module and replacement of faulty seat belt pretensioners or wiring connections.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V051000: Visibility:Defroster/Defogger/Hvac System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As a result, these vehicles fail to comply with Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 103, "Windshield defrosting and defogging systems." The lack of a functioning windshield defrosting and defogging system may decrease the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will repurchase the affected vehicles. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V142000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the auxiliary battery system overheats, there is an increased risk of a fire within the cabin even if the ignition is off.
- **What to do:** FCA US has notified owners, and dealers will properly secure the auxiliary battery fasteners, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V067000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Pedals And Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the brake pedal separates from the pedal assembly, the driver may be unable to brake the vehicle, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will repair the adjustable pedal assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19E059000: Structure](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a rear impact crash, the rear step kit may cause the rear control arms to contact the fuel tank. Contact with the fuel tank may cause a fuel leak, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the bed step, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V020000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A loss of power steering could vary the steering effort required, possibly causing vehicle oversteer or understeer, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler has notified owners, and dealers will properly secure the ground fastener, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V812000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles may have been built with a contaminated electric power steering (EPS) gear that may short circuit, potentially resulting in an intermittent loss of power steering assist. An intermittent loss of power steering assist may cause an inconsistent steering effort, especially during lower speed maneuvers, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the EPS gear assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V556000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The instrument cluster may not properly illuminate driver warnings. As such, these vehicles fails to conform with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 101, "Control and Displays." Failure of the instrument cluster to properly illuminate may prevent the driver from receiving warnings about the vehicle's systems and.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the instrument cluster, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V201000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the steering wheel detaches from the steering column, the driver may lose control of the vehicle, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the steering column, replacing it, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Ram 1500 is a highly capable and comfortable full-size truck, but used-car buyers should perform a diligent inspection. Pay close attention to the operation of the electronic rotary shifter, verify that power steering functions smoothly without warning lights, and inspect the rear window and cabin interior for any signs of water leaks or electrical glitches.
