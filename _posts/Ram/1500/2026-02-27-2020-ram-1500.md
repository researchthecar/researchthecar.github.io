# 2020 Ram 1500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Ram 1500 offers a smooth ride, upscale interior, and strong towing capability, making it a popular choice in the full-size truck market. However, prospective buyers should review owner complaints carefully regarding specific mechanical and electrical trouble spots. While many trucks provide trouble-free service, certain powertrain and steering components require close inspection during a pre-purchase evaluation.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 eTorque

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 with eTorque mild-hybrid system produces 305 horsepower and 269 lb-ft of torque, providing efficient daily driving and decent light-duty capability.

**eTorque System and MGU Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle displays electrical warnings, experiences abnormal electrical behavior, or loses motive power due to Motor Generator Unit (MGU) failure.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty Motor Generator Unit (MGU) or associated eTorque components.

### 5.7L V8 HEMI

The legendary 5.7-liter HEMI V8 pushes out 395 horsepower and 410 lb-ft of torque, offering robust towing performance and strong acceleration for everyday truck duties.

### 3.0L V6 EcoDiesel

The 3.0-liter EcoDiesel V6 delivers 260 horsepower and an impressive 480 lb-ft of torque, prioritizing exceptional low-end pulling power and fuel economy.

**Diesel Exhaust Fluid (DEF) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** DEF system faults trigger warning messages and can eventually prevent the vehicle from operating.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the DEF system sensors and heaters; replace failing components or service the emissions module.

### Fuel System

Delivers fuel from the tank to the engine reliably under normal operating conditions, though occasional fuel pump and filler neck issues have been reported.

**Fuel Pump and Filler Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Fuel spills out during fueling, raw fuel odors fill the cabin, and liquid leaks into the charcoal canister or manifold.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the fuel pump assembly, purge valve, or fuel tank components as needed.

### Engine Management

Manages core engine operations, emissions, and ignition timing to ensure consistent performance and efficiency.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The engine unexpectedly loses power or stalls while driving, illuminating dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose diagnostic trouble codes (DTCs) and replace faulty sensors, fuel pumps, or control modules.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

This 8-speed automatic transmission pairs across various trims and drivetrains to deliver smooth gear changes and optimized fuel economy.

**Shifter or Park Defect**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle fails to register that it is in park or experiences erratic gear shifting behavior.
- **Solution:** Update transmission software or replace the gear selector module and linkage components.

## Steering

### Steering System

Provides responsive handling and electronic power assist, though electronic power steering rack failures have been documented by some owners.

**Steering Rack Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power steering assist accompanied by warning lights on the dashboard, making the steering wheel extremely difficult to turn.
- **Solution:** Replace the electronic power steering (EPS) rack and pinion assembly.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Radio Frequency Hub

Manages cabin accessories, infotainment, and vehicle communication networks, though vulnerable to water intrusion issues from the rear cabin.

**RF Hub and Water Leak Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Water leaks from the rear window or third brake light onto internal modules, causing radio frequency hub failure and electrical draining.
- **Solution:** Reseal the rear window or brake light housing and replace damaged electrical modules.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Occupant restraint system designed to secure passengers during a collision, subject to occasional mechanical retractor issues.

**Seat Belt Retractor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Seat belts fail to properly retract, lock up unexpectedly, or fail to secure occupants correctly.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the faulty seat belt assembly or retractor mechanism.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V622000: Visibility:Rearview Mirrors/Devices:Exterior](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." Missing driver side mirror glass reduces visibility and increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver side mirror glass, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V580000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, a front seat belt retractor may not properly slow the occupant's forward movement after locking. In the event of a crash, if the front seat belts fail to lock, there is an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front seat belt retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V191000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lingering rearview image can distract the driver, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler has notified owners, and dealers will update the radio display software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V407000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The flash memory of the Occupant Restraint Controller (ORC) may become corrupted, disabling the vehicle's air bags and seat belt pretensioners. Disabled air bags and seat belt pretensioners increase the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the ORC or replace it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V208000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Reduced visibility increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will tighten the wiper nuts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V632000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain:Inflator](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A separated inflator end cap or inflator rupture can allow compressed gas to escape, resulting in the end cap or other components being propelled into the vehicle, increasing the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace as necessary, the SABIC assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V374000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles may have an improperly crimped diffuser in the side curtain airbag which may result in the diffuser detaching from the inflator in the event of an air bag deployment. In the event of a crash, if the diffuser detaches from the inflator during air bag deployment, the air bag may not inflate properly, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** FCA US will notify owners, and dealers will replace the side curtain air bag assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V513000: Vehicle Speed Control:Accelerator Pedal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=1500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A pedal that does not return as expected may affect the vehicle's deceleration, thereby increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will modify the driver's floormat to remove the potential for pedal interference, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Ram 1500 remains a compelling full-size pickup truck option thanks to its refined ride quality and versatile engine lineup. Buyers should pay close attention during inspections to potential trouble spots, including eTorque electrical system issues, electronic power steering rack failures, and cabin water leaks that can damage electrical modules. Thoroughly testing all electrical features and reviewing service records will help ensure a reliable ownership experience.
