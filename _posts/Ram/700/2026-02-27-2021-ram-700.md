# 2021 Ram 700

### Overview
The 2021 Ram 700, a compact pickup truck primarily aimed at emerging markets, offers a utilitarian and budget-friendly option. It is built on a unibody platform, sharing components with Fiat's small car offerings. Reliability data for this model is less extensively documented in mainstream North American consumer reports compared to larger Ram trucks. However, based on available information and its shared platform with established Fiat vehicles, the Ram 700 generally exhibits a decent reliability record for its class. Owners often praise its maneuverability and fuel efficiency. Potential concerns, while not widespread, can arise in areas typical for vehicles in this segment, such as certain powertrain components under heavy use or specific electrical accessories.

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Ram 700 demonstrates a generally reliable performance, especially considering its market positioning. The 1.3L Firefly engine is a robust unit with no significant widespread issues reported. The 5-speed manual transmission is also dependable. The CVT, while generally performing well, has seen some isolated reports of hesitation or shuddering, which are typically addressable with software updates or minor repairs. Electrical components, particularly the infotainment system, can occasionally experience minor glitches, but these are usually resolved with software fixes. Overall, the Ram 700 is a solid choice for those seeking a basic, functional, and relatively trouble-free compact pickup, with most potential issues being minor and manageable.
