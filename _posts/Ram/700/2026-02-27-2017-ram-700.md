# 2017 Ram 700

### Overview
The 2017 Ram 700, a compact pickup truck primarily sold in Latin America, is generally considered a utilitarian and robust vehicle. It is built on a unibody platform, sharing components with Fiat's small car offerings. Reliability for the 2017 model year is typically viewed as average to good, with owners appreciating its straightforward mechanicals and lower cost of ownership compared to larger trucks. However, like any vehicle, specific components can experience issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, examining common problem areas reported by owners and technicians.

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Ram 700 demonstrates a generally solid reliability record, particularly concerning its powertrain options (1.6L and 1.8L E.torQ engines paired with either a 5-speed manual or 6-speed automatic transmission). These are typically robust and require minimal intervention beyond standard maintenance. The suspension and exterior components also appear to hold up well under normal operating conditions. The most commonly cited, though not widespread, issue relates to the electrical system, specifically intermittent warning lights that may require diagnostic attention to pinpoint faulty sensors or wiring. Overall, the Ram 700 is a dependable choice for basic transportation and light-duty work, with few significant recurring mechanical faults reported.
