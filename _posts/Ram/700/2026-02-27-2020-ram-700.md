# 2020 Ram 700

### Overview
The 2020 Ram 700, a compact pickup truck primarily sold in Latin America, is generally considered a robust and utilitarian vehicle. It is built on a unibody platform, sharing many components with Fiat's passenger car lineup, which contributes to its accessible maintenance and parts availability. Reliability for the 2020 model year is largely positive, with owners reporting few significant widespread mechanical failures. The focus of this vehicle is on affordability and practicality, rather than high-performance or luxury features. Therefore, common issues tend to be minor and related to its segment positioning. The powertrain options, while not overly complex, have demonstrated reasonable durability under typical usage conditions.

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 Ram 700 demonstrates a commendable level of reliability for its class and market segment. Owners generally find it to be a dependable workhorse with minimal unexpected mechanical problems. The powertrain options, both the 1.4L Fire MPI and 1.6L E.torQ EVO engines paired with either the 5-speed manual or 6-speed automatic transmissions, have proven to be durable. Similarly, the suspension, electrical systems, exterior, and interior components have not shown any prevalent widespread issues. This suggests that the Ram 700 is a cost-effective and reliable choice for those seeking basic transportation and light-duty utility, with maintenance costs typically remaining low due to its straightforward design and widely available parts.
