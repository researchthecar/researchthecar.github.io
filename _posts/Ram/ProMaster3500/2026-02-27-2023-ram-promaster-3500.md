# 2023 Ram ProMaster 3500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Ram ProMaster 3500 is a full-size commercial van designed for heavy-duty hauling and upfitting, powered by a standard V6 gasoline engine mated to a modern automatic transmission. While early feedback from owner reports is generally limited, prospective used buyers should thoroughly inspect the engine bay and cooling system for potential leaks or rare compartment issues during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 (Pentastar)

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 gasoline engine produces approximately 276 horsepower and 250 lb-ft of torque, providing capable power for heavy commercial workloads. It serves as the primary powerplant for the ProMaster lineup, offering straightforward maintenance but requiring careful monitoring of cooling and electrical components.

**Coolant Leak at Radiator Tube Joint**
- **Symptoms:** A fractured radiator tube joint can lead to unexpected coolant leaks while the vehicle is parked, often without triggering an immediate dashboard warning light.
- **Solution:** Inspect the radiator and associated hose joints for cracks or weeping, and replace any damaged cooling system components.

**Engine Compartment Fire Risk**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported rare instances of unexpected fires originating within the engine compartment while the vehicle is parked and stationary.
- **Solution:** Check the engine bay for any signs of electrical chafing, fluid leaks, or previous thermal damage during an inspection.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a 9-speed automatic transmission routing power to the front wheels, this configuration is tuned for commercial duty and highway efficiency. No widespread recurring drivetrain complaints have been widely highlighted by early owners.
