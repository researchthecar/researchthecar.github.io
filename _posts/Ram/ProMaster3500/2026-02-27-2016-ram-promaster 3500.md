## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Electrical Gremlins**
- **Symptoms:** Random warning lights, power window malfunctions, issues with interior lighting.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


### Alternator
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light, dimming headlights, difficulty starting the engine.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900


## Exterior

### Door Latches and Hinges
**Sticking or Binding**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty opening or closing doors, especially sliding side doors.
- **Solution:** Lubrication, adjustment, or replacement of door latches and hinges.
- **Cost:** $150 - $350


### Rear Door Lock Actuators
**Failure to Operate**
- **Symptoms:** Rear doors not locking or unlocking electronically.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the rear door lock actuator.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Interior

### HVAC Blower Motor
**Intermittent Operation**
- **Symptoms:** Air conditioning or heating not blowing consistently, or not blowing at all.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the HVAC blower motor or resistor.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Ram ProMaster 3500 demonstrates good overall reliability, with its core powertrain components (engine and transmission) being particularly strong. The most frequently reported issues tend to be in the electrical system, specifically related to the Body Control Module and alternator, and some exterior components like door latches and rear lock actuators. Interior issues are less common, with the HVAC blower motor being a notable point. Proactive maintenance and addressing minor electrical or door-related concerns early can help ensure the longevity and dependable operation of this commercial van.
