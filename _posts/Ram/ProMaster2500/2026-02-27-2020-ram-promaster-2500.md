# 2020 Ram ProMaster 2500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Ram ProMaster 2500 is a popular choice for commercial fleets and camper van conversions, offering a spacious interior and front-wheel-drive layout. While owner-submitted complaints for this model year are relatively limited, prospective buyers should carefully inspect mechanical and body components. As with any heavily utilized commercial vehicle, thorough pre-purchase inspections are essential to ensure long-term reliability.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 Pentastar

The 3.6L V6 Pentastar gasoline engine produces approximately 280 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, providing reliable power for daily commercial duties. It is a widely used and generally dependable powerplant across the Ram lineup, though routine maintenance is crucial.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission routes power to the front wheels and is engineered to handle heavy payloads. Buyers should test all gear selections and check for smooth operation during a test drive.

**Gear Shifting and Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience steering wheel vibrations while maneuvering at low speeds and occasional difficulty shifting into park.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the shifter linkage, transmission mounts, and internal components for wear or misalignment.

## Exterior

### Driver Side Door

Exterior body components on commercial vans endure heavy daily use, making regular checks of doors and latches important for safety.

**Door Latch and Panel Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The driver side door patch or latch may fail, resulting in the door failing to stay securely closed while operating the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Inspect the door latch mechanism, hinges, and surrounding structure, and replace damaged locking or mounting components as necessary.
