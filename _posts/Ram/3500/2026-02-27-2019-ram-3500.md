# 2019 Ram 3500

### Overview
The 2019 Ram 3500 is a heavy-duty pickup truck designed for demanding work and towing. It generally offers robust performance and durability, particularly with its powerful engine options. Reliability is a strong suit for this model year, with owners reporting satisfaction with its core mechanical components. However, like any vehicle, specific areas can experience issues, though widespread problems are not common. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential repair areas and associated costs for various configurations.

### 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel
**DEF System Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine light illumination, reduced engine power, DEF quality warnings.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of Diesel Exhaust Fluid (DEF) injector or pump, software updates.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1200

**Glow Plug Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Hard starting in cold weather, rough idling.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty glow plugs.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Ram 3500 demonstrates strong overall reliability, especially concerning its powertrain. The 6.4L HEMI V8 is particularly trouble-free. The 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel, while powerful, can occasionally encounter issues with its Diesel Exhaust Fluid (DEF) system or glow plugs, which are common to modern diesel powertrains. Transmission options, both automatic and manual, are generally robust. Electrical gremlins, primarily related to the infotainment system, are the most frequently cited non-powertrain issues. Exterior and interior components are largely free of widespread defects. For owners of the Cummins engine, proactive maintenance of the DEF system is advisable. Overall, the 2019 Ram 3500 is a dependable choice for heavy-duty needs.
