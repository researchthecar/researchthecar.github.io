# 2019 Ram 3500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Ram 3500 is a heavy-duty workhorse capable of immense towing and hauling when properly equipped. However, used-car buyers should thoroughly inspect prospective vehicles for recurring complaints related to fuel systems, electrical anomalies, and steering or suspension components. While heavy-duty trucks frequently see demanding commercial use, paying close attention to maintenance history and prior recall completions will help mitigate unexpected ownership hurdles.

---

## Engine

### 6.4L HEMI V8

The standard gasoline option features a 6.4-liter V8 delivering approximately 410 horsepower and 429 lb-ft of torque, providing robust capability for gas-powered heavy-duty applications.

### 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel (Standard Output)

The Cummins diesel engine offers strong low-end torque well-suited for heavy towing, though buyers should verify the health of related fuel delivery systems.

### 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel (High Output)

The High Output Cummins diesel produces up to 1,000 lb-ft of torque paired with heavy-duty transmissions, delivering maximum pulling power for the Ram 3500 line.

**Fuel Pump Failure and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine stalling, loss of motive power, or high-pressure fuel pump failure leading to debris in the fuel system.
- **Solution:** Inspect the fuel system and ensure all high-pressure fuel pump recalls or service updates have been correctly executed.

## Transmission

### 68RFE 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The standard automatic transmission handles heavy loads well under normal operating conditions, though fluid maintenance records are vital for longevity.

### Aisin AS69RC 6-Speed Heavy-Duty Automatic

The commercial-grade Aisin automatic transmission is engineered for maximum durability and pairs with the high-output diesel engine to manage extreme towing loads.

**Transmission Shifting and Engagement Issues**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission may experience erratic shifting, failure to engage gears properly, or lock up in park requiring a vehicle restart.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition and software calibration levels; update valve body components if symptoms persist.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes and Trailer Controller

The braking system is designed to haul massive loads safely, but auxiliary trailer brake connections and ABS modules require close inspection.

**Trailer Brake Integration Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience intermittent loss of trailer brake response when depressing the brake pedal, often accompanied by ABS or traction control warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect the integrated trailer brake module, wiring harnesses, and ORC control modules for faults or corrosion.

## Steering

### Steering Linkage and Ball Joints

Heavy-duty front steering components are subjected to significant stress, making routine alignment and suspension checks essential.

**Steering Wandering and Component Wear**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may wander across lanes or exhibit excessive steering play due to premature wear on front ball joints and steering linkage components.
- **Solution:** Inspect front-end suspension joints during test drives and replace worn ball joints or steering components as necessary.

## Interior

### Cabin Electronics and Wiring

Electrical systems govern numerous convenience and safety features, making weather seal integrity important for preventing hidden corrosion.

**Water Intrusion and Electrical Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Water leaks through the third brake light, rear window, or firewall cowl can cause corrosion, damp interior components, and communication faults on the CAN bus.
- **Solution:** Check cabin seals around the rear high-mount stop lamp and cowl drains to prevent moisture ingress into electrical modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V439000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The engine calibration software may not warn about low oil volume. Low oil can damage the engine, causing an oil leak or a punctured engine block, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the engine calibration software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V035000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Reduced visibility increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will tighten the wiper nuts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V191000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lingering rearview image can distract the driver, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler has notified owners, and dealers will update the radio display software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V043000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The leaking transmission fluid may contact the turbocharger or another ignition source within the engine compartment, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the transmission valve body separator plate and reprogram the powertrain control module. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V498000: Fuel System, Diesel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the fuel lines, replacing them if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V880000: Fuel System, Diesel:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the HPFP, update the Powertrain Control Module (PCM) software, and inspect and replace additional fuel system components as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V207000: Structure](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** In certain side loading conditions, the bed step may unexpectedly fail while in use. A bed step that unexpectedly fails while in use increases the risk of injury to the user.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the bed step support brace, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V398000: Wheels:Lugs/Nuts/Bolts/Studs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Incorrect information in the Service and Owner's Manuals may cause the flanged lug nuts to be over-tightened, which could result in a broken wheel stud and possible wheel separation. Wheel separation can cause a vehicle crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the torque specification in the owner's manual and other published service documents. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Ram 3500 stands out as a powerful heavy-duty pickup option, but used-car shoppers should approach with attentive inspection routines. Key areas to evaluate include fuel system health, trailer brake integration, and steering component wear. Verifying complete service records and ensuring all relevant manufacturer campaigns have been performed will greatly improve the ownership experience.
