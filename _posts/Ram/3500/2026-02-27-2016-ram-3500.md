# 2016 Ram 3500

### Overview
The 2016 Ram 3500 is a heavy-duty pickup truck known for its robust towing and hauling capabilities. Reliability for this model year is generally considered strong, particularly with its diesel engine options. However, like any complex vehicle, certain components can experience issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns, excluding routine maintenance and normal wear and tear. We will examine the primary engine and transmission choices, as well as common areas like suspension, electrical systems, exterior, and interior components to provide a comprehensive reliability picture for potential owners.

### 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel
**Fuel Injector Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, loss of power, increased exhaust smoke, poor fuel economy.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 per injector

**EGR Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** White smoke from exhaust, coolant loss, overheating, sweet smell from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the EGR cooler.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (68RFE)
**Torque Converter Shudder**
- **Symptoms:** Vibration or shuddering during acceleration, especially at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Fluid flush and filter replacement, or torque converter replacement.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1000 (fluid flush), $1500 - $3000 (torque converter replacement)

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Ram 3500 demonstrates solid reliability, especially when equipped with the 6.4L HEMI V8 engine, which has very few reported issues. The 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel, while powerful and durable, can occasionally face problems with fuel injectors and EGR coolers, which are common for this type of heavy-duty diesel powertrain. The automatic transmission (68RFE) may experience torque converter shudder, but manual transmissions are generally robust. Other systems like suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components are largely free of widespread, significant reliability concerns. Overall, the 2016 Ram 3500 is a dependable choice for heavy-duty work, with potential buyers advised to be aware of the specific diesel engine maintenance needs.
