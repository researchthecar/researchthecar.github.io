# 2020 Ram 3500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Ram 3500 is a heavy-duty powerhouse designed for serious towing and hauling, anchored by robust Cummins diesel and gasoline V8 engine options. While it offers immense capability, prospective buyers should review owner-reported concerns closely. The most notable complaints center on high-pressure fuel pump failures and steering vibrations, which require careful pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 6.4L HEMI V8

The 6.4L HEMI V8 gasoline engine produces 410 horsepower and 429 lb-ft of torque, mated to an 8-speed automatic transmission. It serves as a capable standard powerplant for buyers who prefer gas over diesel maintenance costs.

### 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel I6

The standard-output 6.7L Cummins diesel delivers 370 horsepower and 850 lb-ft of torque, while the High Output version cranks out 400 horsepower and 1,000 lb-ft of torque. Known for massive towing capacity, this engine is generally durable but requires monitoring for high-pressure fuel pump reliability.

**High-Pressure Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Abnormal ticking sounds from the engine bay, sudden engine stalling, or complete loss of motive power while driving.
- **Solution:** Requires inspection and replacement of the high-pressure fuel pump and potentially the entire fuel system if metal contamination occurs.

## Transmission

### ZF 8-Speed Automatic (Gas)

This 8-speed automatic transmission is paired with the 6.4L gas engine, offering smooth shifting and optimized gear selection for heavy loads.

### Aisin 6-Speed Heavy-Duty Automatic (Diesel)

The heavy-duty 6-speed automatic built by Aisin is paired with the high-output diesel engine to manage maximum towing demands. It is widely praised for commercial-grade durability under heavy loads.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes and ABS

The braking system is robustly sized for heavy hauling, though occasional electronic module failures and hardware fastener issues have been reported.

**ABS Module and Brake Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated ABS warning lights, spongy brake pedal response, or complete failure of the hydraulic control unit / ABS module.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty ABS module and hydraulic control unit as needed.

**Brake Caliper Mounting Bolt Loosening**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises from the wheels or loose brake components during inspection.
- **Solution:** Clean threads, apply threadlocker, and torque brake caliper mounting bolts strictly to factory specifications.

## Steering

### Steering and Front Suspension

Front-end stability is crucial for heavy-duty trucks, though some owners experience vibration issues at higher speeds.

**Steering Wheel Shaking and Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Severe trembling or shaking of the steering wheel, particularly at highway speeds or while towing heavy trailers.
- **Solution:** Inspect front-end components, steering damper, wheel balance, and track bar bushings for premature wear.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V439000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The engine calibration software may not warn about low oil volume. Low oil can damage the engine, causing an oil leak or a punctured engine block, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the engine calibration software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V035000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Reduced visibility increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will tighten the wiper nuts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V191000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lingering rearview image can distract the driver, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler has notified owners, and dealers will update the radio display software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V043000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The leaking transmission fluid may contact the turbocharger or another ignition source within the engine compartment, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the transmission valve body separator plate and reprogram the powertrain control module. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V880000: Fuel System, Diesel:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the HPFP, update the Powertrain Control Module (PCM) software, and inspect and replace additional fuel system components as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V207000: Structure](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** In certain side loading conditions, the bed step may unexpectedly fail while in use. A bed step that unexpectedly fails while in use increases the risk of injury to the user.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the bed step support brace, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V398000: Wheels:Lugs/Nuts/Bolts/Studs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Incorrect information in the Service and Owner's Manuals may cause the flanged lug nuts to be over-tightened, which could result in a broken wheel stud and possible wheel separation. Wheel separation can cause a vehicle crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the torque specification in the owner's manual and other published service documents. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V511000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** This can result in engine damage and connecting rod failure, which could potentially puncture the engine block. A damaged engine block may leak oil, which can contact hot engine or exhaust components, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will flash the engine calibration software to enhance the engine warm up protection, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Ram 3500 is a formidable workhorse capable of handling extreme towing and payload requirements. While most owners experience dependable service from the powertrain options, used-car buyers should thoroughly check for fuel system health and front-end steering stability. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic is strongly advised before finalizing a purchase.
