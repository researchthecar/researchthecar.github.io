# 2024 Ram 3500

### Overview
The 2024 Ram 3500 is engineered for heavy-duty performance, offering robust powertrains designed to tackle demanding tasks. Reliability data for the initial production year is still emerging, but early indicators suggest a strong foundation. The truck is available with two primary engine options, both known for their durability in previous iterations, and a heavy-duty automatic transmission. Focus areas for potential concern, as with many heavy-duty trucks, often revolve around the complex emissions systems and the high-stress components associated with towing and hauling. However, the Ram 3500 generally maintains a reputation for solid build quality and dependable operation when properly maintained.

### 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel
**DEF Injector Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light, reduced engine power, rough idling, increased DEF consumption.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Diesel Exhaust Fluid (DEF) injector.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**DPF Regeneration Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light, DPF warning light, reduced power, incomplete regeneration cycles.
- **Solution:** Forced regeneration, cleaning of the Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF), or replacement if severely clogged.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500


## Transmission

**Torque Converter Shudder**
- **Symptoms:** Vibration or shuddering during acceleration, particularly at lower speeds or when shifting.

### Summary Verdict
The 2024 Ram 3500 demonstrates solid initial reliability, particularly with its robust HEMI V8 engine. The Cummins Turbo Diesel, while powerful, shows some potential for issues related to its emissions control systems, specifically the DEF injector and DPF regeneration, which are common areas of concern for modern diesel trucks. Transmission reliability is generally good, though the 6-speed automatic may occasionally exhibit torque converter shudder. Electrical and interior components appear to be holding up well, with only minor infotainment glitches reported. Overall, the Ram 3500 is a dependable heavy-duty truck, with most reported issues being manageable and typical for its class.
