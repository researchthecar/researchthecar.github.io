# 2018 Ram 3500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Ram 3500 is a heavy-duty workhorse capable of immense towing and hauling tasks, but prospective buyers should carefully review known mechanical and safety patterns. While the truck's robust powertrain options make it a favorite for commercial and heavy towing needs, owner complaints highlight persistent issues surrounding braking, stability systems, and electronic controls.

---

## Engine

### 6.4L HEMI V8 Gasoline Engine

The standard 6.4-liter gas V8 delivers stout horsepower and torque for demanding payload tasks. It offers a simpler maintenance profile than the diesel alternative, though fuel economy is lower.

### 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel I6

The available 6.7-liter Cummins inline-six produces massive torque ratings tailored for heavy fifth-wheel and gooseneck towing. When properly maintained, it is known for high-mileage durability, though emissions equipment requires attentive monitoring.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

A traditional heavy-duty manual transmission paired with select diesel configurations, offering direct mechanical control for commercial operators.

### 66FE / Aisin 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Heavy-duty automatic transmissions matched to gas or high-output diesel variants. They are engineered to handle high towing capacities, though routine fluid service is critical.

## Suspension

### Front Suspension and Wheels

Suspension and wheel assemblies are subject to intense stresses when navigating rough terrain or towing heavy fifth-wheel trailers.

**Wheel Cracking and Tire Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Unexplained air loss, visible cracks extending from the center hub on factory wheels, and premature front-end tire wear.
- **Solution:** Inspect wheels for structural cracks during routine tire rotations and replace damaged rims or incorrect load-range tires immediately.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Control Unit and ABS

The braking system features heavy-duty discs designed to stop massive loads, but owners frequently report electronic module failures affecting the anti-lock brake system.

**Hydraulic Control Unit (HCU) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated ABS and traction control warning lights, sudden loss of cruise control, disabled traction control, and unresponsive brake pedal feel.
- **Solution:** Replace the defective Hydraulic Control Unit and associated ABS components once replacement parts become available.

## Steering

### Steering Gear and Linkage

Steering components bear significant loads from front-end weight and large tires, leading to complaints regarding looseness and assist feedback.

**Steering Play and Assist Binding**
- **Symptoms:** Abnormal free play in the steering wheel, sudden light steering feel during cornering, or unexpected binding of the steering and brakes.
- **Solution:** Inspect steering components, linkages, and gear boxes for wear or alignment issues, and verify all prior recall repairs.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Body Control Modules

Cabin electronics and auxiliary control modules experience occasional glitches that can disrupt entertainment and convenience features.

**Radio and Camera Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent backup camera failure, radio freezing, stations jumping automatically, and erratic Bluetooth connectivity.
- **Solution:** Reset or replace the infotainment head unit or update associated body control software modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18E096000: Steering:Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The weakened connection may result in a fractured steering drag link, causing a loss of steering and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Streetcar will notify owners and dealers will inspect and install a new steering knuckle, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V347000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Tailgate:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the tailgate opens while the vehicle is being driven, cargo may fall out the vehicle and create a road hazard, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will repair the tailgate latch, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V398000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." A loss of image in the rearview camera while backing up can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rearview mirror, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V021000: Steering:Linkages:Drag:Link:Connection](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A drag link separation can cause a loss of steering control, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will check the steering linkage jam nut tightness, replacing the drag link assembly or welding the nut to the adjuster sleeve, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V398000: Wheels:Lugs/Nuts/Bolts/Studs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Incorrect information in the Service and Owner's Manuals may cause the flanged lug nuts to be over-tightened, which could result in a broken wheel stud and possible wheel separation. Wheel separation can cause a vehicle crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the torque specification in the owner's manual and other published service documents. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V824000: Equipment](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Chrysler (FCA US LLC) is recalling various Dodge, Chrysler, and RAM vehicles equipped with Kidde Plastic-Handle or Push Button 'Pindicator' Fire Extinguishers. These extinguishers may become clogged, preventing the extinguisher from discharging as expected or requiring excessive force to activate the extinguisher.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 18V100000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Pushing the brake pedal for prolonged periods when the vehicle is running and in PARK may cause the Brake Transmission Shift Interlock (BTSI) pin to stick in the open position. Being able to shift the transmission without pushing the brake pedal and/or without a key in the ignition can increase the risk of an unintended vehicle rollaway that may result in.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners and dealers will update the Body Control Module software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Ram 3500 remains a formidable heavy-duty truck suited for demanding commercial and towing applications, provided it has been thoroughly inspected. Buyers should pay close attention to brake system operation, steering responsiveness, and the status of pending recall repairs. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic specializing in heavy-duty trucks is strongly recommended.
