# 2021 Ram 3500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Ram 3500 is a heavy-duty powerhouse designed for serious towing and hauling, offering immense capability and rugged styling. However, prospective buyers should carefully review owner-reported complaints regarding electrical anomalies, powertrain concerns, and heavy-duty drivetrain components. While heavy-duty trucks frequently see demanding use that can accelerate wear, checking for completed recall services and thoroughly inspecting mechanical systems is essential when shopping on the used market.

---

## Engine

### 6.4L HEMI V8 Gasoline Engine

The standard 6.4-liter HEMI V8 gasoline engine produces approximately 410 horsepower and 429 lb-ft of torque, providing strong capability for everyday heavy-duty tasks without requiring diesel maintenance.

### 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel I6 Engine

The available 6.7-liter Cummins Turbo Diesel inline-6 delivers massive torque—up to 1,075 lb-ft in High Output form—making it the preferred choice for heavy towing, though buyers should monitor fuel system health.

**Fuel System and Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly stall while driving at speed, fail to restart, and require a complete fuel system replacement.
- **Solution:** Have the fuel system inspected by a qualified technician, and ensure all relevant updates or maintenance protocols are observed.

## Transmission

### 68RFE 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The standard 68RFE 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with select gas and diesel configurations, offering proven service intervals but requiring attentive fluid maintenance under heavy towing conditions.

### Aisin 6-Speed Heavy-Duty Automatic Transmission

The available Aisin heavy-duty 6-speed automatic transmission is built to handle the highest torque outputs of the High Output Cummins diesel engine for demanding commercial applications.

**Powertrain and Electrical Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported transmission locking up while traveling up grades, failing to shift into park properly, or illuminating warning lights indicating overheating.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition, check for pending diagnostic trouble codes, and verify proper linkage and software calibration.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

The electrical architecture supports complex towing and cabin technology, though some owners have flagged warning light anomalies.

**Electrical Warning Lights and System Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced warning lights illuminating on the dashboard, accompanied by occasional electrical system faults and related safety recalls.
- **Solution:** Verify that all open electrical recalls have been completed by an authorized dealer and inspect wiring harnesses for signs of wear.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V798000: Electrical System:Wiring:Relays/Solenoids](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An electrical short in the relay may cause a vehicle fire with the ignition on or off. A vehicle fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if needed, replace the Solid State Intake Heater Grid Relay ("Relay"); if FCA US Recall ID Y08 (NHTSA Recall ID 21V-163) is open, then insulating material will be applied to the Relay after inspection. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V925000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The side air bag inflatable curtain (SABIC) assemblies may have been manufactured with the incorrect amount of compressed gas, which can result in an improper air bag deployment. An air bag that deploys improperly can increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the SABIC assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V398000: Wheels:Lugs/Nuts/Bolts/Studs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Incorrect information in the Service and Owner's Manuals may cause the flanged lug nuts to be over-tightened, which could result in a broken wheel stud and possible wheel separation. Wheel separation can cause a vehicle crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the torque specification in the owner's manual and other published service documents. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V163000: Electrical System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An electrical short may occur in the Solid State Intake Heater Grid Relay, which could result in a vehicle fire. A vehicle fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if needed, replace the Solid State Intake Heater Grid Relay ("Relay"); if FCA US Recall ID Y08 (NHTSA Recall ID 21V-163) is open, then insulating material will be applied to the Relay after inspection. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V140000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 126, "Electronic Stability Control Systems." An ESC malfunction indicator light that fails to illuminate during an electronic stability control problem can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the ABS control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V835000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Leaking transmission fluid may contact an ignition source within the engine compartment, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the transmission dipstick and transmission vent assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V904000: Structure:Body:Tailgate:Hinge And Attachments](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The tailgate may not latch properly due to misaligned tailgate strikers, which could result in the tailgate opening while driving. An unintended tailgate opening while driving may result in a loss of unsecured cargo, possibly creating a road hazard and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the tailgate striker alignment to the box latch and adjust, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V060000: Electrical System:Wiring:Relays/Solenoids](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=3500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An electrical short in the relay can result in an engine compartment fire with the ignition on or off, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the relay, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Ram 3500 delivers exceptional capability for buyers needing serious towing and payload performance. While most mechanical systems prove robust under tough working conditions, used-car shoppers should verify that all safety recalls have been addressed and perform a detailed inspection of the fuel system, transmission behavior, and electrical components before purchasing.
