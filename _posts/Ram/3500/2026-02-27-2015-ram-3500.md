# 2015 Ram 3500

### Overview
The 2015 Ram 3500 is a heavy-duty pickup truck designed for demanding work and towing. It generally offers robust performance, particularly with its powerful diesel engine options. Reliability for this model year is considered good, with most reported issues being isolated incidents rather than widespread manufacturing defects. Owners often praise its towing capacity and durability. However, like any vehicle, specific components can experience issues over time, especially under heavy use. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance.

### 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel
**Fuel Injector Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, reduced power, increased fuel consumption, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**EGR Cooler Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** White smoke from exhaust, coolant loss, overheating, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the EGR cooler.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

### Summary Verdict
The 2015 Ram 3500 demonstrates solid reliability, especially for a heavy-duty truck. The 6.7L Cummins diesel engine, while generally robust, can occasionally face issues with fuel injectors and EGR coolers, which are common for this type of powertrain under demanding use. The 68RFE automatic transmission might experience torque converter shudder, but manual transmissions are typically very dependable. Other systems like suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components show no significant widespread reliability concerns. Overall, the 2015 Ram 3500 is a capable and reliable workhorse, with most potential issues being manageable and not indicative of systemic flaws.
