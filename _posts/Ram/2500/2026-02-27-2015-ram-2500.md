# 2015 Ram 2500

### Overview
The 2015 Ram 2500 is a heavy-duty pickup truck known for its robust capability and towing prowess. Generally, it offers good reliability, especially concerning its core powertrain components. However, like any vehicle, specific areas can experience issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential weak points and their associated repair costs for different engine and transmission configurations. Owners can use this data to anticipate potential maintenance needs and understand the typical repair landscape for this model year.

### 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel
**Fuel Injector Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, reduced power, poor fuel economy, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1600

**EGR System Clogging**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine performance, black smoke, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of the Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) system components.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200

### Summary Verdict
The 2015 Ram 2500 generally exhibits strong reliability, particularly with its HEMI V8 engine and manual transmission. The Cummins diesel engine, while powerful, can occasionally face issues with fuel injectors and the EGR system, requiring moderate repair costs. The 68RFE automatic transmission may experience torque converter shudder. Electrical gremlins like battery drain are not uncommon but are typically manageable. Overall, the Ram 2500 is a dependable workhorse, with most potential issues being repairable and not indicative of systemic failure. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to any emerging symptoms are key to ensuring long-term reliability.
