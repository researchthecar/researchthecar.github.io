# 2021 Ram 2500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Ram 2500 is a heavy-duty powerhouse known for its impressive towing capability and comfortable interior. However, prospective buyers should thoroughly review its vehicle history, as owner reports point to specific mechanical, electrical, and structural vulnerabilities.

---

## Engine

### 6.4L HEMI V8 Gasoline Engine

The 6.4L HEMI V8 gasoline engine produces approximately 410 horsepower and 429 lb-ft of torque, offering strong conventional performance. Buyers should monitor for intermittent stalling and fuel delivery concerns.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine stalling while driving, often accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the intake and fuel delivery systems, and check for applicable powertrain control module updates.

### 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel Inline-6

The 6.7L Cummins turbo diesel engine delivers robust low-end torque—up to 850 lb-ft—making it ideal for heavy towing. While generally durable, auxiliary components like the EGR system require careful inspection.

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** The exhaust gas recirculation (EGR) cooler can crack, resulting in coolant leaks and a burning smell.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the faulty EGR cooler assembly.

## Transmission

### 68RFE 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 68RFE 6-speed automatic transmission pairs with the Cummins diesel engine to handle heavy towing duties. Buyers should test proper gear engagement across all ranges, especially when fully warmed up.

**Reverse Engagement Failure**
- **Symptoms:** When the transmission reaches operating temperature, shifting into reverse causes the engine to rev without the vehicle moving.
- **Solution:** Inspect internal transmission components, valve body, or rebuild/replace the transmission as necessary.

### ZF 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The ZF 8-speed automatic transmission is paired with the 6.4L HEMI gasoline engine, offering smooth shifting and optimized gear ratios. It generally provides dependable operation if properly maintained.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes and Calipers

Robust braking power is crucial for a heavy-duty truck, but owner reports suggest keeping a close eye on brake hose integrity and trailer brake controller performance.

**Brake Caliper Binding and Hose Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Deteriorated brake hoses can cause internal restrictions, leading to seized brake calipers, uneven pad wear, and pulling.
- **Solution:** Replace failing brake hoses along with damaged brake calipers, pads, and rotors.

**Integrated Brake Controller Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The integrated trailer brake controller may fail to supply braking power to a connected trailer when stopping.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the trailer brake module and associated wiring harness connections.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera and Display

Electrical accessories provide modern convenience, though owners have reported occasional visibility glitches with display screens and reversing cameras.

**Backup Camera Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** The backup camera may fail to activate or display an excessively bright image when engaging reverse at night.
- **Solution:** Check camera wiring connections and software calibration for the infotainment display.

## Exterior

### Dashboard and Tailgate Structure

Interior fit and finish deserve a careful look, particularly regarding dashboard materials and exterior cargo management features like the tailgate.

**Dashboard Delamination**
- **Symptoms:** Leather or vinyl dash covers begin peeling and shrinking near the defroster vents, which may interfere with passenger airbag deployment.
- **Solution:** Replace or repair the dashboard panel assembly.

**Tailgate Unexpected Unlatching**
- **Symptoms:** The tailgate may unlatch and drop unexpectedly while driving, risking cargo loss.
- **Solution:** Ensure all applicable tailgate latch updates and repairs have been properly executed.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety restraint systems must be fully operational; buyers should test all seat belt buckles and retraction mechanisms during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Seat Belt Retraction Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rear seat belts can become stuck in the extended position, failing to retract and potentially trapping occupants.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace malfunctioning seat belt retractor assemblies.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V798000: Electrical System:Wiring:Relays/Solenoids](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=2500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An electrical short in the relay may cause a vehicle fire with the ignition on or off. A vehicle fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if needed, replace the Solid State Intake Heater Grid Relay ("Relay"); if FCA US Recall ID Y08 (NHTSA Recall ID 21V-163) is open, then insulating material will be applied to the Relay after inspection. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V925000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=2500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The side air bag inflatable curtain (SABIC) assemblies may have been manufactured with the incorrect amount of compressed gas, which can result in an improper air bag deployment. An air bag that deploys improperly can increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the SABIC assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V163000: Electrical System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=2500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An electrical short may occur in the Solid State Intake Heater Grid Relay, which could result in a vehicle fire. A vehicle fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if needed, replace the Solid State Intake Heater Grid Relay ("Relay"); if FCA US Recall ID Y08 (NHTSA Recall ID 21V-163) is open, then insulating material will be applied to the Relay after inspection. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V140000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=2500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 126, "Electronic Stability Control Systems." An ESC malfunction indicator light that fails to illuminate during an electronic stability control problem can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the ABS control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V835000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=2500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Leaking transmission fluid may contact an ignition source within the engine compartment, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the transmission dipstick and transmission vent assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V904000: Structure:Body:Tailgate:Hinge And Attachments](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=2500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The tailgate may not latch properly due to misaligned tailgate strikers, which could result in the tailgate opening while driving. An unintended tailgate opening while driving may result in a loss of unsecured cargo, possibly creating a road hazard and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the tailgate striker alignment to the box latch and adjust, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V060000: Electrical System:Wiring:Relays/Solenoids](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=2500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An electrical short in the relay can result in an engine compartment fire with the ignition on or off, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the relay, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V765000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=2500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The steering column control module may cause the driver's side air bag not to deploy as intended. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." A driver's side air bag that does not deploy as intended increases the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the steering column control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Ram 2500 is a capable heavy-duty truck, but buyers should conduct a thorough inspection of the powertrain, brakes, and electrical systems. Verifying that all open safety campaigns and recall services have been completed will help ensure a more reliable ownership experience.
