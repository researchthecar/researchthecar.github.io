# 2020 Ram 2500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Ram 2500 is a heavy-duty pickup known for its impressive towing capacity, robust frame, and upscale interior cabin. Buyers looking at the used market should weigh its strong working capabilities against a few notable owner-reported concerns, particularly surrounding the diesel fuel and electrical systems.

---

## Engine

### 6.4L HEMI V8 Gas Engine

The standard 6.4-liter HEMI V8 gasoline engine produces 410 horsepower and 429 lb-ft of torque, offering strong conventional performance and payload capability for daily hauling.

### 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel I6 Engine

The available 6.7-liter Cummins inline-six turbo diesel delivers up to 400 horsepower and a massive 1,000 lb-ft of torque, making it the preferred choice for heavy towing and long-haul jobs.

**Fuel Pump and High-Pressure Fuel Pump (HPFP) Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report sudden engine stalling, loss of motive power while driving, or failure to start due to fuel pump issues.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of the failing fuel pump or high-pressure fuel pump assembly by a qualified technician.

**Grid Heater Bolt Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A tapping noise that grows louder, potential loose bolts falling into the intake manifold, leading to internal engine damage or cylinder failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the intake grid heater relay/bolt assembly with an updated aftermarket or manufacturer fix to prevent internal contact.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission (6.4L Gas)

Paired with the gas engine, this 8-speed automatic transmission provides smooth gear changes and optimizes power delivery across varying loads.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission (Diesel)

The heavy-duty 6-speed automatic transmission is engineered to handle the massive torque output of the Cummins diesel engine during rigorous towing operations.

## Brakes

### Brake System

While braking performance is generally robust for heavy loads, owners have experienced isolated mechanical issues with fluid leaks and parking brake locking mechanisms.

**Hydro-Boost and Parking Brake Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Brake fluid leaking into the cab from the hydro booster leading to a loss of braking pressure, or parking brake gears failing to lock securely.
- **Solution:** Replace failing hydro booster units or damaged parking brake hardware assemblies.

## Steering

Owner reports indicate occasional electronic power steering assist drops, requiring increased physical effort to control the vehicle safely.

**Power Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of power steering functionality accompanied by a warning message on the dashboard, significantly increasing steering effort.
- **Solution:** Inspect power steering components, electrical connections, and steering modules; replace faulty pumps or electronic steering components as necessary.

## Safety

### Seat Belt

Reports suggest potential mechanical wear in seat belt retractors, which can impact proper fit and passenger safety.

**Seat Belt Retractor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Seat belts failing to retract properly, staying loose, or locking up incorrectly during daily use.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace malfunctioning seat belt retractor assemblies.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V035000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=2500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Reduced visibility increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will tighten the wiper nuts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V622000: Visibility:Rearview Mirrors/Devices:Exterior](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=2500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." Missing driver side mirror glass reduces visibility and increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver side mirror glass, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V191000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=2500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lingering rearview image can distract the driver, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler has notified owners, and dealers will update the radio display software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V043000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=2500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The leaking transmission fluid may contact the turbocharger or another ignition source within the engine compartment, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the transmission valve body separator plate and reprogram the powertrain control module. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V880000: Fuel System, Diesel:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=2500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the HPFP, update the Powertrain Control Module (PCM) software, and inspect and replace additional fuel system components as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V207000: Structure](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=2500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** In certain side loading conditions, the bed step may unexpectedly fail while in use. A bed step that unexpectedly fails while in use increases the risk of injury to the user.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the bed step support brace, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V140000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=2500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 126, "Electronic Stability Control Systems." An ESC malfunction indicator light that fails to illuminate during an electronic stability control problem can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the ABS control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V835000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=2500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Leaking transmission fluid may contact an ignition source within the engine compartment, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the transmission dipstick and transmission vent assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Ram 2500 remains a formidable heavy-duty truck choice for buyers needing serious towing capability and comfort. However, prospective owners should thoroughly inspect diesel fuel systems, verify maintenance records regarding engine heater bolts, and test all electrical and steering features during a pre-purchase inspection to avoid unexpected repair costs.
