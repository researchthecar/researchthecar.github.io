# 2019 Ram 2500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Ram 2500 is a heavy-duty powerhouse offering substantial towing capacity, a plush cabin, and rugged capability. However, used truck buyers should approach with a clear understanding of potential mechanical and electrical hiccups. While its robust powertrains make light work of heavy loads, owners have reported recurring concerns regarding fuel delivery systems, transmission components, and electronics. Careful pre-purchase inspection can help filter out troublesome units and ensure a dependable workhorse.

---

## Engine

### 6.4L HEMI V8

A naturally aspirated gasoline V8 engine pushing approximately 410 horsepower and 429 lb-ft of torque, offering strong conventional towing and responsive everyday performance.

### 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel I6

A heavy-duty inline-six diesel engine delivering immense torque—ranging up to 850 or 1,000 lb-ft depending on the output tier—designed for serious towing and long-haul durability.

### Fuel System

Delivers fuel to the engine under high pressure, though some owners have experienced premature pump wear and unexpected stalls.

**Fuel Pump Failure and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The truck may act like it is running out of fuel, hesitate under acceleration, lose motive power, or stall unexpectedly while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace failing high-pressure fuel pumps or associated fuel system components.

## Transmission

### 68RFE 6-Speed Automatic

A traditional heavy-duty 6-speed automatic transmission paired with select powertrains, known generally for strength but requiring attention to fluid maintenance and internal wear.

**Internal Component Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Catastrophic transmission failure accompanied by loose internal snap rings, loss of drive gears, or persistent fluid leaks from transmission lines.
- **Solution:** Repair or rebuild the transmission with updated internal components and replace leaking cooler lines.

### AS69RC 6-Speed Automatic

An Aisin heavy-duty 6-speed automatic transmission built to manage high-torque Cummins diesel outputs in commercial and high-capacity towing applications.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Body Control

Manages cabin accessories, displays, and security modules, with occasional reports of screen bubbling and module glitches.

**Screen Delamination and RF Hub Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen can delaminate and cause erratic behavior like random phone calls or phantom controls. Additionally, failure of the RF Hub Module can completely immobilize the vehicle, preventing it from cranking or shifting out of Park.
- **Solution:** Replace the delaminated display screen or reprogram/replace the RF Hub Module.

## Exterior

### Tailgate and Cab Sealing

Covers exterior body fitments, weatherstripping, and tailgate hardware.

**Tailgate Latch Malfunction and Water Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** The tailgate may become difficult to operate, fail to open, or unexpectedly drop open while driving. Rear windows can also leak water into the cabin headliner during heavy rain.
- **Solution:** Inspect tailgate hinges and latches for binding or failure, and reseal or replace rear window frame gaskets.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V035000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=2500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Reduced visibility increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will tighten the wiper nuts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V580000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=2500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, a front seat belt retractor may not properly slow the occupant's forward movement after locking. In the event of a crash, if the front seat belts fail to lock, there is an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front seat belt retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V191000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=2500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." The lingering rearview image can distract the driver, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler has notified owners, and dealers will update the radio display software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V043000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=2500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The leaking transmission fluid may contact the turbocharger or another ignition source within the engine compartment, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the transmission valve body separator plate and reprogram the powertrain control module. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V498000: Fuel System, Diesel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=2500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the fuel lines, replacing them if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V880000: Fuel System, Diesel:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=2500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the HPFP, update the Powertrain Control Module (PCM) software, and inspect and replace additional fuel system components as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V207000: Structure](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=2500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** In certain side loading conditions, the bed step may unexpectedly fail while in use. A bed step that unexpectedly fails while in use increases the risk of injury to the user.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the bed step support brace, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V635000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=2500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The swollen seals can cause the brakes to drag, potentially overheating the brakes and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** FCA US will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front brake calipers and brake hoses and bleed the brakes, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Ram 2500 is a capable and highly capable heavy-duty truck, but prospective buyers should perform a thorough mechanical and electrical check before purchasing. Pay close attention to fuel system reliability, transmission shifting behavior, and potential water leaks around the cab or tailgate. Ensuring that all past recall and maintenance records are up to date will significantly improve your ownership experience.
