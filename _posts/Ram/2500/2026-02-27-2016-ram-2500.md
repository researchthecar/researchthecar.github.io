# 2016 Ram 2500

### Overview
The 2016 Ram 2500 is a heavy-duty pickup truck known for its robust capability and towing prowess. Reliability for this model year is generally considered strong, particularly with its proven powertrain options. Owners often praise its workhorse nature, but like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen more attention than others. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective buyers or current owners.

### 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel
**Fuel Injector Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, reduced power, poor fuel economy, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**EGR System Clogging**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine performance, black smoke, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of EGR components.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Ram 2500 demonstrates solid reliability, especially for a heavy-duty truck. The 6.4L HEMI V8 engine is particularly robust with no significant widespread issues reported. The 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel, while generally dependable, can occasionally experience issues with fuel injectors and EGR system clogging, which are common for diesel engines of this era. Transmission reliability is good, though some owners have reported torque converter shudder with the automatic transmission. Electrical and interior components are generally well-behaved, with only minor reports of interior light issues. Overall, the 2016 Ram 2500 is a capable and reliable choice for those needing heavy-duty performance, with potential buyers advised to be aware of the specific diesel engine maintenance needs.
