# 2019 Ram 4500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 Ram 4500, buyers are looking at a heavy-duty commercial truck designed for demanding towing and hauling tasks. While these medium-duty chassis cabs are built to handle rigorous workloads, reviewing owner feedback and known mechanical traits is crucial before making a purchase. Prospective buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle's powertrain and fuel systems to ensure long-term dependability on the job.

---

## Engine

### 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel I6

The 6.7-liter Cummins inline-six turbo diesel engine provides robust torque outputs tailored for heavy commercial applications. It is paired with heavy-duty transmissions designed to manage substantial gross vehicle weight ratings.

**Fuel Pump Failure and Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported premature fuel pump failure and fuel leaks, presenting potential fire hazards and leaving vehicles inoperable for extended periods.
- **Solution:** Have the fuel system professionally inspected. Replace damaged fuel pumps and leaking lines with updated components.

## Transmission

### Aisin 6-Speed Heavy-Duty Automatic

This heavy-duty automatic transmission is engineered to handle high torque loads associated with commercial towing. Regular fluid maintenance is essential to ensure long-term durability in fleet or heavy-use environments.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V439000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=4500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The engine calibration software may not warn about low oil volume. Low oil can damage the engine, causing an oil leak or a punctured engine block, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the engine calibration software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V035000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=4500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Reduced visibility increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will tighten the wiper nuts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V498000: Fuel System, Diesel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=4500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the fuel lines, replacing them if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V023000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=4500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A worn rear brake caliper flexible line may cause brake fluid leakage which may result in a partial or complete loss of the rear brakes, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Excellance will notify owners, and dealers will install a kit to remedy any damage and to prevent any future damage, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V880000: Fuel System, Diesel:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=4500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the HPFP, update the Powertrain Control Module (PCM) software, and inspect and replace additional fuel system components as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V398000: Wheels:Lugs/Nuts/Bolts/Studs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=4500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Incorrect information in the Service and Owner's Manuals may cause the flanged lug nuts to be over-tightened, which could result in a broken wheel stud and possible wheel separation. Wheel separation can cause a vehicle crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the torque specification in the owner's manual and other published service documents. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V511000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=4500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** This can result in engine damage and connecting rod failure, which could potentially puncture the engine block. A damaged engine block may leak oil, which can contact hot engine or exhaust components, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will flash the engine calibration software to enhance the engine warm up protection, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20E031000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=4500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these parts fail to comply with Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 105, "Hydraulic and Electric Brake Systems." A worn rear brake caliper flexible line may cause brake fluid leakage which may result in a partial or complete loss of the rear brakes, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Ram 4500 is a powerhouse built for commercial utility, but used buyers should proceed with a thorough mechanical pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to the diesel fuel system and verify that all necessary service history and recall work have been properly addressed by a certified dealer.
