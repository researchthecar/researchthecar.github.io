# 2019 Ram 4500

The 2019 Ram 4500 is a heavy-duty truck designed for commercial and demanding vocational use. Reliability is a key consideration for vehicles in this class, as downtime can significantly impact business operations. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, examining common problem areas reported by owners and technicians. ### 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel
**DEF System Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine light illumination, reduced engine power, stalling, issues with emissions compliance.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement of Diesel Exhaust Fluid (DEF) tank, pump, injector, or sensors.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises, black smoke from exhaust, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential repair or replacement of the turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Ram 4500 generally exhibits good reliability, particularly with its core powertrain components. The 6.4L HEMI V8 and both transmission options (68RFE and Aisin AS69RC) show no widespread reported issues. The 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel, while robust, can occasionally experience issues related to its Diesel Exhaust Fluid (DEF) system and turbocharger, which are common in modern diesel trucks and require specialized diagnosis and repair. Electrical systems can sometimes present intermittent faults, necessitating thorough diagnostic checks. Overall, for a heavy-duty commercial vehicle, the 2019 Ram 4500 offers a solid reliability record, with most potential issues being manageable and related to complex emissions or turbo systems.
