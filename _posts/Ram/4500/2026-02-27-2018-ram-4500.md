# 2018 Ram 4500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used heavy-duty commercial truck like the 2018 Ram 4500, evaluating its powertrain options and historical owner feedback is essential for long-term reliability. Commercial vehicles endure heavy workloads, making it crucial to inspect systems like the engine, transmission, and drivetrain thoroughly before purchase. While heavy-duty trucks are built to withstand demanding tasks, prospective buyers should review available owner reports and open recalls to ensure proper maintenance and safety compliance.

---

## Engine

### 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel I6

The 6.7-liter Cummins inline-six turbo diesel produces high torque designed for heavy towing and commercial applications. It is generally robust, but buyers should monitor emissions equipment and fuel delivery components closely.

**Motive Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience sudden illumination of the check engine light followed by a total loss of motive power while driving.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose error codes and inspect the fuel and emissions systems for failure.

## Transmission

### Aisin 6-Speed Heavy-Duty Automatic Transmission

The Aisin six-speed automatic transmission is engineered specifically for heavy-duty commercial medium-duty applications. It offers durable gear sets to handle high torque outputs, though regular fluid maintenance is critical.

### 68RFE 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 68RFE six-speed automatic transmission is a common pairing for standard commercial duty cycles. Proper cooling and fluid changes help ensure longevity under heavy loads.

### Front Drive Shaft and Wheels

Drivetrain components experience high stress in four-wheel-drive medium-duty chassis applications and warrant a pre-purchase lift inspection.

**Drive Shaft and Wheel Fracture**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual vibrations or sudden mechanical failure can occur if the front drive shaft u-joint or aluminum wheels fracture.
- **Solution:** Inspect the front drive shaft, universal joints, and wheel assemblies for signs of structural cracking or wear.

## Safety

### Hydraulic Service Brakes

The hydraulic service brake system on commercial-duty trucks requires careful inspection for wear and active safety campaign compliance.

**Pending Recall Remedy Delays**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have received safety notifications regarding hydraulic service brakes where replacement parts may be temporarily unavailable.
- **Solution:** Check the vehicle identification number (VIN) with a certified dealer to track recall remedy availability and status.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V277000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=4500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the ferrules corrode, the brake fluid may leak and cause a loss of braking, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the brake hoses and replace them if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=4500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V398000: Wheels:Lugs/Nuts/Bolts/Studs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=4500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Incorrect information in the Service and Owner's Manuals may cause the flanged lug nuts to be over-tightened, which could result in a broken wheel stud and possible wheel separation. Wheel separation can cause a vehicle crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the torque specification in the owner's manual and other published service documents. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V236000: Exterior Lighting:Brake Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=4500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." If the brake lights incorrectly illuminate, it can confuse other drivers, possibly increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 18V100000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=4500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Pushing the brake pedal for prolonged periods when the vehicle is running and in PARK may cause the Brake Transmission Shift Interlock (BTSI) pin to stick in the open position. Being able to shift the transmission without pushing the brake pedal and/or without a key in the ignition can increase the risk of an unintended vehicle rollaway that may result in.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners and dealers will update the Body Control Module software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V824000: Equipment](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=4500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Chrysler (FCA US LLC) is recalling various Dodge, Chrysler, and RAM vehicles equipped with Kidde Plastic-Handle or Push Button 'Pindicator' Fire Extinguishers. These extinguishers may become clogged, preventing the extinguisher from discharging as expected or requiring excessive force to activate the extinguisher.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V896000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=4500&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A loss of anti-lock brakes, electronic stability control, or traction control can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the brake HCU, or replace the HCU motor, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Ram 4500 is a highly capable commercial platform, but used buyers should perform a rigorous pre-purchase inspection focusing on the heavy-duty powertrain, braking systems, and any open safety recalls. Ensuring that all maintenance records are transparent and that mechanical components are free of excessive wear will help secure a dependable work truck.
