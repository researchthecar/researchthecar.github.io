# 2016 Ram 4500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Ram 4500 is a heavy-duty chassis cab truck designed for demanding commercial applications, featuring robust towing and hauling capabilities. While standard reliability data is relatively sparse for this heavy-duty tier, used-car buyers should carefully inspect structural elements and foundational powertrain components prior to purchase.

---

## Engine

### 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel I6

This 6.7-liter inline-six turbo diesel engine typically produces around 320 horsepower and heavy low-end torque, making it well-suited for intense towing duties. It is generally regarded as a durable workhorse, though proper maintenance of the emissions and fuel systems is crucial for long-term reliability.

## Transmission

### Aisin 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

This heavy-duty six-speed automatic transmission is engineered to handle massive torque loads in commercial applications. It offers strong durability when properly serviced, though buyers should verify fluid condition and shifting performance under load.

## Exterior

### Rear Wheel Wells

Suspension and frame components are built for heavy commercial payloads, but physical wear and environmental exposure can lead to localized metal fatigue.

**Rear Wheel Well Fractures**
- **Symptoms:** Owners may notice structural cracking or fracturing around the metal wheel wells located over the rear tires during routine inspections.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the rear wheel well structure for cracking, and consult a professional body shop or fabricator for necessary reinforcement or repair.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=4500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V398000: Wheels:Lugs/Nuts/Bolts/Studs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=4500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Incorrect information in the Service and Owner's Manuals may cause the flanged lug nuts to be over-tightened, which could result in a broken wheel stud and possible wheel separation. Wheel separation can cause a vehicle crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the torque specification in the owner's manual and other published service documents. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V824000: Equipment](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=4500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Chrysler (FCA US LLC) is recalling various Dodge, Chrysler, and RAM vehicles equipped with Kidde Plastic-Handle or Push Button 'Pindicator' Fire Extinguishers. These extinguishers may become clogged, preventing the extinguisher from discharging as expected or requiring excessive force to activate the extinguisher.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 17V821000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Lever And Linkage:Column Shift](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=4500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Pushing the brake pedal for prolonged periods when the vehicle is running and in PARK may cause the Brake Transmission Shift Interlock (BTSI) pin to stick in the open position. Being able to shift the transmission without pushing the brake pedal and/or without a key in the ignition can increase the risk of an unintended vehicle rollaway that may result in.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will update the vehicle software, inspect the BTSI, and, as necessary, replace it, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V562000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Diesel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=4500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The leaking water pump can increase the risk of an engine compartment fire.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the water pumps, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V495000: Power Train:Transfer Case (4-Wheel Drive)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=4500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The transfer case may have been manufactured with a misshapen main output shaft, creating voids that may cause a shaft fracture. If the main output shaft fractures, the vehicle may lose motive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the transfer case, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V592000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=4500&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, the Engine Control Module (ECM) may short circuit, causing the engine to stall without warning. An engine that stalls without warning increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the ECM, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2016 Ram 4500, a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the chassis, commercial upfits, and structural integrity is essential. Ensuring the heavy-duty powertrain and suspension have been maintained properly will help secure a dependable commercial vehicle.
