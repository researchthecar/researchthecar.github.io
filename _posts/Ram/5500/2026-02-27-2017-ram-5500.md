# 2017 Ram 5500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 Ram 5500, prospective buyers are looking at a heavy-duty commercial chassis cab designed for intense towing and payload demands. While its rugged build serves fleet and commercial applications well, owner reports indicate potential pain points ranging from drivetrain and transfer case concerns to electrical and brake system glitches. Reviewing maintenance records and verifying the completion of any applicable safety campaigns is crucial when evaluating these hardworking commercial trucks.

---

## Engine

### 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel I6

The 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel inline-six delivers robust low-end torque well-suited for heavy-duty commercial applications. It is generally regarded as a durable workhorse, though supporting electrical accessories require routine attention.

**Unexpected Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle shuts off unexpectedly while running and fails to restart.
- **Solution:** Inspect electrical fuses and replace faulty fuel system components as needed.

### 6.4L HEMI V8 Gasoline Engine

The 6.4L HEMI V8 gasoline engine offers strong horsepower and reliable performance for conventional commercial configurations. It provides an alternative for buyers who prefer gasoline power over diesel maintenance requirements.

## Transmission

### Aisin 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The heavy-duty Aisin 6-speed automatic transmission is engineered to manage high towing capacities and demanding commercial duty cycles. Proper fluid maintenance is essential to ensure long-term durability in fleet service.

### 66FE 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 66FE 6-speed automatic transmission pairs with gasoline engine configurations for commercial applications. It offers smooth shifting under load, though regular inspection is recommended for high-mileage work trucks.

### Transfer Case and Front Driveshaft

Owner reports highlight significant concerns with heavy-duty drivetrain components, notably transfer case fractures and parking brake engagement failures.

**Transfer Case Fracture and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loud banging noises at highway speeds followed by sudden drivetrain vibration, fluid leaks, or total transfer case housing fracture.
- **Solution:** Inspect the transfer case and front driveshaft assembly for physical damage or wear; replace fractured housings and failed driveshafts.

**Rollaway After Parking**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle rolls away after being shifted into park when the parking brake fails to hold the truck stationary.
- **Solution:** Inspect and adjust the parking brake mechanism or replace worn mechanical parking pawl components.

## Suspension

### Front Tires and Wheels

Commercial chassis cabs endure heavy front-end loads, making suspension alignment and tire maintenance critical to prevent premature wear.

**Rapid Front Tire Wear and Blowouts**
- **Symptoms:** Accelerated or uneven tread wear on front tires, potentially leading to unexpected sudden deflation or blowouts at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Check front-end alignment specifications frequently and replace worn commercial-grade tires proactively.

## Brakes

### ABS Pump and Hydraulic System

Braking system reliability is vital for a heavy commercial chassis cab, but owners have reported premature ABS pump faults and electronic warning lights.

**ABS Pump and Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** ABS warning lights illuminate, traction control disables, and cruise control becomes inoperative.
- **Solution:** Diagnose hydraulic brake codes and replace faulty ABS pump and control module assemblies.
- **Cost:** $1459

## Steering

### Steering Alignment

Steering geometry on heavy-duty trucks requires precise alignment to counteract commercial loads and uneven road surfaces.

**Uneven Outer Tread Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable abnormal wear on the outer edge of the right front steering tire despite factory-spec alignment settings.
- **Solution:** Perform thorough front-end component checks and adjust toe or caster settings beyond standard factory ranges if commercial loading requires it.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V332000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** These vehicles are being recalled to address a defect that could prevent the cruise control system from disengaging. If, when using cruise control, there is a short circuit within the vehicle's wiring, the driver may not be able to shut off the cruise control either by depressing the brake pedal or manually turning the system off once it has been engaged.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the software, and perform a software flash on the engine or powertrain control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V398000: Wheels:Lugs/Nuts/Bolts/Studs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Incorrect information in the Service and Owner's Manuals may cause the flanged lug nuts to be over-tightened, which could result in a broken wheel stud and possible wheel separation. Wheel separation can cause a vehicle crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the torque specification in the owner's manual and other published service documents. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V821000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Lever And Linkage:Column Shift](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Pushing the brake pedal for prolonged periods when the vehicle is running and in PARK may cause the Brake Transmission Shift Interlock (BTSI) pin to stick in the open position. Being able to shift the transmission without pushing the brake pedal and/or without a key in the ignition can increase the risk of an unintended vehicle rollaway that may result in.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will update the vehicle software, inspect the BTSI, and, as necessary, replace it, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V100000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Pushing the brake pedal for prolonged periods when the vehicle is running and in PARK may cause the Brake Transmission Shift Interlock (BTSI) pin to stick in the open position. Being able to shift the transmission without pushing the brake pedal and/or without a key in the ignition can increase the risk of an unintended vehicle rollaway that may result in.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners and dealers will update the Body Control Module software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V824000: Equipment](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Chrysler (FCA US LLC) is recalling various Dodge, Chrysler, and RAM vehicles equipped with Kidde Plastic-Handle or Push Button 'Pindicator' Fire Extinguishers. These extinguishers may become clogged, preventing the extinguisher from discharging as expected or requiring excessive force to activate the extinguisher.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 17V562000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Diesel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The leaking water pump can increase the risk of an engine compartment fire.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the water pumps, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V896000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A loss of anti-lock brakes, electronic stability control, or traction control can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the brake HCU, or replace the HCU motor, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Ram 5500 is a powerful commercial work truck built for demanding towing and hauling duties, but used buyers must approach with a thorough inspection mindset. Particular attention should be paid to drivetrain integrity, including transfer case condition and parking brake operation, alongside brake system electronics and front tire wear. Verifying service histories and ensuring all safety recalls have been addressed will help mitigate unexpected out-of-pocket repair expenses.
