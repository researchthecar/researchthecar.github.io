# 2021 Ram 5500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Ram 5500 is a heavy-duty commercial chassis cab designed for demanding work applications, offering robust towing and hauling capabilities. While generally utilized in heavy commercial fleets, prospective used buyers should carefully inspect individual vehicles for mechanical integrity and prior wear. Reviewing owner feedback provides initial insights into potential trouble spots, though individual vehicle maintenance history remains paramount.

---

## Engine

### 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel I6

This inline-six diesel engine produces substantial torque tailored for heavy towing and commercial duty. It is widely relied upon in medium-duty applications, though routine maintenance of the fuel and emissions systems is critical for longevity.

### 6.4L HEMI V8

This gasoline V8 engine delivers strong horsepower and dependable performance for commercial upfits requiring high payload capacities. It serves as a traditional alternative to the diesel option in fleet operations.

### Engine Compartment Wiring and Components

Commercial chassis cabs feature complex electrical wiring to support upfits and heavy-duty accessories, requiring thorough checks for chafing or thermal stress.

**Under-Hood Smoke**
- **Symptoms:** Operators may notice white or gray smoke emitting from under the hood during vehicle operation due to potential electrical or mechanical overheating.
- **Solution:** Trace the source of the smoke, inspect engine bay wiring harnesses and fluid lines, and repair or replace damaged parts.

## Transmission

### Aisin 6-Speed Heavy-Duty Automatic

Paired primarily with the high-output diesel engine, this commercial-grade automatic transmission is built to manage heavy loads. Proper fluid maintenance is essential to ensure long-term durability in severe service use.

### ZF 8-Speed Automatic

Found behind gas engine configurations, this multi-speed automatic provides responsive shifting and optimized gearing for fuel efficiency and power delivery. It offers smooth operation across a wide range of commercial workloads.

## Suspension

### Wheel Studs and Hubs

Heavy-duty commercial trucks undergo extreme wheel loads, making regular inspection of wheel hardware and suspension components vital for safe operation.

**Wheel Stud Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Wheel studs can fracture while driving, potentially causing wheel separation and subsequent damage to brakes, rotors, and wheels.
- **Solution:** Inspect all wheel studs and lug nuts for signs of stretching or damage, and replace compromised components immediately.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V798000: Electrical System:Wiring:Relays/Solenoids](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An electrical short in the relay may cause a vehicle fire with the ignition on or off. A vehicle fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if needed, replace the Solid State Intake Heater Grid Relay ("Relay"); if FCA US Recall ID Y08 (NHTSA Recall ID 21V-163) is open, then insulating material will be applied to the Relay after inspection. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V398000: Wheels:Lugs/Nuts/Bolts/Studs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Incorrect information in the Service and Owner's Manuals may cause the flanged lug nuts to be over-tightened, which could result in a broken wheel stud and possible wheel separation. Wheel separation can cause a vehicle crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the torque specification in the owner's manual and other published service documents. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V163000: Electrical System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An electrical short may occur in the Solid State Intake Heater Grid Relay, which could result in a vehicle fire. A vehicle fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if needed, replace the Solid State Intake Heater Grid Relay ("Relay"); if FCA US Recall ID Y08 (NHTSA Recall ID 21V-163) is open, then insulating material will be applied to the Relay after inspection. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V060000: Electrical System:Wiring:Relays/Solenoids](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An electrical short in the relay can result in an engine compartment fire with the ignition on or off, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the relay, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V765000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The steering column control module may cause the driver's side air bag not to deploy as intended. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." A driver's side air bag that does not deploy as intended increases the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the steering column control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2021 Ram 5500, buyers should focus heavily on the vehicle's specific commercial upfit, service history, and prior workload. While major powertrain options are built for heavy commercial use, individual owner reports highlight the importance of inspecting wheel hardware and under-hood components. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a commercial truck specialist is strongly recommended.
