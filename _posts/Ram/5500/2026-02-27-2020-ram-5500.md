# 2020 Ram 5500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used heavy-duty commercial truck like the 2020 Ram 5500, understanding potential reliability concerns is vital for business operations and heavy hauling. While these chassis cab trucks are built to handle extreme commercial workloads, owner reports highlight specific areas involving brakes, safety systems, and driveline components that warrant a thorough pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel I6

Powered by a heavy-duty 6.7-liter inline-six turbo diesel engine delivering up to 360 horsepower and an immense 800 lb-ft of torque, this powerplant is designed for severe commercial towing and payload duties. It generally offers robust long-term durability when properly maintained, though buyers should verify routine servicing histories.

## Transmission

### Aisin 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with the heavy-duty Aisin six-speed automatic transmission, this drivetrain option is engineered specifically to handle high gross combined weight ratings and demanding commercial applications. It provides dependable shifting performance under heavy loads, provided fluid changes have been kept up to date.

### Transfer Case

Four-wheel-drive models rely on automated transfer cases that require careful diagnostic checks to ensure reliable engagement during heavy work cycles.

**Unexpected Transfer Case Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly shift into neutral while driving at highway speeds, resulting in a sudden loss of power to the wheels.
- **Solution:** Diagnose transfer case electronic actuator and software calibration modules for faults.

## Suspension

### Dual Wheels and Tires

The dual-wheel rear setup supports heavy commercial payloads, making rigorous torque checks and wheel end inspections essential for safe operation.

**Wheel and Tire Separation**
- **Symptoms:** Dual rear wheels or tires may unexpectedly loosen or detach from the vehicle while driving at various speeds.
- **Solution:** Ensure lug nuts are properly torqued to manufacturer specifications and inspect wheel hubs for wear or stud damage during routine maintenance.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brake System

The hydraulic brake system is engineered for heavy stopping power, but owner reports indicate potential electronic stability and stopping responsiveness concerns under heavy loads.

**Brake System and Stability Control Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected electronic stabilization warning lights illuminating during crosswinds or experiencing reduced braking effectiveness while trailering.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the hydraulic brake lines, anti-lock brake sensors, and electronic stability modules for proper operation.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety equipment is critical in commercial vehicles, though isolated owner complaints suggest potential mechanical failures in seat belt retraction during sudden stops.

**Seat Belt Retraction Failure**
- **Symptoms:** During sudden braking maneuvers, the front driver-side seat belt may fail to properly retract or secure.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt retractor assemblies and replace any malfunctioning mechanisms immediately.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V439000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The engine calibration software may not warn about low oil volume. Low oil can damage the engine, causing an oil leak or a punctured engine block, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the engine calibration software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V035000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Reduced visibility increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will tighten the wiper nuts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V023000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A worn rear brake caliper flexible line may cause brake fluid leakage which may result in a partial or complete loss of the rear brakes, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Excellance will notify owners, and dealers will install a kit to remedy any damage and to prevent any future damage, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V880000: Fuel System, Diesel:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the HPFP, update the Powertrain Control Module (PCM) software, and inspect and replace additional fuel system components as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V398000: Wheels:Lugs/Nuts/Bolts/Studs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Incorrect information in the Service and Owner's Manuals may cause the flanged lug nuts to be over-tightened, which could result in a broken wheel stud and possible wheel separation. Wheel separation can cause a vehicle crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the torque specification in the owner's manual and other published service documents. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V511000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** This can result in engine damage and connecting rod failure, which could potentially puncture the engine block. A damaged engine block may leak oil, which can contact hot engine or exhaust components, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will flash the engine calibration software to enhance the engine warm up protection, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V016000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A worn rear brake caliper flexible line may cause brake fluid leakage, which may result in a partial or complete loss of the rear brakes, increasing the risk of a crash.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Ram 5500 is a formidable commercial-grade workhorse capable of managing demanding towing and upfit configurations. Prospective buyers should carefully examine the braking system, verify wheel lug torque and hub integrity, and ensure all powertrain components operate smoothly during a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection.
