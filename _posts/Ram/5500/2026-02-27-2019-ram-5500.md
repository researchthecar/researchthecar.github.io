# 2019 Ram 5500 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 Ram 5500, buyers are looking at a heavy-duty commercial chassis cab designed for demanding towing and upfit applications. While these medium-duty trucks provide immense capability, owner reports and complaint data highlight a few mechanical and electrical areas worth monitoring during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel I6

The 6.7-liter Cummins inline-six turbodiesel is the primary powerhouse for the Ram 5500, delivering high torque outputs suited for heavy commercial pulling. Buyers should carefully evaluate the fuel and emissions systems, as diesel-related components can be costly to repair if neglected.

**Fuel Pump and Fuel System Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected acceleration loss, loud noises from the fuel system, and complete loss of fuel pressure leading to stalled engines.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the high-pressure fuel pump and fuel system components for signs of wear or contamination.

**DEF System and Limp Mode**
- **Symptoms:** Issues with the Diesel Exhaust Fluid (DEF) system can cause the truck to enter limp mode, severely restricting speed and eventually risking complete shutdown.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the DEF system sensors and injectors, and ensure all relevant software updates have been applied.

## Transmission

### Aisin 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The heavy-duty Aisin 6-speed automatic transmission is engineered to handle substantial gross vehicle weight ratings and demanding commercial duty cycles. Routine fluid changes are essential to maintain long-term durability in commercial applications.

### Transfer Case

Four-wheel-drive models utilize a transfer case designed to distribute power to the front axle under heavy loads. Buyers should test four-wheel-drive engagement thoroughly during a test drive.

**Unintended Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** The transfer case may unexpectedly shift itself into neutral while driving at highway speeds, resulting in a sudden loss of propulsion and redlining RPMs.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic transfer case actuator and control module for intermittent faults or wiring damage.

## Suspension

### Tires

Heavy-duty commercial tires endure extreme weight loads and must be monitored closely for wear and structural integrity.

**Tread Separation**
- **Symptoms:** Commercial tires on heavy-duty trucks can experience unexpected tread detachment at highway speeds without prior warning lights.
- **Solution:** Check tire manufacturing dates, tread depth, and inflation pressures regularly, replacing worn commercial-grade tires promptly.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Service Brakes

The hydraulic service braking system is vital for stopping a heavily loaded medium-duty chassis cab. Proper brake maintenance and ABS functionality are crucial for safe operation.

**Brake Lockup on Wet Surfaces**
- **Symptoms:** Applying the service brakes while driving on wet or slippery surfaces can cause the brakes to lock up, leading to vehicle slide and loss of directional control.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and the anti-lock braking system (ABS) module for proper operation.

## Exterior

### Flatbed and Side-Gates

Upfitted flatbed bodies and utility boxes vary widely by vehicle; inspecting the physical condition of latches and tie-downs is an important part of a used commercial truck purchase.

**Side-Gate Latch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Flatbed side-gates may fail to lock securely in place, creating a fall hazard for operators working on the bed.
- **Solution:** Inspect and manually verify all flatbed latches and hinges before loading or unloading cargo.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V439000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The engine calibration software may not warn about low oil volume. Low oil can damage the engine, causing an oil leak or a punctured engine block, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the engine calibration software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V035000: Visibility:Windshield Wiper/Washer:Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Reduced visibility increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will tighten the wiper nuts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V498000: Fuel System, Diesel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the fuel lines, replacing them if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V023000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A worn rear brake caliper flexible line may cause brake fluid leakage which may result in a partial or complete loss of the rear brakes, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Excellance will notify owners, and dealers will install a kit to remedy any damage and to prevent any future damage, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V880000: Fuel System, Diesel:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the HPFP, update the Powertrain Control Module (PCM) software, and inspect and replace additional fuel system components as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V398000: Wheels:Lugs/Nuts/Bolts/Studs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Incorrect information in the Service and Owner's Manuals may cause the flanged lug nuts to be over-tightened, which could result in a broken wheel stud and possible wheel separation. Wheel separation can cause a vehicle crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the torque specification in the owner's manual and other published service documents. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V511000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** This can result in engine damage and connecting rod failure, which could potentially puncture the engine block. A damaged engine block may leak oil, which can contact hot engine or exhaust components, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will flash the engine calibration software to enhance the engine warm up protection, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20E031000: Suspension:Rear](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=5500&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these parts fail to comply with Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 105, "Hydraulic and Electric Brake Systems." A worn rear brake caliper flexible line may cause brake fluid leakage which may result in a partial or complete loss of the rear brakes, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Ram 5500 is a capable and powerful commercial vehicle, but prospective buyers should approach used models with attention to their specific work history. Thoroughly inspecting the diesel fuel system, emissions equipment, transfer case, and brake components will help ensure dependable service on the job.
