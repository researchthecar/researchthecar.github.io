# 2017 Ram ProMaster City

### Overview
The 2017 Ram ProMaster City is a compact commercial van designed for urban delivery and cargo hauling. It prioritizes practicality and fuel efficiency, often featuring a straightforward powertrain. Reliability data for this model year indicates a generally robust performance, with most reported issues being isolated incidents rather than widespread systemic failures. Owners and fleet managers often praise its maneuverability and cargo capacity for its size. However, like any vehicle, specific components can experience issues over time, particularly those related to heavy use in commercial environments. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance.

---


## Engine

### 2.4L Tigershark MultiAir 2 (I4)
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil spots under the vehicle, low oil level warnings, burning oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seals, gaskets, or oil pan.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, coolant loss, visible coolant puddles, sweet smell.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of radiator, hoses, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


## Transmission

### 948TE 9-Speed Automatic
**Harsh Shifting / Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky gear changes, delayed engagement, difficulty shifting between gears.
- **Solution:** Software updates, transmission fluid flush and fill, or internal component repair.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid from transmission seals or lines, burning smell, slipping gears.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seals, lines, or transmission pan gasket.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Ram ProMaster City demonstrates a generally good reliability record for its class. The primary concerns tend to revolve around the powertrain, specifically the 2.4L Tigershark engine and the 9-speed automatic transmission, with reports of oil leaks, cooling system issues, and occasional transmission shifting problems. Electrical gremlins like battery drain and infotainment glitches are also noted, though not universally prevalent. Suspension, exterior, and interior components appear to be relatively trouble-free based on available data. For commercial use, proactive maintenance and prompt attention to any early signs of powertrain issues are recommended to ensure optimal uptime and longevity.
