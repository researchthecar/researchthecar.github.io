# 2017 Ram ProMaster City Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Ram ProMaster City is a functional compact cargo and passenger van favored by delivery fleets and small businesses for its city-friendly footprint and cargo capacity. However, prospective buyers should review used-car reliability carefully, as owners have logged recurring complaints regarding powertrain durability, electrical quirks, and premature brake or suspension wear. Knowing what to inspect can help you spot a well-maintained work van and avoid costly surprise repairs.

---

## Engine

### 3.6L V6 Pentastar Engine

The 3.6-liter Pentastar V6 engine produces roughly 280 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, supplying strong acceleration for hauling heavy loads. While generally robust, higher-mileage work units can occasionally suffer from internal wear or oil consumption issues.

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Gray smoke from the exhaust pipe, illuminated check engine light, and failing oil consumption tests due to piston or ring wear.
- **Solution:** Perform a professional diagnostic oil consumption test and replace worn pistons or motor components if affected.

### 2.4L Tigershark 4-Cylinder Engine

Standard in the smaller ProMaster City variant, the 2.4-liter Tigershark four-cylinder engine prioritizes fuel economy over heavy towing. Routine fluid checks are recommended to ensure long-term dependability.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a 6-speed automatic transmission, these vans handle stop-and-go delivery duty frequently. Owners have reported major drivetrain failures and sudden loss of propulsion, making a thorough pre-purchase transmission test drive essential.

**Sudden Transmission Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Abrupt loss of drive power on the highway or major mechanical noises requiring complete transmission replacement or rebuild.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition, check for shifting delays, and replace or rebuild the transmission unit if catastrophic mechanical failure occurs.
- **Cost:** $4096.04

## Brakes

### Parking Brake and Service Brakes

The braking system must contend with heavy commercial payloads. Owners have noted dangerous instances where the parking brake remains engaged without clear driver notification, leading to severe component damage.

**Parking Brake Dragging and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Parking brake fails to disengage fully, causing overheated rear brakes, glowing rotors, burning smells, and dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect and adjust the parking brake cable assembly and replace damaged brake pads and rotors.
- **Cost:** $6000

## Steering

### Front Suspension and Steering

Front-end components take a beating in urban delivery environments. Suspension bearing plates and CV joints can wear out or rust prematurely, leading to steering pull and vibrations.

**Upper Bearing Plate Corrosion and CV Axle Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Steering wheel jiggle, tire wobble at highway speeds, and suspension springs failing to return to center causing the van to pull.
- **Solution:** Replace rusted upper bearing plates and worn front CV axles.
- **Cost:** $1500.00

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting and Headlights

Electrical complaints center heavily on exterior lighting circuits, particularly frequent headlight bulb failures that require ongoing maintenance.

**Frequent Headlight Bulb Burnout**
- **Symptoms:** Low beam headlights burning out repeatedly every few months with limited warning to the driver.
- **Solution:** Check headlight sockets for corrosion and install heavy-duty replacement bulbs designed to withstand vibration.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V824000: Equipment](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=ProMaster+City&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Chrysler (FCA US LLC) is recalling various Dodge, Chrysler, and RAM vehicles equipped with Kidde Plastic-Handle or Push Button 'Pindicator' Fire Extinguishers. These extinguishers may become clogged, preventing the extinguisher from discharging as expected or requiring excessive force to activate the extinguisher.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 16V953000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=ProMaster+City&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** This vehicle has side curtain air bags that may not inflate properly in the event of a crash. If the side curtain air bags do not inflate properly, the vehicle occupants are at an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 17V601000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Ram&model=ProMaster+City&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." The incorrect tire placard can cause the operator to exceed the vehicle's weight capacity, or inflate the tires to an incorrect pressure, affecting vehicle handling, which can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Chrysler will notify owners, and dealers will replace the incorrect tire placard with a corrected one, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Ram ProMaster City offers practical utility for commercial buyers, but used shoppers should approach with caution. Be sure to thoroughly inspect the transmission for smooth shifting, test the parking brake mechanism, check front steering and suspension components, and verify that all electrical lighting works properly before finalizing a purchase.
