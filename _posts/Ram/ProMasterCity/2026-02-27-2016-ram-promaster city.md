# 2016 Ram ProMaster City

### Overview
The 2016 Ram ProMaster City is a compact commercial van designed for urban delivery and cargo transport. It prioritizes practicality and fuel efficiency, often featuring a robust yet simple mechanical setup. Reliability data for this model year indicates a generally solid performance, with most reported issues being isolated incidents rather than widespread systemic failures. Owners and fleet managers often praise its maneuverability and cargo capacity for its size. However, like any vehicle, specific components can experience issues over time, particularly those related to powertrain and electrical systems under demanding commercial use. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns.

---


## Engine

### 2.4L Tigershark MultiAir 2 (I4)
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil spots under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of seals, gaskets, or oil pan.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400

**Check Engine Light**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light, potential rough idling or reduced power.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of sensor faults (e.g., O2 sensor, camshaft position sensor) or emissions system issues.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600


## Transmission

### 948TE 9-Speed Automatic
**Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky gear changes, delayed engagement, or clunking noises during shifts.
- **Solution:** Software updates for transmission control module, fluid flush and replacement, or internal component inspection.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid from transmission seals or lines, potential for overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking seals, gaskets, or transmission lines.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Ram ProMaster City demonstrates a respectable level of reliability for a commercial vehicle. The primary concerns tend to revolve around the powertrain, specifically the 2.4L Tigershark engine and its 9-speed automatic transmission, with occasional reports of oil leaks, check engine lights, and transmission shifting anomalies. Electrical issues like battery drain and infotainment glitches are also noted but are not overwhelmingly prevalent. The suspension, exterior, and interior components generally hold up well under typical commercial use. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to early warning signs are crucial for maximizing the longevity and minimizing repair costs of the ProMaster City.
