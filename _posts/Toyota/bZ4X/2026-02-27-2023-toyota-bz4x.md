# 2023 Toyota bZ4X Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Toyota bZ4X represents Toyota's entry into the modern all-electric crossover market, offering sleek styling and everyday utility. As a first-year model, early owner feedback collected through NHTSA highlights a few areas worth noting, including reports concerning tire wear and underbody shielding. Prospective buyers should review vehicle history and ensure all safety recalls have been addressed by a certified dealer.

---

## Engine

### Single Motor Electric Powertrain (Front-Wheel Drive)

The front-wheel-drive bZ4X features a single permanent-magnet synchronous electric motor producing approximately 201 horsepower and 196 lb-ft of torque. It provides smooth, efficient acceleration tailored for daily commuting, though early owner feedback highlights few mechanical defects.

### Dual Motor Electric Powertrain (All-Wheel Drive)

The all-wheel-drive bZ4X utilizes dual electric motors generating a combined output of around 214 horsepower and 248 lb-ft of torque. This setup delivers improved traction in adverse weather conditions while maintaining standard EV reliability expectations.

### Underbody Shielding and Bumper

A few owners have noted issues with underbody aerodynamic covers loosening or separating under normal driving conditions.

**Engine Under Cover Detachment**
- **Symptoms:** The plastic underbody engine cover and associated bumper clips can detach unexpectedly during highway driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect the underbody shielding and secure or replace broken fasteners and damaged panels to prevent further drag.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

Equipped with a fixed-gear, single-speed direct-drive automatic transmission standard across all trims, this powertrain delivers seamless power delivery without traditional shifting hesitations. It generally requires minimal maintenance beyond standard fluid checks.

## Suspension

### Tires and Wheels

Owner reports indicate occasional concerns regarding premature tire wear and high-speed vibrations.

**Premature Tire Wear and Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported abnormal tire wear patterns and severe vehicle shaking or vibration at highway speeds above 50 mph.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect wheel alignment, tire balance, and suspension components, replacing worn tires as necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V444000: Wheels:Hub](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=bZ4X&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A wheel detaching from a moving vehicle can result in a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the hub bolts and wheels, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V548000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=bZ4X&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." A vehicle with an incorrect maximum capacity weight value may be overloaded, which can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V577000: Visibility:Defroster/Defogger/Hvac System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=bZ4X&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** During certain HVAC system faults, such as electrical compressor failure, the defroster and defogger system may become inoperative. An inoperative defroster and defogger system can decrease the driver's visibility under certain driving conditions, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the HVAC control ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V744000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=bZ4X&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to freeze or display a blank screen when the vehicle is in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that fails to display an image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the parking assist software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Toyota bZ4X is an interesting option for shoppers transitioning into an all-electric vehicle, backed by Toyota's growing EV ecosystem. While initial owner reports point to minor issues such as accelerated tire wear and loose underbody shields, these can typically be managed with routine inspections. Buyers should verify that any open recall services have been completed before finalizing their purchase.
