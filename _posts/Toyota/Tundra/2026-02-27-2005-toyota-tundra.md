## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, difficulty starting, or complete loss of power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Power Window Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow operation, or unusual noises when attempting to move windows.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the power window motor/regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500 per window


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Trim
**Frame Rust**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust or corrosion on the frame, particularly in areas exposed to salt and moisture. Can lead to structural integrity concerns.
- **Solution:** Frame inspection, rust treatment, and potential frame replacement or repair. In severe cases, replacement may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $500 - $5000+

### Summary Verdict
The 2005 Toyota Tundra is a highly reliable truck, with its powertrain (engine and transmission) being exceptionally durable. The most commonly cited issues are related to the electrical system, specifically alternator failures and power window regulators, which are generally manageable repairs. A significant concern for trucks in rust-prone areas is frame rust, which requires careful inspection and can be a costly repair if advanced. Overall, the Tundra offers a strong value proposition for those seeking a dependable used pickup, provided it has been reasonably maintained and inspected for potential frame corrosion.
