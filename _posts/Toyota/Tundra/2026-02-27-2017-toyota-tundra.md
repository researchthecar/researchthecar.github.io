# 2017 Toyota Tundra Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Toyota Tundra is generally celebrated for its rugged durability, robust hauling capability, and dependable V8 performance. Buyers looking at the used market appreciate its spacious cabin and straightforward mechanical design. However, reviewing owner feedback reveals a few recurring trouble spots involving electrical accessories, trailer brake controllers, and steering components that warrant a careful pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 4.6L V8 Engine

The 4.6-liter V8 engine produces 310 horsepower and 327 lb-ft of torque, offering a capable and generally reliable entry-point powertrain for everyday driving and light towing.

### 5.7L V8 Engine

The larger 5.7-liter V8 engine generates 381 horsepower and 401 lb-ft of torque, delivering strong towing capacity and longevity, though some owners report cold-start and fuel pump concerns.

**Fuel Pump Failure and Starting Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Extended cranking times, difficult starting, rough idling, and occasional stalling.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the fuel pump assembly and replace faulty components if necessary.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with both V8 engine options, the 6-speed automatic transmission is generally robust, though occasional electrical solenoid faults can trigger warning lights and shifting anomalies.

**Solenoid Fault and Check Engine Light**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent check engine light illumination, erratic shifting behavior, and high voltage codes recorded for transmission solenoids.
- **Solution:** Test transmission solenoids and wiring harnesses, replacing faulty components as required.

## Brakes

### Trailer Brake Controller

The factory-integrated trailer brake controller has been a point of frustration for some owners due to intermittent operational failures when towing heavy loads.

**Integrated Brake Controller Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Trailer brakes fail to engage or provide insufficient braking power, increasing stopping distances while towing.
- **Solution:** Inspect the integrated brake controller module, wiring connections, and fuses for faults or replace the control unit.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Pinion

Steering components generally hold up well, but higher-mileage vehicles can experience rack and pinion wear, fluid leaks, or unusual noises during low-speed maneuvers.

**Rack and Pinion Leak and Clunking**
- **Symptoms:** Fluid leaks, a popping or clunking noise when turning the steering wheel, and occasional stiffness.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering gear assembly for leaks or wear and replace the rack and pinion unit if mechanical failure is present.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting and Signals

Electrical accessories are generally reliable, though intermittent headlight outages present a significant safety concern during nighttime driving.

**Intermittent Headlight Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights turn off unexpectedly while driving at night, often triggered when operating the turn signals.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight switches, wiring harnesses, and related relays for loose connections or electrical failure.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V920000: Steering:Hydraulic Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** An oil leak may cause a sudden loss of power steering assist, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the power steering gear assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21E103000: Steering:Hydraulic Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** An oil leak may cause a sudden loss of power steering assist, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the power steering gear assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V831000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An incorrect load information label can result in the operator overloading the vehicle and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 17V051000: Structure:Body:Bumpers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If a person steps on the corner of the bumper that is damaged, a portion of the bumper may break away, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the resin rear bumper reinforcement brackets with steel ones, and replace the rear bumper tread, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V123000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** One of the rear split bench seat leg brackets may not have been properly tightened to the vehicle's floor pan, possibly allowing the seat to move in a crash. A vehicle with an improperly tightened seat leg bracket could fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 207, "Seating Systems," 210, "Seat Belt.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will tighten the five seat leg bracket bolts, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V311000: Wheels:Lugs/Nuts/Bolts/Studs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Lugnuts that crack and detach may cause the wheels to separate from the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** SET will notify owners, and dealers will install new replacement lugnuts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Toyota Tundra remains a dependable, rugged full-size truck choice that holds its value well and provides strong V8 performance. While major mechanical breakdowns are uncommon, prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the integrated trailer brake controller, verify proper exterior lighting operation, and check for any signs of steering rack wear or fluid leaks during a pre-purchase test drive.
