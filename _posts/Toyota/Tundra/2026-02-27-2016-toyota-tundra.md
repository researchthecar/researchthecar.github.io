# 2016 Toyota Tundra Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used full-size truck, the Toyota Tundra remains a popular choice known for rugged capability and enduring mechanical durability. However, prospective buyers should review specific owner-reported areas of concern, particularly regarding trailer brake integration, electrical issues, and steering components. While general maintenance and traditional powertrain toughness keep these trucks on the road, thorough pre-purchase inspections are essential.

---

## Engine

### 4.6L V8 Engine

The 4.6-liter V8 engine produces approximately 310 horsepower and 327 lb-ft of torque, offering solid capability for light towing and daily driving with typical Toyota reliability.

### 5.7L V8 Engine

The heavy-duty 5.7-liter V8 engine puts out 381 horsepower and 401 lb-ft of torque, providing robust towing performance, though occasional fuel system concerns warrant monitoring.

**Fuel Pump Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience crank-no-start conditions, unexpected engine stalling at lower speeds, or illuminated check engine lights due to fuel delivery failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect the fuel pump system and replace faulty fuel pump components or complete assemblies as necessary.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission pairs with both V8 engine options, delivering dependable gear shifts and solid long-term durability under standard operating conditions.

## Brakes

### Integrated Trailer Brake Controller

Factory brake controller issues can compromise heavy towing safety and require module replacement or aftermarket remedies.

**Trailer Brake Controller Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard fails to recognize the trailer brake controller, or the system does not supply adequate electronic braking power to connected trailers.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the factory controller module and wiring harness, or replace the unit with an updated factory or aftermarket brake controller.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Assembly

Steering rack seal failures can lead to sudden fluid loss and require prompt mechanical attention to ensure safe vehicle control.

**Power Steering Fluid Leaks and Rack Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Internal seals blow out leading to power steering fluid leaks, stiffness, or complete loss of steering assistance while driving.
- **Solution:** Replace the leaking steering rack and pinion assembly and flush the power steering system.

## Exterior

### Headlight and Turn Signal Switch

electrical issues involving lighting and multifunction switches can create dangerous visibility hazards during night driving.

**Headlights Failing During Turn Signal Use**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights, taillights, and dashboard illumination turn off intermittently when the turn signal is engaged while headlights are set to automatic mode.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the multifunction switch or faulty lighting control modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V416000: Air Bags:Knee Bolster](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The incorrect bolts may cause the air bag module to loosen over time, affecting the performance of the knee airbag, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 17V051000: Structure:Body:Bumpers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If a person steps on the corner of the bumper that is damaged, a portion of the bumper may break away, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the resin rear bumper reinforcement brackets with steel ones, and replace the rear bumper tread, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V311000: Wheels:Lugs/Nuts/Bolts/Studs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Lugnuts that crack and detach may cause the wheels to separate from the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** SET will notify owners, and dealers will install new replacement lugnuts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The vehicle offers strong mechanical underpinnings and dependable V8 performance, making it a viable option for truck buyers. However, shoppers should carefully test the integrated trailer brake controller, check for steering fluid leaks, and verify proper operation of exterior lights and electrical accessories during a pre-purchase inspection.
