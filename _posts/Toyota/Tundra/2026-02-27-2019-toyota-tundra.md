# 2019 Toyota Tundra Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Toyota Tundra remains a popular choice for truck buyers seeking rugged capability and long-term durability. While it boasts a stout, truck-focused powertrain lineup, used-car shoppers should review known owner complaints regarding fuel system components, electrical accessories, and exterior lighting.

---

## Engine

### 5.7L V8

The 5.7L V8 engine produces 381 horsepower and 401 lb-ft of torque, offering robust towing capability and a proven mechanical track record with occasional fuel pump concerns.

**Fuel Pump Failure and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may experience rough starting, fail to restart, or stall unexpectedly while driving.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the fuel pump assembly and replace failed components as necessary.

### 4.6L V8

The 4.6L V8 engine delivers 310 horsepower and 327 lb-ft of torque, balancing adequate daily utility with respectable long-term reliability.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission comes paired with the V8 engine options, offering dependable shifting and stable towing performance under heavy loads.

## Exterior

### Headlights

Electrical system concerns mostly center on lighting components and accessory modules.

**Headlight Failure and Melting**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice a burning plastic odor, intermittent illumination loss, or localized melting around headlight assemblies.
- **Solution:** Inspect wiring harnesses and replace damaged headlight assemblies or switches.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Safety and driver-assist components generally perform well, though owners have reported occasional warning lights and restraint sensor faults.

**Air Bag Warning and Deployment Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Air bag warning lights may illuminate on the dashboard, signaling a fault in the supplemental restraint system.
- **Solution:** Scan diagnostic trouble codes and repair or replace faulty sensors or wiring harnesses.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V682000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel pump fails, the engine can stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly with an improved one, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V920000: Steering:Hydraulic Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An oil leak may cause a sudden loss of power steering assist, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the power steering gear assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V688000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The headlight electrical circuits may power the high and low beams simultaneously, which could overheat the electrical connector. An overheated electrical connector can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will modify the engine wire harness assembly and replace any damaged bulb, bulb connector, and/or headlight assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
