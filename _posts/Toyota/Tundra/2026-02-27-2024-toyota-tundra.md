# 2024 Toyota Tundra Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2024 Toyota Tundra, prospective buyers will find a modern, highly capable full-size pickup equipped with robust twin-turbocharged engine options. However, owner reports and federal data indicate certain recurring mechanical and electrical concerns—particularly regarding engine stability and sudden stalls—that warrant close inspection before purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L Twin-Turbo V6

The standard 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine delivers impressive horsepower and torque for towing and hauling. Overall reliability is generally solid, though buyers should verify service history and check for any history of drivability updates.

### 3.5L Twin-Turbo V6 Hybrid (i-Force Max)

The i-Force Max hybrid powertrain pairs the 3.5L twin-turbo V6 with an electric motor system to boost low-end response and efficiency. While powerful, some owners have reported critical engine shutdown and stalling events tied to manufacturing debris or electrical management issues.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have experienced unexpected engine shutdowns while driving or complete loss of motive power and throttle hesitation when attempting to accelerate from a complete stop.
- **Solution:** Requires dealer diagnosis, potential software reprogramming, or replacement of affected internal engine components if debris contamination is found.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed automatic transmission provides smooth shifting and optimizes power delivery across a wide gear range. No widespread, severe mechanical failure clusters are prominently reported for this unit.

## Suspension

### Suspension and Tires

The suspension provides a balanced ride for a full-size pickup, though factory tire longevity has been a minor pain point for some drivers.

**Premature Tire Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Factory-equipped tires may exhibit faster-than-expected tread wear under normal driving conditions.
- **Solution:** Inspect remaining tread depth and replace tires with robust aftermarket alternatives if premature wear is present.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system generally brings the truck to a halt effectively, though minor sensor calibration issues and low-speed acoustic complaints have been documented by owners.

**Brake Noise and Electronic Integration Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note unusual grinding or squealing noises during low-speed braking, particularly in reverse, as well as false trailer brake disconnection alerts caused by camera or sensor misalignments.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads and rotors for premature wear; recalibrate 360-degree or backup cameras if electronic towing faults occur.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting and Cameras

Electrical accessories generally function well, but rearview camera glitches and exterior lighting concerns require verification during a used vehicle inspection.

**Backup Camera and Lighting Display Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Rearview camera displays may freeze or show a black screen when shifting into reverse, obstructing driver visibility.
- **Solution:** Check for related manufacturer recall campaigns and have the dealer update software or replace faulty camera modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V757000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An overloaded vehicle increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V125000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Unexpected vehicle movement can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the transmission control ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V548000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." A vehicle with an incorrect maximum capacity weight value may be overloaded, which can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V767000: Tires:Sidewall](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A sudden loss of tire pressure can result in a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the tires as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V322000: Exterior Lighting:Back Up Lights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Reverse light failure may reduce visibility, and not alert pedestrians and other drivers that the vehicle is backing up, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both reverse light assemblies and repair the wire harnesses as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V657000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview image not to display when the vehicle is placed in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not display reduces the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the multimedia display software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V767000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will perform an inspection on the #1 main bearing using vehicle inspection software and drive data, and if necessary, replace the engine. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V038000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera image may not display when the vehicle is placed in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display reduces the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the parking assist ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Toyota Tundra is a formidable modern pickup truck with exceptional capability and high-tech features. However, prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect service records, verify that all safety recalls have been completed, and test drive the vehicle carefully to check for throttle hesitation, stalling risks, or camera anomalies.
