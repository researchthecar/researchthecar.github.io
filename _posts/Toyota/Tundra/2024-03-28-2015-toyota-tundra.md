**Introduction:**

The 2015 Toyota Tundra is a full-size pickup truck available with a choice of two engines:

* 4.6L V8 (310 hp, 327 lb-ft of torque) mated to a 6-speed automatic transmission
* 5.7L V8 (381 hp, 401 lb-ft of torque) mated to a 6-speed automatic transmission or a 6-speed manual transmission

The Tundra is praised for its powerful engines, capable towing and payload capacities, and off-road prowess. However, it has also been criticized for its outdated interior, lack of advanced safety features, and poor fuel economy.

**Engine**

**4.6L V8**

* **VVT-i Actuator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling, rough idling, reduced performance
    * Solution: Replace the VVT-i actuator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5.7L V8**

* **Valve Seat Recession:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil consumption, blue smoke from exhaust, engine knocking
    * Solution: Replace the cylinder head or engine
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Timing Chain Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise from the engine, reduced power, engine stalling
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain and related components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Harsh Shifting:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Abrupt or delayed gear changes, especially when cold
    * Solution: Replace or rebuild the torque converter
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Grinding Gears:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting into or out of gears, grinding noise
    * Solution: Replace the transmission's synchronizers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rear suspension sagging, excessive bouncing, uneven tire wear
    * Solution: Replace the leaf springs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery goes dead after short periods of inactivity
    * Solution: Identify and repair the parasitic drain
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, electrical system malfunctions, battery doesn't charge
    * Solution: Replace the alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Bed Rust:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive rust and corrosion on the truck bed
    * Solution: Rust treatment and bed liner
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Crashing:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Bluetooth connectivity issues, touchscreen freezing, system restarts randomly
    * Solution: Update the software or replace the infotainment unit
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2015 Toyota Tundra is a capable pickup truck with powerful engines and strong towing and payload capacities. However, it has some significant issues that can be expensive to fix, such as the valve seat recession and timing chain failure. The outdated interior and lack of advanced safety features are also drawbacks. Overall, while the Tundra is a reliable workhorse, it is essential to consider the potential costs and inconveniences of the identified issues before making a purchase.
