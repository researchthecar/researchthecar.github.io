# 2022 Toyota Tundra Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The redesigned Toyota Tundra brings modern styling, a robust platform, and powerful new engine options to the full-size truck market. While it offers impressive towing capability and high-tech features, early model years have accumulated significant owner complaints regarding powertrain behavior, throttle hesitation, and accessory durability. Buyers should thoroughly evaluate service history and pay close attention to engine performance during a test drive.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L Twin-Turbo V6

The standard 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine produces robust horsepower and torque, providing strong acceleration and towing performance for daily work and travel.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience unexpected engine stalling while driving at various speeds or sudden loss of motive power accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic testing by a dealer; software updates or hardware inspections may be necessary depending on manufacturer technical service bulletins.

### 3.5L Twin-Turbo V6 Hybrid (i-FORCE MAX)

The available i-FORCE MAX hybrid powertrain pairs the twin-turbo V6 with an electric motor integrated into the bell housing to deliver enhanced low-end torque and efficiency.

**Hybrid Powertrain Hesitation and Surging**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may exhibit heavy surging, sudden drops in speed, or unexpected hesitation when transitioning between electric and gas power.
- **Solution:** Powertrain control module updates or recalibration may help resolve erratic power delivery issues.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed electronically controlled automatic transmission is paired with both engine options, offering wide gear ratios designed to optimize fuel economy and towing response.

**Transmission Control Module Delays**
- **Symptoms:** Unpredictable shifting logic, delayed engagement when shifting between park, reverse, and drive, or hesitation during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Check for available transmission control module software updates or consult a certified technician.

## Suspension

### Front Suspension and Tires

Suspension geometry supports high payload and towing capacities, though checking alignment parameters during routine service is advised.

**Premature Front Tire Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Outer edges of the front tires wear out excessively and prematurely due to factory alignment and camber settings.
- **Solution:** Perform a professional wheel alignment and replace worn tires to prevent rapid tread degradation.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Braking systems incorporate electronic assist features designed to handle heavy loads, though sensor faults and electronic parking brake glitches have been reported.

**Brake System and Parking Brake Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected electronic parking brake engagement, warning lights illuminating on the dash, or jerky regenerative braking behavior in hybrid models.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake sensors, electronic parking brake actuators, and update control modules as recommended by a dealer.

## Electrical

### Power Train Throttle

Drivetrain components manage power delivery to the rear or four-wheel-drive systems, though electronic throttle tuning has drawn owner scrutiny.

**Acceleration Lag and Dead Pedal**
- **Symptoms:** Significant throttle hesitation or a dead pedal effect when attempting to accelerate quickly from a complete stop or rolling turn.
- **Solution:** Software updates issued via manufacturer service bulletins may improve throttle response times.

## Interior

### Driver Seat Trim

Interior cabin amenities provide modern comfort and digital displays, though minor trim durability issues have emerged with frequent use.

**Cracked Seat Shield and Squeaks**
- **Symptoms:** The plastic side shield cover on the driver's seat cracks or breaks under normal use, accompanied by occasional seat squeaks.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged plastic seat trim component with updated factory parts.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V285000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not display an image reduces the driver's view of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the parking assist ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V445000: Power Train:Axle Assembly:Axle Shaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Axle shaft separation can cause a loss of vehicle stability and brake performance, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear axle assembly, tighten the axle flange nuts, and replace any damaged axle-related components if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V501000: Electrical System:Adas:Parking Assist:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Incorrect programming of the parking-assist electronic control unit (ECU) software, may cause the front camera image to appear on the multimedia display instead of the rear camera image when the shifter is placed into reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility.".
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the parking assist ECU, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V661000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." An electronic parking brake that cannot be engaged while in "park," may result in a vehicle rollaway, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the Skid Control ECU, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V111000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A software error in the combination meter's electronic control unit (ECU) may cause the Instrument Panel LCD display to go blank. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 101, "Control and Displays." A blank display will not show critical information such as the speedometer or warning.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the combination meter ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V142000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A tonneau cover that detaches while driving can become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealer will install new hardware (bolts, spacers, washers, nuts, rubber washers and cotter pins) to the existing bracket and tonneau cover. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V566000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install protective materials and a clamp on the fuel tube, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V757000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An overloaded vehicle increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Toyota Tundra remains a capable and powerful full-size truck choice, but prospective buyers should approach early production models with caution. Verifying that all powertrain software updates and recall services have been properly performed is crucial. A thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on engine behavior, throttle response, and electrical systems will help ensure a reliable ownership experience.
