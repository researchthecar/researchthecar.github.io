# 2021 Toyota Tundra Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Toyota Tundra represents the final model year of the truck's second-generation design, carrying a reputation for robust mechanical durability anchored by its stalwart V8 engine. While buyers generally praise its towing capability and classic truck feel, used-car shoppers should review a few known pain points. Owner reports highlight occasional electrical glitches, seat track play, and select steering component concerns that warrant a careful inspection during a test drive.

---

## Engine

### 5.7L V8 (3UR-FE)

The 5.7-liter V8 engine produces 381 horsepower and 401 lb-ft of torque, offering strong towing performance and proven reliability. Overall, this powertrain option is a cornerstone of the truck's dependable reputation despite occasional isolated complaints.

**Unexpected Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle suddenly stops running while driving without prior check engine lights.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the fuel delivery and electronic control systems for fault codes.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission pairs with the V8 engine to deliver smooth shifts across various driving conditions. It is generally robust, though electronic or hydraulic hiccups can occasionally occur.

**Transmission Hesitation and Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle briefly hesitates and jolts while driving, accompanied by an illuminated transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Scan the transmission control module for diagnostic trouble codes and check fluid condition.

## Steering

### Steering Assembly

Issues related to steering assist loss have been noted by owners, making a thorough steering check essential before purchasing.

**Power Steering Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power steering assistance while driving, often requiring an expensive assembly replacement.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering gear and related components for wear or failure, and check for applicable recall status.
- **Cost:** more than $6,000

## Electrical

### Electrical and Instrument Cluster

Electrical complaints center around intermittent screen blanking and accessory power quirks that require careful diagnostic checks.

**Instrument Panel Display Blanking**
- **Symptoms:** The combination meter electronic control LCD display frequently goes blank while driving.
- **Solution:** Update software or replace the instrument cluster assembly if persistence issues remain.

**Key Switch and Accessory Power Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Radio and air conditioning fail to activate upon turning the key until the key is jiggled or restarted.
- **Solution:** Inspect the ignition switch cylinder and associated wiring connections for wear.

## Exterior

### Windshield Wipers

Exterior lighting and wiper mechanisms should be inspected to ensure all components operate smoothly without rattling or stripping.

**Wiper Arm Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The windshield wiper arm stops working or strips out under the stress of accumulated snow.
- **Solution:** Replace the stripped wiper arm assembly and inspect the linkage for proper alignment.

## Interior

### Driver Seat Tracks

Cabin comfort is generally high, though loose or rocking seat tracks are a frequently reported annoyance among owners.

**Excessive Seat Movement**
- **Symptoms:** The driver seat rocks back and forth or shifts laterally when applying brakes, accelerating, or turning.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat track rails, cushions, and mounting hardware; replace faulty seat frame components as necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V688000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The headlight electrical circuits may power the high and low beams simultaneously, which could overheat the electrical connector. An overheated electrical connector can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will modify the engine wire harness assembly and replace any damaged bulb, bulb connector, and/or headlight assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V920000: Steering:Hydraulic Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An oil leak may cause a sudden loss of power steering assist, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the power steering gear assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21E103000: Steering:Hydraulic Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An oil leak may cause a sudden loss of power steering assist, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the power steering gear assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Toyota Tundra remains a compelling choice for used truck buyers seeking traditional V8 capability and long-term durability. While it exhibits strong overall reliability, shoppers should thoroughly check for seat track movement, steering functionality, and proper electrical operation during a pre-purchase inspection.
