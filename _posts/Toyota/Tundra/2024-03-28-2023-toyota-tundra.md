**Introduction**

The 2023 Toyota Tundra is a full-size pickup truck available with a choice of two engine options: a 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged V6 and a 3.4-liter twin-turbocharged i-FORCE MAX hybrid V6. Both engines are paired with a 10-speed automatic transmission.

Owners appreciate the Tundra's powerful engines, spacious interior, and impressive towing capabilities. However, issues have been reported in several areas, as detailed below.

**Engine**

**3.5L Twin-Turbocharged V6**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption**
  * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported their Tundras consuming excessive amounts of oil, leading to frequent top-ups.
  * Solution: Toyota has released a software update to address the issue, but some owners may require additional repairs or engine replacement.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Turbocharger Failure**
  * Description/Symptoms: A few owners have experienced turbocharger failures, resulting in reduced power and increased exhaust smoke.
  * Solution: Turbocharger replacement is typically required.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**3.4L Twin-Turbocharged i-FORCE MAX Hybrid V6**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**10-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Shifting Hesitations**
  * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing hesitations or delays when shifting gears.
  * Solution: Software updates may resolve the issue, but transmission repairs may be necessary in some cases.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Leaf Spring Sagging**
  * Description/Symptoms: In some cases, the rear leaf springs have been reported to sag excessively, affecting ride height and handling.
  * Solution: Leaf spring replacement is recommended.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
  * Description/Symptoms: A small number of owners have experienced excessive battery drain, leading to dead batteries.
  * Solution: The issue may be related to a faulty battery or a parasitic draw. Diagnosis and repairs will vary depending on the cause.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Body Panel Alignment Issues**
  * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported minor issues with body panel alignment, such as uneven gaps between panels.
  * Solution: Panel adjustments or replacements may be necessary.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Squeaks and Rattles**
  * Description/Symptoms: A few owners have experienced annoying squeaks and rattles coming from the dashboard area.
  * Solution: Fixing the issue may involve tightening loose components or applying dampening materials.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

While the 2023 Toyota Tundra is a capable and well-regarded pickup truck, it is not without its issues. The most common concerns include excessive oil consumption, turbocharger failures, shifting hesitations, suspension problems, battery drains, body panel alignment issues, and interior squeaks and rattles. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the extent of repairs required. Owners should be aware of these potential issues and consult with their dealership if any arise.
