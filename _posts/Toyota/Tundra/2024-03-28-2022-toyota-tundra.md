**Introduction to the 2022 Toyota Tundra:**

The 2022 Toyota Tundra is a full-size pickup truck offered with a choice of two engine options: a 3.5L twin-turbocharged V6 and a 5.7L i-FORCE V8. Both engines are paired with a 10-speed automatic transmission. The Tundra is praised for its powerful engines, spacious cabin, and off-road capabilities. However, it has also received some criticism for its fuel consumption and lack of certain features.

**Engine**

**3.5L Twin-Turbocharged V6**

* **Turbocharger Failure:** After prolonged use, the turbochargers may fail, leading to reduced engine performance, excessive oil consumption, and increased exhaust smoke.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty turbochargers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** The timing chain tensioner may fail, causing the timing chain to skip or break, leading to catastrophic engine damage.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty timing chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**5.7L i-FORCE V8**

* **Valve Spring Breakage:** Some engines may experience valve spring breakage, leading to engine misfires, loss of power, and potential catastrophic engine failure.
    * Solution: Replacing the broken valve springs and associated components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **10-Speed Automatic Transmission:**
    * **Shifting Problems:** Some users have reported erratic shifting, delayed gear engagement, and rough shifting during acceleration or deceleration.
        * Solution: Reprogramming the transmission control module or replacing transmission components as necessary.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Leaf Spring Breakage:** Some users have experienced rear leaf spring breakage, resulting in a sagging rear end and potential handling issues.
    * Solution: Replacing the broken leaf springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Infotainment Display Issues:** Some users have reported problems with the infotainment display, including freezing, glitches, and touchscreen responsiveness issues.
    * Solution: Software updates or replacement of the display unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Tail Light condensation:** Accumulation of condensation inside the tail lights may occur, leading to poor visibility during inclement weather.
    * Solution: Replacing the affected tail light assemblies.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Windshield Wiper Motor Failure:** The windshield wiper motor may fail, resulting in intermittent or complete loss of wiper functionality.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty wiper motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Toyota Tundra has received mixed reviews from owners. While it offers powerful engines and a spacious cabin, it has also been plagued by a number of issues, including engine and transmission problems. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but they can be significant for some owners. Before purchasing a Tundra, potential buyers should carefully consider the potential risks associated with these issues.
