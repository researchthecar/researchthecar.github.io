**Introduction:**

The 2017 Toyota Tundra is a full-size pickup truck available in a wide range of configurations. It offers two engine options: a 4.6-liter V8 and a 5.7-liter V8. The 4.6-liter V8 produces 310 horsepower and 327 lb-ft of torque, while the 5.7-liter V8 produces 381 horsepower and 401 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission.

The Tundra is known for its durability, towing capacity, and off-road capabilities. However, it has also received criticism for its fuel economy, ride quality, and lack of modern features.

**Engine**

* **4.6-liter V8**
    * **Excessive Oil Consumption:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The engine consumes excessive amounts of oil, requiring frequent top-ups.
        * Solution: Engine rebuild or replacement.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
        * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 19V555000
* **5.7-liter V8**
    * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner fails, causing the timing chain to slip and the engine to lose power.
        * Solution: Timing chain and tensioner replacement.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **6-speed Automatic**
    * **Shifting Issues:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission hesitates or refuses to shift gears.
        * Solution: Transmission fluid flush, valve body replacement, or transmission rebuild.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension:**
    * **No Issues Found**
* **Rear Suspension:**
    * **Leaf Spring Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The rear leaf springs break, causing the rear of the vehicle to sag.
        * Solution: Leaf spring replacement.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator fails to charge the battery, leading to a loss of electrical power.
    * Solution: Alternator replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Handle Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The tailgate handle breaks or becomes loose, making it difficult to open the tailgate.
    * Solution: Tailgate handle replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard develops cracks, especially in areas exposed to sunlight.
    * Solution: Dashboard replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Steering Wheel Vibration:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The steering wheel vibrates excessively at certain speeds.
    * Solution: Balancing and alignment.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2017 Toyota Tundra is a reliable and capable pickup truck. However, it has experienced some issues, particularly with its engine, transmission, and suspension. The severity of the issues varies, and the cost to fix them can range from minor to significant. However, these issues are not widespread and should not deter potential buyers from considering the Tundra.
