**Introduction**

The 2012 Toyota Tundra is a full-size pickup truck that offers a variety of engine and transmission options. It is known for its reliability, durability, and off-road capabilities. However, like all vehicles, it has some issues that owners have reported.

The Tundra is available with three engine options: a 4.6-liter V8, a 5.7-liter V8, and a 4.0-liter V6. The 4.6-liter V8 produces 310 horsepower and 327 lb-ft of torque, while the 5.7-liter V8 produces 381 horsepower and 401 lb-ft of torque. The 4.0-liter V6 produces 270 horsepower and 278 lb-ft of torque.

The Tundra is also available with a variety of transmission options: a 6-speed automatic, a 5-speed automatic, and a 6-speed manual. The 6-speed automatic is the most popular option, and it is paired with either the 4.6-liter V8 or the 5.7-liter V8. The 5-speed automatic is paired with the 4.0-liter V6, and the 6-speed manual is only available with the 4.6-liter V8.

**Engine**

**4.6-liter V8**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing a vacuum leak. This can lead to a loss of power, poor fuel economy, and a rough idle.
    * Solution: The intake manifold gasket must be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Timing chain tensioner failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner can fail, causing the timing chain to skip. This can lead to catastrophic engine damage.
    * Solution: The timing chain tensioner must be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5.7-liter V8**

* **Valve seat recession**
    * Description/Symptoms: The valve seats can recede, causing a loss of compression and power. This can also lead to premature engine failure.
    * Solution: The cylinder heads must be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $6,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Cam phaser failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The cam phasers can fail, causing a loss of power and fuel economy. This can also lead to a rough idle and poor performance.
    * Solution: The cam phasers must be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
