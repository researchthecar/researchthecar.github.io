# 2006 Toyota Tundra

### Overview
The 2006 Toyota Tundra is a robust full-size pickup truck known for its durability and capability. It was offered with two distinct engine options, both V8s, and a reliable automatic transmission. While generally well-regarded for its mechanical soundness, like any vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners regarding potential issues. This analysis focuses on documented reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into common problems, their symptoms, potential solutions, and estimated repair costs for the 2006 model year.

---


## Engine

### 4.7L i-Force V8 (2UZ-FE)
**Oil Leaks (Valve Covers/Oil Pan)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil drips under the vehicle, low oil level warnings, burning oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets or oil pan gasket.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Timing Belt Replacement Interval**
- **Symptoms:** Not applicable if replaced on schedule. Failure to replace can lead to catastrophic engine damage.
- **Solution:** Scheduled replacement of timing belt, tensioner, and water pump.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1000

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, difficulty starting, dead battery.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Power Window Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow operation, grinding noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the power window motor and regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Exterior

### Body and Paint
**Clear Coat Peeling**
- **Symptoms:** Chalky appearance, peeling or flaking paint, especially on the roof and hood.
- **Solution:** Repainting affected areas or a full vehicle repaint.
- **Cost:** $500 - $3000+

### Summary Verdict
The 2006 Toyota Tundra stands out as a highly reliable truck, particularly its powertrain. The 4.7L V8, while generally dependable, may require attention to oil leaks and adherence to its timing belt service interval. The 5.7L V8 and the automatic transmission are known for their robustness with no significant widespread issues reported. Electrical components like the alternator and power window motors are the most common electrical concerns. Exterior paint, specifically clear coat peeling, can be an aesthetic issue on older models. Overall, the Tundra is a solid choice for those seeking a durable and dependable full-size pickup.
