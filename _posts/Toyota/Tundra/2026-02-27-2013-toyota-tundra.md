### Overview

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Intermittent Power Loss to Accessories**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners have reported occasional issues with power windows, radio, or other accessories not functioning intermittently. This can sometimes be linked to loose connections or minor fuse issues.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of wiring harnesses, checking fuse box connections, and potentially replacing faulty relays or switches.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Trim
**Tailgate Latch Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Reports of the tailgate latch sticking or not engaging properly, making it difficult to open or close securely.
- **Solution:** Lubrication of the latch mechanism, adjustment of the striker, or replacement of the tailgate latch assembly.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2013 Toyota Tundra demonstrates strong overall reliability, particularly with its V8 engine options and robust transmission. The powertrain is a significant strength, with few documented widespread issues. Minor concerns are primarily related to isolated electrical gremlins and some exterior components like the tailgate latch, which are generally repairable at a moderate cost. The suspension and interior components have shown good durability. For prospective buyers, the 2013 Tundra remains a solid choice for a dependable full-size truck, with most potential issues being manageable and not indicative of systemic failures.
