# 2023 Toyota Tundra Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Toyota Tundra delivers a bold redesign, modern tech, and robust capability, but it has faced scrutiny regarding initial quality and powertrain dependability. Used-car buyers should weigh its strong performance and safety features against notable owner complaints concerning engine performance, hesitation, and electrical glitches.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L Twin-Turbo V6

The 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged V6 produces approximately 389 horsepower and 479 lb-ft of torque, offering strong towing capabilities. However, early production models have encountered significant owner reports regarding engine stalling and internal component wear.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine stalling, rough running, knocking noises, and sudden losses of motive power while driving.
- **Solution:** Consult a certified dealer for diagnostic checks and adherence to relevant manufacturer recall notices regarding engine components.

### 3.5L Twin-Turbo V6 Hybrid (i-FORCE MAX)

The i-FORCE MAX hybrid powertrain pairs the 3.5L twin-turbo V6 with an electric motor to generate a robust 437 horsepower and 583 lb-ft of torque. It provides enhanced low-end responsiveness and efficiency for heavy-duty truck tasks.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 10-speed electronically controlled automatic transmission is paired across all trims and drivetrains to optimize fuel economy and power delivery. While generally smooth, some owners note hesitation and shifting delays during low-speed maneuvers.

**Acceleration Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a noticeable 1-2 second delay or lag when attempting to accelerate from a complete stop or rolling stop, occasionally leading to unexpected vehicle surges.
- **Solution:** Have a dealership inspect transmission control modules or apply updated software calibrations if available.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The standard disc brake setup provides reliable stopping power for a full-size truck, though minor squeaking and noise complaints have been reported by early owners.

**Brake Noise and Squeaking**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note loud squeaking and abnormal sounds emitting from the brakes, particularly during initial applications or while reversing.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads and rotors for premature wear or glazing; clean or replace components as necessary.

## Electrical

### Camera and Driver Assistance Systems

Advanced safety features and multi-camera systems enhance situational awareness, though software updates and camera calibrations are critical for proper operation.

**Camera and Recognition System Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Forward-facing and backup cameras experience blurriness, loss of connection, or failure to function properly during stops and reversing maneuvers.
- **Solution:** Have camera sensors recalibrated or updated by a certified technician following software updates or recalls.

## Exterior

### Panoramic Sunroof

The available panoramic sunroof offers an expansive cabin view, but a small number of owners have experienced spontaneous glass breakage.

**Spontaneous Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof glass can explode or shatter unexpectedly while driving on highways without external impact.
- **Solution:** Inspect sunroof assembly integrity and work with a dealership for warranty coverage on stress-related glass failures.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V854000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An incorrect label may lead to unintentionally overloading the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 23V111000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A software error in the combination meter's electronic control unit (ECU) may cause the Instrument Panel LCD display to go blank. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 101, "Control and Displays." A blank display will not show critical information such as the speedometer or warning.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the combination meter ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V142000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A tonneau cover that detaches while driving can become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealer will install new hardware (bolts, spacers, washers, nuts, rubber washers and cotter pins) to the existing bracket and tonneau cover. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V468000: Tires:Temporary/Emergency Spare Tire](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Chains that are not welded sufficiently can break and allow the spare tire to detach, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the spare tire carrier assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V566000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install protective materials and a clamp on the fuel tube, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V633000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An overloaded vehicle increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the load carrying capacity modification label, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V757000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An overloaded vehicle increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V125000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tundra&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Unexpected vehicle movement can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the transmission control ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Toyota Tundra is a capable and modern full-size truck, but prospective buyers should approach with careful inspection. Pay close attention to powertrain behavior, ensure all safety and engine recalls have been completed, and thoroughly test the electronics and glass components during a pre-purchase inspection.
