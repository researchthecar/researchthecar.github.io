**Introduction**

The 1990 Toyota Pickup is a versatile and reliable vehicle known for its durability and off-road capabilities. It's powered by a 2.4-liter inline-four engine with 116 horsepower and 140 lb-ft of torque, mated to either a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission. Drivers appreciate its compact size, good fuel economy, and rugged construction, but criticize its limited interior space and lack of advanced features.

**Engine**

**2.4L Inline-Four Engine**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaks from the intake manifold, causing overheating or engine performance issues.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the valve cover, leading to oil consumption and a potential fire hazard.
    * Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Belt Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: If the timing belt fails, it can cause catastrophic engine damage by bending valves.
    * Solution: Replace the timing belt and tensioner according to the maintenance schedule (every 60,000 miles).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Grinding Gears**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may make grinding noises when shifting, indicating worn synchronizers.
    * Solution: Rebuild the transmission with new synchronizers and other components as needed.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Clutch Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The clutch may slip or become difficult to engage, making it hard to drive the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace the clutch assembly, including the disc, pressure plate, and release bearing.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Delayed Engagement**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may delay in engaging after shifting, causing hesitation or jerking.
    * Solution: Replace or repair transmission solenoids or other internal components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Slipping Gears**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip between gears, resulting in loss of power and engine revving.
    * Solution: Overhaul the transmission to replace worn or damaged components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The leaf springs may crack or break, leading to a sagging suspension and reduced handling.
    * Solution: Replace the leaf springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600 per pair
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Shock Absorber Wear**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn shock absorbers can cause a rough ride, reduced stability, and longer braking distances.
    * Solution: Replace the shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per pair
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator may fail to charge the battery, leading to electrical system problems and potential engine stalling.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Starter Solenoid Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The starter solenoid may fail to engage the starter, preventing the engine from starting.
    * Solution: Replace the starter solenoid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust can develop in various areas of the exterior, including the body panels, frame, and undercarriage.
    * Solution: Repair or replace rusted components as needed.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of damage
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Broken Window Regulators**
    * Description/Symptoms: The window regulators may fail, causing the windows to become inoperable.
    * Solution: Replace the window regulators.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per window
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Dashboard Cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard may develop cracks due to sun exposure or heat.
    * Solution: Replace or repair the dashboard.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Sun Visor Sagging**
    * Description/Symptoms: The sun visors may sag or become loose due to worn brackets.
    * Solution: Replace the visor brackets or the entire sun visor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200 per visor
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1990 Toyota Pickup is a reliable vehicle with some common issues, mostly related to engine gaskets, transmission problems, and suspension wear. The severity of these issues can vary depending on the age, mileage, and maintenance history of the vehicle. The cost to fix these issues ranges from minor to moderate, but regular maintenance and preventive repairs can help prevent major breakdowns.
