The 2021 Toyota Hilux is a rugged and reliable pickup truck known for its off-road capabilities and durability. Engine options for this model year include a 2.4L Turbo Diesel producing 147 hp and 295 lb-ft of torque, a 2.7L inline-4 gasoline engine producing 164 hp and 181 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.8L Turbo Diesel producing 201 hp and 369 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options vary depending on the engine and trim level, including a 6-speed manual and a 6-speed automatic. Owners generally appreciate the Hilux for its dependability and toughness, but some have noted concerns about ride comfort and fuel economy.

**Engine**

**2.8L Turbo Diesel**

*   **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The DPF can become clogged, especially with frequent short trips. This can cause reduced engine performance, increased fuel consumption, and warning lights on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Performing a forced DPF regeneration, driving at higher speeds for an extended period to allow the DPF to clean itself, or replacing the DPF.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3000+ depending on the severity of the issue and whether the DPF needs to be replaced.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Injector failure can occur. This can cause issues with engine knocking and rough running.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+ per injector, depending on labor and parts.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.4L Turbo Diesel**
*   **No Issues Found**

**2.7L Inline-4 Gasoline Engine**
*   **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic (Aisin AWR6B45)**
*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported harsh or jerky shifting, particularly between certain gears.
    *   Solution: Software update, transmission fluid flush or replacement, or in severe cases, transmission repair or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $5,000+ depending on the severity of the issue.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Manual (Aisin RA60)**
*   **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

*   **Leaf Spring Squeaking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Squeaking noises coming from the rear suspension, particularly when driving over bumps or uneven surfaces.
    *   Solution: Lubricating the leaf springs, replacing worn-out bushings, or replacing the leaf springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $1000+, depending on the severity of the issue and the chosen solution.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Damper Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shock damper failure can occur, resulting in reduced ride control and increased body roll.
    *   Solution: Replacing the damaged dampers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+, depending on part brands.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Issues with the infotainment system, such as freezing, lagging, or unexpected reboots.
    *   Solution: Software update, resetting the system, or replacing the head unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $1,500+, depending on the severity of the issue and the solution.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Reverse Camera Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or complete failure of the reverse camera system.
    *   Solution: Check camera connections, replace damaged camera, or replace the head unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+, depending on the severity of the issue and the solution.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Rattling**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling or buzzing noises coming from the dashboard, particularly when driving on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Adding sound insulation, tightening loose fasteners, or replacing damaged trim pieces.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+, depending on the severity of the issue and the solution.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Toyota Hilux is generally a reliable vehicle, but some owners have experienced issues with the diesel engines (DPF clogging and injector issues), harsh shifting with the automatic transmission, suspension noises, and electrical glitches. The severity and cost of these issues can vary, but addressing them promptly is important to maintain the vehicle's performance and longevity. The diesel-related issues can potentially be quite expensive.
