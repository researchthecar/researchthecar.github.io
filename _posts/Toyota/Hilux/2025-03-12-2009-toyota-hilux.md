The 2009 Toyota Hilux is a rugged and reliable pickup truck known for its durability and off-road capability. Engine options included a 2.7L inline-4 gasoline engine producing approximately 158 hp and 180 lb-ft of torque, a 4.0L V6 gasoline engine producing approximately 236 hp and 266 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.5L turbodiesel inline-4 producing various outputs depending on the specific market. Transmission options generally included a 5-speed manual and a 4 or 5-speed automatic, varying by engine and region. Owners often praise the Hilux for its longevity and dependability, while common complaints involve ride comfort and relatively basic interior features.

**Engine**

**2.7L Inline-4 Gasoline**

*   **Engine Knock/Pinging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine produces a knocking or pinging sound, especially under load. This can be due to carbon buildup, low-quality fuel, or issues with the engine's knock sensor.
    *   Solution: Try using higher-octane fuel, cleaning the fuel injectors and intake, or replacing the knock sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**4.0L V6 Gasoline**

*   **Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leak near the valve covers, resulting in a burning smell and potential oil loss.
    *   Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Timing Chain Cover Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leak near the front of the engine, potentially causing oil consumption and a dirty engine bay.
    *   Solution: Reseal or replace the timing chain cover gasket.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Turbodiesel Inline-4**

*   **Injector Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, excessive smoke, and reduced power.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty injectors. It's often recommended to replace all injectors at the same time.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1200+ depending on the number of injectors replaced.
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noises, loss of power, and excessive black smoke.
    *   Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifts between gears, particularly between 2nd and 3rd.
    *   Solution: Perform a transmission fluid flush and filter replacement. If the problem persists, valve body issues may require repair or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Automatic**

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibration at low speeds, especially when accelerating from a stop.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Manual**

*   **Clutch Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clutch slippage, difficulty shifting, or a high clutch pedal engagement point.
    *   Solution: Clutch replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Leaf Springs**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear suspension, reduced load-carrying capacity, and a bumpy ride.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaf springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Worn Shocks/Struts**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, and excessive body roll.
    *   Solution: Replace the shocks and struts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Alternator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, battery light illumination, and difficulty starting the engine.
    *   Solution: Replace the alternator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Faulty Airbag Clockspring**
    * Description/Symptoms: Airbag light illuminated, non-functional steering wheel controls, and potentially a non-functional horn.
    * Solution: Replace the clockspring.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust (Especially in Rust-Prone Areas)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust formation on the body panels, frame, and undercarriage. This is particularly common in areas that use road salt.
    *   Solution: Rust repair or panel replacement. Treat any affected area with rust inhibitor, filler, primer, and paint.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $2000+ depending on the severity.
     * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Cracked Dashboard**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracking of the dashboard material, particularly in hot climates due to sun exposure.
    *   Solution: Dashboard replacement or installation of a dashboard cover.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2009 Toyota Hilux is generally a reliable vehicle but is prone to specific issues depending on the engine and transmission configuration. Diesel models can suffer from injector and turbocharger problems, while gasoline engines may experience oil leaks. Suspension wear and rust are common issues, especially in harsher climates. Interior plastics may be prone to cracking. Repair costs range from minor fixes costing a few hundred dollars to more significant repairs exceeding $1000, particularly for turbocharger replacements or extensive rust repair. It would be best if you looked out for rust and oil leaks, depending on the region you are in when purchasing the vehicle.
