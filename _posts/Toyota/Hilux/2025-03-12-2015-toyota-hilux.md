The 2015 Toyota Hilux is a rugged and reliable pickup truck known for its durability and off-road capability. The 2015 model year offered several engine options, including a 2.5L D-4D diesel (142 hp, 253 lb-ft torque), a 3.0L D-4D diesel (169 hp, 265 lb-ft torque), and a 2.7L gasoline engine (158 hp, 177 lb-ft torque). Transmission options generally include a 5-speed manual, 6-speed manual, and a 5-speed automatic, depending on the engine and market. Owners often praise the Hilux for its toughness, longevity, and resale value. Common dislikes include the ride quality and some consider the interior to be basic.

**Engine**

**2.5L D-4D Diesel**

* **Injector Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, reduced power, excessive black smoke.
    * Solution: Replace faulty injectors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.0L D-4D Diesel**

* **EGR Valve Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, reduced power, poor fuel economy. Clogging of the EGR valve due to carbon buildup.
    * Solution: Clean or replace the EGR valve.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: DPF warning light, reduced power, engine running in limp mode.
    * Solution: Forced DPF regeneration, or DPF replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 for cleaning, $1000 - $3000+ for replacement.
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.7L Gasoline**

* **Oxygen Sensor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, reduced fuel economy, rough idle.
    * Solution: Replace faulty oxygen sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, unusual noises when engaging the clutch.
    * Solution: Replace the clutch disc, pressure plate, and possibly the flywheel.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **Harsh Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noticeably harsh or jerky shifting between gears.
    * Solution: Perform a transmission fluid flush and filter change. If the issue persists, further diagnosis and repair (valve body, solenoids) may be needed.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300 for fluid and filter, $500 - $1500+ for more extensive repairs.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Sagging**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rear end sagging, reduced load-carrying capacity, poor ride quality.
    * Solution: Replace leaf springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Worn Shock Absorbers**
    * Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, excessive body roll.
    * Solution: Replace shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Faulty Air Conditioning Compressor**
    * Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning not cooling properly, unusual noises from the compressor.
    * Solution: Replace AC compressor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery light illuminated, dimming headlights, difficulty starting the engine, eventually leading to a dead battery.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust forming around the door seams, wheel wells, and undercarriage, especially in areas with heavy road salt usage.
    * Solution: Sanding, rust treatment, and repainting affected areas. In severe cases, panel replacement might be required.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2000+ (depending on severity)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing on the dashboard surface, particularly in hot climates.
    * Solution: Dashboard replacement or repair.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Toyota Hilux, while generally reliable, can experience a range of issues from engine-related problems like injector failure and EGR valve issues to suspension and electrical faults. Diesel models, in particular, are prone to DPF and EGR issues. Suspension issues may include leaf spring sagging and worn shocks. Dashboard cracking can also occur, especially in hotter climates. The severity and cost of these repairs vary greatly, with engine and transmission work potentially being the most expensive. Overall, it's important to thoroughly inspect any used 2015 Hilux for these common issues before purchase.
