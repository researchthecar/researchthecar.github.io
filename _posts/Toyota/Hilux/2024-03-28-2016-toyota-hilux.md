**Vehicle Introduction**

The 2016 Toyota Hilux is a mid-size pickup truck available in single, extended, and double cab configurations. It features a 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine with 150 horsepower and 178 pound-feet of torque paired with a six-speed manual or automatic transmission. Other engine options include a 2.7-liter four-cylinder diesel engine with 164 horsepower and 310 pound-feet of torque paired with a six-speed manual or automatic transmission, and a 4.0-liter V6 engine with 278 horsepower and 324 pound-feet of torque paired with a five-speed automatic transmission. The Hilux is praised for its off-road capabilities, reliability, and spacious interior. However, some owners have reported various issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**2.4-Liter Four-Cylinder Engine**

* **Timing Chain Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature stretching or failure of the timing chain, causing a loss of power and knocking or rattling noises from the engine.
    * Solution: Replacement of the timing chain and associated components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.7-Liter Four-Cylinder Diesel Engine**

* **Fuel Injector Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning fuel injectors导致困难启动, poor performance, and increased exhaust emissions.
    * Solution: Replacement of the faulty fuel injectors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Turbocharger Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, black smoke from the exhaust, and whistling or whining noises during acceleration.
    * Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger and associated components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**4.0-Liter V6 Engine**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Clutch Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slipping, chattering, or difficulty engaging gears.
    * Solution: Replacement of the clutch disk, pressure plate, and release bearing.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Shifting Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifts, slipping, or hesitation during gear changes.
    * Solution: Transmission fluid flush, solenoid replacement, or transmission rebuild.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 18V-218

**Suspension**

* **Rear Leaf Spring Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging or broken rear leaf springs, resulting in a lowered ride height and reduced load capacity.
    * Solution: Replacement of the leaf springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rapid battery discharge when the vehicle is parked, causing difficulty starting.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the electrical system, such as alternator, starter, or wiring.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Bed Rust**
    * Description/Symptoms: Corrosion and rust forming on the metal bed of the pickup truck.
    * Solution: Bed liner installation, paint touch-ups, or replacement of the bed.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dash Cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracking or warping of the plastic dashboard material.
    * Solution: Dashboard replacement or repair.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Toyota Hilux has received generally positive reviews, but some owners have reported issues with the timing chain, fuel injectors, turbocharger, clutch, transmission shifting, rear leaf springs, battery drain, bed rust, and dash cracking. The cost to fix these issues can vary significantly depending on the severity of the issue and the location of the repair. While the Hilux is known for its durability and reliability, these issues should be considered when purchasing or owning a used vehicle.
