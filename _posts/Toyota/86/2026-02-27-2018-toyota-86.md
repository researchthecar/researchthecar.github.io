# 2018 Toyota 86 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Toyota 86 offers engaging rear-wheel-drive handling and a focused sports car experience for driving enthusiasts. When evaluating a used model, prospective buyers should review available maintenance records and perform a thorough test drive to check for any mechanical or handling irregularities.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Boxer Engine

This 2.0-liter horizontally opposed four-cylinder engine produces approximately 205 horsepower and 156 lb-ft of torque when paired with the manual transmission. It delivers spirited performance and a low center of gravity, though regular fluid maintenance is critical for long-term health.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional six-speed manual gearbox offers short throws and direct engagement, making it a favorite for driving purists. Buyers should inspect the clutch condition and shift smoothness during a test drive.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The six-speed automatic transmission features steering-wheel-mounted paddle shifters for manual gear selection. It provides smooth everyday commuting while retaining responsive behavior when driven dynamically.

## Steering

### Steering Wheel and Front Suspension

Owner reports indicate potential steering vibration during braking maneuvers at high speeds.

**Steering Wheel Shaking During Braking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience noticeable shimmies and shaking in the steering wheel and front end when applying the brakes at highway speeds. This can make vehicle control challenging on mountain grades and freeways.
- **Solution:** Inspect front brake rotors for warping or uneven pad deposits, and check steering and suspension components for wear.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V587000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=86&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the low-pressure fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Toyota 86 remains a compelling choice for a sporty daily driver or weekend cruiser. While isolated owner complaints highlight front-end vibrations during braking, a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection will help ensure the vehicle is in top mechanical condition.
