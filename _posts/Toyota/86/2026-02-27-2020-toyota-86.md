# 2020 Toyota 86 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Toyota 86 continues to offer sports car enthusiasts a lightweight, rear-wheel-drive platform built in partnership with Subaru. Buyers looking at this model should weigh its engaging handling dynamics against its specialized mechanical layout. While owner feedback is generally limited, a careful inspection of the vehicle's engine and supporting systems is always recommended during a pre-purchase evaluation.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Horizontally-Opposed 4-Cylinder

This 2.0-liter naturally aspirated Subaru Boxer 4-cylinder engine produces approximately 205 horsepower and 156 lb-ft of torque when paired with the manual transmission. It sits low in the chassis to improve handling balance, though its unique boxer layout requires specific familiarity during maintenance.

**Unexpected Engine Behavior While Braking**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experienced unexpected surging or movement while idling or stopped with the brake applied.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the throttle body, idle air control, and braking system integration to diagnose potential vacuum leaks or electronic throttle issues.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

A traditional 6-speed manual gearbox offering short throws and direct engagement for enthusiast drivers. Overall mechanical reliability is generally solid when properly maintained.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A conventional 6-speed automatic transmission featuring steering-wheel-mounted paddle shifters for manual control. It provides relaxed cruising while maintaining responsive gear changes.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Toyota 86 remains a compelling choice for buyers seeking pure rear-wheel-drive handling and classic sports car proportions. Because these vehicles are frequently driven with enthusiasm, prospective owners should prioritize a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection covering the powertrain, suspension, and maintenance history.
