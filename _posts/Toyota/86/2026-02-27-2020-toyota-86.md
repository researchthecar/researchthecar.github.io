# 2020 Toyota 86

### Overview
The 2020 Toyota 86, a rear-wheel-drive sports coupe, is generally recognized for its engaging driving dynamics and accessible performance. Built on a platform shared with Subaru (as the BRZ), it benefits from a well-engineered chassis and a responsive, naturally aspirated boxer engine. Reliability for the 2020 model year is typically strong, reflecting Toyota's reputation for building durable vehicles. Owners generally report satisfaction with the mechanical components, with most common concerns revolving around minor interior wear or occasional electronic glitches, rather than significant powertrain failures. The focus for the 86 has always been on driving experience, and for the most part, it delivers this without major reliability drawbacks.

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 Toyota 86 demonstrates a strong reliability record, consistent with Toyota's brand reputation. The powertrain, including the 2.0L boxer engine and both manual and automatic transmissions, is generally trouble-free. Suspension components are also robust. The most common areas for concern are minor electrical issues, particularly with the infotainment system, and some reports of premature battery failure. Interior wear on seat bolsters is also noted, though this is often considered typical for a sports car with frequent use. Overall, the 86 is a reliable choice for enthusiasts prioritizing driving experience without significant mechanical worries.
