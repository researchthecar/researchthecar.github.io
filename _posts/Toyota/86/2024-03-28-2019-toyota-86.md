**Introduction:**

The 2019 Toyota 86 is a rear-wheel-drive sports car known for its agile handling, naturally aspirated engine, and affordable price. It is offered with a choice of a 2.0-liter boxer-4 engine paired with either a 6-speed manual or 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally praise the car's driving dynamics, interior build quality, and sporty appearance, but criticize its lack of power, cramped rear seats, and dated infotainment system.

**Engine**

**2.0L Boxer-4**

* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature chain wear, rattling noises, potential engine damage
    * Solution: Install an updated tensioner and chain
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Valve Spring Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Insufficient valve spring pressure, reduced engine power, potential engine damage
    * Solution: Replace affected valve springs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Slave Cylinder Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking fluid, difficulty engaging gears, potential drivability loss
    * Solution: Replace the clutch slave cylinder
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $350+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering, slipping, or hesitation during gear changes, potential transmission damage
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Spring Perch Corrosion:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Corrosion on the rear spring perches, potential suspension damage, reduced handling
    * Solution: Inspect and replace affected spring perches
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **ECU Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine performance issues, warning lights, potential engine damage
    * Solution: Re-flash or replace the ECU
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery discharge, difficulty starting engine
    * Solution: Replace the alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlamp Condensation:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlamps
    * Solution: Inspect and seal any leaks
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Lag:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slow response times, occasional freezing
    * Solution: Update the infotainment system software
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Free for warranty-covered updates
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Climate Control Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Temperature inconsistencies, fan speed issues
    * Solution: Inspect and repair or replace the climate control module
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2019 Toyota 86 has some minor but potentially costly issues related to the engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical systems. The severity of these issues varies, with some, like the ECU malfunction, potentially leading to engine damage if not addressed. Estimated repair costs range from as low as $100 for condensation in the headlamps to over $2,000 for a torque converter failure. It is important for owners to be aware of these issues and monitor their vehicles accordingly.
