The 2019 Toyota 86 is a rear-wheel-drive sports car known for its nimble handling and affordable price point. It's equipped with a 2.0-liter horizontally opposed (Boxer) four-cylinder engine producing 205 horsepower and 156 lb-ft of torque when paired with the 6-speed manual transmission, or 200 horsepower and 151 lb-ft of torque with the 6-speed automatic transmission. Common praises include its driving dynamics and sporty aesthetic, while common criticisms involve its lack of power and somewhat spartan interior.

**Engine**

**2.0L Boxer Engine**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. This is most noticeable at higher mileage.
    *   Solution: While some owners have success with switching to a different oil viscosity, a more permanent solution may involve replacing piston rings or even the entire engine in severe cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $7,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Valve Spring Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported valve spring failures that can lead to engine damage. Symptoms can include misfires, rough idling, or a check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty valve springs. This often requires removing the engine from the vehicle.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

*   **Synchro Wear/Grinding**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting into certain gears, especially 2nd and 3rd, accompanied by a grinding noise. This is often caused by worn synchros.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn synchros or rebuilding the transmission.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported occasional rough or jerky shifting, particularly during low-speed maneuvers.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a transmission fluid flush and software update can alleviate the issue. In other cases, valve body replacement may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Head Unit Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported the infotainment system freezing, crashing, or failing to connect to Bluetooth devices reliably.
    *   Solution: Attempting a software update, or replacing the head unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **TPMS Sensor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Tire Pressure Monitoring System (TPMS) sensor failure, resulting in a constant TPMS warning light on the dashboard, even when tire pressures are correct.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty TPMS sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200 per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Tail Light Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup or condensation inside the tail light housings.
    *   Solution: Replacing the tail light housings or attempting to seal the existing ones.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Buzzing Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling or buzzing noises emanating from various interior panels, particularly the dashboard and door panels.
    *   Solution: Locating the source of the rattle and applying sound deadening material or padding to secure the loose components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+ (depending on severity and if done professionally)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Toyota 86, while generally reliable, does have some potential issues owners should be aware of. The most concerning are excessive oil consumption and valve spring failures in the engine, which can lead to significant repair costs. Transmission issues, particularly synchro wear in the manual transmission, can also be costly to address. Other issues such as infotainment glitches and interior rattles are less severe but can be annoying. Overall, potential buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle, check its maintenance history, and be prepared for the possibility of addressing some of these common issues.
