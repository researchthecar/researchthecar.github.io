# 2017 Toyota 86

### Overview
The 2017 Toyota 86, a rear-wheel-drive sports coupe, is generally regarded as a reliable vehicle, particularly for its segment. It shares its platform with the Subaru BRZ, benefiting from Subaru's engineering. The car is praised for its engaging driving dynamics and accessible performance. Reliability data suggests that while most owners report a positive experience, there are a few specific areas that have seen occasional attention. The focus is on mechanical components and less on widespread electrical gremlins, which is a good sign for long-term ownership. Maintenance is straightforward, and parts are generally available, contributing to its overall appeal as a fun yet practical sports car.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Naturally Aspirated Boxer
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Slightly higher than average oil consumption, especially under spirited driving conditions. May require more frequent checks and top-offs.
- **Solution:** Regular oil level checks and top-offs. In some cases, a software update or revised PCV system may help.
- **Cost:** $50 - $200 (for oil and filter)

**Exhaust Manifold Cracks**
- **Symptoms:** Exhaust leaks, ticking noise on startup, potential loss of power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the exhaust manifold.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Toyota 86 is a solid choice for those seeking an affordable and engaging sports car with generally good reliability. The core mechanicals, including the engine and transmissions (both manual and automatic), have proven to be robust. The most commonly cited issues are related to the engine, specifically a tendency for slightly increased oil consumption under hard use and occasional exhaust manifold cracks, both of which are manageable. Other systems like suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components show no widespread significant problems. Overall, the 86 offers a dependable platform for spirited driving, with minor potential issues that do not detract significantly from its overall reliability rating.
