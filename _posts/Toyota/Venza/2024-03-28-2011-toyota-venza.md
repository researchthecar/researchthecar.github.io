## Introduction to the 2011 Toyota Venza

The 2011 Toyota Venza is a midsize crossover SUV that combines the spaciousness of an SUV with the fuel efficiency of a sedan. It offers two engine options: a 2.7-liter four-cylinder that produces 182 horsepower and 182 lb-ft of torque and a 3.5-liter V6 that generates 268 horsepower and 246 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a six-speed automatic with front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive for the four-cylinder engine and a six-speed automatic with all-wheel drive for the V6 engine.

Owners appreciate the Venza's comfortable ride, spacious interior, and versatile cargo area. However, some have reported various issues with the vehicle.

### Engine

**2.7L Four-Cylinder**

* **Intake Valve Carbon Buildup**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, rough idling, and poor fuel economy
    * Solution: Clean the intake valves and throttle body
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $350 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine noise, reduced power, and potential engine damage
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $1500
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 20V436000

**3.5L V6**

* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leak from the top of the engine
    * Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $350
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Crankshaft Position Sensor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling, difficulty starting, and reduced power
    * Solution: Replace the crankshaft position sensor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

### Transmission

**Six-Speed Automatic (2.7L)**

* **Transmission Fluid Leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: Fluid leaking from the transmission
    * Solution: Repair the leak source and replace the transmission fluid
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Six-Speed Automatic (3.5L)**

* **Slipping Transmission**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping, rough shifts, or inability to shift
    * Solution: Overhaul or replace the transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2500 - $4000
    * Recalls: None Found

### Suspension

* **Strut Mount Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noise and vibration from the front suspension
    * Solution: Replace the strut mounts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300 per strut
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Sway Bar Link Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Knocking or rattling noise from the suspension
    * Solution: Replace the sway bar links
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250 per side
    * Recalls: None Found

### Electrical

* **Electrical Harness Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Various electrical problems, such as intermittent power to accessories, starting issues, and dashboard warning lights
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the wiring harness
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

### Exterior

* **Tailgate Latch Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Tailgate will not open or close properly
    * Solution: Replace the tailgate latch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

### Interior

* **Center Console Lid Breakage**
    * Description/Symptoms: Center console lid cracks or breaks
    * Solution: Replace the center console lid
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Bluetooth Connectivity Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty connecting to Bluetooth devices or audio issues
    * Solution: Software update or Bluetooth module replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150
    * Recalls: None Found

### Summary

While the Toyota Venza is a generally reliable vehicle, some owners have reported various issues, including engine problems, transmission issues, and electrical concerns. The severity and cost of these issues can vary, so it is important to have your car inspected by a qualified mechanic if you experience any problems.
