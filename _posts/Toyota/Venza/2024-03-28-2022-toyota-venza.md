**2022 Toyota Venza**

The 2022 Toyota Venza is a midsize SUV offered with two powertrain options: a 2.5-liter inline-four engine paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission, and a 2.5-liter inline-four engine paired with an electronically controlled continuously variable transmission (e-CVT). The gasoline-powered engine produces 219 horsepower and 185 lb-ft of torque, while the hybrid powertrain generates a combined 219 horsepower.

Praised for its spacious cabin, fuel efficiency, and comfortable ride, the Venza has received some complaints from owners regarding certain issues.

**Engine**

**2.5-Liter Inline-Four Engine (Gasoline)**

* **Engine Knock:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Knocking or rattling sound coming from the engine
    * Solution: Replacing engine components (e.g., timing chain, pistons)
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: N/A

* **Oil Consumption:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-ups
    * Solution: Replacing engine components (e.g., piston rings, oil pump) or treating with oil additives
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: N/A

**2.5-Liter Inline-Four Engine (Hybrid)**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Eight-Speed Automatic Transmission (Gasoline)**

* **Shifting Problems:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, delayed shifts, or hard shifts
    * Solution: Updating transmission software or replacing transmission components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: N/A

* **Transmission Shudder:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vibration felt through the vehicle during acceleration or deceleration
    * Solution: Replacing transmission fluid or transmission components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: N/A

**Electronically Controlled Continuously Variable Transmission (Hybrid)**

* **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Knock:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Knocking or rattling noise while driving over bumps
    * Solution: Replacing suspension components (e.g., struts, bushings)
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: N/A

* **Rear Suspension Sagging:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive drooping of the rear of the vehicle under load
    * Solution: Replacing springs or shock absorbers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery losing power prematurely, requiring frequent jumps or replacements
    * Solution: Replacing battery or repairing electrical system components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Electrical Short:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Various electrical malfunctions or warnings, such as flickering lights or dashboard issues
    * Solution: Identifying and repairing the short circuit or faulty component
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Windshield Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Small cracks or chips appearing on the windshield
    * Solution: Replacing windshield
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Paint Peeling:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clear coat or paint peeling off from the vehicle's body
    * Solution: Repainting affected area or repainting the entire vehicle
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattle:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clicking or rattling sound coming from the dashboard area
    * Solution: Tightening loose components or replacing dashboard parts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Seat Recline Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Front seats not reclining properly or becoming stuck in certain positions
    * Solution: Repairing or replacing seat reclining mechanism
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

While the 2022 Toyota Venza is generally a reliable SUV, it has experienced some issues, particularly with its engine and transmission. The engine knock and oil consumption issues can be expensive to repair, while shifting problems and transmission shudder can impact driving performance. Electrical problems and exterior issues are relatively minor but can still be inconvenient. The suspension and interior issues are less common but can still be costly to resolve. Overall, the severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and its cause.
