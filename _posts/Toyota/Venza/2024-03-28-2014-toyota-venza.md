**Introduction:**

The 2014 Toyota Venza is a mid-size crossover SUV powered by a standard 2.7-liter four-cylinder engine producing 181 horsepower and 182 lb-ft of torque. It is paired with a six-speed automatic transmission. A 3.5-liter V6 engine with 268 horsepower and 246 lb-ft of torque is optional, mated to a six-speed automatic transmission. The Venza offers a comfortable ride, spacious interior, and good fuel economy. However, it is criticized for its lack of third-row seating and dated infotainment system.

**Engine**

**2.7-Liter Four-Cylinder**

**Intake Valve Carbon Buildup**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive carbon buildup on intake valves can lead to engine knocking, rough idling, and reduced power.
Solution: Cleaning or replacing the intake valves.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
Recalls: None Found

**3.5-Liter V6**

**Oil Leak from Valve Cover Gasket**
Description/Symptoms: Leakage of oil from the gasket between the valve cover and cylinder head, causing oil consumption and smoke.
Solution: Replacing the valve cover gasket.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

**Transmission Slipping**
Description/Symptoms: Inability to smoothly shift gears or transmission may hesitate during acceleration.
Solution: Replacing or repairing the transmission components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Front Strut Mount Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged strut mounts can result in clunking noises, reduced handling, and tire wear.
Solution: Replacing the front strut mounts.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: Vehicle battery discharges quickly even when not in use.
Solution: Diagnosing and repairing the electrical system to identify the source of the drain.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Fog Light Condensation**
Description/Symptoms: Accumulation of moisture inside the fog lights, reducing visibility in certain weather conditions.
Solution: Replacing the affected fog lights.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Dashboard Rattle**
Description/Symptoms: Noises emanating from the dashboard, particularly during uneven road conditions.
Solution: Identifying and securing the source of the rattle.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2014 Toyota Venza is a reliable vehicle with few significant issues reported. The engine and transmission problems are relatively minor and can be resolved at a reasonable cost. The suspension and electrical issues are more common, but still not widespread. The exterior and interior issues are mainly minor inconveniences. Overall, the Venza provides good value for money and is a solid choice for those seeking a comfortable and practical crossover SUV.
