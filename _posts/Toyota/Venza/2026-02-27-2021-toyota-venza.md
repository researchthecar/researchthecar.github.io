# 2021 Toyota Venza Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Toyota Venza brings stylish styling and excellent fuel efficiency to the midsize hybrid crossover market. As a modern Toyota hybrid, it generally enjoys a reputation for solid mechanical durability, but used-car buyers should review potential owner-reported trouble spots such as spontaneous windshield cracking and minor electronics glitches.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Hybrid Engine

The 2.5-liter four-cylinder hybrid powertrain pairs with electric motors to produce a net 219 horsepower, offering smooth daily acceleration and impressive fuel economy. This setup is fundamentally robust and shares its proven architecture with other mainstream Toyota hybrid models.

## Transmission

### Electronically Controlled Continuously Variable Transmission (ECVT)

An electronic continuously variable transmission manages power delivery seamlessly between the gasoline engine and electric motors. It typically delivers trouble-free operation and smooth power transitions under normal driving conditions.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Braking system complaints involve intermittent pedal feedback anomalies and post-software-update parking brake quirks.

**Brake Pedal Feel and Unexpected Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** Some drivers have reported unusual firmness, temporary loss of expected deceleration, or unexpected parking brake engagement upon coming to a stop.
- **Solution:** Verify that all relevant traction control and braking software recalls or updates have been properly performed by a certified dealer.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Headlights

Electrical system reports primarily center on central display malfunctions and headlight performance nuances.

**Infotainment Screen and Headlight Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** The central touchscreen display may intermittently fail, go blank, or exhibit connectivity loops, while some owners note dark shadows or beam distribution issues from the headlights.
- **Solution:** Check all infotainment functions and software versions during a test drive. Dealership software updates or hardware module replacements may be necessary if issues persist.

## Exterior

### Visibility and Windshield

Owner complaints highlight susceptibility to unexpected windshield stress cracks, which can impair forward visibility and require out-of-pocket replacement.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report that long cracks develop suddenly across the windshield without any obvious impact from rocks or road debris.
- **Solution:** Inspect the windshield thoroughly before purchase; replacement requires a professional glass swap, often including recalibration of advanced safety cameras.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V239000: Electronic Stability Control (Esc):Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Venza&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The Skid Control ECU software may not turn on the Vehicle Stability Control (VSC) system when the vehicle is restarted after the driver has disabled the VSC system. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 126, "Electronic Stability Control Systems." Driving with a deactivated stability.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Skid Control ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Toyota Venza is a comfortable, highly efficient hybrid crossover that generally maintains Toyota's strong reputation for powertrain reliability. Prospective buyers should carefully inspect the windshield for stress fractures, test all infotainment and electronic features, and ensure that all manufacturer recalls and software updates have been completed.
