# 2022 Toyota Venza Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Toyota Venza is a stylish and efficient two-row hybrid crossover, offering standard all-wheel drive and strong fuel economy. While generally appreciated for its smooth ride and upscale interior, used-car buyers should review a few recurring owner complaints regarding exterior lighting patterns, sudden windshield stress cracks, and occasional hesitation or unexpected acceleration traits reported in specific driving conditions.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Hybrid

The Venza features a 2.5-liter four-cylinder hybrid powertrain producing a combined 219 horsepower paired with an electronically controlled CVT. This setup delivers excellent fuel efficiency and standard electronic all-wheel drive, though hybrid system software calibration remains important for smooth throttle response.

**Acceleration Delay**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report pressing the accelerator pedal after stopping or at a green light, resulting in a momentary delay or lack of immediate power delivery before the vehicle responds.
- **Solution:** Have the powertrain control module (PCM) and hybrid system software checked by a dealer for any applicable updates.

## Transmission

### Electronically Controlled Continuously Variable Transmission (eCVT)

Power is routed through a planetary-gear hybrid transmission seamlessly blending gas and electric power. It generally operates reliably with minimal mechanical complaints reported by owners.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

The electrical system controls advanced LED lighting and driver assists, with headlight beam projection patterns receiving the highest volume of owner scrutiny.

**Headlight Shadowing and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note that the low-beam headlights cast distinct shadow lines resembling a pulled shade across the road at night, reducing nighttime visibility. Individual headlamp failures have also been reported.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight housings and bulbs for failure; headlamp beam adjustments may help address projection patterns, though some owners find the design inherently polarizing.

### Windshield and Glass

Exterior body and glass elements are generally durable, though reports indicate susceptibility to thermal stress fractures on factory windshields.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** The front windshield develops long stress cracks originating near the bottom base or corners, frequently during cold weather changes, without direct impact from road debris.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged windshield through an auto glass specialist, ensuring proper recalibration of any attached driver-assist cameras.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance and Brakes

Safety equipment includes comprehensive Toyota Safety Sense features, though rare owner accounts point to unusual low-speed vehicle surges.

**Unexpected Vehicle Surge**
- **Symptoms:** In isolated incidents during low-speed parking maneuvers or while using brake hold, the vehicle has unexpectedly lurched or lunged forward despite steady brake pedal application.
- **Solution:** Have the braking system, hybrid regeneration software, and collision avoidance sensors inspected by a qualified technician.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V239000: Electronic Stability Control (Esc):Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Venza&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The Skid Control ECU software may not turn on the Vehicle Stability Control (VSC) system when the vehicle is restarted after the driver has disabled the VSC system. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 126, "Electronic Stability Control Systems." Driving with a deactivated stability.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Skid Control ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Toyota Venza is a comfortable, highly efficient hybrid crossover that brings premium style to the mainstream market. Prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the windshield for stress cracks, verify proper headlight performance during a test drive after dark, and pay close attention to low-speed throttle and brake behavior to ensure everything operates smoothly.
