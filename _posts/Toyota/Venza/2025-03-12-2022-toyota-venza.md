The 2022 Toyota Venza is a hybrid-only mid-size SUV that offers a blend of fuel efficiency and a comfortable, upscale interior. It is powered by a 2.5-liter 4-cylinder engine paired with three electric motors, producing a combined 219 horsepower. The Venza uses an Electronically Controlled Continuously Variable Transmission (ECVT). People generally praise the Venza for its smooth ride, quiet cabin, excellent fuel economy, and stylish design. Criticisms often include its limited cargo space, lack of a more powerful engine option, and reliance on touch-sensitive controls for some functions.

**Electric Motor**

**2.5L Hybrid System**
*   **Battery Degradation/Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced electric range, decreased fuel economy, and/or "Check Hybrid System" warning light. In some cases, the battery may fail completely, preventing the vehicle from starting.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the hybrid battery pack.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $8,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Electronically Controlled Continuously Variable Transmission (ECVT)**
*   **Hesitation/Jerking During Acceleration**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported a hesitation or slight jerking sensation during acceleration, particularly at lower speeds. This can feel like a momentary lag or a rough shift.
    *   Solution: Software updates to the transmission control module (TCM) have been reported to improve or resolve this issue. In some cases, a more in-depth diagnosis of the transmission may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software update: $100 - $300. More intensive repairs: $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may freeze, become unresponsive, or crash intermittently. This can affect navigation, audio, climate control, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Performing a system reset, updating the software, or, in some cases, replacing the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software update: $0 - $150. Replacement of unit: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Blind Spot Monitoring, Lane Departure Alert, etc.)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: False alerts, failure to detect objects, or complete system failure related to the various driver-assistance systems.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of the faulty sensor(s) or recalibration of the system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Buzzing Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling sounds emanating from the dashboard, door panels, or rear cargo area while driving.
    *   Solution: Identifying and securing the source of the rattle, which may involve tightening loose fasteners, adding padding, or replacing damaged trim pieces.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+ (depending on the complexity and source of the rattle)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the cabin through the sunroof, typically during heavy rain or car washes.
    *   Solution: Cleaning the sunroof drain tubes to remove any obstructions, repairing or replacing damaged sunroof seals, or resealing the sunroof assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Cleaning drains: $50 - $150. Seal replacement: $200 - $500+. Resealing Assembly: $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Summary:**

The 2022 Toyota Venza, while generally reliable, has some common issues owners have reported. Electrical issues with the infotainment system and sensors appear to be the most prevalent, followed by interior rattling. The hybrid system, while generally reliable, has a potential for battery degradation over time. Sunroof leaks, while less common, can also be a source of frustration. The severity of these issues varies, with some being minor annoyances and others requiring more significant repairs. The cost to fix these issues can range from a few dollars for a software update or cleaning a drain to several thousand dollars for replacing a hybrid battery pack or infotainment system.
