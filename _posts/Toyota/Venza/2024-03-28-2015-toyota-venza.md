**Introduction**

The 2015 Toyota Venza is a mid-size crossover SUV that offers a comfortable and reliable driving experience. It is powered by a standard 2.7L 4-cylinder engine mated to a 6-speed automatic transmission. The Venza also offers an optional 3.5L V6 engine with a 6-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

* **2.7L 4-Cylinder**
    * **Excessive Oil Consumption**
        * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption may require frequent top-ups or lead to engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace piston rings or engine assembly.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **3.5L V6**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Automatic**
    * **Shift Hesitation**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may hesitate or jerk during gear changes.
        * Solution: Update transmission software or replace transmission valve body.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Lower Control Arm Bushings**
    * **Premature Wear**
        * Description/Symptoms: Excessive wear on the lower control arm bushings can cause alignment issues and premature tire wear.
        * Solution: Replace lower control arm bushings.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electrical Short in Driver Side Door**
    * Description/Symptoms: A short in the driver's side door can cause electrical problems such as power window failure or door lock malfunction.
    * Solution: Replace faulty wiring harness or door module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Failed Side Mirror Adjustment Switch**
    * Description/Symptoms: The side mirror adjustment switch may become inoperable, preventing mirror adjustments.
    * Solution: Replace side mirror adjustment switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Faulty Power Lock/Unlock Switch**
    * Description/Symptoms: The power lock/unlock switch may stick or become inoperable, affecting lock/unlock functionality.
    * Solution: Replace power lock/unlock switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2015 Toyota Venza is a reliable vehicle with relatively few major issues. The engine oil consumption issue and the electrical short in the driver side door are the most severe and potentially expensive repairs. However, most issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix.
