**Introduction**

The 2021 Toyota Venza is a mid-size crossover SUV that combines sleek styling with advanced technology. It comes with a standard 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine and an optional 3.3-liter V6 engine. Both engines are paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission. The Venza has been praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and impressive fuel economy. However, some owners have reported experiencing issues with certain components.

**Engine**

**2.5-liter Four-Cylinder Engine**

* **Valve Spring Recall:** Toyota has issued a recall for certain Venza models with the 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine due to a potential issue with the valve springs. Affected vehicles may experience a loss of engine power or stalling. The solution involves replacing the valve springs. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** Free (under warranty)
* **Fuel Injector Failure:** Some owners have reported experiencing fuel injector failures, which can cause rough idling or starting problems. The solution involves replacing the faulty fuel injectors. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+
* **Intake Manifold Cracking:** Owners have reported issues with cracking intake manifolds, which can lead to coolant leaks or rough engine performance. The solution involves replacing the intake manifold. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $800+

**3.3-liter V6 Engine**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Eight-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Shudder:** Some owners have reported experiencing transmission shudder when shifting gears. The issue may be caused by a faulty torque converter or valve body. The solution involves replacing the affected components. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Hesitation When Accelerating:** Owners have also reported experiencing hesitation when accelerating, especially at low speeds. The cause of the issue is still under investigation, but it may be related to a faulty transmission control module. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Bearing Failure:** Some owners have reported premature failure of the strut mount bearings, which can cause a clunking or knocking noise from the suspension. The solution involves replacing the affected bearings. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Some owners have experienced excessive battery drain on their Venza. This can be caused by a faulty alternator, parasitic drain, or other electrical issues. The solution involves diagnosing and repairing the underlying cause. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $600+
* **Infotainment System Glitches:** Owners have also reported occasional glitches with the infotainment system, including unresponsive touchscreens and audio issues. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $0 - $500+ (depending on the severity of the issue)

**Interior**

* **Panoramic Sunroof Issues:** Some owners have reported water leaks or rattling noises coming from the panoramic sunroof. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+
* **Climate Control Malfunctions:** Owners have also experienced issues with the climate control system, including ineffective heating or cooling performance. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $600+

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Summary**

The 2021 Toyota Venza is generally a reliable and well-equipped vehicle. However, some owners have encountered issues with certain components, including the engine, transmission, electrical system, and interior. While some of these issues can be minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more severe and require costly repairs. It is important for potential buyers to be aware of these potential issues and to factor them into their purchasing decision.
