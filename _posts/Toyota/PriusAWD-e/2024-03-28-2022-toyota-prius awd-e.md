**Introduction**

The 2022 Toyota Prius AWD-e is a hybrid vehicle that combines a 1.8-liter four-cylinder engine with two electric motors. It delivers 121 horsepower and 105 lb-ft of torque and comes standard with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). The Prius AWD-e offers all-wheel drive, making it a versatile choice for various road conditions. It also boasts fuel efficiency of up to 54 mpg in the city and 50 mpg on the highway.

While the Prius AWD-e has received praise for its fuel efficiency and reliability, it has not been without reported issues.

**Engine**

**1.8-Liter Four-Cylinder**

* **Engine Oil Consumption (Excessive)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported excessive engine oil consumption, requiring frequent top-ups. The issue may be caused by worn piston rings or valve seals.
    * Solution: Replace the affected parts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Belt Tensioner Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing belt tensioner may fail prematurely, leading to engine damage if not addressed.
    * Solution: Replace the tensioner and timing belt.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

* **Hesitation and Shuddering**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced hesitation and shuddering during acceleration or gear changes.
    * Solution: Reprogram the transmission control module (TCM) or replace the CVT assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Mount Bearing Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The front strut mount bearings may wear prematurely, leading to noise and vibration during driving.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mount bearings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Headlight Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture can accumulate inside the headlight assemblies, causing condensation and reduced visibility.
    * Solution: Replace the headlight assemblies.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **12-Volt Battery Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The 12-volt battery may fail prematurely, causing the vehicle to not start or have electrical issues.
    * Solution: Replace the battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Trim Panel Fading**
    * Description/Symptoms: The exterior trim panels may fade or discolor over time, affecting the vehicle's aesthetics.
    * Solution: Replace or repaint the affected panels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Lag**
    * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may experience lag or slow response times.
    * Solution: Update the system software or replace the head unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Climate Control Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control system may malfunction, leading to inconsistent heating or cooling.
    * Solution: Replace the climate control module or affected actuators.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Toyota Prius AWD-e has been generally well-received for its fuel efficiency and reliability. However, like any vehicle, it is not without its potential issues. Some of the commonly reported problems include excessive engine oil consumption, timing belt tensioner failure, CVT hesitation, front strut mount bearing failure, headlight condensation, 12-volt battery failure, trim panel fading, infotainment system lag, and climate control malfunctions. While the severity and cost of these issues can vary, they are important to consider when making a purchasing decision.
