### Hybrid Battery System
**Reduced Hybrid Battery Performance**
- **Symptoms:** Decreased fuel economy, reduced electric-only driving range, check hybrid system warning light.
- **Solution:** Hybrid battery pack replacement.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $4,000


### 12V Auxiliary Battery
**Premature 12V Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the vehicle, electrical system malfunctions (e.g., lights flickering, infotainment issues).
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V auxiliary battery.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Toyota Prius C demonstrates strong overall reliability, consistent with Toyota's reputation. The powertrain, including the 1.5L hybrid engine and ECVT transmission, is exceptionally robust with no widespread reported issues. The primary areas where owners might encounter expenses are related to the electrical system, specifically the potential for hybrid battery degradation over time and the occasional premature failure of the 12V auxiliary battery. These are not uncommon for hybrid vehicles as they age. Exterior and interior components generally hold up well, with no significant recurring problems noted. For prospective buyers, focusing on the vehicle's hybrid battery health is advisable, but overall, the Prius C remains a highly dependable choice for economical and reliable transportation.
