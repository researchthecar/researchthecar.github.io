## 2013 Toyota Prius C

The 2013 Toyota Prius C is a subcompact hybrid hatchback that offers an impressive 50 mpg combined fuel economy. It comes standard with a 1.5-liter inline-four engine and a continuously variable transmission (CVT). Other engine and transmission options include a 1.8-liter inline-four engine and a six-speed automatic transmission.

Despite its fuel efficiency and affordability, the Prius C has received mixed reviews from consumers. Some people find it to be cramped and uncomfortable, while others appreciate its low cost and fuel savings.

## Engine

### 1.5L Inline-Four Engine

- **Cylinder Head Gasket Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Blown head gasket can result in coolant leaks, overheating, and white smoke from the exhaust
    - Solution: Replace the cylinder head gasket
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
    - Recalls: None Found

### 1.8L Inline-Four Engine

- **No Issues Found**

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

- **Transmission Slipping:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The transmission experiences a loss of power or hesitates when shifting
    - Solution: Replace the transmission fluid and/or the transmission control module (TCM)
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000
    - Recalls: None Found

### Six-Speed Automatic Transmission

- **No Issues Found**

## Suspension

- **Strut Mount Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Worn strut mounts can cause squeaking and knocking noises, as well as poor handling
    - Solution: Replace the strut mounts
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    - Recalls: None Found

## Electrical

- **Battery Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The car struggles to start or experiences a loss of power
    - Solution: Replace the battery
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    - Recalls: None Found

- **Alternator Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The car has difficulty charging the battery, causing dim lights and other electrical problems
    - Solution: Replace the alternator
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    - Recalls: None Found

## Exterior

- **Paint Peeling (Front Bumper)**
    - Description/Symptoms: The paint on the front bumper peels or flakes off
    - Solution: Repaint the front bumper
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    - Recalls: None Found

## Interior

- **Dashboard Rattle:**
    - Description/Symptoms: The dashboard rattles or vibrates at certain speeds or when driving over bumps
    - Solution: Tighten the dashboard bolts or apply damping material
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    - Recalls: None Found

## Summary

Overall, the 2013 Toyota Prius C is a reliable and fuel-efficient vehicle. However, it does have some potential issues, including cylinder head gasket failure, transmission slipping, strut mount failure, and paint peeling. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but most are relatively minor and affordable to fix.
