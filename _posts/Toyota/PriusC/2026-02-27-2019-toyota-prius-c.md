# 2019 Toyota Prius C Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Toyota Prius C represents the final model year for Toyota's subcompact hybrid hatchback, offering exceptional fuel economy and nimble city driving dynamics. Known for its dependable hybrid powertrain and straightforward mechanical design, this vehicle generally attracts buyers seeking ultra-low operating costs. While owner complaints in public logs are minimal, used car shoppers should still evaluate the overall vehicle condition and hybrid system health before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L 4-Cylinder Hybrid (1NZ-FXE)

The 1.5-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine operates on the Atkinson cycle and combines with an electric motor to generate a total system output of 99 horsepower. It is paired with an electronically controlled planetary-type continuously variable transmission, offering robust reliability and stellar fuel efficiency suited for daily commuting.

## Transmission

### Electronically Controlled Continuously Variable Transmission (ECVT)

The hybrid transaxle seamlessly blends power from the gasoline engine and electric motor without traditional gear ratios. This design is exceptionally durable and rarely experiences mechanical failures under normal operating conditions.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V491000: Hybrid Propulsion System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Prius+C&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The DC-DC converter may fail resulting in the auxiliary battery no longer receiving power from the converter, which may cause a loss of drive. A loss of drive increases the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the DC-DC converter with a new one, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Toyota Prius C maintains a strong reputation for dependability, largely thanks to its proven hybrid powertrain and efficient subcompact design. Buyers looking at used models will find a solid commuter car with minimal documented recurring mechanical issues. As with any used hybrid, checking the health of the hybrid battery and ensuring a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection remain sensible steps.
