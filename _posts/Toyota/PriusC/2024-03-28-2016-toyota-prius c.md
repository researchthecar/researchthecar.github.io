**2016 Toyota Prius C**

The 2016 Toyota Prius C is a subcompact hybrid hatchback that offers fuel-efficient and environmentally friendly transportation. It comes with a 1.5-liter four-cylinder engine paired with an electric motor, delivering 99 horsepower and 103 lb-ft of torque. The Prius C is available with two different transmission options: a Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) and a 4-speed automatic transmission.

Owners of the 2016 Toyota Prius C have reported various issues, ranging from minor annoyances to more serious problems. Here is a comprehensive list of these issues categorized by major components:

**Engine**

* **Engine Knock**
    * Description/Symptoms: Knocking or ticking sounds coming from the engine, especially when accelerating or under load.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the connecting rod bearings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-ups.
    * Solution: Piston ring replacement or engine overhaul.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **CVT Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering, slipping, or complete failure of the transmission.
    * Solution: Replacement of the CVT unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **4-Speed Automatic Transmission Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slips or fails to shift properly.
    * Solution: Internal transmission repair or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Bearing Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling sounds from the front suspension, especially over rough roads.
    * Solution: Replacement of the strut mount bearings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery drains quickly or fails to hold a charge.
    * Solution: Inspection and replacement of faulty electrical components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Electrical System Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Electrical components malfunctioning, such as lights, gauges, or power windows.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of faulty electrical systems.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rear Hatchback Struts**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hatchback struts fail, causing the hatch to not stay open.
    * Solution: Replacement of the struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Climate control system malfunctions, blowing hot or cold air despite settings.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of faulty climate control components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Radio Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Radio system fails to turn on or malfunctions, such as losing preset stations.
    * Solution: Replacement of the radio.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Toyota Prius C has received positive reviews for its fuel efficiency and eco-friendliness. However, it has been plagued by a number of issues, including engine problems, transmission failures, suspension issues, and electrical malfunctions. The severity and cost of these issues can vary, and some may require significant repairs. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems before making a purchase decision.
