# 2016 Toyota Prius C

### Overview
The 2016 Toyota Prius C is a subcompact hybrid hatchback known for its exceptional fuel efficiency and urban maneuverability. As a member of the renowned Toyota Prius family, it inherits a reputation for reliability, particularly in its hybrid powertrain. This model is designed for city driving, offering a comfortable ride and a practical interior for its size. While generally robust, like any vehicle, it can experience specific issues. This report details common technical reliability data, focusing on potential component failures beyond standard maintenance, to provide a comprehensive understanding of its long-term durability.

### Hybrid Battery System
**Reduced Hybrid Battery Performance**
- **Symptoms:** Decreased fuel economy, reduced electric-only driving range, hybrid system warning lights.
- **Solution:** Hybrid battery pack replacement.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $4,000


### 12V Auxiliary Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the gasoline engine, electrical system malfunctions (e.g., infotainment, lights), warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V auxiliary battery.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Toyota Prius C demonstrates strong overall reliability, consistent with Toyota's reputation. The hybrid powertrain, including the engine and transmission, is exceptionally robust with no widespread reported issues. The primary areas where owners might encounter costs beyond routine maintenance are related to the hybrid battery system, which can degrade over time, and the 12V auxiliary battery, which has a typical lifespan. Minor electrical glitches within the infotainment system can occur but are usually resolvable with software updates. The exterior and interior components are generally durable. With proactive maintenance and awareness of potential hybrid battery aging, the 2016 Prius C is a dependable and economical choice.
