# 2012 Toyota Prius C

### Overview
The 2012 Toyota Prius C is a subcompact hybrid hatchback known for its exceptional fuel efficiency and urban maneuverability. As a member of the renowned Toyota Prius family, it inherits a reputation for reliability, particularly in its hybrid powertrain. This model is designed for cost-conscious drivers prioritizing low running costs and environmental friendliness. While generally robust, like any vehicle, it can experience specific issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas and their associated repair costs for owners and prospective buyers.

## Electrical

### Hybrid Battery System
**Hybrid Battery Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced fuel economy, loss of electric-only driving range, dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Hybrid battery pack replacement.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $4,000


### 12V Auxiliary Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the vehicle, electrical system malfunctions (lights flickering, infotainment issues).
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V auxiliary battery.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Toyota Prius C is a highly reliable vehicle, especially concerning its core powertrain. The hybrid system is generally robust, with most reported issues stemming from the long-term health of the hybrid battery pack, which can degrade over time and require replacement. The 12V auxiliary battery is another component that may require attention. Other systems like the engine, transmission, suspension, exterior, and interior components show a strong track record with no widespread significant failures reported. Owners should be aware of potential hybrid battery costs as the vehicle ages, but overall, the Prius C offers excellent long-term dependability and low running costs.
