**Introduction**

The 1991 Toyota Corolla is a subcompact car available in multiple trim levels. It boasts a 1.6L inline-4 engine that produces 105 horsepower and 105 lb-ft of torque. The Corolla offers a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission. It is known for its reliability, fuel efficiency, and affordability. However, like any vehicle, it has encountered some issues over the years.

**Engine**

**1.6L Inline-4 Engine**

* **Head gasket failure:** This issue can cause coolant and oil to leak from the engine. Symptoms include coolant loss, overheating, and engine performance issues. The fix involves replacing the head gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1200+
* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** This issue can cause rough idling, reduced engine performance, and increased emissions. Symptoms include a hissing or whistling noise from the engine. The fix involves replacing the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Clutch failure:** This issue can cause difficulty shifting gears or a loss of power. Symptoms include a slipping or chattering clutch. The fix involves replacing the clutch assembly. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission slipping:** This issue can cause difficulty shifting gears or a loss of power. Symptoms include a lack of response from the transmission when shifting. The fix involves rebuilding or replacing the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1200 - $2500+

**Suspension**

* **Strut failure:** This issue can cause a decline in handling and stability of the vehicle. Symptoms include excessive bouncing or clunking sounds from the suspension. The fix involves replacing the front or rear struts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+ per pair
* **Ball joint failure:** This issue can increase steering play and clunking noises during cornering. The fix involves replacing the ball joints. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per side

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** This issue can prevent the battery from charging. Symptoms include dim headlights, flickering dashboard lights, and electrical system malfunctions. The fix involves replacing the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Starter failure:** This issue can prevent the engine from starting. Symptoms include a clicking or grinding noise when attempting to start the engine. The fix involves replacing the starter motor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Exterior**

* **Rust on body panels:** This issue can affect the aesthetic appearance of the vehicle and lead to structural problems. Symptoms include visible rust spots on the body. The fix involves repairing or replacing the affected body panels. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity of the rust
* **Door lock malfunctions:** This issue can prevent the doors from locking or unlocking properly. Symptoms include difficulty opening or closing the doors. The fix involves replacing the door lock actuators or repairing the door lock mechanism. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracking:** This issue can affect the aesthetics of the interior. Symptoms include cracks or fading on the dashboard. The fix involves replacing the dashboard. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
* **Air conditioning failure:** This issue can cause a reduction in cabin comfort. Symptoms include a lack of cold air from the vents. The fix involves repairing or replacing the air conditioning compressor, condenser, or other components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+

**Summary**

Overall, the 1991 Toyota Corolla is a reliable vehicle, but it has encountered some common issues over the years. Engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior issues can arise, with varying costs to fix. While some issues like head gasket failure and transmission slipping can be more severe and expensive to fix, many of the other issues are relatively minor and can be resolved for a reasonable cost. It's important to note that these issues are based on reported instances and may not affect every Corolla produced.
