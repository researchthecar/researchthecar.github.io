**Introduction:**

The 2009 Toyota Corolla is a compact sedan offered with two engine options: a 1.8L 4-cylinder and a 2.4L 4-cylinder. The 1.8L engine produces 132 horsepower and 128 lb-ft of torque, while the 2.4L engine produces 158 horsepower and 162 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired to either a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission. The Corolla is known for its reliability and fuel efficiency, but it has also had some reported issues over the years.

**Engine**

**1.8L 4-Cylinder Engine with 5-Speed Manual Transmission**

**Intake Valve Carbon Buildup**
Description/Symptoms: Carbon deposits accumulate on the intake valves, reducing airflow and engine performance. This can cause rough idling, hesitation, and loss of power.
Solution: Cleaning the intake valves and decarbonizing the engine.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
Recalls: None Found

**2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine with 4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

**Engine Oil Consumption**
Description/Symptoms: The engine may consume excessive oil, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage. Symptoms include blue smoke from the exhaust and an oil leak under the vehicle.
Solution: Replacing the piston rings or valve seals.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**1.8L 4-Cylinder Engine with 5-Speed Manual Transmission**

**No Issues Found**

**2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine with 4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

**Transmission Slipping**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip or hesitate when shifting gears, causing a loss of power and jerky acceleration.
Solution: Replacing the transmission fluid and filter, or rebuilding the transmission.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Dashboard Rattle**
Description/Symptoms: The dashboard may rattle or make noise, particularly over rough roads.
Solution: Tightening the dashboard screws or adding sound-dampening material.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2009 Toyota Corolla has a good reliability record, but there are a few issues that owners should be aware of. The most severe issue is the engine oil consumption in the 2.4L engine, which can be a costly repair. The other issues are relatively minor and can be resolved with routine maintenance or inexpensive repairs.
