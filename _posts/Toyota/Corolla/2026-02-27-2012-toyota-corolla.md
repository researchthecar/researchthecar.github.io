# 2012 Toyota Corolla

### Overview
The 2012 Toyota Corolla is widely recognized for its exceptional reliability and durability, making it a popular choice for budget-conscious buyers and those seeking a dependable daily driver. Toyota's engineering prowess is evident in the Corolla's robust powertrain and well-built chassis, which contribute to its strong resale value and low ownership costs. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the 2012 Corolla generally exhibits a strong track record, with most owners reporting satisfaction with its performance and longevity. Common areas of concern, though infrequent, tend to be minor and addressable.

- **Solution:** Replacement of window regulator or motor.
- **Cost:** $250 - $600

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Premature battery discharge, potentially due to parasitic draw from accessories or faulty modules.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of parasitic draw, or replacement of faulty module.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Toyota Corolla stands out as a highly reliable vehicle with a commendable track record. Its powertrain, including the 1.8L engine and available CVT or manual transmissions, is exceptionally robust and rarely presents significant issues. The suspension and exterior components are also generally trouble-free. The most commonly reported, albeit infrequent, issues tend to be electrical in nature, such as intermittent power window problems or occasional battery drain, which are typically manageable with professional diagnosis and repair. Overall, the 2012 Corolla offers excellent long-term value and dependability, making it a sound investment for those prioritizing reliability.
