**Introduction to the 2022 Toyota Corolla**

The 2022 Toyota Corolla is a compact sedan known for its reliability, fuel efficiency, and affordability. It comes with a choice of two engine options: a 1.8-liter 4-cylinder with 139 horsepower and 126 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.0-liter 4-cylinder with 169 horsepower and 151 lb-ft of torque. Both engines can be paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT) or a 6-speed manual transmission.

Despite its popularity, the 2022 Toyota Corolla has had some reported issues.

**Engine**

**1.8-Liter 4-Cylinder**

* **Valve Spring Failure:** This issue affects early production models and can lead to a catastrophic engine failure. The symptoms include knocking sounds from the engine, reduced power, and eventually engine seizure. The solution is to replace the affected valve springs.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2,000 - $3,000+
* **Recalls:** NHTSA Campaign Number 22V-475

**2.0-Liter 4-Cylinder**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

* **Premature Failure:** There have been reports of the CVT failing prematurely, causing the vehicle to lose power or become inoperable. The symptoms include slipping gears, a burning smell, and warning lights on the dashboard. The solution is to replace the transmission.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $3,500 - $5,000+
* **Recalls:** NHTSA Campaign Number 22V-191

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Faulty Wiring Harness:** Some vehicles have experienced electrical issues due to a faulty wiring harness. The symptoms can include flickering lights, power outages, and engine stalling. The solution is to replace the wiring harness.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Malfunction:** The door handles on some vehicles have become loose or inoperable. The symptoms include difficulty opening the doors and the handles falling off. The solution is to replace the affected door handles.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $300
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:** The climate control system in some vehicles has been reported to malfunction, causing the temperature to fluctuate or the system to fail altogether. The solution is to replace the affected components.
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary**

While the 2022 Toyota Corolla is generally a reliable vehicle, there have been some reported issues. The most severe and expensive issue is the valve spring failure on the 1.8-liter engine, which has been the subject of a recall. Other issues include premature CVT failure, faulty wiring harnesses, door handle malfunctions, and climate control malfunctions. It's important to note that these issues may not affect all vehicles and that the severity and cost of repairs can vary depending on specific circumstances.
