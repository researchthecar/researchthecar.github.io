## 1990 Toyota Corolla

The 1990 Toyota Corolla is a compact car known for its reliability and fuel efficiency. It was available with two 4-cylinder engine options, a 1.6-liter and a 1.8-liter, paired with either a 4-speed automatic or 5-speed manual transmission.

Drivers appreciate the Corolla's affordability, practicality, and low maintenance costs. However, it has its drawbacks, including limited interior space, especially in the rear seats.

### **Engine**
- **1.6-liter 4-Cylinder Engine**
  - **Oil Leaks from Valve Cover Gasket**: Oil starts leaking from the valve cover area, causing drops to accumulate underneath the engine.
    - Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

- **1.8-liter 4-Cylinder Engine**
  - **No Issues Found**

### **Transmission**
- **4-Speed Automatic Transmission**
  - **Transmission Slipping**: The transmission struggles to engage gears and slips noticeably, causing the vehicle to lose power.
    - Solution: Replace the transmission fluid and filter, or repair/replace internal components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
  - **Torque Converter Failure**: The torque converter fails to transmit power effectively, causing the transmission to overheat and shudder.
    - Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
- **5-Speed Manual Transmission**
  - **Stuck Clutch Pedal**: The clutch pedal becomes difficult to release or engage due to a faulty clutch master or slave cylinder.
    - Solution: Replace the faulty cylinder.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

### **Suspension**
  - **Strut Mounts Deteriorating**: The rubber mounts that support the strut assemblies become worn and cracked, causing a rattling or knocking noise.
    - Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
  - **Rear Sway Bar Links Failing**: The links that connect the sway bar to the suspension components fail or become loose, leading to excessive body roll and poor handling.
    - Solution: Replace the sway bar links.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

### **Electrical**
  - **Alternator Failure**: The alternator malfunctions, causing a loss of charging power and electrical issues.
    - Solution: Replace the alternator.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
  - **Starter Motor Problems**: The starter motor fails to engage the flywheel, preventing the engine from starting.
    - Solution: Replace the starter motor or repair the solenoid.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

### **Exterior**
  - **Door Locks Malfunctioning**: The door locks become stuck or inoperable due to faulty solenoids or mechanical issues.
    - Solution: Repair or replace the affected door locks.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
  - **Sunroof Leaks**: The sunroof seal degrades over time, allowing water to enter the cabin during rain.
    - Solution: Replace the sunroof seal.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

### **Interior**
  - **Dashboard Cracking**: The dashboard material develops cracks due to sun exposure and heat.
    - Solution: Replace the dashboard or repair the cracks using a dashboard overlay kit.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
  - **Power Window Switches Malfunctioning**: The power window switches fail or become intermittent, preventing the windows from operating properly.
    - Solution: Replace the faulty window switches.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

### Summary
The 1990 Toyota Corolla is a reliable and fuel-efficient vehicle with few major issues. However, potential owners should be aware of the potential for transmission problems, especially with the automatic transmission. The electrical system and suspension components can also develop issues, leading to increased maintenance costs.
