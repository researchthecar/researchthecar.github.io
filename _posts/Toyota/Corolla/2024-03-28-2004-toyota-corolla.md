**Introduction:**

The 2004 Toyota Corolla is a compact sedan known for its reliability, fuel efficiency, and value. It comes with a choice of two engine and transmission options:

**Engine:**
- 1.8L 4-cylinder (130 hp, 125 lb-ft torque)
- 1.8L VVTi 4-cylinder (143 hp, 161 lb-ft torque)

**Transmission:**
- 4-speed automatic
- 5-speed manual

Owners generally appreciate the Corolla's dependability, smooth ride, and spacious interior. However, there have been some reported issues with certain aspects of the vehicle.

**Engine:**

**1.8L 4-cylinder (130 hp)**
- **Excessive oil consumption:** Some owners have experienced high oil consumption, leading to engine damage.
    - Solution: Replace piston rings and valve guides.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**1.8L VVTi 4-cylinder (143 hp)**
- **Coolant leak:** The coolant hose connecting the engine to the radiator may develop a leak.
    - Solution: Replace coolant hose.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission:**

**4-speed automatic**
- **Shifting problems:** The transmission may experience hard shifting or slipping between gears.
    - Solution: Replace or rebuild transmission.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**5-speed manual**
- **No issues found**

**Suspension:**
- **Premature front strut wear:** The front struts may wear out prematurely, leading to a harsh ride.
    - Solution: Replace front struts.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000
    - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical:**
- **Battery drain:** The battery may drain prematurely due to a faulty alternator or parasitic draw.
    - Solution: Test and replace alternator or identify and eliminate the parasitic draw.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
    - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior:**
- **Sunroof leaks:** The sunroof may develop leaks, allowing water to enter the cabin.
    - Solution: Replace sunroof seals.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    - Recalls: None Found

**Interior:**
- **Dashboard rattle:** The dashboard may develop a rattle under certain conditions.
    - Solution: Identify and secure loose components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2004 Toyota Corolla is a reliable and affordable vehicle. However, it has some potential issues, particularly with the engine, transmission, and suspension. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific part and the extent of the problem.
