# 2021 Toyota RAV4 Prime Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Toyota RAV4 Prime is a plug-in hybrid compact SUV known for its impressive electric-only driving range, brisk acceleration, and standard all-wheel-drive capability. While it builds on Toyota's solid reputation for hybrid reliability, used-car buyers should review owner-reported complaints regarding electrical charging systems, sporadic braking quirks over rough pavement, and occasional incidents involving sudden acceleration.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Plug-in Hybrid (e-CVT)

Features a 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine paired with electric motors producing a net 302 horsepower, matched to an electronically controlled continuously variable transmission (e-CVT) and electronic all-wheel drive. This powertrain delivers strong performance and excellent efficiency, though early production models feature a few reported fuel delivery and charging quirks.

**Fuel Tank Filling Limitations**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report being unable to fully fill the fuel tank, with pumps clicking off prematurely and leaving noticeable remaining capacity.
- **Solution:** Dealer inspection and potential replacement of the fuel tank or fuel sender components if found to be defective.

## Transmission

### Electronically Controlled Continuously Variable Transmission (e-CVT)

An electronically controlled continuously variable transmission (e-CVT) seamlessly blends power from the gasoline engine and electric motors to deliver smooth, efficient power to the all-wheel-drive system with a strong reliability record.

## Suspension

### Suspension and Tires

Handles ride comfort and road handling, though factory tires and alignment settings may require close monitoring.

**Premature Tire Wear and Cupping**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual cabin vibrations, loud road noise, and uneven tire wear patterns indicating alignment sensitivity.
- **Solution:** Perform a professional four-wheel alignment and replace tires as necessary.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes & ABS Integration

The regenerative braking system integrates with traditional friction brakes, though some drivers note unusual feedback on rough or slick roads.

**Brake Slip and Grinding on Rough Surfaces**
- **Symptoms:** A momentary grinding noise or a slipping sensation accompanied by a temporary loss of firm braking feel when traveling over bumps or wet pavement.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the anti-lock brake system and regenerative braking software calibration.

## Electrical

### Onboard Charger and Electrical System

Manages high-voltage power distribution, onboard charging, and accessory electronics, though owners have noted occasional charging system faults.

**Charging System Failures and Odors**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual arcing noises, electrical burning smells, warning lights on the dashboard, and failure to accept a charge when plugging in Level 2 charging equipment.
- **Solution:** Professional diagnostic inspection and replacement of faulty onboard charging modules or affected high-voltage wiring connectors.

## Exterior

### Glass and Sunroof

Covers exterior glass and body openings, with isolated owner complaints regarding spontaneous glass breakage.

**Spontaneous Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden cracking or shattering of the panoramic sunroof or rear windshield while driving or closing doors without external impact.
- **Solution:** Replace damaged glass panels under comprehensive insurance or warranty if applicable, ensuring proper frame alignment.

## Safety

### Collision Avoidance and Powertrain Controls

Includes advanced driver assistance systems and electronic safety features designed to protect occupants, though sporadic low-speed incidents have been reported.

**Unexpected Acceleration Reports**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle suddenly surging forward or accelerating unexpectedly while maneuvering at low speeds or preparing to change gears.
- **Solution:** Verify floor mat placement and have dealer check electronic throttle body and pedal sensor assemblies.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V698000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=RAV4+Prime&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Adjusting the horizontal headlight beam can increase glare, reducing the visibility of an oncoming driver or the vehicle operator, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will close the aiming caps on the headlights, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V239000: Electronic Stability Control (Esc):Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=RAV4+Prime&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The Skid Control ECU software may not turn on the Vehicle Stability Control (VSC) system when the vehicle is restarted after the driver has disabled the VSC system. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 126, "Electronic Stability Control Systems." Driving with a deactivated stability.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Skid Control ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V041000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Hybrid Management:Power Control Unit/Module (Hpcu)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=RAV4+Prime&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** During certain cold weather driving conditions, while driving in EV Mode, the hybrid vehicle control engine control unit (HEV ECU) software may cause the hybrid system to shut down if the accelerator is rapidly pressed to further accelerate the vehicle, resulting in a stall. An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the HEV ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V478000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Dc/Dc Converter](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=RAV4+Prime&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A short circuit can cause the DC-DC converter to overheat, increasing the risk of a vehicle fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the DC-DC converter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Toyota RAV4 Prime is a highly capable and efficient plug-in hybrid that offers strong overall performance, but used-car buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle before purchase. Pay close attention to service records regarding charging system bulletins, check for proper brake operation over uneven surfaces, and verify that all safety and electrical systems function as intended.
