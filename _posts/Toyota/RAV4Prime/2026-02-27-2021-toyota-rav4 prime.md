# 2021 Toyota RAV4 Prime

### Overview
The 2021 Toyota RAV4 Prime, a plug-in hybrid variant of the popular compact SUV, is generally lauded for its impressive fuel efficiency, strong performance, and Toyota's reputation for reliability. It combines a 2.5L four-cylinder engine with electric motors to deliver a potent and efficient powertrain. Owners often praise its comfortable ride, practical interior, and advanced safety features. While the RAV4 Prime benefits from Toyota's extensive engineering and testing, like any vehicle, it can experience specific issues. This report focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of attention for owners and prospective buyers.

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Toyota RAV4 Prime demonstrates a high level of technical reliability, consistent with Toyota's brand reputation. The powertrain, including the 2.5L hybrid engine and eCVT transmission, shows no significant widespread issues. The chassis, suspension, exterior, and interior components are also generally robust. The primary areas where minor concerns have been noted are within the electrical system, specifically rare anomalies with the battery cooling system and occasional software glitches in the infotainment unit. These issues are typically minor and addressable through software updates or professional diagnosis. Overall, the RAV4 Prime is a dependable choice with a strong track record for durability.
