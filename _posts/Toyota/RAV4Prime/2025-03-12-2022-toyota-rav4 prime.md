The 2022 Toyota RAV4 Prime is a plug-in hybrid SUV, offering a blend of efficiency and performance. It features a 2.5-liter inline-4 engine paired with electric motors, producing a combined 302 horsepower and ample torque for quick acceleration. While owners appreciate its fuel economy, electric driving range, and sporty handling, some have noted concerns regarding the infotainment system, availability of the vehicle, and certain quality issues.

**Electric Motor**

**2.5L Inline-4 Engine with Electric Motors**

*   **High Voltage Battery Degradation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced electric driving range over time, especially in colder temperatures. Some owners report noticeable drop in range within the first year.
    *   Solution: Toyota warranty covers hybrid components for a specific period. If degradation exceeds specified limits, battery replacement may be covered.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Battery replacement can range from $6,000 - $8,000+ if not covered by warranty.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen occasionally freezes, becomes unresponsive, or crashes, requiring a system reboot. This can affect navigation, audio, and climate controls.
    *   Solution: Software updates from Toyota may address some of these issues. In some cases, replacement of the head unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 if software update; $500 - $1500+ for head unit replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Charging Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported inconsistent charging behavior, including failure to charge, slow charging speeds, or errors during the charging process. This can occur with both Level 1 and Level 2 charging.
    *   Solution: Check the charging cable and charging port for damage. Ensure the charging equipment is compatible and functioning correctly. Resetting the charging system might help. If the issue persists, a dealer inspection may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 for troubleshooting and resets. Cable/charger replacement $200 - $700+. Diagnosis and repair of charging system $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported rattling or squeaking noises emanating from the dashboard, seats, or rear cargo area, particularly when driving on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Identifying the source of the noise and tightening or lubricating the affected components. Applying felt tape to contact points can also help.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 if self-repaired; $100 - $300+ at a repair shop, depending on the complexity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Seat Comfort Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Complaints of inadequate lumbar support or general discomfort, especially on longer drives.
    *   Solution: Consider aftermarket seat cushions or lumbar support accessories.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $20 - $200+ for aftermarket accessories.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Automatic Liftgate Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: The power liftgate failing to open or close properly, sometimes accompanied by error messages.
    * Solution: Inspect the liftgate struts and sensors. Resetting the system may resolve the issue. Replacement of the struts or sensors may be required in some cases.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the cause and required parts.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2022 Toyota RAV4 Prime generally offers a reliable driving experience, but owners have reported issues that are largely focused around electrical components and some minor interior nuisances. The most concerning issue is high voltage battery degradation, as the cost can be high. The infotainment issues, while annoying, can often be addressed with software updates. The interior and exterior issues are mostly minor inconveniences. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems and inspect the vehicle thoroughly before purchase, paying particular attention to the infotainment system and charging functions. Checking the car's maintenance history for any warranty claims on the hybrid system is also recommended.
