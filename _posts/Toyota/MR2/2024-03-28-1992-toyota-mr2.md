**Introduction:**

The 1992 Toyota MR2 is a mid-engine, rear-wheel-drive sports car powered by a 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine. It is known for its agile handling, performance, and affordability. However, like many vehicles, it has some common issues that owners have reported.

**Engine**

**2.0L 3S-GE Engine**

* **Blown Head Gasket:** Leaks coolant and oil, causes overheating and loss of power. Requires replacement of the head gasket. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1000 - $2000+
* **Piston Ring Failure:** Worn piston rings can cause oil leaks, burning, and reduced engine performance. Requires engine rebuild or replacement. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2000+
* **Timing Belt Tensioner Failure:** Can cause timing belt to slip or break, leading to catastrophic engine damage. Requires replacement of tensioner and belt. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1000
* **Oil Pump Failure:** Insufficient oil pressure can damage engine components. Requires replacement of oil pump. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1000
* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** Causes oil leak and engine bay smoke. Requires resealing of valve cover. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500
* **Exhaust Manifold Warping:** Causes exhaust leaks and engine noise. Requires replacement of manifold. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1000
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** Leaks coolant and air, causing engine performance issues. Requires replacement of gasket. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1000

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Clutch Master Cylinder Failure:** Leaking fluid can cause clutch to slip or engage poorly. Requires replacement of master cylinder. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1000
* **Clutch Slave Cylinder Failure:** Leaking fluid can cause clutch to slip or engage poorly. Requires replacement of slave cylinder. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1000
* **Synchronizer Failure:** Worn synchronizers can make gear shifting difficult or impossible. Requires transmission rebuild or replacement. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1000 - $2000+

**Suspension**

* **Strut Bushing Failure:** Worn strut bushings can cause knocking noises and poor handling. Requires replacement of bushings. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500
* **Lower Control Arm Bushings:** Worn bushings can cause knocking noises and poor handling. Requires replacement of bushings. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Failure to charge battery can lead to vehicle not starting. Requires replacement of alternator. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1000
* **Starter Failure:** Worn starter solenoid or other components can cause engine not starting. Requires replacement of starter. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1000
* **Electrical Wiring Harness Issues:** Loose or damaged wires can cause various electrical problems, such as instrument cluster failures or lights not working. Requires repair or replacement of wiring. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1000+

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Undercarriage:** Exposure to moisture and road salts can cause rust on frame and suspension components. Requires rust removal, treatment, and protective coating. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $2000+
* **Cracked Windshield:** Impacts from rocks or other debris can lead to cracked windshields. Requires replacement. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500

**Interior**

* **Cracked Dashboard:** Exposure to UV rays can cause dashboard to crack and deteriorate. Requires replacement or repair. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1000
* **Faulty Gauge Cluster:** Malfunctioning gauges can provide inaccurate readings or fail completely. Requires repair or replacement of cluster. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1000
* **Cruise Control Failure:** Inoperative cruise control can be caused by faulty switches, wiring, or actuator. Requires repair or replacement of affected components. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $800

**Summary:**

The 1992 Toyota MR2 is a generally reliable and engaging sports car, but it does have some common issues that owners should be aware of. While most issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, some problems, such as engine or transmission failures, can be costly to repair. Potential buyers should carefully inspect used MR2s and consider budgeting for potential repairs.
