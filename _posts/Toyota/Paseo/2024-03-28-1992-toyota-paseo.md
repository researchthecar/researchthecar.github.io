The 1992 Toyota Paseo is a compact sports car that was produced from 1991 to 1999. It was available with a 1.5-liter inline-four engine and a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission. The Paseo was praised for its handling and fuel efficiency, but it was criticized for its lack of power and interior space.

**Engine**

* **1.5L Inline-Four**
    * **Oil Leaks:** Leaking oil from the valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket, or rear main seal.
        * **Solution:** Replace the faulty gasket or seal.
        * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400
        * **Recalls:** None Found
    * **Timing Belt Failure:** The timing belt can fail prematurely, leading to catastrophic engine damage.
        * **Solution:** Replace the timing belt every 60,000 miles.
        * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $400 - $600
        * **Recalls:** None Found
    * **Spark Plug Failure:** The spark plugs can fail prematurely, causing misfires and poor engine performance.
        * **Solution:** Replace the spark plugs every 30,000 miles.
        * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $50 - $100
        * **Recalls:** None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Manual**
    * **Gear Grinding:** Difficulty shifting gears or grinding noises when shifting.
        * **Solution:** Replace the worn or damaged synchronizers.
        * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $600 - $800
        * **Recalls:** None Found
* **4-Speed Automatic**
    * **Transmission Slippage:** Difficulty engaging or staying in gear.
        * **Solution:** Replace the faulty transmission solenoid or torque converter.
        * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000
        * **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Lower Control Arm Bushings:** Worn bushings can cause clunking noises and poor handling.
    * **Solution:** Replace the worn bushings.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400
    * **Recalls:** None Found
* **Rear Shock Absorbers:** Worn shock absorbers can lead to a rough ride and poor handling.
    * **Solution:** Replace the worn shock absorbers.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, leading to a loss of electrical power.
    * **Solution:** Replace the faulty alternator.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $700
    * **Recalls:** None Found
* **Starter Failure:** The starter can fail, preventing the engine from starting.
    * **Solution:** Replace the faulty starter.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $500
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** The body and frame are prone to rust, especially in areas with salt or moisture.
    * **Solution:** Apply anti-rust treatments and repair any existing rust.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** Varies depending on the severity of the rust.
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Interior**

* **HVAC Blower Failure:** The HVAC blower motor can fail, preventing airflow into the cabin.
    * **Solution:** Replace the faulty blower motor.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400
    * **Recalls:** None Found
* **Power Window Failure:** The power window switches or motors can fail, preventing the windows from operating.
    * **Solution:** Replace the faulty switch or motor.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $200 per window
    * **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary**

The 1992 Toyota Paseo is a reliable and fun-to-drive sports car, but it has some known issues that can be expensive to fix. The most common issues include timing belt failure, transmission slippage, and suspension problems. However, these issues are not widespread and the Paseo is generally considered to be a well-built vehicle.
