The 2023 Toyota GR Corolla is a high-performance hatchback powered by a 1.6L G16E-GTS turbocharged three-cylinder engine producing 300 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque (295 lb-ft in the Circuit and Morizo Editions). It is exclusively paired with a six-speed manual transmission. Owners generally praise the GR Corolla for its engaging driving dynamics, potent engine, and practical hatchback design. Common complaints include limited rear seat space, a firm ride, and relatively high price point for its segment.

**Engine**

**1.6L G16E-GTS Turbocharged Inline-3**
*   **Coolant Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaking from a hose in the engine bay, specifically near the turbocharger. Drivers notice a sweet smell, or a puddle of coolant.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking hose and clamps with revised parts
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher-than-normal oil consumption, requiring them to add oil between service intervals. Visible blue smoke from the exhaust pipe indicates this.
    *   Solution: It is currently being investigated.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Unknown
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Knocking Noise**
    * Description/Symptoms: The engine could make a knocking noise which can be a sign of engine failure.
    * Solution: Full engine replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Unknown
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

*   **Grinding when shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Grinding when shifting, most commonly when shifting into third gear, especially when cold.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the transmission, gear oil.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system occasionally freezes, crashes, or experiences glitches. This can affect navigation, audio, and other functions.
    *   Solution: Software updates from Toyota may address this issue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: N/A (Software update)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Check Engine Light (CEL) illuminated**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The check engine light appears on the dashboard. The cause can vary, but some owners have reported issues related to sensor malfunctions or emissions control components.
    *   Solution: Diagnostic scan to determine the specific fault code and then repair or replacement of the faulty component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (depending on the issue)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Poorly Aligned Panels**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent panel gaps and overall poor panel alignment
    *   Solution: Realign panels
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$1,000
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling Sounds**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling noises are heard from the dashboard or rear hatch area.
    *   Solution: Locating and securing the source of the rattle, possibly by adding sound deadening material or tightening loose trim pieces.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2023 Toyota GR Corolla, while praised for its performance, has exhibited some common issues. Engine issues, such as coolant leaks and potential oil consumption, and transmission issues with the 6-speed manual can be a concern, and potentially high cost to fix. Electrical glitches and interior rattles are less severe. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues and thoroughly inspect any prospective purchase.
