The 2022 Toyota 4Runner is a rugged, body-on-frame SUV known for its off-road capability and reliability. It comes standard with a 4.0-liter V6 engine producing 270 horsepower and 278 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. Some owners appreciate the 4Runner's durability, off-road prowess, and Toyota's reputation for reliability. Common complaints include its outdated technology, poor fuel economy, and less refined on-road driving experience compared to newer SUVs.

**Engine**

**4.0L V6 (1GR-FE)**

*   **Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leak from the valve cover gasket. Symptoms include oil smell, visible oil leak near the valve covers, and low oil level.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the valve cover gasket.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Secondary Air Injection Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The secondary air injection pump helps reduce emissions during cold starts. Failure can trigger a check engine light and cause the vehicle to fail emissions testing. Symptoms may include a loud noise during startup.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the secondary air injection pump.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic (A750F)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rough shifting, particularly between certain gears, or hesitation when accelerating.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a transmission fluid flush and software update may resolve the issue. More severe cases may require valve body repair or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+ (fluid flush), $800 - $2000+ (valve body)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **KDSS (Kinetic Dynamic Suspension System) Leaks/Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The KDSS system, designed to improve off-road articulation, can experience leaks from the hydraulic cylinders or malfunction due to sensor issues. Symptoms may include uneven ride height, clunking noises, and warning lights.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the leaking hydraulic cylinder or faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Entune Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The older Entune infotainment system can freeze, crash, or exhibit slow response times.
    *   Solution: Software updates may improve performance. In some cases, replacement of the head unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update), $500 - $1500+ (head unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Backup Camera Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or complete failure of the backup camera.
    *   Solution: Check wiring connections. Replacement of the camera.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Roof Rack Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks through the roof rack mounting points into the cabin.
    *   Solution: Resealing the roof rack mounting points with sealant or replacing the gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Poor Fit and Finish (Rattles/Noises)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattles and noises from various interior trim pieces, particularly in the dashboard and door panels.
    *   Solution: Tightening loose trim pieces, adding padding or sound deadening material.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ (DIY or shop labor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Heated Seat Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Heated seats not working or working intermittently.
    *   Solution: Check wiring connections, replace heating element, or replace the switch.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Toyota 4Runner generally holds up to Toyota's reputation for reliability. However, owners have reported some issues. Most common complaints revolve around the infotainment system and minor leaks. While some issues like KDSS malfunction can be costly to repair, the majority of complaints are relatively minor and can be addressed without significant expense. The potential for costly repairs due to KDSS or secondary air injection failure should be considered when evaluating a used 4Runner.
