# 2019 Toyota 4Runner Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Toyota 4Runner continues the vehicle's long-standing reputation for rugged capability and mechanical durability, leaning heavily on its traditional truck-based architecture. While buyers generally praise its off-road prowess and enduring resale value, used car shoppers should note that a handful of owner-reported concerns have emerged around electrical glitches, steering alignment sensitivities, and intermittent powertrain hesitation. Thoroughly inspecting suspension wear, tracking alignment history, and verifying all electronics during a pre-purchase test drive will help ensure a solid ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L V6

The 4.0-liter V6 engine produces 270 horsepower and 278 lb-ft of torque, providing dependable motivation for both daily commuting and heavy trail work. It is widely regarded as a robust and long-lasting powerplant, though isolated complaints indicate occasional drivability hesitation.

**Engine Stalling and Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected engine stalling, illuminated check engine warning lights, and occasional sluggish acceleration response when attempting to pass at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning for stored trouble codes, inspecting the fuel delivery system, and updating powertrain control modules as needed.

## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic

The 5-speed automatic transmission is paired exclusively with the V6 engine across rear-wheel and four-wheel-drive configurations. While mechanically dated compared to modern multi-speed gearboxes, it is known for enduring tough service environments.

**Shifting Hesitation and Gear Engagement Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners have noted delayed engagement, clunking sounds when shifting between reverse and drive, or a minor vibration when accelerating past low speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition, check software calibration, and evaluate drivetrain mount wear.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Alignment

Steering system complaints generally revolve around sensitivity to road crown and alignment upkeep rather than catastrophic mechanical failure.

**Highway Drift and Steering Shimmy**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced a persistent pull or drift to the right at highway speeds, along with minor steering wheel vibration requiring frequent alignment checks.
- **Solution:** Perform a professional wheel alignment, check steering linkage components, and inspect front tires for uneven wear patterns.

## Exterior

### Glass and Sunroof

Exterior body and glass components are generally resilient, though rare reports of spontaneous stress fractures in factory glass warrant a careful walk-around inspection.

**Spontaneous Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** A few drivers have reported unexpected shattering or cracking of the windshield or sunroof glass while driving under normal conditions without any visible impact.
- **Solution:** Replace damaged glass panels through comprehensive auto insurance or authorized glass service providers.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V682000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=4Runner&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel pump fails, the engine can stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly with an improved one, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V753000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=4Runner&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An overloaded vehicle can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 19V670000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=4Runner&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** During installation, certain steering and suspension components may not have been properly tightened. Loose fasteners may cause a loss of vehicle control, or cause an accessory to detach from the vehicle and become a road hazard.
- **What to do:** SET will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and re-tighten the steering and suspension fasteners as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V244000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=4Runner&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The text on the load capacity label may become illegible. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." If the driver cannot read the label, they may unknowingly overload the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GST will notify owners, and dealers will provide corrected load carrying capacity modification labels, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Toyota 4Runner remains a top contender in the used midsize SUV market for buyers prioritizing off-road capability, cargo utility, and enduring resale value. While it exhibits a few recurring owner complaints regarding steering alignment sensitivity and minor powertrain hesitation, its overall mechanical foundation is notably tough. Prospective buyers should perform a thorough test drive to check for steering tracking and ensure all electronics and cameras function properly.
