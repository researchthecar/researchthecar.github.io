**Introduction**

The 2018 Toyota 4Runner is a midsize SUV known for its off-road capabilities and rugged exterior. It is offered with two engine options: a 4.0L V6 producing 270 horsepower and 278 lb-ft of torque, and a 4.6L V8 producing 301 horsepower and 329 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission.

Owners appreciate the 4Runner's reliability, durability, and off-road performance. However, there are some issues that have been reported with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**4.0L V6**

•	**Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Leaking coolant, overheating, rough idle, loss of power
Solution: Replace intake manifold gaskets
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
Recalls: N/A

•	**Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise from engine, engine misfires, loss of power
Solution: Replace timing chain tensioner
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000
Recalls: N/A

**4.6L V8**

•	**No Major Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

•	**Shifting Issues**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, slipping gears, delayed shifting
Solution: Replace transmission fluid, perform transmission flush, or rebuild/replace transmission
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $4,000
Recalls: N/A

**Suspension**

•	**Rear Axle Seal Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Leaking fluid from rear axle, noise while driving
Solution: Replace rear axle seal
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
Recalls: N/A

**Electrical**

•	**Alternator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Battery warning light on, dim headlights, difficulty starting vehicle
Solution: Replace alternator
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
Recalls: N/A

•	**Starter Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting vehicle, no noise when turning key
Solution: Replace starter
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
Recalls: N/A

**Exterior**

•	**No Major Issues Found**

**Interior**

•	**Center Console Lid Latch Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Center console lid won't stay closed
Solution: Replace center console lid latch
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
Recalls: N/A

•	**Rear Seatbelt Sensor Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Seatbelt warning light stays on even when seatbelt is buckled
Solution: Replace rear seatbelt sensor
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
Recalls: N/A

**Summary**

The 2018 Toyota 4Runner is a generally reliable and durable vehicle, but there are some potential issues that owners should be aware of. The most common issues reported are intake manifold gasket failure, timing chain tensioner failure, and shifting issues. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but some of them can be expensive to repair. Overall, the 4Runner offers a solid combination of off-road capability, reliability, and comfort, but it's important to be aware of potential issues before purchasing one.
