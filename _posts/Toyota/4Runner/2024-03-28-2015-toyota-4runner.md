**2015 Toyota 4Runner**

The 2015 Toyota 4Runner is a mid-size SUV known for its rugged capabilities and off-road performance. It's powered by a 4.0L V6 engine producing 270 horsepower and 278 lb-ft of torque and is paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission.

Owners praise the 4Runner's reliability, off-road prowess, and spacious interior. However, some have reported issues with its aging design and lack of modern features.

**Engine**

**4.0L V6**

- **Oil leaks:** Leaks can occur from the valve cover gaskets or oil pan gasket. Symptoms include low oil levels and visible oil stains under the vehicle. The gaskets need to be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
- **Timing chain tensioner failure:** A faulty timing chain tensioner can lead to excessive noise, reduced engine performance, and potential engine damage. The tensioner needs to be replaced along with the timing chain. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
- **Carbon buildup on intake valves:** Over time, carbon deposits can accumulate on the intake valves, reducing engine efficiency and performance. The valves need to be cleaned or replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

- **Transmission slipping:** The transmission may experience slipping, especially when shifting gears or under heavy acceleration. This can be due to worn clutches or solenoids. The transmission may need to be rebuilt or replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
- **Torque converter shudder:** A vibration or shuddering sensation during acceleration can indicate a faulty torque converter. The torque converter needs to be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
- **No issues found.**

**Suspension**

- **Leaking shock absorbers:** Worn or damaged shock absorbers can leak fluid, leading to a rough ride and reduced handling. The shock absorbers need to be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per shock
- **Broken leaf springs:** The leaf springs can break or become weak, resulting in a sagging or uneven ride. The leaf springs need to be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
- **No issues found.**

**Electrical**

- **Battery drain:** A parasitic draw can drain the battery over time. This can be caused by a faulty alternator, starter, or other electrical component. The source of the draw needs to be identified and repaired. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
- **No issues found.**

**Exterior**

- **Rust:** Some owners have reported rust issues on the body panels, especially around the rear wheel wells. The rusted areas need to be repaired or replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of damage
- **No issues found.**

**Interior**

- **Dashboard rattling:** The dashboard may rattle or make noises while driving, particularly on rough roads. This can be due to loose trim panels or faulty mounting points. The panels or mounting points need to be tightened or replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
- **HVAC system problems:** The HVAC system may experience issues such as blower motor failure, temperature control problems, or AC compressor issues. The affected component needs to be repaired or replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the component and labor required
- **No issues found.**

**Summary**

The 2015 Toyota 4Runner is generally a reliable vehicle, but it has some known issues that can impact its performance and ownership experience. The most common problems include oil leaks, timing chain tensioner failure, and transmission issues. The severity of these issues can vary, and the cost of repairs can range from minor to major. It's important to factor these potential issues into consideration when purchasing or maintaining a 4Runner and to consult with a qualified mechanic for proper diagnosis and repairs.
