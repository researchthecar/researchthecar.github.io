**Introduction:**
The 1998 Toyota 4Runner is a mid-size SUV that is known for its reliability, off-road capabilities, and comfortable interior. It is available with multiple engine and transmission options, including a 3.4L V6 and a 4-speed automatic transmission. The 3.4L V6 produces 183 horsepower and 217 lb-ft of torque. Some common issues with the 1998 Toyota 4Runner include:

**Engine:**
* **3.4L V6**
    * **Oil Pump Failure:** Symptoms include low oil pressure, engine knocking, and engine seizure. Solution: Replace the oil pump. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * **Timing Belt Failure:** Symptoms include engine stalling, rough idle, and loss of power. Solution: Replace the timing belt. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* No Other Issues Found

**Transmission:**
* **4-Speed Automatic**
    * **Transmission Slipping:** Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, delayed engagement, and loss of power. Solution: Replace the transmission or rebuild it. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * **Torque Converter Failure:** Symptoms include transmission shuddering, overheating, and slipping. Solution: Replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* No Other Issues Found

**Exterior:**
* **Rust and Corrosion:** Symptoms include bubbling paint, rust spots, and structural damage. Solution: Treat the rust and repaint the affected area. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of the damage.
* **Cracked Windshield:** Symptoms include a crack or chip in the windshield. Solution: Replace the windshield. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Interior:**
* **Power Window Failure:** Symptoms include the window not moving or moving slowly. Solution: Replace the power window motor or switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* No Other Issues Found

**Summary:**
The 1998 Toyota 4Runner is a reliable and capable SUV, but it is not without its issues. The most common problems include engine oil pump failure, timing belt failure, transmission slipping, torque converter failure, and alternator failure. The severity of these issues can vary, and the cost to fix them can range from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars. However, despite these issues, the 4Runner remains a popular choice for those who need a durable and off-road capable SUV.
