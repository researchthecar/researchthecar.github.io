# 2008 Toyota 4Runner

### Overview
The 2008 Toyota 4Runner is a robust mid-size SUV known for its off-road capability and overall reliability. Built on a sturdy body-on-frame chassis, it generally performs well across various conditions. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected repairs, like any vehicle, certain components can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems that may arise, providing insights into potential repair areas and their associated costs for different powertrain configurations.

### X-REAS System (Optional)
**Leaking Shocks/Struts**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced ride comfort, clunking noises, visible fluid leaks from shock absorbers.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking X-REAS components. Often replaced with standard aftermarket suspension components if X-REAS parts are unavailable or too costly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500 (for X-REAS replacement), $500 - $1000 (for conversion to standard suspension)

### Summary Verdict
The 2008 Toyota 4Runner stands out as a highly reliable vehicle, particularly for its segment. The powertrain options, including the 4.0L V6 and 4.7L V8 engines paired with the 5-speed automatic transmission, are known for their durability and longevity. The primary area where owners might encounter specific issues is with the optional X-REAS suspension system, which can develop leaks over time, impacting ride quality. Additionally, the exterior tailgate handle is a known weak point that can crack or break. However, these are relatively minor and isolated concerns in an otherwise mechanically sound and dependable SUV. Overall, the 2008 4Runner is a strong recommendation for those seeking a capable and reliable off-road-oriented vehicle.
