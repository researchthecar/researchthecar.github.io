**Introduction**
The 2001 Toyota 4Runner is a mid-size SUV known for its off-road capabilities and reliability. It offers a 4.7L V8 engine mated to a 4-speed automatic transmission, producing 235 horsepower and 320 lb-ft of torque. The 4Runner is praised for its ruggedness, spacious interior, and off-road performance. However, it has received some criticism for its outdated interior, lack of modern safety features, and poor fuel economy.

**Engine**
**4.7L V8**

**Timing Belt Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The timing belt can fail prematurely, causing the engine to stop running. Symptoms include a loud noise from the engine, loss of power, and a check engine light.
Solution: Replace the timing belt and any damaged components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
**4-Speed Automatic**

**Transmission Slippage**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip out of gear, causing the vehicle to lose power or jerk. Symptoms include difficulty shifting, delayed engagement, and a check engine light.
Solution: Repair or replace the transmission.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
Recalls: None Found

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**
**Alternator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The alternator can fail, preventing the battery from charging. Symptoms include dim headlights, difficulty starting the vehicle, and a warning light on the dashboard.
Solution: Replace the alternator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
**Rust on Frame**
Description/Symptoms: The frame can rust prematurely, especially in areas with high road salt use. Symptoms include visible rust on the frame, weakened structural integrity, and a grinding noise when driving over bumps.
Solution: Repair or replace the rusted frame sections.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
**Cracked Dashboard**
Description/Symptoms: The dashboard can crack over time, especially in areas exposed to sunlight. Symptoms include visible cracks on the dashboard surface.
Solution: Replace the dashboard.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2001 Toyota 4Runner is a reliable and capable SUV with a few common issues. The most severe issues are timing belt failure and transmission slippage, which can be costly to repair. Other issues, such as alternator failure and dashboard cracking, are less severe but can still be annoying. Overall, the 4Runner's reliability and off-road performance outweigh its issues, making it a popular choice for outdoor enthusiasts.
