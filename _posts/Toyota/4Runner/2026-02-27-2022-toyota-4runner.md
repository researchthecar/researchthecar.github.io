# 2022 Toyota 4Runner Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Toyota 4Runner continues the vehicle's long-standing reputation for ruggedness, utilizing a traditional body-on-frame platform and time-tested mechanical components. While its overall durability remains a strong selling point for off-road enthusiasts and everyday drivers alike, owner reports submitted to safety agencies highlight a few recurring mechanical and electrical quirks. Prospective buyers should carefully evaluate ride quality, steering response, and electronics during a test drive to ensure the vehicle meets expectations.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L V6

The 4.0-liter V6 engine produces 270 horsepower and 278 lb-ft of torque, paired with a traditional 5-speed automatic transmission. This powertrain is widely regarded as robust and dependable, though occasional accessory or seal leaks can occur.

**Oil Leak**
- **Symptoms:** An unexpected pool of oil forms underneath the vehicle during routine operation.
- **Solution:** Inspect engine gaskets and seals, and have a certified technician replace any failing components.

## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic

The 5-speed automatic transmission is paired exclusively with the V6 engine across rear-wheel and four-wheel-drive configurations. While mechanically dated compared to modern multi-speed gearboxes, it is known for long-term mechanical durability.

## Suspension

### Suspension System

Suspension setups—particularly specialized trims utilizing the XREAS hydraulic system—provide great off-road articulation but require careful inspection for leaks as mileage climbs.

**XREAS Suspension Wear and Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Unnatural rolling or bouncing sensation around curves, alongside sagging front struts or component leaks.
- **Solution:** Inspect the XREAS hydraulic lines and struts for fluid leaks or structural sagging, and replace components as necessary.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Braking performance is adequate for a heavy SUV, but internal hydraulic failures should be diagnosed immediately if pedal pressure feels inconsistent.

**Brake Pedal Softness**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal slowly travels to the floor when holding the vehicle stationary, leading to vehicle creep.
- **Solution:** Have the master cylinder and hydraulic brake system thoroughly inspected and bled or repaired by a professional.

## Steering

### Steering Column and Front End

Steering components generally hold up well, though some owners report high-speed vibrations related to front-end geometry or driveline angles.

**Highway Steering Shake**
- **Symptoms:** A noticeable vibration or shimmy can be felt in the steering wheel, typically between 50 and 65 mph.
- **Solution:** Check front wheel balance, alignment, CV joints, and needle bearings for wear.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Controls

Electrical accessories are generally reliable, though occasional glitches in dashboard displays and steering wheel switch controls have been documented.

**Center Console Display Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display goes blank or turns off unexpectedly, disabling the backup camera and audio controls.
- **Solution:** Check software versions, fuses, and connections, or have the head unit serviced by a dealership.

**Spiral Cable Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Turning the steering wheel causes radio channels to change, phone calls to drop, or the airbag warning light to illuminate.
- **Solution:** Replace the steering column spiral cable assembly.
- **Cost:** $1200

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety restraint systems perform their primary protective role well, but individual hardware components like retractors can occasionally bind.

**Seat Belt Jamming**
- **Symptoms:** The driver or passenger seat belt becomes stuck and will not easily pull out from the retractor mechanism.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat belt assembly and replace the retractor mechanism if the locking mechanism fails.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V963000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=4Runner&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." An inoperable driver's side high beam may reduce visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install an updated wiring harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V310000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=4Runner&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The load carrying capacity modification labels may not be permanent and can fade, becoming illegible. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An illegible label may allow the vehicle to be overloaded, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 22V480000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=4Runner&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An incorrect label may lead to unintentionally overloading the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Toyota 4Runner is a dependable, rugged choice for buyers seeking traditional truck-based utility and strong off-road capability. While owner complaints point to occasional steering vibrations, brake hydraulic issues, and minor electrical or infotainment glitches, the vehicle's core mechanical layout remains resilient. Buyers should conduct a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection focusing on the steering, suspension bushings, and brake pedal firmness.
