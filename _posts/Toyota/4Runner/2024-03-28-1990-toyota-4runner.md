**Introduction:**

The 1990 Toyota 4Runner is a mid-size SUV known for its ruggedness, reliability, and off-road capabilities. It came equipped with two engine options: a 2.4-liter inline-4 and a 3.0-liter V6, each paired with a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission. The 4Runner was praised for its spacious interior, but criticism arose regarding its lack of refinement and average fuel economy.

**Engine**

**2.4-liter Inline-4**

* **Failed Head Gasket:** Causes overheating, white smoke from exhaust, and loss of coolant. Requires replacing the head gasket and resurfacing the cylinder head. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
* **Cracked Exhaust Manifold:** Causes a loud exhaust leak. Requires replacing the exhaust manifold. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
* **Oil Pan Gasket Leak:** Causes oil to leak from the bottom of the engine. Requires replacing the oil pan gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250+

**3.0-liter V6**

* **Timing Belt Failure:** Occurs around 60,000 miles and can cause catastrophic engine damage. Requires replacing the timing belt, tensioner, and water pump. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:** Causes coolant and oil to leak into the cylinders. Requires replacing the intake manifold gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
* **Alternator Failure:** Causes the battery to not charge. Requires replacing the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**Transmission**

**5-speed Manual**

* **Grinding Gears:** Causes difficulty shifting. Requires replacing the clutch plate, pressure plate, and flywheel. Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000+

**4-speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping:** Occurs when the transmission fails to engage properly. Requires rebuilding or replacing the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Torque Converter Failure:** Causes vibration and slipping. Requires replacing the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $700 - $1,000+

**Electrical**

* **Faulty Ignition Switch:** Causes the engine to not start. Requires replacing the ignition switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250+
* **Electrical Short:** Causes various electrical system malfunctions, such as blowing fuses or draining the battery. Requires diagnosing and fixing the short. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies
* **Alternator Failure:** Causes the battery to not charge. Requires replacing the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**Suspension**

* **Worn Front-End Components:** Causes clunking noises and poor handling. Requires replacing the ball joints, tie rod ends, and sway bar links. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Broken Rear Leaf Springs:** Causes the rear end to sag. Requires replacing the leaf springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
* **Shocks and Struts:** Wear out over time and can cause a rough ride. Requires replacing the shocks and struts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** Can occur on the frame, body panels, and undercarriage. Requires removing the rust and applying protective coatings. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies
* **Loose Side Mirrors:** Vibrate at higher speeds. Requires tightening or replacing the mirror mounts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
* **Fading Headlights:** Reduce nighttime visibility. Requires replacing the headlight lenses or bulbs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:** May cause inconsistent temperatures or failure to operate. Requires diagnosing and repairing the climate control module. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Broken Window Regulators:** Can cause windows to stick or fall. Requires replacing the window regulator motor or mechanism. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Squeaky Seats:** Can be caused by loose seat bolts or worn seat springs. Requires tightening the bolts or replacing the springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Summary:**

The 1990 Toyota 4Runner has several potential issues, with the most common and expensive being related to the engine and transmission. Regular maintenance and timely repairs can help prevent or mitigate these issues. However, some of the problems can be significant and require professional attention. The overall reliability and durability of the 4Runner make it a popular choice among off-road enthusiasts, but buyers should be aware of these potential issues before purchasing.
