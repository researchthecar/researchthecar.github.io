**Introduction to the 1991 Toyota 4Runner**

The 1991 Toyota 4Runner is a mid-size SUV known for its reliability, off-road capabilities, and comfortable interior. It was offered with two engine options: a 2.4L inline-4 and a 3.0L V6. Both engines came with either a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission. Despite its strengths, the 4Runner has some common issues.

**Engine**

**2.4L Inline-4**

* **Head gasket failure:** Blown head gaskets can cause coolant leaks, overheating, and engine damage.
  * Solution: Replace the head gasket.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500+
  * Recalls: None Found

* **Timing chain tensioner failure:** A faulty timing chain tensioner can lead to a noisy timing chain and potential engine damage.
  * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
  * Recalls: None Found

**3.0L V6**

* **Valve cover gasket leaks:** Worn valve cover gaskets can cause oil leaks.
  * Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
  * Recalls: None Found

* **Power window motor failure:** Inoperative power windows are often caused by faulty window motors.
  * Solution: Replace the window motor.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
  * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Clutch wear:** Worn clutches can cause slipping and difficulty shifting gears.
  * Solution: Replace the clutch.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $1500
  * Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission fluid leaks:** Leaks in the transmission case or lines can result in transmission failure.
  * Solution: Repair or replace the leaking component.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Leaf spring breakage:** Worn or overloaded leaf springs can break, affecting ride quality and handling.
  * Solution: Replace the leaf springs.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1200
  * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** A faulty alternator can prevent the battery from charging, leading to electrical problems.
  * Solution: Replace the alternator.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
  * Recalls: None Found

* **Starter failure:** A malfunctioning starter can prevent the engine from starting.
  * Solution: Replace the starter.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
  * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** The body is prone to rust, especially in areas exposed to moisture and road salt.
  * Solution: Rust prevention measures and repairs.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity of the rust
  * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracking:** The dashboard can crack due to exposure to heat and sunlight.
  * Solution: Replace or cover the dashboard with a protective material.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
  * Recalls: None Found

* **Air conditioning failure:** Faulty compressors or leaks in refrigerant lines can cause air conditioning failure.
  * Solution: Diagnose and repair the air conditioning system.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

While the 1991 Toyota 4Runner is a reliable and capable SUV, it has some common issues that can be costly to repair. Engine issues like head gasket failure and valve cover gasket leaks are significant and can impact overall engine performance. Transmission problems and electrical failures can also be concerning. Rust and dashboard cracking are external and interior issues that can affect the vehicle's appearance and resale value. Overall, the cost of these issues can vary depending on the severity and extent of repairs required. It is important to consider these potential issues when purchasing or maintaining a 1991 Toyota 4Runner.
