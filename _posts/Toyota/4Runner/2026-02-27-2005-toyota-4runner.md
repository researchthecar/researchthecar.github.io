# 2005 Toyota 4Runner

### Overview
The 2005 Toyota 4Runner is a highly regarded mid-size SUV known for its robust construction and off-road capabilities. Built on a truck chassis, it offers a durable and reliable platform. This generation of the 4Runner generally exhibits strong mechanical integrity, with common issues being relatively minor and often related to age rather than inherent design flaws. Owners typically praise its longevity and performance, especially when properly maintained. The available powertrains, including the V6 and V8 engines, are known for their dependability, though specific components can occasionally require attention as the vehicle ages. Overall, the 2005 4Runner remains a solid choice for those seeking a dependable used SUV.

## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Front Lower Ball Joint Separation**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises from the front suspension, potential for the ball joint to separate from the steering knuckle.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the affected ball joint(s).
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Rear Differential Breather Hose**
- **Symptoms:** Water or debris entering the rear differential, leading to premature wear or failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the breather hose if cracked or damaged. Ensure differential fluid is changed.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250


## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Electrical Gremlins**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical components may malfunction intermittently, such as power windows, door locks, or interior lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


### Alternator
**Early Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, difficulty starting the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Exterior

### Frame Rust
**Corrosion on Frame Rails**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust and corrosion on the frame, particularly in areas exposed to salt and moisture. Can compromise structural integrity.
- **Solution:** Rust treatment, undercoating, or in severe cases, frame replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $3000+


### Tailgate Handle
**Cracking or Breaking**
- **Symptoms:** The plastic tailgate handle can become brittle and crack or break, making it difficult to open the tailgate.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the tailgate handle assembly.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


## Interior

### Headliner Sagging
**Fabric Separation**
- **Symptoms:** The fabric on the headliner begins to sag or detach from the backing board, especially in warmer climates.
- **Solution:** Re-gluing the headliner fabric or replacing the headliner assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


### HVAC Blower Motor
**Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air coming from the vents, or intermittent fan operation.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the HVAC blower motor and resistor.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2005 Toyota 4Runner is a very reliable vehicle with a strong track record. The powertrains are generally bulletproof. The most common issues tend to be related to age and environmental exposure, such as frame rust in certain regions and occasional suspension components like ball joints. Electrical gremlins and interior wear are also possible but not widespread. With proactive maintenance and addressing minor issues promptly, the 2005 4Runner can continue to provide dependable service for many years.
