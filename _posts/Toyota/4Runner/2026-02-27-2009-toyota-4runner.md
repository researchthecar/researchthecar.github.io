### Overview
The 2009 Toyota 4Runner is a robust mid-size SUV known for its off-road capability and overall reliability. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into common problems and their potential resolutions for the 2009 model year across its available powertrains.

### Adjustable Air Suspension (Optional)
**Air Spring Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may sag on one side, loss of ride height, compressor running excessively.
- **Solution:** Replace leaking air springs or associated air lines. Compressor may need replacement if overworked.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800 per corner

**Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to adjust ride height, constant compressor operation, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replace the air suspension compressor unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000

### Summary Verdict
The 2009 Toyota 4Runner stands out as a highly reliable SUV, particularly with its standard powertrain options. The 4.0L V6 and 4.7L V8 engines, along with the 5-speed automatic transmission, are known for their durability and longevity, with no significant widespread mechanical failures reported. The primary area where owners might encounter issues is with the optional adjustable air suspension system, which can develop leaks or compressor failures over time, leading to repair costs. The electrical, exterior, and interior components generally perform well without common defects. Overall, the 2009 4Runner is a solid choice for those seeking a dependable vehicle, especially if opting for the standard suspension.
