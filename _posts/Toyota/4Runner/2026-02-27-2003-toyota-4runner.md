# 2003 Toyota 4Runner

### Overview
The 2003 Toyota 4Runner is a highly regarded mid-size SUV known for its robust construction and off-road capabilities. Built on a truck chassis, it offers durability and a comfortable ride for its era. This generation (3rd gen facelift) is generally praised for its reliability, with many owners reporting minimal major mechanical problems. Common issues tend to be related to age and mileage, but widespread, recurring defects are uncommon. The 4Runner's strong reputation for longevity is a significant factor in its enduring popularity, making it a solid choice for those seeking a dependable used SUV, especially for adventurous use.

---


## Engine

### 2.7L 3RZ-FE (4-Cyl)
**Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, white smoke from exhaust, coolant loss, milky oil.
- **Solution:** Replacement of head gasket and potentially resurfacing of cylinder head.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover/Rear Main Seal)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil drips under the vehicle, burning oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gasket or rear main seal.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700 (Valve Cover), $600 - $1,200 (Rear Main Seal)


### 3.4L 5VZ-FE (V6)
**Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, white smoke from exhaust, coolant loss, milky oil.
- **Solution:** Replacement of head gasket and potentially resurfacing of cylinder head.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Timing Belt Failure (if not replaced on schedule)**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden engine stoppage, no start, potential internal engine damage.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing belt, tensioner, idler pulleys, and water pump. If failure occurred, engine rebuild may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1,000 (Preventative), $2,000+ (Post-Failure)

## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Upper Ball Joint Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, uneven tire wear, loose steering feel.
- **Solution:** Replacement of upper ball joints.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Rear Differential Breather Clog**
- **Symptoms:** Seal leaks, potential differential damage due to pressure buildup.
- **Solution:** Clean or relocate differential breather.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300

## Exterior

### Body and Frame
**Frame Rust (especially in rust-belt areas)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust on frame rails, potential structural compromise.
- **Solution:** Rust treatment, repair, or frame replacement (often cost-prohibitive).
- **Cost:** $500 - $5,000+ (depending on severity)

**Clear Coat Peeling**
- **Symptoms:** Fading paint, peeling clear coat, especially on the roof and hood.
- **Solution:** Repainting affected areas.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,000+

### Summary Verdict
The 2003 Toyota 4Runner is a remarkably reliable vehicle, especially considering its age. The most significant potential issues revolve around the engine, particularly head gasket failures on both the 4-cylinder and V6 engines, and timing belt maintenance for the V6. Frame rust is a concern in areas with harsh winters. However, these are often manageable with proper maintenance and inspection. The transmission, suspension, electrical, and interior components generally hold up very well. Overall, it's a durable SUV that, with diligent upkeep, can continue to serve its owner reliably for many years.
