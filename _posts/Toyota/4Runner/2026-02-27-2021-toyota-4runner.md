# 2021 Toyota 4Runner Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Toyota 4Runner is widely celebrated for its rugged, old-school body-on-frame durability and legendary longevity. However, used car buyers should still inspect specific areas based on owner reports, including occasional reports of steering wheel vibrations at highway speeds, electrical quirks like door lock actuator failures, and isolated suspension or braking complaints.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L V6

This 4.0-liter naturally aspirated V6 engine produces approximately 270 horsepower and 278 lb-ft of torque. It is widely regarded as an exceptionally durable, bulletproof powerplant capable of high mileage with basic maintenance.

## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic

Paired exclusively with the V6, this traditional 5-speed automatic transmission prioritizes mechanical simplicity and long-term reliability over fuel economy, offering smooth shifts for daily driving and trail use alike.

## Suspension

### Suspension & Shocks

Suspension setups are built tough for off-roading, but specialized off-road dampers may require closer inspection for noise and wear.

**TRD Pro Suspension Clunk**
- **Symptoms:** Specialized off-road shock absorbers (such as FOX shocks on TRD Pro models) may develop a mechanical clunking noise at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect shocks for wear or leakage, and replace components under warranty or service guidelines if internal failure is confirmed.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Braking components provide adequate stopping power for a heavy SUV, but routine inspections are recommended due to vehicle weight.

**Brake Pedal Softness / Premature Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected soft pedal feel or rapid pad and rotor wear in isolated driving scenarios.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake lines, bleed the hydraulic brake system, and replace worn pads or rotors as necessary.

## Steering

### Steering & Front End

The steering system is generally robust, though highway vibration sensitivities remain a common topic among owners.

**Steering Wheel Shake**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a noticeable vibration or shaking in the steering wheel, often most prominent at highway speeds between 55 and 65 mph.
- **Solution:** Check and re-balance tires (road-force balancing is often recommended) and verify front-end alignment.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera & Electronics

Advanced safety features generally operate reliably, though intermittent display glitches can occur.

**Intermittent Backup Camera Display**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment screen displays a blank or black screen instead of the rear-view camera feed when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera wiring harnesses, electrical connections, and replace the camera unit or display module if malfunctioning.

## Exterior

### Door Latches & Locks

Cabin electrical systems hold up well, though convenience accessories like door locks can occasionally fail prematurely.

**Door Lock Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power door locks fail to engage or respond, affecting individual side doors or the rear liftgate.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty door lock actuator mechanism.

### Sunroof

Exterior panels and trim are durable, but glass components like sunroofs carry rare reports of spontaneous breakage.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Glass Fracture**
- **Symptoms:** The factory glass sunroof panel shatters unexpectedly while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Replace the shattered glass assembly and inspect the surrounding frame for alignment or stress points.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V480000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=4Runner&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An incorrect label may lead to unintentionally overloading the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The Toyota 4Runner remains a top-tier choice for buyers seeking exceptional mechanical durability, strong resale value, and genuine off-road capability. While you should keep an eye out for minor issues such as steering wheel vibrations, door lock actuator durability, and occasional electronic quirks, its fundamental powertrain is remarkably dependable. A thorough pre-purchase inspection that checks the steering feel, brakes, and electrical accessories will help ensure you find a reliable example.
