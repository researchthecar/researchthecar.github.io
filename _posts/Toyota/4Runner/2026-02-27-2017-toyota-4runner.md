# 2017 Toyota 4Runner

### Overview
The 2017 Toyota 4Runner is a body-on-frame SUV renowned for its ruggedness and off-road capability. It generally exhibits strong reliability, a hallmark of Toyota vehicles. The powertrain, particularly the 4.0L V6 engine and its associated transmission, is well-proven and known for durability. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected repairs, like any vehicle, some specific areas have seen occasional reports of issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas and their associated costs for the 2017 model year.

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Toyota 4Runner stands out as a highly reliable vehicle, particularly concerning its core mechanical components like the engine and transmission. The robust 4.0L V6 and 5-speed automatic are known for their longevity and minimal issues. While the chassis and exterior/interior components generally hold up well, there are a few minor electrical concerns that owners might encounter, such as occasional infotainment system glitches and premature battery drain. These electrical issues are not widespread but are worth noting. Overall, the 4Runner offers excellent long-term reliability, making it a solid choice for those seeking a durable SUV.
