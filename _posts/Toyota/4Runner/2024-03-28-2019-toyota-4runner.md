**Introduction:**

The 2019 Toyota 4Runner is a versatile midsize SUV known for its rugged off-road capabilities and reliable performance. It features a 270-horsepower 4.0-liter V6 engine paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission as standard. An optional 301-horsepower 2.7-liter turbocharged 4-cylinder engine with a 6-speed automatic transmission is also available.

While the 4Runner is highly praised for its durability and off-road prowess, it is not without its reported issues.

**Engine**

* **4.0L V6 Engine**
    * **Timing Chain Rattle:** Description/Symptoms: Audible rattling noise from the engine on startup or during acceleration. Solution: Replacement of the timing chain assembly. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * **Leaking Oil Pan Gasket:** Description/Symptoms: Oil seepage from the oil pan gasket. Solution: Replacement of the oil pan gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

* **2.7L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Shifting Issues:** Description/Symptoms: Difficulty or delayed shifting, slipping gears. Solution: May involve transmission fluid flush, software update, or replacement of transmission components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
    * **Torque Converter shudder:** Description/Symptoms: Vibration felt during low-speed acceleration or when shifting gears. Solution: Replacement of the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

* **6-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Front Sway Bar Link Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise when going over bumps or turning. Solution: Replacement of the sway bar link. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Rear Differential Leak:** Description/Symptoms: Leaking fluid from the rear differential. Solution: Repair or replacement of the differential seal. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Electrical**

* **Electrical Sensor Malfunctions:** Description/Symptoms: Various electrical issues, including dashboard warning lights, malfunctioning components, or starting problems. Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of faulty sensors or wiring. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Windshield Cracking:** Description/Symptoms: Unusual cracking or chipping of the windshield. Solution: Replacement of the windshield. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
* **Tailgate Paint Peeling:** Description/Symptoms: Peeling paint on the tailgate or other exterior panels. Solution: Repair or replacement of affected panels. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattle:** Description/Symptoms: Noises or rattling coming from the dashboard. Solution: Tightening of loose components or replacement of trim pieces. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **Air Conditioning System Malfunctions:** Description/Symptoms: Insufficient cooling or heating, refrigerant leaks. Solution: Diagnosis and repair or replacement of AC components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Seat Heater Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning seat heaters. Solution: Repair or replacement of seat heater elements. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Summary:**

The 2019 Toyota 4Runner has some reported issues, primarily with the engine (timing chain rattle, oil leak), transmission (shifting issues, torque converter shudder), and suspension (sway bar link failure, rear differential leak). However, many of these issues are relatively minor and can be repaired at a reasonable cost. The vehicle's overall reliability remains strong, and it is known for its durability and off-road capabilities.
