# 2006 Toyota 4Runner

### Overview
The 2006 Toyota 4Runner is a highly regarded mid-size SUV known for its robust construction and off-road capabilities. Built on a body-on-frame chassis, it offers durability and a comfortable ride for its class. This generation of the 4Runner generally exhibits strong reliability, with owners often praising its longevity and minimal mechanical problems. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the 4Runner's design and Toyota's engineering contribute to a positive ownership experience, with most common concerns being minor and related to age rather than inherent design flaws. Regular maintenance is key to preserving its excellent reputation.

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming lights, battery warning light, difficulty starting, or complete loss of power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow operation, or falling down.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Exterior

### Body and Trim
**Rust on Frame**
- **Symptoms:** Visible corrosion, particularly on the frame rails, leading to structural weakness.
- **Solution:** Frame rust treatment, repair, or in severe cases, frame replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $3000+

**Clear Coat Peeling**
- **Symptoms:** Paint clear coat delaminating or peeling, especially on the roof and hood.
- **Solution:** Repainting affected areas.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

### Summary Verdict
The 2006 Toyota 4Runner stands out as a remarkably reliable vehicle, especially considering its age and off-road oriented design. The powertrain, including both the 4.0L V6 and the 4.7L V8 engines, along with the 5-speed automatic transmission, are known for their durability and longevity with proper maintenance. The chassis and suspension components are generally robust. The most common issues tend to be related to age and environmental factors, such as potential frame rust in certain climates and occasional electrical component failures like alternators or window regulators. Exterior paint issues like clear coat peeling can also occur. Overall, the 2006 4Runner is a solid choice for those seeking a dependable SUV, with most potential problems being manageable and not indicative of systemic design flaws.
