**Introduction:**

The 2017 Toyota 4Runner is a rugged and capable SUV known for its off-road prowess. It comes standard with a 4.0L V6 engine and a 5-speed automatic transmission. A 3.5L V6 engine and a 6-speed manual or 5-speed automatic transmission are also available. The 4Runner is praised for its reliability, off-road capabilities, and comfortable interior, but it has been criticized for its outdated infotainment system and limited cargo space.

## **Engine**
### **4.0L V6**
- **Leaking Oil Pan Gasket:**
-- Description/Symptoms: Oil leak from the oil pan gasket, visible under the vehicle.
-- Solution: Replace the oil pan gasket.
-- Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
-- Recalls: None Found
- **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
-- Description/Symptoms: Engine noise, loss of power, and check engine light.
-- Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner and guides.
-- Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
-- Recalls: None Found

## **Transmission**
### **5-Speed Automatic**
- **Hard or Delayed Shifting:**
-- Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, especially when cold.
-- Solution: Flush the transmission fluid and replace the transmission filter.
-- Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
-- Recalls: None Found
### **6-Speed Manual**
- **No Issues Found**

## **Suspension**
- **Leaf Spring Breakage:**
-- Description/Symptoms: Loud noise when hitting bumps, sagging rear end.
-- Solution: Replace the leaf springs.
-- Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
-- Recalls: None Found
- **Front Lower Control Arm Bushing Failure:**
-- Description/Symptoms: Unusual noises when turning or going over bumps, imprecise steering.
-- Solution: Replace the lower control arm bushings.
-- Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
-- Recalls: 18V386

## **Electrical**
- **Alternator Failure:**
-- Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging properly, electrical system problems.
-- Solution: Replace the alternator.
-- Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
-- Recalls: None Found
- **Starter Failure:**
-- Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the vehicle, grinding noises.
-- Solution: Replace the starter.
-- Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
-- Recalls: None Found

## **Exterior**
- **Door Handle Failure:**
-- Description/Symptoms: Door handles becoming loose or breaking, difficult to open doors.
-- Solution: Replace the door handles.
-- Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
-- Recalls: None Found

## **Interior**
- **Air Conditioner Compressor Failure:**
-- Description/Symptoms: Poor or no air conditioning.
-- Solution: Replace the air conditioner compressor.
-- Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
-- Recalls: None Found
- **Dashboard Cracking:**
-- Description/Symptoms: Cracks forming on the dashboard.
-- Solution: Replace the dashboard.
-- Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
-- Recalls: None Found

## **Summary**

Overall, the 2017 Toyota 4Runner is a reliable and capable vehicle, but it is not without its problems. Some of the most common issues include oil leaks, timing chain tensioner failure, hard or delayed shifting, leaf spring breakage, and electrical problems. While these issues can be costly to fix, they are generally not severe and can be addressed with regular maintenance and repairs.
