**Introduction:**

The 2023 Toyota 4Runner is a midsize SUV known for its off-road capabilities and durability. It is available with two engine options: a 4.0L V6 with 270 horsepower and 278 lb-ft of torque and a 3.5L V6 with 295 horsepower and 263 lb-ft of torque. The 4.0L V6 is paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission, while the 3.5L V6 is paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Despite its positive qualities, the 4Runner has some reported issues that owners have encountered.

**Engine**

**4.0L V6 with 5-speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:** The engine may consume excessive amounts of oil, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage. Symptoms include:
    * Blue smoke from the exhaust
    * Low oil pressure warning light
    * Increased oil consumption between oil changes
* Solution: Replace piston rings and valve seals.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
* Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Shifting Problems:** The transmission may experience difficulty shifting gears, resulting in rough or delayed gear changes. Symptoms include:
    * Hesitation or slipping when shifting
    * Grinding or clunking noises during shifting
* Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Sagging:** The rear suspension may sag over time, leading to uneven tire wear and decreased ride quality. Symptoms include:
    * Lowered ride height in the rear
    * Uneven tire wear
* Solution: Replace rear suspension components (e.g., springs, shock absorbers).
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
* Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** The vehicle's battery may drain prematurely, leading to difficulty starting the car. Symptoms include:
    * Reduced battery life
    * Difficulty starting the engine
* Solution: Inspect and replace the faulty battery or alternator.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Hood Flutter:** The hood may flutter or vibrate at certain speeds, creating noise and potential damage. Symptoms include:
    * Fluttering or vibration of the hood
    * Scratches or dents on the hood
* Solution: Install hood dampeners or adjust the hood latch.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
* Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Freezing:** The infotainment system may freeze or malfunction, affecting navigation, music, and other features. Symptoms include:
    * System not responding
    * Touch screen not functioning
* Solution: Software updates, module replacements, or system reset.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

While the 2023 Toyota 4Runner offers off-road performance and durability, it has some reported issues that owners may encounter. Engine oil consumption, transmission problems, and suspension issues are some of the more prevalent ones. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but it is important to be aware of them when considering this vehicle.
