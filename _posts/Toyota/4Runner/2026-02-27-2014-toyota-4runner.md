# 2014 Toyota 4Runner

### Overview
The 2014 Toyota 4Runner is a robust body-on-frame SUV renowned for its off-road capability and durability. This generation, particularly the 2014 model year, generally exhibits strong reliability, building on Toyota's reputation for long-term dependability. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected repairs, like any vehicle, certain components can occasionally present issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of attention for owners and prospective buyers.

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Toyota 4Runner stands out as a highly reliable vehicle, with its core powertrain (4.0L V6 engine and 5-speed automatic transmission) demonstrating exceptional durability and a lack of widespread issues. The majority of reported concerns are minor and infrequent, such as occasional infotainment system glitches or a specific issue with the Kinetic Dynamic Suspension System (KDSS) on some models, which can involve hydraulic leaks. Exterior components like the tailgate handle have also seen isolated reports of failure. Overall, the 4Runner's robust construction and proven mechanicals contribute to a strong reliability record, making it a dependable choice for those seeking an off-road capable SUV with long-term value.
