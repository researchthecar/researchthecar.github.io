# 2018 Toyota 4Runner Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Toyota 4Runner is widely praised for its rugged body-on-frame build, legendary off-road capability, and enduring mechanical durability. While its core powertrain is a proven, old-school workhorse, used car buyers should still inspect common trouble spots such as steering wheel vibrations, occasional brake pressure concerns, and isolated fuel system issues.

---

## Engine

### 4.0L V6 Engine

The 2018 4Runner features a 4.0-liter naturally aspirated V6 engine producing approximately 270 horsepower and 278 lb-ft of torque. Known for its tough, reliable design, it is paired exclusively with a traditional automatic setup and offers dependable long-term durability for daily driving and trail use.

**Fuel Pump Failure / Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine stalling while driving, accompanied by warning lights or a sudden inability to keep the vehicle running.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the faulty fuel pump or associated electrical relays.

## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a 5-speed ECT-i automatic transmission featuring sequential shift mode, this rugged gearbox is tailored for heavy-duty truck application. While lacking the gear counts of modern multi-speed units, it is mechanically robust and holds up well over high mileage.

## Suspension

### Front Suspension

The 4Runner's suspension is built to absorb rough off-road impacts, but high-mileage examples or vehicles operated in extreme climates should be checked for worn dampers and fractured springs.

**Shock Spring Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Front shock springs can snap or crack prematurely, particularly when exposed to harsh cold weather or severe driving conditions.
- **Solution:** Inspect front coil springs for structural fractures and replace damaged suspension struts or springs.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides adequate stopping power for a heavy SUV, but prospective buyers should carefully test brake pedal firmness during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Brake Pedal Softness and Pressure Loss**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal may suddenly feel soft, travel close to the floor, or fail to hold the vehicle completely stationary at a stop.
- **Solution:** Thoroughly check the brake master cylinder, inspect brake lines for leaks, and bleed or replace hydraulic components as necessary.

## Steering

### Steering Column and Wheel

Steering feedback is generally truck-like and direct, though a subset of owners have reported steering shaft wear or high-speed vibration requiring professional balancing or component replacement.

**Steering Vibration and Clunking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a noticeable clunking noise when turning or a front-end steering wheel vibration at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect the upper steering column shaft assembly, dust boot cover, and lower components; replace worn shafts as recommended.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Standard safety equipment includes active airbags and three-point seat belts, though buyers should verify that all restraint mechanisms and anchor points are free from moisture damage or rust.

**Seat Belt Hardware Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have discovered unexpected rust accumulation on seat belt rails and mounting hardware, potentially impacting structural integrity.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt anchor points and replace corroded hardware or assemblies to ensure proper safety operation.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V753000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=4Runner&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An overloaded vehicle can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 19V670000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=4Runner&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** During installation, certain steering and suspension components may not have been properly tightened. Loose fasteners may cause a loss of vehicle control, or cause an accessory to detach from the vehicle and become a road hazard.
- **What to do:** SET will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and re-tighten the steering and suspension fasteners as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V831000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=4Runner&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An incorrect load information label can result in the operator overloading the vehicle and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 17V763000: Structure:Body:Hood](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=4Runner&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the Billet Grille Overlay detaches while driving, it can become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** SET will notify owners, and dealers will replace the securing U-nuts with upgraded hardware, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V682000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=4Runner&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel pump fails, the engine can stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly with an improved one, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Toyota 4Runner remains a highly dependable choice for used car buyers who value long-term durability and rugged utility. While it exhibits minor owner-reported complaints regarding steering vibrations, brake pedal feel, and occasional fuel pump or suspension wear, its robust engine and traditional layout make it a solid investment. Always perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the frame, brakes, and steering components.
