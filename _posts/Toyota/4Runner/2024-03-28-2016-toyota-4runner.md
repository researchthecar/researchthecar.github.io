**Introduction to the 2016 Toyota 4Runner**

The 2016 Toyota 4Runner is a mid-size SUV that offers a rugged and capable off-roading experience. It is available with two engine options: a 4.0-liter V6 and a 2.7-liter I-4. The V6 engine produces 270 horsepower and 278 lb-ft of torque, while the I-4 engine produces 154 horsepower and 180 lb-ft of torque. Both engines can be paired with either a 5-speed automatic transmission or a 6-speed manual transmission. The 4Runner also offers a variety of trim levels, including the SR5, TRD Off-Road, TRD Pro, and Limited.

Despite its rugged exterior and off-road capabilities, the 4Runner has been plagued by a number of issues since its release. These issues range from minor annoyances to more serious problems that can affect the vehicle's performance and reliability.

**Engine**

* **V6 Engine**
    * **Timing Belt Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The timing belt can fail prematurely, causing the engine to lose power and stall.
        * Solution: Replace the timing belt.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **I-4 Engine**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **Both Transmissions**
    * **Transmission Fluid Leaks**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission fluid can leak from the transmission case, causing the transmission to slip or fail.
        * Solution: Replace the transmission fluid seals.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
        * Recalls: None Found
* **5-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Torque Converter Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The torque converter can fail, causing the transmission to lose power and stall.
        * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **6-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension**
    * **Leaf Spring Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The leaf springs can break or sag, causing the rear of the vehicle to sag and the ride quality to suffer.
        * Solution: Replace the leaf springs.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge**
        * Description/Symptoms: The battery can discharge prematurely, leaving the vehicle unable to start.
        * Solution: Replace the battery.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The alternator can fail, causing the battery to discharge and the vehicle to lose power.
        * Solution: Replace the alternator.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Body Corrosion**
        * Description/Symptoms: The body can corrode prematurely, especially in areas where salt is used on the roads.
        * Solution: Rustproofing can be applied, but no permanent fix is available.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity of the corrosion
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Roof Rack Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The roof rack can fail, causing the crossbars or cargo to fall off the vehicle.
        * Solution: Replace the roof rack.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
        * Recalls: NHTSA Recall ID: 20V570000

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Vibration**
        * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard can vibrate excessively, especially at higher speeds.
        * Solution: Add sound deadening material to the dashboard.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Climate Control Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The climate control system can fail to operate properly, causing the temperature in the cabin to become uncomfortable.
        * Solution: Replace the climate control module.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Toyota 4Runner is a capable off-road vehicle that has been plagued by a number of issues since its release. These issues range from minor annoyances to more serious problems that can affect the vehicle's performance and reliability.

Overall, the severity of the issues with the 4Runner is moderate. While some of the issues can be expensive to fix, they are not typically catastrophic and can be fixed relatively easily.
