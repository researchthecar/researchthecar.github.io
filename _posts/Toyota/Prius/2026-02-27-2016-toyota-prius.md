# 2016 Toyota Prius Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Toyota Prius remains a benchmark for hybrid efficiency and everyday practicality, offering low running costs and strong fuel economy. While generally dependable, used shoppers should keep a close eye on a few recurring issues involving hybrid brake actuator components, coolant leaks, and headlight performance.

---

## Engine

### 1.8L 4-Cylinder Hybrid Engine

The 1.8-liter four-cylinder hybrid engine pairs with an electric motor to produce a combined 121 horsepower. It is fundamentally durable and achieves exceptional fuel efficiency, though minor cooling system leaks have been noted by some owners.

**Coolant Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant levels in the reservoir and engine overheating warnings due to leaks from the heat exchanger into the exhaust.
- **Solution:** Inspect the heat exchanger and exhaust system; replace leaking components as necessary.

## Transmission

### Electronically Controlled Continuously Variable Transmission (ECVT)

The planetary-gear electronic continuously variable transmission distributes power seamlessly between the gasoline engine and electric motors. It is generally robust and free of mechanical design flaws.

## Brakes

### Brake Actuator and Booster

The regenerative braking system integrates hydraulic components with electronic controls to maximize efficiency.

**Brake Failure and Low Pressure Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated ABS, traction control, and brake warning lights accompanied by messages such as 'Braking Power Low, Stop in a Safe Place' and a soft or unresponsive brake pedal.
- **Solution:** Have the brake actuator, accumulator, and booster pump diagnosed and replaced by a qualified technician if failure occurs.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical accessories generally hold up well, though headlight performance and moisture sealing have drawn complaints.

**Headlight Aim and Condensation**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights perceived as blindingly bright or poorly aimed to oncoming traffic, or condensation buildup inside the headlight assembly reducing light output.
- **Solution:** Adjust headlight housing aim or replace headlight assemblies showing internal moisture intrusion.

### Structure and Visibility

Exterior panels and glass generally endure normal weather conditions, though cosmetic paint degradation and glass durability require attention.

**Paint Peeling and Windshield Distortion**
- **Symptoms:** Exterior body paint or trim peeling on sun-exposed panels, or optical distortion and cracking in the windshield glass.
- **Solution:** Repaint affected body panels or replace damaged windshields with OEM glass.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V658000: Hybrid Propulsion System: Inverter](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Prius&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A capacitor within the hybrid system may not have been properly bolted to the inverter housing. If the capacitor was not properly secured, vibrations from driving the vehicle may damage the capacitor, shutting down the hybrid system, causing a loss of motive power and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the inverter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V579000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Prius&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A portion of the engine wire harness connected to the hybrid Power Control Unit (PCU), could contact the cover at this connection and wear, causing an electrical short circuit. An electrical short can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the engine wire harness assembly and replace it if damage is found. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V672000: Air Bags:Frontal:Passenger Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Prius&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A front passenger air bag that unintentionally inflates can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front passenger air bag assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V085000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Prius&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The air bag pressure sensors and/or the acceleration sensors may fail causing the side/curtain shield air bags and/or the front air bags to not deploy in the event of a crash. If the air bags fail to deploy in a crash, it can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the sensors as necessary based on their serial numbers, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V741000: Parking Brake](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Prius&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the parking brake cable disengages from the mechanism and the transmission is left in a gear other than 'Park' while the ignition is on and the driver leaves the vehicle, the vehicle may roll unexpectedly, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will install a clip at the parking brake cable end to prevent the cable from disengaging, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Toyota Prius is an economical and dependable daily driver that rewards prospective buyers with legendary fuel economy. When evaluating a used model, ensure the braking system operates without warning lights and check the cooling system and headlights for any signs of trouble.
