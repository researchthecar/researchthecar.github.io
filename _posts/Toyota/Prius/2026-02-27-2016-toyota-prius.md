# 2016 Toyota Prius

### Overview
The 2016 Toyota Prius, a pioneer in hybrid technology, is generally recognized for its exceptional reliability and fuel efficiency. This model year continues Toyota's legacy of building durable vehicles, with a focus on its advanced hybrid powertrain. Owners typically report high satisfaction with its long-term performance and low running costs. While no vehicle is entirely without potential issues, the 2016 Prius demonstrates a strong track record, particularly in its core hybrid components and overall build quality. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond standard maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and prospective buyers.

## Electrical

### Hybrid Battery System
**Hybrid Battery Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced fuel efficiency, loss of electric-only driving range, dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Hybrid battery replacement.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $4,000


### 12V Auxiliary Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the vehicle, electrical system malfunctions (e.g., infotainment, lights).
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V auxiliary battery.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Toyota Prius stands out as a highly reliable vehicle, particularly in its powertrain and core mechanical components. The hybrid system, a hallmark of Toyota's engineering, generally performs exceptionally well over the long term. The most commonly cited potential issues revolve around the hybrid battery's eventual degradation, which is a known factor for most hybrid vehicles and has a significant replacement cost. The 12V auxiliary battery can also experience premature failure. Minor electrical glitches within the infotainment system are occasionally reported but are not widespread. Overall, the 2016 Prius offers excellent long-term value and dependability, with most owners experiencing minimal unscheduled repairs beyond routine maintenance and the eventual hybrid battery replacement.
