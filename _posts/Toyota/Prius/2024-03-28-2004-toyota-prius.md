**Introduction:**

The 2004 Toyota Prius is a hybrid electric vehicle that combines a 1.5-liter gasoline engine with an electric motor to achieve excellent fuel economy. It is available with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). The Prius is known for its reliability and fuel efficiency, but it has also been reported to have some issues.

**Engine**

**1.5-Liter Gasoline Engine**

**Head Gasket Failure**
* Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, and engine knocking
* Solution: Replace head gasket
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
* Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

**Transmission Shuddering**
* Description/Symptoms: Vibrations or shaking when accelerating or decelerating
* Solution: Replace or repair CVT
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Front Suspension Strut Failure**
* Description/Symptoms: Noises when turning or going over bumps, unstable handling
* Solution: Replace front suspension struts
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
* Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Inverter Failure**
* Description/Symptoms: Power loss, warning lights, vehicle won't start
* Solution: Replace inverter
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,500+
* Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Sunroof Seal Failure**
* Description/Symptoms: Water leaks, wind noise
* Solution: Replace sunroof seal
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Dashboard Cracking**
* Description/Symptoms: Cracks on the surface of the dashboard
* Solution: Replace dashboard or cover with dash mat
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2004 Toyota Prius is generally a reliable vehicle with excellent fuel economy. However, it has some potential issues, including head gasket failure, transmission shuddering, suspension strut failure, and electrical problems. The cost of repairs can vary depending on the severity of the issue and the location of the repair shop.
