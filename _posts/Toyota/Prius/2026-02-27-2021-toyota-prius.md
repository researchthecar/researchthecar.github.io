# 2021 Toyota Prius Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Toyota Prius continues to uphold the brand's reputation for stellar fuel efficiency and practical daily driving. As with any modern hybrid, prospective buyers should review available owner feedback regarding powertrain transitions and driver-assist technologies. Overall, the vehicle remains a popular and generally dependable choice in the used hybrid market.

---

## Engine

### 1.8L 4-Cylinder Hybrid Synergy Drive

Powered by a 1.8-liter 4-cylinder engine paired with an electric motor system producing a combined 121 horsepower, this setup is renowned for exceptional fuel economy. The hybrid system seamlessly blends electric and gas power, though rare owner complaints note unusual transition behavior.

**Hybrid System Transition and Speed Control Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported unexpected engine engagement during deceleration or instances where the vehicle momentarily accelerated independently.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the hybrid control module, electronic throttle body, and software calibrations for any anomalies.

## Transmission

### Electronically Controlled Continuously Variable Transmission (e-CVT)

Equipped with an electronically controlled continuously variable transmission managing front-wheel drive or available all-wheel drive. This planetary gear-type e-CVT is mechanically robust and known for long-term durability in hybrid applications.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Tires

Standard factory wheel and tire assemblies fitted for daily commuting.

**Premature Tire Damage**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden tire deflation or blowouts experienced shortly after vehicle purchase, often attributed to external road hazards.
- **Solution:** Inspect tire tread condition and pressure regularly, replacing damaged or punctured tires immediately.

## Safety

### Lane Keeping Assist

Safety technologies include Toyota Safety Sense 2.0 as standard equipment.

**Lane Assist Tracking Deviation**
- **Symptoms:** During radar cruise control operation, the lane keeping assist feature may occasionally track toward the edge of the lane or attempt to veer outward.
- **Solution:** Check for available dealer software updates regarding the advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS) and recalibrate sensors if necessary.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Toyota Prius offers exceptional efficiency and dependable everyday transportation. While isolated owner complaints highlight minor electronic and driver-assist quirks, they do not detract from the car's overall solid reputation. Used car shoppers should perform a thorough test drive, verifying proper hybrid transition behavior and driver-assistance calibration before purchase.
