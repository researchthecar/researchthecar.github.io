# 2021 Toyota Prius

### Overview
The 2021 Toyota Prius continues its legacy of fuel efficiency and reliability, making it a popular choice for eco-conscious drivers. This model year benefits from Toyota's extensive experience with hybrid technology, generally resulting in a robust and dependable vehicle. While no car is entirely immune to issues, the Prius typically demonstrates strong performance across its core systems. Owners generally report satisfaction with its low running costs and consistent operation. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for the 2021 model year.

**Degradation over time**

**Premature failure**

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Toyota Prius is a highly reliable vehicle, consistent with Toyota's reputation. The powertrain, including the hybrid system and eCVT, shows excellent durability. The primary areas where owners might encounter issues are related to the longevity of the 12V auxiliary battery and potential degradation of the main hybrid battery pack over extended mileage, though this is a common characteristic of hybrid systems. Minor electrical gremlins, such as infotainment glitches, are also occasionally reported but are typically resolved with software updates. Overall, the Prius offers a dependable ownership experience with minimal unexpected mechanical failures.
