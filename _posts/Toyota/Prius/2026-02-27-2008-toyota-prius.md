# 2008 Toyota Prius Reliability & Common Issues

### Overview

The 2008 Toyota Prius is widely regarded for its exceptional powertrain longevity and fuel efficiency. However, like any vehicle of its age, it has accumulated a significant number of owner reports detailing specific, recurring failure points. The issues highlighted in this report represent a sample of owner-reported complaints submitted to safety regulators; they do not guarantee that every vehicle will experience these problems, though they are critical areas for prospective buyers to inspect.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Hybrid Inline-4 (1NZ-FXE)

The 1.5-liter gasoline engine is paired with an electric motor to generate a combined 110 horsepower. It is highly regarded for fuel economy and long-term durability. However, high-mileage examples frequently suffer from auxiliary cooling system issues such as inverter and water pump failures.

**Inverter or Engine Water Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report sudden illumination of dashboard warning lights (including the 'red triangle of death'), followed by a loss of engine power, loss of acceleration, and disabled air conditioning. In some cases, a seized water pump can blow critical electrical system fuses, preventing the vehicle from restarting.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed inverter water pump or engine coolant water pump, and replace any blown fuses.
- **Cost:** Around $1,500+ out of pocket for replacement.

## Transmission

### e-CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)

The e-CVT uses an electronically controlled planetary gear set to seamlessly blend power from the engine and electric motor. This unit is exceptionally robust and rarely fails under normal driving conditions, but replacement costs are extremely high if a failure occurs.

**Gear Shifter Popping Into Neutral**
- **Symptoms:** In hot weather or when the cabin interior becomes excessively hot, the gear shift lever may fail to stay engaged in Drive or Reverse. Owners report that the shifter automatically pops back into Neutral, requiring them to physically hold the lever in position to keep the vehicle in gear while driving.
- **Solution:** Replace the electronic gear shift lever assembly.

**Complete e-CVT Mechanical Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A less common but severe failure mode involves the vehicle refusing to start, move, or shift into any gear, sometimes accompanied by internal grinding noises and diagnostic trouble codes.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the complete e-CVT transmission assembly.
- **Cost:** Approximately $6,500 for total dealer replacement.

## Brakes

### Brake System Components

**ABS Actuator and Accumulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of electronically assisted power braking, accompanied by a hard or soft brake pedal feel, loud grinding/buzzing noises from the engine bay, and illuminated ABS, VSC, and Brake warning lights. Stopping distances are significantly increased.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed ABS actuator and accumulator assembly, and bleed the brake system.
- **Cost:** Typically ranges from $2,100 to over $3,000 out of pocket.

## Electrical

### Digital Instrument Cluster

**Blank Combination Meter Display**
- **Symptoms:** The digital dashboard display goes completely blank intermittently or permanently. Drivers lose access to the speedometer, fuel gauge, odometer, and gear indicator. In some instances, the vehicle may also refuse to shut down when the power button is pressed.
- **Solution:** Replace the combination meter circuit board or assembly.
- **Cost:** Approximately $1,580 out of pocket at an authorized dealership.

### Exterior Lighting System

**Intermittent HID Headlight Failure**
- **Symptoms:** High-Intensity Discharge (HID) headlight bulbs randomly flicker or shut off completely while driving, severely reducing night visibility. Turning the headlight switch off and back on resets the bulbs temporarily.
- **Solution:** Replace the failing HID bulbs, igniters, or melted headlight wiring harnesses.

## Interior

### Cabin Components and Latches

**Melted Hatchback Trunk Release Rubber**
- **Symptoms:** The exterior rubber release button cover on the rear hatch melts, degrades, and becomes extremely gooey or sticky to the touch. Eventually, this rubber breaks apart, letting moisture in and causing the electronic latch to jam shut, preventing access to the cargo area.
- **Solution:** Replace the rear hatch latch assembly and the outer rubber button cover.
- **Cost:** Dealer and repair shop quotes range from $200 to $540.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**Recall 12V537000: Steering**
- **Issue:** Toyota is recalling certain model year 2004-2009 Prius vehicles manufactured August 5, 2003, through March 30, 2009.  The affected vehicles have a steering intermediate extension shaft assembly built with metal splines that complete the connection from the steering wheel to the steering gear.  These splines may have been manufactured with an insufficient hardness. Over time, the splines may wear and eventually fail resulting in a loss of steering ability, increasing the risk of a vehicle crash. Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the extension shaft and replace it if it is one with insufficient hardness, free of charge.  The recall began during December 2012.  Owners may contact their Toyota dealer or the Toyota Customer Experience Center at 1-800-331-4331.
- **Remedy:** Check recall completion status with the manufacturer or NHTSA.

**Recall 12V536000: Engine And Engine Cooling**
- **Issue:** Toyota is recalling certain model year 2004-2009 Prius vehicles manufactured August 6, 2003, through March 30, 2009 and FCHV-adv vehicles manufactured December 12, 2008, through September 13, 2011.  During manufacturing, a scratch may have occurred inside of the electrically driven water pump at the coil wire. The coil wire may corrode at the scratched portion. The corroded coil wire may break and the water pump could stop.  The corroded coil wire could cause a short circuit between coil wires and a possible open fuse, creating a stall-like condition of the hybrid system while the vehicle is being driven.  This may increase the risk of a vehicle crash. Toyota will notify its owners, and dealers will replace the electric water pump, free of charge.  The recall began on January 17, 2013.  Owners may contact their Toyota dealer or the Toyota Customer Experience Center at 1-800-331-4331.
- **Remedy:** Check recall completion status with the manufacturer or NHTSA.

**Recall 09V388000: Vehicle Speed Control:Accelerator Pedal**
- **Issue:** TOYOTA IS RECALLING CERTAIN MODEL YEAR 2004-2010 PASSENGER VEHICLES.  THE ACCELERATOR PEDAL CAN GET STUCK IN THE WIDE OPEN POSITION DUE TO ITS BEING TRAPPED BY AN UNSECURED OR INCOMPATIBLE DRIVER'S FLOOR MAT. A STUCK OPEN ACCELERATOR PEDAL MAY RESULT IN VERY HIGH VEHICLE SPEEDS AND MAKE IT DIFFICULT TO STOP THE VEHICLE, WHICH COULD CAUSE A CRASH, SERIOUS INJURY OR DEATH. TOYOTA FILED AN AMENDED DEFECT REPORT ON NOVEMBER 25, 2009, STATING THAT DEALERS WILL MODIFY THE ACCELERATOR PEDAL AND, ON CERTAIN VEHICLES, ALTER THE SHAPE OF THE FLOOR SURFACE UNDER THE PEDAL.  THESE CHANGES ADDRESS THE RISK OF PEDAL ENTRAPMENT DUE TO INTERFERENCE WITH THE FLOOR MAT.  REDESIGNED ACCELERATOR PEDALS WILL BECOME AVAILABLE BEGINNING IN APRIL 2010 AND DEALERS WILL REPLACE ANY MODIFIED PEDAL WITH THE NEW PEDAL IF DESIRED.  ALSO, DEALERS WILL REPLACE ANY GENUINE TOYOTA OR LEXUS ALL-WEATHER FLOOR MATS WITH REDESIGNED ALL-WEATHER MATS, OR REPURCHASE THE PREVIOUS MATS FROM OWNERS WHO DO NOT WANT THE NEW ONES.  ADDITIONALLY, SOFTWARE MODIFICATIONS WILL BE INSTALLED ON CAMRY, AVALON AND LEXUS ES 350, IS 350 AND IS 250 MODELS THAT WILL ENSURE THAT THE BRAKE OVERRIDES THE ACCELERATOR IN THE EVENT BOTH BRAKE AND ACCELERATOR PEDALS ARE APPLIED.  TOYOTA WILL BEGIN MAILING LETTERS TO OWNERS ON DECEMBER 31, 2009.  OWNERS MAY CONTACT TOYOTA AT 1-800-331-4331, LEXUS AT 1-800-255-3987.
- **Remedy:** Check recall completion status with the manufacturer or NHTSA.

**Recall 10V036000: Equipment:Other:Labels**
- **Issue:** SOUTHEAST TOYOTA IS RECALLING CERTAIN MODEL YEAR 2005-2011 PASSENGER VEHICLES FOR FAILING TO COMPLY WITH THE REQUIREMENTS OF FEDERAL MOTOR VEHICLE SAFETY STANDARD NO. 110, "TIRE SELECTION AND RIMS."  THESE VEHICLES WERE SOLD WITHOUT THE REQUISITE LOAD CARRYING CAPACITY MODIFICATION LABELS. A DRIVER MAY OVERLOAD A VEHICLE WHICH MAY INCREASE THE RISK OF A CRASH. DEALERS WILL MAIL TO CONSUMERS THE CORRECTED LABEL OR THE CUSTOMER WILL HAVE THE OPTION FOR DEALERS TO INSTALL THE LABEL FREE OF CHARGE.  THE SAFETY RECALL BEGAN ON MAY 27, 2010.  OWNERS MAY CONTACT SOUTHEAST TOYOTA AT 1-800-301-6859.
- **Remedy:** Check recall completion status with the manufacturer or NHTSA.

### Summary Verdict

The 2008 Toyota Prius remains a highly reliable used hybrid option, but buyers must proceed with caution regarding high-mileage failures. While the engine and e-CVT are durable, common electrical and braking system faults—specifically the blank combination meter and failing ABS actuator—can cost thousands to repair out of pocket. If these items have already been serviced or are in good working order, the vehicle represents an exceptionally cost-effective daily commuter.
