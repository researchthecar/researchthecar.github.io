**2010 Toyota Prius**

The 2010 Toyota Prius is a compact hybrid vehicle available with a 1.8-liter 4-cylinder gasoline engine paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). It has an output of 98 horsepower and 105 lb-ft of torque. The Prius is known for its fuel efficiency, spacious interior, and reliability. However, it has also been subject to several issues.

**Engine**
* **1.8-liter 4-cylinder engine**

**Oil Consumption**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, causing the engine to burn oil and produce blue smoke from the exhaust.
Solution: Replace piston rings and valve stem seals.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
Recalls: 10V-645, 11V-291

**Transmission**
* **Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

**Transmission Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping, shuddering, or refusing to engage gears.
Solution: Replace or rebuild the transmission.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**
* **Electrical System Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Random electrical issues, including flickering lights, loss of power, and dashboard warning lights.
Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty electrical component (e.g., alternator, battery, wiring).
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
* **Windshield Cracking**
Description/Symptoms: Windshield develops cracks or chips easily.
Solution: Replace the windshield.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: 14V-065

**Interior**
* **Dashboard Cracking**
Description/Symptoms: Dashboard develops cracks or peeling.
Solution: Replace the dashboard.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2010 Toyota Prius is generally a reliable vehicle, but it has been subject to several issues, particularly with the engine and transmission. These issues can range from minor inconveniences to major repairs, with estimated costs ranging from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars. It is important to note that these issues may not be present in all Prius models and that regular maintenance and timely repairs can help prevent or mitigate these problems.
