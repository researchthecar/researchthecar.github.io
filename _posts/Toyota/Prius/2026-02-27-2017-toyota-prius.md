# 2017 Toyota Prius Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Toyota Prius remains a benchmark for hybrid efficiency and generally solid build quality, though used car shoppers should inspect specific areas. While major mechanical failures are relatively infrequent, owner reports highlight a few recurring problem areas—particularly concerning engine cooling components, brake response, and exterior glass durability. Knowing what to look out for during a pre-purchase inspection can help buyers secure a dependable and highly efficient commuter.

---

## Engine

### 1.8L 4-Cylinder Hybrid (2ZR-FXE)

The 1.8-liter four-cylinder hybrid engine pairs with an electric motor system to deliver around 121 total system horsepower. Highly regarded for its exceptional fuel economy, this powertrain is generally robust but requires careful attention to the cooling and exhaust heat exchanger systems.

**Coolant Leak and Exhaust Smoke**
- **Symptoms:** Vents blowing cold air, check engine light illumination, persistent loss of coolant in the reservoir, and white smoke from the exhaust due to internal coolant leakage.
- **Solution:** Inspect the exhaust heat exchanger and head gasket; replace faulty heat exchanger assemblies or gaskets as needed.

**Engine Stalling and Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Engine hesitates or experiences a multi-second delay when depressing the accelerator pedal after coming to a complete stop in hot weather.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scan for pending powertrain codes and update or service fuel/hybrid management software if necessary.

## Transmission

### Electronically Controlled Continuously Variable Transmission (ECVT)

The planetary gear-type electronic continuously variable transmission smoothly blends power from the gasoline engine and electric motors. It is exceptionally durable with very few mechanical complaints reported by owners.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The regenerative braking system works in tandem with traditional hydraulic friction brakes to recapture energy. While generally reliable, some owners have reported unexpected brake pedal behavior and premature rotor wear.

**Brake Failure or Traction Control Interruption**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal depression accompanied by the traction control warning indicator illuminating, leading to a temporary failure or unexpected reduction in braking responsiveness.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake actuator, ABS sensors, and brake fluid pressure; service or replace malfunctioning hydraulic control modules.

## Steering

### Steering Wheel and Column

The electric power steering system provides light and responsive handling for city driving, though cosmetic wear on the steering wheel wrap is common over time.

**Steering Wheel Material Peeling and Stiff Steering**
- **Symptoms:** Steering wheel surface material bubbling and peeling around the upper grip, or intermittent stiffness making the steering difficult to turn.
- **Solution:** Replace the steering wheel cover or inspect the electric power steering motor and sensors.

## Exterior

### Visibility and Glass

Exterior panels and lighting are generally durable, though windshield glass has drawn isolated complaints regarding stress fractures.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Cracks developing across the windshield from the lower edges upward while the vehicle is parked or sitting in warm weather without impact from debris.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged windshield with an OEM or quality aftermarket glass unit.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V579000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Prius&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A portion of the engine wire harness connected to the hybrid Power Control Unit (PCU), could contact the cover at this connection and wear, causing an electrical short circuit. An electrical short can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the engine wire harness assembly and replace it if damage is found. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V295000: Tires:Temporary/Emergency Spare Tire](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Prius&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A spare tire that is not inflated to the proper value may not perform as intended, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Gulf States will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the spare tires, adjusting their pressure as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V741000: Parking Brake](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Prius&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the parking brake cable disengages from the mechanism and the transmission is left in a gear other than 'Park' while the ignition is on and the driver leaves the vehicle, the vehicle may roll unexpectedly, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will install a clip at the parking brake cable end to prevent the cable from disengaging, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Toyota Prius remains a smart choice for buyers prioritizing exceptional fuel efficiency and everyday practicality. While most systems hold up very well over high mileages, prospective buyers should thoroughly check the cooling system for coolant leaks, test the brakes for normal regenerative response, and inspect the windshield and steering wheel for cosmetic or stress-related wear during a pre-purchase inspection.
