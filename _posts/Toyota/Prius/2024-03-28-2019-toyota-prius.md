**2019 Toyota Prius**

The 2019 Toyota Prius is a hybrid electric vehicle with a 1.8-liter four-cylinder engine and an electric motor that generates 121 horsepower and 105 lb-ft of torque. It comes with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). People like the Prius for its fuel efficiency, reliability, and low maintenance costs. However, some people dislike its small size, lack of power, and uninspiring driving dynamics.

**Engine**

* **1.8L Four-Cylinder Engine**
    * **Intake Valve Sticking**
        * Description/Symptoms: The intake valves can become stuck in the open or closed position, causing the engine to run rough, misfire, and lose power.
        * Solution: Replace the intake valves.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **CVT**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension**
    * **Strut Bearing Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The strut bearing can fail, causing a clunking or rattling noise when driving over bumps or turning.
        * Solution: Replace the strut bearing.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery**
    * **12 Volt Battery Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The 12-volt battery can fail, causing the vehicle to lose power and become inoperable.
        * Solution: Replace the 12-volt battery.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlights**
    * **Headlight Condensation**
        * Description/Symptoms: Condensation can form inside the headlights, making them less effective and causing them to fail prematurely.
        * Solution: Replace the headlights.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Cabin Noise**
    * Description/Symptoms: The cabin can be noisy, especially at highway speeds.
        * Solution: Install sound-dampening materials.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Overall Summary**

The 2019 Toyota Prius is a reliable and fuel-efficient vehicle with few major issues. However, some owners have reported problems with the intake valves, strut bearings, and headlights. The cost to fix these issues can range from $100 to $1,500, depending on the severity of the problem.
