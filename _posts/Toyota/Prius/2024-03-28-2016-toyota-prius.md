**Introduction:**

The 2016 Toyota Prius is a hybrid electric vehicle available with two engine options: a 1.8L 4-cylinder gasoline engine and a hybrid system combining the gasoline engine with an electric motor. The Prius is offered with a continuously variable transmission (CVT).

**Engine**

**1.8L 4-Cylinder Gasoline Engine**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Description/Symptoms: The engine consumes an excessive amount of oil, requiring frequent top-offs. Solution: Replace faulty piston rings and/or valve seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+ Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 17V534000

**Transmission**

**CVT**

* **Hesitation/Jerking:** Description/Symptoms: The transmission hesitates or jerks during shifting. Solution: Update transmission software and/or replace valve body. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 17V304000

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Noise:** Description/Symptoms: A rattling or clunking noise is heard from the rear suspension. Solution: Replace faulty rear suspension components (e.g., sway bar links, bushings). Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **12V Battery Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The 12V battery prematurely fails, leading to starting issues. Solution: Replace the faulty battery. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $150+ Recalls: None Found

* **Hybrid Battery Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The hybrid battery pack deteriorates over time, affecting performance and fuel economy. Solution: Replace the hybrid battery pack. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+ Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Windshield Cracking:** Description/Symptoms: The windshield cracks easily due to temperature changes or minor impacts. Solution: Replace the windshield. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Headliner Sagging:** Description/Symptoms: The headliner fabric sags or detaches from the ceiling. Solution: Reattach or replace the headliner fabric. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+ Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2016 Toyota Prius has a few notable issues, including excessive oil consumption in the gasoline engine, transmission hesitation/jerking, rear suspension noise, and premature battery failures. While the issues may vary in severity and cost, they generally do not detract from the overall reliability and fuel efficiency of the vehicle.
