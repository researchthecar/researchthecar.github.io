**Introduction:**

The 2012 Toyota Prius is a hybrid electric vehicle that is known for its fuel efficiency and environmental friendliness. It comes in two trim levels: the Two and the Three. The Two comes with a 1.8-liter 4-cylinder engine that produces 98 horsepower and 105 lb-ft of torque, mated to a continuously variable transmission (CVT). The Three comes with the same engine but produces 134 horsepower and 151 lb-ft of torque, thanks to an electric motor that assists the gasoline engine. Both trims are front-wheel drive.

People like the Prius for its fuel efficiency, reliability, and low maintenance costs. However, some common complaints about the vehicle include its lack of power, cramped interior, and uninspiring driving experience.

**1.8L 4-Cylinder + Electric Motor (Three)**

**Intake Manifold Issue**
Description/Symptoms: A faulty intake manifold can cause a loss of power, poor fuel economy, and engine knocking.
Solution: Replacing the intake manifold.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Fuel Injector Issue**
Description/Symptoms: Faulty fuel injectors can cause rough idling, misfiring, and poor acceleration.
Solution: Replacing the fuel injectors.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Transmission**
**Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

**Shuddering**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission may shudder or vibrate during acceleration or deceleration.
Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the CVT.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+

**Slipping**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip or hesitate during gear changes.
Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the CVT.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

**12-Volt Battery Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The 12-volt battery may fail prematurely, leading to electrical problems and difficulty starting the vehicle.
Solution: Replacing the 12-volt battery.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
Recalls: 14V351

**Exterior**

**Headlight Condensation**
Description/Symptoms: Moisture can build up inside the headlights, causing condensation and impairing visibility.
Solution: Replacing the headlights or using an anti-fog sealant.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Interior**

**Dashboard Rattle**
Description/Symptoms: The dashboard may rattle or squeak, especially over rough roads.
Solution: Tightening or replacing the dashboard components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Seat Fabric Pilling**
Description/Symptoms: The seat fabric may pill or become worn over time, especially in high-traffic areas.
Solution: Replacing the seat covers or using seat protectors.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+

**Summary**

Overall, the 2012 Toyota Prius is a reliable and fuel-efficient vehicle. However, it can experience some issues, such as engine and transmission problems, as well as electrical and interior issues. The severity of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and its extent. The cost to fix these issues can also vary widely, but generally falls within the range of $100 to $4,000 or more.
