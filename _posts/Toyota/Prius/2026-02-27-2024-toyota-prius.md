# 2024 Toyota Prius Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Toyota Prius introduces a striking, modern design paired with impressive fuel efficiency and upgraded hybrid performance. While owners generally praise its driving dynamics and fuel economy, early production models have accumulated a handful of owner-reported concerns via federal safety databases. Prospective buyers should review these emerging complaints—ranging from tire wear to electrical quirks—to ensure a well-rounded purchasing decision.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Hybrid (M20A-FXS)

The 2.0-liter four-cylinder hybrid engine pairs with electric motor generators to produce a combined 194 horsepower in front-wheel-drive configurations. This powertrain delivers strong, responsive acceleration while maintaining exceptional fuel efficiency, making it a reliable core component of the vehicle.

## Transmission

### Electronically Controlled Continuously Variable Transmission (eCVT)

An electronically controlled continuously variable transmission manages power distribution seamlessly between the gas engine and electric motors. Known for smooth operation and durability, this transmission option requires routine fluid inspections but rarely exhibits mechanical failures.

## Suspension

### Tires and Wheels

The suspension and wheel setup provides a balanced ride, though factory low-profile tire options warrant careful routine inspection.

**Premature Tire Wear and Sidewall Damage**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced rapid inner tread wear and unexpected sidewall damage on factory-equipped low-profile tires.
- **Solution:** Inspect tire tread depths regularly, maintain proper inflation pressures, and replace worn tires as needed.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical architecture supports advanced infotainment, driver-assistance features, and exterior lighting systems.

**Headlight Functionality Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report concerns regarding headlight brightness design and visibility under certain operating conditions.
- **Solution:** Consult a local dealership to check for any applicable software adjustments or service guidance.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V274000: Electrical System:Wiring:Switches/Knobs/Buttons](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Prius&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Water may enter the rear door switch and cause a short circuit, allowing an unlocked rear door to open unexpectedly. A door that unexpectedly opens while the vehicle is moving or during a crash increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both right and left rear door opener switches, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V548000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Prius&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." A vehicle with an incorrect maximum capacity weight value may be overloaded, which can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V744000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Prius&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to freeze or display a blank screen when the vehicle is in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that fails to display an image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the parking assist software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V049000: Electrical System:Wiring:Switches/Knobs/Buttons](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Prius&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Water may enter the rear door switch and cause a short circuit, allowing an unlocked rear door to open unexpectedly. A door that unexpectedly opens while the vehicle is moving increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will modify the rear door switch circuits, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Toyota Prius remains a highly efficient and technologically advanced hybrid option for daily commuters and road-trip enthusiasts alike. While initial owner reports highlight minor issues such as tire wear patterns and specific electrical lighting concerns, the core hybrid powertrain is robust. Used-car buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on tires, infotainment operation, and maintenance history.
