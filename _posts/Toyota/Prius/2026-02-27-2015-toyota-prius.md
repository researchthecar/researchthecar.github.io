# 2015 Toyota Prius

### Overview
The 2015 Toyota Prius is widely recognized for its exceptional reliability and fuel efficiency, making it a popular choice for eco-conscious drivers. As a hybrid vehicle, it combines a gasoline engine with an electric motor and battery pack, a system that has proven remarkably robust over the years. This generation of the Prius, part of the third facelift, continued to refine the formula, offering a comfortable ride and a practical interior. Owners generally report high satisfaction with its long-term durability, with common maintenance items being typical for any vehicle rather than specific model weaknesses. The hybrid system, in particular, has a strong track record.

## Electrical

### Hybrid Battery System
**Hybrid Battery Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced fuel economy, loss of electric-only driving range, dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Hybrid battery replacement.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $4,000


### 12V Auxiliary Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the gasoline engine, electrical system malfunctions (e.g., infotainment, lights).
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V battery.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2015 Toyota Prius stands out as a highly reliable vehicle, particularly in its powertrain and core mechanical components. The hybrid system, a key feature, has demonstrated excellent longevity. The most common significant repair concern revolves around the hybrid battery pack, which, while expensive to replace, typically lasts for many years and miles. The 12V auxiliary battery can also be a point of failure, though this is a common issue across many vehicle types. Minor issues like headlight condensation are occasionally reported but are generally not indicative of major system flaws. Overall, the 2015 Prius is a dependable choice with a strong reputation for low running costs and minimal unexpected repairs.
