**Introduction:**

The 1992 Toyota Celica is a sporty coupe known for its sleek styling, handling, and reliable performance. It was available with various engine and transmission options, including:

- 1.6L 4-cylinder engine with 115 hp and 105 lb-ft of torque
- 1.8L 4-cylinder engine with 125 hp and 117 lb-ft of torque
- 2.0L 4-cylinder engine with 158 hp and 130 lb-ft of torque
- 5-speed manual transmission
- 4-speed automatic transmission

Drivers appreciate the Celica's sharp handling, comfortable interior, and excellent fuel economy. However, like any vehicle, it has certain areas where issues may arise.

**Engine**

**1.6L 4-Cylinder Engine**
- **Oil Leaks:** Description/Symptoms: Engine leaks oil from the valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket, or rear main seal, resulting in oil spots under the vehicle. Solution: Replace the faulty gaskets or seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

- **Timing Chain Rattle:** Description/Symptoms: The timing chain becomes loose or worn, causing a rattling noise upon acceleration or cold starts. Solution: Replace the timing chain and related components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1200+

**1.8L 4-Cylinder Engine**
- **No Issues Found**

**2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine**
- **Head Gasket Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The head gasket fails, causing coolant and oil to mix, leading to overheating and engine damage. Solution: Replace the head gasket and resurface the cylinder heads. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500+

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**
- **Gear Grinding:** Description/Symptoms: Gears grind when shifting, making it difficult to engage or disengage gears. Solution: Replace the worn or damaged gears, synchros, or shift forks. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1000+

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**
- **Shift Solenoid Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Shift solenoids malfunction, causing delayed or erratic gear shifts. Solution: Replace the faulty shift solenoids and related electrical components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+

**Suspension**

- **Strut Mount Bearing Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Front strut mount bearings wear out, causing a clunking noise over bumps and a loss of steering stability. Solution: Replace the strut mount bearings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

- **Rear Sway Bar Links:** Description/Symptoms: Rear sway bar links become loose or worn, causing a rattling noise in the rear end and reduced cornering stability. Solution: Replace the sway bar links. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Electrical**

- **Alternator Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The alternator fails to charge the battery, causing electrical problems such as dim lights, difficulty starting, or engine stalling. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $900+

- **Starter Solenoid Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The starter solenoid fails, preventing the starter from engaging or disengaging the engine. Solution: Replace the starter solenoid or the entire starter motor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+

**Exterior**

- **Door Lock Actuator Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Door lock actuators fail to open or close the doors electronically, requiring manual operation. Solution: Replace the faulty actuators. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+

**Interior**

- **Dashboard Gauge Cluster Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Dashboard gauges stop working or fluctuate erratically, providing inaccurate readings for fuel, speed, RPM, etc. Solution: Replace the faulty gauge cluster or repair the electrical connections. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+

- **Air Conditioner Blower Motor Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The air conditioner blower motor fails, reducing or eliminating airflow from the vents. Solution: Replace the blower motor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Summary**

Overall, the 1992 Toyota Celica is a reliable and enjoyable vehicle. However, some issues to be aware of include oil leaks, timing chain rattle, head gasket failure, gear grinding, shift solenoid failure, and various electrical and interior component failures. The estimated cost of repairs for these issues can range from moderate to expensive, but many can be prevented with regular maintenance and care.
