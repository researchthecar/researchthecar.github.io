**Introduction:**

The 2004 Toyota Celica is a sporty coupe that was praised for its handling, performance, and affordability. It was available with a choice of a 140-horsepower 1.8-liter engine or a 180-horsepower 1.8-liter engine paired with either a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission. Despite its positive attributes, the Celica also had some issues that are worth noting.

**Engine**

* **1.8-liter Engine:**

**Oil Consumption:**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
Solution: Replace piston rings and valve stem seals.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

**Intake Valve Carbon Buildup:**
Description/Symptoms: Carbon buildup on the intake valves can reduce engine power and increase fuel consumption.
Solution: Remove the intake manifold and clean the valve deposits.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

* **1.8-liter VTLi Engine:**
**Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner may fail, causing a rattling noise and potential engine damage.
Solution: Replace the timing chain and tensioner.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
Recalls: TSB 05-034-10

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Manual Transmission:**
**Grinding Gears:**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, particularly between 2nd and 3rd gear.
Solution: Replace the synchronizers in the transmission.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200

* **4-Speed Automatic Transmission:**
 **Slipping:**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip or shudder during gear changes.
Solution: Overhaul the transmission or replace the torque converter.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500

**Suspension**

**Strut Mount Bearing Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Worn strut mount bearings can cause clunking or knocking noises when turning or going over bumps.
Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600

**Electrical**

**Alternator Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: The alternator may fail, leading to electrical system problems and battery drainage.
Solution: Replace the alternator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
Recalls: N/A

**Exterior**

**Trunk Rust:**
Description/Symptoms: The trunk area can experience premature rusting, especially near the spoiler and taillights.
Solution: Repair and repaint the affected areas.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Interior**

**Climate Control Actuator Malfunction:**
Description/Symptoms: The climate control actuator may fail, resulting in malfunctioning air conditioning or heating.
Solution: Replace the climate control actuator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
Recalls: N/A

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2004 Toyota Celica is a reliable and engaging vehicle, but it has some potential issues that buyers should be aware of. The engine oil consumption and timing chain tensioner failure are the most significant concerns, as they can lead to costly repairs. However, these issues are not widespread, and many Celica owners enjoy trouble-free ownership for years to come.
