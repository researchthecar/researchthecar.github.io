**Introduction:**

The 2019 Toyota Sequoia is a full-size SUV available with two engine and transmission options. The first is a 5.7L V8 engine paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission, producing 381 horsepower and 401 lb-ft of torque. The second option is a 5.7L V8 engine paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission, producing 381 horsepower and 401 lb-ft of torque. Popular features of the Sequoia include its spacious interior, powerful engine, and towing capacity. Common complaints about the Sequoia include its outdated interior design, poor fuel economy, and lack of advanced safety features.

**Engine**

**5.7L V8 with 6-speed Automatic Transmission**

**Valve Spring Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Premature wear on the valve springs can lead to valve float at high RPMs, causing a loss of power and potentially catastrophic engine failure.
Solution: Replace the faulty valve springs with upgraded or revised versions.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $3000+
Recalls: None Found

**5.7L V8 with 8-speed Automatic Transmission**

**No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**6-speed Automatic Transmission**

**Harsh Shifting**
Description/Symptoms: Transmission may exhibit rough or delayed shifts, especially when cold or under heavy load.
Solution: Update the transmission software or replace the valve body.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
Recalls: None Found

**8-speed Automatic Transmission**

**No Issues Found**

**Summary:**

The 2019 Toyota Sequoia experiences a range of issues across various components. Engine issues include valve spring failure on the 5.7L V8 with 6-speed automatic transmission, while transmission issues involve harsh shifting on the same transmission option. The electrical system may experience fuel pump failure, while the interior can suffer from HVAC control unit malfunctions. Overall, the severity and cost of these issues vary, and it is important for owners to be aware of potential problems and address them promptly to maintain the reliability and performance of their vehicles.
