**Introduction:**

The 2017 Toyota Sequoia is a full-size SUV praised for its spacious interior, reliable performance, and impressive towing capacity. It boasts a standard 5.7L V8 engine mated to a six-speed automatic transmission, generating 381 horsepower and 401 lb-ft of torque.

**Engine**

**5.7L V8 Engine**

* **Oil Consumption:** Excessive oil consumption due to piston ring failure. Symptoms include low oil levels, engine knocking, and increased exhaust smoke. Solution: Engine rebuild or replacement. Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000+
* **VVT-i Sensor Failure:** Malfunctioning variable valve timing (VVT-i) sensor leads to performance issues, reduced fuel efficiency, and Check Engine light. Solution: Sensor replacement. Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Slipping Gears:** Gears slipping or refusing to engage, particularly when shifting from park to drive or drive to reverse. Solution: Transmission repair or rebuild. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
* **Shift Delay:** Delayed or hesitant shifting between gears, accompanied by a slipping sensation. Solution: Transmission fluid replacement, solenoid repair, or transmission rebuild. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,500+

**Suspension**

* **Rear Leaf Spring Sag:** Premature sagging of rear leaf springs, resulting in reduced load-carrying capacity and handling issues. Solution: Spring replacement. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Strut Failure:** Worn or damaged front struts cause clunking noises, poor ride quality, and premature tire wear. Solution: Strut replacement. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Premature failure of alternator, leading to battery drainage and electrical system issues. Solution: Alternator replacement. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Battery Drain:** Battery discharges prematurely due to parasitic electrical draw when the vehicle is turned off. Solution: Identify and fix the source of the draw. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Loose Exterior Trim:** Certain exterior trim pieces may become loose or detach, creating wind noise and aesthetic issues. Solution: Reattaching or replacing the trim. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattle:** Excessive rattling or squeaking noises coming from the dashboard area. Solution: Identifying and fixing loose or misaligned components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
* **Center Console Leak:** Water leaking from the center console during heavy rain or car washes. Solution: Fixing the seal around the console. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Summary:**

While the 2017 Toyota Sequoia is generally reliable, owners have encountered some issues related to oil consumption, transmission problems, and electrical system failures. The severity and cost of these issues can vary significantly, with some requiring major repairs, while others can be resolved with relatively inexpensive fixes. It's important for potential buyers to be aware of these potential issues and factor them into their purchase decision.
