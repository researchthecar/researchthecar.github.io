# 2015 Toyota Sequoia

### Overview
The 2015 Toyota Sequoia is a full-size SUV known for its robust build and family-friendly features. It typically offers strong reliability, a hallmark of Toyota vehicles. This model year benefits from a proven powertrain and a durable chassis, making it a solid choice for those needing substantial towing and passenger capacity. While generally dependable, like any vehicle, it can encounter specific issues, particularly as it ages. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas and their associated repair costs for various components.

### Summary Verdict
The 2015 Toyota Sequoia demonstrates strong overall reliability, particularly in its powertrain and chassis components. The 5.7L V8 engine and 6-speed automatic transmission are known for their durability and longevity, with no significant widespread issues reported. The primary areas where minor concerns have been noted are within the electrical system, specifically occasional infotainment system glitches and potential battery drain issues. These are generally manageable and not indicative of systemic failures. Exterior and interior components are also typically robust. For prospective buyers, focusing on a thorough pre-purchase inspection, especially for the electrical system, is advisable, but the 2015 Sequoia remains a highly reliable choice in the full-size SUV segment.
