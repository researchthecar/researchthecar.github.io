# 2019 Toyota Sequoia Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 Toyota Sequoia, buyers can generally expect the robust build quality and longevity associated with the brand's traditional full-size platform. While owner feedback submitted to federal safety regulators highlights a few isolated mechanical and body concerns, the vehicle remains a popular choice for families needing substantial towing capacity and three-row seating.

---

## Engine

### 5.7L V8 (381 hp / 401 lb-ft)

The 5.7-liter V8 engine produces 381 horsepower and 401 lb-ft of torque, paired exclusively with a six-speed automatic transmission. This powertrain is widely regarded as durable and reliable, though prospective buyers should verify that open fuel system campaigns have been addressed.

**Fuel Pump Concern**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported receiving interim recall notices regarding potential fuel pump failures, though actual operational failures vary.
- **Solution:** Check manufacturer records to see if the official recall remedy has been performed by a certified dealer.

**Fuel Door Hinge Binding**
- **Symptoms:** The fuel filler door hinge, pin, or door can become bent or misaligned, preventing proper opening and closing.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the damaged fuel door hinge assembly as needed.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic

The conventional six-speed automatic transmission distributes power reliably to either the rear wheels or the four-wheel-drive system. It generally exhibits strong long-term durability without widespread recurring mechanical complaints.

## Suspension

### Air Suspension System

The adjustable air suspension system on higher trims provides a plush ride quality but can occasionally develop ride-height discrepancies requiring sensor or air spring adjustments.

**Rear Ride Height Lean**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may exhibit an uneven stance, with one side sitting significantly lower than the other due to air suspension imbalance or component wear.
- **Solution:** Inspect the air springs, height sensors, and control lines, and calibrate or replace faulty components.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The standard braking hardware handles stopping duties adequately for a heavy full-size SUV, provided that parking brake cables and assemblies are routinely inspected and adjusted.

**Parking Brake Adjustment Loss**
- **Symptoms:** The parking brake may lose holding power, particularly after heavy use on steep inclines or when towing trailers.
- **Solution:** Inspect and readjust the parking brake cable mechanism and shoes to restore proper tension and grip.

## Steering

### Power Steering Rack

The steering system provides responsive handling for a large SUV, though high-mileage examples should be inspected for potential fluid leaks around the steering rack.

**Steering Rack Boot Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Power steering fluid leaks may develop around the rack and pinion boots, often noticed during routine vehicle inspections.
- **Solution:** Replace the leaking steering rack assembly or worn seals to prevent total loss of power steering assistance.
- **Cost:** $2000

## Exterior

### Moonroof

Exterior panels and trim are generally well-assembled, though rare incidents involving stress fractures on glass moonroofs have been reported by owners.

**Moonroof Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The glass panel on the moonroof may spontaneously fracture or explode while driving at highway speeds without external impact.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged moonroof glass assembly and check the frame alignment to ensure proper stress distribution.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Occupant protection features are generally robust, but buyers should carefully inspect all seatbelt anchors—especially in the third row—to ensure they remain securely fastened.

**Third-Row Seatbelt Anchor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Third-row seatbelts can unexpectedly detach or pull away from their lower mounting bolts during vehicle operation.
- **Solution:** Inspect seatbelt mounting hardware for secure torque specifications and replace damaged anchor brackets immediately.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Toyota Sequoia is a dependable, rugged full-size SUV powered by a proven V8 engine and traditional drivetrain. While buyers should check for minor issues such as steering rack leaks, air suspension unevenness, and open recall campaigns, a thorough pre-purchase inspection will help ensure a reliable ownership experience.
