# 2020 Toyota Sequoia Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a used 2020 Toyota Sequoia means looking at one of the final years of the vehicle's long-running second generation. Known for its old-school body-on-frame toughness and robust V8 capability, this full-size SUV is built to last. While consumer complaint data for this model year is very sparse, prospective buyers should still inspect suspension and powertrain components during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 5.7L V8 3UR-FE

The 5.7-liter V8 engine produces 381 horsepower and 401 lb-ft of torque, providing strong towing capacity and steady performance. It is widely regarded as a highly durable and dependable powerplant in the Toyota lineup.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission comes paired with the standard V8 engine across rear-wheel and four-wheel-drive configurations. It generally offers smooth operation and a solid track record for mechanical longevity.

## Suspension

### Front Struts

Handles the vehicle's heavy front weight and road impacts.

**Front Strut Grinding and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The front suspension may produce grinding or rumbling noises, indicating potential component wear or failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect the front struts for leaks or structural wear and replace them if necessary.

## Steering

### Steering and Drivetrain

Steering and drivetrain systems responsible for directional control and power delivery.

**Intermittent Acceleration Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may temporarily fail to accelerate as expected while cruising at highway speeds, returning to normal operation after a short duration.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician scan for powertrain control module codes and check throttle body and fuel system operation.

## Exterior

### Moonroof

Factory glass roof panel and surrounding seals.

**Moonroof Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The moonroof glass may fracture or explode unexpectedly while driving without prior warning or visible impact.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged moonroof glass assembly and inspect the surrounding weather stripping and frame for stress.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V682000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sequoia&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel pump fails, the engine can stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly with an improved one, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21E103000: Steering:Hydraulic Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sequoia&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** An oil leak may cause a sudden loss of power steering assist, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the power steering gear assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Toyota Sequoia maintains a strong reputation for durability, backed by minimal owner complaints in early data pools. Buyers should perform standard used-car checks, paying close attention to front suspension components and verifying that all electronics and glass panels are in proper condition.
