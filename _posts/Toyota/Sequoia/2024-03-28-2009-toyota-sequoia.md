**Introduction**

The 2009 Toyota Sequoia is a full-size SUV known for its spacious interior, powerful engine, and off-road capabilities. It comes with a standard 4.7L V8 engine that produces 276 horsepower and 318 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**5.7L V8 with 6-Speed Automatic Transmission**
* **Loud knocking noise:** Worn or damaged engine bearings can cause a loud knocking noise. Solution involves replacing the bearings, costing around $1,000 - $2,000+.
* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** A failing intake manifold gasket can cause a vacuum leak and make the engine run rough. The gasket needs to be replaced, costing around $400 - $700+.
* **Throttle position sensor (TPS) failure:** A faulty TPS can cause the engine to run erratically or stall. The sensor needs to be replaced, costing around $200 - $400+.

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic Transmission**
* **TCC (Torque Converter Clutch) failure:** The TCC can fail, causing vibrations and slipping. The TCC needs to be replaced, costing around $1,000 - $1,500+.
* **Transmission fluid leak:** A leak in the transmission fluid line can cause the transmission to malfunction. The line needs to be replaced, costing around $200 - $500+.

**Suspension**

* **Front lower control arm bushings wear out prematurely:** The bushings wear out, causing a clunking noise over bumps. Solution involves replacing the bushings, costing around $300 - $500+.
* **Rear leaf springs sag:** The leaf springs can sag over time, reducing the vehicle's rear height and causing a rough ride. Solution involves replacing the leaf springs, costing around $700 - $1,200+.

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** A parasitic draw on the battery can drain it overnight. Solution involves finding and fixing the draw, costing around $100 - $200+.
* **Alternator failure:** A faulty alternator can cause the battery to not charge properly. The alternator needs to be replaced, costing around $500 - $800+.

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate handle breaks:** The plastic tailgate handle can break, making it difficult to open the tailgate. Solution involves replacing the handle, costing around $100 - $200+.

**Interior**

* **Climate control fan motor failure:** The fan motor for the climate control can fail, preventing air from blowing through the vents. The motor needs to be replaced, costing around $200 - $400+.
* **Door lock actuator failure:** A faulty door lock actuator can prevent the door from locking or unlocking properly. The actuator needs to be replaced, costing around $150 - $300+.

**Summary**

The 2009 Toyota Sequoia has some potential issues, particularly with the engine and transmission. However, the severity of the issues is generally moderate, and the repair costs are within the average range for most vehicles. Overall, the Sequoia is a reliable SUV that owners generally appreciate for its spaciousness and off-road capabilities.
