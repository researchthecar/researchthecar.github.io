# 2023 Toyota Sequoia

### Overview
The 2023 Toyota Sequoia, a full-size SUV, is built on the robust TNGA-F platform, shared with other Toyota heavy-duty vehicles. It features a standard 3.5L V6 twin-turbo hybrid powertrain, offering a blend of power and efficiency. Initial reliability data suggests a strong performance, characteristic of Toyota's reputation. The vehicle is designed for durability and capability, with a focus on advanced technology and comfort. While new to the market in its current generation, the underlying components and engineering principles are well-established, pointing towards a positive reliability outlook for most systems. Owners should expect a dependable experience with minimal widespread mechanical concerns.

### Summary Verdict
The 2023 Toyota Sequoia demonstrates strong initial reliability, particularly with its innovative i-FORCE MAX hybrid powertrain and robust 10-speed automatic transmission. Major mechanical components like the engine and transmission show no widespread issues. The suspension system is also performing well. Electrical systems, specifically the infotainment, may experience minor software glitches, and there's a possibility of early 12V battery failure, though these are not endemic. Exterior and interior components are generally holding up well. Overall, the Sequoia is a reliable choice for a full-size SUV, with most potential issues being minor and easily resolvable.
