**2021 Toyota Sequoia**

The 2021 Toyota Sequoia is a full-size SUV that seats up to eight passengers. It is available with a 5.7-liter V8 engine that produces 381 horsepower and 401 lb-ft of torque, paired with a six-speed automatic transmission. Other engine and transmission options are not available.

Some of the pros of the 2021 Toyota Sequoia include its spacious interior, powerful engine, and off-road capabilities. However, it also has some cons, such as its poor fuel economy, outdated infotainment system, and limited cargo space.

## **Engine**

**5.7L V8 Engine**

- **Loud lifter noise:** The lifters in the engine can become noisy, causing a ticking or tapping sound.
    - Solution: Replace the lifters.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

- **Oil leaks:** The engine can develop oil leaks, particularly from the valve covers or oil pan gasket.
    - Solution: Replace the leaking gasket or seal.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found

## **Transmission**

**6-speed Automatic Transmission**

- **Shifting issues:** The transmission may hesitate or jerk when shifting gears.
    - Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

## **Suspension**

- **Rear suspension noise:** The rear suspension can make a clunking or rattling noise, especially over uneven roads.
    - Solution: Replace the rear suspension bushings or shock absorbers.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

## **Electrical**

- **Battery drain:** The battery can drain prematurely, even when the vehicle is not in use.
    - Solution: Replace the battery or check for parasitic drains on the electrical system.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found

- **Headlight condensation:** Moisture can accumulate inside the headlight assemblies, causing condensation or fogging.
    - Solution: Replace the faulty headlights.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

## **Exterior**

- **Roof rack noise:** The roof rack can make a whistling or howling noise when driving at highway speeds.
    - Solution: Install wind deflectors or adjust the rack mounting bolts.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    - Recalls: None Found

## **Interior**

- **Climate control issues:** The climate control system may not function properly, causing uneven temperatures in the cabin.
    - Solution: Replace the blend door actuator or temperature sensor.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found

- **Infotainment system glitches:** The infotainment system may experience glitches, such as freezing or unresponsive touchscreen.
    - Solution: Update the system software or replace the infotainment unit.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2021 Toyota Sequoia is a reliable SUV with some minor issues that are relatively inexpensive to fix. The most common issues are related to the engine, transmission, and electrical system. Although there are no major recalls for this model year, owners should be aware of these potential problems and have them addressed as needed.
