# 2012 Toyota Sequoia

### Overview
The 2012 Toyota Sequoia is a full-size SUV known for its robust construction and capability, often favored for towing and family hauling. Reliability is generally a strong suit for Toyota vehicles, and the Sequoia typically follows this trend. While it's a large and complex vehicle, most owners report a positive ownership experience with fewer significant mechanical or electrical gremlins compared to some competitors. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen more attention from owners and mechanics over time. This analysis focuses on documented issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective buyers or current owners.

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Toyota Sequoia stands out as a highly reliable large SUV. Its V8 engines and robust transmission are generally trouble-free, and major issues are uncommon. The primary areas that may require attention are some electrical components, specifically the infotainment system and potential battery drain issues, which are not uncommon for vehicles of this age and complexity. Exterior and interior components are typically durable. Overall, the Sequoia offers a dependable ownership experience, with most reported problems being minor and manageable. Routine maintenance is key to ensuring its long-term performance.
