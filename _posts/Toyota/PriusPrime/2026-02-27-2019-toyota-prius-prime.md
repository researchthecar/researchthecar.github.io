# 2019 Toyota Prius Prime Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Toyota Prius Prime is a plug-in hybrid known for its impressive fuel efficiency and eco-friendly design. While overall powertrain reliability remains a strong point for Toyota, used-car buyers should review owner-reported concerns regarding suspension, brakes, and electrical accessories when evaluating a pre-owned model.

---

## Engine

### 1.8L 4-Cylinder Hybrid (2ZR-FXE)

This 1.8-liter four-cylinder Atkinson-cycle engine works in tandem with the electric drive system to produce a net 121 horsepower. It is exceptionally durable and forms the highly reliable heart of the Prius Prime hybrid lineup.

## Transmission

### Electronically Controlled Continuously Variable Transmission (ECVT)

The planetary-gear electronic continuously variable transmission routes power seamlessly between the gasoline engine and electric motors. It has a long-standing reputation for smooth operation and strong mechanical durability.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The regenerative braking system blends seamlessly with traditional friction brakes, though isolated heat-related concerns have been reported by owners.

**Rotor Heat and Squealing**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported unusual squealing noises and rotor heat issues requiring dealership inspection.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the brake pads, rotors, and calipers for excessive heat buildup or premature wear.

## Electrical

### Electrical System and Door Locks

Electrical accessories generally function reliably, though isolated electronic glitches have been documented by early owners.

**Unexpected Door Locking**
- **Symptoms:** Doors have been reported to lock automatically on their own while the vehicle is plugged in and charging.
- **Solution:** Have the body control module and key fob software inspected by a dealer if erratic locking behavior occurs.

## Interior

### Tires and Wheels

Suspension components ride comfortably, but factory tire durability has drawn owner complaints regarding sudden deflation.

**Premature Tire Failure and Tread Separation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have experienced sudden tire blowouts, rapid pressure loss, or tread separation from factory-equipped tires without early dashboard warnings.
- **Solution:** Inspect tire tread depth and manufacture dates regularly, and replace worn tires promptly.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V544000: Electronic Stability Control (Esc)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Prius+Prime&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 126, "Electronic Stability Control Systems." Deactivation of the vehicle stability control system or a loss of braking assist increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace the brake booster pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Toyota Prius Prime offers stellar efficiency and solid mechanical engineering, making it a smart pick for fuel-conscious buyers. Prospective owners should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the brakes, tires, and electrical accessories to ensure everything operates smoothly.
