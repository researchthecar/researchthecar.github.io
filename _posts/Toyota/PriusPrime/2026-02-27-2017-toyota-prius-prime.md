# 2017 Toyota Prius Prime Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Toyota Prius Prime stands out as a highly efficient plug-in hybrid that offers impressive fuel economy and advanced tech features. While Toyota enjoys a strong reputation for overall reliability, owners have reported a handful of recurring mechanical and electrical concerns that prospective buyers should keep in mind during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 1.8L 4-Cylinder Hybrid Engine

Features a 1.8-liter 4-cylinder engine paired with an electric motor system, producing a combined 121 horsepower. It is generally robust and efficient, though some owners have reported intermittent drivability issues.

**Engine Stalling and Acceleration Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note that the vehicle can occasionally stall or experience a significant delay in acceleration when transitioning from electric power to gas, or after coming to a full stop.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the hybrid propulsion system control modules and software updates.

**Coolant Leaks and Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Unexplained coolant loss, engine overheating, or white smoke from the exhaust pipe.
- **Solution:** Inspect the engine cooling system and head gasket for leaks or structural failure.

## Transmission

### Electronically Controlled Continuously Variable Transmission (eCVT)

An advanced planetary-gear eCVT that seamlessly blends electric motor output with gasoline engine power. It is typically long-lasting and requires minimal maintenance.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Regenerative braking system that captures energy during deceleration while providing standard friction braking stopping power.

**Brake Noise and Premature Rotor Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual clicking or ringing noises during deceleration, accompanied by grooved or prematurely worn brake rotors and pads.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads and rotors for uneven wear and replace components as necessary.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and 12V Battery

Manages cabin electronics, infotainment displays, and the auxiliary 12V power system.

**Touchscreen Freezing and 12V Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The large infotainment touchscreen may randomly shut down and reboot, or the 12V starter battery may fail prematurely.
- **Solution:** Test the 12V battery health and check for available software updates for the infotainment system.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Glass

Covers exterior glass, lighting, and body panels.

**Spontaneous Windshield Cracks**
- **Symptoms:** Windshields developing long cracks stretching from the bottom or edges without any clear impact from rocks or road debris.
- **Solution:** Inspect the windshield thoroughly for stress cracks and replace the glass if compromised.

## Safety

### Advanced Driver Assistance and Airbags

Includes standard safety features, collision avoidance technology, and airbag deployment networks.

**Unintended Automatic Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The automatic emergency braking system may engage suddenly and forcefully without an apparent obstacle or collision warning.
- **Solution:** Have dealership technicians inspect sensor alignment and check for software updates related to the collision avoidance system.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Toyota Prius Prime is a compelling choice for buyers seeking exceptional fuel economy and plug-in capability. While it maintains Toyota's strong baseline reputation for durability, used car shoppers should thoroughly check for potential electrical glitches, brake wear, and proper hybrid system transition behavior during a test drive.
