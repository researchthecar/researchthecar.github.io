The 2015 Toyota Prius V is a hybrid vehicle with a 1.8L inline-4 engine and a Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT). It produces 134 horsepower and 105 lb-ft of torque. The Prius V is known for its fuel efficiency, spacious interior, and reliability. However, it has also been reported to have some issues.

**Engine**

* **1.8L Inline-4**

    * **Oil Consumption**
        * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace piston rings and oil seals.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 19V325000
* **EGR System Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Failure of the Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) system, causing reduced engine power and increased emissions.
        * Solution: Replace EGR valve.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **CVT**

    * **Shuddering**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission shuddering or vibration during acceleration or deceleration.
        * Solution: Update transmission software or replace transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $5,000+
        * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 20V200000

**Suspension**

* **Lower Ball Joint Wear**
        * Description/Symptoms: Premature wear of lower ball joints, leading to reduced steering stability and potential wheel separation.
        * Solution: Replace ball joints.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **12V Battery Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Premature failure of 12V battery, causing difficulty starting the vehicle.
        * Solution: Replace battery.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rear Spoiler Paint Peeling**
        * Description/Symptoms: Paint peeling on the rear spoiler, affecting the vehicle's appearance.
        * Solution: Repaint spoiler.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Creaking**
        * Description/Symptoms: Creaking or rattling noise from the dashboard, particularly during temperature changes.
        * Solution: Lubricate or replace dashboard components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Climate Control Malfunction**
        * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning climate control system, causing issues with heating or cooling the vehicle.
        * Solution: Repair or replace climate control module.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2015 Toyota Prius V is a reliable vehicle with good fuel economy and a comfortable interior. However, it has been reported to have some issues, including oil consumption, EGR system failure, and transmission shuddering. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but they can be costly to repair. It is recommended to have the vehicle inspected by a qualified mechanic before purchasing to identify any potential problems.
