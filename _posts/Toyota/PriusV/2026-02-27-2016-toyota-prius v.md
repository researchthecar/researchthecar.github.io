# 2016 Toyota Prius V

### Overview
The 2016 Toyota Prius V is a hybrid wagon known for its exceptional fuel efficiency and practicality. As a model within the established Prius lineage, it generally inherits a reputation for strong reliability, particularly in its powertrain and core hybrid systems. Owners typically report satisfaction with its low running costs and dependable performance for daily commuting and family use. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the Prius V's design prioritizes longevity and efficiency, making it a solid choice for those seeking a reliable and economical vehicle. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond standard maintenance.

## Electrical

### Hybrid Battery System
**Reduced Hybrid Battery Performance**
- **Symptoms:** Decreased fuel economy, reduced electric-only driving range, warning lights related to hybrid system.
- **Solution:** Hybrid battery pack replacement.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $4,000


### 12V Auxiliary Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the gasoline engine, electrical system malfunctions (e.g., infotainment, lights), warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the 12V auxiliary battery.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Toyota Prius V demonstrates strong overall reliability, consistent with Toyota's reputation. The hybrid powertrain is exceptionally robust, with the primary potential concern being the long-term health of the hybrid battery pack, which can eventually require replacement. The 12V auxiliary battery is also a component that may need attention over time. Electrical gremlins in the infotainment system are occasionally reported but are generally minor. Other areas like the engine, transmission, suspension, and exterior components show very few documented widespread issues, indicating a well-built and durable vehicle for its model year.
