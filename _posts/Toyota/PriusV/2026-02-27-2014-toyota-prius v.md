# 2014 Toyota Prius V

### Overview
The 2014 Toyota Prius V is a hybrid wagon known for its exceptional fuel efficiency and practicality. As a member of the renowned Prius family, it generally inherits a strong reputation for reliability, particularly in its powertrain. This model combines a gasoline engine with electric motors, offering a smooth and quiet driving experience. Owners often praise its spacious interior and versatile cargo capacity, making it a popular choice for families and those needing extra utility. While the hybrid system is a key feature, its overall mechanical and electrical components are designed for longevity, reflecting Toyota's commitment to durable vehicle engineering. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond standard maintenance.

## Electrical

### Hybrid Battery System
**Reduced Hybrid Battery Capacity**
- **Symptoms:** Decreased fuel economy, reduced electric-only driving range, dashboard warning lights (e.g., hybrid system warning).
- **Solution:** Hybrid battery pack replacement.
- **Cost:** $2,500 - $4,000


### Inverter/Converter
**Inverter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may enter 'limp mode', loss of power, hybrid system warning lights, potential for the vehicle to not start.
- **Solution:** Inverter/converter replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Toyota Prius V demonstrates strong overall reliability, consistent with Toyota's reputation. The hybrid powertrain, including the 1.8L engine and ECVT transmission, is exceptionally robust with no widespread common failures reported. The primary areas of concern, though not universally experienced, revolve around the electrical system, specifically the hybrid battery pack and the inverter/converter, which can be costly to replace if they fail. These components are subject to degradation over time and mileage. Other categories like suspension, exterior, and interior show no significant widespread issues, indicating good build quality and component durability for this model year.
