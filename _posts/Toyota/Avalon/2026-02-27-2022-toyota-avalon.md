# 2022 Toyota Avalon Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2022 Toyota Avalon, buyers will find a refined, spacious sedan that generally commands a strong reputation for comfort and longevity. Because this model year represents the final stretch of the Avalon's production run, it benefits from years of powertrain refinement. While overall owner satisfaction remains high, prospective buyers should still inspect specific areas like vehicle electronics and standard maintenance points during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6

The 3.5-liter V6 engine produces approximately 301 horsepower and 267 lb-ft of torque, offering smooth and confident acceleration. It is broadly considered a dependable powerplant, though isolated gasket leaks require careful inspection.

**Head Gasket Oil Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Owners may notice an oil leak originating from where the bank 1 cylinder head gasket meets the timing cover.
- **Solution:** Requires replacing the affected head gasket, which involves significant labor to access.
- **Cost:** $6,500

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Hybrid

The 2.5-liter hybrid four-cylinder engine paired with electric motors delivers around 215 total system horsepower with exceptional fuel efficiency. This powertrain is historically robust and well-suited for daily commuting.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic

The eight-speed automatic transmission delivers power smoothly to the front wheels with well-timed gear shifts. It generally exhibits solid long-term durability with standard fluid maintenance.

### Electronically Controlled Continuously Variable Transmission (ECVT)

The dedicated hybrid ECVT provides seamless power delivery optimized for efficiency across varied driving conditions. It is a mature, low-maintenance design known for reliable operation.

## Suspension

### Tires and Sidewalls

Suspension components provide a plush, comfortable ride quality, but original equipment tires should be closely monitored for wear and sudden failure risks.

**Unexpected Tire Blowout and Sidewall Separation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience sudden tire deflation or outer sidewall separation while driving at highway speeds, sometimes without prior low-pressure warnings.
- **Solution:** Inspect tires regularly for wear and structural integrity, and replace damaged or defective tires immediately.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Charging Ports

Electrical accessories and cabin technology generally function well, though screen blackouts and accessory port failures have been reported by some owners.

**Infotainment Screen Failure and Non-Working Charging Ports**
- **Symptoms:** The central infotainment display may suddenly go black, causing a loss of GPS, audio, and vehicle information, accompanied by inoperable charging ports.
- **Solution:** Often requires a system reset, software update, or potential component replacement if the hardware fails.
- **Cost:** $200

## Exterior

### Glass and Trim

Exterior body panels and lighting fixtures fit tightly, though rare instances of spontaneous glass breakage have been documented by drivers.

**Sudden Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Occupants may hear a loud explosion-like noise while driving, followed by vehicle glass shattering unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Inspect all windows and the windshield for stress cracks or chips, and replace shattered glass promptly.

## Safety

### Seat Belts and Airbags

Safety features and driver-assist suites offer comprehensive protection, though individual harness sensors and seat belt buckles should be checked if warning lights appear.

**Seat Belt Buckle Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** An airbag warning light may illuminate on the dashboard due to a faulty connection or switch inside the seat belt buckle mechanism.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat belt buckle assembly and replace the faulty latch mechanism or sensor as necessary.
- **Cost:** $50 to $250

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Toyota Avalon stands out as a dependable, highly comfortable full-size sedan option for used car buyers. While isolated owner complaints highlight potential concerns like infotainment glitches, tire wear, and rare engine gasket leaks, the vehicle's overall mechanical baseline remains solid. Buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on electronics, the engine bay, and tire condition to ensure a worry-free ownership experience.
