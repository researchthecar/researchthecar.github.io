**Introduction:**

The 2022 Toyota Avalon is a full-size sedan known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and reliable performance. It is available with two engine and transmission options:

- **3.5L V6:** 301 horsepower, 267 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission
- **2.5L Hybrid:** 215 horsepower (combined), paired with an Electronically Controlled Continuously Variable Transmission (ECVT)

**Engine**

**3.5L V6**

- **Oil Consumption:** Excessive oil consumption due to faulty piston rings. Symptoms include low oil levels, blue smoke from the exhaust, and engine damage if left untreated. Solution: Engine rebuild or replacement. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+
- **Timing Chain Noise:** Rattling or grinding noises from the timing chain. Symptoms can vary depending on engine speed. Solution: Timing chain replacement. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

- **Shifting Problems:** Difficulty shifting gears, rough shifts, or hesitation. Symptoms can include delayed engagement, slipping gears, or a loss of power. Solution: Transmission repair or rebuild. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+

**Suspension**

- **Rear Shock Absorbers:** Leaking or malfunctioning shock absorbers. Symptoms include a rough ride, poor handling, and increased braking distance. Solution: Shock absorber replacement. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+

**Electrical**

- **Battery Drain:** Excessive battery drain when the vehicle is parked. Symptoms include a dead battery or difficulty starting the vehicle. Solution: Battery replacement or diagnosis and repair of electrical components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
- **Headlight Malfunction:** Headlights may flicker, dim, or fail completely. Symptoms include poor visibility at night. Solution: Headlight bulb or ballast replacement. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
- **Infotainment System Glitches:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touchscreen, or audio issues. Symptoms can vary depending on the specific issue. Solution: Software updates, module replacement, or navigation head unit replacement. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $2,000+

**Exterior**

- **Paint Peeling:** Peeling or flaking paint on the door handles, mirrors, or roof. Symptoms include a loss of paint adhesion. Solution: Paint touch-up or panel replacement. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,200+
- **Rear Bumper Scratches:** Scratches or scuffs on the rear bumper. Symptoms include a damaged appearance. Solution: Bumper touch-up or replacement. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Interior**

- **Door Panel Buzzing:** Buzzing or rattling noises from the door panels. Symptoms can include annoying vibrations while driving. Solution: Tightening or replacing door panel clips or bolts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
- **Steering Wheel Vibration:** Vibration or shaking felt through the steering wheel. Symptoms can vary depending on speed and road conditions. Solution: Diagnosis and repair of suspension, steering, or balancing issues. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
- **Climate Control Malfunction:** Failure of the heating, ventilation, or air conditioning system. Symptoms can include insufficient heating or cooling, poor airflow, or a malfunctioning compressor. Solution: Compressor replacement, thermostat replacement, or climate control module replacement. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Summary:**

While the 2022 Toyota Avalon is generally a reliable vehicle, it has experienced some issues, particularly with the engine, transmission, and suspension. The severity of the problems can vary, and the cost to fix can range from minor to major. It is important to note that not all Avalons will encounter all of these issues, and regular maintenance and proper care can help extend the life of the vehicle.
