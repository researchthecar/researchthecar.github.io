**Introduction:**
The 2006 Toyota Avalon is a full-size sedan known for its reliability, comfort, and fuel efficiency. It features a 3.5L V6 engine paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission, producing 268 horsepower and 248 lb-ft of torque. The Avalon has received praise for its spacious interior, smooth ride quality, and impressive safety features, but it has also been criticized for its bland styling and lack of performance.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6**

* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** The valve cover gasket may fail, causing an oil leak. Symptoms include an oil leak under the engine or a burning oil smell. The solution is to replace the valve cover gasket, which costs around $150-$250 for parts and labor.

* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** The timing chain tensioner may fail, causing the timing chain to skip or break. Symptoms include a rattling noise, rough running, or a loss of power. The solution is to replace the timing chain tensioner, which costs around $500-$800 for parts and labor.

**Transmission:**

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Shift Solenoid Failure:** The transmission shift solenoid may fail, causing the transmission to shift erratically or not shift at all. Symptoms include harsh shifts, delayed shifts, or a lack of shifting. The solution is to replace the transmission shift solenoid, which costs around $200-$400 for parts and labor.

* **Transmission Fluid Leak:** The transmission fluid lines or seals may leak, causing a loss of transmission fluid. Symptoms include a low transmission fluid level or a leak under the transmission. The solution is to replace the leaking components, which costs around $200-$500 for parts and labor.

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Bearing Failure:** The strut mount bearings may fail, causing a clunking or rattling noise from the front or rear of the vehicle. Symptoms also include unstable handling or a loss of alignment. The solution is to replace the strut mount bearings, which costs around $200-$300 for parts and labor.

* **Control Arm Bushing Wear:** The control arm bushings may wear out, causing a knocking or clunking noise from the front end of the vehicle. Symptoms also include vibrations in the steering wheel or poor handling. The solution is to replace the control arm bushings, which costs around $300-$500 for parts and labor.

**Electrical**

* **Power Window Switch Failure:** The power window switches may fail, causing the windows to stop working. Symptoms include a lack of response from the window switches or intermittent window operation. The solution is to replace the faulty power window switches, which costs around $100-$200 for parts and labor.

* **Battery Discharge:** The battery may discharge prematurely, leaving drivers stranded. Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle or the need to jump-start the battery frequently. The solution is to replace the battery, which costs around $150-$250 for parts and labor, or diagnose any underlying electrical issues causing the discharge.

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Panel Detachment:** The sunroof panel may detach from its tracks, causing it to fall into the car's interior. Symptoms include a rattling noise or the sunroof not opening or closing properly. The solution is to replace the sunroof panel, which costs around $500-$800 for parts and labor.

**Interior**

* **Rear Seatbelt Buckle Corrosion:** The rear seatbelt buckles may corrode, causing them to stick or be difficult to use. Symptoms include difficulty engaging or releasing the seat belt or the buckle becoming stuck. The solution is to replace the faulty seatbelt buckles, which costs around $50-$100 for parts and labor.

* **HVAC Blower Motor Failure:** The HVAC blower motor may fail, causing the heating, ventilation, and air conditioning system to stop working. Symptoms include no airflow from the vents or excessive noise from the blower motor. The solution is to replace the blower motor, which costs around $200-$300 for parts and labor.

**Summary**
Overall, the 2006 Toyota Avalon is a reliable and comfortable vehicle with a few minor issues that can be relatively inexpensive to repair. The most significant issues to watch out for include transmission shift solenoid failure, timing chain tensioner failure, and valve cover gasket leaks. While these issues can be costly to fix, they are not common and are likely to occur only with vehicles that have been neglected or have high mileage.
