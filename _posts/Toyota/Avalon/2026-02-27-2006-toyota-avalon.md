# 2006 Toyota Avalon

### Overview

Owners often praise its longevity and the relatively low cost of maintenance compared to some competitors. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for prospective buyers or current owners.

### Standard Electrical System
**Occasional Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery may discharge prematurely, leading to starting issues. Can be intermittent.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify parasitic draw from accessories or faulty modules. Replacement of faulty component or battery.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500

**Power Window Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Individual power windows may stop working or operate slowly.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the power window motor and regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $250 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2006 Toyota Avalon stands out as a highly reliable vehicle, with its powertrain and core mechanical components demonstrating exceptional durability. The most commonly cited issues are minor electrical gremlins, such as occasional battery drain or power window motor failures, which are generally manageable and not indicative of systemic problems. The suspension and exterior/interior components also hold up well over time. Overall, the 2006 Avalon is a strong contender for those seeking a dependable and cost-effective used sedan, with a low probability of encountering major, expensive repairs beyond standard maintenance.
