# 2021 Toyota Avalon Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Toyota Avalon, buyers will find a spacious and comfortable sedan known for strong reliability overall, though owner reports point to a few specific areas worth inspecting prior to purchase. Prospective owners should review vehicle history records and ensure any relevant safety campaigns have been properly addressed by a certified dealer.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6

This naturally aspirated 3.5-liter V6 engine produces approximately 301 horsepower and 267 lb-ft of torque, offering brisk acceleration for daily driving and highway passing. It is widely regarded as a robust and dependable powerplant with a strong reputation for longevity.

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Hybrid

The 2.5-liter four-cylinder hybrid powertrain pairs electric motors with a gas engine to deliver an efficient total output of 215 horsepower. This setup is favored for its excellent fuel economy and generally dependable mechanical performance.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

This conventional 8-speed automatic transmission comes paired with the V6 engine, offering smooth and responsive shifts. It is generally considered reliable with few widespread mechanical complaints.

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The electronically controlled planetary continuously variable transmission is paired exclusively with the hybrid powertrain. It provides seamless power delivery geared toward optimizing fuel efficiency.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Owner feedback points to occasional mechanical noises during braking, though overall stopping performance remains standard for the segment.

**Braking Thunk Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported a noticeable 'thunk' sound when applying the brakes during normal stopping maneuvers.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the brake calipers, pads, and suspension mounting points to verify components are secure.

## Safety

### Air Bags

Safety equipment generally performs well, though sensor and warning light concerns have been noted by some owners.

**Occupant Classification Sensor Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced intermittent airbag warning lights or issues with the front passenger seat sensor failing to register an occupant properly.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealership to check for open safety recalls or software updates regarding the OCS sensor system.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V734000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Avalon&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Breakaway pins within the steering column, designed to absorb energy and reduce injury during a crash, could have been damaged during production, possibly affecting the performance of the driver's air bag in the event of a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the steering column, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V865000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Avalon&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A short circuit may develop in the Occupant Classification System (OCS) sensor, preventing the front passenger air bag from deploying. An air bag that does not deploy during a crash increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the sensor as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Toyota Avalon remains a solid choice for a used full-size sedan, combining comfort, strong fuel economy options, and traditional Toyota reliability. While minor owner complaints regarding sensor warnings and brake noises exist, a thorough pre-purchase inspection and verification of completed recall service will help ensure a confident buying experience.
