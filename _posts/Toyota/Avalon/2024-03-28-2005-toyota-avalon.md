The 2005 Toyota Avalon is a full-size sedan known for its reliability, comfort, and spacious interior. It is powered by a 3.0-liter V6 engine that produces 210 horsepower and 220 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. Other engine and transmission options include a 3.5-liter V6 with 268 horsepower and 248 lb-ft of torque, mated to a 6-speed automatic transmission.

While the Avalon is generally a well-regarded vehicle, there are some known issues that owners have reported.

**Engine**

* **3.0L V6**
    * **Oil Consumption:** Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace piston rings or perform an engine overhaul.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $6,000+
    * **Timing Belt Failure:** The timing belt can fail prematurely, causing catastrophic engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace the timing belt and related components as per the recommended maintenance schedule.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign ID 03V314000
* **3.5L V6**
    * **Valve Train Noise:** Some owners have reported a ticking noise from the valve train, particularly at cold start-up.
        * Solution: Replace the valve lifters.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * **Spark Plug Failure:** The spark plugs can wear out prematurely, causing misfiring and reduced engine performance.
        * Solution: Replace the spark plugs.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Automatic**
    * **Slipping Gears:** The transmission may slip out of gear, especially when shifting from drive to reverse or vice versa.
        * Solution: Replace the transmission valve body or perform a transmission overhaul.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
* **6-Speed Automatic**
    * **Transmission Shudder:** The transmission may shudder or vibrate during acceleration or shifting.
        * Solution: Replace the torque converter or perform a transmission overhaul.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure:** The strut mounts can wear out, causing a clunking noise when driving over bumps or potholes.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, leading to a loss of electrical power and potential battery failure.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Battery Drain:** The battery may drain prematurely, even when the vehicle is not in use.
    * Solution: Check for parasitic electrical draws and replace the battery if necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
* **Ignition Coil Failure:** The ignition coils can fail, causing misfiring and reduced engine performance.
    * Solution: Replace the ignition coils.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:** Headlights may develop condensation inside the housing, reducing visibility.
    * Solution: Replace the headlight assembly or reseal the existing one.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:** The climate control system may malfunction, failing to heat or cool the cabin effectively.
    * Solution: Replace the climate control module or related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Dashboard Rattle:** The dashboard may rattle or make creaking noises while driving.
    * Solution: Apply sound dampening material or tighten loose components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Summary**

Overall, the 2005 Toyota Avalon is a reliable and well-built vehicle. However, some known issues can occur, particularly with the engine and transmission. The severity and cost of these issues vary depending on the specific problem. It is important to have regular maintenance performed and address any issues promptly to prevent more costly repairs down the road.
