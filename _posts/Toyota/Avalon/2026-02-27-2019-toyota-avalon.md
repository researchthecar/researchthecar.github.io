# 2019 Toyota Avalon Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Toyota Avalon generally delivers the strong reliability and comfort expected from the flagship sedan, but it does present a few notable trouble spots worth checking during a pre-purchase inspection. While the mechanical layout is fundamentally solid, owners have reported concerns ranging from fuel system hiccups and transmission shift behavior to minor electrical and safety module glitches. Prospective buyers should carefully verify powertrain performance and ensure all safety and infotainment systems operate correctly.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6

The 3.5-liter V6 engine produces 301 horsepower and 267 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth, robust acceleration. It is widely considered a highly durable and dependable powerplant when properly maintained.

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Hybrid

The 2.5-liter hybrid powertrain pairs a four-cylinder engine with electric motors for a net output of 215 horsepower. It offers exceptional fuel economy and strong, reliable daily driving characteristics.

### Fuel System

Issues related to fuel delivery and starting performance have been noted by some owners, occasionally leading to unexpected stalls.

**Fuel Pump Failure and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience engine stalling while driving, extended crank times, or failure to start because the fuel line does not maintain proper pressure.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the fuel pump and pressure regulator; replace faulty fuel pump assemblies or lines as necessary.

## Transmission

### Direct Shift 8-Speed Automatic

The 8-speed automatic transmission paired with the V6 provides responsive gearing, though some owners have reported rough shift quality or hesitation at low speeds.

**Erratic Shifting and Whining**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice low-speed gear skipping, rough or hard downshifts, jerking sensations, and occasional high-pitched whining noises.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician inspect the transmission software calibration and mechanical components; transmission replacement may be required in severe cases.

### Electronically Controlled CVT

The hybrid model utilizes an electronic continuously variable transmission that delivers seamless power delivery and strong long-term dependability.

## Electrical

### Data Communication Module (DCM) and Audio

In-cabin electronics and telematics modules can occasionally exhibit connectivity flaws or audio anomalies requiring software resets or hardware replacement.

**DCM Communication Failure and Speaker Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to connect mobile apps or update vehicle software, alongside intermittent high-pitched ear-piercing noises emitting from the speakers.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the faulty Data Communication Module or update system software according to dealership procedures.

### Advanced Driver Assistance Systems

Active safety features enhance overall protection, though sensor modules can occasionally trigger false warnings or require calibration updates.

**Blind Spot and Rear Cross-Traffic Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Recurring warning alerts for blind spot monitoring or rear cross-traffic system malfunctions on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Reprogram or replace the affected sensor modules following manufacturer service bulletin guidelines.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Toyota Avalon stands out as a spacious, refined, and generally dependable used large sedan. While powertrain complaints regarding low-speed transmission shifts and occasional fuel system or electrical glitches do exist, they do not overshadow the vehicle's overall high build quality. Buyers should prioritize a thorough test drive focusing on smooth gear transitions and verify that all electronic safety features function without warning lights.
