The 2022 Toyota Avalon is a full-size sedan known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and reputation for reliability. Engine options include a 3.5L V6 producing 301 horsepower and 267 lb-ft of torque and a 2.5L Hybrid Inline-4 producing 215 net horsepower. The V6 engine is paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. While praised for its smooth ride and comfortable cabin, some owners have noted the Avalon's somewhat uninspired handling and conservative styling as drawbacks. This document outlines some common issues and complaints reported by owners of the 2022 Toyota Avalon.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing excessive oil consumption, requiring them to add oil between scheduled oil changes. Symptoms include low oil level warnings, the need to frequently top off oil, and potentially blue-tinted exhaust smoke.
    *   Solution: A TSB (Technical Service Bulletin) may be available to address this issue, potentially involving updated piston rings or software recalibration. In severe cases, engine repair or replacement might be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electric Motor**
No Issues Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (V6 Models)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing harsh or jerky shifting, particularly between lower gears. Some have also noticed hesitation when accelerating from a stop.
    *   Solution: The issue might stem from software programming or hardware issues. A transmission fluid flush and software update may be able to resolve the issue. More severe cases may require valve body replacement or, in rare instances, a transmission replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Entune Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have complained about the Entune infotainment system experiencing glitches, freezing, and connectivity issues with Apple CarPlay and Android Auto.
    *   Solution: Updating the system software is the first step. If the issue persists, a replacement of the infotainment system may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Backup Camera Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent failure or distorted image from the backup camera.
    *   Solution: Check the camera connections and wiring. A faulty camera module might require replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Paint Quality**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported that the paint seems thinner and more susceptible to chipping than previous models.
    *   Solution: There is no permanent solution outside of having the paint professionally repaired or reapplied.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Buzzing Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rattling or buzzing noises emanating from various areas within the cabin, such as the dashboard or door panels.
    *   Solution: Identifying the source of the noise is crucial. It may involve tightening loose trim pieces, adding padding, or replacing defective components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2022 Toyota Avalon is a generally reliable vehicle, but some owners have reported issues ranging from minor infotainment glitches to more significant engine concerns. The oil consumption issue, while not widespread, can be a costly repair if it necessitates major engine work. Electrical and infotainment problems, though less severe, can be frustrating. Overall, the severity of reported issues varies, and potential buyers should be aware of these common complaints during their pre-purchase inspection.
