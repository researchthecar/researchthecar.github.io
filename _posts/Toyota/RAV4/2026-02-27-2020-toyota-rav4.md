# 2020 Toyota RAV4

### Overview
The 2020 Toyota RAV4 is a popular compact SUV known for its practicality, fuel efficiency, and Toyota's reputation for reliability. This model year offers several powertrain options, including gasoline and hybrid variants, catering to a wide range of consumer needs. Generally, the 2020 RAV4 has demonstrated strong performance with a focus on durability and low ownership costs. While no vehicle is entirely without potential issues, the RAV4 typically ranks high in its segment for dependability, with most reported concerns being minor and easily addressed. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability data, excluding routine maintenance and common wear-and-tear items.

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 Toyota RAV4 generally exhibits excellent reliability, aligning with Toyota's strong reputation. The powertrain options, including the 2.5L gasoline and hybrid variants, are robust and have few documented widespread issues. Minor concerns are primarily related to the infotainment system experiencing occasional glitches and a few reports of premature 12V battery failure, which are not uncommon in modern vehicles. These issues are typically manageable with software updates or battery replacement. Overall, the 2020 RAV4 is a dependable choice for consumers seeking a practical and reliable compact SUV with low expected ownership costs.
