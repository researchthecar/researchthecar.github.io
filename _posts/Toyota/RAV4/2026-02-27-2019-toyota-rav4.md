# 2019 Toyota RAV4 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Toyota RAV4 marked the debut of the vehicle's fifth generation, bringing bolder styling, an updated interior, and improved driving dynamics. While it remains a popular choice on the used market thanks to its strong resale value and updated safety features, this launch year exhibits a few notable mechanical and body hardware patterns that prospective buyers should inspect closely.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder

The 2.5-liter Dynamic Force four-cylinder engine produces approximately 203 horsepower and offers responsive daily driving combined with solid fuel economy. It is generally robust, though early models require attention to specific cooling and fuel system components.

**Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience unexpected fuel delivery issues or difficulty filling the fuel tank completely due to pump and tank design limitations.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel system operation and replace faulty fuel pumps or sender units as required.

**Coolant Bypass Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated warning lights, engine maintenance messages, or coolant leaks that can lead to engine overheating.
- **Solution:** Inspect the coolant bypass valve and replace the unit if leaking or stuck.

## Transmission

### Direct Shift 8-Speed Automatic

The 8-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly in most driving conditions, though some early production units have been reported to exhibit rough shifting or rare premature wear.

**Rough Shifting and Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky gear changes at lower speeds, abrupt downshifts, or catastrophic internal transmission failure resulting in a loss of forward drive.
- **Solution:** Update transmission control modules if applicable, or inspect for mechanical wear requiring transmission rebuild or replacement.

## Exterior

### Liftgate Hinges and Structure

Exterior body hardware is generally well-assembled, though the rear liftgate hinge area is an acknowledged trouble spot on this model year.

**Liftgate Hinge Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rear liftgate hinges cracking, tearing away from the vehicle body, misaligning, and causing water leaks or operational failure of the automated hatch.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear hatch hinges for stress fractures and repair or reinforce mounting points.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety equipment generally meets high crash-test standards, but secondary latch mechanisms should be verified during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Seat Belt Latch and Locking Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Driver or passenger seat belts failing to lock properly or sticking during normal operation.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt assemblies and replace faulty retractors or buckles under warranty or service guidelines.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V682000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=RAV4&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel pump fails, the engine can stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly with an improved one, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V064000: Engine And Engine Cooling](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=RAV4&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A coolant leak can cause the engine to overheat and cause a vehicle stall in conventional gasoline vehicles, increasing the risk of a crash. Additionally, for all involved vehicles, the engine may get damaged mechanically, possibly resulting in an engine oil leak, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the engine including the engine block, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V373000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=RAV4&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A loss of power steering assist could increase steering effort at low vehicle speeds, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the power steering gear box, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V286000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm:Lower Arm](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=RAV4&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If a suspension control arm breaks, it can result in a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace both front lower suspension arms, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V503000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=RAV4&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An incorrect label may lead to unintentionally overloading the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 19V576000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=RAV4&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Due to a damaged electrical connector, the back-up camera may not activate when the vehicle is in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply to Federal Motor Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." If the backup camera system does not activate and the driver does not check his/her surroundings while reversing, there is an increased risk.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the back-up camera system and replace the audio display unit, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V244000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=RAV4&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The text on the load capacity label may become illegible. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." If the driver cannot read the label, they may unknowingly overload the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GST will notify owners, and dealers will provide corrected load carrying capacity modification labels, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Toyota RAV4 is a capable and versatile compact SUV, but as the first year of a redesigned generation, it carries a few distinct mechanical risks. Buyers should pay close attention to transmission shift quality, check for any coolant leaks around the engine bay, and inspect the rear liftgate hinges for stress cracks before finalizing a purchase.
