## 2003 Toyota RAV4

The 2003 Toyota RAV4 is a compact SUV that offers a comfortable and reliable driving experience. It is available with a choice of two engine options: a 2.0-liter inline-four and a 2.4-liter inline-four. Both engines are paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission.

The RAV4 has received praise for its spacious interior, fuel efficiency, and off-road capabilities. However, it has also been criticized for its lack of power and outdated interior design.

## Engine

**2.0-liter Inline-Four**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil to leak. Symptoms include overheating, rough idling, and reduced power. The solution is to replace the gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000.
* **Timing chain tensioner failure:** The timing chain tensioner can fail, leading to a noisy engine and potentially catastrophic engine damage. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine and reduced power. The solution is to replace the timing chain tensioner, timing chain, and related components. Estimated cost to fix: $2,000 - $3,000+.

**2.4-liter Inline-Four**

* **No major issues found.**

## Transmission

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission slipping:** The transmission may slip or hesitate when shifting gears. Symptoms include rough shifting, loss of power, and increased fuel consumption. The solution is to rebuild or replace the transmission. Estimated cost to fix: $1,500 - $3,000+.
* **Torque converter failure:** The torque converter may fail, causing shuddering or vibration when shifting gears. Symptoms include slipping gears, rough shifting, and transmission overheating. The solution is to replace the torque converter. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,000.

## Suspension

* **Strut failure:** The struts may fail, leading to poor handling, excessive bouncing, and uneven tire wear. Symptoms include a clunking noise when hitting bumps, difficulty steering, and a bouncy ride. The solution is to replace the struts. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000 per axle.
* **Ball joint failure:** The ball joints may fail, causing the vehicle to wander or pull to one side. Symptoms include a clunking or grinding noise when turning or braking, and excessive tire wear. The solution is to replace the ball joints. Estimated cost to fix: $300 - $500 per joint.

## Electrical

* **Alternator failure:** The alternator may fail, leading to a loss of electrical power. Symptoms include dimming lights, a weak battery, and difficulty starting the vehicle. The solution is to replace the alternator. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000.
* **Starter failure:** The starter may fail, preventing the vehicle from starting. Symptoms include clicking noises when attempting to start the vehicle, or a complete lack of response. The solution is to replace the starter. Estimated cost to fix: $300 - $500.

## Exterior

* **Fog light condensation:** The fog lights may develop condensation on the inside, impairing their function. Symptoms include fogged-up fog lights, and decreased visibility in foggy conditions. The solution is to replace the fog lights. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400.
* **Door handle failure:** The exterior door handles may fail, making it difficult or impossible to open the doors. Symptoms include a loose or broken handle, or a handle that does not latch properly. The solution is to replace the door handle. Estimated cost to fix: $100 - $200 per handle.

## Interior

* **Dashboard cracking:** The dashboard may develop cracks, especially in hot or sunny climates. Symptoms include visible cracks on the dashboard, and a potential for dashboard pieces to fall off. The solution is to replace the dashboard. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000.
* **Seat fabric tearing:** The seat fabric may tear, especially on the driver's seat. Symptoms include visible tears in the fabric, and decreased seat comfort. The solution is to repair or replace the seat fabric. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $500.

## Summary

The 2003 Toyota RAV4 is generally a reliable vehicle, but it is not without its issues. The most common problems include engine intake manifold gasket failure, transmission slipping, and suspension strut failure. These issues can be costly to fix, but they are generally not catastrophic. Overall, the RAV4 is a good choice for those seeking a fuel-efficient and reliable SUV.
