## 2005 Toyota RAV4

The 2005 Toyota RAV4 is a compact SUV that is known for its reliability and versatility. It is available with two engine options: a 2.4-liter inline-4 engine that produces 160 horsepower and 161 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.0-liter V6 engine that produces 220 horsepower and 218 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. The RAV4 is also available with either front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive.

People who like the RAV4 appreciate its fuel efficiency, spacious interior, and off-road capabilities. However, some people have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, and other components.

**Engine**

**2.4-liter Inline-4 Engine**

- **Internal Oil Leaks:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, oil leaks from the engine block or cylinder head
    - Solution: Replace the oil pan gasket, reseal the engine block, or replace the cylinder head
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

- **Exhaust Manifold Cracking:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Loud rattling or ticking noise from the engine, decreased engine power
    - Solution: Replace the exhaust manifold
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    - Recalls: None Found

**3.0-liter V6 Engine**

- **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the valve cover
    - Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

- **Shifting Delays:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Transmission hesitates or delays when shifting gears
    - Solution: Replace the transmission fluid and filter, or replace the solenoids
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000
    - Recalls: None Found

- **Transmission Slipping:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Transmission slips or fails to engage gears
    - Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **Strut Bushing Wear:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Knocking or clunking noises from the suspension, unstable handling
    - Solution: Replace the strut bushings
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    - Recalls: None Found

- **Ball Joint Wear:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Loose or rattling front end, excessive tire wear
    - Solution: Replace the ball joints
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Alternator Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging, electrical system failure
    - Solution: Replace the alternator
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200
    - Recalls: None Found

- **Starter Motor Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Vehicle fails to start, starter motor clicks but does not turn over the engine
    - Solution: Replace the starter motor
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
    - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Headlight Condensation:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Condensation or water inside the headlight housing
    - Solution: Replace the headlight assembly
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Power Window Switch Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Power windows fail to operate or operate intermittently
    - Solution: Replace the power window switch
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    - Recalls: None Found

- **Radio Display Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Radio display is blank or unresponsive
    - Solution: Replace the radio unit
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800
    - Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2005 Toyota RAV4 is generally a reliable vehicle, but it has some common issues that owners should be aware of. These issues include engine oil leaks, transmission shifting delays, and suspension component wear. The severity and cost of these issues can vary, but they are generally not major problems.
