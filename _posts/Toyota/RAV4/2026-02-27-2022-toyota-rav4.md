# 2022 Toyota RAV4 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Toyota RAV4 is a popular compact SUV known for its practicality, efficiency, and strong resale value. While Toyota generally enjoys a solid reputation for long-term durability, owner complaints submitted to safety agencies highlight a few recurring problem areas that prospective buyers should inspect closely. Common areas of concern include minor electrical glitches, door lock actuator failures, and isolated reports of engine stalling or hesitation.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2.5-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine produces approximately 203 horsepower and is paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission, offering a balanced blend of daily driveability and fuel economy.

**Engine Hesitation and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light illuminates, accompanied by engine hesitation during acceleration, shaking, or unexpected stalling during or after driving in wet conditions.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic evaluation by a qualified technician; may require addressing specific technical service bulletins or sensor updates.
- **Cost:** $2,549.65

### 2.5L Hybrid 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2.5-liter hybrid powertrain pairs the gasoline engine with electric motors to deliver a net 219 horsepower paired with an electronically controlled continuously variable transmission (e-CVT) and standard electronic all-wheel drive.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed conventional automatic transmission equipped on gas-only models, providing smooth gear changes and reliable highway cruising performance.

### Electronically Controlled CVT (Hybrid)

A planetary-gear electronic continuously variable transmission utilized in hybrid models to seamlessly blend electric and gasoline power sources for maximum efficiency.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System

Electrical complaints for this model year occasionally focus on cabin technology, touchscreen responsiveness, and accessory power consistency.

**Infotainment Screen Reboot**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen display intermittently goes black, flashes, freezes, or continuously reboots while driving.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates or reset the infotainment system; persistent hardware failures may require head unit replacement.

## Exterior

### Door Locks and Latches

Exterior body hardware and door components are generally sturdy, though front door lock actuators have generated localized owner complaints.

**Door Lock Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power door locks fail to lock or unlock consistently via the key fob or interior switch, often accompanied by a prolonged clicking noise.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty door lock actuator assembly within the affected door panel.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety systems generally perform well in crash testing, though individual mechanical components like belt buckles require verification during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Seat Belt Latch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The front seat belt buckle fails to catch or securely latch when inserted, despite the absence of foreign debris.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat belt buckle assembly and replace it if the internal latch mechanism is defective.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Toyota RAV4 remains a dependable and versatile choice within the compact SUV segment, backed by strong overall owner satisfaction and robust powertrain options. While the reported issues—such as intermittent infotainment reboots, door lock actuator failures, and occasional engine hesitation—are worth checking during a test drive, they do not detract fundamentally from the vehicle's strong daily utility. Prospective buyers should thoroughly test electronic features, climate controls, and door mechanisms before finalizing their purchase.
