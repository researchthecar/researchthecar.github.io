The 2006 Toyota RAV4 is a popular compact SUV known for its reliability and practicality. Engine options include a 2.4L inline-4 producing 166 hp and 165 lb-ft of torque and a 3.5L V6 generating 269 hp and 246 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were offered with a 4-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the RAV4 for its fuel efficiency, comfortable ride, and ample cargo space. Common complaints include the somewhat dated interior and occasional reports of excessive oil consumption, particularly in higher mileage vehicles.

**Engine**

**2.4L Inline-4**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The engine consumes an abnormal amount of oil, often requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. Blue smoke may be visible from the exhaust, and the low oil light may illuminate.
    *   Solution: In some cases, replacing the piston rings may resolve the issue. More severe cases could require engine replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+

*   **Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from the valve cover gasket, often visible as oil residue on the engine block. The smell of burning oil may also be present.
    *   Solution: Replacing the valve cover gasket.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300

**3.5L V6**

*   **VVTi Oil Line Leak (Early Production)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leak from the VVTi (Variable Valve Timing Intelligent) oil line.
    *   Solution: Replacing the original rubber hose with an updated metal line.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400
    *   Recalls: 90L

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic (Both Engines)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The transmission may exhibit harsh or jerky shifts, or may slip out of gear, especially when accelerating or climbing hills.
    *   Solution: Depending on the severity, a transmission fluid flush may temporarily improve the condition. More serious cases require transmission repair or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (fluid flush) / $2,000 - $4,000+ (replacement)

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Suspension Sagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The rear suspension sags, especially when carrying passengers or cargo. This can lead to a bouncy ride and reduced handling.
    *   Solution: Replacing the rear shocks and/or springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800

**Electrical**

*   **Power Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power door locks fail to operate, either intermittently or completely.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $350 per door

**Exterior**

*   **Rust on Rear Hatch**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust developing around the license plate area or near the hinges of the rear hatch.
    *   Solution: Repairing and repainting the affected area.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking (in Hot Climates)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing on the dashboard, especially in areas exposed to direct sunlight.
    *   Solution: Replacing the dashboard or using a dashboard cover.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (cover) / $800 - $1500+ (replacement)

**Summary**

The 2006 Toyota RAV4, while generally reliable, does have some common issues. Engine problems like excessive oil consumption and VVTi oil line leaks are concerns, with repairs potentially costing a significant amount. Transmission issues can also be expensive to address. Suspension and electrical problems are generally less costly but should be considered. While the RAV4 is known for its longevity, potential buyers should be aware of these common issues and factor in potential repair costs when making a purchase.
