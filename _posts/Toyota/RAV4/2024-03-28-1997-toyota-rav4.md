**Introduction**

The 1997 Toyota RAV4 is a compact SUV with a 2.0L 4-cylinder engine that produces 120 horsepower and 125 pound-feet of torque. It is available with either a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission. The RAV4 is known for its reliability, off-road capability, and spacious interior. However, it has also been reported to have some issues.

**Engine**

**2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and reduced engine performance. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
* **Head Gasket Failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and white smoke from the exhaust. Solution: Replace the head gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Timing Belt Failure:** Symptoms include a loud noise from the engine, decreased power, and stalling. Solution: Replace the timing belt. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** Symptoms include oil leaks and a burning smell. Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **5th Gear Pop-Out:** Symptoms include the transmission popping out of 5th gear during acceleration or deceleration. Solution: Replace the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
* **Synchro Wear:** Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears and grinding noises. Solution: Replace the synchros. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Fluid Leaks:** Symptoms include fluid leaks and a burning smell. Solution: Replace the transmission fluid and seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **Shift Solenoid Failure:** Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears and slipping. Solution: Replace the shift solenoid. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+
* **Torque Converter Failure:** Symptoms include slipping, shuddering, and overheating. Solution: Replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Strut Failure:** Symptoms include a clunking noise, decreased ride quality, and uneven tire wear. Solution: Replace the front suspension struts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $700+
* **Rear Suspension Spring Sagging:** Symptoms include a low ride height and reduced handling. Solution: Replace the rear suspension springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Symptoms include dim headlights, a dead battery, and warning lights on the dashboard. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Starter Failure:** Symptoms include no start condition and a clicking noise. Solution: Replace the starter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Battery Drain:** Symptoms include a dead battery after the vehicle has been parked. Solution: Test the battery and electrical system for any parasitic drains and repair as necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** Symptoms include visible rust on the body, frame, and undercarriage. Solution: Remove the rust and apply a protective coating. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **Door Panel Sagging:** Symptoms include loose or sagging door panels. Solution: Reattach or replace the door panel clips. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:** Symptoms include cracks on the dashboard. Solution: Replace the dashboard. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Climate Control Malfunction:** Symptoms include inoperative or erratic heating and A/C. Solution: Repair or replace the climate control unit. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Power Window Failure:** Symptoms include inoperative power windows. Solution: Repair or replace the power window motors or switches. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Summary**

The 1997 Toyota RAV4 is a reliable and capable vehicle, but it is not without its issues. The most common issues reported by owners include engine intake manifold gasket failure, head gasket failure, transmission fluid leaks, and suspension strut failure. The cost to fix these issues can vary depending on the severity of the problem and the specific parts that need to be replaced. Overall, the RAV4 is a good value for the money, but it is important to be aware of the potential issues that it may encounter.
