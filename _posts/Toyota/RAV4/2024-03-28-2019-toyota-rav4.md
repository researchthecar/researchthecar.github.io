**Introduction:**
The 2019 Toyota RAV4 is a compact SUV that offers a choice of two engine options: a 2.5-liter inline-4 and a 2.5-liter inline-4 hybrid. The 2.5-liter inline-4 produces 203 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque, while the 2.5-liter inline-4 hybrid produces a combined 219 horsepower. Both engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. The RAV4 is praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and excellent fuel economy. However, some owners have reported issues with various components of the vehicle.

**Engine**
**2.5-liter Inline-4**
* **Oil Consumption:** Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of piston rings or valve seals
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.5-liter Inline-4 Hybrid**
* **Hybrid Battery Failure:** Some owners have experienced premature failure of the hybrid battery.
    * Solution: Replacement of the hybrid battery pack
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
**8-Speed Automatic**
* **Shifting Issues:** Some owners have reported delayed or rough shifting, particularly at low speeds.
    * Solution: Reflashing of the transmission control module or replacement of transmission components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 20V678000: Transmission Control Module (TCM) Software Update

**Suspension**
* **Bushings Wear:** Some owners have reported premature wear of suspension bushings, leading to noise and poor handling.
    * Solution: Replacement of bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
* **Electrical System Malfunction:** Some owners have experienced electrical system malfunctions, including dashboard warning lights, power loss, or battery drain.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of faulty electrical components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the cause
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 21V627000: Brake Pedal Switch

**Exterior**
* **Roof Rail Noise:** Some owners have reported excessive wind noise coming from the roof rails.
    * Solution: Adjustment or replacement of roof rails
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
* **Center Console Vibration:** Some owners have reported a vibrating center console at certain speeds.
    * Solution: Replacement of center console components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**
Overall, the 2019 Toyota RAV4 has received generally positive reviews. However, some owners have encountered issues with various components, including the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, and interior. The severity and cost of the issues vary depending on the problem. Some issues, such as oil consumption, transmission shifting issues, and electrical system malfunctions, can be more serious and expensive to fix.
