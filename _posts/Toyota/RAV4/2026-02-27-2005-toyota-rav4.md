# 2005 Toyota RAV4

### Overview
The 2005 Toyota RAV4 is generally considered a reliable compact SUV, known for its practicality and durability. This generation (XA20) offered a choice between a 2.0L and a more powerful 2.4L engine, both paired with either a 5-speed manual or a 4-speed automatic transmission. While most owners report a positive experience, some common areas of concern have been identified over time, primarily related to the powertrain and certain electrical components. However, for its age, the RAV4 demonstrates a solid track record, with many vehicles still performing well with proper maintenance. The focus here is on documented, non-wear-and-tear related issues.

## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Power Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical components may stop working intermittently, such as power windows, door locks, or interior lights. Can sometimes be related to corrosion or loose connections within the BCM.
- **Solution:** Inspection and cleaning of BCM connectors, potential BCM replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


### Alternator
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light illuminates, dimming headlights, difficulty starting the engine, or complete loss of electrical power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2005 Toyota RAV4 stands out as a dependable compact SUV with a strong overall reliability record. The powertrain, including both engine and transmission options, is generally robust and free from widespread common failures. The primary areas where owners might encounter issues are related to the electrical system, specifically the Body Control Module and the alternator, which can experience premature failure. Suspension and exterior/interior components are typically durable. With diligent maintenance and addressing any emerging electrical quirks promptly, the 2005 RAV4 remains a sensible choice for those seeking a used vehicle that can offer many more years of service.
