# 2007 Toyota Matrix

### Overview
The 2007 Toyota Matrix is generally considered a reliable compact hatchback, known for its practicality and fuel efficiency. It shares its platform with the Corolla, inheriting much of its robust engineering. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected repairs, like any vehicle, certain components can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems that may arise, providing insights into potential repair areas and their associated costs. The Matrix was offered with different engine and transmission choices, each with its own reliability profile.

---


## Engine

### 1.8L 1ZZ-FE (126 hp)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning, potentially leading to low oil levels and engine damage if not monitored.
- **Solution:** Piston ring replacement, PCV valve cleaning/replacement.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Coolant Leaks (Head Gasket)**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, white smoke from exhaust, coolant in oil, or oil in coolant.
- **Solution:** Head gasket replacement.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2000


### 2.4L 2AZ-FE (158 hp)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Similar to the 1.8L, excessive oil burning can occur.
- **Solution:** Piston ring replacement, PCV valve cleaning/replacement.
- **Cost:** $900 - $1600

**Timing Chain Rattle**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup.
- **Solution:** Timing chain and tensioner replacement.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200

### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Toyota Matrix is a dependable vehicle with a strong track record for reliability. The most commonly cited issues, particularly with the 1.8L engine, revolve around oil consumption and potential head gasket failures, which can be costly to repair but are not universal. The 2.4L engine also has reports of oil consumption and timing chain noise. Transmissions, both manual and automatic, are generally robust. Other systems like suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components show good durability with no widespread significant problems reported. Regular maintenance and monitoring of oil levels are key to ensuring long-term reliability.
