# 2011 Toyota Matrix

### Overview
The 2011 Toyota Matrix is a compact hatchback known for its practicality and Toyota's reputation for reliability. It offers a comfortable ride and versatile cargo space, making it a popular choice for daily commuting and light utility. This model year generally benefits from Toyota's robust engineering, with many owners reporting few significant mechanical problems. However, like any vehicle, specific components can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems that may require attention.

## Electrical

### Body Control Module
**Intermittent Power Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical components (lights, power windows, radio) may stop working intermittently or completely.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement of the Body Control Module (BCM).
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,000


### Alternator
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light illumination, dimming headlights, difficulty starting the engine.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2011 Toyota Matrix is a generally reliable vehicle, upholding Toyota's reputation for durability. The most commonly cited non-wear-and-tear issue is oil consumption in the 2.4L engine, which requires diligent monitoring. Electrical components, particularly the Body Control Module and alternator, can also be points of failure. However, the 1.8L engine and both transmission options (manual and automatic) are typically robust. Overall, with regular maintenance and attention to potential oil consumption, the 2011 Matrix offers a dependable ownership experience.
