**Introduction:**

The 2007 Toyota Matrix is a compact crossover SUV available with either a 1.8L or 2.4L 4-cylinder engine, paired with either a 5-speed manual, 4-speed automatic, or continuously variable transmission (CVT). Praised for its spacious interior, fuel efficiency, and versatility, the Matrix has some reported issues that potential buyers should be aware of.

**Engine**

**1.8L 4-Cylinder**

- **Oil Consumption:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage
  - Solution: Replace piston rings or engine rebuild
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

**2.4L 4-Cylinder**

- **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Premature failure of the timing chain tensioner, causing rattling noises, engine rough idle, and potential catastrophic engine damage
  - Solution: Replace timing chain tensioner and related components
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

- **Gear Grinding and Difficulty Shifting:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Gears are hard to engage, grinding noises, and difficulty shifting, especially when cold
  - Solution: Replace worn synchronizers or rebuild transmission
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
  - Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic**

- **Transmission Slipping and Jerking:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Transmission slips or hesitates during gear shifts, causing rough acceleration or shuddering
  - Solution: Replace or rebuild transmission
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

- **Noise and Vibration:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Excessive noise or vibration during acceleration or deceleration
  - Solution: Replace or rebuild CVT
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **Strut Mount Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Premature failure of front strut mounts, causing clunking noises and poor handling
  - Solution: Replace strut mounts
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Alternator Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Electrical system malfunctions, battery drains prematurely, and warning lights illuminate
  - Solution: Replace alternator
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Door Lock Actuator Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Door locks fail to operate electrically or manually
  - Solution: Replace door lock actuator
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $350+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Dashboard Rattling:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Excessive rattling or buzzing noises from the dashboard
  - Solution: Install sound deadening material or tighten loose components
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2007 Toyota Matrix has several potential issues, including engine oil consumption, timing chain tensioner failure, transmission problems, and suspension issues. Some of these issues can be expensive to repair, so it's important to consider them when purchasing a used Matrix. However, the Matrix is still a reliable vehicle when properly maintained, offering good fuel efficiency and a spacious interior.
