The 2004 Toyota Solara is a stylish and reliable coupe or convertible known for its comfortable ride and Toyota's reputation for dependability. Engine options included a 2.4L 4-cylinder and a 3.3L V6. The 2.4L engine produced approximately 157 horsepower and 162 lb-ft of torque. Owners often praised the Solara for its smooth ride and fuel efficiency, but some disliked the somewhat bland styling and occasional issues with age-related components.

**Engine**

**2.4L 4-Cylinder (2AZ-FE)**
*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can develop from various seals and gaskets, including the valve cover gasket, timing cover, and rear main seal. Symptoms include visible oil leaks, oil consumption, and a burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gaskets and seals is the standard fix.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the location and severity of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some 2AZ-FE engines may experience excessive oil consumption due to piston ring issues. Symptoms include needing to add oil frequently between oil changes and blue smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Replacing the piston rings is the primary repair. This is a labor-intensive repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.3L V6 (3MZ-FE)**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Similar to the 2.4L, the V6 can develop oil leaks from valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, and other seals.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected gaskets and seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+, varying by location.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Variable Valve Timing (VVT-i) System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The VVT-i system may experience issues due to a faulty VVT-i solenoid or oil sludge buildup. Symptoms include rough idling, decreased fuel economy, and a check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacing the VVT-i solenoid and performing an engine flush to remove sludge.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Automatic Transmission (U241E/U151E)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners reported harsh shifting or slipping between gears, particularly in older models. This can be caused by worn valve bodies or low transmission fluid.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush, valve body replacement, or full transmission rebuild/replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $3,000+, depending on the severity and repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Strut/Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Worn struts and shocks can lead to a bouncy ride, poor handling, and increased braking distances.
    *   Solution: Replacing the struts and/or shocks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ for a complete replacement on all four corners.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sway Bar Link Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises, especially when going over bumps, can indicate worn sway bar links.
    * Solution: Replacing the sway bar links.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ per axle.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Power Window Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power windows may stop working or operate intermittently. This can be due to a faulty window motor or switch.
    *   Solution: Replacing the window motor or switch.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Instrument Cluster Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The instrument cluster can experience issues such as dimming, flickering, or complete failure.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the instrument cluster.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust can form on the undercarriage and lower body panels, especially in areas with road salt use.
    * Solution: Rust repair and prevention measures.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies greatly depending on severity, $100 - $2000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof may fail to open or close properly, or leak. This can be caused by a faulty motor, damaged seals, or clogged drain tubes.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the sunroof motor, seals, or clearing the drain tubes.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2004 Toyota Solara is a generally reliable vehicle, but owners may encounter some issues as the car ages. Common problems include oil leaks, potential transmission issues, and electrical problems. The severity and cost of these issues can vary greatly depending on the condition of the vehicle and the extent of the repairs needed. While some issues like oil leaks are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others such as transmission or engine problems can be more costly. Buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle and address any identified issues promptly to ensure long-term reliability.
