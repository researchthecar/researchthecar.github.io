**Introduction**

The 2003 Toyota Solara was a mid-size coupe and convertible offered with two engine options: a 2.4-liter inline-4 and a 3.3-liter V6. The inline-4 produced 157 horsepower and 162 lb-ft of torque, while the V6 produced 225 horsepower and 242 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Solara was praised for its stylish design, comfortable interior, and reliable performance, but it was criticized for its lack of power and handling.

**Engine**

- **2.4-liter Inline-4**
    - **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Description/Symptoms: Burning excessive amounts of oil, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage. Solution: Replacing the piston rings, valve seals, or entire engine. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $4,000+ Recalls: None Found
    - **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Worn or broken timing chain tensioner, resulting in a rattling noise from the engine and potential timing chain failure. Solution: Replacing the timing chain tensioner. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700 Recalls: None Found
- **3.3-liter V6**
    - **Ignition Coil Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Faulty ignition coil, causing misfires, reduced engine power, and potential engine damage. Solution: Replacing the ignition coil. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
- **4-Speed Automatic**
    - **Slipping or Hesitation:** Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping or hesitating when shifting gears. Solution: Replacing the transmission fluid, replacing worn or damaged components, or overhauling the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $2,500+ Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **Front Strut Mount Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged strut mount, causing clunking noises and reduced suspension performance. Solution: Replacing the strut mount. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Alternator Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Faulty alternator, resulting in insufficient charging of the battery and potential electrical system failure. Solution: Replacing the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800 Recalls: None Found
- **Starter Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Faulty starter, causing difficulty starting the vehicle. Solution: Replacing the starter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Window Regulator Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Faulty window regulator, causing windows to become stuck or inoperable. Solution: Replacing the window regulator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600 Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Climate Control Panel Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning climate control panel, resulting in incorrect or no air flow, temperature control, or other climate-related functions. Solution: Replacing the climate control panel. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2003 Toyota Solara has a mixed reliability record with some significant issues to watch out for, particularly with the engine and transmission. The cost of repairs can vary depending on the specific issue and complexity of the fix. Some issues, such as oil consumption and transmission problems, can be expensive to address and may potentially lead to more serious engine or transmission damage. Electrical and interior issues are generally less severe and less expensive to resolve.
