**Introduction**
The 1991 Toyota Tercel is a compact and fuel-efficient car that was available in sedan, coupe, and hatchback body styles. It came standard with a 1.5-liter inline-four engine that produced 84 horsepower and 98 lb-ft of torque. A 1.6-liter inline-four engine with 103 horsepower and 105 lb-ft of torque was optional. Both engines were available with a five-speed manual transmission or a four-speed automatic transmission.

Owners of the 1991 Toyota Tercel appreciate its reliability, fuel economy, and affordability. However, like any vehicle, it is not without its issues.

**Engine**
* **1.5-liter Inline-Four Engine**
    * **Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
        * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from the top of the engine, causing a burning oil smell and low oil levels.
        * Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250
        * Recalls: None Found
* **1.6-liter Inline-Four Engine**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**
* **Five-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **Clutch Master Cylinder Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, especially into first gear. The clutch pedal may feel spongy or hard to press.
        * Solution: Replace the clutch master cylinder.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Four-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Transmission Slipping**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission hesitates or slips when shifting gears.
        * Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
* **Front Struts**
    * **Leaking Shock Absorbers**
        * Description/Symptoms: The front end of the vehicle bounces excessively over bumps or dips.
        * Solution: Replace the front struts.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Shocks**
    * **Worn Bushings**
        * Description/Symptoms: Knocking or rattling noises from the rear of the vehicle when driving over bumps.
        * Solution: Replace the rear shock bushings.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
* **Alternator**
    * **Voltage Regulator Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The battery light on the dashboard illuminates. The vehicle may experience electrical problems, such as dim lights or flickering gauges.
        * Solution: Replace the alternator.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Ignition Coil**
    * **Failed Ignition Coil**
        * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle stalls or hesitates during acceleration. The engine may run rough or misfire.
        * Solution: Replace the ignition coil.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
* **Headlights**
    * **Cloudy or Dim Headlights**
        * Description/Symptoms: The headlights become cloudy or dim, reducing visibility at night.
        * Solution: Replace the headlights.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $100
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
* **Dashboard**
    * **Cracked Dashboard**
        * Description/Symptoms: Cracks or warping on the top of the dashboard.
        * Solution: Replace the dashboard.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Door Panels**
    * **Loose or Rattling Door Panels**
        * Description/Symptoms: The door panels rattle or vibrate when driving over bumps or rough roads.
        * Solution: Tighten or replace the door panel clips.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $100
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 1991 Toyota Tercel is a reliable and fuel-efficient car, but it is not without its issues. The most common problems include a leaking valve cover gasket, clutch master cylinder failure, transmission slipping, leaky shock absorbers, and a voltage regulator failure. While these issues can be costly to fix, they are not typically severe and can be prevented with regular maintenance.
