**Introduction:**
The 2017 Toyota Hiace is a versatile van offered with a 2.8L diesel engine mated to a 6-speed automatic transmission. It's praised for its spacious interior, reliability, and practicality. However, it has been reported with some issues in specific areas.

**Engine**
- 2.8L Diesel Engine

**Excessive Oil Consumption:**
* Description/Symptoms: Consumes excessive oil between oil changes, requiring frequent top-ups.
* Solution: Piston ring or cylinder bore replacement.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**Turbocharger Failure:**
* Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, black or blue smoke from exhaust, loud whistling noise.
* Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,500+
* Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
- 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

**Shifting Problems:**
* Description/Symptoms: Hesitation or delayed shifts, difficulty shifting into specific gears.
* Solution: Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, or transmission overhaul in severe cases.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
* Recalls: None Found

- No Issues Found

**Exterior**
- No Issues Found

**Interior**
- Climate Control Malfunction:**
* Description/Symptoms: Insufficient or no cooling or heating, fan not working.
* Solution: Blower motor or AC compressor replacement.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* Recalls: None Found

**Door Handle Breakage:**
* Description/Symptoms: Handles snap off or become loose, making it difficult to open doors.
* Solution: Handle replacement.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**
While the 2017 Toyota Hiace is generally a reliable vehicle, there have been some reported issues with the engine, transmission, and interior. The severity of these issues can range from minor inconveniences to major repairs requiring significant expense. Regular maintenance and addressing issues promptly can help mitigate potential problems and extend the life of the vehicle.
