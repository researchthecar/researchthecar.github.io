## 2021 Toyota Hiace

The 2021 Toyota Hiace is a full-size van offered in multiple configurations, including cargo, passenger, and crew cab versions. It features a powerful 2.8L diesel engine, paired with either a 6-speed manual or a 6-speed automatic transmission. The Hiace has received praise for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and reliable performance, but some owners have reported issues with certain components.

### Engine

**2.8L Diesel Engine**

* **EGR Valve Failure:** Can cause a loss of power, increased emissions, and a check engine light.
  * Solution: Replace the EGR valve.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Turbocharger Failure:** Can lead to reduced engine power, excessive smoke, and whistling noises.
  * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
  * Recalls: None Found

### Transmission

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Gear Shifter Issues:** Some users have reported difficulties shifting gears smoothly, especially in cold weather.
  * Solution: Replace the gear selector assembly or lubricate the shifter linkage.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Clutch Failure:** May cause slipping, shuddering, or difficulty engaging gears.
  * Solution: Replace the clutch assembly.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
  * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Slipping:** Can result in delayed or inconsistent gear changes, loss of power, or shuddering.
  * Solution: Overhaul or replace the transmission.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

### Suspension

* **Rear Leaf Spring Breakage:** Some vehicles have experienced breakage of the rear leaf springs, leading to a sagging rear end.
  * Solution: Replace the leaf springs.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
  * Recalls: None Found

### Electrical

* **Battery Drain:** Some owners have reported rapid battery draining, even when the vehicle is not in use.
  * Solution: Inspect and repair any electrical faults or replace the battery.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical Shorts:** Can cause various electrical issues, such as flickering lights, inoperative gauges, or power window failures.
  * Solution: Identify and repair the shorted circuits.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
  * Recalls: None Found

### Exterior

* **Door Lock Failure:** Some vehicles have experienced issues with the door locks, resulting in difficulties locking or unlocking the doors.
  * Solution: Replace the door lock actuators or door lock assembly.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Bumper Rust:** Some vehicles have developed rust on the rear bumper, especially in coastal or high-humidity areas.
  * Solution: None found.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: N/A
  * Recalls: None Found

### Interior

* **Steering Wheel Squeak:** Some drivers have reported a squeaking noise coming from the steering wheel when turning.
  * Solution: Lubricate the steering column or replace the steering wheel assembly.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Air Conditioning Fan Noise:** Some vehicles have experienced loud or excessive noise from the air conditioning fan, even at low speeds.
  * Solution: Replace the air conditioning fan motor or blower regulator.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
  * Recalls: None Found

### Summary

While the 2021 Toyota Hiace offers many dependable features, it has not been immune to occasional issues with certain components. The reported engine and transmission problems, while potentially serious, appear to be relatively uncommon. Suspension issues and electrical faults have also been reported, but their severity and frequency vary. Interior and exterior issues tend to be more minor and less expensive to repair. Overall, the Hiace remains a generally reliable and practical vehicle for various transportation needs.
