**2009 Toyota Hiace**

The 2009 Toyota Hiace is a popular commercial van known for its spacious interior, reliability, and durability. It is available with a range of engine and transmission options, including:

* **2.7-liter 4-cylinder diesel engine** (130 hp, 250 lb-ft torque)
* **3.0-liter 4-cylinder diesel engine** (171 hp, 293 lb-ft torque)
* **5-speed manual transmission**
* **4-speed automatic transmission**

**Engine**

**2.7-liter 4-cylinder Diesel Engine**

* **Fuel Injector Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, black smoke from exhaust, reduced power
    * Solution: Replace fuel injectors
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Turbocharger Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, loud whining noise
    * Solution: Replace turbocharger
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.0-liter 4-cylinder Diesel Engine**

* **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Clogging**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced power, increased fuel consumption, difficulty starting
    * Solution: Clean or replace DPF
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **EGR Valve Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, black smoke from exhaust
    * Solution: Replace EGR valve
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-speed Manual Transmission**

* **Gear Grinding**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noise
    * Solution: Replace or repair transmission gears
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**4-speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Clutch Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, difficulty shifting gears
    * Solution: Replace clutch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Breakage**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging or uneven suspension, loud clunking noise
    * Solution: Replace leaf springs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dim headlights, difficulty starting, battery warning light on
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery goes dead after overnight parking
    * Solution: Check for parasitic drain, replace battery if necessary
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust**
    * Description/Symptoms: Corrosion on body panels, frame, or suspension components
    * Solution: Rust treatment or replacement of affected parts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on extent of rust
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Heater or air conditioner not working properly
    * Solution: Repair or replace climate control components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Seat Adjustment Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty adjusting seats manually or power
    * Solution: Adjust or replace seat adjustment mechanism
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2009 Toyota Hiace is a generally reliable vehicle, but it is not without its issues. The engine and transmission issues are the most severe and expensive to fix, while the interior and exterior issues are relatively minor and inexpensive. Overall, the Hiace is a solid choice for commercial use, but potential buyers should be aware of the potential problems it may encounter.
