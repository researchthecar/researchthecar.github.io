**Introduction:**

The 2011 Toyota Hiace is a versatile van renowned for its spacious interior, reliable engine, and durable construction. It's available with various seating configurations and cargo space options, making it suitable for commercial and personal use. The Hiace is powered by a robust 2.7-liter inline-four engine mated to a five-speed manual or four-speed automatic transmission. Despite its practicality, the Hiace has received mixed reviews, with some owners praising its dependability and others expressing concerns about its sluggish acceleration and outdated design.

**Engine**

**2.7L Inline-Four Engine**

* **Head Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, engine knocking, white smoke from exhaust
    * Solution: Replace head gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise from engine, especially during startup and acceleration
    * Solution: Replace timing chain tensioner
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $700+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Five-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **No Issues Found**

**Four-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Slipping:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, loss of power during acceleration
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Shock Absorber Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Poor ride quality, excessive bouncing, uneven tire wear
    * Solution: Replace shock absorbers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Leaf Spring Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, poor handling, metal-on-metal scraping noise
    * Solution: Replace leaf springs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimmed headlights, loss of power, battery warning light
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Starter Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting engine, grinding noise
    * Solution: Replace starter
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Body Panel:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Brownish or reddish discoloration on metal surfaces
    * Solution: Sand, repaint, and apply rust protection
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Antenna Mast Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Antenna mast stuck in retracted position
    * Solution: Replace antenna mast
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattle:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noises emanating from dashboard, especially over rough roads
    * Solution: Tighten or replace loose dashboard components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2011 Toyota Hiace exhibits a range of issues, affecting various components. Common concerns include head gasket failure, timing chain tensioner failure, transmission slipping, and suspension problems. While some issues are minor and relatively inexpensive to fix, others can be more severe and costly. Prospective buyers should carefully research potential issues before purchasing a used Hiace and consider a thorough pre-purchase inspection to assess the vehicle's condition.
