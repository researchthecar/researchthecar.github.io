# 2009 Toyota Hiace

### Overview
The 2009 Toyota Hiace is a commercial van known for its robust build and utilitarian design, often favored for its reliability in demanding work environments. This generation of the Hiace typically offers a range of dependable diesel and gasoline engines, paired with robust manual or automatic transmissions. Its focus on durability means that while routine maintenance is essential, major component failures are relatively uncommon. Owners generally report high satisfaction with its long-term performance and low running costs, making it a popular choice for businesses and individuals requiring a workhorse vehicle. The Hiace's straightforward engineering contributes significantly to its reputation for resilience.

## Engine

### 2.5L 1KD-FTV Turbo Diesel
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, excessive smoke, whining noise from the engine bay.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger unit.
- **Cost:** $1200 - $2500

**Injector Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, poor fuel economy, engine misfires, difficulty starting.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of fuel injectors.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900

### Summary Verdict
The 2009 Toyota Hiace demonstrates strong reliability, particularly with its diesel engine options, though common wear items like turbochargers and injectors can require attention over time. The manual transmission is generally robust, with clutch wear being the primary concern. The automatic transmission is also considered dependable. Suspension bushings are a typical wear item for vehicles of this age and usage. Electrical issues, such as alternator failure, are not uncommon but are generally manageable. Overall, the Hiace is a solid choice for reliability, with most issues being repairable and not indicative of systemic design flaws. Regular maintenance is key to maximizing its lifespan.
