**Introduction**

The 2016 Toyota Hiace is a versatile commercial van renowned for its spacious interior, robust build quality, and dependable performance. It comes with a variety of engine and transmission options, offering flexibility to suit different needs. Some of the engine options include a 2.5L gasoline engine, a 2.8L turbo-diesel engine, and a 3.5L V6 engine. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual, a 6-speed automatic, and a 5-speed automated manual transmission. While the Toyota Hiace has received widespread acclaim for its practicality and reliability, certain issues have been reported by owners.

**Engine**

**2.5L Gasoline Engine**
* **Valve Body Failure:** The valve body within the transmission can malfunction, resulting in delayed shifts, loss of gears, or complete transmission failure. **Solution:** Replacement of the valve body. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+ **Recalls:** None Found

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Some engines have been known to consume excessive amounts of oil, leading to oil leaks and potential engine damage. **Solution:** Engine rebuild or replacement. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $3,000 - $5,000+ **Recalls:** None Found

**2.8L Turbo-Diesel Engine**
* **Turbocharger Failure:** The turbocharger may fail, resulting in a loss of power and increased exhaust emissions. **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2,000 - $3,000+ **Recalls:** None Found

* **Injector Failure:** Fuel injectors may malfunction, causing engine misfires, rough idling, and increased fuel consumption. **Solution:** Replacement of the injectors. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+ **Recalls:** None Found

**3.5L V6 Engine**
* **Timing Belt Failure:** The timing belt may fail prematurely, resulting in catastrophic engine damage. **Solution:** Replacement of the timing belt. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $2,500+ **Recalls:** None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**
* **Synchro Ring Wear:** Over time, the synchro rings within the transmission may wear out, causing difficulty shifting gears or grinding noises. **Solution:** Replacement of the synchro rings. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $600 - $1,000+ **Recalls:** None Found

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**
* **Valve Body Failure:** Similar to the valve body issue in the 2.5L gasoline engine, the valve body can fail in the 6-speed automatic transmission, leading to similar symptoms. **Solution:** Replacement of the valve body. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+ **Recalls:** None Found

**5-Speed Automated Manual Transmission**
* **Clutch Failure:** The clutch within the automated manual transmission may wear out prematurely, resulting in difficulty shifting gears or a loss of power. **Solution:** Replacement of the clutch. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $800 - $1,200+ **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Leaf Spring Failure:** The rear leaf springs may sag or break, leading to a loss of ride quality and potential handling issues. **Solution:** Replacement of the leaf springs. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $400 - $600+ **Recalls:** None Found

* **Front Coil Spring Breakage:** The front coil springs may break, causing a decrease in ride height and potential handling problems. **Solution:** Replacement of the coil springs. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $500+ **Recalls:** None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** The battery may drain prematurely, leaving the vehicle unable to start. **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of the electrical system causing the drain. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** Varies depending on the cause **Recalls:** None Found

* **Starter Motor Failure:** The starter motor may fail, preventing the engine from starting. **Solution:** Replacement of the starter motor. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $800+ **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** The vehicle may be prone to rust formation on the body panels, especially in areas exposed to moisture. **Solution:** Rust prevention measures such as regular waxing or rust-proofing treatments. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** Varies depending on the severity of the rust **Recalls:** None Found

* **Paint Peeling:** The paint may peel or fade prematurely on certain model years. **Solution:** Repainting or touch-up of the affected areas. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** Varies depending on the extent of damage **Recalls:** None Found

**Interior**

* **HVAC System Malfunction:** The heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) system may malfunction, resulting in poor air flow, inconsistent temperatures, or even failure. **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of the HVAC system. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** Varies depending on the specific issue **Recalls:** None Found

* **Door Lock Failure:** The door locks may malfunction, either failing to open or close properly. **Solution:** Replacement of the door lock actuators or mechanisms. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+ **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2016 Toyota Hiace is a reliable and practical vehicle. However, certain issues can arise, particularly with the engine, transmission, and suspension systems. While some issues may be minor and relatively inexpensive to fix, others can be more severe and costly. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues and consider factoring them into their purchase decision.
