**Introduction**

The 2007 Toyota Hiace is a versatile and reliable van available in multiple engine and transmission configurations. It offers spacious interiors, practical features, and a strong reputation for durability. However, like any vehicle, it may experience certain issues that can affect its performance or comfort.

**Engine**

**2.7L 4-Cylinder Gasoline**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Worn piston rings or valve seals can lead to increased oil consumption. Fix involves engine disassembly and replacement of affected parts. $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Valve Body Failure:** Malfunctioning valve bodies can cause transmission problems, such as erratic shifting or slipping. Replacement of the valve body is necessary. $1,000 - $2,000+

**3.0L 4-Cylinder Diesel**

* **Turbocharger Failure:** Turbochargers can fail due to lubrication issues or worn components, resulting in reduced engine performance. Replacement of the turbocharger is required. $2,000 - $3,000+
* **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Clogging:** DPFs can become clogged over time, causing performance issues and increased fuel consumption. Cleaning or replacement of the DPF is necessary. $500 - $1,200+

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Wear:** Worn clutches can slip or chatter during gear engagement. Replacement of the clutch is necessary. $500 - $1,000+
* **Synchronizer Wear:** Worn synchronizers can make shifting difficult or cause grinding noises. Transmission disassembly and replacement of synchronizers is required. $800 - $1,500+

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping:** Worn clutch packs or bands can cause the transmission to slip gears or experience loss of power. Transmission rebuilding or replacement may be necessary. $1,500 - $3,000+

**Suspension**

* **Strut/Shock Absorber Wear:** Worn struts or shock absorbers can lead to a rough or bouncy ride. Replacement of the affected components is recommended. $400 - $800+
* **Ball Joint Wear:** Worn ball joints can cause noise and instability while driving. Replacement of ball joints is necessary. $200 - $400+ per joint

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Worn or malfunctioning alternators can lead to battery depletion and electrical system issues. Replacement of the alternator is required. $400 - $800+
* **Starter Failure:** Worn or malfunctioning starters can cause problems with engine starting. Replacement of the starter is necessary. $300 - $600+

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Hinge Failure:** Worn or broken tailgate hinges can prevent the tailgate from opening or closing properly. Replacement of the hinges is necessary. $200 - $400+
* **Rust:** Underbody and exterior rust can occur in areas where anti-corrosion treatment is inadequate. Corrosion protection measures are recommended. $500 - $1,500+ depending on severity

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:** Some vehicles experience cracking of the dashboard over time. Replacement of the dashboard is not required and cosmetic repairs can be considered. $300 - $600+
* **Seatbelt Pretensioner Issues:** Faulty seatbelt pretensioners may not deploy or lock properly in an accident. Replacement or servicing of the affected pretensioners is necessary. $500 - $1,000+

**Summary**

The Toyota Hiace is generally a reliable vehicle, but it may experience certain issues that range in severity and cost. Engine and transmission problems can be more expensive to fix, while electrical and suspension issues tend to be more affordable. Exterior and interior issues can vary in terms of cosmetic concerns versus safety issues. Regular maintenance and proper care can help prevent or mitigate some of these problems and ensure a long and trouble-free service life for the vehicle.
