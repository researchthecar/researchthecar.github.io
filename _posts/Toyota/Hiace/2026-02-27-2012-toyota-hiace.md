## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent starting issues, dimming lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose and replace faulty parasitic drain component (e.g., relay, module, accessory).
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light illuminated, dimming headlights, engine stalling.
- **Solution:** Replace alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Trim
**Rusting**
- **Symptoms:** Corrosion on wheel arches, door sills, and underbody, especially in humid or coastal climates.
- **Solution:** Rust treatment and repair, rustproofing application.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1500+


## Interior

### Seating and Upholstery
**Wear and Tear on Seats**
- **Symptoms:** Fabric fraying or tearing on driver's seat bolsters due to frequent ingress/egress.
- **Solution:** Seat cover installation or professional upholstery repair/replacement.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500


### HVAC System
**Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, or intermittent fan speed.
- **Solution:** Replace blower motor or resistor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Toyota Hiace generally exhibits strong reliability, consistent with Toyota's reputation. The powertrain (engines and transmissions) is exceptionally robust with no widespread common failures reported. The primary areas where owners might encounter issues are related to the electrical system, specifically battery drain and alternator failures, which are not uncommon in vehicles of this age and mileage. Exterior rust can be a concern in certain environments, requiring proactive maintenance. Interior wear on high-use areas like the driver's seat is also a possibility. Overall, the Hiace remains a dependable workhorse, with most potential issues being manageable and repairable.
