# 2022 Toyota Supra

### Overview
The 2022 Toyota Supra, a joint venture with BMW, offers a compelling blend of performance and style. Available with two distinct turbocharged inline-six and inline-four engine options, and an 8-speed automatic transmission, it aims for a premium sports car experience. Reliability data for the 2022 model year, while generally positive, shows a few areas that warrant attention. Owners have reported some specific concerns, particularly related to the powertrain and certain electronic features. However, for the most part, the Supra maintains a reputation for solid build quality and engaging driving dynamics, with most reported issues being isolated rather than widespread.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Intermittent Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, Bluetooth connectivity issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, potential replacement of head unit.
- **Cost:** $100 - $1500


### Driver Assistance Systems
**False Warnings/Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Erratic behavior from adaptive cruise control, lane keeping assist, or parking sensors.
- **Solution:** Sensor recalibration, software updates, inspection of wiring harnesses.
- **Cost:** $150 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Toyota Supra demonstrates strong mechanical reliability, with its engines and transmissions generally performing without significant issues. The primary areas of concern are concentrated within the electrical system, specifically the infotainment unit and driver assistance features, which can experience occasional glitches or false warnings. These are often resolvable with software updates or recalibration. Exterior and interior components are largely free from widespread complaints. Overall, the Supra is a reliable sports car, but potential buyers should be aware of the minor electrical quirks that may arise.
