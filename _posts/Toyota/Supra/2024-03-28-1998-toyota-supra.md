**Introduction:**

The 1998 Toyota Supra is a legendary sports car powered by a potent 3.0-liter inline-six engine. It features a sleek design and agile handling, making it a favorite among enthusiasts. However, like any vehicle, the Supra has experienced some issues over the years.

**Engine**

**3.0L 2JZ-GTE Turbo**

**Blown Head Gasket**
Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, loss of power
Solution: Replace head gasket
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
Recalls: None Found

**Cracked Cylinder Head**
Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, white smoke from exhaust, overheating
Solution: Replace cylinder head
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**W58 Manual**

**Grinding Gears**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, metallic grinding noise
Solution: Replace worn synchronizers
Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
Recalls: None Found

**A340E Automatic**

**Slipping Gears**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty engaging gears, loss of power
Solution: Replace worn clutches or transmission
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Front Control Arm Bushings**
Description/Symptoms: Squeaking, rattling, poor handling
Solution: Replace worn bushings
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
Recalls: None Found

**Rear Differential Bushing**
Description/Symptoms: Whining or clunking noise, loss of traction
Solution: Replace worn bushing
Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Alternator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, difficulty starting, battery warning light
Solution: Replace alternator
Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
Recalls: None Found

**Starter Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Engine does not crank, clicking noise
Solution: Replace starter
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Sunroof Leaks**
Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the cabin
Solution: Replace worn seals or sunroof assembly
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Air Conditioning Compressor Failure**
Description/Symptoms: No cold air, humming noise
Solution: Replace compressor
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
Recalls: None Found

**Power Window Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Window does not move, buzzing noise
Solution: Replace window motor or switch
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 1998 Toyota Supra is a reliable and enjoyable vehicle, but it is not without its issues. The most significant problems include blown head gaskets, cracked cylinder heads, and transmission failures. However, these issues are relatively uncommon, and the cost of repairs is not typically prohibitive.
