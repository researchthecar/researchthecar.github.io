# 2021 Toyota Supra Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Toyota Supra delivers thrilling rear-wheel-drive performance derived from its BMW partnership, offering a choice of potent turbocharged engines. While overall mechanical satisfaction tends to be high among enthusiasts, used car buyers should review ownership history and monitor for sporadic owner-reported complaints regarding electrical anomalies and braking behavior.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

This entry-level 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 255 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. It offers a lighter front-end feel and nimble handling dynamics for everyday driving while maintaining reasonable efficiency.

### 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6

The larger 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six engine generates roughly 382 horsepower and 368 lb-ft of torque, providing rapid acceleration. It is a favored choice for performance enthusiasts, though buyers should watch for occasional starter-related complaints.

**Starter Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may fail to start immediately when pressing the ignition button, occasionally displaying a starter malfunction message.
- **Solution:** Have the starter motor and related electrical connections inspected by a qualified technician and replaced if necessary.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The ZF 8-speed automatic transmission delivers rapid, smooth gear changes and pairs seamlessly with both engine options. It is widely regarded as robust and responsive during spirited driving.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake system complaints highlight isolated incidents involving assist loss or premature ABS engagement.

**Brake Assist Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience a sudden loss of power brake assist, requiring significantly higher pedal effort to stop the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Inspect the braking system, vacuum or electronic assist units, and related sensors for underlying faults.

**Inadvertent ABS Activation**
- **Symptoms:** The anti-lock braking system may unexpectedly engage during normal light deceleration, causing an unusual pulsing sensation.
- **Solution:** Have wheel speed sensors and the ABS control module diagnosed for faults or erratic signal readings.

## Steering

### Steering Linkage

Steering components generally offer sharp and direct feedback, though isolated structural failures have been reported.

**Tie Rod Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A tie rod may fracture unexpectedly during low-speed maneuvers, leading to a sudden loss of steering control on the affected side.
- **Solution:** Inspect steering components for structural damage or premature wear and replace compromised tie rods.

## Exterior

### Climate Control System

Interior electronics and climate controls are generally modern and functional, with occasional climate motor faults.

**Blender Door Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The cabin climate control may blow mismatched temperatures (such as cold air on one side and warm on the other) due to a stuck blend door.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty blend door actuator motor responsible for directing airflow and temperature mixing.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V554000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Supra&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these assemblies fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." During a crash, a seat belt retractor that does not function as intended increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the seat belt retractors (including the seat belts) and, if necessary, replace the retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V700000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Supra&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The failed weld may allow fuel to leak, increasing the risk of a fire in the presence of an ignition source.
- **What to do:** BMW will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel tank, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V598000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Supra&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** During engine start, the engine management software may cause loss of braking assist. Loss of braking assist can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the engine management software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V636000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly:Relay](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Supra&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The engine starter relay may corrode, causing the relay to overheat and short circuit. A short circuit in the starter relay may increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the engine starter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V056000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Supra&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The engine starter may overheat and catch fire due to wear on an internal component. Fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the engine starter, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Toyota Supra is an exciting sports car offering strong performance and sharp handling. While major mechanical failures are uncommon, prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle's service history, verify all electrical and braking functions, and check for open recall campaign statuses prior to purchase.
