# 2023 Toyota Highlander Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Toyota Highlander is a popular three-row midsize crossover known for its family-friendly versatility, comfortable ride, and updated technology features. As a relatively recent model year, it generally maintains a strong reputation for overall reliability. However, looking through owner feedback and reported complaints reveals a few specific areas that potential buyers should keep on their radar, ranging from powertrain concerns to minor body and interior accessory hiccups.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Turbocharged Inline-4

This 2.4-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 265 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard gas-powered option for the model year. It balances adequate daily power with modern efficiency, though buyers should verify proper fuel system integrity during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Fuel Odor or Leak Concern**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers notice an intermittent gasoline odor inside or around the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Have the fuel lines and connections inspected by a certified technician or dealership.

### 2.5L Hybrid Inline-4

This 2.5-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder hybrid powertrain pairs with electric motors to deliver an estimated 243 net horsepower while maximizing fuel economy. It provides a smooth, efficient driving experience well-suited for family commuting and long-distance road trips.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

This conventional 8-speed automatic transmission works alongside the standard turbocharged engine to deliver power smoothly to either the front wheels or all-wheel drive system. It generally offers predictable shifting behavior under normal driving conditions.

### Electronically Controlled CVT

This electronic continuously variable transmission is integrated with the hybrid powertrain to optimize efficiency and seamless power delivery. It provides responsive low-speed acceleration while maintaining high fuel economy ratings.

## Exterior

### Power Liftgate and Latch

Cabin features and latch assemblies generally hold up well, though convenience elements like power liftgates can occasionally exhibit erratic sensor behavior.

**Liftgate Operation Failure in Cold Weather**
- **Symptoms:** The tailgate refuses to open or reverses direction and beeps when attempting to close in low ambient temperatures.
- **Solution:** Inspect liftgate struts, sensors, and latch mechanisms for alignment or sensor blockages, and consult a dealer if adjustment is needed.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety equipment and restraint systems are comprehensive, though electronic sensors managing seat belt alerts can occasionally produce nuisance warnings.

**False Seat Belt Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** The seat belt warning chime sounds intermittently even when occupants are properly buckled.
- **Solution:** Check seat belt buckle sensors and wiring harnesses for proper connection or software updates at a dealership.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V480000: Steering:Column](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." A deactivated driver's air bag that does not deploy as intended increases the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the spiral cable assembly, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V720000: Structure:Body:Bumpers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A detached front bumper cover can become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will repair or replace the upper and lower front bumper covers as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V419000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." Tires with an insufficient load rating may be overloaded and fail, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GST will inspect and replace the tires as necessary, and install a new tire information label as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V452000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." Tires with an insufficient load rating can be overloaded and fail, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** SET will inspect and replace the tires and information placard, as necessary free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V548000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." A vehicle with an incorrect maximum capacity weight value may be overloaded, which can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V040000: Steering:Column](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." A deactivated driver's air bag will not deploy as intended, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the spiral cable assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V595000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** An instrument panel display that does not show critical information can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the instrument panel software over-the-air (OTA) for non-PHEV vehicles, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V744000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to freeze or display a blank screen when the vehicle is in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that fails to display an image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the parking assist software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Toyota Highlander remains a dependable and capable choice in the midsize SUV segment, offering efficient powertrain choices and practical family features. While most owners enjoy trouble-free driving, prospective buyers should carefully verify the condition of the fuel system, test all power accessories including the liftgate, and ensure any open safety campaigns or recalls have been addressed by a certified dealer before finalizing a purchase.
