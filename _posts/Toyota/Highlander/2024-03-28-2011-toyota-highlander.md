**Introduction:**

The 2011 Toyota Highlander is a midsize SUV known for its reliability, spacious interior, and versatile seating options. It is offered with a choice of two engines and three transmission options: a 3.5L V6 paired with a 5-speed automatic or a 6-speed automatic transmission, and a 2.7L I4 paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Despite its positive reputation, the Highlander has faced several issues that owners have reported.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6**

**Intake Manifold Cracking**
* Description/Symptoms: Cracking in the intake manifold can cause coolant leaks, engine overheating, and reduced performance.
* Solution: Replacement of the intake manifold.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
* Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

**Shift Lock Malfunction**
* Description/Symptoms: The shift lock may malfunction, preventing the driver from shifting out of park.
* Solution: Replacement of the shift interlock solenoid.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic**

**Shifting Problems**
* Description/Symptoms: Transmission may hesitate, slip, or refuse to shift gears properly.
* Solution: May require transmission fluid replacement, filter cleaning, or in some cases, transmission rebuild.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $3,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Strut Bearing Failure**
* Description/Symptoms: Failure of the strut bearing can cause excessive noise while driving or rattling when turning.
* Solution: Replacement of the strut bearing and associated parts.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Power Window Motor Failure**
* Description/Symptoms: Power window motor may fail, causing the window to become inoperable.
* Solution: Replacement of the power window motor.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Door Hinge Alignment Issues**
* Description/Symptoms: Doors may not open or close properly due to misalignment of the hinges.
* Solution: Adjustment of the door hinges.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
* Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Dashboard Rattle**
* Description/Symptoms: Dashboard may rattle while driving, especially on rough roads.
* Solution: Tightening of loose screws or adding sound deadening material.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
* Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

While the 2011 Toyota Highlander is generally considered a reliable vehicle, it has encountered some issues that owners have reported. The most common issues involve the engine intake manifold, transmission shifting problems, and suspension strut bearing failure. The severity of these issues can vary, with some being relatively minor and others potentially costly to repair.
