**Introduction:**

The 2012 Toyota Highlander is a midsize SUV known for its reliability, spacious cabin, and comfortable ride. It comes with two engine options, a 3.5-liter V6 producing 270 horsepower and 248 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.0-liter V6 hybrid producing 280 horsepower combined. Transmission options include a 5-speed automatic for the V6 engine and a continuously variable transmission (CVT) for the hybrid model. Despite its positive attributes, some owners have reported specific issues with their Highlanders.

**Engine**

**3.5-liter V6**

* **VVT-i Oil Control Valve Issue:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The Variable Valve Timing-intelligent (VVT-i) oil control valve may fail, causing an engine oil leak. Symptoms include low oil levels and a possible check engine light.
    * Solution: Replace the VVT-i oil control valve.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.0-liter V6 Hybrid**

* **Battery Degradation Issue:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The hybrid battery pack may degrade over time, leading to reduced fuel efficiency and decreased battery life. Symptoms include decreased power and mileage.
    * Solution: Replace the hybrid battery pack.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slippage Issue:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may experience slippage, resulting in hesitant acceleration and difficulty shifting gears. Symptoms include slipping gears, lurching, and delayed gear engagement.
    * Solution: Overhaul or replace the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

* **CVT Belt Failure Issue:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The CVT belt may fail prematurely, causing loss of power and transmission failure. Symptoms include no movement when shifting into gear, slipping, and a burning smell.
    * Solution: Replace the CVT belt and inspect other components for damage.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $3,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Bearing Failure Issue:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The strut mount bearings may fail, causing a clunking noise from the front suspension when turning or driving over bumps. Symptoms include a rattling or clunking sound from the front end.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mount bearings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure Issue:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator may fail, leading to electrical system problems and battery discharge. Symptoms include difficulty starting, flickering headlights, and electrical system malfunctions.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Panel Gap Issue:**
    * Description/Symptoms: There may be a gap between the dashboard panel and the windshield, allowing water to seep into the cabin. Symptoms include water leaking onto the dashboard and floorboard during rain.
    * Solution: Seal the gap with sealant or replace the dashboard panel.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rear Liftgate Strut Issue:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear liftgate struts may fail, causing the liftgate to drop unexpectedly. Symptoms include difficulty opening or closing the liftgate, and potential damage to the liftgate or vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace the liftgate struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

While the 2012 Toyota Highlander is a generally reliable vehicle, some specific issues have been reported. The most significant and costly issues involve transmission problems
