**2020 Toyota Highlander**

The 2020 Toyota Highlander is a popular midsize SUV known for its spacious interior, reliability, and advanced safety features. It's offered with a choice of two engines: a 3.5-liter V6 and a 2.5-liter inline-four. The V6 is paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission, while the inline-four is mated to an 8-speed Direct Shift-CVT.

Owners generally praise the Highlander for its smooth ride, comfortable seats, and impressive fuel economy. However, some have reported issues with various components of the vehicle.

**Engine**

* **3.5-liter V6**
    * **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
        * Solution: Engine rebuild or replacement
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $10,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **2.5-liter Inline-Four**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **8-Speed Automatic**
    * **Shifting Hesitation:** Some owners have experienced hesitant or delayed shifts, terutama in the lower gears.
        * Solution: Transmission fluid flush or replacement
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found
* **8-Speed Direct Shift-CVT**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension:** Some owners have reported that the rear suspension provides an uncomfortable ride, especially over rough roads.
        * Solution: Upgraded or aftermarket rear suspension components
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** A few owners have experienced excessive battery drain, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle.
        * Solution: Replacement of the battery or alternator
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Infotainment System:** Some owners have reported issues with the infotainment system freezing or becoming unresponsive.
        * Solution: Software update or replacement of the infotainment unit
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Plastic Trim:** Some owners have reported that the plastic trim on the exterior of the vehicle can become damaged or crack easily.
        * Solution: Replacement of the trim
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **WiFi Hotspot:** Some owners have reported that the WiFi hotspot feature is unreliable or does not work properly.
        * Solution: Software update or replacement of the hotspot component
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Climate Control:** A few owners have experienced issues with the climate control system not blowing air properly or not maintaining the desired temperature.
        * Solution: Inspection and repair of the climate control system components
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

While the 2020 Toyota Highlander is generally a reliable vehicle, some owners have reported issues with various components, including the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, exterior, and interior. The severity and cost of these issues vary depending on the specific problem and the extent of the damage. It's important to note that not all Highlanders will experience these issues, and many owners have reported a positive experience with the vehicle.
