# 2018 Toyota Highlander Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Toyota Highlander is widely recognized as a dependable and family-friendly midsize crossover, offering strong safety scores and a comfortable ride. However, prospective buyers should review specific mechanical areas, notably transmission durability concerns and occasional electrical or braking complaints reported by owners.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (2GR-FKS)

The 3.5-liter V6 engine produces approximately 295 horsepower and 263 lb-ft of torque, providing strong, smooth acceleration and solid overall durability.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report sudden engine shaking, check engine lights flashing, and a rapid drop in power while driving on highways or streets.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning required; repairs may involve addressing fuel system components or faulty sensors.

### 3.5L V6 Hybrid

The hybrid powertrain pairs a 3.5-liter V6 with electric motors for a net 306 horsepower, delivering enhanced fuel economy with typical Toyota hybrid reliability.

## Transmission

### Direct Shift 8-Speed Automatic

The 8-speed automatic transmission routes power to the front wheels or available all-wheel-drive systems, but requires close attention during a pre-purchase inspection due to documented owner complaints regarding premature wear and whining noises.

**Transmission Failure and Whining Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note a distinct whining noise from the transaxle, gear slippage, delay in shifting, or complete transmission failure leaving the vehicle stranded.
- **Solution:** Transmission replacement or internal rebuild, which can involve significant out-of-pocket expenses if outside warranty coverage.
- **Cost:** $8,000

## Brakes

### Brake System

The braking system provides adequate stopping power under normal conditions, though some owners have reported premature rotor vibrations and vacuum-related warnings.

**Low Braking Power and Soft Pedals**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience soft brake pedals, severe vibration when slowing down from highway speeds, or dashboard warnings regarding low braking power.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake rotors, pads, and vacuum pump systems, replacing worn or failing hardware as needed.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Standard safety features generally perform well, though mechanical wear on seat belt retractors has been noted by a subset of owners.

**Seat Belt Retractor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The driver-side seat belt becomes difficult to pull out or fails to retract properly, posing a safety hazard.
- **Solution:** Replace the seat belt assembly.
- **Cost:** $500

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V682000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel pump fails, the engine can stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly with an improved one, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V012000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel pump fails, the engine can stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18E107000: Electrical System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The ECM damage may result in the engine stalling, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Wanda will notify owners, and service centers will replace the windshields, and inspect the ECM for damage, having it replaced, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V831000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An incorrect load information label can result in the operator overloading the vehicle and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 18V211000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist:Vacuum](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A sudden loss of braking assist can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the brake booster vacuum pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Toyota Highlander remains a compelling choice for used SUV shoppers thanks to its spacious interior, strong V6 engine options, and stellar safety reputation. However, buyers should prioritize a thorough mechanical inspection focusing on the 8-speed automatic transmission for any signs of whining or slippage, as well as verifying proper brake and electrical functionality.
