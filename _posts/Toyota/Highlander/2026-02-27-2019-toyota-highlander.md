# 2019 Toyota Highlander Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Toyota Highlander is a popular three-row midsize crossover known for its comfort, strong safety credentials, and overall practicality for families. However, used car buyers should pay close attention to powertrain behavior, particularly concerning transmission noises and fuel system or stalling complaints reported by owners. While Toyotas generally hold a strong reputation for durability, reviewing these specific mechanical and electrical trouble spots can help prospective owners spot issues before buying.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

The 3.5-liter V6 engine produces approximately 295 horsepower and 263 lb-ft of torque, providing robust acceleration for highway driving. It is generally regarded as a dependable powerplant, though isolated owner reports note fuel system and misfire anomalies.

**Engine Stalling and Fuel Pump Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine stalling while driving or in traffic, accompanied by check engine lights and potential fuel delivery interruptions.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic inspection of the fuel system; faulty components like the fuel pump may require replacement.

**Engine Misfire and Exhaust Smoke**
- **Symptoms:** White smoke appearing in the exhaust followed by a blinking check engine light indicating an active engine misfire.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the ignition coils, spark plugs, and cylinder compression to identify the root cause of the misfire.

### 2.7L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.7-liter 4-cylinder engine generates around 185 horsepower and is paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission, offering a more efficiency-focused choice for daily commutes.

### 3.5L V6 Hybrid Powertrain

Combining a 3.5-liter V6 with electric motors and an electronic continuously variable transmission (ECVT), this setup delivers standard all-wheel drive and strong fuel economy for a three-row crossover.

## Transmission

### UA80F 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

This 8-speed automatic transmission is paired with the V6 gas engine configuration. While offering smooth highway cruising ratios, some owners have flagged high-pitched whining and shifting irregularities.

**Transmission Whining Noise and Hard Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** A high-pitched whining noise during acceleration, accompanied by rough shifting, hesitation, or unexpected downshifts.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition and consult a dealership or specialist for potential internal mechanical assessment or transmission replacement if failure occurs.

## Electrical

### Overhead Console and Audio System

Cabin electronics generally function well, though specific trim levels experience intermittent audio or overhead module anomalies.

**Loud Piercing Startup Noise**
- **Symptoms:** A sudden, extremely loud or squealing noise emanating from the overhead console or SOS speaker upon starting the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Dealer diagnosis and software updates or hardware replacement of affected communication modules.

## Exterior

### Sunroof / Moonroof

The available panoramic roof enhances cabin light but has drawn owner complaints regarding rare instances of spontaneous glass breakage.

**Spontaneous Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof or moonroof glass shatters or explodes outwardly while driving at highway speeds without prior impact.
- **Solution:** Replace the shattered glass panel and inspect the surrounding frame for structural stress or debris.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V682000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel pump fails, the engine can stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly with an improved one, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V012000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel pump fails, the engine can stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V244000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The text on the load capacity label may become illegible. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." If the driver cannot read the label, they may unknowingly overload the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GST will notify owners, and dealers will provide corrected load carrying capacity modification labels, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Toyota Highlander remains a practical, comfortable choice for families seeking a three-row crossover with a strong safety record. However, used car buyers should perform a thorough test drive to listen for transmission whining noises and verify proper shifting behavior. Checking service records for fuel system maintenance and ensuring all electrical features operate correctly will help secure a reliable purchase.
