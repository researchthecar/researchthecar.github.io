# 2016 Toyota Highlander Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Toyota Highlander is a popular and generally dependable midsize crossover SUV known for family-friendly practicality, comfortable ride quality, and strong resale value. While its mechanical foundation is robust, used-car buyers should review potential trouble spots—including occasional reports of brake and electrical system glitches—to ensure a solid purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.7L 4-Cylinder

A 2.7-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine making approximately 185 horsepower paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. This base option is less common and offers modest acceleration, but it shares the general durability associated with Toyota powertrains.

### 3.5L V6

A smooth 3.5-liter V6 engine generating around 270 horsepower matched to a 6-speed automatic transmission. This is the most popular powerplant for the Highlander, delivering strong performance and good long-term reliability for towing and highway driving.

### 3.5L V6 Hybrid

A hybrid powertrain combining a 3.5-liter V6 engine with electric motors to produce roughly 280 net horsepower routed through an electronically controlled CVT. It provides excellent fuel economy for a three-row crossover, though buyers should verify hybrid battery health.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A traditional 6-speed automatic transmission used on standard four-cylinder and V6 models, appreciated for its smooth shifting and straightforward reliability compared to more complex modern gearboxes.

### CVT Automatic Transmission

An electronically controlled continuously variable transmission dedicated to the hybrid model variant, designed to optimize fuel efficiency across varied driving conditions.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Standard disc braking system generally offers confident stopping power, though minor pedal hardware issues have been reported by some owners.

**Brake Pedal Pad Detachment**
- **Symptoms:** The rubber pad on the brake pedal can come loose or detach during operation, potentially causing a driver's foot to slip off the pedal.
- **Solution:** Inspect the rubber pedal cover and replace or securely reattach it if worn or loose.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical accessories and lighting are generally dependable, but rear light housings require inspection for weather-seal integrity.

**Tail Light Water Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Moisture or standing water accumulates inside the rear tailgate-mounted tail light assemblies, potentially causing bulb failure or electrical shorting.
- **Solution:** Inspect tail light seals for gaps and replace the light housing assembly if gaskets have failed.

### Seats and Trim

Cabin materials hold up well over time, though occasional interior trim squeaks and noises can develop in the rear seating rows.

**Second-Row Seat Squeak**
- **Symptoms:** An annoying squeaking noise originates from the second-row folding seat mechanism or cables when driving over bumpy roads.
- **Solution:** Lubricate the folding cables and hinge mechanisms or adjust seat latch components.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Toyota Highlander remains a sensible and dependable choice for used SUV buyers, backed by strong engine options and high overall build quality. While minor issues such as rear tail light moisture accumulation and interior trim noises have been noted by owners, regular pre-purchase inspections can easily screen out these concerns.
