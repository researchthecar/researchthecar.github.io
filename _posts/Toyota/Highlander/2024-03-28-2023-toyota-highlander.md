**2023 Toyota Highlander**

The 2023 Toyota Highlander is a reliable midsize SUV that offers a comfortable ride, spacious cabin, and advanced safety features. It is available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 2.5-liter 4-cylinder (203 horsepower, 184 lb-ft of torque), a 2.5-liter 4-cylinder hybrid (243 horsepower), a 3.5-liter V6 (295 horsepower, 263 lb-ft of torque), and a 3.5-liter V6 hybrid (360 horsepower). The Highlander is praised for its comfortable seats, generous cargo space, and excellent fuel economy. However, some owners have reported issues with certain components.

**Engine**

* **2.5-liter 4-cylinder**
    * **Oil Consumption:** Excessive oil consumption, resulting in low oil levels and potential engine damage.
        * Solution: Engine replacement or overhaul
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $10,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **2.5-liter 4-cylinder Hybrid**
    * **Battery Drain:** Premature battery discharge, leaving the vehicle unable to start.
        * Solution: Battery replacement
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500
        * Recalls: None Found
* **3.5-liter V6**
    * **Fuel Injector Failure:** Malfunctioning fuel injectors can cause misfires, reduced power, and increased fuel consumption.
        * Solution: Fuel injector replacement
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200
        * Recalls: None Found
* **3.5-liter V6 Hybrid**
    * **Water Pump Failure:** Leaking or failing water pump, leading to overheating and potential engine damage.
        * Solution: Water pump replacement
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **8-speed Automatic Transmission (AWD)**
    * **Shuddering or Slipping:** Transmission shudders or slips during gear changes, indicating potential clutch or solenoid issues.
        * Solution: Transmission repair or replacement
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Continuously Variable Transmission (FWD)**
    * **Noise or Vibration:** Excessive noise or vibration during acceleration or deceleration, pointing to belt or pulley problems.
        * Solution: CVT repair or replacement
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Struts:** Premature wear or failure of the front struts, resulting in a bumpy ride and poor handling.
    * Solution: Strut replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Electrical components remain active after the vehicle is turned off, leading to battery discharge.
    * Solution: Identify and repair the source of the power drain
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the source
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Latch:** Faulty tailgate latch that fails to engage properly, posing a safety hazard.
    * Solution: Tailgate latch replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:** Inoperative or malfunctioning climate control system, leading to issues with heating, ventilation, and cooling.
    * Solution: Climate control module replacement or repair
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2023 Toyota Highlander is generally a reliable SUV, but some owners have reported issues with certain components, particularly in the engine, transmission, and suspension systems. The severity and cost of these issues can vary, and some require extensive repairs. It is important to note that not all Highlanders will experience these issues, and regular maintenance and inspections can help mitigate potential problems.
