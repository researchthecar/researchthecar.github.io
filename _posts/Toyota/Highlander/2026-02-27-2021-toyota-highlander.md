# 2021 Toyota Highlander Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Toyota Highlander is generally praised for its comfort, safety features, and family-friendly versatility. However, used-car buyers should review specific powertrain and braking patterns reported by owners. While Toyota has a strong reputation for durability, certain recurring issues—particularly concerning transmission whine and intermittent brake hesitation—warrant careful pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

The 3.5-liter V6 engine produces approximately 295 horsepower and 263 lb-ft of torque, providing robust acceleration and towing capability for a midsize three-row crossover. It is generally robust, though occasional owner complaints involve hesitation or surging under specific low-speed acceleration conditions.

**Engine Stalling and Power Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle engine revs high during acceleration from low speeds while the vehicle barely moves, mimicking a slipping transmission or lag.
- **Solution:** Inspect powertrain control modules, throttle body, and transmission interaction; update software or replace components as diagnosed.

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Hybrid Powertrain

The 2.5-liter four-cylinder hybrid system pairs electric motors with a gasoline engine to deliver exceptional fuel economy around 36 mpg combined. Overall reliability for the hybrid system is high, though minor complaints surround fuel gauge and tank capacity reporting.

**Fuel System and Range Inaccuracies**
- **Symptoms:** Fuel gauge reads empty or fails to show correct fuel levels, preventing full tank fills and throwing off estimated driving range.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel sending units and tank baffling; replace faulty fuel pump or gauge assemblies if required.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The conventional gas model utilizes an 8-speed automatic transmission designed to optimize highway fuel efficiency and smooth gear selection. However, a notable cluster of owner reports highlights high-pitched whining noises and high-cost failures requiring careful pre-purchase verification.

**Transmission Whining Noise and Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A faint or high-pitched whining noise develops during acceleration, often worsening over time and potentially leading to complete transmission failure.
- **Solution:** Perform thorough diagnostic testing; dealership repairs may involve transmission replacement in severe cases.
- **Cost:** $8,000 to $11,000 for replacement

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides confident stopping power under normal conditions, but owners have documented intermittent hesitation or delayed pedal engagement at low speeds.

**Brake Pedal Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** When pressing the brake pedal to slow down in traffic, brakes temporarily fail to engage for a few seconds, causing the vehicle to lurch forward.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake booster, master cylinder, and ABS actuator modules; bleed brake lines and replace failing hydraulic components.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Warning Systems

Advanced driver assistance systems and electronic architecture streamline safety and convenience, though electrical issues and low-voltage triggers can occasionally disable multiple electronic aids at once.

**Multiple Warning Lights and Sensor Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard illuminates multiple warnings simultaneously—including ABS, Check Engine, Brake, and Blind Spot Monitor unavailable notices—often accompanied by stiff power steering due to low voltage abnormalities.
- **Solution:** Test battery health, charging system output, and wheel speed sensors; clear codes and update software modules as needed.

## Exterior

### Power Liftgate and Body Trim

Exterior body hardware is generally durable, though the power liftgate struts and motor controls are prone to sticking or failing over time.

**Power Liftgate Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Power liftgate fails to open or close fully, stops partway through its cycle, or requires manual assistance especially in colder weather.
- **Solution:** Reset liftgate height memory settings, or replace faulty strut motor controls and actuators.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V720000: Structure:Body:Bumpers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A detached front bumper cover can become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will repair or replace the upper and lower front bumper covers as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V865000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A short circuit may develop in the Occupant Classification System (OCS) sensor, preventing the front passenger air bag from deploying. An air bag that does not deploy during a crash increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the sensor as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V452000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." Tires with an insufficient load rating can be overloaded and fail, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** SET will inspect and replace the tires and information placard, as necessary free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V128000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly:Recliner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A seat back that fails to lock may not properly restrain an occupant during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the return springs in the seat back recliner assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Toyota Highlander remains a compelling choice for families seeking a spacious, well-equipped three-row crossover. Buyers should prioritize models with a documented service history and perform a thorough test drive to listen for any abnormal transmission whine or inspect brake responsiveness. Securing an extended warranty or comprehensive pre-purchase inspection can offer valuable peace of mind against potential high-cost powertrain repairs.
