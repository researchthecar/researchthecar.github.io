# 2020 Toyota Highlander Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Toyota Highlander is a popular midsize three-row crossover known for its comfort, safety features, and family-friendly utility. While it maintains a reputation for general durability, used-car buyers should review specific owner-reported pain points involving powertrain performance, braking sensations, and body components before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

The 3.5-liter V6 engine produces approximately 295 horsepower and 263 lb-ft of torque, offering strong acceleration and confident towing capability. It is generally regarded as a robust and dependable powerplant with few widespread mechanical defects.

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Hybrid Engine

The 2.5-liter hybrid four-cylinder system pairs with electric motors to deliver an efficient EPA-estimated fuel economy profile while producing a combined 243 horsepower. This system offers excellent efficiency for daily commuting and long-distance driving.

## Transmission

### Direct Shift 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with the V6 engine to deliver smooth gear ratios, though some owners have flagged durability and drivability concerns.

**Transmission Whining and Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report a noticeable whining noise, sudden loss of drive power, or unexpected transmission failure at moderate mileages.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic inspection by a certified technician and, in severe cases, transmission replacement.

**Shifting Delays and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Hesitation or momentary stalling when attempting to accelerate into moving traffic, followed by a harsh engagement or thump.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates or consult a dealership for transmission inspection and fluid condition checks.

### Electronically Controlled CVT (Hybrid)

The planetary gear-type continuously variable transmission used in hybrid models manages power blending between the gas engine and electric drive motors smoothly and reliably.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes and Regenerative Braking

The braking system provides adequate stopping power, though hybrid regenerative transitions can occasionally yield unexpected pedal feedback.

**Inconsistent Brake Pedal Feel**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a brief lurching sensation, momentary loss of braking pressure, or surging when applying brakes at low speeds or downhill.
- **Solution:** Software updates or brake system bleeding may be performed by dealerships to improve transition feel.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Power Liftgate

Cabin electronics generally function well, but occasional glitches in infotainment displays and power accessories have been reported by owners.

**Screen Blackout and Liftgate Latch Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The navigation or radio display screen goes completely blank while driving, or the rear power liftgate fails to latch fully and chimes continuously.
- **Solution:** System resets or replacement of faulty latch actuators and control modules may be required.

## Exterior

### Sunroof Glass

Exterior body panels fit tightly, though sporadic reports of tempered glass failure require careful inspection of the roof panel.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof glass can fracture or explode suddenly while driving at highway speeds without prior impact from debris.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the sunroof assembly through insurance or warranty claims where applicable.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V633000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Incorrect seat trim covers on one or both of the front seats can prevent the seat-mounted side air bag from deploying properly. Improper deployment of the seat-mounted side air bag increases the risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the front seats and replace the seat trim covers as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V162000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Due to an Engine Control Unit (ECU) programming error, fuel may not be correctly supplied to the engine while using the stop and restart feature. Improper fuel supply programming can result in a vehicle stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the ECU, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V720000: Structure:Body:Bumpers](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A detached front bumper cover can become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will repair or replace the upper and lower front bumper covers as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V865000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A short circuit may develop in the Occupant Classification System (OCS) sensor, preventing the front passenger air bag from deploying. An air bag that does not deploy during a crash increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the sensor as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Toyota Highlander remains a dependable and well-rounded family SUV, offering strong safety scores and practical powertrain choices. Buyers should thoroughly test drive the vehicle to check for transmission responsiveness and smooth brake transitions, while also inspecting exterior glass and electronic accessories.
