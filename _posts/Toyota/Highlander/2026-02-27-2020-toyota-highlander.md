# 2020 Toyota Highlander

### Overview
The 2020 Toyota Highlander, a popular mid-size SUV, generally exhibits strong reliability, a hallmark of the Toyota brand. It was introduced with a redesigned platform and a new hybrid powertrain option, alongside the familiar gasoline engine. Owners generally report a positive ownership experience with fewer unexpected repairs compared to many competitors. The vehicle's robust build quality and well-engineered components contribute to its reputation for dependability. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the Highlander's track record suggests a low probability of significant mechanical or electrical failures within its initial years of service. Routine maintenance is key to preserving its long-term reliability.

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 Toyota Highlander stands out as a highly reliable vehicle, particularly for its segment. The powertrain options, both the 3.5L V6 and the 2.5L Hybrid, have demonstrated excellent durability with no significant widespread issues reported. The transmission systems are also robust. Minor electrical concerns, such as occasional infotainment glitches or premature 12V battery failure, are the most commonly cited, though these are not pervasive. Exterior and interior components generally hold up well, reflecting Toyota's commitment to quality materials and construction. Overall, the 2020 Highlander is a dependable choice with a low likelihood of major mechanical problems, making it a sound investment for families seeking a reliable SUV.
