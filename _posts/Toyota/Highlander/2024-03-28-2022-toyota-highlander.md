**Introduction:**

The 2022 Toyota Highlander is a three-row SUV known for its reliability, versatility, and spacious interior. It comes with two engine options: a 3.5L V6 with 295 horsepower and 263 lb-ft of torque and a 2.5L four-cylinder hybrid with 243 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include an eight-speed automatic for the V6 and a continuously variable transmission (CVT) for the hybrid. While the Highlander is generally well-received, some owners have reported issues with certain components.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Owners may experience higher-than-normal oil consumption, requiring frequent top-ups.
    * Solution: Toyota has issued a technical service bulletin (TSB) to address the issue, which involves replacing specific engine components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
    * Recalls: None found

**2.5L Hybrid**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Eight-Speed Automatic (3.5L V6)**

* **Shift Hesitation:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may hesitate or jerk during gear changes, especially when shifting from first to second gear.
    * Solution: A software update from Toyota addresses the issue.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $100
    * Recalls: None found

**CVT (2.5L Hybrid)**

* **Noise and Vibration:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Owners may notice unusual noise or vibration during acceleration or deceleration.
    * Solution: Toyota has released a TSB for the issue, which involves replacing the chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Noise:**
    * Description/Symptoms: A clunking noise may be heard from the front suspension, especially when driving over uneven surfaces.
    * Solution: Toyota has issued a recall (22V-144) to replace the front strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (under warranty)

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle's battery may drain prematurely, causing difficulty starting.
    * Solution: A parasitic draw diagnosis is necessary to identify the source of the drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    * Recalls: None found

**Exterior**

* **Rear Bumper Cover Sagging:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear bumper cover may sag or detach from the body due to improper installation.
    * Solution: Toyota has issued a TSB to address the issue by replacing the rear bumper cover.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (under warranty)

**Interior**

* **HVAC Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) system may malfunction, causing irregular airflow or temperature control issues.
    * Solution: A software update or replacement of the HVAC control module may be required.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600

**Summary:**

While the 2022 Toyota Highlander is a commendable SUV, it has faced some issues with specific components. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences (shift hesitation) to more significant concerns (excessive oil consumption). Estimated repair costs also vary, but some repairs are covered under warranty. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and consider their impact on their purchase decision.
