# 2017 Toyota Highlander

### Overview
The 2017 Toyota Highlander is generally regarded as a reliable mid-size SUV, building on Toyota's reputation for dependability. It offers a comfortable ride, practical interior space, and a good range of features for its class. This model year saw the introduction of a refreshed powertrain and updated styling. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected repairs, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen more attention from mechanics and owners. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for the 2017 Highlander across its various configurations.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen display may freeze, become unresponsive, or reboot unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional reports of battery drain, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle. This can sometimes be linked to parasitic draw from certain modules.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the source of the drain, which may involve checking modules, aftermarket accessories, or replacing the battery.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Toyota Highlander stands out as a highly reliable vehicle, with its powertrain and chassis components demonstrating excellent durability. The most frequently cited technical concerns are related to the infotainment system, specifically screen freezing or unresponsiveness, and occasional reports of intermittent battery drain. These electrical issues, while not pervasive, are the primary areas where owners might encounter unexpected repair costs. Overall, the Highlander offers a strong value proposition for those seeking a dependable family SUV with a low probability of major mechanical failures.
