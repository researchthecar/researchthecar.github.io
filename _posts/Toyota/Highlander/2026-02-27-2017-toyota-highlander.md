# 2017 Toyota Highlander Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Toyota Highlander remains a popular choice in the midsize three-row crossover segment, offering a spacious interior and strong safety features. However, prospective buyers should review reported mechanical tendencies, particularly concerning powertrain refinement, electrical accessories, and specific mechanical components. While many owners enjoy trouble-free operation, investigating service history and verifying whether specific component updates or recalls have been performed is essential.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

The 3.5-liter V6 engine produces approximately 295 horsepower and 263 lb-ft of torque, providing robust acceleration and towing capability for a family SUV. It is generally regarded as durable, though owners have reported occasional fuel delivery and electrical concerns.

### 2.7L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.7-liter 4-cylinder engine generates around 185 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque, paired exclusively with front-wheel drive. While economical, it offers modest acceleration when the vehicle is fully loaded with passengers and cargo.

### Fuel Pump & Delivery

Supplies pressurized fuel to the engine injectors, ensuring consistent combustion and performance.

**Fuel Pump Failure and Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience sudden engine stalling, rough idling, or a failure to start due to an inoperable fuel pump.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the faulty fuel pump assembly with an updated unit.

## Transmission

### Direct Shift 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

This 8-speed automatic transmission is paired with the V6 engine to optimize highway fuel economy and power delivery. Some owners have reported shift quality issues and mechanical noise requiring professional evaluation.

**Transmission Whining and Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice an audible whining noise during coasting or acceleration, accompanied by delayed shifting or hesitation.
- **Solution:** Consult dealership technical service bulletins for software updates or mechanical inspection; severe cases may require transmission replacement.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with the 4-cylinder engine, this traditional 6-speed automatic offers straightforward gear selection and proven mechanical simplicity.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Responsible for hydraulic stopping power and brake assist functions under varying driving conditions.

**Brake Power Low Warning**
- **Symptoms:** A dashboard warning message indicating low brake power may appear, accompanied by a hard brake pedal feel or starting difficulties.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake booster and vacuum pump assembly, and replace faulty components as necessary.
- **Cost:** $4,000

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Manages power distribution, accessory operation, and the automatic start-stop ignition cycle.

**Auto Start-Stop Stalling and Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may lose electrical power completely or fail to restart when idling at a stop light with the auto start-stop feature engaged.
- **Solution:** Disable the auto start-stop feature manually if issues persist, or test the battery and charging system for excessive parasitic draw.

## Exterior

### Body Structure & Paint

Protects the vehicle cabin and framework from environmental elements and structural wear.

**Paint Bubbling and Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Paint on exterior body panels, such as doors or hoods, may bubble, peel, or show early signs of rust.
- **Solution:** Professional body shop evaluation and repounding or sealing of affected panels.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18E107000: Electrical System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The ECM damage may result in the engine stalling, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Wanda will notify owners, and service centers will replace the windshields, and inspect the ECM for damage, having it replaced, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V520000: Equipment](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The fasteners for the roof rails may not be torqued properly. The roof rails may loosen and possibly detach from the vehicle, becoming a road hazard and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GST will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and properly torque the fasteners, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V295000: Tires:Temporary/Emergency Spare Tire](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A spare tire that is not inflated to the proper value may not perform as intended, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Gulf States will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the spare tires, adjusting their pressure as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V682000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel pump fails, the engine can stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly with an improved one, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Toyota Highlander is a capable and spacious family hauler, but used buyers should perform targeted checks during a pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to transmission shift quality and listen for any unusual whining noises during a test drive. Additionally, verify the health of the electrical system, test the auto start-stop function, and check exterior paint condition to ensure long-term satisfaction.
