**2018 Toyota Highlander**

The 2018 Toyota Highlander is a midsize SUV that offers a spacious cabin, a powerful engine, and a wide range of features. It is available with three engine options: a 2.7-liter four-cylinder, a 3.5-liter V6, and a 3.5-liter V6 hybrid. The four-cylinder engine produces 185 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque, while the V6 engine produces 295 horsepower and 263 lb-ft of torque. The hybrid powertrain combines the V6 engine with an electric motor to produce a combined output of 306 horsepower. All three engines are paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission.

People who like the 2018 Toyota Highlander praise its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and fuel-efficient engine options. However, some people have also complained about its lack of advanced safety features, its high price, and its bland styling.

**Engine**

* **2.7-liter four-cylinder**
    * **Piston ring failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, blue smoke from the exhaust
        * Solution: Replace piston rings
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **3.5-liter V6**
    * **Timing chain guide failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise from the engine, loss of power
        * Solution: Replace timing chain guide
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **3.5-liter V6 hybrid**
    * **Hybrid battery failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, warning lights on the dashboard
        * Solution: Replace hybrid battery
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $5,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **Eight-speed automatic**
    * **Shifting problems**
        * Description/Symptoms: Rough shifting, hesitation when shifting, slipping gears
        * Solution: Replace transmission fluid, reprogram transmission computer
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension strut failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise from the front end, uneven tire wear
    * Solution: Replace front suspension struts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electrical shorts**
    * Description/Symptoms: Blown fuses, flickering lights, loss of power
    * Solution: Repair or replace faulty wiring
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the cabin through the sunroof
    * Solution: Replace sunroof seal
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard rattles**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattles or squeaks from the dashboard
    * Solution: Identify and fix the source of the rattle
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **HVAC system failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: AC or heater not working, blower motor noise
    * Solution: Repair or replace faulty HVAC components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2018 Toyota Highlander is a reliable and well-rounded SUV. However, it is not without its issues. Some of the most common problems reported by owners include engine problems, transmission problems, suspension problems, electrical problems, and interior problems. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the extent of the damage.
