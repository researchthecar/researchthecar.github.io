# 2024 Toyota Highlander Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Toyota Highlander, prospective buyers generally expect high levels of everyday practicality and solid build quality. While the vehicle remains a popular family hauling choice, owner reports filed with safety authorities point to a few recurring areas of concern, particularly regarding powertrain behavior, electronics, and convenience features. Reviewing these patterns can help shoppers identify potential trouble spots before finalizing a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.4-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 265 horsepower and 310 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard gas-powered option. It delivers robust everyday acceleration, though buyers should watch for occasional reports concerning fuel system odors.

**Fuel Odor and Vapor**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers notice an intermittent gasoline smell or vapor escaping near the fuel tank area, particularly after the engine cycles or runs for extended periods.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the fuel cap, lines, and EVAP system for potential leaks or faulty seals.

### 2.5L Hybrid Inline-4

The 2.5-liter hybrid inline-4 powertrain pairs a naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine with electric motors to generate a net 243 horsepower. It offers exceptional fuel efficiency for daily commuting and family transport.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The conventional gas model utilizes an 8-speed automatic transmission designed to balance highway cruising efficiency with responsive gear changes across front-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations.

**Powertrain Malfunction and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly experience hesitation, random stalling on the highway, or complete loss of reverse and drive capabilities due to internal component issues.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis by a certified dealership or transmission specialist; major internal failures may necessitate transmission replacement or rebuilding.

### Electronically Controlled CVT

The hybrid model utilizes an electronically controlled continuously variable transmission (e-CVT) tailored to manage power blending smoothly between the gas engine and electric drive system.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Braking system complaints frequently center on the nuanced interaction between traditional hydraulic brakes and regenerative hybrid components at low speeds.

**Regenerative Braking Inconsistency**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience pulsing brake pedals, temporary decreases in braking pressure when driving downhill, or sudden unexpected forward jolts when the system transitions between regenerative and conventional friction braking.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and software calibration for the braking control modules to ensure proper system synchronization.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Display

Electrical components govern extensive modern driver aids and large displays, which occasionally manifest software bugs and connectivity quirks.

**Infotainment Screen Darkening and Navigation Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** The main touchscreen display intermittently turns excessively dark, requiring a vehicle restart, or wireless Apple CarPlay maps the vehicle's position incorrectly in fields or on wrong streets.
- **Solution:** Check for available software and firmware updates through a local dealership to resolve display stability and mapping communication errors.

## Exterior

### Seats and Liftgate

Cabin convenience features like folding seats and power tailgates generally function well but have prompted occasional owner grievances regarding physical latching and sensor consistency.

**Second-Row Latch and Liftgate Operation Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Second-row seats may fail to latch securely when adjusted forward, and powered liftgates can occasionally drop unexpectedly or fail to close properly via control buttons.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat latch mechanisms and liftgate struts or sensors for mechanical binding, and lubricate or replace faulty hardware as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V419000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." Tires with an insufficient load rating may be overloaded and fail, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GST will inspect and replace the tires as necessary, and install a new tire information label as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V452000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." Tires with an insufficient load rating can be overloaded and fail, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** SET will inspect and replace the tires and information placard, as necessary free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V548000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." A vehicle with an incorrect maximum capacity weight value may be overloaded, which can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V595000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** An instrument panel display that does not show critical information can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the instrument panel software over-the-air (OTA) for non-PHEV vehicles, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V744000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to freeze or display a blank screen when the vehicle is in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that fails to display an image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the parking assist software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V872000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other:Anchorage](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A seat belt that does not properly restrain an occupant increases the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reinstall the seat belt anchor with a new bolt, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V128000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly:Recliner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Highlander&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A seat back that fails to lock may not properly restrain an occupant during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the return springs in the seat back recliner assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

Overall, this model offers a spacious and versatile cabin accompanied by efficient powertrain choices, making it a staple in the midsize SUV segment. However, used car buyers should perform a thorough test drive to check for smooth transmission operation, responsive braking transitions, and fully functional cabin electronics. Verifying service records and ensuring any open recall remedies have been completed will help secure a more dependable ownership experience.
