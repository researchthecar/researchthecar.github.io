# 2023 Toyota Mirai Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2023 Toyota Mirai, buyers are looking at a hydrogen fuel cell electric vehicle that offers a smooth, zero-emission ride and premium comfort. While advanced fuel cell technology provides a unique driving experience, prospective owners should thoroughly review owner feedback regarding propulsion and powertrain performance before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### Hydrogen Fuel Cell Electric Drive

The hydrogen fuel cell electric powertrain generates electricity on board to power the electric drive motor, delivering refined performance without traditional gasoline consumption. Because hydrogen fueling infrastructure remains limited to specific regions, buyers must carefully evaluate local fuel availability alongside overall propulsion reliability.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported experiencing a sudden and total loss of motive power while operating the vehicle in traffic, accompanied by multiple cascading warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle thoroughly inspected by a certified hydrogen technician to diagnose potential fuel cell stack or power control module faults.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct Drive

The single-speed direct drive transmission delivers seamless power directly from the electric motor to the rear wheels without traditional gear shifting. This setup is mechanically simple and generally robust, contributing to a smooth driving experience.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V124000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Mirai&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Due to incorrect programming of the parking-assist electronic control unit (ECU) software, the rearview camera image may not immediately display within a couple of seconds after the shifter is placed into reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the parking assist ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V744000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Mirai&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to freeze or display a blank screen when the vehicle is in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that fails to display an image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the parking assist software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Toyota Mirai brings innovative hydrogen fuel cell technology to the used market, offering distinct styling and a quiet, eco-friendly ride. Prospective buyers should confirm the operational health of the fuel cell system and ensure convenient access to hydrogen refueling stations before finalizing their purchase.
