**Introduction**

The 2016 Toyota Mirai is a hydrogen-powered fuel cell electric vehicle (FCEV) that produces electricity through a chemical reaction between hydrogen and oxygen. It has a range of up to 312 miles on a single tank of hydrogen and a top speed of 111 mph. The Mirai is known for its advanced technology, environmental friendliness, and spacious interior. However, like any vehicle, it has some reported issues that owners have encountered.

**Suspension**

* **Rear Shock Absorbers**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive bouncing or swaying of the rear end, especially over bumps.
    * Solution: Replacement of the rear shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **12-Volt Battery**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the vehicle, dim headlights, electrical system malfunctions.
    * Solution: Replacement of the 12-volt battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Fuel Cell Stack**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced power output, increased hydrogen consumption, check engine light.
    * Solution: Replacement or repair of the fuel cell stack.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $10,000 - $25,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 22V269000

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture accumulation inside the headlight assemblies.
    * Solution: Replacement or repair of the headlights with improved seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattle**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noisy rattling or vibration from the dashboard, particularly at low speeds or when driving on rough roads.
    * Solution: Tightening or replacement of loose components in the dashboard.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Steering Wheel Controls**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning buttons or switches on the steering wheel, causing issues with cruise control, audio controls, or phone connectivity.
    * Solution: Replacement of the steering wheel control unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2016 Toyota Mirai is a technologically advanced vehicle with generally good reliability. However, it is important to be aware of the potential issues mentioned above, particularly the costly fuel cell stack issue. If you are considering purchasing a Mirai, it is recommended to have it inspected by a qualified mechanic and to factor in the potential costs associated with these repairs.
