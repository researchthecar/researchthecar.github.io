**2020 Toyota Mirai**

The 2020 Toyota Mirai is a hydrogen fuel cell-powered sedan. It has a single engine option, a 182-horsepower fuel cell with an electric motor. The only transmission option is a continuously variable transmission (CVT).

People like the Mirai for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and impressive fuel economy. However, some people have complained about its high price and limited driving range.

**Engine**

**182hp Fuel Cell**

**Fuel Cell Malfunction:**
Description/Symptoms: The fuel cell may malfunction, causing the vehicle to lose power.
Solution: Replacing the fuel cell.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $10,000 - $15,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT**

**Transmission Shudder:**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission may shudder during acceleration or deceleration.
Solution: Replacing the transmission fluid and filter.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**No Issues Found**

**No Issues Found**

**Exterior**

**No Issues Found**

**Interior**

**Faulty Climate Control Unit:**
Description/Symptoms: The climate control unit may malfunction, causing the air conditioning or heating to not work properly.
Solution: Replacing the climate control unit.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Summary**

The 2020 Toyota Mirai is a reliable vehicle with few reported problems. The most serious issue is the fuel cell malfunction, which can be expensive to fix. However, this issue is rare. Overall, the Mirai is a good choice for those looking for a fuel-efficient and environmentally friendly vehicle.
