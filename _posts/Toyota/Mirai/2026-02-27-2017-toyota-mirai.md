# 2017 Toyota Mirai Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 Toyota Mirai, buyers are looking at a pioneering hydrogen fuel cell electric vehicle available in select regions like California. Because it represents alternative-fuel technology, understanding its unique powertrain and owner-reported quirks is essential. While NHTSA owner complaints are subjective reports rather than proven engineering defects, reviewing them helps highlight potential owner frustrations during everyday use.

---

## Engine

### Hydrogen Fuel Cell Stack / Electric Motor

The 2017 Toyota Mirai utilizes a hydrogen fuel cell stack combined with an electric motor generating approximately 151 horsepower and 247 lb-ft of torque. This alternative powertrain delivers smooth electric propulsion but requires access to specialized hydrogen refueling stations. Buyers should verify local fuel availability and system health before purchase.

**Fuel System Incident Report**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have filed reports regarding unexpected vehicle incidents while operating the fuel and propulsion systems.
- **Solution:** Have the fuel cell stack and hydrogen storage infrastructure inspected thoroughly by a certified hydrogen-trained Toyota technician.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Reduction Gear

Power is transmitted to the front wheels via a direct single-speed reduction gear automatic transmission. This setup is generally mechanically simple and highly reliable compared to traditional multi-speed gearboxes.

## Interior

### Mirrors and Dashboard

Exterior visibility components generally function well, though specific lighting angles can cause reflections.

**Dashboard Glare on Side Mirrors**
- **Symptoms:** Bright sunlight reflects the dashboard layout onto the exterior side view mirrors, obstructing clear visibility.
- **Solution:** Using a non-reflective dashboard cover or mat can help minimize distracting glare.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Safety features are designed to protect occupants, though occasional electronic glitches can occur.

**Unintended Automatic Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The automatic emergency braking system may occasionally engage abruptly without any obstruction ahead, startling drivers.
- **Solution:** Check for any applicable dealer software updates or sensor recalibrations addressing forward collision control modules.

### Seat Belts

Cabin safety hardware meets standard specifications, but usability for child restraints may require extra effort.

**Difficult Child Seat Latch Access**
- **Symptoms:** The lower child safety seat anchors are deeply embedded and challenging to locate or attach due to seat padding design.
- **Solution:** Carefully maneuver child seats using specialized guide clips or alternative securing methods if compatible.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Toyota Mirai offers an innovative hydrogen-powered driving experience, but prospective buyers must carefully weigh local fuel station infrastructure and specialized maintenance requirements. While major mechanical failures are rare, reviewing owner feedback regarding driver-assist sensors and cabin ergonomics ensures you know what to expect. Always secure a professional pre-purchase inspection from a technician certified to service hydrogen fuel cell vehicles.
