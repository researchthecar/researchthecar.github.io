# 2024 Toyota Mirai Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Toyota Mirai represents a unique zero-emission driving experience, utilizing a hydrogen fuel cell electric powertrain to generate electricity on board. Because hydrogen infrastructure remains extremely limited and owner complaint data for this model year is minimal, prospective buyers should carefully weigh the regional availability of fueling stations against the vehicle's advanced technology and smooth ride.

---

## Engine

### Hydrogen Fuel Cell Electric Powertrain

This advanced system combines a compressed hydrogen fuel cell stack with an electric motor, generating approximately 182 horsepower and 300 lb-ft of torque. The powertrain delivers silent, emissions-free acceleration, though long-term reliability depends heavily on specialized maintenance and fuel cell care.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct Drive Transmission

A single-speed direct drive automatic transmission transfers power seamlessly to the rear wheels without gear changes. The setup is mechanically simple and generally robust, offering a refined and predictable driving dynamic.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V744000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Mirai&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to freeze or display a blank screen when the vehicle is in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that fails to display an image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the parking assist software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V341000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Mirai&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 101, "Control and Displays" and in addition, for the subject Mirai vehicles, FMVSS 305, "Electric-powered vehicles: Electrolyte Spillage and Electrical Shock Protection." An instrument panel display that fails to show critical safety.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the combination meter software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Toyota Mirai is a technologically sophisticated hydrogen fuel cell vehicle that offers a unique alternative to traditional battery-electric cars. However, due to its specialized infrastructure requirements and limited historical complaint data, buyers must ensure local hydrogen fueling stations are accessible before committing to purchase.
