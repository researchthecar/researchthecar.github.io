# 2019 Toyota Mirai

### Overview
The 2019 Toyota Mirai represents a significant step in alternative fuel vehicles, utilizing a hydrogen fuel cell system to generate electricity. This innovative powertrain offers a unique driving experience with zero tailpipe emissions. As a first-generation production vehicle in many markets, its reliability is a key consideration for potential owners. While the core fuel cell technology has proven robust, like any complex system, specific components and associated systems require careful examination. This analysis focuses on documented reliability trends and potential concerns beyond standard maintenance, providing insights into the long-term viability and ownership experience of the 2019 Mirai.

---


## Engine

### Fuel Cell Stack
**Reduced Performance Over Time**
- **Symptoms:** Gradual decrease in power output, reduced range, increased hydrogen consumption.
- **Solution:** Regular diagnostic checks, potential stack refurbishment or replacement.
- **Cost:** $5,000 - $15,000+

**Water Management System Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Error codes related to coolant or water circulation, potential freezing in cold weather, reduced efficiency.
- **Solution:** Inspection and repair of pumps, valves, and sensors within the water management system.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,200

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Toyota Mirai demonstrates a generally strong reliability record, particularly for its innovative fuel cell powertrain. The primary areas of concern revolve around the long-term performance and potential degradation of the fuel cell stack, which can be a significant repair cost. Issues with the water management system and occasional software glitches in the infotainment system are also noted. The 12V auxiliary battery has shown some instances of premature failure. However, many core components, including the electric motor, transmission, suspension, and exterior/interior elements, have not reported widespread issues. Owners should be aware of the specialized nature of fuel cell maintenance and the associated costs, especially for stack-related repairs.
