**Introduction:**

The 2023 Toyota Mirai is a hydrogen-powered fuel cell vehicle that offers an impressive range of up to 402 miles on a single fill. It's equipped with a 1.5-liter 3-cylinder engine that serves as a generator for the electric motor, producing a combined horsepower of 182 and 247 lb-ft of torque. The Mirai is praised for its spacious interior, advanced safety features, and smooth driving experience, while some find its exterior styling polarizing.

**Engine**

**1.5-liter 3-cylinder**

* **Issue:** Engine coolant leak
     * Description/Symptoms: Leaking coolant, engine overheating
     * Solution: Replace coolant hoses or engine seals
     * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
     * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Issue:** Front suspension noise
     * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noise while driving over bumps
     * Solution: Replace or lubricate front suspension components such as control arms or bushings
     * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
     * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Issue:** 12-volt battery failure
     * Description/Symptoms: Battery warning light, difficulty starting the vehicle
     * Solution: Replace 12-volt battery
     * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
     * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Issue:** Side mirror condensation
     * Description/Symptoms: Moisture accumulating inside the side mirrors
     * Solution: Replace or repair side mirror
     * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
     * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Issue:** Climate control malfunction
     * Description/Symptoms: Insufficient heating or cooling, strange noises from climate control system
     * Solution: Replace or repair climate control components
     * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
     * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

While the 2023 Toyota Mirai is a technologically advanced and environmentally friendly vehicle, it has experienced a few issues. Engine coolant leaks and front suspension noise are notable concerns. Other issues include 12-volt battery failure, side mirror condensation, and climate control malfunctions. These issues can be costly to repair, ranging from a few hundred dollars to over $800. It's important for owners to be aware of these potential problems and address them promptly to ensure the safety and reliability of their Mirai.
