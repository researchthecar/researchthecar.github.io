**Introduction:**
The 2022 Toyota Mirai is a hydrogen-powered sedan that boasts an impressive fuel efficiency rating of up to 67 mpg equivalent. It is available with a single engine and transmission option: a 182-horsepower, 247-pound-foot electric motor paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). Despite its eco-friendly credentials, the Mirai has faced some issues reported by owners.

**Engine**
**Hydrogen Fuel Cell System:**
* **Issue:** Fuel cell stack degradation
* Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, reduced fuel economy, and increased emissions
* Solution: Replace fuel cell stack
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+
* Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 22V341000, 20V392000

**Transmission**
**Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT):**
* **Issue:** No issues found

**Suspension**
* **Issue:** No issues found

**Electrical**
* **Issue:** 12V battery drain
* Description/Symptoms: Vehicle fails to start, electrical system malfunctions
* Solution: Replace 12V battery
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
* Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
* **Issue:** Headlight condensation
* Description/Symptoms: Moisture inside headlights
* Solution: Replace headlight assembly
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
* **Issue:** Infotainment system glitches
* Description/Symptoms: System freezes, touch screen unresponsive
* Solution: Software update
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
* Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**
While the 2022 Toyota Mirai offers excellent fuel efficiency, it has faced some issues related to the fuel cell system and vehicle electrical systems. The severity of these issues varies, with some requiring costly repairs. Despite these issues, many owners appreciate the Mirai's advanced technology and environmentally friendly nature.
