# 2022 Toyota Mirai Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Toyota Mirai, buyers are looking at an advanced hydrogen fuel cell electric vehicle that offers a unique driving experience. While owner feedback highlights innovative engineering, some drivers have reported concerns regarding propulsion and advanced driver assistance systems. Careful inspection and understanding of hydrogen refueling infrastructure are recommended before purchase.

---

## Engine

### Electrical and Fuel Propulsion System

Manages power distribution across the fuel cell system, high-voltage battery, and cabin electronics.

**Main Battery and Display Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced dashboard displays shutting off unexpectedly and the main vehicle battery failing to respond, preventing the vehicle from starting.
- **Solution:** Have the auxiliary and main electrical systems, battery connections, and charging components tested by a qualified specialist.

## Transmission

### Hydrogen Fuel Cell Powertrain

The Toyota Mirai utilizes a hydrogen fuel cell stack combined with an electric motor generating approximately 182 horsepower and 300 lb-ft of torque. It delivers smooth, zero-emission propulsion, though its reliability depends heavily on proper maintenance of specialized fuel cell components.

**Motive Power Loss and Acceleration Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported instances where the vehicle experiences sudden deceleration, hesitation when pressing the accelerator pedal, or unexpected loss of motive power while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic evaluation by a certified technician is required to inspect the fuel cell stack, propulsion system sensors, and related control modules.

### Direct-Drive Single-Speed Transmission

The Mirai uses a single-speed direct-drive transmission designed to seamlessly transfer electric motor power to the rear wheels. This setup is generally mechanically simple and durable, with no major recurring owner complaints noted.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Integrates cameras and radar to support automatic emergency braking and semi-autonomous driving features.

**Advanced Driver Assistance Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have noted instances where emergency braking systems or advanced drive packages failed to engage or react as expected during potential collision scenarios.
- **Solution:** Inspect sensor alignments, radar units, and software calibration through an authorized service center.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V239000: Electronic Stability Control (Esc):Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Mirai&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The Skid Control ECU software may not turn on the Vehicle Stability Control (VSC) system when the vehicle is restarted after the driver has disabled the VSC system. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 126, "Electronic Stability Control Systems." Driving with a deactivated stability.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Skid Control ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The Toyota Mirai presents an intriguing option for buyers interested in hydrogen fuel cell technology, offering refined rear-wheel-drive electric performance. However, potential owners should carefully verify the operational history of the electrical and propulsion systems, as well as ensure local hydrogen fuel availability.
