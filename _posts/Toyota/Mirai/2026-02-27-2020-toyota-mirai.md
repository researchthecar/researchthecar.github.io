# 2020 Toyota Mirai Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Toyota Mirai represents a unique zero-emission hydrogen fuel cell vehicle offering distinctive styling and an alternative to traditional battery-electric cars. While overall complaint volume for this model year is very low, prospective buyers should always evaluate basic powertrain health and vehicle control systems carefully during a test drive.

---

## Engine

### Hydrogen Fuel Cell Electric Powertrain

The 2020 Mirai utilizes a proton exchange membrane fuel cell generating electricity to power an electric motor producing around 151 horsepower and 247 lb-ft of torque. This innovative setup generally delivers smooth performance, though specialized maintenance and refueling infrastructure remain key factors for owners.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct Drive Automatic

Power is transmitted to the front wheels via a single-speed direct drive automatic transmission suited for electric propulsion. This setup features minimal moving parts compared to conventional gearboxes, leading to a largely trouble-free mechanical history.

## Safety

### Throttle and Acceleration Control

Isolated reports regarding speed control require careful inspection of driver inputs and electronic throttle systems.

**Unintended Acceleration**
- **Symptoms:** An owner reported that the vehicle suddenly accelerated backward out of a driveway at high speed while attempting a slow maneuver, resulting in a loss of control and a collision.
- **Solution:** Thoroughly inspect the accelerator pedal assembly, floor mat positioning, and electronic throttle control modules for proper operation.

The 2020 Toyota Mirai is a rare fuel-cell vehicle with minimal reported reliability issues in public databases. Buyers should verify proper maintenance history, ensure local hydrogen fueling availability, and inspect vehicle control systems prior to purchase.
