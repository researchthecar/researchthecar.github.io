The 2000 Toyota Land Cruiser is a full-size SUV known for its reliability, off-road capability, and longevity. It came with a 4.7L 2UZ-FE V8 engine producing 230 horsepower and 320 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally praise its robust build quality, comfortable ride, and go-anywhere attitude. Criticisms often focus on its fuel economy and somewhat dated technology.

**Engine**

**4.7L 2UZ-FE V8**

*   **Valve Cover Gasket Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the valve cover gaskets, resulting in burning oil smells and potential oil loss.
    *   Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

*   **Cooling System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Radiator leaks, thermostat failures, and water pump failures can lead to overheating.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty components (radiator, thermostat, water pump).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Water pump: $300 - $600, Radiator: $400 - $800, Thermostat: $100 - $300

*   **Oxygen Sensor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light illumination, poor fuel economy, and rough running.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty oxygen sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300

**Transmission**

**A343F 4-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or delayed shifts, slipping between gears, and general transmission performance degradation.
    *   Solution: Fluid flush and filter replacement, or transmission rebuild/replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Fluid flush: $150 - $300, Rebuild: $2,500 - $4,500, Replacement: $3,500 - $6,000+

**Suspension**

*   **Suspension Height Control (AHC) System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The AHC system can develop leaks in the lines or the pump can fail, causing the vehicle to sit unevenly or not adjust ride height.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace leaking lines, or replace the AHC pump. In some cases, owners opt to replace the AHC system with a traditional coil spring suspension.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: AHC Pump: $800 - $1500+, Coil Conversion: $1500 - $3000+

*   **Worn Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the suspension, loose steering, and poor handling.
    *   Solution: Replace worn bushings (sway bar bushings, control arm bushings).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ depending on the bushings replaced.

**Electrical**

*   **Starter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle fails to start, clicking noise when attempting to start.
    *   Solution: Replace the starter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600

*   **Alternator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, battery not charging, vehicle stalling.
    *   Solution: Replace the alternator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $700

**Exterior**

*   **Rust (Frame and Body)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust formation on the frame, undercarriage, and body panels, particularly in areas with road salt exposure. This is especially critical to inspect around suspension mounting points and the rear of the vehicle.
    *   Solution: Rust repair, rust converter application, or frame replacement (in severe cases).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies greatly depending on severity, ranging from $100 for minor rust repair to $5,000+ for extensive frame work.

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof motor failure, track issues, or leaks.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the sunroof motor or track components. Reseal the sunroof.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+

**Summary**

The 2000 Toyota Land Cruiser, while generally reliable, has some common issues that potential buyers should be aware of. Engine issues like valve cover gasket leaks and cooling system problems, along with transmission issues such as harsh shifting, are important to inspect. Suspension, electrical, and exterior issues are also areas of concern, especially rust. The severity and cost of these repairs can vary significantly, so a thorough pre-purchase inspection is highly recommended.
