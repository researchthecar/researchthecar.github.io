The 2010 Toyota Land Cruiser is a full-size SUV known for its off-road capabilities, reliability, and luxurious features. It came standard with a 5.7L V8 engine producing 381 horsepower and 401 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the Land Cruiser for its durability, comfort, and go-anywhere attitude. Criticisms frequently include its poor fuel economy and large size, making it difficult to maneuver in tight spaces.

**Engine**

**5.7L V8 (3UR-FE)**

*   **Air Injection Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light illuminated. Diagnostic trouble codes (DTCs) related to the air injection system (e.g., P2440, P2442) are stored. The engine may run rough or exhibit reduced performance.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty air injection pump(s). Some owners have also opted for bypass kits, although these may not be legal in all areas.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Exhaust Manifold Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Ticking or hissing sound, especially upon cold start. Smell of exhaust fumes in the cabin. Visible cracks on the exhaust manifold.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the cracked exhaust manifold(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Visible oil leaks on the engine. Oil drips on the ground. Burning oil smell. Low oil level.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gaskets or seals. Common leak areas include the valve cover gaskets, timing chain cover, and oil pan gasket.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+ depending on the source of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic (AB60F)**

*   **Torque Converter Shudder**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibration, especially during light acceleration or when maintaining a constant speed. Can be more pronounced at lower speeds.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the torque converter or a transmission flush using a Toyota-approved fluid. In severe cases, a transmission rebuild may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $3000+ depending on the severity and chosen solution.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Hard Shifting/Delayed Engagement**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or jerky shifts between gears. Noticeable delay when shifting from Park or Neutral into Drive or Reverse.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement. If the problem persists, valve body issues or internal damage may require professional diagnosis and repair or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Hydraulic Suspension Issues (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sagging suspension, uneven ride height, failure to adjust height properly, warning lights on the dashboard. Leaks from the hydraulic accumulators or struts.
    *   Solution: Replacement of faulty accumulators, struts, or height sensors. Repair or replacement of hydraulic lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $3000+ depending on the component that needs replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rear Air Suspension Problems (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, compressor failure, warning lights.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the air springs, compressor, or ride height sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Navigation System/Radio Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, unresponsive touchscreen, inability to play audio, loss of navigation function.
    *   Solution: Software update (if available). Replacement of the head unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (software update) - $1500+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Window/Door Lock Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent operation of power windows or door locks. Complete failure of one or more window/lock mechanisms.
    *   Solution: Replacement of faulty window motors, door lock actuators, or wiring harness repair.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+ per window/door.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust (Frame/Undercarriage)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Visible rust on the frame, especially in areas exposed to road salt.
    *   Solution: Rust removal, treatment, and protective coating. In severe cases, frame repair or replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $5000+ depending on the severity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing on the dashboard, particularly in areas exposed to sunlight.
    *   Solution: Dashboard replacement or dashboard cover.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (cover) - $2000+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2010 Toyota Land Cruiser, while generally reliable, can experience several issues, particularly related to the air injection system, exhaust manifolds, and hydraulic/air suspension (if equipped). Electrical issues with the navigation system and power windows/locks are also relatively common. Rust on the frame can be a significant concern, especially in regions with harsh winters. While some repairs are relatively inexpensive, others, such as suspension and engine work, can be quite costly. Prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle, paying close attention to the suspension, undercarriage for rust, and engine for signs of leaks or unusual noises.
