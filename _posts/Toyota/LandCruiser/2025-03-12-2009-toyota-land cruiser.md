The 2009 Toyota Land Cruiser is a full-size SUV renowned for its off-road capability, reliability, and luxurious features. The sole engine option for this model year is the 5.7L 3UR-FE V8 engine, producing 381 horsepower and 401 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners appreciate the Land Cruiser's robust build quality, spacious interior, and ability to tackle challenging terrain. Common dislikes include its poor fuel economy and high purchase price.

**Engine**

**5.7L 3UR-FE V8**
*   **Valve Cover Gasket Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks may develop around the valve cover gaskets, leading to a burning smell and potential oil loss.
    *   Solution: Replacing the valve cover gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Water Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The water pump can fail, leading to overheating. Symptoms include engine overheating, coolant leaks, and a whining noise from the engine.
    *   Solution: Replacing the water pump.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibrations at low speeds, especially during light acceleration. This can be caused by a failing torque converter.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the torque converter or transmission.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Occasional harsh or delayed shifting between gears.
    * Solution: Fluid flush, valve body replacement, or transmission replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Problems (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The AHC (Active Height Control) system can experience issues such as sagging, uneven ride height, or failure to adjust. This is more common in vehicles with higher mileage.
    *   Solution: Replacing air struts, sensors, or the air compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ per component.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Navigation System/Radio Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The factory navigation and radio systems can experience failures, including screen freezing, loss of function, or complete failure.
    *   Solution: Replacing or repairing the head unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Seat Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: Power seats may become inoperable or move intermittently.
    * Solution: Repairing or replacing the seat motor or switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust on Undercarriage**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust can develop on the undercarriage, especially in regions with harsh winters or coastal environments. This can affect various components, including the frame, suspension, and exhaust system.
    *   Solution: Rust remediation and prevention measures, such as undercoating. Replacement of severely corroded parts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $5,000+ depending on the severity
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sunroof may become stuck, fail to open or close properly, or leak.
    *   Solution: Repairing or replacing the sunroof motor, tracks, or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2009 Toyota Land Cruiser is a generally reliable vehicle, but owners should be aware of the potential issues outlined above. Engine issues like valve cover leaks and water pump failure, as well as transmission issues such as torque converter problems and harsh shifting, can be expensive to repair. The air suspension, if equipped, may also require maintenance. Electrical issues with the navigation system and power seats can also be problematic. Addressing these issues promptly can help ensure the Land Cruiser continues to provide years of reliable service. While the vehicle is known for its longevity, older models may require significant maintenance due to age and mileage. Overall, the severity and cost of potential issues can vary widely, ranging from relatively minor repairs to more substantial and expensive replacements.
