**Introduction:**

The 2004 Toyota Land Cruiser is a full-size SUV known for its ruggedness, reliability, and off-road capabilities. It is available with two engine options: a 4.7L V8 (235 hp, 320 lb-ft) and a 5.7L V8 (381 hp, 401 lb-ft). Transmission options include a 4-speed automatic or a 6-speed manual.

People who love the Land Cruiser appreciate its powerful engines, spacious and luxurious interior, and impressive off-road performance. However, some owners have reported certain issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**4.7L V8**

- **Leaking valve cover gaskets:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the valve covers, leading to engine oil loss and possible engine damage.
  - Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Intake manifold gasket failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Coolant and/or oil leaks from the intake manifold gaskets, leading to engine overheating or oil starvation.
  - Solution: Replace the intake manifold gaskets.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Knock sensor failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: The knock sensor detects engine knock and adjusts timing accordingly. When it fails, it can cause loss of power, poor engine performance, and increased fuel consumption.
  - Solution: Replace the knock sensor.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
  - Recalls: None Found

**5.7L V8**

- **Spark plug blowout:**
  - Description/Symptoms: The spark plugs can blow out of the engine head, causing engine misfires, loss of power, and increased fuel consumption.
  - Solution: Replace the spark plugs and inspect for cylinder head damage.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
  - Recalls: 05V490000

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

- **Torque converter shudder:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Vibrations or shuddering during acceleration.
  - Solution: Replace the torque converter.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,500
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Leaking transmission pan gasket:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Transmission fluid leaks from the pan gasket, leading to fluid loss and transmission damage.
  - Solution: Replace the transmission pan gasket.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
  - Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Manual**

- **Clunky shifting:**
  - Description/Symptoms: The transmission does not shift smoothly, especially when cold.
  - Solution: Replace or adjust the linkage.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Grinding noises:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Noises while shifting gears.
  - Solution: Inspect and replace damaged synchros or other internal components.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000
  - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **Worn ball joints:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Loose or rattling steering, uneven tire wear, and poor handling.
  - Solution: Replace the ball joints.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Leaking shock absorbers:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Excessive bouncing or unstable handling.
  - Solution: Replace the shock absorbers.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200
  - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Alternator failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, battery drain, and warning lights on the dashboard.
  - Solution: Replace the alternator.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Battery problems:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the car or dead battery.
  - Solution: Replace the battery or inspect the charging system.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
  - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Rusty exhaust:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Corrosion on the exhaust system, leading to noise and potential exhaust leaks.
  - Solution: Replace the rusted exhaust components.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
  - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Heater core failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Loss of heat from the heater.
  - Solution: Replace the heater core.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Malfunctioning dashboard lights:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Dashboard lights do not illuminate or flicker.
  - Solution: Replace the bulbs or inspect for electrical issues.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
  - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2004 Toyota Land Cruiser is generally a reliable vehicle, however, some owners have reported issues such as engine gasket leaks, transmission problems, suspension wear, and electrical malfunctions. The severity of these issues varies, but some can be costly to repair. It is important to note that not all Land Cruisers experience these issues, and many owners are very satisfied with their vehicles. However, it is recommended to have a thorough inspection conducted before purchasing a used 2004 Land Cruiser.
