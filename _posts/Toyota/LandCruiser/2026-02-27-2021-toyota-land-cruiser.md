# 2021 Toyota Land Cruiser Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Toyota Land Cruiser represents the final model year for this legendary generation of the full-size luxury SUV, known for its exceptional build quality and rugged off-road capability. Buyers looking at the used market will find a stout machine, though it remains important to inspect service history and individual component conditions during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 5.7L V8 (3UR-FE)

The 5.7-liter V8 engine produces 381 horsepower and 401 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. This powertrain is generally celebrated for long-term durability, though isolated cooling component leaks can occasionally surface.

**Water Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A water pump failure can occur prematurely, leading to coolant leaks or overheating warnings on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed water pump and refill the coolant system, inspecting surrounding hoses for wear.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission delivers power smoothly to the standard full-time four-wheel-drive system. It maintains a strong reputation for dependability with no widespread recurring complaints reported by owners.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Toyota Land Cruiser is an exceptionally solid choice for used SUV buyers who value longevity, luxury, and trail-ready capability. While isolated component concerns like early water pump wear can happen, the overall mechanical pedigree remains top-tier. Always perform a thorough inspection before finalizing your purchase.
