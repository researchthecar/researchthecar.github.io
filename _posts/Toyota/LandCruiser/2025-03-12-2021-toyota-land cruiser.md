The 2021 Toyota Land Cruiser is a full-size SUV known for its off-road prowess, reliability, and luxurious features. It is powered by a 5.7-liter V8 engine producing 381 horsepower and 401 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners appreciate the Land Cruiser's comfortable ride, spacious interior, and go-anywhere capability. Criticisms often include its poor fuel economy, high price, and somewhat dated technology compared to newer competitors.

**Engine**

**5.7L V8**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report oil leaks, often originating from the valve cover gaskets or timing chain cover. Symptoms include visible oil drips under the vehicle and a burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gasket or seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+ depending on the source and complexity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

* **Starter Motor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the engine, clicking sound when turning the key, or complete failure to start.
    * Solution: Replacement of the starter motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced torque converter shudder or vibration, particularly at lower speeds or during light acceleration.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Adaptive Variable Suspension (AVS) Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunction of the AVS system, resulting in a harsh or uneven ride, warning lights on the dashboard. The AVS system uses electronically controlled shocks to change the dampening rate in real time.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty AVS shock absorbers or related components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+ per shock
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Navigation/Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Infotainment system freezing, slow response times, navigation errors, or Bluetooth connectivity problems.
    *   Solution: Software update or replacement of the head unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (software update) - $2,000+ (head unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: False alarms, non-functional sensors, or warning lights indicating a system malfunction.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof fails to open or close properly, gets stuck, or leaks water.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the sunroof motor, track, or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Toyota Land Cruiser maintains Toyota's reputation for reliability, but some owners have reported issues. The most common problems appear to be related to engine oil leaks, torque converter issues, and electrical glitches. Suspension problems can also occur and are costly to repair. Overall, the severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to potentially expensive repairs. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems and consider a thorough inspection before purchasing a used 2021 Land Cruiser.
