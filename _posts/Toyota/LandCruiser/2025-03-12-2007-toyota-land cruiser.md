The 2007 Toyota Land Cruiser is a full-size SUV known for its exceptional off-road capabilities, reliability, and luxurious features. The Land Cruiser came standard with a 4.7L 2UZ-FE V8 engine producing 275 horsepower and 320 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. Owners appreciate the Land Cruiser's ruggedness, dependability, and spacious interior. Common complaints include its poor fuel economy and high price tag, both during initial purchase and for maintenance/repairs.

**Engine**

**4.7L 2UZ-FE V8**

*   **Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the valve cover gaskets can occur as the gaskets age and harden. This can lead to oil consumption, burning smells, and visible oil residue on the engine.
    *   Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Exhaust Manifold Cracks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks can develop in the exhaust manifolds, typically near the welds. This leads to exhaust leaks, causing a ticking noise that is more pronounced when the engine is cold.
    *   Solution: Replace the cracked exhaust manifold.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Starter Motor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The starter motor can fail over time, leading to difficulty starting the engine or a clicking sound when trying to start.
    * Solution: Replace the starter motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**A750F 5-Speed Automatic**

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The torque converter can experience issues like shuddering at low speeds or failure, leading to transmission slipping or complete loss of drive.
    *   Solution: Replace the torque converter. In some cases, a transmission rebuild might be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $3000+ depending on the extent of damage and repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Hydraulic Suspension System (AHC) Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Adjustable Height Control (AHC) system can experience issues such as sagging, uneven ride height, or complete failure. This can be due to leaks in the hydraulic lines, failing accumulators (spheres), or a faulty pump.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the leaking lines, accumulators, or pump. Flushing the system and replacing the fluid may also be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+, depending on the component needing replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rear Air Suspension Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some Land Cruisers are equipped with rear air suspension. Leaks can occur in the air springs (bags), leading to a sagging rear end or a constantly running compressor.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking air springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1000+ per air spring.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Navigation System Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The navigation system screen may go blank, freeze, or become unresponsive.
    *   Solution: Replacing or repairing the navigation unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+ depending on repair vs replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Window Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power windows may stop working or operate slowly due to a failing window motor.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty window motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per window.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust on Undercarriage/Frame**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust can develop on the undercarriage and frame, especially in areas with harsh winters or coastal environments.
    *   Solution: Rust repair, rustproofing, or frame replacement (in severe cases).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $5000+ depending on severity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The dashboard can develop cracks, especially in warmer climates due to sun exposure.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the dashboard or use of a dashboard cover.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (cover) - $1500+ (replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2007 Toyota Land Cruiser is a capable and reliable SUV, but like all vehicles, it has its share of common problems. The most significant concerns revolve around the AHC suspension system, potential rust issues, and engine-related leaks. While some repairs, like valve cover gasket replacement, are relatively affordable, others, like AHC system repairs or transmission work, can be quite costly. Addressing these potential issues proactively can help extend the life of the Land Cruiser and maintain its value.
