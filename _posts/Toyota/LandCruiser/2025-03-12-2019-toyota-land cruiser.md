The 2019 Toyota Land Cruiser is a full-size SUV known for its exceptional off-road capabilities, reliability, and luxurious features. It comes standard with a 5.7-liter V8 engine producing 381 horsepower and 401 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. There are no other engine or transmission options. Owners appreciate its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and strong resale value. Common dislikes include its relatively poor fuel economy and high price point.

**Engine**

**5.7L V8**

* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported oil leaks originating from various locations including the valve cover gaskets and timing chain cover. Symptoms include visible oil drips, burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    * Solution: Replacing the affected gaskets or seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1500+, depending on the source of the leak.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Water Pump Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The water pump may fail prematurely. Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and a whining noise from the engine bay.
    * Solution: Replacing the water pump and related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

* **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing harsh shifting, particularly between certain gears, or hesitation during acceleration. This can be due to issues with the valve body or torque converter.
    * Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the valve body, flushing the transmission fluid, or replacing the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3000+ depending on the repair.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Height Control System Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The Land Cruiser features an adjustable height control system. Some owners have reported issues with this system, including the vehicle not adjusting to the desired height or displaying error messages.
    * Solution: Diagnosing and repairing or replacing the faulty components, such as the air compressor, height sensors, or air struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Navigation System Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reports of the infotainment system freezing, glitching, or failing to update.
    * Solution: Software updates, or replacing the navigation unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (software update) - $2000+ (replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced premature battery drain, especially if the vehicle is not driven regularly.
    * Solution: Identifying and addressing parasitic draws, replacing the battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: In hot climates, the dashboard material can become brittle and crack, especially along seams or in direct sunlight.
    * Solution: Replacing the dashboard or using a dashboard cover to prevent further damage.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $2000+ depending on method and materials.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Toyota Land Cruiser is generally a reliable vehicle, but some owners have reported issues with the engine (oil leaks, water pump), transmission (harsh shifting), suspension (height control system), electrical components (navigation system, battery drain), and interior (dashboard cracking). The severity and cost of these issues can vary, but some repairs can be expensive, particularly those related to the suspension and infotainment system. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and have a qualified mechanic inspect the vehicle before purchase.
