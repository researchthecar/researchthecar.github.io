**2005 Toyota Land Cruiser**

The 2005 Toyota Land Cruiser is a full-size SUV known for its ruggedness and off-road capabilities. It offers various engine and transmission options, including a 4.7-liter V8 with a 5-speed automatic transmission, a 5.7-liter V8 with a 6-speed automatic transmission, and a 4.2-liter diesel V8 with a 6-speed automatic transmission. People appreciate its spacious interior, reliability, and off-road performance, but common complaints include its limited fuel economy and high maintenance costs.

**Engine**

* **4.7-liter V8**
    * **Leaking intake manifold gasket**
        * Description/Symptoms: A coolant leak on the intake manifold, often accompanied by a decrease in engine performance.
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **5.7-liter V8**
    * **Carbon buildup on intake valves**
        * Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, especially at higher RPMs.
        * Solution: Clean or replace the intake valves.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **4.2-liter diesel V8**
    * **Turbo failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, black smoke from the exhaust, and a loud whistling noise.
        * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-speed automatic transmission**
    * **Harsh shifting**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission shifts roughly, especially during acceleration or deceleration.
        * Solution: Flush the transmission fluid or replace the shift solenoids.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800
        * Recalls: None Found
* **6-speed automatic transmission**
    * **Transmission fluid leak**
        * Description/Symptoms: Fluid leaking from the transmission case or other components.
        * Solution: Replace the leaking component, such as seals or gaskets.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension**
    * **Worn upper control arms**
        * Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or rattling noises when turning or over bumps.
        * Solution: Replace the upper control arms.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear suspension**
    * **Leaking rear shocks**
        * Description/Symptoms: Reduced shock absorption, resulting in a rough ride.
        * Solution: Replace the rear shocks.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery**
    * **Premature battery failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Battery fails prematurely, requiring frequent replacements.
        * Solution: Replace the battery and check for any underlying electrical issues.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator**
    * **Voltage regulator failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Alternator not charging the battery properly, leading to electrical problems.
        * Solution: Replace the alternator or voltage regulator.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Body panels**
    * **Rust on frame**
        * Description/Symptoms: Corrosion on the vehicle's frame, especially in areas exposed to salt or moisture.
        * Solution: Repair or replace the affected frame section.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Windshield**
    * **Cracking**
        * Description/Symptoms: Cracks on the windshield, often caused by small stones or debris.
        * Solution: Replace the windshield.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard**
    * **Peeling or cracking dash**
        * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard material becomes sticky, peels, or cracks.
        * Solution: Replace or repair the dashboard.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Climate control system**
    * **Faulty blend door actuator**
        * Description/Symptoms: Temperature control issues, such as hot air blowing when cold air is requested.
        * Solution: Replace the blend door actuator.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2005 Toyota Land Cruiser is a reliable and rugged vehicle, but it does have some common issues. Engine problems can be expensive to fix, particularly for the 4.2-liter diesel engine. Suspension and electrical components can also fail prematurely. However, overall, the Land Cruiser is still a popular choice for off-road enthusiasts and those seeking a durable SUV. The severity of the issues varies, and many of them can be prevented with proper maintenance and care.
