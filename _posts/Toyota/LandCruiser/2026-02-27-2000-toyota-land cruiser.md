# 2000 Toyota Land Cruiser

### Overview
The 2000 Toyota Land Cruiser is renowned for its robust build quality and legendary reliability, making it a highly sought-after vehicle for both on-road comfort and off-road capability. This generation, often referred to as the 100-series, continued Toyota's tradition of producing durable SUVs. While generally very dependable, like any vehicle, it has a few specific areas that owners have reported issues with over time. This analysis focuses on common mechanical and electrical components, excluding routine maintenance and standard wear and tear, to provide a clear picture of its long-term reliability.

---


## Engine

### 4.7L V8 (2UZ-FE)
**Timing Belt Replacement**
- **Symptoms:** Engine performance degradation, potential for catastrophic engine damage if belt breaks.
- **Solution:** Scheduled replacement of timing belt, tensioners, and water pump.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1000

**Rear Main Seal Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Oil drips or puddles under the vehicle, particularly near the transmission.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the rear main crankshaft seal.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

## Suspension

### Standard Independent Front Suspension (IFS)
**Upper Ball Joint Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises from the front end, especially over bumps; uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn upper ball joints.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


### Adaptive Variable Suspension (AVS) / Active Height Control (AHC)
**Hydraulic Leaks / Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one side, warning lights on the dashboard, loss of adjustable ride height.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of hydraulic lines, accumulators, or the AHC pump.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $3000+


## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Issues with power windows, door locks, interior lights, or dashboard indicators not functioning correctly.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200


### Alternator
**Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, difficulty starting, or complete electrical failure.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

### Headlight Lenses
**Clouding / Yellowing**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced light output, cosmetic degradation.
- **Solution:** Restoration using polishing kits or replacement of headlight assemblies.

## Interior

### Leather Upholstery
**Cracking / Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Visible cracks and wear on seats, especially on driver's side bolsters.
- **Solution:** Cleaning, conditioning, or professional repair/replacement of leather sections.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1000+


### HVAC Blower Motor
**Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, or intermittent airflow, regardless of fan speed setting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the HVAC blower motor and resistor.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2000 Toyota Land Cruiser (100-series) is a highly reliable vehicle with a strong reputation for durability. The 4.7L V8 engine is generally robust, though timely replacement of the timing belt and associated components is crucial. Transmission options are also known for their longevity. Potential issues tend to arise in the suspension, particularly with the optional AVS/AHC system, and some electrical components like the BCM or alternator can require attention over time. Interior wear on leather is common but cosmetic. Overall, with proper maintenance and addressing specific known issues, the 2000 Land Cruiser remains an excellent choice for those seeking a dependable and capable SUV.
