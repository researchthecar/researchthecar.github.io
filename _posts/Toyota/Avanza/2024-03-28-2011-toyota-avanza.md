**Introduction:**
The 2011 Toyota Avanza is a compact MPV that has been well-received for its spacious interior and affordable price point. It comes with a choice of two engine options: a 1.3-liter 4-cylinder and a 1.5-liter 4-cylinder. Both engines are mated to a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Avanza is praised for its practicality, fuel efficiency, and reliability. However, it is not without its flaws.

**Engine**
**1.3-liter 4-cylinder**
- **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Premature wear or failure of the timing chain tensioner can cause a rattling noise and power loss.
    - Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
- **Valve Train Noise:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Excessive valve train noise, such as ticking or tapping, may indicate issues with the camshaft, lifters, or valves.
    - Solution: Diagnose and replace the affected components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,200+

**1.5-liter 4-cylinder**
- **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**
**5-speed Manual**
- **Clutch Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Loss of clutch engagement, difficulty shifting gears, or a burning smell.
    - Solution: Replace the clutch assembly.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+

**4-speed Automatic**
- **Transmission Slipping:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Gears slipping or refusing to engage, rough shifting, or delayed shifting.
    - Solution: Repair or replace the transmission.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    - Recalls: NHTSA 16V-503

**Suspension**
- **Shocks and Struts Wear:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Bumpy or rough ride, excessive body roll, or uneven tire wear.
    - Solution: Replace shocks and struts.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Electrical**
- **Starter Motor Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Inability to start the engine, clicking or grinding noise when starting.
    - Solution: Replace starter motor.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+

- **Battery Drain:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Battery discharges rapidly, even when the vehicle is not in use.
    - Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical system issues, such as faulty wiring or battery terminal corrosion.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Exterior**
- **Front Bumper Cracking:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Cracks or deformation of the front bumper.
    - Solution: Repair or replace the bumper.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+

- **Door Alignment Issues:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty closing doors or uneven panel gaps.
    - Solution: Adjust door hinges or door panels.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+

**Interior**
- **Air Conditioning Malfunction:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Weak or no airflow from the air conditioning or heater.
    - Solution: Diagnose and repair AC system components, such as compressor, evaporator, or condenser.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

- **Dashboard Squeaks and Rattles:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Noises coming from the dashboard or interior panels.
    - Solution: Locate and secure loose trim or components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+

**Summary:**
While the 2011 Toyota Avanza is generally a reliable vehicle, it is not immune to certain issues. The most common problems reported by owners include timing chain tensioner failure, valve train noise, transmission slipping, and shocks and struts wear. These issues can range in severity from minor inconveniences to major repairs. The estimated cost to fix these issues varies depending on the severity of the problem and the location of the repair. Overall, the Toyota Avanza is a practical and affordable vehicle, but potential buyers should be aware of the potential issues before purchasing.
