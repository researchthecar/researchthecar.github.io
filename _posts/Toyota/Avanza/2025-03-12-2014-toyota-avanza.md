The 2014 Toyota Avanza is a compact multi-purpose vehicle (MPV) known for its practicality and affordability. The 2014 Avanza in the Philippines market primarily features a 1.3-liter or 1.5-liter gasoline engine. The 1.3-liter engine produces around 95 horsepower and 121 Nm of torque, while the 1.5-liter generates approximately 103 horsepower and 136 Nm of torque. Both engines are typically paired with either a 5-speed manual or a 4-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the Avanza for its spacious interior, fuel efficiency, and reliability, making it a popular choice for families and businesses. Common complaints include its somewhat underpowered engine, basic interior, and relatively simple features.

**Engine**

**1.3L 4-Cylinder**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks may develop around the valve cover gasket or oil pan gasket. Signs include visible oil drips under the car, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected gasket.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**1.5L 4-Cylinder**

*   **Rough Idling**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The engine may experience rough idling, especially when cold. This can be caused by a dirty or malfunctioning idle air control valve (IACV) or issues with the fuel injectors.
    *   Solution: Cleaning or replacing the IACV or cleaning/replacing the fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

*   **Delayed Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The transmission may exhibit delayed or harsh shifting between gears, especially when accelerating or decelerating.
    *   Solution: A transmission flush and filter change might help, but in some cases, valve body repair or replacement is needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Manual**

*   **Difficult Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported difficulty shifting gears, particularly into first or reverse. This can be caused by worn synchros or a clutch issue.
    *   Solution: Replacing worn synchros or the clutch assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Shock Absorbers/Struts**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The ride becomes bouncy, and the vehicle may exhibit excessive body roll during turns.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn shock absorbers or struts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Creaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Creaking or squeaking noises coming from the suspension, especially when going over bumps. This can be due to worn bushings or ball joints.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn bushings or ball joints.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Power Window Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power windows may stop working or operate intermittently. This is often due to a faulty window motor or switch.
    *   Solution: Replacing the window motor or switch.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Central Locking Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The central locking system may fail to lock or unlock all doors simultaneously.
    *   Solution: Check the central locking module and door lock actuators. Replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Formation of rust on the undercarriage, around the wheel wells, or along the body panels is a common issue, especially in areas with harsh weather conditions or road salt.
    *   Solution: Sanding and repainting the affected areas, or applying rust inhibitors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+ (depending on severity)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Air Conditioning Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The air conditioning system may blow warm air or not function at all. This can be due to a refrigerant leak, a faulty compressor, or a malfunctioning blower motor.
    *   Solution: Repairing leaks, replacing the compressor or blower motor, and recharging the refrigerant.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracking of the dashboard surface, particularly in hot climates, is a frequent issue.
    *   Solution: Replacing the dashboard cover or the entire dashboard.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**
The 2014 Toyota Avanza, while generally reliable, has some common issues that potential buyers should be aware of. Engine oil leaks and rough idling can be encountered with the engines, and delayed or difficult shifting can arise with the transmission. Suspension issues like worn shocks/struts and creaking noises can affect ride quality. Electrical problems with power windows and central locking, exterior rust, and interior AC and dashboard issues can also be expected. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but addressing them can range from relatively inexpensive fixes to more substantial repairs.
