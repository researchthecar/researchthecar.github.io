**Introduction**

The 2019 Toyota Avanza is a subcompact MPV that offers a spacious cabin, versatility, and fuel efficiency. It is available with two engine options: a 1.3-liter 4-cylinder and a 1.5-liter 4-cylinder, paired with either a 5-speed manual or a 4-speed automatic transmission. While the Avanza is generally a reliable vehicle, it has had some reported issues.

**Engine**

**1.3-liter 4-cylinder**

* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from the valve cover gasket, causing a burning smell in the cabin.
   - Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
   - Recalls: None Found

**1.5-liter 4-cylinder**

* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise from the engine, loss of power, and potential engine damage.
   - Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
   - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-speed Manual**

* **Clutch Master Cylinder Failure:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, excessive pedal travel, and leaking brake fluid.
   - Solution: Replace the clutch master cylinder.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
   - Recalls: None Found

**4-speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Shift Solenoid Malfunction:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifting, delayed gear engagement, or inability to shift gears.
   - Solution: Replace the transmission shift solenoid.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
   - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Bearing Failure:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or rattling noise from the front end, especially when going over bumps.
   - Solution: Replace the front strut bearings.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
   - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging, dim headlights, and difficulty starting the vehicle.
   - Solution: Replace the alternator.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
   - Recalls: None Found

* **Tail Light Socket Corrosion:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning tail lights due to corrosion in the socket.
   - Solution: Clean or replace the tail light socket.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $100
   - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Malfunction:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening or closing the door handles.
   - Solution: Replace the door handle assembly.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
   - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Cracks developing on the dashboard due to sun exposure or temperature fluctuations.
   - Solution: Replace the dashboard panel.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
   - Recalls: None Found

* **Rear Seat Latch Failure:**
   - Description/Symptoms: Rear seat latching mechanism malfunctioning, allowing the seat to move unexpectedly.
   - Solution: Replace the rear seat latch assembly.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250
   - Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2019 Toyota Avanza is a reliable vehicle, but it has had some issues reported. The most common issues include the valve cover gasket leak, timing chain tensioner failure, transmission shift solenoid malfunction, front strut bearing failure, and electrical problems. While these issues can be inconvenient, they are typically inexpensive to fix.
