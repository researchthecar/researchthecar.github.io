### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Toyota Avanza demonstrates a strong track record for reliability across its core mechanical and electrical systems. Both the 1.3L and 1.5L engines, along with the manual and automatic transmissions, are generally trouble-free, with no significant widespread issues reported. Similarly, the suspension, electrical components, exterior, and interior have proven durable. This suggests that the Avanza is a dependable vehicle, with most potential concerns likely stemming from age and mileage rather than inherent design flaws. Owners can expect a low incidence of major mechanical failures, making it a cost-effective choice for long-term ownership.
