**Introduction:**

The 2007 Toyota FJ Cruiser is a rugged, off-road-capable SUV that became an instant hit with enthusiasts. It was offered with a single engine and transmission option: a 4.0-liter V6 engine paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. The V6 produced 239 horsepower and 278 lb-ft of torque, providing ample power for both on- and off-road adventures. Owners praise the FJ Cruiser's retro styling, exceptional off-road capabilities, and proven reliability. However, it has not been without its issues.

**Engine**

* **4.0-liter V6**
    * **Valve cover gasket leak:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Oil leak from the top of the engine, visible on the valve covers or spark plugs.
        * Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * **Timing chain tensioner failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Rattling or knocking noise from the engine, especially during cold starts.
        * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * **Exhaust manifold crack:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Check engine light and loss of power due to exhaust gases leaking from a cracked manifold.
        * Solution: Replace the exhaust manifold.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    * **Throttle position sensor failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Poor engine performance, hesitation, or stalling.
        * Solution: Replace the throttle position sensor.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300

**Transmission**

* **5-speed automatic**
    * **Transmission shudder:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Vibration or shudder during acceleration or shifting gears.
        * Solution: Flush and replace the transmission fluid. May require transmission rebuild in severe cases.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,200+
    * **Torque converter failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering, slipping, or overheating of the transmission, leading to loss of power.
        * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500
    * **Valve body failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, delayed or no engagement of gears, or transmission slipping.
        * Solution: Replace the valve body.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Suspension**

* **Front upper control arm bushing failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn or torn bushings causing noise, vibration, or alignment issues.
    * Solution: Replace the upper control arms.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
* **Rear leaf spring failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging or broken leaf springs, resulting in poor ride quality or loss of suspension support.
    * Solution: Replace the leaf springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
* **Loose or worn steering rack:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive play or vibration in the steering wheel, especially during turns.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the steering rack.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,200

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, flickering gauges, or complete loss of electrical power.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
* **Battery drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery discharge over time, leading to difficulty starting or electrical system malfunctions.
    * Solution: Test and diagnose the cause of the battery drain and repair as needed.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
* **Fuse box melting:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Melting or burn marks on the fuse box, causing electrical system malfunctions.
    * Solution: Replace the fuse box.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Rear differential lock switch failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inability to engage or disengage the rear differential lock.
    * Solution: Replace the rear differential lock switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300

**Exterior**

* **Rear window wiper motor failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rear wiper not functioning properly or making excessive noise.
    * Solution: Replace the rear wiper motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Window seal leaks:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the vehicle's interior during rain or car washes.
    * Solution: Replace or repair the window seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
* **Fuel filler neck rust:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Corrosion and rust on the fuel filler neck, causing difficulty refueling or fuel leaks.
    * Solution: Replace the fuel filler neck.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Sunroof drain blockage or damage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the vehicle's interior through the sunroof due to clogged or damaged drains.
    * Solution: Clean or replace the sunroof drains.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300

**Interior**

* **Faulty gauge cluster:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Erratic or malfunctioning gauges, such as speedometer, tachometer, or fuel gauge.
    * Solution: Replace the gauge cluster.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
* **HVAC blend door actuator failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inability to adjust the air temperature or ventilation modes.
    * Solution: Replace the HVAC blend door actuator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Cupholder breakage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Broken or loose cupholders, unable to hold drinks securely.
    * Solution: Replace the cupholders.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150

**Summary:**

The 2007 Toyota FJ Cruiser has received a mixed reputation regarding reliability. While it is generally praised for its off-road capabilities and ruggedness, it has been plagued by numerous mechanical and electrical issues. The engine, transmission, and suspension components have been the most problematic areas, with various issues ranging from oil leaks to major component failures. Electrical problems and interior malfunctions have also been reported. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and its extent. Regular maintenance and repairs are crucial to keep the FJ Cruiser in good condition and avoid costly breakdowns.
