The 2008 Toyota FJ Cruiser is a retro-styled SUV known for its off-road capability and distinctive design. It came standard with a 4.0-liter V6 engine producing around 239 horsepower and 278 lb-ft of torque. The FJ Cruiser offered either a 5-speed automatic or a 6-speed manual transmission, depending on the configuration (4x4 vs. 4x2). Owners often praise its ruggedness, reliability, and unique appearance. Common dislikes include its fuel economy, limited rear visibility due to thick pillars and small rear windows, and the unconventional rear-hinged doors.

**Engine**

**4.0L 1GR-FE V6**

*   **Valve Cover Gasket Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the valve cover gaskets, leading to oil consumption, burning smell, and visible oil residue on the engine.
    *   Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Air Injection Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The secondary air injection pump can fail, causing the check engine light to illuminate. This system helps reduce emissions during cold starts.
    *   Solution: Replace the air injection pump and/or bypass the system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**A750F 5-Speed Automatic (4x4 Models)**

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibrations during acceleration, especially at lower speeds. This can be caused by a failing torque converter.
    *   Solution: Replace or rebuild the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**RA61F 6-Speed Manual (4x4 Models)**
*   No Issues Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Strut Mount Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or clunking noises from the front suspension, particularly when going over bumps or turning.
    *   Solution: Replace the front strut mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rear Shock Absorber Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Visible oil leaking from the rear shock absorbers, leading to a bouncy or unstable ride.
    *   Solution: Replace the rear shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Alternator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Battery light illumination, dimming headlights, difficulty starting the vehicle, and potential complete electrical failure.
    *   Solution: Replace the alternator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Door Hinge Issues (Rear Doors)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The rear-hinged doors can sometimes sag or become difficult to close properly due to wear on the hinges.
    *   Solution: Adjust or replace the door hinges.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+ (depending on severity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sun Visor Sagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sun visors may become loose and sag, obstructing the driver's view.
    *   Solution: Replace the sun visors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2008 Toyota FJ Cruiser, while generally reliable, has some common issues to be aware of. Engine issues like valve cover gasket leaks and air injection pump failures can be costly. Potential transmission problems with the automatic variant should be inspected. Suspension and electrical issues, like strut mount failures and alternator problems, are relatively common but may not be as severe. The rear door hinge issue is a unique quirk of the FJ Cruiser's design and could require attention. Overall, addressing these potential issues proactively can help ensure the longevity and reliability of the FJ Cruiser.
