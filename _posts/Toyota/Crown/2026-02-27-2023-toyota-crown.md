# 2023 Toyota Crown Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Toyota Crown introduces a distinct high-riding sedan silhouette to the market, paired with advanced hybrid powertrains and standard all-wheel drive. While generally praised for its comfort and efficiency, early owner reports submitted to federal safety regulators highlight a few electronic quirks and mechanical concerns that potential buyers should keep in mind.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Hybrid

This naturally aspirated 2.5-liter four-cylinder hybrid engine pairs with electric motors to produce a combined 236 horsepower through an electronic continuously variable transmission. It offers strong fuel economy and dependable everyday performance for daily commuting.

### 2.4L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Hybrid Max

This performance-oriented 2.4-liter turbocharged hybrid engine generates a robust 340 horsepower, paired with a traditional 6-speed automatic transmission. It delivers punchy acceleration and dynamic responsiveness for drivers seeking a sportier feel.

## Transmission

### Electronic Continuously Variable Transmission (e-CVT)

The e-CVT works alongside the standard hybrid system to seamlessly blend electric and gas power. It is generally reliable, though occasional software-related drivability reports have been noted by owners.

**Acceleration Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported instances where the vehicle momentarily hesitates or stops responding after coming to a complete stop and attempting to accelerate.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle inspected by a dealership to check for powertrain control module updates or software calibrations.

### 6-Speed Direct Shift Automatic Transmission

Paired exclusively with the Hybrid Max powertrain, this traditional 6-speed automatic provides firm and predictable shifts. It is designed to handle higher torque loads while maintaining long-term durability.

## Electrical

### Instrument Panel & Infotainment

The Crown relies heavily on digital displays for instrumentation and multimedia controls, which can occasionally require software updates to resolve glitching.

**Blank Display Screen**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced the instrument panel or infotainment screens turning completely blank during vehicle operation.
- **Solution:** Consult a local dealership regarding applicable software updates or manufacturer campaign repairs.

### Backup Camera & Display

Driver assistance and safety cameras generally operate as intended, though isolated electronic glitches can temporarily affect display visibility.

**Rear Camera Glitch**
- **Symptoms:** The rear backup camera may fail to turn off when shifting into Drive, occasionally resulting in a total loss of the multimedia display.
- **Solution:** Restarting the vehicle typically resets the system, but persistent issues should be diagnosed by a certified service center.

## Exterior

### Driver Side Window and Door Seals

Exterior body fitment is generally solid, though wind sealing around the front doors has occasionally generated owner complaints on early models.

**Wind Noise and Window Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice abnormal window vibration at highway speeds accompanied by excessive wind noise entering through the door handle area.
- **Solution:** Have a dealership inspect the door seals, window tracks, and alignment to ensure proper acoustic sealing.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V442000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Crown&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Due to insufficient laser welding, the camera cases may separate, allowing water to leak into the cameras and short circuit. The rearview and front view camera images may not appear on the displays or display a distorted image.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the rearview and frontview cameras as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V548000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Crown&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." A vehicle with an incorrect maximum capacity weight value may be overloaded, which can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V595000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Crown&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** An instrument panel display that does not show critical information can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the instrument panel software over-the-air (OTA) for non-PHEV vehicles, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V744000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Crown&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to freeze or display a blank screen when the vehicle is in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that fails to display an image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the parking assist software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Toyota Crown is a technologically advanced and comfortable cruiser, benefiting from Toyota's proven hybrid expertise. While early owner complaints point toward minor electronic display glitches and occasional drivability software concerns, these issues do not point to widespread mechanical failure. Used car buyers should verify that all software updates have been performed and test drive the vehicle to ensure smooth acceleration and proper infotainment operation.
