# 2024 Toyota Crown Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Toyota Crown is a distinct, high-riding sedan offering innovative hybrid powertrains and standard all-wheel drive. As a newer addition to the market, owner feedback highlights a mix of advanced tech functionality, minor mechanical quirks, and occasional driver-assistance anomalies.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Turbocharged Hybrid 4-Cylinder

This high-output turbocharged hybrid powertrain pairs a 2.4-liter 4-cylinder engine with electric motors to produce approximately 340 horsepower, exclusive to the Platinum trim. It delivers strong performance, though early owner reports note occasional turbo lag or acceleration hesitation from a stop.

**Acceleration Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report occasional hesitation or a delay in torque delivery when attempting to accelerate from a complete stop.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealership to check for applicable powertrain software updates or service bulletins addressing throttle response.

### 2.5L Hybrid 4-Cylinder

A naturally aspirated 2.5-liter 4-cylinder hybrid system paired with electronic all-wheel drive, generating around 236 net horsepower. It prioritizes everyday efficiency and smooth power delivery for standard trims.

## Transmission

### Direct Shift 6-Speed Automatic

Paired exclusively with the 2.4L turbocharged hybrid powertrain on the Platinum trim, this traditional 6-speed automatic provides distinct gear ratios for a more engaging driving dynamic.

### Electronically Controlled Continuously Variable Transmission (eCVT)

Standard on the 2.5L hybrid models, this eCVT ensures seamless power blending between the gas engine and electric motors for optimized fuel economy.

## Brakes

### Electric Power Steering (EPS) and Brake Hold

Steering and braking assist systems generally operate smoothly, though rare electronic glitches involving driver-aid integrations have been noted.

**Unexpected Movement with Brake Hold Engaged**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported instances where the vehicle momentarily rolled forward after several minutes with the automatic brake hold feature active, accompanied by an EPS warning light.
- **Solution:** Avoid relying solely on the automatic hold feature for extended stationary periods on inclines until inspected or updated by a dealer.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Instrument Cluster

Advanced electronics govern most cabin functions, leading to occasional software quirks reported by early adopters.

**Screen Freezing and Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** The main instrument panel or infotainment display may occasionally freeze on specific menus or go blank during operation.
- **Solution:** Restart the vehicle or visit a dealership to check for software patches that stabilize the display system.

**Hybrid System Warning Messages**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have observed unexpected hybrid system warning lamps illuminating on the dashboard intermittently.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose the hybrid control module and clear or update fault codes.

## Exterior

### Body Hardware and Condenser

Exterior panels are solidly built, though minor vulnerabilities in lower grille protection have been flagged by owners.

**A/C Condenser Road Damage**
- **Symptoms:** Road debris or small stones can penetrate the lower grille area and puncture the air conditioning condenser, causing the A/C to stop working.
- **Solution:** Inspect the front condenser area for exposure and consider adding protective aftermarket mesh screening if driving in debris-heavy areas.

**[Recall 25V595000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Crown&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** An instrument panel display that does not show critical information can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the instrument panel software over-the-air (OTA) for non-PHEV vehicles, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V744000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Crown&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to freeze or display a blank screen when the vehicle is in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that fails to display an image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the parking assist software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Toyota Crown is a technologically advanced and comfortable cruiser, but buyers should review service history for any software updates or early component quirks. While owner complaints point to occasional electronic glitches and minor hybrid system alerts, the vehicle remains generally well-received. Prospective buyers should thoroughly test drive both powertrain options and ensure all software recalls or technical updates have been applied.
