**Introduction**

The 2017 Toyota Corolla iM is a compact hatchback offered with a single engine and transmission option: a 1.8L 4-cylinder engine paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). The engine generates 137 horsepower and 126 lb-ft of torque. The Corolla iM is praised for its fuel efficiency, spacious interior, and sporty handling, but criticized for its lack of power and premium features.

**Engine**

* **1.8L 4-cylinder with CVT**

**Transmission**

* **CVT**

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure**
Description/Symptoms: The alternator may fail, causing electrical issues such as battery drain, dimming lights, or engine stalling.
Solution: Replace the alternator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

* **Starter motor failure**
Description/Symptoms: The starter motor may fail, preventing the car from starting.
Solution: Replace the starter motor.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rear bumper paint peeling**
Description/Symptoms: The paint on the rear bumper may peel or chip, exposing the underlying plastic.
Solution: Repaint the rear bumper.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Squeaky dashboard**
Description/Symptoms: The dashboard may squeak or rattle over bumps or rough roads.
Solution: Apply foam or felt tape to the contact points between the dashboard and other components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $100
Recalls: None Found

* **Loose seat belt buckle**
Description/Symptoms: The seat belt buckle may become loose or rattle.
Solution: Tighten or replace the seat belt buckle.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Toyota Corolla iM generally experiences minor issues, with the most common being electrical failures and interior squeaks. The estimated cost to fix these issues ranges from $50 to $1,000. Overall, the Corolla iM is a reliable vehicle with low maintenance costs.
