# 2024 Toyota GR86 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Toyota GR86 is celebrated for its agile handling, engaging rear-wheel-drive dynamics, and sporty character. For buyers exploring the used market, understanding potential owner-reported areas of concern can help ensure a smooth ownership experience. While overall mechanical sentiment remains strong, reviewing specific owner feedback highlights a few localized items to keep an eye on during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Flat-4 Boxer Engine

The 2.4-liter naturally aspirated flat-four boxer engine produces approximately 228 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque, delivering brisk acceleration and a low center of gravity. It is the sole engine option available for this model year.

### Fuel Lines

Under-hood fuel routing requires periodic visual checks by owners to ensure lines do not rub against adjacent brackets or components.

**Fuel Line Chafing and Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported observing physical wear, chafing, or rubbing on fuel lines located in the engine bay near the intake manifold.
- **Solution:** Inspect the fuel lines under the hood for contact with surrounding components and consider protective sleeving or replacement if excessive wear is detected.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The 6-speed manual transmission offers short, direct throws suited for enthusiast driving and pairs directly with the rear-wheel-drive layout.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission features steering-wheel-mounted paddle shifters for drivers seeking a more relaxed daily commuting experience without sacrificing manual control.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V548000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=GR86&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." A vehicle with an incorrect maximum capacity weight value may be overloaded, which can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The Toyota GR86 offers an exciting driving experience backed by solid overall reliability. Used-car buyers should perform a thorough visual inspection of the engine bay, paying particular attention to under-hood fuel line routing and potential chafing. Aside from these minor owner-reported observations, standard routine maintenance and a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection will help secure a dependable and enjoyable sports car.
