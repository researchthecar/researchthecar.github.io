The 2020 Toyota C-HR is a subcompact crossover SUV known for its distinctive styling, good fuel economy, and standard safety features. It is powered by a 2.0-liter inline-4 engine producing 144 horsepower and 139 lb-ft of torque, paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). Owners often praise the C-HR's unique design and responsive handling, but some dislike its limited cargo space and lack of all-wheel drive.

**Engine**

**2.0L Inline-4 Engine**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing higher than normal oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. Symptoms include low oil level warnings, engine knocking, and potential engine damage if left unaddressed.
    *   Solution: Monitoring oil levels regularly, performing oil consumption tests at a dealership to determine the rate of consumption, and addressing any underlying issues, such as worn piston rings or valve seals, is the appropriate course of action. In severe cases, engine repair or replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)**

*   **CVT Hesitation/Jerking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported hesitation or jerking during acceleration, particularly at lower speeds. This can manifest as a delay in response when pressing the gas pedal or a feeling of the vehicle lurching forward.
    *   Solution: A software update to the transmission control module (TCM) may resolve the issue. In other cases, a CVT fluid flush or replacement may be recommended. In severe cases, CVT replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $5,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Wear of Rear Shocks/Struts**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported rear shocks/struts wearing out prematurely, leading to a bouncy or unstable ride, especially over bumps. Symptoms include excessive bouncing after hitting bumps, poor handling, and unusual noises from the rear suspension.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the rear shocks/struts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Malfunctioning Infotainment System**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Issues with the infotainment system include freezing, crashing, and unresponsiveness. This can affect navigation, audio, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Resetting the infotainment system may temporarily resolve the issue. However, a software update or replacement of the infotainment unit may be required for a permanent fix.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Backup Camera Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The backup camera may fail to display an image, show a distorted image, or intermittently work. This poses a safety risk during reversing.
    *   Solution: Check the camera connections and wiring for damage. A faulty camera requires replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Cracking of Plastic Bumper Covers**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported cracking of the front or rear plastic bumper covers, even without significant impact. This is often attributed to temperature changes and material stress.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the cracked bumper cover.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling Noises from Dashboard**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling noises emanating from the dashboard area, particularly when driving on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Identifying the source of the rattle and securing the loose components. This may involve adding padding or tightening fasteners.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2020 Toyota C-HR generally exhibits a mix of minor and moderate issues. The most frequently reported problems involve the infotainment system, the CVT, and suspension components. While some issues like excessive oil consumption and CVT problems can be costly to fix, others, such as rattling noises, are less severe and less expensive to address. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these common issues and thoroughly inspect the vehicle before purchase.
