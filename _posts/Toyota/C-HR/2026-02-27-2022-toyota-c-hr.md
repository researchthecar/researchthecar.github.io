# 2022 Toyota C-HR Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Toyota C-HR offers distinctive styling and standard safety features, making it an appealing option for city driving. While owner reports for this model year are limited, prospective buyers should review available powertrain options and check maintenance records carefully.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine produces approximately 144 horsepower and 139 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting. This engine is generally considered dependable and pairs with front-wheel drive.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The continuously variable transmission directs power to the front wheels. While generally functional, buyers should listen closely for unusual whining or grinding noises during a test drive.

**Internal Transmission Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected mechanical noise from the front end followed by total transmission failure requiring replacement.
- **Solution:** Have the transmission thoroughly inspected by a qualified technician, and replace the unit if internal failure or component damage is confirmed.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Toyota C-HR is a stylish subcompact crossover with generally solid core mechanics, though isolated reports of major transmission trouble warrant attention. Buyers should perform a thorough test drive to check for unusual drivetrain noises and verify any available service history.
