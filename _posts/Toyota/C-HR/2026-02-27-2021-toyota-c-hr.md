# 2021 Toyota C-HR Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Toyota C-HR, buyers will find a stylish, compact crossover with distinctive styling and nimble handling. While Toyota generally enjoys a strong reputation for dependability, owners and mechanics have flagged significant concerns regarding the continuous variable transmission (CVT), which warrants careful inspection before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces 144 horsepower and 139 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting. This engine is generally robust and shares solid reliability traits with other modern Toyota powerplants.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The continuously variable transmission routes power to the front wheels. It is the primary area of mechanical concern for this model year due to reports of internal bearing wear.

**Transmission Internal Bearing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report an abnormal, progressive whining or growling noise from the transmission, which can eventually lead to slipping, deceleration issues, or total transmission failure.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the transmission for bearing noise. Failed units typically require complete transaxle replacement.
- **Cost:** $13,000

## Safety

### Seat Belt

Safety equipment generally performs well, though occasional complaints point toward individual seat belt operation concerns.

**Seat Belt Retractor or Function Concern**
- **Symptoms:** Occupants may experience issues with seat belt operation or movement.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belts for proper latching, retraction, and webbing condition, replacing components if defects are found.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V107000: Forward Collision Avoidance: Sensing System: Radar](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=C-HR&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The radar sensor may not have been activated during production, making the pre-collision system (PCS) inoperative. An inoperative PCS that does not provide a malfunction indicator to the driver, or braking assist as expected, can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and activate the radar sensor as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Toyota C-HR offers appealing urban style and efficient everyday performance, but potential buyers must proceed with caution. Because of documented complaints regarding costly CVT transaxle failures, a pre-purchase inspection by an independent mechanic who can specifically listen for transmission whining or bearing noise is strongly recommended.
