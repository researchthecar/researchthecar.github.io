**Vehicle Introduction:**
The 2020 Toyota Yaris R is a sporty hatchback powered by a 1.5-liter, 3-cylinder engine producing 106 horsepower and 103 lb-ft of torque. It is available with a 6-speed manual transmission or a CVT. The Yaris R is praised for its nimble handling, fuel efficiency, and affordability, but some owners have reported various issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**6-speed Manual**
* **Grinding during shifting**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty engaging gears, grinding noise when shifting, especially into second and third gear.
Solution: Replace or repair damaged synchronizers or shift forks.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
Recalls: None Found

**CVT**
* **Hesitation or slipping**
Description/Symptoms: Transmission hesitates to shift or slips gears, causing a loss of power or rough shifting.
Solution: Reprogramming the transmission control module or replacing the CVT fluid and filter.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut noise**
Description/Symptoms: Knocking or clunking sound from the front suspension, especially during turns or over bumps.
Solution: Replace worn or damaged struts.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+ per side
Recalls: None Found

* **Rear suspension spring breakage**
Description/Symptoms: Rear suspension spring breaks, causing the vehicle to sag or handle poorly.
Solution: Replace the broken spring.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain**
Description/Symptoms: Battery drains quickly when the vehicle is parked, causing difficulty starting.
Solution: Diagnose and repair the cause of the parasitic drain, such as faulty modules or electrical components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue
Recalls: None Found

* **Headlight condensation**
Description/Symptoms: Moisture accumulates inside the headlight housing, causing condensation to form on the inside of the lens.
Solution: Replace the headlight assembly.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door handle failure**
Description/Symptoms: Door handle becomes loose or breaks, making it difficult to open the door.
Solution: Replace the door handle assembly.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment screen cracking**
Description/Symptoms: Touchscreen display cracks or becomes unresponsive.
Solution: Replace the infotainment unit.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

* **Seatbelt warning light stays on**
Description/Symptoms: Seatbelt warning light remains illuminated even when the seatbelt is fastened.
Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the seatbelt switch or wiring harness.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**
While the 2020 Toyota Yaris R is generally a reliable vehicle, it has experienced some issues with its transmission, suspension, and electrical systems. Some of these issues can be minor annoyances, while others may require more expensive repairs. Overall, the severity and cost of the issues vary depending on the specific problem encountered.
