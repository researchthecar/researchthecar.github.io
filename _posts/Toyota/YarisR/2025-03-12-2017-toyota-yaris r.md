The 2017 Toyota Yaris iA (renamed Yaris R for the Mexican market) is a subcompact car known for its fuel efficiency and reliability. It comes with a 1.5-liter four-cylinder engine producing 106 horsepower and 103 lb-ft of torque, paired with either a 6-speed manual or a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the car's affordability, fuel economy, and ease of driving in urban environments. Common dislikes include the engine's limited power, especially on highways and the relatively basic interior.

**Engine**

**1.5L Four-Cylinder Engine**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than expected oil consumption, requiring frequent top-ups between oil changes. This is typically noticed with higher mileage vehicles. Symptoms include the low oil pressure light illuminating or noticing a burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Regular monitoring of oil levels and using the recommended oil type and viscosity. In severe cases, engine component replacement may be necessary, but this is less common.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 (oil change), $3000 - $5000+ (Engine replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

* **Engine Misfires**
    * Description/Symptoms: The engine may experience occasional misfires, which can be caused by faulty ignition coils, spark plugs, or fuel injectors.
    * Solution: Diagnosing the root cause and replacing the faulty component.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have noticed rough or jerky shifting, particularly between certain gears. This can be more pronounced when the transmission is cold.
    *   Solution: Performing a transmission fluid flush and inspection of the valve body. In some cases, a software update or transmission replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+ (fluid flush), $500 - $1500+ (valve body), $3000 - $5000+ (transmission replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Wear of Shocks and Struts**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported that the shocks and struts wear out faster than expected, leading to a bouncy or unstable ride.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn shocks and struts with new ones.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Backup Camera Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or complete failure of the backup camera. The screen may display a black or distorted image.
    *   Solution: Inspecting the camera wiring and connections. If the wiring is intact, replacing the backup camera unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Rattling Noise from Dashboard**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling or buzzing noises coming from the dashboard, particularly on rough roads. This can be caused by loose trim pieces or components.
    *   Solution: Locating the source of the rattle and securing the loose components. This may involve adding padding or replacing clips.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Toyota Yaris R is generally a reliable vehicle, but some owners have reported issues such as excessive oil consumption, harsh shifting, premature suspension wear, backup camera failure, and dashboard rattles. The severity and cost of these issues vary, with some requiring only minor repairs and others potentially involving more expensive component replacements. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these common complaints and have the car thoroughly inspected before purchasing.
