# 2019 Toyota Yaris Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 Toyota Yaris, buyers generally find a subcompact car known for nimble handling and impressive fuel economy. While Toyota vehicles typically enjoy strong reputations for dependability, owner reports submitted to safety authorities highlight a few specific areas to check closely during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2019 Toyota Yaris is powered by a 1.5-liter four-cylinder engine producing around 106 horsepower and 103 lb-ft of torque. This naturally aspirated engine is designed primarily for efficient daily commuting and delivers dependable performance under normal driving conditions.

### Fuel System

Delivers fuel from the tank to the engine cylinders under regulated pressure. Proper maintenance of this system is essential to prevent unexpected roadside stalls.

**Fuel Pump Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected engine stalling, hesitation, or dashboard warning lights appearing shortly after fuel system service.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the fuel pump operation and verify that any related recall updates or component replacements were properly executed.
- **Cost:** $280.00

### Climate Control

Regulates cabin temperature and airflow. Proper functionality ensures comfortable commuting in varying weather conditions.

**Air Conditioning Blower Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Air conditioning airflow gradually weakens and loses cooling capability during extended drives lasting longer than one hour.
- **Solution:** Inspect the climate control blower motor, evaporator, and control switches for signs of freezing or electrical resistance.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a traditional 6-speed automatic transmission paired with front-wheel drive, offering smooth power delivery and conventional shifting feel. Overall, it serves as a robust option for everyday city and highway driving.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

A 6-speed manual transmission was available for select trims, offering drivers more direct control over gear selection. This gearbox is straightforward and generally trouble-free when paired with a healthy clutch.

## Steering

### Steering and Alignment

Manages wheel direction and responsive handling. Suspension and steering geometry must be properly calibrated to ensure straight-line stability.

**Alignment and Steering Drift**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may exhibit a pulling sensation or tracking issues shortly after purchase, requiring multiple alignment adjustments.
- **Solution:** Check front-end suspension components and ensure a professional four-wheel alignment is performed if tracking irregularities are noted during a test drive.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Powers all onboard electronics, sensors, and starting systems. A healthy charging system is critical for preventing unexpected electrical issues.

**Battery Drain and Electrical Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden battery failure preventing vehicle startup or temporary loss of dashboard and accessory power while driving.
- **Solution:** Test the battery health, charging system output, and inspect wiring harnesses for any intermittent connection issues.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V503000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Yaris&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An incorrect label may lead to unintentionally overloading the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 21V617000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Yaris&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A fuel pump failure may cause an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the low-pressure fuel pump assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V244000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Yaris&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The text on the load capacity label may become illegible. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." If the driver cannot read the label, they may unknowingly overload the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GST will notify owners, and dealers will provide corrected load carrying capacity modification labels, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Toyota Yaris is generally a reliable and economical subcompact vehicle, but used-car buyers should perform a thorough test drive and mechanical inspection. Pay close attention to steering tracking, climate control performance during longer runs, and verify that all safety recalls—particularly those involving fuel delivery components—have been fully addressed.
