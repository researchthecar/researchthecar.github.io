# 2009 Toyota Yaris

### Overview
The 2009 Toyota Yaris is generally regarded as a highly reliable subcompact car, known for its fuel efficiency and low running costs. It was designed with simplicity and durability in mind, making it a popular choice for budget-conscious buyers and those seeking dependable daily transportation. While no vehicle is entirely immune to problems, the Yaris from this era typically exhibits strong performance across its major systems, with common issues being relatively minor and infrequent. Owners often praise its straightforward mechanics, which contribute to its overall positive reliability record and ease of maintenance.

## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming lights, battery warning light, difficulty starting, dead battery.
- **Solution:** Replace alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Power Window Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Window not operating, slow operation, grinding noise.
- **Solution:** Replace power window motor and regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2009 Toyota Yaris stands out as a remarkably reliable vehicle, especially within its segment. Its powertrain components (engine and transmission) are exceptionally robust, with very few reported significant issues. The suspension system also generally holds up well. The most common areas for potential concern, though still infrequent, lie within the electrical system, specifically with alternator failures and power window motor issues. Exterior and interior components are typically durable. Overall, the Yaris offers excellent long-term reliability, making it a sound choice for those prioritizing dependability and low maintenance costs.
