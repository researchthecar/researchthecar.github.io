**Introduction:**

The 2017 Toyota Yaris is a subcompact hatchback renowned for its fuel efficiency, affordability, and maneuverability in urban settings. It comes equipped with a 1.5-liter inline-4 engine, paired with either a 6-speed manual transmission or a continuously variable transmission (CVT). The engine produces 106 horsepower and 103 lb-ft of torque. Despite its compact size, the Yaris offers a spacious interior, ample cargo capacity, and a comfortable ride. However, the vehicle has also been met with some reported issues.

**Engine**

**1.5-Liter Inline-4**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Significant oil loss between oil changes, resulting in low oil levels and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replacing piston rings and valve stem seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000
* **Timing Chain Rattle:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Metallic rattling noise from the engine, particularly during cold starts or acceleration.
    * Solution: Replacing the timing chain and tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200
* **Coolant Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of coolant, causing overheating and engine damage.
    * Solution: Repairing or replacing the faulty coolant system components, such as hoses, gaskets, or the radiator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

* **Grinding Noises When Shifting:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, accompanied by grinding or binding noises.
    * Solution: Replacing or repairing the damaged synchronizers or gears within the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000

**CVT**

* **Hesitation or Jerking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission hesitation, slipping, or jerking during acceleration or gear changes.
    * Solution: Updating the transmission control module (TCM) software or replacing the CVT fluid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,500
* **Premature CVT Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Complete transmission failure, often caused by overheating or lack of maintenance.
    * Solution: Replacing the entire CVT assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $6,000+

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Mount Noise:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noise from the front suspension, particularly during turns or over bumps.
    * Solution: Replacing the worn strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rapid battery discharge, leading to a dead battery and starting issues.
    * Solution: Identifying and fixing the underlying cause, such as a faulty alternator, parasitic draw, or electrical component.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **Headlight Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Headlights failing to illuminate or flickering intermittently.
    * Solution: Replacing the headlight bulbs or repairing the electrical connection.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500

**Exterior**

* **Rear Bumper Sagging:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Drooping or sagging of the rear bumper, resulting in an uneven appearance.
    * Solution: Replacing the bumper brackets or reinforcing them.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Display glitches, touchscreen issues, or navigation system errors.
    * Solution: Updating the software or replacing the infotainment system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
* **Seat Adjustment Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty adjusting the driver's seat, such as problems with seat movement or lumbar support.
    * Solution: Repairing or replacing the seat adjustment mechanism.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700

**Summary:**

While the 2017 Toyota Yaris is generally reliable and economical, it has encountered some reported issues that owners should be aware of. The severity of the problems varies, with some requiring minor repairs, while others can be more costly or time-consuming to fix. It is essential to address any issues promptly to prevent further damage and ensure optimal performance and safety.
