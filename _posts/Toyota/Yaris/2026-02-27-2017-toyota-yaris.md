# 2017 Toyota Yaris Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Toyota Yaris offers a practical and budget-friendly ownership experience typical of subcompact cars, though prospective buyers should review a few known issues. While overall mechanical reputation remains strong, owner feedback highlights occasional concerns ranging from electrical and fuel system quirks to minor component wear. Reviewing service records and performing a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help ensure a reliable used vehicle.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L I4

Powered by a 1.5-liter four-cylinder engine producing around 106 horsepower, this efficient powerplant is generally durable and well-suited for daily commuting. Routine maintenance and periodic sensor checks keep this engine running dependably over high mileage.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may stall unexpectedly shortly after starting and shifting into gear, or experience fluctuating idle RPMs while stopped.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel delivery components, throttle body cleanliness, and related electrical sensors for faults.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual

The 6-speed manual transmission offers direct gear control and engaging shifts for drivers who prefer a traditional stick-shift experience. Normal clutch maintenance is expected over the vehicle's lifespan.

**Manual Transmission Shudder**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice a shudder or vibration from the clutch area when pulling away from a dead stop or when cruising in higher gears.
- **Solution:** Inspect the clutch assembly and flywheel for premature wear or defect, and replace components as necessary.

### 6-Speed Automatic

The 6-speed automatic transmission provides smooth power delivery and optimized highway cruising efficiency. Routine fluid changes help maintain long-term durability.

**Transmission Grinding or Fluid Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual grinding noises or shifts may develop during routine driving, prompting diagnostic evaluation.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition and levels, and run an OBD diagnostic scan to pinpoint internal component wear.

## Steering

### Steering Column

The electric power steering system delivers light and responsive maneuvering at low speeds, though highway steering feel should be carefully evaluated during a test drive.

**Steering Binding**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel may feel like it is lightly binding or locking up during straight-line highway driving, requiring minor extra effort to correct.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the steering column and electronic power steering components for mechanical binding.

## Safety

### Air Bag

The restraint system includes standard front and side airbags designed to protect occupants in a collision. Monitoring warning lamps ensures the system remains fully functional.

**Airbag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** An airbag warning indicator may illuminate on the dashboard, often associated with diagnostic trouble codes indicating circuit or sensor faults.
- **Solution:** Scan the supplemental restraint system (SRS) for specific trouble codes and repair faulty wiring harnesses or seat sensors.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V319000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Yaris&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The wire harnesses for the side air bag sensors located within the front doors may crack and corrode, possibly causing the non-deployment or improper deployment of the front seat side air bags and the curtain shield air bags. In the event of a crash, front seat side air bags and curtain shield air bags that do not deploy as intended can increase the risk of.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the wire harness assembly in the front doors, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V503000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Yaris&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An incorrect label may lead to unintentionally overloading the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 17V295000: Tires:Temporary/Emergency Spare Tire](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Yaris&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A spare tire that is not inflated to the proper value may not perform as intended, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Gulf States will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the spare tires, adjusting their pressure as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Toyota Yaris remains a dependable and economical choice for budget-conscious buyers, offering respectable fuel economy and straightforward mechanical systems. While owners have reported occasional issues regarding steering feel, transmission behavior, and electrical sensors, these do not overshadow the vehicle's overall dependability. Buyers should perform a thorough test drive focusing on steering response and transmission operation, and check service records to confirm proper maintenance.
