**Introduction:**

The 2016 Toyota Yaris is a subcompact hatchback available with either a 1.5L 4-cylinder engine (106 horsepower, 103 lb-ft) paired with a 5-speed manual transmission or a continuously variable transmission (CVT). The Yaris is praised for its fuel efficiency, reliability, and affordability, while criticisms include its lackluster performance, limited interior space, and outdated infotainment system.

**Engine**

**1.5L 4-Cylinder**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **No Issues Found**

**CVT**

* **Delayed Acceleration**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hesitation or delay in acceleration from a stop or during overtaking.
    * Solution: Software update or CVT replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Noise**
    * Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or rattling noise from the front suspension when driving over bumps.
    * Solution: Replacing the strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Electrical**

* **Starter Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the vehicle, especially in cold weather.
    * Solution: Replacing the starter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Breakage**
    * Description/Symptoms: Door handles breaking off, especially the interior handles.
    * Solution: Replacing the door handles.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ per door
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Headliner Sagging**
    * Description/Symptoms: The roof liner fabric sagging or detaching from the ceiling.
    * Solution: Replacing or reattaching the headliner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
* **Climate Control Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Climate control system not functioning properly, such as blowing hot air only or not adjusting fan speed.
    * Solution: Replacing the climate control unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2016 Toyota Yaris has some common issues, particularly with the CVT transmission and electrical components. However, many of these issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to repair. The Yaris remains a reliable and fuel-efficient vehicle for its class, with the majority of owners reporting few problems over time.
