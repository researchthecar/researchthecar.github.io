# 2006 Toyota Yaris

### Overview
The 2006 Toyota Yaris is generally recognized for its strong reliability, a hallmark of Toyota's engineering. This subcompact car was designed with fuel efficiency and practicality in mind, making it a popular choice for urban driving and budget-conscious consumers. While no vehicle is entirely without potential issues, the Yaris from this era typically exhibits a robust build quality. Owners often praise its low running costs and dependable performance over the years. This analysis focuses on common reported technical issues, excluding routine maintenance and expected wear and tear, to provide a clear picture of its long-term durability.

## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light illuminated, difficulty starting the engine, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Power Window Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating up or down, slow window operation.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the power window motor and regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Exterior

### Standard Body and Trim
**Headlight Condensation**
- **Symptoms:** Moisture or fogging inside headlight assemblies.
- **Solution:** Ensure seals are intact; sometimes requires headlight unit replacement if severe.
- **Cost:** $50 - $300 (depending on repair)


## Interior

### Standard Interior Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Resistor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Fan only works on the highest speed setting, or not at all.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250

### Summary Verdict
The 2006 Toyota Yaris stands out as a highly reliable vehicle, with its powertrain (engine and transmission) demonstrating exceptional durability and a lack of widespread common failures. The primary areas where minor issues have been noted are within the electrical system, specifically concerning the alternator and power window motors, and some interior components like the HVAC blower motor resistor. Exterior issues are generally minor, such as headlight condensation. Overall, the Yaris is a dependable choice, with most potential problems being relatively inexpensive to address and not indicative of systemic design flaws. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring its continued longevity.
