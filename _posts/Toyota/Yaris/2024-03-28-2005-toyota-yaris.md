**Introduction:**

The 2005 Toyota Yaris is a subcompact hatchback available with a 1.5-liter 4-cylinder engine and a 4-speed automatic or 5-speed manual transmission. Known for its fuel efficiency and reliability, the Yaris also boasts a spacious interior and comfortable ride. However, some owners have encountered specific issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**1.5L 4-Cylinder Engine**

* **Oil Consumption:** Excessive oil consumption may occur, requiring frequent oil top-ups.
    * **Solution:** Replace piston rings and valve stem seals.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Timing Chain Issues:** The timing chain may stretch or break prematurely, causing engine damage.
    * **Solution:** Replace timing chain and related components.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Engine Knock:** Knocking sounds from the engine may indicate worn piston rings or connecting rod bearings.
    * **Solution:** Replace affected components.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $3,000+

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Slipping Gears:** Transmission may slip out of gear, especially when accelerating or under heavy load.
    * **Solution:** Replace torque converter or rebuild transmission.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,200 - $2,000+

**4-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Strut Problems:** Front struts may wear prematurely, causing clunking noises and poor ride quality.
    * **Solution:** Replace struts.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $400 - $800+

* **Rear Suspension Noise:** Noises from the rear suspension may indicate worn control arms or bushings.
    * **Solution:** Replace affected components.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator may fail prematurely, causing battery discharge and potential electrical issues.
    * **Solution:** Replace alternator.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $600+
* **Starter Problems:** The starter may fail to engage or experience other issues, preventing the engine from starting.
    * **Solution:** Replace starter or repair wiring.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Actuators:** Door lock actuators may fail, causing the doors to lock or unlock inoperable.
    * **Solution:** Replace actuators.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattles:** The dashboard may develop rattles due to loose components.
    * **Solution:** Tighten or replace loose components.
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $200+
* **Air Conditioning Issues:** The air conditioning system may fail to cool or may develop other problems.
    * **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair as needed (e.g., refrigerant leak repair, compressor replacement).
    * **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $1,000+

**Summary:**

Overall, while the 2005 Toyota Yaris is generally a reliable vehicle, it is not without its share of issues. Engine and transmission problems can be costly to repair, while suspension and electrical issues are less severe and expensive. Interior issues are typically minor and can be resolved at a reasonable cost.
