**2023 Toyota Corolla Cross**

The 2023 Toyota Corolla Cross is a subcompact crossover SUV that debuted in 2021. It slots between the smaller Toyota C-HR and the larger Toyota RAV4 in Toyota's lineup. The Corolla Cross is available with two different powertrains: a 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine with 169 horsepower and 151 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine with 171 horsepower and 185 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a CVT automatic transmission. The Corolla Cross comes standard with front-wheel drive, and all-wheel drive is available as an option.

The Corolla Cross has been praised for its spacious interior, fuel efficiency, and comfortable ride. However, it has also been criticized for its lack of power and some of its interior materials.

**Engine**

* **2.0-Liter Four-Cylinder Engine**
    * **Excessive Oil Consumption**
        * Description/Symptoms: Vehicles may experience excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace the piston rings and valve stem seals.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **2.5-Liter Four-Cylinder Engine**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **CVT Automatic Transmission**
    * **Hesitation or Shuddering During Acceleration**
        * Description/Symptoms: Vehicles may exhibit hesitation or shuddering during acceleration, especially when driving at low speeds or uphill.
        * Solution: Update the transmission software or replace the transmission fluid.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery**
    * **Premature Battery Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Batteries may fail prematurely, resulting in difficulty starting the vehicle or electrical system malfunctions.
        * Solution: Replace the battery.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical System**
    * **Loose or Faulty Wiring Harness**
        * Description/Symptoms: Loose or faulty wiring harnesses may cause electrical issues such as flickering lights, malfunctioning gauges, or difficulty starting the vehicle.
        * Solution: Inspect and repair or replace the affected wiring harness.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlights**
    * **Condensation Inside Headlight Assembly**
        * Description/Symptoms: Condensation may accumulate inside the headlight assembly, potentially affecting visibility.
        * Solution: Inspect the headlight seals and replace if necessary.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard**
    * **Rattling or Vibration**
        * Description/Symptoms: Rattling or vibration noises may originate from the dashboard area, especially on rough roads.
        * Solution: Inspect and tighten loose components or apply sound deadening material.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Seatbelts**
    * **Faulty Seatbelt Buckle**
        * Description/Symptoms: Seatbelt buckles may fail to latch or release properly, posing a safety hazard.
        * Solution: Replace the affected seatbelt buckle.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2023 Toyota Corolla Cross has a few reported issues, but most are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix. The most common issues appear to involve excessive oil consumption with the 2.0-liter engine, battery failure, and loose or faulty wiring harnesses. Overall, the Corolla Cross is a reliable and well-equipped vehicle, but it's important to be aware of these potential issues before making a purchase decision.
