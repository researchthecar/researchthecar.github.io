The 2024 Toyota Camry remains a popular choice for its reputation for reliability, fuel efficiency, and comfortable ride. It comes standard with a 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine producing 203 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Other powertrain options include a hybrid powertrain with a 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine paired with an electric motor and a continuously variable transmission (CVT). While lauded for its dependability and comfortable interior, some owners have reported issues, which is to be expected in any vehicle. Some common praises for the Camry include its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and fuel efficiency. Common dislikes tend to focus on the somewhat bland styling and lack of driving excitement compared to some competitors.

**Engine**

**2.5L I4**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report higher-than-expected oil consumption, requiring more frequent oil top-offs between scheduled oil changes. Symptoms may include low oil level warnings and a burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Monitor oil levels regularly. A compression test and leak-down test can help determine the source of the oil consumption. Depending on the findings, solutions can range from replacing PCV valve to more extensive engine work, such as piston ring replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (PCV valve) to $2,000+ (engine work)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing harsh or jerky shifts, especially during low-speed acceleration or deceleration. Hesitation when accelerating from a stop has also been reported.
    *   Solution: A transmission fluid flush may resolve minor issues. More severe cases may require valve body replacement or transmission replacement. Ensure the transmission fluid level is correct.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (fluid flush) to $3,000+ (transmission replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Lagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may freeze, lag, or become unresponsive. This can affect navigation, audio, and climate control functions.
    *   Solution: Perform a system reset. Check for software updates and install them. If the problem persists, the head unit may need replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (reset/update) to $800+ (head unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported rattling or squeaking noises emanating from the dashboard, door panels, or rear deck.
    *   Solution: Identify the source of the noise and tighten any loose fasteners or apply felt tape to prevent friction between parts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (DIY) to $100 - $300+ (professional diagnosis and repair)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2024 Toyota Camry generally maintains Toyota's reputation for reliability. The most commonly reported issues involve the engine, transmission, and electrical system. Engine issues such as excessive oil consumption can be concerning and potentially costly to repair. Transmission issues may range from minor inconveniences to more serious problems requiring replacement. Electrical issues are more likely to be inconveniences than major safety concerns. Overall, while some issues exist, the Camry remains a generally dependable vehicle.
