# 2007 Toyota Camry

### Overview
The 2007 Toyota Camry is generally regarded as a reliable vehicle, continuing Toyota's reputation for durability. It was offered with several powertrain options, including efficient four-cylinder and powerful V6 engines, paired with either automatic or manual transmissions. While the Camry is known for its longevity, like any vehicle, it can experience specific issues as it ages. This analysis focuses on documented common problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and prospective buyers of the 2007 model year.

---


## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder (2AZ-FE)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning, potentially leading to low oil levels and engine damage if not monitored.
- **Solution:** Piston ring replacement or PCV valve cleaning/replacement.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1500

**Variable Valve Timing (VVT-i) Actuator**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the engine, especially on startup; check engine light may illuminate.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the VVT-i actuator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


### 3.5L V6 (2GR-FE)
**Carbon Buildup**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, reduced engine power, potential misfires.
- **Solution:** Intake manifold cleaning, walnut blasting, or fuel system cleaning.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (U250E)
**Harsh Shifting / Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Delayed engagement, rough shifts between gears, or slipping sensation.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement; potential internal repairs or transmission replacement if severe.
- **Cost:** $250 - $600 (fluid/filter), $2000 - $4000 (rebuild/replacement)

## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Electrical Gremlins**
- **Symptoms:** Power window malfunctions, interior light issues, or dashboard warning lights appearing randomly.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


### Alternator
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, difficulty starting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Exterior

### Paint and Body
**Clear Coat Peeling**
- **Symptoms:** Fading or peeling clear coat, particularly on the roof, hood, and trunk.
- **Solution:** Repainting affected panels.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500 per panel


## Interior

### HVAC Blower Motor
**Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air coming from vents or intermittent fan speed.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor and resistor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


### Seat Fabric
**Wear on Driver's Seat Bolster**
- **Symptoms:** Visible wear and tear on the outer edge of the driver's seat.
- **Solution:** Seat cover replacement or professional upholstery repair.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Toyota Camry is a solid choice for reliability, with most owners reporting few major issues. The most commonly cited problems revolve around the 2.4L engine's oil consumption and VVT-i actuator, and potential issues with the automatic transmission's shifting. Electrical components like the alternator and BCM can also be points of failure. Exterior paint can degrade over time, and interior wear on the driver's seat is common. Despite these points, the Camry's overall durability and availability of parts make it a dependable used car option when properly maintained.
