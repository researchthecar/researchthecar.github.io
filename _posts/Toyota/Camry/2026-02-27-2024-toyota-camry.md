# 2024 Toyota Camry Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Toyota Camry, understanding its mechanical layout and common owner-reported concerns helps you make an informed purchase. While the vehicle generally maintains a reputation for daily dependability, reviewing specific powertrain options and reported trouble spots can guide your inspection and negotiation process.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder

This naturally aspirated 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine produces around 203 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque, balancing everyday efficiency with adequate acceleration. It is a proven powerplant known for robust longevity when properly maintained with regular fluid changes.

### 3.5L V6

The smooth 3.5-liter V6 delivers roughly 301 horsepower and 267 lb-ft of torque for a more spirited driving dynamic. This motor is widely regarded as durable, though buyers should verify routine service records to ensure long-term health.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly across various trims and drivetrains, though high-mileage examples warrant a thorough mechanical check.

**Transmission Wear and Shifting Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported premature mechanical wear, unusual behavior, or shifting irregularities at higher mileages.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the transmission fluid condition and perform a diagnostic scan for stored fault codes.

## Steering

### Power Steering

Steering assist systems generally operate smoothly, but intermittent electrical warnings require professional diagnosis.

**Power Steering Assist Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience sudden dashboard warnings for low steering power accompanied by temporary stiffness in the steering wheel.
- **Solution:** Check the electric power steering connections, wiring harnesses, and control modules for intermittent faults.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical accessories and lighting components should be fully tested during a nighttime or garage test drive.

**Headlight Beam Projection**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights appear aimed too low, limiting forward visibility during nighttime driving.
- **Solution:** Have a technician verify proper headlight housing alignment and calibration against factory specifications.

## Interior

### Seats

Interior cabin components are generally resilient, though high-touch plastic trim pieces can experience wear.

**Driver Seat Cushion Shield Breakage**
- **Symptoms:** Internal plastic brackets and clips on the seat cushion shield break, causing power seat switches to become loose or unsupported.
- **Solution:** Inspect power seat adjustments and replace broken plastic trim clips or the cushion shield assembly.

## Safety

### Air Bags

Safety system complaints primarily center around warning indicators and sensor operation.

**Air Bag Warning and Deployment Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have noted unexpected dashboard warning lights or instances where frontal impacts did not trigger airbag deployment.
- **Solution:** Inspect the supplemental restraint system (SRS) module and check for any active warning lights or pending diagnostic trouble codes.

### Seat Belt

Seat belt mechanisms are critical safety items that should be manually verified for secure latching.

**Seat Belt Latch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The front passenger seat belt buckle fails to lock securely, accompanied by audible warning alerts.
- **Solution:** Test all seat belt buckles physically during a pre-purchase inspection and replace faulty latch assemblies as necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V128000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Camry&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 202, "Head Restraints." A head restraint bracket that is insufficiently welded may not properly restrain an occupant during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the 60% rear seat-back frame sub-assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

Overall, this vehicle offers a sensible blend of everyday comfort and proven powertrain options, but buyers should remain diligent. Conducting a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection focusing on the transmission, steering electronics, and safety restraint systems will help ensure a dependable ownership experience.
