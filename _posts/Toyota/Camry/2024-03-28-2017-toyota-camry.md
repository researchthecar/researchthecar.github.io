## 2017 Toyota Camry

The 2017 Toyota Camry is a midsize sedan that comes in eight trims: L, LE, SE, XLE, XSE, Hybrid LE, Hybrid SE, and Hybrid XLE. It is offered with five engine and transmission options:

* 2.5L 4-cylinder with 8-speed automatic transmission (181 hp, 182 lb-ft)
* 3.5L V6 with 8-speed automatic transmission (301 hp, 267 lb-ft)
* 2.5L 4-cylinder hybrid with CVT (203 hp, 244 lb-ft)
* 3.5L V6 hybrid with CVT (219 hp, 214 lb-ft)
* 2.5L 4-cylinder with 6-speed automatic transmission (181 hp, 182 lb-ft) (LE Eco trim only)

The Camry is praised for its reliability, fuel efficiency, and comfortable ride. However, it has also faced some criticism for its bland styling and lack of optional features on lower trims.

**Engine**
* **2.5L 4-cylinder with 8-speed automatic transmission**
    * **Excessive oil consumption:** Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-ups or oil changes. The issue can be caused by a faulty piston ring or valve stem seal. Solution: Replace the affected parts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    * **Throttle body issues:** The throttle body may become dirty or faulty, causing hesitation or stalling. Solution: Clean or replace the throttle body. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **3.5L V6 with 8-speed automatic transmission**
    * **Check engine light issues:** The check engine light may illuminate due to various issues, including faulty oxygen sensors or catalytic converters. Solution: Diagnose and repair the underlying issue. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * **Transmission shudder:** Some owners have experienced a shudder or vibration when shifting gears. Solution: Update the transmission software or replace the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
* **2.5L 4-cylinder hybrid with CVT**
    * **No Issues Found**
* **3.5L V6 hybrid with CVT**
    * **No Issues Found**
* **2.5L 4-cylinder with 6-speed automatic transmission (LE Eco trim only)**
    * **Transmission slipping:** The transmission may slip or hesitate when shifting gears. Solution: Replace the transmission or transmission components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+

**Transmission**
* **8-speed automatic transmission (2.5L 4-cylinder and 3.5L V6 engines)**
    * **See Engine section for related issues.**
* **CVT (2.5L 4-cylinder hybrid and 3.5L V6 hybrid)**
    * **No Issues Found**
* **6-speed automatic transmission (2.5L 4-cylinder LE Eco trim)**
    * **See Engine section for related issues.**

**Suspension**
* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**
* **Battery drain:** Some owners have reported a battery drain issue that may cause the vehicle to struggle to start. Solution: Diagnose and repair the underlying electrical issue. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
* **Headlight issues:** Some owners have experienced problems with the headlights, including flickering, dimming, or failing to turn on. Solution: Inspect and replace the affected bulbs or other components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
* **Infotainment system issues:** The infotainment system may experience glitches, such as crashing, freezing, or displaying errors. Solution: Update the software or replace the infotainment unit. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Exterior**
* **No major issues found**

**Interior**
* **Dashboard rattles:** Some owners have reported rattles or squeaks coming from the dashboard or other interior panels. Solution: Inspect and tighten loose components or apply soundproofing materials. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
* **Seat adjustment issues:** Some owners have experienced problems with the electric seat adjustments, such as malfunctioning switches or sticky mechanisms. Solution: Repair or replace the affected components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Recalls**
* **NHTSA Recall Number:** 19V713
    * **Issue:** Power window master switch may malfunction
    * **Solution:** Replace the power window master switch
* **NHTSA Recall Number:** 20V340
    * **Issue:** Fuel pump may fail
    * **Solution:** Replace the fuel pump

**Summary**
The 2017 Toyota Camry is generally a reliable vehicle, but it has faced some issues, particularly with the engine and transmission. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but some can be costly to repair.
