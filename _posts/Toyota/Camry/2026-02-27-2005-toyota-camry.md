# 2005 Toyota Camry

### Overview
The 2005 Toyota Camry is widely recognized for its robust build quality and dependable performance, making it a popular choice in the mid-size sedan segment. This generation of Camry is known for its longevity and relatively low ownership costs, appealing to a broad range of drivers seeking a reliable daily driver. While generally very solid, like any vehicle, certain components have seen more attention than others over time. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and prospective buyers.

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder (2AZ-FE)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning, potentially leading to low oil levels and engine damage if not monitored.
- **Solution:** Piston ring replacement or oil control ring replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Variable Valve Timing (VVT-i) Actuator**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the engine, especially on startup or at idle.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the VVT-i actuator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Power Window Actuators**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows failing to operate or moving slowly.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the power window motor/regulator.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400 per window

**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light illuminated, dimming headlights, engine stalling.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2005 Toyota Camry stands out as a highly reliable vehicle, particularly for its powertrain and overall build. The most commonly cited issues revolve around the 2.4L 4-cylinder engine's tendency for oil consumption and potential VVT-i actuator noise, which are manageable with regular checks and timely repairs. Electrical components like power window regulators and alternators can also require attention. However, these are not typically indicative of systemic failure and are often repairable at reasonable costs. The V6 engines and transmissions are exceptionally robust. Overall, the Camry offers excellent long-term value and dependability, with most potential issues being well-documented and addressable.
