**Introduction:**

The 2014 Toyota Camry is a midsize sedan known for its reliability and value. It offers a range of engine and transmission options, including a 2.5-liter four-cylinder, a 3.5-liter V6, and a 2.5-liter hybrid. The four-cylinder engine produces 178 horsepower and 170 lb-ft of torque, while the V6 generates 268 horsepower and 248 lb-ft of torque. The hybrid system combines the four-cylinder engine with an electric motor to achieve a combined output of 200 horsepower. Despite its overall popularity, the 2014 Camry has experienced some reported issues.

**Engine**

**2.5-liter Four-Cylinder**

* **Intake manifold leaks (2AR-FE engine):** Leaks can develop around the intake manifold gasket, causing coolant loss and overheating.
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 14V314000
* **Piston ring failure:** Excessive oil consumption and engine knocking may indicate piston ring failure.
    * Solution: Engine rebuild or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.5-liter V6**

* **Oil leaks:** Leaks may occur from the valve covers, oil pan, or rear main seal.
    * Solution: Replace leaking seals or gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission slipping:** Shifts may hesitate or slip when accelerating or shifting gears.
    * Solution: Replace transmission solenoid or valve body.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**CVT (Hybrid Only)**

* **Transmission shudder:** Jerking or shuddering during acceleration or deceleration.
    * Solution: Software update or replace transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut mount bearing failure:** Noise or vibration during turns or over bumps.
    * Solution: Replace strut mount bearings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** Battery may discharge quickly when parked.
    * Solution: Test and replace faulty battery or electrical component.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Navigation system malfunction:** Inability to use GPS or other navigation features.
    * Solution: Software update or replace navigation unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight condensation:** Accumulation of moisture inside the headlight assemblies.
    * Solution: Replace headlight assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Center console rattle:** Loose or vibrating center console.
    * Solution: Replace or adjust console components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2014 Toyota Camry is a reliable vehicle, but it has experienced some issues. The most severe and costly issues involve the engine, particularly the intake manifold leaks and piston ring failure. Transmission issues are also common and can be expensive to repair. Minor electrical and exterior issues are typically less costly to address.
