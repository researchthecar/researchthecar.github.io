# 2012 Toyota Camry

### Overview
The 2012 Toyota Camry, a popular mid-size sedan, is generally recognized for its strong reliability and durability. This generation, the XV50, aimed to blend comfort with a more engaging driving experience. Across its various powertrain options, the Camry typically offers a dependable ownership experience, with owners reporting satisfaction with its long-term performance. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the 2012 Camry stands out for its robust engineering and consistent quality, making it a solid choice for those seeking a reliable daily driver. Common areas of attention, though infrequent, often relate to minor electrical glitches or specific component wear over extended mileage.

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional freezing or unresponsiveness of the touchscreen infotainment system.
- **Solution:** Software updates or system reset. In rare cases, replacement of the head unit may be required.
- **Cost:** $100 - $1500

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Premature battery discharge, potentially linked to parasitic draw from certain modules.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the source of the drain, followed by repair or replacement of faulty components.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Toyota Camry is a highly reliable vehicle, consistently performing well across major mechanical and structural components. The powertrain options, including the 2.5L 4-cylinder and 3.5L V6 engines paired with the 6-speed automatic transmission, are known for their durability and lack of widespread issues. While minor electrical gremlins like infotainment system glitches or occasional battery drain can occur, they are not pervasive and are generally manageable. The suspension, exterior, and interior components also hold up well over time. Overall, the 2012 Camry offers a dependable and cost-effective ownership experience with minimal unexpected repair costs related to major failures.
