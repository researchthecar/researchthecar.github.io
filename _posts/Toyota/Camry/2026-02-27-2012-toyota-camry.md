# 2012 Toyota Camry Reliability & Common Issues

### Overview

The 2012 Toyota Camry is a highly popular midsize sedan praised for its practicality, comfort, and general mechanical resilience. However, like any used vehicle, it has been the subject of owner-reported complaints submitted to the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA). These complaints serve as a helpful sample of issues some owners have faced, though they do not guarantee that every 2012 Camry will experience these problems. Prospective buyers should focus on key areas such as transmission behavior, steering column rattling, and electrical accessories during their pre-purchase inspections.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder (2AR-FE)

This 2.5-liter inline four-cylinder engine produces approximately 178 horsepower and 170 lb-ft of torque. It is highly regarded for its long-term durability and mechanical simplicity, making it the most common and practical choice for used-car buyers.

_No major issues reported for this configuration._

### 3.5L V6 (2GR-FE)

The 3.5-liter dual-overhead-cam V6 engine delivers a robust 268 horsepower and 248 lb-ft of torque. It offers smooth, strong acceleration and excellent highway performance with a solid track record for mechanical longevity.

_No major issues reported for this configuration._

### 2.5L Hybrid (2AR-FXE)

This hybrid powertrain pairs an Atkinson-cycle 2.5-liter four-cylinder with an electric motor to generate a combined 200 horsepower. It is an exceptionally fuel-efficient option, though used buyers should verify the health of the hybrid battery pack and cooling system.

_No major issues reported for this configuration._

### General / multiple configurations

**Engine Stalling and Sudden Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Reports of the engine stalling during hard stops, quick deceleration, or losing motive power unexpectedly while driving, sometimes accompanied by flashing dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Thorough engine diagnostics are required. Some owners report rapid oil consumption as a contributor to low oil levels and subsequent stalling, while others point to throttle body or gasket issues.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (U760E / U660E)

This conventional six-speed automatic transmission is paired with both the four-cylinder (U760E) and V6 (U660E) gasoline engines. While typically smooth, it is the focus of a significant number of owner complaints regarding torque converter shudder.

**Torque Converter Shudder**
- **Symptoms:** Vibration, abnormal shaking, or hesitation when accelerating under light load, typically at speeds between 20 and 50 mph or when shifting between 4th and 5th gears.
- **Solution:** Reprogramming the Engine Control Module (ECM) or replacing the transmission's torque converter.
- **Cost:** Quoted out-of-pocket costs range from $2,500 to $4,500 for a full torque converter replacement.

## Suspension

### Standard Suspension

**Control Arm Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or popping noises during braking or low-speed maneuvers, potentially due to severe corrosion on the control arms.
- **Solution:** Replacing the front lower control arm and getting a professional alignment.

## Brakes

### Brake System

**Brake Booster and Actuator Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** ABS, traction control, and master warning lights illuminating on the dashboard, accompanied by reduced braking power, a stiff pedal, or extended stopping distances.
- **Solution:** Replacing the brake booster assembly or the brake actuator module.

**Sudden Unintended Acceleration**
- **Symptoms:** Reports of the vehicle accelerating rapidly or surging forward unexpectedly, with the brakes reportedly failing to respond or slow down the car.
- **Solution:** A thorough safety diagnostic of the throttle pedal assembly, floor mat clearance, and electronic brake-override systems.

## Steering

### Steering Gear and Column

**Steering Column Rattling and Damper Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A clicking, knocking, or rattling noise from the steering wheel area, especially when driving on rough roads or turning the wheel.
- **Solution:** Replacing the small, degraded plastic steering column damper bushing (part # 4525428040). This requires significant labor to disassemble the steering column.
- **Cost:** Although the part itself costs under $30, intensive labor drives dealer repair estimates between $750 and $1,000.

**Electric Power Steering (EPS) Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The EPS warning light illuminating on the dashboard, accompanied by a sudden, heavy resistance in steering assist, making the vehicle highly difficult to steer.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scan and potential replacement of the electric steering motor or steering column control module.

## Electrical

### Door Lock Actuators

**Intermittent Door Lock Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Individual door locks failing to lock or unlock when using the key fob, interior switches, or automatic shifting. The mechanism may make a clicking sound but fail to operate.
- **Solution:** Replacing the door lock actuator assembly for the affected door(s).
- **Cost:** Owners report out-of-pocket repair costs ranging from $642 to $656 per door actuator.

### Infotainment System

**Center Console Display and Audio Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of audio functionality, slow or unresponsive GPS and touchscreen operations, and occasional display flickering.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic testing of the unit's software and wiring harness, which may require replacement of the infotainment head unit or steering column wiring harness.
- **Cost:** Replacing the receiver head unit is reported to cost around $827, and replacing a damaged wiring harness can cost up to $650.

## Exterior

### Body Panels and Glass

**Spontaneous Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The sunroof glass suddenly shattering with a loud pop or explosion-like noise while driving at highway speeds, even in mild weather.
- **Solution:** Replacing the sunroof/moonroof glass panel.

## Interior

### Cabin Accommodations

**Driver's Seat Track Play**
- **Symptoms:** The driver's seat wiggling or shifting slightly backward and forward when accelerating, braking, or entering the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Inspecting and tightening or replacing the driver's seat track rails or mounting brackets.

## Safety

### Occupant Restraints

**Airbag Warning Light and Spiral Cable Faults**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning light remaining illuminated on the dash, sometimes accompanied by a non-functional horn or clicking noises within the steering wheel when turning.
- **Solution:** Replacing the steering wheel spiral cable (clock spring) or clearing a clogged A/C evaporator drain hose to prevent water leakage from damaging steering column electrical modules.
- **Cost:** Repairs for steering column airbag issues are reported to cost around $900 out-of-pocket.

**Airbag Non-Deployment in Collisions**
- **Symptoms:** Airbags failing to deploy during moderate-to-severe front or passenger-side collisions.
- **Solution:** Inspection of the front impact sensors, wiring harnesses, and the main SRS control unit.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 13V014000: Air Bags:Frontal:Sensor/Control Module-Inactive](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Camry&modelYear=2012)**
- **Issue:** During modification by SET to include accessories such as leather seat covers, seat heaters or headrest DVD systems, these vehicles may not have had the passenger seat occupant sensing system calibration tested. Without passing the calibration test, the occupant sensing system may not operate as designed.
- **What to do:** Southeast Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will test the sensitivity of the occupant detection sensors, and recalibrate them as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 13V442000: Electrical System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Camry&modelYear=2012)**
- **Issue:** In the affected vehicles, the drain hose for the air conditioning condenser may become clogged causing water to accumulate at the bottom of the air conditioning condenser unit housing. The accumulated water may then leak through a seam in the housing onto the air bag control module potentially resulting in a short circuit of the module.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will seal the air conditioning condenser unit housing and install a protective cover on the airbag control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

### Summary Verdict

The 2012 Toyota Camry remains a highly practical and generally reliable choice for a used midsize sedan. However, prospective buyers must be aware of the widely documented torque converter shudder issue affecting automatic models, as well as the potential for rattling steering columns and failing door lock actuators. Conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection that includes testing the transmission at speeds between 20-50 mph, listening for steering clicks, and checking all door locks is highly recommended to avoid costly out-of-pocket repairs.
