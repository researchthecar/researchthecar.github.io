# 2012 Toyota Camry Reliability & Common Issues

> Provider: `gemini`. Generated: `2026-07-13T00:18:08+00:00`.

### Overview

The 2012 Toyota Camry is the subject of numerous consumer complaints, with most issues centered around the engine, drivetrain, and transmission systems. Specifically, owners frequently report transmission shuddering, steering column rattling, electrical issues involving door lock actuators, and unexpected engine stalling. While historically a popular model, these documented reports suggest potential buyers should carefully inspect several key components.

---

## Transmission

### Power Train

**Transmission Shudder and Torque Converter Defect**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report a brief shuddering sensation, vibration, and shaking during acceleration under light load, particularly between 20 and 50 mph. Some reports note transmission slippage, hesitation, power loss when merging, and the vehicle rolling forward slightly when placed in park.
- **Solution:** Updating the Engine Control Module (ECM) software or completely replacing the torque converter or transmission assembly.
- **Cost:** $4,500 quoted for torque converter replacement.
- **Evidence:** `nhtsa-complaint-11664573`, `nhtsa-complaint-11664166`, `nhtsa-complaint-11659138`, `nhtsa-complaint-11649727`, `nhtsa-complaint-11647824`, `nhtsa-complaint-11602666`, `nhtsa-complaint-11572523`, `nhtsa-complaint-11561974`, `nhtsa-complaint-11547589`, `nhtsa-complaint-11546924`, `nhtsa-complaint-11525341`, `nhtsa-complaint-11511998`

## Drivetrain

### Power Train

**Acceleration Hesitation and Sluggishness**
- **Symptoms:** Reports describe abnormal shaking and vibration while accelerating, severe lag when moving from a complete stop, and a temporary loss of motive power that requires releasing and re-depressing the accelerator pedal.
- **Solution:** Replacing the torque converter or performing software updates.
- **Cost:** $2,500 quoted for torque converter replacement.
- **Evidence:** `nhtsa-complaint-11734130`, `nhtsa-complaint-11681409`, `nhtsa-complaint-11518096`, `nhtsa-complaint-11517849`, `nhtsa-complaint-11500820`, `nhtsa-complaint-11498484`, `nhtsa-complaint-11474210`, `nhtsa-complaint-11465318`, `nhtsa-complaint-11463855`, `nhtsa-complaint-11451868`, `nhtsa-complaint-11434769`, `nhtsa-complaint-11432421`

## Engine

### Engine

**Engine Stalling and Rapid Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may shut off or stall unexpectedly, particularly when performing hard or quick stops. Drivers report that the vehicle consumes synthetic engine oil exceptionally fast with no leaks or dashboard warning lights appearing prior to the stall.
- **Solution:** Frequently checking and topping off engine oil, or replacing throttle bodies and gaskets.
- **Evidence:** `nhtsa-complaint-11579394`, `nhtsa-complaint-11709755`, `nhtsa-complaint-11698853`, `nhtsa-complaint-11676858`, `nhtsa-complaint-11668312`, `nhtsa-complaint-11651976`, `nhtsa-complaint-11602428`, `nhtsa-complaint-11558152`

## Steering

### Steering Column

**Steering Column Rattle and Bushing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners describe a rattle, tapping, or metallic knocking sound coming from the left side of the engine or cabin area, especially on rough roads. Other symptoms include the steering wheel shaking over 60 mph or feeling unusually tight.
- **Solution:** Replacing a degraded plastic steering damper coupling gear (part 45254-28040) or intermediate steering shaft.
- **Cost:** Labor costs are estimated between $750 and $1,000 due to the extensive teardown required, even though the replacement part itself is inexpensive ($5 - $30).
- **Evidence:** `nhtsa-complaint-11592385`, `nhtsa-complaint-11424275`, `nhtsa-complaint-11526189`, `nhtsa-complaint-11584246`, `nhtsa-complaint-11538025`, `nhtsa-complaint-11407300`, `nhtsa-complaint-11364761`, `nhtsa-complaint-11360312`, `nhtsa-complaint-11344234`

## Electrical

### Electrical System

**Intermittent Door Lock Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report that door locks fail to lock or unlock when using the key fob or internal locking buttons. The failure often occurs intermittently, forcing drivers to manually lock or unlock affected doors.
- **Solution:** Replacing the faulty door lock actuators.
- **Cost:** Quoted between $642 and $656 per door lock actuator replacement.
- **Evidence:** `nhtsa-complaint-11230798`, `nhtsa-complaint-11229608`, `nhtsa-complaint-11220313`, `nhtsa-complaint-11205034`, `nhtsa-complaint-11128442`

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

**Sudden Unintended Acceleration and Brake Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers state that while attempting to brake, the vehicle suddenly accelerated or surged forward, with the brakes failing to respond or slow the vehicle. In some instances, ABS, Brake, and Traction Control warning lights illuminated simultaneously.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of the brake booster, brake light switch, or brake actuator.
- **Evidence:** `nhtsa-complaint-11645309`, `nhtsa-complaint-11624333`, `nhtsa-complaint-11533568`, `nhtsa-complaint-11496792`, `nhtsa-complaint-11456540`, `nhtsa-complaint-11309904`, `nhtsa-complaint-11140681`, `nhtsa-complaint-11131408`

## Safety

### Air Bag

**Air Bag Warning Light and Non-Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** Reports indicate persistent air bag and seat belt warning lights on the dashboard. In some collisions, the airbags failed to deploy. Other symptoms include a clicking sound inside the steering wheel accompanied by a non-functional horn, or water leaking from the airbag compartment seam.
- **Solution:** Replacing faulty steering column airbag pins, screws, clockspring, or clearing clogged AC condenser drain hoses.
- **Cost:** $900 quoted for steering column airbag repair.
- **Evidence:** `nhtsa-complaint-11655194`, `nhtsa-complaint-11601838`, `nhtsa-complaint-11561729`, `nhtsa-complaint-11462528`, `nhtsa-complaint-11450881`, `nhtsa-complaint-11449205`, `nhtsa-complaint-11408272`

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**Recall 13V014000: Air Bags:Frontal:Sensor/Control Module-Inactive**
- **Issue:** Southeast Toyota Distributors, LLC (SET) is recalling certain models interspersed through model years 2009 through 2013 as follows: model year 2009-2012 Tacoma, 4Runner, Camry, Camry Hybrid, Prius, and RAV4; model year 2009-2010 Avalon, FJ Cruiser, and Highlander Hybrid; model year 2010-2013 model year Corolla, Sienna and Tundra; model year 2009-2013 Highlander and Venza; model year 2012 Prius V; and model year 2010-2012 Sequoia.  During modification by SET to include accessories such as leather seat covers, seat heaters or headrest DVD systems, these vehicles may not have had the passenger seat occupant sensing system calibration tested.  Without passing the calibration test, the occupant sensing system may not operate as designed. If the front passenger seat occupant sensing system is out of calibration, the front passenger airbags may not deploy or they may deploy inappropriately for the passenger's size and position.  This could increase the risk of personal injury during the event of a vehicle crash necessitating airbag deployment. Southeast Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will test the sensitivity of the occupant detection sensors, and recalibrate them as necessary.  The recall began on March 21, 2013.  Owners may contact Southeast Toyota at 1-800-301-6859.
- **Remedy:** Check recall completion status with the manufacturer or NHTSA.
- **Evidence:** `nhtsa-recall-13V014000`

**Recall 13V442000: Electrical System**
- **Issue:** Toyota is recalling certain model year 2012-2013 Avalon, Avalon HV, Venza, Camry, and Camry HV vehicles.  In the affected vehicles, the drain hose for the air conditioning condenser may become clogged causing water to accumulate at the bottom of the air conditioning condenser unit housing.  The accumulated water may then leak through a seam in the housing onto the air bag control module potentially resulting in a short circuit of the module. A short circuit may cause the air bags to become disabled or inadvertently deploy.  An inadvertent airbag deployment can increase the risk of injury or the possibility of a crash.  An inoperative airbag can increase the risk of injury in a severe crash.  The power steering assist could also become inoperable resulting in increased steering effort and can increase the risk of a crash at low speeds. Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will seal the air conditioning condenser unit housing and install a protective cover on the airbag control module, free of charge.  The recall began on February 5, 2014.  Owners may contact Toyota at 1-800-331-4331.
- **Remedy:** Check recall completion status with the manufacturer or NHTSA.
- **Evidence:** `nhtsa-recall-13V442000`

### Summary Verdict

The 2012 Toyota Camry has several notable recurring issues that potential used-car buyers must monitor. While it can be a reliable vehicle, the prominent transmission and drivetrain shuddering issues are highly reported and can lead to costly torque converter replacements (up to $4,500). Furthermore, steering column rattles due to degraded coupling dampers are common, and though the replacement parts are inexpensive, labor costs can easily reach $750 to $1,000. It is highly recommended to have these systems, along with the door lock actuators, thoroughly inspected prior to purchase.

## Warnings

- Test drive the vehicle between 20 and 50 mph to check for transmission/drivetrain shuddering or acceleration hesitation, which may indicate an impending torque converter failure.
- Listen carefully for any rattling or knocking noises coming from the steering wheel area when turning or driving over rough surfaces.
- Operate the remote key fob multiple times to ensure all doors lock and unlock reliably, avoiding costly actuator replacements.
- Monitor engine oil levels frequently, as some drivers report rapid oil consumption leading to sudden stalling without prior dashboard warnings.
