**Introduction**

The 2023 Toyota Camry is a midsize sedan offered with a range of powertrains, including a 2.5-liter 4-cylinder, a 3.5-liter V6, and a hybrid system that combines a 2.5-liter 4-cylinder engine with an electric motor. The Camry is known for its reliability, fuel efficiency, and spacious interior, but it has also been reported to have certain issues.

**Engine**

**2.5-liter 4-cylinder**

* **Excessive oil consumption:** Some owners have reported that their 2.5-liter engines consume excessive amounts of oil, requiring frequent top-offs.
    * Solution: Replacing the piston rings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.5-liter V6**

* **Timing chain guide failure:** In some cases, the timing chain guide in the 3.5-liter V6 engine can fail, leading to engine damage.
    * Solution: Replacing the timing chain and guide.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $3,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Hesitation during acceleration:** Some owners have reported that the 8-speed automatic transmission hesitates or jerks during acceleration.
    * Solution: Updating the transmission software or replacing the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** Some owners have experienced rapid battery drain, particularly when the vehicle is parked for extended periods.
    * Solution: Replacing the battery or identifying and fixing electrical drains.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Fog light condensation:** Some owners have reported that condensation builds up inside the fog lights, impairing visibility.
    * Solution: Replacing the fog lights or installing a vent kit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Uncomfortable seats:** Some owners have complained that the front seats are uncomfortable, particularly on long drives.
    * Solution: Purchasing aftermarket seat covers or having the seats reupholstered.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2023 Toyota Camry is a reliable and well-built vehicle, but it has been reported to have some issues, particularly with the engine, transmission, and electrical systems. While some of these issues can be minor and cost-effective to fix, others can be more serious and expensive to repair. Potential owners should be aware of these issues before making a purchase decision.
