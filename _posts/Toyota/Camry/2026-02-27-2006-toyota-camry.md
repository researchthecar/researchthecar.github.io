# 2006 Toyota Camry

### Overview
The 2006 Toyota Camry is widely recognized for its robust build quality and long-term reliability, making it a popular choice in the mid-size sedan segment. This generation (XV30) continued Toyota's legacy of dependable transportation, offering a comfortable ride and a practical interior. While generally very reliable, like any vehicle, certain components can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented common problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and prospective buyers. The Camry's reputation for durability is largely upheld by its well-engineered powertrain and solid construction.

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder (2AZ-FE)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning, potentially leading to low oil levels and engine damage if not monitored.
- **Solution:** Engine rebuild or replacement, piston ring replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $4,000

**Variable Valve Timing (VVT-i) Actuator**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the engine, especially on startup; check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the VVT-i actuator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Electrical Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical components malfunctioning, such as power windows, door locks, or interior lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,000


### Alternator
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light illuminated, dimming headlights, difficulty starting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Exterior

### Body Panels & Paint
**Clear Coat Peeling**
- **Symptoms:** The clear coat on the roof, hood, and trunk begins to peel or flake, especially on vehicles exposed to harsh sunlight.
- **Solution:** Repainting affected panels.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,000+


### Headlights
**Clouding/Yellowing**
- **Symptoms:** Headlight lenses become cloudy or yellow, reducing light output and visibility.
- **Solution:** Headlight restoration kit or replacement of headlight assemblies.
- **Cost:** $50 - $300


## Interior

### HVAC Blower Motor
**Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air coming from the vents, or intermittent fan speed.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor.
- **Cost:** $150 - $350


### Driver's Seat Upholstery
**Wear and Tear**
- **Symptoms:** The fabric or leather on the driver's seat can show significant wear, tearing, or cracking over time due to frequent use.
- **Solution:** Seat cover installation or professional upholstery repair/replacement.
- **Cost:** $100 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2006 Toyota Camry is a highly reliable vehicle, with most owners reporting satisfaction and few major mechanical issues. The 4-cylinder engine, while generally robust, can be prone to oil consumption and VVT-i actuator problems, which are the most commonly cited engine-specific concerns. Transmission and suspension systems are typically very durable. Electrical gremlins, particularly related to the BCM, and exterior paint issues like clear coat peeling are the most frequent non-powertrain complaints. Interior wear on the driver's seat is also common due to age and usage. Overall, the Camry remains a solid choice for those seeking a dependable used car, provided these potential issues are addressed or have been previously resolved.
