# 2016 Toyota Camry Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Toyota Camry, buyers generally expect stellar reliability and longevity. While this model year maintains a strong reputation for everyday dependability, reviewing owner reports highlights a few recurring areas to inspect carefully. Prospective buyers should thoroughly check electronics, structural seals, and safety systems during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder

This naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine produces 178 horsepower and 170 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard and most common powerplant for this model year. It is widely praised for its durability, fuel efficiency, and low maintenance requirements.

### 3.5L V6

The available V6 engine generates a robust 268 horsepower and 248 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration and smooth highway passing power. It pairs strong performance with solid mechanical longevity when properly maintained.

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Hybrid

The hybrid powertrain combines a 2.5-liter 4-cylinder engine with an electric motor to deliver a net 200 horsepower and exceptional city fuel economy. The hybrid system has proven to be remarkably robust over long-term ownership.

## Transmission

### Vehicle Speed Control

Components related to electronic throttle control and vehicle speed management generally perform well, though rare reports note hesitation or surging.

**Unintended Acceleration and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported sudden unexpected engine surges or intermittent engine stalling while operating the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the electronic throttle body, accelerator pedal sensor, and related powertrain control modules.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The conventional 6-speed automatic transmission delivers seamless gear changes and works reliably across all standard trim levels. Routine fluid checks ensure long-term shifting smoothness.

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The electronically controlled CVT found in hybrid variants optimizes fuel delivery and power distribution smoothly. It requires minimal specialized upkeep beyond standard manufacturer recommendations.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Touch Screen

In-cabin electronics and infotainment displays can experience intermittent freezing or unresponsiveness, particularly during extreme temperatures.

**Touch Screen Freeze and Radio Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display screen freezes, becomes unresponsive to touch, or steering wheel audio controls stop working.
- **Solution:** Update the software firmware or replace the radio head unit if internal hardware has failed.

## Exterior

### Structure and Body Panels

Exterior body inspections should focus on paint condition around the windshield glass and solid construction of door frames.

**Paint Peel and Door Intrusion Bar Welds**
- **Symptoms:** Small paint bubbling or peeling around windshield pillars, or rattling noises caused by failed door intrusion bar welds.
- **Solution:** Check body panels and door structures for corrosion or weld separation, and consult a body shop for professional reinforcement.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Supplemental restraint systems are critical for occupant protection, making a scan for stored diagnostic trouble codes essential during a pre-purchase evaluation.

**Air Bag Warning Lights and Non-Deployment Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note persistent airbag warning lights illuminated on the dashboard or instances where airbags failed to deploy during severe frontal impacts.
- **Solution:** Inspect the airbag control module, wiring harnesses, and seat sensors for fault codes or loose connections.

### Seat Belts

Seat belt mechanisms should be manually tested across all seating positions to ensure smooth extension and secure retraction.

**Seat Belt Retractor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Driver and passenger side rear seat belts fail to retract properly, remaining loose or stuck.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt assemblies and replace faulty retractors or tensioners as necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V420000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Camry&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The Load Carrying Capacity Modification Label may not reflect the correct added weight of the installed accessories. As a result, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims for Passenger Cars." An incorrect label may lead an owner to overload the vehicle, increasing the.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 16V215000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Camry&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As a result, the front passenger air bag and the front passenger knee air bag may not deploy as intended in certain crash scenarios. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." Air bags that do not deploy as intended during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will recalibrate the OCS, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

This vehicle remains a solid used-car choice for buyers seeking practical transportation, comfort, and strong resale value. While owner complaints point to occasional infotainment glitches, seat belt retractor issues, and structural paint wear, the core mechanical systems remain remarkably tough. Conducting a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection that checks all electronics, safety restraints, and body seals will help ensure a dependable ownership experience.
