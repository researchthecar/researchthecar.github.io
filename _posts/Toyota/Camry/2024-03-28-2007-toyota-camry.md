**Introduction to the 2007 Toyota Camry:**

The 2007 Toyota Camry is a mid-size sedan available with various engine and transmission options. The base engine is a 2.4-liter four-cylinder producing 158 horsepower and 161 lb-ft of torque. A 3.5-liter V6 with 268 horsepower and 248 lb-ft of torque is also offered. Transmission choices include a five-speed manual, a five-speed automatic, and a six-speed automatic. The Camry is known for its reliability, fuel efficiency, and spacious interior. However, some owners have reported various issues with the vehicle, which are detailed below.

**Engine**

**2.4L Four-Cylinder**

* **Oil Consumption:** Some engines experience excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
* **Timing Chain Failure:** The timing chain can fail prematurely, causing engine damage.
* **Ignition Coil Failure:** The ignition coils can fail, leading to misfires and poor engine performance.

**3.5L V6**

* **VVT-i System Failure:** The Variable Valve Timing-intelligent (VVT-i) system components can fail, resulting in reduced engine power and fuel efficiency.
* **Water Pump Failure:** The water pump can fail, leading to overheating and potential damage to the engine.

**Transmission**

**Five-Speed Automatic**

* **Shift Solenoid Failure:** The shift solenoids can fail, causing delayed or erratic shifting.

**Six-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slippage:** Some transmissions experience slippage, leading to loss of power and acceleration issues.

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure:** The strut mounts can fail, leading to noise, vibration, and reduced handling.
* **Sway Bar Link Failure:** The sway bar links can fail, causing rattling noises and reduced stability.

**Electrical**

* **Electrical Gremlins:** Various electrical issues, including flickering lights, dash warnings, and inoperative components, have been reported.

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Moisture Accumulation:** The headlights can accumulate moisture, leading to poor visibility.

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:** The dashboard can develop cracks, especially in hot weather.
* **Climate Control Malfunctions:** The climate control system can experience issues, leading to ineffective heating or cooling.

**Summary:**

The 2007 Toyota Camry is a generally reliable vehicle, but it has experienced some issues. Engine oil consumption, timing chain failure, VVT-i system failure, and transmission slippage are significant concerns. While the severity and cost of these issues vary, owners should be aware of potential problems and address them promptly.
