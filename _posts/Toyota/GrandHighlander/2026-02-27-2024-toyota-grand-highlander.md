# 2024 Toyota Grand Highlander Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Toyota Grand Highlander offers spacious family-friendly accommodations, but buyers exploring used models should review initial owner complaints carefully. Early production models have drawn reports concerning electrical warning systems, hesitation during acceleration, and body structure concerns like hood flutter. Evaluating maintenance records and addressing software updates can help ensure a smoother ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 2.4-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine delivers strong daily drivability and robust torque for a large three-row SUV. It balances performance with modern efficiency, though early models require attention to software and sensor management.

**Loss of Motive Power and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected hesitation, sluggish acceleration, or sudden engine stalling when attempting to move from a stop or while navigating intersections.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the powertrain control module and fuel delivery system for diagnostic trouble codes and perform any applicable software updates.

**Electrical Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** Multiple warning messages and dashboard lights illuminating simultaneously regarding the engine, hybrid components, or auxiliary systems during highway driving.
- **Solution:** Scan the vehicle computer for diagnostic trouble codes and check sensor wiring harnesses and software calibrations.

### 2.5L Hybrid 4-Cylinder

The 2.5-liter hybrid powertrain pairs a four-cylinder engine with electric motors to maximize fuel economy across city and highway driving. It is a popular choice for families prioritizing efficiency, though routine checks of fuel and electrical systems are recommended.

**Fuel Odor or Leak Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have occasionally noted unusual fuel smells near the vehicle after parking or operating in residential areas.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel lines, tank connections, and EVAP system components for secure fittings and potential leaks.

### 2.4L Hybrid Max Turbo

The Hybrid Max combines a 2.4-liter turbo engine with high-output electric motors to deliver peak horsepower and strong towing capability. It provides brisk acceleration for highway passing and heavy passenger loads.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission is paired with traditional gas-powered models to provide smooth gear selections and highway cruising efficiency. Routine inspection of axle assemblies helps prevent premature wear.

**Transmission Shudder and Drivetrain Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have noted occasional power delivery shudders during acceleration or clicking noises originating from the axle areas during low-speed turns.
- **Solution:** Inspect axle shafts and transmission mounts, and verify if technical service guidelines or software recalibrations apply.

### CVT / Electronic Continuously Variable Transmission

The electronic continuously variable transmission manages power delivery seamlessly in hybrid variants, optimizing energy flow between the gas engine and electric motors. It operates quietly and contributes to overall fuel savings.

### Direct Shift 6-Speed Automatic

The Direct Shift automatic transmission supports high-output hybrid configurations, delivering responsive performance under demanding driving conditions.

## Suspension

### Suspension and Cabin Pressure

Suspension components generally provide a compliant ride, though isolated cabin acoustics and low-speed noises have been noted by owners.

**Low-Frequency Cabin Noise and Popping**
- **Symptoms:** Occupants may experience a low-frequency bass sound or ear pressure changes at low speeds, along with occasional popping sensations near the steering or pedal area.
- **Solution:** Inspect suspension bushings, stabilizer bar mounts, and body sealing points for proper fitment.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Sensor System

Electrical and infotainment systems govern modern driver assistance and connectivity features, requiring occasional software updates to maintain stability.

**Infotainment Blank Screen**
- **Symptoms:** The center touchscreen display may intermittently go black or freeze while driving, requiring a system or vehicle restart.
- **Solution:** Update the infotainment system software to the latest manufacturer release or inspect display module connections.

## Exterior

### Hood Structure

Exterior body panels on early production models occasionally exhibit aerodynamic sensitivity at elevated speeds.

**Hood Vibration and Flutter**
- **Symptoms:** At highway speeds, the metal hood panel can visibly flex or vibrate under strong headwinds, causing driver distraction due to surface glare.
- **Solution:** Have a dealership inspect hood latch adjustments and structural bracing to ensure proper rigidity.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

- **Issue:** The driver and passenger side curtain air bags may not unroll as intended and could partially deploy outside of an open window. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 226, "Ejection Mitigation." An improper deployment of the side curtain air bag can reduce occupant protection during a.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver and passenger curtain shield air bag assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V548000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Grand+Highlander&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." A vehicle with an incorrect maximum capacity weight value may be overloaded, which can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V595000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Grand+Highlander&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** An instrument panel display that does not show critical information can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the instrument panel software over-the-air (OTA) for non-PHEV vehicles, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V744000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Grand+Highlander&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the rearview camera to freeze or display a blank screen when the vehicle is in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that fails to display an image can reduce the driver's view behind the vehicle.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the parking assist software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Toyota Grand Highlander remains a compelling choice for families seeking generous cargo room, passenger comfort, and efficient powertrain choices. While early owner feedback highlights areas such as electrical software stability, sporadic engine hesitation, and hood vibration, many of these concerns can be managed through authorized dealer inspections and software updates. Used car buyers should verify that all open updates and recalls have been completed before finalizing a purchase.
