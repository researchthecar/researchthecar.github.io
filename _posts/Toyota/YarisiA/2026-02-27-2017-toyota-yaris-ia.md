# 2017 Toyota Yaris iA Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Toyota Yaris iA brings a blend of nimble handling and great fuel economy, largely thanks to its Mazda roots during the partnership era. While generally considered an economical and reliable commuter choice, used car buyers should pay attention to a few recurring owner complaints, particularly concerning highway engine stalls, steering component binding, and touchscreen infotainment defects.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

Equipped with a 1.5-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine producing around 106 horsepower, the Yaris iA offers efficient daily driving performance. Overall reliability is typically strong, though isolated owner reports mention unexpected stalling.

**Engine Stalling at Highway Speeds**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may suddenly stall while driving at higher highway speeds, posing a safety hazard.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the fuel delivery system, sensors, and powertrain control modules for fault codes.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

Paired with a crisp 6-speed manual or an optional 6-speed automatic transmission, the powertrain is generally dependable. Buyers should test-drive manual models thoroughly to ensure smooth clutch engagement without vibration.

**Manual Transmission Shudder**
- **Symptoms:** Clutch pedal and drivetrain shudder or vibration when pulling away from a stop or shifting into higher gears.
- **Solution:** Inspect the clutch assembly, pressure plate, and flywheel for premature wear or defects.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The conventional 6-speed automatic transmission provides smooth gear changes and contributes to the car's efficiency. No widespread major reliability concerns are prominently reported for this unit.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Column

The electric power steering system delivers responsive handling, though some owners have reported rare binding issues at sustained highway speeds.

**Steering Binding at Highway Speeds**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel may lightly lock, stick, or require extra effort to adjust while driving straight at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Have the steering column and rack inspected by a dealer or specialist; components may require adjustment or replacement if binding occurs.

## Electrical

### Infotainment Display & Windshield

Exterior trim, lighting, and glass are generally standard for the class, with occasional reports of premature windshield cracking and infotainment screen delamination.

**Infotainment Screen Delamination and Ghost Touches**
- **Symptoms:** Spider cracking around the edges of the display glass and touchscreen registering inputs without being touched.
- **Solution:** Replace the infotainment display unit if adhesive failure and touch unresponsiveness impair operation.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Safety equipment includes standard airbags and stability controls, but used car buyers should verify that all SRS warning lights function correctly and remain unilluminated.

**Air Bag Warning and Non-Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** Air bag warning lights illuminated on the dash, or airbags failing to deploy during front or side impact collisions.
- **Solution:** Perform a diagnostic scan of the Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) modules and wiring harnesses if warning lights appear.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Toyota Yaris iA remains an appealing, fuel-efficient choice for budget-conscious buyers and daily commuters. While mechanical basics are solid, prospective purchasers should carefully inspect the steering feel during a test drive, check the infotainment touchscreen for adhesive defects, and ensure the vehicle has a clean pre-purchase inspection history.
