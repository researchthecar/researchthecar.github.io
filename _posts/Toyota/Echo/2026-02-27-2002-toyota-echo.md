# 2002 Toyota Echo

### Overview
The 2002 Toyota Echo, a subcompact car known for its fuel efficiency and affordability, generally presents a solid reliability record. Designed with simplicity and practicality in mind, it avoids overly complex systems that often lead to costly repairs in other vehicles. While it's an older model, many owners report consistent performance with routine maintenance. The Echo's straightforward engineering contributes significantly to its reputation for being a dependable and economical choice for daily commuting and city driving. Its compact size and maneuverability make it an attractive option for new drivers or those seeking a no-frills transportation solution.

### Summary Verdict
The 2002 Toyota Echo is a highly reliable vehicle, particularly in its powertrain. The 1.5L engine and both transmission options are known for their durability and longevity with proper maintenance. The most common reported issues tend to be in the electrical system, specifically with the alternator and power window motors, which are relatively common for vehicles of this age. Suspension, exterior, and interior components generally hold up well. Overall, the Echo is a dependable choice, with most potential issues being minor and manageable, contributing to its low cost of ownership.
