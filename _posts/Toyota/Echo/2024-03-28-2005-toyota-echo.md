**2005 Toyota Echo**

The 2005 Toyota Echo is a subcompact car that is known for its reliability and fuel efficiency. It was available with a 1.5-liter inline-four engine and a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Echo was discontinued in 2005, but it remains a popular choice for budget-minded car buyers.

**Engine**

**1.5L Inline-Four**
* **Leaking oil pan gasket:** Oil leaks from the oil pan gasket, causing a loss of oil and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the oil pan gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing coolant and/or oil leaks.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**
* **Slipping gears:** The transmission may slip gears, causing a loss of power and potential damage to the transmission.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Worn front struts:** The front struts may wear out, causing a loss of suspension performance and handling.
    * Solution: Replace the front struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear sway bar link failure:** The rear sway bar links may fail, causing a loss of suspension stability and handling.
    * Solution: Replace the rear sway bar links.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Faulty alternator:** The alternator may fail, causing a loss of electrical power to the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery drain:** The battery may drain prematurely, leaving the vehicle unable to start.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the cause of the battery drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the cause
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on body panels:** The body panels may rust over time, especially in areas prone to corrosion.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the rusted body panels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity of the rust
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Tail light assembly cracking:** The tail light assembly may crack or break, causing water leaks and other issues.
    * Solution: Replace the tail light assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Insufficient headroom:** The Echo has limited headroom, which can be uncomfortable for taller drivers.
* **Poor sound insulation:** The Echo has poor sound insulation, which can make road noise intrusive.
* **Climate control issues:** The climate control system may experience issues, such as malfunctioning fans or temperature control problems.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the cause of the issue.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2005 Toyota Echo is a reliable and fuel-efficient car, but it does have some common issues. The most serious issues are related to the transmission and suspension, which can be expensive to repair. However, most of the Echo's problems are minor and can be fixed for a reasonable cost.
