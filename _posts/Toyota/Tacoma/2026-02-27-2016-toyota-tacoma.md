# 2016 Toyota Tacoma

### Overview
The 2016 Toyota Tacoma is a highly regarded mid-size pickup truck known for its robust build quality and off-road prowess. As the first model year of the third generation, it introduced significant updates, including a new powertrain option and revised styling. While generally reliable, like any vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention from owners. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for owners and prospective buyers of this popular truck.

### 3.5L V6 (2GR-FKS)
**Stalling at Idle**
- **Symptoms:** Engine may stall unexpectedly when coming to a stop or at idle.
- **Solution:** Software update for the engine control module (ECM) or potential fuel system cleaning.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Toyota Tacoma demonstrates strong overall reliability, a hallmark of the model. The 2.7L 4-cylinder engine and manual transmission are particularly robust with no significant widespread issues. The 3.5L V6, while generally dependable, has seen some reports of stalling at idle, often resolvable with software updates. The automatic transmission can occasionally exhibit minor hesitation. Electrical gremlins are infrequent, primarily limited to infotainment system glitches. Exterior and interior components are typically durable. For owners, addressing potential V6 engine stalling and automatic transmission behavior proactively can ensure continued dependable service from this capable truck.
