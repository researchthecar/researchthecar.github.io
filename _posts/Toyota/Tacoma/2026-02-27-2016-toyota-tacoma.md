# 2016 Toyota Tacoma Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Toyota Tacoma is a midsize pickup truck renowned for its rugged capability and strong resale value. However, as the debut year for the truck's third generation, buyers should be aware of a few recurring mechanical and structural complaints ranging from transmission hesitation to body paint degradation.

---

## Engine

### 2.7L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.7-liter four-cylinder engine produces approximately 159 horsepower and 180 lb-ft of torque, offering adequate power for daily commuting and basic utility tasks while generally maintaining solid mechanical reliability.

### 3.5L V6 Engine

The 3.5-liter V6 engine delivers 278 horsepower and 265 lb-ft of torque, providing robust towing and hauling performance. While dependable overall, this engine option is associated with occasional reports of timing cover oil leaks and fuel pump issues.

**Timing Cover Oil Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil leaks on the engine block, low engine oil levels, or burning oil smells from oil dripping onto hot components.
- **Solution:** Requires resealing the timing cover, a labor-intensive procedure that involves removing various engine accessories.

**Engine Stalling and Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of engine power while driving, engine stumbling, and unexpected stalling on the highway.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty low-pressure fuel pump assembly.

### Brake Master Cylinder

The braking system provides adequate stopping power under normal conditions, though master cylinder leaks can pose hazardous situations if left unaddressed.

**Master Cylinder Fluid Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Brake fluid leaking into the passenger cabin, pooling onto the brake pedal and driver's footwell, leading to spongy brake pedal feel.
- **Solution:** Replace the failing brake master cylinder assembly.
- **Cost:** $2,000

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission pairs with both engine options but frequently drew owner complaints regarding shifting hesitation and rough engagement.

**Transmission Shaking and Hard Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle shakes in lower gears when slowing down, transmission hesitation, and harsh jerks when shifting between Reverse and Drive.
- **Solution:** Reprogram the transmission control module or inspect internal mechanical components if damage has occurred.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The 6-speed manual transmission caters to enthusiasts wanting direct gear control, though some owners report mechanical stiffness or getting stuck in gear.

**Manual Shifter Getting Stuck**
- **Symptoms:** After driving at highway speeds, the manual transmission becomes difficult to shift and gets stuck in gear.
- **Solution:** Inspect and repair the internal shifter components or linkage assembly.

## Brakes

### Third Brake Light Housing

Exterior body seals and trim usually protect the cabin effectively, except for a known vulnerability around the high-mount third brake light seal.

**Water Leakage Through Third Brake Light**
- **Symptoms:** Water leaking into the cab during rain or car washes, causing a mildew smell and potential interior headliner damage.
- **Solution:** Replace the third brake light gasket or reseal the light housing fixture.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System

Cabin electronics manage infotainment and connectivity features, with select owner reports highlighting software bugs and freezing issues.

**Entune System Freezing and Reseting**
- **Symptoms:** The factory infotainment display freezes, becomes completely unresponsive, or abruptly reboots while driving.
- **Solution:** Update the software or replace the head unit if hardware failure persists.

## Exterior

### Paint and Clear Coat

Exterior paint finishes are generally durable, though some vehicles develop premature clear coat breakdown on sun-exposed panels.

**Clear Coat Delamination and Paint Fading**
- **Symptoms:** Severe clear coat peeling, oxidation, and paint fading on horizontal surfaces such as the hood and roof.
- **Solution:** Repaint or professionally refinish the affected body panels.

## Interior

### Heated Seats

Interior comfort amenities generally hold up well, but the heated seat wiring has experienced localized thermal melting failures.

**Melted Seat Heater Wiring**
- **Symptoms:** Heated seats stop working, accompanied by a burning smell, melted wiring connectors, and charred residue on the seat control module.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged wiring harness and seat heating module.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V425000: Structure:Body:Hood:Hinge And Attachments](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tacoma&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the hood scoop detaches, it may become a road hazard, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** SET will notify owners, and dealers will mechanically fasten the scoop to the hood, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V356000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tacoma&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the crank position sensor with an improved design, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V285000: Power Train:Driveline:Differential Unit](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tacoma&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the vehicle is operated with an insufficient amount of oil in the rear differential, the differential may seize and cause a loss of control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rear differential for oil leakage. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V656000: Air Bags:Knee Bolster](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tacoma&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The incorrect bolts may loosen over time and affect the performance of the driver's knee air bag, increasing the risk of driver injury during a crash that necessitates deployment of the air bags.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will install the correct driver's knee air bag module mounting bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Toyota Tacoma maintains the model's reputation for strong resale value and rugged off-road capability, but as a first-model-year vehicle, it comes with specific quirks to watch for. Prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the V6 engine for oil leaks around the timing cover, test the automatic transmission for smooth engagement, and check the cabin for any signs of water intrusion from the third brake light or melted seat heater wiring.
