# 2022 Toyota Tacoma Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Toyota Tacoma remains a popular choice in the midsize truck segment, carrying forward a reputation for rugged durability and strong resale value. While overall mechanical satisfaction tends to be high, owner feedback and safety reports highlight several specific areas worth monitoring during a pre-purchase inspection. Buyers should pay close attention to potential powertrain concerns, recurring body paint flaws on specific colors, and braking feel.

---

## Engine

### 2.7L 4-Cylinder

A naturally aspirated 2.7-liter inline-4 engine producing approximately 159 horsepower and 180 lb-ft of torque. It serves as the base utility powerplant for lower trims, offering straightforward mechanical design suited for light-duty tasks.

### 3.5L V6

A 3.5-liter V6 engine generating around 278 horsepower and 265 lb-ft of torque, providing standard power for higher trims and towing. It generally proves dependable, though isolated owner reports point to fuel delivery and stalling glitches.

**Engine Stalling and Fuel System Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Long cranking times before starting, unexpected engine stalling, and potential power loss while driving.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the fuel system pressure and inspect the low-pressure fuel pump or related components for failure.

### Master Cylinder and Brake Pedal

The braking system provides adequate stopping power under most conditions, though owners have noted occasional master cylinder failures and soft pedal travel.

**Soft Pedal and Master Cylinder Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal sinking nearly to the floor when stopped, mushy pedal feel, or complete master cylinder failure resulting in reduced stopping power.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty brake master cylinder or bleeding the hydraulic system to restore proper brake pressure.
- **Cost:** $1100

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic

A traditional 6-speed automatic transmission paired with either engine option across rear-wheel and four-wheel-drive configurations. Most units operate reliably, but early wear issues have been documented by some owners.

**Transmission Failure and Fluid Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Premature internal transmission failure, burnt fluid odors, or sudden loss of drive under normal operating speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition and level; replace or rebuild the transmission unit if internal component failure has occurred.

### 6-Speed Manual

A 6-speed manual transmission available on select V6 four-wheel-drive configurations for enthusiasts seeking a more connected shifting experience.

**Gear Disengagement**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission popping out of second gear after resting on level ground, requiring a brief delay before re-engaging.
- **Solution:** Inspect internal shifter linkages and synchros; professional transmission teardown may be necessary.

## Exterior

### Paint and Body Structure

Exterior body panels are generally sturdy, but specific paint codes—most notably Super White—have exhibited widespread flaking and adhesion concerns.

**Paint Flaking and Peeling**
- **Symptoms:** Paint chipping or peeling around window frames, door edges, and interior fenders, particularly on white finish vehicles.
- **Solution:** Inspect affected body panels and consult dealer paint warranty programs or professional body shops for repainting.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V310000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tacoma&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The load carrying capacity modification labels may not be permanent and can fade, becoming illegible. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An illegible label may allow the vehicle to be overloaded, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 22V520000: Child Seat:Vehicle Latch Anchor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tacoma&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 225, "Child Restraint Anchorage System." An insufficient weld may prevent the child seat from being anchored properly, allowing the seat to move during a sudden stop or crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the upper child seat anchors and reweld the child seat anchors, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V152000: Power Train:Axle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tacoma&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Axle shaft separation can cause a loss of vehicle stability and brake performance, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear axle assembly, tighten the axle retaining nuts as necessary, and repair or replace any damaged components, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Toyota Tacoma retains strong overall appeal for used truck buyers thanks to its robust build and high resale value. However, prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect prospective vehicles for white paint peeling, ensure smooth braking performance without soft pedal symptoms, and verify proper engine and transmission operation during a test drive.
