# 2017 Toyota Tacoma Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Toyota Tacoma remains a popular choice in the midsize truck market, praised for its strong resale value and rugged capability. However, used-car buyers should review owner complaints regarding several recurring mechanical and body concerns. While Toyota's reputation for durability largely holds true, specific trouble spots—ranging from fuel system issues to paint and body corrosion—warrant a thorough pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.7L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.7-liter four-cylinder engine produces 159 horsepower and 180 lb-ft of torque, offering adequate power for daily driving and light utility use with generally dependable mechanical longevity.

### 3.5L V6 Engine

The 3.5-liter V6 engine generates 278 horsepower and 265 lb-ft of torque, providing robust towing capability and highway passing power. While generally solid, it accounts for the majority of reported engine stall and coolant leak complaints.

**Engine Stalling and Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported unexpected engine stalling while driving or highway speeds dropping, often linked to fuel pump failure or sensor malfunctions.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the fuel pump and related sensors, and replace failed components if necessary.

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant levels or visible puddles beneath the vehicle, often originating from bypass joints or pipes.
- **Solution:** Inspect the cooling system pipes and bypass joints, then replace leaking hoses or fittings.

### Brake Master Cylinder and Booster

The braking system provides firm stopping power under normal conditions, though master cylinder leaks require prompt attention to maintain safety.

**Brake Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Brake fluid drips onto the driver's side floor mat from beneath the dash, potentially leading to a spongy pedal or reduced braking performance.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake master cylinder and booster assembly, and replace leaking components immediately.

## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 5-speed manual transmission pairs with the base four-cylinder engine, offering a direct driving feel with few widespread mechanical complaints.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The 6-speed manual transmission is paired with select V6 configurations, appealing to enthusiasts seeking traditional control over their gear shifts.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission handles power delivery across most trims but has logged owner reports concerning rough shifts, cold-weather hesitation, and occasional linkage concerns.

**Unintended Neutral Shift and Shifting Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience rough engagement, hesitation when slowing down in cold weather, or the transmission unexpectedly shifting into neutral.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition and software calibration; repair or replace internal components or linkage as needed.

## Exterior

### Body Structure and Paint

Exterior panels generally withstand harsh use, but certain factory paint colors and configurations are prone to premature clear coat degradation and chipping.

**Paint Peeling and Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Factory paint and clear coat exhibit premature fading, oxidation, chipping, and peeling around window frames and door edges.
- **Solution:** Repaint affected body panels or consult dealership paint programs where applicable.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Steering Clock Spring

Standard safety equipment functions reliably overall, though steering wheel electrical components like the clock spring can wear out over time.

**Clock Spring Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of steering wheel controls, inoperable cruise control, and an illuminated airbag warning light.
- **Solution:** Replace the steering column clock spring assembly to restore electrical continuity.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V831000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tacoma&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An incorrect load information label can result in the operator overloading the vehicle and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 17V356000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tacoma&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the crank position sensor with an improved design, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V285000: Power Train:Driveline:Differential Unit](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tacoma&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the vehicle is operated with an insufficient amount of oil in the rear differential, the differential may seize and cause a loss of control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rear differential for oil leakage. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V211000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist:Vacuum](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tacoma&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A sudden loss of braking assist can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the brake booster vacuum pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V682000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tacoma&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel pump fails, the engine can stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly with an improved one, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Toyota Tacoma is a dependable midsize truck with exceptional long-term value, but buyers should perform a detailed inspection before purchasing. Pay close attention to potential fuel pump issues, transmission shifting behavior, brake master cylinder leaks, and the condition of the exterior paint. A comprehensive pre-purchase check by a qualified mechanic will help ensure you secure a reliable example.
