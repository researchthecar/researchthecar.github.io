**Introduction:**

The 2022 Toyota Tacoma is a mid-size pickup truck that offers a range of engine and transmission options. The base engine is a 2.7L inline-4 with 159 horsepower and 180 lb-ft of torque. An optional 3.5L V6 produces 278 horsepower and 265 lb-ft of torque. Transmission choices include a 6-speed automatic, a 6-speed manual, and a 10-speed automatic. The Tacoma is known for its off-road prowess, durability, and reliability. However, like any vehicle, it has some reported issues.

**Engine**

**2.7L Inline-4**

* **Excessive oil consumption:** Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-ups between oil changes.
* **Piston ring failure:** Premature piston ring failure has been reported in some 2.7L engines, leading to excessive oil consumption and engine damage.
* **Timing chain noise:** A rattling or ticking noise from the timing chain has been observed in some engines.
    * Solution: Timing chain replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Fuel injector failure:** Failed fuel injectors can cause engine misfires and poor performance.
    * Solution: Fuel injector replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **Recalls:** None Found

**3.5L V6**

* **Valve seat recession:** Premature valve seat recession has been reported in some 3.5L V6 engines, leading to loss of compression and engine damage.
* **Timing chain failure:** Timing chain failure has been reported in some 3.5L V6 engines, causing catastrophic engine damage.
    * Solution: Timing chain and tensioner replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000
    * Recalls: T-SB-0141-22

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Shift shudder:** Some owners have reported a shuddering sensation during gear shifts.
    * Solution: Transmission software update, transmission fluid change
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
* **TCC shudder:** A shudder or vibration may be felt when the torque converter clutch (TCC) engages.
    * Solution: TCC solenoid replacement, transmission fluid change
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**6-Speed Manual**

* **Grinding when shifting:** Shifting into or out of gear may be difficult or met with grinding noises.
    * Solution: Synchronizer replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**10-Speed Automatic**

* **Shift delays or hesitations:** Some owners have reported delays or hesitations during gear shifts.
    * Solution: Transmission software update, transmission fluid change
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500

**Suspension**

* **Leaf spring squeak:** The leaf springs can squeak when the suspension compresses or rebounds.
    * Solution: Leaf spring lubrication, shimming
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200

**Electrical**

* **Electrical harness damage:** Rodents have been known to chew on the electrical harness, causing electrical system malfunctions.
    * Solution: Electrical harness repair or replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Rust on undercarriage:** Surface rust has been observed on the undercarriage of some Tacomas, especially in areas with high humidity or salt exposure.
    * Solution: Rust treatment, undercoating
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per year for rust prevention

**Interior**

* **Seat recliner failure:** The seat recliner mechanism has been reported to fail, causing the seat to recline unexpectedly.
    * Solution: Seat recliner repair or replacement
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **Dashboard rattle:** Some owners have reported a rattling noise coming from the dashboard.
    * Solution: Dashboard component replacement or tightening
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500

**Summary:**

While the 2022 Toyota Tacoma is generally a reliable vehicle, it has some reported issues that owners should be aware of. The severity and cost of the issues vary, but some can be significant. It's important to keep up with regular maintenance and address any issues promptly to help ensure the longevity and performance of the vehicle.
