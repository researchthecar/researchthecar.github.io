# 2024 Toyota Tacoma Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Toyota Tacoma represents the fully redesigned fourth generation of this midsize pickup, bringing modern turbocharged engine options, available hybrid powertrains, and updated technology. While buyers praise its rugged capability and updated styling, early-production models have accumulated notable owner complaints centered around powertrain operation, braking components, and minor electrical glitches. Prospective used-car buyers should review service history carefully, particularly regarding active recalls and transmission behavior.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L Turbocharged Inline-4

The base 2.4-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces robust low-end torque compared to the outgoing V6, typically ranging from 228 to 278 horsepower depending on the specific tune. It serves as the primary powerplant for the lineup, balancing everyday driveability with utility.

### 2.4L Turbocharged Hybrid i-FORCE MAX

The available i-FORCE MAX hybrid powertrain pairs the 2.4-liter turbo engine with an electric motor integrated into the transmission to generate an impressive 326 horsepower and 465 lb-ft of torque. It delivers strong acceleration and enhanced capability, though complex electronic integration requires attentive diagnostic care.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission handles power delivery for most of the Tacoma lineup. While designed for efficiency and smooth highway cruising, early owner reports indicate occasional shifting irregularities and electronic fault codes.

**Transmission Slipping and Rough Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report intermittent transmission slipping, harsh gear engagement, and rough shifting, particularly after the vehicle has sat overnight.
- **Solution:** Have a dealership inspect the transmission control module and fluid levels; software updates or fluid adjustments may be required.

**Unintended Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle unexpectedly slips or shifts into neutral or encounters gear selection faults while operating.
- **Solution:** Consult a certified technician for diagnostic trouble codes and potential powertrain control module reprogramming.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

A traditional 6-speed manual transmission is available on select configurations for enthusiasts seeking direct gear control. It generally offers robust mechanical feedback, though isolated linkage and clutch actuation complaints have surfaced.

**Gear Engagement Lockout**
- **Symptoms:** The manual shifter occasionally blocks entry into first gear when attempting to pull away from a complete stop.
- **Solution:** Inspect the clutch hydraulic system and linkage alignment; bleed the system or replace worn master/slave cylinders if necessary.

## Suspension

### Rear Leaf Springs and Suspension

The suspension is engineered to handle heavy payloads and off-road articulation, but isolated reports of early component wear require buyers to check the undercarriage.

**Premature Leaf Spring Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Creaking noises, loud clunks, and structural failure of the rear leaf springs during normal highway driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear leaf spring assemblies for cracking or deformation and replace damaged components under warranty.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes and Hydraulic Lines

The braking system provides adequate stopping power for normal utility use, but owners have reported concerns regarding hydraulic pressure and post-recall service performance.

**Brake Pedal Travel and Reduced Braking Performance**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience excessive brake pedal travel, spongy pedal feel, or noticeably reduced stopping power after recall service.
- **Solution:** Have the hydraulic brake system thoroughly inspected and bled by a qualified technician to remove trapped air or address component faults.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Display Systems

Modern digital displays and advanced driver assistance screens dominate the cabin, though software glitches and intermittent display resets have frustrated some owners.

**Infotainment Screen Blackout**
- **Symptoms:** The central touchscreen display blacks out intermittently or fails to operate correctly after software update procedures.
- **Solution:** Return the vehicle to the dealership to verify and re-flash the infotainment software module.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V548000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tacoma&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." A vehicle with an incorrect maximum capacity weight value may be overloaded, which can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V058000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tacoma&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak can reduce braking ability and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both rear brake hoses, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V595000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tacoma&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** An instrument panel display that does not show critical information can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the instrument panel software over-the-air (OTA) for non-PHEV vehicles, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V179000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tacoma&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." A vehicle with an incorrect maximum capacity weight value may be overloaded, which can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the load carrying capacity modification label, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Toyota Tacoma introduces exciting powertrain updates and rugged capability, but as a first-model-year vehicle, it comes with a higher concentration of early-production teething issues. Used-car buyers should thoroughly inspect transmission shift quality, check for completion of relevant safety recalls, and verify proper electronic operation before purchasing.
