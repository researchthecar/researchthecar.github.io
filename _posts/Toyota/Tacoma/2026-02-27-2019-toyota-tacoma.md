# 2019 Toyota Tacoma Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Toyota Tacoma carries a stellar reputation for long-term durability, impressive off-road capability, and strong resale value, making it a favorite in the midsize truck segment. However, prospective buyers should review specific mechanical areas and owner-reported glitches. While the truck is generally rugged, certain powertrain options and components have recurring complaints that warrant a careful pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.7L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.7-liter four-cylinder engine produces 159 horsepower and 180 lb-ft of torque, offering basic utility and adequate efficiency for daily driving. It features a straightforward design with a solid track record for mechanical endurance.

### 3.5L V6 Engine

The 3.5-liter V6 generates 278 horsepower and 265 lb-ft of torque, providing robust towing capability and responsive highway performance. While generally reliable, it shares potential exposure to fuel pump concerns that can lead to sudden stalling.

**Engine Stalling and Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine stalling, hesitation, vehicle shuddering, and illumination of the check engine light while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the low-pressure fuel pump assembly if faulty, checking for stored diagnostic trouble codes like P0087.

### Brake Master Cylinder

The braking system generally provides confident stopping power, but internal master cylinder leaks require prompt attention to avoid total brake pressure loss.

**Brake Fluid Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Brake fluid leaking internally into the cabin, pooling down the brake pedal arm onto the driver-side floor mat.
- **Solution:** Replace the failing brake master cylinder and flush the brake hydraulic system.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with both engine options across various trims and drivetrains. Owners frequently note occasional gear hunting and shift hesitation.

**Transmission Shift Hesitation and Judder**
- **Symptoms:** Unintended shifting behavior, jerking during acceleration, harsh downshifts, and vibration or judder when slowing down.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition and consult a dealer for potential software updates or adaptive shift relearns.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The 6-speed manual transmission appeals to enthusiasts seeking direct gear control, though some owners report minor mechanical noises during low-speed coasting.

**Manual Transmission Neutral Rattle**
- **Symptoms:** Audible rattling noises emanating from the transmission housing when rolling at low speeds in neutral.
- **Solution:** Have the clutch and transmission components inspected by a qualified technician to isolate internal wear.

## Steering

### Steering Rack

The power steering system delivers predictable feedback, though occasional premature steering rack leaks have been reported at relatively low mileage.

**Steering Rack Failure and Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Steering fluid leaks, loose or heavy steering feel, and premature mechanical wear of the steering rack assembly.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering gear assembly and replace the steering rack if internal seals or housing components fail.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Connectivity

Cabin electronics generally function well, though early infotainment software iterations occasionally suffer from Bluetooth pairing stability issues.

**Bluetooth Disconnection**
- **Symptoms:** Smartphones repeatedly dropping Bluetooth pairing connections, disrupting hands-free calling functionality.
- **Solution:** Update the head unit firmware and clear paired device history on both the phone and vehicle systems.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V682000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tacoma&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel pump fails, the engine can stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly with an improved one, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V012000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tacoma&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel pump fails, the engine can stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V888000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Master Cylinder](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tacoma&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Reduced brake performance can lengthen the distance needed to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the brake master cylinder, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V244000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tacoma&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The text on the load capacity label may become illegible. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." If the driver cannot read the label, they may unknowingly overload the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** GST will notify owners, and dealers will provide corrected load carrying capacity modification labels, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Toyota Tacoma remains a solid and dependable choice for truck buyers looking for lasting toughness and high residual value. While owner complaints highlight specific issues such as fuel pump performance, automatic transmission shift quirks, and occasional brake master cylinder leaks, these can be managed with a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Buyers should verify that all open recalls have been completed and test drive the vehicle to ensure smooth shifting and responsive braking.
