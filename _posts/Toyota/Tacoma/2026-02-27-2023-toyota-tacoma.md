# 2023 Toyota Tacoma Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Toyota Tacoma continues the model's long-standing reputation for ruggedness and resale value, but prospective buyers should still look closely at owner-reported issues. While overall mechanical complaints remain relatively moderate for a heavily used truck platform, real-world owner feedback highlights specific concerns ranging from minor electrical quirks to mechanical and safety component glitches. Reviewing these areas carefully can help ensure you select a well-maintained example.

---

## Engine

### 2.7L 4-Cylinder

This entry-level naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine produces approximately 159 horsepower and 180 lb-ft of torque. It offers basic utility and simple mechanical architecture, though acceleration feels modest under heavy loads.

### 3.5L V6

The larger 3.5-liter V6 generates around 278 horsepower and 265 lb-ft of torque, providing much-improved towing capability and passing power. It is generally robust, though minor acoustic quirks have been noted by owners.

**Valvetrain Noise ('Taco Tick')**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report a deep knocking or ticking noise originating from the valvetrain, often involving the timing chain, tensioners, or pulleys.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the valvetrain; dealer networks often characterize normal operating sounds as baseline characteristics rather than mechanical defects.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission caters to enthusiasts seeking direct mechanical control over gear selection. It pairs reliably with select engine configurations when properly maintained.

### 6-Speed Automatic

The 6-speed automatic transmission manages daily driving and towing duties smoothly across diverse trim levels. It is broadly dependable, though routine fluid checks remain essential.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake component complaints highlight occasional vibration and early wear symptoms that require physical inspection during a pre-purchase test drive.

**Brake Judder and Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience persistent shuddering and vibration sensations when applying the brakes, sometimes persisting despite multiple dealership service visits.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake rotors for warping or uneven pad wear, and resurface or replace hardware as necessary.

## Electrical

### Wiring and Accessories

Electrical systems generally function well, though cabin blower motor failures and exterior lighting housing tabs have occasionally frustrated owners.

**Rodent Damage to Soy-Based Wiring**
- **Symptoms:** Electrical faults and warning lights triggered by rodents chewing through soy-based wire insulation.
- **Solution:** Install rodent deterrents in parking areas and inspect wiring harnesses for chewed insulation during routine maintenance.

## Safety

### Air Bags

Safety system grievances primarily involve airbag deployment behavior during collisions, emphasizing the importance of a clean structural accident history.

**Unexpected Non-Deployment in Collisions**
- **Symptoms:** Owners report instances where specific airbags failed to deploy or only side curtain airbags activated during significant impact events.
- **Solution:** Verify vehicle history reports for prior structural repairs and ensure airbag diagnostic systems show no active diagnostic trouble codes.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V520000: Child Seat:Vehicle Latch Anchor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tacoma&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 225, "Child Restraint Anchorage System." An insufficient weld may prevent the child seat from being anchored properly, allowing the seat to move during a sudden stop or crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the upper child seat anchors and reweld the child seat anchors, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V480000: Steering:Column](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tacoma&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." A deactivated driver's air bag that does not deploy as intended increases the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the spiral cable assembly, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V152000: Power Train:Axle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tacoma&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Axle shaft separation can cause a loss of vehicle stability and brake performance, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear axle assembly, tighten the axle retaining nuts as necessary, and repair or replace any damaged components, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V040000: Steering:Column](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Tacoma&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." A deactivated driver's air bag will not deploy as intended, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the spiral cable assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Toyota Tacoma remains a dependable midsize truck choice with strong enduring value, but used buyers should remain vigilant. Thoroughly inspect the service history, check for proper brake performance, examine wiring harnesses for rodent activity, and verify that all safety systems and recall completions are fully documented before purchasing.
