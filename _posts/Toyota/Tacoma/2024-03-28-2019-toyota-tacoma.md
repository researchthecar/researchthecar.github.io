**Introduction:**

The 2019 Toyota Tacoma is a midsize pickup truck available with three engine options: a 2.7-liter inline-four (159 hp, 180 lb-ft), a 3.5-liter V6 (278 hp, 265 lb-ft), and a 2.8-liter turbocharged inline-four diesel (178 hp, 325 lb-ft). Transmission options include a six-speed manual, six-speed automatic, and eight-speed automatic. The Tacoma is known for its off-road capability, reliability, and towing capacity. However, it has received criticism for its lack of interior refinement and outdated infotainment system.

**Engine**

* **2.7-liter Inline-Four**
    * **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Description/Symptoms: Engine burns excessive amounts of oil, resulting in low oil levels and engine damage. Solution: Replace piston rings and oil consumption rings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * **Timing Chain Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Timing chain or tensioner fails, causing the valves to become mistimed. Results in engine damage if not caught promptly. Solution: Replace timing chain, tensioner, and related components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
* **3.5-liter V6**
    * **Piston Ring Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Piston rings fail, leading to excessive oil consumption and poor compression. Solution: Overhaul or replace engine. Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000+
    * **Water Pump Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Water pump fails, causing overheating and possible engine damage. Solution: Replace water pump. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** Description/Symptoms: Valve cover gasket fails, allowing oil to leak. Solution: Replace valve cover gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **2.8-liter Turbocharged Inline-Four Diesel**
    * **Excessive Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) System Soot Buildup:** Description/Symptoms: Soot builds up in the EGR system, reducing engine performance and fuel economy. Solution: Clean or replace EGR valve and related components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    * **Fuel Injector Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Fuel injectors fail, causing engine misfires and reduced performance. Solution: Replace fuel injectors. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Transmission**

* **Six-Speed Manual**
    * **Shifting Difficulty:** Description/Symptoms: Transmission becomes difficult to shift into or out of gears. Solution: Lubricate or replace shift linkage components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **Six-Speed Automatic**
    * **Torque Converter Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Torque converter fails, causing slipping and shuddering during gear changes. Solution: Replace torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
* **Eight-Speed Automatic**
    * **Delayed Shifting:** Description/Symptoms: Transmission hesitates or delays during gear changes. Solution: Update transmission software or replace valve body. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Leaf springs break or sag, causing the vehicle to lose stability and ride height. Solution: Replace leaf springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
* **Shock Absorber Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Shock absorbers wear out, resulting in excessive bouncing and poor ride quality. Solution: Replace shock absorbers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Battery drains quickly or fails to hold a charge. Solution: Replace battery. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
* **Alternator Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Alternator fails, preventing the battery from charging. Solution: Replace alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Wiring Harness Issues:** Description/Symptoms: Electrical harnesses become damaged or loose, causing electrical malfunctions. Solution: Repair or replace wiring harness. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:** Description/Symptoms: Moisture builds up inside the headlight housing. Solution: Replace headlight assembly or seal housing. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Tailgate Latches:** Description/Symptoms: Tailgate latches fail, preventing the tailgate from closing properly. Solution: Replace tailgate latches. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Interior**

* **Instrument Cluster Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Instrument cluster fails to display information or becomes intermittent. Solution: Replace instrument cluster. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **HVAC System Malfunction:** Description/Symptoms: Heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) system fails to operate properly. Solution: Diagnose and repair HVAC components as needed. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Seat Heater Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Seat heater fails to heat up or becomes intermittent. Solution: Replace seat heater element. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500

**Summary:**

The 2019 Toyota Tacoma has a number of reported issues that can range from minor inconveniences to more severe and costly repairs. The engine and transmission issues, particularly the excessive oil consumption and timing chain failure, are significant concerns that warrant attention. Other issues, such as suspension failures and electrical malfunctions, are less common but can still be costly to address. It is important for owners and potential buyers of the 2019 Tacoma to be aware of these issues and take appropriate measures to diagnose and repair them if necessary.
