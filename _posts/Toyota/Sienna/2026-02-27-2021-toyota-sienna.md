# 2021 Toyota Sienna Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Toyota Sienna marks a dramatic generation shift by adopting a standard hybrid powertrain exclusively, offering stellar fuel economy for a family hauler. However, being the launch year of a brand-new generation, it comes with a few notable owner-reported quirks and early-production teething issues. Prospective buyers should carefully evaluate brake feel, electronics, and overall vehicle condition before purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L Hybrid I4

The 2.5-liter four-cylinder hybrid system pairs with electric motors to deliver 245 net horsepower, prioritizing high efficiency and smooth city commuting. It generally provides dependable operation, though early production models feature minor hybrid propulsion complaints.

**Unexpected Acceleration Delay**
- **Symptoms:** The electric motor briefly accelerates from a complete stop before experiencing a sudden loss of acceleration despite continuous pedal input.
- **Solution:** Consult a certified dealer for powertrain software updates and throttle inspection.

### Front Struts

The suspension is tuned for family comfort, though isolated early wear issues on front components require inspection during a pre-purchase evaluation.

**Premature Strut Leakage**
- **Symptoms:** Front suspension struts develop fluid leaks prematurely, leading to a compromised ride quality.
- **Solution:** Inspect front struts for oil residue and replace worn strut assemblies as necessary.

## Transmission

### Electronically Controlled CVT (eCVT)

The eCVT seamlessly blends power from the gasoline engine and electric motors, providing smooth power delivery across front-wheel and available all-wheel-drive configurations.

## Brakes

### Brake Control System

The brake system relies on a sophisticated regenerative setup blending friction and electric braking. Some drivers report intermittent stopping delays or transitional surge sensations.

**Brake Pressure Release and Surge**
- **Symptoms:** The brakes momentarily lose pressure or release during low-speed stops, causing the vehicle to lurch or surge forward unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Have the hybrid regenerative braking system and brake control module inspected by a Toyota specialist.

## Interior

### Seats and Trim

Cabin comfort is generally high, though early-model trim fitment and seat rattles have drawn owner frustrations.

**Seat Rattle and Interior Trim Fitment**
- **Symptoms:** Abnormal rattles emanate from second-row seating areas, accompanied by dashboard error messages to check rear seats.
- **Solution:** Secure loose seat mountings and check trim clip alignments.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Safety equipment includes a comprehensive array of airbags and advanced driver assists, though sporadic SRS warning light activations have been documented by owners.

**SRS Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard airbag warning light illuminates intermittently, often pointing toward second-row seat-mounted airbag harness or module malfunctions.
- **Solution:** Scan diagnostic codes at a dealership to isolate faulty seat connectors or airbag modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V239000: Electronic Stability Control (Esc):Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sienna&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The Skid Control ECU software may not turn on the Vehicle Stability Control (VSC) system when the vehicle is restarted after the driver has disabled the VSC system. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 126, "Electronic Stability Control Systems." Driving with a deactivated stability.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Skid Control ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V877000: Equipment Adaptive/Mobility:Wheelchair Restraints/Securement:Latch/Anchor:](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sienna&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The retractors may not lock, preventing the wheelchair from being properly secured. An unsecured wheelchair can move during transit, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Toyota Sienna brings exceptional fuel economy and modern styling to the minivan segment, but as a redesigned first-year model, it requires careful scrutiny. Buyers should thoroughly test the hybrid braking transitions and inspect for any suspension or electrical quirks during a pre-purchase test drive.
