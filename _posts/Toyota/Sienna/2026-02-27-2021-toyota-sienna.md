# 2021 Toyota Sienna Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Toyota Sienna marks the debut of the vehicle's fourth generation, transitioning exclusively to a hybrid powertrain paired with front-wheel or all-wheel drive. As a completely redesigned model year for owner-reported complaints, early production units exhibit a relatively clean slate, though used-car buyers should still review service histories for standard hybrid components and minor interior fittings.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Hybrid

This 2.5-liter four-cylinder hybrid engine produces a combined 245 horsepower, utilizing Toyota's established hybrid synergy drive system. It offers exceptional fuel efficiency for a large family vehicle with strong, proven long-term mechanical durability.

## Transmission

### Electronically Controlled Continuously Variable Transmission (e-CVT)

An electronically controlled continuously variable transmission distributes power seamlessly between the hybrid motors and wheels. It is generally robust and lacks traditional clutch packs or gear sets prone to conventional automatic wear.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V239000: Electronic Stability Control (Esc):Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sienna&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The Skid Control ECU software may not turn on the Vehicle Stability Control (VSC) system when the vehicle is restarted after the driver has disabled the VSC system. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 126, "Electronic Stability Control Systems." Driving with a deactivated stability.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Skid Control ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V877000: Equipment Adaptive/Mobility:Wheelchair Restraints/Securement:Latch/Anchor:](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sienna&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The retractors may not lock, preventing the wheelchair from being properly secured. An unsecured wheelchair can move during transit, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Toyota Sienna benefits from its comprehensive redesign and standard hybrid efficiency, making it a highly compelling choice in the used minivan market. Because it represents the first year of a new generation, buyers should verify proper hybrid system function and inspect general interior wear during a pre-purchase test drive.
