**Introduction**

The 2005 Toyota Sienna is a minivan that offers a spacious interior, reliable performance, and a comfortable ride. It is available with two engine options: a 3.3-liter V6 producing 230 horsepower and 242 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.5-liter V6 producing 266 horsepower and 245 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission.

Owners praise the Sienna for its roomy cabin, versatile seating configurations, and impressive fuel economy. However, it is not without its drawbacks. Some common issues reported by owners include:

**Engine**

**3.3-Liter V6**

* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaks from the valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, or rear main seal.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty gaskets or seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Belt Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing belt snaps, causing significant engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the timing belt according to the manufacturer's recommended intervals (typically every 90,000 miles).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.5-Liter V6**

* **Water Pump Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The water pump seizes up, causing the engine to overheat.
    * Solution: Replace the water pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Valve Spring Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Valve springs break, causing the engine to misfire or run erratically.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty valve springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **Shifting Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slips, hesitates, or refuses to shift.
    * Solution: Replace the transmission solenoid or rebuild the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut or Shock Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn or broken struts or shocks cause a rough ride, excessive body roll, and uneven tire wear.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty struts or shocks.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ per axle
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Sway Bar Link Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loose or broken sway bar links cause excessive body roll and noise when turning or driving over bumps.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty sway bar links.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ per side
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator fails, causing the battery to drain and the electrical system to fail.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Starter Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The starter motor fails, preventing the engine from starting.
    * Solution: Replace the starter motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust develops on the body panels, especially around the wheel wells and rockers.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the rusted areas.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity and location of the rust
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks develop on the dashboard due to sun exposure or excessive heat.
    * Solution: Replace the dashboard.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Climate Control Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control system malfunctions, causing the AC or heater to stop working properly.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty component, such as the blower motor or blend door actuator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2005 Toyota Sienna is a reliable minivan with few major issues. However, the potential for engine and transmission problems, as well as rust and interior cracking, should be taken into account. The severity and cost of these issues vary, so it's important to consider specific vehicles and their maintenance history before making a purchase.
