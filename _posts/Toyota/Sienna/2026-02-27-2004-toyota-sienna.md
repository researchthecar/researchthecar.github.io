# 2004 Toyota Sienna

### Overview
The 2004 Toyota Sienna, a popular minivan, generally offers good reliability, reflecting Toyota's reputation for durable vehicles. This model year is known for its practicality and family-friendly features. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected repairs, like any vehicle, certain components can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented common problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and prospective buyers of the 2004 Sienna.

---


## Engine

### 3.3L V6 (3MZ-FE)
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil stains under the vehicle, low oil level warnings, burning oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking seals (e.g., valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket).
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Timing Belt Replacement**
- **Symptoms:** No direct symptoms, but critical maintenance item. Failure can cause catastrophic engine damage.
- **Solution:** Scheduled replacement of timing belt, tensioner, and water pump.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1000


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (U151E)
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid from transmission lines or seals, rough shifting, slipping gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking seals, hoses, or transmission cooler lines.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900

**Slipping or Delayed Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Hesitation during gear changes, engine revving without acceleration, harsh shifts.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid and filter change, solenoid replacement, or in severe cases, transmission rebuild/replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $3000+


## Suspension

### Standard Suspension
**Worn Struts/Shocks**
- **Symptoms:** Bouncy ride, excessive body roll, clunking noises over bumps, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn struts and/or shocks.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900

**Sway Bar Links**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or rattling noises when turning or going over uneven surfaces.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn sway bar links.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Electrical Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not working, door locks malfunctioning, interior lights acting erratically.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1200


### Alternator
**Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light illuminated, dead battery.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

### Sliding Doors
**Sticking or Difficult Operation**
- **Symptoms:** Sliding doors are hard to open or close, may get stuck.
- **Solution:** Lubrication of tracks, adjustment of rollers, or replacement of worn components.
- **Cost:** $100 - $400

**No widespread issues reported.**

### HVAC System
**Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air coming from vents, or air only comes out at certain speeds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor or blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2004 Toyota Sienna is a generally reliable minivan, particularly for its age. The 3.3L V6 engine is robust, though owners should be mindful of scheduled timing belt replacements and potential oil leaks as the vehicle ages. The automatic transmission is also fairly dependable, but fluid leaks and shifting issues can arise. Common wear items like struts and sway bar links are typical for a vehicle of this mileage. Electrical issues, such as BCM malfunctions or alternator failure, can occur but are not overwhelmingly prevalent. Interior and exterior components are generally durable, with sliding door operation being a minor point of attention. Overall, with proper maintenance, the 2004 Sienna remains a solid choice for family transport.
