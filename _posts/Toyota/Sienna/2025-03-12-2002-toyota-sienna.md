The 2002 Toyota Sienna is a popular minivan known for its reliability, spacious interior, and smooth ride. It comes standard with a 3.0L 1MZ-FE V6 engine producing around 210 horsepower and 220 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate its dependability and practicality, but common complaints revolve around issues typical of older vehicles, such as rust, electrical problems, and some mechanical wear.

**Engine**

**3.0L 1MZ-FE V6**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks commonly occur from the valve cover gaskets and the oil pan gasket. Symptoms include visible oil leaks on the engine, oil spots under the vehicle, and a burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets and/or oil pan gasket.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ depending on location.
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Sludge Buildup**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The 1MZ-FE engine is known to develop oil sludge if oil changes are neglected. Symptoms include rough running, poor performance, and potential engine damage.
    *   Solution: Regular oil changes with high-quality oil can help prevent sludge. In severe cases, engine cleaning or replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $5,000+ depending on the severity.
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Oxygen Sensor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oxygen sensors can fail, causing a check engine light and potentially affecting fuel economy and engine performance.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty oxygen sensor(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ per sensor.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

*   **Shift Flare/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The 4-speed automatic transmission can exhibit shift flare or slipping, particularly between gears. This is often due to worn clutch packs or valve body issues.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush, valve body repair/replacement, or a full transmission rebuild/replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (fluid flush) - $3,000+ (rebuild/replacement).
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Strut/Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Worn struts and shock absorbers can lead to a bouncy ride, poor handling, and increased stopping distances.
    *   Solution: Replace the struts and/or shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per axle.
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Sway Bar Link Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The sway bar links wear out and can cause clunking noises, especially when going over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replace the sway bar links.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ per side.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Power Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power door locks may fail to operate properly, either not locking/unlocking or making unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ per door.
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Sliding Door Issues (Power)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some models have power sliding doors that can develop issues. These can include door failures, inoperable doors, doors that do not latch, etc.
    *   Solution: Depending on the exact issue, possible solutions include replacing the motor, replacing the latch, or a new sliding door cable.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $700+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust is a common issue, especially in regions with road salt. Common areas for rust include the rocker panels, wheel wells, and undercarriage.
    *   Solution: Rust repair or panel replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies widely depending on severity, $100 - $2,000+.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Summary**

The 2002 Toyota Sienna is a generally reliable vehicle, but owners should be aware of potential issues related to its age. The most common concerns are oil leaks, transmission slippage, and electrical problems. The severity and cost of repairs vary widely depending on the specific issue and the condition of the vehicle. Regular maintenance, particularly oil changes, is crucial to prevent or mitigate some of these problems.
