**Introduction:**

The 1998 Toyota Sienna is a midsize minivan that was offered with multiple engine and transmission options. The base engine was a 3.0-liter V6 with 150 horsepower and 180 lb-ft of torque. A more powerful 3.3-liter V6 with 205 horsepower and 220 lb-ft of torque was also available. Both engines were mated to a four-speed automatic transmission.

Owners of the 1998 Toyota Sienna appreciate its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and reliable performance. However, like any vehicle, the Sienna has its share of reported issues.

**Engine**

**3.0-liter V6**

* **Oil consumption:** Some owners have reported excessive oil consumption, particularly in vehicles with high mileage. This can be caused by worn piston rings or valve seals.
  * Solution: Replace worn components
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**3.3-liter V6**

* **Head gasket failure:** A small number of owners have reported head gasket failures. This is a serious issue that can lead to coolant leaking into the engine oil or vice versa.
  * Solution: Replace the head gasket
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Four-speed Automatic**

* **Shifting problems:** Some owners have experienced shifting problems, such as delayed or rough shifting. This can be caused by worn clutches or solenoids.
  * Solution: Replace the affected components
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut failure:** Some owners have reported worn or leaking struts. This can lead to a rough ride and decreased handling.
  * Solution: Replace the struts
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ per pair
  * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** Some owners have experienced alternator failures. This can lead to a loss of power or a dead battery.
  * Solution: Replace the alternator
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Power window failure:** Some owners have reported power window failures. This can be caused by faulty switches or motors.
  * Solution: Replace the affected components
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per window
  * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate control issues:** Some owners have reported problems with the climate control system, such as erratic temperature control or fan failure.
  * Solution: Replace the affected components
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 1998 Toyota Sienna is a reliable and practical vehicle. However, like any vehicle, it has its share of reported issues. Most of these issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix. However, some more serious issues, such as engine or transmission problems, can be more costly to repair.
