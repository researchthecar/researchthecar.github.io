# 2018 Toyota Sienna Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Toyota Sienna is widely recognized as a dependable and versatile family mover, offering spacious accommodations and available all-wheel drive. While the vehicle maintains a strong reputation for longevity, used car buyers should review specific owner-reported concerns regarding transmission performance and power accessories. Reviewing service records and conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Direct-Injection (2GR-FKS)

The 2018 Sienna is powered by a robust 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 296 horsepower and 263 lb-ft of torque, paired with a smooth direct-injection system. This engine generally offers strong, reliable performance for daily driving and highway cruising when properly maintained.

**Motive Power Interruption**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected jerking while driving, accompanied by a sudden loss of motive power or engine stalling.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the fuel system, electronic throttle body, and powertrain control module for fault codes or component failure.

## Transmission

### Direct Shift 8-Speed Automatic (UA80E/UA80F)

Equipped with an 8-speed automatic transmission available in both front-wheel drive and all-wheel drive configurations, this gearbox is designed to optimize fuel economy. Buyers should test-drive the vehicle carefully to check for smooth gear engagement and listen for unusual noises.

**Transmission Whining and Shifting Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note a noticeable whining noise that increases with vehicle speed, along with occasional hesitation or erratic shifting behavior.
- **Solution:** Consult a transmission specialist to check fluid condition and internal components; severe mechanical wear may require professional overhaul or replacement.
- **Cost:** $6,500 - $7,000

## Suspension

### Suspension and Tires

The suspension setup balances ride comfort with the demands of hauling passengers and cargo, though proper maintenance is key to preventing uneven tire wear.

**Premature or Uneven Tire Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Owners experience rapid tread wear along the inner or outer shoulders of the tires, occasionally exposing steel cords despite routine alignments.
- **Solution:** Inspect alignment settings, suspension components, and tire pressure regularly, and replace worn tires promptly to maintain safe handling.

## Safety

### Sliding Doors

Exterior hardware and power sliding doors require routine inspection to ensure smooth daily operation of convenience features.

**Sliding Door Cable and Latch Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Power sliding door cables may snap, or doors may fail to stay open securely, creating potential safety hazards.
- **Solution:** Inspect sliding door cables, rollers, and motors for wear; replace damaged mechanical assemblies as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V682000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sienna&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel pump fails, the engine can stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly with an improved one, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V012000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sienna&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel pump fails, the engine can stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V532000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sienna&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the steering shaft extension completely fractures, it can cause a loss of steering, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Braun has notified owners, and dealers will replace the steering shaft extensions, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V410000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sienna&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The brake lines may have incorrect tube nut fittings installed, which may cause a pressure drop within the braking system. The reduction in the braking system pressure may cause a loss of braking power in the rear braking system, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Braun has notified owners, and dealers will install an adaptor to correct the issue, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V503000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sienna&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An incorrect label may lead to unintentionally overloading the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 19V553000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sienna&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The contact may cause failure of the rear brake hoses, resulting in a loss of vehicle control and reduced braking ability, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Vantage will notify owners, and dealers will install a set of rear brake hoses that will provide the proper clearance, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V130000: Wheels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sienna&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** These wheels may not have been properly cooled during manufacturing. If the wheels crack at the spokes, the wheel may separate from the hub assembly causing a loss of vehicle control and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the wheels and replace them if necessary depending on their production location, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Toyota Sienna remains a solid choice for families seeking space, capability, and long-term durability. While prospective buyers should carefully evaluate the transmission for signs of whining or rough shifting, and inspect the tires and sliding doors for wear, a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection will help secure a reliable model.
