# 2024 Toyota Sienna Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Toyota Sienna continues its reign as a top-tier family hauler, combining standard hybrid efficiency with practical space and all-wheel-drive capability. While buyers generally praise its versatility, owner reports highlight a few specific areas of concern, notably around braking feel and minor accessory components.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L Hybrid I4

This 2.5-liter four-cylinder hybrid powertrain pairs electric motors with a gasoline engine to generate a net 245 horsepower. It delivers exceptional fuel economy for a full-size minivan, though a small number of owners have flagged rare engine component failures or unexpected acceleration sensations during braking maneuvers.

## Transmission

### Electronically Controlled CVT

The continuously variable transmission seamlessly distributes power from the hybrid system to either the front wheels or an electronic all-wheel-drive setup. It prioritizes smooth acceleration and maximum fuel efficiency, offering reliable everyday operation.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The regenerative braking system blends electrical resistance with traditional friction brakes to capture energy. While generally effective, some drivers report unusual surging sensations while trying to stop.

**Unexpected Acceleration While Braking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note that the vehicle may briefly lunge forward or surge for a few seconds while the brake pedal is depressed, creating potential stopping hazards.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle inspected by a qualified dealer or mechanic to check for software updates or sensor anomalies related to the regenerative braking system.

## Exterior

### Side Mirrors

Exterior panels and mirrors are generally durable, though the driver-side mirror is prone to high-speed vibration in some instances.

**Mirror Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** The driver-side exterior mirror visibly vibrates or wobbles at highway speeds or on bumpy roads, distorting the reflected image.
- **Solution:** Inspect mirror assembly mounts; dealerships may consider this normal characteristic behavior unless internal retention clips are physically broken.

### Power Liftgate

Interior convenience features provide excellent utility, though power-operated cargo gates require occasional alignment checks.

**Liftgate Failure to Latch**
- **Symptoms:** The power rear liftgate or trunk starts its closing sequence normally but aborts just before latching, or makes a screeching noise during operation.
- **Solution:** Lubricate hinges or have the liftgate actuator motor and pinch sensors recalibrated or replaced if misalignment occurs.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V877000: Equipment Adaptive/Mobility:Wheelchair Restraints/Securement:Latch/Anchor:](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sienna&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The retractors may not lock, preventing the wheelchair from being properly secured. An unsecured wheelchair can move during transit, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The Toyota Sienna remains a reliable and highly efficient choice for families looking for hybrid minivan utility. While prospective buyers should keep an eye out for rare reports of momentary brake surge behavior and minor power liftgate or mirror quirks, the vehicle overall maintains a strong reputation for day-to-day dependability.
