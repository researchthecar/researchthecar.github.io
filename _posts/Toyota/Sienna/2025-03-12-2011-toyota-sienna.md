The 2011 Toyota Sienna is a popular minivan known for its reliability, spacious interior, and comfortable ride. It came with two engine options: a 2.7L 4-cylinder engine producing 187 horsepower and 186 lb-ft of torque, and a more powerful 3.5L V6 engine producing 266 horsepower and 245 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the Sienna for its versatility, smooth driving experience, and ample cargo space. Common complaints include issues with the sliding doors, excessive oil consumption in some V6 models, and certain electrical glitches.

**Engine**

**2.7L 1AR-FE Inline-4**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than normal oil consumption.
    *   Solution: Regularly monitor oil levels and top off as needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: N/A
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.5L 2GR-FE V6**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than normal oil consumption.
    *   Solution: Replacing piston rings and pistons.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**U660E 6-Speed Automatic**
* **Harsh Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners report harsh or jerky shifts, especially during acceleration or deceleration. This can be more pronounced at lower speeds.
    * Solution: Replacing the transmission valve body.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Suspension Sagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rear suspension sagging, especially when loaded, leading to reduced ride height and potential bottoming out.
    *   Solution: Replacing rear shocks or springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sliding Door Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power sliding doors failing to open or close properly, or becoming unresponsive. This can be due to issues with the motor, cables, or sensors.
    *   Solution: Replacing sliding door motor, cable, or sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Outlet Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power outlets (cigarette lighter or auxiliary power) ceasing to function.
    *   Solution: Replacing the blown fuse or replacing the outlet if it has failed internally.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $5 - $100+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Backup Camera Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Backup camera image is distorted or non-existent
    *   Solution: Replacing the backup camera.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust on Liftgate**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust developing around the rear liftgate, particularly near the license plate area or around the window.
    *   Solution: Repairing and repainting the affected area, or replacing the liftgate if the rust is extensive.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracking of the dashboard, especially in warmer climates or with prolonged sun exposure.
    *   Solution: Replacing the dashboard.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2011 Toyota Sienna is generally a reliable vehicle, but owners should be aware of potential issues such as excessive oil consumption, sliding door malfunctions, and dashboard cracking. While some issues, like excessive oil consumption, can be costly to repair, others are relatively minor. Overall, the Sienna remains a practical and versatile minivan, but potential buyers should carefully inspect used models and address any identified problems promptly.
