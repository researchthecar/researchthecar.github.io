**Introduction**

The 2013 Toyota Sienna is a versatile minivan that offers plenty of space, comfort, and features for families. It is powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine that produces 266 horsepower and 245 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with a six-speed automatic transmission. People like the Sienna for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and excellent safety ratings. However, some owners have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, and other components.

**Engine**

* **3.5L V6 Engine**
    * **Valve Spring Breakage:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The valve springs can break, causing the engine to stall or run rough.
        * Solution: Replace the broken valve springs.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign ID Numbers 14V600, 14V601
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner can fail, causing the timing chain to skip or break. This can lead to engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Transmission Slipping:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip or hesitate when shifting gears.
        * Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Corrosion:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The strut mounts can corrode, causing a clunking or rattling noise from the front suspension.
        * Solution: Replace the corroded strut mounts.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Sway Bar End Link Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The rear sway bar end links can fail, causing a clunking or rattling noise from the rear suspension.
        * Solution: Replace the failed sway bar end links.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The alternator can fail, causing the battery to drain and the vehicle to stall.
        * Solution: Replace the alternator.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Tail Light Assembly Water Leakage:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Water can leak into the tail light assemblies, causing the lights to malfunction.
        * Solution: Replace the leaking tail light assemblies.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Radio Touchscreen Malfunction:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The touchscreen on the radio can become unresponsive or display incorrect information.
        * Solution: Replace the defective touchscreen.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Sliding Door Motor Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: The sliding door motor can fail, causing the door to open or close slowly or not at all.
        * Solution: Replace the failed motor.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2013 Toyota Sienna is a solid choice for families, but it is not without its issues. Some owners have reported problems with the engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical system. The severity and cost of the issues can vary depending on the specific problem.
