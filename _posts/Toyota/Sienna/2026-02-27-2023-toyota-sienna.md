# 2023 Toyota Sienna Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Toyota Sienna continues the vehicle's tradition as a fuel-efficient and family-friendly hybrid minivan, offering standard all-wheel-drive options on many trims and a spacious interior. Because it is a relatively modern model year, owner reports are minimal, though prospective buyers should still verify overall vehicle condition and functionality during a test drive.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Hybrid

This 2.5-liter four-cylinder hybrid powertrain produces a combined 245 horsepower and utilizes an electronically controlled continuously variable transmission. It provides exceptional fuel economy for a full-size minivan and generally exhibits strong overall durability.

## Transmission

### Electronically Controlled CVT (e-CVT)

The e-CVT seamlessly distributes power from the hybrid system to the front wheels or available electronic all-wheel-drive system. It operates smoothly and requires routine maintenance like any standard modern Toyota hybrid transaxle.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V877000: Equipment Adaptive/Mobility:Wheelchair Restraints/Securement:Latch/Anchor:](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sienna&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The retractors may not lock, preventing the wheelchair from being properly secured. An unsecured wheelchair can move during transit, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Toyota Sienna is a highly efficient and dependable choice in the minivan segment, backed by a proven hybrid platform. While complaints are virtually absent for this model year, buyers should always conduct a thorough personal inspection and review vehicle history records before purchasing.
