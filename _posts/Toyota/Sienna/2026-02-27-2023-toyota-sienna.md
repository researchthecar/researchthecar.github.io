# 2023 Toyota Sienna Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Toyota Sienna continues to be a top choice for family hauling, praised for its standard hybrid fuel efficiency and spacious cabin. However, used car buyers should review known owner complaints regarding intermittent electronic glitches, braking surges, and sunroof durability.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L Hybrid I4

The 2.5-liter four-cylinder hybrid engine pairs with electric motors to deliver an efficient 245 net horsepower. It offers exceptional fuel economy for a minivan, though buyers should verify proper hybrid system operation during a test drive.

**Hybrid System Malfunction and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** A 'hybrid system malfunction' warning appears on the dashboard, and the vehicle can suddenly lose power or experience restricted speed on inclines.
- **Solution:** Have the hybrid control modules and powertrain diagnostics inspected by a certified dealer or mechanic.

## Transmission

### eCVT Automatic

The electronically controlled continuously variable transmission (eCVT) provides smooth power delivery and integrates seamlessly with the hybrid system. It generally exhibits solid reliability, with no widespread mechanical failure trends reported.

## Electrical

### Service Brakes

The braking system blends regenerative and traditional friction braking. While generally effective, some drivers experience unusual surging behaviors at lower speeds.

**Low-Speed Braking Lurch**
- **Symptoms:** While applying steady pressure to stop at low speeds, the brakes can momentarily feel like they are releasing pressure, causing the vehicle to lurch or surge forward.
- **Solution:** Check for any applicable software updates or have the regenerative braking system inspected by a qualified technician.

### Electrical System

Electrical accessories like power doors and keyless entry add great convenience but can occasionally require sensor recalibration or actuator service.

**Sliding Door and Key Fob Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Power sliding doors may occasionally unlatch or open unexpectedly, or key fobs may fail to communicate with the vehicle lock mechanisms.
- **Solution:** Inspect door sensors, actuators, and key fob programming or battery levels.

## Exterior

### Sunroof / Moonroof

Exterior glass components are generally durable, though a small number of owners have encountered spontaneous glass breakage.

**Spontaneous Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The sunroof or moonroof glass may crack or shatter loudly without warning while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged glass panel and inspect the frame for any manufacturing stress points or alignment flaws.

## Safety

### Seats

Interior seating configurations offer great modularity, but buyers should test seat stability, especially when installing child safety restraints.

**Rear Seat Play**
- **Symptoms:** The second or third-row seats can exhibit excessive play or looseness, making it difficult to secure child safety seats tightly.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat mounting tracks and latch mechanisms for wear or loose anchor bolts.

### Airbags & Pre-Collision

Advanced safety suites provide robust protection, but owners should watch for cabin leaks that could compromise sensitive wiring harnesses.

**Water Intrusion and Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** Water leaks into the cabin can affect electrical components tied to the Supplemental Restraint System, triggering warning indicators.
- **Solution:** Inspect sunroof drains and window seals to prevent moisture from reaching sensitive airbag control modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V877000: Equipment Adaptive/Mobility:Wheelchair Restraints/Securement:Latch/Anchor:](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sienna&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The retractors may not lock, preventing the wheelchair from being properly secured. An unsecured wheelchair can move during transit, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The vehicle remains a practical and highly efficient family hauler, but used car shoppers should thoroughly test the brakes at low speeds, check all electronic sliding doors, and inspect the sunroof and interior seating mechanisms prior to purchase.
