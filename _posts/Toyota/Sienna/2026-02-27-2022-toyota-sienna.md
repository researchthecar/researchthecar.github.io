# 2022 Toyota Sienna Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Toyota Sienna continues the model's reputation as a practical and efficient family mover, featuring a standard hybrid powertrain across all trims. Used-car buyers looking at this model year will generally find a modern, highly fuel-efficient minivan equipped with contemporary tech and safety features. While early owner reports are relatively light, evaluating specific mechanical and electrical components remains an essential part of a thorough pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Hybrid

This 2.5-liter four-cylinder hybrid powertrain pairs with electric motors to produce a combined 245 horsepower. It delivers exceptional fuel economy for a three-row family vehicle with minimal mechanical complaints reported by owners.

## Transmission

### Electronically Controlled Continuously Variable Transmission (eCVT)

An electronically controlled continuously variable transmission distributes power seamlessly to either the front wheels or the electronic all-wheel-drive system. It is generally regarded as smooth and durable in everyday driving conditions.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V889000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other:Anchorage](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sienna&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** During seat belt pre-tensioner deployment, the incorrect webbing guide may damage the seat belt webbing, causing it to tear and increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the outboard second-row seat belt assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V239000: Electronic Stability Control (Esc):Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sienna&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The Skid Control ECU software may not turn on the Vehicle Stability Control (VSC) system when the vehicle is restarted after the driver has disabled the VSC system. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 126, "Electronic Stability Control Systems." Driving with a deactivated stability.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Skid Control ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V877000: Equipment Adaptive/Mobility:Wheelchair Restraints/Securement:Latch/Anchor:](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sienna&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The retractors may not lock, preventing the wheelchair from being properly secured. An unsecured wheelchair can move during transit, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Toyota Sienna is a solid used-car prospect, largely thanks to its efficient hybrid powertrain and strong baseline reputation for reliability. Buyers should still perform a standard pre-purchase inspection to check for any minor electronic or cosmetic flaws before finalizing a purchase.
