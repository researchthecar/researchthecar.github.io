# 2017 Toyota Sienna Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Toyota Sienna is a highly practical family minivan known for its spacious interior and available all-wheel-drive configuration. While it generally offers dependable family transportation, used car buyers should pay close attention to potential powertrain quirks and accessory electrical issues reported by owners.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6

Equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 engine generating around 296 horsepower and 263 lb-ft of torque, this powertrain provides strong acceleration and capable highway performance for a minivan. It is generally robust, though occasional fuel pump and stalling complaints have been noted by owners.

**Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of engine power while driving, engine stalling, and illuminated check engine lights.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed factory fuel pump module with an updated component.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle unexpectedly stalls while idling, starting, or driving, sometimes requiring a tow.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fuel delivery or electrical ignition components and replace faulty parts as needed.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

This 8-speed automatic transmission is paired with front-wheel drive or available all-wheel drive, offering smooth cruising when operating properly. However, some owners have reported premature wear, whining noises, and shifting hesitation.

**Transmission Slipping and Whining**
- **Symptoms:** High-pitched whining noises, transmission slipping between gears, gear hunting, and failure to engage reverse or higher gears.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid levels and condition, check diagnostic trouble codes, and consult a dealer for transmission repair or replacement.

**Electrical Warning Lights and Drivetrain Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Check AWD System lights and transmission-related diagnostic trouble codes appearing on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Scan transmission control module codes and perform recommended software updates or hardware repairs.

## Exterior

### Lighting and Dashboard Controls

The electrical system manages various convenience and safety features, with occasional owner reports centering on lighting switch behaviors and dashboard displays.

**Headlight and Dashboard Illumination Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard illumination or instrument cluster lights failing to activate unless the headlight selector is specifically set to the on or automatic mode.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight switches, wiring harnesses, and instrument cluster settings.

### Sliding Doors and Latches

Cabin convenience features like power sliding doors are generally helpful for families but can occasionally experience latching or alignment glitches over time.

**Sliding Door Latch Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Power sliding doors failing to catch when opening or closing, or unexpectedly reversing direction during operation.
- **Solution:** Clean door tracks and latches of debris, or replace faulty door latch actuators and sensors.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Occupant safety systems are standard equipment, though isolated complaints highlight the need to verify proper seat belt latch engagement across all rows.

**Seat Belt Latch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Seat belt buckles failing to securely latch or hold the seat belt tongue due to internal debris or mechanical wear.
- **Solution:** Clean out debris from the receptacle or replace the seat belt assembly if latching mechanism fails.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V532000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sienna&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the steering shaft extension completely fractures, it can cause a loss of steering, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Braun has notified owners, and dealers will replace the steering shaft extensions, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V410000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sienna&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The brake lines may have incorrect tube nut fittings installed, which may cause a pressure drop within the braking system. The reduction in the braking system pressure may cause a loss of braking power in the rear braking system, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Braun has notified owners, and dealers will install an adaptor to correct the issue, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V503000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sienna&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An incorrect label may lead to unintentionally overloading the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 19V553000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sienna&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The contact may cause failure of the rear brake hoses, resulting in a loss of vehicle control and reduced braking ability, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Vantage will notify owners, and dealers will install a set of rear brake hoses that will provide the proper clearance, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V831000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sienna&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An incorrect load information label can result in the operator overloading the vehicle and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 17V295000: Tires:Temporary/Emergency Spare Tire](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sienna&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A spare tire that is not inflated to the proper value may not perform as intended, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Gulf States will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the spare tires, adjusting their pressure as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V211000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist:Vacuum](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sienna&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A sudden loss of braking assist can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the brake booster vacuum pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V682000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Toyota&model=Sienna&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel pump fails, the engine can stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Toyota will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly with an improved one, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Toyota Sienna remains a versatile and popular choice for families seeking space and available all-wheel drive. Prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the transmission for any signs of whining or harsh shifting, and verify that the fuel system and power sliding doors operate smoothly during a pre-purchase test drive.
