**Introduction to the 2020 Toyota GR Supra**

The 2020 Toyota GR Supra is a highly anticipated sports car that marks the return of the legendary Supra nameplate. It is offered with a choice of two turbocharged engines: a 2.0-liter inline-four and a 3.0-liter inline-six. Both engines are paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission. The GR Supra has been praised for its impressive performance, handling, and styling. However, some owners have reported issues with certain aspects of the vehicle.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Inline-Four**

* **Faulty wastegate actuator**
    * Description/Symptoms: The wastegate actuator can fail, causing the turbocharger to overboost, which can lead to engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the wastegate actuator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Premature spark plug failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The spark plugs can fail prematurely, causing misfires and engine performance issues.
    * Solution: Replace the spark plugs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.0-liter Inline-Six**

* **Fuel injector issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The fuel injectors can become clogged or malfunction, causing rough idling, loss of power, and difficulty starting.
    * Solution: Inspect and clean the fuel injectors. If necessary, replace them.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission shudder**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission can experience shuddering during gear changes.
    * Solution: Update the transmission fluid and software.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $200 (if under warranty)
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Premature transmission failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission can fail prematurely, resulting in loss of power and the inability to drive the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear suspension noise**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear suspension can make a clunking noise when driving over uneven surfaces.
    * Solution: Lubricate or replace the rear suspension components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery can drain quickly, even when the vehicle is not in use.
    * Solution: Inspect for parasitic power draw and replace faulty components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Infotainment system screen flickering**
    * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system screen can flicker or become unresponsive.
    * Solution: Update the software or replace the screen.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Door lock malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The door locks can malfunction, making it difficult to lock or unlock the doors.
    * Solution: Replace the door lock actuators.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2020 Toyota GR Supra is a high-performance sports car that has received positive reviews overall. However, some owners have experienced issues with the engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical systems. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem. It is important to note that most of these issues are relatively minor and can be resolved with a reasonable amount of cost.
