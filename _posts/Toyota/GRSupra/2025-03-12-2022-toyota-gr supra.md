The 2022 Toyota GR Supra is a sports car known for its sharp handling, quick acceleration, and stylish design. It comes with two engine options: a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 producing 255 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-6 producing 382 horsepower and 368 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Drivers often praise the Supra's engaging driving dynamics and unique aesthetic. Some common complaints revolve around limited headroom, a relatively small cargo area, and in earlier models, some have complaints about the infotainment system.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**

*   **No Issues Found**

**3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (Both Engines)**

*   **Hesitation/Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported occasional hesitation or slightly rough shifting, especially during low-speed maneuvers or when downshifting. This is not a widespread issue, but a noticeable concern for some drivers.
    *   Solution: Software updates to the Transmission Control Module (TCM) may improve shifting behavior. In more severe cases, transmission fluid flush and fill has been performed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$300+ depending on the severity and chosen service.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Glitches/Freezing**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported intermittent freezing, slow response times, or unexpected reboots of the infotainment system. This seems to be more prevalent in earlier production models.
    * Solution: Software updates from Toyota can often address these issues. In some cases, replacing the infotainment system's head unit may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Software Update) - $1500+ (Head Unit Replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Poor Panel Alignment**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported uneven gaps between body panels.
    *   Solution: Realignment of body panels.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Buzzing Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rattling or buzzing noises emanating from the dashboard or door panels, particularly on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Identifying the source of the noise and applying felt tape or padding to eliminate the vibrations.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Toyota GR Supra is generally a reliable sports car. The most commonly reported issues are related to the infotainment system and minor interior rattles. The powertrain has very few consistent issues reported. Repair costs range from minimal for software updates to potentially more significant for head unit replacement or body panel alignment. Overall, the issues reported are not severe or widespread, but potential buyers should be aware of them and check for these symptoms during inspection.
