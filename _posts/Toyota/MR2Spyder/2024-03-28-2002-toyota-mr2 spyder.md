**Introduction:**

The 2002 Toyota MR2 Spyder is a mid-engine, two-seater sports car known for its handling and performance. It is offered with a single engine and transmission option: a 1.8L 1ZZ-FE inline-four engine paired with a 5-speed manual transmission. The MR2 Spyder produces 138 horsepower and 125 lb-ft of torque. Despite its impressive performance, the MR2 Spyder has several issues that potential buyers should be aware of.

**Engine**

**1.8L 1ZZ-FE Inline-Four**

* **Oil Consumption:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-ups.
    * Solution: Piston ring replacement or engine rebuild.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leak from the valve cover gasket, causing a burning oil smell.
    * Solution: Replace valve cover gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling or ticking noise from the engine, especially during startup.
    * Solution: Replace timing chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Grinding Gears:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, accompanied by grinding noises.
    * Solution: Replace worn gears or synchronizers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Clutch Slipping:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power or acceleration, especially when climbing hills or under load.
    * Solution: Replace clutch assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Front and Rear Suspension:**

* **Strut Mount Bearing Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Knocking or clunking noise when turning or going over bumps.
    * Solution: Replace strut mount bearings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Suspension Bushing Deterioration:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loose or rattling rear suspension, resulting in poor handling.
    * Solution: Replace worn bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**General Electrical System:**

* **Headlight Switch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Headlights or taillights not functioning properly, intermittent or flickering lights.
    * Solution: Replace headlight switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging properly, dim or flickering lights, difficulty starting the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Body Parts and Trim:**

* **Plastic Bumper Cover Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks or breaks in the plastic bumper cover, especially in cold weather.
    * Solution: Replace bumper cover.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Targa Roof Seal Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin through the targa roof seals.
    * Solution: Replace or reseal targa roof seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Dashboard and Gauges:**

* **Speedometer Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Speedometer not functioning properly, displaying inaccurate readings.
    * Solution: Replace speedometer assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Gauge Cluster Illumination Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Instrument panel gauges not illuminating properly or intermittently.
    * Solution: Replace gauge cluster or repair electrical connections.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2002 Toyota MR2 Spyder has a few notable issues that potential buyers should consider before purchasing. While the engine and transmission are generally reliable, issues such as oil consumption, valve cover gasket leaks, and timing chain tensioner failure can be costly to repair. Suspension and electrical issues are also common and can impact both performance and safety. Exterior and interior problems are generally minor but should still be factored into the overall cost of ownership.
