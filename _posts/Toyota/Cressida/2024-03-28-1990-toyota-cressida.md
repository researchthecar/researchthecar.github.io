**Introduction:**

The 1990 Toyota Cressida is a mid-size luxury sedan known for its reliability, comfort, and performance. It was available with two engine options: a 3.0-liter V6 (190 hp, 190 lb-ft of torque) and a 4.0-liter V8 (260 hp, 315 lb-ft of torque). Both engines were paired with a standard 4-speed automatic transmission. The Cressida was praised for its smooth handling, luxurious interior, and responsive acceleration. However, it also had some shortcomings, including a dated exterior design and a lack of modern features.

**Engine Issues:**

**3.0-liter V6 (1MZ-FE)**
- **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Leaks coolant externally, causing overheating.
   - Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
   - Recalls: None Found
- **Water pump failure:** Leaks coolant, causing overheating.
   - Solution: Replace water pump.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
   - Recalls: None Found
- **Valve cover gasket leaks:** Oil leaks from the valve covers, causing smoke and oil consumption.
   - Solution: Replace valve cover gaskets.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
   - Recalls: None Found

**4.0-liter V8 (1UZ-FE)**
- **Exhaust manifold cracking:** Cracks in the exhaust manifold, causing exhaust leaks.
   - Solution: Replace exhaust manifold.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
   - Recalls: None Found
- **Camshaft position sensor failure:** Engine misfires, rough idle, and stalling.
   - Solution: Replace camshaft position sensor.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
   - Recalls: None Found
- **Fuel injectors sticking:** Engine misfires, rough idle, and decreased fuel economy.
   - Solution: Clean or replace fuel injectors.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
   - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission Issues:**

**4-speed Automatic Transmission (A340E)**
- **Transmission slipping:** Difficulty shifting gears or inconsistent gear engagement.
   - Solution: Overhaul or replace transmission.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
   - Recalls: None Found
- **Torque converter shudder:** Vibration when shifting gears.
   - Solution: Replace torque converter.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
   - Recalls: None Found
- **Solenoid failure:** Transmission does not shift gears properly.
   - Solution: Replace failed solenoid.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
   - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension Issues:**

- **Strut failure:** Leaks fluid, causing uneven ride height and poor handling.
   - Solution: Replace struts.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
   - Recalls: None Found
- **Lower control arm bushings worn:** Causes clunking noise and poor handling.
   - Solution: Replace control arm bushings.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
   - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical Issues:**

- **Alternator failure:** Battery does not charge, electrical system malfunctions.
   - Solution: Replace alternator.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
   - Recalls: None Found
- **Starter motor failure:** Engine does not crank.
   - Solution: Replace starter motor.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
   - Recalls: None Found
- **Window regulator failure:** Windows do not operate properly.
   - Solution: Replace window regulator.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
   - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior Issues:**

- **Sunroof leak:** Water leaks into the cabin through the sunroof.
   - Solution: Replace or repair sunroof seals.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
   - Recalls: None Found
- **Headlight housing fades:** Headlights turn cloudy or yellowed over time.
   - Solution: Restore or replace headlight housings.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
   - Recalls: None Found

**Interior Issues:**

- **Climate control panel failure:** HVAC system malfunctions.
   - Solution: Replace climate control panel.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
   - Recalls: None Found
- **Door lock actuators fail:** Doors do not lock or unlock properly.
   - Solution: Replace door lock actuators.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
   - Recalls: None Found
- **Seat heater element failure:** Seat heaters do not work.
   - Solution: Replace seat heater element.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
   - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 1990 Toyota Cressida has some common problems, particularly with the engine, transmission, and suspension. Engine issues include intake manifold gasket failure, water pump failure, and valve cover gasket leaks. The automatic transmission may experience slipping, torque converter shudder, and solenoid failure. Suspension issues include strut failure and worn lower control arm bushings. Owners should also be aware of potential electrical problems with the alternator, starter motor, and window regulators. Exterior issues include sunroof leaks and headlight housing fading. Interior problems include climate control panel failure, door lock actuator failure, and seat heater element failure. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but they can be significant in some cases.
