# 2016 Kia Soul Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2016 Kia Soul, prospective buyers will find a popular, distinctive hatchback known for its practical cabin space and comfortable ride. However, owner complaints point to several notable mechanical and cosmetic concerns that require careful inspection. The most serious reports involve engine performance issues, catalytic converter failures, and unexpected stalling, while exterior body paint peeling on certain factory finishes is also a frequent complaint.

---

## Engine

### 1.6L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 1.6-liter four-cylinder engine produces around 130 horsepower and is paired with manual or automatic transmissions, offering adequate efficiency for daily commuting. Buyers should verify maintenance records and test for proper engine performance and emissions health.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences sudden loss of motive power, engine hesitation, or unexpected stalling while driving, often accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose underlying fuel, ignition, or powertrain control module issues; replace faulty sensors or components as needed.

**Catalytic Converter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light illuminates, followed by high engine revving, smoke from the exhaust, engine knocking, or loss of power.
- **Solution:** Inspect exhaust and emissions components; replace damaged catalytic converters and address root causes of excessive heat or oil consumption.

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The larger 2.0-liter direct-injection four-cylinder engine delivers roughly 164 horsepower for livelier acceleration. It shares similar reliability considerations regarding oil consumption and exhaust emissions as the base powerplant.

**Engine Stalling and Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine or warning lights, unexpected engine stalling, and potential smoke or oil consumption issues.
- **Solution:** Perform thorough diagnostic testing, check fluid levels frequently, and replace failing sensors or internal components.

### Electric Motor (Soul EV)

The electric powertrain variant delivers zero-emission driving with instant torque. It generally presents fewer mechanical engine concerns, though battery health and electronic gear shift systems should be carefully inspected.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission offers direct gear control for base trims. Routine clutch checks and smooth shifting behavior are recommended during a pre-purchase inspection.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission provides smooth power delivery across most trims. Ensuring regular fluid changes helps maintain long-term durability.

### Single-Speed Direct Drive (Soul EV)

The single-speed automatic transmission found in the electric variant provides seamless acceleration without traditional gear shifting. It requires minimal routine maintenance.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brakes

The braking system provides dependable stopping power under normal conditions, but buyers in northern or salt-heavy climates should check for brake line corrosion.

**Brake Line Corrosion and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Brakes feel spongy, become inoperable while driving, or brake warning lights illuminate due to rusted or leaking fluid lines.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake lines and hydraulic components for rust or leaks; replace deteriorated brake lines and flush brake fluid.

## Exterior

### Structure and Paint

Exterior body panels and structural integrity are generally sound, though certain factory paint colors are prone to premature clear coat failure and peeling.

**Factory Paint Peeling and Delamination**
- **Symptoms:** White or other factory paint finishes begin bubbling, cracking, peeling, or chipping away from the hood, roof, mirrors, and door handles.
- **Solution:** Repaint affected body panels or apply protective clear coats; consult local body shops for professional restoration.

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical accessories generally operate reliably, though intermittent lighting and switch failures have been reported by some owners.

**Headlight and Turn Signal Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights flicker, turn off and on randomly without warning, or turn signals blink at an unusually rapid pace.
- **Solution:** Inspect wiring harnesses, headlight switches, and bulb sockets; replace faulty electrical components as necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V120000: Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Soul&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Piston damage may result in an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash. The leaking oil may contact the exhaust, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Kia will notify owners, and dealers will upgrade the Catalytic Overheating Protection Engine Control Unit logic to prevent overheating of the catalytic converter. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V608000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Soul&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the pinion gear separates, steering will be lost, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Kia will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and secure the pinion plug or replace the steering gear assembly, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Kia Soul remains an appealing choice for used-car buyers seeking versatility, quirky styling, and everyday usability. However, shoppers should prioritize a thorough mechanical inspection focusing on engine performance, exhaust and catalytic converter health, and fluid levels. Additionally, check exterior paint condition and test all lighting and brake components to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
