**Introduction:**

The 2019 Kia Optima is a midsize sedan that offers a comfortable ride, spacious interior, and a variety of engine and transmission options. It is available with a 2.4L inline-four engine producing 185 horsepower and 178 lb-ft of torque, a 1.6L turbocharged inline-four engine producing 178 horsepower and 195 lb-ft of torque, or a 2.0L turbocharged inline-four engine producing 245 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque. The base engine comes mated to a six-speed automatic transmission, while the turbocharged engines are paired with a seven-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission. While the Optima offers a lot of value for its price, it has also been plagued by a variety of issues.

**Engine**

**2.4L Inline-Four Engine**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Some owners have reported that their vehicles are consuming excessive amounts of oil, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage. The issue is caused by a defective piston ring design.
    * Solution: Replacement of the piston rings, which can cost between $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** The timing chain tensioner can fail prematurely, leading to a rattling noise, loss of power, and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replacement of the timing chain tensioner, which can cost between $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**1.6L Turbocharged Inline-Four Engine**

* **Turbocharger Failure:** The turbocharger can fail prematurely, leading to a loss of power and increased fuel consumption.
    * Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger, which can cost between $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Fuel Injector Failure:** The fuel injectors can fail intermittently, causing rough idling, stalling, and poor fuel economy.
    * Solution: Replacement of the fuel injectors, which can cost between $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-Four Engine**

* **Excessive Carbon Buildup:** The engine can experience excessive carbon buildup on the intake valves, leading to decreased performance and fuel economy.
    * Solution: Cleaning or replacement of the intake valves, which can cost between $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Shuddering:** Some owners have reported that their vehicles experience a shuddering sensation when shifting gears. The issue is caused by a faulty torque converter.
    * Solution: Replacement of the torque converter, which can cost between $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Premature Failure:** The transmission can fail prematurely, leading to a loss of power and the inability to shift gears.
    * Solution: Replacement of the transmission, which can cost between $3,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Seven-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission**

* **Clutch Wear:** The clutches can wear prematurely, leading to slipping, shuddering, and difficulty shifting gears.
    * Solution: Replacement of the clutches, which can cost between $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Mechatronic Unit Failure:** The mechatronic unit, which controls the transmission, can fail prematurely, leading to a loss of power and the inability to shift gears.
    * Solution: Replacement of the mechatronic unit, which can cost between $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure:** The strut mounts can fail prematurely, leading to a clunking noise and poor handling.
    * Solution: Replacement of the strut mounts, which can cost between $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Lower Control Arm Bushing Failure:** The lower control arm bushings can fail, leading to a clunking noise and poor handling.
    * Solution: Replacement of the lower control arm bushings, which can cost between $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Some owners have reported that their vehicles experience a battery drain, leading to dead batteries. The issue is caused by a parasitic draw from the electrical system.
    * Solution: Identification and repair of the parasitic draw, which can cost between $50 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Infotainment System Issues:** The infotainment system can experience a variety of issues, including freezing, lagging, and loss of connectivity.
    * Solution: Software updates or replacement of the infotainment unit, which can cost between $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Leaks:** Some owners have reported that their vehicles experience sunroof leaks, leading to water damage in the interior.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the sunroof seal, which can cost between $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Exterior Trim Issues:** The exterior trim pieces can fade or peel prematurely, affecting the appearance of the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replacement of the exterior trim pieces, which can cost between $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunctions:** The climate control system can malfunction, leading to inconsistent temperatures or a lack of airflow.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the climate control components, which can cost between $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Seatbelt Buckle Issues:** The seatbelt buckles can become stuck or difficult to operate.
    * Solution: Replacement of the seatbelt buckles, which can cost between $50 - $100+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2019 Kia Optima has been plagued by a variety of issues, including engine problems, transmission failures, suspension issues, electrical problems, and interior malfunctions. While the severity and cost of these issues can vary, they can significantly impact the reliability and owner satisfaction of the vehicle. It is important for potential buyers to be aware of these issues before making a purchase decision.
