# 2018 Kia Cadenza Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2018 Kia Cadenza, buyers will find a refined and comfortable full-size sedan that generally offers a smooth driving experience. However, owner reports submitted to safety authorities highlight a few notable powertrain and electrical concerns that prospective owners should inspect closely during a test drive.

---

## Engine

### 3.3L V6 GDI

The 2018 Kia Cadenza comes equipped with a 3.3-liter direct-injection V6 engine producing approximately 290 horsepower and 253 lb-ft of torque. It provides brisk, smooth acceleration appropriate for a full-size sedan, though buyers should verify service records and listen for unusual engine noises.

**Engine Stalling and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine stalling while stopped or driving, sometimes accompanied by clicking or ticking noises and sudden power loss leading to engine seizure.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic testing by a qualified mechanic; severe cases may necessitate major engine repairs or replacement.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission channels power smoothly to the front wheels. It is generally robust, though regular fluid maintenance is recommended to ensure longevity.

## Safety

### Airbags

Standard safety equipment includes multiple airbags and advanced driver assistance features designed to protect occupants.

**Airbag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning light illuminates unexpectedly on the dashboard, signaling a potential fault in the Supplemental Restraint System.
- **Solution:** Have the airbag control module and sensors scanned by a professional to identify the faulty component and replace it as necessary.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V137000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Cadenza&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The electrical circuit in the Hydraulic Electronic Control Unit (HECU) may short-circuit, which can cause a fire in the engine compartment. An engine compartment fire can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Kia will notify owners, and dealers will replace certain fuses in the electrical junction box. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Kia Cadenza is an appealing full-size sedan praised for its comfort, upscale cabin, and smooth V6 performance. While most vehicles provide trouble-free miles, prospective buyers should carefully review maintenance history, check for any recurring engine noises or stalling symptoms, and ensure all safety warning lights function correctly before purchasing.
