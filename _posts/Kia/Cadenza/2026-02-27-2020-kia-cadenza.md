# 2020 Kia Cadenza Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Kia Cadenza offers a comfortable ride, upscale cabin features, and a refined driving experience as a full-size sedan. Used-car buyers looking at this model year will find a well-appointed vehicle with a solid overall platform, though shoppers should always perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection to verify proper operation of all mechanical and electronic systems.

---

## Engine

### 3.3L V6 GDI

This 3.3-liter direct-injected V6 engine produces approximately 290 horsepower and 253 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth and responsive acceleration. It generally serves as a dependable powerhouse for daily driving when properly maintained.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic

The 8-speed automatic transmission delivers seamless gear shifts designed to maximize highway efficiency and low-end responsiveness. It is widely considered a durable unit within Kia's lineup, matching well with the V6 engine.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V022000: Visibility:Power Window Devices And Controls](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Cadenza&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 118, "Power-Operated Window Systems." Rear power windows that operate unexpectedly can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Driver Door Module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Kia Cadenza presents itself as a solid choice in the used large sedan segment, offering a smooth powertrain and generous feature content. Because used vehicle condition depends heavily on prior maintenance and handling, buyers should test drive all features and review service records before finalizing a purchase.
