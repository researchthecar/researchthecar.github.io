The 2016 Kia Cadenza is a full-size sedan known for its luxurious features, comfortable ride, and spacious interior. It came standard with a 3.3-liter Lambda II V6 engine producing 290 horsepower and 253 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. People generally appreciate the Cadenza's upscale styling, quiet cabin, and long list of standard features. Some common complaints include the somewhat uninspired driving dynamics and a lack of advanced technology features compared to some competitors.

**Engine**

**3.3L Lambda II V6**

*   **Engine Stalling/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing engine stalling or hesitation, especially when accelerating from a stop or at low speeds. This can be intermittent and difficult to diagnose.
    *   Solution: Potential causes include a faulty mass airflow sensor, dirty throttle body, or issues with the fuel system. Diagnosis by a qualified technician is recommended.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+ depending on the cause.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported oil leaks, typically from the valve cover gaskets or the oil pan gasket. Signs include visible oil under the car, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replacing the leaking gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ depending on the location and severity of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the 6-speed automatic transmission, including rough or delayed shifting, especially between certain gears. This can also manifest as a hesitation when accelerating.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a software update or transmission fluid flush can resolve the issue. In more severe cases, transmission repairs or replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (fluid flush) - $3,000+ (transmission replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Suspension Noise**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the rear suspension, particularly when driving over bumps. This can be caused by worn sway bar links or bushings.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn sway bar links or bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced excessive battery drain, leading to difficulty starting the car, especially after it has been sitting for an extended period.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing and addressing the source of the battery drain, which could include a faulty alternator, a parasitic draw from an electrical component, or a bad battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+ depending on the cause.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the infotainment system freezing, crashing, or becoming unresponsive.
    *   Solution: Performing a system reset, updating the software (if available), or replacing the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (reset) - $800+ (replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Automatic Trunk Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The automatic trunk may fail to open or close properly, or may get stuck.
    *   Solution: Inspecting and replacing the trunk struts, latch mechanism, or associated wiring.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Navigation System Not Updating**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Navigation system map data is out of date, which can cause incorrect routing.
    *   Solution: Update map data via USB or dealer software upgrade.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ for map updates
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2016 Kia Cadenza, while generally reliable, has some common issues owners should be aware of. Engine issues like stalling and oil leaks can be moderately expensive to repair. Transmission problems, while not always prevalent, can be costly to address if extensive repairs are needed. Electrical and suspension issues are generally less severe. Overall, pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic is advisable to identify and address potential problems.
