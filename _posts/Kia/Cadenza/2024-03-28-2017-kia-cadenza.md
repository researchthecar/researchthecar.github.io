**2017 Kia Cadenza**

The 2017 Kia Cadenza is a full-size sedan that offers a combination of comfort, technology, and performance. It is powered by a 3.3-liter V6 engine that produces 290 horsepower and 253 lb-ft of torque. The Cadenza comes standard with an 8-speed automatic transmission and is available in both front-wheel and all-wheel drive configurations.

Some of the features that people like about the Cadenza include its spacious and comfortable interior, its stylish exterior design, and its impressive performance. However, some people have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, and other components.

**Engine**

* **3.3L V6 Engine**
    * **Excessive Oil Consumption**
        * Description/Symptoms: The engine may consume excessive amounts of oil, resulting in low oil levels and potential engine damage.
        * Solution: Dealers will replace the engine.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $5,000 - $10,000
        * Recalls: NHTSA ID Number: 19V576000, 20V192000
    * **Knocking/Tapping Noise**
        * Description/Symptoms: The engine may make a knocking or tapping noise, particularly when starting or accelerating.
        * Solution: Replacing the hydraulic lifters has been reported to resolve the issue.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **8-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Delayed Engagement**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may hesitate or delay when shifting gears, particularly from a stop.
        * Solution: Reprogramming the transmission control module (TCM) or replacing the transmission may be necessary.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Draining**
        * Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain prematurely, even when the vehicle is not in use.
        * Solution: Replacing the battery or checking for parasitic drains may be required.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Malfunctioning Rearview Camera**
        * Description/Symptoms: The rearview camera may malfunction, resulting in no display or distorted images.
        * Solution: Replacing the rearview camera or wiring harness may be necessary.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Cracked Tail Light Lenses**
        * Description/Symptoms: The tail light lenses may crack or develop condensation inside the housing.
        * Solution: Replacing the cracked tail light lenses is the recommended repair.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200
        * Recalls: NHTSA ID Number: 19V353000

**Interior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Summary**

The 2017 Kia Cadenza is a comfortable and stylish sedan with a powerful engine and a long list of features. However, it has been reported to have issues with excessive oil consumption, knocking/tapping noises in the engine, delayed engagement in the transmission, battery draining, and malfunctioning rearview cameras. The severity of these issues varies, and some can be relatively inexpensive to fix, while others may require more costly repairs.
