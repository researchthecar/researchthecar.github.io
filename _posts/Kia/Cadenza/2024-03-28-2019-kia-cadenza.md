**Introduction**

The 2019 Kia Cadenza is a midsize luxury sedan that offers a refined and comfortable ride. It comes standard with a 3.3L V6 engine that produces 290 horsepower and 253 lb-ft of torque. A 2.0L turbocharged inline-four engine that produces 260 horsepower and 264 lb-ft of torque is available as an option. The Cadenza is paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission.

Some of the things people like about the Cadenza include its spacious cabin, comfortable seats, and impressive safety features. However, some owners have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, and other components.

**Engine**

* **3.3L V6 Engine**
    * **Excessive Oil Consumption**
        * Description/Symptoms: The engine may consume excessive amounts of oil, requiring frequent top-ups.
        * Solution: Repair or replace the piston rings.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Timing Chain Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain may stretch or break, resulting in engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace the timing chain and related components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **2.0L Turbocharged Inline-Four Engine**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **Eight-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Rough Shifting**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may shift gears roughly or hesitate when shifting.
        * Solution: Update the transmission software or replace the transmission control module.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Transmission Fluid Leak**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may leak fluid, causing low fluid levels and transmission problems.
        * Solution: Inspect the transmission for leaks and repair or replace the faulty component.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain quickly, even when the vehicle is not in use.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty battery or alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical System Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The electrical system may experience random failures, such as loss of power or flickering lights.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty electrical component.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The door handles may stick or become difficult to operate.
    * Solution: Replace the door handle assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Lag**
    * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may experience lag or freezing issues.
    * Solution: Update the infotainment software or replace the system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Free (software update) or $500 - $1,500+ (system replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Climate Control Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control system may not function properly, resulting in inconsistent temperatures or airflow.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty climate control component.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Kia Cadenza is a solid midsize luxury sedan that offers a comfortable and well-equipped interior. However, some owners have reported issues with the engine, transmission, and other components. While the severity and cost of these issues can vary, it is important to be aware of potential problems before purchasing or owning a Cadenza.
