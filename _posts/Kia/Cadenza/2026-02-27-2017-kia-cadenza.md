# 2017 Kia Cadenza Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 Kia Cadenza, buyers will find a stylish, spacious full-size sedan known for a comfortable ride and upscale cabin features. However, reviewing owner-submitted complaints reveals several recurring mechanical and electrical areas worth inspecting closely during a pre-purchase evaluation. While many owners enjoy the smooth highway cruising and feature set, keeping an eye on specific trouble spots such as fuel odors, braking feel, and thermostat longevity can help ensure a more reliable ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 3.3L V6 GDI

The 3.3-liter Lambda GDI V6 engine produces around 290 horsepower and 253 lb-ft of torque, paired with a smooth delivery well-suited for a large sedan. Overall, this powertrain option is generally robust, though owners have occasionally noted auxiliary cooling and fuel system concerns.

### Fuel

Issues related to fuel system odors require careful inspection of lines and seals to ensure safety and prevent potential leaks.

**Fuel Leak or Odor**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice a strong raw fuel smell around the vehicle, particularly after filling up the gas tank.
- **Solution:** Inspect the fuel tank, lines, and fuel pump assembly for leaks or failing seals, and replace compromised components.

Cooling system and thermostat management components can occasionally trigger warning lights and require replacement to restore proper cabin heating and engine temperature regulation.

**Engine Stalling and Thermostat Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may display warning lights, experience electronic thermostat malfunctions resulting in cold air blowing when heat is requested, or suffer from rough running conditions.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning for trouble codes, followed by replacing faulty electronic thermostats or addressing carbon buildup.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The front-wheel-drive Cadenza utilizes an 8-speed automatic transmission designed for smooth, efficient gear changes. This transmission option generally operates reliably without widespread mechanical failure patterns reported by owners.

## Brakes

### Brake System

Hydraulic brake performance is critical; buyers should test brake responsiveness during a test drive and verify that any relevant recall campaigns have been completed.

**Brake Pedal Softness or Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported instances where the brake pedal traveled further than expected toward the floor or felt unresponsive during slowing maneuvers.
- **Solution:** Thoroughly inspect the hydraulic brake system, master cylinder, and brake lines, and perform necessary bleeding or component replacements.

## Exterior

### Door Lock Actuators

Power accessory components like door actuators can wear out over time, leading to localized locking issues.

**Power Door Lock Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Doors may fail to lock or unlock using the power switches or key fob due to internal actuator failure.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed door lock actuator motor within the affected door assembly.

## Safety

### Seat Belt

Ensuring all seat belts latch and retract smoothly is essential for occupant safety during daily use.

**Seat Belt Latch and Retractor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Front passenger or driver seat belts may fail to latch securely or refuse to retract properly into the housing.
- **Solution:** Have the dealer or a qualified technician inspect the seat belt assembly and replace faulty upper or lower retractor mechanisms.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V137000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Cadenza&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The electrical circuit in the Hydraulic Electronic Control Unit (HECU) may short-circuit, which can cause a fire in the engine compartment. An engine compartment fire can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Kia will notify owners, and dealers will replace certain fuses in the electrical junction box. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V190000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist:Vacuum:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Cadenza&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Reduced braking performance can lengthen the distance needed to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Kia will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the brake vacuum hose, replacing it as necessary, free charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Kia Cadenza offers a compelling blend of comfort, luxury features, and V6 performance for used-car shoppers. While the powertrain is generally dependable, buyers should carefully evaluate the vehicle's service history, test the brakes and heating system, and ensure that all safety recalls and minor electrical concerns have been properly addressed by a professional before purchase.
