The 2016 Kia Sedona is a minivan praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and value proposition compared to its competitors. It generally receives positive reviews for family-friendly features and a user-friendly infotainment system. However, some common complaints include less-than-stellar fuel economy and occasional issues with certain components. All 2016 Sedonas came equipped with a 3.3-liter V6 engine producing 276 horsepower and 248 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**3.3L V6**

*   **Engine Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks are reported from various locations, including the valve cover gaskets and the oil pan. Symptoms include visible oil drips under the vehicle, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking gaskets or seals. For oil pan leaks, resealing or replacing the oil pan may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

* **Knocking Noise**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reports of unusual knocking noises coming from the engine bay of the vehicle.
    * Solution: Depending on the issue, replacing the engine could be the only fix.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing harsh or erratic shifting between gears, as well as slipping or hesitation during acceleration. This can be more noticeable in lower gears.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a transmission fluid flush and filter change can alleviate the symptoms. However, more severe cases may require valve body replacement, transmission rebuild, or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sliding Door Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The power sliding doors may fail to open or close properly, or may exhibit intermittent functionality. This can be due to a faulty motor, sensor, or wiring issue.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair the wiring harness. Replace the sliding door motor or sensor if necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Dead Battery**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Battery dies after a short amount of time, vehicle struggles to turn over to start
    *   Solution: Replacing the battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rear Liftgate Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulties opening or closing the rear liftgate. It might get stuck, make unusual noises, or not respond to the button or key fob.
    *   Solution: Adjusting the liftgate or replacing the actuators.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Navigation/Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The navigation or infotainment system may freeze, crash, or become unresponsive. This can affect access to radio, navigation, and other features.
    *   Solution: A software update may resolve the issue. In some cases, replacing the head unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2016 Kia Sedona has some reported issues, but generally receives good reviews. The issues that owners report vary in severity and cost. Engine oil leaks and transmission problems can be costly to repair, while electrical and infotainment issues may be less severe. Buyers should be aware of these potential problems and perform a thorough inspection before purchasing a used 2016 Sedona.
