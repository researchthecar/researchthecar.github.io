**Introduction:**

The 2003 Kia Sedona is a popular minivan known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and value for money. It is offered with two engine and transmission options: a 3.5L V6 engine paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission and a 2.9L V6 engine paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**
- **3.5L V6 Engine**
   - **Oil Consumption:** Excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
   Description/Symptoms: Blue smoke from the exhaust, low oil level on the dipstick, rattling or knocking noises from the engine.
   Solution: Engine rebuild or replacement.
   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
   Recalls: N/A

- **2.9L V6 Engine**
   - **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**
- **5-speed Automatic Transmission**
   - **Slipping Gears:** Inability to engage gears smoothly, resulting in delayed or rough shifting.
   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, hesitation or jerking during shifts.
   Solution: Transmission rebuild or replacement.
   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
   Recalls: N/A

- **4-speed Automatic Transmission**
   - **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**
- **Strut Assembly Failure:** Premature failure of the strut assemblies, leading to excessive bounce or swaying while driving.
Description/Symptoms: Reduced handling stability, uneven tire wear, clunking or rattling noises over bumps.
Solution: Strut assembly replacement.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000 per axle
Recalls: N/A

**Electrical**
- **Alternator Failure:** Premature failure of the alternator, leading to battery draining and electrical system malfunctions.
Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, difficulty starting the engine, warning lights on the dashboard.
Solution: Alternator replacement.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $350
Recalls: N/A

**Exterior**
- **Rusty Exhaust System:** Corrosion of the exhaust system components, leading to holes or reduced performance.
Description/Symptoms: Rust spots on the exhaust system, rattling or clanging noises from the exhaust.
Solution: Exhaust system replacement.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
Recalls: N/A

**Interior**
- **Panel Gaps and Rattles:** Loose panels and rattling noises in the interior, reducing overall quality.
Description/Symptoms: Gaps between interior panels, rattling or vibrating noises from the dashboard or door panels.
Solution: Adjustment or replacement of interior panels.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250
Recalls: N/A

**Summary:**

The 2003 Kia Sedona has some known issues, particularly with the 3.5L V6 engine and the 5-speed automatic transmission. These issues can be costly to repair, ranging from $100 to $5,000+. However, it's important to note that not all Sedonas will experience these problems. The severity and cost of the issues should be taken into consideration when making a purchase decision.
