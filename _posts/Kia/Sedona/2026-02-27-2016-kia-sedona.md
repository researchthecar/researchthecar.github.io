**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**

### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Premature battery failure or dead battery issues, potentially linked to parasitic draw from certain modules.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the source of the drain, which may involve checking modules, wiring, or aftermarket accessories.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Kia Sedona demonstrates a generally solid reliability record, particularly concerning its powertrain (3.3L V6 engine and 6-speed automatic transmission) and core chassis components. Most owners report satisfaction with the vehicle's durability in these areas. However, some electrical gremlins have been noted, primarily related to the infotainment system experiencing freezing or unresponsiveness, and occasional intermittent battery drain issues that require diagnosis. These electrical concerns, while not affecting all vehicles, are the most common technical complaints. Exterior and interior components appear to hold up well with no widespread issues reported. Overall, the 2016 Sedona is a reliable choice for a minivan, with the caveat that potential buyers should be aware of and prepared for possible electrical system quirks.
