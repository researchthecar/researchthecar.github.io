**Introduction**

The 2012 Kia Sedona is a minivan that offers versatility, space, and a comfortable driving experience. It comes standard with a 3.5-liter V6 engine and a six-speed automatic transmission. A 2.7-liter V6 engine and a five-speed automatic transmission are also available. The Sedona is praised for its spacious interior, user-friendly infotainment system, and smooth ride. However, it has received criticism for its uninspiring handling and lackluster fuel economy.

**Engine**

**3.5-liter V6 Engine**

**Oil Leak from Valve Cover Gasket**
Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from the valve cover gasket, causing a burning smell and reduced oil levels.
Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $350

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

**Shifting Delay**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission may delay when shifting gears, causing hesitation and rough shifting.
Solution: Reprogram the transmission control module (TCM) or replace the transmission.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800

**Suspension**

**Front Suspension Bushings**
Description/Symptoms: Worn front suspension bushings can cause excessive noise and vibration when driving over bumps or rough roads.
Solution: Replace the worn bushings.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300

**Electrical**

**Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain quickly, leaving the vehicle unable to start.
Solution: Check for faulty components, such as the alternator, starter, or battery, and replace as necessary.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $600+

**Exterior**

**Tail Light Assembly**
Description/Symptoms: Moisture or condensation may accumulate inside the tail light assembly, causing it to malfunction or fail.
Solution: Replace the tail light assembly.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300

**Interior**

**Dashboard Lights Intermittent**
Description/Symptoms: The dashboard lights may intermittently dim or malfunction, affecting the visibility of important information.
Solution: Replace the faulty dashboard light bulbs or repair the electrical connection.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200

**Summary**

While the 2012 Kia Sedona offers a spacious interior and comfortable driving experience, it has been plagued by a number of issues, including oil leaks, transmission problems, and electrical malfunctions. The severity of these issues can vary, with some being relatively minor and others potentially more costly. It is important for potential buyers to be aware of these issues before making a purchase and to factor in the potential cost of repairs.
