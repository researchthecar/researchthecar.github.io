The 2014 Kia Sedona is a minivan offering a spacious interior, comfortable ride, and a variety of features for families. It was only available with one engine and transmission option: a 3.3-liter Lambda II V6 engine producing 276 horsepower and 248 lb-ft of torque paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the Sedona's ample cargo space, comfortable seating, and smooth ride. However, some common complaints include issues with the vehicle's electrical components, specifically the door locks and the TPMS sensors.

**Engine**

**3.3L Lambda II V6**

*   **Engine Stalling**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The engine stalls unexpectedly while driving, especially at lower speeds or when idling. This can be intermittent and may or may not be accompanied by a check engine light.
    *   Solution: The specific cause can vary, but possible solutions include cleaning the throttle body, replacing the mass airflow sensor (MAF), or addressing issues with the fuel system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks are found around the valve cover gaskets or the oil pan.
    *   Solution: Replacing the valve cover gaskets and/or resealing the oil pan.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Experiencing harsh or jerky shifts between gears, especially when accelerating or decelerating.
    *   Solution: Replacing the transmission fluid, or replacing solenoids.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature wear and tear on the rear shock absorbers can lead to a bouncy ride, reduced handling stability, and unusual noises from the rear suspension.
    *   Solution: Replacing the worn rear shock absorbers with new ones.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Power Door Lock Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The power door locks may malfunction, failing to lock or unlock consistently, or randomly locking and unlocking.
    *   Solution: Replacing the affected door lock actuator(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+ per door
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **TPMS Sensor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The Tire Pressure Monitoring System (TPMS) sensors may fail prematurely, causing the TPMS light to illuminate even when tire pressures are correct.
    *   Solution: Replacing the faulty TPMS sensor(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sliding Door Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The power sliding doors may experience problems opening or closing, getting stuck, or making unusual noises during operation.
    *   Solution: Replacing the door motor, or a faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sticking Dashboard Buttons**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rubberized coating on the dashboard buttons degrades and gets sticky.
    * Solution: Clean the buttons or replace the affected component.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $250+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2014 Kia Sedona can be a practical choice for families, but potential buyers should be aware of some common issues. Electrical problems with door locks and TPMS sensors are fairly common and can be relatively inexpensive to address. Engine and transmission issues, while potentially more serious, are less frequently reported but could lead to more significant repair costs. Overall, a thorough inspection by a qualified mechanic is recommended before purchasing a used 2014 Sedona, particularly focusing on the operation of the sliding doors, electrical components, and the engine and transmission.
