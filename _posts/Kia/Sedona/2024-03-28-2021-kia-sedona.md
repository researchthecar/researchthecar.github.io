The 2021 Kia Sedona is a minivan that seats up to eight passengers. It is available with a 3.3-liter V6 engine that produces 276 horsepower and 248 lb-ft of torque, or a 3.5-liter V6 engine that produces 290 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission.

The Sedona has received positive reviews for its spacious interior, comfortable seats, and user-friendly technology. However, it has also been criticized for its mediocre fuel economy and lack of advanced safety features.

**Electrical**

  - **Alternator Failure**: The alternator may fail, causing the battery to drain and the vehicle to stall. The alternator must be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Interior**

  - **Dashboard Rattle**: The dashboard may rattle due to a loose component. The loose component must be identified and secured. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
  - **Climate Control Issues**: The climate control system may malfunction, causing the temperature to be incorrect. The climate control module may need to be replaced. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Summary**

The 2021 Kia Sedona is a generally reliable vehicle with a few minor issues. The most significant issues are the electrical problems, which can be expensive to fix. Overall, the Sedona is a good choice for families who need a spacious and comfortable minivan.
