**Introduction**

The 2021 Kia Forte is a compact sedan available in a variety of trim levels and powertrain options. It boasts a spacious cabin, comfortable ride, and a plethora of standard features. The Forte comes with a choice of either a 2.0-liter inline-four engine producing 147 horsepower and 132 lb-ft of torque or a 1.6-liter turbocharged inline-four engine pumping out 201 horsepower and 195 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a six-speed manual, six-speed automatic, and a continuously variable transmission (CVT). While the Forte has received positive reviews from both critics and owners, there have been some reported issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Inline-Four**

* **Intake Valve Deposits**
    * Description/Symptoms: Carbon buildup on the intake valves can cause engine knocking, reduced power, and decreased fuel efficiency.
    * Solution: Intake valve cleaning typically involves removing the intake manifold and manually cleaning the valves.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: A faulty timing chain tensioner can lead to excessive chain slack, causing the timing to be misaligned and the engine to run poorly.
    * Solution: Replacing the timing chain tensioner and timing chain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**1.6-liter Turbocharged Inline-Four**

* **Turbocharger Wastegate Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: A malfunctioning turbocharger wastegate can result in excessive boost pressure, potentially damaging the engine.
    * Solution: Replacing the turbocharger wastegate assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Fuel Injector Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Faulty fuel injectors can cause misfiring, rough idling, and reduced fuel economy.
    * Solution: Replacing the failed fuel injectors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per injector
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual**

* **Shifting Difficulties**
    * Description/Symptoms: Issues with the manual transmission can make it difficult to shift gears smoothly, causing grinding noises and potential damage to the transmission.
    * Solution: Replacing or repairing damaged components within the transmission, such as the clutch, synchronizers, or shift forks.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Six-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip out of gear while driving, causing a loss of power and potential safety hazards.
    * Solution: Rebuilding or replacing the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

* **CVT Shudder or Jerking**
    * Description/Symptoms: The CVT may exhibit shuddering or jerking sensations during acceleration or deceleration.
    * Solution: Reprogramming the transmission control unit (TCU) or replacing the CVT fluid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Bearing Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged strut bearings can lead to clunking or rattling noises when driving over bumps or uneven road surfaces.
    * Solution: Replacing the strut bearings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per strut
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain prematurely, causing difficulty starting the vehicle or other electrical system malfunctions.
    * Solution: Identifying and fixing the underlying cause of the battery drain, such as faulty wiring or parasitic draw.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture can accumulate inside the headlight assemblies, causing foggy or cloudy headlights.
    * Solution: Resealing the headlight assemblies or replacing them if necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per headlight
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment Screen Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen may become unresponsive, display glitches, or experience other issues.
    * Solution: Software updates, resetting the system, or replacing the infotainment unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Climate Control Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control system may not function properly, resulting in issues with heating or cooling.
    * Solution: Diagnosing and repairing the affected components within the climate control system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Kia Forte has received overall positive reviews, with owners praising its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and impressive feature set. However, it has not been without its issues. Some engine and transmission problems can be costly to repair, and several electrical and interior issues have been reported. While these issues do not appear to be widespread or inherently severe, they should be considered when evaluating the vehicle.
