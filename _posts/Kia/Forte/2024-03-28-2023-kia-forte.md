**Introduction:**

The 2023 Kia Forte is a compact sedan known for its sporty handling, spacious interior, and budget-friendly pricing. It offers two engine options: a 147-horsepower 2.0-liter four-cylinder and a 201-horsepower 1.6-liter turbocharged four-cylinder, both mated to a continuously variable transmission (CVT). While the Forte has received positive reviews for its performance and value proposition, it has also faced some issues reported by owners.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Four-Cylinder**

* **Knocking Noise:** Some owners have reported a knocking noise coming from the engine at higher RPMs.
* Description/Symptoms: A loud, rhythmic knocking sound that increases with engine speed.
* Solution: Replacing the engine oil pump.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* Recalls: None Found

**1.6-liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

* **Turbocharger Failure:** A few owners have experienced premature failure of the turbocharger.
* Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, strange noises coming from the engine, and excessive smoke.
* Solution: Replacing the turbocharger.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
* Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

* **Hesitation and Jerking:** Some owners have noticed hesitation or jerking during acceleration or shifting gears.
* Description/Symptoms: The car may hesitate or jerk when shifting between gears or when accelerating from a stop.
* Solution: Updating the transmission software or replacing the CVT fluid.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Noise:** A number of owners have reported a clunking or knocking noise coming from the rear suspension when driving over uneven surfaces.
* Description/Symptoms: A loud, intermittent noise from the rear of the vehicle.
* Solution: Replacing the rear sway bar bushings or end links.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500
* Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Some owners have experienced rapid battery drain, even when the vehicle is turned off.
* Description/Symptoms: The car may fail to start or experience electrical problems due to a drained battery.
* Solution: Replacing the battery or testing for electrical leaks.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
* Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Failure:** A few owners have had issues with the exterior door handles breaking or becoming loose.
* Description/Symptoms: The door handles may become stiff, loose, or fail to open the door.
* Solution: Replacing the door handles.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattle:** Some owners have reported a rattling noise coming from the dashboard.
* Description/Symptoms: A rattling or buzzing sound from the upper portion of the dashboard.
* Solution: Tightening loose screws or replacing the dashboard panel.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500
* Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

While the 2023 Kia Forte is a generally reliable vehicle, it has faced some issues, especially within the engine, transmission, and suspension components. The severity and cost of repairs vary depending on the issue, but some problems, such as turbocharger failure, can be costly. It is important for owners to be aware of these potential issues and to address them promptly to prevent further damage and expenses.
