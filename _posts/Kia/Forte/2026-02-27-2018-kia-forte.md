# 2018 Kia Forte Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Kia Forte offers budget-conscious compact car buyers an appealing blend of modern features, decent fuel economy, and a straightforward driving experience. However, used car shoppers should review potential mechanical weak points, particularly concerning engine performance and unexpected stalling. While many owners enjoy trouble-free commuting, checking service records and verifying proper maintenance history is essential before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine (MPI)

The base 2.0-liter multi-point injection 4-cylinder engine produces approximately 147 horsepower and is designed for daily efficiency. It provides adequate power for city driving but requires attentive maintenance to avoid performance dips.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine stalling when slowing down, coming to a full stop, or making turns, often accompanied by a loss of acceleration power and warning lights.
- **Solution:** Have a certified mechanic inspect the fuel system, ignition components, and engine sensors, and check for any related diagnostic trouble codes.

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine (GDI)

The available 2.0-liter gasoline direct-injection 4-cylinder engine pushes around 164 horsepower for a slightly more spirited response in higher trims. It offers a good balance of pep and fuel economy for highway commuting.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The standard 6-speed manual transmission gives drivers direct control and generally exhibits solid mechanical durability when the clutch is maintained properly.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The conventional 6-speed automatic transmission pairs well with the base engine for smooth daily cruising, though occasional shifting hesitation has been noted by some owners.

**Powertrain Hesitation and Shifting Irregularities**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may hesitate, struggle to pick up speed smoothly, or exhibit rough shifting behavior at certain speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition and update powertrain control modules if necessary.

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The intelligent variable transmission (IVT/CVT) maximizes fuel efficiency across various trims, providing seamless acceleration without traditional gear steps.

## Suspension

### Front Control Arms

The suspension system delivers a comfortable, compliant ride for a compact sedan, but buyers in rust-prone regions should thoroughly inspect undercarriage components.

**Severe Control Arm Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Front lower control arms may experience heavy rust and premature degradation, risking structural detachment.
- **Solution:** Inspect the undercarriage for rust damage and replace compromised control arms immediately.

## Exterior

### Trunk and Door Latches

Interior locking mechanisms and latches generally hold up, though occasional trunk latch malfunctions have been reported.

**Erroneous Trunk Opening or Latch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The trunk door may unlatch unexpectedly while parked or fail to close and latch securely after use.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the trunk latch assembly or electronic release switch.

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical accessories function reliably overall, though minor annoyances like frequent bulb replacements can occur.

**Frequent Bulb Burnout**
- **Symptoms:** Running lights, headlights, or rear turn signal bulbs fail repeatedly within short operating intervals.
- **Solution:** Check bulb sockets for corrosion and replace burnt-out bulbs with quality components.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V031000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Forte&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Circuit damage may result in deactivated air bags that will not deploy in a crash. Air bags that do not deploy as intended can increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the ACU, and either update the software or replace the unit, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V622000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Trunk Lid:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Forte&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The trunk latch may become damaged, preventing the opening of the trunk from the inside. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 401, "Internal Trunk Release." A person inside the trunk compartment may become trapped, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the trunk latch assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V260000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication:Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Forte&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Foreign particles may stick inside the oil pump, which may cause an oil pump failure and engine damage. A damaged engine may stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Kia will notify owners, and dealers will replace the oil pan, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Kia Forte is a sensible choice for budget-minded used car buyers, offering comfortable daily driving and good feature availability. Prospective owners should carefully test drive the vehicle to check for any engine hesitation or stalling tendencies, inspect the undercarriage for rust, and ensure all electrical features and latches operate correctly. A pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic is strongly recommended.
