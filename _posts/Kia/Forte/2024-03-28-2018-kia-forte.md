**2018 Kia Forte**

The 2018 Kia Forte is a compact sedan available with two engine and transmission options:

* 2.0L Inline-4 (147 hp, 132 lb-ft torque), paired with a 6-speed manual or a continuously variable transmission (CVT)
* 1.6L Turbocharged Inline-4 (201 hp, 195 lb-ft torque), paired with a 7-speed dual-clutch transmission (DCT)

Owners praise the Forte for its stylish design, spacious interior, and responsive handling. However, some have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, and other components.

**Engine**

**2.0L Inline-4**

* **Piston Ring Failure (High-Mileage Vehicles):**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, blue smoke from exhaust, reduced engine power
    * Solution: Engine rebuild with new piston rings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**1.6L Turbocharged Inline-4**

* **Intake Valve Carbon Buildup:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hesitation or stumbling during acceleration, reduced fuel economy
    * Solution: Intake valve cleaning service
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Wastegate Actuator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced turbocharger boost, check engine light
    * Solution: Replace wastegate actuator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

* **Shifter Linkage Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises
    * Solution: Replace shifter linkage bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**CVT**

* **Belt and Pulley Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slipping, shuddering, or grinding during acceleration
    * Solution: Replace CVT belt and pulleys
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**7-Speed DCT**

* **Transmission Control Module Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, check engine light
    * Solution: Update transmission control module software
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $200 (if under warranty)
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 19V218000

**Suspension**

* **Rear Shock Absorber Leakage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Bouncy or unstable ride, particularly over bumps
    * Solution: Replace rear shock absorbers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Control Arm Bushing Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vibration or clunking noises when turning
    * Solution: Replace control arm bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, electrical system failure
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery goes dead after extended periods of inactivity
    * Solution: Diagnose and resolve electrical component causing drain
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the cause
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Door handle breaks or becomes difficult to operate
    * Solution: Replace door handle
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Center Console Armrest Squeak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or rattling noise from center console armrest
    * Solution: Apply lubricant or replace armrest
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2018 Kia Forte has some potential reliability concerns, particularly with its engine and transmission components. However, most issues are relatively minor and can be resolved for a reasonable cost. The Forte remains a generally reliable and affordable compact sedan, although owners should be aware of these potential issues before purchasing.
