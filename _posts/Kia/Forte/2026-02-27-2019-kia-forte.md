# 2019 Kia Forte Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 Kia Forte, prospective buyers will find a compact sedan that generally offers solid daily practicality and modern features. However, owner reports and service history highlight certain areas that require careful inspection before purchase. While drivetrain and electrical quirks surface in a portion of owner feedback, being aware of these common trouble spots can help you navigate your test drive and pre-purchase inspection effectively.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces around 147 horsepower and 132 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard powerplant for most models. It balances fuel efficiency with modest daily performance, though buyers should verify regular maintenance intervals during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected engine stalling while operating the vehicle, alongside hesitation or sudden loss of motive power during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic perform a diagnostic scan of the ignition, fuel, and electrical systems to identify faulty sensors or wiring harnesses.

## Transmission

### Intelligent Variable Transmission (IVT)

Paired with the base engine, the Intelligent Variable Transmission (IVT) is designed to optimize fuel economy through continuous gear ratios. While generally adequate for commuting, buyers should closely evaluate smooth engagement and responsiveness during a test drive.

**Transmission Sluggishness and Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note transmission slipping, hesitation, getting stuck in gear, or sluggish acceleration, particularly in colder weather conditions.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition, check for updated control module software, or consult a transmission specialist for internal component wear.

## Suspension

### Suspension and Alignment

Suspension tuning delivers a balanced ride for daily commuting, but maintaining proper wheel alignment and tire health is essential for straight-line stability.

**Vehicle Pulling and Highway Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may drift or pull persistently to one side, or exhibit noticeable shaking and vibration at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Perform a professional wheel alignment, check tire wear patterns, and inspect suspension bushings and wheel balancing.

## Steering

### Steering Column

The electric power steering system provides light effort for parking and stable feel at highway speeds, though mechanical wear in the column components can develop.

**Grinding Noises and Steering Play**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may hear loud grinding or clunking noises originating from the steering column during low-speed maneuvers or turns.
- **Solution:** Inspect the clockspring and intermediate steering shaft; replacement of worn internal components or the entire column may be required.

## Exterior

### Wiring Harness and Lighting

Electrical architecture supports cabin electronics and exterior lighting, though intermittent wiring issues have been noted by a subset of owners.

**Headlamp Wiring and Electrical Shortages**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners have reported smoke, premature bulb burnout, or electrical shorts originating from headlamp assemblies and main wiring harnesses.
- **Solution:** Examine headlight connectors, wiring harnesses, and fuses for signs of thermal damage, melting, or corrosion.

## Safety

### Forward Collision-Avoidance System

Driver assistance technologies add a layer of modern safety, but electrical sensitivities in sensors and wiring harnesses can sometimes trigger false alerts.

**Unexpected Warning Lights and Activation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience unexpected activation of collision warning systems or sudden illumination of electrical warning lights without any actual road hazard present.
- **Solution:** Inspect sensor alignment, clean camera and radar surfaces, and check associated wiring harnesses for loose connections or chafing.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V771000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Forte&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The headlights may not have been manufactured with the correct low beam aiming. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." If the headlights are not properly aimed, the driver may have reduced visibility, increasing the risk.
- **What to do:** Kia will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the headlight aiming and replace the headlights, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V459000: Power Train:Axle Assembly:Axle Shaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Forte&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The left front axle driveshaft may not have been heat-treated, which can cause it to break. A broken driveshaft can result in a sudden loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Kia will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the left front axle driveshaft for a heat treatment verification code, and replace it if the code is missing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Kia Forte is an appealing compact option featuring contemporary styling and good fuel economy, but a thorough pre-purchase inspection is strongly recommended. Pay close attention to powertrain responsiveness, electrical accessories, and steering feel during your test drive to ensure the vehicle has been well-maintained by previous owners.
