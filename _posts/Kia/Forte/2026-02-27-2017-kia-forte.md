# 2017 Kia Forte Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Kia Forte offers a practical and budget-friendly compact car option, but prospective buyers should review recurring owner complaints. While many drivers enjoy its fuel efficiency and straightforward features, specific mechanical and electrical concerns have been reported to safety authorities.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder MPI Engine

A 2.0-liter multi-point injection four-cylinder engine producing around 147 horsepower, paired with standard drivetrains. It provides adequate daily commuting performance, though some owners have noted drivability and stalling concerns.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report that the engine may suddenly stall while driving or lose motive power unexpectedly, often accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the underlying fuel delivery or ignition fault and replace failing components such as ignition coils or fuel pump assemblies.

**Engine Misfire**
- **Symptoms:** Rough running, shaking, and illuminated check engine lights due to ignition misfires.
- **Solution:** Inspect spark plugs and ignition coils, replacing faulty units as needed.

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder GDI Engine

A direct-injection 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine generating approximately 164 horsepower found in higher trims. It offers livelier acceleration while maintaining competitive fuel economy.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

A traditional 6-speed manual transmission offering direct gear selection and generally reliable operation for drivers who prefer a three-pedal setup.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A conventional 6-speed automatic transmission paired with the base engine option, delivering smooth and predictable shifts during daily driving.

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission (DCT)

A 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission offered on sportier trims for quicker gear changes, though buyers should test-drive thoroughly to ensure smooth low-speed operation.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brakes and HECU

Brake system feedback indicates occasional hydraulic unit malfunctions triggering warning lights.

**ABS and Hydraulic Control Unit Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** ABS warning lights illuminate on the dashboard, pointing toward hydraulic electronic control unit (HECU) failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect the braking system and replace faulty HECU modules or sensors.

## Steering

### Electronic Power Steering

Steering complaints center around occasional electronic assist drops and alignment tendencies.

**Power Steering Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Temporary loss of electronic power steering assistance, making the steering wheel significantly heavier to turn.
- **Solution:** Inspect steering control modules, wiring connections, and steering column components.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Issues relating to exterior bulbs, headlight switches, and taillight moisture intrusion frequently appear in owner reports.

**Headlight and Taillight Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights, brake lights, or taillights intermittently fail or burn out prematurely, sometimes due to moisture condensation in the housings.
- **Solution:** Replace burnt bulbs, damaged sockets, or sealed light assemblies as necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V031000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Forte&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Circuit damage may result in deactivated air bags that will not deploy in a crash. Air bags that do not deploy as intended can increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the ACU, and either update the software or replace the unit, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V622000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Trunk Lid:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Forte&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The trunk latch may become damaged, preventing the opening of the trunk from the inside. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 401, "Internal Trunk Release." A person inside the trunk compartment may become trapped, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the trunk latch assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V260000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Oil/Lubrication:Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Forte&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Foreign particles may stick inside the oil pump, which may cause an oil pump failure and engine damage. A damaged engine may stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Kia will notify owners, and dealers will replace the oil pan, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2017 Kia Forte, a pre-purchase inspection is strongly recommended to check for electrical glitches, lighting housing condition, and engine drivability. While many owners experience trouble-free commuting, verifying that all recall and maintenance work has been completed will help ensure a safer and more reliable ownership experience.
