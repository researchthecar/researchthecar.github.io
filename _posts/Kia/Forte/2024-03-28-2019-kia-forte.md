**2019 Kia Forte Introduction**

The 2019 Kia Forte is a compact sedan available with three engine and transmission options:

* **1.8L GDI Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT):** 147 horsepower, 132 lb-ft torque
* **2.0L GDI 6-speed automatic transmission:** 147 horsepower, 132 lb-ft torque
* **1.6L Turbo GDI 7-speed dual-clutch transmission:** 201 horsepower, 195 lb-ft torque

Praised for its stylish design, fuel efficiency, and value, the Forte also has some reported issues.

**Engine**

**1.8L GDI CVT**

* **Engine Stalling:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine unexpectedly stalls while driving or idling.
    * Solution: Software update or replacement of throttle body.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
    * Recalls: NA
* **Intake Manifold Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Visible cracks in the intake manifold, causing rough idling and reduced engine performance.
    * Solution: Replacement of intake manifold.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0L GDI 6-Speed Automatic**

* **No Issues Found**

**1.6L Turbo GDI 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission**

* **Transmission Fluid Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leak from the transmission housing, leading to low transmission fluid levels and performance issues.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of transmission housing gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Transmission Shudder:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vibrations or shuddering during gear changes.
    * Solution: Software update or replacement of transmission clutches.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Noise:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Squeaks or clunks from the rear suspension, especially over rough roads.
    * Solution: Replacement of rear suspension bushings or struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rapid battery discharge, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of faulty electrical components, such as a parasitic draw.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Leaks:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaking through the sunroof during heavy rain.
    * Solution: Adjustment or replacement of sunroof seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Center Console Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks in the plastic center console.
    * Solution: Replacement of center console.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Cruise Control Lever Clicking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clicking noise when engaging or disengaging cruise control.
    * Solution: Replacement of cruise control lever.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

While the 2019 Kia Forte is generally a reliable vehicle, it has some known issues, particularly with the 1.8L GDI engine and 1.6L Turbo GDI transmission. These issues can range in severity from minor annoyances to potential safety concerns. The cost to fix these issues varies depending on the problem and the specific parts involved. Overall, the Forte remains a solid choice for those seeking a value-oriented sedan, but it's essential to be aware of potential issues before making a purchase.
