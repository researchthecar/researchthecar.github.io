# 2020 Kia Telluride Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Kia Telluride has won widespread praise for its comfortable ride, upscale cabin, and practical three-row layout. However, used-car buyers should review recurring owner complaints covering exterior paint defects, sunroof mechanical failures, and engine oil consumption.

---

## Engine

### 3.8L V6 Engine

Equipped with a 3.8-liter V6 engine producing approximately 291 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, providing strong power for daily driving. While generally capable, buyers should monitor potential oil consumption issues.

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle burns through engine oil rapidly, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels regularly and consult a dealer or mechanic for diagnostic testing.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Engine unexpectedly stalls while driving, accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes and inspect engine management sensors or fuel delivery systems.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Features an 8-speed automatic transmission paired with front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive, delivering smooth shifts under normal conditions.

**Transmission Slipping or Rough Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences jerking, hesitation, or slipping sensation during acceleration and gear changes.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition and update software modules if indicated.

## Exterior

### Exterior Paint and Structure

Paint adhesion defects on certain pearl and white color options can lead to unsightly peeling and exposed metal.

**Paint Peeling and Delamination**
- **Symptoms:** Factory paint, particularly on white and pearl finishes, flakes or peels off in chunks from the hood and roof.
- **Solution:** Repaint affected body panels; consult dealership regarding warranty coverage options.

### Sunroof Assembly

Exterior glass and trim components generally hold up well, though panoramic sunroof mechanisms have a history of track jamming and costly out-of-warranty failures.

**Sunroof Track Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sunroof jams, sticks open, or breaks its internal track and wind deflector mechanism during operation.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged track or sunroof assembly.
- **Cost:** $4,000 to $6,000

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical systems control advanced features and lighting, with some owners reporting high-beam headlight failures.

**Headlight Failure**
- **Symptoms:** High beams or low-beam headlights fail intermittently or stop working entirely.
- **Solution:** Inspect bulbs, fuses, and wiring harnesses or replace headlight assemblies as needed.

**[Recall 20V436000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Telluride&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Inoperative trailer brake lights can fail to warn other drivers that the vehicle is slowing, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Kia will notify owners, and dealers will install an additional wire harness to the main fuse box to signal the trailer brake lights, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V626000: Trailer Hitches](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Telluride&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Debris and moisture accumulation on the tow hitch harness module printed circuit board (PCB) may cause an electrical short, which can result in a fire. A fire while parked or driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a new fuse and a wiring harness extension. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V148000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Telluride&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Inoperable high beam headlights may reduce visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair or replace the high beam headlights, as necessary free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V214000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Telluride&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The intermediate shaft and right front driveshaft may not be fully engaged, which can result in vehicle roll away while in PARK. A vehicle roll away increases the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the electronic parking brake software and replace any damaged intermediate shafts as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V407000: Seats:Front Assembly:Power Adjust](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Telluride&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The front power seat motor may overheat due to a stuck power seat slide knob, which can result in a fire while parked or driving. A fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a bracket for the power seat switch back covers and replace the seat slide knobs, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V430000: Seats:Front Assembly:Power Adjust](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Telluride&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The front power seat motor may overheat due to a stuck power seat slide knob or an improper recall 24V407 repair, which can result in a fire while parked or driving. A fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install an electronic fuse assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

The vehicle offers strong family appeal, but used-car shoppers should thoroughly inspect the exterior paint, test all sunroof functions, and verify engine oil history before purchasing.
