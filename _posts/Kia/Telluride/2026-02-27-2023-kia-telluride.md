## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Occasional Glitches/Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment screen may freeze, become unresponsive, or display errors. Issues with Apple CarPlay/Android Auto connectivity.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system resets, or potential replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $100 - $1500 (depending on severity and warranty coverage)


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Premature battery discharge, requiring jump starts or battery replacement. May be linked to parasitic draw from certain electronic modules.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, repair of faulty modules, or battery replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2023 Kia Telluride demonstrates strong overall reliability, particularly in its core powertrain components (engine and transmission), which have shown no significant widespread issues. The chassis and suspension systems are also generally robust. The primary areas of concern are within the electrical system, specifically occasional infotainment glitches and intermittent battery drain issues. These are not uncommon in modern vehicles with complex electronics but are worth noting. Exterior and interior components have largely been free of reported widespread defects. With proactive attention to potential electrical quirks, the Telluride is a dependable choice for families seeking a comfortable and capable SUV.
