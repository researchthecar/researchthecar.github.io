# 2021 Kia Telluride Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Kia Telluride has won widespread praise for its upscale styling, comfortable three-row cabin, and strong feature list. As a used vehicle purchase, it remains a popular choice for families looking for space and value. However, prospective buyers should review common owner-reported trouble spots, particularly concerning sunroof assembly durability, exterior lighting issues, and occasional powertrain or electrical hiccups.

---

## Engine

### 3.8L V6 (Lambda II GDI)

Powered by a 3.8-liter V6 engine producing approximately 291 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth and capable performance for everyday driving. While generally robust, buyers should keep an eye out for minor fluid leaks and diagnostic warning lights reported by some owners.

**Electrical Warning Lights and Diagnostics**
- **Symptoms:** Illumination of warning lights on the dashboard accompanied by check-engine codes or drive-mode alerts.
- **Solution:** Scan diagnostic trouble codes at a qualified repair facility and address faulty sensors or software calibration updates.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected hesitation, reduced power delivery during acceleration, or excessive oil consumption warnings.
- **Solution:** Perform thorough diagnostic testing on fuel and ignition systems; monitor oil consumption levels regularly.

**Oil Filter Housing Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible fluid trails under the vehicle or burning smells associated with minor oil leaks near the filter housing.
- **Solution:** Inspect the oil filter cap and housing O-rings during routine oil changes; replace degraded housings or seals.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with a responsive 8-speed automatic transmission available in both front-wheel drive and all-wheel drive configurations. Most owners experience seamless operation, though occasional shift quality complaints warrant a thorough test drive before purchase.

**Shifting Hesitation and Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Rough shifting, hesitation, or shuddering when accelerating from a stop or changing gears.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition, update transmission control modules, or inspect related components like the MAP sensor.

## Suspension

### Rear Suspension and Shocks

The suspension setup delivers a comfortable, compliant ride suited for long road trips, though rear shock longevity and CV boot wear are areas to monitor.

**Rear Shock Instability and Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive bounciness over rough roads, thumping noises from the rear, or a generally unstable ride feel.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear shock absorbers and CV axle boots for wear or failure; replace worn suspension components as necessary.
- **Cost:** $777.61

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical systems power a wealth of modern conveniences, but exterior lighting failures have been a recurring point of frustration for a subset of drivers.

**Headlight Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent failure or complete loss of high beam or low beam headlight functionality.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight bulbs, wiring harnesses, and control modules; replace failed assemblies.

### Sunroof and Windshield

Exterior glass and sunroof components require careful inspection during a used vehicle evaluation, as sunroof track jams and fragile windshield glass represent notable complaint areas.

**Sunroof Mechanism and Deflector Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loud wind noise, broken deflector arms, or the sunroof getting stuck open and failing to close properly.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace broken wind deflector arms or the complete sunroof cassette assembly if structural tracks fail.
- **Cost:** $3,000 to $6,441

**Windshield Cracks**
- **Symptoms:** Spontaneous cracking or susceptibility to chips and fractures from minor road debris impacts.
- **Solution:** Inspect the windshield thoroughly before purchase and repair small chips immediately to prevent spreading.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V164000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Telluride&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The Occupant Classification and Detection System (OCS/ODS) unit may not detect the presence of a child restraint system in the front passenger seat. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." In the event of a crash, deployment of the front passenger.
- **What to do:** Kia will notify owners, and dealers will replace the OCS/ODS unit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V626000: Trailer Hitches](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Telluride&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Debris and moisture accumulation on the tow hitch harness module printed circuit board (PCB) may cause an electrical short, which can result in a fire. A fire while parked or driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a new fuse and a wiring harness extension. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V214000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Telluride&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The intermediate shaft and right front driveshaft may not be fully engaged, which can result in vehicle roll away while in PARK. A vehicle roll away increases the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the electronic parking brake software and replace any damaged intermediate shafts as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V407000: Seats:Front Assembly:Power Adjust](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Telluride&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The front power seat motor may overheat due to a stuck power seat slide knob, which can result in a fire while parked or driving. A fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a bracket for the power seat switch back covers and replace the seat slide knobs, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V430000: Seats:Front Assembly:Power Adjust](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Telluride&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The front power seat motor may overheat due to a stuck power seat slide knob or an improper recall 24V407 repair, which can result in a fire while parked or driving. A fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install an electronic fuse assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The vehicle offers strong utility, comfort, and performance, making it a compelling option in the midsize SUV segment. Buyers should perform a meticulous pre-purchase inspection focusing on the sunroof operation, rear suspension health, electrical accessories, and front glass condition to avoid unexpected out-of-pocket repair expenses.
