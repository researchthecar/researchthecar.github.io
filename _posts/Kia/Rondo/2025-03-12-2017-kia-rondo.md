Here's a comprehensive breakdown of common issues and complaints reported for the 2017 Kia Rondo, categorized by major components and specific configurations where applicable. This information is designed to be accurate and consistent for your website.

The 2017 Kia Rondo, also known as the Kia Carens in some markets, is a compact MPV (Multi-Purpose Vehicle) offering a practical blend of passenger and cargo space. The 2017 Kia Rondo was only available with a 2.0L Inline-4 engine, producing around 164 horsepower and 156 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. It's praised for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and value, but some common complaints include the smaller engine lacking power when fully loaded, and less than stellar fuel economy compared to competitors.

**Engine**

**2.0L Inline-4 (G4NA)**

* **Engine Knocking/Ticking Noise**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported a knocking or ticking noise emanating from the engine, particularly during cold starts or under load. This may be related to the oil control valve.
    * Solution: Diagnosis by a qualified mechanic is essential. Could involve replacing the OCV.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (depending on diagnosis and parts needed)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported needing to add oil more frequently than expected between oil changes. Some have even reported oil consumption rates exceeding manufacturer specifications.
    * Solution: Oil consumption test and potentially engine rebuild/replacement, if deemed excessive by manufacturer standards.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (oil consumption test) to $3,000+ (engine repair/replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Catalytic Converter Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Malfunction Indicator Light (MIL) illuminating, often accompanied by a P0420 code (Catalyst System Efficiency Below Threshold). Can lead to reduced engine performance and failed emissions tests.
    * Solution: Diagnosis to confirm catalytic converter failure and replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Jerking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners experience harsh or jerky shifting between gears, particularly during acceleration or deceleration.
    *   Solution: Check transmission fluid level and condition. A fluid flush may resolve the issue, but in more severe cases, valve body replacement or transmission rebuild/replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (fluid flush) to $3,000+ (transmission rebuild/replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip, causing the engine to rev higher than normal without a corresponding increase in speed.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis to determine cause of slipping. Could be low fluid, worn clutches, or valve body issues.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (fluid flush) to $3,000+ (transmission rebuild/replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Shock Absorber Leaks/Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature failure of rear shock absorbers leading to bouncy ride, especially over bumps. Visible leakage of fluid from the shock absorber.
    * Solution: Replacement of rear shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 (pair)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Noisy Suspension**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises coming from the suspension when driving over uneven surfaces.
    * Solution: Inspect and replace worn sway bar links, bushings, or ball joints.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on components needing replacement
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery draining overnight or within a few days of being parked, making it difficult to start the vehicle.
    * Solution: Diagnose and address parasitic drain, often caused by aftermarket accessories, faulty relays, or a failing alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (depending on source of drain)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Malfunctioning Door Locks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Door locks failing to lock or unlock properly, either manually or with the remote.
    * Solution: Check and replace faulty door lock actuators.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300 per door
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Infotainment System Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Freezing, Crashing, or Unresponsiveness of the infotainment system.
    * Solution: Software update, infotainment system reset, or replacement of the unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) to $800+ (replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rear Wiper Motor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rear windshield wiper failing to operate or operating intermittently.
    * Solution: Replacement of the rear wiper motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Driver's Seat Height Adjustment Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Driver's seat height adjustment lever or motor failing, preventing the seat from being raised or lowered.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the seat height adjustment mechanism or motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Sunroof Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sunroof failing to open or close smoothly, getting stuck, or leaking.
    * Solution: Lubrication, track adjustment, or replacement of the sunroof motor or components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 (lubrication) to $500+ (motor/component replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Kia Rondo, while a practical and versatile vehicle, exhibits several common issues. Engine problems like knocking noises, oil consumption, and catalytic converter failures can be costly to address. Transmission issues like harsh shifting and slipping also present potential repair expenses. Electrical glitches and suspension problems contribute to the overall cost of ownership. While some issues are relatively minor, others, such as engine and transmission repairs, can be significant. Careful inspection and proactive maintenance are crucial for mitigating these potential problems.
