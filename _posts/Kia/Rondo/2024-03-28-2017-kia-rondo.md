**Introduction:**

The 2017 Kia Rondo is a compact MPV that offers a spacious cabin and a comfortable ride. It is available with three engine options: a 2.0-liter inline-four, a 2.4-liter inline-four, and a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four. The 2.0-liter inline-four produces 150 horsepower and 141 lb-ft of torque, while the 2.4-liter inline-four produces 173 horsepower and 166 lb-ft of torque. The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four produces 261 horsepower and 269 lb-ft of torque. The Rondo can be equipped with either a six-speed manual transmission or a six-speed automatic transmission.

Owners of the 2017 Kia Rondo generally appreciate its spaciousness, comfortable ride, and affordability. However, there have been some complaints about its lack of power, poor fuel economy, and outdated design.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Inline-Four**

* **No Issues Found**

**2.4-liter Inline-Four**

* **No Issues Found**

**2.0-liter Turbocharged Inline-Four**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption**
Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported that their Rondo consumes excessive amounts of oil, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage. Symptoms include blue smoke from the exhaust, reduced engine power, and illuminated check engine light.
Solution: The solution involves replacing the piston rings and/or valve stem seals.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
Recalls: N/A

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **No Issues Found**

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Slipping**
Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported that their Rondo's transmission slips or hesitates when shifting gears. Symptoms include rough shifting, delayed engagement, and loss of power.
Solution: The solution involves replacing the transmission's torque converter, clutches, or other internal components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
Recalls: N/A

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Starter Motor Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported that their Rondo's starter motor fails to engage, preventing the engine from starting. Symptoms include a clicking noise when attempting to start the vehicle and no engine cranking.
Solution: The solution involves replacing the starter motor.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $250-$500+
Recalls: N/A

* **Battery Draining**
Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported that their Rondo's battery drains prematurely, even when the vehicle is turned off. Symptoms include a dead battery, difficulty starting the vehicle, and electrical malfunctions.
Solution: The solution involves identifying and repairing the source of the electrical draw, which could be a faulty component or parasitic drain.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$500+
Recalls: N/A

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Lock Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported that their Rondo's tailgate lock fails to open or close properly. Symptoms include a stuck tailgate, difficulty accessing the rear cargo area, and potential security risks.
Solution: The solution involves replacing the tailgate lock actuator or other related components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$400+
Recalls: N/A

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunctions**
Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported that their Rondo's climate control system malfunctions, leading to incorrect temperatures or lack of air flow. Symptoms include fluctuating temperatures, reduced air flow, and potential discomfort.
Solution: The solution involves diagnosing and repairing the faulty component, which could be a sensor, actuator, or other related part.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $150-$500+
Recalls: N/A

* **Power Window Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported that their Rondo's power windows fail to operate properly. Symptoms include windows that do not open or close, or operate erratically.
Solution: The solution involves replacing the window motor, switch, or other related components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$600+
Recalls: N/A

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2017 Kia Rondo has received mixed reviews regarding its reliability. While it does not appear to have any major or widespread issues, it has been subject to several recalls and has experienced some problems with excessive oil consumption, transmission slipping, starter motor failure, and battery draining. These issues can be costly to repair, with some estimates exceeding $2,000. As with any used vehicle, it is important to have it inspected by a qualified mechanic before purchasing.
