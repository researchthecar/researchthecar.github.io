# 2019 Kia K900 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Shopping for a used 2019 Kia K900 offers buyers a chance to experience a luxurious, high-end flagship sedan at a fraction of its original price. As with any luxury vehicle loaded with advanced electronics and complex powertrains, prospective owners should carefully inspect mechanical and electrical systems before purchasing. Reviewing owner reports and maintaining a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help uncover hidden issues.

---

## Engine

### 3.3L Twin-Turbocharged V6

This 3.3-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine produces approximately 365 horsepower and 376 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth and robust acceleration. It serves as the primary powerplant for the K900, balancing luxury performance with everyday drivability.

### 5.0L V8

The larger 5.0-liter naturally aspirated V8 engine delivers around 420 horsepower and 376 lb-ft of torque for effortless cruising power. While less common, this traditional powerhouse offers refined performance suited for a flagship sedan.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission comes paired with the vehicle's powertrain options to deliver seamless, responsive gear shifts. It contributes to the quiet and composed highway driving experience expected in a luxury sedan.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V169000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Gasoline:Turbo/Supercharger:Hoses/Plumbing](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=K900&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An oil leak in the presence of hot engine components can increase the risk of an engine compartment fire while driving.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the left turbocharger oil feed pipe and hose assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Kia K900 provides an impressive blend of comfort, technology, and performance for used luxury car buyers. While major mechanical failures are not widely reported in initial owner feedback, keeping a close eye on powertrain responsiveness and electronics during a test drive is wise. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection remains the best tool to ensure the vehicle has been well-maintained.
