# 2017 Kia K900 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Kia K900 represents a compelling value in the full-size luxury sedan segment, offering high-end features and strong performance at a fraction of the cost of its European competitors. While owner complaints in public databases are quite sparse, used-car buyers should thoroughly inspect vehicle systems and maintenance histories to ensure optimal reliability.

---

## Engine

### 3.8L V6

Equipped with a 3.8-liter V6 engine producing around 311 horsepower, this base option delivers smooth, adequate power for daily driving. It generally offers a dependable ownership experience with standard routine maintenance.

### 5.0L V8

Powered by a robust 5.0-liter V8 engine generating approximately 420 horsepower, this option provides brisk acceleration and refined cruising dynamics. It is mechanically solid, though buyers should watch for proper service records and fluid changes.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with both available engines, this 8-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly to the rear wheels. It is known for seamless gear shifts and generally strong mechanical durability under normal driving conditions.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brake System

Owner reports indicate rare instances of sudden brake stiffness, requiring careful diagnostic evaluation during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Hard Brake Pedal and Unexpected Lunge**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal becomes exceptionally stiff ("rock hard"), and the vehicle may lunge forward unexpectedly when attempting to stop.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the brake booster, vacuum lines, and hydraulic components immediately to identify the failure point.

## Exterior

### Door Panels

Body and trim components should be checked visually for structural panel integrity during a walkaround inspection.

**Door Panel Rippling and Separation**
- **Symptoms:** Passenger door panels show visible rippling and structural separation from the interior intrusion bars.
- **Solution:** Inspect door panels for physical defects and consult a dealership or body shop regarding potential trim replacement or repair.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V051000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=K900&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The Hydraulic Electronic Control Unit (HECU) module could malfunction and cause an electrical short, which could result in an engine compartment fire. An engine compartment fire while parked or driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a new fuse for the HECU circuit board, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V652000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=K900&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The Hydraulic Electronic Control Unit (HECU) may experience an electrical short, which can result in an engine compartment fire while parked or driving. An engine compartment fire while parked or driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the HECU fuse, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Kia K900 is a rare luxury cruiser with a very low volume of reported owner complaints, suggesting generally stable foundational reliability. Prospective buyers should prioritize a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection focusing on the braking system and exterior trim to ensure everything functions as intended.
