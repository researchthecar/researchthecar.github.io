The 2019 Kia Sorento is a mid-size SUV that offers a comfortable ride and a spacious interior. Available engine options include a 2.4L inline-4 (185 hp, 178 lb-ft of torque) and a 3.3L V6 (290 hp, 252 lb-ft of torque). Both engines are typically paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the Sorento's value, ease of use, and available features. Common complaints include transmission hesitation and some electrical glitches.

**Engine**

**2.4L Inline-4**

*   **Engine Stalling**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The engine may stall while driving or idling, particularly at low speeds or when coming to a stop.
    *   Solution: This can be due to faulty fuel injectors that can cause stalling.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.3L V6**

*   **Engine Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks may develop around the valve cover gaskets or the front timing cover. This could lead to oil consumption, visible oil spots under the vehicle, and a burning oil smell.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking gaskets or seals. A more extensive repair may be needed if the leak is from the front timing cover.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $1000+, depending on the source and severity of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (Both Engines)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported harsh or erratic shifting, particularly when accelerating or decelerating. Some also report hesitation when shifting between gears.
    *   Solution: A software update or transmission control module (TCM) recalibration may fix the issue. In some cases, replacement of the valve body may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$150 (Software Update), $700-$1500+ (Valve Body Replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Suspension Noise**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported a clunking or squeaking noise coming from the rear suspension, especially when going over bumps.
    *   Solution: This may be due to worn sway bar bushings or end links. Replacing these components usually resolves the issue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain quickly, especially if the vehicle is not driven for extended periods. This can result in difficulty starting the engine.
    *   Solution: Check for parasitic draws on the electrical system. This could be due to a faulty module, aftermarket accessory, or a failing battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (Diagnostic) - $500+ (Depending on the cause)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced issues with the infotainment system, such as freezing, glitches, or failure to connect to Bluetooth devices.
    *   Solution: A software update may resolve the issue. In some cases, the infotainment unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Software Update) - $500+ (Infotainment Unit Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Condensation may build up inside the headlight housings, reducing visibility.
    *   Solution: Removing and drying the headlight assembly can temporarily fix this. Replacing the headlight housing can be a long term solution
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0-$300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Automatic Door Locks Malfunctioning**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Automatic door locks will randomly lock or unlock the vehicle without anyone interacting with the system.
    *   Solution: Replacing the automatic door lock actuator may solve the issue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $350+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Kia Sorento presents a mix of minor and moderate issues. Engine oil leaks on the V6 and transmission issues can be costly to repair, while electrical glitches and suspension noises are generally less severe. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these common problems and inspect the vehicle thoroughly or seek a pre-purchase inspection to assess its condition.
