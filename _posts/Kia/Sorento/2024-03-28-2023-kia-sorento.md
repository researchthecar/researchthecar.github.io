**Introduction:**

The 2023 Kia Sorento is a midsize SUV that offers versatility, comfort, and advanced technology. It is available with two engine and transmission options: a 2.5-liter 4-cylinder engine paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission and a 2.5-liter turbocharged 4-cylinder engine paired with an 8-speed dual-clutch transmission. The Sorento is praised for its spacious cabin, intuitive infotainment system, and excellent safety features. However, some owners have reported certain issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**2.5-liter 4-cylinder Engine with 8-speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Engine Stalling:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The engine suddenly stalls while driving.
    * Solution: Software update to reprogram the engine control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (covered under warranty)
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Coolant Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks from the engine, causing overheating and engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty coolant hose or gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**2.5-liter 4-cylinder Engine with 8-speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Slipping:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission slips gears, causing rough shifts and difficulty accelerating.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty transmission valve body or torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Transmission Noise:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Abnormal noises, such as grinding or whining, coming from the transmission.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty transmission bearings or gears.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.5-liter Turbocharged 4-cylinder Engine with 8-speed Dual-Clutch Transmission**

* **Clutch Pack Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission clutch pack prematurely fails, causing shuddering or slipping during gear shifts.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty clutch pack.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 22V-931

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Noise:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises coming from the front suspension.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty sway bar end links or bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electrical Harness Issues:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Various electrical components, such as the headlights or infotainment system, malfunction due to faulty electrical harnesses.
    * Solution: Inspect and repair the damaged wiring harness.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery drains prematurely, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty battery or repair the parasitic draw on the electrical system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Latch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The tailgate latch unexpectedly fails to engage, causing the tailgate to open while driving.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty tailgate latch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Blind Spot Monitor Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The blind spot monitoring system fails to alert the driver of vehicles in adjacent lanes.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty blind spot monitoring sensor or module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 22V-845
* **Climate Control Issues:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control system fails to regulate temperature effectively.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty temperature control module or blend door actuator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2023 Kia Sorento has received generally positive reviews, but some owners have reported issues with the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior components. The severity and cost of the issues vary depending on the specific problem. While some issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more significant and require costly repairs. Potential buyers should be aware of these reported issues and consider them when making a purchasing decision.
