# 2020 Kia Sorento Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Kia Sorento is a versatile midsize SUV offering comfortable three-row seating and a user-friendly cabin. However, potential buyers should review owner complaints regarding various mechanical and electrical systems. While many owners enjoy its features and ride quality, keeping a close eye on maintenance history and known trouble spots can help ensure a more reliable ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.4-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces approximately 185 horsepower, offering adequate efficiency for daily commuting but modest acceleration when the vehicle is fully loaded.

### 3.3L V6 Engine

The 3.3-liter V6 engine generates around 290 horsepower, providing robust performance and a higher towing capacity, though it requires attentive monitoring for oil levels and potential drivability concerns.

**Engine Misfire and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported sudden engine misfires, a noticeable lag when pressing the gas pedal, and significant internal engine damage or oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Have a certified mechanic inspect spark plugs, fuel delivery, and cylinder compression, and address worn components promptly.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may unexpectedly stall or lose motive power while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by illuminated warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose underlying electrical or fuel system faults and ensure all relevant updates or repairs are performed.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission pairs with the base four-cylinder engine to deliver smooth, predictable gear changes suited for everyday driving.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission is paired with the V6 engine, offering responsive gear selection to optimize fuel economy and power delivery.

**Erratic Shifting and Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced rough or erratic shifting, particularly when the transmission is cold, resulting in hesitation or skipping when accelerating from a stop.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition and consult a dealer to determine if software updates or hardware replacements are necessary.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Braking performance is adequate under normal conditions, but reported incidents of pedal pulsation and locking highlight the importance of thorough brake inspections.

**Brake Locking and Pulsation Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Brakes may exhibit unexpected locking at low speeds, unusual metal-on-metal rubbing noises, or poor anti-lock brake responsiveness during hard stops.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, calipers, and ABS sensors for wear, corrosion, or hydraulic malfunctions.

## Steering

### Steering Column and Assembly

Steering feedback is generally light and manageable, but mechanical noises and vibrations require timely professional diagnosis.

**Steering Noises and Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may hear abnormal squeaking or loud clunking noises from the steering wheel area, sometimes accompanied by steering vibrations.
- **Solution:** Have a technician inspect the steering shaft, column joints, and power steering components for wear or misalignment.

## Exterior

### Lighting and Warning Systems

electrical issues involving exterior lighting and dashboard warnings have been noted by some owners, requiring careful inspection of wiring harnesses and switches.

**Headlight and Electrical Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights, turn signals, and dashboard systems may temporarily or completely fail without warning while driving at night.
- **Solution:** Inspect lighting modules, fuses, and body control units for faults or loose connections.

### Glass and Sunroof

Exterior body panels are generally sturdy, though isolated reports of spontaneous glass breakage warrant a careful check of all windows and the sunroof.

**Spontaneous Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The sunroof or side windows may suddenly shatter or explode while driving at highway speeds without any apparent external impact.
- **Solution:** Replace damaged glass panels and inspect frames for structural stress or manufacturing defects.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety equipment generally performs well, but malfunctioning seat belt latches pose a critical safety risk that should be verified before purchase.

**Seat Belt Unlatching**
- **Symptoms:** Front seat belts have been reported to randomly unlatch or dislodge during vehicle operation.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt buckles and latch assemblies for mechanical failure and replace faulty components.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V703000: Trailer Hitches](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Sorento&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Debris and moisture accumulation on the tow hitch harness module printed circuit board (PCB) may cause an electrical short, which can result in a fire. A fire while parked or driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the vehicle, and, as necessary, replace the fuse and/or tow hitch harness module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Kia Sorento is a comfortable and feature-packed family SUV, but prospective buyers should approach with a degree of caution. Reviewing service records for transmission behavior, engine oil consumption, and electrical stability is strongly recommended. Prioritizing a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection can help catch potential issues before finalizing your purchase.
