**Introduction:**
The 2017 Kia Sorento is a midsize SUV known for its spacious interior, versatility, and value for money. It comes with multiple engine and transmission options, including a 2.4L inline-four, a 3.3L V6, and a 2.0L turbocharged inline-four. Transmission options include a 6-speed automatic and an 8-speed automatic. While the Sorento has received praise for its comfort and technology features, some owners have reported issues with various components.

**Engine**

**2.4L Inline-Four**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Oil consumption exceeding 1 quart per 1,000 miles, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
* **Engine Knock:** Knocking or rattling sounds during acceleration, caused by worn or damaged engine components.
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** Malfunction of the timing chain tensioner, resulting in noisy operation or catastrophic engine failure.

**3.3L V6**

* **Exhaust Manifold Issues:** Cracking or warping of the exhaust manifold, causing exhaust leaks and potential engine performance issues.
* **Oil Filter Housing Leak:** Leaking from the oil filter housing, resulting in oil loss and contamination.

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-Four**

* **Turbocharger Failure:** Premature failure of the turbocharger, leading to a loss of power and increased emissions.

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Shifting Problems:** Delayed or hard shifting, slipping gears, or a lack of responsiveness.
* **Transmission Fluid Leakage:** Leaking from the transmission housing, causing low fluid levels and potential transmission damage.

**8-Speed Automatic**

* **Hesitation and Shuddering:** Hesitation or shuddering during acceleration or gear changes, caused by issues with the transmission control module or internal components.

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Noise:** Squeaking or rattling noises from the rear suspension, particularly over bumps or rough roads.
* **Front Strut Failure:** Premature failure of the front struts, leading to poor handling and a rough ride.

**Electrical**

* **Battery Discharge:** Rapid battery discharge, causing the vehicle to fail to start after a period of time.
* **Electrical System Malfunctions:** Intermittent electrical issues, such as flickering lights, power windows malfunctioning, or radio problems.

**Exterior**

* **Roof Rack Leaking:** Water leaking into the vehicle's interior through the roof rack mounting points.
* **Rear Bumper Cover Damage:** Damage to the rear bumper cover caused by improper installation or manufacturing defects.

**Interior**

* **Seat Belt Buckle Failure:** Malfunctioning seat belt buckles, leading to difficulties fastening or unfastening seat belts.
* **Climate Control Issues:** Problems with the climate control system, including insufficient cooling or heating, fan noise, or malfunctioning buttons.

**Summary:**
The 2017 Kia Sorento offers a plethora of features and value, but some owners have experienced issues with various components. While the severity and cost of these issues can vary, potential buyers should be aware of the reported problems before purchasing. Precautionary measures, such as regular maintenance and inspections, can help minimize the risk of encountering these issues and ensure the longevity of the vehicle.
