**Introduction**

The 2022 Kia Sorento is a midsize SUV available with a choice of three engine and two transmission options. The 2.5L naturally aspirated inline-4 engine produces 191 horsepower and 182 lb-ft of torque, while the 2.5L turbocharged inline-4 engine generates 281 horsepower and 311 lb-ft of torque. The 1.6L turbocharged hybrid inline-4 engine paired with an electric motor delivers a combined output of 227 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. The base transmission is an 8-speed automatic, with a 7-speed dual-clutch automatic available for the 2.5L turbocharged engine. The Sorento has received praise for its spacious cabin, comfortable ride, and impressive fuel economy. However, some owners have reported encountering various issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**2.5L Naturally Aspirated Inline-4 Engine**

* **No Issues Found**

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-4 Engine**

* **Engine Stalling at Low Speeds**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine shuts off while driving at low speeds, typically when braking or coming to a stop.
    * Solution: Software update to adjust engine management system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Free (under warranty)
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 22V817
* **Turbocharger Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, loss of acceleration, and illuminated check engine light.
    * Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Fuel Injector Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, and difficulty starting the engine.
    * Solution: Replacement of the fuel injectors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**1.6L Turbocharged Hybrid Inline-4 Engine**

* **Hybrid System Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced fuel efficiency, loss of power, and illuminated hybrid system warning light.
    * Solution: Software update to address the issue.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Free (under warranty)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Shift Hesitations and Jerks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Delayed or rough gear changes, especially during acceleration or deceleration.
    * Solution: Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+

**7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission**

* **Clutch Wear and Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slipping or shuddering clutch, difficulty shifting gears, and illuminated transmission warning light.
    * Solution: Replacement of the clutch assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
* **Transmission Control Module Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, loss of gears, and illuminated check engine light.
    * Solution: Replacement of the transmission control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Suspension**

* **Strut Bearing Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling or clunking noises when turning the steering wheel or driving over bumps.
    * Solution: Replacement of the strut bearings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rapid battery discharge when the vehicle is parked.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the underlying electrical fault.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the cause

**Exterior**

* **Front Bumper Sagging**
    * Description/Symptoms: Front bumper appears loose or misaligned.
    * Solution: Realignment or replacement of the bumper mounting brackets.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Leakage**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water entering the cabin through the sunroof.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the sunroof weatherstripping.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Infotainment System Freeze**
    * Description/Symptoms: Infotainment system becomes unresponsive and requires a restart.
    * Solution: Software update or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Summary**

Overall, the 2022 Kia Sorento has received positive feedback from owners. However, some issues have been reported, with severity ranging from minor to potentially costly. Common issues include engine stalling, turbocharger malfunctions, and transmission problems. Some electrical and interior issues have also been encountered. While some of these issues can be resolved through warranty repairs, others may require significant expenses.
