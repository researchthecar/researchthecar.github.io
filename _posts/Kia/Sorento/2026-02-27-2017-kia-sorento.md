# 2017 Kia Sorento

### Overview
The 2017 Kia Sorento is a mid-size SUV known for its practicality, available seating for up to seven passengers, and a comfortable ride. It was offered with a choice of three distinct powertrains: a 2.4-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder, a more potent 3.3-liter V6, and a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder, all paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Reliability for the 2017 model year is generally considered good, with most owners reporting satisfaction. However, like any vehicle, some specific components and systems have seen more attention than others. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and prospective buyers.

## Engine

### 2.4L GDI (Theta II)
**Engine Knock/Rattling**
- **Symptoms:** Audible knocking or rattling noise from the engine, particularly during acceleration or at idle. May be accompanied by a check engine light.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential engine internal inspection, possible short block replacement under warranty if applicable.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $4,000+

### 2.0L Turbo GDI (Theta II)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable decrease in oil level between changes, potentially leading to low oil warnings or engine damage if not monitored.
- **Solution:** Regular oil level checks, investigation into potential leaks or internal consumption issues.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500 (for monitoring and top-ups)


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (A6GF1/A6LF2/A6LF3)
**Hesitation/Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission may hesitate to shift gears, or exhibit jerky movements during shifts, especially at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Software update for transmission control module, or internal transmission inspection and repair.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,200

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen unresponsiveness, freezing, or random reboots of the infotainment system.
- **Solution:** Software update, or potential replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1,000

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Premature battery discharge, requiring frequent jump starts or battery replacement.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw from electrical components, or replacement of faulty modules.
- **Cost:** $150 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Kia Sorento generally presents a reliable profile, particularly for models equipped with the 3.3L V6 engine. The most frequently cited technical concerns revolve around potential engine issues with the 2.4L GDI engine, specifically engine knock, and some instances of oil consumption with the 2.0L Turbo. Transmission issues, such as hesitation or jerking, are also noted but often addressable with software updates. Electrical gremlins, primarily related to the infotainment system and occasional battery drain, are present but not overwhelmingly common. Overall, the Sorento offers a solid ownership experience, with most potential issues being manageable or covered under warranty if still applicable. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to any unusual noises or warning lights are recommended.
