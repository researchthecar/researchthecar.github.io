# 2017 Kia Sorento Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Kia Sorento is a popular midsize SUV offering a comfortable ride, spacious cabin, and up to three rows of seating. However, used-car buyers should approach this model year with care, as owner complaints and technical feedback point to several recurring mechanical and electrical concerns. Paying close attention to the powertrain and known service history can help you spot potential trouble spots before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine

A naturally aspirated 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine producing around 185 horsepower, serving as the base powerplant for lower trims. Buyers should verify regular oil maintenance records, as this engine family can be prone to oil consumption and related issues.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine stalling, activation of warning lights, and sudden loss of motive power while driving at various speeds.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic testing by a qualified mechanic to inspect fuel delivery, sensors, or internal engine components.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

A turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine rated at approximately 240 horsepower, offering stronger low-end acceleration for upper-trim models. It requires diligent maintenance and premium fuel considerations for optimal longevity.

### 3.3L V6 Engine

A smooth 3.3-liter V6 engine generating roughly 290 horsepower, providing robust performance and a higher towing capacity. While generally capable, prospective buyers should listen closely for abnormal valvetrain or bearing noises during a cold start.

**Unusual Engine Knock and Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note sudden loud knocking noises from the engine bay, accompanied by low oil pressure warnings and visible oil leaks.
- **Solution:** Inspection by a certified technician is necessary; severe internal wear may require extensive engine repair or replacement.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A conventional 6-speed automatic transmission paired with select engine configurations, known for generally smooth shifting behavior. Routine fluid changes help maintain long-term durability and prevent premature wear.

## Steering

### Steering Column and Gear

Provides electronic power steering assist tailored for daily driving comfort. A subset of owners report unnatural resistance or sticking during extended highway trips.

**Sticky or Notchy Steering**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a steering wheel that feels sticky, stiff, or notchy when attempting to return to center or navigate gentle highway curves.
- **Solution:** Have a mechanic inspect the steering shaft, electric power steering motor, and alignment components for binding or wear.

## Exterior

### Headlights and Exterior Lighting

Manages vehicle lighting, accessory power, and various electronic modules throughout the cabin. Exterior lighting glitches are among the more frequent owner complaints for this model year.

**Headlight Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights intermittently shut off or fail to operate properly on certain switch settings, creating hazardous night-driving conditions.
- **Solution:** Inspect the headlight switch assembly, wiring harnesses, and related relays for faults, replacing defective components as needed.

### Exterior Paint and Finish

Covers exterior sheet metal and factory paint finishes. Certain color options have shown susceptibility to adhesion failure over time.

**Premature Paint Peeling**
- **Symptoms:** The exterior paint begins to bubble, flake, or peel away from body panels such as the hood and roof.
- **Solution:** Repainting affected body panels by a professional body shop is typically required to restore the finish.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V907000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Sorento&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Kia has notified owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the fuel pipe, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V586000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Sorento&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the crankshaft fails, the engine will stall, increasing the risk of a crash. A failed crankshaft also increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Kia will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the crankshaft, and, as necessary, replace the engine sub assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V862000: Trailer Hitches](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Sorento&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, " Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." If the trailer brake lights stay illuminated while being used, other drivers may be confused, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Kia will notify owners, and dealers will replace the trailer tow hitch harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V703000: Trailer Hitches](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Sorento&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Debris and moisture accumulation on the tow hitch harness module printed circuit board (PCB) may cause an electrical short, which can result in a fire. A fire while parked or driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the vehicle, and, as necessary, replace the fuse and/or tow hitch harness module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Kia Sorento offers a compelling blend of utility, comfort, and feature availability, but prospective buyers should perform a thorough inspection. Pay special attention to engine health, electrical accessories like headlights, and steering feel during a test drive. Verifying a complete service history can significantly reduce the risk of inheriting unexpected repair bills.
