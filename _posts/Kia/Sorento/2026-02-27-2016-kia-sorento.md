# 2016 Kia Sorento

### Overview
The 2016 Kia Sorento, a popular mid-size SUV, generally offers a solid ownership experience with a focus on practicality and value. Across its various engine and transmission configurations, reliability is largely consistent, though some specific components have seen more attention than others. Owners can expect a dependable vehicle for daily commuting and family duties. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for prospective and current owners.

---


## Engine

### 2.4L GDI (Theta II)
**Engine Knock/Rattling**
- **Symptoms:** Audible knocking or rattling noise from the engine, particularly at idle or under acceleration. May be accompanied by a check engine light.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement of connecting rod bearings. This has been a subject of recalls and technical service bulletins for certain Theta II engines.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,500

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen display may freeze, become unresponsive, or reboot intermittently.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery may drain prematurely, leading to starting issues. Can be caused by parasitic draw from various modules.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the source of the parasitic draw and repair or replace the faulty component.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Kia Sorento presents a generally reliable profile, with most systems performing as expected. The primary areas of concern revolve around potential engine issues, specifically with the Theta II engine's tendency for knocking, which has been addressed through recalls and service bulletins. Transmission issues, while not widespread, can manifest as hesitation or jerking, often rectifiable with software updates. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment screen unresponsiveness and intermittent battery drain, are also noted but typically manageable. Overall, the Sorento is a practical choice, but prospective buyers should be aware of the engine-related bulletins and ensure any pre-owned model has had relevant checks.
