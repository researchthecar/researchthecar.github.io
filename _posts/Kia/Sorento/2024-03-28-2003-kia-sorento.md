**Introduction:**

The 2003 Kia Sorento is a mid-size SUV that was praised for its comfort, spacious interior, and optional V6 engine. It was offered with a 2.4L inline-4 engine or a 3.5L V6 engine, paired with either a 5-speed manual transmission, a 4-speed automatic transmission, or a 5-speed automatic transmission. However, like any vehicle, the 2003 Sorento has experienced its share of issues over the years.

**Engine:**

**2.4L Inline-4 Engine**

* **Head Gasket Failure:** Symptoms include overheating, coolant leaks, and white smoke from the exhaust. Solution: Replacing the head gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+.

**3.5L V6 Engine**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:** Symptoms include a vacuum leak, which can cause rough idle, poor acceleration, and increased fuel consumption. Solution: Replacing the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000.

**Transmission:**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Synchronizer Wear:** Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises, and a loose shifter. Solution: Rebuilding or replacing the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+.

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Torque Converter Failure:** Symptoms include slipping gears, shuddering, and delayed engagement. Solution: Replacing the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+.

**5-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Valve Body Malfunction:** Symptoms include delayed shifting, harsh shifts, and no engagement. Solution: Replacing the valve body. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+.

**Suspension:**

* **Front Strut Failure:** Symptoms include a clunking noise, poor handling, and uneven tire wear. Solution: Replacing the front struts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+ per axle.

* **Rear Shock Absorber Failure:** Symptoms include bouncing, a rough ride, and poor handling. Solution: Replacing the rear shock absorbers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per axle.

**Electrical:**

* **Alternator Failure:** Symptoms include difficulty starting, flickering lights, and a battery that won't hold a charge. Solution: Replacing the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+.

* **Starter Failure:** Symptoms include a clicking noise when starting the engine or complete failure to start. Solution: Replacing the starter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+.

**Exterior:**

* **Door Handle Breakage:** Symptoms include difficulty opening doors or handles that come off in the hand. Solution: Replacing the door handles. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ per door.

**Interior:**

* **Dashboard Cracking:** Symptoms include cracks appearing on the dashboard. Solution: Replacing the dashboard. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+.

* **Power Window Failure:** Symptoms include inoperative power windows or windows that move slowly. Solution: Replacing the window motor or switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per window.

**Summary:**

The 2003 Kia Sorento has experienced several issues, including engine gasket failures, transmission problems, and suspension failures. Some of these issues are severe and can be costly to repair. However, it's important to note that not all Sorentos will experience these problems, and the severity and cost of repairs can vary depending on the specific issue.
