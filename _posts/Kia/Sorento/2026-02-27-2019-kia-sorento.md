# 2019 Kia Sorento Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Kia Sorento is a versatile midsize SUV offering three rows of seating and an array of features, making it a popular choice for families. While it provides a comfortable ride and intuitive technology, used car buyers should approach with a degree of caution. Certain powertrain configurations and exterior elements have accumulated notable complaints from owners, highlighting areas that require careful pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder

The base 2.4-liter 4-cylinder engine produces around 185 horsepower and serves as the entry-level powerplant for lower trims, though it requires close monitoring for oil consumption issues.

**Excessive Oil Consumption and Engine Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report high oil consumption requiring frequent top-offs, followed by excessive noise, shuddering, and eventual complete engine failure.
- **Solution:** May require engine replacement or extensive internal repairs if catastrophic failure occurs.

### 3.3L V6

The 3.3-liter V6 engine pushes out roughly 290 horsepower, providing robust towing capacity and brisk acceleration for higher trims, but owners have flagged occasional stalling and hesitation.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loud knocking noises from the engine accompanied by illuminated warning lights and a total loss of acceleration.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnostic evaluation by a certified technician; repairs may involve fuel system or internal engine component replacement.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with the base 4-cylinder engine, this 6-speed automatic provides reliable shifting under normal commuting conditions.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Matched with the V6 engine, the 8-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth highway cruising performance, though some owners note occasional hesitation during initial throttle tip-in.

**Acceleration Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle stumbles or hesitates for a second or two when the driver attempts to accelerate, creating potential safety concerns in traffic.
- **Solution:** Software updates or reprogramming by a dealer may improve shift logic; persistent mechanical hesitation may require component service.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System

The interior electronics manage vehicle entertainment and driver aids, though certain infotainment bugs and backup camera glitches have been documented by owners.

**Infotainment Reboot and Screen Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen display or backup camera intermittently freezes, displays a black or blinking screen, or spontaneously reboots while driving.
- **Solution:** Installing available software updates or replacing faulty head unit hardware can often resolve recurring display reboots.

## Exterior

### Body Structure and Paint

Exterior styling is modern and durable overall, but specific paint colors—notably Snow White Pearl—have exhibited widespread peeling and bubbling concerns.

**Paint Peeling and Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Paint begins bubbling and peeling away down to the bare metal, particularly on horizontal surfaces like the hood and roof.
- **Solution:** Requires professional sanding and repainting of affected body panels, which can be costly if outside warranty coverage.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V703000: Trailer Hitches](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Sorento&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Debris and moisture accumulation on the tow hitch harness module printed circuit board (PCB) may cause an electrical short, which can result in a fire. A fire while parked or driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the vehicle, and, as necessary, replace the fuse and/or tow hitch harness module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V639000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Sorento&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not display an image can reduce the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rearview camera and add additional water proofing to the camera harness, or replace the camera as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2019 Kia Sorento, a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection is highly recommended. Buyers should carefully verify service records, check for any signs of excessive oil consumption, test all infotainment and safety electronics, and thoroughly inspect the exterior paint condition. Choosing a model with a well-documented maintenance history can help mitigate potential ownership risks.
