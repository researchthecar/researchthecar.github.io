# 2019 Kia Sorento

### Overview
The 2019 Kia Sorento is a mid-size SUV known for its practicality, comfortable ride, and available three-row seating. It offers a choice of two gasoline engines and two transmission options, catering to different driving needs. Reliability for the 2019 model year is generally considered good, with most owners reporting few significant mechanical problems. Common areas of concern, while not widespread, tend to be related to minor electrical glitches or specific engine performance nuances, rather than systemic failures. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring the longevity of this versatile family vehicle.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment screen may occasionally freeze or become unresponsive, requiring a system reset.
- **Solution:** Software update or system reset. In rare cases, replacement of the unit may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $100 - $1500 (depending on repair)


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners have reported intermittent battery drain issues, leading to a dead battery. This can be caused by various modules not going to sleep properly.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the parasitic draw, potentially involving software updates or replacement of faulty modules.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Kia Sorento generally presents a solid reliability record. While most major mechanical components like the engine and transmission are robust, minor electrical issues, particularly with the infotainment system and occasional battery drain, have been noted by some owners. These are typically not deal-breakers and can often be resolved with software updates or targeted diagnostics. Overall, the Sorento is a dependable choice for families seeking a practical and comfortable SUV, provided routine maintenance is performed and potential minor electrical quirks are addressed promptly.
