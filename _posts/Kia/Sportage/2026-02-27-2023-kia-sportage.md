# 2023 Kia Sportage Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Kia Sportage brings a striking redesign, significantly larger cabin space, and modern technology to the compact SUV market. However, as a first-model-year vehicle following a major overhaul, it has drawn various owner complaints concerning its brakes, electrical systems, and powertrain components. Prospective buyers should carefully review these known issue areas during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

The standard 2.5-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine produces 187 horsepower and 178 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate everyday power for the compact SUV. It balances decent fuel efficiency with mainstream performance, though early-production examples have reported occasional stalling and hesitation concerns.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may unexpectedly stall or lose motive power while driving or when coming to a stop, often accompanied by dashboard warning lights or a complete loss of accelerator response.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic evaluation by a certified technician is required to identify faulty sensors, software glitches, or fuel delivery issues.

### 1.6L Turbocharged Hybrid/Plug-in Hybrid Powertrain

The available 1.6-liter turbocharged 4-cylinder hybrid and plug-in hybrid options pair with electric motors to deliver up to 227 or 261 combined horsepower. These electrified variants offer enhanced fuel economy and stronger low-end acceleration compared to the base non-hybrid engine.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The non-hybrid models utilize an 8-speed conventional automatic transmission designed to deliver smooth gear shifts and optimized highway efficiency. It pairs reasonably well with the 2.5L engine, with relatively few mechanical drivetrain complaints noted in early owner feedback.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides adequate stopping power under normal conditions, but multiple owner reports highlight premature wear on brake rotors requiring early service interventions.

**Premature Rotor Wear and Grinding**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience premature brake grinding, vibration, and excessive wear on brake rotors, sometimes starting under 15,000 miles.
- **Solution:** Inspection of brake pads and rotors, followed by replacement or resurfacing of affected components.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Tie Rods

The electronic power steering system provides light and responsive handling for daily driving, though early wear on front-end steering linkages has been noted in owner service records.

**Tie Rod and Steering Component Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Premature wear or failure of inner and outer tie rods, leading to loose steering feel or mechanical component failure at relatively low mileages.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of worn steering linkage components followed by a professional wheel alignment.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Instrument Cluster

The interior features dual widescreen digital displays on higher trims, which offer modern connectivity and navigation capabilities but have proven prone to software freezes and intermittent display blackouts.

**Screen Blanking and Infotainment Reboots**
- **Symptoms:** The digital instrument cluster or central infotainment screen goes completely blank, failing to display speed, odometer readings, or backup camera feeds.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of the infotainment head unit or cluster modules by a dealer.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Advanced safety features include multiple airbags and collision warning systems designed to protect occupants, though sensor faults and isolated deployment complaints have been documented by owners.

**Air Bag Warning and Non-Deployment Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Air bag warning lights illuminate on the dashboard, or front airbags fail to deploy during significant frontal collisions.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning of the Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) and replacement of faulty sensors or wiring harnesses.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V651000: Electrical System:Alternator/Generator/Regulator](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Sportage&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall increases the risk of a crash, and an electrical surge increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten the alternator battery positive terminal nut, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V878000: Air Bags:Side/Window:Curtain](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Sportage&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A twisted air bag can deploy improperly, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and reinstall the side curtain air bags as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V298000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Sportage&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 101, "Control and Displays," and 138, "Tire Pressure Monitoring Systems." A blank instrument cluster display will not show critical information, such as tire pressure, warning messages, and warning lights, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the instrument cluster software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V410000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Power Assist](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Sportage&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The brake booster diaphragm may become misaligned and cause an internal vacuum leak, which can result in a loss of power brake assist. A loss of power brake assist can extend the distance required to stop, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the brake booster as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V531000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Fluid/Lubricant:Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Sportage&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** An electric oil pump assembly that overheats increases the risk of a vehicle fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the electric oil pump controller, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V025000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Sportage&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A detached roof molding can create a road hazard for other vehicles, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the roof molding and replace or secure it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Kia Sportage is an appealing used compact SUV thanks to its bold styling, generous interior room, and modern tech features. However, as the debut year for a redesigned generation, it exhibits a higher-than-average volume of early owner complaints regarding brake rotor wear, electrical screen blackouts, and occasional powertrain stalling. Buyers should prioritize models with complete dealer service histories and conduct a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the brakes, electrical system, and steering components.
