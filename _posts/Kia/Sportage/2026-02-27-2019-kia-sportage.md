# 2019 Kia Sportage Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Kia Sportage offers a stylish exterior, comfortable ride, and user-friendly technology, making it an appealing compact SUV on the used market. However, prospective buyers should carefully review powertrain history and owner-reported complaints regarding engine performance and electrical systems before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder

The base 2.4-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine produces 181 horsepower and 175 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate everyday performance for daily commutes.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected engine stalling, hesitation when pressing the gas pedal, and temporary loss of motive power while driving.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic testing by a qualified technician is required; repairs may involve software updates, sensor replacements, or addressing internal engine wear.

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience abnormal internal oil loss, leading to low oil levels between scheduled oil changes.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels regularly and consult a dealership or mechanic for oil consumption testing and potential warranty remedies.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The available 2.0-liter turbocharged 4-cylinder engine generates 240 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, offering stronger acceleration and passing power for higher-trim models.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission pairs with both engine options and front- or all-wheel drive, delivering smooth gear changes under normal operating conditions.

**Powertrain Hesitation and Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners have noted hesitation, vibration, or a lack of responsiveness during acceleration within specific speed and RPM ranges.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition, check for updated powertrain control module software, and perform mechanical inspections if vibration persists.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical and lighting components generally function well, though headlight beam patterns and display systems require working order verification during a used car inspection.

**Headlight Beam Performance**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note that halogen headlights or high beams may project partial shadows or fail to illuminate the road adequately at night.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight alignment, bulbs, and housing assemblies to ensure optimal nighttime visibility.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V137000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Sportage&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The electrical circuit in the Hydraulic Electronic Control Unit (HECU) may short-circuit, which can cause a fire in the engine compartment. An engine compartment fire can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Kia will notify owners, and dealers will replace certain fuses in the electrical junction box. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V703000: Trailer Hitches](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Sportage&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Debris and moisture accumulation on the tow hitch harness module printed circuit board (PCB) may cause an electrical short, which can result in a fire. A fire while parked or driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the vehicle, and, as necessary, replace the fuse and/or tow hitch harness module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Kia Sportage is a comfortable and practical compact SUV, but used buyers should prioritize vehicles with documented service records and verified engine health. Thoroughly test driving the vehicle and checking for past warranty or recall completions will help mitigate potential ownership risks.
