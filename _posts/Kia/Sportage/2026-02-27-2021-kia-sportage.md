# 2021 Kia Sportage Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Kia Sportage offers a stylish compact crossover option with comfortable ride dynamics and a well-appointed interior. However, prospective buyers should review known owner complaints concerning unexpected engine stalls, electrical warnings, and infotainment screen delamination. While many drivers enjoy its everyday utility, checking service records and inspecting common failure points is recommended.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine

The standard 2.4-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces 181 horsepower and 175 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate daily acceleration. Owners report occasional drivability quirks, so verifying proper maintenance history is advised.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may unexpectedly lose motive power or stall while driving, sometimes accompanied by warning lights or oil leaks from detached drain plugs.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the engine management system, oil pan components, and electrical sensors for underlying faults.

**Battery Discharge Warning**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported intermittent battery discharge warning messages appearing on the dashboard display while parked or operating the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Test the battery and charging system health to ensure proper alternator output and parasitic draw limits.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

The available 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates 240 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, offering significantly punchier performance for higher trims. It generally delivers reliable operation when subjected to consistent oil change intervals.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The standard six-speed automatic transmission pairs with both engine options across front-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations. It is generally regarded as a durable unit with smooth gear transitions, though regular fluid maintenance is recommended.

## Electrical

### Infotainment Touchscreen

Electrical and cabin accessories generally function well, though specific components like the infotainment display warrant careful pre-purchase inspection.

**Screen Delamination**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen display develops bubbles or separation in the UV gel adhesive, leading to unresponsive touch controls or visual distortion.
- **Solution:** Replace the infotainment display unit if the delamination interferes with core vehicle controls.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V137000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Sportage&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The electrical circuit in the Hydraulic Electronic Control Unit (HECU) may short-circuit, which can cause a fire in the engine compartment. An engine compartment fire can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Kia will notify owners, and dealers will replace certain fuses in the electrical junction box. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V703000: Trailer Hitches](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Sportage&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Debris and moisture accumulation on the tow hitch harness module printed circuit board (PCB) may cause an electrical short, which can result in a fire. A fire while parked or driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the vehicle, and, as necessary, replace the fuse and/or tow hitch harness module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Kia Sportage is a comfortable and practical compact SUV, but buyers should approach with a discerning eye. Pay close attention to engine performance, verify that infotainment and electrical systems operate correctly, and ensure all relevant recall maintenance has been addressed before finalizing a purchase.
