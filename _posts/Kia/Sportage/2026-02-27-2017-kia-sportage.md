## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen freezing or unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, freezes, or restarts unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Software update, or replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500


### Battery Drain
**Premature battery failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery drains quickly, leading to starting issues. May be related to parasitic draw from certain modules.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, repair of faulty modules or wiring, or battery replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Kia Sportage generally demonstrates solid reliability, particularly with its naturally aspirated engines and standard automatic transmission. The primary areas of concern are the 7-speed dual-clutch transmission, which can exhibit shifting issues, and some electrical components, notably the infotainment system and potential battery drain problems. These issues are not universal but have been reported by a segment of owners. Most reported problems are repairable with software updates or component replacement, with costs varying. Overall, the Sportage is a dependable choice, but potential buyers should be aware of these specific technical points.
