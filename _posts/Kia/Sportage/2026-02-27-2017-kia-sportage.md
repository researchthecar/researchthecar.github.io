# 2017 Kia Sportage Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Kia Sportage offers a distinctive exterior, a well-appointed interior, and comfortable driving dynamics, making it a popular choice in the compact SUV segment. However, prospective buyers should review owner complaints carefully, particularly regarding powertrain performance and electrical accessories. While many owners enjoy daily driving, staying informed on reported mechanical patterns can help buyers spot potential issues during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.4-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces around 181 horsepower and provides adequate power for daily commutes. Buyers should monitor oil levels and watch for service warning indicators during test drives.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience sudden engine stalling while driving, entering limp mode, or losing motive power unexpectedly on highways or city streets.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis typically involves checking fault codes, updating software, or replacing faulty sensors such as knock sensors.

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers frequently notice low oil levels and may need to add oil between regular maintenance intervals, risking potential engine damage if neglected.
- **Solution:** Perform regular oil level checks and consult a mechanic for oil consumption testing and potential warranty or repair options.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

The optional 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates roughly 240 horsepower, offering stronger acceleration and responsive performance for highway passing. Maintenance history is vital when considering turbocharged options.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive across trims. While generally smooth when functioning correctly, owners have reported occasional slipping and shifting concerns.

**Transmission Slipping and Shifting Delays**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report the transmission slipping out of gear, becoming stuck in a single gear, or experiencing harsh and erratic upshifting.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition, check electrical sensor plates, or repair/replace faulty internal components.

## Exterior

### Power Windows and Door Latches

Electrical accessories such as window regulators and door latch mechanisms require careful testing during a used car inspection to ensure all switches and locks function smoothly.

**Power Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Windows fail to roll up or down properly, often accompanied by motor noise without actual glass movement.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed window regulator and motor assembly.

### Sunroof Glass

Exterior body structures and glass panels should be closely examined, paying particular attention to panoramic sunroof options for any hairline cracks or stress signs.

**Panaromic Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The panoramic sunroof glass may rupture or shatter spontaneously while driving at highway speeds without prior impact.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged glass panel and inspect the frame assembly for alignment and stress points.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V907000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Sportage&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Kia has notified owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the fuel pipe, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V137000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Sportage&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The electrical circuit in the Hydraulic Electronic Control Unit (HECU) may short-circuit, which can cause a fire in the engine compartment. An engine compartment fire can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Kia will notify owners, and dealers will replace certain fuses in the electrical junction box. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V862000: Trailer Hitches](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Sportage&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, " Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." If the trailer brake lights stay illuminated while being used, other drivers may be confused, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Kia will notify owners, and dealers will replace the trailer tow hitch harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V703000: Trailer Hitches](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Sportage&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Debris and moisture accumulation on the tow hitch harness module printed circuit board (PCB) may cause an electrical short, which can result in a fire. A fire while parked or driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the vehicle, and, as necessary, replace the fuse and/or tow hitch harness module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Kia Sportage is an appealing compact SUV with comfortable road manners and a practical cabin layout. However, used car buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on engine operation, oil consumption history, transmission shifting behavior, and electrical accessories like power windows. Verifying service records and confirming proper maintenance can significantly reduce the risk of unexpected repair bills.
