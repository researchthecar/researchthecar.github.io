**Introduction:**
The 2019 Kia Niro EV is an all-electric subcompact crossover SUV. It features a permanent magnet synchronous electric motor that produces 201 horsepower and 291 lb-ft of torque. The Niro EV has a range of up to 239 miles on a single charge. It is praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and advanced safety features. However, some owners have experienced issues with the vehicle's electrical system, suspension, and interior.

**Suspension**
**Front Suspension**
- **Strut Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: The front struts can fail, causing the vehicle to sag and bounce excessively. There may also be a clunking or rattling noise when driving over bumps.
  - Solution: Replace the failed struts.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $800+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical System**
- **Battery Issues**
  - Description/Symptoms: The battery can discharge quickly, especially in cold weather. This can lead to the vehicle losing power and becoming stranded.
  - Solution: Replace the battery.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Charging System Issues**
  - Description/Symptoms: The vehicle may not charge properly, or the charging process may be slow. This can be caused by a variety of issues, including a faulty charging cable, damaged charging port, or malfunctioning charging system.
  - Solution: Diagnose and repair the underlying issue.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
No Issues Found

**Interior**
- **Dashboard Squeaks and Rattles**
  - Description/Symptoms: The dashboard may squeak or rattle when driving over rough roads. This is often caused by loose screws or clips.
  - Solution: Tighten the loose screws or clips.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
  - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**
The 2019 Kia Niro EV is a generally reliable vehicle, but it has experienced some issues with its suspension, electrical system, and interior. The severity of these issues ranges from minor annoyances to more serious problems that could affect the safety and performance of the vehicle. The cost to fix these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the location of the repair. Overall, the Niro EV is a solid choice for those in search of an electric crossover SUV, but it is important to be aware of the potential issues that may arise.
