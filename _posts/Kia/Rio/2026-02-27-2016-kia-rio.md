# 2016 Kia Rio Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Kia Rio is a subcompact commuter car that offers decent fuel economy and straightforward packaging, making it an attractive option for budget-conscious buyers. However, used car shoppers should review potential mechanical and electrical concerns documented by owners, including engine drivability issues and lighting failures. Keeping a close eye on maintenance history and checking for completed safety recalls will help ensure a more reliable ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 1.6L GDI 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2016 Kia Rio is powered by a 1.6-liter Gamma GDI four-cylinder engine producing 138 horsepower and 123 lb-ft of torque. This direct-injection engine provides adequate pep for city commuting but requires attentive oil level monitoring to avoid internal wear.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report the engine unexpectedly stalling or shutting off while driving at speed or coming to a stop.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the fuel delivery system, throttle body, and sensors; replace faulty components as necessary.

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Low oil levels observed on the dipstick between routine oil changes, potentially leading to catalytic converter strain.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels frequently, check for leaks, and inspect the PCV valve and piston rings if consumption is heavy.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with the 1.6L engine, the conventional 6-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth gear changes and reliable daily operation. It generally exhibits fewer widespread mechanical complaints than the engine or electrical systems.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The available 6-speed manual transmission offers direct driver engagement and a simpler mechanical layout. It remains a durable option when standard clutch maintenance is performed.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical complaints center on lighting components and circuit durability, requiring owners to periodically check bulb and assembly operation.

**Premature Tail Light and Headlight Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rear LED tail light assemblies and low beam headlights burn out prematurely or fail intermittently.
- **Solution:** Replace burned-out bulbs or faulty light assemblies with updated replacement units.

### Trunk Latch

Interior convenience features generally hold up well, though trunk latch mechanisms have been reported to stick or fail over time.

**Trunk Latch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The trunk fails to stay closed securely or pops open unexpectedly while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the trunk latch mechanism or actuator assembly.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V594000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Trunk Lid:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Rio&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 401, "Internal Trunk Release." A person inside the trunk compartment may become trapped, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the trunk latch base subassembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V652000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Rio&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The Hydraulic Electronic Control Unit (HECU) may experience an electrical short, which can result in an engine compartment fire while parked or driving. An engine compartment fire while parked or driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the HECU fuse, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Kia Rio is an affordable and efficient subcompact car, but prospective buyers should inspect it thoroughly before purchasing. Pay close attention to engine oil levels, test all exterior lights, and verify that all safety recalls—particularly those concerning hydraulic control units—have been completed by an authorized dealer.
