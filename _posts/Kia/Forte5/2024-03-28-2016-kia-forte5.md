**Introduction**
The 2016 Kia Forte5 is a compact hatchback available with three engine options: a 1.8L inline-4 producing 148 horsepower and 131 lb-ft of torque, a 2.0L inline-4 producing 173 horsepower and 154 lb-ft of torque, and a 1.6L turbocharged inline-4 producing 201 horsepower and 195 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 6-speed manual, a 6-speed automatic, and a continuously variable transmission (CVT). Owners praise the Forte5's affordability, sporty handling, and comfortable interior, while criticizing its lack of power from the base engine and subpar fuel economy.

**Engine**

**1.8L Inline-4**

**Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
Description/Symptoms: Oil leak from the top of the engine
Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket
Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Inline-4**

**No Issues Found**

**1.6L Turbocharged Inline-4**

**Oil Consumption**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption
Solution: Replace the piston rings and engine assembly
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

**No Issues Found**

**6-Speed Automatic**

**Torque Converter Shudder**
Description/Symptoms: Vibration or shuddering when shifting gears
Solution: Replace the torque converter
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
Recalls: None Found

**CVT**

**Belt Slipping**
Description/Symptoms: Loss of power or abnormal noises
Solution: Replace the CVT belt and pulleys
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Front Strut Mounts**
Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises when driving over bumps
Solution: Replace the front strut mounts
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: Battery dies after sitting for a period of time
Solution: Diagnose and fix the source of the power drain
Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Front Bumper Clips**
Description/Symptoms: Front bumper loose or sagging
Solution: Replace the bumper clips
Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Climate Control Malfunction**
Description/Symptoms: Heating or cooling system not working properly
Solution: Diagnose and fix the issue with the climate control module
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Sunroof Drain Plugs**
Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the cabin when it rains
Solution: Clear the sunroof drain plugs or replace them if damaged
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2016 Kia Forte5 faces moderate severity issues affecting its engine, transmission, and electrical systems. The most severe and costly issue is the oil consumption problem with the 1.6L turbocharged engine. Overall, the reliability of the Forte5 is average, with some issues being relatively inexpensive to fix while others can be quite costly.
