The 2021 Kia Forte5 is a compact hatchback known for its stylish design, practical cargo space, and value-oriented pricing. The standard engine is a 2.0L inline-4 cylinder producing 147 horsepower and 132 lb-ft of torque. The Forte5 GT features a more powerful 1.6L turbocharged inline-4 cylinder producing 201 horsepower and 195 lb-ft of torque. The 2.0L engine is paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT), while the GT features a 7-speed dual-clutch transmission (DCT). Owners generally appreciate the Forte5's fuel efficiency, user-friendly technology, and comfortable ride. Common dislikes include the CVT's performance in the base model and some interior material quality.

**Engine**

**2.0L Inline-4 (CVT)**

*   **Engine Hesitation/Stalling**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report hesitation during acceleration or even stalling at low speeds. This can be intermittent and may occur more frequently in colder weather.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis is difficult, often requiring software updates or replacement of sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**1.6L Turbocharged Inline-4 (7-Speed DCT)**

*   **DCT Hesitation/Jerking at Low Speeds**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The 7-speed DCT can exhibit noticeable hesitation or jerking, particularly at low speeds or during stop-and-go traffic. This is a common characteristic of dual-clutch transmissions, but some owners find it excessive.
    *   Solution: DCT software updates may improve the situation. In some cases, clutch replacement has been reported.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $2,000+ (clutch replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT (Paired with 2.0L Engine)**

*   **CVT Shudder/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reports of shuddering or hesitation during acceleration, especially at lower speeds. Can feel like the transmission is struggling to find the right gear ratio.
    *   Solution: Some owners have had success with CVT fluid changes or software updates. In more severe cases, CVT replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $4,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**7-Speed DCT (Paired with 1.6L Turbo Engine)**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noticeable harshness during gear changes, especially in lower gears. Can also cause an uncomfortable jerk when upshifting and downshifting
    *   Solution: Some owners have had success with DCT software updates. In some cases, clutch replacement has been reported.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system can freeze, become unresponsive, or crash unexpectedly. This can affect navigation, audio, and other functions.
    *   Solution: Software updates are the most common solution. In some instances, replacement of the infotainment unit may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,000+ (unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Backup Camera Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or complete failure of the backup camera. Image may be distorted, blurry, or not display at all.
    *   Solution: Check wiring connections and replace the camera unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Windshield Wipers Fail to Shut Off Properly**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Windshield wipers continue to operate even after being switched off.
    *   Solution: The windshield wiper switch or motor may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Windshield Cracks Easily**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have noted that the windshield seems prone to cracking from small impacts.
    *   Solution: Windshield replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling Noises from Dashboard/Panels**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported rattling noises originating from the dashboard or door panels, particularly when driving on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Securing loose panels or adding padding to reduce vibration.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $200+ (depending on complexity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Kia Forte5 appears to have a mix of minor and moderate issues. Electrical issues, particularly with the infotainment system, are common. Some owners report more significant transmission issues, especially with the CVT, which could lead to costly repairs. Overall, the vehicle seems relatively reliable, but potential buyers should be aware of the infotainment glitches and possible transmission problems. Addressing TSBs and recalls (if any) is crucial for mitigating potential issues.
