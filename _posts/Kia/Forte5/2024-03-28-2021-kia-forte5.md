**Introduction:**

The 2021 Kia Forte5 is a compact hatchback offered with a single engine and transmission option: a 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). The engine produces 147 horsepower and 132 lb-ft of torque. Praised for its sporty handling, spacious interior, and impressive fuel economy, the Forte5 faces some consumer complaints regarding reliability and certain component issues.

**Engine**

**2.0L Four-Cylinder**

* **Knocking or Ticking Noise at Startup:**
    * Description/Symptoms: A faint knocking or ticking sound coming from the engine after startup.
    * Solution: Valve adjustment or replacement of faulty valve components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: "None Found"

* **Oil Consumption:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Engine rebuild or piston ring replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: "None Found"

**Transmission**

**Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

* **Jerking or Shuddering:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission jerks or shudders during acceleration or shifting.
    * Solution: CVT fluid replacement or transmission software update.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: "None Found"

* **Slipping or Whining:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission fails to engage properly, slips between gears, or makes a whining noise.
    * Solution: Major transmission repair or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: "None Found"

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery drains prematurely, even when the vehicle is parked.
    * Solution: Faulty electrical components, including the alternator or battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: "None Found"

* **Headlight Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Headlights flicker, dim, or fail completely.
    * Solution: Replacement of faulty headlight bulbs or wiring.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: "None Found"

**Exterior**

* **Dent or Rust on Hood:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dent or rust spots on the hood, especially in vehicles with black paint.
    * Solution: Replacement of the hood.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: "None Found"

* **Door Handle Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Exterior door handle breaks or becomes difficult to operate.
    * Solution: Replacement of the door handle.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: "None Found"

**Interior**

* **Infotainment Screen Lag or Freezing:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen becomes unresponsive or freezes while driving.
    * Solution: Software update or replacement of the infotainment system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: "None Found"

* **Cracked or Damaged Plastic Trim:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Plastic trim pieces in the interior, such as the dashboard or door panels, crack or become damaged.
    * Solution: Replacement of the damaged trim pieces.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: "None Found"

**Summary**

Overall, the 2021 Kia Forte5 faces some notable issues, particularly with its engine, transmission, and electrical system. The severity and cost of repairs vary depending on the issue, but some repairs, especially those related to major engine or transmission problems, can be expensive.
