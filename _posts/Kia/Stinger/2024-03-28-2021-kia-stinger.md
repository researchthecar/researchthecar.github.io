**2021 Kia Stinger**

The 2021 Kia Stinger is a performance-oriented sedan that offers a range of engine and transmission options. The base engine is a 2.5-liter turbocharged inline-four that produces 300 horsepower and 311 lb-ft of torque. A 3.3-liter twin-turbocharged V6 is available as an upgrade, and it produces 368 horsepower and 376 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are mated to an eight-speed automatic transmission. The Stinger is praised for its powerful engines, well-handling chassis, and sleek design. However, it has also been criticized for its cramped rear seat and limited cargo space.

**Engine**
**2.5-liter Turbocharged Inline-Four**
* **Valve Stem Carbon Buildup**
   * Description/Symptoms: Carbon deposits build up on the valve stems, causing decreased engine performance, misfires, and increased emissions.
   * Solution: Remove the cylinder heads and clean the valve stems.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
   * Recalls: None Found
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak**
   * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold gasket fails, causing vacuum leaks, decreased engine performance, and increased fuel consumption.
   * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
   * Recalls: None Found

**3.3-liter Twin-Turbocharged V6**
* **Turbocharger Failure**
   * Description/Symptoms: The turbochargers fail due to faulty bearings or seals, resulting in decreased boost pressure, reduced engine power, and increased turbo lag.
   * Solution: Replace the failed turbochargers.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+ per turbocharger
   * Recalls: None Found
* **Fuel Injector Failure**
   * Description/Symptoms: The fuel injectors become clogged or fail, causing misfires, rough idling, and decreased engine performance.
   * Solution: Replace the faulty fuel injectors.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
   * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
**Eight-Speed Automatic**
* **Transmission Fluid Leak**
   * Description/Symptoms: The transmission fluid leaks due to a faulty seal or gasket, resulting in decreased transmission fluid levels, overheating, and premature transmission failure.
   * Solution: Replace the faulty seal or gasket and flush the transmission fluid.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
   * Recalls: None Found
* **Shifting Problems**
   * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may experience delayed or hard shifts, slipping, or refusal to shift gears.
   * Solution: Reprogram the transmission control module or replace the faulty transmission components.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
   * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
* **Strut Mount Failure**
   * Description/Symptoms: The strut mounts fail due to premature wear, causing knocking, rattling, and decreased stability.
   * Solution: Replace the failed strut mounts.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
   * Recalls: None Found
* **Control Arm Bushing Wear**
   * Description/Symptoms: The control arm bushings prematurely wear out, causing squeaking, excessive tire wear, and decreased handling.
   * Solution: Replace the worn control arm bushings.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
   * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
* **Battery Drain**
   * Description/Symptoms: The battery prematurely drains due to a faulty parasitic draw, such as a defective alternator or starter.
   * Solution: Identify and repair the faulty component causing the battery drain.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
   * Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical Short**
   * Description/Symptoms: A faulty electrical component or wiring may cause a short, leading to fuses blowing, warning lights illuminating, and potential electrical fires.
   * Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty electrical component or wiring.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
   * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
* **Hood Prop Failure**
   * Description/Symptoms: The hood prop fails, causing the hood to fall and potentially damage the windshield or other components.
   * Solution: Replace the faulty hood prop.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
   * Recalls: None Found
* **Exterior Trim Discoloration**
   * Description/Symptoms: The exterior trim may prematurely fade or discolor due to sun exposure or other environmental factors.
   * Solution: Replace the discolored trim or apply a protective coating.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
   * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
* **Dashboard Creaking**
   * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard may creak or rattle due to loose or poorly installed components.
   * Solution: Identify and tighten the loose components or apply sound dampening material.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
   * Recalls: None Found
* **Headliner Sagging**
   * Description/Symptoms: The headliner may prematurely sag or detach due to defective adhesive or improper installation.
   * Solution: Reattach the sagging headliner or replace it.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
   * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Kia Stinger is a performance-oriented sedan that offers a wide range of engine and transmission options. However, it has been plagued by several issues, which vary in severity and cost. Some of the more serious issues, such as turbocharger failure and transmission problems, can be expensive to fix. However, many of the other issues are more minor and can be resolved at a lower cost. Overall, the Stinger is a well-rounded vehicle with some areas that need improvement.
