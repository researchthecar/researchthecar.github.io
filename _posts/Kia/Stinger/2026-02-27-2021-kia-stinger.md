# 2021 Kia Stinger Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Kia Stinger is a performance-oriented sports sedan known for its engaging driving dynamics and upscale cabin feel. While generally offering an exciting ride, used car buyers should review owner reports regarding certain mechanical and body components. Conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help identify potential issues early.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The base 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces around 252 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, offering lively daily performance. It is a generally solid powerplant, though routine maintenance is essential for longevity.

### 3.3L Twin-Turbocharged V6

The 3.3-liter twin-turbocharged V6 delivers robust performance with approximately 365 horsepower and 376 lb-ft of torque. While highly regarded for its power delivery, buyers should monitor the fuel system for potential high-pressure pump concerns reported by some owners.

**High Pressure Fuel Pump / Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may stutter, skip, or lose motive power unexpectedly while driving or when the auto-stop feature engages, accompanied by a check engine light.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic diagnose the high-pressure fuel system and replace failing components as necessary.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission comes paired with both rear-wheel drive and all-wheel drive configurations, delivering smooth and responsive shifts. No widespread, recurring transmission failures dominate owner complaints for this model year.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes and Rotors

The braking system provides strong stopping power, but multiple owners have reported premature rotor warping and accelerated pad wear requiring early replacement.

**Premature Rotor Warping and Pad Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal vibration, premature brake pad wear, metal-to-metal grinding noises, or a braking sensation where the brakes temporarily feel unresponsive or 'skip'.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and calipers for excessive wear or sticking components, and replace warped rotors and worn pads.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Windshield Washers

Exterior body components are generally durable, though isolated owner reports note rare issues with spontaneous glass breakage and winter wiper nozzle freezing.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The glass sunroof panel suddenly shatters or explodes while driving without any apparent external impact, dropping glass shards into the cabin.
- **Solution:** Have the damaged sunroof assembly completely replaced and inspect the surrounding frame for stress or alignment issues.

**Freezing Windshield Washer Nozzles**
- **Symptoms:** Windshield washer fluid nozzles freeze during below-freezing temperatures, preventing fluid flow and severely reducing outward visibility.
- **Solution:** Clear any ice buildup and ensure winter-grade washer fluid with a lower freezing point is utilized.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V862000: Electrical System:Instrument Panel:Fuel Gauge](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Stinger&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A software error in the instrument cluster may cause an inaccurate fuel gauge reading. An inaccurate fuel gauge may result in the vehicle unexpectedly running out of fuel and stalling, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V560000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Stinger&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Insufficient fuel supply to the engine can result in a loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Kia will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the fuel pump and replace the jet nozzle housing assembly, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V518000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Stinger&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** While driving, an engine compartment fire may occur in the area where the Anti-lock Brake Hydraulic Electronic Control Unit (HECU) is located. An engine compartment fire can increase the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Kia will notify owners, and dealers will install a new fuse kit in the electrical junction box, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V634000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Stinger&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the engine control unit software, inspect, and replace the fuel pump as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V169000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Gasoline:Turbo/Supercharger:Hoses/Plumbing](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Stinger&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An oil leak in the presence of hot engine components can increase the risk of an engine compartment fire while driving.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the left turbocharger oil feed pipe and hose assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Kia Stinger is an appealing used sports sedan that delivers strong performance and sharp styling. While most mechanical systems are reliable, prospective buyers should carefully inspect the service brakes for premature wear and verify that the fuel and sunroof systems operate properly during a pre-purchase test drive.
