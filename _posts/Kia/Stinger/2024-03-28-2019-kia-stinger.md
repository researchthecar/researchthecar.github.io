**Introduction:**

The 2019 Kia Stinger is a high-performance sedan that offers a sleek exterior design, a luxurious interior, and impressive power. It comes equipped with a 2.0L turbocharged 4-cylinder engine producing 255 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, or a 3.3L twin-turbocharged V6 engine with 365 horsepower and 376 lb-ft of torque. Both engines can be paired with a 6-speed manual transmission, 8-speed automatic transmission, or an available 10-speed automatic transmission. The Stinger is praised for its agile handling, comfortable ride, and spacious cabin, but some owners have reported encountering various issues with their vehicles.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine**

**Engine Knock:**
Description/Symptoms: Unusual knocking or rattling noises coming from the engine.
Solution: Replacing the connecting rod bearings.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
Recalls: None Found

**3.3L Twin-Turbocharged V6 Engine**

**Fuel Line Leak:**
Description/Symptoms: Fuel leaking from a faulty fuel line.
Solution: Replacing the fuel line.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

**Clutch Issues:**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty engaging or disengaging the clutch, slipping gears.
Solution: Replacing the clutch assembly.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,500+
Recalls: None Found

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission**

**Transmission Shudder:**
Description/Symptoms: Vibrations or shuddering during gear shifts.
Solution: Updating the transmission software or replacing the transmission fluid.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $250+
Recalls: None Found

**10-Speed Automatic Transmission**

**Shift Delay:**
Description/Symptoms: Delay in gear shifts, especially when accelerating.
Solution: Updating the transmission software.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $0
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

**Battery Drain:**
Description/Symptoms: Battery draining prematurely.
Solution: Replacing the battery or repairing electrical leaks.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
Recalls: 20V-628

**Exterior**

**Sunroof Leaks:**
Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the cabin through the sunroof seals.
Solution: Replacing or adjusting the sunroof seals.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Headliner Noise:**
Description/Symptoms: Rattling or buzzing noises coming from the headliner.
Solution: Tightening or replacing headliner clips.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2019 Kia Stinger is a generally reliable vehicle, but some owners have reported issues with engine knock, fuel line leaks, clutch problems, transmission shudder, shift delays, battery drain, sunroof leaks, and headliner noise. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but many are relatively minor and can be resolved for a reasonable cost.
