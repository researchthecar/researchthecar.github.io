**Introduction:**

The 2023 Kia Seltos is a compact SUV that offers a combination of style, versatility, and affordability. It is powered by a 2.0L inline-four engine paired with either a continuously variable transmission (CVT) or an optional 1.6L turbocharged inline-four mated to a dual-clutch transmission (DCT). The Seltos has received praise for its spacious interior, infotainment system, and standard safety features, but it has also been subject to some reported issues.

**Engine**

* **2.0L Inline-Four**
    * **Knocking Noise:**
        * Description/Symptoms: A persistent knocking noise coming from the engine, especially during acceleration.
        * Solution: Replacement of faulty camshaft followers or lifters.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Oil Consumption:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption requiring frequent top-ups.
        * Solution: Diagnosis and repair may involve replacing piston rings or valve seals.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

* **1.6L Turbocharged Inline-Four**
    * **Boost Leakage:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power and reduced throttle response due to a leak in the turbocharger system.
        * Solution: Replacement of faulty turbocharger or associated components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **CVT**
    * **Lagging Response:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Delayed or jerky gear changes, especially during initial acceleration.
        * Solution: Software updates or transmission fluid replacement.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Noise:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Grinding or whining noises coming from the transmission.
        * Solution: Replacement of faulty transmission components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
        * Recalls: None Found

* **DCT**
    * **Hesitation and Shuddering:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Vehicle hesitation or shuddering during gear changes.
        * Solution: Software updates or replacement of clutch components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Roof Rack Crossbars Sagging:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Roof rack crossbars may sag or bend when weight is applied.
    * Solution: Replacement of faulty crossbars.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Noise:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive wind noise or rattling coming from the sunroof.
    * Solution: Adjustment or lubrication of sunroof components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Seat Belt Buckle Corrosion:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Seat belt buckle becomes corroded, making it difficult to fasten.
    * Solution: Replacement of faulty seat belt buckle.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2023 Kia Seltos has received mixed reviews, with some owners reporting significant issues while others have had a positive experience. The most prevalent issues seem to be related to the engine, with concerns about knocking noises, oil consumption, and turbocharger leaks. The CVT transmission has also experienced some reported issues, such as lagging response and noise. While the interior and exterior have had fewer reported problems, there have been instances of sunroof noise and seat belt buckle corrosion.

Overall, the severity of the issues varies, with some being relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, while others could be more costly and time-consuming. Potential buyers should thoroughly research and consider these issues before making a purchase decision.
