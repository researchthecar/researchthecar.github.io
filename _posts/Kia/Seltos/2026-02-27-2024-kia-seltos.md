# 2024 Kia Seltos Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2024 Kia Seltos, prospective buyers will find a stylish, feature-packed subcompact SUV that generally offers solid daily drivability. However, owner reports submitted to safety databases highlight a handful of notable electrical, powertrain, and body concerns that deserve a careful inspection before purchasing. While many drivers enjoy its modern technology and efficient packaging, verifying that critical software updates and components are functioning properly can help ensure a more dependable ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder

The base 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces 146 horsepower and 132 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting. It prioritizes fuel efficiency over spirited acceleration and serves as a sensible option for budget-conscious buyers.

### 1.6L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The upgraded 1.6-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine delivers a more robust 195 horsepower and 195 lb-ft of torque for punchier performance. It offers stronger passing power on the highway, though shoppers should ensure regular maintenance intervals were closely followed.

## Transmission

### Intelligent Variable Transmission (IVT)

The Intelligent Variable Transmission (IVT) is paired with the base 2.0L engine to optimize fuel economy during daily driving. Buyers should test-drive models thoroughly to check for smooth operation and responsive acceleration.

**Powertrain Jerking and Acceleration Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have experienced sudden loss of power, severe shaking during acceleration, and jerking or hesitation between gear shifts.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the transmission control module and powertrain components for software updates or mechanical wear.

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed traditional automatic transmission comes matched with the 1.6L turbocharged engine and all-wheel drive models. It generally delivers smoother, more traditional shift points compared to continuously variable setups.

## Suspension

### Front Suspension

The suspension system maintains a comfortable ride quality for city commuting, though steering articulation points should be checked for noises.

**Steering and Suspension Popping Noises**
- **Symptoms:** A noticeable popping or clicking sound can occur from the front wheel area when turning the steering wheel at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect suspension joints, struts, and steering linkage components for binding or premature wear.

## Brakes

### Brakes and Sensors

Safety and driver assistance systems offer modern protection, but physical wear items like brakes should be physically verified.

**Premature Front Brake Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Front brakes may begin producing grinding noises or exhibit premature wear symptoms at very low mileages.
- **Solution:** Have a mechanic measure brake pad thickness and rotor condition during a pre-purchase inspection and replace components if worn.

## Electrical

### Instrument Cluster and Infotainment

Electrical system reports indicate occasional sensitivity to extreme cold temperatures affecting display panels and control modules.

**Cold Weather Dashboard and Display Blackout**
- **Symptoms:** Under freezing temperatures, the digital dashboard and main infotainment displays may go completely black, failing to show speed or fuel levels.
- **Solution:** Inspect software calibration and check vehicle electrical connectors; temporary recovery often occurs once the cabin warms up.

**Engine Stop-Start System Reset**
- **Symptoms:** When the automatic stop-and-start feature restarts the engine, infotainment and instrument clusters can shut down and reset temporarily.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealer to determine if specialized software updates are available to stabilize the electrical load during engine restart.

## Exterior

### Windshield and Glass

Body glass components generally provide good visibility, though a few drivers note vulnerability to environmental impacts.

**Windshield Cracking and Easy Chipping**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported prominent stress cracks spanning the glass or high susceptibility to chips from minor road debris.
- **Solution:** Carefully inspect the windshield for pre-existing chips or stress fractures prior to purchase, and verify comprehensive auto glass insurance coverage.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V531000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Fluid/Lubricant:Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Seltos&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** An electric oil pump assembly that overheats increases the risk of a vehicle fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the electric oil pump controller, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V830000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Seltos&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Due to a manufacturing error, the side curtain air bags may deploy unexpectedly. An air bag that deploys unexpectedly can increase the risk of injury or crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace both side curtain air bag modules as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Kia Seltos presents a compelling mix of style, modern tech, and practical utility for used car shoppers, but it is not without potential flaws. Buyers should prioritize a thorough test drive to evaluate transmission smoothness and check for any cold-weather electrical quirks or display blackouts. Scheduling an independent pre-purchase inspection focusing on the brakes, front suspension, and glass condition will help ensure you secure a reliable and trouble-free vehicle.
