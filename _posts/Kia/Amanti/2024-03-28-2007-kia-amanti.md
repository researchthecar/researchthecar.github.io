**Introduction to the 2007 Kia Amanti**

The 2007 Kia Amanti is a full-size sedan available with either a 3.8L V6 engine or a 2.7L V6 engine. The 3.8L V6 produces 264 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, while the 2.7L V6 produces 194 horsepower and 183 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. The Amanti is praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and impressive standard features, but some drivers have reported concerns with certain aspects of the vehicle.

**Engine**

**3.8L V6 Engine**

* **Oil Consumption Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage
    * Solution: Inspect engine for leaks and replace faulty components, such as valve stem seals or piston rings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking intake manifold gasket, causing a vacuum leak and engine performance issues
    * Solution: Replace faulty intake manifold gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.7L V6 Engine**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Hesitation or Jerking During Gear Shifts**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission hesitation or jerking when shifting gears, especially in low or reverse
    * Solution: Repair or replace transmission solenoid(s), update transmission software
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Slipping Gears**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping gears or failing to engage properly
    * Solution: Replace faulty transmission components, such as clutches or torque converter
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Struts/Shocks Wear or Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged struts or shocks, leading to poor ride quality and reduced stability
    * Solution: Replace faulty struts or shocks
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Control Arm Bushings Wear or Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged control arm bushings, causing clunking noises, misalignment, and excessive tire wear
    * Solution: Replace faulty control arm bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Failing alternator, resulting in loss of power or battery issues
    * Solution: Replace faulty alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive battery drain, leading to a dead battery or starting problems
    * Solution: Inspect electrical system for parasitic draws, such as faulty components or wiring
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Fog Light Lens Discoloration**
    * Description/Symptoms: Discolored or yellowing fog light lenses, reducing visibility and aesthetics
    * Solution: Replace faulty fog light lenses
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Navigation System Software Crashes**
    * Description/Symptoms: Navigation system software crashes or malfunctions, rendering the system unusable
    * Solution: Update navigation system software or replace faulty components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2007 Kia Amanti has a moderate number of reported issues, with the most common concerns being engine oil consumption, transmission problems, and suspension components wear. While some of these issues can be minor, others can be costly to repair. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and factor them into their decision-making process.
