The 2024 Kia Carnival is a minivan known for its stylish design, spacious interior, and a wealth of features. It is powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing 290 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise its comfortable ride, user-friendly technology, and ample cargo space. Common dislikes include limited all-wheel-drive availability and some concerns about long-term reliability.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6**

*   **Engine Stalling**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported instances of the engine stalling unexpectedly, particularly at low speeds or when idling. This may or may not be accompanied by warning lights on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis typically involves checking the fuel system, ignition system, and sensors. The fix might involve replacing faulty sensors (such as the crankshaft or camshaft position sensor), cleaning or replacing the throttle body, or addressing fuel pump issues.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported oil leaks, typically from around the valve cover gasket or the oil pan gasket.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking gasket or resealing the affected area.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reports of rough or jerky shifting, especially between certain gears.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush and software update, or in more severe cases, valve body replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system occasionally freezes, crashes, or becomes unresponsive. This can affect navigation, audio, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Software updates from the dealer are often the first step. In some cases, replacement of the infotainment unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Software Update) - $1000+ (Unit Replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Sliding Door Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Automatic sliding doors may fail to open or close properly, or get stuck.
    * Solution: May require recalibration or replacement of the motor that controls the doors
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Automatic Tailgate Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the automatic tailgate failing to open or close reliably, or getting stuck.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis typically involves checking the tailgate struts, sensors, and motor. Replacement of faulty parts may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Creaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling or creaking noises emanating from the dashboard, door panels, or rear cargo area.
    *   Solution: Identifying the source of the noise and tightening or padding the affected components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2024 Kia Carnival generally experiences a range of issues, from minor inconveniences like infotainment glitches and interior noises to more concerning problems such as engine stalling and transmission issues. The severity and cost of repairs can vary greatly depending on the specific issue. While some problems may be resolved with simple software updates or adjustments, others may require more extensive and costly repairs. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and thoroughly inspect the vehicle before purchase.
