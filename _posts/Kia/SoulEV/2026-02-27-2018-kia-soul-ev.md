# 2018 Kia Soul EV Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2018 Kia Soul EV, buyers will find a quirky, highly practical city hatchback powered entirely by electricity rather than gasoline. While it offers smooth, zero-emissions driving and a distinctive cabin layout, potential owners should look closely at historical owner feedback. Complaints center around battery and charging performance fluctuations, random electrical glitches, and powertrain control issues that warrant a thorough pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 109-hp Electric Motor & Lithium-ion Polymer Battery

The electric powertrain features a 109-horsepower electric motor paired with a high-capacity lithium-ion polymer battery pack, delivering immediate torque for urban commuting. It offers a unique driving experience compared to gasoline models, though older EV battery health and thermal management systems require careful verification.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct Drive Transmission

A single-speed direct drive transmission delivers power smoothly to the wheels without traditional gear shifting. While mechanically simpler than multi-gear automatics, owners must ensure the parking mechanism engages securely.

**Unexpected Vehicle Rolling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle rolled down a hill after being placed in park, creating a significant safety hazard.
- **Solution:** Have the parking pawl and gear selector linkage inspected by a qualified technician.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Electronic Control Unit (HECU)

Safety systems rely heavily on sensors and electronic control modules to maintain stability and braking control. Failures in major control units can disable critical driver-assist features.

**HECU Failure**
- **Symptoms:** ABS and traction control warning lights illuminate on the dashboard due to an internal HECU failure.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty HECU assembly.
- **Cost:** $2377.16

## Electrical

### Charging and Power Control System

The electrical system manages high-voltage battery charging, onboard diagnostics, and cabin electronics. High-voltage battery degradation or software glitches can heavily impact everyday usability.

**Battery Charging Capacity Reduction**
- **Symptoms:** Power fluctuations occur within the electrical system, severely reducing the battery charging capacity to as low as eight percent while keeping the EV warning light illuminated.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning by a certified EV technician is required to check battery module health and charging hardware.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V031000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Soul+EV&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Circuit damage may result in deactivated air bags that will not deploy in a crash. Air bags that do not deploy as intended can increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the ACU, and either update the software or replace the unit, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V218000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Soul+EV&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A battery electrical short-circuit increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update and/or replace the battery management system and software, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V724000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Gear Position Indication (Prndl)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=Soul+EV&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Unintended vehicle movement can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the electronic control unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Kia Soul EV can serve as an efficient, budget-friendly commuter vehicle for local driving if the battery pack retains a healthy state of charge. However, prospective buyers should prioritize a professional EV health inspection to check for charging anomalies, dashboard warning lights, and software updates before making a purchase.
