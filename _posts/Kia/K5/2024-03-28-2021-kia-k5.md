**Introduction:**

The 2021 Kia K5 is a sleek and sporty midsize sedan offered with a variety of engine and transmission options. It boasts a spacious cabin, cutting-edge technology features, and a comfortable ride. However, like any vehicle, the K5 has its share of potential issues that owners have reported.

**Engine**

**1.8L GDI 4-Cylinder**
* **Oil Consumption:** Excessive oil consumption leading to low oil levels, engine knocking, and potential damage. Solution involves replacing piston rings and oil pump. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
* **Turbocharger Failure:** Premature failure of the turbocharger, resulting in loss of power and increased fuel consumption. Solution requires turbocharger replacement. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Knock Sensor Issues:** Faulty knock sensors can provide incorrect readings, leading to reduced engine performance and potential damage. Solution involves replacing the knock sensor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Recalls:**
    * NHTSA Campaign Number: 22V-017: Engine bearing damage may cause rod knock, leading to engine failure.
    * NHTSA Campaign Number: 22V-151: Fuel pump failure may result in a stall or difficulty starting the engine.

**2.5L GDI 4-Cylinder**

* **No Issues Found**

**1.6L T-GDI 4-Cylinder**

* **Turbocharger Wastegate Sticking:** Sticky wastegate can prevent the turbocharger from boosting properly, leading to reduced engine power. Solution involves cleaning or replacing the wastegate. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (FWD)**

* **Transmission Slippage:** Transmission may experience slippage, resulting in hesitation or lack of power when accelerating. Solution involves transmission rebuild or replacement. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
* **Recalls:**
    * NHTSA Campaign Number: 22V-017: Incorrect transmission control module software may lead to transmission failure.

**8-Speed Automatic (AWD)**

* **No Issues Found**

**8-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic (DCT)**

* **Clutch Engagement Issues:** Clutch may engage abruptly or slip, causing shuddering, hesitation, or difficulty shifting gears. Solution may involve clutch replacement or software updates. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Suspension**

* **Struts/Shocks Failure:** Premature failure of struts or shocks, resulting in a rough or bouncy ride and reduced handling. Solution involves replacing the affected components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+
* **Control Arm Bushings Wear:** Worn control arm bushings can cause excessive noise, vibrations, and impaired handling. Solution requires replacement of the bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Excessive battery drain can lead to a dead battery, particularly after the vehicle has been parked for extended periods. Solution involves identifying and fixing the electrical components responsible for the drain. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
* **Rearview Camera Issues:** Rearview camera may fail to display or provide a clear image, impairing rear visibility. Solution involves replacing the camera or addressing any wiring issues. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:** Condensation may form inside the headlight assemblies, reducing visibility and potentially damaging the lights. Solution involves resealing the lights or replacing them if necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
* **Sunroof Leak:** Sunroof may leak, allowing water to enter the cabin. Solution requires sealing the leak or replacing the sunroof assembly. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunctions:** Climate control system may not function properly, resulting in insufficient heating or cooling. Solution involves diagnosing and repairing the affected components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
* **Navigation System Lag:** Navigation system may experience lag or freezing, impairing the user interface. Solution involves software updates or hardware replacement. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2021 Kia K5 appears to have some potential issues, particularly in the engine and transmission categories. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific components affected and the extent of the damage. Owners should be aware of these issues and consider having their vehicles regularly inspected by a qualified mechanic to address any potential problems promptly.
