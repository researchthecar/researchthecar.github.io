# 2022 Kia EV6 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Kia EV6 brings impressive electric performance, striking design, and ultra-fast charging capability to the crossover market. However, prospective buyers should carefully review owner-reported electrical and charging concerns before purchasing a used model. While it offers excellent range and driving dynamics, understanding recurring component patterns is key to a confident buying decision.

---

## Engine

### Standard Range Electric Motor (58 kWh)

The rear-wheel-drive Standard Range model features a single permanent-magnet synchronous motor producing approximately 167 horsepower. It delivers smooth, efficient everyday acceleration for budget-conscious buyers.

### Long Range Single Motor (77.4 kWh)

The single-motor Long Range configuration generates around 225 horsepower directed to the rear wheels. It balances strong highway passing power with maximum driving range.

### Long Range Dual Motor AWD (77.4 kWh)

The dual-motor all-wheel-drive setup outputs approximately 320 horsepower, providing brisk acceleration and enhanced all-weather traction. It serves as a popular choice for performance-oriented drivers.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Reduction Gearbox

A direct-drive single-speed transmission channels power seamlessly from the electric motor to the wheels without traditional gear shifting. It is mechanically simple and generally robust.

## Brakes

### Regenerative Braking System

The braking system blends regenerative deceleration with traditional friction brakes for efficient stopping performance.

**Brake Light Inactivation During One-Pedal Driving**
- **Symptoms:** Brake lights fail to illuminate during rapid deceleration when utilizing high regenerative or one-pedal driving modes.
- **Solution:** Consult dealership for software updates or apply service advisories where applicable.

## Electrical

### Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU)

The electrical system centers around advanced power management modules, though owner reports indicate that ICCU failures represent a primary reliability concern.

**ICCU Failure and Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle displays warning lights, loses motive power while driving, or fails to accept Level 1 and Level 2 AC home charging.
- **Solution:** Dealer replacement of the Integrated Charging Control Unit and associated fuses or 12V battery.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Liftgate Structure

Exterior body and glass components generally hold up well, though isolated structural and glass incidents have been documented.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Sunroof glass suddenly fractures or explodes outward while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the glass assembly through insurance or warranty coverage.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V322000: Parking Brake:Electrical:Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=EV6&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Vehicle rollaway can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the Shifter Control Unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V200000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=EV6&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU) may become damaged and stop charging the 12-Volt battery, which can result in a loss of drive power. A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the ICCU and its fuse, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V867000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=EV6&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU) may become damaged and stop charging the 12-volt battery, which can result in a loss of drive power. A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the ICCU and its fuse, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V431000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=EV6&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The high voltage battery cells may contain misaligned electrodes, which can lead to a fire while parked or driving. A fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the high voltage battery system assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The used EV6 is an appealing electric crossover praised for its rapid charging speeds, comfort, and performance. However, buyers should verify whether the Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU) has been inspected or replaced under applicable campaigns, as charging and power loss failures are the most prominent owner complaints. Ensure a thorough pre-purchase inspection before finalizing your purchase.
