# 2023 Kia EV6 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Kia EV6 is a striking, tech-forward all-electric crossover that shares its modern E-GMP platform with other Hyundai-Kia EVs. While praised for its rapid charging capabilities, engaging driving dynamics, and futuristic interior, used-car buyers need to be aware of prominent electrical system complaints. Specifically, recurring issues involving the Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU) have been reported by owners, which can lead to charging failures or unexpected losses of motive power while driving.

---

## Engine

### Standard Range Electric Motor (58 kWh)

The entry-level rear-wheel-drive powertrain pairs a 58 kWh battery with a rear-mounted electric motor, generating approximately 167 horsepower. It offers smooth, instant acceleration suited for daily commuting, though long-distance range is more limited than larger battery trims.

### Long Range Electric Motor (77.4 kWh)

The Long Range setup uses a 77.4 kWh battery pack powering either a rear-wheel-drive motor (225 hp) or a dual-motor all-wheel-drive layout (320 hp). This powertrain balances substantial driving range with brisk performance, providing confident highway passing power and robust everyday capability.

### GT High-Performance Dual Motors (77.4 kWh)

At the top of the lineup, the GT trim utilizes high-output dual electric motors producing a staggering 576 horsepower paired with the 77.4 kWh battery. It delivers supercar-level acceleration and sports-car handling dynamics, though efficiency and overall driving range are reduced compared to standard models.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct Drive Transmission

Electric vehicles utilize a simplified, single-speed reduction gear transmission rather than a conventional multi-gear automatic or manual gearbox. This setup delivers seamless power delivery without shifting hesitation, requiring minimal mechanical maintenance throughout the vehicle's lifespan.

### Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU)

**ICCU Failure and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Owners experience dashboard warnings such as 'Stop Vehicle Check Power Supply' or sudden power loss, sometimes accompanied by a complete shutdown of electrical systems and propulsion while driving.
- **Solution:** Dealerships replace the faulty ICCU unit and update associated charging and powertrain software under warranty.
- **Cost:** Covered under warranty when applicable.

**Charging System and AC/DC Faults**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may fail to accept Level 1, Level 2, or DC fast charging, occasionally tripping home circuit breakers or dropping charge rates unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Inspection and replacement of the ICCU or auxiliary fuses, alongside manufacturer software updates designed to regulate charging current.

## Suspension

### Tires and Wheels

**Vibration and Acoustic Foam Separation**
- **Symptoms:** Severe vehicle vibrations that are difficult to control at highway speeds, often caused by internal tire sound-absorbing foam lining tearing or balling up.
- **Solution:** Balance or replace affected tires, and have technicians inspect tire liners for structural separation.

## Brakes

### Brake System & Regenerative Braking

**Regenerative Braking and Pedal Feel Inconsistencies**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note unexpected noises, vibrations during braking, or brief wheel lock-ups when coming to a stop, alongside issues with one-pedal driving modes disengaging unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Have dealership technicians inspect the brake actuator assembly, check regenerative braking software calibrations, and verify brake pad and rotor wear.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V057000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=EV6&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the drive shaft, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V200000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=EV6&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU) may become damaged and stop charging the 12-Volt battery, which can result in a loss of drive power. A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the ICCU and its fuse, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V867000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=EV6&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU) may become damaged and stop charging the 12-volt battery, which can result in a loss of drive power. A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the ICCU and its fuse, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V431000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=EV6&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The high voltage battery cells may contain misaligned electrodes, which can lead to a fire while parked or driving. A fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the high voltage battery system assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Kia EV6 is an appealing choice in the used electric vehicle market thanks to its exceptional design, quick charging capabilities, and impressive driving performance. However, prospective buyers must thoroughly verify the vehicle's electrical history—specifically confirming whether the Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU) has been inspected, updated, or replaced under warranty. Ensuring these electrical updates have been successfully performed will significantly minimize the risk of unexpected power loss or charging complications.
