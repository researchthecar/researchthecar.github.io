# 2024 Kia EV6 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Kia EV6 is a sleek, highly-rated all-electric crossover offering impressive driving dynamics, rapid charging capabilities, and a futuristic cabin. However, prospective buyers should thoroughly review its electrical and charging infrastructure history. While it delivers strong performance across its various powertrain configurations, early-production and current owner feedback highlights critical concerns surrounding electrical systems and charging components that demand careful scrutiny before purchase.

---

## Engine

### Electric Motor / Propulsion System

The electric propulsion system utilizes permanent magnet synchronous motors paired with varying battery capacities, delivering outputs ranging from standard single-motor setups to high-performance dual-motor configurations exceeding 500 horsepower. These motors offer brisk, near-silent acceleration and responsive power delivery. Buyers should verify that all recall campaigns related to the charging and power electronics have been fully completed.

**Loss of Motive Power and ICCU Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a sudden loss of motive power, dashboard warnings to pull over safely, and entry into 'turtle mode' or total electrical shutdown.
- **Solution:** Authorized dealerships must inspect and replace faulty Integrated Charging Control Units (ICCU) and associated high-voltage fuses, as well as apply required manufacturer software updates.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Reduction Gearbox

The EV6 relies on a fixed-ratio, single-speed reduction gear coupled to the electric drive axle to deliver smooth, uninterrupted power to the wheels across all driving modes. This direct-drive transmission layout is mechanically simple, highly efficient, and generally free of complex gear-shifting mechanical failures.

## Suspension

### Tires and Wheels

Maintains wheel alignment, ride comfort, and road contact under heavy electric vehicle loads.

**Accelerated Rear Tire Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rear tires exhibit significantly faster tread wear compared to the front tires, reaching wear bars prematurely.
- **Solution:** Perform regular four-wheel alignments, check tire pressures frequently, and rotate tires according to manufacturer guidelines.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes and Regenerative System

Responsible for stopping power, blending regenerative energy capture with conventional hydraulic braking.

**Regenerative Braking and Unexpected Acceleration Reports**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unusual vehicle behavior during low-speed maneuvers or reversing while blending regenerative braking with traditional friction brakes.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pedal sensors, software calibration for regenerative braking, and wheel speed sensors for proper operation.

## Electrical

### 12V Battery and Charging System

Manages vehicle accessory power, low-voltage charging, and onboard electrical architecture.

**Electrical System Malfunction and 12V Depletion**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle displays sudden low-voltage battery warnings, experiences total electrical failure, or fails to start even when the high-voltage pack retains an adequate state of charge.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician test the 12V battery health, inspect the ICCU charging logic, and ensure all relevant software patches are installed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V200000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=EV6&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU) may become damaged and stop charging the 12-Volt battery, which can result in a loss of drive power. A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the ICCU and its fuse, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V867000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=EV6&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU) may become damaged and stop charging the 12-volt battery, which can result in a loss of drive power. A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the ICCU and its fuse, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V431000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=EV6&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The high voltage battery cells may contain misaligned electrodes, which can lead to a fire while parked or driving. A fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the high voltage battery system assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Kia EV6 is an appealing choice for electric vehicle shoppers thanks to its modern technology, excellent driving range, and striking design. However, used-car buyers must proceed with caution regarding the vehicle's electrical system. Before finalizing a purchase, ensure that a pre-purchase inspection confirms the health of the 12V battery and that all manufacturer recall services—particularly those concerning the ICCU—have been documented and performed.
