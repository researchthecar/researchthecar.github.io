# 2024 Kia EV9 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Kia EV9 is a spacious, all-electric three-row SUV offering modern styling, advanced technology, and practical family-hauling capabilities. As a relatively new entry to the electric vehicle market, it has drawn attention for its impressive comfort and features, but early model years feature a few notable pain points. Prospective buyers should carefully review electrical systems, charging components, and steering or suspension wear reported by owners.

---

## Engine

### Single-Motor Electric Powertrain

The single-motor powertrain delivers rear-wheel drive focused on efficiency and everyday driving range, with smooth, linear power delivery typical of modern electric vehicles.

## Transmission

### Dual-Motor Electric Powertrain

The dual-motor all-wheel-drive powertrain provides robust horsepower and strong torque for enhanced traction and rapid acceleration, though owners have reported occasional charging and power unit control issues.

**Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle stops working, displays a warning to check the electric vehicle system, and fails to charge.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty ICCU assembly through an authorized dealer, though parts backorders may delay repairs.

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

The single-speed direct-drive transmission provides seamless, shift-free power delivery across all trims and drivetrains, proving mechanically simple and highly reliable.

## Suspension

### Tires and Wheels

The heavy curb weight of the electric platform places high demands on tires and suspension components, requiring vigilant maintenance to prevent premature wear.

**Premature Tire Wear and Tread Separation**
- **Symptoms:** Factory or replacement tires wear out rapidly at low mileage, leading to steel belts showing or catastrophic tire failure.
- **Solution:** Replace worn tires and perform thorough suspension alignment and weight-rating checks.

## Steering

### Steering and Alignment Assembly

Steering responsiveness is praised for a large SUV, but several owners report persistent high-speed vibration issues that require careful diagnostic checks.

**Highway Steering Wheel Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel and center console vibrate consistently when driving at highway speeds above 65 mph, persisting even after multiple tire balances and alignments.
- **Solution:** Inspect tires, wheels, and suspension components for out-of-round conditions or alignment discrepancies.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Instrument Cluster

Electrical architecture supports extensive cabin displays and driver assistance features, though occasional screen blanking and module communication faults have been noted by early adopters.

**Blank Display Screen on Startup**
- **Symptoms:** The digital instrument cluster and infotainment display fail to turn on when starting the vehicle, leaving the speedometer and blind-spot indicators non-functional.
- **Solution:** Perform software resets or have the dealership inspect and update display control modules.

### Power Seats

Interior cabin amenities offer high-end comfort, but power-adjust mechanisms on front seats have exhibited shifting and movement glitches.

**Shifting or Unintended Seat Movement**
- **Symptoms:** The driver's seat pops, shifts slightly during tight turns, or unexpectedly moves forward and folds on its own.
- **Solution:** Inspect power seat tracks, mounting bolts, and control switches for mechanical or electrical faults.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety equipment generally performs well, though rear seatbelt latching mechanisms and occupant detection sensors require inspection if warning lights persist.

**Rear Seat Belt and Indicator Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Rear seat belts fail to latch or retract properly in quick succession, accompanied by persistent false dashboard warnings indicating passengers are buckled when seats are empty.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace malfunctioning seat belt assemblies and buckle sensors.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V271000: Power Train:Driveline:Differential Unit](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=EV9&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The differential gear bolts in the gear drive unit may not be tightened properly, which can result in a loss of drive power or cause the differential to lock up. A loss of drive power or differential lock up can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the gear drive unit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V338000: Seat Belts:Front:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=EV9&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat belt retractor cover may not be secured properly, which can cause the front passenger seat belt automatic locking retractor (ALR) and emergency locking retractor (ELR) to not operate as intended. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 208, "Occupant Crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the front passenger seat belt assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V400000: Structure:Interior Panels:Ceiling](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=EV9&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The headliner may not adequately protect passengers from impact in a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 201, "Occupant Protection In Interior Impact." A headliner with inadequate impact protection increases the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the impact absorbing plates mounted on the headliner, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V693000: Parking Brake:Electrical:Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=EV9&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause the remote smart parking assist feature, which allows the operator to remotely move the vehicle, to miscalculate stopping distances. A remote smart parking assist feature that miscalculates stopping distances may increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the integrated electronic brake software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V757000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=EV9&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 101, "Controls and Displays." An instrument panel screen that fails to display critical safety information, such as the speedometer or warning lights, can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V962000: Seats:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=EV9&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Seats without mounting bolts may not properly restrain an occupant, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and install mounting bolts as necessary free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V431000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Kia&model=EV9&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The high voltage battery cells may contain misaligned electrodes, which can lead to a fire while parked or driving. A fire increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the high voltage battery system assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Kia EV9 brings compelling all-electric performance and three-row practicality to the used market, but buyers should proceed with due diligence. Pay close attention to charging system health (specifically the ICCU), high-speed steering vibrations, and tire wear given the vehicle's heavy battery weight. Always arrange for a pre-purchase inspection by an EV-certified technician before finalizing your purchase.
