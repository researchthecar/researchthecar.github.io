The 2023 Volkswagen Atlas Cross Sport is a mid-size SUV known for its stylish design and spacious interior. It offers a comfortable ride and ample cargo space, making it a popular choice for families. However, some owners have reported certain issues. The standard engine is a 2.0L Turbocharged 4-cylinder producing 235 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. A 3.6L V6 engine, producing 276 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque, is also available. Both engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Common likes include its sleek appearance and user-friendly technology, while dislikes often involve fuel economy and some reported reliability concerns.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whistling or whining noise from the engine, check engine light illumination.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Low coolant level, overheating, coolant smell, visible leaks near the engine.
    *   Solution: Replacement of faulty coolant hoses, water pump, or radiator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.6L V6**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil spots under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacement of valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, or other leaking seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or jerky shifts, especially between certain gears, hesitation when accelerating.
    *   Solution: Software update of the transmission control module (TCM), or, in severe cases, transmission replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $6,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Hesitation or Delay in Engagement**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Delay in shifting from Park to Drive or Reverse, hesitation when accelerating from a stop.
    *   Solution: Software update of the TCM, or, in severe cases, valve body replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Wear of Suspension Components**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the suspension, uneven tire wear, poor handling.
    *   Solution: Replacement of worn-out struts, shocks, control arms, or sway bar links.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+ per corner
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, Crashing, Bluetooth connectivity problems, unresponsive touchscreen.
    *   Solution: Software update of the infotainment system or replacement of the unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent error messages, check engine light illumination, malfunctioning driver-assistance systems (e.g., adaptive cruise control, lane assist).
    * Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of faulty sensors (e.g., oxygen sensors, ABS sensors, TPMS sensors).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $800+ per sensor
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the cabin, especially during rain or after a car wash.
    *   Solution: Cleaning the sunroof drain tubes, resealing the sunroof assembly, or replacing damaged seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Malfunctioning Power Windows/Locks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Windows not operating properly, power locks failing to engage or disengage.
    *   Solution: Replacement of window motor, door lock actuator, or associated wiring.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+ per window/lock
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rattling or Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Annoying noises emanating from the dashboard, door panels, or rear cargo area.
    *   Solution: Locating and securing loose trim pieces, applying felt tape to contact points.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2023 Volkswagen Atlas Cross Sport, while generally well-received, does have some reported issues. Engine issues such as turbocharger failure and oil leaks can be costly to repair. Transmission problems can lead to rough shifting. Electrical glitches, particularly within the infotainment system, are also common. While most issues are relatively minor, some repairs can be expensive. The severity of the issues varies, but addressing them promptly is crucial to maintaining the vehicle's reliability and longevity.
