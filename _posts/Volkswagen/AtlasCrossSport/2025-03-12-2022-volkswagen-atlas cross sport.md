The 2022 Volkswagen Atlas Cross Sport is a mid-size SUV known for its spacious interior and comfortable ride. Engine options include a 2.0L Turbocharged 4-cylinder engine producing 235 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.6L VR6 engine producing 276 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the vehicle's ample cargo space and user-friendly infotainment system, but some dislike its fuel economy and occasionally clunky transmission.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, check engine light illuminated, whistling sound from the engine bay.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.6L VR6**

*   **Timing Chain Tensioner Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise upon startup, especially in cold weather. Can lead to engine damage if left unresolved.
    *   Solution: Replacement of timing chain tensioners and potentially the timing chain itself.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (Both Engines)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Jerking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noticeable hard shifts between gears, especially at lower speeds. Can also manifest as a jerking sensation during acceleration.
    *   Solution: Software update/reprogramming of the transmission control module (TCM). In some cases, valve body replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (software update) - $1,800+ (valve body replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Wear of Struts/Shocks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, excessive body roll during cornering, uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replacement of struts/shocks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,000+ (depending on parts and labor)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen freezes, becomes unresponsive, or randomly restarts.
    *   Solution: Software update or, in some cases, replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,200+ (unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Sensor Failures (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning parking sensors, blind spot monitoring system failing to detect vehicles, error messages on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty sensor(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the cabin, especially during heavy rain.
    *   Solution: Cleaning and resealing the sunroof drain tubes, or replacing the sunroof seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Rear Hatch Alignment Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rear hatch not closing properly, requiring excessive force to close, or not aligning flush with the body.
    *   Solution: Adjustment of the hatch hinges and latch mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises from Dashboard**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various rattling or squeaking noises emanating from the dashboard area, especially when driving on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Locating and securing the source of the noise (loose trim panels, wiring harnesses, etc.).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Malfunctioning Power Seats**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power seats not adjusting properly, getting stuck in certain positions, or making unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the seat motor or control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Volkswagen Atlas Cross Sport has some common issues, ranging from minor annoyances like dashboard rattles to more significant concerns like turbocharger or timing chain problems. Electrical issues such as infotainment glitches and sensor failures are also reported. While some fixes are relatively inexpensive, others, like turbo or valve body replacements, can be costly. The severity of these issues varies, but potential buyers should be aware of these common complaints and have the vehicle thoroughly inspected before purchasing.
