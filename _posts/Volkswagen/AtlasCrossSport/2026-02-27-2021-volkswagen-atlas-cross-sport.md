# 2021 Volkswagen Atlas Cross Sport Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Volkswagen Atlas Cross Sport is a stylish, midsize two-row crossover offering spacious cabin accommodations and confident highway manners. While it shares much of its underlying platform and powertrain options with the larger three-row Atlas, used-car buyers should review common owner-reported complaints regarding electrical issues, exterior lighting failures, and brake noise. Being mindful of these potential trouble spots can help prospective buyers inspect vehicles thoroughly before purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

This turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine produces approximately 235 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate daily pep and efficient cruising. It serves as the standard base powerplant for the lineup.

### 3.6L V6

The naturally aspirated 3.6-liter V6 engine pushes out around 276 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque, offering stronger towing capability and smoother acceleration. Owners generally appreciate its performance, though occasional electronic and fuel-related complaints have been noted.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine stalling or sudden hesitation while driving in traffic or at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis by a qualified technician is required to check fuel delivery, throttle body operation, and electronic control modules.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed conventional automatic transmission pairs smoothly with both engine options across front-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations. It generally operates reliably without major widespread mechanical failure clusters.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides adequate stopping power for a heavy crossover, though some owners have experienced bothersome noises and early wear.

**Brake Noise and Premature Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Loud screeching, squeaking, or metal-on-metal grinding noises during low-speed stops, reversing, or cold starts.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads and rotors for premature wear; replacement of pads and resurfacing or replacement of rotors may be necessary.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Exterior lighting systems provide good illumination, but owners have reported premature failure of integrated LED daytime running lamps requiring expensive component replacements.

**Headlight and Daytime Running Light Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Daytime running LEDs or entire headlamp assemblies fail prematurely, triggering warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Often requires replacing the entire headlight assembly since individual internal LEDs are generally not serviceable.
- **Cost:** High replacement cost due to integrated LED design

### Hood Latch & Release

Interior convenience features are generally well-designed, though occasional mechanical failures such as a snapped hood release cable have been reported by owners.

**Broken Hood Release Cable**
- **Symptoms:** The hood release cable inside the cabin snaps or stretches, making it extremely difficult or impossible to open the hood.
- **Solution:** Replace the broken hood release cable and latch mechanism.

## Safety

### Forward Collision & Driver Assistance

Advanced safety and driver-assistance features enhance protection on the road, though sensor calibration issues can occasionally trigger false warnings or automated braking.

**Unintended Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience sudden ghost braking or unexpected activation of collision avoidance systems without imminent obstacles.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates related to camera and radar calibration at a dealership or service center.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V132000: Wheels:Rim](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas+Cross+Sport&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An insufficiently attached wheel may separate from the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will inspect all four wheels, and as necessary, replace the wheels, lug bolts ,and wheel bearings, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V812000: Wheels:Lugs/Nuts/Bolts/Studs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas+Cross+Sport&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The bolts may loosen and cause a loss of control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the tightness of the wheel bearing bolts and replace the bolts, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V892000: Visibility:Defroster/Defogger/Hvac System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas+Cross+Sport&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The air conditioning system drain tube could be blocked, causing water to leak inside the vehicle near the air bag control module. A water leak may damage the air bag control module, causing an inadvertent air bag deployment and increasing the risk of injury or a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will repair the drain tube and replace the air bag control module, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V702000: Tires:Sidewall](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas+Cross+Sport&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Both conditions increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will inspect DOT date code and mold number on the vehicle's tires to see if they are affected, replacing them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V712000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas+Cross+Sport&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A damaged steering knuckle can fail, causing a loss of steering, thereby increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen has notified owners, and dealers will inspect and, as necessary, replace the steering knuckle, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V716000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas+Cross+Sport&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The rear view camera could malfunction during an ignition cycle, leading to a black screen or infotainment system freeze. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A black or frozen rear view image reduces the driver's visibility when reversing, increasing the risk of.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will update the infotainment system software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V026000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas+Cross+Sport&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A fractured steering knuckle can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, as necessary, replace one or both steering knuckles, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V616000: Electrical System:Wiring:Relays/Solenoids](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas+Cross+Sport&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Damaged relays may have been installed in the engine compartment fuse box, which could inadvertently activate the horn or engine starter. A horn or starter that remains activated for an extended period of time can overheat, increasing the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and as necessary, replace the relays, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Volkswagen Atlas Cross Sport is a comfortable and practical choice for used-car buyers seeking generous passenger space and modern styling. While major mechanical drivetrain components are generally dependable, prospective buyers should carefully check the exterior lighting functionality, test the brakes for unusual noises, and verify that all driver-assistance systems and interior latches operate correctly during a pre-purchase inspection.
