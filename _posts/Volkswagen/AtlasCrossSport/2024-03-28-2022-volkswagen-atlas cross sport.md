**Introduction:**
The 2022 Volkswagen Atlas Cross Sport is a midsize SUV that seats seven passengers. It is available with either a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine that produces 235 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, or a 3.6-liter V6 engine that produces 276 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission. The Atlas Cross Sport is praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and impressive towing capacity. However, it is criticized for its high fuel consumption and lack of off-road capability.

**Engine**
- **2.0L Turbocharged Inline-Four Engine:**
    - **Oil Filter Housing Leak:**
        - Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from the oil filter housing.
        - Solution: Replace the oil filter housing gasket.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    - **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
        - Description/Symptoms: Engine rattling or ticking noises, reduced engine power, or check engine light illuminated.
        - Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner and timing chain.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
- **3.6L V6 Engine:**
    - **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**
- **Eight-Speed Automatic Transmission:**
    - **Transmission Slipping:**
        - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, hesitation or shuddering during acceleration.
        - Solution: Replace the transmission fluid and filter, or rebuild the transmission.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,500
    - **Torque Converter Failure:**
        - Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, shuddering or vibration during acceleration.
        - Solution: Replace the torque converter.
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000
    - **Recalls:**
        - **NHTSA Campaign Number:** 22V-071
            - Description: Transmission may shift into Neutral without warning.
            - Solution: Software update.
        - **NHTSA Campaign Number:** 22V-539
            - Description: Transmission may overheat and fail.
            - Solution: Software update.

**Suspension**
- **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**
- **Electrical Gremlins:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Intermittent power loss, flickering lights, or malfunctioning electronic components.
    - Solution: Inspect the electrical system for loose connections or faulty components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
- **Battery Draining:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Battery loses charge quickly, even when the vehicle is not in use.
    - Solution: Test the battery and replace it if necessary. Inspect the electrical system for parasitic draws.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500

**Exterior**
- **No Issues Found**

**Interior**
- **Interior Noise:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Excessive road noise, wind noise, or rattling noises.
    - Solution: Inspect the door seals, window seals, and weatherstripping for any damage or gaps.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
- **HVAC Issues:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning not blowing cold air, heater not working, or malfunctioning climate control.
    - Solution: Inspect the refrigerant levels, blower motor, and climate control module.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Summary:**

The 2022 Volkswagen Atlas Cross Sport is a reliable SUV with few major issues. The engine and transmission are generally reliable, but there have been some reports of oil leaks, timing chain tensioner failures, and transmission slipping. Electrical gremlins and interior noise are the most common complaints, but these issues are typically not severe or expensive to fix. However, some electrical issues, such as battery draining, can be difficult to diagnose and repair. Overall, the Atlas Cross Sport is a solid choice for a midsize SUV, especially for families who need a spacious and comfortable vehicle.
