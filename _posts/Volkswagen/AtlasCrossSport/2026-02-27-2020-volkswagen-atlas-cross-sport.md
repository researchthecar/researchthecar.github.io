# 2020 Volkswagen Atlas Cross Sport Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Volkswagen Atlas Cross Sport is a stylish, midsize two-row SUV that offers plenty of cabin space and a comfortable ride for families. However, prospective buyers should review common owner complaints regarding electrical anomalies, steering components, and exterior lighting failures before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

This base powerplant uses a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generating approximately 235 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate daily acceleration and respectable efficiency for city driving.

### 3.6L V6

The available 3.6-liter naturally aspirated V6 pushes out around 276 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque, offering smoother power delivery and stronger towing capabilities for buyers needing extra utility.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with both engine options and available in front-wheel or 4Motion all-wheel-drive configurations, this 8-speed automatic generally delivers smooth, responsive gear shifts during normal daily driving.

## Brakes

### Brake System

Brake system feedback highlights occasional premature pad and rotor wear requiring attentive maintenance.

**Premature Brake Wear and Fluid Alerts**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note persistent squeaking, grinding, premature rotor wear, and sudden brake fluid warnings.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and fluid lines for premature wear or leaks during a pre-purchase inspection.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Column

Steering complaints frequently center around mechanical wear in the steering rack leading to unwanted noises and premature component replacement.

**Steering Rack Failure and Clunking Noises**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report hearing a clunking or clicking noise when turning the steering wheel, sometimes requiring full steering rack replacements.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering assembly for play or abnormal noise during test drives; replace the steering rack if internal mechanical wear is confirmed.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting and DRLs

Exterior lighting issues can become costly out-of-pocket expenses due to integrated LED headlight assembly designs.

**Daytime Running Light (DRL) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** LED daytime running lights fail prematurely, often requiring the replacement of the entire headlight assembly.
- **Solution:** Test all exterior lighting before purchase. Replacing failed LED units typically requires installing a new headlight assembly.
- **Cost:** $2300

## Safety

### Air Bags and Forward Collision

Safety electronics require careful validation to ensure driver-assistance features and supplemental restraint systems operate without dashboard error alerts.

**Airbag Warning Light and Unexpected Braking**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warnings illuminate for airbag system errors, while forward-collision sensors may occasionally trigger unexpected emergency braking events.
- **Solution:** Scan the vehicle computer for stored diagnostic trouble codes related to occupant detection systems and advanced driver assistance modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V892000: Visibility:Defroster/Defogger/Hvac System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas+Cross+Sport&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The air conditioning system drain tube could be blocked, causing water to leak inside the vehicle near the air bag control module. A water leak may damage the air bag control module, causing an inadvertent air bag deployment and increasing the risk of injury or a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will repair the drain tube and replace the air bag control module, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V504000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas+Cross+Sport&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." A headlight aimed incorrectly aimed reduces the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will adjust the driver's side headlight, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V505000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas+Cross+Sport&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Parts of the front seat frame and backrest adjuster may not have been welded properly. In the event of a rear-end crash, the backrest adjustment could break, possibly reducing the amount of occupant restraint, thus increasing risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the front seats, and, as necessary, replace the seatback frames, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V712000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas+Cross+Sport&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A damaged steering knuckle can fail, causing a loss of steering, thereby increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen has notified owners, and dealers will inspect and, as necessary, replace the steering knuckle, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V702000: Tires:Sidewall](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas+Cross+Sport&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Both conditions increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will inspect DOT date code and mold number on the vehicle's tires to see if they are affected, replacing them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V026000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas+Cross+Sport&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A fractured steering knuckle can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, as necessary, replace one or both steering knuckles, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V086000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas+Cross+Sport&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." Without the reduced GVWR label, the vehicle could be overloaded, leading to a loss of control and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install the reduced GVWR label, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V152000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas+Cross+Sport&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The door wiring harness electrical contacts may corrode, disrupting the electrical connection and delaying the deployment of the driver or passenger front side air bag during a side impact crash. A delayed air bag deployment can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will secure and, as necessary, replace any the front door wiring harnesses showing a fault code, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Volkswagen Atlas Cross Sport is a comfortable, spacious choice for daily commuting and family road trips, but buyers should carefully verify vehicle history and electronic functions. Pay close attention to steering feel, exterior LED lighting operation, and service records to avoid unexpected repair costs.
