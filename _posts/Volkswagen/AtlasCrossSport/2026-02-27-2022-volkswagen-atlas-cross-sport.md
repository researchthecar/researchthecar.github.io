# 2022 Volkswagen Atlas Cross Sport Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Volkswagen Atlas Cross Sport is a stylish and spacious midsize crossover that shares its core platform and mechanical components with the standard Atlas. While owners appreciate its comfort and road manners, used car buyers should note several recurring complaints regarding engine performance, electrical glitches, and braking noises. Reviewing maintenance records and performing a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help mitigate potential ownership surprises.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

A 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing approximately 235 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate daily power and efficiency. Buyers should inspect for any minor oil leaks or turbo-related component wear.

**Throttle and PCV System Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated EPC light, rough idle, and oil leaks around the charge air inlet and diverter valve.
- **Solution:** Inspect the PCV valve, turbo diverter valve, and throttle control system, replacing faulty seals or valves as needed.

### 3.6L V6

A 3.6-liter naturally aspirated VR6 engine rated at 276 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque, offering smooth acceleration for towing and highway passing. While generally robust, a small number of owners have reported stalling and electrical integration concerns.

**Engine Stalling and Shifter Defects**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected engine stalling while driving or difficulty getting the vehicle to restart and stay in park.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scan of the powertrain control module and inspection of the gear selector switch and ignition components.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed traditional automatic transmission paired with either front-wheel drive or 4Motion all-wheel drive, delivering smooth and responsive shifts. No widespread or recurring mechanical transmission failures dominate owner complaints for this model year.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The hydraulic brake system provides solid stopping power for a heavy crossover, though several drivers experience early pad and rotor noise that requires dealer inspection.

**Premature Brake Grinding and Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Loud metallic grinding, scratching, and squealing sounds when depressing the brake pedal, especially after the vehicle has been sitting.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads and rotors for premature wear or glazing; replace pads and resurface or replace rotors if necessary.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical architecture controls modern lighting, infotainment, and driver aids, with the most notable complaints centering around costly LED headlight assembly replacements.

**Daytime Running Light (DRL) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Daytime running LED bulbs or assemblies failing prematurely on either the driver or passenger side.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed LED bulb or entire headlamp assembly according to dealership specifications.
- **Cost:** $1,400 - $2,000

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V152000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas+Cross+Sport&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The door wiring harness electrical contacts may corrode, disrupting the electrical connection and delaying the deployment of the driver or passenger front side air bag during a side impact crash. A delayed air bag deployment can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will secure and, as necessary, replace any the front door wiring harnesses showing a fault code, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V245000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Fuel Injection System:Injectors](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas+Cross+Sport&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Long fuel injectors with missing filters may leak fuel, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the long fuel injectors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V514000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas+Cross+Sport&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The manufacturing process of the eMMC memory module in the infotainment system may cause the rearview camera image not to display. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not display an image reduces the driver's rear view, increasing the.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the infotainment software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V657000: Trailer Hitches](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas+Cross+Sport&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Loose bolts may fall out, causing the remaining bolts to break, which could lead to separation of the trailer hitch and trailer, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will tighten the trailer hitch bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V753000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas+Cross+Sport&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The engine connecting rod bearings may become damaged, which can result in engine failure. A damaged engine can result in engine failure and a stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace an affected engine, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V807000: Suspension:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas+Cross+Sport&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The driver-side rear trailing arm bolts may not have been tightened properly, allowing them to loosen and fall out. Unsecured bolts may cause the trailing arm to fracture, resulting in a sudden loss of vehicle control and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver side rear trailing arm bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V464000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas+Cross+Sport&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A deactivated passenger air bag increases the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the PODS sensor mat and wiring harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Volkswagen Atlas Cross Sport is an appealing used choice for families wanting distinctive styling and generous interior space. While major drivetrain components are generally dependable, prospective buyers should carefully check the brakes for premature wear, test all electronic systems and exterior lighting, and review service records to ensure all applicable safety recalls have been addressed.
