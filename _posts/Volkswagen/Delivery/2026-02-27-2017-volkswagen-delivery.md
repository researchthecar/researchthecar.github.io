# 2017 Volkswagen Delivery

### Overview
The 2017 Volkswagen Delivery, a versatile light commercial vehicle, is generally recognized for its robust construction and utilitarian design, aimed at providing reliable service for businesses. While specific model year data can be sparse for commercial vehicles, general trends for this generation suggest a focus on durability. Owners often praise its practicality and fuel efficiency, particularly in diesel variants. However, like any vehicle, certain components can experience issues over time, especially under heavy usage. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for prospective buyers and current owners.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L TDI Diesel
**Turbocharger Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, excessive smoke, whistling noises, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the turbocharger unit.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**EGR Valve Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, poor acceleration, increased emissions, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Cleaning or replacement of the EGR valve.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


### 3.0L V6 TDI Diesel
**Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Clogging**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine performance, increased fuel consumption, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Forced regeneration or DPF replacement.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2,000


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Clutch kit replacement (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $700 - $1,500


### 7-Speed DSG Automatic
**Mechatronics Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Harsh shifting, transmission warning lights, inability to select gears.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the mechatronics unit.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Ball Joints/Tie Rod Ends**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn suspension components.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, electrical system malfunctions.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900

**Wiring Harness Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent electrical faults, corrosion on connectors.
- **Solution:** Inspection, repair, or replacement of damaged wiring.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1,000


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Seals
**Door Seal Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Water leaks into the cabin, wind noise.
- **Solution:** Replacement of door seals.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, inconsistent fan speed.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Volkswagen Delivery demonstrates a generally solid reliability record for a commercial vehicle, with most reported issues stemming from components subjected to significant wear and tear under demanding operational conditions. The diesel engines, particularly the 2.0L TDI, can experience issues with the turbocharger and EGR system, while the 3.0L V6 may face DPF clogging. Automatic transmissions, specifically the DSG, can be prone to mechatronics unit failures. Suspension components like ball joints and tie rod ends are common wear items. Electrical issues such as alternator failure and wiring harness problems are also noted. While not immune to faults, addressing these potential issues proactively can ensure the continued dependable service of the Volkswagen Delivery.
