**Introduction:**

The 2020 Volkswagen Delivery is a compact commercial van offered in both diesel and gasoline engine options. The diesel engine is a 2.0L inline-4 producing 144 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed manual transmission. The gasoline engine is a 2.5L inline-4 producing 172 horsepower and 172 lb-ft of torque, paired with either a 6-speed manual transmission or a 6-speed automatic transmission. The Delivery is praised for its spacious cargo area, comfortable interior, and fuel efficiency, but it has received some criticism for its lack of power and slow acceleration.

**Engine**

**2.0L Diesel Engine**

* **Intake Manifold Flap Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold flaps may fail, leading to reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption, and a check engine light.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Gasoline Engine**

* **Fuel Injector Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The fuel injectors may fail, resulting in rough idling, poor acceleration, and a check engine light.
    * Solution: Replace the fuel injectors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Synchronizer Ring Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The synchronizer rings may wear prematurely, leading to difficulty shifting gears, especially into 2nd and 3rd gear.
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Solenoid Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The solenoids may fail, causing the transmission to shift erratically or not shift at all.
    * Solution: Replace the solenoids.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Mount Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The front strut mounts may fail, leading to a clunking or popping noise when turning or driving over rough roads.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator may fail, causing the battery to drain and the electrical system to malfunction.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The door handles may become loose or stick, making it difficult to open or close the doors.
    * Solution: Replace or repair the door handles.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Squeaks and Rattles:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard may creak or rattle, especially on rough roads.
    * Solution: Tighten or replace the dashboard components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2020 Volkswagen Delivery has a few common issues, with the engine and transmission being the most concerning. The intake manifold flap failure on the diesel engine can lead to serious performance problems, while the transmission issues can be expensive to repair. The suspension, electrical, and exterior issues are less severe but still worth considering. Overall, the Delivery is a reliable and practical commercial van, but it is important to be aware of these potential problems before purchasing one.
