**Introduction:**

The 1998 Volkswagen Pointer, a subcompact sedan, was offered with a variety of engine and transmission options. It featured a spacious interior, comfortable ride, and economical fuel consumption. However, it had some drawbacks, such as a lack of power and handling agility.

**Engine**

**1.6L Petrol**
- **Leaking Head Gasket:** A faulty head gasket can cause coolant and oil leaks, overheating, and rough idling. Replacement of the head gasket and associated components is required. ($500 - $1000+)

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**
- **Worn Synchronizers:** Over time, the synchronizers in the manual transmission can wear out, leading to difficulty shifting gears. Replacement of the synchronizers and associated parts is necessary. ($300 - $500+)

**4-Speed Automatic**
- **Faulty Torque Converter:** A malfunctioning torque converter can result in slipping gears, rough shifting, and excessive vibration. Replacement of the torque converter and transmission fluid is required. ($800 - $1200+)

**Suspension**

**No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

**Faulty Alternator:** The alternator may fail, resulting in insufficient charging of the battery and electrical problems. Replacement of the alternator is required. ($300 - $500+)

**Exterior**

**Cracked Tail Light Lenses:** Excessive heat or impact can cause the tail light lenses to crack or become discolored. Replacement of the affected tail lights is necessary. ($100 - $200+)

**Interior**

- **Faulty Window Switches:** The power window switches may malfunction, causing the windows to become inoperable. Replacement of the affected switches is required. ($100 - $200+)
- **Broken Door Handles:** The interior door handles can become brittle and break over time. Replacement of the door handles is required. ($50 - $150+)

**Summary:**

The 1998 Volkswagen Pointer is a reliable and affordable vehicle with a few common issues. The severity of the issues is generally minor, with most repairs costing under $500. However, some more significant problems, such as a faulty torque converter or a leaking head gasket, can be costly to rectify.
