# 2018 Volkswagen Beetle Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Volkswagen Beetle offers iconic retro styling combined with modern turbocharged performance. As buyers consider a used model, understanding its powertrain options and minor reported quirks can help ensure a satisfying ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 174 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque, providing lively acceleration for daily driving. It is generally regarded as a robust and refined power plant when properly maintained.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic with Tiptronic

The 6-speed traditional automatic transmission delivers smooth gear changes and pairs well with the turbocharged engine across various trims. Owners typically experience good long-term reliability with routine fluid changes.

## Electrical

### Body Electrical and Infotainment

Electrical complaints are infrequent but typically center around intermittent infotainment glitches or accessory control anomalies.

**Spontaneous Window Operation**
- **Symptoms:** Driver and passenger side windows may lower or raise independently without driver input or even with the key removed from the ignition.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the window regulator switches, door control modules, or body control module for faults.

**Center Display Blackout**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment touchscreen occasionally goes black, temporarily disabling the backup camera, radio, and phone connectivity.
- **Solution:** Restart the vehicle after a brief waiting period, or visit a dealership to check for software updates or head unit hardware issues.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V369000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Beetle&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A reduction of braking performance can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Audi dealers will bleed the rear brakes. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V615000: Electrical System:Ignition:Anti-Theft:Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Beetle&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A build-up of silicate on the shift lever micro switch contacts may enable the key to be removed from the ignition when the transmission shift lever is not in "Park." Removing the key while the shift lever is in a position other than "Park" increases the risk of an unintended vehicle rollaway that may result in personal injury or a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will install an additional switch and circuit board, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20E064000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Master Cylinder](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Beetle&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If there is a brake fluid leak, it can reduce braking ability, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V834000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Beetle&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** An inflator explosion may result in sharp metal fragments striking the driver or other occupants resulting in serious injury or death.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver's side front air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Volkswagen Beetle is generally a dependable and fun-to-drive used vehicle option. Prospective buyers should thoroughly test all electrical accessories, windows, and infotainment functions during a pre-purchase inspection to confirm everything operates as intended.
