# 2017 Volkswagen Beetle Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 Volkswagen Beetle, buyers will find a stylish and iconic compact car that generally delivers a fun driving experience. While overall ownership feedback is fairly manageable, prospective owners should review potential owner-reported quirks regarding electrical accessories, window regulators, and suspension wear.

---

## Engine

### 1.8L Turbocharged I4

The 1.8-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 170 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque. It offers a balanced mix of efficiency and peppy acceleration for daily driving, though routine maintenance is key to long-term reliability.

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine delivers around 210 horsepower for higher-performance trims like the R-Line. It provides sporty power delivery while maintaining respectable highway efficiency, though buyers should verify proper maintenance history.

### Rear Electric Motor and Electronics

Electrical architecture supports various convenience features, though occasional issues related to moisture or accessory modules have been documented by owners.

**Persistent Rear Motor Noise After Shutdown**
- **Symptoms:** A noticeable electrical motor noise or burning odor emanates from the rear area of the vehicle after the engine is shut off, particularly following rain exposure.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the electrical system and affected modules for moisture intrusion or relay faults.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The conventional 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with standard front-wheel drive on various trims. It offers smooth, traditional shifting dynamics and generally reliable everyday performance when properly serviced.

## Suspension

### Front Suspension Struts

Suspension components handle daily road imperfections, but owners have occasionally reported isolated issues such as prematurely fractured coil springs.

**Broken Front Coil Spring**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may hear an unusual clanking or snapping noise coming from the front wheel housing while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect the suspension assembly and replace the broken coil spring and related strut components as necessary.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera and Display

Safety and driver assistance features generally function as intended, though minor display glitches can occasionally affect rearview visibility systems.

**Intermittent Rearview Camera Black Screen**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display shows a blank or black screen when shifting into reverse, rendering the backup camera temporarily inoperable.
- **Solution:** Inspect the camera wiring harness, trunk lid connections, or reset the infotainment display unit.

## Exterior

### Window Regulators

Interior cabin components offer a retro aesthetic, though mechanical window regulators can experience premature wear over time.

**Power Window Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The window glass fails to roll up or down smoothly, often indicating a failed window regulator mechanism.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty window regulator assembly to restore proper power window operation.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V615000: Electrical System:Ignition:Anti-Theft:Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Beetle&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A build-up of silicate on the shift lever micro switch contacts may enable the key to be removed from the ignition when the transmission shift lever is not in "Park." Removing the key while the shift lever is in a position other than "Park" increases the risk of an unintended vehicle rollaway that may result in personal injury or a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will install an additional switch and circuit board, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V136000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Beetle&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." Overloading the vehicle may affect vehicle handling or result in tire damage, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will install a corrected tire information label, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20E064000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Master Cylinder](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Beetle&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If there is a brake fluid leak, it can reduce braking ability, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V834000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Beetle&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** An inflator explosion may result in sharp metal fragments striking the driver or other occupants resulting in serious injury or death.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver's side front air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Volkswagen Beetle is a charming and generally dependable used vehicle, provided buyers carefully inspect potential trouble spots like window regulators and electrical accessories. A pre-purchase inspection by a knowledgeable mechanic is recommended to catch any hidden suspension or electrical quirks before finalizing your purchase.
