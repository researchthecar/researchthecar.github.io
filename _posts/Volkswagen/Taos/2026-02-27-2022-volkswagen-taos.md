# 2022 Volkswagen Taos Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Volkswagen Taos enters the compact SUV market with European styling, excellent cargo space, and nimble handling. However, used car buyers should approach early model years with care. Owners have reported a notable volume of mechanical complaints, particularly involving engine coolant leaks and head gasket failures. While it offers a comfortable ride and modern features, thorough pre-purchase inspections are strongly recommended for used shoppers.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 1.5-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 158 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power and efficiency for daily commuting. Buyers should carefully evaluate this engine option due to documented owner reports concerning coolant leaks and head gasket durability.

**Coolant Leaks and Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated low coolant warning light, sweet smell of coolant, or engine overheating while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect the back of the engine for leaks; replacement of the cylinder head gasket and associated components may be required.

**Engine Stalling and Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected engine stalling or significant lag and hesitation when pressing the throttle from a stopped position.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scan of engine management systems and software updates or component replacement as advised by a technician.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic (AWD)

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission is paired with all-wheel-drive models to deliver quick and seamless gear changes. Some owners have experienced early mechanical or electronic faults requiring professional intervention.

**Mechatronic Unit Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Premature failure of the mechatronic unit leading to power loss, starting difficulties, or unexpected stalling.
- **Solution:** Replacement or reprogramming of the mechatronic control unit by a qualified service center.

### 8-Speed Conventional Automatic (FWD)

The 8-speed conventional automatic transmission comes equipped on front-wheel-drive models, offering smooth and reliable power delivery during everyday driving.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Braking components provide secure stopping power under normal conditions, though some drivers experience accelerated wear and noise on rear brake assemblies.

**Premature Rear Brake Wear and Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Screeching, grinding, or abnormal noise when applying the brake pedal at low speeds, alongside early wear on rear brake pads and rotors.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear brake pads and rotors, and replace worn components as necessary.

## Exterior

### Exterior Structure and Roof Seams

Exterior body panels feature modern styling, but isolated owner reports highlight premature paint peeling and clear coat defects along upper roof surfaces.

**Paint Peeling and Chipping**
- **Symptoms:** Clear coat peeling, chipping, or bubbling along exterior panel edges and roof seams.
- **Solution:** Professional paint touch-up or panel repainting by an auto body repair specialist.

## Safety

### Infotainment and Safety Modules

Electrical systems power a wide array of driver-assistance and convenience features, though occasional glitches in infotainment displays and modules have been noted by owners.

**Backup Camera and Display Blackout**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment display screen goes black or freezes during operation, causing the rearview camera to become unavailable while reversing.
- **Solution:** Reset the infotainment system or replace faulty display and module units.

## Recall Check

**[Recall 21V651000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Taos&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak may cause a stall, increasing the risk of a crash. A fuel leak also increases the risk of fire in the presence of an ignition source.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the connection and replace the fuel supply line and connector as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V615000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Engine Control Module (Ecu/Ecm):Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Taos&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The engine control unit (ECU) software may cause a stall when coming to a stop. An engine stall increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Volkswagen Taos offers plenty of interior room and pleasant driving dynamics, making it an appealing choice in the small SUV segment. However, because early models carry reports of significant engine coolant loss, head gasket concerns, and electrical quirks, buyers should proceed cautiously. Prioritize a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a knowledgeable mechanic to verify engine health and check for any history of fluid leaks before finalizing a purchase.
