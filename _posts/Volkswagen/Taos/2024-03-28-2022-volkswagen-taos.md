## 2022 Volkswagen Taos

The 2022 Volkswagen Taos is a subcompact SUV that slots beneath the larger Tiguan in the automaker's lineup. It is available with a choice of two engines: a 1.5-liter turbocharged four-cylinder with 158 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque, or a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder with 184 horsepower and 221 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are mated to an eight-speed automatic transmission.

People like the Taos for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and fuel-efficient engines. However, some have complained about its lack of power, especially with the base engine.

**Engine**

**1.5-liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

* **Carbon Buildup on Intake Valves**
Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, rough idle, and increased fuel consumption.
Solution: Clean the intake valves.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**2.0-liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

* No Issues Found

**Transmission**

**Eight-Speed Automatic**

* **Shift Hesitation or Jerking**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission may hesitate or jerk when shifting gears.
Solution: Update the transmission software or replace the transmission.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Noisy Rear Suspension**
Description/Symptoms: A clunking or rattling noise coming from the rear of the vehicle, especially over bumps.
Solution: Replace the rear sway bar links.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electrical System Malfunctions**
Description/Symptoms: Various electrical system malfunctions, such as flickering lights, power window failures, and infotainment system issues.
Solution: Update the electrical system software or replace faulty components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Loose Exterior Trim**
Description/Symptoms: Exterior trim pieces, such as door handles or bumper covers, may become loose or fall off.
Solution: Reattach or replace the loose trim pieces.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Rattle**
Description/Symptoms: A rattling noise coming from the sunroof, especially when driving over rough roads.
Solution: Adjust the sunroof alignment or replace the sunroof seal.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

* **Climate Control Malfunctions**
Description/Symptoms: The climate control system may not work properly, such as failing to heat or cool the cabin.
Solution: Update the climate control software or replace faulty components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

## Summary

The 2022 Volkswagen Taos is a well-rounded subcompact SUV with a spacious interior and fuel-efficient engines. However, it has been plagued by a number of issues, including carbon buildup on the intake valves, transmission problems, and electrical system malfunctions. While some of these issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more severe and costly.
