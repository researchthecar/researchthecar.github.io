**Introduction:**

The 2017 Volkswagen Constellation is a heavy-duty truck with a gross vehicle weight rating (GVWR) of up to 33,000 lb. It is available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 6.7L V8 diesel engine with 350 hp and 660 lb-ft of torque, and a 10.3L V8 diesel engine with 450 hp and 1,250 lb-ft of torque. Both engines can be paired with a 12-speed automated manual transmission (AMT).

The Constellation is known for its powerful engines, rugged construction, and comfortable interior. However, it has also been plagued by a number of issues, some of which are severe and expensive to fix.

**Engine**

**6.7L V8 Diesel Engine**

- **Fuel Injector Failure:** The fuel injectors can fail, causing the engine to run rough, misfire, and lose power. The fix involves replacing the faulty injectors, which can cost around $1,000 - $2,000+.
- **Turbocharger Failure:** The turbocharger can fail, causing a loss of power and decreased fuel economy. The fix involves replacing the turbocharger, which can cost around $2,000 - $3,000+.

**10.3L V8 Diesel Engine**

- **Head Gasket Failure:** The head gasket can fail, causing coolant and oil to leak. The fix involves replacing the head gasket, which can cost around $2,000 - $3,000+.
- **EGR Valve Failure:** The exhaust gas recirculation (EGR) valve can fail, causing the engine to run rough and emit excessive emissions. The fix involves replacing the EGR valve, which can cost around $500 - $1,000+.

**Transmission**

**12-Speed AMT**

- **Shifting Problems:** The transmission may have difficulty shifting gears, causing rough or delayed shifts. The fix involves reprogramming the transmission control module (TCM), which can cost around $200 - $500+.
- **Clutch Failure:** The clutch can fail, causing the transmission to slip or not engage. The fix involves replacing the clutch, which can cost around $1,500 - $2,000+.

**Suspension**

- **Leaf Spring Failure:** The leaf springs can fail, causing the truck to sag and ride rough. The fix involves replacing the leaf springs, which can cost around $500 - $1,000+.
- **Rear Axle Bearing Failure:** The rear axle bearings can fail, causing a grinding noise and loss of stability. The fix involves replacing the bearings, which can cost around $1,000 - $1,500+.

**Electrical**

- **Battery Drain:** The battery can drain prematurely, causing the truck to fail to start. The fix involves identifying and repairing the source of the drain, which can cost around $200 - $500+.
- **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the battery to not charge properly. The fix involves replacing the alternator, which can cost around $500 - $1,000+.

**Exterior**

- **Rust:** The body panels can rust prematurely, especially in areas where salt is used on the roads. The fix involves repairing or replacing the rusted panels, which can cost around $500 - $2,000+.
- **Headlight Failure:** The headlights can fail prematurely, causing reduced visibility at night. The fix involves replacing the headlights, which can cost around $200 - $500+.
