The 2002 Volkswagen Cabrio is a front-wheel-drive convertible powered by a 2.0L SOHC 8-valve 4-cylinder engine producing 115 horsepower and 122 lb-ft of torque. It was offered exclusively with a 4-speed automatic transmission. Owners often appreciate the Cabrio's simple design, affordability, and open-air driving experience, while common dislikes include its somewhat dated styling, modest performance, and potential for reliability issues due to its age.

**Engine**

**2.0L SOHC 8-Valve 4-Cylinder**

* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can develop from various locations, including the valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket, rear main seal, and oil cooler. Symptoms include visible oil leaks, a burning oil smell, and low oil level.
    * Solution: Replacing the affected gaskets or seals. In some cases, the oil cooler itself may need replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+ depending on the location and severity of the leak.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Coolant Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks can occur from hoses, the water pump, the coolant flange, or the radiator. Symptoms include visible coolant leaks, low coolant level, and overheating.
    * Solution: Replacing the leaking hoses, water pump, coolant flange, or radiator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $20 - $400+ depending on the location and affected part.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Mass Air Flow (MAF) Sensor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The MAF sensor can fail, leading to poor engine performance, rough idling, and a check engine light.
    * Solution: Replacing the MAF sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Oxygen (O2) Sensor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: O2 sensor failures can cause poor fuel economy, rough running, and a check engine light.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty O2 sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Slipping or Erratic Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission may slip between gears, shift erratically, or fail to shift altogether.
    * Solution: This could involve a transmission fluid change, but more often requires replacement of solenoids, valve body repair, or a full transmission rebuild.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (fluid change) - $2,000+ (rebuild or replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Delayed Engagement**
    * Description/Symptoms: Delay when shifting into drive or reverse.
    * Solution: Could be due to low fluid, but often indicates internal wear requiring rebuild or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (fluid change) - $2,000+ (rebuild or replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Worn Suspension Bushings**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn bushings in the control arms, sway bar, and struts can cause clunking noises, poor handling, and uneven tire wear.
    * Solution: Replacing the worn bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the number of bushings replaced.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Strut/Shock Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking struts or shocks can cause a bouncy ride, poor handling, and reduced braking performance.
    * Solution: Replacing the struts or shocks.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per axle.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Convertible Top Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The convertible top mechanism can experience problems, including motor failure, hydraulic leaks, or switch malfunctions. This results in the top being slow to open or close, or not functioning at all.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the specific issue, which could involve replacing the motor, hydraulic pump, hydraulic cylinders, or switches.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+ depending on the severity of the issue and parts needed.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Power Window Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The power window regulators and motors can fail, causing the windows to become inoperable.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty regulator or motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400 per window.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Central Locking System Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: The central locking system can fail, causing the doors to not lock or unlock properly.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the locking system, which could involve replacing actuators, control modules, or wiring.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Convertible Top Wear and Tear**
    * Description/Symptoms: The fabric convertible top is prone to wear and tear, including tears, rips, and leaks, especially along seams and near the rear window.
    * Solution: Repairing small tears with patches or replacing the entire top.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150 (patching), $800 - $2000+ (replacement).
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Rust**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust can develop in common areas such as the wheel wells, rocker panels, and around the windshield and rear window seals.
    * Solution: Repairing or replacing rusted panels, depending on the extent of the damage.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+ depending on severity and area.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Seat Upholstery Degradation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cloth seat material can tear or wear through, especially on the driver's seat.
    * Solution: Repairing or replacing the seat upholstery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Dashboard Cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard can crack due to sun exposure and age.
    * Solution: Replacing the dashboard or using a dashboard cover.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 (cover) - $800+ (replacement).
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2002 Volkswagen Cabrio can be an enjoyable convertible, but potential buyers should be aware of the common issues associated with its age and design. Engine oil and coolant leaks, convertible top malfunctions, and transmission problems are among the most frequent and potentially costly repairs. Rust can also be a concern, depending on the vehicle's history and location. While some repairs can be relatively inexpensive, others, such as transmission rebuilds or convertible top replacements, can be quite costly. Thorough inspection and preventative maintenance are crucial for minimizing potential issues and enjoying a reliable ownership experience.
