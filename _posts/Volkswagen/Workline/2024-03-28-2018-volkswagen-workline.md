**Introduction:**

The 2018 Volkswagen Workline is a compact cargo van powered by a 2.5L inline-4 engine mated to a 6-speed automatic transmission. The Workline delivers 170 horsepower and 170 lb-ft of torque. It offers a practical and spacious cargo area with a payload capacity of over 1,500 pounds. While praised for its versatility and performance, the Workline has also faced some reported issues affecting various components.

**Engine**

**2.5L Inline-4**

**Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Premature wear and failure of the timing chain tensioner, leading to a rattling noise and potential engine damage.
Solution: Replacement of the timing chain tensioner and associated components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
Recalls: None Found

**Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
Description/Symptoms: Deterioration of the valve cover gasket, causing an oil leak and potential burning smell.
Solution: Replacement of the valve cover gasket.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

**Transmission Slippage**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears or sudden loss of power during acceleration, indicating transmission slippage.
Solution: Rebuild or replacement of the transmission.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Lower Control Arm Bushings**
Description/Symptoms: Premature wear or failure of the lower control arm bushings, leading to excessive play and a rough ride.
Solution: Replacement of the lower control arm bushings.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Battery Discharge**
Description/Symptoms: Rapid battery discharge or failure in cold weather or after long periods of inactivity.
Solution: Replacement of the battery or alternator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Tailgate Latch Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning tailgate latch, causing the tailgate to open unintentionally or fail to close properly.
Solution: Replacement of the tailgate latch and associated components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Radio Malfunction**
Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or complete failure of the radio system, including loss of audio and display.
Solution: Replacement of the radio head unit or associated components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2018 Volkswagen Workline has faced several reported issues, primarily affecting the engine, transmission, and exterior components. While some issues, like the timing chain tensioner failure, are relatively severe and costly to repair, others, such as the radio malfunction, are less significant. Overall, the cost of repairs for the reported issues can vary widely, potentially impacting the vehicle's reliability and long-term ownership costs.
