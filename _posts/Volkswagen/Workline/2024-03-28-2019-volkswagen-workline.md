**Introduction to the 2019 Volkswagen Workline**

The 2019 Volkswagen Workline is a compact commercial vehicle available in single-cab and crew-cab configurations. It comes with a standard 2.0L inline-four engine producing 184 horsepower and 221 lb-ft of torque paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. Rear-wheel drive is standard, and all-wheel drive is optional.

Owners appreciate the Workline's powerful engine, spacious cabin, and practical features. However, some issues have been reported with certain components.

**Engine**

**2.0L Inline-Four**

* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature wear of the timing chain tensioner, resulting in rattling or ticking noises, reduced engine power, and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replacement of the timing chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slippage**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, delayed engagement, or loss of power while driving.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of transmission components, such as the torque converter or solenoids.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Leaking Front Struts**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the front shocks, causing a soft and bouncy ride, reduced handling, and potential safety concerns.
    * Solution: Replacement of the front struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800 per strut
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery discharges quickly, resulting in difficulty starting the vehicle.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of electrical system components, such as faulty sensors or wiring.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Body Panels**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature rust development on the body panels, especially around the wheel wells and lower door panels.
    * Solution: Rust repair or replacement of affected body panels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ per panel
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Cracked Dashboard**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks or splits in the plastic dashboard material.
    * Solution: Dashboard replacement or repair.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2019 Volkswagen Workline has received mixed reviews. While owners appreciate its power and practicality, certain issues have been reported, particularly with the engine, transmission, and suspension. The severity of these issues can vary, and the cost to fix them can be significant. It is recommended to research and consider known issues before making a purchase decision.
