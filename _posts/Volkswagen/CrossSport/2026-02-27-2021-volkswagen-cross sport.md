# 2021 Volkswagen Cross Sport

### Overview
The 2021 Volkswagen Atlas Cross Sport is a mid-size SUV that offers a comfortable ride and a spacious interior, appealing to families and those seeking more utility than a standard sedan. It generally presents a solid reliability profile for its model year, with most owners reporting a positive ownership experience. Common areas of concern, while not widespread, tend to be concentrated in specific electronic features and occasional powertrain nuances, particularly with the turbocharged engine option. However, for the most part, the Atlas Cross Sport is a dependable vehicle that balances practicality with a touch of sportiness, making it a competitive choice in its segment. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring its long-term performance.

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4
**Turbocharger Lag/Boost Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced acceleration, hesitation during acceleration, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential turbocharger replacement or repair.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant level, visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, overheating.
- **Solution:** Inspection and repair/replacement of leaking hoses, gaskets, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Volkswagen Atlas Cross Sport demonstrates a generally good reliability record. While the 2.0L turbocharged engine has seen some reports of turbocharger issues and coolant leaks, and the transmission can occasionally exhibit shifting quirks, these are not universal problems. The V6 engine and other major systems like suspension, exterior, and interior components are largely trouble-free. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment screen and driver-assist sensors, are the most frequently cited minor annoyances. Overall, with diligent maintenance and addressing any early warning signs, the Atlas Cross Sport is expected to provide reliable service for its owners.
