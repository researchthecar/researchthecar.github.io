The 2021 Volkswagen Atlas Cross Sport is a mid-size SUV known for its stylish design and comfortable ride. It offers two engine options: a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder producing 235 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.6-liter V6 generating 276 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often appreciate the spacious interior, user-friendly technology, and smooth ride. However, some common complaints include issues with the infotainment system, some reliability concerns, and higher fuel consumption compared to competitors.

**Engine**

**2.0L TSI 4-Cylinder**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, check engine light illuminated, whistling sound from the engine bay. Can be caused by oil starvation or general wear and tear.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.6L VR6**

*   **Carbon Buildup**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, decreased fuel economy, and a noticeable decrease in overall engine performance.
    *   Solution: Cleaning the intake valves by walnut blasting.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (All Engines)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or jerky shifts between gears, particularly at lower speeds. Hesitation or delayed engagement when accelerating from a stop.
    *   Solution: Software update, transmission fluid change, or valve body replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500 for software update, $200 - $400 for fluid change, $800 - $2000+ for valve body replacement.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Bushing Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the front or rear suspension when driving over bumps. Reduced handling precision and stability.
    *   Solution: Replacement of worn bushings (control arm bushings, sway bar bushings).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600 per bushing replacement, depending on location and labor.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, lagging, unresponsive touchscreen, Bluetooth connectivity problems, and issues with Apple CarPlay/Android Auto.
    *   Solution: Software updates, system reset, or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 for software update, $100-$300 for system reset, $800 - $1500+ for infotainment unit replacement.
    * Recalls: None Found
*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: False alarms, intermittent operation, or complete failure of parking sensors or blind spot monitoring system.
    *   Solution: Replacement of faulty sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per sensor replacement.
    * Recalls: None Found
*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle is difficult to start, or the battery is dead in the morning.
    *   Solution: Battery replacement. Check for parasitic draw.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin, especially after rain.
    *   Solution: Cleaning the sunroof drains, resealing the sunroof, or replacing the sunroof seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400 for cleaning drains or resealing, $500+ for seal replacement.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various rattling noises from the dashboard, door panels, or rear cargo area.
    *   Solution: Identifying the source of the rattle and tightening loose components or applying sound-dampening material.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Highly variable, depending on the source and complexity. Can range from DIY fix to $100 - $500+ at a shop.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2021 Volkswagen Atlas Cross Sport has a range of potential issues. Some issues like carbon buildup and bushing wear are relatively inexpensive to fix, with costs ranging from a few hundred dollars. More serious problems, such as transmission issues, sensor failures or turbocharger failures, can be more expensive, potentially costing several thousand dollars to repair. While not all vehicles will experience these issues, being aware of them can help potential buyers make an informed decision.
