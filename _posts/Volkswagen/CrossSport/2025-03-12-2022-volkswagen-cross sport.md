The 2022 Volkswagen Atlas Cross Sport is a mid-size SUV known for its stylish design, spacious interior, and comfortable ride. Engine options include a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing 235 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.6-liter V6 engine delivering 276 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the Cross Sport's ample cargo space, user-friendly technology, and smooth driving experience. Some common criticisms include fuel economy and the use of lower-quality interior materials in certain areas.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noise from the engine, loss of power, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.6L V6**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil spots under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level. Common leak points are valve cover gaskets and the oil pan gasket.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking gasket(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ depending on the location and complexity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (Both Engine Options)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or jerky gear changes, delayed engagement, slipping between gears.
    *   Solution: Transmission software update, or in severe cases, valve body replacement or complete transmission replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (software update) - $4,000+ (transmission replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Bushing Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the front or rear suspension, especially when going over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replace worn control arm bushings or sway bar bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ depending on the affected bushing and labor rates.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, lagging, unresponsive touchscreen, Bluetooth connectivity problems.
    *   Solution: Software update, or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,200+ (unit replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Sensor Failures (Adaptive Cruise Control, Lane Assist)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Warning lights on the dashboard, adaptive cruise control or lane assist systems not functioning properly.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the faulty sensor (e.g., radar sensor, camera) and replace it.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+ depending on the sensor type and location.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water stains on the headliner, wet carpets after rain.
    *   Solution: Clean and reseal the sunroof drains, or replace the sunroof seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling sounds coming from the dashboard, door panels, or rear cargo area.
    *   Solution: Locating and securing the source of the rattle, which may involve tightening screws, adding foam padding, or replacing loose trim pieces.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+ depending on the severity and location.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Volkswagen Atlas Cross Sport has a few common issues reported by owners. Engine issues, particularly turbocharger failure in the 2.0L engine and oil leaks in the 3.6L, can be relatively costly to repair. Transmission problems, rough shifting, and hesitation are also reported and might require more extensive repairs. The electrical system, especially the infotainment, can also pose issues. Overall, the severity of these issues varies, but addressing them promptly can prevent further damage and maintain the vehicle's reliability.
