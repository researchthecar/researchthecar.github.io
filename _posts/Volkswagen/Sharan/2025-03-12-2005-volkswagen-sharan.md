The 2005 Volkswagen Sharan is a versatile multi-purpose vehicle (MPV) known for its spacious interior and practicality. Engine options included a 2.0L inline-4 petrol engine, a 1.8L Turbocharged inline-4 petrol engine, and a 1.9L TDI diesel engine. The 1.9L TDI, one of the most popular choices, produced around 115 horsepower and 225 lb-ft of torque depending on the specific version. Transmission options were typically a 5-speed manual, 6-speed manual, or a 5-speed automatic. Owners generally appreciated the Sharan's roominess and comfortable ride, making it ideal for families. Common complaints often revolved around reliability issues and the cost of repairs, especially concerning electrical components and some engine-specific problems.

**Engine**

**1.9L TDI Diesel**

*   **Turbo Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noise from the turbocharger, loss of power, excessive smoke from the exhaust.
    *   Solution: Replace the turbocharger. Inspect and clean or replace oil feed lines to prevent recurrence.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+

*   **EGR Valve Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, stalling, poor fuel economy, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Clean or replace the EGR valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough running engine, poor fuel economy, excessive smoke.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and replace faulty fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+

**1.8L Turbocharged Inline-4 Petrol**

* **Coil Pack Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough running engine, check engine light.
    * Solution: Replace faulty coil packs. Replacing all at once is recommended.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

*   **Clutch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clutch slippage, difficulty shifting gears, high clutch pedal engagement point.
    *   Solution: Replace the clutch disc, pressure plate, and throw-out bearing. Consider replacing the flywheel as well.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200

**Electrical**

*   **Central Locking System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Doors not locking or unlocking properly, remote key not working.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace the central locking control module or door lock actuators.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+

*   **Window Regulator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Windows not going up or down, clicking noise from the door panel.
    *   Solution: Replace the window regulator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

*   **Instrument Cluster Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Gauges not working, warning lights illuminated incorrectly, complete cluster failure.
    *   Solution: Repair or replace the instrument cluster.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600

**Exterior**

*   **Sliding Door Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening or closing the sliding doors, latching issues, motor failure (if equipped with power sliding doors).
    *   Solution: Adjust door alignment, lubricate moving parts, repair or replace door latch mechanisms or the power sliding door motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $600+

**Interior**

*   **Climate Control Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inconsistent temperature, blower motor failure, failure to switch between modes (heat/AC/defrost).
    *   Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace the blower motor, climate control module, or blend door actuators.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+

**Summary**
The 2005 Volkswagen Sharan, while praised for its practicality and spaciousness, can suffer from several reliability issues. Engine problems such as turbo failure, EGR valve issues, and fuel injector problems can be costly to fix. Electrical issues such as central locking malfunctions, window regulator failures, and instrument cluster problems are very common. Exterior and interior problems such as sliding door problems and climate control issues may also arise. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to major repairs, and the costs can vary widely depending on the specific problem and the repair shop. Potential buyers should be aware of these common issues and consider a thorough inspection before purchasing a used Sharan.
