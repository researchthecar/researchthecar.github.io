**Introduction**

The 2005 Volkswagen Sharan is a practical and versatile family vehicle. Available with a choice of gasoline and diesel engines paired with either a manual or automatic transmission, the Sharan offers a spacious interior and ample cargo space. While it is generally reliable, some owners have reported issues with specific components that can impact the overall ownership experience.

**Engine**

**2.0L FSI Gasoline**

- **Leaking Fuel Injectors:** Symptoms include a strong fuel odor, reduced engine performance, and increased fuel consumption. The solution involves replacing the faulty fuel injectors. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
- **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** Symptoms include a rattling noise on startup, reduced engine power, and possible engine damage. The solution is to replace the defective timing chain tensioner. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1200+

**2.8L V6 Gasoline**

- **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** Symptoms include vacuum leaks, rough idling, and reduced engine power. The solution involves replacing the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+

**1.9L TDI Diesel**

- **Turbocharger Failure:** Symptoms include reduced engine power, increased exhaust smoke, and excessive oil consumption. The solution involves replacing the failed turbocharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1200 - $1800+
- **Fuel Injector Failure:** Symptoms include rough idling, reduced engine power, and increased emissions. The solution involves replacing the faulty fuel injectors. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

- **Clutch Problems:** Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, slipping, and premature clutch wear. The solution involves replacing the clutch components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1200+

**5-Speed Automatic**

- **Transmission Fluid Leaks:** Symptoms include low transmission fluid levels, rough shifting, and reduced fuel economy. The solution involves identifying the source of the leak and repairing or replacing the affected components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Suspension**

**Independent Front/Semi-Independent Rear**

- **Strut Mount Failure:** Symptoms include excessive noise and vibration, reduced handling, and uneven tire wear. The solution involves replacing the worn or damaged strut mounts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
- **Control Arm Bushings Wear:** Symptoms include excessive play in the suspension, reduced handling, and unusual tire wear. The solution involves replacing the worn or damaged control arm bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+

**Electrical**

- **Battery Drain:** Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle, dimming lights, and reduced electrical system functionality. The solution involves identifying and repairing or replacing the source of the electrical drain. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
- **Alternator Failure:** Symptoms include a warning light on the dashboard, flickering or dimming lights, and a dead battery. The solution involves replacing the faulty alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+

**Exterior**

- **Sunroof Water Leaks:** Symptoms include water entering the vehicle through the sunroof. The solution involves sealing or replacing the sunroof drainage system. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Interior**

- **Door Lock Failure:** Symptoms include difficulty locking or unlocking the doors, loose or broken door handles, and power lock malfunctions. The solution involves replacing the affected door lock actuators or handles. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
- **Climate Control Malfunction:** Symptoms include reduced or no air flow, inaccurate temperature control, and unusual noises. The solution involves diagnosing and repairing or replacing the affected climate control components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+

**Summary**

The 2005 Volkswagen Sharan is generally a reliable vehicle, but it has some potential issues that can affect its ownership experience. Engine problems, transmission issues, and electrical problems are the most common and can be costly to repair. Suspension and exterior issues are less frequent but can still impact the overall functionality of the vehicle. Interior issues are relatively minor and tend to be less expensive to address. Overall, the severity and cost of the potential issues should be considered when evaluating the Sharan as a used car purchase.
