**1990 Volkswagen Panel**

The 1990 Volkswagen Panel is a versatile cargo van that offers ample space and practicality. It is powered by a 2.1-liter inline-four engine that produces 109 horsepower and 133 lb-ft of torque. Power is sent to the rear wheels through a standard five-speed manual transmission.

Drivers appreciate the Panel's spacious cargo area, rugged construction, and affordable price. However, it has received criticism for its low power output and basic interior.

**Engine**

* **2.1L Inline-Four**

  * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
    Description/Symptoms: Coolant leak, loss of coolant, overheating engine
    Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **Five-Speed Manual**

  * **Clutch Failure**
    Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, slipping clutch, burning smell
    Solution: Replace clutch, flywheel if required
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **No Issues Found**

**Exterior**

* **Prone to Rust**
    Description/Symptoms: Rust spots on body panels, particularly around wheel wells and lower doors
    Solution: Regular rust prevention treatments, rust repair
    Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on severity
    Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracks**
    Description/Symptoms: Cracks in the plastic dashboard, particularly around the air vents
    Solution: Replace dashboard
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000
    Recalls: None Found
* **Door Lock Failure**
    Description/Symptoms: Doors fail to lock or unlock, handles become loose
    Solution: Replace door lock actuators
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1990 Volkswagen Panel is a reliable and practical vehicle, but it is not without its issues. The most common problems are related to the intake manifold gasket and clutch failure. Although the rust issues can be a concern, they can be mitigated with regular rust prevention measures. The cost of repairs for the Panel is generally affordable, making it a cost-effective option for those in need of a spacious cargo van.
