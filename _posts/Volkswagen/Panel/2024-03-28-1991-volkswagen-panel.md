**Introduction:**

The 1991 Volkswagen Panel was a versatile and spacious van that offered both passenger and cargo configurations. It was powered by a 2.1L inline-4 engine that produced 95 horsepower and 122 lb-ft of torque. The engine was paired with a 5-speed manual transmission.

The Panel was known for its practical interior, which could easily be configured for various needs. However, it also had some drawbacks, including a lack of power and subpar fuel economy.

**Engine**

**2.1L Inline-4**

* **Blown Head Gasket:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of coolant, overheating, rough idle
    * Solution: Replace head gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Cracked Cylinder Head:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of coolant, white smoke from exhaust
    * Solution: Replace cylinder head
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Synchronizer Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises
    * Solution: Replace synchronizers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive bouncing, poor handling, clunking noises
    * Solution: Replace struts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Control Arm Bushings Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loose steering, vibrations, uneven tire wear
    * Solution: Replace control arm bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery dies overnight, electrical components fail
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical system
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Ignition Switch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vehicle won't start, key gets stuck
    * Solution: Replace ignition switch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Surface rust on body panels, frame, or suspension
    * Solution: Remove rust and apply protective coating
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Door Lock Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Doors won't lock or unlock properly
    * Solution: Repair or replace door lock mechanism
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Window Regulator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Windows won't roll up or down, motor noise
    * Solution: Replace window regulator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 1991 Volkswagen Panel had a number of potential issues, including head gasket and cylinder head problems, transmission synchronizer wear, suspension issues, and electrical problems. While some of these issues were minor and inexpensive to fix, others could be more severe and costly. Overall, the Panel was a reliable and versatile vehicle, but it is important to be aware of its potential problems before making a purchasing decision.
