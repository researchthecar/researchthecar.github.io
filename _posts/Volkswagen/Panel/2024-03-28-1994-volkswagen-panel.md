**Introduction:**
The 1994 Volkswagen Panel is a compact van offered with a single 2.5L 5-cylinder engine and 4-speed automatic transmission. It produces 102 horsepower and 142 lb-ft of torque. The Panel is known for its spacious cargo area, maneuverability, and durability. However, like any vehicle, it has some common issues that owners may encounter.

**Engine**
* **2.5L 5-cylinder**
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and rough idling. Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * **Head gasket failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and white smoke from the exhaust. Solution: Replace head gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * **Timing belt failure:** Symptoms include sudden engine failure and a loud noise from the engine bay. Solution: Replace timing belt and related components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * **Crankshaft position sensor failure:** Symptoms include intermittent engine stalling and difficulty starting. Solution: Replace crankshaft position sensor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * **Mass airflow sensor failure:** Symptoms include rough idling, poor acceleration, and increased fuel consumption. Solution: Replace mass airflow sensor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+

**Transmission**
* **4-speed automatic**
    * **Transmission slipping:** Symptoms include hesitation or delayed engagement of gears, and difficulty shifting. Solution: Replace or rebuild transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * **Torque converter failure:** Symptoms include shuddering or vibration during acceleration, and overheating. Solution: Replace torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * **Shift solenoid failure:** Symptoms include erratic shifting, slipping, or difficulty shifting into certain gears. Solution: Replace shift solenoid. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Suspension**
* **Worn ball joints:** Symptoms include clunking or popping noises from the front end, and uneven tire wear. Solution: Replace ball joints. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Worn tie rods:** Symptoms include vibration in the steering wheel, loose steering, and difficulty keeping the vehicle in a straight line. Solution: Replace tie rods. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
* **Worn shocks or struts:** Symptoms include a rough or bouncy ride, poor handling, and excessive body roll. Solution: Replace shocks or struts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+

**Electrical**
* **Alternator failure:** Symptoms include dim or flickering lights, reduced battery life, and a warning light on the dashboard. Solution: Replace alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
* **Starter failure:** Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle or a clicking noise when turning the key. Solution: Replace starter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Faulty ignition switch:** Symptoms include intermittent starting problems, flickering lights, and electrical malfunctions. Solution: Replace ignition switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
* **Faulty window switch:** Symptoms include difficulty operating the electric windows. Solution: Replace window switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150+

**Exterior**
* **Door handle breakage:** Symptoms include difficulty opening or closing the doors. Solution: Replace door handle. Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $100+
* **Sunroof leaks:** Symptoms include water leaking into the vehicle during rain or car washes. Solution: Repair or replace sunroof seal. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Interior**
* **Dashboard cracking:** Symptoms include cracks or peeling on the dashboard. Solution: Replace dashboard or have it repaired. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **HVAC controls failure:** Symptoms include difficulty controlling the heating, ventilation, or air conditioning. Solution: Repair or replace HVAC controls. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
* **Faulty power locks:** Symptoms include difficulty locking or unlocking the doors using the power switches. Solution: Repair or replace power lock actuators. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Summary**
The 1994 Volkswagen Panel is a reliable and versatile van, but it is not without its fair share of issues. Common problems include engine gasket failures, transmission problems, suspension wear, electrical faults, and interior malfunctions. While not all issues are severe, some can be costly to repair, such as engine and transmission problems. Before purchasing a used Volkswagen Panel, it's important to have it inspected by a qualified mechanic to identify potential issues and estimate repair costs.
