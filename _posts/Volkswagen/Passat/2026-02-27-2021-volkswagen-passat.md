# 2021 Volkswagen Passat Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Volkswagen Passat serves as a spacious and comfortable midsize sedan option for used-car buyers, featuring straightforward powertrain choices and a refined ride. While owner-reported complaints are minimal, prospective buyers should review available vehicle history and perform a thorough test drive to check for any electrical or body-related quirks common to modern sedans.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 174 horsepower and 206 lb-ft of torque, offering responsive power delivery for daily commuting. This powerplant generally maintains a solid reputation for reliability when properly maintained.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission pairs with the front-wheel-drive layout to provide smooth and predictable shifts. It is a conventional torque-converter unit known for straightforward operation and steady long-term performance.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V812000: Wheels:Lugs/Nuts/Bolts/Studs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Passat&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The bolts may loosen and cause a loss of control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the tightness of the wheel bearing bolts and replace the bolts, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Volkswagen Passat presents a balanced choice for used sedan shoppers, offering ample cabin space and a comfortable ride without widespread mechanical trouble spots. Buyers should inspect the vehicle thoroughly and ensure all electronic features function correctly before finalizing a purchase.
