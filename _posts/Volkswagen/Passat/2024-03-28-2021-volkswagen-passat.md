**Introduction:**

The 2021 Volkswagen Passat is a midsize sedan that offers a balance of comfort, performance, and fuel efficiency. It comes in a variety of trim levels and two engine options: the 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 and the 3.6L VR6. The Passat is also available with a 6-speed manual transmission, an 8-speed automatic transmission, and a 7-speed dual-clutch transmission.

People like the Passat for its spacious interior, intuitive infotainment system, and comfortable ride. However, some have reported issues with certain components, as outlined below:

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**

* **Turbocharger failure:** The turbocharger can fail prematurely, causing a loss of power and an increase in emissions.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Oil leaks:** The engine may develop oil leaks from the valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket, or timing chain cover.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking gasket or cover.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.6L VR6**

* **Timing chain failure:** The timing chain can stretch or break, causing catastrophic engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain and related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Clutch failure:** The clutch can wear out prematurely, causing difficulty shifting or a loss of power.
    * Solution: Replace the clutch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800
    * Recalls: None Found

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission slipping:** The transmission may slip gears, causing a loss of power or an inability to shift.
    * Solution: Replace or repair the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**7-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission**

* **Mechatronic unit failure:** The mechatronic unit controls the transmission and can fail, causing a loss of power or an inability to shift.
    * Solution: Replace the mechatronic unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut failure:** The struts can wear out prematurely, causing a loss of stability and handling.
    * Solution: Replace the struts and related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Ball joint failure:** The ball joints can wear out prematurely, causing a loss of stability and handling.
    * Solution: Replace the ball joints.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery failure:** The battery can fail prematurely, causing a loss of power.
    * Solution: Replace the battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Starter failure:** The starter can fail prematurely, causing difficulty starting the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace the starter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof leaks:** The sunroof can develop leaks, allowing water to enter the cabin.
    * Solution: Replace the sunroof seals or repair the sunroof drain channels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Headliner sagging:** The headliner can sag or detach from the roof, creating an unsightly appearance.
    * Solution: Replace or repair the headliner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Volkswagen Passat has some potential issues to be aware of, such as turbocharger failure, timing chain failure, and transmission slipping. However, many of these issues are covered under the vehicle's warranty and can be fixed at a reasonable cost. Overall, the Passat is a reliable and comfortable sedan that offers a good value for its price.
