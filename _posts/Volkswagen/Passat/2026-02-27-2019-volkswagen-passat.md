# 2019 Volkswagen Passat Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Volkswagen Passat offers a spacious cabin and comfortable ride, making it an appealing option for used midsize sedan shoppers. While owner reports to federal safety regulators outline a few isolated mechanical and electrical concerns, prospective buyers should carefully evaluate powertrain performance and electronics during a test drive to ensure everything operates smoothly.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

Powered by a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine producing 174 horsepower and 206 lb-ft of torque, this powerplant offers adequate everyday acceleration. It provides a balanced blend of efficiency and performance for daily commuting.

**Fuel Injector and Driveability Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected engine shaking, a sudden loss of motive power, and the illumination of the check engine or EPC warning lights.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the fuel system and diagnostic trouble codes, replacing faulty fuel injectors or sensors as needed.

### Remote Start System

Electrical accessories generally function well, though specific convenience features like remote start warrant functional checks.

**Remote Start Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The factory remote start feature may cause the engine to briefly turn over and then immediately shut down without starting the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealership or Volkswagen technician to check for software updates or programming fixes related to the remote start module.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic

Equipped with a conventional 6-speed automatic transmission paired with front-wheel drive, this setup delivers smooth gear changes and reliable everyday operation.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Standard braking components provide stopping power, though proper pedal clearance and response should be verified during inspection.

**Pedal Travel and Response Delays**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners experienced delayed brake engagement when pressing the pedal, or noticed unusual physical positioning and slow pedal return.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake pedal linkage, master cylinder, and hydraulic system for proper clearance and pressure.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Driver assistance features add modern security, though sensor calibration is critical to prevent erratic behavior.

**Unintended Collision Braking Activation**
- **Symptoms:** The collision avoidance system may independently trigger sudden braking events while driving even when no obstacles are present.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle's sensor calibration and radar alignment checked by a trained technician.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V389000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Passat&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The blocking cap disabling the adjustment of the headlight's horizontal aim may not have been installed. As such, these vehicles fail to conform to Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Without the blocking cap, the headlight aim can be adjusted out of specification, reducing.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, as necessary, block the headlight horizontal adjustment, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Volkswagen Passat is a comfortable and practical used sedan choice, provided it passes a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Buyers should pay close attention to engine performance, brake pedal feel, and the operation of onboard electronic features before finalizing a purchase.
