**Introduction:**

The 2004 Volkswagen Passat is a compact sedan known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and fuel-efficient engines. Available engine options include a 2.0L turbocharged inline-four (170 hp/166 lb-ft), a 2.8L VR6 (201 hp/181 lb-ft), and a 1.8L turbocharged inline-four (180 hp/173 lb-ft). Transmission options include a 5-speed manual, a 5-speed automatic, and a 6-speed automatic. Owners appreciate the Passat's refined interior, ample cargo space, and fun-to-drive handling. However, some issues have been reported with certain components.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-Four**

* **Carbon Buildup on Intake Valves:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, rough idle, increased emissions.
    * Solution: Valve cleaning.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattle or knocking noise during engine startup.
    * Solution: Replacement of timing chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
* **Turbocharger Oil Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from the turbocharger.
    * Solution: Replacement of turbocharger oil seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.8L VR6**

* **Intake Manifold Runner Control Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine performance, loss of power.
    * Solution: Replacement of intake manifold runner control unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**1.8L Turbocharged Inline-Four**

* **Timing Belt Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Catastrophic engine failure.
    * Solution: Replacement of timing belt and related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: 05V079000

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Reverse Gear Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty or inability to engage reverse gear.
    * Solution: Replacement of transmission or reverse gear components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping, shuddering, or overheating.
    * Solution: Replacement of torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Mechatronic Unit Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission stuck in gear, delayed shifting, gear hunting.
    * Solution: Replacement of mechatronic unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Control Arm Bushings Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noise over bumps.
    * Solution: Replacement of control arm bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging, warning lights illuminated.
    * Solution: Replacement of alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: 06V242000

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Corrosion:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Door handles corroding or peeling.
    * Solution: Replacement of door handles.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Interior**

* **Antenna Mast Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Radio reception issues, antenna not extending.
    * Solution: Replacement of antenna mast.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
* **Instrument Cluster Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Gauges not functioning, warning lights not illuminated.
    * Solution: Replacement of instrument cluster.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Summary:**

The 2004 Volkswagen Passat is generally a reliable vehicle, but it has some known issues that can be costly to fix. Engine problems, such as timing chain tensioner failure and carbon buildup, are the most severe and expensive. Transmission issues can also be significant, with potential expenses up to $3,000+. Electrical and interior issues are less severe but can still add up. Overall, the Passat is a solid choice for those who are aware of the potential issues and are willing to budget accordingly.
