# 2016 Volkswagen Passat Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Volkswagen Passat offers a comfortable ride, spacious cabin, and composed road manners, making it a popular choice in the midsize sedan market. When shopping for a used model, prospective buyers should carefully review service records and address potential problem areas like electrical glitches, fuel system odors, and steering-wheel controls. While many owners enjoy trouble-free daily driving, keeping an eye out for known mechanical and safety complaints can help you secure a reliable example.

---

## Engine

### 1.8L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

A 1.8-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine making approximately 170 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard power plant for most trims. It offers a balance of efficiency and everyday drivability, though routine maintenance is essential to prevent longevity issues.

### 3.6L V6

A smooth 3.6-liter naturally aspirated V6 engine pushing around 280 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, paired exclusively with higher-tier trims. It provides strong acceleration and passing power at the expense of slightly higher fuel consumption.

### Fuel System

Owner complaints highlight sporadic fuel odors and potential leaks around the pump or under the hood, which require prompt professional inspection.

**Fuel Odor and Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report a strong smell of gasoline inside the cabin or raw fuel leaks under the hood during or after driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel lines, pump seals, and related components for degradation; replace faulty parts or address according to manufacturer recall campaigns.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A conventional 6-speed automatic transmission paired with the 1.8L and V6 engines, generally regarded as durable despite occasional complaints of rough low-speed shifting.

**Harsh Downshifts and Clunking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice an audible bang or harsh mechanical clunk when utilizing manual/Tiptronic downshifting at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition and software calibration levels; update software or service transmission components as needed.

## Suspension

### Wheel Bearings

Front wheel bearings have been reported to fail earlier than expected in some vehicles, creating noticeable noise and requiring replacement.

**Premature Wheel Bearing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Whining or humming noises from the wheels that increase with vehicle speed.
- **Solution:** Replace worn front or rear wheel bearing assemblies.
- **Cost:** $1,000

## Exterior

### Headlight Housing and Lighting

Electrical issues can occasionally manifest as headlight failures or melting harness components, demanding careful inspection of the lighting circuits.

**Headlight Wiring and Melting**
- **Symptoms:** A burning smell inside the cabin or sudden headlight failure due to thermal degradation in the electrical housing.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight wiring harnesses and housing units; replace melted connectors, bulbs, or assemblies to prevent fire risks.

## Safety

### Clock Spring and Air Bag

Failures within the steering column clock spring can disable essential safety features and driver controls on the steering wheel.

**Clock Spring Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated airbag warning lights accompanied by clicking noises, non-functional steering wheel horn, cruise control, and audio controls.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty steering column clock spring assembly.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V389000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Passat&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The blocking cap disabling the adjustment of the headlight's horizontal aim may not have been installed. As such, these vehicles fail to conform to Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Without the blocking cap, the headlight aim can be adjusted out of specification, reducing.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, as necessary, block the headlight horizontal adjustment, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V403000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Fuel Injection System:Fuel Rail](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Passat&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak, in the presence of an ignition source, can result in a vehicle fire.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will replace the cylinder heads, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V705000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Passat&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the camshaft lobe shears off there would be a loss of brake assist, lengthening the distance needed to stop the vehicle and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen dealer will update the engine control module (ECM) software. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V561000: Unknown Or Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Passat&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Vehicles that do not meet all regulatory requirements may have an increased risk of a crash or injury to vehicle occupants.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 21V321000: Unknown Or Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Passat&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Vehicles that do not meet all regulatory requirements may have an increased risk of injury, crash, or fire.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Volkswagen Passat presents a mixed reliability picture: while it offers an upscale interior and comfortable ride, buyers should thoroughly inspect for potential fuel odors, clock spring failures, and electrical wiring concerns. Checking service histories and verifying that all recall work has been completed will significantly reduce the risk of unexpected repair bills.
