# 2017 Volkswagen Passat Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Volkswagen Passat is a spacious midsize sedan known for its comfortable ride and ample cabin room, making it an appealing option for commuters and families. However, used-car buyers should review potential trouble spots, particularly regarding electrical accessories, fuel system components, and braking reports. While many owners enjoy trouble-free driving, checking service records and performing a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help avoid unexpected repair bills.

---

## Engine

### 1.8L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The standard 1.8-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces 170 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque, paired with a conventional automatic transmission. It offers a smooth power delivery suited for daily commuting, though routine maintenance is essential to longevity.

### 3.6L V6

The available 3.6-liter VR6 engine generates 280 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, providing robust acceleration for highway passing. It pairs with a dual-clutch automatic transmission for brisk shifts, though it demands careful attention to service history.

### Fuel System

Fuel delivery components warrant careful checks during a pre-purchase inspection to rule out leaks or electrical overheating.

**Fuel Pump Module Overheating and Odors**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may detect a strong fuel smell inside the vehicle cabin or notice fuel leaks near the rear passenger wheel area due to pump or control module failure.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty fuel pump control modules or leaking fuel pump assemblies as needed.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The traditional 6-speed torque-converter automatic transmission comes paired with the 1.8L engine and generally provides reliable, smooth shifting under normal driving conditions.

### 6-Speed Direct Shift Gearbox (DSG) Automatic

The 6-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission accompanies the 3.6L V6 engine, offering rapid gear changes. Regular fluid changes are critical to maintaining smooth operation over high mileage.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Brake System

Brake system complaints include isolated reports of fluid leaks and pedal firmness issues that require prompt professional inspection.

**Brake Fluid Leaks and Pedal Stiffness**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected illumination of the brake fluid warning light accompanied by fluid loss, or rare instances of the brake pedal feeling stiff or locking up during minor application.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake lines, calipers, and master cylinder for leaks or wear, and replace damaged hydraulic components immediately.

## Steering

### Steering Wheel Clockspring

Steering column electrical connections can fail over time, disabling the horn, airbag readiness, and steering-wheel controls.

**Clockspring Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A crunching noise while turning the steering wheel often precedes the airbag warning light illuminating, horn failure, and loss of steering wheel button functions.
- **Solution:** Replace the steering wheel clockspring assembly.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Seat Heaters

Cabin electrical accessories, including infotainment units and seat heaters, can exhibit intermittent electronic failures.

**Seat Heater Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Seat warmers may fail to turn on properly, cross-activate, or overheat to the point of scorching the seat material.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat heating elements and control modules, replacing damaged wiring or switches.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V561000: Unknown Or Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Passat&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Vehicles that do not meet all regulatory requirements may have an increased risk of a crash or injury to vehicle occupants.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 21V321000: Unknown Or Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Passat&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Vehicles that do not meet all regulatory requirements may have an increased risk of injury, crash, or fire.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 18V536000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Passat&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The brake caliper bracket mounting bolts on these vehicles may not have been tightened sufficiently. The loose brake bracket bolts can allow the caliper to move, reducing braking ability or causing a loss of vehicle control and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the brake caliper bracket bolt tightness, replacing and re-torque the bolts as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V679000: Unknown Or Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Passat&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Modifications made while the vehicles were in an internal evaluation period may cause the affected vehicles to not comply with all of the applicable regulatory requirements. If the vehicles do not meet all regulatory requirements, there could be an increased risk of a crash, fire, or injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 19V389000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Passat&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The blocking cap disabling the adjustment of the headlight's horizontal aim may not have been installed. As such, these vehicles fail to conform to Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Without the blocking cap, the headlight aim can be adjusted out of specification, reducing.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, as necessary, block the headlight horizontal adjustment, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V075000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Fluid](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Passat&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A low brake fluid level may lengthen the distance needed to stop the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will replace the affected brake lines, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V834000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Passat&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** An inflator explosion may result in sharp metal fragments striking the driver or other occupants resulting in serious injury or death.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver's side front air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Volkswagen Passat offers a compelling mix of spaciousness and ride comfort, but prospective buyers should carefully verify the condition of its electrical accessories, fuel system, and steering clockspring. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a knowledgeable mechanic will help ensure these potential trouble areas have not been neglected.
