# 2019 Volkswagen Golf R Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 Volkswagen Golf R, buyers will find a high-performance hatchback that combines everyday practicality with turbocharged power and all-wheel drive. While owner reports to federal safety regulators highlight a few scattered concerns regarding electrical accessories, driver-assistance features, and wheel durability, the vehicle generally maintains a dedicated following for its engaging driving dynamics.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces around 288 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, delivering brisk performance across the rev range. It is generally robust, though individual owner reports note rare electronic or throttle-related anomalies.

**Unexpected Acceleration**
- **Symptoms:** A driver reported an incident where the vehicle unexpectedly revved and accelerated while making a slow turn into a parking space, despite the foot being on the brake.
- **Solution:** Have the throttle body, accelerator pedal sensor, and engine control module inspected for faults or software anomalies.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic

The quick-shifting 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission channels power efficiently to the 4Motion all-wheel-drive system and is widely praised for its performance when properly maintained.

### 6-Speed Manual

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission offers an engaging, hands-on driving experience for enthusiasts who prefer shifting their own gears.

## Electrical

### 12V Power Outlet & Fuse System

Electrical complaints are generally minor, centered around accessory power outlets and key fob proximity detection reported by some owners.

**12V Power Outlet Fuse Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Using the factory-provided air pump in the 12V DC power outlet causes the associated fuse to blow repeatedly.
- **Solution:** Check the fuse rating and replace blown fuses, and verify the power draw of any plugged-in accessories.

## Safety

### Wheels and Alloy Rims

The suspension provides a firm, sport-tuned ride, though low-profile tires mounted on large alloy wheels leave the rims susceptible to damage from harsh road impacts.

**Factory Alloy Wheel Bending**
- **Symptoms:** Large factory alloy wheels can develop bends on the inner and outer lips, potentially compromising tire seal and driving safety.
- **Solution:** Inspect wheels thoroughly for structural damage or runout during a pre-purchase inspection and replace bent rims if necessary.

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety and driver-assistance features provide helpful protection, though sensitive sensors can occasionally trigger false warnings or limit cruise control functionality under adverse conditions.

**False Collision Warnings and Braking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have experienced unexpected activations of the front collision warning system and sudden emergency braking while driving straight at moderate speeds.
- **Solution:** Ensure camera and radar sensors are clean and free of obstruction, and consult a dealer for potential software updates.

**Adaptive Cruise Control Limitations**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note that the adaptive cruise control system cannot be toggled off in favor of traditional, non-adaptive cruise control when sensors become dirty.
- **Solution:** Keep the front sensor area clean of dirt, snow, and debris to maintain proper system operation.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V321000: Unknown Or Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Golf+R&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Vehicles that do not meet all regulatory requirements may have an increased risk of injury, crash, or fire.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 22V815000: Tires:Pressure Monitoring And Regulating Systems](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Golf+R&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The tire pressure monitoring system may not detect an air pressure loss in adequate time when deflating occurs among all four tires simultaneously. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 138, "Tire Pressure Monitoring Systems." An undetected sudden loss of pressure in all four tires.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the TPMS software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Volkswagen Golf R is a thrilling and versatile hot hatch that appeals strongly to driving enthusiasts. Prospective buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on wheel condition, driver-assistance sensor calibration, and general electrical accessory function to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
