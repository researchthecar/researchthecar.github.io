**Introduction**

The 2017 Volkswagen Golf SportWagen is a compact station wagon that offers a comfortable and practical driving experience. It is available with a choice of two engines: a 1.8L turbocharged gasoline engine and a 2.0L turbocharged diesel engine. Both engines are paired with a 6-speed manual or automatic transmission. The Golf SportWagen is praised for its spacious interior, fuel efficiency, and handling. However, it is criticized for its lack of some advanced safety features and its relatively high price.

**Engine**

**1.8L Turbocharged Gasoline Engine**

* **Water pump failure:** Premature failure of the water pump can lead to coolant leaks and overheating.
    * Solution: Replace the water pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Turbocharger failure:** Failure of the turbocharger can cause a loss of power and increased engine noise.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged Diesel Engine**

* **Emissions system issues:** The emissions control system can experience various problems, such as clogged EGR coolers and faulty sensors.
    * Solution: Clean or replace the affected components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: Several recalls related to emissions system issues

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Grinding gears:** Difficulty shifting gears or grinding noises during shifting.
    * Solution: Inspect and repair damaged components in the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Shift hesitation or shuddering:** Delays or rough shifting during gear changes.
    * Solution: Update transmission software or replace transmission components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Premature strut failure:** Worn or damaged front struts can cause clunking noises and reduced ride comfort.
    * Solution: Replace the struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Rear suspension noise:** Excessive noise or vibration from the rear suspension.
    * Solution: Inspect and repair worn or damaged suspension components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** The battery may lose charge prematurely due to faulty electrical components or accessories.
    * Solution: Inspect and replace the affected components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Electrical system malfunctions:** Various electrical system malfunctions, such as flickering lights or power window issues.
    * Solution: Repair or replace faulty electrical components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof leaks:** Water leakage into the cabin through the sunroof.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the sunroof seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Headlight condensation:** Moisture accumulation within the headlight housing.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the headlight housing.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Seat belt buckle issues:** Seat belt buckles may fail to engage or release properly.
    * Solution: Replace the seat belt buckles.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: Several recalls related to seat belt buckle issues

* **Dashboard rattles or noises:** Excessive noise or vibrations from the dashboard.
    * Solution: Inspect and secure loose components or replace the dashboard.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Volkswagen Golf SportWagen has some notable issues, particularly with its emissions system on the diesel engine, water pump on the gasoline engine, and transmission problems. While some issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more costly and time-consuming to repair. Overall, the severity and cost of these issues should be considered when evaluating the vehicle's reliability and long-term ownership costs.
