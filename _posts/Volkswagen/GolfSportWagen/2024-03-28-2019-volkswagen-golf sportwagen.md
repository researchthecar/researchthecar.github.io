## 2019 Volkswagen Golf SportWagen

The 2019 Volkswagen Golf SportWagen is a compact station wagon that offers a roomy interior, plenty of cargo space, and a comfortable ride. It's available with a choice of two engines: a 1.4-liter turbocharged inline-four with 147 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four with 220 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a six-speed manual transmission or an eight-speed automatic transmission.

Owners of the 2019 Volkswagen Golf SportWagen generally praise its spacious interior, comfortable seats, and user-friendly infotainment system. However, some owners have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, exterior, and interior.

**Engine**

* **1.4-liter Turbocharged Inline-Four Engine**
    * **Intake Valve Carbon Buildup**
        * Description/Symptoms: The intake valves can become clogged with carbon deposits over time, which can lead to reduced engine performance, rough idling, and increased emissions.
        * Solution: The intake valves need to be cleaned or replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **2.0-liter Turbocharged Inline-Four Engine**
    * **Fuel Injector Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The fuel injectors can fail, which can lead to engine misfires, reduced fuel economy, and increased emissions.
        * Solution: The fuel injectors need to be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **Six-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **Grinding Noises When Shifting**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may make grinding noises when shifting gears, especially when the transmission is cold.
        * Solution: The transmission fluid may need to be replaced or the synchronizers may need to be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Eight-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Hesitation When Shifting**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may hesitate when shifting gears, especially when accelerating or decelerating.
        * Solution: The transmission fluid may need to be replaced or the transmission control module may need to be reprogrammed.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Strut Mount Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The front suspension strut mounts can fail, which can lead to clunking noises, a rough ride, and decreased handling.
    * Solution: The strut mounts need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain prematurely, which can make it difficult to start the vehicle.
    * Solution: The charging system may need to be tested and repaired or the battery may need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Windshield Wiper Motor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The windshield wiper motor may fail, which can make it difficult to see in bad weather.
    * Solution: The wiper motor needs to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Software Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may experience software issues, such as freezing or crashing.
    * Solution: The software may need to be updated or the infotainment system may need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Climate Control System Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control system may fail, which can make it difficult to maintain a comfortable temperature in the cabin.
    * Solution: The climate control system may need to be repaired or replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Volkswagen Golf SportWagen is a capable and versatile vehicle that offers a comfortable ride and plenty of space for passengers and cargo. However, some owners have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, exterior, and interior. While some of these issues are relatively minor, others can be more serious and expensive to fix. Overall, the severity and cost of the issues found in the 2019 Volkswagen Golf SportWagen are comparable to those found in other vehicles in its class.
