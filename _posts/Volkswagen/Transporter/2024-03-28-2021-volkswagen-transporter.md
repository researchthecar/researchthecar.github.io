**Introduction to the 2021 Volkswagen Transporter**

The 2021 Volkswagen Transporter is a versatile and spacious van available in a range of configurations, including panel van, kombi, and crew cab. It features a 2.0L 4-cylinder turbocharged diesel engine that delivers 150 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed manual or an 8-speed automatic transmission. The Transporter is known for its durability, practicality, and fuel efficiency, making it a popular choice for businesses and tradespeople. However, some owners have reported various issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

* **2.0L Diesel**

 - **Intake manifold cracks:** Cracks can develop on the intake manifold, causing air leaks and reduced engine performance. This issue is typically fixed by replacing the intake manifold. $200 - $1000+
 - **Turbocharger failure:** The turbocharger can fail prematurely, leading to a loss of power and increased engine noise. This issue may require replacing the turbocharger and associated parts. $2000 - $3000+
 - **Diesel particulate filter (DPF) clogging:** The DPF can become clogged with soot, causing reduced engine power and increased fuel consumption. This issue can be resolved by cleaning or replacing the DPF. $600 - $1200+
 
**Transmission**

* **6-speed Manual**

 - **Shifting difficulties:** Some owners have reported difficulty shifting gears, particularly when the transmission is cold. This issue may require adjustments or repairs to the shifter mechanism. $300 - $600+

* **8-speed Automatic**

 - **Transmission shudder:** The transmission may shudder or vibrate during acceleration or gear changes. This issue may be caused by a faulty torque converter or transmission control module. $1000 - $3000+

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension noise:** Some owners have reported a knocking or rattling noise from the front suspension, especially over rough roads. This noise may be caused by worn or damaged suspension components, such as ball joints or tie rods. $200 - $500+

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** Some owners have experienced rapid battery drain, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle. This issue may be caused by a faulty electrical component or a parasitic draw on the battery. $200 - $500+

 * **Electrical gremlins:** Various electrical issues have been reported, including malfunctions with the infotainment system, power windows, and other electrical components. These issues may require troubleshooting and repairs to the specific affected components. Cost varies depending on the issue. 

**Interior**

* **Dashboard rattles:** Some owners have reported rattles or buzzes coming from the dashboard area. This issue may be caused by loose or misaligned trim panels or other components. $100 - $500+

* **Air conditioning problems:** Some owners have experienced issues with the air conditioning system, including insufficient cooling or malfunctioning controls. This issue may require repairs to the compressor, condenser, or other components of the AC system. $500 - $1500+

**Exterior**

* **Rear door latch failure:** The rear door latches may fail to latch properly, resulting in difficulty closing the doors. This issue may require repairs or replacement of the door latches. $200 - $500+

**Summary**

Overall, the 2021 Volkswagen Transporter seems to have a few issues, with some being more common and severe than others. Engine and transmission issues are the most concerning, as they can be expensive to repair. Electrical and suspension problems are also reported, but their severity and cost of repair vary depending on the issue. While the Transporter remains a popular choice for its practicality and fuel efficiency, potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and factor them into their purchase decision.
