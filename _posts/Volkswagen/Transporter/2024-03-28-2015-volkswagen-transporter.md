**Introduction**

The 2015 Volkswagen Transporter is a versatile and capable commercial vehicle that offers a range of engine and transmission options. The 2.0L TDI engine delivers 180 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, while the 2.5L gasoline engine produces 170 horsepower and 170 lb-ft of torque. Both engines can be paired with a 6-speed manual or 6-speed automatic transmission. Owners of the Transporter appreciate its spacious cargo area, comfortable interior, and strong performance. However, the vehicle has also faced some reliability concerns.

**Engine**

**2.0L TDI**

* **Turbocharger failure:** The turbocharger can fail prematurely, leading to reduced engine power and increased emissions.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Fuel injector issues:** Faulty fuel injectors can cause rough idling, poor fuel economy, and engine misfires.
    * Solution: Replace the fuel injectors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **EGR valve problems:** The exhaust gas recirculation (EGR) valve can become clogged or malfunction, leading to reduced engine performance and increased emissions.
    * Solution: Clean or replace the EGR valve.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**2.5L Gasoline**

* **Timing chain tensioner failure:** The timing chain tensioner can fail, resulting in a rattling noise from the engine and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Intake manifold gasket leak:** The intake manifold gasket can leak, causing vacuum leaks and reduced engine performance.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Oxygen sensor failure:** The oxygen sensor can fail, leading to inaccurate air-fuel mixture adjustments and reduced fuel economy.
    * Solution: Replace the oxygen sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

* **Difficulty shifting:** The transmission may be difficult to shift, especially when cold.
    * Solution: Lubricate the shift linkage or replace the clutch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission shudder:** The transmission may shudder during shifting, particularly when driving at low speeds.
    * Solution: Flush the transmission fluid and replace the solenoids.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Torque converter failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing a loss of power and a whining noise.
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension noise:** The front suspension can make noise when driving over bumps or uneven surfaces.
    * Solution: Replace the struts or shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Rear suspension sagging:** The rear suspension can sag, especially when carrying heavy loads.
    * Solution: Replace the rear springs or shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+

**Electrical**

* **Electrical system glitches:** The electrical system can experience glitches, such as intermittent power loss or warning lights.
    * Solution: Update the software or replace electrical components as needed.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Side mirror vibration:** The side mirrors may vibrate at highway speeds.
    * Solution: Replace the side mirror assemblies.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Rear window wiper failure:** The rear window wiper may fail to operate.
    * Solution: Replace the rear wiper motor or switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Interior**

* **Rear seat headrest detachment:** The rear seat headrests can detach from the seats.
    * Solution: Inspect and replace the headrest assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
* **Dash panel cracking:** The dash panel can crack over time.
    * Solution: Replace the dash panel.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Recalls**

* NHTSA Campaign ID: 15V393000 - Brake fluid leak
* NHTSA Campaign ID: 17E036000 - Transmission failure
* NHTSA Campaign ID: 19E053000 - Tailgate latch failure

**Summary**

The 2015 Volkswagen Transporter is a versatile vehicle with several engine and transmission options. However, it has experienced a number of reliability issues, including turbocharger failure, transmission shudder, and electrical system glitches. Some of these issues can be costly to repair, with estimated costs ranging from $500 to over $2,000. The severity of the issues varies, with some being more serious than others. Owners should be aware of these potential problems and consider the potential cost of repairs before purchasing this vehicle.
