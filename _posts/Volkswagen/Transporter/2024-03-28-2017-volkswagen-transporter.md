**2017 Volkswagen Transporter**

The 2017 Volkswagen Transporter is a versatile and practical commercial vehicle that offers a range of engine and transmission options to suit various needs. It is known for its spacious cabin, comfortable ride, and robust construction. However, like any vehicle, it has had its fair share of issues reported by owners.

**Engine**

* **2.0L TDI**

    * **EGR Valve Failure:** Sticking or malfunctioning EGR valve can lead to reduced engine performance, increased emissions, and illuminated check engine light. Symptoms include rough idle, excessive smoke, and decreased power. Solution involves replacing the EGR valve. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1200+
    * **Injector Failure:** Faulty injectors can cause misfiring, rough running, and decreased fuel efficiency. Symptoms include difficulty starting, uneven engine speed, and increased emissions. Solution involves replacing the malfunctioning injectors. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+ per injector
    * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** A failing timing chain tensioner can lead to excessive noise and potentially catastrophic engine damage if not addressed promptly. Symptoms include rattling or grinding noises from the engine. Solution involves replacing the faulty tensioner and timing chain components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $3000+

* **3.2L VR6**

    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:** A leaking intake manifold gasket can cause rough idle, loss of power, and decreased fuel efficiency. Symptoms include vacuum leaks, hissing noises, and poor throttle response. Solution involves replacing the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Manual**

    * **Clutch Failure:** Premature clutch wear can lead to slipping or difficulty shifting gears. Symptoms include shuddering during take-off, slipping gears, and difficulty engaging gears. Solution involves replacing the clutch assembly. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1200 - $1800+

* **7-Speed DSG**

    * **Mechatronic Unit Failure:** The mechatronic unit controls the transmission's operation and can fail causing a variety of symptoms. These include gear selection problems, transmission slipping, and illuminated warning lights. Solution involves replacing or repairing the mechatronic unit. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $3500+
    * **Selector Lever Linkage Breakage:** A broken selector lever linkage can prevent the transmission from shifting properly or becoming stuck in gear. Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears or the gearshift becoming loose. Solution involves replacing the broken linkage components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Breakage:** Overloading or heavy-duty use can lead to broken leaf springs, affecting the vehicle's ride and handling characteristics. Symptoms include a sagging suspension, uneven tire wear, and clunking noises. Solution involves replacing the broken leaf springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1000+ per leaf spring

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** A parasitic draw on the battery can lead to premature battery discharge and starting problems. Symptoms include difficulty starting, dimming lights or gauges, and a dead battery. Solution involves identifying and eliminating the source of the parasitic draw. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Hinge Failure:** A weak or damaged tailgate hinge can cause the tailgate to sag or become difficult to open or close. Symptoms include misalignment of the tailgate, rattling noises, or difficulty closing the tailgate completely. Solution involves replacing the faulty hinge. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
* **Sliding Door Roller Failure:** Worn or broken rollers in the sliding door mechanism can cause the door to become difficult to open or close. Symptoms include binding or grinding noises, and hesitation or resistance when opening or closing the door. Solution involves replacing the worn or broken rollers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Interior**

* **Windshield Wiper Stalk Failure:** A faulty windshield wiper stalk can cause the wipers to operate intermittently, not at all, or operate at the wrong speed. Symptoms include inoperative wipers, intermittent wiping, or incorrect wiper speed. Solution involves replacing the wiper stalk assembly. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
* **Power Window Switch Malfunction:** Sticking or malfunctioning power window switches can prevent the windows from operating properly. Symptoms include difficulty raising or lowering windows, intermittent operation, or windows getting stuck. Solution involves replacing the faulty switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Summary**

Overall, the 2017 Volkswagen Transporter has a mixed record in terms of reliability. While some owners have experienced significant issues, especially with the 2.0L TDI engine, others have reported few problems. The severity and cost of the issues vary depending on the specific components affected. Potential buyers should carefully consider the potential issues and maintenance costs associated with this vehicle.
