## Volkswagen Transporter (1990)

The 1990 Volkswagen Transporter was a popular choice for families and businesses alike. It was available with a variety of engine and transmission options. The Transporter was praised for its spacious interior and versatile seating configurations. However, it was also known to have several common issues.

**Engine**

- **2.1L Diesel Engine:**
  - **Timing Belt Failure:** The timing belt is a critical component that synchronizes the timing of the engine's valves. If the timing belt fails, it can cause catastrophic engine damage.
    - Solution: Replace the timing belt according to the manufacturer's recommended intervals.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found
  - **Fuel Injection Pump Failure:** The fuel injection pump is responsible for delivering fuel to the engine's cylinders. If it fails, the engine will not be able to run.
    - Solution: Replace the fuel injection pump.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

- **1.9L Petrol Engine:**
  - **Head Gasket Failure:** The head gasket is a seal between the engine's cylinder head and the engine block. If the head gasket fails, it can cause coolant and oil to leak.
    - Solution: Replace the head gasket.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

- **4-Speed Manual Transmission:**
  - **Gearbox Failure:** The gearbox is a critical component of the transmission. If it fails, the transmission will not be able to shift gears.
    - Solution: Replace the gearbox.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    - Recalls: None Found

- **3-Speed Automatic Transmission:**
  - **Torque Converter Failure:** The torque converter is a fluid coupling that connects the engine to the transmission. If it fails, it can cause the transmission to slip or shudder.
    - Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **Front Suspension Struts:** The front suspension struts are responsible for absorbing shock and providing stability. If they fail, the vehicle will handle poorly and may be unsafe to drive.
  - Solution: Replace the front suspension struts.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Alternator Failure:** The alternator is responsible for charging the vehicle's battery. If it fails, the battery will not be able to power the vehicle's electrical components.
  - Solution: Replace the alternator.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Battery Drain:** The battery can drain prematurely if there is a parasitic draw on the electrical system. This can be caused by a faulty component or a short circuit.
  - Solution: Find and repair the source of the parasitic draw.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Rust:** The Transporter is prone to rust, especially in the undercarriage and body panels.
  - Solution: Prevent rust by applying rust-proofing treatment regularly.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Climate Control Malfunction:** The climate control system can malfunction, causing the vehicle's heating and cooling system to not work properly.
  - Solution: Repair or replace the faulty component in the climate control system.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Dashboard Rattle:** The dashboard can rattle due to loose components or poor fitment.
  - Solution: Tighten or replace the loose components.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The Volkswagen Transporter is a versatile vehicle with a spacious interior. However, it is known to have several common issues that can be expensive to repair. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues before purchasing a used Transporter. The severity of the issues varies, but some can be costly to fix.
