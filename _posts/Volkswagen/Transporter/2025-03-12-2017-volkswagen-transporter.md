The 2017 Volkswagen Transporter T6 is a popular van known for its practicality, versatility, and comfortable ride. Available engine options include various diesel and petrol engines. While many owners appreciate its spacious interior and robust build quality, some have reported specific issues.

**Engine**

**2.0 TDI Diesel**

*   **EGR Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine management light illumination, reduced power, increased emissions, rough idling.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the EGR valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: DPF light illumination, reduced power, frequent regeneration cycles, potential for engine damage if ignored. This issue is exacerbated by frequent short journeys.
    *   Solution: Forced DPF regeneration (if not too blocked), replacement of the DPF.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (forced regeneration) to $1500+ (DPF replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil drips under the vehicle, low oil level warnings, oil smell. Leaks may originate from the valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket, or turbocharger oil lines.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the leaking gaskets or seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the location of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.0 TSI Petrol**

*No Issues Found*

**Transmission**

**DSG (Direct Shift Gearbox) Automatic Transmission**

*   **Mechatronic Unit Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or jerky shifting, failure to engage gears, warning lights on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacement or repair of the mechatronic unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Wear (Especially in models used for frequent stop-start driving or towing)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clutch slippage, difficulty engaging gears, vibrations during acceleration.
    *   Solution: Clutch replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Manual Transmission**

*No Issues Found*

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Suspension Components**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or knocking noises from the suspension, uneven tire wear, poor handling. Common culprits include worn ball joints, tie rod ends, and sway bar links.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the worn suspension components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per component, depending on location and if it is a DIY project.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Central Locking Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Doors not locking or unlocking properly, failure of remote locking system.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the central locking system, which may involve replacing faulty door lock actuators or control modules.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dead battery, difficulty starting the vehicle, requiring frequent jump starts.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the electrical system to identify the source of the drain, which could be a faulty module, wiring issue, or aftermarket accessory.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sliding Door Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening or closing the sliding door, sticking or binding, unusual noises.
    *   Solution: Adjustment and lubrication of the door mechanism, repair or replacement of damaged rollers or cables.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Air Conditioning Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Weak or no cold air, unusual noises from the AC system, failure of the AC compressor.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the AC system, which may involve recharging the refrigerant, replacing the compressor, or repairing leaks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Volkswagen Transporter, while generally reliable, can experience issues with its EGR valve, DPF, automatic transmission, and electrical components. The severity and cost of these issues can vary greatly. Potential buyers should be aware of these common problems and thoroughly inspect any used Transporter before purchase, paying close attention to the service history and overall condition.
