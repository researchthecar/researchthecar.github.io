The 2022 Volkswagen Crafter is a popular choice for tradespeople and businesses needing a versatile and spacious van. Typically equipped with a 2.0-liter TDI diesel engine (varying outputs available, such as 140 hp and around 250 lb-ft of torque), it offers decent fuel economy and practicality. Other engine options may be available depending on the market. It's often praised for its comfortable driving position, spacious cargo area, and available technology. Common complaints often revolve around the ride quality (especially when unladen), and some reliability concerns.

**Engine**

**2.0L TDI Diesel**

*   **DPF (Diesel Particulate Filter) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Frequent DPF regeneration cycles, DPF warning light illumination, reduced engine performance, and potential for complete DPF blockage if regeneration is not successful. Can be caused by driving short distances at low speeds.
    *   Solution: Forced DPF regeneration by a mechanic, replacement of the DPF if severely blocked.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+ depending on the severity and the repair/replacement needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **EGR (Exhaust Gas Recirculation) Valve Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, poor acceleration, increased emissions, and engine warning light illumination. The EGR valve can become clogged with carbon deposits.
    *   Solution: Cleaning or replacement of the EGR valve.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough or Delayed Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noticeable delays when shifting gears, harsh or jerky shifts, particularly between lower gears.
    *   Solution: Software update (TCU reflash), transmission fluid change, or valve body replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (software update) - $1500+ (valve body)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various warning lights illuminating on the dashboard (ABS, ESP, TPMS), potentially leading to incorrect system behavior or reduced functionality.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis with an OBD-II scanner to identify the faulty sensor, followed by sensor replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the sensor location and type.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, slow response times, Bluetooth connectivity problems, or complete system failure.
    *   Solution: Software update, system reset, or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Software update) - $1000+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sliding Door Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulties opening or closing the sliding door, sticking, or excessive noise during operation.
    *   Solution: Lubrication of the door tracks and rollers, adjustment of the door alignment, or replacement of worn rollers/guides.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust around the door seams**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust forming on the edges or seams of the cargo or side door.
    *   Solution: Sanding, rust removal and painting.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300+ depending on the area and severity of the rust.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Volkswagen Crafter can experience a range of issues, from relatively minor electrical glitches and DPF issues to more serious transmission problems. The severity of these issues can vary, with potential repair costs ranging from a few hundred dollars for sensor replacements to several thousand dollars for major engine or transmission work. Regular maintenance, including timely oil changes and addressing warning lights promptly, can help mitigate some of these issues.
