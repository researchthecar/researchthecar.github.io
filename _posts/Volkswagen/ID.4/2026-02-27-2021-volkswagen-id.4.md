# 2021 Volkswagen ID.4

### Overview
The 2021 Volkswagen ID.4, a fully electric compact SUV, marked VW's significant entry into the mainstream EV market. Designed with a focus on practicality, comfort, and modern technology, it offers a spacious interior and a smooth driving experience. Its platform is built on the modular electric drive matrix (MEB), shared with other VW Group EVs. Early models have generally shown good initial reliability, though as with any new generation of technology, some specific areas have seen more attention from owners and technicians. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond standard maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and prospective buyers.

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Volkswagen ID.4 demonstrates a generally strong start in terms of reliability, particularly concerning its electric powertrain and core driving components. The primary areas of concern tend to be software-related, such as infotainment system glitches, which are common in new technology and often resolvable with updates. Some owners have reported issues with the 12V battery, a component crucial for auxiliary systems in EVs, and occasional charging interruptions. These are not indicative of widespread powertrain failure but rather specific electrical system quirks. Overall, the ID.4 offers a promising reliability profile for an inaugural electric model, with most issues being manageable and not severely impacting the vehicle's core functionality.
