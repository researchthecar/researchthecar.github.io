The 2021 Volkswagen ID.4 marked VW's significant entry into the electric vehicle market. It came standard with a single rear-mounted electric motor producing 201 horsepower and 229 lb-ft of torque. A dual-motor, all-wheel-drive version, boosting output to 295 horsepower, was introduced later. The ID.4 is praised for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and user-friendly technology, making it a practical EV for daily use. However, some owners have reported issues with the infotainment system, software glitches, and occasional charging problems.

**Battery/Charging System**

*   **12V Battery Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced issues with the 12V battery draining quickly, leading to the vehicle not starting or displaying various error messages. This issue seems to be linked to software glitches that prevent the vehicle from properly going into sleep mode, draining the battery.
    *   Solution: Software update to address the parasitic drain. Replacement of the 12V battery if it has been damaged by excessive draining.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix:
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Charging Port Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported problems with the charging port failing to release the charging cable after a charging session. This can be caused by a faulty latching mechanism in the charge port.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the charging port assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix:

*   **Slow DC Fast Charging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported longer than expected DC fast charging times, with the vehicle taking significantly longer to reach 80% charge compared to advertised times.
    *   Solution: VW released software updates aimed at improving charging performance, however many users still report issues.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix:

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system is one of the most common sources of complaints. Issues include screen freezes, slow response times, system crashes, and loss of functionality (e.g., navigation, audio).
    *   Solution: Software updates are the primary method for addressing these problems, but some owners have reported that issues persist even after updates. In some cases, hardware replacement of the infotainment unit has been necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix:
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Software Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A wide range of software-related problems, including error messages, malfunctioning sensors, and unexpected behavior of vehicle systems. These glitches often manifest as warning lights on the dashboard or features not working as expected.
    *   Solution: Software updates are the primary solution, but some issues may require dealer intervention or more specific module reprogramming.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix:
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Key Fob Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent connectivity with the vehicle, failing to lock/unlock doors, or start the vehicle remotely.
    *   Solution: Battery replacement in the key fob, software updates to the vehicle's keyless entry system, or replacement of the key fob itself.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix:
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2021 Volkswagen ID.4 is a well-received EV, but it has faced several reliability issues, mainly concentrated in its electrical systems, particularly with the infotainment system and software. The issues range in severity, from minor inconveniences like infotainment glitches to more significant problems like electric motor failure or sunroof leaks. Addressing these issues often involves software updates, but hardware replacements may be necessary in some cases. Depending on the issue, the cost to fix can range from relatively minor to quite expensive.
