# 2021 Volkswagen ID.4 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Volkswagen ID.4 marks the brand's earnest entry into the mainstream all-electric crossover market, offering a comfortable ride, spacious cabin, and composed handling. However, as an early model-year electric vehicle, owner reports highlight several recurring glitches—particularly involving software instability, electrical displays, and electronic door handles. Buyers should carefully evaluate software update histories and verify that major electrical and safety campaigns have been successfully completed.

---

## Engine

### Single-Motor Permanent Magnet Synchronous (Rear-Wheel Drive)

The standard rear-wheel-drive powertrain utilizes a single permanent magnet synchronous motor producing approximately 201 horsepower and 229 lb-ft of torque. It provides smooth, linear acceleration suitable for daily commuting and offers the highest EPA-estimated driving range for this model year.

### Dual-Motor Permanent Magnet Synchronous (All-Wheel Drive)

The dual-motor all-wheel-drive configuration adds an asynchronous front motor to boost total output to approximately 295 horsepower. This setup improves all-weather traction and acceleration responsiveness while maintaining balanced everyday drivability.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

A single-speed direct-drive automatic transmission manages power delivery from the electric motors without conventional gear shifts. It operates quietly and reliably, requiring minimal mechanical maintenance compared to traditional multi-speed gearboxes.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Brake system concerns generally relate to software calibration affecting regenerative braking integration and electronic brake control.

**Electronic Brake System and Pedal Response**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected vehicle movement while holding the brake pedal in traffic, or inconsistent regenerative and friction braking feel after software updates.
- **Solution:** Check for applicable electronic brake software updates or have the regenerative braking system inspected by a qualified technician.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Instrument Cluster Displays

Electrical system complaints frequently center around screen blackouts, software glitches, and intermittent infotainment reboots.

**Screen Blanking and Reboots**
- **Symptoms:** Both the central infotainment screen and the driver instrument cluster randomly go blank and reboot while driving, temporarily disabling the speedometer and backup camera.
- **Solution:** Perform software updates or visit a dealer to address lingering display stability issues.

## Exterior

### Exterior Door Handles and Latches

Exterior electronic door locks and charge-port doors can occasionally experience mechanical or electrical latch failures.

**Electronic Door Handle Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Electronic door handles fail to unlatch the doors properly, requiring multiple attempts to open or leaving occupants temporarily locked out.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty electronic actuators or handles, noting that some out-of-warranty replacements may incur significant repair costs.
- **Cost:** $900

## Safety

### Air Bag and Restraints System

Safety complaints occasionally highlight airbag warning lights and software-related emergency communication system errors.

**Airbag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** An intermittent or persistent airbag warning light illuminates on the dashboard, indicating a potential fault in the occupant detection or restraint system.
- **Solution:** Have a dealer diagnose the airbag sensor wiring and passenger occupancy detection system (PODS) to clear fault codes and restore full functionality.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V852000: Steering:Gear Box (Other Than Rack And Pinion)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=ID.4&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An incorrectly assembled or missing drive bushing may cause a loss of steering control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** A dealer will replace the steering gear, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V607000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=ID.4&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect, and replace as necessary, the rear outboard seat belt assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V606000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=ID.4&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect, and replace as necessary, the middle-rear seat belt assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V413000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=ID.4&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Adjusting the horizontal headlight beam can increase glare, reducing visibility for the driver and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will remove the caps from the vertical aim and install them on the horizontal aim, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V162000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=ID.4&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Incorrect soldering of the flexible printed circuit assembled (FPCA), inside the high voltage (HV) battery, may cause an unreliable connection. An unreliable battery connection may cause a stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the battery cell modules, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V858000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=ID.4&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An incorrectly folded air bag may not protect the occupant as intended during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will replace the passenger air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V040000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Battery:Management System/Energy Control Module (Bms/Becm):Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=ID.4&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Software programing may cause the high voltage (HV) battery management control module to restart or the pulse inverter to deactivate. A reset or deactivation may cause a loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the HV battery management control unit and the pulse inverter control unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V312000: Structure:Body:Door:Handle:Exterior](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=ID.4&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles may have been manufactured with door handles that allow water to enter the circuit board assembly, which can cause the doors to open unexpectedly when the vehicle is subject to high lateral forces at speeds below 9 mph/15 kmh. A vehicle door that opens unexpectedly while the vehicle is in motion increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the vehicle software, and inspect the door handles as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Volkswagen ID.4 delivers an appealing blend of electric efficiency, refined ride quality, and practical utility. However, prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle's service records to ensure all software updates have been applied, and verify that electrical displays, door latches, and safety systems operate without hesitation.
