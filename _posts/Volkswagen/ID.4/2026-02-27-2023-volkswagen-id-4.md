# 2023 Volkswagen ID.4 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Volkswagen ID.4 is a popular all-electric crossover offering a comfortable ride, spacious interior, and flexible powertrain choices. However, prospective buyers should note that owners have reported a variety of recurring electrical and body hardware glitches. While the vehicle delivers strong everyday utility, checking for software updates and completed recall work is essential before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### Single Motor RWD (Electric)

The rear-wheel-drive electric motor setup delivers approximately 201 horsepower, providing smooth and efficient daily acceleration. This baseline powertrain option is generally mechanically robust, though it shares the broader electronic and software ecosystem of the vehicle.

### Dual Motor AWD (Electric)

The dual-motor all-wheel-drive configuration increases output to around 295 horsepower, enhancing traction and acceleration response in poor weather conditions. Like its RWD counterpart, the hardware is fundamentally solid, but buyers should ensure all vehicle software updates are current.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct Drive

A single-speed automatic transmission delivers power seamlessly to the wheels without traditional gear shifts. This direct-drive unit has proven mechanically reliable and requires minimal maintenance compared to conventional internal-combustion transmissions.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System

The vehicle relies heavily on touch-sensitive panels and digital screens, which can occasionally experience software instability or unexpected reboots.

**Infotainment Screen Freezing and Rebooting**
- **Symptoms:** The central display and instrument cluster may freeze, go blank, or spontaneously reboot while driving, resulting in temporary loss of navigation, climate controls, and audio.
- **Solution:** Perform software updates at an authorized dealership or reset the system; persistent failures may require module replacement.

## Exterior

### Door Locks and Latches

Electrical and mechanical components within the door assemblies can experience operational failures, sometimes intersecting with manufacturer recall campaigns.

**Door Latch and Actuator Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Doors may fail to open or latch securely from either the inside or outside, occasionally requiring excessive physical force or failing to seal properly.
- **Solution:** Inspect door hardware, realign latch bolts, or replace faulty door lock actuators and handles as necessary.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced driver assistance systems generally function as intended, though sporadic sensor or software glitches can lead to erratic behavior.

**Unexpected Braking or Acceleration**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report instances where the vehicle may unexpectedly apply the brakes or experience unintended acceleration anomalies during low-speed maneuvering or parking.
- **Solution:** Have driver-assistance sensors, camera calibrations, and software control modules inspected by a qualified technician.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V823000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=ID.4&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." An inoperative rearview camera display can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rearview camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V830000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Traction Motor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=ID.4&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Coolant that leaks into the high-voltage system may impact safety-critical systems and warning lights, causing a loss of vehicle control or unintended air bag deployment, and increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the electric motor, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V956000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery:Cables](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=ID.4&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The 12-Volt battery charging cable may contact the steering column shaft and cause the cable insulation to wear, possibly resulting in a short circuit. A short circuit may cause a loss of drive power, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the 12-Volt battery charging cable and repair or replace it, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V750000: Structure](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=ID.4&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The interior sunshade for the panoramic glass roof may be insufficiently fire retardant. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 302, "Flammability of Interior Materials." In a fire, fabric that is not fire retardant may further fuel a fire, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will apply flame retardant to the sunshade fabric, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V344000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=ID.4&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 101, "Control and Displays" and 111, "Rear Visibility." Displays that do not show critical information, such as vehicle speed or the rearview camera image, increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the vehicle software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V643000: Air Bags:Frontal:Passenger Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=ID.4&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** An improperly secured air bag inflator may not deploy as intended, increasing the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the air bag as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V651000: Structure:Body:Door:Handle:Exterior](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=ID.4&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The door handles may allow water to enter the circuit board assembly, which can cause the doors to open unexpectedly. A vehicle door that opens unexpectedly while driving increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen dealers will inspect and replace the door handles as necessary, and update the vehicle software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V875000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=ID.4&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera image may not display when the vehicle is placed in reverse or when the park function is manually activated. ## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Volkswagen ID.4 is a comfortable and modern electric crossover, but used car shoppers should perform a thorough inspection. Pay close attention to infotainment stability, electronic door latches, and ensure that all applicable safety recalls have been fully addressed by a dealer.
