**Vehicle Introduction**

The 1995 Volkswagen Corrado is a stylish and sporty coupe that was offered with a variety of engine and transmission options. The base model came with a 2.0-liter inline-4 engine that produced 115 horsepower and 122 lb-ft of torque. The VR6 model featured a narrow-angle V6 engine that produced 178 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque. The Corrado was praised for its handling, performance, and comfort, but it was criticized for its cramped interior and limited trunk space.

**Engine**

**2.0L Inline-4**
* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Intake gaskets can fail and cause leaks, leading to performance issues and decreased power.
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Crankshaft position sensor failure:** The crankshaft position sensor can fail, leading to starting issues and engine stalling.
    * Solution: Replace crankshaft position sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**VR6**
* **Head gasket failure:** Head gaskets can fail, leading to coolant leaks and overheating.
    * Solution: Replace head gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing chain tensioner failure:** The timing chain tensioner can fail, leading to timing issues and engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace timing chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $900+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

    * Recalls: None Found

    * Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic**

    * Recalls: None Found
* **Torque converter failure:** The torque converter can fail, leading to transmission slippage and power loss.

**Summary**

The 1995 Volkswagen Corrado is a reliable and fun-to-drive vehicle, but it is not without its issues. The most common problems with the Corrado include engine gasket failures, transmission shifting issues, suspension noise, electrical failures, and interior cosmetic issues. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific part that has failed and the location of the repair. However, overall, the Corrado is a well-built vehicle that provides owners with a unique and enjoyable driving experience.
