**Introduction:**
The 1990 Volkswagen Corrado is a German-made sports car that was offered with two engine options: a 1.8L 16-valve inline-4 and a 2.0L 16-valve inline-4. Both engines were paired with a 5-speed manual transmission. The Corrado was known for its sleek design, responsive handling, and decent performance. However, it was also known for its reliability issues.

**Engine**
* **1.8L 16-valve inline-4**
    * **Timing belt failure:** The timing belt is responsible for synchronizing the camshaft and crankshaft. If the timing belt fails, it can cause catastrophic engine damage. Symptoms of timing belt failure include a sudden loss of power, unusual noises from the engine, and difficulty starting. The solution is to replace the timing belt and any other damaged components. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure:** This issue is common on early model years. Symptoms of intake manifold gasket failure include a decrease in engine power, rough idle, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000
    * **Head gasket failure:** Another common issue on early model years. Symptoms of head gasket failure include coolant leaks, overheating, and loss of compression. The solution is to replace the head gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **2.0L 16-valve inline-4**
    * **No major issues found**

**Transmission**

* **5-speed manual transmission**
    * **No major issues found**

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension:**
    * **Strut mount bearing failure:** This issue is common on cars with higher mileage. Symptoms of strut mount bearing failure include a clunking or knocking noise when turning or driving over bumps. The solution is to replace the strut mount bearings. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Rear suspension:**
    * **No major issues found**

**Electrical**

* **Electrical system:**
    * **Alternator failure:** This issue can occur on cars with higher mileage. Symptoms of alternator failure include difficulty starting the car, flickering headlights, and a dead battery. The solution is to replace the alternator. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000
    * **Starter failure:** Another issue that can occur on cars with higher mileage. Symptoms of starter failure include difficulty starting the car or a clicking noise when turning the key. The solution is to replace the starter. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Wiring harness issues:** Some Corrados have been known to experience wiring harness issues, which can cause various electrical problems. Symptoms can range from flickering lights to engine control issues. The solution is to repair or replace the affected wiring. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Exterior**

* **Body panels:**
    * **Rust:** The Corrado is prone to rust, especially in areas such as the fenders, doors, and trunk lid. The solution is to repair or replace the affected body panels. Estimated cost to fix: Varies depending on the extent of the damage
* **Sunroof:**
    * **Leaking sunroof:** The Corrado's sunroof is known to leak, especially on older models. Symptoms of a leaking sunroof include water dripping into the cabin or a musty smell. The solution is to replace the sunroof seals. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000

**Interior**

* **Dashboard:**
    * **Cracking dashboard:** The Corrado's dashboard is prone to cracking, especially on cars that have been exposed to excessive heat or sunlight. The solution is to replace the dashboard. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Air conditioning system:**
    * **A/C compressor failure:** This issue can occur on cars with higher mileage. Symptoms of A/C compressor failure include no cold air coming from the vents or a loud noise when the A/C is turned on. The solution is to replace the A/C compressor. Estimated cost to fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Summary**
The 1990 Volkswagen Corrado is a fun and stylish sports car that has a few reliability issues. Some of the most common problems include timing belt failure, intake manifold gasket failure, and electrical issues. The severity and cost of repairs can vary depending on the specific issue and the age and mileage of the car.
