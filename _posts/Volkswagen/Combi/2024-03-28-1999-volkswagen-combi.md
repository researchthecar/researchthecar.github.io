**Introduction**

The 1999 Volkswagen Combi is a compact van that offers ample cargo space, versatility, and a robust engine. It is powered by a 2.0-liter inline-four engine that produces 112 horsepower and 128 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed manual transmission. Other engine and transmission options are not available for this model year.

Drivers appreciate the Combi's spacious interior, fuel efficiency, and reliable performance. However, it has also been subject to some mechanical and cosmetic issues.

**Engine**

* **2.0L Inline-Four Engine**
    * **Oil leaks:** From the valve cover gasket or oil pan gasket. Symptoms include a burning oil smell, low oil levels, and smoke from the engine. Solution: Replace the faulty gasket(s). Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    * **Timing belt issues:** Can fail prematurely, leading to catastrophic engine damage. Symptoms include a squealing or clicking noise, difficulty starting, and reduced engine power. Solution: Replace the timing belt and associated components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Causes a vacuum leak, leading to rough idling, decreased performance, and increased emissions. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **Clutch issues:** Premature wear or failure of the clutch. Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, slipping during acceleration, and a burning smell. Solution: Replace the clutch components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    * **Synchronizer failure:** Causes difficulty shifting into specific gears. Symptoms include grinding noises or a lack of engagement. Solution: Replace the faulty synchronizer(s). Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension:**
    * **Strut mounts:** Can wear out, leading to a loose or rattling suspension. Symptoms include clunking noises, reduced stability, and premature tire wear. Solution: Replace the strut mounts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per axle
* **Rear suspension:**
    * **Rear axle bushings:** Can deteriorate, causing a loose suspension. Symptoms include swaying or drifting during turns, uneven tire wear, and increased road noise. Solution: Replace the rear axle bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator issues:** Can fail unexpectedly, leading to a loss of electrical power. Symptoms include dim headlights, dashboard warnings, and difficulty starting. Solution: Replace the alternator and inspect related components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Starter problems:** Can fail, preventing the vehicle from starting. Symptoms include a clicking sound or no response when turning the key. Solution: Replace the starter motor or related electrical components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** Can form on the body panels, particularly in neglected or accident-damaged areas. Symptoms include visible corrosion, bubbling paint, and weakened structure. Solution: Repair or replace rusted panels, apply rustproofing treatment. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies widely depending on the extent of damage
* **Tail light housing:** Can develop cracks or leaks, leading to water damage. Symptoms include condensation or water inside the housing. Solution: Replace the tail light housing. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per side

**Interior**

* **Dashboard:**
    * **Cracks or fading:** Can occur on the exposed plastic surfaces due to sun damage or wear and tear. Solution: Replace or repair the damaged dashboard panels. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1,000+
* **Door panels:**
    * **Loose or rattling:** Can be caused by broken clips or worn-out fasteners. Symptoms include rattling noises or difficulty opening/closing doors. Solution: Replace or tighten the faulty clips or fasteners. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per door

**Summary**

The 1999 Volkswagen Combi has several potential mechanical and cosmetic issues, although the severity and cost of these issues vary widely. Some owners have reported expensive repairs related to engine problems, while others have experienced only minor issues. Regular maintenance and timely repairs can help mitigate these issues and extend the lifespan of the vehicle.
