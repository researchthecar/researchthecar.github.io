**Introduction:**

The 1998 Volkswagen Combi is a versatile and economical vehicle that has been praised for its spacious interior, reliable engine, and rugged construction. It was available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 1.4L inline-4 with 75 horsepower and 82 lb-ft of torque, a 1.6L inline-4 with 101 horsepower and 103 lb-ft of torque, and a 1.8L inline-4 with 122 horsepower and 129 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options included a 5-speed manual, a 4-speed automatic, and a continuously variable transmission (CVT).

Despite its overall reliability, the 1998 Volkswagen Combi has been known to experience certain issues.

**Engine**

**1.4L Inline-4**
* **Oil leaks from valve cover gasket:** The valve cover gasket can fail, leading to oil leaks. Symptoms include oil spots under the vehicle and a burning oil smell. Fix: Replace the valve cover gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
* **Timing belt failure:** The timing belt can fail, causing catastrophic engine damage. Symptoms include a loud snapping noise, engine stalling, and no start conditions. Fix: Replace the timing belt and related components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+

**1.6L Inline-4**
* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, leading to vacuum leaks and engine performance issues. Symptoms include rough idle, reduced power, and increased fuel consumption. Fix: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
* **Water pump failure:** The water pump can fail, leading to overheating and engine damage. Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating gauge readings, and steam from the engine. Fix: Replace the water pump. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**1.8L Inline-4**
* **Coolant flange leaks:** The coolant flange can develop leaks, leading to coolant loss and overheating. Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating gauge readings, and steam from the engine. Fix: Replace the coolant flange. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250+
* **Camshaft position sensor failure:** The camshaft position sensor can fail, causing engine performance issues and stalling. Symptoms include rough idle, reduced power, and engine stalling. Fix: Replace the camshaft position sensor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**
* **Clutch failure:** The clutch can wear out or become damaged, leading to slipping or difficulty shifting gears. Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, slipping gears, and a burning smell. Fix: Replace the clutch assembly. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+

**4-Speed Automatic**
* **Transmission fluid leaks:** The transmission fluid lines or seals can develop leaks, leading to transmission fluid loss and transmission damage. Symptoms include slipping gears, delayed gear engagement, and transmission overheating. Fix: Repair or replace the leaking components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Torque converter failure:** The torque converter can fail, leading to transmission shuddering, slipping, and overheating. Symptoms include transmission shuddering, slipping gears, and overheating. Fix: Replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+

**CVT**
* **Belt and pulley wear:** The CVT belt and pulleys can wear out over time, leading to slipping and transmission failure. Symptoms include slipping gears, delayed gear engagement, and transmission overheating. Fix: Replace the CVT belt and pulleys. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000+

**Suspension**

* **Strut failure:** The struts can fail, leading to poor handling and decreased ride comfort. Symptoms include excessive bouncing, nosediving during braking, and uneven tire wear. Fix: Replace the struts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+ per axle
* **Ball joint failure:** The ball joints can fail, leading to unstable handling and excessive tire wear. Symptoms include clunking noises, vibration, and uneven tire wear. Fix: Replace the ball joints. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per side

**Electrical**

* **Battery failure:** The battery can fail, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle or electrical system failure. Symptoms include difficulty starting, dim lights, and electrical component failure. Fix: Replace the battery. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
* **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail, leading to insufficient charging of the battery and electrical system failure. Symptoms include dim lights, battery warning light, and electrical component failure. Fix: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
* **Starter failure:** The starter can fail, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle or complete failure to start. Symptoms include difficulty starting, grinding noises, and no start conditions. Fix: Replace the starter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** The body can develop rust, especially around the wheel wells, fenders, and undercarriage. Symptoms include visible rust spots, bubbling paint, and weakened metal. Fix: Repair or replace rusted areas. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of the rust.
* **Headlight switch failure:** The headlight switch can fail, leading to inoperable headlights. Symptoms include no headlights, intermittent headlight operation, and headlight flickering. Fix: Replace the headlight switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracks:** The dashboard can develop cracks over time due to heat and UV exposure. Symptoms include visible cracks on the dashboard. Fix: Replace the dashboard. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Door lock failure:** The door locks can fail, leading to difficulty locking or unlocking the doors. Symptoms include difficulty turning the key in the lock, doors not locking or unlocking properly, and door lock actuators not working. Fix: Repair or replace the door lock assembly. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ per door

**Summary:**

Overall, the 1998 Volkswagen Combi is a reliable and versatile vehicle with a good track record. However, it has been known to experience certain issues, particularly with its engine, transmission, and suspension components. The severity of these issues can vary, and the cost to fix them can be significant. It's important to consider the potential costs and maintenance requirements when purchasing a used 1998 Volkswagen Combi.
