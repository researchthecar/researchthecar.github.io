**Introduction**

The 1991 Volkswagen Combi is a popular minivan that offers spacious seating and a comfortable ride. It is available with a range of engine and transmission options, including a 1.8L inline-4 with a 5-speed manual transmission or a 2.5L inline-5 with a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Combi is known for its reliability and durability, but it has a few common issues that owners may encounter.

**Engine**

* **1.8L Inline-4**
    * **Head gasket failure:** Oil and coolant may leak from the head gasket, causing overheating and engine damage. Solution: Replace the head gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $700+
    * **Timing belt failure:** The timing belt may break, causing the engine to stall and potentially resulting in catastrophic engine damage. Solution: Replace the timing belt and associated components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
* **2.5L Inline-5**
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket may fail, causing vacuum leaks and engine performance issues. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Manual**
    * **Clutch failure:** The clutch may slip or wear out, making it difficult to shift gears. Solution: Replace the clutch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
* **4-Speed Automatic**
    * **Transmission slipping:** The transmission may slip out of gear when driving, causing a loss of power. Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+

**Suspension**

* **Front struts:** The front struts may wear out, causing a loss of ride quality and handling. Solution: Replace the front struts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
* **Rear shocks:** The rear shocks may wear out, causing a loss of ride quality and handling. Solution: Replace the rear shocks. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** The alternator may fail, causing the battery to discharge and preventing the vehicle from starting. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
* **Starter failure:** The starter may fail, preventing the engine from starting. Solution: Replace the starter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Battery drain:** The battery may drain prematurely due to a parasitic draw from electrical components. Solution: Diagnose the source of the parasitic draw and repair as needed. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the source of the draw

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** The Combi may develop rust on the body panels, particularly around the wheel arches and lower door panels. Solution: Remove the rust and apply protective coatings. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity of the rust
* **Door lock failure:** The door locks may fail, making it difficult to open or close the doors. Solution: Replace the door lock actuator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
* **Tailgate rattle:** The tailgate may rattle when closed, especially on rough roads. Solution: Adjust the tailgate hinges and latch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracks:** The dashboard may develop cracks over time, especially in vehicles that are exposed to sunlight. Solution: Replace or repair the dashboard. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Window switches failure:** The window switches may fail, causing the windows to become inoperable. Solution: Replace the window switches. Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150+
* **Air conditioner vent failure:** The air conditioner vents may break or become loose, restricting airflow. Solution: Replace or repair the air conditioner vents. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Summary**

Overall, the 1991 Volkswagen Combi is a reliable and durable vehicle. However, it does have a few common issues that owners may encounter. These issues can range in severity from minor annoyances to more serious mechanical problems. The cost to fix these issues can also vary depending on the severity of the problem and the location of the repair.
