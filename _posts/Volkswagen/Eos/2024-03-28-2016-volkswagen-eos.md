The 2016 Volkswagen Eos is a compact convertible that offers a stylish design and a comfortable ride. It comes with a choice of two engine options: a 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 (210 hp/207 lb-ft) and a 3.6L V6 (280 hp/265 lb-ft). Both engines are paired with a 6-speed DSG dual-clutch transmission. While the Eos is praised for its handling and performance, it has received some criticism for its cramped interior and limited cargo space.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged I-4**

* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner can fail, causing the timing chain to jump or break, resulting in catastrophic engine damage. Solution: Replace the timing chain and tensioner. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+ Recalls: 17V125

* **Carbon Buildup on Intake Valves:** Description/Symptoms: Carbon buildup on the intake valves can restrict airflow, reducing engine power and performance. Solution: Clean the intake valves. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**3.6L V6**

* **No Significant Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**6-Speed DSG Dual-Clutch Transmission**

* **Mechatronic Unit Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The mechatronic unit, which controls the transmission's shifting, can fail, causing the transmission to shift erratically or not at all. Solution: Replace the mechatronic unit. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+ Recalls: 17V427

**Suspension**

* **Front Lower Control Arm Bushing Wear:** Description/Symptoms: The front lower control arm bushings can wear out prematurely, causing clunking noises and poor handling. Solution: Replace the bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

* **Rear Shock Absorber Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The rear shock absorbers can fail, resulting in a bouncy or rough ride. Solution: Replace the shock absorbers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Electrical**

* **Electrical Shorts in Wiring Harness:** Description/Symptoms: Electrical shorts in the wiring harness can cause various electrical issues, such as flickering lights, malfunctioning gauges, or even engine problems. Solution: Repair or replace the affected wiring. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+

* **Battery Drain:** Description/Symptoms: The battery can drain excessively, leaving the vehicle unable to start. Solution: Diagnose and repair the cause of the battery drain, such as a faulty alternator or parasitic drain. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Convertible Top Problems:** Description/Symptoms: The convertible top may fail to open or close properly, or may leak water when closed. Solution: Repair or replace the convertible top mechanism or seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Interior**

* **Sunroof Wind Deflector Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The sunroof wind deflector may fail, causing excessive wind noise or buffeting while driving with the sunroof open. Solution: Replace the wind deflector. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Overall Summary**

The 2016 Volkswagen Eos has a number of common issues that can be expensive to repair. The most severe issues include timing chain tensioner failure (2.0L engine) and mechatronic unit failure (DSG transmission). These issues can potentially cause catastrophic engine or transmission damage. Other common issues include electrical shorts, battery drain, convertible top problems, and sunroof wind deflector failure. While some of these issues are minor and relatively inexpensive to fix, others can be quite costly, especially if they are not addressed promptly.
