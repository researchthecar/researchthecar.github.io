**Introduction**

The 2016 Volkswagen Volksbus is a versatile and spacious people-mover, known for its comfortable ride and family-friendly features. It is available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including:

* 2.8L V6 with 8-speed automatic transmission (201 hp, 225 lb-ft of torque)
* 3.6L V6 with 8-speed automatic transmission (280 hp, 266 lb-ft of torque)

While the Volksbus is generally well-regarded, owners have reported a number of issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

* **2.8L V6**
    * **Oil leak from camshaft adjuster:** This issue can cause a visible oil leak from the engine.
        * Solution: Replace the camshaft adjuster.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $700 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **3.6L V6**
    * **Timing chain tensioner failure:** This issue can lead to engine damage if not addressed promptly.
        * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
        * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 17V092000

**Transmission**

* **8-speed automatic transmission**
    * **Clutch pack failure:** This issue can cause the transmission to slip or lose power.
        * Solution: Replace the clutch packs.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front control arm bushings:** These bushings can wear out prematurely, leading to excessive noise and vibration.
    * Solution: Replace the control arm bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Headlight switch failure:** This issue can cause the headlights to become inoperable.
    * Solution: Replace the headlight switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 16V339000

**Exterior**

* **Loose rear bumper cover:** The rear bumper cover may become loose, resulting in a rattling sound.
    * Solution: Reattach the bumper cover.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $100+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **HVAC blower motor failure:** This issue can cause the blower motor to stop working, resulting in a loss of heating or cooling.
    * Solution: Replace the blower motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Volkswagen Volksbus has a number of reported issues, including engine oil leaks, transmission clutch pack failures, and electrical problems. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but some can be significant. Overall, the Volksbus is a generally reliable vehicle, but potential buyers should be aware of these issues before making a purchase.
