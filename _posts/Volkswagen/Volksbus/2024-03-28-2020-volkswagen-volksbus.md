## 2020 Volkswagen Volksbus: Owner Reported Problems

The 2020 Volkswagen Volksbus is a front-engine, front-wheel-drive compact MPV available in two trim levels: Trendline and Comfortline. Power comes from a 2.0-liter, turbocharged inline-four engine producing 201 horsepower and 207 lb-ft of torque paired with a six-speed automatic transmission.

Owners of the 2020 Volkswagen Volksbus appreciate its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and peppy engine. However, the vehicle has also been reported to have numerous issues, as outlined below:

**Engine**
* 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-Four

**Transmission**
* Six-Speed Automatic

**Suspension**
* **Leaking Shock Absorbers**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil leakage around the top of the shock absorbers, resulting in a loss of damping and stability.
Solution: Replacement of the faulty shock absorbers.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
* **Malfunctioning Door Lock Modules**
Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or complete failure of the door lock modules, causing the doors to lock or unlock erratically.
Solution: Replacement of the affected door lock modules.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

* **Faulty Engine Control Module (ECM)**
Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling, poor performance, and potential damage to other engine components due to incorrect fuel and ignition control.
Solution: Replacement of the faulty ECM.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
* **Rusting Brake Rotors**
Description/Symptoms: Premature rusting of the brake rotors, leading to increased brake noise, reduced braking performance, and potential component failure.
Solution: Replacement of the rusted brake rotors.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

* **Cracking Headlights**
Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing in the headlight lenses, allowing moisture to enter and potentially damaging the internal components.
Solution: Replacement of the affected headlights.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
* **Squeaky Dashboard**
Description/Symptoms: Noises and rattles emanating from the dashboard, particularly on rough road surfaces.
Solution: Inspection and tightening of loose dashboard components or application of sound dampening material.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
Recalls: None Found

* **Malfunctioning Climate Control System**
Description/Symptoms: Failure of the climate control system to regulate temperature or airflow, resulting in uncomfortable cabin conditions.
Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the affected system components, such as the AC compressor, blower motor, or control module.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
While the 2020 Volkswagen Volksbus offers a spacious and enjoyable ride, it has been plagued by a number of issues that can be costly to repair. The leaking shock absorbers, faulty engine control module, and premature rust can impact the vehicle's safety and reliability. Additionally, the malfunctioning climate control system and squeaky dashboard can lead to discomfort and inconvenience. Overall, the severity and cost of these issues should be considered carefully before purchasing or leasing a 2020 Volkswagen Volksbus.
