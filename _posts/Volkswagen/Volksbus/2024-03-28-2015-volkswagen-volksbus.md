**Introduction:**

The 2015 Volkswagen Volksbus is a versatile and spacious van that offers a comfortable ride and impressive fuel economy. It is available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 2.0L turbocharged gasoline engine that produces 201 horsepower and 207 lb-ft of torque. The Volksbus is praised for its practicality, affordability, and fun-to-drive nature, but it has also received criticism for its lack of modern technology and safety features.

**Engine**

* **2.0L Turbocharged Gasoline Engine**
    * **Oil Cooler Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Premature failure of the oil cooler can lead to coolant and oil leaking, overheating, and engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace the oil cooler.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Fuel Injector Issues**
        * Description/Symptoms: Faulty fuel injectors can cause engine misfires, reduced power, and increased fuel consumption.
        * Solution: Replace the affected fuel injectors.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **No Issues Found**
* **6-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Transmission Fluid Leaks**
        * Description/Symptoms: Leaks from seals or gaskets can cause loss of transmission fluid, leading to slipping gears, shuddering, and overheating.
        * Solution: Replace the leaking seals or gaskets.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle's battery drains prematurely, even when the engine is off.
    * Solution: Identify the source of the drain and repair or replace the affected component.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the cause
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical Short**
    * Description/Symptoms: An electrical short can cause various issues, such as blown fuses, inoperable lights, or even a fire.
    * Solution: Locate and repair the short circuit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the location and severity of the short
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Body Panels**
    * Description/Symptoms: Corrosion can develop on the body panels, especially in areas prone to road salt or moisture.
    * Solution: Remove the rust and apply a rust-resistant treatment.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of the rust
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard may develop cracks over time, especially in areas exposed to sunlight.
    * Solution: Replace the dashboard or use a dash cover to prevent further cracking.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2015 Volkswagen Volksbus has some notable issues, particularly with the engine and transmission. The oil cooler failure and fuel injector problems with the 2.0L turbocharged gasoline engine can be costly to repair. The 6-speed automatic transmission may experience transmission fluid leaks, leading to further problems if not addressed promptly. However, the Volksbus has generally good reliability in terms of suspension, electrical, and exterior components.
