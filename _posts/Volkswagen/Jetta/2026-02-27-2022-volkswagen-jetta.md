# 2022 Volkswagen Jetta Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2022 Volkswagen Jetta, buyers get a compact sedan known for solid fuel economy and everyday comfort. While many owners enjoy trouble-free daily driving, owner reports collected by safety regulators highlight a few recurring mechanical and electrical concerns that prospective buyers should keep in mind during an inspection.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 1.5-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 158 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque, offering a balanced mix of efficiency and responsive power for daily commuting. Overall, it provides reliable everyday performance, though buyers should watch for coolant consumption or cooling system anomalies.

**Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, illuminated warning lights, and engine overheating caused by premature head gasket failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect the cooling system and engine block for leaks; replace the head gasket if compromised.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected engine stalls while driving, accompanied by warning lights on the instrument cluster.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes related to fuel delivery, ignition, or powertrain management modules.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The conventional 8-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth shifts and pairs well with the standard turbo engine for highway cruising. It generally maintains a solid reputation for durability when routine fluid maintenance is observed.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The 6-speed manual transmission is available on select base trims, providing a traditional, engaging driving experience for enthusiasts. It is mechanically straightforward, though clutch wear depends heavily on prior driving habits.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides adequate stopping power under normal conditions, but some owners report accelerated wear on rear brake components.

**Premature Rear Brake Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rapid thinning of rear brake pads and rotors, squealing or growling noises when braking, or failure to pass vehicle inspections.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear brake pads and rotors for premature wear; replace friction materials and resurface or replace rotors as needed.

## Safety

### Travel Assist and Forward Collision

Driver assistance technologies enhance highway safety, though sensor glitches can intermittently disable features like adaptive cruise control.

**Steering and Travel Assist Warning Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Frequent warning messages indicating that Travel Assist or adaptive cruise control is unavailable, accompanied by repeated chimes or flashing lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering wheel sensor assembly and software modules; update or replace faulty steering wheel components.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V514000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Jetta&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The manufacturing process of the eMMC memory module in the infotainment system may cause the rearview camera image not to display. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." ## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Volkswagen Jetta is a stylish and efficient daily driver, but used car shoppers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to the condition of the rear brakes, verify that all driver assistance features operate without warning chimes, and check the engine bay for any signs of coolant leaks.
