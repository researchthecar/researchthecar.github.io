**Introduction**: The 1998 Volkswagen Jetta is a compact sedan known for its stylish design, comfortable interior, and fuel efficiency. It was available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 2.0L inline-four with a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission, and a 1.8T inline-four with a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**
- 2.0L Inline-Four
-- **Crankshaft Position Sensor Failure**: The crankshaft position sensor may fail, leading to intermittent engine stalling, rough idling, and difficulty starting. Replacement of the sensor is necessary. $100 - $200+
-- **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak**: The intake manifold gasket can develop a leak, causing a vacuum leak and decreased engine performance. Replacement of the gasket is required. $200 - $400+
- 1.8T Inline-Four

-- **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**: The timing chain tensioner may fail, leading to engine noise, decreased performance, and potentially catastrophic engine damage. Replacement of the tensioner and related components is necessary. $1,000 - $2,500+

**Transmission**
- 5-Speed Manual
-- **Clutch Replacement**: The clutch may wear out over time, leading to difficulty shifting gears and eventually slipping. Replacement of the clutch is necessary. $400 - $800+
- 4-Speed Automatic
-- **Transmission Slippage**: The transmission may develop slippage, causing hesitation and difficulty accelerating. Replacement or rebuild of the transmission may be necessary. $1,500 - $3,000+

**Suspension**
- **Strut Mount Bearing Failure**: The strut mount bearings may fail, causing a clunking noise when driving over bumps. Replacement of the strut mounts is necessary. $200 - $400+
- **Control Arm Bushing Failure**: The control arm bushings may wear out over time, leading to decreased ride quality and handling. Replacement of the bushings is recommended. $100 - $200+ per bushing

**Electrical**
- **Alternator Failure**: The alternator may fail, leading to a loss of electrical power and potential battery discharge. Replacement of the alternator is necessary. $500 - $1,000+
- **Battery Draining**: The battery may drain prematurely due to a faulty electrical component or parasitic drain. Diagnosis and repair of the underlying issue is required. $100 - $500+

**Exterior**
- **Power Window Failure**: The power window motors or switches may fail, causing one or more windows to become inoperable. Replacement of the faulty components is necessary. $200 - $600+ per window
- **Door Lock Failure**: The door locks may fail to operate properly due to a faulty lock actuator or wiring issue. Replacement of the actuator or wiring repair is required. $100 - $400+ per lock

**Interior**
- **Climate Control Panel Failure**: The climate control panel may fail to operate properly, causing issues with heating or air conditioning. Replacement of the panel or related components may be necessary. $200 - $600+
- **Dashboard Warning Lights**: Various dashboard warning lights may illuminate intermittently or persistently due to sensor or electrical faults. Diagnosis and repair of the underlying issue is required. $50 - $500+ per issue

**Summary**
The 1998 Volkswagen Jetta is known for its comfort and fuel efficiency but may have some potential issues. Engine and transmission problems can be expensive to repair, while electrical and suspension issues are generally more affordable to fix. It's important to consider these potential issues when purchasing or maintaining a Jetta of this year.
