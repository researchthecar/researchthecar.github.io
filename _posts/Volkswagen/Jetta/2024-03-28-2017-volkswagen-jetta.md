**Introduction:**

The 2017 Volkswagen Jetta is a compact sedan that is available with a variety of engine and transmission options. The base engine is a 1.4-liter turbocharged four-cylinder that produces 150 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque. A more powerful 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder is also available, producing 210 horsepower and 206 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a six-speed manual, an eight-speed automatic, and a six-speed dual-clutch automatic.

The Jetta is known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and good fuel economy. However, it has also been criticized for its lackluster performance and some questionable design choices.

**Engine**

**1.4-liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

- **Fuel Pump Failure:**
   - Description/Symptoms: The fuel pump can fail, causing the engine to stall or lose power.
   - Solution: Replace the fuel pump.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
   - Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 18V586000

**2.0-liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

- **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual**

- **Clutch Problems:**
   - Description/Symptoms: The clutch can wear out prematurely, causing difficulty shifting gears or slipping gears.
   - Solution: Replace the clutch.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
   - Recalls: None Found

**Eight-Speed Automatic**

- **Software Problems:**
   - Description/Symptoms: The transmission control module can develop software problems, causing erratic shifting or transmission failure.
   - Solution: Update the transmission software.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
   - Recalls: None Found

**Six-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic**

- **Mechatronic Unit Failure:**
   - Description/Symptoms: The mechatronic unit, which controls the transmission, can fail, causing transmission failure.
   - Solution: Replace the mechatronic unit.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
   - Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 18V508000

**Suspension**

- **Strut Mount Failure:**
   - Description/Symptoms: The strut mounts can fail, causing a knocking or rattling noise while driving.
   - Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
   - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Battery Drain:**
   - Description/Symptoms: The battery can drain prematurely, causing the vehicle to not start.
   - Solution: Diagnose and repair the cause of the battery drain.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
   - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Headlight Condensation:**
   - Description/Symptoms: The headlights can develop condensation inside the lenses, impairing visibility at night.
   - Solution: Replace the headlight lenses.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
   - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Climate Control Issues:**
   - Description/Symptoms: The climate control system can malfunction, causing the temperature to be too hot or too cold.
   - Solution: Diagnose and repair the cause of the malfunction.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
   - Recalls: None Found

- **Bluetooth Connectivity Problems:**
   - Description/Symptoms: The Bluetooth system can experience connectivity issues, making it difficult to pair with devices.
   - Solution: Update the Bluetooth software or replace the Bluetooth module.
   - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
   - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2017 Volkswagen Jetta has several issues that can affect its performance, reliability, and comfort. The most severe and expensive issue is the mechatronic unit failure in the six-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission. Other issues, such as clutch problems and fuel pump failure, can also be costly to repair. Overall, the 2017 Jetta is not as reliable as some of its competitors, and potential buyers should be aware of the potential issues before making a purchase.
