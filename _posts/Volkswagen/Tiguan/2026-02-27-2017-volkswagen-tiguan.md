# 2017 Volkswagen Tiguan

### Overview
The 2017 Volkswagen Tiguan, a compact SUV, generally offers a solid ownership experience with a focus on practicality and a refined driving feel. While it doesn't suffer from widespread, critical failures, like any vehicle, it has certain areas that have seen more attention from owners and technicians. Common concerns tend to revolve around the powertrain, particularly specific engine and transmission combinations, and some electrical gremlins. However, for the most part, the Tiguan is considered a reliable option in its segment, with most reported issues being manageable and not indicative of systemic design flaws. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring its longevity.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L TSI (Turbocharged Direct Injection)
**PCV Valve Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idle, check engine light, potential oil leaks, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) valve assembly.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500

**Water Pump Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, overheating, visible coolant residue around the pump.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the water pump and thermostat assembly.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic (09G/09K)
**Harsh Shifting / Delayed Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky gear changes, hesitation when shifting into drive or reverse, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Software update, transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, or potential valve body repair/replacement.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1200

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing / Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, system freezes or reboots randomly.
- **Solution:** Software update, or replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1000


### Power Tailgate
**Malfunctioning Operation**
- **Symptoms:** Tailgate fails to open or close, stops mid-operation, error messages.
- **Solution:** Adjustment of sensors, lubrication of hinges, or replacement of tailgate struts/motors.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Volkswagen Tiguan presents a generally reliable profile for a compact SUV. While the 2.0L TSI engine can experience issues like PCV valve failures and water pump leaks, and the 6-speed automatic transmission may develop shifting problems, these are not universally reported and often repairable. The manual transmission and other major systems like suspension, exterior, and interior components show good durability. Electrical issues, particularly with the infotainment system and power tailgate, are the most common secondary concerns. Overall, with diligent maintenance and addressing any emerging issues promptly, the 2017 Tiguan is a dependable choice.
