# 2017 Volkswagen Tiguan Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Volkswagen Tiguan offers European driving dynamics and a relatively upscale cabin, but prospective buyers should approach with careful inspection. Based on owner feedback and NHTSA reports, this model year can suffer from notable electrical, steering, and engine-related concerns that require vigilance during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

Powered by a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing roughly 200 horsepower, this powertrain provides lively acceleration. However, buyers should closely monitor cooling, timing, and fuel system health to avoid unexpected repairs.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle unexpectedly loses power while driving, enters limp mode, or triggers check engine and ECU warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes, inspect the fuel delivery system and high-pressure fuel pump, and repair or replace failing components.

**Timing Chain Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noises upon startup or illuminated check engine lights related to engine timing.
- **Solution:** Inspect timing chain tensioners and guides; replace the timing chain assembly if excessive stretch or wear is detected.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The conventional 6-speed automatic transmission generally pairs smoothly with the turbo engine. While major mechanical failures are less common in owner complaints, smooth shifting and fluid condition should be verified during a test drive.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Pinion

Issues with the electric power steering system can lead to a sudden loss of assist, requiring a complete replacement of the rack and pinion assembly.

**Power Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power steering functionality, making the steering wheel exceptionally heavy and difficult to maneuver, often accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic steering gear and related electrical components; replace the steering rack and pinion assembly if internal failure is confirmed.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Clock Spring

Safety equipment relies heavily on electrical connections in the steering column, where clock spring wear can disable warning lights and driver airbags.

**Airbag Warning Light and Clock Spring Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Airbag warning light illuminates on the dashboard, accompanied by potential loss of steering wheel controls or horn functionality.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering column clock spring and wiring harness; replace faulty clock spring components to restore proper airbag circuit continuity.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V329000: Unknown Or Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Tiguan&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Modifications made while the vehicles were in an internal evaluation period may cause the affected vehicles to not comply with all of the applicable regulatory requirements. If the vehicles do not meet all regulatory requirements, there could be an increased risk of a crash, fire, or injury.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will repair the vehicles to make them fully compliant or Volkswagen will repurchase them if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V955000: Seat Belts:Pretensioner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Tiguan&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Additionally, certain 2017 Audi Q7, A4 Sedan, A4 Allroad, and 2018 Audi Q5 vehicles are being recalled because the seat belt pretensioners may not activate properly. In the event of a crash, if the air bags and/or the seat belt pretensioners do not inflate or function properly, the vehicle occupants have an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen and Audi will notify owners, and dealers will replace the affected air bags and seat belt pretensioners, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V102000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Tiguan&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The front driver air bag installed on these vehicles may not be intended for use in vehicles sold in the United States. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." In the event of a crash, the incorrect air bags may not protect the driver as intended.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will replace the steering wheel and front driver air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V321000: Unknown Or Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Tiguan&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Vehicles that do not meet all regulatory requirements may have an increased risk of injury, crash, or fire.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Volkswagen Tiguan can be an enjoyable compact SUV, but it demands careful evaluation before purchase. Buyers should prioritize a thorough pre-purchase inspection checking the steering rack, engine timing components, and electrical systems to steer clear of costly surprises.
