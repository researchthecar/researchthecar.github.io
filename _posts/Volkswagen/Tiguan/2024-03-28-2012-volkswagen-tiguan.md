**Introduction:**

The 2012 Volkswagen Tiguan is a compact SUV that offers a comfortable ride and a variety of features. It is powered by a 2.0L inline-4 engine that produces 200 horsepower and 207 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with either a six-speed automatic transmission or a six-speed manual transmission.

Owners of the 2012 Volkswagen Tiguan praise the vehicle for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and good fuel economy. However, some owners have reported experiencing various issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**2.0L Inline-4 Engine**

* **Faulty Timing Chain Tensioner:**
   * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner can fail, causing the timing chain to slip and potentially damage the engine. This can lead to a loss of power, rough idling, and knocking noises from the engine.
   * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
   * Recalls: None Found
* **Carbon Buildup on Intake Valves:**
   * Description/Symptoms: Carbon buildup on the intake valves can restrict airflow into the engine, causing a loss of power and reduced fuel economy. This is more common in vehicles that are driven primarily for short distances.
   * Solution: Clean the intake valves.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
   * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Shifting Problems:**
   * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may experience shifting problems, such as slipping, hesitation, or delayed shifts. This can be caused by a variety of issues, including worn gears, a faulty transmission control module, or low transmission fluid levels.
   * Solution: Repair or replace the transmission as needed.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
   * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Worn Coil Springs:**
   * Description/Symptoms: The coil springs in the suspension can become worn over time, leading to a loss of ride height and handling problems. This is more common in vehicles that are used for off-road driving or in areas with poor road conditions.
   * Solution: Replace the coil springs.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
   * Recalls: None Found
* **Leaking Struts:**
   * Description/Symptoms: The struts in the suspension can develop leaks, causing the vehicle to bounce or ride rough. This can also lead to reduced braking performance.
   * Solution: Replace the struts.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500
   * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Faulty Power Window Motors:**
   * Description/Symptoms: The power window motors can fail, causing the windows to become inoperable. This can be a safety hazard, especially if the windows become stuck in the open position.
   * Solution: Replace the power window motors.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
   * Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical Shorts:**
   * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle may experience electrical shorts, which can cause a variety of issues, such as flickering lights, power loss, and starting problems. This can be a serious safety hazard.
   * Solution: Locate and repair the electrical short.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
   * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Undercarriage:**
   * Description/Symptoms: The undercarriage of the vehicle can develop rust, especially in areas where the vehicle is exposed to salt or other corrosive materials. This can weaken the undercarriage and lead to structural problems.
   * Solution: Inspect the undercarriage for rust and apply anti-rust coating as needed.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
   * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Squeaks and Rattles:**
   * Description/Symptoms: The interior of the vehicle may experience squeaks and rattles, especially over rough roads. This can be caused by loose trim pieces or other components.
   * Solution: Identify and tighten or replace the loose components.
   * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
   * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2012 Volkswagen Tiguan is a generally reliable vehicle, but it has been known to experience some issues, particularly with the engine, transmission, and electrical systems. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the extent of the damage. Overall, it is important to have the vehicle inspected by a qualified mechanic if any issues are suspected.
