## Introduction

The 2018 Volkswagen Tiguan is a compact SUV that seats up to seven passengers. It is available with a variety of engine and transmission options, including a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine with 184 horsepower and 221 lb-ft of torque, a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine with 220 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, an eight-speed automatic transmission, and a six-speed manual transmission. The Tiguan is praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and user-friendly infotainment system. However, it has also been criticized for its lack of power with the base engine, and its high price for the higher trim levels.

### Engine
**2.0-liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder Engine with 184 Horsepower**
- **Excessive Oil Consumption:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Burning a quart of oil every 500-1000 miles.
    - Solution: Replace piston rings and engine block.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $6,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

### Transmission
**Eight-Speed Automatic Transmission**
- **Shifting Problems:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Hard shifting, delayed shifting, or no shifting at all.
    - Solution: Replace transmission control module or transmission.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $4,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

### Suspension
- **No Issues Found**

### Electrical
- **Battery Drain:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Battery dies after sitting for a few days.
    - Solution: Replace battery or check for parasitic draw.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    - Recalls: None Found

### Exterior
- **No Issues Found**

### Interior
**Center Console**
- **Climate Control Display Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Display screen goes black or unresponsive.
    - Solution: Replace climate control unit.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found

### Other
**Recall Information**
- NHTSA Campaign Number: 19V222000
    - Description: Fuel pump may fail, causing engine to stall.
- NHTSA Campaign Number: 18V524000
    - Description: Airbag control unit may not detect a crash, preventing airbags from deploying.

### Summary

The 2018 Volkswagen Tiguan has a variety of issues, but most of them are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix. The most serious issue is the excessive oil consumption, which can lead to engine damage if not addressed. The Tiguan is a generally reliable vehicle, but it is important to be aware of these potential problems before purchasing one.
