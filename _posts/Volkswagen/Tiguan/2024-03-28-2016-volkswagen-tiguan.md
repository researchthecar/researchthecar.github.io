**Introduction to the 2016 Volkswagen Tiguan**

The 2016 Volkswagen Tiguan is a compact SUV that offers a spacious interior, user-friendly infotainment system, and a variety of engine and transmission options. It is powered by a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine that produces 184 horsepower and 221 lb-ft of torque, paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission. Other engine and transmission options include a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine that produces 200 horsepower and 207 lb-ft of torque, paired with a six-speed automatic transmission, and a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine that produces 220 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, paired with a seven-speed DSG transmission.

Drivers appreciate the Tiguan's comfortable ride, ample cargo space, and peppy engine. However, some common issues have been reported with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder Engine**

* **Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Engine repair or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noises from the engine, such as rattling or clicking, indicating a faulty timing chain tensioner.
    * Solution: Replacement of the timing chain tensioner and related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder Engine (200 hp)**

* **No Issues Found**

**2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder Engine (220 hp)**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Eight-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Mechatronic Unit Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping or refusing to shift gears, leading to loss of power.
    * Solution: Replacement of the mechatronic unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 17V769000

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **No Issues Found**

**Seven-Speed DSG Transmission**

* **Clutch Pack Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears or slipping gears, leading to reduced power and drivability.
    * Solution: Replacement of the clutch pack and associated parts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Mount Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noises or vibrations during driving, indicating worn or damaged front strut mounts.
    * Solution: Replacement of the front strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Shock Absorber Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Reduced ride comfort, excessive bouncing, or uneven tire wear, indicating failed rear shock absorbers.
    * Solution: Replacement of the rear shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rapid battery discharge, leading to starting problems or electrical component failure.
    * Solution: Testing and repair of electrical components causing the drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical Wiring Harness Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Electrical malfunctions or failures due to damaged or faulty wiring harnesses.
    * Solution: Inspection and replacement of affected wiring harnesses.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 16V304000

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Seal Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaks around the sunroof, causing water damage to the interior or electrical components.
    * Solution: Replacement of the sunroof seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inoperative climate control system, leading to uncomfortable cabin temperatures.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the climate control system, often involving replacement of components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Seat Latch Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty latching or unlatching the rear seats, compromising safety and convenience.
    * Solution: Replacement of the rear seat latch assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Volkswagen Tiguan offers a comfortable and spacious ride with a variety of engine and transmission options. However, it has been plagued by a number of issues, including excessive oil consumption, timing chain tensioner failure, and transmission problems. These issues can be costly to repair, and some have led to recalls. Overall, the 2016 Volkswagen Tiguan has received mixed reviews, with some drivers expressing satisfaction while others have encountered significant problems.
