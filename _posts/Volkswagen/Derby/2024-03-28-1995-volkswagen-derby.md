**Introduction:**

The 1995 Volkswagen Derby was a subcompact car that offered a blend of affordability and practicality. It was available with a range of engine and transmission options, including a 1.3-liter engine with a 5-speed manual transmission as standard. Other options included a 1.6-liter engine with a 4-speed automatic transmission or a 1.9-liter turbodiesel engine with a 5-speed manual transmission. The Derby was praised for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and fuel efficiency. However, it was also criticized for its lack of power, especially with the smaller engines.

**Engine**
**1.3L**
- **Leaking head gasket:** Symptoms include white smoke from the exhaust, overheating, and loss of coolant. Solution: Replace the head gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+ Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 19V512000
- **Oil consumption:** Excessive oil consumption can be caused by worn piston rings or valve guides. Solution: Replace the worn parts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+ Recalls: None Found
- **Timing belt failure:** The timing belt connects the crankshaft to the camshaft and must be replaced every 60,000 miles. Failure to replace the timing belt can result in catastrophic engine damage. Solution: Replace the timing belt and water pump. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+ Recalls: None Found

**1.6L**
- **Misfires:** Misfires can be caused by faulty spark plugs, ignition coils, or fuel injectors. Solution: Replace the faulty parts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ Recalls: None Found
- **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Symptoms include loss of coolant, overheating, and a rough idle. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+ Recalls: None Found

**1.9L Turbodiesel**
- **Fuel injector failure:** Symptoms include hard starting, loss of power, and black smoke from the exhaust. Solution: Replace the faulty fuel injector(s). Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ Recalls: None Found
- **Turbocharger failure:** Symptoms include loss of power, excessive smoke from the exhaust, and a whining noise. Solution: Replace the turbocharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2000+ Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
**5-Speed Manual**
- **Difficulty shifting gears:** Symptoms include grinding or difficulty selecting gears. Solution: Adjust or replace the clutch, shifter linkage, or transmission internals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ Recalls: None Found
- **Transmission fluid leaks:** Symptoms include leaks from the transmission pan or seals. Solution: Replace the faulty seals or gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic**
- **Shifting problems:** Symptoms include delayed or harsh shifting. Solution: Flush the transmission fluid and replace the filter. May require further internal repairs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+ Recalls: None Found
- **Torque converter failure:** Symptoms include slipping gears or loss of power. Solution: Replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2000+ Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
- **Worn struts:** Symptoms include a bouncy ride, poor handling, and uneven tire wear. Solution: Replace the worn struts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+ Recalls: None Found
- **Suspension bushings failure:** Worn suspension bushings can cause noise, vibration, and poor handling. Solution: Replace the faulty bushings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+ Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
- **Battery failure:** Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle or dimming headlights. Solution: Replace the battery. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ Recalls: None Found
- **Alternator failure:** Symptoms include a discharged battery or flickering lights. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+ Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
- **Rust:** The Derby is prone to rust on the body panels, especially the fenders and rocker panels. Solution: Repair or replace the rusted areas. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity Recalls: None Found
- **Door lock failure:** Symptoms include difficulty locking or unlocking the doors. Solution: Replace the faulty door lock actuators or mechanisms. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
- **Dashboard cracks:** Symptoms include cracks on the dashboard. Solution: Replace the dashboard or repair the cracks with epoxy. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ Recalls: None Found
- **Power window failure:** Symptoms include windows that do not operate or make excessive noise. Solution: Replace the faulty window motors or switches. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 1995 Volkswagen Derby has a number of potential issues, including engine oil consumption, timing belt failure, transmission fluid leaks, and suspension wear. These issues can vary in severity and cost to fix. However, overall, the Derby is a reliable and affordable car that offers a comfortable ride and good fuel economy.
