The 2016 Volkswagen Worker is a commercial vehicle not sold in the United States. It is primarily available in South America. Since this vehicle is not well-documented in English sources, and has a limited available of data, the following information is limited to information available.

**Engine**

**1.6L Diesel**

*   **Glow Plug Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting in cold weather, engine misfires, check engine light illuminated.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty glow plugs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300

**Transmission**

**Manual Transmission (Specific Type Unknown)**

*   **Difficulty Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty engaging gears, grinding noises during shifting.
    *   Solution: Inspect and potentially replace the clutch, pressure plate, or synchros.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1500+

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Bushing Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the suspension, loose handling, uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replace worn suspension bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per axle

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dead battery, difficulty starting, electrical components malfunctioning.
    *   Solution: Identify and repair the source of the parasitic draw, replace the battery if necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $75 - $300+

**Exterior**

*   **Door Handle Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Door handle breaking or becoming loose, difficulty opening the door.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty door handle.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing on the dashboard surface, especially in areas exposed to direct sunlight.
    *   Solution: Dashboard replacement or repair with a filler/covering.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+

**Summary**

The 2016 Volkswagen Worker, while primarily a commercial vehicle, can experience a range of issues. The severity of these issues varies from relatively minor (door handles, glow plugs) to more significant (transmission or suspension problems). The estimated costs to fix these issues also vary greatly, but potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems.
