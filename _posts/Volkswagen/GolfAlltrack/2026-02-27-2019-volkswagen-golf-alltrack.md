# 2019 Volkswagen Golf Alltrack Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Volkswagen Golf Alltrack combines rugged wagon styling with standard 4Motion all-wheel drive, making it an appealing choice for buyers seeking utility and everyday drivability. As a used vehicle, its mechanical reliability is generally tied to proper maintenance of its turbocharged engine and dual-clutch transmission. While official owner-reported complaints are sparse for this model year, prospective buyers should still inspect key powertrain components and electronic features during a pre-purchase evaluation.

---

## Engine

### 1.8L Turbocharged I4

The 1.8-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 170 horsepower and 199 lb-ft of torque, offering lively power delivery and respectable efficiency. It is generally robust when serviced on schedule, though routine inspections for carbon buildup and fluid leaks are recommended for used models.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission provides a direct and engaging connection for enthusiast drivers. It has a solid reputation for durability provided the clutch assembly has not experienced premature wear from aggressive driving.

### 6-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed DSG dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers rapid, seamless gear changes and pairs well with the all-wheel-drive system. Routine fluid and filter changes every 40,000 miles are critical to ensuring long-term shift quality and reliability.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V879000: Wheels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Golf+Alltrack&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The excessive stress can cause steering, traction or other stability issues or breakage relating to the front wheels, and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen has notified owners, and dealers will replace the wheel bearing assemblies, wheels, and wheel bolts, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V815000: Tires:Pressure Monitoring And Regulating Systems](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Golf+Alltrack&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The tire pressure monitoring system may not detect an air pressure loss in adequate time when deflating occurs among all four tires simultaneously. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 138, "Tire Pressure Monitoring Systems." An undetected sudden loss of pressure in all four tires.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the TPMS software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V604000: Electrical System:Ignition:Switch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Golf+Alltrack&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Ignition switch failure can cause the electrical system to shut down and the engine to stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the ignition switch, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Volkswagen Golf Alltrack stands out as a versatile, well-rounded wagon with a strong powertrain lineup and reassuring all-weather capability. Because available owner complaint data is minimal, buyers should focus on verifying a complete service history—particularly regarding transmission fluid maintenance—and conducting a thorough mechanical inspection before purchasing.
