# 2016 Volkswagen CC Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Volkswagen CC, understanding its mechanical layout and common owner-reported trouble spots helps you inspect prospective purchases more effectively. While owner complaints offer valuable insight into potential vulnerabilities, they reflect individual experiences rather than guaranteed defects. A thorough pre-purchase inspection can catch many of these issues before they turn into expensive surprises.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 produces roughly 200 horsepower and provides lively performance paired with front-wheel drive. Regular maintenance and oil changes are vital to keeping this direct-injection powerplant running reliably over high mileage.

**Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report the vehicle stuttering in traffic, failing to receive gas, coasting to a stop, or failing to start upon pushing the ignition.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the faulty fuel pump or associated fuel delivery components.

### 3.6L V6

The 3.6L naturally aspirated V6 delivers approximately 280 horsepower and smooth power delivery, often paired with 4Motion all-wheel drive. It offers robust performance but demands attentive upkeep on cooling and ignition systems.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic (DSG)

The 6-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission offers rapid, sporty gear shifts typically found on front-wheel-drive 2.0T trims. Prospective buyers should test drive from a cold start to ensure smooth clutch engagement.

**Cold Start Hesitation and Clunking**
- **Symptoms:** When shifting into drive from a cold start, the vehicle may hesitate, fail to respond to the accelerator pedal, shake, and produce a loud clunking noise.
- **Solution:** Diagnose transmission mechatronic unit health and check software adaptation or fluid levels.

### 6-Speed Conventional Automatic

The conventional 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with V6 and 4Motion all-wheel-drive models, providing smooth gear changes and a more traditional torque-converter driving feel. Fluid changes at recommended intervals help preserve long-term durability.

## Steering

### Steering Wheel Clock Spring

Safety equipment includes standard front and side airbags alongside electronic stability controls designed to protect occupants in a collision.

**Clock Spring Breakage**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning light illuminates, steering wheel audio controls stop working, the horn fails, and a noise may emit from the steering column during turns.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged steering wheel clock spring assembly.
- **Cost:** Over $800

## Exterior

### Adaptive Front Lighting System

The electrical system manages advanced cabin electronics and exterior lighting, though complex wiring harnesses require careful diagnostic troubleshooting when faults arise.

**AFS Warning Message**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers frequently notice dashboard warning messages regarding the adaptive front lighting system.
- **Solution:** Scan vehicle modules for headlight sensor or motor faults and replace malfunctioning headlamp assemblies if necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V014000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=CC&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Upon deployment of the driver's frontal air bag, excessive internal pressure may cause the inflator to explode. In the event of a crash necessitating deployment of the driver's frontal air bag, the inflator could explode with metal fragments striking the vehicle occupants potentially resulting in serious injury or death.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver's frontal air bag inflator with an alternative inflator, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V329000: Unknown Or Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=CC&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Modifications made while the vehicles were in an internal evaluation period may cause the affected vehicles to not comply with all of the applicable regulatory requirements. If the vehicles do not meet all regulatory requirements, there could be an increased risk of a crash, fire, or injury.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will repair the vehicles to make them fully compliant or Volkswagen will repurchase them if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V535000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=CC&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The air bag Electronic Control Unit (ECU) may have a defective power supply capacitor that can result in air bag deactivation or inadvertent deployment of the air bags. Deactivated air bags increases the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will install new software and replace the air bag ECU as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V287000: Seats:Front Assembly:Head Restraint](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=CC&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Incorrect front seat head restraints (rests) may have been installed, possibly resulting in the seat occupant's head being inadequately restrained in the event of a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 202a, "Head Restraints." If the seat occupant's head is inadequately.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front seat head restraints, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V509000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=CC&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Interruption of electrical power to the fuel pump control module may cause the fuel pump to fail. If the fuel pump fails, the engine will stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump control module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V464000: Latches/Locks/Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=CC&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Vibrations, such as those created by opening and closing a vehicle door, may cause the rear child door locks to disengage, allowing a child to open a rear door while inside the vehicle. If an unrestrained child unexpectedly opens a door, they may fall from the vehicle increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will inspect, and if necessary, replace the child lock(s), free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Volkswagen CC offers distinct styling and engaging driving dynamics, but potential buyers should pay close attention to known trouble spots like steering wheel clock springs and fuel delivery components. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection performed by a qualified technician can help verify the condition of the engine, transmission, and electrical systems before finalizing a purchase.
