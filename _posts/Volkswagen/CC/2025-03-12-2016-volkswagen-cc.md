The 2016 Volkswagen CC is a stylish and sporty four-door coupe known for its sleek design and comfortable ride. It offers a blend of luxury and performance, making it an attractive option for those seeking a more refined driving experience. The sole engine option for the 2016 CC is a 2.0L TSI turbocharged inline-4 engine producing 200 horsepower and 207 lb-ft of torque. This engine is paired with either a 6-speed manual or a 6-speed DSG automatic transmission. Some owners appreciate the CC's upscale interior and handling, while others have noted concerns about reliability and maintenance costs.

**Engine**

**2.0L TSI Turbocharged Inline-4**

*   **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise on startup, especially when cold. Can lead to catastrophic engine damage if the chain jumps timing.
    *   Solution: Replacing the timing chain, tensioner, guides, and related components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **PCV (Positive Crankcase Ventilation) Valve Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, oil leaks, whistling noise from the engine, check engine light illuminated.
    *   Solution: Replacing the PCV valve or the entire valve cover assembly, depending on the specific failure.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Water Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, check engine light illuminated.
    *   Solution: Replacing the water pump and thermostat.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed DSG Automatic Transmission**

*   **Mechatronic Unit Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, delayed engagement, transmission slipping, inability to shift gears, transmission malfunction warning light illuminated.
    *   Solution: Replacing or rebuilding the Mechatronic unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Clutch Pack Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Slipping during acceleration, jerky shifts, shuddering.
    *   Solution: Replacing the clutch packs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Front Strut Mount Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noise from the front suspension, especially when going over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replacing the front strut mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Power Window Regulator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Window not moving up or down, or moving erratically.
    *   Solution: Replacing the power window regulator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Car battery dying frequently, even when the car is not in use.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing and repairing the source of the parasitic draw (e.g., faulty module, improperly installed aftermarket accessories).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ (depending on the cause)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof failing to open or close properly, leaking.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the sunroof motor, seals, or complete assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Navigation/Infotainment System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, Crashing, Unresponsive touch screen.
    *   Solution: Software update or replacement of the head unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2016 Volkswagen CC, while stylish and enjoyable to drive, can be prone to certain mechanical and electrical issues. The most concerning are the timing chain and Mechatronic Unit failures, which can be costly to repair. Other issues like PCV valve failure, water pump leaks, and electrical problems can also add to the ownership expenses. Potential buyers should be aware of these common problems and thoroughly inspect the vehicle before purchase or consider purchasing an extended warranty to mitigate potential repair costs.
