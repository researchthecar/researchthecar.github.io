**Introduction:**

The 2014 Volkswagen CC is a stylish and sporty coupe that offers a comfortable and refined driving experience. It is powered by a 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 engine that produces 200 horsepower and 207 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. The CC also comes with a variety of features such as a panoramic sunroof, leather seats, and a premium sound system. While the CC is generally well-regarded, there are some potential issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine:**

* **2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**

  * **Turbocharger Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine performance loss, excessive smoke from the exhaust, and a whistling noise.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

  * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine noise, especially at startup and idle, and possible engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission:**

* **6-Speed Automatic:**

  * **Transmission Control Module (TCM) Problems:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission shifting issues, including slipping, jerking, and refusing to shift gears.
    * Solution: Update the TCM software or replace it if necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

  * **Mechatronic Unit Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, transmission jerking, and failure to shift gears.
    * Solution: Replace the mechatronic unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension:**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical:**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery discharge while the vehicle is parked, especially if there are multiple electrical devices plugged in.
    * Solution: Diagnose and fix the root cause of the electrical draw, such as a faulty module or accessory.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: VW15E003

**Exterior:**

* **Sunroof Drain Plugs Clogging:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water accumulation in the headliner due to clogged sunroof drain plugs.
    * Solution: Clean and unclog the drain plugs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Free (if done by owner) - $100+ (if done by mechanic)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior:**

* **Passenger Seat Occupancy Sensor Issues:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Airbag light stays on even with a passenger in the seat.
    * Solution: Replace the seat occupancy sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2014 Volkswagen CC presents some potential issues to consider, particularly with the engine and transmission. Engine problems can be costly to fix, while electrical and interior issues are generally less severe and more affordable. It is recommended to research and address any known issues before purchasing a used CC to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
