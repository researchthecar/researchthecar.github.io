**Introduction:**

The 2017 Volkswagen CC is a stylish and sporty sedan that offers a comfortable ride and a spacious interior. It comes with a 2.0L turbocharged four-cylinder engine that produces 220 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, paired with a six-speed automatic transmission. The CC is known for its sleek design, premium interior, and responsive handling. However, it also has some common issues that owners have reported.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder Engine**

* **Carbon Buildup on Intake Valves:** This issue occurs when carbon deposits accumulate on the intake valves, reducing airflow and causing performance issues.
    * Symptoms: Reduced engine power, rough idling, hesitation during acceleration
    * Solution: Cleaning or replacing the intake valves
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** The timing chain tensioner can fail, causing the timing chain to slip and potentially damaging the engine.
    * Symptoms: Engine rattling or knocking, loss of power, check engine light
    * Solution: Replacing the timing chain and tensioner
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Hesitation on Upshifts:** The transmission may hesitate or delay upshifting, causing a loss of power and poor fuel economy.
    * Symptoms: Delay in shifting gears, rough shifting
    * Solution: Reprogramming the transmission control module or replacing the valve body
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Strut Mount Failure:** The front suspension strut mount can fail, causing a clunking noise and reduced handling stability.
    * Symptoms: Clunking noise from the front of the vehicle, excessive tire wear
    * Solution: Replacing the strut mount
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Rear Suspension Sway Bar Links:** The rear suspension sway bar links can become loose or damaged, causing a rattling noise and reduced stability.
    * Symptoms: Rattling noise from the rear of the vehicle, reduced handling stability
    * Solution: Replacing the sway bar links
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain Issue:** The vehicle's battery may drain prematurely, causing difficulty starting.
    * Symptoms: Frequent battery replacement, dead battery, electrical issues
    * Solution: Testing and repairing electrical components, replacing the battery
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000
* **HID Headlight Ballast Failure:** The HID headlight ballast can fail, causing one or both headlights to not illuminate.
    * Symptoms: One or both headlights not working
    * Solution: Replacing the headlight ballast
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Drain Plugs Clogging:** The sunroof drain plugs can become clogged, causing water to accumulate and leak into the interior.
    * Symptoms: Water dripping from the sunroof, water stains on the headliner
    * Solution: Cleaning or replacing the drain plugs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Issues:** The climate control system may malfunction, causing the air conditioning or heating to not work properly.
    * Symptoms: AC not blowing cold air, difficult to maintain desired temperature
    * Solution: Troubleshooting and repairing the climate control system
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000
* **Steering Wheel Controls Malfunctioning:** The steering wheel controls may malfunction, preventing the driver from adjusting volume, cruise control, or other functions.
    * Symptoms: Buttons not working, intermittent connectivity
    * Solution: Diagnosing and repairing the steering wheel controls
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Summary:**

The 2017 Volkswagen CC is a stylish and comfortable sedan, but it has some common issues that owners should be aware of. The most severe issues include timing chain tensioner failure and suspension strut mount failure, which can be costly to repair. Electrical problems and interior malfunctions are also relatively common, although they tend to be less expensive to fix. Overall, the severity of the issues varies, and the cost to fix them can range from a few hundred dollars to thousands of dollars.
