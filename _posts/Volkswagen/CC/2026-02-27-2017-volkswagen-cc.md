# 2017 Volkswagen CC Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Volkswagen CC blends coupe-like styling with a comfortable cabin and confident road manners, representing the final model year for this stylish sedan. While owners generally praise its driving dynamics and upscale interior, used car buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle's electronic systems and complex powertrain options before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces around 200 horsepower and 207 lb-ft of torque, offering lively acceleration and reasonable efficiency. It is generally regarded as a robust powerplant provided routine maintenance and timely oil changes have been performed.

### 3.6L V6

The 3.6-liter naturally aspirated V6 engine delivers roughly 280 horsepower and 265 lb-ft of torque, paired exclusively with all-wheel drive on select trims. This engine provides smooth, strong power delivery, though prospective buyers should anticipate higher fuel consumption and slightly increased maintenance complexity.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission is paired with front-wheel drive on base engine configurations. It offers an engaging shift feel and straightforward mechanical reliability for drivers who prefer a three-pedal setup.

### 6-Speed DSG Dual-Clutch Automatic

The 6-speed direct-shift gearbox (DSG) dual-clutch automatic provides lightning-fast gear changes and efficient power transfer. However, owners should verify proper service history and watch for potential mechatronic unit malfunctions.

**Mechatronic Unit Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Catastrophic transmission failure leaving the vehicle unable to select gears or drive properly.
- **Solution:** Requires replacement or repair of the mechatronic control unit and associated hydraulic components.

### 6-Speed Conventional Automatic

The conventional 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with the V6 engine and all-wheel-drive systems. It delivers smooth shifts and avoids the complex mechanical quirks associated with dual-clutch units.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System

In-cabin electronics and infotainment features can occasionally exhibit software glitches or unresponsiveness.

**Radio and Navigation Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The radio changes channels automatically, and voice commands or navigation functions become unresponsive.
- **Solution:** Diagnose software anomalies; updates or head unit replacement may be required to restore proper operation.

### Body Electrical and Lighting

Body electrical systems generally operate reliably, though sporadic module glitches can drain the battery or trigger ghost warning lights.

**Random Electrical Activation and Dead Battery**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights, instrument panel lights, and warning indicators illuminate spontaneously while parked, accompanied by an unresponsive key fob.
- **Solution:** Inspect the body control module, battery condition, and parasitic draws to identify failing electrical components.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V014000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Side:Inflator Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=CC&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Upon deployment of the driver's frontal air bag, excessive internal pressure may cause the inflator to explode. In the event of a crash necessitating deployment of the driver's frontal air bag, the inflator could explode with metal fragments striking the vehicle occupants potentially resulting in serious injury or death.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver's frontal air bag inflator with an alternative inflator, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V321000: Unknown Or Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=CC&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Vehicles that do not meet all regulatory requirements may have an increased risk of injury, crash, or fire.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 17V287000: Seats:Front Assembly:Head Restraint](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=CC&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Incorrect front seat head restraints (rests) may have been installed, possibly resulting in the seat occupant's head being inadequately restrained in the event of a crash. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 202a, "Head Restraints." If the seat occupant's head is inadequately.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will replace the front seat head restraints, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Volkswagen CC offers distinct styling and a pleasant ride, but used buyers should prioritize a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to the operation of the transmission—especially dual-clutch variants—and test all interior electronics and accessories to ensure trouble-free ownership.
