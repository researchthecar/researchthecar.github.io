**Introduction:**

The 1999 Volkswagen Sedan is a compact sedan renowned for its fuel efficiency and affordability. It is powered by a 2.0L inline-4 engine producing 115 horsepower and 122 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. While the Volkswagen Sedan is generally praised for its practicality and value, certain issues have been reported.

**Engine**

**2.0L Inline-4**

* **Oil pan gasket leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine oil leaking from the engine, visible as a puddle under the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace oil pan gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Intake manifold gasket leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaking from the engine, visible as a puddle under the vehicle or as white smoke from the exhaust.
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Crankshaft position sensor failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling or rough idling, difficulty starting.
    * Solution: Replace crankshaft position sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission slipping:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping out of gear or hesitating during shifts.
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Solenoid failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hard shifting or delayed gear engagement.
    * Solution: Replace solenoid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front struts leaking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive bouncing or noise from front end of vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace front struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear shocks leaking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive bouncing or noise from rear end of vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace rear shocks.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery drains quickly:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery dies frequently, even after charging.
    * Solution: Inspect electrical system for draw, replace battery if necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on cause
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Alternator failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery warning light on, dim headlights or electronics cutting out.
    * Solution: Replace alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door handle breaking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Door handle breaks off or becomes loose.
    * Solution: Replace door handle.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear hatch latch failing:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rear hatch will not open or close properly.
    * Solution: Replace rear hatch latch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Window switch failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Power window does not operate, often due to failed electrical switch.
    * Solution: Replace power window switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Dashboard lights flickering:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dashboard lights flicker or fail to illuminate.
    * Solution: Replace faulty light bulbs or repair electrical wiring.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on cause
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 1999 Volkswagen Sedan is a generally reliable vehicle, but some issues can be experienced. The most common issues are oil leaks, transmission problems, and suspension issues. While these issues can be expensive to repair, they are not necessarily severe and the vehicle remains a good value for its price range.
