**Introduction**

The 1997 Volkswagen Sedan, also known as the Golf, was available with a variety of engine and transmission options. The 2.0L engine produced 115 horsepower and 122 lb-ft of torque, while the 1.9L diesel engine produced 90 horsepower and 155 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options included a 5-speed manual and a 4-speed automatic. Drivers praised the Sedan for its affordability, fun-to-drive nature, and comfortable interior. However, it also had some common issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

**2.0L Engine**

* **Timing chain failure:** The timing chain can stretch or break, causing the engine to lose power or stall.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain and tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Intake manifold gasket leak:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing a vacuum leak that leads to rough idling and decreased engine performance.
    * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**1.9L Diesel Engine**

* **Fuel injector failure:** The fuel injectors can clog or fail, causing the engine to run poorly or stall.
    * Solution: Replace the fuel injectors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Turbocharger failure:** The turbocharger can fail, resulting in a loss of power and increased exhaust smoke.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Clutch failure:** The clutch can wear out, making it difficult to shift gears or causing the car to stall.
    * Solution: Replace the clutch and pressure plate.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission slipping:** The transmission may slip out of gear or hesitate to shift, causing rough gear changes or loss of power.
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut mount failure:** The strut mounts can wear out, causing the car to make noises or ride rough.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Control arm bushing failure:** The control arm bushings can wear out, leading to loose steering and poor handling.
    * Solution: Replace the control arm bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the battery to drain and the car to stall.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
* **Starter failure:** The starter can fail, preventing the car from starting.
    * Solution: Replace the starter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** The Sedan is prone to rust, especially in the wheel wells, rocker panels, and undercarriage.
    * Solution: Address rust spots promptly to prevent further damage.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ (depending on the extent of the rust)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Heater core failure:** The heater core can leak, causing coolant to enter the passenger compartment and fog up the windows.
    * Solution: Replace the heater core.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1997 Volkswagen Sedan is a generally reliable vehicle with some potential issues. Engine and transmission problems can be costly to fix, while other issues like suspension wear and rust are more affordable to address. Owners should be aware of these potential issues before purchasing a Sedan and budget accordingly for maintenance and repairs.
