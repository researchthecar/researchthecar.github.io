**2000 Volkswagen Sedan**

The 2000 Volkswagen Sedan, also known as the Jetta IV, is a compact sedan that was available with a variety of engine and transmission options. Engine options included a 2.0L I4, a 1.8L I4 turbocharged engine, and a 2.8L V6. Transmission options included a 5-speed manual transmission, a 4-speed automatic transmission, and a 5-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**2.0L I4**
* **Valve cover leaking:** The valve cover gasket may fail, causing an oil leak. The symptoms include an oil leak from the top of the engine and a burning oil smell. The solution is to replace the valve cover gasket. The estimated cost to fix is $200 - $300.
* **Timing chain tensioner failure:** The timing chain tensioner may fail, causing the timing chain to skip. The symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine and a loss of power. The solution is to replace the timing chain tensioner. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $700.

**1.8L I4 turbocharged**
* **Turbocharger failure:** The turbocharger may fail, causing a loss of power and a decrease in fuel economy. The symptoms include a whistling noise from the engine, a decrease in boost pressure, and a black smoke from the exhaust. The solution is to replace the turbocharger. The estimated cost to fix is $1,000 - $1,500.
* **Intake manifold leaking:** The intake manifold gasket may fail, causing a vacuum leak. The symptoms include a rough idle, a decrease in power, and a decrease in fuel economy. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. The estimated cost to fix is $200 - $300.

**2.8L V6**
* **Head gasket failure:** The head gasket may fail, causing a coolant leak and a loss of compression. The symptoms include a white smoke from the exhaust, a coolant leak, and a decrease in power. The solution is to replace the head gasket. The estimated cost to fix is $1,200 - $1,500.
* **Intake manifold leaking:** The intake manifold gasket may fail, causing a vacuum leak. The symptoms include a rough idle, a decrease in power, and a decrease in fuel economy. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. The estimated cost to fix is $200 - $300.

**Transmission**

**5-speed manual transmission**
* **Synchro failure:** The synchronizer rings may wear out, causing difficulty shifting gears. The symptoms include a grinding noise when shifting gears and a difficulty getting the transmission into gear. The solution is to replace the synchronizer rings. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $700.
* **Clutch failure:** The clutch may wear out, causing slipping and difficulty engaging gears. The symptoms include a slipping clutch, a burning smell, and a difficulty getting the transmission into gear. The solution is to replace the clutch. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $700.

**4-speed automatic transmission**
* **Torque converter failure:** The torque converter may fail, causing a loss of power and a shuddering when shifting gears. The symptoms include a slipping transmission, a decrease in power, and a shuddering when shifting gears. The solution is to replace the torque converter. The estimated cost to fix is $1,000 - $1,500.
* **Transmission control module failure:** The transmission control module (TCM) may fail, causing the transmission to shift erratically or not at all. The symptoms include a rough shifting transmission, a check engine light, and a loss of power. The solution is to replace the TCM. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $700.

**5-speed automatic transmission**
* **Valve body failure:** The valve body may fail, causing the transmission to shift erratically or not at all. The symptoms include a rough shifting transmission, a check engine light, and a loss of power. The solution is to replace the valve body. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $700.
* **Transmission control module failure:** The transmission control module (TCM) may fail, causing the transmission to shift erratically or not at all. The symptoms include a rough shifting transmission, a check engine light, and a loss of power. The solution is to replace the TCM. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $700.

**Suspension**

* **Strut failure:** The struts may fail, causing a loss of stability and control. The symptoms include a rough ride, a decrease in handling, and a clunking noise from the suspension. The solution is to replace the struts. The estimated cost to fix is $500 - $700.
* **Control arm bushing failure:** The control arm bushings may fail, causing a decrease in handling and stability. The symptoms include a clunking noise from the suspension, a decrease in handling, and a rough ride. The solution is to replace the control arm bushings. The estimated cost to fix is $200 - $300.

**Electrical**

* **Battery failure:** The battery may fail, causing the vehicle to not start. The symptoms include a dead battery, a clicking noise when trying to start the vehicle, and a dimming of the headlights. The solution is to replace the battery. The estimated cost to fix is $100 - $200.
* **Starter failure:** The starter may fail, causing the vehicle to not start. The symptoms include a clicking noise when trying to start the vehicle and a no-crank condition. The solution is to replace the starter. The estimated cost to fix is $300 - $500.

**Exterior**

* **Headlight failure:** The headlights may fail, causing a decrease in visibility at night. The symptoms include a dim or flickering headlight, a no-headlight condition, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the headlight bulb. The estimated cost to fix is $100 - $200.
* **Taillight failure:** The taillights may fail, causing a decrease in visibility at night. The symptoms include a dim or flickering taillight, a no-taillight condition, and a check engine light. The solution is to replace the taillight bulb. The estimated cost to fix is $100 - $200.

**Interior**

* **Dashboard rattling:** The dashboard may rattle, causing a noise while driving. The symptoms include a rattling noise from the dashboard, a loose dashboard, and a vibration from the dashboard. The estimated cost to fix is $100 - $200.

**Summary**

The 2000 Volkswagen Sedan is a reliable and affordable compact sedan. However, it is not without its problems. Some of the most common issues include valve cover leaks, timing chain tensioner failure, turbocharger failure, intake manifold leaks, head gasket failure, synchro failure, clutch failure, torque converter failure, transmission control module failure, valve body failure, strut failure, control arm bushing failure, battery failure, starter failure, headlight failure, taillight failure, dashboard rattling, and seat belt buckle failure.
