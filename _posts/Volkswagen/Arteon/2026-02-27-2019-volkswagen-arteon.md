# 2019 Volkswagen Arteon Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 Volkswagen Arteon, buyers will find a sleek, stylish fastback offering dynamic styling and comfortable grand touring capabilities. While owner complaints are relatively sparse, reviewing specific mechanical, electrical, and structural feedback can help prospective owners spot potential trouble areas during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 268 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, offering brisk acceleration for daily driving. This powerplant is generally robust, though regular maintenance is key to long-term reliability.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission paired with front-wheel drive delivers smooth gear changes and efficient highway cruising. It has a solid reputation overall, with few systemic complaints reported by owners.

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission is paired with 4Motion all-wheel-drive models, providing quick shifts and responsive power delivery. Buyers should evaluate shift smoothness during a test drive to ensure proper clutch operation.

## Suspension

### Factory Tires

Suspension components generally hold up well, though wheel balance and factory tire condition require close attention.

**High-Speed Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle starts shaking without warning while driving at highway speeds around 50 MPH.
- **Solution:** Check wheel balance, tire wear patterns, and alignment; replace tires if flat-spotted or unevenly worn.

## Brakes

### Brake Rotors

Steering feel is generally crisp, though braking vibrations can sometimes be misinterpreted as steering system feedback.

**Premature Rotor Warping**
- **Symptoms:** Heavy vibration is felt in the steering wheel when applying light pressure to the brakes at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the front brake rotors and pads, ensuring proper torque and alignment.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Rear Camera

Electrical systems offer modern connectivity, though occasional software glitches can affect driver assistance displays.

**ParkPilot Unavailable Message**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen displays a pop-up warning stating that ParkPilot is unavailable, and the rearview camera occasionally fails to function.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates or faulty camera modules through a qualified service center.

## Exterior

### Sunroof

Exterior styling and body panels are durable, but glass components warrant careful inspection.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The sunroof glass suddenly shatters outward at highway speeds, producing a loud bang resembling a gunshot.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof glass for stress fractures and replace the assembly if damage occurs.

## Safety

### Passenger Occupant Detection System (PODS)

Safety features are comprehensive, but owners should verify that all airbag and occupant detection recalls have been completed.

**PODS Sensor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The Passenger Occupant Detection System detects a malfunction, deactivates the passenger airbag, and displays an airbag warning light.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle evaluated by a dealership to address the occupant detection system malfunction under applicable recall or service guidelines.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V135000: Service Brakes](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Arteon&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." An increased actuating force or a disconnected input rod may affect braking, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and correct the mounting of the pressure rod only or replace the electronic brake booster and mounting of the pressure rod, depending of the inspection results, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Volkswagen Arteon is an appealing choice for used-car buyers seeking distinctive fastback styling and refined road manners. While most reported issues center around isolated electrical glitches, tire vibrations, and rare glass or brake concerns, a thorough pre-purchase inspection will help ensure you find a reliable example.
