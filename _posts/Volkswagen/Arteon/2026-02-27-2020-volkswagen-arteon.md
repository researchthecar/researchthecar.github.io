# 2020 Volkswagen Arteon Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Volkswagen Arteon serves as a stylish, upscale fastback option for buyers looking for something more distinctive than a traditional midsize sedan. Powered by a standard turbocharged engine paired with an efficient automatic transmission, it offers a refined driving experience and plenty of standard features. While overall owner complaint volume remains relatively low for this model year, prospective buyers should review potential electrical issues during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

This 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 268 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. It provides brisk acceleration for daily driving while maintaining reasonable efficiency, though buyers should verify regular maintenance history.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with front-wheel drive models, this 8-speed conventional automatic transmission delivers smooth, refined shifts. It generally proves dependable when fluid changes are performed at recommended intervals.

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission

Exclusive to 4Motion all-wheel-drive variants, this 7-speed dual-clutch transmission offers lightning-quick gear changes. Drivers should expect a slightly more direct mechanical feel at low speeds compared to standard torque-converter automatics.

## Interior

### SOS Emergency Call Module

The electrical system manages various convenience and safety features, but owner reports suggest occasional software or hardware glitches within telematics components.

**SOS Module Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported receiving recurring dashboard error messages regarding the emergency call function and telematics system failure.
- **Solution:** The issue is typically addressed by reprogramming or replacing the faulty telematics control module at a dealership or service center.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Volkswagen Arteon is an appealing choice for used-car shoppers seeking standout design and strong performance. Powertrain reliability appears stable across its available engine and transmission configurations, and isolated electrical reports represent manageable owner complaints rather than widespread mechanical failures. A thorough inspection focusing on electronics and telematics features will help ensure a confident purchase.
