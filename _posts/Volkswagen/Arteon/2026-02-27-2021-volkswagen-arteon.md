# 2021 Volkswagen Arteon Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Volkswagen Arteon combines sleek, fastback styling with practical liftback utility and turbocharged performance. As a used vehicle purchase, it offers distinct styling and engaging road manners, though buyers should evaluate its powertrain configurations and monitor owner-reported electrical and equipment quirks during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (268 hp)

The 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 268 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. It provides brisk acceleration for daily driving, though used buyers should verify regular maintenance intervals.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic Transmission

The 7-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission delivers quick, seamless gear changes and pairs with front-wheel drive or 4Motion all-wheel drive. Smooth operation at low speeds requires proper clutch adaptation and fluid maintenance.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V695000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Arteon&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The pressure input rod connection on the brake booster may be loose and could detach. A detached pressure input rod will cause the brake pedal to fail, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will tighten the pressure input rod connection, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V274000: Tires:Pressure Monitoring And Regulating Systems](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Arteon&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The Tire Pressure Monitoring System (TPMS) sensors were incorrectly calibrated and may not detect the position of a wheel sensor after tire rotation. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 138, "Tire Pressure Monitoring Systems." The TPMS may misidentify a tire with low air pressure.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will replace the wheel electronics and tire valves, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V716000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Arteon&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The rear view camera could malfunction during an ignition cycle, leading to a black screen or infotainment system freeze. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility."
