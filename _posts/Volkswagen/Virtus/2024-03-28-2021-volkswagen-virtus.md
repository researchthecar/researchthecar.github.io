**Introduction:**

The 2021 Volkswagen Virtus is a compact sedan that offers a blend of style, practicality, and affordability. It comes with a choice of two engine options: a 1.6-liter naturally aspirated engine with 110 horsepower and 114 lb-ft of torque and a 1.5-liter turbocharged engine with 150 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are mated to a 6-speed automatic transmission. The Virtus has received positive reviews for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and fuel efficiency. However, like any car, it has also had its share of reported issues.

**Engine**

**1.6-liter Naturally Aspirated Engine**

* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noisy engine, reduced power, and possibly a Check Engine Light.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Fuel Pump Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine stalling, difficulty starting, or reduced fuel efficiency.
    * Solution: Replace the fuel pump.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**1.5-liter Turbocharged Engine**

* **Oil Consumption:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption requiring frequent top-ups.
    * Solution: Replace piston rings or valve stem seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Turbocharger Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, unusual noises from the engine bay.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Shifting Issues:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hesitation during shifts, rough shifting, or difficulty engaging gears.
    * Solution: Transmission fluid flush, solenoid replacement, or transmission rebuilt.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Transmission Fluid Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking transmission fluid, visible underneath the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking component or seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noisy suspension, decreased handling, and difficulty maintaining alignment.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty strut.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Coil Spring Breakage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sudden drop in ride height, scraping or rubbing noises, and impaired handling.
    * Solution: Replace the broken coil spring.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dead battery after periods of inactivity.
    * Solution: Check for parasitic battery drain and address the underlying issue.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical Short Circuit:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Blown fuses, smoke or burning smell, or malfunctioning electrical components.
    * Solution: Identify and repair the source of the short circuit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty locking or unlocking doors, handles not operating properly.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty door lock assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Windshield Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Small cracks or chips in the windshield that can spread and impair visibility.
    * Solution: Replace the windshield.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **HVAC Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inoperative air conditioning, heating, or ventilation system.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty HVAC control module, compressor, or other components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Sunroof Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the vehicle from the sunroof during rain or car wash.
    * Solution: Replace the sunroof seal or address any other leaks.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the reported issues with the 2021 Volkswagen Virtus are generally minor and can be resolved at a reasonable cost. However, some issues, such as excessive oil consumption and turbocharger failure, can be more severe and expensive to fix. It's important to note that these issues are not common across the entire Virtus lineup and may only affect a small number of vehicles.
