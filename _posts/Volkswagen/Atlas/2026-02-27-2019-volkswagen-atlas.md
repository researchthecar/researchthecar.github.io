# 2019 Volkswagen Atlas Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Volkswagen Atlas offers spacious three-row seating and family-friendly utility, but prospective buyers should review recurring owner-reported complaints carefully. While many drivers enjoy its comfort and road manners, public feedback highlights potential trouble spots ranging from electrical glitches to drivetrain and steering concerns.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The base 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 235 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate daily power for the Atlas, though some owners prefer the larger V6 for heavier towing and fully loaded highway driving.

### 3.6L V6

The available 3.6L naturally aspirated V6 engine generates 276 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque. It offers smoother power delivery for hauling families and cargo, though select owners have reported drivability hiccups like stalling.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may stall unexpectedly at low speeds, during deceleration, or while coming to a stop, accompanied by dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fuel system components, throttle body, or associated engine sensors; replace failing parts as necessary.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission pairs with both engine options, routing power smoothly through front-wheel drive or available 4Motion all-wheel drive. It generally shifts reliably in everyday commuting conditions.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes and ABS

Braking performance is normally secure for a heavy crossover, but isolated owner complaints highlight critical ABS and booster warnings that require immediate professional inspection.

**ABS and Brake System Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal becomes unresponsive or difficult to press, accompanied by multiple dashboard safety warnings and potential ABS wiring harness faults.
- **Solution:** Inspect the ABS module, brake booster, and related wiring harnesses for damage or corrosion; replace faulty modules or harnesses.
- **Cost:** Out-of-pocket repair costs vary significantly depending on whether the issue stems from a simple sensor, wiring harness repair, or a complete brake booster replacement.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Front Suspension

The steering system provides light, easy maneuverability for a large SUV, but owner reports indicate occasional front-end vibrations and steering rack wear over time.

**Steering Rack Failure and Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent front-end shaking, steering wheel vibration at highway speeds, and abnormal mechanical play or resistance.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering rack and pinion assembly, wheel alignment, and suspension components; replace worn steering racks or struts if required.

## Electrical

### Instrument Panel and Wiring

The electrical architecture supports extensive cabin technology and digital displays, but complex wiring harnesses and module software can occasionally trigger intermittent glitches.

**Instrument Panel Blackout**
- **Symptoms:** Digital instrument cluster or infotainment screen goes completely dark, failing to display vital vehicle data or audio controls.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the instrument cluster module and associated fuses; replace the cluster assembly if internal hardware fails.
- **Cost:** Replacement of a failed instrument cluster panel can be costly, with out-of-pocket dealer expenses reported around $2,300.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

The safety suite includes modern airbag configurations and advanced driver assists, though some drivers experience electrical warnings tied to occupant sensors and airbag modules.

**Airbag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard displays persistent warning messages such as 'Error: Airbag, Faulty airbag' indicating a fault in the supplemental restraint system.
- **Solution:** Scan diagnostic trouble codes to pinpoint faulty sensors, wiring harnesses, or control modules; repair or replace defective components.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V614000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire. An engine stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel tank, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V904000: Suspension:Rear:Springs:Coil Springs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If a coil spring fractures while driving, it may damage a rear tire causing a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen has notified owners, and dealers will replace the rear axle coil springs, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V389000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The blocking cap disabling the adjustment of the headlight's horizontal aim may not have been installed. As such, these vehicles fail to conform to Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." Without the blocking cap, the headlight aim can be adjusted out of specification, reducing.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, as necessary, block the headlight horizontal adjustment, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V824000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The instrument cluster may not provide an audible warning to let the driver know that the key is still in the ignition when the door is open. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 114, "Theft Protection." If the driver is not notified by an audible noise that the key is left in.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the instrument cluster, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V218000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** In an event of a side impact crash, an incorrect side airbag may not deploy as intended, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 21V142000: Tires:Sidewall](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Over cured tires may develop a break in the sidewall, resulting in sudden air loss or belt edge separation which could lead to tread/belt loss. Either condition can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the tires, and replace them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V086000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." Without the reduced GVWR label, the vehicle could be overloaded, leading to a loss of control and increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install the reduced GVWR label, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V152000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The door wiring harness electrical contacts may corrode, disrupting the electrical connection and delaying the deployment of the driver or passenger front side air bag during a side impact crash. A delayed air bag deployment can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will secure and, as necessary, replace any the front door wiring harnesses showing a fault code, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Volkswagen Atlas is a compelling choice for buyers seeking generous passenger room and composed driving dynamics. However, prospective owners should thoroughly check the vehicle's history for electrical issues, steering vibrations, and brake or airbag warning lights. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic is strongly advised before finalizing a deal.
