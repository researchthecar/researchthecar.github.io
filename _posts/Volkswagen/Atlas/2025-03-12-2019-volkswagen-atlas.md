The 2019 Volkswagen Atlas is a mid-size SUV known for its spaciousness and comfortable ride, making it a popular choice for families. It was offered with two engine options: a 2.0L turbocharged four-cylinder producing 235 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.6L VR6 engine generating 276 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the Atlas for its ample cargo space and user-friendly infotainment system. Common complaints include reports of electrical issues and some concerns about long-term reliability.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

* **Turbocharger Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whistling sound from the engine, check engine light.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.6L VR6**

* **Carbon Buildup on Intake Valves**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, hesitation upon acceleration, reduced fuel economy.
    * Solution: Perform a carbon cleaning service on the intake valves.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Coolant Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Low coolant level, overheating, visible coolant leaks near the water pump or thermostat housing.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty water pump, thermostat housing, or associated hoses.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (All Engines)**

* **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noticeable hard shifts, especially between lower gears, hesitation when accelerating from a stop.
    * Solution: Software update to the transmission control module (TCM), or in severe cases, valve body replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (software update) - $2,000+ (valve body replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

* **Infotainment System Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: Freezing, slow response, screen blackouts, Bluetooth connectivity problems.
    * Solution: Software update, replacement of the infotainment unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update if covered under warranty) - $1,200+ (replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Door locks not responding to the key fob or interior switch, doors not locking/unlocking properly.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty door lock actuator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Backup Camera Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Backup camera displaying a black screen, distorted image, or no image at all.
    * Solution: Check wiring connections, replace the backup camera.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaking into the cabin during rain, damp headliner.
    * Solution: Clean and inspect sunroof drains, reseal the sunroof.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    * Description/Symptoms: Various rattles and squeaks emanating from the dashboard, doors, or rear cargo area.
    * Solution: Tighten loose trim pieces, apply felt tape to contact points.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the source of the noise. DIY fix possible, or $100 - $300+ at a shop.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2019 Volkswagen Atlas, while praised for its spacious interior and comfortable ride, has a number of potential issues that owners should be aware of. Electrical problems, particularly with the infotainment system and door locks, are relatively common and can range from minor inconveniences to more costly repairs. Engine issues like carbon buildup (3.6L) and turbocharger failure (2.0L) are also reported. Transmission issues can also be costly to address. While some issues may be minor and easily resolved, others can be more significant and require professional attention. The overall severity of issues ranges from minor annoyances like rattles to potentially expensive repairs involving the engine, transmission, and electrical systems. Addressing these issues promptly can help maintain the vehicle's reliability and prevent further damage.
