**2022 Volkswagen Atlas**

The 2022 Volkswagen Atlas is a midsize SUV with seating for up to seven passengers. It offers a choice of two engines and two transmissions. The base engine is a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder rated at 235 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. The optional engine is a 3.6-liter V6 rated at 276 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder**

* **Fuel pump failure:** The fuel pump can fail, causing the engine to stall or hesitate. The fuel pump needs to be replaced. $800 - $1,200+
* **Timing chain tensioner failure:** The timing chain tensioner can fail, causing the timing chain to skip and damaging the engine. The timing chain and tensioner need to be replaced. $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Carbon buildup on intake valves:** Carbon can build up on the intake valves, reducing engine performance. The intake valves need to be cleaned. $300 - $500+

**3.6-liter V6**

* **No issues found**

**Transmission**

**Eight-speed automatic transmission**

* **Transmission fluid leak:** The transmission fluid can leak from the seals or gaskets. The seals or gaskets need to be replaced. $300 - $500+
* **Transmission slipping:** The transmission can slip, causing the vehicle to hesitate or lose power. The transmission needs to be overhauled or replaced. $2,000 - $3,500+
* **Shift solenoid failure:** The shift solenoid can fail, causing the transmission to shift erratically or not shift at all. The shift solenoid needs to be replaced. $200 - $400+

**Suspension**

* **Front strut mount failure:** The front strut mount can fail, causing the vehicle to bounce or handle poorly. The strut mount needs to be replaced. $500 - $700+
* **Rear shock absorber failure:** The rear shock absorbers can fail, causing the vehicle to bounce or handle poorly. The shock absorbers need to be replaced. $400 - $600+

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** The battery can drain prematurely, even when the vehicle is not in use. The battery needs to be replaced. $150 - $250+
* **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the battery to not charge. The alternator needs to be replaced. $500 - $700+

**Exterior**

* **Heated side mirror failure:** The heated side mirrors can fail to operate. The side mirrors need to be replaced. $250 - $400+
* **Sunroof leak:** The sunroof can leak, allowing water into the vehicle. The sunroof seal needs to be replaced. $300 - $500+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard rattle:** The dashboard can rattle, creating a noise while driving. The dashboard needs to be inspected and repaired. $200 - $400+
* **Infotainment system glitch:** The infotainment system can glitch, causing the screen to freeze or display errors. The infotainment system needs to be updated or replaced. $500 - $1,000+

**Summary**

The 2022 Volkswagen Atlas has a number of issues that can affect its reliability. Some of these issues are minor and inexpensive to fix, while others are more serious and can cost thousands of dollars to repair. Overall, the Atlas is a solid SUV, but it is important to be aware of the potential issues before purchasing one.
