# 2023 Volkswagen Atlas Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Volkswagen Atlas, buyers will find a spacious, feature-packed midsize SUV that offers strong family utility and comfortable road manners. However, review of owner feedback and technical data highlights several recurring mechanical and electrical concerns—particularly relating to unexpected engine stalling and electrical anomalies—that prospective owners should inspect closely before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine provides a balanced blend of everyday efficiency and capable power for the Atlas. While generally robust, a subset of owners have reported unexpected drivability quirks and stalling incidents.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly lose power and stall while driving forward, reversing, or coming to a stop, requiring the driver to shift into park and restart.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis by a qualified technician is required; potential culprits include fuel system components, emissions valves, or software calibration updates.

### 3.6L V6

The 3.6-liter V6 engine offers smooth, traditional power delivery and confident towing capability for larger families. It is a known quantity in the Volkswagen lineup, though buyers should verify maintenance history.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic

The conventional 8-speed automatic transmission shifts smoothly across various driving conditions and pairs well with both engine options. No widespread mechanical failure patterns stand out, making it a dependable part of the powertrain.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical systems on modern SUVs control a vast array of convenience and safety features, but lighting components have occasionally required premature replacement.

**Daytime Running Light Failure**
- **Symptoms:** One or both of the LED daytime running lights (DRLs) may fail prematurely, sometimes triggering warning indicators on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed lighting assembly or module as necessary.

## Safety

### Air Bag

Safety systems are critical, and while standard protection equipment is comprehensive, sensor reliability and associated recall compliance require careful pre-purchase verification.

**Airbag Warning Light and Sensor Delays**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning light may illuminate unexpectedly, often associated with occupant detection sensor issues.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle inspected by a dealership to address active safety recalls or replace faulty sensor components.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V152000: Air Bags:Side/Window](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The door wiring harness electrical contacts may corrode, disrupting the electrical connection and delaying the deployment of the driver or passenger front side air bag during a side impact crash. A delayed air bag deployment can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will secure and, as necessary, replace any the front door wiring harnesses showing a fault code, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V718000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak may increase the distance required to stop, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the affected brake lines, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V753000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The engine connecting rod bearings may become damaged, which can result in engine failure. A damaged engine can result in engine failure and a stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and, if necessary, replace an affected engine, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V464000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A deactivated passenger air bag increases the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the PODS sensor mat and wiring harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V434000: Service Brakes](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Certain bolts on the chassis, brake system and/or suspension (such as bolts for certain cross member, engine mount, control arm, subframe, rear brake carrier, etc.) of affected vehicles may not have been correctly tightened during the manufacturing process. A loss of vehicle control increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the bolts as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Volkswagen Atlas remains an attractive choice for used-car buyers seeking generous passenger room and modern technology. Prospective owners should prioritize a thorough pre-purchase inspection—paying close attention to engine idle quality, electrical accessories, and outstanding recall status—to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
