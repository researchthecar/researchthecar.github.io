# 2021 Volkswagen Atlas Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Volkswagen Atlas brings spacious family-friendly three-row capability and comfortable road manners to the midsize SUV segment. However, prospective buyers should review common owner grievances spanning exterior lighting, powertrain electronics, and auxiliary body components. While many examples provide dependable family transport, checking service history for resolved electrical bugs and lighting replacements is wise before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 235 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, offering efficient daily driving. It provides adequate motivation for the Atlas but requires attention to cooling and fuel system electronics.

**Engine Stalling and Fuel Pump Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle cuts off while idling in hot weather, stalls when shifting into reverse, or triggers an EPC dashboard error.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the fuel pump control module and replace it if faulty.

### 3.6L V6

The naturally aspirated 3.6-liter V6 generates around 276 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque, delivering smooth power delivery and a higher towing capacity. It is a traditional powertrain choice for buyers needing extra passing power.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed conventional automatic transmission pairs smoothly with both engine choices across front-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations. It generally operates reliably with routine fluid maintenance.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Column

Steering system components deliver stable road feedback, though wheel alignment and steering gear checks are recommended during pre-purchase inspections.

**Highway Steering Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Steering wheel shakes or exhibits abnormal vibrations at highway speeds above 65 mph.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering rack, wheel balance, and suspension components for wear.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Electrical complaints center frequently on exterior lighting failures, particularly with integrated LED daytime running strips.

**Daytime Running Light (DRL) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** LED daytime running light strips burn out prematurely, flicker, or stop working entirely on one or both sides of the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Replace the affected headlight or LED strip assembly.

### Hood Latch Release

Cabin hardware and mechanical interface components are generally robust, though minor assembly wear items can occasionally fail.

**Broken Hood Release Cable**
- **Symptoms:** The hood release handle inside the cabin loses tension or snaps, making it impossible to open the hood for routine maintenance.
- **Solution:** Replace the broken hood release cable and latch mechanism.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V716000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The rear view camera could malfunction during an ignition cycle, leading to a black screen or infotainment system freeze. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A black or frozen rear view image reduces the driver's visibility when reversing, increasing the risk of.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will update the infotainment system software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V026000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A fractured steering knuckle can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and, as necessary, replace one or both steering knuckles, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V132000: Wheels:Rim](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An insufficiently attached wheel may separate from the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will inspect all four wheels, and as necessary, replace the wheels, lug bolts ,and wheel bearings, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V812000: Wheels:Lugs/Nuts/Bolts/Studs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The bolts may loosen and cause a loss of control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the tightness of the wheel bearing bolts and replace the bolts, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V892000: Visibility:Defroster/Defogger/Hvac System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The air conditioning system drain tube could be blocked, causing water to leak inside the vehicle near the air bag control module. A water leak may damage the air bag control module, causing an inadvertent air bag deployment and increasing the risk of injury or a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will repair the drain tube and replace the air bag control module, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V505000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Parts of the front seat frame and backrest adjuster may not have been welded properly. In the event of a rear-end crash, the backrest adjustment could break, possibly reducing the amount of occupant restraint, thus increasing risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the front seats, and, as necessary, replace the seatback frames, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V504000: Exterior Lighting:Headlights](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment." A headlight aimed incorrectly aimed reduces the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will adjust the driver's side headlight, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V702000: Tires:Sidewall](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Atlas&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Both conditions increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will inspect DOT date code and mold number on the vehicle's tires to see if they are affected, replacing them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Volkswagen Atlas is a compelling choice for families seeking generous passenger and cargo space, but used car shoppers should perform a thorough mechanical and electrical inspection. Pay close attention to the operation of daytime running lights, verify smooth engine idling and warm-start behavior, and ensure the hood release mechanism functions properly. Securing a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection will help filter out vehicles with unresolved owner-reported issues.
