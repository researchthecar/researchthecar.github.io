The 2023 Volkswagen Atlas is a mid-size SUV that offers a spacious interior and comfortable ride. Engine options include a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder producing 235 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.6-liter V6 producing 276 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the Atlas for its ample cargo space and third-row seating, while some criticisms include lower fuel economy than competitors and a less engaging driving experience.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, whistling or whining noise from the engine bay, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

* **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: Low oil level warning light, the need to add oil frequently between oil changes, blue smoke from the exhaust pipe.
    * Solution: Replacement of piston rings, valve stem seals, or potentially engine replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3.6L V6**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (Applies to both 2.0L Turbo and 3.6L V6)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Jerky shifts, especially during acceleration or deceleration; hesitation when shifting gears; transmission slipping.
    *   Solution: Software update for the transmission control module (TCM), valve body replacement, or complete transmission replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $5000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Wear of Strut Mounts**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or knocking noise from the front suspension, especially when going over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replacement of strut mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Touchscreen unresponsive, system restarts unexpectedly, loss of functionality.
    *   Solution: Software update for the infotainment system, replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Malfunctioning Sensors**
    * Description/Symptoms: Faulty readings from the sensors, dash warning lights illuminate.
    * Solution: Replace faulty sensor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Water Leaks Through Sunroof/Panoramic Roof**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water stains on headliner, damp carpets, musty odor in the cabin after rain.
    *   Solution: Cleaning and resealing sunroof drains, replacing sunroof seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises from Dashboard or Trim Panels**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Annoying noises coming from the dashboard, door panels, or other interior trim pieces, especially when driving on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Identifying and securing loose trim pieces with clips or foam padding.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2023 Volkswagen Atlas has some reported issues, with the most concerning relating to the 2.0L Turbo engine (turbo failure, excessive oil consumption) and the 8-speed automatic transmission (rough shifting). Electrical issues with the infotainment system and suspension issues are also present. The severity of these issues ranges from minor annoyances to potentially expensive repairs. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems and thoroughly inspect the vehicle before purchasing.
