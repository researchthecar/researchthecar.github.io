The 2022 Volkswagen Atlas is a mid-size SUV known for its spaciousness, comfortable ride, and user-friendly technology. It is available with two engine options: a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder producing 235 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.6-liter V6 generating 276 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the Atlas for its ample cargo space and third-row seating suitable for adults, but some criticize its fuel economy and the base engine's acceleration.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whistling sound from the engine, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.6L V6**

*No Issues Found*

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (Both Engines)**

*   **Rough or Delayed Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh shifts between gears, noticeable delay when shifting, particularly during acceleration or deceleration.
    *   Solution: Some owners have had success with transmission software updates. In more severe cases, valve body replacement or full transmission replacement is required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (software update) - $5,000+ (transmission replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*No Issues Found*

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen freezes, becomes unresponsive, or restarts unexpectedly. This can affect navigation, audio, and climate controls.
    *   Solution: Software updates, infotainment system reset, or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,500+ (unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Sensor Failure (e.g., Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning or intermittent operation of parking sensors or blind spot monitoring system. False alarms or failure to detect obstacles/vehicles.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Faulty Wire Harness**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent electrical issues, check engine lights, and the vehicle randomly shutting off.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty wire harness.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin from the sunroof, often during heavy rain.
    *   Solution: Cleaning and resealing the sunroof drains, or replacing the sunroof seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various rattling noises from the dashboard, door panels, or rear cargo area while driving.
    *   Solution: Locating and securing the loose components causing the rattles.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+ depending on the severity and source of the rattle.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Volkswagen Atlas presents a mixed bag of potential issues. While some owners report relatively trouble-free experiences, others have encountered problems with the turbocharger, transmission, infotainment system, sunroof leaks, and rattling noises. The severity ranges from minor inconveniences like rattles to more substantial concerns like turbocharger and transmission failures, which can be costly to repair. Potential buyers should be aware of these common issues and consider a thorough inspection before purchasing.
