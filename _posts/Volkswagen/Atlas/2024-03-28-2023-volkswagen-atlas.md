**2023 Volkswagen Atlas**

The 2023 Volkswagen Atlas is a three-row SUV that offers a spacious interior and a range of powerful engine options. It is available with a 2.0L turbocharged inline-four engine producing 235 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, or a 3.6L VR6 engine producing 276 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are mated to an eight-speed automatic transmission. The Atlas is also available with all-wheel drive.

While the Atlas is a popular choice for families, there have been some reported issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-Four Engine**

* **Intake Manifold Valve Sticking:** The intake manifold valve can become stuck, causing the engine to run rough and hesitate. The solution is to replace the intake manifold, which costs around $1,500 to $2,000.
* **Timing Chain Failure:** The timing chain can fail prematurely, causing the engine to stall or seize. The solution is to replace the timing chain and related components, which costs around $2,500 to $3,500.

**3.6L VR6 Engine**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Eight-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Shift Solenoid Failure:** The shift solenoid can fail, causing the transmission to hesitate or slip. The solution is to replace the shift solenoid, which costs around $400 to $600.
* **Transmission Fluid Leaks:** The transmission fluid can leak from the transmission pan or other seals. The solution is to replace the leaking seal or pan, which costs around $200 to $500.

**Suspension**

* **Strut Failure:** The struts can fail prematurely, causing the vehicle to ride rough and handle poorly. The solution is to replace the struts, which costs around $800 to $1,200.
* **Bushings Worn:** The suspension bushings can wear out prematurely, causing the suspension to squeak and rattle. The solution is to replace the bushings, which costs around $150 to $300.

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** The battery can drain prematurely, even when the vehicle is not running. The solution is to replace the battery or find and repair the electrical drain, which can cost anywhere from $100 to $1,000.
* **Module Failure:** The electronic modules in the vehicle can fail, causing a variety of electrical problems. The solution is to replace the failed module, which costs around $500 to $2,000.

**Exterior**

* **Panoramic Sunroof Leaks:** The panoramic sunroof can leak water into the cabin. The solution is to replace the sunroof seal, which costs around $500 to $800.
* **Liftgate Struts Fail:** The liftgate struts can fail prematurely, causing the liftgate to open or close slowly. The solution is to replace the struts, which costs around $200 to $400.

**Interior**

* **Seat Heater Failure:** The seat heaters can fail, causing the seats to no longer heat up. The solution is to replace the heating element, which costs around $200 to $400.
* **Infotainment System Freezes:** The infotainment system can freeze or crash, causing the display to go blank. The solution is to restart the system or update the software, which is usually free of charge.

**Summary**

The 2023 Volkswagen Atlas is a popular choice for families, but there are some reported issues with the vehicle. The most common issues seem to be with the engine, transmission, and electrical system. The severity of the issues varies, with some being relatively minor and others being more serious. The cost to fix the issues can also vary, with some repairs being relatively inexpensive and others being more costly. Overall, the Atlas is a reliable vehicle, but it is important to be aware of the potential issues before purchasing one.
