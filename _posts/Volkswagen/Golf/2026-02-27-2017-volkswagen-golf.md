# 2017 Volkswagen Golf Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Volkswagen Golf offers engaging driving dynamics, a refined cabin, and versatile hatchback practicality. However, used car buyers should carefully evaluate powertrain options and research known mechanical tendencies before purchasing, as certain recurring issues have been documented by owners.

---

## Engine

### 1.8L Turbocharged Inline-4

A 1.8-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine making approximately 170 horsepower and 199 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard powerplant for most non-performance Golf models with steady highway capability and decent everyday efficiency.

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 (GTI / R)

A high-output 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine delivering between 210 and 292 horsepower depending on the performance trim, offering spirited acceleration for enthusiast drivers.

### Fuel System

Manages fuel delivery from the tank to the engine.

**Fuel Pump and Suction Jet Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Gasoline overflowing from the filler neck during refueling, fuel odors, or sudden engine stalling.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace faulty fuel pumps or suction jet assemblies as required.

### Cooling System

Regulates engine operating temperatures.

**Coolant Leaks and Thermostat Housing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant warning lights, visible fluid puddles under the vehicle, or engine running warm.
- **Solution:** Replace cracked thermostat housings or leaking gaskets.

## Transmission

### 5-Speed / 6-Speed Manual Transmission

Traditional manual transmissions pairing direct driver control with solid everyday reliability when maintained with regular fluid changes.

### 6-Speed / 7-Speed DSG Automatic Transmission

Direct-shift dual-clutch automatic transmissions known for rapid gear changes and responsive performance.

**Gear Selector and Park Position Recognition**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle fails to recognize when it is shifted into Park, preventing doors from locking or triggering dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect and repair or replace the microswitch or shifter assembly components.

## Steering

### Clock Spring and Steering Sensors

Maintains electrical connections for steering wheel-mounted controls and safety airbags.

**Clock Spring Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated airbag warning lights, non-functioning steering wheel controls, or snapping noises when turning the wheel.
- **Solution:** Replace the clock spring assembly.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Electronics

Controls cabin accessories, displays, and infotainment connectivity.

**Infotainment System Rebooting**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen display and infotainment system continuously restart every 30 seconds to a few minutes.
- **Solution:** Perform software updates or replace the head unit if hardware failure is diagnosed.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Glass

Encloses the cabin and manages exterior weather sealing.

**Panoramic Sunroof Leaks and Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Water stains on the headliner, wet floor carpets, musty odors, or sudden spontaneous glass breakage.
- **Solution:** Clear blocked drain tubes, repair frame weld seals, or replace shattered glass panels.

- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will install an additional switch and circuit board, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V188000: Suspension:Rear:Springs:Coil Springs](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Golf&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If a coil spring fractures while driving, it may damage a rear tire causing a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rear axle coil springs, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V955000: Seat Belts:Pretensioner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Golf&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Additionally, certain 2017 Audi Q7, A4 Sedan, A4 Allroad, and 2018 Audi Q5 vehicles are being recalled because the seat belt pretensioners may not activate properly. In the event of a crash, if the air bags and/or the seat belt pretensioners do not inflate or function properly, the vehicle occupants have an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Volkswagen and Audi will notify owners, and dealers will replace the affected air bags and seat belt pretensioners, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V321000: Unknown Or Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Golf&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Vehicles that do not meet all regulatory requirements may have an increased risk of injury, crash, or fire.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V110000: Fuel System, Gasoline](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Volkswagen&model=Golf&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the suction pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Volkswagen Golf remains an appealing hatch for buyers seeking upscale driving dynamics and efficiency, but due diligence is essential. Prioritize inspecting service records for fuel system maintenance, coolant integrity, and sunroof drainage to avoid unexpected repair bills.
