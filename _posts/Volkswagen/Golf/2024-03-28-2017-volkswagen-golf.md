**Introduction**

The 2017 Volkswagen Golf is a compact hatchback that offers a sporty and fuel-efficient driving experience. It is available with various engine and transmission options, including a 1.8L turbocharged inline-4 engine with a 5-speed manual or 6-speed automatic transmission, a 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 engine with a 6-speed automatic transmission, and a 2.0L turbocharged diesel inline-4 engine with a 6-speed manual or 6-speed DSG automatic transmission. The Golf is praised for its handling, performance, and fuel economy, but some owners have reported issues with certain components.

**Engine**

* **1.8L Turbocharged Inline-4 Engine**
    * **Intake Manifold Problems**
        * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold may crack, causing a loss of power and increased fuel consumption.
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Carbon Buildup**
        * Description/Symptoms: Carbon buildup on the intake valves can cause reduced engine performance and fuel economy.
        * Solution: Clean the intake valves.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
        * Recalls: None Found
* **2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4 Engine**
    * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner may fail, resulting in a rattling noise and loss of engine power.
        * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200
        * Recalls: 2016-2018 Volkswagen Golf (NHTSA Campaign Number: 19V418)
* **2.0L Turbocharged Diesel Inline-4 Engine**
    * **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
        * Description/Symptoms: The DPF may clog, causing reduced engine performance and increased emissions.
        * Solution: Clean or replace the DPF.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **No Issues Found**
* **6-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Shift Solenoid Problems**
        * Description/Symptoms: The shift solenoids may fail, causing rough or delayed shifting.
        * Solution: Replace the shift solenoids.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Mechatronic Unit Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The mechatronic unit controls the transmission and may fail, causing loss of drive.
        * Solution: Replace the mechatronic unit.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000
        * Recalls: 2016-2018 Volkswagen Golf (NHTSA Campaign Number: 16V656)
* **6-Speed DSG Automatic Transmission**
    * **Clutch Pack Problems**
        * Description/Symptoms: The clutch pack may wear prematurely, causing slipping and loss of power.
        * Solution: Replace the clutch pack.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Noise**
    * Description/Symptoms: The front suspension may make a rattling or knocking noise when driving over bumps.
    * Solution: Replace the front suspension components (e.g., control arm bushings, ball joints).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain prematurely, causing the vehicle to not start.
    * Solution: Diagnose and fix the source of the battery drain (e.g., faulty module, parasitic draw).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may experience glitches, freezes, or connectivity issues.
    * Solution: Update the software or replace the infotainment unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Climate Control Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control system may not function properly, resulting in poor heating or cooling performance.
    * Solution: Diagnose and fix the issue (e.g., replace blend door actuator, check refrigerant levels).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

* **No Issues Found**

**Summary**

While the 2017 Volkswagen Golf is generally a reliable vehicle, certain components can experience issues. The most severe and expensive issues are related to the timing chain tensioner and mechatronic unit failures, which can cost thousands of dollars to repair. Engine and transmission problems are more common in higher-mileage vehicles, and some electrical and interior problems can be minor and easy to resolve. Overall, the issues reported with the 2017 Volkswagen Golf are not widespread, and many owners have had positive experiences with the vehicle.
