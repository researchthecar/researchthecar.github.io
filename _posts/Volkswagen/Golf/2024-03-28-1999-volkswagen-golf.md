**Introduction**

The 1999 Volkswagen Golf is a compact hatchback that was available with a variety of engine and transmission options. It was praised for its handling, performance, and fuel efficiency. However, it also had several issues that plagued owners.

**Engine**

* **2.0L 8V SOHC**
    * **Oil Sludge Buildup**
        * Description/Symptoms: Sludge buildup can cause engine damage, including blocked oil passages and worn bearings.
        * Solution: Regular oil changes and synthetic oil can help prevent sludge buildup.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * **Engine Timing Chain Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Can cause catastrophic engine damage. Listen for a rattling noise from the engine.
        * Solution: Replacement of the timing chain and associated components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    * **Coolant Flange Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: This can cause coolant leaks and lead to overheating.
        * Solution: Replace the coolant flange.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200


* **2.0L 16V SOHC**

* **1.9L TDI Diesel**
    * **Fuel System Problems**
        * Description/Symptoms: Fuel injectors or the fuel pump can malfunction. Symptoms include decreased performance and poor fuel economy.
        * Solution: Replace the affected components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+


**Transmission**

* **5-speed Manual Transmission**

* **4-speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Transmission Slipping**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, slipping gears, or refusal to engage gears.
        * Solution: Repair or rebuild the transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Bearings**
    * Description/Symptoms: Causes rattling or clunking sounds over bumps.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mount bearings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

* **Control Arm Bushings**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn bushings can lead to excessive rear wheel camber and trembling of the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace the control arm bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Failure to charge the battery or diming of the lights.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Can cause the battery to discharge quickly, preventing the engine from starting.
    * Solution: Identify and fix the source of the drain (often caused by faulty electronics).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the source of the drain

**Exterior**

* **Rust**
    * Description/Symptoms: Corrosion can occur on various body panels, especially around the wheel arches and rocker panels.
    * Solution: Rust prevention treatments or panel replacements.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Interior**

* **Airbag Control Module Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Warning lights may illuminate, and the airbags may not deploy in a crash.
    * Solution: Replace the airbag control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

* **Climate Control Panel Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Buttons may not function, or the climate control system may not operate properly.
    * Solution: Replace the climate control panel assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+

**Recalls**

There are several recalls associated with the 1999 Volkswagen Golf, including:

* NHTSA Campaign Number: 99V096000: Power Window Switch May Malfunction
* NHTSA Campaign Number: 99V218000: Incorrect Front Lower Control Arm Bushing May Cause Excessive Rear Wheel Camber
* NHTSA Campaign Number: 01V067000: Airbag Control Module May Not Function Properly

**Summary**

The 1999 Volkswagen Golf has a number of issues that can be costly to repair. While it was praised for its performance and fuel efficiency, the engine sludge buildup, transmission problems, and electrical issues can significantly impact the ownership experience. It is important to be aware of these potential problems before purchasing a used Golf.
