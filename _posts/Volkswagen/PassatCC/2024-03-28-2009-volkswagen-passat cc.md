**Introduction:**

The 2009 Volkswagen Passat CC is a midsize luxury sedan that offers a stylish and sporty alternative to traditional sedans. It is available with a choice of three engines: a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder, a 2.5-liter naturally aspirated five-cylinder, and a 3.6-liter narrow-angle V6. The 2.0-liter engine produces 200 horsepower and 207 lb-ft of torque, while the 2.5-liter engine produces 170 horsepower and 177 lb-ft of torque. The 3.6-liter engine produces 280 horsepower and 265 lb-ft of torque. All engines are mated to a six-speed automatic transmission.

The Passat CC is known for its sleek design, spacious interior, and comfortable ride. However, it has also been plagued by a number of reliability issues.

**Engine:**

**2.0-liter Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

- **Timing chain tensioner failure:** The timing chain tensioner can fail, causing the timing chain to skip, which can result in engine damage. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, reduced engine power, and check engine light illumination. The solution is to replace the timing chain tensioner and timing chain. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $1,500 - $2,500+.
- **Intake manifold failure:** The intake manifold can crack, causing a loss of engine power and a vacuum leak. Symptoms include a decrease in engine power, a hissing noise from the engine, and check engine light illumination. The solution is to replace the intake manifold. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $500 - $1,000.
- **Water pump failure:** The water pump can fail, causing the engine to overheat. Symptoms include a loss of coolant, a high engine temperature gauge reading, and steam coming from the engine. The solution is to replace the water pump. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $500 - $1,000.

**2.5-liter Naturally Aspirated Five-Cylinder**

- **No Issues Found**

**3.6-liter Narrow-Angle V6**

- **Oil leak:** The V6 engine is prone to oil leaks from the valve cover gaskets and camshaft seals. Symptoms include a loss of oil, a burning smell, and a check engine light illumination. The solution is to replace the leaking gaskets or seals. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $500 - $1,000.

**Transmission:**

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

- **Shifting problems:** The transmission may experience shifting problems, such as slipping gears or refusing to shift gears. Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, a delay in shifting gears, and check engine light illumination. The solution is to reprogram the transmission control module or replace the transmission. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $500 - $2,000+.
- **Torque converter failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing the transmission to slip or shudder. Symptoms include difficulty accelerating, a vibration or shudder when shifting gears, and check engine light illumination. The solution is to replace the torque converter. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $1,000 - $2,000+.

**Suspension:**

- **No Issues Found**

**Electrical:**

- **Battery drain:** The battery can drain prematurely due to a faulty alternator or a parasitic draw. Symptoms include difficulty starting the car, a weak battery, and a check engine light illumination. The solution is to replace the alternator or find and fix the parasitic draw. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $500 - $1,500+.
- **Electrical short:** The electrical system can experience short circuits, which can cause a variety of problems, such as blown fuses, failed components, and check engine light illumination. The solution is to diagnose the short circuit and repair or replace the affected components. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $500 - $2,000+.

**Exterior:**

- **No Issues Found**

**Interior:**

- **Climate control failure:** The climate control system can fail, causing the heating or air conditioning to not work properly. Symptoms include a lack of heat or cold air, a malfunctioning fan, and check engine light illumination. The solution is to diagnose and repair or replace the faulty component. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $500 - $2,000+.
- **Power window failure:** The power window motors or switches can fail, causing the windows to not open or close properly. Symptoms include difficulty operating the windows, a buzzing noise from the window motors, and a check engine light illumination. The solution is to replace the faulty motor or switch. The estimated cost to fix this issue is $500 - $1,000+.

**Summary:**

The 2009 Volkswagen Passat CC is a stylish and sporty midsize luxury sedan, but it has been plagued by a number of reliability issues, including engine failures, transmission problems, electrical problems, and interior problems. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem, but some repairs can be very expensive. It is important to weigh the potential risks of ownership against the vehicle's strengths before making a decision about whether or not to purchase a 2009 Volkswagen Passat CC.
