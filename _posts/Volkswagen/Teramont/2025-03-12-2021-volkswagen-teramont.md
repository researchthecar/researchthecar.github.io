The 2021 Volkswagen Teramont (also known as the Atlas) is a mid-size SUV known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and user-friendly technology. Available engine options for the 2021 Teramont included a 2.0L Turbocharged inline-4 engine producing 235 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque and a 3.6L VR6 engine that delivers 276 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the Teramont's ample cargo space and third-row seating, making it suitable for families. Common complaints often revolve around fuel economy, some aspects of the interior quality, and certain electronic glitches.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whistling noise from the engine, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.6L VR6**

*   **Timing Chain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise upon startup, engine misfires, check engine light. Can cause severe engine damage if left unaddressed.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $3500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting between gears, particularly during acceleration or deceleration. May experience a shudder feeling.
    *   Solution: Software update/reprogramming of the transmission control module (TCM). In more severe cases, valve body replacement or complete transmission rebuild may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300 (Software Update); $800 - $1500+ (Valve Body); $4000 - $7000+ (Transmission Rebuild/Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen freezes, becomes unresponsive, or restarts spontaneously. This can affect navigation, audio, and climate controls.
    *   Solution: Software update for the infotainment system. In some cases, replacement of the head unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Software Update if covered under warranty); $500 - $1500+ (Head Unit Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Backup Camera Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The backup camera displays a distorted image, a black screen, or fails to activate when the vehicle is in reverse.
    *   Solution: Inspection and potential replacement of the camera unit or wiring.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin, particularly around the headliner, during rain or car washes.
    *   Solution: Cleaning of sunroof drain tubes. In some cases, resealing or replacement of the sunroof seal may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 (Drain Cleaning); $300 - $800+ (Seal Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling Noises from Dashboard/Trim**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Annoying rattling or squeaking noises emanating from the dashboard, door panels, or other interior trim pieces while driving, especially on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Locating and securing the loose trim pieces or applying foam padding to dampen the vibrations.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+ (depending on the complexity and if performed by a professional)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Volkswagen Teramont, while spacious and family-friendly, can present owners with a range of potential issues. The engine issues, particularly with the timing chain in the 3.6L VR6, can be costly to repair. Transmission problems with rough shifting are also a concern. Electrical issues with the infotainment system and backup camera are relatively common and can be frustrating. Exterior leaks and interior rattles, while less critical, can detract from the overall ownership experience. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to potentially expensive repairs, so a thorough inspection is recommended before purchasing a used 2021 Teramont.
