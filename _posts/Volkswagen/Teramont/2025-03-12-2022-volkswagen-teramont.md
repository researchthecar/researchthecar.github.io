The 2022 Volkswagen Teramont (known as the Atlas in some markets) is a mid-size SUV known for its spaciousness and comfortable ride. It typically comes with two engine options: a 2.0L turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing around 235 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.6L VR6 engine producing around 276 horsepower and 266 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are generally paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the Teramont for its ample cargo space and third-row seating, making it suitable for families. Common complaints include fuel economy and some reports of minor electronic glitches.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whining sound from the engine bay, check engine light illuminated.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.6L VR6**

*   **Water Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, low coolant level warning light.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the water pump and potentially the thermostat.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (All Engines)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noticeable hesitation or jerky shifts between gears, particularly at lower speeds.
    *   Solution: Software update to the transmission control module (TCM). In some cases, valve body replacement might be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300 (Software Update), $1500 - $3000+ (Valve Body Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen freezes, becomes unresponsive, or restarts randomly.
    *   Solution: Software update to the infotainment system. If the problem persists, the infotainment unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $200 (Software Update), $800 - $1500+ (Unit Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Sensor Malfunctions (e.g., Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: False alarms, intermittent failure of the system, or error messages displayed on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis and replacement of the faulty sensor(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin during rain, especially around the sunroof area.
    *   Solution: Cleaning and resealing the sunroof drains. In severe cases, the sunroof seal or assembly may need replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 (Drain Cleaning), $500 - $1500+ (Seal/Assembly Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Third-Row Seat Latch Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty folding or unfolding the third-row seats due to a malfunctioning latch mechanism.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the faulty latch mechanism.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Rattling Noises from Dashboard or Trim**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Annoying rattling sounds emanating from the dashboard or other interior trim pieces while driving.
    *   Solution: Identifying and securing the loose components causing the rattle. This may involve adding padding or tightening fasteners.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Volkswagen Teramont presents a mix of potential issues. Engine problems, such as turbocharger or water pump failure, can be costly, while transmission issues like rough shifting might be resolved with software updates or valve body replacements. Electrical glitches, especially with the infotainment system and sensors, are common annoyances. Sunroof leaks and interior trim rattles also contribute to owner complaints. Overall, the severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to potentially expensive repairs, making it crucial for prospective buyers to be aware and thoroughly inspect the vehicle.
