**Introduction**
The 1995 Suzuki Sidekick is a compact SUV that was offered with two engine options: a 1.6-liter four-cylinder engine and a 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine. Both engines were available with either a five-speed manual transmission or a four-speed automatic transmission. The Sidekick was praised for its off-road capabilities, affordability, and fuel efficiency, but it was criticized for its lack of power and outdated interior.

**Engine**
**1.6-liter Four-Cylinder Engine**
**No Issues Found**

**2.0-liter Four-Cylinder Engine**
**Valve Cover Gasket Leaks**
Description/Symptoms: Oil leaking from the top of the engine, visible in the spark plug tubes.
Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
**Five-Speed Manual Transmission**
**Shifter Bushing Wear**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, especially into reverse.
Solution: Replace the shifter bushings.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
Recalls: None Found

**Four-Speed Automatic Transmission**
**No Issues Found**

**Suspension**
**Front Suspension Struts**
Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises when driving over bumps, uneven tire wear.
Solution: Replace the front suspension struts.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

**Rear Suspension Shocks**
Description/Symptoms: Bouncing or swaying when driving, difficulty controlling the vehicle over bumps.
Solution: Replace the rear suspension shocks.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
**Alternator Failures**
Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging, dim headlights, electrical system problems.
Solution: Replace the alternator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

**Starter Solenoid Issues**
Description/Symptoms: Engine cranks but does not start, clicking noises when starting.
Solution: Replace the starter solenoid.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
**Rusty Tailgate**
Description/Symptoms: Corrosion on the tailgate, visible on the outside and inside.
Solution: Repair or replace the tailgate.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
**Dashboard Cracking**
Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing on the dashboard, especially in areas exposed to sunlight.
Solution: Replace the dashboard or use a dashboard cover.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1995 Suzuki Sidekick is a reliable and affordable compact SUV with decent off-road capabilities. However, it does have some common issues, including valve cover gasket leaks, valve cover gasket leaks, starter solenoid issues, rusty tailgates, and dashboard cracking. The severity of these issues varies, and the cost to fix them can range from $100 to $600 or more. Overall, the Sidekick is a good value for the price, but buyers should be aware of these potential problems.
