The 2018 Suzuki Ciaz is a subcompact sedan known for its fuel efficiency, spacious interior, and affordability. Depending on the market, engine options included a 1.4-liter K14B petrol engine producing around 91 horsepower and 96 lb-ft of torque, as well as a 1.3-liter D13A diesel engine. Transmission options typically consisted of a 5-speed manual or a 4-speed automatic transmission depending on the engine option. Owners generally appreciate the Ciaz for its comfortable ride and value for money but some criticize its somewhat underpowered engines and basic interior.

**Engine**

**1.4L K14B Petrol**

*   **Idling Issues/Rough Running**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, stalling, or hesitation, especially when the engine is cold. Can sometimes be accompanied by the check engine light illuminating.
    *   Solution: Cleaning the throttle body, replacing spark plugs, or checking for vacuum leaks. In some cases, it may be necessary to update the engine control unit (ECU) software.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**1.3L D13A Diesel**

*   **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: DPF clogging can lead to reduced engine performance, increased fuel consumption, and the DPF warning light illuminating.
    *   Solution: Forced DPF regeneration by a mechanic, or in severe cases, DPF replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

*   **Delayed or Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noticeable delay in shifting gears, or harsh, jerky shifts between gears.
    *   Solution: Replacing the transmission fluid. If that doesn't fix the issue, the valve body or the transmission itself might need to be rebuilt or replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $2500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Bushing Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or knocking noises from the suspension, especially when going over bumps. Can lead to uneven tire wear and poor handling.
    *   Solution: Replacing worn suspension bushings (control arm bushings, sway bar bushings).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Sensor Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various warning lights illuminating on the dashboard (ABS, airbag, etc.).
    *   Solution: Diagnosing the specific sensor that has failed and replacing it.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $80 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Air Conditioning Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Weak or no cold air blowing from the air conditioning vents.
    *   Solution: Refrigerant Recharge, compressor replacement, or replacing other A/C components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $75 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Summary**

The 2018 Suzuki Ciaz can experience some common issues, primarily relating to the engine, transmission, and suspension. The severity of these issues can range from minor inconveniences to more costly repairs. Addressing these issues promptly can prevent further damage and maintain the vehicle's reliability.
