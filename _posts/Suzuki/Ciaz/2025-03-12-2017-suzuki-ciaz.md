The 2017 Suzuki Ciaz is a subcompact sedan known for its fuel efficiency, spacious interior, and affordable price point. The main engine option is a 1.4-liter K14B inline-4 gasoline engine producing around 91 horsepower and 96 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual and a 4-speed automatic. Owners generally appreciate the Ciaz for its comfortable ride and good value, but some common complaints revolve around its relatively weak engine performance and dated transmission options.

**Engine**

**1.4L K14B Inline-4**

*   **Excessive Engine Noise**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report excessive engine noise, particularly at higher RPMs or during acceleration. This is often described as a rattling or ticking sound.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis by a mechanic is necessary to pinpoint the source of the noise. It could be due to worn valve train components, timing chain issues, or other internal engine problems.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+ depending on the source of the noise and the required repairs.
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Reduced Fuel Economy**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced a decrease in fuel economy over time, even with regular maintenance.
    *   Solution: Check for common causes of reduced fuel economy, such as dirty air filter, faulty oxygen sensor, or worn spark plugs. Consider cleaning the fuel injectors and throttle body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+, depending on the cause.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report harsh or jerky shifting, particularly between first and second gear.
    *   Solution: A transmission fluid flush may improve shifting quality. In some cases, the issue may be due to a faulty shift solenoid or other internal transmission component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+, depending on the cause.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Delayed Engagement**
    * Description/Symptoms: Delay in engagement of the transmission when shifting from park or neutral to drive or reverse.
    * Solution: Check transmission fluid level and condition, and ensure it is filled with the correct type of fluid. Have a qualified mechanic inspect the transmission for internal damage, such as worn clutches or valve body issues.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $2000+ depending on the cause and extent of repair.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Malfunctioning Power Windows**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or complete failure of the power windows to operate.
    *   Solution: Check the power window switch, wiring harness, and window motor. Replace the faulty component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain, resulting in difficulty starting the vehicle.
    *   Solution: Check for parasitic draws from aftermarket accessories or faulty electrical components. Test the alternator for proper charging.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling Dashboard/Trim**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling or squeaking noises emanating from the dashboard or other interior trim panels, especially on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Locate the source of the rattle and secure the loose trim piece with foam padding or clips.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $100 (DIY or small repair shop)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*No Issues Found*

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Suspension Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners report premature wear of the suspension components, such as shock absorbers and bushings, resulting in a bumpy or unstable ride.
    *   Solution: Replace worn shock absorbers, struts, and bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Suzuki Ciaz generally experiences minor issues, with the most common complaints relating to the engine and transmission. Engine noise and harsh shifting are recurring themes. While some electrical and suspension issues may arise, they are not widespread. The cost of repairs is generally moderate, with most issues falling within the $100 - $800 range, although some engine-related problems could be more expensive. It's important to note that the severity and frequency of these issues can vary depending on driving habits and maintenance history.
