The 2022 Suzuki Ciaz is a subcompact sedan known for its fuel efficiency, spacious interior, and affordable price point. It is equipped with a 1.5-liter K15B inline-four gasoline engine producing around 103 horsepower and 101 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options typically include a 5-speed manual and a 4-speed automatic. Owners generally appreciate the Ciaz for its comfortable ride and value for money, but some criticisms include its somewhat dated design and modest performance compared to rivals.

**Engine**

**1.5L K15B**
*   **Engine Misfires**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, decreased power, check engine light illuminated. This could be due to faulty ignition coils or spark plugs.
    *   Solution: Replace faulty ignition coils and/or spark plugs. Diagnose any underlying issues causing the misfires.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ depending on the number of coils/plugs replaced and labor rates.
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noticeably decreasing oil levels between oil changes, blue exhaust smoke.
    *   Solution: Check for leaks, perform a compression test to check the health of the piston rings. Replacing the piston rings may be necessary in severe cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 (for minor leak fixes) to $1000+ (for major engine work like piston ring replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**
*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noticeable jerking or clunking during gear changes, especially between 1st and 2nd gear.
    *   Solution: Check and replace the transmission fluid, inspect valve body, software update, or transmission replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (fluid change) to $2500+ (transmission replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Manual**
*   No Issues Found

**Suspension**
*   **Premature Wear of Suspension Bushings**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the suspension, especially when going over bumps. Reduced handling precision.
    *   Solution: Replace worn suspension bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$500+ depending on the number of bushings replaced and the labor rates.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen freezes, becomes unresponsive, or restarts intermittently.
    *   Solution: Software update. If the problem persists, the head unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update at dealership) to $500+ (head unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Car won't start after sitting for a short period of time. Needing to jump start the vehicle.
    * Solution: Test and replace the battery. Inspect the vehicle's electrical system for parasitic draws.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
*   No Issues Found

**Interior**
*   **Dashboard Cracking/Discoloration**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing on the dashboard surface, particularly in areas exposed to direct sunlight. Discoloration of the dashboard material.
    *   Solution: Dashboard replacement or use of dashboard covers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (dashboard cover) to $800+ (dashboard replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Suzuki Ciaz presents a mix of minor to moderate issues. Engine misfires and oil consumption with the 1.5L engine and harsh shifting in the 4-speed automatic are potential powertrain concerns. Suspension bushing wear can affect ride quality. Electrical glitches with the infotainment system and dashboard problems are also reported. Repair costs can range from a few hundred dollars for minor fixes to over a thousand for more extensive repairs. Overall, the 2022 Suzuki Ciaz appears to be a reliable car with some potentially costly, but not catastrophic, issues.
