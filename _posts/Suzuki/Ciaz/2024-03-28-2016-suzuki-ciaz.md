**Introduction**

The 2016 Suzuki Ciaz is a compact sedan that offers a combination of practical features, fuel efficiency, and affordability. It is available with two engine options: a 1.4-liter petrol engine and a 1.3-liter diesel engine. Both engines come paired with a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission. The 1.4-liter petrol engine produces 91 horsepower and 130 Nm of torque, while the 1.3-liter diesel engine produces 90 horsepower and 200 Nm of torque. Owners praise the Ciaz for its spacious cabin, comfortable ride, and excellent fuel economy. However, some have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, exterior, and interior.

**Engine**

* **1.4-liter Petrol Engine**
    * **Leaking head gasket:** Symptoms include white smoke from the exhaust, overheating, and coolant leaks. Solution: Replace the head gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * **Timing chain tensioner failure:** Symptoms include rattling noises from the engine, especially at idle. Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **1.3-liter Diesel Engine**
    * **Fuel injector failure:** Symptoms include rough idling, poor acceleration, and increased fuel consumption. Solution: Replace the faulty fuel injector. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * **Turbocharger failure:** Symptoms include loss of power, black smoke from the exhaust, and overheating. Solution: Replace the turbocharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Manual Transmission:**
    * **Synchro issues:** Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, especially when cold. Solution: Replace the faulty synchro. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **4-Speed Automatic Transmission:**
    * **Solenoid failure:** Symptoms include delayed gear shifting, harsh shifting, and transmission slipping. Solution: Replace the faulty solenoid. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * **Torque converter failure:** Symptoms include overheating, shuddering, and loss of power. Solution: Replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** Symptoms include dim headlights, flickering gauges, and difficulty starting the vehicle. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Battery discharge:** Symptoms include the vehicle failing to start after sitting for periods of time. Solution: Check for parasitic drains and replace the battery if necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Exterior**

* **Body panel gaps and misalignment:** Symptoms include uneven panel gaps and misalignment of body panels. Solution: Adjust and align the body panels. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Headlight condensation:** Symptoms include moisture inside the headlight assemblies. Solution: Replace the headlight assemblies or install anti-fog bulbs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Interior**

* **Squeaky dashboard:** Symptoms include squeaking noises coming from the dashboard. Solution: Apply foam or felt tape to the contact points. Estimated Cost to Fix: DIY or $100 - $200+
* **HVAC issues:** Symptoms include weak airflow, uneven temperature distribution, and malfunctioning climate controls. Solution: Diagnose the issue and replace the faulty components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
* **Window regulator failure:** Symptoms include difficulty opening or closing the windows. Solution: Replace the faulty window regulator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Summary**

The 2016 Suzuki Ciaz is a reliable and affordable compact sedan, but it has been reported to have some issues, particularly with its engine, transmission, and electrical system. These issues can vary in severity and cost to fix, but overall, they should not be a major concern for potential buyers.
