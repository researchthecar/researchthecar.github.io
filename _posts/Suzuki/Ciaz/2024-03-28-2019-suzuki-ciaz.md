**Introduction:**

The 2019 Suzuki Ciaz is a compact sedan known for its fuel efficiency, spacious interior, and affordable price. It is offered with a 1.5-liter naturally aspirated gasoline engine paired with either a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**1.5-Liter Gasoline Engine with 5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **No Issues Found**

**1.5-Liter Gasoline Engine with 4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Hesitation and Jerking During Acceleration**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle may hesitate or jerk during acceleration, especially at low speeds.
    * Solution: Replacement of the torque converter and transmission fluid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **No Issues Found**

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Slipping**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip or fail to engage properly, resulting in a loss of power.
    * Solution: Rebuild or replacement of the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Premature Wear of Front Suspension Components**
    * Description/Symptoms: The front suspension components, such as bushings, ball joints, and struts, may wear out prematurely, leading to noise and a decline in handling.
    * Solution: Replacement of the affected components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The vehicle's battery may drain over time, even when not in use.
    * Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the electrical system, often involving the replacement of a faulty component such as the alternator or battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Faulty Headlight Bulbs**
    * Description/Symptoms: The headlight bulbs may burn out prematurely or fail to illuminate properly.
    * Solution: Replacement of the affected bulbs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Cracked Dashboard**
    * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard may develop cracks or deform due to excessive heat exposure.
    * Solution: Replacement of the dashboard.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2019 Suzuki Ciaz generally receives positive feedback for its low fuel consumption, spacious interior, and reliability. However, it has been reported to have some issues, particularly with the 4-speed automatic transmission and front suspension components. The severity and cost of the issues can vary depending on the specific part and labor costs in the owner's location.
