The 2004 Suzuki Aerio is a compact hatchback available with two engine options: a 2.0-liter inline-four and a 2.3-liter V6. Both engines are paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Aerio has received positive reviews for its reliability, fuel efficiency, and spacious interior, but it has been criticized for its lack of performance and sluggish handling.

## Engine
### 2.0-liter Inline-Four
**Intake Manifold Gasket Leak**
Description/Symptoms: A faulty intake manifold gasket can cause a vacuum leak, resulting in rough idling, poor performance, and increased fuel consumption.
Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
Recalls: None Found

**Timing Belt Tensioner Failure**
Description/Symptoms: A failing timing belt tensioner can cause the timing belt to jump or snap, resulting in catastrophic engine damage.
Solution: Replace the timing belt tensioner and timing belt.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700
Recalls: 04V384000

## Transmission
**4-speed Automatic Transmission**
**Shift Solenoid Failure**
Description/Symptoms: A faulty shift solenoid can cause transmission shifting problems, such as delayed shifts, hard shifts, or slipping gears.
Solution: Replace the shift solenoid.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission Line Leak**
Description/Symptoms: A leak in the transmission lines can cause transmission fluid to leak out, resulting in transmission slipping and overheating.
Solution: Repair or replace the transmission line.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250
Recalls: None Found

## Interior
**Dashboard Rattle**
Description/Symptoms: The dashboard may rattle over rough roads due to loose mounting points.
Solution: Secure the dashboard by tightening or replacing the mounting points.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
Recalls: None Found

## Summary
The 2004 Suzuki Aerio is a reliable and fuel-efficient vehicle with a few minor issues. The most common engine issues are the intake manifold gasket leak and the timing belt tensioner failure. The transmission issues include the shift solenoid failure and the transmission line leak. Interior issues are mostly limited to dashboard rattles. Overall, the Aerio's issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix.
