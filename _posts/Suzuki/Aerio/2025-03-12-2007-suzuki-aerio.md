The 2007 Suzuki Aerio is a compact car available as a sedan or hatchback, known for its spacious interior and fuel efficiency. All 2007 Aerios were equipped with a 2.3L inline-four engine producing 155 horsepower and 152 lb-ft of torque, paired with either a 5-speed manual or a 4-speed automatic transmission. Owners often appreciate its affordability and practicality, while some criticisms include its somewhat dated styling and less refined driving experience compared to some competitors.

**Engine**

**2.3L Inline-Four**

*   **Timing Chain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive rattling noise from the engine, especially during startup or when the engine is cold. This can lead to poor engine performance and potential engine damage if left unattended.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oxygen Sensor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check Engine Light illumination, decreased fuel economy, rough idling, and potential stalling.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty oxygen sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Catalytic Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check Engine Light illumination, reduced engine performance, and potential failure to pass emissions testing.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the catalytic converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

*   **Delayed or Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noticeable delay when shifting between gears, harsh or jerky shifts, and potential slipping.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Mount Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive vibration, clunking noises during acceleration or deceleration, and potential movement of the shifter.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the transmission mount.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    *   Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Manual**
*   No Issues Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Struts/Shocks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, excessive body roll during turns, and potential noise over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the struts and/or shocks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Broken or Worn Coil Springs**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The vehicle is visibly sagging to one side, suspension noise, and a noticeable change in ride quality.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the broken or worn coil springs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Power Window Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or complete failure of the power windows to operate.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the power window motor or regulator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Door Lock Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inability to lock or unlock doors with the remote or door switch.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty door lock actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **ABS Sensor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: ABS light is illuminated on the dash, possibly combined with traction control light. Intermittent or permanent ABS failure.
    *   Solution: Replacement of faulty ABS sensor
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust Issues (Especially in areas with road salt)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust forming around wheel wells, rocker panels, and other body panels.
    *   Solution: Rust repair or body panel replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+ (depending on severity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracking of the dashboard, especially in sun-exposed areas.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the dashboard.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2007 Suzuki Aerio, while generally reliable for its age, is prone to a few common issues. Engine-related problems like timing chain issues and oxygen sensor failure can be costly. The automatic transmission can also have issues related to harsh shifting. Electrical problems like power window and door lock actuator failures are also common. Owners should be aware of potential rust issues, especially in colder climates. The overall severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to more significant repairs, with costs varying accordingly. Buyers should inspect the vehicle carefully and address any identified issues promptly.
