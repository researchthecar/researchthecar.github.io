**Introduction**

The 2017 Suzuki Grand Vitara is a compact SUV powered by a 2.4L I-4 engine producing 166 horsepower and 162 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 6-speed manual or 7-speed automatic transmission. The Grand Vitara is praised for its off-road capability, spacious interior, and value for money. However, it has received criticism for its outdated design, sluggish performance, and poor fuel economy.

**Engine**

**2.4L I-4**

* **Timing Chain Failure:** The timing chain can stretch or break prematurely, causing catastrophic engine damage. Symptoms include rattling noises, power loss, and illuminated check engine light. Solution involves replacing the timing chain, guides, and tensioners. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
* **Valve Stem Seal Leak:** Worn valve stem seals can cause excessive oil consumption and smoking from the exhaust. Symptoms include blue smoke on startup or acceleration, and low oil levels. Solution involves replacing the valve stem seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Failure:** The clutch can wear out prematurely, leading to difficulty shifting gears or slipping. Symptoms include grinding noises, difficulty engaging gears, and loss of power. Solution involves replacing the clutch assembly. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+

**7-Speed Automatic**

* **Shift Solenoid Failure:** A malfunctioning shift solenoid can cause harsh shifting, delayed gear engagement, or slipping. Symptoms include transmission slipping or clunking during shifting. Solution involves replacing the faulty solenoid. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Torque Converter Failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing shuddering, overheating, or loss of power. Symptoms include vibrations, slipping, and difficulty accelerating. Solution involves rebuilding or replacing the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Suspension**

* **Rear Shock Absorber Failure:** The rear shock absorbers can wear out prematurely, leading to poor ride quality and handling. Symptoms include bouncing, excessive body roll, and instability. Solution involves replacing the rear shock absorbers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Front Strut Mount Failure:** The front strut mounts can fail, causing knocking noises, vibrations, and poor handling. Symptoms include rattling sounds over bumps or during turning. Solution involves replacing the strut mounts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, leading to loss of electrical power and battery drain. Symptoms include dim headlights, flickering interior lights, and difficulty starting the vehicle. Solution involves replacing the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
* **Starter Relay Failure:** The starter relay can fail, preventing the starter from engaging and starting the engine. Symptoms include a clicking sound when turning the key, but no engine crank. Solution involves replacing the starter relay. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Failure:** The door handles can become loose or break, making it difficult to open or close the doors. Symptoms include loose handles, difficulty opening or closing the doors, and rattling sounds. Solution involves replacing the door handle assemblies. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Cracked Windshield:** The windshield can crack or chip easily, especially if impacted by road debris. Symptoms include cracks or chips in the glass, reduced visibility, and potential leaks. Solution involves replacing the windshield. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattle:** The dashboard can rattle or creak over bumps or rough roads. Symptoms include annoying noises coming from the dashboard area. Solution involves identifying loose or poorly fitted components and securing or replacing them. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
* **Broken Seat Adjuster:** The seat adjuster can break or become loose, making it difficult to adjust the seat position. Symptoms include inability to adjust the seat, loose or wobbly seats, and discomfort. Solution involves repairing or replacing the seat adjuster mechanism. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Summary**

The 2017 Suzuki Grand Vitara has several known issues, primarily related to its engine, transmission, and suspension. The timing chain failure is a serious concern that can lead to catastrophic engine damage. The other issues range from minor annoyances to potentially costly repairs. While the Grand Vitara offers good value for money and off-road capability, potential buyers should be aware of these issues and factor in the potential costs of repairs.
