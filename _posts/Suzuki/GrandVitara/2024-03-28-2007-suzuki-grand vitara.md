**Introduction:**

The 2007 Suzuki Grand Vitara is a compact SUV that offers both on- and off-road capabilities. It's powered by a 2.7-liter V6 engine that produces 185 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque. The Grand Vitara comes standard with a five-speed manual transmission, but a four-speed automatic is available.

People like the Grand Vitara for its rugged design, spacious interior, and affordable price. However, some owners have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, and other components.

**Engine**

* **2.7-liter V6 Engine:**
    * **Oil Leaks:** Oil may leak from the engine's valve cover gaskets or oil pan gasket.
        * Solution: Replace the faulty gaskets.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** The timing chain tensioner can fail, causing the timing chain to skip or break.
        * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner and timing chain.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing air and coolant leaks.
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **Five-Speed Manual Transmission:**
    * **Shifting Difficulties:** The transmission may be difficult to shift, especially when cold.
        * Solution: Replace the transmission fluid and shift linkage.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
* **Four-Speed Automatic Transmission:**
    * **Slipping Gears:** The transmission may slip gears, especially when under load.
        * Solution: Replace the faulty clutches or bands.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension:**
    * **Strut Failure:** The front struts may fail, causing the vehicle to bounce or bottom out.
        * Solution: Replace the front struts.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** The battery may drain prematurely, especially when the vehicle is parked.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty battery or alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** The body may rust prematurely, especially in areas exposed to road salt.
    * Solution: Apply anti-rust treatment or replace the affected body panels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**Interior**

* **Plastic Trim Cracking:** The plastic trim pieces in the interior may crack or break.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty trim pieces.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2007 Suzuki Grand Vitara has been plagued by a number of issues, including engine leaks, transmission problems, and suspension failures. These issues can be costly to repair, especially if they are not addressed promptly. However, the Grand Vitara is still a popular choice for buyers who are looking for a rugged and affordable SUV.
