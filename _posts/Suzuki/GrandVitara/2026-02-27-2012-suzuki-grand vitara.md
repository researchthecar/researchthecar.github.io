## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Power Window Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows may become slow, inoperative, or make grinding noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the power window motor and regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Premature battery discharge, requiring frequent jump starts or battery replacement.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify parasitic draw from components like the radio, alarm system, or control modules, followed by repair or replacement of faulty components.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Suzuki Grand Vitara generally exhibits good mechanical reliability, with its powertrain and chassis components proving robust for most owners. The most commonly cited issues tend to be in the electrical system, specifically related to power window actuators and occasional battery drain problems, which are not uncommon for vehicles of this age. These electrical faults are typically repairable with moderate cost. Overall, the Grand Vitara is a dependable choice for those prioritizing off-road capability and a sturdy build, provided attention is paid to potential electrical quirks.
