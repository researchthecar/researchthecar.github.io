**Introduction:**

The 2002 Suzuki Grand Vitara is a compact SUV known for its ruggedness, off-road capabilities, and value for money. It was available with two engine options and a choice between a manual or automatic transmission. The 2.5L V6 engine produces 155 horsepower and 162 lb-ft of torque, while the 2.7L V6 engine produces 185 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque. The Grand Vitara has received mixed reviews, with users praising its off-road performance but criticizing its dated interior and lack of modern features.

**Engine**

* **2.5L V6**
    * **Intake camshaft adjuster failure:** Symptoms include engine knocking or ticking noise, reduced power, and increased fuel consumption. Solution: Replace the intake camshaft adjuster. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * **Cylinder head gasket failure:** Symptoms include coolant leaks, overheating, and white smoke from the exhaust. Solution: Replace the cylinder head gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * **Valve cover gasket leak:** Symptoms include oil leaks from the valve cover area. Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **2.7L V6**
    * **No major issues found**

**Transmission**

* **4-speed automatic:**
    * **Transmission fluid leaks:** Symptoms include fluid leaks from the transmission pan or other parts of the transmission. Solution: Replace the leaking component or rebuild the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * **Shifting problems:** Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, delayed engagement, or slipping gears. Solution: Replace or repair the transmission solenoids, valve body, or other internal components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
* **5-speed manual:**
    * **No major issues found**

**Suspension**

* **Front struts:**
    * **Strut mount bearing failure:** Symptoms include clunking or rattling noises from the front suspension, especially during turns or over bumps. Solution: Replace the strut mount bearing. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per side
* **Rear shock absorbers:**
    * **Premature wear:** Symptoms include excessive bouncing or swaying of the rear end, especially over bumps or uneven surfaces. Solution: Replace the rear shock absorbers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600 per pair
* **Sway bar links:**
    * **Broken or loose:** Symptoms include clunking or rattling noises from the front or rear suspension, especially over bumps or uneven surfaces. Solution: Replace the broken or loose sway bar links. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 per side

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** Symptoms include dimming or flickering lights, battery warning light on, or difficulty starting the vehicle. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
* **Battery drain:** Symptoms include a dead battery after sitting for a period of time. Solution: Find and repair the source of the battery drain, which could be a faulty electrical component or parasitic draw. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
* **Fuse box failures:** Symptoms include various electrical functions not working, such as lighting, power windows, or audio system. Solution: Check and replace any blown fuses in the fuse box. Estimated Cost to Fix: $10 - $100

**Exterior**

* **Door locks:**
    * **Door lock actuator failure:** Symptoms include difficulty locking or unlocking the doors, or power locks not working. Solution: Replace the faulty door lock actuator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per door
* **Window regulators:**
    * **Broken or damaged window regulator:** Symptoms include difficulty raising or lowering the windows, or windows getting stuck. Solution: Replace the broken or damaged window regulator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per window

**Interior**

* **Climate control:**
    * **Blend door actuator failure:** Symptoms include difficulty adjusting the temperature or airflow from the climate control system. Solution: Replace the blend door actuator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **Dashboard lights:**
    * **Dashboard lights flickering or not working:** Symptoms include dim or flickering dashboard lights, or complete failure of the dashboard lights. Solution: Replace or repair the faulty wiring, bulbs, or dashboard components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+
* **Power seat motors:**
    * **Power seat motor failure:** Symptoms include difficulty adjusting the power seats, or seats not moving at all. Solution: Replace the faulty power seat motor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per seat

**Summary**

The 2002 Suzuki Grand Vitara is a reliable and capable SUV with a few common issues that can affect its performance, safety, and comfort. While engine and transmission problems are rare, the vehicle is prone to suspension issues, electrical problems, and minor interior malfunctions. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific issue, with some requiring significant repairs and others being relatively inexpensive to fix.
