**2015 Suzuki Grand Vitara**

The 2015 Suzuki Grand Vitara is a compact SUV that was available with a 2.4-liter 4-cylinder engine and a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission. It produced 166 horsepower and 162 lb-ft of torque, and was praised for its off-road capabilities and fuel economy. However, it was also criticized for its lack of power and dated interior.

**Engine**

**2.4-liter 4-cylinder**

**Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner may fail, causing the timing chain to skip or break. This can lead to catastrophic engine damage.
Solution: Replace the timing chain and tensioner.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-speed Manual**

**No Issues Found**

**4-speed Automatic**

**Slipping Gears**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip gears, especially when shifting into higher gears.
Solution: Replace or rebuild the transmission.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
Recalls: None Found

**No Issues Found**

**Exterior**

**Rear Wiper Motor Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The rear wiper motor may fail, causing the rear wiper to stop working.
Solution: Replace the rear wiper motor.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Air Conditioning Compressor Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The air conditioning compressor may fail, causing the air conditioning to stop working.
Solution: Replace the air conditioning compressor.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Suzuki Grand Vitara is a generally reliable vehicle with some known issues. The most severe issue is the timing chain tensioner failure, which can lead to catastrophic engine damage. Other issues include slipping gears in the automatic transmission, alternator failure, and rear wiper motor failure. The cost of repairs can be significant, especially for the more severe issues.
