**2016 Suzuki Grand Vitara**

The 2016 Suzuki Grand Vitara is a compact SUV that is no longer in production. It comes standard with a 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine that produces 165 horsepower and 162 lb-ft of torque. It is available with either a five-speed manual or a four-speed automatic transmission.

People like the Grand Vitara for its rugged off-road capabilities, spacious interior, and low price point. However, it is also known for its poor fuel economy and lack of modern features.

**Engine**

**2.4-liter Four-Cylinder**

* **Cylinder Head Corrosion**
    * Description/Symptoms: Corrosion can occur on the cylinder head, leading to coolant leaks, overheating, and eventually engine failure.
    * Solution: The cylinder head must be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Noise**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain can stretch or break, causing rattling or whining noises.
    * Solution: The timing chain and related components must be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Five-Speed Manual**

* **Transmission Fluid Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaks can occur from the transmission pan, seals, or lines.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Difficulty Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Gears may be difficult to shift or may pop out of gear.
    * Solution: Repairs or replacement of the shifter assembly or transmission may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **Grinding Noises**
    * Description/Symptoms: Grinding noises may be heard when shifting or accelerating.
    * Solution: Transmission may need to be overhauled or replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Four-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Control Module (TCM) Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The TCM may fail, causing the transmission to malfunction.
    * Solution: Replace the TCM.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Valve Body Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The valve body may fail, leading to slipping gears or other transmission problems.
    * Solution: Replace the valve body.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Front Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Bearing Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The strut mount bearings can wear out, causing clunking or rattling noises when turning or going over bumps.
    * Solution: Replace the strut mount bearings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Rear Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Corrosion**
    * Description/Symptoms: The leaf springs can corrode, leading to sagging or a rough ride.
    * Solution: Replace the leaf springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator may fail, causing the battery to drain and the vehicle to stall.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Wiring Harness Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The wiring harness may be damaged or faulty, causing electrical problems such as flickering lights, non-working gauges, or starting issues.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the wiring harness.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust and Corrosion**
    * Description/Symptoms: The body and frame of the Grand Vitara are prone to rust and corrosion, especially in areas with harsh winters.
    * Solution: Rustproofing, paint touch-ups, or body repairs may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Window Regulator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The window regulators may fail, causing the windows to become stuck or operate slowly.
    * Solution: Replace the window regulators.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control system may malfunction, causing the heater or air conditioner to not work properly.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the climate control module or other components may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Dashboard Cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard may crack or warp over time.
    * Solution: Replacement of the dashboard may be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Suzuki Grand Vitara is a reliable and capable SUV, but it does have its share of issues. The most common problems are related to the engine, transmission, and suspension. While some of the issues can be minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more severe and costly. Potential owners should be aware of these issues before purchasing a Grand Vitara.
