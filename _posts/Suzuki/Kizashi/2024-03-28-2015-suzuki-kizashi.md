**2015 Suzuki Kizashi**

The 2015 Suzuki Kizashi is a midsize sedan offered with a single 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine rated at 185 horsepower and 170 lb-ft of torque paired exclusively to a continuously variable transmission (CVT). Praised for its sharp handling, comfortable interior, and spacious trunk, it has some drawbacks, including its lackluster powertrain and limited rear visibility.

**Interior**

- **Heated and Ventilated Seats**
  - Description/Symptoms: The heating and ventilation function of the heated and ventilated seats may not work properly, resulting in discomfort for passengers.
  - Solution: Depending on the cause of the issue, the seat assembly may need to be inspected, replaced, or repaired.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

- **Power Windows**
  - Description/Symptoms: The power windows may fail to operate correctly, leading to difficulty opening or closing the windows.
  - Solution: The window regulator or motor may need to be inspected and repaired or replaced.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Suzuki Kizashi seems to be a generally reliable vehicle. While there are some reported issues with its interior electronics, particularly the heated and ventilated seats and power windows, these appear to be isolated cases. Overall, the cost of repairs for these issues is relatively low to moderate, making the Kizashi a cost-effective option in its class.
