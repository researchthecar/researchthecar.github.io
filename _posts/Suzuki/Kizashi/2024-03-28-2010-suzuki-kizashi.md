**Introduction**

The 2010 Suzuki Kizashi is a midsize sedan that offers a sporty driving experience with its 2.4-liter, 185-horsepower engine and 6-speed manual or CVT transmission. It also comes with a well-equipped interior and a sleek exterior design. While the Kizashi has been praised for its handling and performance, some owners have reported experiencing various issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

* **2.4L Engine**
    * **Oil Leaks from Camshaft Seals:** Oil can leak from the camshaft seals, causing oil levels to drop and potentially leading to engine damage.
    * **Intake Valve Deposits:** Carbon deposits can build up on the intake valves, reducing engine performance and causing rough idling.
    * **Exhaust Manifold Gasket Failure:** The exhaust manifold gasket can fail, causing exhaust leaks and reduced engine power.
        * Solution: Replace the gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250-$500
        * Recalls: None Found
* **2.0L Engine (Optional)**
    * No Issues Found

**Transmission**

* **6-speed Manual Transmission**
    * **Clutch Master Cylinder Failure:** The clutch master cylinder can fail, causing difficulty shifting gears or preventing shifting altogether.
        * Solution: Replace the clutch master cylinder.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $350-$600
    * **Transmission Gear Grind:** Gears may grind during shifting, especially when the transmission is cold.
        * Solution: Replace the transmission oil or adjust the shift linkage.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$250
* **CVT Transmission**
    * No Issues Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Noise:** The front suspension can make noise over bumps or uneven road surfaces.
    * Solution: Replace the worn suspension components (e.g., struts, shocks, bushings).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400-$800
* **Rear Suspension Sagging:** The rear suspension may sag over time, causing uneven tire wear and reduced handling.
    * Solution: Replace the rear springs or struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250-$500

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** The battery may drain quickly, even when the car is parked.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty battery or alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150-$300
* **Headlight Malfunction:** The headlights may flicker or fail prematurely.
    * Solution: Replace the headlight bulbs or repair the wiring.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$300

**Exterior**

* **Door Handles Sticking:** The door handles may become sticky or difficult to operate.
    * Solution: Lubricate or replace the door handle assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$200
* **Windshield Wipers Not Working:** The windshield wipers may malfunction or stop working altogether.
    * Solution: Replace the wiper motor or adjust the linkage.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150-$300

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Creaks or Rattles:** The dashboard may make creaking or rattling noises, especially over rough roads.
    * Solution: Tighten the dashboard components or add sound dampening material.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$250
* **Heater Fan Malfunction:** The heater fan may fail to blow air, or it may blow air at an incorrect temperature.
    * Solution: Replace the heater fan motor or repair the wiring.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$400

**Summary**

Overall, the 2010 Suzuki Kizashi has received mixed reviews from owners. While it offers a sporty driving experience and a comfortable interior, it has been plagued by a number of issues, including engine oil leaks, transmission problems, and electrical malfunctions. The severity of the issues varies, but some can be costly to repair. It is important for potential buyers to be aware of these potential problems before purchasing a Kizashi.
