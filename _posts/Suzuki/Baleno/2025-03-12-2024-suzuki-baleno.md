The 2024 Suzuki Baleno is a subcompact hatchback known for its fuel efficiency and affordability. Depending on the market, engine options typically include a 1.2-liter four-cylinder petrol engine, which produces around 82 horsepower and 83 lb-ft of torque. Some models also feature a mild-hybrid system to improve fuel economy. Available transmissions are usually a 5-speed manual or an automatic transmission, depending on the market. Owners often praise the Baleno for its spacious interior, smooth ride, and easy handling, while some common criticisms include its basic interior materials and engine performance.

**Engine**

**1.2L Four-Cylinder Petrol Engine**

*   **Excessive Engine Vibration at Idle**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report experiencing excessive engine vibration, particularly when the car is idling. This can be felt through the steering wheel and seats.
    *   Solution: A software update to the engine control unit (ECU) can sometimes mitigate the issue. If the problem persists, a mechanic should inspect the engine mounts, fuel injectors, and spark plugs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Loss of Power/Hesitation During Acceleration**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report a noticeable lag or hesitation when accelerating, especially at lower speeds. This can make merging onto highways or overtaking other vehicles difficult.
    *   Solution: Inspect the Mass Airflow Sensor (MAF) for debris or damage. Also, check the fuel filter and fuel pump for proper operation. It might be a good idea to check the Throttle Position Sensor (TPS)
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**
*   **Difficulty Shifting into Gears**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some drivers have reported difficulty shifting smoothly into certain gears, particularly first and second. This can be accompanied by a grinding noise.
    *   Solution: Inspect the clutch cable (if applicable) or hydraulic system for proper adjustment and leaks. The transmission fluid level and condition should also be checked. If the problem persists, the synchros inside the transmission may be worn and require replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Automatic Transmission**
*   **Jerky Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced jerky or abrupt shifts between gears, as well as hesitation when accelerating. This is more apparent during stop-and-go traffic conditions.
    *   Solution: Begin by checking the transmission fluid level and condition. If the fluid is low or contaminated, a fluid flush and filter replacement may be necessary. If the problem persists, a mechanic should diagnose the transmission control module (TCM) and solenoids for potential issues.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Excessive Body Roll**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have noted that the vehicle exhibits noticeable body roll when cornering, especially at higher speeds. This can make the car feel less stable and confident in turns.
    *   Solution: Upgrading the vehicle's suspension components, such as the sway bars, shocks, or springs, can help reduce body roll.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may occasionally freeze, crash, or become unresponsive. This can affect access to navigation, audio, and other features.
    *   Solution: A software update from the dealership can often resolve these issues. If the problem persists, the head unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing a dead battery, especially if the car sits for an extended period.
    *   Solution: Have the battery tested for its ability to hold a charge. Also, check for any parasitic drains that may be caused by faulty wiring or aftermarket accessories.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Rattling Noises from Dashboard/Door Panels**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have complained about rattling noises emanating from the dashboard or door panels, especially when driving on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Tightening loose screws or fasteners and adding padding or insulation can help reduce these noises.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**
The 2024 Suzuki Baleno has relatively few major issues reported, with most complaints centering around minor mechanical and electrical problems. The issues are usually minor or a software issue that requires a quick update. There is also the possibility it could be a sensor, such as the MAS or TPS. Suspension issues are also common, as it lacks higher end suspension components.
