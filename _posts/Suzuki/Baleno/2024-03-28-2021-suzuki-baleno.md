**Overview**

The 2021 Suzuki Baleno is a subcompact hatchback powered by a 1.4L inline-four engine paired with a 5-speed manual or a 4-speed automatic transmission. It offers 91 horsepower and 98 lb-ft of torque. While it offers ample interior space and a comfortable ride, it faces criticism for its lackluster engine performance and dated interior design.

**Engine**

**1.4L Inline-Four**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Description: High oil consumption, leading to engine oil starvation and potential engine damage. Symptoms: Blue smoke from the exhaust, low oil levels, and frequent oil changes. Solution: Replace faulty piston rings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
* **Timing Chain Rattle:** Description: Noise from the timing chain due to excessive wear or a faulty tensioner. Symptoms: Rattle or ticking noise from the engine at startup or acceleration. Solution: Replace the timing chain or tensioner. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Transmission**

**5-speed Manual**

* **Grinding Gears:** Description: Difficult shifting gears, accompanied by a grinding noise. Symptoms: Difficulty engaging gears, especially when downshifting. Solution: Replace faulty synchronizers or bearings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+

**4-speed Automatic**

* **Delayed Gear Shifts:** Description: Transmission delays in shifting gears, causing hesitation or jerkiness. Symptoms: Slow acceleration and inconsistent shifting patterns. Solution: Update transmission software or replace faulty solenoids. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Description: Battery discharges quickly when the vehicle is parked. Symptoms: Difficulty starting the vehicle, dim headlights, and electrical failures. Solution: Replace faulty battery or alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+
* **Electrical Shorts:** Description: Faulty wiring or connections causing electrical malfunctions. Symptoms: Blown fuses, flickering lights, or random warning lights on the dashboard. Solution: Inspect and repair faulty wiring or connections. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Malfunction:** Description: Door handles not opening or closing properly. Symptoms: Difficulty opening or closing doors, or handles breaking off. Solution: Replace faulty door handles or latches. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattle:** Description: Noise or vibration from the dashboard while driving. Symptoms: Buzzing or rattle noise coming from the dashboard. Solution: Tighten loose panels or replace faulty clips. Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
* **Seatbelt Pretensioner Failure:** Description: Seatbelt pretensioners failing to activate in a collision. Symptoms: No seatbelt retraction or a sudden jolt after a collision. Solution: Replace faulty seatbelt pretensioners. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Air Conditioning Malfunction:** Description: Air conditioning system blowing warm air or not functioning properly. Symptoms: Weak or non-existent airflow, abnormal noises from the compressor. Solution: Recharge refrigerant, replace faulty compressor, or repair electrical issues. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Summary**

The 2021 Suzuki Baleno faces several common issues, particularly concerning its engine, transmission, and interior. Engine oil consumption and timing chain rattle are major concerns that could lead to costly repairs. Transmission problems can also affect the vehicle's reliability. While electrical and exterior issues are less severe, they can be frustrating and inconvenient. Overall, the Baleno's reliability issues are likely to deter potential buyers, especially considering its lackluster performance and dated interior.
