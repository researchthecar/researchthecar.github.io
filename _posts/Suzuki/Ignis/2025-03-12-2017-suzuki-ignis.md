The 2017 Suzuki Ignis is a subcompact hatchback known for its fuel efficiency, nimble handling, and distinctive styling. Engine options for this year included a 1.2-liter four-cylinder petrol engine producing around 89 horsepower and 88 lb-ft of torque. It was available with a 5-speed manual transmission or a CVT automatic transmission. The Ignis is generally liked for its affordability and ease of parking, but some dislike its small size and relatively basic interior.

**Engine**

**1.2L Four-Cylinder Petrol Engine**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher than expected oil consumption, requiring more frequent top-ups between scheduled services. Symptoms include the oil warning light illuminating frequently.
    *   Solution: Regular monitoring of oil levels and topping up as needed. In severe cases, an engine inspection and potential repair or replacement of piston rings may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix:
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Engine Misfires**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The engine may experience occasional misfires, leading to rough idling and reduced power. This could be caused by faulty ignition coils or spark plugs.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the faulty component (ignition coil or spark plug) and replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

*   **Difficulty Shifting into Gear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have experienced difficulty shifting into certain gears, particularly first or reverse. This can be caused by worn synchros or a misadjusted clutch cable.
    *   Solution: Adjustment of the clutch cable or replacement of the worn synchros.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ (depending on the extent of internal damage)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**CVT Automatic Transmission**

*   **Jerky Acceleration**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported a jerky or hesitant acceleration, especially at lower speeds. This can be caused by CVT transmission control issues.
    *   Solution: CVT fluid change, or replacement of the CVT transmission control module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Wear of Suspension Components**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have noted that components like the shock absorbers and struts wear out more quickly than expected, leading to a bouncy ride and reduced handling.
    *   Solution: Replacement of worn shocks/struts and inspection of other suspension components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+ (depending on the extent of replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain quickly, especially if the car is not driven frequently. This can be caused by a parasitic drain from an electrical component or a faulty battery.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of the electrical system to identify the source of the drain, or replacement of the battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250+ (depending on the cause and if battery replacement is needed)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Infotainment System Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may experience occasional glitches, such as freezing, failing to connect to Bluetooth, or issues with the touchscreen.
    *   Solution: Software update or replacement of the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (if a software update fixes the issue) to $500+ (for replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rusting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported finding rust spots on body panels.
    *   Solution: Rust repair or panel replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners have complained about rattling noises coming from the dashboard or door panels.
    *   Solution: Tighten loose panels or components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Suzuki Ignis, while generally reliable, has a few common issues. The most notable potential problems revolve around engine oil consumption and transmission performance (particularly with the CVT). Electrical issues and interior rattles are also reported. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to potentially costly repairs, with costs varying significantly depending on the specific problem and its cause. Addressing these issues promptly is recommended to ensure the longevity and reliability of the vehicle.
