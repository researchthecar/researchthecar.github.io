**Introduction**
The 2021 Suzuki Ignis is a compact crossover SUV known for its affordability, fuel efficiency, and compact size. It comes with a 1.2-liter gasoline engine paired with either a 5-speed manual transmission or a continuously variable transmission (CVT). Buyers can opt for a 1.0-liter inline-3 turbo engine with a 6-speed automatic transmission. The Ignis has received praise for its off-road capabilities, maneuverability, and spacious interior for its size. However, it is criticized for its lack of power, especially on the highway, and its outdated interior design.

**Engine**
- 1.2-liter gasoline engine with 5-speed manual transmission
  - **Timing chain tensioner failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner can fail prematurely, leading to a rattling sound from the engine, poor performance, and possible catastrophic engine damage.
    - Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner and related components.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    - Recalls: None Found
- 1.2-liter gasoline engine with CVT
  - **CVT malfunction**
    - Description/Symptoms: The CVT may exhibit slipping, hesitation, or shuddering during acceleration or gear changes.
    - Solution: Replace or overhaul the CVT.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $4000+
    - Recalls: None Found
- 1.0-liter inline-3 turbo engine with 6-speed automatic transmission
  - No Issues Found

**Transmission**
- 5-speed manual transmission
  - No Issues Found
- CVT
  - See issues under Engine section
- 6-speed automatic transmission
  - **Torque converter shudder**
    - Description/Symptoms: The torque converter may shudder or vibrate during acceleration or gear changes.
    - Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500+
    - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
- **Rear suspension bushing failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: The rear suspension bushings can wear out prematurely, leading to a knocking or rattling sound from the rear of the vehicle, especially over bumps and rough roads.
  - Solution: Replace the rear suspension bushings.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
  - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
- **Battery drain**
  - Description/Symptoms: The battery may discharge prematurely, causing difficulty starting the vehicle or a complete loss of power.
  - Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace the cause of the battery drain, such as faulty electrical components or a weak battery.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
- **Roof rail rust**
  - Description/Symptoms: The roof rails may develop rust and corrosion over time.
  - Solution: Inspect and clean the roof rails regularly, and apply rust protection products as needed.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: N/A (maintenance)
  - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
- **Instrument cluster malfunction**
  - Description/Symptoms: The instrument cluster may experience intermittent failures or display incorrect information.
  - Solution: Replace the instrument cluster.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Climate control issues**
  - Description/Symptoms: The climate control system may not function properly, such as failing to adjust the temperature or blow air through the vents.
  - Solution: Diagnose and repair or replace faulty components in the climate control system.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2021 Suzuki Ignis has a few reported issues, mainly affecting the engine, transmission, and suspension. The severity of these issues can range from minor inconveniences to potential safety hazards. The cost to fix these problems can vary depending on the specific issue and the severity of the damage. It's important to note that these issues may not affect all vehicles and that regular maintenance can help prevent or identify potential problems early on.
