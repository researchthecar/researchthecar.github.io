**Introduction:**
The 1999 Suzuki Esteem is a compact sedan known for its affordability, fuel efficiency, and responsive handling. Engine options include a 1.6L inline-4 producing 97 horsepower and 98 lb-ft of torque, and a 1.8L inline-4 producing 121 horsepower and 120 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual and a 4-speed automatic.

**Engine**
**1.6L Inline-4**
* **Oil Pressure Sensor Failure:** Causes low oil pressure readings, resulting in reduced engine performance.
* Solution: Replace oil pressure sensor.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300

**1.8L Inline-4**
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:** Causes a loss of vacuum, resulting in poor engine performance and increased emissions.
* Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Transmission**
**5-Speed Manual**
* **Clutch Master Cylinder Failure:** Causes a loss of hydraulic pressure, resulting in a spongy or inoperable clutch pedal.
* Solution: Replace clutch master cylinder.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
* **Input Shaft Bearing Failure:** Causes grinding noises and difficulty shifting gears.
* Solution: Replace input shaft bearing.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+

**4-Speed Automatic**
* **Solenoid Pack Failure:** Causes harsh shifting, slipping, or no engagement in gears.
* Solution: Replace solenoid pack.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+

**Suspension**
* **Front Lower Control Arm Bushing Wear:** Causes clunking noises, reduced handling, and premature tire wear.
* Solution: Replace front lower control arm bushings.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300

**Electrical**
* **Battery Drain:** Caused by a parasitic drain on the battery, resulting in difficulty starting or a dead battery.
* Solution: Identify and repair source of parasitic drain.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+

**Exterior**
* **Door Handle Cable Failure:** Causes difficulty opening doors.
* Solution: Replace door handle cable.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200

**Interior**
* **Power Window Motor Failure:** Causes one or more power windows to stop working.
* Solution: Replace power window motor.
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300

**Summary:**
The 1999 Suzuki Esteem has a few common issues to watch out for, primarily related to the engine, transmission, and suspension. The severity of the issues can range from minor inconveniences to major repairs. Repair costs can vary widely depending on the specific issue and the availability of parts. Overall, the Esteem is a reliable vehicle, but potential buyers should be aware of these issues to make an informed decision.
