**Introduction:**

The 2004 Suzuki Forenza is a compact sedan offered with two engine and transmission options: a 2.0L inline-4 with a 5-speed manual transmission or a 5-speed automatic transmission, and a 2.3L V6 with a 5-speed manual transmission or a 5-speed automatic transmission. The 2.0L engine produces 125 horsepower and 133 lb-ft of torque, while the 2.3L engine produces 149 horsepower and 156 lb-ft of torque. Users praise the Forenza for its affordability, spacious interior, and fuel efficiency, while some dislike its bland styling.

**Engine**

* **2.0L Inline-4**
    * **Excessive Oil Consumption:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Engine burns excessive oil, causing low oil levels and potential engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace the engine with a revised version or perform major engine repairs.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
        * Recalls: 04V503000
    * **Timing Chain Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Failure of the timing chain can cause engine timing issues, resulting in engine misfires, stalling, or catastrophic engine failure.
        * Solution: Replace the timing chain and related components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **2.3L V6**
    * **Engine Valve Sticking:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Engine valves stick in the cylinder head, causing reduced engine power, misfires, or potential engine damage.
        * Solution: Replace the cylinder head or perform major engine repairs.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **Gear Grinding:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission gears grind or pop out of gear during shifting, particularly in lower gears.
        * Solution: Replace or repair the faulty transmission components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **5-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Transmission Slippage:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slips out of gear or fails to engage smoothly, causing poor acceleration and potential safety issues.
        * Solution: Repair or replace the transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Noise:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling or clunking noises from the front suspension, especially over bumps or rough roads.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty suspension components, such as struts or control arms.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Alternator fails to charge the battery, leading to electrical system problems, such as dim lights, difficulty starting, and potential stalling.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Exterior door handles break or become loose, making it difficult to open the doors.
    * Solution: Replace the door handles.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
    * Recalls: 04V512000

**Interior**

* **Center Console Buttons Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Buttons on the center console (e.g., window controls, climate controls) malfunction or become unresponsive.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty console or repair the electrical connections.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2004 Suzuki Forenza has some significant issues, particularly with its engine and transmission. Some of these issues can be expensive to repair and may require major engine or transmission replacement. Other issues, such as exterior and interior problems, are less severe and less costly to fix. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these issues and factor them into their decision before purchasing a used 2004 Suzuki Forenza.
