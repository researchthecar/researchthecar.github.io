The 2007 Suzuki SX4 is a compact crossover SUV that is available with a choice of two engines and two transmissions. The base engine is a 2.0-liter four-cylinder that produces 143 horsepower and 136 lb-ft of torque. The optional engine is a 2.4-liter four-cylinder that produces 165 horsepower and 162 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are available with either a five-speed manual transmission or a four-speed automatic transmission.

People like the SX4 for its affordability, fuel efficiency, and versatility. However, some common problems that have been reported with the vehicle include:

**Engine**

**2.0L four-cylinder**
- **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing a vacuum leak and a loss of power. The symptoms of this issue include a rough idle, a decrease in fuel economy, and a check engine light. To fix this issue, the intake manifold gasket must be replaced. The estimated cost of this repair is $200-$300.
- **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** The timing chain tensioner can fail, causing the timing chain to skip and the engine to lose power. The symptoms of this issue include a rattling noise from the engine, a loss of power, and a check engine light. To fix this issue, the timing chain tensioner must be replaced. The estimated cost of this repair is $800-$1000.
- **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** The valve cover gasket can leak, causing oil to leak out of the engine. The symptoms of this issue include an oil leak on the ground, a low oil level, and a check engine light. To fix this issue, the valve cover gasket must be replaced. The estimated cost of this repair is $100-$200.

**2.4L four-cylinder**
- **Crankshaft Position Sensor Failure:** The crankshaft position sensor can fail, causing the engine to lose spark and die. The symptoms of this issue include a no-start condition, a loss of power, and a check engine light. To fix this issue, the crankshaft position sensor must be replaced. The estimated cost of this repair is $100-$200.
- **Catalytic Converter Failure:** The catalytic converter can fail, causing the engine to lose power and run poorly. The symptoms of this issue include a loss of power, a decrease in fuel economy, and a check engine light. To fix this issue, the catalytic converter must be replaced. The estimated cost of this repair is $800-$1000.
- **Exhaust Manifold Gasket Leak:** The exhaust manifold gasket can leak, causing a vacuum leak and a loss of power. The symptoms of this issue include a rough idle, a decrease in fuel economy, and a check engine light. To fix this issue, the exhaust manifold gasket must be replaced. The estimated cost of this repair is $200-$300.

**Transmission**

**Five-speed manual transmission**
- **Clutch Master Cylinder Failure:** The clutch master cylinder can fail, causing the clutch to become inoperable. The symptoms of this issue include a soft or spongy clutch pedal, a loss of power, and a check engine light. To fix this issue, the clutch master cylinder must be replaced. The estimated cost of this repair is $200-$300.
- **Input Shaft Bearing Failure:** The input shaft bearing can fail, causing the transmission to make a grinding noise. The symptoms of this issue include a grinding noise when shifting gears, a loss of power, and a check engine light. To fix this issue, the input shaft bearing must be replaced. The estimated cost of this repair is $500-$700.
- **Output Shaft Seal Leak:** The output shaft seal can leak, causing fluid to leak out of the transmission. The symptoms of this issue include a fluid leak on the ground, a low fluid level, and a check engine light. To fix this issue, the output shaft seal must be replaced. The estimated cost of this repair is $100-$200.

**Four-speed automatic transmission**
- **Torque Converter Failure:** The torque converter can fail, causing the transmission to slip and lose power. The symptoms of this issue include a slipping transmission, a loss of power, and a check engine light. To fix this issue, the torque converter must be replaced. The estimated cost of this repair is $1000-$1500.
- **Solenoid Failure:** The solenoids in the transmission can fail, causing the transmission to shift improperly. The symptoms of this issue include a rough or jerky shift, a loss of power, and a check engine light. To fix this issue, the failed solenoids must be replaced. The estimated cost of this repair is $300-$500.
- **Transmission Fluid Leak:** The transmission pan gasket or other seals can fail, causing fluid to leak out of the transmission. The symptoms of this issue include a fluid leak on the ground, a low fluid level, and a check engine light. To fix this issue, the transmission pan gasket or other seals must be replaced. The estimated cost of this repair is $100-$200.

**Suspension**

- **Strut Mount Failure:** The strut mounts can fail, causing the suspension to make a clunking noise. The symptoms of this issue include a clunking noise when driving over bumps, a decrease in ride quality, and a loss of alignment. To fix this issue, the strut mounts must be replaced. The estimated cost of this repair is $200-$400.
- **Control Arm Bushing Failure:** The control arm bushings can fail, causing the suspension to make a squeaking or groaning noise. The symptoms of this issue include a squeaking or groaning noise when turning, a decrease in ride quality, and a loss of alignment. To fix this issue, the control arm bushings must be replaced. The estimated cost of this repair is $200-$300.
- **Ball Joint Failure:** The ball joints can fail, causing the suspension to become loose and unstable. The symptoms of this issue include a knocking or clunking noise when driving over bumps, a decrease in ride quality, and a loss of alignment. To fix this issue, the ball joints must be replaced. The estimated cost of this repair is $200-$400.

**Electrical**

- **Battery Failure:** The battery can fail, causing the car to not start. The symptoms of this issue include a no-start condition, a flickering or dim dashboard, and a check engine light. To fix this issue, the battery must be replaced. The estimated cost of this repair is $100-$200.
- **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the car to lose power. The symptoms of this issue include a loss of power, a dim or flickering dashboard, and a check engine light. To fix this issue, the alternator must be replaced. The estimated cost of this repair is $300-$500.
- **Starter Failure:** The starter can fail, causing the car to not start. The symptoms of this issue include a no-start condition, a clicking noise when trying to start the car, and a check engine light. To fix this issue, the starter must be replaced. The estimated cost of this repair is $200-$400.

**Exterior**

- **Door Lock Actuator Failure:** The door lock actuators can fail, causing the doors to become inoperable. The symptoms of this issue include a door that does not lock or unlock properly, a check engine light, and a clicking noise when trying to lock or unlock the doors. To fix this issue, the door lock actuator must be replaced. The estimated cost of this repair is $200-$300.
- **Window Motor Failure:** The window motor can fail, causing the window to become inoperable. The symptoms of this issue include a window that does not go up or down properly, a check engine light, and a clicking noise when trying to raise or lower the window. To fix this issue, the window motor must be replaced. The estimated cost of this repair is $200-$400.
- **Mirror Control Failure:** The mirror control can fail, causing the mirrors to become inoperable. The symptoms of this issue include a mirror that does not adjust properly, a check engine light, and a clicking noise when trying to adjust the mirror. To fix this issue, the mirror control must be replaced. The estimated cost of this repair is $100-$200.

**Interior**

- **Climate Control Failure:** The climate control system can fail, causing the car to become too hot or too cold. The symptoms of this issue include a malfunctioning air conditioning or heater, a check engine light, and a clicking noise when trying to adjust the climate control. To fix this issue, the climate control system must be diagnosed and repaired. The estimated cost of this repair is $200-$400.
- **Cruise Control Failure:** The cruise control system can fail, causing the car to not be able to maintain a set speed. The symptoms of this issue include a cruise control system that does not work properly, a check engine light, and a clicking noise when trying to set the cruise control. To fix this issue, the cruise control system must be diagnosed and repaired. The estimated cost of this repair is $100-$200.
