The 2007 Suzuki SX4 is a compact car that was available as a hatchback or sedan. The most common engine option is a 2.0-liter inline-4 cylinder engine producing around 143 horsepower and 136 lb-ft of torque. The SX4 was praised for its affordability, available all-wheel drive, and nimble handling. Criticisms often included its somewhat underpowered engine and basic interior. Transmission options included a 5-speed manual and a 4-speed automatic.

**Engine**

**2.0L Inline-4 Cylinder**

*   **Timing Chain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive engine noise, rattling sounds, especially upon startup. This can lead to poor engine performance and eventually complete engine failure if not addressed.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Faulty Mass Air Flow (MAF) Sensor**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, stalling, poor fuel economy, check engine light illuminated.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the MAF sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Low coolant levels, overheating, visible leaks under the vehicle.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of leaking hoses, water pump, radiator, or other cooling system components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+ depending on the source and severity of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noticeable delays or harshness during gear changes, especially between 2nd and 3rd gear. Transmission may slip out of gear.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a fluid flush and filter change may help. In more severe cases, transmission rebuild or replacement is necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (fluid change) - $3000+ (rebuild/replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibration at highway speeds, especially when accelerating. Stalling or difficulty engaging gears.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**
*No Issues Found*

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Struts/Shocks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, excessive body roll during cornering, clunking noises from the suspension.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the struts and/or shocks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $1000+ per axle.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rear Wheel Bearing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Grinding or humming noise coming from the rear wheels, especially noticeable at higher speeds. Play in the rear wheel when lifted.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the rear wheel bearings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per wheel.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Power Window Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Windows failing to operate, intermittent operation, or only working in one direction.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the power window motor, regulator, or switch.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+ per window.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **TPMS Sensor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Tire Pressure Monitoring System (TPMS) warning light illuminated despite proper tire inflation.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty TPMS sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ per sensor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Faulty ABS Sensor**
    *   Description/Symptoms: ABS light illuminated on the dashboard, reduced braking power, increased stopping distances, the ABS system is not functioning as intended.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty ABS sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$400
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust forming on the undercarriage, wheel wells, and around body panels, especially in areas with harsh winters and road salt usage.
    *   Solution: Rust repair or panel replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2000+ depending on severity and location.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Cracked Dashboard**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracking of the dashboard, especially in areas exposed to direct sunlight and high temperatures.
    *   Solution: Dashboard replacement or repair (using dashboard covers or fillers).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+ depending on the method.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Air Conditioning Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning system blowing warm air, or not cooling effectively.
    *   Solution: Recharge the AC system. If that does not work, replace the compressor, condenser, or other AC components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50-$100 (Recharge) - $400 - $1200 (Replacement of parts).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2007 Suzuki SX4 has several common issues that owners should be aware of. Engine problems, particularly timing chain issues, can be costly to repair. Transmission problems with the automatic variant can also be a concern. Suspension and electrical issues are less severe but still common. Rust is a potential issue, especially in colder climates. Overall, the severity and cost of these issues range from minor annoyances to significant repairs. Prospective buyers should have a mechanic inspect the car thoroughly before purchasing.
