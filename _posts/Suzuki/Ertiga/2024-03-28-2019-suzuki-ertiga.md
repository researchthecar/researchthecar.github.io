**Introduction:**

The 2019 Suzuki Ertiga is a compact MPV known for its spacious cabin, fuel efficiency, and affordability. It comes with a sole engine option: a 1.5-liter inline-4 gasoline engine that generates 103 horsepower and 138 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with either a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission. Despite its overall practicality, the Ertiga has some reported issues that potential buyers should be aware of.

**Engine**

**1.5-liter Inline-4 Gasoline Engine**

- **Intake Valve Carbon Buildup:**
Description/Symptoms: Carbon deposits can accumulate on the intake valves, causing reduced engine performance, rough idling, and increased fuel consumption.
Solution: The intake valves need to be cleaned manually or using chemical solvents.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
- **Alternator Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: The alternator may fail prematurely, leading to a loss of electrical power and potential engine stalling.
Solution: Replace the faulty alternator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
- **Engine Overheating:**
Description/Symptoms: The engine may overheat due to a faulty cooling system component, such as a leaking radiator or a malfunctioning thermostat.
Solution: Repair or replace the affected component.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $1000+
- **Power Loss:**
Description/Symptoms: Sudden loss of engine power during acceleration or at highway speeds.
Solution: Inspect the throttle body, fuel pump, and ignition system for any issues.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission:**
- **Gear Grinding:**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears or grinding noises when shifting.
Solution: Replace or repair faulty synchronizers or other transmission components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
- **Clutch Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Clutch slippage or difficulty engaging gears.
Solution: Replace the clutch disc, pressure plate, and release bearing.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission:**
- **Transmission Slipping:**
Description/Symptoms: Gears slipping or not engaging properly, leading to erratic shifting or loss of power.
Solution: Overhaul or replace the transmission.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $3000+
- **Valve Body Failure:**
Description/Symptoms: Faulty valve body can cause delayed or erratic gear shifts, as well as check engine light illumination.
Solution: Replace the valve body.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+

**Suspension**

- **Premature Suspension Wear:**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive wear or damage to suspension components, such as struts, shocks, or bushings.
Solution: Inspect and replace worn or damaged parts.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
- **Rear Suspension Sagging:**
Description/Symptoms: Rear end of the vehicle sagging or unevenly loaded, especially when carrying heavy loads.
Solution: Replace or reinforce rear suspension springs or struts.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Electrical**

- **Battery Drain:**
Description/Symptoms: Battery drains quickly when parked overnight or over multiple days.
Solution: Inspect for parasitic drain and repair or replace faulty components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
- **Electrical Short:**
Description/Symptoms: Electrical components malfunctioning or behaving erratically, such as flickering lights, blown fuses, or inoperative gauges.
Solution: Trace and repair the short circuit.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Exterior**

- **Rust:**
Description/Symptoms: Premature rust formation on body panels, especially in coastal areas or areas with high humidity.
Solution: Rust repair and prevention measures are required.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+
- **Door Handle Malfunction:**
Description/Symptoms: Exterior door handles may become loose or break, making it difficult to open the doors.
Solution: Replace faulty door handles.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Interior**

- **Dashboard Rattle:**
Description/Symptoms: Noises or vibrations coming from the dashboard, especially over rough roads or during acceleration.
Solution: Inspect and tighten loose dashboard components or add damping material.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
- **Squeaky Seats:**
Description/Symptoms: Squeaking noises coming from the seats while sitting or moving.
Solution: Lubricate seat rails or replace faulty seat mechanisms.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
- **AC Malfunction:**
Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning system blowing warm air or not cooling effectively.
Solution: Inspect and repair or replace AC compressor, condenser, or evaporator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+

**Summary**

The 2019 Suzuki Ertiga is a generally reliable vehicle, but it does have some reported issues. While the engine and transmission problems are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, the potential for rust and premature suspension wear should be considered. Interior issues are mostly minor and can be resolved at a reasonable cost. Overall, the Ertiga offers good value for money, but potential buyers should be aware of the potential issues before making a purchase decision.
