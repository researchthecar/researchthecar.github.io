The 2022 Suzuki Ertiga is a popular MPV known for its affordability, fuel efficiency, and spacious interior, making it a practical choice for families. It is primarily powered by a 1.5-liter K15B inline-4 gasoline engine producing around 103 horsepower and 102 lb-ft of torque, paired with either a 5-speed manual or a 4-speed automatic transmission depending on the market. Owners generally appreciate its value for money, spaciousness, and fuel economy. Common complaints revolve around its somewhat underpowered engine, basic interior features, and dated automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**1.5L K15B Inline-4 Gasoline Engine**

*   **Engine Misfires/Rough Idle**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, rough idling, decreased fuel efficiency, and potential stalling. Check engine light may illuminate.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and replace faulty ignition coils, spark plugs, or fuel injectors. Clean or replace the mass airflow (MAF) sensor. Check for vacuum leaks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the component and labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noticeable decrease in engine oil level between oil changes. Blue smoke from the exhaust pipe can be a sign of oil burning.
    *   Solution: Inspect the engine for leaks, particularly around the valve stem seals and piston rings. Replacing the affected components may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+ depending on the extent of the damage and labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifts between gears, especially during acceleration or deceleration.
    *   Solution: Perform a transmission fluid flush and replacement. If the issue persists, a more thorough inspection of the valve body and solenoids may be necessary. Replacement or repair of the valve body/solenoids.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Wear of Shock Absorbers**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, excessive body roll when cornering, and longer stopping distances.
    *   Solution: Replace the worn shock absorbers. Consider replacing the struts as well for optimal performance.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may freeze, become unresponsive, or crash intermittently.
    *   Solution: Try performing a system reset. If the problem persists, a software update or replacement of the unit may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Faulty Door Lock Actuators**
    * Description/Symptoms: The door lock actuators may fail and not lock or unlock the door properly. This can be caused by wear or corrosion of the locking mechanism, causing a buzzing noise when attempting to lock or unlock the doors.
    *   Solution: The door lock actuator must be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises from Dashboard**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Annoying rattling or squeaking noises coming from the dashboard, especially on uneven roads.
    *   Solution: Tighten any loose screws or fasteners. Apply felt tape or foam padding to areas where plastic components may be rubbing together.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $100+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2022 Suzuki Ertiga generally experiences issues related to engine misfires, rough automatic transmission shifting, premature wear of shock absorbers, infotainment glitches, and interior noises. While the engine and transmission issues can be moderately expensive to address, most other issues are relatively minor and can be resolved without significant cost. It is important to note that engine and transmission issues are uncommon and are more likely to be discovered at higher mileage. Overall, the Suzuki Ertiga can be a dependable and budget-friendly vehicle, but buyers should be aware of these potential problems and inspect the car thoroughly before purchasing.
