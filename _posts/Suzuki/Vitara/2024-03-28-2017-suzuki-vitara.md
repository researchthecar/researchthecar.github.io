**2017 Suzuki Vitara**

The 2017 Suzuki Vitara is a compact SUV that offers a combination of versatility, comfort, and fuel efficiency. It is powered by a 1.6-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine that produces 115 horsepower and 110 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with a six-speed automatic transmission. The Vitara boasts a spacious interior, a user-friendly infotainment system, and a variety of safety features. However, like any vehicle, it is not without its problems.

**Engine**

**1.6-Liter Naturally Aspirated Four-Cylinder**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Consumes excessive amounts of oil, resulting in low oil levels and potential engine damage.
  * Solution: Replace piston rings or cylinder head.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Harsh Shifting:** Transmission may exhibit rough or delayed shifts, especially during acceleration or deceleration.
  * Solution: Update transmission software or replace transmission fluid.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Major Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Battery may discharge prematurely due to a parasitic draw.
  * Solution: Diagnose electrical system and repair or replace faulty components.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Malfunction:** Door handles may break or become loose, making it difficult to open or close the doors.
  * Solution: Replace door handles.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment System Glitches:** Infotainment system may experience intermittent problems, such as freezing or lagging.
  * Solution: Update software or replace infotainment unit.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found
* **Steering Wheel Noise:** Steering wheel may rattle or make noise when turning.
  * Solution: Lubricate steering column or replace steering rack.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Suzuki Vitara is a generally reliable vehicle, but it has its share of issues. The most severe problems are related to excessive oil consumption and harsh shifting. However, these issues are relatively rare. Other problems, such as electrical issues and interior glitches, are more common but typically less costly to repair. Overall, the Vitara offers a good value for the money, but it is important to be aware of its potential issues before purchasing.
