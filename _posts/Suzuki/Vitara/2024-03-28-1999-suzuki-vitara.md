**Introduction**

The 1999 Suzuki Vitara is a compact SUV that offers rugged capabilities and versatile functionality. It comes with an array of engine and transmission options, including:

* **J20A 2.0L V6:** 127 horsepower, 143 lb-ft of torque
* **J20A ** 2.0L V6** with All Wheel Drive:** 127 horsepower, 143 lb-ft of torque
* **J20A 2.0L V6** with Manual Transmission:** 127 horsepower, 143 lb-ft of torque
* **J20A ** 2.0L V6** with Automatic Transmission:** 127 horsepower, 143 lb-ft of torque
* **H25A 2.5L V6:** 153 horsepower, 169 lb-ft of torque
* **H25A 2.5L V6** with All Wheel Drive:** 153 horsepower, 169 lb-ft of torque
* **H25A 2.5L V6** with Manual Transmission:** 153 horsepower, 169 lb-ft of torque
* **H25A 2.5L V6** with Automatic Transmission:** 153 horsepower, 169 lb-ft of torque

Owners generally praise the Vitara for its off-roading prowess, reliability, and affordability. However, it has some notable shortcomings, such as its limited interior space, lackluster fuel economy, and outdated design.

**Engine**

**J20A 2.0L V6**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, rough idling, engine hesitation
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: 01V221000

**H25A 2.5L V6**

* **Head Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, white smoke from exhaust
    * Solution: Replace head gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Manual Transmission**

* **No Issues Found**

**Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Slipping:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, loss of power
    * Solution: Overhaul or replace transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Mount Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling or clunking noise, reduced ride comfort
    * Solution: Replace strut mounts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery discharge, dimming lights, electrical system failure
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rusty Body Panels:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust spots, bubbling paint
    * Solution: Sand, repair, and repaint affected areas
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracked or warped dashboard
    * Solution: Replace dashboard
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800

**Summary**

Overall, the 1999 Suzuki Vitara is a capable and affordable SUV, but it is not without its issues. The most serious potential problem is the head gasket failure on the 2.5L engine. Other common issues include intake manifold gasket leaks, transmission slipping, strut mount failure, and rusting body panels. While many of these issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, they can still be a nuisance for owners.
