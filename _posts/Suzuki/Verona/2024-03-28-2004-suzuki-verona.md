**Introduction:**

The 2004 Suzuki Verona was a mid-size sedan that offered a combination of comfort, performance, and value. It was available with two engine options and three transmission options. The 2.5L V6 engine produced 160 horsepower and 167 lb-ft of torque, while the 3.5L V6 engine produced 215 horsepower and 222 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options included a 5-speed manual, a 4-speed automatic, and a continuously variable transmission (CVT).

**Engine**

**2.5L V6 Engine**

* **Engine ticking noise:** Description/Symptoms: A ticking sound coming from the engine at idle or under acceleration. Solution: Replacement of the timing chain tensioner. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**3.5L V6 Engine**

* No Issues Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* No Issues Found

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission slipping or shuddering:** Description/Symptoms: The transmission slips or shudders during gear changes. Solution: Replacement of the transmission fluid and filter, or overhaul of the transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+

**CVT Transmission**

* **CVT belt failure:** Description/Symptoms: Transmission failure, loss of power, or jerking. Solution: Replacement of the CVT belt and pulleys. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+

**Suspension**

* **Strut mount failure:** Description/Symptoms: Clunking or knocking noises from the front or rear of the vehicle when going over bumps. Solution: Replacement of the strut mounts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** Description/Symptoms: Dim headlights, electrical system malfunctions, or starting problems. Solution: Replacement of the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Battery drain:** Description/Symptoms: Battery drains quickly when the vehicle is parked. Solution: Diagnosis and repair of the electrical system, or replacement of the battery. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Door lock actuator failure:** Description/Symptoms: Door locks fail to operate electronically or manually. Solution: Replacement of the door lock actuators. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracking:** Description/Symptoms: Cracks or splits in the dashboard material. Solution: Replacement of the dashboard. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Air conditioning blower motor failure:** Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning fails to blow air into the cabin. Solution: Replacement of the blower motor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Summary**

The 2004 Suzuki Verona has some known issues that can be costly to repair, especially in the areas of transmission and suspension. However, the issues are not widespread and the vehicle offers good value for its price point.
