# 2012 Suzuki SX4 Crossover

### Overview
The 2012 Suzuki SX4 Crossover, a compact all-wheel-drive vehicle, generally presents a respectable reliability record for its class and age. It was designed to offer practicality and affordability, often appealing to budget-conscious buyers. While not without its potential weak points, many owners report a positive ownership experience with routine maintenance being the primary concern. The vehicle's mechanical simplicity contributes to its overall durability, though specific components, particularly in the powertrain and electrical systems, can occasionally require attention. Understanding these potential issues can help owners proactively address them, ensuring a longer lifespan and continued reliable performance from their SX4 Crossover.

## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Electrical Gremlins**
- **Symptoms:** Random power losses, non-functioning accessories (windows, locks), dashboard warning lights illuminating without cause.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


### Alternator
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, difficulty starting, dead battery.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Suzuki SX4 Crossover is generally considered a reliable vehicle, especially given its affordable price point. The 2.0L engine and manual transmission options are particularly robust. The CVT transmission, while convenient, has shown some susceptibility to leaks and overheating issues, which can be costly to repair. Electrical gremlins related to the BCM and premature alternator failure are also noted concerns. Exterior and interior components are largely free of widespread problems, with minor issues like headlight condensation being relatively common for vehicles of this age. Overall, with diligent maintenance, particularly for the CVT, the SX4 Crossover can be a dependable choice.
