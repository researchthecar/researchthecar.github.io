**Introduction to the 2004 Suzuki Swift+**

The 2004 Suzuki Swift+ is a subcompact hatchback powered by a 1.5-liter gasoline engine. The engine produces 100 horsepower and 98 lb-ft of torque. It comes standard with a 5-speed manual transmission and an optional 4-speed automatic transmission. The Swift+ is praised for its fuel efficiency, affordability, and nimble handling. However, it has received some negative feedback regarding its limited rear seat space and lack of standard features.

**Engine**

* **1.5-Liter Engine**
    * **Intake Valve Seat Recession**
        * Description/Symptoms: Premature wear of intake valve seats can cause a loss of compression, rough idle, and poor performance.
        * Solution: Replace intake valve seats and valves.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800+
        * Recalls: None found
    * **Crankshaft Position Sensor Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: A faulty crankshaft position sensor can disrupt engine timing, leading to stalling, rough idle, and difficulty starting.
        * Solution: Replace crankshaft position sensor.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
        * Recalls: None found

**Transmission**

* **Manual Transmission**
    * **Synchronizer Ring Wear**
        * Description/Symptoms: Worn synchronizer rings can make it difficult to shift gears smoothly, resulting in grinding noises, popping out of gear, and hard shifting.
        * Solution: Replace worn syncho rings.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
        * Recalls: None found
* **Automatic Transmission**
    * **Torque Converter Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: A faulty torque converter can cause shuddering, slipping, or overheating of the transmission.
        * Solution: Replace torque converter.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: None found

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Mount Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged strut mounts can cause a clunking or rattling noise when going over bumps.
    * Solution: Replace strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400+
    * Recalls: None found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: A failing alternator can cause dimming lights, electrical system malfunctions, and difficulty starting the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None found
* **Window Switch Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Faulty window switches can prevent windows from operating properly.
    * Solution: Replace window switches.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None found

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Door handles can break or become loose, making it difficult to open or close the doors.
    * Solution: Replace door handles.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150+
    * Recalls: None found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Climate control system may not function properly, leading to uneven heating or cooling.
    * Solution: Repair or replace climate control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None found
* **Instrument Cluster Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: Gauges or displays on the instrument cluster may fail or provide inaccurate readings.
    * Solution: Replace instrument cluster.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None found

**Summary**

The 2004 Suzuki Swift+ has a few notable issues that can be costly to repair, such as intake valve seat recession and torque converter failure. However, the majority of issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, such as window switch failure and door handle failure. Overall, the Swift+ is a reliable vehicle with good fuel efficiency and handling, but it is important to be aware of potential problems before purchasing.
