**Introduction:**

The 2019 Suzuki S-Cross is a compact crossover SUV available with two engine options and two transmission choices. The base engine is a 1.6-liter gasoline unit producing 118 horsepower and 115 lb-ft of torque, paired with either a 5-speed manual or a 6-speed automatic transmission. The higher-powered engine is a 1.4-liter turbocharged gasoline unit that generates 140 horsepower and 162 lb-ft of torque, which comes exclusively with a 6-speed automatic transmission.

Owners of the Suzuki S-Cross have praised its practicality, fuel efficiency, and value, but some have reported issues with various components of the vehicle.

**Engine**

**1.6-liter Gasoline Engine**

* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leak from the valve cover gasket, visible as black stains around the top of the engine.
    * Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Rattle:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise coming from the front of the engine at startup or during acceleration.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found

**1.4-liter Turbocharged Gasoline Engine**

* **Turbocharger Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, high-pitched whining noise from the turbocharger, or blue smoke from the exhaust.
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Gear Grinding:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, especially into second gear, accompanied by grinding noises.
    * Solution: Replace the gear synchronizers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Delayed Gear Shifts:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission takes longer than normal to shift gears, especially when upshifting.
    * Solution: Flush and replace the transmission fluid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Top Mount Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noise and vibration when driving over bumps, especially at low speeds.
    * Solution: Replace the front strut top mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Rear Shock Absorber Leaking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leak from the rear shock absorbers, visible as wet spots on the exterior of the shock absorbers.
    * Solution: Replace the rear shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electrical System Malfunctions:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Electrical components, such as lights, power windows, or the stereo, may malfunction or fail intermittently.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the specific electrical fault.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery drains quickly, even when the vehicle is not in use.
    * Solution: Check for parasitic draws on the battery and rectify them.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Roof Rail Trim Peeling:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The trim on the roof rails begins to peel or lift.
    * Solution: Replace the affected trim pieces.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Door Handle Paint Chipping:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Paint chips and scratches on the door handles.
    * Solution: Touch up the paint or replace the door handles.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Issues:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Climate control system malfunctioning, resulting in inconsistent cabin temperature or airflow.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty climate control components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Dashboard Rattle:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling or buzzing noise coming from the dashboard area.
    * Solution: Identify and secure the source of the rattle.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Other**

* **Brake Pedal Fade:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Brake pedal feeling spongy or requiring excessive pressure to stop the vehicle.
    * Solution: Inspect and replace the brake pads, rotors, or brake fluid if necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2019 Suzuki S-Cross has experienced some issues, the most severe of which are turbocharger failure, transmission problems, and brake pedal fade. The cost to fix these issues can be significant, ranging from $1,500 to $2,500 for turbocharger replacement and $400 to $600 for timing chain repair. Other issues are less severe and can be addressed for a relatively low cost.
