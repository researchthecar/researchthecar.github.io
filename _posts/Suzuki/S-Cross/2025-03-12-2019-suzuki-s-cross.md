The 2019 Suzuki S-Cross is a subcompact crossover SUV that offers a blend of practicality and affordability. Engine options typically include a 1.0L Boosterjet turbocharged three-cylinder engine producing around 110 horsepower and 125 lb-ft of torque, a 1.4L Boosterjet turbocharged four-cylinder engine delivering approximately 140 horsepower and 162 lb-ft of torque, and a 1.6L naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine. Transmission choices can include a 5-speed manual, a 6-speed manual, or a 6-speed automatic. Owners often praise the S-Cross for its fuel efficiency, spacious interior, and value for money. Common criticisms include its somewhat bland styling and relatively basic interior compared to some competitors.

**Engine**

**1.4L Boosterjet Turbocharged Four-Cylinder Engine**
*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, whistling noise from the engine bay, check engine light illuminated.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Direct Injection Carbon Buildup**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, misfires, reduced fuel economy, hesitation during acceleration.
    *   Solution: Carbon cleaning of the intake valves, potentially requiring walnut blasting or chemical cleaning.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**1.0L Boosterjet Turbocharged Three-Cylinder Engine**
*   **Timing Chain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise upon startup, engine misfires, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the timing chain, guides, and tensioner.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Hesitation and Jerky Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Delay when shifting gears, particularly when accelerating from a stop or during downshifts; harsh or jerky gear changes.
    * Solution: Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement. In more severe cases, valve body repair or replacement may be required.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive bouncing, poor handling, knocking noise from the rear suspension.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the rear shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Car fails to start, requiring a jump start. Battery repeatedly drains even after charging.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis of parasitic draw, potentially involving faulty modules or accessories. Replacement of the battery if necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Infotainment screen freezes, becomes unresponsive, or restarts intermittently.
    *   Solution: Software update of the infotainment system. Replacement of the infotainment unit may be required if software updates do not resolve the issue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (if software update resolves) - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Rattling Noises from Dashboard**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Annoying rattling or buzzing sounds emanating from the dashboard area, especially when driving on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Identifying the source of the rattle and securing loose components, such as wiring harnesses or trim pieces.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 2019 Suzuki S-Cross generally exhibits a range of common issues, with engine and transmission problems being prominent. Turbocharger failures, timing chain issues, and carbon buildup can be costly to address. Electrical issues, such as battery drain and infotainment malfunctions, can also be frustrating. Suspension problems may affect ride quality and handling. While some issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more significant and require substantial repairs, potentially impacting the overall ownership experience.
