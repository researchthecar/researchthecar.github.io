# 2012 Suzuki Swift

### Overview
The 2012 Suzuki Swift is generally considered a reliable subcompact car, known for its agile handling and fuel efficiency. While it doesn't typically suffer from widespread major mechanical failures, like any vehicle, certain components can experience issues over time. Owners often report satisfaction with its day-to-day usability and low running costs. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond standard maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for the 1.2L and 1.4L engine options, and manual/automatic transmissions.

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Suzuki Swift demonstrates strong reliability, particularly in its powertrain and chassis components. The most commonly cited technical issue, though not widespread, is an occasional electrical gremlin leading to battery drain, which is typically resolvable with targeted diagnostics. Other categories like engine, transmission, suspension, exterior, and interior show no significant recurring problems beyond normal wear and tear. For potential buyers, the Swift represents a sensible choice for dependable, low-cost transportation, with the caveat to check for any signs of battery drain if the vehicle has been sitting unused for long periods.
