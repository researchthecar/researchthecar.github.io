**2015 Suzuki Swift**

The 2015 Suzuki Swift is a subcompact hatchback that offers a balance of practicality, fuel efficiency, and affordability. It is available with a 1.2-liter gasoline engine or a 1.0-liter Boosterjet turbocharged engine, paired with either a 5-speed manual or a continuously variable transmission (CVT).

The Swift is praised for its nimble handling, comfortable interior, and impressive fuel economy. However, it has received some criticism for its lack of power and refinement.

**Engine**

* **1.2-liter Gasoline Engine**
    * **Engine Misfiring**
        * Description/Symptoms: Engine hesitation, rough idle, and reduced power.
        * Solution: Replace faulty ignition coils or spark plugs.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **1.0-liter Boosterjet Turbocharged Engine**
    * **Turbocharger Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive oil consumption, and loud noises from the engine.
        * Solution: Replace the turbocharger.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-speed Manual Transmission**
    * **Gear Grinding**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, especially when cold or under load.
        * Solution: Adjust or replace worn synchronizers.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**
    * **Hesitation and Jerking**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slips or feels like it is hesitating during acceleration or gear changes.
        * Solution: Replace the CVT fluid and filter or reprogram the transmission control unit (TCU).
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Mounts**
    * **Noise and Vibration**
        * Description/Symptoms: Loud clunking or grinding noises from the front suspension, especially when turning or going over bumps.
        * Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery goes dead or struggles to start the vehicle.
    * Solution: Check for parasitic electrical draws and replace the battery if necessary.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rear Hatchback**
    * **Misalignment**
        * Description/Symptoms: Rear hatchback does not close properly or sits unevenly.
        * Solution: Adjust the hatchback hinges.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control System**
    * **Inoperative A/C**
        * Description/Symptoms: Air conditioning system fails to blow cold air.
        * Solution: Diagnose and repair the cause of the A/C failure, such as a refrigerant leak or faulty compressor.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2015 Suzuki Swift generally receives positive reviews for its reliability and affordability. However, there are some potential issues to consider, such as engine misfires, turbocharger failure, and transmission hesitations. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the age and mileage of the vehicle. Overall, the Swift offers a good value for money with some minor drawbacks.
