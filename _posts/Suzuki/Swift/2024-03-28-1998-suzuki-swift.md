**Introduction:**

The 1998 Suzuki Swift is a compact hatchback known for its fuel efficiency, affordability, and sporty handling. Additional engine options include a 1.3L and 1.6L four-cylinder, while transmission options include a 5-speed manual and a 4-speed automatic.

**Engine**

**1.3L Four-Cylinder**

* **No Issues Found**

**1.6L Four-Cylinder**

* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leak from the top of the engine.
    * Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **No Issues Found**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slippage**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slips out of gear or hesitates to shift.
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noises and vibrations when driving over bumps.
    * Solution: Replace the front struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery dies quickly even when the vehicle is not in use.
    * Solution: Inspect electrical system for parasitic draw, replace battery as needed.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture inside the headlight lenses.
    * Solution: Replace the headlight lenses.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Power Window Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Power windows do not operate.
    * Solution: Replace the power window motor or switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $350
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 1998 Suzuki Swift is generally a reliable vehicle with few major issues. However, common problems include valve cover gasket leaks, transmission slippage, and suspension failure. The cost of repairs can vary depending on the severity of the issue and the location of the repair. Overall, the Swift is a budget-friendly option with low maintenance costs compared to other vehicles in its class.
