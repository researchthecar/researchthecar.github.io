Understanding these potential pitfalls can help in proactive maintenance and informed purchasing decisions for this generation of Swift.

## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Window Regulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows may become slow, stop working, or make grinding noises.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the window regulator mechanism.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300 per window

**Alternator Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light illuminated, difficulty starting the engine.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


## Exterior

### Standard Body and Trim
**Rust on Wheel Arches and Sills**
- **Symptoms:** Visible corrosion and bubbling paintwork, particularly in areas exposed to salt and moisture.
- **Solution:** Rust treatment and repair, potentially involving panel replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800 per area


## Interior

### Standard Interior Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, or intermittent airflow that changes with engine RPM.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the HVAC blower motor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Suzuki Swift is generally a reliable vehicle, with its powertrain components (engine and transmission) showing a strong track record of durability. The most commonly reported issues tend to be related to electrical components, such as window regulators and alternators, and some minor exterior rust concerns in specific areas. Interior issues like HVAC blower motor failure are also noted. These are not typically catastrophic failures but rather items that may require attention as the vehicle ages. Overall, for its segment and age, the Swift offers a good balance of affordability and dependability, provided these potential issues are addressed.
