**2007 Suzuki Swift**

The 2007 Suzuki Swift is a subcompact hatchback renowned for its fuel efficiency, affordability, and maneuverability. It is available with two engine options: a 1.3-liter inline-four and a 1.5-liter inline-four. Transmission choices include a five-speed manual and a four-speed automatic. Despite its positive attributes, the Swift has faced several issues, ranging from minor annoyances to more severe problems.

**Engine**

**1.3-liter Inline-Four**

- **Intake Manifold Cracking:** The intake manifold may develop cracks, causing air leaks and poor engine performance. Symptoms include a loss of power, rough idling, and increased fuel consumption.
- Solution: Replace the intake manifold.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
- Recalls: None Found

**1.5-liter Inline-Four**

- **Timing Chain Failure:** The timing chain may fail prematurely, resulting in catastrophic engine damage. Symptoms include a rattling noise, loss of power, and engine stalling.
- Solution: Replace the timing chain.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
- Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Five-Speed Manual**

- **Clutch Failure:** The clutch may wear out prematurely, leading to difficulty shifting gears and slippage. Symptoms include a grinding noise when shifting, difficulty engaging gears, and slipping when accelerating.
- Solution: Replace the clutch.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
- Recalls: None Found

**Four-Speed Automatic**

- **Transmission Fluid Leaks:** The transmission may develop leaks, causing fluid loss and reduced transmission efficiency. Symptoms include a lack of lubrication, difficulty shifting gears, and overheating.
- Solution: Repair the leak and replace the transmission fluid.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
- Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **Front Strut Mount Failure:** The front strut mounts may fail, causing excessive noise and vibration. Symptoms include a rattling or thumping noise when driving over bumps, and reduced handling stability.
- Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
- Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Alternator Failure:** The alternator may fail, leading to a loss of power to the electrical system. Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle, dim headlights, and flickering dashboard lights.
- Solution: Replace the alternator.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
- Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Rear Spoiler Cracking:** The rear spoiler can crack due to exposure to sunlight and heat. Symptoms include visible cracks or peeling on the spoiler.
- Solution: Replace the spoiler.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
- Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Window Regulators Failing:** The window regulators may fail, causing the windows to become stuck or inoperable. Symptoms include difficulty operating the windows, or windows that do not move smoothly.
- Solution: Replace the window regulators.
- Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per window
- Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2007 Suzuki Swift has had several reported issues that vary in severity and cost to fix. While some issues are minor annoyances, others, such as the timing chain failure, can be catastrophic. Potential buyers should carefully consider these issues and the associated repair costs before purchasing this vehicle.
