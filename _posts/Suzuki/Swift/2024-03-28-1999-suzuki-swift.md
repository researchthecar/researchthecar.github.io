**Introduction:**

The 1999 Suzuki Swift is a compact hatchback praised for its affordability, fuel efficiency, and nimble handling. The base model features a 1.3-liter 4-cylinder engine paired with a 5-speed manual transmission, producing 85 horsepower and 94 lb-ft of torque. Alternatively, a 1.6-liter 4-cylinder engine with 100 horsepower and 103 lb-ft of torque was available with either a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission. Despite its positive attributes, the Suzuki Swift has faced some issues over the years.

**Engine**

* **1.3-liter Engine**
    * **Valve Cover Gasket Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the valve cover, causing a burning oil smell and reduced oil levels.
        * Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
        * Recalls: None Found
* **1.6-liter Engine**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks from the intake manifold gasket, resulting in overheating and reduced engine performance.
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **Clutch Master Cylinder Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, spongy clutch pedal, or clutch not engaging.
        * Solution: Replace the clutch master cylinder.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400
        * Recalls: None Found
* **4-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Transmission Fluid Leak**
        * Description/Symptoms: Leaking transmission fluid leads to low fluid levels and reduced transmission performance.
        * Solution: Repair or replace the transmission fluid leak.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Strut Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noisy suspension, reduced handling, and uneven tire wear.
    * Solution: Replace the front suspension struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, difficulty starting the engine, or electrical system failure.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Tail Light Assembly Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inoperative or malfunctioning tail lights.
    * Solution: Replace the tail light assembly.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Instrument Cluster Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Inaccurate or malfunctioning gauges, warning lights, and displays.
    * Solution: Replace the instrument cluster.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 1999 Suzuki Swift has faced a limited number of issues, ranging in severity and cost. Engine and transmission problems are more common, while other issues such as suspension, electrical, and interior components are less frequent. The estimated repair costs fall between $100 and $600+, depending on the issue. Despite these issues, the Swift remains a reliable and affordable option for those seeking a compact and fuel-efficient hatchback.
