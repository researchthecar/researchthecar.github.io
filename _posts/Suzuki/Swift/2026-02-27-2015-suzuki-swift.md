## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent battery drain, leading to starting issues.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify parasitic draw, potentially replacing faulty modules or wiring.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional freezing or unresponsiveness of the infotainment screen.
- **Solution:** Software update or, in rare cases, replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2015 Suzuki Swift is a generally dependable vehicle with a strong track record for reliability. The powertrain (engine and transmission) and chassis components are robust and rarely present significant issues outside of normal wear. The primary areas where minor concerns have been noted are within the electrical system, specifically related to occasional battery drain and infotainment system glitches. These are typically manageable and not indicative of systemic failures. Overall, the Swift offers good value and reliability for its class, making it a sensible choice for budget-conscious buyers prioritizing dependability.
