The 2008 Suzuki Swift is a subcompact car known for its fuel efficiency and nimble handling. The 2008 models were available with a 1.5-liter inline-4 gasoline engine producing approximately 100 horsepower and 98 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual and a 4-speed automatic. Owners appreciate the Swift's affordability, sporty feel, and reliability, while some dislike its small size and relatively basic interior.

**Engine**

**1.5L I4 Gasoline Engine**

* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can develop from various areas, including the valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket, or front crankshaft seal. Symptoms include visible oil spots under the car, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
    * Solution: Replacing the leaking gasket or seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Ignition Coil Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Misfires, rough idling, decreased fuel economy, and a check engine light illuminated. Diagnosis involves identifying the faulty coil with a diagnostic scanner.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty ignition coil.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Rough Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Harsh or jerky shifts between gears, especially when cold. This can be caused by worn or contaminated transmission fluid or issues with the valve body.
    * Solution: Performing a transmission fluid flush and filter replacement. In more severe cases, the valve body may need repair or replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Worn Suspension Bushings**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the suspension, especially over bumps, along with vague steering and poor handling. This is often due to worn control arm bushings or sway bar bushings.
    * Solution: Replacing the worn suspension bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Strut Mount Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noises when turning the steering wheel, rattling sounds when driving over bumps.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Power Window Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Power windows failing to operate or operating intermittently. This can be due to a faulty window motor, switch, or wiring issue.
    * Solution: Diagnosing the specific component and replacing the faulty window motor, switch, or repairing the wiring.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Central Locking Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: Doors not locking or unlocking properly with the remote or door switch. This could stem from a faulty door actuator or a problem with the central locking module.
    * Solution: Replacing the door actuator or repairing/replacing the central locking module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust forming around the wheel wells, door panels, and undercarriage, especially in regions with harsh winters or road salt usage.
    * Solution: Removing the rust and applying rust inhibitor, or replacing the rusted body panels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracking of the dashboard due to sun exposure and temperature changes.
    * Solution: Replacing the entire dashboard, or installing a dashboard cover.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2008 Suzuki Swift is generally a reliable vehicle, but owners may encounter issues like oil leaks, electrical faults (power windows and central locking), and worn suspension components. The severity of these issues varies, and repair costs can range from relatively inexpensive fixes to more substantial expenses, particularly for rust repair or dashboard replacement. It is important to get a pre-purchase inspection to look for these issues prior to buying.
