**Introduction to the 2022 Suzuki Swift**

The 2022 Suzuki Swift is a subcompact hatchback known for its affordability, fuel efficiency, and sporty handling. It is offered with two engine options: a 1.2-liter K12C DualJet engine with 82 horsepower and 79 lb-ft of torque, and a 1.4-liter K14C Boosterjet engine with 138 horsepower and 170 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual, a 6-speed automatic, and a CVT.

While the Swift is generally reliable, there are a few issues that owners have reported.

**Engine**

**1.2-liter K12C DualJet Engine**

* **Intake Valve Deposits:** Carbon deposits can accumulate on the intake valves, leading to reduced engine performance and fuel economy.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Engine hesitation, rough idle, loss of power
* **Solution:** Cleaning the intake valves
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $50 - $150
* **Recalls:** None Found

**1.4-liter K14C Boosterjet Engine**

* **Turbocharger Failure:** The turbocharger can fail prematurely, resulting in a loss of power and engine damage.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Blue smoke from the exhaust, loss of power, check engine light
* **Solution:** Replacing the turbocharger
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $2,500
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Gear Grinding:** Difficulty shifting gears, particularly into reverse
* **Description/Symptoms:** Grinding noise when shifting, difficulty engaging gears
* **Solution:** Replacing worn gears or synchronizers
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000
* **Recalls:** None Found

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping:** The transmission may slip gears, causing a loss of power or delayed acceleration.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Hesitation or jerking during acceleration, loss of power
* **Solution:** Replacing the torque converter or repairing internal transmission components
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $3,000
* **Recalls:** None Found

**CVT**

* **Belt Wear:** The CVT belt can wear prematurely, leading to slipping or failure.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Hesitation or jerking during acceleration, loss of power
* **Solution:** Replacing the CVT belt
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $800 - $1,500
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Corrosion:** The strut mounts can corrode over time, leading to a loss of stability and handling.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Clunking or rattling noises from the front suspension, poor handling
* **Solution:** Replacing the strut mounts
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $500
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, leading to a loss of power and a dead battery.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, loss of electrical power
* **Solution:** Replacing the alternator
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $800
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

* **Windshield Cracking:** The windshield can crack easily from road debris
* **Description/Symptoms:** Cracks in the windshield
* **Solution:** Replacing the windshield
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Interior**

* **Seat Belt Lock Failure:** The seat belt lock can fail to engage properly, posing a safety hazard.
* **Description/Symptoms:** Inability to engage the seat belt
* **Solution:** Replacing the seat belt lock mechanism
* **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $200
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Suzuki Swift is generally a reliable vehicle, but there are some potential issues that owners should be aware of. The most severe issues involve the turbocharger and transmission, which can be costly to repair. However, many of the issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix. Overall, the Swift offers good value for the price, but owners should factor in the potential for repairs.
