**Introduction**

The 2017 Suzuki Swift is a subcompact hatchback available with two engine and transmission options: a 1.4-liter inline-four with a 5-speed manual or 6-speed automatic transmission and a 1.0-liter inline-three turbocharged engine with a CVT automatic transmission. It's known for its sporty handling, fuel efficiency, and affordability. However, it also has some reported issues.

**Engine**

* **1.4L Inline-Four with 5-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **Knocking Noise:** A distinctive knocking sound is heard from the engine at low RPMs.
        * Solution: Replace the engine's timing chain and tensioner.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **1.4L Inline-Four with 6-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **No Issues Found**
* **1.0L Inline-Three Turbo with CVT Automatic Transmission**
    * **Turbocharger Failure:** The turbocharger can fail prematurely, leading to reduced engine power and increased exhaust emissions.
        * Solution: Replace the faulty turbocharger.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **Clutch Problems:** The clutch can become worn out prematurely, resulting in difficulty shifting or slipping gears.
        * Solution: Replace the faulty clutch kit.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found
* **6-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Shifting Delays:** The transmission may experience delayed shifts or hesitations between gears.
        * Solution: Reprogram the transmission control module or replace the transmission valve body.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found
* **CVT Automatic Transmission**
    * **Shuddering or Jerking:** The transmission may shudder or jerk during acceleration or gear changes.
        * Solution: Replace the CVT belt or reprogram the transmission control module.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** The vehicle's battery can drain prematurely when the car is parked.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty battery or check for parasitic electrical drains.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Cruise Control Malfunction:** The cruise control system may become inoperable or cause the vehicle to accelerate or decelerate unintentionally.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty cruise control switch or update the software.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattle:** The dashboard may rattle or make noise when driving over rough roads.
    * Solution: Identify and tighten the loose components causing the rattle.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Free to $200
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Climate Control Issues:** The climate control system may malfunction, resulting in insufficient cooling or heating performance.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty climate control module or actuators.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 Suzuki Swift has several potential issues, particularly with the 1.0L engine, CVT transmission, and electrical system. The severity of the issues varies from minor annoyances to major mechanical failures. The estimated cost to fix these issues can range from a few hundred dollars to over $1,000. It's important for owners to be aware of these potential problems and address them promptly to avoid further damage or safety hazards.
