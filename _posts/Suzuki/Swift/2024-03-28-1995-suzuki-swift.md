**Introduction:**

The 1995 Suzuki Swift is a subcompact hatchback that was offered with a variety of engine and transmission options. The standard engine was a 1.0-liter three-cylinder that produced 69 horsepower and 63 lb-ft of torque, while the optional engine was a 1.3-liter four-cylinder that produced 85 horsepower and 86 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were paired with a five-speed manual transmission.

Drivers praised the Swift for its fuel efficiency, affordability, and agility, but criticized it for its lack of power and bare-bones interior.

**Engine**

* **1.0L Three-Cylinder Engine**

  * **Head gasket failure:** This issue can cause coolant and oil to mix, leading to overheating and engine damage. Symptoms include white smoke from the exhaust, coolant loss, and oil contamination. The solution is to replace the head gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $600 - $1,200+.
  * **Valve clearance issues:** This issue can cause ticking noises from the engine, poor performance, and increased fuel consumption. Symptoms include a ticking sound from the valve train, reduced engine power, and decreased fuel efficiency. The solution is to adjust the valve clearances. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400.

* **1.3L Four-Cylinder Engine**

  * **Intake manifold gasket failure:** This issue can cause vacuum leaks, leading to poor performance and increased fuel consumption. Symptoms include rough idling, hesitation on acceleration, and decreased fuel efficiency. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gasket. Estimated cost to fix: $200 - $400.
  * **Timing belt failure:** This issue can cause catastrophic engine damage if not repaired promptly. Symptoms include a loud squealing noise from the engine, loss of power, and complete engine failure. The solution is to replace the timing belt. Estimated cost to fix: $600 - $1,200+.

**Transmission**

* **Five-Speed Manual Transmission**

  * **Difficulty shifting gears:** This issue can make driving difficult and potentially dangerous. Symptoms include grinding noises when shifting, difficulty engaging gears, and popping out of gear. The solution is to replace the worn or damaged transmission components. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+.

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** This issue can cause a loss of power to the vehicle, leading to stalling and potential damage to the electrical system. Symptoms include dim or flickering headlights, difficulty starting the vehicle, and warning lights on the dashboard. The solution is to replace the alternator. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+.
* **Battery drain:** This issue can leave the vehicle unable to start. Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle, dim or weak lights, and a rapid loss of battery power. The solution is to find and repair the source of the drain. Estimated cost to fix: Varies depending on the cause.

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** This issue can affect the body panels, frame, and other metal components of the vehicle. Symptoms include peeling or bubbling paint, rust spots, and holes in the metal. The solution is to repair or replace the rusted components. Estimated cost to fix: Varies depending on the severity of the rust.

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracking:** This issue can affect the appearance of the interior and potentially interfere with the function of the dashboard controls. Symptoms include cracks in the dashboard material. The solution is to replace the dashboard. Estimated cost to fix: $500 - $1,000+.
* **Seat fabric tearing:** This issue can affect the appearance and comfort of the interior. Symptoms include tears in the seat fabric. The solution is to repair or replace the seat fabric. Estimated cost to fix: Varies depending on the seat material.

**Summary:**

The 1995 Suzuki Swift is a reliable and affordable subcompact hatchback that offers good fuel efficiency. However, it does have some common issues that can be costly to repair, such as head gasket failure, timing belt failure, and dashboard cracking. The severity and cost of these issues should be taken into consideration when purchasing or owning a Swift.
