**Introduction:**

The 2012 Scion tC is a compact sports coupe powered by a 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine. It was available with a six-speed manual or a six-speed automatic transmission. The tC was praised for its stylish design, powerful engine, and sporty handling. However, it was criticized for its cramped rear seat, lack of cargo space, and poor fuel economy.

**Engine**

**2.5-Liter Four-Cylinder Engine (180 hp/173 lb-ft torque)**

**Engine Knock**
Description/Symptoms: A loud knocking noise coming from the engine.
Solution: Replacement of the engine block
Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Six-Speed Manual Transmission**

**Grinding Gears**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears or a grinding noise when shifting.
Solution: Replacement of the transmission synchronizers
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000-$2,000
Recalls: None Found

**Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

**Slipping Gears**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission fails to engage gears properly, causing the car to lose power.
Solution: Replacement of the transmission valve body
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500-$2,500
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Front Suspension**

**Clunking Noise**
Description/Symptoms: A loud clunking noise coming from the front of the car when going over bumps.
Solution: Replacement of the front strut mounts
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500-$1,000
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Battery Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The battery dies prematurely or fails to hold a charge.
Solution: Replacement of the battery
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$200
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Dashboard Trim Issues**
Description/Symptoms: The dashboard trim rattles or becomes loose.
Solution: Replacement of the dashboard trim
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$500
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Sunroof Issues**
Description/Symptoms: The sunroof fails to open or close properly or leaks water.
Solution: Repair or replacement of the sunroof motor or seals
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500-$1,500
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2012 Scion tC is a reliable and fun-to-drive car, but it is not without its issues. The most common problems are engine knocking, transmission issues, suspension noises, and electrical problems. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the extent of the repairs needed.
