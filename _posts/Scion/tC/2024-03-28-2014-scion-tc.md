**Introduction:**

The 2014 Scion tC is a compact sports coupe powered by a 2.5-liter inline-four engine that produces 180 horsepower and 173 lb-ft of torque. It was offered with either a six-speed manual transmission or a six-speed automatic transmission. The tC was popular for its stylish design, affordability, and engaging driving experience. However, it also had some common issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

**2.5L Inline-Four Engine (Six-Speed Manual Transmission)**

* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** Oil may leak from the valve cover gasket, causing an oil drip.
    * Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $350+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** The timing chain tensioner may fail, leading to engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner and associated components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Inline-Four Engine (Six-Speed Automatic Transmission)**

* **Transmission Fluid Leak:** The transmission may leak fluid from the input or output shaft seals.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking seals and add new transmission fluid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension Noise:** A rattling or clunking sound may come from the front suspension.
    * Solution: Inspect and replace worn suspension components, such as the struts, springs, or sway bar links.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electrical System Malfunction:** The electrical system may experience issues, such as flickering lights, power window failures, or battery drain.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty electrical components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the issue.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Tail Light Moisture:** Moisture may accumulate inside the tail lights.
    * Solution: Seal the taillights or replace them with new ones.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattling:** The dashboard may rattle over rough roads.
    * Solution: Inspect and tighten loose screws or apply foam padding to reduce vibrations.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: DIY or minimal cost
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Climate Control Issues:** The climate control system may malfunction, causing the AC to not work or the heat to be inconsistent.
    * Solution: Diagnose and replace the faulty components, such as the blower motor, heater core, or control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2014 Scion tC is a reasonably reliable vehicle with some minor issues. The engine and suspension problems are the most severe and expensive to fix. However, most of the issues listed here are relatively minor and can be resolved at a reasonable cost.
