# 2016 Scion tC Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Scion tC continues the model's tradition of offering sporty styling and straightforward practicality in a reliable hatchback package. Powered by a dependable four-cylinder engine paired with manual or automatic transmission choices, it generally serves as a low-stress daily driver. Buyers exploring the used market should review owner feedback carefully, keeping in mind that available complaint reports highlight isolated structural and body accessory concerns.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder (1AR-FE)

The 2.5-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces approximately 179 horsepower and 172 lb-ft of torque. It is widely known for strong durability and straightforward maintenance when properly serviced.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The conventional 6-speed manual transmission offers a direct shifting feel and robust mechanical longevity for everyday driving.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission features sequential shift modes, delivering smooth gear changes and reliable daily performance.

## Exterior

### Sunroof / Moonroof

Exterior glass and body panels are generally durable, though owners have submitted sporadic complaints regarding unexpected sunroof glass breakage.

**Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report that the factory glass sunroof or moonroof can suddenly fracture or explode outward while driving at highway speeds without impact from external debris.
- **Solution:** Inspect the glass assembly for stress cracks before purchasing and replace the tempered glass panel if failure occurs.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety equipment generally performs reliably, but isolated owner reports note mechanical issues with seat belt retractors.

**Seat Belt Retractor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The front driver's side seat belt retractor may fail to extend or retract properly, leaving the belt inoperable.
- **Solution:** Have the seat belt assembly inspected by a certified technician and replace the faulty retractor mechanism as needed.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Scion tC remains a solid choice for a used compact coupe, backed by a resilient powertrain and generally positive owner satisfaction. While isolated owner reports note concerns such as unexpected sunroof glass breakage and seat belt retractor wear, these issues do not point to widespread systemic defects. Used car buyers should conduct a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the body, glass, and safety systems to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
