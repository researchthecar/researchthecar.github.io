**Introduction**

The 2016 Scion tC is a compact sports coupe with a 2.5-liter 4-cylinder engine that produces 179 horsepower and 172 lb-ft of torque. It is offered with a 6-speed manual or 6-speed automatic transmission. The tC is known for its sporty handling, spacious interior, and low price point. However, it has been criticized for its lack of features and poor fuel economy.

**Engine**

**2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking coolant, engine overheating
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing chain guide failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise from engine, reduced engine power
    * Solution: Replace timing chain guide
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Clutch wear**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears
    * Solution: Replace clutch
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Torque converter shudder**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vibrations during acceleration, especially when cold
    * Solution: Replace torque converter
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Transmission fluid leak**
    * Description/Symptoms: Leaking transmission fluid, slipping gears
    * Solution: Repair or replace transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front struts leaking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises, reduced ride quality
    * Solution: Replace front struts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, battery discharge
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery goes dead after sitting for a while
    * Solution: Test for parasitic drain, replace battery or electrical components as needed
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof rattle**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattle from sunroof, especially when driving over bumps
    * Solution: Adjust sunroof alignment, lubricate sunroof tracks
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Door panel rattling**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise from door panels
    * Solution: Tighten door panel bolts, add insulation
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Heated seat failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Heated seats not working
    * Solution: Replace heated seat element
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2016 Scion tC is a reliable and affordable sports coupe. However, it has some potential issues, including engine gasket failure, timing chain guide failure, clutch wear, and torque converter shudder. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but some can be expensive to repair. Overall, the tC is a good value for the price, but it is important to be aware of the potential issues before purchasing.
