**Introduction**

The 2016 Scion FR-S is a compact sports car that offers agile handling, a responsive powertrain, and a budget-friendly price tag. It is available with a 2.0-liter naturally aspirated Boxer-4 engine that produces 200 horsepower and 151 lb-ft of torque, paired with either a 6-speed manual or 6-speed automatic transmission. Drivers appreciate its sharp handling, engaging driving experience, and affordability, but some criticize its cramped interior, limited cargo space, and lack of advanced features.

**Engine**

**2.0-Liter Boxer-4**

* **Valve Spring Failure:** Symptoms include a loud rattling noise from the engine, reduced power, and potential engine damage. Solution: Replacement of the valve springs and associated components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Oil Consumption Issue:** Some vehicles experience excessive oil consumption, requiring frequent top-ups. Solution: Replacement of piston rings and other internal components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+
* **Rear Main Seal Leak:** The rear main seal can fail, resulting in an oil leak. Solution: Replacement of the seal and associated gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Fuel Pump Failure:** In some cases, the fuel pump may fail,导致的困难启动, reduced power, and stalling. Solution: Replacement of the fuel pump. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual**

* **Shifter Linkage Adjustment Issue:** The shifter linkage may become misaligned, resulting in difficulty shifting gears. Solution: Adjustment or replacement of the shifter linkage. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **Clutch Failure:** In some instances, the clutch may fail prematurely, leading to slipping or difficulty engaging gears. Solution: Replacement of the clutch assembly. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000

**Suspension**

* **Strut Top Bearing Failure:** The front strut top bearings may wear prematurely, resulting in a clunking or grinding noise when turning. Solution: Replacement of the strut top bearings. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
* **Rear Sway Bar Link Failure:** The rear sway bar links can fail, causing a rattling noise and reduced handling stability. Solution: Replacement of the sway bar links. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
* **Rear Spring Sagging:** Some vehicles may experience rear spring sagging, leading to a decrease in ride height and handling. Solution: Replacement of the rear springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800

**Electrical**

* **Headlight Wiring Harness Melting:** The headlight wiring harness can overheat and melt, causing headlight failure. Solution: Replacement of the wiring harness. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator may fail, resulting in charging system issues and potential battery drain. Solution: Replacement of the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Battery Drain:** Some vehicles may experience a parasitic battery drain, leading to a dead battery. Solution: Identification and repair of the source of the drain. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000

**Exterior**

* **Cracked Windshield:** The windshield can crack prematurely due to temperature changes or road debris impact. Solution: Replacement of the windshield. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Clear Coat Peeling:** The clear coat on the paint can peel or fade in some cases. Solution: Repainting or touch-up of the affected areas. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
* **Loose Body Panels:** Some vehicles may experience loose body panels, such as the hood or trunk lid. Solution: Adjustment or tightening of the mounting hardware. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattle:** The dashboard may rattle or make noise over bumps. Solution: Identification and reinforcement of the source of the rattle. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
* **Seat Belt Sensor Failure:** The seat belt sensor may fail, causing the seat belt warning light to stay illuminated or the airbags to not deploy in the event of an accident. Solution: Replacement of the seat belt sensor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
* **Door Lock Actuator Failure:** The door lock actuator may fail, resulting in difficulty locking or unlocking the doors. Solution: Replacement of the actuator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400

**Summary**

The 2016 Scion FR-S is a fun-to-drive sports car with a budget-friendly price. However, it has several notable issues, including valve spring failure, excessive oil consumption, a rear main seal leak, and other engine problems. The transmission may also experience issues, particularly with the 6-speed manual. Suspension and electrical problems are less common but can still occur. Exterior issues, such as cracked windshields and clear coat peeling, may also affect the vehicle's appearance. Interior problems include dashboard rattles, seat belt sensor failures, and door lock actuator failures. Overall, the Scion FR-S has a moderate severity of issues, with repair costs ranging from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars. It is important for potential buyers to be aware of these issues before making a purchase decision.
