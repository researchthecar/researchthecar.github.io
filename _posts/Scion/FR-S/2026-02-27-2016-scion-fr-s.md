# 2016 Scion FR-S Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Scion FR-S offers an engaging, rear-wheel-drive sports car experience co-developed with Subaru, featuring sharp handling and a driver-focused cockpit. While generally well-regarded by driving enthusiasts, used car shoppers should review powertrain maintenance records and listen closely for mechanical noises during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Horizontally-Opposed 4-Cylinder (FA20)

The 2.0-liter naturally aspirated boxer four-cylinder engine produces approximately 200 horsepower and 151 lb-ft of torque. It delivers lively performance and high-revving fun, though owners should monitor for characteristic valvetrain and fuel pump noises.

**High Pressure Fuel Pump Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice an audible ticking or squealing noise coming from the high-pressure fuel pump area at idle, which can indicate a potential pump malfunction.
- **Solution:** Have the fuel pump inspected by a qualified technician and replaced if internal wear or abnormal noise exceeds normal operating parameters.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The 6-speed manual transmission pairs directly with the rear-wheel-drive layout for an athletic, hands-on driving experience, though manual clutches and bearings require careful pre-purchase inspection.

**Throw-Out Bearing Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A squealing or chirping noise present when the vehicle is started or idling with the clutch engaged can indicate premature wear of the throw-out bearing.
- **Solution:** Replace the failing throw-out bearing with updated revised parts and high-temperature grease designed to prevent premature drying out.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission with Paddle Shifters

The 6-speed automatic transmission provides smooth daily shifts and manual control via steering wheel paddle shifters, making it a reliable cruiser for everyday driving.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Scion FR-S is an appealing choice for driving enthusiasts seeking sharp handling and rear-wheel-drive dynamics. Buyers should carefully evaluate potential clutch noises and fuel pump operation during a thorough test drive and pre-purchase inspection to ensure long-term reliability.
