# 2015 Scion iQ

### Overview

However, like any vehicle, specific components can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas and their associated costs for the 2015 model year.

### Standard Electrical System

- **Symptoms:** Premature battery discharge, difficulty starting.
- **Solution:** Diagnose and replace faulty parasitic draw component (e.g., accessory, module) or upgrade battery.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

**Infotainment System Glitches**

### Summary Verdict
The 2015 Scion iQ is generally a reliable vehicle, particularly in its powertrain and mechanical components. The 1.3L engine and CVT transmission have proven to be robust for their intended use. The most commonly reported technical issues tend to be in the electrical system, specifically related to battery drain and occasional infotainment system glitches. These are not typically catastrophic failures but can be inconvenient and costly to diagnose and repair. Exterior and interior components, along with suspension, show good reliability with no widespread reported problems. Overall, the iQ offers dependable transportation for city dwellers, provided potential electrical gremlins are addressed.
