# 2012 Scion iQ

### Overview
The 2012 Scion iQ, a subcompact city car, is known for its exceptional fuel efficiency and compact size, making it ideal for urban environments. Its unique three-plus-one seating arrangement and tight turning radius are key selling points. From a reliability standpoint, the iQ generally performs well, benefiting from Toyota's engineering and manufacturing standards. Owners often praise its low running costs and straightforward mechanicals. However, like any vehicle, it has specific areas that have seen more attention than others, particularly concerning its unique powertrain and some interior features. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance.

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Scion iQ is generally a reliable vehicle, with its powertrain and chassis components exhibiting strong durability. The most commonly cited technical issues tend to be minor, such as occasional infotainment system glitches or slight hesitations with the CVT. Driver's seat wear is also a point of note, largely due to the vehicle's design and intended use. Overall, for its segment and age, the iQ offers a dependable ownership experience with minimal major mechanical concerns, provided routine maintenance is followed.
