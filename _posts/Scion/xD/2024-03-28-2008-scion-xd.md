**Introduction:**

The 2008 Scion xD is a subcompact hatchback that became popular for its youthful design and affordability. It was available with two engine and transmission options: a 1.8L 4-cylinder engine paired with a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission, and a more powerful 2.4L 4-cylinder engine paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. The hatchback was praised for its spacious interior, fuel efficiency, and playful handling. However, it also had some shortcomings, including limited power, a cramped back seat, and an outdated interior.

**Engine**

**1.8L 4-Cylinder**
* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** Slight leakage from under the valve cover due to a faulty gasket. **Solution:** Replace valve cover gasket. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $300
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** Premature failure of the timing chain tensioner, leading to engine timing issues and potential damage. **Solution:** Replace timing chain tensioner and timing chain. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,200 - $1,500+ **Recalls:** NHTSA Campaign Number: 14V236000

**2.4L 4-Cylinder**
* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**
* **Shifting Difficulty:** Difficulty shifting into certain gears due to worn or damaged synchronizers. **Solution:** Replace or repair synchronizers. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $700+

**4-Speed Automatic**
* **Transmission Slipping:** Loss of power or delayed engagement while driving due to worn clutches or solenoid issues. **Solution:** Overhaul or replace transmission. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2,000 - $3,000+
* **Torque Converter Shudder:** Vibration or shuddering when accelerating or shifting due to a faulty torque converter. **Solution:** Replace torque converter. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,000 - $1,500+

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Bushing Deterioration:** Deteriorating or broken rear suspension bushings, leading to excessive noise and poor handling. **Solution:** Replace rear suspension bushings. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Premature failure of the alternator, leading to charging system issues and potential battery drain. **Solution:** Replace alternator. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $400 - $600
* **Battery Drain:** Excessive battery drain when the vehicle is parked due to a faulty component or short circuit. **Solution:** Diagnose and repair the source of the drain. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $500+
* **Power Window Failure:** Failure of one or more power window motors, preventing operation of the windows. **Solution:** Replace faulty power window motor or control module. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400 per window

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:** Moisture accumulation inside the headlight assemblies due to poor sealing. **Solution:** Replace or reseal headlight assemblies. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattles:** Rattling or buzzing noises from the dashboard due to loose or misaligned panels. **Solution:** Tighten or replace loose panels. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $200
* **Climate Control Panel Failure:** Failure of the climate control panel, preventing adjustment of the temperature or fan settings. **Solution:** Replace climate control panel. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $500+

**Summary:**

The 2008 Scion xD has some common issues that owners should be aware of. The most severe issues include timing chain tensioner failure and transmission problems, which can be costly to repair. However, many of the other issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix. Overall, the xD is a reliable and affordable vehicle, but potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems before making a purchase.
