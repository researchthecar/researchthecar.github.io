# 2005 Scion xA

### Overview
The 2005 Scion xA, a subcompact hatchback, was known for its affordability and fuel efficiency. As a Toyota-derived product, it generally inherited a reputation for solid reliability, especially for its era. Owners often praised its straightforward mechanics and low running costs. While not a performance-oriented vehicle, its design focused on practicality and dependability for daily commuting and urban driving. This analysis focuses on common reported issues beyond routine maintenance, aiming to provide a clear picture of its long-term mechanical and electrical health.

## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, difficulty starting, dead battery.
- **Solution:** Replace alternator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Power Window Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow operation, grinding noises.
- **Solution:** Replace power window motor and regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2005 Scion xA is generally considered a reliable vehicle, particularly in its powertrain. The 1.5L engine and both transmission options (manual and automatic) rarely present significant issues beyond normal wear. The most commonly reported non-wear-related problems tend to be in the electrical system, specifically alternator failures and power window motor issues. Interior components like the HVAC blower motor can also be a point of failure. Suspension and exterior components are typically robust. Overall, the xA offers good value with predictable maintenance needs, with electrical and HVAC being the primary areas to monitor for potential repairs.
