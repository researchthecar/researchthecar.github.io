### Overview
The 2004 Scion xA, a subcompact hatchback, was known for its affordability and fuel efficiency. ## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light illuminated, difficulty starting the engine, complete loss of electrical power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Power Window Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow window operation, grinding noises when operating windows.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the power window motor and regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400 per window


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Trim
**Clear Coat Peeling**
- **Symptoms:** Fading paint, bubbling or peeling clear coat, particularly on the roof and hood.
- **Solution:** Repainting affected areas or a full vehicle repaint.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000+


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, intermittent airflow, unusual noises from the dashboard when HVAC is on.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor and resistor.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2004 Scion xA is generally a reliable vehicle, with its powertrain components (engine and transmission) showing very few widespread issues. The most commonly reported problems tend to be related to the electrical system, specifically alternator failures and power window motor issues. Exterior paint degradation, such as clear coat peeling, is also a concern for some owners, often due to age and sun exposure. Interior electrical components like the HVAC blower motor can also fail. Despite these potential issues, the xA remains a solid choice for budget-conscious buyers seeking dependable transportation, provided these common wear items are addressed.
