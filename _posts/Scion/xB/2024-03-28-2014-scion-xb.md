**Introduction**
The 2014 Scion xB is a subcompact hatchback offered with a single engine and transmission option. The 2.4L inline-four engine produces 158 horsepower and 162 ft-lb of torque, paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. The xB is known for its boxy design and spacious interior, but it has also been criticized for its lack of power and outdated technology.

**Engine**
* 2.4L inline-four

**Transmission**
* 4-speed automatic

**Suspension**
* **Front suspension clunking**
    * Description/Symptoms: A clunking noise coming from the front suspension, especially over bumps or rough roads.
    * Solution: Replacing the front strut mounts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **Rear suspension sagging**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear of the vehicle sagging due to weak or broken rear springs.
    * Solution: Replacing the rear springs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**Electrical**
* **Battery drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery drains prematurely, especially when the vehicle is parked.
    * Solution: Identifying and repairing electrical drains, such as a faulty alternator or parasitic battery draw.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the source of the drain
* **Headlight condensation**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture or condensation forming inside the headlight housings.
    * Solution: Replacing the headlight assemblies
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ per headlight

**Exterior**
* **Door handle failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The exterior door handles becoming loose or inoperable.
    * Solution: Replacing the affected door handle
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+ per handle

**Interior**
* **Dashboard rattling**
    * Description/Symptoms: A rattling noise coming from the dashboard, particularly over uneven surfaces.
    * Solution: Tightening or replacing loose dashboard components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
* **Seatbelt sensor malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The seatbelt warning light staying on or intermittent chimes when the seatbelt is fastened.
    * Solution: Replacing the seatbelt buckle or sensor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Summary**
The 2014 Scion xB is a relatively reliable vehicle with some notable issues. Suspension problems are the most common, followed by electrical and exterior issues. The severity of the issues can vary, with some being minor annoyances and others requiring more significant repairs. The cost of repairs can also vary significantly depending on the specific issue and the labor rates in your area.
