**Introduction:**

The 2009 Scion xB is a subcompact hatchback known for its boxy design, spacious interior, and affordable price. It is available with two engine options: a 1.5-liter inline-four and a 2.4-liter inline-four. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual, 4-speed automatic, and 5-speed automatic. Despite its popularity, the 2009 Scion xB has been plagued by several issues that have affected various components.

**Engine**

**1.5-Liter Inline-Four**

* **Valve Spring Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature failure of valve springs, leading to loss of engine power, increased oil consumption, and engine noise.
    * Solution: Replacement of affected valve springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+.
    * Recalls: None Found.

**2.4-Liter Inline-Four**

* **No Issues Found.**

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Shifting Difficulty:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, especially into reverse.
    * Solution: Adjustment or replacement of shift linkage and/or transmission mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+.
    * Recalls: None Found.

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slipping:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission loses power while driving, resulting in difficulty accelerating.
    * Solution: Rebuild or replacement of transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+.
    * Recalls: None Found.

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **No Issues Found.**

**Suspension**

* **Strut Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature failure of front or rear struts, leading to excessive vibrations, bouncing, and decreased handling.
    * Solution: Replacement of struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+.
    * Recalls: None Found.

**Electrical**

* **Battery Draining:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery drains while the vehicle is parked, leading to a dead battery.
    * Solution: Removal and replacement of defective battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+.
    * Recalls: None Found.

* **Radio Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Radio loses power or functionality intermittently or completely.
    * Solution: Reset or replacement of radio unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+.
    * Recalls: None Found.

**Exterior**

* **Door Lock Actuator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Power door locks fail to function, leaving doors unlocked or locked.
    * Solution: Replacement of defective door lock actuator(s).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+.
    * Recalls: None Found.

**Interior**

* **Seat Recliner Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Seat recliners break or fail to function properly.
    * Solution: Replacement of seat recliner mechanism.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+.
    * Recalls: None Found.

* **Dashboard Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dashboard cracks prematurely or develops gaps.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of dashboard.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+.
    * Recalls: None Found.

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2009 Scion xB has experienced several issues that can be inconvenient and costly to repair. The most serious issue involves transmission failure, which can lead to expensive replacements. Suspension issues and electrical problems can also affect the vehicle's performance and safety. Interior problems are relatively minor but can still be annoying to deal with. Potential owners should be aware of these issues before purchasing a 2009 Scion xB and factor in the potential costs of repairs into their decision.
