**Introduction:**

The 2015 Scion xB is a compact hatchback that was discontinued in 2015. The vehicle was powered by a 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine that produced 158 horsepower and 162 lb-ft of torque. It was paired with either a five-speed manual or four-speed automatic transmission. The xB was known for its boxy styling, spacious interior, and fuel efficiency. However, the vehicle had some reliability issues that can be concerning to potential buyers.

**Engine**

* **2.4L Four-Cylinder Engine**
    * **Oil Consumption:** Excess oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage. **Solution:** Engine rebuild or replacement. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2,000 - $4,000+
    * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** The timing chain tensioner fails, causing the timing chain to skip and potentially lead to engine damage. **Solution:** Replace timing chain tensioner. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000
    * **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** A leak in the valve cover gasket can allow oil to leak into the spark plug wells, causing misfires and engine damage. **Solution:** Replace valve cover gasket. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400

**Transmission**

* **Five-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **Clutch Failure:** Premature clutch failure, leading to slipping and difficulty engaging gears. **Solution:** Replace clutch. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000
* **Four-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Transmission Slips:** The transmission slips or hesitates during gear changes. **Solution:** Transmission rebuild or replacement. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $1,500 - $3,000+

**Suspension**

* **Rear Shock Absorbers:** Premature wear of rear shock absorbers, causing a bumpy ride and reduced handling. **Solution:** Replace rear shock absorbers. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $400 - $800
* **Front Sway Bar Links:** Worn front sway bar links can cause clunking noises and affect handling. **Solution:** Replace sway bar links. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator fails to charge the battery, leading to electrical problems and potential engine stalls. **Solution:** Replace alternator. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $800
* **Starter Failure:** The starter fails to engage the engine, preventing the vehicle from starting. **Solution:** Replace starter. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $600

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Struts:** Weak tailgate struts fail to hold the tailgate open, leading to a potential safety hazard. **Solution:** Replace tailgate struts. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Issues:** Malfunctioning climate control system, leading to insufficient heating or cooling. **Solution:** Diagnose and repair climate control components. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000+
* **Window Regulator Failure:** The window regulators fail, causing windows to stick or become inoperable. **Solution:** Replace window regulators. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $300 - $600

**Summary**

The 2015 Scion xB has some reliability issues that can be costly to fix. The most serious issues include oil consumption, timing chain tensioner failure, and transmission problems. While some issues may not be severe, they can still be annoying and affect the overall ownership experience. It's important for potential buyers to be aware of these issues and factor in the potential cost of repairs when considering the purchase of this vehicle.
