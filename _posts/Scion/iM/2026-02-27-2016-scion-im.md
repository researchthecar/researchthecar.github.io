# 2016 Scion iM Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Scion iM is a practical hatchback that benefits from Toyota's reputation for dependable engineering, offering buyers a straightforward commuter car. While overall owner satisfaction is high, used car shoppers should review reported owner complaints regarding transmission behavior and minor electrical or interior quirks before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 1.8L 4-Cylinder (2ZR-FAE)

Powered by a 1.8-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine producing approximately 137 horsepower and 126 lb-ft of torque. This powerplant is generally robust and shares its core architecture with other reliable Toyota models.

**Rough Idle**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle develops a rough idle while stopped at traffic lights after the engine has warmed up.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the throttle body, spark plugs, and fuel system for carbon buildup or sensor anomalies.

## Transmission

### CVT Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) featuring simulated gear steps. Buyers should test drive thoroughly to ensure smooth acceleration and proper gear engagement.

**Transmission Slipping and High RPMs**
- **Symptoms:** The automatic transmission occasionally slips, gets stuck in high RPMs without upshifting properly, or fails to accelerate adequately.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition and level, and have a transmission specialist scan the control module for fault codes.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

Paired with a traditional 6-speed manual transmission for drivers preferring a more connected shifting experience. Manual variants are generally sturdy, though clutch wear depends heavily on prior driving habits.

**Clutch Slippage and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The clutch pedal assembly or disc begins slipping while driving at highway speeds, eventually losing power delivery to the wheels.
- **Solution:** Inspect the clutch disc, pressure plate, and hydraulic slave cylinder, and replace worn components as necessary.

## Interior

### Rear Headrests

Cabin ergonomics are generally comfortable, though specific design elements like rear headrest placement can impact outward visibility.

**Obstructed Rear Visibility**
- **Symptoms:** The rear seat headrests are tall and create significant blind spots on both sides of the rear window.
- **Solution:** Adjust or remove the rear headrests when rear passengers are not present to improve rearward visibility.

## Safety

### Horn Assembly

Handles auxiliary vehicle electronics and driver-control systems. Owners have occasionally noted issues with peripheral components like the horn.

**Intermittent or Non-Working Horn**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle horn operates intermittently before failing completely, creating a safety hazard when attempting to alert other drivers.
- **Solution:** Test the horn fuse, relay, and steering wheel clockspring, and replace the horn unit if it has failed internally.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Scion iM is a dependable used hatchback choice that benefits from straightforward mechanical underpinnings. Prospective buyers should carefully evaluate the operation of either the CVT or manual transmission during a test drive, and verify that all electrical accessories and safety features function as expected.
