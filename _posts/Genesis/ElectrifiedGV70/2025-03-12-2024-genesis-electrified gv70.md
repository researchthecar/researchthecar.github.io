The 2024 Genesis Electrified GV70 is an all-electric luxury SUV that offers a blend of performance, technology, and sophisticated design. It is solely available as an all-electric vehicle. The Electrified GV70 boasts a dual-motor all-wheel-drive system producing a combined 429 horsepower and 516 lb-ft of torque. (In boost mode, it can temporarily increase to 483 hp). People appreciate the Electrified GV70 for its smooth and quiet ride, luxurious interior, and fast charging capabilities. Some common dislikes include the higher price point compared to gas-powered competitors and range limitations that come with electric vehicles.

**Electric Motor**

**Dual-Motor AWD (429 hp / 516 lb-ft)**

*   **Reduced Power/Turtle Mode**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vehicle enters "turtle mode" with severely reduced power. This often happens during longer drives, especially at higher speeds or with frequent acceleration. Diagnostic codes may indicate issues with battery temperature management or motor control.
    *   Solution: Genesis dealerships are investigating and applying software updates to improve battery thermal management. In some cases, faulty battery management system (BMS) components or drive motor components may need replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $1,000+ depending on the issue being addressed under warranty
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Battery System**

*   **Charging Issues/Slow Charging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Difficulty initiating charging, slow charging speeds (especially at DC fast charging stations), or the charging process terminating unexpectedly. Error messages related to battery health or charging system may appear.
    *   Solution: Dealerships are investigating and replacing faulty charging ports, battery management system (BMS) modules, or sections of the high-voltage battery pack in some cases. Software updates related to charging control and communication protocols have also been released.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $500+ depending on the issue being addressed under warranty
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen freezes, becomes unresponsive, or restarts unexpectedly. This can affect navigation, audio, climate controls, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Dealerships are attempting to resolve the issue with software updates for the infotainment system. If the issue persists, the head unit may need replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $1,000+ depending on if replaced under warranty.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Digital Key Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The digital key feature (using a smartphone to lock/unlock and start the vehicle) experiences intermittent connection issues, fails to authenticate, or doesn't work at all.
    *   Solution: Potential fixes involve updating the Genesis app and the vehicle's software. Sometimes, a reset or re-pairing of the digital key is required. In more severe cases, the vehicle's communication module may need to be inspected.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $200+ depending on if replaced under warranty.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Automatic Tailgate Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The automatic tailgate malfunctions, sometimes failing to open or close correctly. It may also become stuck or emit unusual noises during operation.
    *   Solution: Technicians may recalibrate the tailgate or replace faulty actuators and sensors that control the tailgate's movement. Damaged hinges can also contribute to the issue and may need replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $500+ depending on if replaced under warranty.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Advanced Driver Assistance System (ADAS) Errors**
    *   Description/Symptoms: False warnings or malfunctions related to ADAS features, such as lane keeping assist, adaptive cruise control, and blind spot monitoring. Sensors may be overly sensitive or fail to detect objects properly.
    *   Solution: Calibration of the ADAS sensors and modules is typically necessary. The windshield and front camera may also need to be inspected or replaced. Additionally, software updates might be required to address known bugs.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $500+ depending on if replaced under warranty.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Driver's seat adjustment malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Electric seat adjustment functions will not operate when the user presses the controls, often accompanied by a clicking sound or a message on the dash.
    *   Solution: The seat's motor or control module will likely need replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $500+ depending on if replaced under warranty.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2024 Genesis Electrified GV70, while praised for its luxury and performance, experiences some common issues, particularly with the electrical system, including the infotainment system and ADAS features. Issues with battery charging and reduced power can also be problematic. Most of these issues, if addressed under warranty, will have minimal to no cost. However, out-of-warranty repairs on these advanced systems can become quite expensive. Buyers should be aware of these potential problems and thoroughly inspect the vehicle's systems before purchase.
