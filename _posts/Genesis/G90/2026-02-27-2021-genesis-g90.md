# 2021 Genesis G90 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Genesis G90 represents a compelling flagship luxury sedan experience, offering impressive comfort, high-end amenities, and robust powertrain performance. While generally praised for its refined road manners, prospective buyers should review owner-reported concerns regarding electronics and steering behavior to ensure a comprehensive evaluation during a test drive.

---

## Engine

### 3.3L Twin-Turbocharged V6

This 3.3-liter twin-turbo V6 produces approximately 365 horsepower and 376 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth and responsive acceleration. It offers a balanced blend of everyday drivability and luxury-grade refinement for this flagship sedan.

### 5.0L Naturally Aspirated V8

The 5.0-liter naturally aspirated V8 delivers a robust 420 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque for effortless power delivery. It is a traditional luxury powerplant, though owners should verify proper maintenance history.

**Engine Stalling and Shaking**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle begins to shake, emits smoke, and stalls out completely, failing to restart or remain running.
- **Solution:** Have a certified mechanic or dealership perform a complete powertrain diagnostic to identify fuel or electrical failures.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

This smooth-shifting 8-speed automatic pairs seamlessly with both available engine options and rear-wheel or all-wheel drive configurations. It delivers reliable power delivery suited for a high-end luxury vehicle.

## Suspension

### Factory Wheels and Tires

The suspension delivers a plush ride quality typical of executive sedans, though routine inspection of tire wear patterns is recommended.

**Factory Tire Sizing Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported receiving vehicles equipped with potentially mismatched or improperly sized tires directly from the factory.
- **Solution:** Verify that all installed tires match the manufacturer placard specifications during a pre-purchase inspection.

## Steering

### Power Steering System

Steering components generally provide precise control, but intermittent electronic assist behavior has been noted in isolated owner reports.

**Steering Wheel Jerking and Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** Steering wheel unexpectedly jerks left and right while driving at highway speeds, accompanied by a flickering power steering warning light.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic power steering module, wiring harness, and steering angle sensors for faults.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Display Console

Electrical architecture supports numerous luxury amenities, though central screen reliability warrants thorough testing before purchase.

**Center Console Screen Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The main infotainment and viewing display screen completely fails and goes black, disabling access to crucial safety systems like the backup and front cameras.
- **Solution:** Have the dealership or a qualified technician check display module connections, software, or replace the head unit if necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V191000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Gasoline:Turbo/Supercharger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Genesis&model=G90&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An oil leak in the presence of hot engine components can increase the risk of an engine compartment fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the left turbocharger oil feed pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Genesis G90 delivers impressive luxury and performance, making it an attractive pre-owned flagship option. Buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle's electronics, infotainment displays, and steering systems during a pre-purchase evaluation to ensure all comfort and safety features function correctly.
