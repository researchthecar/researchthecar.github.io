# 2017 Genesis G90 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Genesis G90 is a flagship luxury sedan offering exceptional comfort, high-end features, and robust powertrain options. While owner feedback highlights its impressive value and luxurious ride, a small number of drivers have reported mechanical and electrical concerns, ranging from intermittent transmission hesitation to steering and cooling anomalies.

---

## Engine

### 3.3L Twin-Turbocharged V6

A 3.3-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine producing approximately 365 horsepower and 376 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth and responsive acceleration for everyday driving.

### 5.0L Naturally Aspirated V8

A robust 5.0-liter V8 engine generating around 420 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, delivering effortless high-speed performance and refined luxury driving dynamics.

**Check Engine Warning and Cooling Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected check engine warning lights illuminating while driving, alongside occasional cooling system concerns leading to overheating.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician diagnose the fault codes, inspect cooling components such as the thermostat and water pump, and repair or replace faulty parts as needed.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission paired with either rear-wheel drive or all-wheel drive, designed to deliver seamless gear changes and smooth power delivery.

**Transmission Hesitation and Shifting Irregularities**
- **Symptoms:** Some drivers experience delayed response or sluggish acceleration when turning from a stop, as well as occasional harsh or jerky shifting at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition, update control modules if applicable, and consult a dealership or specialist for diagnostic evaluation.

## Steering

### Steering System

Power-assisted steering setup providing light effort at low speeds and steady stability on the highway.

**Steering Wheel Sticking**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel may stick during left or right turns, requiring the driver to manually return it to the neutral position.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering column, linkages, and electronic power steering components for mechanical binding or sensor failure.

## Exterior

### Lighting and Electronics

Comprehensive electrical architecture supporting advanced driver assistance features, comfort amenities, and exterior lighting systems.

**Headlight Adjustment and Electrical Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Auto-leveling headlights may fail to maintain proper adjustment, causing low beams to point either too high or too low, affecting nighttime visibility.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight leveling sensors and wiring harnesses, and recalibrate or replace faulty components.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V538000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Exhaust System:Manifold/Header/Muffler/Tail Pipe](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Genesis&model=G90&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Oil leaking onto the exhaust manifold increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will replace the turbocharger oil supply pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V107000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly:Solenoid](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Genesis&model=G90&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Water may enter the starter solenoid and cause an electrical short, which can result in an engine compartment fire while parked or driving. An engine compartment fire while parked or driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a remedy relay kit in the engine junction box, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V191000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Gasoline:Turbo/Supercharger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Genesis&model=G90&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** An oil leak in the presence of hot engine components can increase the risk of an engine compartment fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the left turbocharger oil feed pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Genesis G90 remains a compelling choice for used-car buyers seeking executive-level luxury at a fraction of its original price. While most owners enjoy a trouble-free experience, prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle's powertrain responsiveness, steering operation, and cooling systems during a pre-purchase inspection.
