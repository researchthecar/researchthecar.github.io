Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints for the 2024 Genesis GV80, categorized by component and engine/transmission configuration. The 2024 Genesis GV80 is a luxury SUV lauded for its stylish design, comfortable ride, and generous features. However, like any vehicle, it's not without its potential problems. While owners appreciate its elegant interior, advanced technology, and value proposition compared to European rivals, some have voiced concerns about certain aspects, including infotainment glitches and the availability of replacement parts. The GV80 offers a choice of two engines: a 2.5L turbocharged inline-4 producing 300 horsepower and 311 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.5L twin-turbocharged V6 delivering 375 horsepower and 391 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission.

**Engine**

**2.5L Turbocharged Inline-4**

*   **Engine Hesitation/Stalling**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported instances of the engine hesitating or stalling, particularly during acceleration or when coming to a stop. This may be intermittent and not easily reproducible at the dealership.
    *   Solution: The exact cause is difficult to pinpoint and may require software updates, fuel system cleaning, or sensor replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+ (depending on the root cause and necessary repairs)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.5L Twin-Turbocharged V6**

*   **Oil Leak**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported oil leaks from the turbocharger oil supply lines. Symptoms include seeing oil spots under the car and the smell of burning oil.
    *   Solution: Replace the turbocharger oil supply lines and associated gaskets/seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic (Both Engines)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Clunking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners experience harsh or jerky shifting, particularly between lower gears. A clunking noise may also be present during shifts.
    *   Solution: Software update to the transmission control module (TCM). If the problem persists, replacement of valve body or complete transmission replacement may be needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $6,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Air Suspension Malfunction (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Models equipped with air suspension may experience issues such as the vehicle not leveling properly, error messages on the dashboard, or the suspension failing to adjust.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis may involve checking the air compressor, air struts, height sensors, and related wiring. Replacement of faulty components may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Rebooting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may freeze, become unresponsive, or spontaneously reboot. This can affect navigation, audio, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Software update to the infotainment system. Replacement of the head unit may be needed if the problem persists.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,500+ (head unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Sensor Malfunctions (e.g., Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Faulty sensors can lead to inaccurate readings, false alerts, or complete system failure. This can affect safety features like parking assist, blind spot monitoring, and lane keeping assist.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and replace the faulty sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per sensor
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain, particularly in vehicles with a lot of electronic features.
    *   Solution: Perform a parasitic draw test to isolate the cause. Update software that may be causing the draw, or replace faulty components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report rattling or squeaking noises emanating from various interior components, such as the dashboard, door panels, or seats.
    *   Solution: Locate the source of the noise and apply felt tape or other dampening materials to prevent friction and vibration.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $500+ (depending on complexity and whether professional help is needed)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2024 Genesis GV80 generally presents with issues that range from minor inconveniences (like infotainment glitches and interior rattles) to more serious concerns like engine oil leaks or transmission problems. Electrical issues, particularly those involving the infotainment system and sensors, seem to be relatively common. The potential cost to fix these issues varies widely depending on the specific problem, with engine and transmission repairs potentially being the most expensive. While the GV80 offers a luxurious experience, potential buyers should be aware of these common complaints and thoroughly inspect any vehicle before purchase.
