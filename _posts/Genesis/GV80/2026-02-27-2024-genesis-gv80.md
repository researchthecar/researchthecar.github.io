# 2024 Genesis GV80 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury midsize SUV, the luxury-focused features and striking design often stand out first. However, reviewing owner-submitted feedback is an essential part of evaluating overall dependability. While many enjoy a plush ride and advanced cabin technology, early owner reports highlight a few electronic, electrical, and powertrain quirks that prospective buyers should keep on their radar.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L Turbocharged Inline-4

This base 2.5-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces around 300 horsepower and 311 lb-ft of torque, offering brisk acceleration for daily driving. It provides a solid balance of efficiency and power for everyday commuting without feeling underpowered.

### 3.5L Twin-Turbocharged V6

The available 3.5-liter twin-turbo V6 delivers a robust 375 horsepower and 391 lb-ft of torque for effortless highway passing. It pairs smooth power delivery with high-end refinement, though buyers should verify all electronic software updates have been applied.

**Unexpected Powertrain Shutdown and Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report that the vehicle can unexpectedly shut down while driving at highway speeds, shifting itself into neutral and accessory mode without prior warning.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle inspected by a certified dealership to check for powertrain control module updates, electrical faults, or related service campaigns.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The smooth-shifting 8-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly to the rear wheels or available all-wheel-drive systems. It generally operates transparently in the background, contributing to a refined driving character.

## Steering

### Steering Assembly

Power-assisted steering is responsive and weighted appropriately for a luxury cruiser, though isolated mechanical noise concerns have been noted.

**Low-Pitch Steering Hum**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice an abnormal low-pitched humming noise developing from the steering system during normal operation.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealership to evaluate the steering column and apply any relevant manufacturer service bulletins or component adjustments.

## Exterior

### Windshield Glass

Exterior styling is highlighted by expansive glass panels and distinctive lighting signatures, though certain glass components may require extra vigilance against road hazards.

**Prone Windshield Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported that the factory windshield is unusually susceptible to chipping and cracking from minor road debris at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect the windshield thoroughly prior to purchase and verify comprehensive auto glass insurance coverage.

## Safety

### Forward Collision-Avoidance Assist

Advanced driver assistance features offer comprehensive protection, but sensitive sensors can occasionally trigger unwarranted alerts or braking events.

**False Positive Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may intermittently apply the brakes abruptly while starting from a stop, accompanied by a collision warning when no obstructions are present.
- **Solution:** Check for sensor calibrations or software updates at an authorized service center to resolve radar and camera misinterpretations.

### Power Folding Seats

The upscale cabin provides flexible cargo configurations via power-operated components, though users should exercise caution with automated seat controls.

**Rear Seat Folding Mechanism Lacks Obstruction Stop**
- **Symptoms:** The power-folding rear seats may continue to actuate when a passenger remains seated and the control button is pressed, creating a potential safety hazard.
- **Solution:** Avoid operating power seat features while occupants are seated, and have the switch assembly or control module inspected if malfunction persists.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V105000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Genesis&model=GV80&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 101, "Controls and Displays." An instrument panel display that fails to show critical safety information, such as the speedometer or warning lights, increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and update the instrument panel display software, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V229000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Genesis&model=GV80&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten, or replace the fuel pipe as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The vehicle delivers an appealing blend of upscale comfort, strong powertrain options, and impressive technology. While most owners enjoy a trouble-free ownership experience, prospective buyers should carefully inspect electrical systems, test-drive to verify smooth powertrain operation, and ensure all open service bulletins or recalls have been addressed by a certified dealer.
