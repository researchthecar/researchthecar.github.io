# 2024 Genesis GV60 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Genesis GV60 is a striking and luxurious all-electric crossover that shares its advanced platform with other acclaimed EV offerings from the Hyundai-Kia group. While early adopters praise its premium interior, rapid acceleration, and cutting-edge tech features, used car shoppers should review reported owner complaints regarding electrical glitches and sporadic display failures. As an advanced EV, proper software maintenance and verification of past charging system updates are key to a trouble-free ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### Standard Dual-Motor AWD Electric Powertrain

This standard dual-motor electric setup produces approximately 314 horsepower and 446 lb-ft of torque, delivering swift, seamless acceleration and confident all-weather capability. It offers robust performance with fewer traditional mechanical wear points than a combustion engine, though buyers should monitor overall electrical health.

## Transmission

### Performance Dual-Motor AWD Electric Powertrain

Found in the top-tier Performance trim, this upgraded dual-motor setup churns out up to 429 horsepower (with temporary boost modes pushing even higher), delivering exhilarating acceleration. It represents the pinnacle of GV60 performance, but relies heavily on complex software management.

**Sudden Loss of Propulsion**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported experiencing a 'Check Electric Vehicle System' warning message followed by a sudden loss of vehicle propulsion while driving at normal speeds.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle inspected by a certified Genesis dealer to diagnose electrical faults, verify software versions, and check powertrain control modules.

### Single-Speed Direct-Drive Transmission

This single-speed direct-drive unit routes power smoothly from the electric motors to the wheels without traditional gear shifting. It is mechanically simple and generally highly reliable for daily driving.

## Electrical

### Instrument Cluster and Infotainment Display

The electrical architecture drives numerous interior and convenience features, though software bugs can occasionally disrupt core instrument displays.

**Blank Instrument Cluster Display**
- **Symptoms:** Upon starting the vehicle, the main instrument cluster display periodically remains completely black or dark, preventing the driver from viewing speed, battery percentage, or safety alerts.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealer for potential software updates, though owners note that fixes may require multiple dealer visits or module replacements.

## Exterior

### Windshield Chrome Trim

Exterior trim elements provide a polished aesthetic, though rare body hardware detachments have been noted by owners.

**Detaching Exterior Trim**
- **Symptoms:** The long chrome trim piece running along the top of the windshield can loosen and detach entirely while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect the windshield exterior trim for looseness and have clips or adhesive reinforced by a technician if necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V204000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Genesis&model=GV60&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU) may become damaged and stop charging the 12-Volt battery, which can result in a loss of drive power. A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the ICCU and its fuse, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V584000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Genesis&model=GV60&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera image that does not display decreases the driver's visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V868000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Genesis&model=GV60&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The Integrated Charging Control Unit (ICCU) may become damaged and stop charging the 12-volt battery, which can result in a loss of drive power. A loss of drive power increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the ICCU and its fuse, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V474000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Genesis&model=GV60&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 101, "Controls and Displays." An instrument panel display that fails to show critical safety information, such as the speedometer or warning lights, increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V624000: Structure:Exterior Trim](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Genesis&model=GV60&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Detached molding can create a road hazard for other vehicles, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the windshield molding, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Genesis GV60 offers an upscale, tech-forward electric vehicle experience with impressive performance options. While major mechanical failures are rare, used buyers should thoroughly inspect the electrical systems, verify that all integrated charging and software updates have been performed, and check exterior trim security during a test drive.
