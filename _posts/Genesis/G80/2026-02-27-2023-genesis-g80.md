# 2023 Genesis G80 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Genesis G80 delivers striking luxury, advanced cabin tech, and a refined ride that competes strongly in the executive sedan market. While overall owner feedback is limited, prospective buyers should review available safety notices and minor reported issues during their pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2.5-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 300 horsepower and 311 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration for daily driving. This powerplant balances respectable fuel economy with smooth power delivery for the luxury sedan.

### 3.5L Twin-Turbocharged V6

The 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged V6 generates roughly 375 horsepower and 391 lb-ft of torque, offering effortless highway passing power. It serves as the high-performance option for drivers seeking robust acceleration.

**Fuel Odor and System Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice a strong fuel odor inside the cabin after fueling, sometimes accompanied by an illuminated check engine light.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle inspected by a qualified technician to check for fuel system leaks or address applicable manufacturer safety campaigns.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly to the rear wheels or all-wheel-drive system. It features responsive gear shifts designed to maximize both efficiency and refinement.

## Suspension

### Wheels and Tires

Factory wheel and tire assemblies generally provide a comfortable ride, though individual tire durability can vary based on driving conditions.

**Premature Tire Tread Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Original equipment tires may experience unexpected tread wear, exposing internal cords and structural plies prematurely.
- **Solution:** Inspect tire tread depth and structural integrity during routine maintenance, and replace worn tires promptly.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

The vehicle relies heavily on advanced electronic architecture to manage comfort, convenience, and monitoring features.

**Persistent Service Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** Service required warning lights may remain illuminated on the dashboard even after routine maintenance and attempted resets.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealership or specialized service center equipped with factory diagnostic software to properly clear persistent dashboard warnings.

## Exterior

### Sunroof

Exterior glass and panoramic sunroof components enhance cabin openness but require careful inspection for stress cracks.

**Unexpected Glass Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** The sunroof glass panel may develop unexpected cracks without any apparent impact from outside objects.
- **Solution:** Have the glass inspected by a professional and replaced under warranty or comprehensive insurance if structural cracking occurs.

## Safety

### Air Bags

Comprehensive airbag and restraint networks are designed to protect cabin occupants during severe impacts.

**Air Bag Non-Deployment in Severe Collisions**
- **Symptoms:** In certain high-impact collision scenarios, supplementary restraint systems may fail to deploy as expected by occupants.
- **Solution:** Ensure all vehicle safety restraint modules are thoroughly inspected by certified professionals after any significant impact.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V094000: Seat Belts:Front](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Genesis&model=G80&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the front driver-side and/or passenger-side seat belt pretensioners may explode upon deployment. An exploding seat belt pretensioner can project metal fragments into the vehicle, strike vehicle occupants, and result in injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will secure the seat belt pretensioners with a cap, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V282000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Genesis&model=G80&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the fuel pump assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V105000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Genesis&model=G80&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 101, "Controls and Displays." An instrument panel display that fails to show critical safety information, such as the speedometer or warning lights, increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and update the instrument panel display software, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V229000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Genesis&model=G80&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten, or replace the fuel pipe as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

The 2023 Genesis G80 offers a compelling blend of luxury, performance, and sophisticated features for used-car shoppers. While minor owner-reported complaints exist regarding electrical warnings, glass durability, and fuel system notices, a thorough pre-purchase inspection and a check for completed recall services will help ensure a confident purchase.
