# 2021 Genesis G80 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Genesis G80 delivers striking styling, a luxurious cabin, and advanced technology that rivals established European luxury sedans. While most owners enjoy a smooth and refined driving experience, a small number of driver-reported complaints highlight specific areas to inspect closely during a used vehicle purchase, including tires, electrical accessories, and powertrain behavior.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L Turbocharged Inline-4

The base 2.5-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces around 300 horsepower, offering brisk acceleration and efficient everyday performance. It is paired with a smooth-shifting automatic transmission that generally proves dependable for daily driving.

### 3.5L Twin-Turbocharged V6

The available 3.5-liter twin-turbocharged V6 generates roughly 375 horsepower, providing robust passing power and effortless highway cruising. This engine option is well-regarded for its refined power delivery and overall mechanical composure.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 8-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly to either the rear wheels or all four wheels. While generally seamless in operation, prospective buyers should verify proper shifting performance during a test drive.

**Transmission Disengagement and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported instances where the transmission unexpectedly disengaged while driving at moderate speeds, causing the vehicle to stall before restarting on its own.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the transmission control module, software calibration, and powertrain wiring harness for potential fault codes.

## Suspension

### Tires

The suspension and wheel setup is tuned for high-speed stability and comfort, though low-profile factory tire options demand careful inspection for wear and sidewall integrity.

**Sidewall Failure and Blowouts**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced unexpected inner or outer sidewall blowouts and rapid pressure loss on factory-equipped low-profile tires within normal mileage intervals.
- **Solution:** Inspect the condition, tread depth, and manufacturing date of all installed tires, and replace any units showing sidewall aging or damage.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides strong stopping power suited for a luxury sedan, though any unusual noises or pedal feedback should be evaluated by a technician.

**Brake Pedal Tapping and Feedback**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice a distinct tapping sound and tactile vibration originating from the brake pedal when the vehicle is stationary in drive.
- **Solution:** Have the brake booster, master cylinder, and anti-lock braking system inspected by a professional mechanic if unusual pedal feedback occurs.

## Electrical

### Power Windows and Switches

Advanced electronics govern much of the cabin experience, making a thorough test of all power accessories, infotainment functions, and warning displays essential before purchase.

**Intermittent Window Operation**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows may fail to roll up or down from the door switches, sometimes recurring shortly after initial dealership repair attempts.
- **Solution:** Test all window switches and regulators during a pre-purchase inspection to ensure consistent electrical connectivity and motor response.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V208000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Genesis&model=G80&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The fuel tube connecting the high pressure fuel pump to the fuel rail may not seal properly, which could allow fuel to leak. A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will replace the high pressure fuel tube, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V094000: Seat Belts:Front](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Genesis&model=G80&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the front driver-side and/or passenger-side seat belt pretensioners may explode upon deployment. An exploding seat belt pretensioner can project metal fragments into the vehicle, strike vehicle occupants, and result in injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will secure the seat belt pretensioners with a cap, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V630000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Genesis&model=G80&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V229000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Genesis&model=G80&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and tighten, or replace the fuel pipe as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Genesis G80 is an appealing pre-owned luxury choice that combines high-end comfort with strong performance options. Prospective buyers should perform a detailed inspection focusing on tire condition, electrical accessories like power windows, and smooth transmission operation during a test drive to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
