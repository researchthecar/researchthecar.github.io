# 2019 Genesis G80 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Genesis G80 delivers a quiet, luxurious ride with a well-appointed cabin and substantial road presence, making it an attractive option on the used luxury car market. While it generally offers robust comfort and traditional rear-wheel or all-wheel-drive capability, prospective buyers should review owner-reported concerns regarding certain electrical accessories, climate control stability, and minor body or trim hardware. Reviewing maintenance records and testing all electronics during a pre-purchase inspection will help ensure a dependable ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 3.8L V6 Engine

The naturally aspirated 3.8-liter V6 produces 311 horsepower and 293 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard and most common powerplant for the G80. It generally provides smooth power delivery and dependable long-term mechanical reliability when properly maintained.

### 3.3L Twin-Turbo V6 Engine

The 3.3-liter twin-turbocharged V6 powers the Sport trim, generating 365 horsepower and 376 lb-ft of torque for quicker acceleration. Buyers should ensure regular oil change intervals were kept to protect the turbochargers over high mileage.

### 5.0L V8 Engine

The optional 5.0-liter V8 engine puts out 420 horsepower and 383 lb-ft of torque, offering effortless highway passing power. This larger displacement motor is mechanically robust but commands higher fuel consumption during daily commuting.

### Climate Control System

Cabin electronics and climate controls generally function well, though isolated owner reports point toward potential air conditioning longevity concerns.

**Air Conditioning Shutdown**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported that the air conditioning system may unexpectedly shut down or stop cooling properly after several hours of continuous operation.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician diagnose the climate control module, refrigerant levels, or compressor electronics to isolate the failure point.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

An 8-speed automatic transmission is paired across all engine options, channeling power smoothly to either the rear wheels or an all-wheel-drive system. It shifts cleanly in normal driving conditions and exhibits solid durability in pre-owned models.

## Electrical

### Starter Motor

Starting system components are typically long-lasting, but isolated failures can occur if environmental moisture compromises the starter housing.

**Starter Failure From Water Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may fail to crank or start due to a failed starter motor, sometimes attributed by technicians to moisture exposure.
- **Solution:** Replace the starter motor assembly and inspect surrounding shields or drainage paths to prevent repeat moisture exposure.

## Exterior

### Sunroof Assembly

Exterior trim and panels hold up well against daily wear, though panoramic sunroof mechanisms require periodic lubrication to prevent unwanted noises.

**Panoramic Sunroof Binding Noises**
- **Symptoms:** Owners may hear crunching, creaking, or binding noises originating from the panoramic sunroof glass and frame assembly while navigating turns or uneven pavement.
- **Solution:** Lubricate the sunroof seals and track assembly, or have a dealer inspect the glass alignment and mounting hardware.

### Trunk Latch

Interior cabin materials and luxury appointments are durable, with convenience features mostly operating reliably outside of occasional power-assist motor glitches.

**Power Trunk Latch Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The power trunk mechanism may fail to open electronically, occasionally rendering the interior manual release ineffective.
- **Solution:** Replace the failed power trunk latch actuator assembly.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety suites provide helpful driver aids, though sensor calibration and software calibration are critical for smooth operation.

**Unintended Braking Intervention**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may occasionally experience unexpected or overly aggressive automatic braking application while slowing down for routine stops.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates related to the forward collision avoidance and advanced driver assistance systems at a authorized service center.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V160000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Genesis&model=G80&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The Anti-Lock Brake System (ABS) module could malfunction and short circuit. An electrical short circuit inside the ABS module could cause an engine compartment fire while parked or driving.
- **What to do:** Hyundai will notify owners, and dealers will replace the ABS module fuse, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V107000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly:Solenoid](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Genesis&model=G80&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Water may enter the starter solenoid and cause an electrical short, which can result in an engine compartment fire while parked or driving. An engine compartment fire while parked or driving can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a remedy relay kit in the engine junction box, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V191000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Gasoline:Turbo/Supercharger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Genesis&model=G80&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An oil leak in the presence of hot engine components can increase the risk of an engine compartment fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the left turbocharger oil feed pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Genesis G80 remains a compelling choice for used-car buyers seeking executive-level comfort, strong engine options, and a refined ride without paying German luxury prices. While most mechanical systems and powertrains hold up well, buyers should thoroughly test the air conditioning, panoramic sunroof, and power accessories during a pre-purchase inspection to ensure everything operates smoothly.
