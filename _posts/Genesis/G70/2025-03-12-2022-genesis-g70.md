The 2022 Genesis G70 is a luxury sport sedan that offers a blend of performance, comfort, and style. Engine options include a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder producing 252 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque and a 3.3-liter twin-turbocharged V6 generating 365 horsepower and 376 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the G70's engaging driving dynamics, luxurious interior, and competitive pricing. Common dislikes include the relatively small rear seat and some infotainment system quirks.

**Engine**

**2.0T GDI**

*   **Engine Misfires**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, reduced power, check engine light illuminated. Possible causes include faulty ignition coils, spark plugs, or fuel injectors.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the cause of the misfire and replace the faulty component (coil, plug, or injector).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+ depending on the component and labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Visible oil leaks on the engine block or underneath the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level. Possible causes include leaking valve cover gasket or timing chain cover.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking gasket or seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ depending on the location of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**3.3T GDI**

*   **Oil Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Visible oil leaks on the engine block or underneath the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level.
    *   Solution: Replace the leaking gasket or seal. Common areas include the valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, and turbocharger oil lines.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1200+ depending on the location of the leak.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reduced power, turbo lag, whistling noise from the engine, check engine light. Potential causes include faulty wastegate or damaged turbocharger impeller.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the turbocharger and replace the faulty component or the entire turbocharger unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $3000+ depending on the issue.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Abrupt or jerky shifts, especially between lower gears.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a transmission control module (TCM) re-flash may resolve the issue. In other cases, replacement of valve body components or the entire transmission may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (re-flash) - $5000+ (transmission replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Delayed Engagement**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noticeable delay when shifting from park or neutral into drive or reverse.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the cause of the delay. It could be low transmission fluid, a faulty solenoid, or internal transmission damage. Repair or replace the affected components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (fluid change) - $4000+ (internal repair).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Rebooting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen freezes, becomes unresponsive, or randomly reboots.
    *   Solution: Software updates are often released to address these issues. If the problem persists, the infotainment unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1500+ (unit replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The battery drains quickly, especially when the car is not in use.
    *   Solution: Check for parasitic drains caused by aftermarket accessories or faulty electrical components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the cause.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling or squeaking noises from the dashboard, door panels, or rear deck.
    *   Solution: Identify the source of the noise and tighten any loose fasteners or apply felt tape to dampen vibrations.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+ depending on the source and complexity.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Genesis G70 presents a mix of minor and potentially costly issues. Engine oil leaks and turbocharger problems in the 3.3T, along with transmission issues like harsh shifting, are among the more concerning. Electrical glitches with the infotainment system and battery drain can be frustrating. Most of the repairs are likely to cost a few hundred dollars, although complex issues like turbocharger failure or transmission replacement can quickly escalate into thousands of dollars. Overall, prospective buyers should be aware of these potential issues and factor them into their purchasing decision, seeking thorough pre-purchase inspections.
