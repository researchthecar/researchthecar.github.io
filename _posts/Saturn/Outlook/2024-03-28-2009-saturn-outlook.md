**Introduction:**
The 2009 Saturn Outlook is a midsize crossover SUV available with two engine options: a 3.6L V6 and a 4.6L V8. Both engines are paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. The Outlook received favorable reviews for its comfort, spacious interior, and standard safety features, but it was criticized for its lack of power and poor fuel economy.

**Engine**
- **3.6L V6/6-speed automatic:**
    - **Intake manifold failure:** Description/Symptoms: Cracking of the intake manifold can cause coolant and oil leaks, check engine light, and loss of power. Solution: Replacement of the intake manifold. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,800 - $2,500+. Recalls: None Found
    - **Piston ring failure:** Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged piston rings can lead to excessive oil consumption, blue smoke from the exhaust, and a decrease in power. Solution: Overhaul of the engine. Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
- **4.6L V8/6-speed automatic:**
    - **Oil pump failure:** Description/Symptoms: Failure of the oil pump can result in a loss of oil pressure, which can lead to engine damage. Solution: Replacement of the oil pump. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,000. Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
- **6-speed automatic:**
    - **Torque converter failure:** Description/Symptoms: Failure of the torque converter can cause shuddering, slipping, or loss of power while driving. Solution: Replacement of the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000. Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
- **Rear suspension**
    - **Leaf spring breakage:** Description/Symptoms: Broken leaf springs can cause a harsh ride, a decrease in handling, and a potential for the vehicle to lean to one side. Solution: Replacement of the leaf springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+ per side. Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
- **Battery:**
    - **Premature battery failure:** Description/Symptoms: Some vehicles may experience premature battery failure due to a faulty alternator or other electrical issues. Solution: Replacement of the battery and/or repair of the electrical system. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Exterior**
- **Sunroof:**
    - **Sunroof leaks:** Description/Symptoms: The sunroof may leak water into the cabin. Solution: Repair or replacement of the sunroof seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+

**Interior**
- **Dashboard panel cracking:** Description/Symptoms: Some vehicles may experience cracking of the dashboard panel due to exposure to heat or sunlight. Solution: Replacement of the dashboard panel. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800+
- **Power window failure:** Description/Symptoms: Power windows may fail to operate due to a faulty switch or motor. Solution: Repair or replacement of the switch or motor. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Summary:**
The 2009 Saturn Outlook is a spacious and comfortable crossover SUV, but it is not without its issues. The engine and transmission can experience severe problems that can be expensive to repair. The suspension, electrical system, and interior also have some common issues. Overall, these issues are not as serious or costly to fix as the engine and transmission problems, but they can still be frustrating for owners.
