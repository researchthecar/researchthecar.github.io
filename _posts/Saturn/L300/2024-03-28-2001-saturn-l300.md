**Introduction:**
The 2001 Saturn L300 is a compact sedan powered by a 2.2-liter DOHC inline-4 engine mated to a 4-speed automatic transmission. It produces 150 horsepower and 155 lb-ft of torque. The L300 received praise for its fuel efficiency and affordability but criticism for its lackluster performance and dated interior.

**Engine**
* **2.2L DOHC I4**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, engine overheating, rough idle
        * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Engine rattling, loss of power, check engine light
        * Solution: Replace timing chain tensioner
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
* **4-Speed Automatic**
    * **Transmission Slipping**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission struggles to engage gears, loss of power
        * Solution: May require a transmission rebuild or replacement
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
* **Struts**
    * **Strut Mount Bearing Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or grinding noises when turning or braking, uneven tire wear
        * Solution: Replace strut mount bearing
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200 per strut
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Control Arms**
    * **Ball Joint Wear**
        * Description/Symptoms: Loose steering, clunking or popping noises when driving over bumps
        * Solution: Replace ball joints
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per side
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
* **Alternator**
    * **Alternator Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Battery light illuminated, dimming headlights, engine stalling
        * Solution: Replace alternator
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery**
    * **Battery Drainage**
        * Description/Symptoms: Dead battery after sitting for a period of time
        * Solution: Check for parasitic draw, replace battery if necessary
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
* **Door Handles**
    * **Frozen Door Handles**
        * Description/Symptoms: Door handles become stuck or difficult to operate in cold weather
        * Solution: Lubricate door handles
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: Free
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Power Mirrors**
    * **Power Mirror Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Mirrors do not adjust or move when using controls
        * Solution: Replace power mirror motor
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
* **Climate Control**
    * **HVAC Fan Motor Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Heater or air conditioner not blowing air
        * Solution: Replace HVAC fan motor
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Power Windows**
    * **Power Window Regulator Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Window gets stuck or falls when raised
        * Solution: Replace power window regulator
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per window
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2001 Saturn L300 has several common issues, particularly with its engine, suspension, and electrical components. While the issues are generally not severe or costly to fix, they can be frustrating to deal with. However, the L300 remains a relatively affordable and reliable vehicle for its class, with its fuel efficiency and low maintenance costs making it a solid value proposition.
