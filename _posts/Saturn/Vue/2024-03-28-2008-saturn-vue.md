**Introduction:**

The 2008 Saturn Vue was a popular midsize SUV that offered a versatile and affordable option for families and adventurers. It was available with several engine and transmission options, including:

* **Engine:**
    * 2.2L Ecotec I4 (155 horsepower, 152 lb-ft of torque)
    * 3.5L V6 (222 horsepower, 220 lb-ft of torque)
* **Transmission:**
    * 5-speed manual transmission
    * 4-speed automatic transmission
    * CVT (continuously variable transmission)

Owners generally praised the Vue for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and impressive fuel economy. However, some reported various issues with specific components.

**Engine**

**2.2L Ecotec I4**

* **Timing Chain Guide Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noise from the engine, reduced power, and possible engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace timing chain guides.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA 10V-099

**3.5L V6**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, and rough idle.
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-speed Manual Transmission**

* **Bearing Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Grinding noises when shifting gears, difficulty shifting, and premature transmission failure.
    * Solution: Replace transmission bearings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $700 - $1,200
    * Recalls: None Found

**4-speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Slipping:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hesitation or delay when shifting gears, loss of power, and poor fuel economy.
    * Solution: Service transmission (replace fluid and filter) or rebuild/replace transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**CVT**

* **Belt Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Juddering or vibration when accelerating or shifting gears, transmission noises, and potential transmission failure.
    * Solution: Replace CVT belt and pulleys.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Leaking Struts:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noisy suspension, uneven tire wear, and poor handling.
    * Solution: Replace leaking struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800 per pair
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Ignition Switch Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Key does not turn, vehicle won't start, or electrical accessories malfunction.
    * Solution: Replace ignition switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: NHTSA 14V-394

**Exterior**

* **Cracked Rear Bumper Fascia:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Physical crack or damage to the rear bumper fascia.
    * Solution: Replace rear bumper fascia.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Center Console Armrest Lid Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Lid becomes loose or detached from center console.
    * Solution: Replace center console armrest lid or entire center console.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2008 Saturn Vue had some common issues with its engine, transmission, and suspension. The most severe and expensive issue is the timing chain guide failure on the 2.2L Ecotec engine. However, many of the issues are relatively minor and can be resolved with moderate repair costs. Owners should be aware of these potential problems before purchasing a used Saturn Vue.
