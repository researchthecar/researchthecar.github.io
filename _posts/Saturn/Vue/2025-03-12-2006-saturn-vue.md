The 2006 Saturn Vue was a compact SUV known for its unique styling and available V6 power. Engine options included a 2.2L Inline-4 producing 143 horsepower and 152 lb-ft of torque, a 3.5L V6 producing 250 horsepower and 242 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.5L V6 Hybrid producing 248 horsepower and 242 lb-ft of torque. Some owners appreciated its fuel economy and available all-wheel drive. Common dislikes included interior quality, ride comfort and some reliability concerns.

**Engine**

**2.2L Inline-4**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners reported excessive oil consumption requiring frequent top-offs. This can lead to engine damage if not monitored.
    *   Solution: Checking the oil level regularly and addressing any leaks are recommended. If the consumption is severe, piston ring replacement or engine replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $4000+

**3.5L V6**

* **Timing Belt Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing belt can fail prematurely. Symptoms include engine misfires, rough running, or complete engine failure. This engine is an interference engine which means that timing belt failure can result in severe engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the timing belt, water pump, and related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500

**3.5L V6 Hybrid**

No Issues Found

**Transmission**

**Aisin AF33 5-Speed Automatic Transmission (3.5L V6)**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners experience harsh or erratic shifting, particularly between gears. This can be caused by valve body issues or solenoid failures.
    *   Solution: Replacing the valve body or individual solenoids, or a full transmission rebuild may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3000+

**Suspension**

*   **Strut Mount Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature wear and failure of strut mounts, resulting in clunking noises, especially over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Electrical**

*   **Ignition Switch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The ignition switch can fail, preventing the vehicle from starting or causing it to stall while driving. This was a known issue addressed by a recall on other GM vehicles from this time period.
    *   Solution: Replace the ignition switch.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Window Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power window motors can fail, preventing the windows from operating.
    *   Solution: Replace the power window motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $350

**Exterior**

*   **Rust on Rear Hatch**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust developing around the rear hatch, especially near the license plate area.
    *   Solution: Repair and repaint the affected areas.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracking of the dashboard, particularly in areas exposed to sunlight.
    *   Solution: Replace or repair the dashboard.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+

**Summary**

The 2006 Saturn Vue has a range of potential issues affecting various components. Engine problems like excessive oil consumption and timing belt failure can be costly, particularly for the V6 models. Transmission issues, mainly harsh shifting, also contribute to repair expenses. Suspension and electrical problems like strut mount and ignition switch failures add to the overall maintenance burden. Interior and exterior concerns, like dashboard cracking and rust, are primarily cosmetic but can impact the vehicle's value and appearance. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but addressing them promptly is crucial for maintaining the vehicle's reliability and longevity.
