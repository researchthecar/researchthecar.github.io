**Introduction:**

The 2003 Saturn L200 is a compact sedan offered with three engine and transmission options:

* 2.2L Ecotec inline-4 with a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission (140 hp, 150 lb-ft torque)
* 2.2L Ecotec inline-4 with a continuously variable transmission (CVT) (140 hp, 150 lb-ft torque)
* 3.0L V6 with a 4-speed automatic transmission (185 hp, 205 lb-ft torque)

Owners generally appreciate the L200's affordability, spacious interior, and fuel efficiency, but common complaints include sluggish performance and a noisy cabin.

**Engine**

**2.2L Ecotec Inline-4**

* *Intake Valve Sticking*
  * Description/Symptoms: Reduced power, rough idle, check engine light
  * Solution: Replace intake valves
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* *No Issues Found*

**4-Speed Automatic**

* *Slipping Gears*
  * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slips out of gear, difficulty shifting
  * Solution: Replace transmission clutches or torque converter
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Front**

* *Strut Mount Bearing Failure*
  * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noise over bumps
  * Solution: Replace strut mount bearings
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Rear**

* *No Issues Found*

**Electrical**

* *Alternator Failure*
  * Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging, warning light on
  * Solution: Replace alternator
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
  * Recalls: None Found

* *Starter Motor Failure*
  * Description/Symptoms: Engine does not crank
  * Solution: Replace starter motor
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* *No Issues Found*

**Interior**

* *Climate Control Failure*
  * Description/Symptoms: AC or heat not working
  * Solution: Replace AC compressor or heater core
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
  * Recalls: None Found

* *Power Window Failure*
  * Description/Symptoms: Window does not roll up or down
  * Solution: Replace power window motor or switch
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

While the 2003 Saturn L200 offers a spacious interior and fuel efficiency, it is plagued by several significant issues that can impact performance and reliability. Common problems include intake valve sticking, transmission slipping, strut mount bearing failure, electrical failures, and climate control issues. These issues can be costly to repair, with estimates ranging from $200 to over $2500. As a result, potential buyers should carefully consider these issues before purchasing an L200.
