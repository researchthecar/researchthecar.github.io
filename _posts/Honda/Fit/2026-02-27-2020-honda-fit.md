# 2020 Honda Fit Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Honda Fit represents the final model year of the subcompact hatchback's third generation, offering unmatched interior flexibility and fuel efficiency. While generally regarded as a practical and dependable commuter, used car buyers should pay attention to owner-reported concerns regarding electronic dashboard displays, advanced safety camera malfunctions, and occasional engine misfires. Reviewing these specific areas during a pre-purchase inspection can help ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Earth Dreams 4-Cylinder Engine

The 2020 Honda Fit is powered by a 1.5-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine producing 130 horsepower and 114 lb-ft of torque. This direct-injected powerplant delivers excellent fuel economy and adequate pep for city driving, though owners should monitor for potential carbon accumulation over time.

**Engine Misfire**
- **Symptoms:** The engine may experience a misfire while idling at a stop light, accompanied by dashboard error codes and diagnostic trouble codes such as P0302.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the ignition system, fuel injectors, and valves for carbon buildup, and replace faulty components as necessary.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Power is sent to the front wheels via either a 6-speed manual transmission or a continuously variable transmission (CVT). The CVT provides smooth acceleration and optimizes fuel efficiency, though routine fluid changes are vital for long-term durability.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The 6-speed manual transmission offers direct shift engagement for drivers preferring traditional control. It is generally robust and trouble-free when paired with the 1.5L engine.

## Steering

### Power Steering Rack

Steering complaints indicate potential sticking or binding in the power steering rack that requires prompt mechanical evaluation.

**Sticky Steering and Noise**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel may suddenly stick during turns, accompanied by a grinding or hissing sound, making the vehicle difficult to control smoothly.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electric power steering rack and steering linkage, and replace components if mechanical binding or internal failure is confirmed.

## Electrical

### Instrument Cluster and Dashboard Display

Electrical system reports largely center on minor instrument panel and gauge glitches during extreme temperatures.

**Scrambled or Blank Display**
- **Symptoms:** Upon starting the vehicle, the dashboard display showing time, mileage, fuel gauge, and outdoor temperature may appear scrambled, flicker, or go completely blank.
- **Solution:** If the display does not correct itself after warming up or restarting, the instrument cluster may require professional diagnosis or replacement.
- **Cost:** $1,100

## Exterior

### Windshield and Rear Glass

Exterior glass reports highlight rare instances of spontaneous rear window or windshield breakage.

**Spontaneous Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The rear hatch window or windshield may shatter unexpectedly while parked or driving without any notable impact.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged glass panel through a qualified auto glass repair shop.
- **Cost:** $1,000

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance and Camera System

Advanced safety systems provide helpful driver assistance, but camera or radar module failures can disable multiple features simultaneously.

**Camera and Radar Sensor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The multi-purpose camera sensor system can experience internal electronic failure, disabling critical driver aids like Adaptive Cruise Control, Lane Keep Assist, and Collision Braking.
- **Solution:** Have the camera and radar modules diagnosed by a dealership or specialist, and replace malfunctioning sensor units if necessary.
- **Cost:** $2,000

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V046000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Fit&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera image may not display when the engine is started with a key, due to a design error in the audio display power circuit. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." An inoperative rearview camera display can reduce the driver's rear visibility.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the display audio unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V064000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Fit&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, failing to suppress the air bag as intended. An air bag that deploys unintentionally during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Honda Fit is a versatile and efficient subcompact choice for used car buyers, provided it passes a thorough inspection. While major mechanical failures are uncommon, prospective buyers should verify that the instrument cluster, driver-assist safety cameras, and power steering system operate without issue. Checking for these specific items during a test drive will help ensure a reliable and satisfying purchase.
