# 2017 Honda Fit Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Honda Fit remains a popular subcompact hatchback praised for its exceptional cargo versatility, fuel economy, and nimble handling. While generally dependable, used car buyers should carefully inspect specific areas such as the engine's fuel system and the vehicle's exterior paint condition. Reviewing owner feedback reveals a handful of recurring owner complaints that are helpful to keep in mind during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Earth Dreams 4-Cylinder Engine

The 1.5-liter direct-injected 4-cylinder engine produces approximately 130 horsepower and 114 lb-ft of torque. It offers responsive performance and high fuel efficiency, though owners occasionally report fuel system and starting concerns.

**Fuel Injector Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report engine hesitation upon starting, illuminated warning lights, and diagnostic trouble codes indicating fuel delivery problems.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the fault codes and replace malfunctioning fuel injectors with updated components.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly lose power or stall while driving on the road or highway, accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect the fuel system and electronic engine management components, replacing failed sensors or injectors as necessary.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The CVT pairs with the 1.5L engine on most trims to maximize fuel economy. While widespread mechanical failures are uncommon, some owners have reported unexpected neutral shifting behavior.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 6-speed manual transmission is available on base trims, offering a more engaging driving experience with standard reliability.

## Steering

### Rack and Pinion Steering

The electric power steering system generally performs well, though high-mileage examples may develop wear in the steering rack.

**Steering Clunking and Rack Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Turning the steering wheel sharply to the left or right can elicit an audible clunking noise, indicating potential internal wear.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering assembly and replace the rack and pinion unit if excessive play or mechanical failure is diagnosed.

## Exterior

### Paint and Body Structure

Exterior body panels and paint condition require visual inspection, as some owners have experienced premature clear coat breakdown.

**Paint Peeling and Delamination**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have observed clear coat peeling, paint delamination, and surface rust appearing on the roof, hood, and doors.
- **Solution:** Repaint affected body panels or apply protective clear coat layers to prevent further metal exposure and corrosion.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Honda Fit is a practical and versatile hatchback that makes a smart used vehicle choice when properly maintained. Buyers should pay close attention to engine startup behavior, test for any fuel delivery hesitations, and thoroughly inspect the exterior paint for signs of peeling or premature wear. Conducting a pre-purchase inspection can help ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
