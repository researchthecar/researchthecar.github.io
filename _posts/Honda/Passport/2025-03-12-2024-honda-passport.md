The 2024 Honda Passport is a mid-size SUV known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and capable all-wheel-drive system. It comes standard with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing 280 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission. People generally appreciate the Passport's reliability, practicality, and off-road capability for its class. Some common criticisms include its somewhat bland styling, lower fuel economy compared to competitors, and lack of a hybrid option.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6**

*   **VCM (Variable Cylinder Management) Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues related to Honda's VCM system, which deactivates cylinders to improve fuel economy. Symptoms can include vibration, rough idling, and hesitation during acceleration. While not as widely reported as on older Honda models, the possibility remains.
    *   Solution: Disabling the VCM system with aftermarket devices or modifying driving habits can sometimes mitigate the issue. In more severe cases, cleaning or replacing VCM components may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Diagnosis and minor repairs (cleaning) - $100 - $300. More extensive repairs (component replacement) $500 - $1500+.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**9-Speed Automatic**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing harsh or jerky shifting, particularly during low-speed driving or when downshifting. Hesitation or delayed engagement when accelerating from a stop has also been reported.
    *   Solution: A software update of the transmission control module (TCM) may resolve the issue. In some cases, a transmission fluid change or valve body replacement could be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software update: $100 - $200. Fluid change: $150 - $300. Valve body replacement: $800 - $1500+.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent freezing, lagging, or unexpected reboots of the infotainment system. Connectivity issues with Apple CarPlay or Android Auto.
    *   Solution: Software updates are the most common fix. If the issue persists, replacement of the infotainment unit may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Software update: $0 - $100. Infotainment unit replacement: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported instances of the battery draining unexpectedly, especially if the vehicle is left parked for an extended period.
    *   Solution: Diagnostic testing to identify the source of the parasitic drain. Common causes include faulty sensors, improperly installed aftermarket accessories, or a failing battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Diagnosis: $100 - $200. Battery replacement: $200 - $400. Repairing parasitic drain: $200 - $500+.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Creaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported hearing rattling or creaking noises emanating from the dashboard, door panels, or rear cargo area, particularly when driving on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Identifying the source of the noise and tightening or replacing the affected components. Applying felt tape or sound-deadening material can also help.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Diagnosis and minor repairs: $100 - $300. More extensive repairs (component replacement): $300 - $800+.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2024 Honda Passport is a generally reliable vehicle, but like any car, it can have issues. The most commonly reported problems involve the 9-speed transmission's shifting behavior and glitches with the infotainment system. Some owners have also noted battery drain issues and rattling noises within the cabin. The severity of these issues varies, with some being minor annoyances and others requiring more significant repairs. The cost to fix these problems can range from a few hundred dollars for software updates or minor component replacements to over a thousand dollars for more extensive repairs like transmission valve body replacement or infotainment system replacement.
