# 2019 Honda Passport

### Overview
The 2019 Honda Passport is a mid-size SUV known for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and capable performance, especially for its class. It generally exhibits Honda's reputation for reliability, making it a popular choice for families and individuals seeking a dependable vehicle. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected repairs, like any vehicle, there are a few areas that have seen occasional reports of issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas and their associated costs for the 2019 model year.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic (9HP28)
**Hesitation/Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners report occasional hesitation or shuddering during gear shifts, particularly at lower speeds or when accelerating from a stop.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the transmission control module (TCM) have been known to resolve some of these issues. In more persistent cases, internal transmission repairs might be necessary.
- **Cost:** $150 - $1,200

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**System Glitches/Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional reports of the infotainment screen freezing, becoming unresponsive, or experiencing minor glitches.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the infotainment system are often the first step. A system reset or, in rare cases, replacement of the unit may be required.
- **Cost:** $100 - $1,500


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** A few owners have reported intermittent battery drain issues, leading to a dead battery.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify the parasitic draw is crucial. This could involve checking various modules, aftermarket accessories, or the battery itself. Replacement of faulty components or the battery may be needed.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Honda Passport is a highly reliable SUV, aligning with Honda's strong reputation. The primary powertrain components, including the 3.5L V6 engine and the 9-speed automatic transmission, are generally robust. The most frequently cited, though not widespread, issue relates to the transmission's shifting behavior, which can sometimes be addressed with software updates. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment glitches and occasional battery drain, are also noted but appear to be isolated incidents. Overall, the Passport offers a dependable ownership experience with a low probability of major mechanical failures, making it a solid choice in the competitive mid-size SUV segment.
