# 2022 Honda Passport Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Honda Passport offers spacious midsize utility and a standard V6 engine, making it a popular choice for families and adventurers alike. While generally regarded as a capable and comfortable daily driver, owners have reported a handful of recurring mechanical and electrical concerns. Prospective buyers should review common trouble spots, particularly regarding steering components, braking feel, and electrical accessories.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

Powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 280 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, the Passport delivers strong acceleration and capable performance. Overall reliability is typically high, though owners note occasional glitches with the automatic start-stop system.

**Auto Idle-Stop System Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle's auto idle-stop system fails to restart the engine after shutting down at a traffic light or complete stop, occasionally leaving the vehicle stalled in traffic.
- **Solution:** Drivers can manually disable the idle-stop feature upon starting the vehicle, or have a dealer inspect the starting and charging systems for underlying electrical faults.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a 9-speed automatic transmission paired with front-wheel drive or torque-vectoring all-wheel drive, the Passport provides smooth highway cruising. While generally durable, isolated reports indicate potential shifting hesitation and rare mechanical failures.

**Transmission Hesitation and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have experienced unexpected transmission hesitation when depressing the accelerator, or in severe cases, complete unexpected transmission failure while driving.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the transmission fluid condition and run diagnostic trouble codes; software updates or hardware repairs may be required.

## Brakes

### Brake Pedal and Hydraulic System

Brake system complaints generally center around pedal feel, startup stiffness, and minor hardware noises rather than total stopping loss.

**Stiff Brake Pedal on Startup**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal feels hard and unyielding when depressed during vehicle startup, sometimes requiring multiple presses before the vehicle will start.
- **Solution:** Inspect the brake booster, master cylinder, and vacuum lines for proper operation and leaks.

**Brake Pedal Squeaking and Clevis Pin Wear**
- **Symptoms:** An abnormal squeaking sound emits from the brake pedal when depressed, often linked to wear on the clevis pin and cotter pin.
- **Solution:** Lubricate or replace the affected clevis pin and cotter pin components at the pedal assembly.

## Steering

### Steering Assembly

Issues involving steering stiffness and mechanical noise require prompt attention to maintain vehicle control and responsiveness.

**Stiff Steering and Clunking Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers notice unusually stiff steering accompanied by an audible clanking noise resembling gears hitting when turning the steering wheel left or right.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering rack and related linkage for wear or mechanical binding, and check for applicable manufacturer service campaigns.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Lighting

electrical issues involving infotainment screens and exterior lighting can occasionally frustrate owners and require software patches or component replacements.

**Infotainment Freezing and Backup Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment touchscreen constantly freezes or blacks out, disabling the radio, GPS, and rearview backup camera display.
- **Solution:** Perform a system reboot, update the infotainment software, or replace faulty display hardware if issues persist.

**Headlight Condensation**
- **Symptoms:** Moisture accumulates inside the headlight assemblies, impacting nighttime visibility and exterior appearance.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight seals and housings for leaks; dealers may address persistent issues under technical service bulletins.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V174000: Visibility:Rearview Mirrors/Devices:Exterior](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Passport&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The heating pads behind both side-view mirrors may not be bonded properly, allowing the mirror glass to detach. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." Detached mirror glass can reduce driver visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both left and right side-view mirrors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V431000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Passport&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Due to a faulty Media Oriented Systems Transport (MOST) communication coaxial cable connector, the rearview camera image may not appear on the display. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not display an image can reduce the driver's.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the MOST cable harness and install a straightening cover over the vehicle cable connector, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V458000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Master Cylinder](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Passport&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Brake master cylinder separation can cause a loss of brake function and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the brake booster assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V365000: Structure:Frame And Members](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Passport&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Failure of rear suspension components, such as the rear control arm, can cause a loss of vehicle handling and control, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear subframe and install a rear subframe reinforcement kit and, as necessary, repair or replace the rear subframe components, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Honda Passport is a comfortable and capable midsize SUV with plenty of everyday utility, but used car buyers should inspect it thoroughly before purchasing. Pay close attention to steering feel, brake pedal operation, and the reliability of electronic accessories like the infotainment system and auto start-stop feature. A pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic can help catch these potential trouble spots early.
