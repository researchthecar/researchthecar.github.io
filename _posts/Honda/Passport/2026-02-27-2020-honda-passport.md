# 2020 Honda Passport

### Overview
The 2020 Honda Passport is a mid-size SUV known for its practicality, spacious interior, and capable performance, especially for its class. It generally exhibits Honda's reputation for reliability, offering a comfortable ride and decent fuel economy. The Passport is built on a unibody construction, sharing its platform with the Honda Ridgeline. It is primarily powered by a robust V6 engine paired with a smooth-shifting automatic transmission, making it a competent daily driver and a capable option for light off-roading or hauling. Owners often praise its user-friendly technology and versatile cargo space, making it a strong contender in the competitive SUV market. Overall, the 2020 Passport is considered a dependable choice for families and individuals seeking a well-rounded and reliable vehicle.

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 Honda Passport demonstrates strong reliability, aligning with Honda's reputation. The powertrain, consisting of the 3.5L V6 engine and 9-speed automatic transmission, is robust and generally free from significant issues. Suspension and exterior components are also reported to be durable. The primary area of concern, though not widespread, involves occasional glitches with the infotainment system, which can typically be resolved with software updates. Overall, the 2020 Passport is a dependable SUV with minimal reported mechanical or major electrical faults, making it a solid choice for buyers prioritizing long-term reliability.
