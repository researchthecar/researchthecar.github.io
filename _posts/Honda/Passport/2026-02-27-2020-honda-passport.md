# 2020 Honda Passport Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Honda Passport is a midsize two-row SUV that balances family-friendly utility with a rugged aesthetic. Powered by a stout V6 engine, it provides strong everyday performance and comfortable road manners. However, prospective buyers should review recurring owner complaints regarding advanced driver-assistance systems, occasional electrical glitches, and powertrain quirks.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

The 2020 Honda Passport comes equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 280 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque. This naturally aspirated powerplant is generally regarded as robust and durable, providing strong acceleration for highway passing and towing.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired exclusively with the V6 engine, the 9-speed automatic transmission delivers power smoothly to either front-wheel drive or the optional Intelligent Torque Management all-wheel-drive system. It offers responsive shifting and helps optimize fuel efficiency on the highway.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System

Electrical complaints center on touchscreen stability, audio system anomalies, and occasional battery drain related to auxiliary features.

**Infotainment Reboot and Audio Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience sudden screen freezing, blank displays, or loud cracking and popping noises emanating from the vehicle speakers even when the radio is powered off.
- **Solution:** Software updates or dealership wiring harness additions have been attempted, though some owners report persistent intermittent behavior.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety suites provide helpful driver aids, but a subset of owners report phantom braking events and sensor fault warnings.

**Unexpected Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The collision mitigation braking system may erroneously trigger sudden, unintended automatic braking when no immediate road hazard or vehicle is present in front.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician inspect sensor calibration and check for diagnostic trouble codes within the multi-purpose camera and radar units.

- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 20V439000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Passport&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) numbers 101, "Control and Displays" and 111, "Rearview Mirrors." Operating a vehicle without a functioning instrument panel or rearview camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the instrument panel control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V067000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Passport&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** If the operator is unable to refer to the label information, the vehicle may be overloaded, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the certification label, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V165000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Passport&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Overcured tires may develop a break in the sidewall, resulting in sudden air loss or belt edge separation, which could lead to tread/belt loss. Either condition can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will inspect all four tires and replace them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V174000: Visibility:Rearview Mirrors/Devices:Exterior](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Passport&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The heating pads behind both side-view mirrors may not be bonded properly, allowing the mirror glass to detach. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." Detached mirror glass can reduce driver visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both left and right side-view mirrors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V431000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Passport&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Due to a faulty Media Oriented Systems Transport (MOST) communication coaxial cable connector, the rearview camera image may not appear on the display. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not display an image can reduce the driver's.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the MOST cable harness and install a straightening cover over the vehicle cable connector, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Passport&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V064000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Passport&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, failing to suppress the air bag as intended. An air bag that deploys unintentionally during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Honda Passport is a capable and spacious utility vehicle that offers strong V6 performance and everyday usability. While its core mechanical foundation is solid, buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle during a test drive to ensure the infotainment electronics function correctly and that safety sensors do not exhibit erratic behavior.
