# 2023 Honda Passport Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Honda Passport, understanding common owner complaints can help you evaluate a prospective purchase. While this midsize SUV generally offers a comfortable ride and practical utility, drivers have reported isolated issues ranging from electrical and braking concerns to transmission shift behaviors. Reviewing these areas during a pre-purchase inspection can give you greater confidence on the road.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

Equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing around 280 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, providing strong acceleration for daily driving and highway passing. This naturally aspirated powerplant is generally regarded as robust, though minor accessory component complaints like air conditioning seal leaks have been noted by some owners.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission and available all-wheel drive, it delivers smooth cruising capabilities. Some owners have reported intermittent hesitation or gear-shifting anomalies that warrant a thorough test drive.

**Gear Hesitation and Shifting Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have experienced gear searching, high engine revs, and unpredictable power delivery or hesitation during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealer or qualified technician for potential powertrain control module software updates or diagnostic checks.

## Brakes

### Brake System

The braking system provides adequate stopping power for a midsize SUV, but pre-owned buyers should verify rotor condition and check for any reported shudder or abnormal noises during test drives.

**Brake Noise and Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported unusual knocking noises, grinding sounds, or steering wheel and brake pedal vibrations at various speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, calipers, and mounting hardware for wear, warping, or loose bolts.

## Steering

### Steering Column and Linkage

Steering response is generally praised, but potential buyers should check for unusual vibrations or clunking sounds during low-speed maneuvers.

**Steering Vibration and Knocking**
- **Symptoms:** A vibrating steering wheel or a loud knocking noise when turning the steering wheel left or right while parked or driving.
- **Solution:** Have a mechanic inspect the steering components, suspension joints, and intermediate shafts for wear or looseness.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety features enhance driver awareness, though sensor calibration and system sensitivity should be verified.

**Unintended Automatic Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may occasionally trigger unexpected automatic emergency braking events in specific traffic locations or conditions.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates related to the Honda Sensing suite and ensure camera/radar sensors are clean and properly calibrated.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V431000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Passport&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Due to a faulty Media Oriented Systems Transport (MOST) communication coaxial cable connector, the rearview camera image may not appear on the display. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not display an image can reduce the driver's.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the MOST cable harness and install a straightening cover over the vehicle cable connector, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V458000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Master Cylinder](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Passport&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Brake master cylinder separation can cause a loss of brake function and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the brake booster assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V184000: Steering:Rack And Pinion](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Passport&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A loss of steering control increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the steering gearbox as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V900000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly:Filler Pipe And Cap](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Passport&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the fuel filler neck tube and pipe as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V365000: Structure:Frame And Members](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Passport&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Failure of rear suspension components, such as the rear control arm, can cause a loss of vehicle handling and control, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear subframe and install a rear subframe reinforcement kit and, as necessary, repair or replace the rear subframe components, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Honda Passport is a capable and spacious utility vehicle, but prospective buyers should perform a diligent inspection before finalizing a purchase. Pay close attention to transmission shift behavior, brake pedal feel, and steering responsiveness during your test drive. Ensuring all software updates have been applied can help mitigate reported electronic and driving quirks.
