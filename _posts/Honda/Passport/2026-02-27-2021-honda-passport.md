# 2021 Honda Passport Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Honda Passport is a midsize two-row SUV that blends family-friendly practicality with rugged styling. Powered by a capable V6 engine, it provides strong everyday performance and generous cargo space, making it a popular choice for used-car buyers seeking utility. However, prospective buyers should review owner complaints regarding advanced driver-assistance features, electrical quirks, and occasional powertrain concerns.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

Equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 280 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, offering robust acceleration and towing capability. While generally durable, a small subset of owners have reported unexpected engine performance issues and starting system glitches.

**Engine Stalling and Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences sudden engine shutdown while driving at highway speeds or failure to restart, sometimes accompanied by idle stop system warnings.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic inspection by a qualified technician; may require component replacement such as starters, sensors, or major mechanical repairs.
- **Cost:** $2,500

**Fuel Injector Misfires**
- **Symptoms:** Engine running rough, hesitation, or illumination of check engine lights due to fuel delivery problems.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel injection system and replace faulty injectors or related components.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Features a 9-speed automatic transmission paired with front-wheel drive or intelligent variable torque management all-wheel drive. Most drivers find operation smooth, though occasional harsh engagement or hesitation has been reported.

**Unintended Neutral Shift and Shifting Delays**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle unexpectedly slips into neutral while driving, or experiences slow engagement when shifting between reverse and drive.
- **Solution:** Check transmission software calibration, fluid levels, and mechanical sensors; software updates or valve body service may be required.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Braking performance is generally confident, though isolated owner reports point to pedal feel anomalies and master cylinder assembly concerns.

**Brake Master Cylinder and Pedal Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal travels excessively to the floor, wheels lock up unexpectedly, or loose components are found during inspection.
- **Solution:** Inspect master cylinder, brake booster, and mounting hardware for proper torque and wear; replace damaged components.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Infotainment System

The vehicle relies heavily on centralized electronics and touchscreen interfaces, where intermittent software glitches and audio anomalies have occasionally troubled owners.

**Audio Static and Dashboard Blackouts**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment speakers emit static crackling sounds causing audio to drop out, or instrument cluster displays go blank temporarily while driving.
- **Solution:** Perform software updates for the infotainment system or inspect wiring harness and display modules.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance & Honda Sensing

Driver assistance features enhance daily safety, but sensitive camera and radar systems can trigger false collision warnings or unexpected braking events under certain conditions.

**False Braking and Sensor Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Collision mitigation braking system activates unexpectedly without obstacles, or warning lights appear due to multipurpose front camera or radar sensor failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera and radar modules, clear obstructions, calibrate sensors, or replace faulty radar/camera hardware.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V174000: Visibility:Rearview Mirrors/Devices:Exterior](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Passport&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The heating pads behind both side-view mirrors may not be bonded properly, allowing the mirror glass to detach. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." Detached mirror glass can reduce driver visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both left and right side-view mirrors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V431000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Passport&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Due to a faulty Media Oriented Systems Transport (MOST) communication coaxial cable connector, the rearview camera image may not appear on the display. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not display an image can reduce the driver's.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the MOST cable harness and install a straightening cover over the vehicle cable connector, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V458000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Master Cylinder](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Passport&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Brake master cylinder separation can cause a loss of brake function and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the brake booster assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V064000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Passport&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, failing to suppress the air bag as intended. An air bag that deploys unintentionally during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Passport&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Passport&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V365000: Structure:Frame And Members](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Passport&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Failure of rear suspension components, such as the rear control arm, can cause a loss of vehicle handling and control, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear subframe and install a rear subframe reinforcement kit and, as necessary, repair or replace the rear subframe components, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Honda Passport is an appealing midsize SUV offering ample passenger room, strong V6 performance, and versatile cargo capacity. While overall mechanical dependability is solid, buyers should thoroughly test drive the vehicle to check for proper transmission shifting, verify that all Honda Sensing safety features operate without warning lights, and inspect the electrical and starting systems prior to purchase.
