Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints reported by owners of the 2022 Honda Insight. The 2022 Honda Insight is a hybrid sedan that pairs a 1.5-liter four-cylinder engine with an electric motor to produce a combined 151 horsepower and 197 lb-ft of torque. It uses an e-CVT transmission. Owners often praise the Insight for its fuel efficiency, comfortable ride, and quiet cabin. Common dislikes include its somewhat uninspiring performance and limited rear headroom compared to some competitors.

**Electric Motor**

No Issues Found

**Transmission**

**e-CVT**

*   **Harsh Shifts/Jerking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report occasional harsh shifts or a jerking sensation, especially at lower speeds. This is more pronounced during acceleration or deceleration.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a software update to the transmission control module (TCM) can resolve the issue. In other cases, physical repair or replacement might be needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ for software update, $3,000+ for replacement
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Lagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen freezes, lags, or becomes unresponsive. This can affect navigation, audio, and climate controls.
    *   Solution: A software update by the dealership is the first step. If the problem persists, the head unit may need replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $150 for software update (if not under warranty), $500 - $1500+ for head unit replacement
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the 12V battery draining prematurely, especially if the car is left unused for extended periods. This can lead to starting problems.
    *   Solution: Check for any aftermarket accessories that might be drawing power when the car is off. A parasitic draw test can help pinpoint the source. A battery replacement might be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Headlight Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture or condensation buildup inside the headlight housing.
    *   Solution: Replacing the headlight.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+ per headlight
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Buzzing Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling or buzzing noises emanating from the dashboard, door panels, or rear deck.
    *   Solution: The source of the noise needs to be identified. This could involve tightening loose trim pieces, adding padding or felt, or replacing damaged components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $500+ depending on the complexity of the fix
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Seat Discomfort**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some drivers have reported that the front seats lack sufficient lumbar support or are uncomfortable for long drives.
    *   Solution: Aftermarket seat cushions or lumbar support can be added. There are no specific OEM fixes.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $20 - $200+ for aftermarket solutions
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2022 Honda Insight appears to have relatively few major issues, with most complaints centering around electrical glitches (infotainment system), some minor transmission quirks, and interior noises. While the headlight condensation is a concern, it can be costly to repair, but is not dangerous. The cost of repairs is generally moderate, but replacing the head unit or transmission can be expensive. Overall, the severity of issues is generally low to moderate.
