# 2021 Honda Insight

### Overview
The 2021 Honda Insight is a compact hybrid sedan known for its fuel efficiency and comfortable ride. As a hybrid vehicle, it combines a gasoline engine with an electric motor to optimize performance and reduce emissions. Reliability is generally a strong suit for Honda, and the Insight typically follows this trend. Owners often praise its smooth powertrain and well-built interior. However, like any vehicle, specific components can occasionally experience issues. This report details common technical reliability data for the 2021 Honda Insight, focusing on potential problem areas beyond standard maintenance.

### Hybrid Battery System
**Reduced Hybrid System Performance**
- **Symptoms:** Decreased fuel efficiency, reduced electric-only driving range, warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential replacement of hybrid battery modules or the entire battery pack.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $5,000+

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Honda Insight demonstrates strong overall reliability, consistent with Honda's reputation. The powertrain, consisting of the 2.0L Atkinson-cycle engine and e-CVT transmission, is generally robust and free from widespread mechanical complaints. Similarly, the suspension, exterior, and interior components are typically durable. The primary areas where owners might encounter issues are related to the hybrid battery system, which can degrade over time and lead to performance issues, and occasional software glitches within the infotainment system. These electrical components represent the most common, albeit not universal, reliability concerns for this model year.
