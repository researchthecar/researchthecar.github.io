# 2021 Honda Insight Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Honda Insight is generally recognized as a fuel-efficient and practical hybrid sedan, offering refined daily driving manners and solid build quality. However, like any modern vehicle, owner reports highlight a few specific areas worth keeping an eye on—ranging from advanced driver assistance system quirks to rare electrical and braking sensor faults. Reviewing these known owner complaints can help used car shoppers perform targeted checks during a test drive.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L 4-Cylinder Hybrid Engine

The 1.5-liter Atkinson-cycle four-cylinder engine is paired with an electric motor to produce a combined 151 horsepower. It is tied to an electronic continuously variable transmission (e-CVT) and delivers excellent real-world fuel economy with minimal mechanical trouble reported by owners.

## Transmission

### Electronic Continuously Variable Transmission (e-CVT)

The e-CVT manages the power delivery seamlessly between the gasoline engine and electric propulsion motor. It is generally regarded as a durable unit requiring routine fluid changes and little out-of-the-ordinary maintenance.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Regenerative braking systems combine traditional hydraulic components with electronic pedal simulators, which require careful diagnosis if warning messages appear.

**Brake System Warning and Pedal Simulator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience a 'Brake System Problem' error message on the instrument cluster or infotainment screen, accompanied in rare cases by a stiff or unresponsive brake pedal feel simulator.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle scanned for diagnostic codes related to the servo brake control unit and pedal simulator assembly; faulty units require professional evaluation and potential replacement.

## Electrical

### Power Control Unit & Infotainment

Electrical architecture governs both convenience features and core powertrain controls, making timely diagnosis of warning alerts essential.

**Power Control Unit (PCU) Fail-Safe Mode**
- **Symptoms:** The Power Control Unit that manages the 12-volt accessory battery charging can experience a malfunction, sending the vehicle into fail-safe mode, causing loss of power steering/brakes, and illuminating warning lights.
- **Solution:** Inspect the PCU software and electrical connections; failing power control hardware or software logic may require dealership reprogramming or component replacement.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Driver assistance systems enhance safety, but sensor calibration issues or reflections can occasionally trigger false warnings or unexpected braking inputs.

**Unexpected Automatic Braking**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported instances where the collision mitigation braking system or automatic emergency braking activates suddenly without any obstruction, vehicles, or pedestrians ahead.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician or dealership inspect the forward-facing camera and radar alignment, and ensure all ADAS software is updated to the latest manufacturer specifications.

### Air Bags

The restraint systems generally operate reliably, though rare electrical glitches can trigger unexpected warnings or deployments.

**Inadvertent Air Bag Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** Rare reports indicate that front driver or passenger-side air bags may deploy unexpectedly without an impact or collision event.
- **Solution:** Consult a Honda dealer to check for relevant diagnostic trouble codes in the Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) module and inspect wiring harnesses.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V798000: Electrical System:Propulsion System:Dc/Dc Converter](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Insight&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A loss of drive power can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will update the power converter unit (PCU) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V064000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Insight&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, failing to suppress the air bag as intended. An air bag that deploys unintentionally during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Insight&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Honda Insight remains a highly efficient and comfortable choice for used hybrid buyers, boasting strong fuel economy ratings and a generally robust mechanical layout. While owner complaints point to occasional electronic issues, such as sensitive collision avoidance systems or rare brake and power control warnings, thorough pre-purchase inspections can help uncover any lingering issues. Buyers should test all driver-assistance features, verify that recall services have been completed, and ensure the hybrid charging systems are functioning properly before purchasing.
