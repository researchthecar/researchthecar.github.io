# 2014 Honda Ridgeline

### Overview
The 2014 Honda Ridgeline is a mid-size pickup truck known for its unique unibody construction, car-like ride, and versatile cargo bed. It was designed to offer a blend of SUV comfort and truck utility, making it a popular choice for those who need hauling capability without the ruggedness of a traditional body-on-frame truck. Reliability for the 2014 model year is generally considered good, with owners reporting fewer significant mechanical issues compared to some competitors. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen more attention from owners and mechanics. This report details common concerns and provides an overview of the Ridgeline's technical reliability.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6
**Timing Belt Replacement**
- **Symptoms:** While not a failure, the timing belt requires periodic replacement at recommended intervals (typically around 105,000 miles). Neglecting this can lead to severe engine damage.
- **Solution:** Scheduled replacement by a qualified mechanic.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1000

**Variable Cylinder Management (VCM) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Rough idling, engine hesitation, potential for oil consumption, and check engine lights related to cylinder deactivation.
- **Solution:** Software updates, VCM deactivators, or in severe cases, engine repair or replacement.
- **Cost:** $100 - $1500+

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Honda Ridgeline generally exhibits good reliability, aligning with Honda's reputation for durable vehicles. The 3.5L V6 engine, while robust, has a known requirement for timely timing belt replacement and potential issues with the Variable Cylinder Management (VCM) system, which can manifest as rough idling or oil consumption. The 5-speed automatic transmission and the suspension components are typically trouble-free. Electrical concerns are minor, often limited to infotainment system glitches that can usually be resolved with software updates. Exterior and interior components are also reported to hold up well over time. Overall, the Ridgeline is a dependable choice, with most potential issues being manageable with proper maintenance and addressing VCM-related concerns proactively.
