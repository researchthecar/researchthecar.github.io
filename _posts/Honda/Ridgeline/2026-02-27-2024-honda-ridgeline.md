# 2024 Honda Ridgeline Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Honda Ridgeline offers a comfortable, car-like ride, a practical unibody layout, and standard all-wheel drive paired with a reliable V6 engine. However, used car buyers should note a handful of recurring owner complaints reported to federal safety authorities, primarily involving advanced driver-assistance systems, electrical functions, and occasional powertrain behavior. While many drivers enjoy its versatility, checking for software updates and ensuring electrical components are fully operational during a pre-purchase inspection is a smart precaution.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

Equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 280 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth acceleration for daily driving and towing. Overall, the engine architecture is well-regarded for longevity, though minor electronic integration glitches with idle systems have been noted by some owners.

**Engine Stalling and Start/Stop Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience unexpected engine stalling while idling at a stop light, or lose complete electrical power and fail to restart after the automatic stop/stop system engages.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the starting and charging system, and check for any relevant powertrain control module software updates.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission and standard i-VTM4 all-wheel drive, offering smooth gear changes and confident all-weather traction. Routine fluid checks and software upkeep help maintain long-term reliability.

**Unexpected Shifting to Neutral**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly shift into neutral while driving at speed, accompanied by warning messages on the instrument cluster advising the driver to see a dealer.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealership to check transmission control module calibrations and diagnose any logged diagnostic trouble codes.

## Electrical

### Electrical System & Lighting

Covers vehicle wiring, charging components, and exterior lighting controls that occasionally exhibit operational quirks.

**Battery Drain and Headlight Operation**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported premature battery drain linked to idle systems, as well as automatic high beams activating improperly and potentially blinding oncoming traffic.
- **Solution:** Test the battery and charging system draw, and verify that exterior lighting control modules are properly adjusted or updated.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Encompasses advanced safety suites designed to mitigate front-end collisions, though sensitive sensor calibration can occasionally result in nuisance warnings.

**False Braking Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** The Collision Mitigation Braking System (CMBS) can trigger unexpected steering wheel vibrations and visual warnings or false braking events when encountering oncoming traffic or roadside objects.
- **Solution:** Have a dealership inspect and recalibrate the camera and radar sensors associated with the driver-assistance suite.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V321000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that does not display an image can reduce the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the RVC tailgate wire harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Honda Ridgeline is a practical, comfortable midsize truck that handles daily commuting and light utility work with ease. While owner complaints point to occasional electronic glitches, sensor sensitivity in safety features, and isolated powertrain behavior, these issues do not overshadow its core mechanical competence. Prospective buyers should perform a thorough test drive, verify all driver-assistance features and electrical accessories work properly, and check for any outstanding software updates.
