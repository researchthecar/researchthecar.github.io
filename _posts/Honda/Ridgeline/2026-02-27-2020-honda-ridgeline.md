# 2020 Honda Ridgeline

### Overview
The 2020 Honda Ridgeline is a mid-size pickup truck known for its comfortable ride, car-like handling, and versatile in-bed trunk. It primarily targets buyers who need utility but prefer a more refined driving experience than traditional body-on-frame trucks. Reliability for the 2020 model year is generally strong, reflecting Honda's reputation for building durable vehicles. Most reported issues are minor and infrequent, with the powertrain and core mechanicals proving to be robust. Owners typically praise its fuel efficiency for its class and its practicality for everyday use and light-duty hauling. The Ridgeline's unique construction contributes to its quiet cabin and smooth operation, making it a popular choice for those who prioritize comfort and everyday usability.

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 Honda Ridgeline demonstrates strong overall reliability, with its V6 engine and automatic transmissions being particularly robust. The unibody construction and independent suspension contribute to a comfortable and stable ride, with no significant widespread issues reported in these areas. The primary areas where minor concerns have surfaced are within the electrical system, specifically occasional infotainment glitches and, less commonly, premature battery drain. These issues are generally manageable through software updates or component replacement. For the most part, the 2020 Ridgeline is a dependable choice, offering a blend of utility and refinement with a low probability of major mechanical failures.
