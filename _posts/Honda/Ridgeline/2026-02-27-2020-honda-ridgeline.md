# 2020 Honda Ridgeline Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Honda Ridgeline brings unibody comfort, clever storage, and everyday usability to the midsize truck market. While owners appreciate its smooth ride and flexible interior packaging, pre-owned shoppers should review a few recurring trouble spots documented by federal safety and consumer reports. electrical issues—particularly related to backup cameras and infotainment harnesses—alongside complaints concerning the automatic idle-stop engine system, represent the bulk of common owner concerns.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (280 hp, 262 lb-ft)

Powered by a standard 3.5-liter V6 engine producing 280 horsepower paired with direct injection and VTEC, the Ridgeline provides smooth and confident acceleration. Overall reliability is generally strong, though the automatic stop-start system has drawn persistent owner complaints regarding unexpected stalling.

**Engine Stalling via Auto Idle-Stop**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle engine shuts off automatically at a traffic stop, but fails to restart when the brake pedal is released, occasionally stranding the driver in traffic.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic inspection of the Auto Idle-Stop (AIS) system, battery, and starter components; software updates or sensor replacements may be required.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a 9-speed automatic transmission featuring intelligent variable torque management (i-VTM4) all-wheel drive on most trims, it offers refined highway cruising. While mechanically robust, occasional electronic or selector faults can trigger sudden neutral shifts.

**Unintended Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle unexpectedly shifts into neutral while driving or reversing, accompanied by dashboard warnings prompting the driver to stop safely.
- **Solution:** Check transmission control module (TCM) software versions, update firmware, or inspect internal shift actuators and wiring harnesses.

## Electrical

### Backup Camera and Display System

The electrical architecture supports advanced driver assists and interior electronics, but recurring harness wear near the tailgate can lead to intermittent camera and wiring faults.

**Backup Camera Failure or Blank Screen**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display shows a black or distorted screen when shifting into reverse, rendering the rearview camera feed useless.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace pinched tailgate wiring harnesses or faulty camera modules, noting that previous recall repairs may require updated hardware installation.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Honda Sensing safety suite components provide crucial driver aids, though sensitivity in the forward collision and camera systems can occasionally trigger false warnings or unexpected braking.

**Unexpected Automatic Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The collision mitigation braking system abruptly engages the brakes without warning when approaching minor roadside objects or other vehicles.
- **Solution:** Calibrate front bumper radar sensors and windshield multipurpose cameras, and check for applicable sensor software updates.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20E068000: Equipment](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** If the tonneau cover is not properly secured in the open or closed position, wind resistance may cause the cover to flip and buckle. A separated panel may become a road hazard, increasing the risk of crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify all registered owners of 2017-2020 Honda Ridgelines, and dealers will install tethers onto any Honda Genuine Accessory Tonneau Cover to prevent panel separation and apply warning labels, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V932000: Structure:Body:Hood](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The hood latch striker may become damaged and separate from the hood, which can result in the hood opening while driving. A hood that opens while driving can obstruct the driver's view and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will either repair the hood latch striker, or replace the hood if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V174000: Visibility:Rearview Mirrors/Devices:Exterior](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The heating pads behind both side-view mirrors may not be bonded properly, allowing the mirror glass to detach. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." Detached mirror glass can reduce driver visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both left and right side-view mirrors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V458000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Master Cylinder](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Brake master cylinder separation can cause a loss of brake function and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the brake booster assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V064000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, failing to suppress the air bag as intended. An air bag that deploys unintentionally during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V321000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that does not display an image can reduce the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the RVC tailgate wire harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Honda Ridgeline is a versatile and comfortable midsize truck option for everyday utility, but buyers should test all electrical accessories and verify powertrain operation thoroughly before purchase. Pay close attention to the behavior of the auto idle-stop system during a test drive and ensure that any past recall repairs for backup cameras or structural items have been fully completed by a certified dealer.
