# 2018 Honda Ridgeline Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Honda Ridgeline, buyers generally find a comfortable, car-like midsize truck that offers versatile storage and smooth daily driving dynamics. While it earns praise for its clever dual-action tailgate and unibody ride comfort, prospective owners should review common owner-reported complaints regarding electrical issues, rearview camera failures, and powertrain concerns. Being aware of these areas can help you thoroughly inspect a potential purchase and negotiate with confidence.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

The 3.5-liter V6 engine produces approximately 280 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, providing strong acceleration and respectable towing capability for everyday utility. Overall, this powerplant is generally robust, though a small number of owners have reported isolated mechanical and starting troubles.

### Fuel System

Fuel delivery components route gasoline from the tank to the engine cylinders. While normally dependable, owners have occasionally flagged pump and nozzle-related concerns.

**Fuel Pump and Delivery Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience engine stalling while driving, difficulty filling the fuel tank, or unusual pump shut-off behavior at the gas station.
- **Solution:** Inspect the fuel pump and related lines; replace faulty components as necessary.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission routes power to front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive models. Proper fluid maintenance is critical, as neglected units can develop shifting vibrations and reliability issues.

**Transmission Judder and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported surging, shuddering vibrations at lower speeds, transmission warning lights, and occasional overheating or loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition and software calibration; mechanical repairs or fluid flushes may be required if wear is present.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Wiring

Electrical architecture controls vehicle convenience features, security systems, and cabin electronics. Intermittent module failures can lead to drained batteries and erratic accessory behavior.

**Battery Drain and Keyless Access Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard lights flash unexpectedly, vehicle alarms trigger randomly, and the keyless access control unit can cause severe battery drain.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the keyless access control unit and electrical modules; replace malfunctioning modules or wiring harnesses.

### Back-Over Prevention and Cameras

Safety systems rely heavily on exterior cameras and sensors to assist with parking and collision avoidance. Tailgate movement can place stress on internal wiring over time.

**Backup Camera Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The rearview camera display turns black or stops working entirely when shifting into reverse, often due to wire harness fatigue in the tailgate.
- **Solution:** Inspect the wiring harness running into the tailgate and replace or repair damaged wires and camera components.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V053000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A crack in the fuel pump feed port can allow pressurized fuel to leak out, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump, if necessary, and install a fuel pump cover, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V221000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the rivets can break causing the seats not to be secured to the floor. As such, these vehicles may fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 207, "Seating Systems," and 210, "Seat Belt Assembly Anchorages." In the event of a crash, if the seat does not remain secured to the floor.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver seat frame assembly in Acura MDX vehicles, and replace the driver and front passenger seat slide rail frames in Honda Pilot and Ridgeline vehicles, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20E068000: Equipment](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the tonneau cover is not properly secured in the open or closed position, wind resistance may cause the cover to flip and buckle. A separated panel may become a road hazard, increasing the risk of crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify all registered owners of 2017-2020 Honda Ridgelines, and dealers will install tethers onto any Honda Genuine Accessory Tonneau Cover to prevent panel separation and apply warning labels, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V932000: Structure:Body:Hood](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The hood latch striker may become damaged and separate from the hood, which can result in the hood opening while driving. A hood that opens while driving can obstruct the driver's view and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will either repair the hood latch striker, or replace the hood if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V867000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** An inoperative rearview camera can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rearview camera wiring harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V365000: Structure:Frame And Members](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Failure of rear suspension components, such as the rear control arm, can cause a loss of vehicle handling and control, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear subframe and install a rear subframe reinforcement kit and, as necessary, repair or replace the rear subframe components, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The Honda Ridgeline remains a practical, smooth-riding choice for used truck buyers who prioritize interior comfort and bed versatility over heavy-duty towing. While major mechanical failures are relatively uncommon, buyers should carefully test all electrical accessories, verify the health of the transmission, and check that the rearview camera functions properly during a pre-purchase inspection.
