# 2017 Honda Ridgeline

### Overview
The 2017 Honda Ridgeline, a unique unibody pickup truck, generally exhibits strong reliability, reflecting Honda's reputation for dependable vehicles. It was introduced with a single, well-established powertrain option, simplifying potential issues. The Ridgeline's design prioritizes comfort and practicality for everyday use rather than heavy-duty hauling, which contributes to its overall durability. Owners often praise its car-like ride, versatile in-bed trunk, and quiet cabin. While no vehicle is entirely immune to problems, the 2017 model year has a solid track record, with most reported issues being minor or related to specific component failures rather than systemic design flaws. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring its longevity.

**Infotainment System Glitches**

### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**

- **Solution:** Diagnosis to identify parasitic draw from modules or accessories, repair or replacement of faulty components.

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Honda Ridgeline stands out as a highly reliable vehicle, particularly given its unique segment positioning. The standard 3.5L V6 engine and 6-speed automatic transmission have proven robust with no significant widespread issues reported. While the electrical system, particularly the infotainment unit, can experience occasional glitches, and minor issues like tailgate latches or HVAC resistors have been noted, these are generally not indicative of major systemic problems. Overall, the Ridgeline offers a dependable ownership experience with a strong emphasis on comfort and everyday usability, making it a solid choice for those seeking a practical and reliable truck-alternative.
