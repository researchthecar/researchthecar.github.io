# 2021 Honda Ridgeline Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Honda Ridgeline offers a comfortable unibody ride and practical features, making it a popular choice for light-truck buyers. While owners generally praise its everyday drivability and utility, owner complaints submitted to safety agencies highlight certain recurring areas of concern. Prospective buyers should carefully evaluate the vehicle's powertrain performance, driver-assist electronics, and steering components during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (280 hp, 262 lb-ft)

Equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 producing approximately 280 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, mated to an all-wheel-drive system. This engine generally delivers smooth performance, though a small number of owners have reported unexpected mechanical failures.

**Engine Knocking and Limp Mode**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine knocking, sputtering, and sudden entry into limp mode accompanied by an illuminated check engine light.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the engine diagnostics and internal components to determine if mechanical repairs or component replacements are necessary.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Features a 9-speed automatic transmission paired with standard i-VTM4 all-wheel drive. While it provides refined gear changes, buyers should monitor for intermittent shifting anomalies.

**Unexpected Shifting to Neutral**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly shift into neutral while driving or waiting at a stoplight, triggering a transmission failure warning alert.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to a dealership or certified transmission specialist to check for diagnostic trouble codes and software updates.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Subframe

The steering system provides responsive handling, but owners have reported occasional rack failures and suspension fastener loosening.

**Steering Rack Failure and Clunking Noises**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice a distinct clunking noise and physical feedback when turning the steering wheel left or right, sometimes attributed to steering rack wear or loose subframe hardware.
- **Solution:** Inspect steering rack integrity, subframe bolts, and lower control arms, and retorque or replace worn components as needed.
- **Cost:** $4,000

## Electrical

### Honda Sensing Camera System

Advanced safety features rely on multi-purpose cameras and sensors, which can occasionally trigger warning lights if system faults occur.

**Forward Collision and Lane Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The forward-facing camera system may experience internal electronic failures, disabling features like collision mitigation braking, adaptive cruise control, and lane keeping assist while illuminating multiple dash warning lights.
- **Solution:** Have the camera module recalibrated or replaced by a qualified service center.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Exterior lighting systems provide good illumination, though minor housing moisture and sensor calibration quirks have been noted by owners.

**Headlight Moisture and Auto High Beam Sensitivity**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note moisture accumulating inside headlight housings and overly sensitive auto high beams that may fail to engage or stay on too long.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight housing seals for moisture intrusion and consult a dealer regarding sensor calibration.

### Glass and Windshield Components

Exterior body and glass components are generally durable, but rare occurrences of spontaneous glass breakage and parts availability delays warrant attention.

**Sunroof Shattering and Washer Nozzle Backorder**
- **Symptoms:** Rare reports indicate that the moonroof glass panel may fracture spontaneously while driving, while washer nozzles can fail and face long parts backorders.
- **Solution:** Inspect sunroof glass for any pre-existing stress and replace faulty washer nozzles or wiper components as parts become available.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V900000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the second-row center seat belt assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V174000: Visibility:Rearview Mirrors/Devices:Exterior](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The heating pads behind both side-view mirrors may not be bonded properly, allowing the mirror glass to detach. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." Detached mirror glass can reduce driver visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both left and right side-view mirrors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V458000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Master Cylinder](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Brake master cylinder separation can cause a loss of brake function and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the brake booster assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V064000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, failing to suppress the air bag as intended. An air bag that deploys unintentionally during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V321000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that does not display an image can reduce the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the RVC tailgate wire harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V365000: Structure:Frame And Members](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Failure of rear suspension components, such as the rear control arm, can cause a loss of vehicle handling and control, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear subframe and install a rear subframe reinforcement kit and, as necessary, repair or replace the rear subframe components, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Honda Ridgeline is a comfortable and practical midsize truck, but buyers should conduct a thorough inspection before purchasing. Pay close attention to the operation of the powertrain, steering responsiveness, and the functionality of all advanced safety electronics. Verifying that all open service items have been addressed will help ensure a more dependable ownership experience.
