# 2006 Honda Ridgeline

### Overview
The 2006 Honda Ridgeline is a unique mid-size pickup truck built on a unibody platform, sharing components with Honda's SUV lineup. This design contributes to a comfortable, car-like ride and good handling for its class. Reliability is generally a strong suit for Honda vehicles, and the Ridgeline is no exception. While no vehicle is entirely free of potential issues, the 2006 model year benefits from Honda's established engineering and manufacturing practices. Owners often praise its practicality, innovative features like the lockable trunk, and its overall durability. This analysis focuses on common reported issues beyond standard maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for prospective buyers or current owners.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (J35A9)
**Timing Belt Replacement**
- **Symptoms:** While not a failure, this is a critical maintenance item. Neglecting it can lead to catastrophic engine damage.
- **Solution:** Scheduled replacement at recommended intervals (typically 105,000 miles).
- **Cost:** $600 - $1000

**Variable Cylinder Management (VCM) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Hesitation, rough idle, check engine light, potential oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Software update, deactivation system, or engine repair/replacement in severe cases.
- **Cost:** $100 - $3000+

## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming lights, battery warning light, difficulty starting, dead battery.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Power Window Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Windows not moving up or down, slow operation.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the power window motor/regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Exterior

### Body Panels and Trim
**Tailgate Hinge Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Rust or corrosion around the tailgate hinges, potentially affecting operation.
- **Solution:** Cleaning, rust treatment, or replacement of hinge components.

### Summary Verdict
The 2006 Honda Ridgeline generally exhibits good reliability, consistent with Honda's reputation. The primary mechanical concerns revolve around the timing belt maintenance and potential issues with the Variable Cylinder Management (VCM) system, which can manifest in various ways and require attention. Electrical components like the alternator and power window regulators are occasional points of failure, as is common in vehicles of this age. Exterior issues are typically minor, such as localized corrosion. Overall, the Ridgeline is a dependable choice, but prospective owners should be aware of the VCM system and ensure proper maintenance history, especially regarding the timing belt.
