# 2019 Honda Ridgeline Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Honda Ridgeline is a practical, car-like midsize pickup truck powered by a refined V6 engine. While it offers a comfortable ride and clever cargo solutions, used buyers should be aware of several reported trouble spots. Chief among these are powertrain concerns centered around the transmission and torque converter, alongside recurring electrical and fuel system complaints.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (J35Y6)

Equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 280 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth and capable performance. Overall reliability is generally solid, though owners have reported occasional fuel system and electrical warnings.

**Fuel Injector and Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Hesitation upon acceleration, illuminated check engine and emission warning lights, or unexpected engine stalling.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic inspection by a qualified technician followed by replacement of faulty fuel injectors or the fuel pump.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Utilizes a 6-speed automatic transmission paired with front-wheel drive or available i-VTM4 all-wheel drive. Buyers should thoroughly check for smooth gear engagement and any history of torque converter shudder or failure.

**Torque Converter Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission error messages flashing on the dash, harsh shifting, bucking, clanging, or sudden drops in motive power accompanied by diagnostic trouble code P0741.
- **Solution:** Professional transmission diagnosis, fluid flush, or torque converter and transmission replacement.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Hydraulic braking hardware responsible for stopping the vehicle under various driving conditions.

**Brake Pedal Stiffness or Softness**
- **Symptoms:** Brake pedal feels extremely hard upon startup, or alternatively exhibits a soft and spongy feel requiring excessive pedal travel to stop the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Inspect the master cylinder and brake booster; replace components as needed.

## Electrical

### Keyless Entry and Alarm System

Manages cabin electronics, security features, and infotainment displays. While generally trouble-free, control unit glitches can lead to unexpected battery drain.

**Random Alarm Activation and Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle alarm goes off randomly at all hours, draining the battery and occasionally preventing the truck from starting.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the keyless entry/TPMS control unit located behind the glove box.

### Backup Camera and Visibility

Includes driver-assistance technologies and safety monitoring systems designed to protect occupants and aid parking maneuvers.

**Inoperable or Cloudy Rear-View Camera**
- **Symptoms:** Rear-view camera displays a black screen, a flashing screen, or a cloudy picture, impairing visibility when backing up.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera wiring connections and replace the backup camera unit if necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20E068000: Equipment](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the tonneau cover is not properly secured in the open or closed position, wind resistance may cause the cover to flip and buckle. A separated panel may become a road hazard, increasing the risk of crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify all registered owners of 2017-2020 Honda Ridgelines, and dealers will install tethers onto any Honda Genuine Accessory Tonneau Cover to prevent panel separation and apply warning labels, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V932000: Structure:Body:Hood](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The hood latch striker may become damaged and separate from the hood, which can result in the hood opening while driving. A hood that opens while driving can obstruct the driver's view and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will either repair the hood latch striker, or replace the hood if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V215000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V053000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A crack in the fuel pump feed port can allow pressurized fuel to leak out, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump, if necessary, and install a fuel pump cover, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V664000: Seat Belts:Pretensioner](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) control unit may have a manufacturing error, possibly resulting in the air bags or seatbelt pretensioners not deploying in the event of a crash. In the event of a crash, if the air bags or seatbelt pretensioners do not function as intended, there would be an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the SRS unit, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V298000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Gasoline:Belts And Associated Pulleys](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Teeth separation from the timing belt may result in an engine stall, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the vehicle and replace any affected timing belt free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V848000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash necessitating right side curtain air bag deployment, the clip may not hold the center pillar trim panel in position and it may potentially interfere with the curtain air bag deployment. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 214, "Side Impact Protection,".
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will install a bracket on the right center pillar that will securely hold the center pillar trim clip, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V867000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An inoperative rearview camera can reduce the driver's rear visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rearview camera wiring harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Honda Ridgeline stands out as a comfortable and versatile alternative to traditional body-on-frame trucks, but potential buyers must proceed with diligence. Pay close attention to transmission shift quality during a test drive to rule out torque converter issues, and verify that all electrical accessories and safety cameras function properly. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by an independent mechanic is strongly recommended.
