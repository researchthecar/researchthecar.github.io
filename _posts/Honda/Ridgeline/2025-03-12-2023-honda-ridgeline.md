The 2023 Honda Ridgeline is a mid-size pickup truck known for its comfortable ride, car-like handling, and innovative features like the in-bed trunk and dual-action tailgate. All 2023 Ridgelines come standard with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing 280 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission. What people often like about the Ridgeline are its practicality, comfortable ride, and spacious cabin. Some common dislikes include its relatively high price compared to other trucks in its class, and its limited off-road capability.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6**

*   **VCM (Variable Cylinder Management) System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the VCM system, which deactivates cylinders to improve fuel economy. Symptoms can include rough idling, vibrations, and decreased fuel economy. While not widespread, some owners experience this, especially as the vehicle ages.
    *   Solution: Potential solutions include disabling the VCM system using aftermarket devices (VCM Tuner II), replacing faulty VCM components (solenoids, sensors), or performing a software update (if available) from Honda.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the component and repair shop.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**9-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing harsh or jerky shifting, particularly between lower gears. Hesitation when accelerating is also a reported concern. This is less common in the 2023 model year compared to some earlier versions of the 9-speed.
    *   Solution: A software update to the transmission control module (TCM) might resolve the issue. In some cases, transmission fluid replacement or even valve body replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ for software update, $300+ for fluid change, $1000+ for valve body replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Glitches**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Occasional freezing, lagging, or unresponsive touch screen. Some owners have also reported issues with the navigation system.
    *   Solution: A software update to the infotainment system is often the first step. Resetting the system can sometimes resolve temporary glitches.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Usually covered by warranty if within the warranty period.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Backup Camera Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported the backup camera image being blurry, flickering, or not displaying at all.
    *   Solution: Check the camera lens for dirt or obstructions. Inspect the wiring connections to the camera. Replacement of the camera may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+ depending on if it's just a loose wire or requires replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Bed Trunk Lid Alignment Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported the bed trunk lid not aligning properly, leading to water leaks or difficulty closing.
    *   Solution: Adjusting the hinges and latches of the bed trunk lid.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ depending on the complexity of the adjustment
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Creaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rattling or creaking noises from the dashboard or door panels.
    *   Solution: Identifying the source of the noise and securing loose panels or components. Applying felt tape or lubricant to reduce friction.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0-$200 depending on how easy it is to identify and correct the problem.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2023 Honda Ridgeline generally has few widespread, serious issues. The most commonly reported issues involve the infotainment system and minor interior noises. Engine and transmission issues are less frequent but can occur. Most issues are relatively minor and can be resolved with software updates or adjustments. The severity of the issues is generally low to moderate, and the cost of repairs can range from minimal to several hundred dollars, depending on the specific problem and repair needed.
