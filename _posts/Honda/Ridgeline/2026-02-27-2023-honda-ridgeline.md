# 2023 Honda Ridgeline Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Honda Ridgeline offers the everyday comfort and smooth ride expected from a unibody midsize truck, backed by a standard V6 engine and all-wheel drive. While it remains a practical choice for light-duty truck buyers, shoppers should review owner-reported complaints concerning electrical accessories, driver-assistance system behavior, and steering components.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

Equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 280 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, providing strong acceleration and towing capability. The engine generally delivers dependable performance, though isolated owner reports mention power loss or mechanical noises.

**Motive Power Loss and Engine Knocking**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light accompanied by a flashing light, loss of engine power, and occasional engine knocking or smoke under the hood.
- **Solution:** Requires professional inspection by a certified technician; repairs may involve addressing component failures or internal engine service.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission and standard i-VTM4 all-wheel drive, delivering smooth highway cruising and responsive gear selection. Most drivers find the powertrain refined, though occasional shifting delays have been noted by owners.

**Shifting Delays and Neutral Drop**
- **Symptoms:** Extended pauses when shifting into drive or unexpected shifting into neutral while starting or driving, sometimes displaying dashboard warning messages.
- **Solution:** Have a dealer or transmission specialist inspect the transmission control module and fluid condition for diagnostic fault codes.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Tie Rods

The steering system provides precise handling for a midsize truck, though early wear on inner tie rods or steering rack components can develop in some vehicles.

**Steering Rack Play and Clunking Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive play in the steering assembly, front-end rattling or clunking sounds when driving over rough surfaces, and potential state inspection failures.
- **Solution:** Inspect inner and outer tie rods as well as the steering rack; replace worn components as necessary.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting and Auto High Beams

Exterior lighting systems provide good illumination, but automated features such as auto high beams can occasionally exhibit overly sensitive or inconsistent behavior.

**Erratic Headlight Operation**
- **Symptoms:** Automatic high beams failing to dim properly for oncoming traffic or randomly flashing on and off, potentially blinding other drivers.
- **Solution:** Consult a dealer to check lighting software calibration, sensors, and associated switches.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety features provide helpful driver alerts, though sensor calibration sensitivities can occasionally trigger false positive braking events.

**Unexpected Automatic Braking**
- **Symptoms:** Collision warning alerts and sudden unintended brake activation when navigating curves or passing opposing traffic.
- **Solution:** Have dealer technicians verify sensor alignment and check for any available software updates for the collision mitigation system.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V458000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Master Cylinder](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Brake master cylinder separation can cause a loss of brake function and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the brake booster assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V184000: Steering:Rack And Pinion](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A loss of steering control increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the steering gearbox as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V321000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A rearview camera that does not display an image can reduce the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the RVC tailgate wire harness, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V365000: Structure:Frame And Members](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Ridgeline&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Failure of rear suspension components, such as the rear control arm, can cause a loss of vehicle handling and control, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear subframe and install a rear subframe reinforcement kit and, as necessary, repair or replace the rear subframe components, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Honda Ridgeline is generally a comfortable and capable daily driver, but used-car buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection. Pay close attention to steering component wear, transmission engagement smoothness, and the proper operation of driver-assistance electronics during a test drive.
