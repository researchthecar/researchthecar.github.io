**Introduction to the 2017 Honda Ridgeline**

The 2017 Honda Ridgeline is a midsize pickup truck that offers a unique combination of car-like comfort and truck-like capability. It is available with a single engine and transmission option: a 3.5-liter V6 engine paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. The V6 engine produces 280 horsepower and 262 pound-feet of torque.

The Ridgeline is praised for its spacious and comfortable interior, which is uncharacteristic of most pickup trucks. It also boasts a host of advanced safety features, including lane departure warning and automatic emergency braking. However, some owners have reported issues with the vehicle's reliability and build quality.

**Engine**

**3.5-liter V6**

* **VCM Oil Consumption**
    * Description/Symptoms: The VCM system, which is designed to improve fuel economy, can lead to excessive oil consumption in some engines. Symptoms include low oil levels and blue smoke from the exhaust.
    * Solution: Honda has issued a technical service bulletin (TSB) to address this issue. The solution involves replacing the piston rings and valve seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Shifting Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing rough or delayed shifting.
    * Solution: The solution may involve replacing the transmission fluid or the transmission control module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front End Clunking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing a clunking noise from the front suspension.
    * Solution: The issue may be caused by worn-out sway bar links or struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing excessive battery drain.
    * Solution: The issue may be caused by a faulty alternator or a parasitic drain.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **BCM Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The body control module (BCM) is responsible for controlling various electrical functions in the vehicle. Some owners have reported experiencing issues with the BCM, such as inoperative power windows or door locks.
    * Solution: The BCM may need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Hinge Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing tailgate hinge failure, which can cause the tailgate to sag or fall off.
    * Solution: The tailgate hinges may need to be repaired or replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Touchscreen Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported experiencing issues with the touchscreen display, such as unresponsive or delayed inputs.
    * Solution: The touchscreen may need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2017 Honda Ridgeline has a mixed record of reliability. While some owners have praised its comfort and features, others have experienced significant issues with the engine, transmission, and electrical systems. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem.
