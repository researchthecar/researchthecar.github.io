# 2004 Honda S2000

### Overview
The 2004 Honda S2000 is a high-performance roadster celebrated for its exhilarating driving dynamics and rev-happy engine. As a sports car, it prioritizes performance over absolute comfort and practicality. Reliability is generally strong, a hallmark of Honda engineering, but specific components can experience issues under hard use or with age. Owners should be aware of potential concerns related to the high-revving nature of its engine and the demands placed on its drivetrain and suspension. Regular maintenance and attentive driving are key to preserving the S2000's legendary performance and longevity.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L F20C Inline-4
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Increased oil level drops between changes, blue smoke from exhaust under acceleration.
- **Solution:** Engine rebuild or replacement of piston rings and valve stem seals.
- **Cost:** $2,500 - $5,000

**VTEC Solenoid Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check Engine Light, loss of power above 6000 RPM.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the VTEC solenoid.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

**Clutch Master Cylinder Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Soft clutch pedal, difficulty shifting gears, clutch fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the clutch master cylinder.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual
**Synchronizer Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty shifting into certain gears, grinding noises when shifting.
- **Solution:** Transmission rebuild with replacement of worn synchronizers.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

**Clutch Release Bearing Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Whirring or rattling noise when clutch pedal is depressed.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the clutch release bearing and potentially the clutch assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500


## Suspension

### Independent Double Wishbone
**Ball Joint Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, uneven tire wear, vague steering.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Shock Absorber Leakage/Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Bouncy ride, reduced handling precision, visible fluid leaks on shocks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of shock absorbers.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Instrument Cluster Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Flickering gauges, dead pixels on digital displays, intermittent power loss to cluster.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the instrument cluster.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,000

**Window Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow window operation.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the power window motor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Exterior

### Soft Top
**Soft Top Fabric Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking, tears in fabric, difficulty operating the top.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the soft top fabric or the entire soft top assembly.
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,500


### Headlight Lens Hazing
**Headlight Lens Hazing**
- **Symptoms:** Yellowed or cloudy headlight lenses, reduced light output.
- **Solution:** Headlight restoration kit or replacement of headlight assemblies.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500


## Interior

### Standard Interior Components
**Driver's Seat Bolster Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Tearing or excessive wear on the side bolster of the driver's seat due to ingress/egress.
- **Solution:** Seat cover replacement or professional upholstery repair.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

### Summary Verdict
The 2004 Honda S2000 is a reliable sports car with a strong mechanical foundation. The primary areas of concern revolve around the high-revving nature of the F20C engine, which can lead to increased oil consumption if not properly maintained or if driven excessively hard without adequate care. Transmission issues, particularly synchronizer wear, can occur with age and mileage. Suspension components like ball joints and shocks are subject to wear, as expected in a performance vehicle. Electrical gremlins, such as instrument cluster problems, and exterior wear like soft top degradation are also noted. Overall, with diligent maintenance and addressing issues promptly, the S2000 remains a rewarding and dependable sports car.
