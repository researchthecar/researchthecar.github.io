# 2017 Honda Odyssey Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used minivan, the 2017 Honda Odyssey stands out as a practical and popular family choice, backed by a strong reputation for versatility and comfort. However, prospective buyers should review potential mechanical and electrical hiccups reported by owners. While many vehicles serve families reliably, paying close attention to powertrain performance and sliding door electronics during a test drive can help you spot issues before buying.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6

The 3.5-liter V6 engine delivers roughly 248 horsepower and strong performance suited for hauling families and cargo. It is generally robust, though owners have reported isolated issues with misfires and stalling.

**Engine Misfire**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report the check engine light flashing, accompanied by cylinder misfires, rough running, and potential piston buildup over time.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the misfire code, inspect spark plugs and ignition coils, and address any underlying fuel or carbon accumulation issues.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may jerk, experience unexpected stalls while accelerating, or die and coast to a stop.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the fuel system, sensors, and related powertrain controls.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

This 6-speed automatic transmission pairs with the V6 engine for smooth highway cruising, though buyers should verify proper shifting behavior and check for torque converter service history.

**Transmission Shudder and Torque Converter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience rough shifting at low speeds or a noticeable shudder, sometimes associated with diagnostic trouble codes like P0741.
- **Solution:** Inspect the transmission and torque converter for wear; some repairs or replacements may be covered under extended warranty extensions provided by the manufacturer.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

Standard braking components provide adequate stopping power for the heavy minivan platform, though routine checks for pad life and rotor warping are recommended.

**Brake Vibration and Pad Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice front-end shaking or vibration when applying the brakes, often attributed to uneven pad wear or heat buildup.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads and rotors, resurface or replace worn rotors, and ensure proper caliper movement.
- **Cost:** $200

## Electrical

### Body Electrical and Locks

The electrical system controls numerous convenience features, but water intrusion and switch wear can occasionally disrupt power locks and sliding doors.

**Power Door and Electrical Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have experienced erratic alarm activations, doors locking and immediately unlocking, and power sliding doors or turn signals failing after rainstorms.
- **Solution:** Inspect door latches, body control modules, and wiring harnesses for moisture intrusion or mechanical wear.

## Exterior

### Sliding Doors

Power sliding doors are a minivan staple, but the mechanical cables and latches require periodic inspection to ensure safe operation.

**Sliding Door Cable Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The sliding door cables can snap unexpectedly, causing the door to bind, jam, or potentially fall off its track.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sliding door tracks, rollers, and cables for fraying or damage, and replace worn components promptly.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V725000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If a seat is placed between either of the two positions when attaching the seat to the vehicle floor, the seat will not latch properly to the seat striker, allowing the seat to tip forward unexpectedly during braking. If the seat tips forward during braking, it can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will install a bracket to the second row outboard floor strikers, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Honda Odyssey remains a versatile and family-friendly choice, but used car buyers should perform a thorough inspection before purchasing. Pay close attention to transmission shift quality, test all power sliding doors and electrical accessories, and ensure the engine runs smoothly without misfires. Reviewing maintenance records for past warranty repairs on the torque converter can provide extra peace of mind.
