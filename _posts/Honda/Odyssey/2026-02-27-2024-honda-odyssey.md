# 2024 Honda Odyssey Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Honda Odyssey continues to be a top choice for families, offering a spacious cabin, comfortable ride, and standard V6 performance. However, owner complaints filed with federal safety regulators highlight a few recurring operational glitches that prospective buyers should keep on their radar. The most notable concerns center around the vehicle's idle start-stop system and advanced driver assistance features, which occasionally lead to unexpected stalls or unprompted braking events.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

Equipped with a naturally aspirated 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 280 horsepower, providing strong acceleration and highway passing power. While generally robust, the engine's integration with modern automatic start-stop systems has been a frequent source of owner frustration.

**Idle Start-Stop Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** When coming to a stop at a traffic light, the engine shuts off as part of the auto start-stop feature but fails to restart when the brake pedal is released, sometimes throwing the vehicle into park and leaving the driver stranded in active traffic.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic evaluation by a dealership or technician; software updates or replacement of prematurely worn starting components may be necessary.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

Features a 10-speed automatic transmission paired with front-wheel drive, designed to optimize both highway fuel economy and power delivery. Shift quality is generally smooth, though occasional harshness in lower gears has been reported.

**Hard Shifting in Lower Gears**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience rough or jarring shifts when accelerating from a stop or moving through lower gears, which can unsettle the vehicle on slick or wet road surfaces.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician inspect the transmission control module for updated software calibration or fluid condition.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Accessory Electronics

Manages cabin connectivity, displays, and safety monitoring systems. Electrical complaints are generally isolated to auxiliary features like infotainment audio and start-stop electronics.

**Infotainment Audio Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Occupants may hear intermittent loud popping or crackling noises emanating from the cabin speakers, pointing to faults within the vehicle's fiber-optic media bus network.
- **Solution:** Dealer service may need to diagnose MOST bus connections and replace defective audio control units or wiring components.

## Exterior

### Sliding Doors and Seating

Focuses on family-friendly utility features, including power sliding doors and flexible seating configurations. Power door mechanisms require periodic maintenance to ensure smooth, reliable operation.

**Sliding Door Jams**
- **Symptoms:** The automatic sliding doors occasionally fail to respond to interior switches, exterior handles, or key fob remotes, potentially trapping passengers or restricting entry.
- **Solution:** Inspect door tracks, latch mechanisms, and actuators for alignment issues or debris buildup; lubricate or replace faulty door sensors.

### Sunroof and Glass

Encompasses body panels, lighting, and glass assemblies. Spontaneous glass breakage remains a rare but significant reported concern for select highway drivers.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle's moonroof glass unexpectedly shatters or explodes while driving at highway speeds without prior impact or warning.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged glass panel under comprehensive insurance or warranty where applicable, ensuring the frame is inspected for stress points.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance System

Includes Honda Sensing safety technologies designed to mitigate collisions and assist the driver. While valuable for family safety, sensitivity issues in the collision avoidance programming can occasionally produce false warnings or unintended brake engagement.

**Phantom Braking and Radar Sensor Faults**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle's collision mitigation system occasionally triggers unprompted emergency braking events or displays radar sensor faults while driving on open highways without obstacles present.
- **Solution:** Have dealership technicians recalibrate the forward-facing camera and radar sensors and check for system software updates.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V114000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The steering gear box assembly may not have been tightened properly, allowing water to enter the assembly and cause corrosion and binding gears. Corroded and binding steering gears can result in a loss of steering control and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the electric power steering rack, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V877000: Equipment Adaptive/Mobility:Wheelchair Restraints/Securement:Latch/Anchor:](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The retractors may not lock, preventing the wheelchair from being properly secured. An unsecured wheelchair can move during transit, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Honda Odyssey remains a practical, comfortable, and well-rounded minivan that excels at family hauling. While owner reports point to specific annoyances—such as occasional start-stop stalling, adaptive safety sensitivity quirks, and power door operational hiccups—these issues do not detract from its overall utility. Used car buyers should ensure that any prospective vehicle has had all relevant software updates performed and thoroughly test the start-stop feature and electronics during a pre-purchase test drive.
