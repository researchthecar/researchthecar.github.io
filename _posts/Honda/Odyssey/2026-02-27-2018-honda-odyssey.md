# 2018 Honda Odyssey Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Honda Odyssey is a popular choice for families seeking a versatile and feature-packed minivan. However, used car buyers should approach this model year with care due to a notable cluster of electrical, ADAS, and transmission-related complaints reported to safety authorities.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

Powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine generating approximately 280 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, providing smooth and capable acceleration for daily driving. While generally durable mechanically, this powerplant is tied to occasional owner reports regarding unexpected stalling and electronic management hiccups.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences sudden engine stalling or enters limp mode with multiple warning lights illuminated.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic inspection required; may require software updates, sensor replacements, or addressing fuel system components.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped on lower trims, the 9-speed automatic transmission offers efficient highway cruising. Some owners report shift hesitation or sluggish engagement during low-speed maneuvers.

**Sluggish Shifting and Engagement Delay**
- **Symptoms:** Delayed response when accelerating from a stop or shifting between reverse and drive.
- **Solution:** Check for available manufacturer software updates or service bulletins addressing shift quality.

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

Available on upper trims, the 10-speed automatic provides refined power delivery. Buyers should test drive thoroughly to ensure smooth operation across all gears.

**Random Shifting and Gear Engagement Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle unexpectedly hesitates, jerks at low speeds, or experiences rough gear selection.
- **Solution:** Perform transmission fluid service and consult dealer records for applicable software flashes.

## Exterior

### Power Tailgate Module

Electrical accessories and convenience modules can experience water intrusion issues around the rear hatch seals, leading to recurring component corrosion.

**Water Intrusion and Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power liftgate failure alerts flashing on the dash and eventual loss of power liftgate functionality.
- **Solution:** Repair water leak seals and replace the corroded tailgate control module.
- **Cost:** Close to $2000

### Sliding Doors and Latches

Power sliding doors offer great convenience for families but are prone to sensor sensitivity issues and latch failures over time.

**Sliding Door Reverse and Latch Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Power sliding doors reverse midway during closing or fail to latch securely, triggering warning alerts.
- **Solution:** Clean door tracks, adjust latch sensors, or replace faulty door actuators.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance Camera

Advanced driver-assistance systems rely heavily on front-facing cameras, which have been reported to fail out of warranty, disabling multiple safety features simultaneously.

**Camera and ADAS System Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden dashboard warning lights for adaptive cruise control, lane keeping assist, and collision mitigation systems.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the multipurpose front camera assembly.
- **Cost:** $2000+

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V299000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Loose battery terminal connections or a degraded battery may cause the Transmission Control Unit (TCU) to unexpectedly reboot. A damaged parking rod can allow the vehicle to roll when parked, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will ensure the battery terminal connections are secure and will update the TCU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V777000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." A reduction of braking performance increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Owners will be notified, and Honda or Acura dealers will bleed the brakes, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V795000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the power sliding doors fails to latch, the doors may open while moving, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the left and right power sliding door rear latch assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V213000: Service Brakes, Air:Antilock:Wheel Speed Sensor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake System" and 404, "Electronic Stability Control Systems." If the rear wheel speeds cannot be correctly monitored, the anti-lock brake system may not engage properly to prevent the wheels from locking up, increasing.
- **What to do:** Braun has notified owners, and dealers will inspect and correct the rear wheel speed sensor harnesses, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V397000: Seat Belts](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The seat belt tongue for the third row center seating position may not be compatible with the latch, preventing the seat belt from latching securely. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." If in the event of a crash, the seat belt cannot restrain.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the third row center seat belt to make sure that it has the correct tongue, replacing the seat belt, if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V437000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A power sliding door that fails to latch may open while moving, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the power sliding door outer handle cables, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V439000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) numbers 101, "Control and Displays" and 111, "Rearview Mirrors." Operating a vehicle without a functioning instrument panel or rearview camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the instrument panel control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V066000: Electrical System:Wiring](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** An intermittent electrical short could overheat the wire harness, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the third row seat accessory power outlet wire harness for damage, replacing it as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Honda Odyssey remains a practical and comfortable family hauler, but used car buyers should conduct a thorough inspection prior to purchase. Pay close attention to transmission shift quality, test all advanced safety and driver-assistance features, and check for any signs of electrical or sliding door malfunctions.
