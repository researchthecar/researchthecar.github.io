## 2022 Honda Odyssey

The 2022 Honda Odyssey is a minivan that is offered with two engine options: a 3.5-liter V6 engine and a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine. Both engines are paired with a 10-speed automatic transmission. The V6 engine produces 280 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, while the turbocharged inline-four engine produces 248 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque. The Odyssey is known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and a host of standard features. However, there have been some issues reported with the 2022 model.

**Engine**

**3.5-liter V6 Engine**

* **Piston Ring Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, blue smoke from the exhaust, decreased engine power
    * Solution: Replace the piston rings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Valve Train Noise:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Ticking or rattling noise from the engine
    * Solution: Replace the valve lifters
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.0-liter Turbocharged Inline-Four Engine**

* **Turbocharger Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, increased noise from the engine, blue or black smoke from the exhaust
    * Solution: Replace the turbocharger
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**10-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Shifting Issues:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hard shifting, slipping gears, delayed shifting
    * Solution: Update the transmission control module software
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: None

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Knocking or clunking noise from the front of the vehicle, poor handling
    * Solution: Replace the strut mounts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Rear Shock Absorber Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Bouncy rear suspension, reduced handling
    * Solution: Replace the rear shock absorbers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dead battery after a period of time, difficulty starting the vehicle
    * Solution: Replace the battery or update the software
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, flickering dashboard lights, difficulty starting the vehicle
    * Solution: Replace the alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the headlight housings
    * Solution: Replace the headlight housings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattle:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling or buzzing noise coming from the dashboard area
    * Solution: Tighten or replace loose components in the dashboard
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Navigation System Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Navigation system not working correctly, freezing, or displaying incorrect information
    * Solution: Update the navigation system software
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: None
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Honda Odyssey has several noteworthy issues, particularly with the engine and drivetrain components. The piston ring failure and valve train noise issues with the V6 engine are serious problems that can lead to costly repairs. Additionally, the 10-speed automatic transmission's shifting issues and transmission fluid leak can both impact the vehicle's overall performance. The cost of these repairs can range from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars, depending on the severity of the issue. While the Odyssey offers a spacious and comfortable interior, as well as a host of standard features, potential buyers should be aware of the potential issues associated with this vehicle's powertrain.
