# 2016 Honda Odyssey Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Honda Odyssey remains a popular choice for family hauling, praised for its spacious interior and versatile seating. However, prospective used car buyers should approach with a degree of awareness regarding recurring mechanical and electrical grievances reported to federal safety regulators. Key areas of concern involve engine misfires related to cylinder management systems, transmission shudder, and sliding door mechanisms.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

Equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 248 horsepower and 250 lb-ft of torque, providing strong acceleration for a minivan. Reliability is generally decent, but buyers should watch for oil consumption and misfire symptoms associated with cylinder deactivation.

**Engine Misfire and Piston Ring Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Owners experience engine misfires, illuminated check engine lights, fouled spark plugs, and loss of motive power due to oil fouling in cylinders.
- **Solution:** Diagnose affected cylinders, replace spark plugs, and address piston ring wear or variable cylinder management system issues.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Features a 6-speed automatic transmission paired with front-wheel drive. While generally robust, it can develop notable shudder or shifting roughness over time that requires fluid maintenance.

**Transmission Shudder and Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle shudders or judders during highway cruising speeds, and the tachometer bounces when attempting to accelerate or decelerate.
- **Solution:** Perform a transmission fluid flush or software update per technical service recommendations; severe cases may require mechanical component replacement.

## Steering

### Steering Column

Steering hardware is generally durable, though occasional noises in the column can develop as the vehicle ages.

**Steering Column Clicking and Popping**
- **Symptoms:** An abnormal clicking or popping noise emanates from the steering column when turning the wheel at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect steering components and apply corrective service adjustments as specified by manufacturer bulletins.

## Electrical

### Electrical System & Keyless Entry

electrical issues can occasionally plague comfort and convenience features, most notably involving battery drains caused by moisture intrusion or sensor failures.

**Battery Drain and Flickering Dash**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard flickers, headlights turn on unexpectedly after the vehicle is off, and the battery is frequently drained leaving the car unable to start.
- **Solution:** Inspect keyless entry computer boards for corrosion and replace faulty electrical modules or drained batteries.

## Exterior

### Sliding Doors

The power sliding doors are a convenience staple for minivans but require periodic maintenance to prevent track binding and sensor glitches.

**Sliding Door Track and Latch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sliding doors fail to close fully, emit warning beeps, become difficult to manually operate, or derail from their upper and side tracks.
- **Solution:** Inspect and clean door tracks, lubricate cables, or replace faulty door latches and track assemblies.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V170000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Due to a manufacturing error, if the walk-in release lever is used to fold a fully reclined left outboard seatback forward, the seatback may stay in the unlocked position (free-folding) or unexpectedly unlock without engaging the walk-in or recliner lever. An unlocked second row left outboard seatback increases the risk of injury to the seat occupant during.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will confirm proper operation of the second row left outboard seatback, and install a support bracket to the seat recliner. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V725000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If a seat is placed between either of the two positions when attaching the seat to the vehicle floor, the seat will not latch properly to the seat striker, allowing the seat to tip forward unexpectedly during braking. If the seat tips forward during braking, it can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will install a bracket to the second row outboard floor strikers, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V932000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles have a second row center seat with a horizontal adjuster bar that may stick in the unlocked position, allowing the seat to unexpectedly slide. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 207, "Seat Systems." An unlocked second row center seat may increase the risk.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the slide function and replace the horizontal position adjustment bar, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V933000: Seats:Mid/Rear Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** This release lever may remain in the unlocked position allowing the seats to move unexpectedly. An unlocked second row outboard seat increases the risk of injury to the seat occupant during a crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will install an additional bracket and spring to both second row outboard seats, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V417000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source may result in a fire.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel tanks, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Honda Odyssey is a capable and spacious family transport option, but used car shoppers should perform a thorough inspection prior to purchase. Pay close attention to engine service history regarding spark plugs and piston rings, test-drive the vehicle at highway speeds to check for transmission shudder, and verify that the power sliding doors and electrical accessories operate smoothly.
