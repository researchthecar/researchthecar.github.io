# 2005 Honda Odyssey Reliability & Common Issues

> Provider: `gemini`. Generated: `2026-07-13T00:14:42+00:00`.

### Overview

The 2005 Honda Odyssey has a substantial record of owner complaints concerning safety, mechanical, and structural failures. Chief among these issues are sliding doors that fail to stay latched or fall completely off their tracks, severe transmission shuddering and premature failure, heavy steering effort due to power steering pump degradation, and persistent airbag warning indicators. Used-car buyers should carefully evaluate these common fault points, many of which can require highly expensive out-of-pocket repairs.

---

## Safety

### Air Bag

**Persistent Airbag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers frequently report that the airbag warning light remains illuminated continuously, the passenger airbag fails to function, or the 'Check Airbags' message appears intermittently on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of the airbag assembly, passenger seat sensors, or clock spring (which some owners report also resolves associated horn failures).
- **Evidence:** `nhtsa-complaint-11414588`, `nhtsa-complaint-11378657`, `nhtsa-complaint-11257589`, `nhtsa-complaint-11241666`, `nhtsa-complaint-11232559`, `nhtsa-complaint-11207925`, `nhtsa-complaint-11162624`, `nhtsa-complaint-11104573`, `nhtsa-complaint-11080197`

## Interior

### Latch

**Sliding Door Latch and Spontaneous Opening**
- **Symptoms:** Rear sliding doors fail to latch properly, generate a loud constant warning beep while driving, and may spontaneously fly open while the vehicle is in motion, particularly during cold or freezing weather.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the rear power sliding door latches or actuators, or manually turning off power to the doors.
- **Evidence:** `nhtsa-complaint-11271138`, `nhtsa-complaint-11231412`, `nhtsa-complaint-11229104`, `nhtsa-complaint-11165329`, `nhtsa-complaint-11153263`

### Structure

**Front Passenger Door Latch and Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The front passenger door fails to open from either the inside or outside, even when the door lock knob is in the unlocked position.
- **Solution:** Mechanics may need to destroy the interior door panel to gain access to the door mechanism, requiring replacement of the latch, actuator, and the interior door panel.
- **Cost:** $1,092.56
- **Evidence:** `nhtsa-complaint-11297221`

## Transmission

### Power Train

**Transmission Shuddering, Slipping, and Catastrophic Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Shuddering or shaking of the entire vehicle during acceleration (particularly between 55-65 mph or on inclines), hard gear shifting, whining noises, metal clunking when shifting from Park, and sudden total loss of power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of transmission solenoids (such as pressure control solenoids) or a complete replacement of the transmission assembly.
- **Cost:** Between $3,000 and $6,000
- **Evidence:** `nhtsa-complaint-11422023`, `nhtsa-complaint-11232639`, `nhtsa-complaint-11121799`, `nhtsa-complaint-10978780`, `nhtsa-complaint-10881788`, `nhtsa-complaint-10881866`, `nhtsa-complaint-10705689`, `nhtsa-complaint-10641662`, `nhtsa-complaint-10609124`, `nhtsa-complaint-10605069`

## Brakes

### Brake

**Brake Failure or Spongy Pedal Response**
- **Symptoms:** Spongy brake pedal, intermittent brake failure requiring extreme pedal pressure to stop, or the VSA light illuminating due to a fractured ABS modulator.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of the brake master cylinder or ABS/VSA modulator.
- **Evidence:** `nhtsa-complaint-11399184`, `nhtsa-complaint-10721342`, `nhtsa-complaint-10621326`, `nhtsa-complaint-10357028`, `nhtsa-complaint-10457014`

**Severe Noise and Rust Pitting of Rotors**
- **Symptoms:** Horrific grinding or scratching noises when braking down hills (particularly during warm or summer weather) and premature rust pitting of both front and rear brake rotors.
- **Solution:** Resurfacing of brake drums/discs or full replacement of the rotors and pads.
- **Cost:** $1,500
- **Evidence:** `nhtsa-complaint-10462706`, `nhtsa-complaint-10425144`, `nhtsa-complaint-10380510`, `nhtsa-complaint-10376581`, `nhtsa-complaint-10328225`

## Engine

### Engine

**Uncontrolled Engine Surging**
- **Symptoms:** The engine suddenly revs or surges to 5000 or 6000 RPM when coming to a stop or when shifted into reverse, forcing the driver to apply extreme braking force and shift into neutral.
- **Solution:** Dealership diagnostics using a monitor, though standard valve adjustments and timing belt/water pump replacements have reportedly failed to resolve it.
- **Cost:** $3,216.06
- **Evidence:** `nhtsa-complaint-11590841`

**Premature Engine Mount Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Failure of the primary engine mounts, which can subsequently place stress on other mounts and affect transmission alignment.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the failed engine mounts.
- **Cost:** $2,000
- **Evidence:** `nhtsa-complaint-11221789`, `nhtsa-complaint-11382811`

## Steering

### Steering

**Power Steering Pump Failure and Tight Steering**
- **Symptoms:** Steering wheel becomes extremely tight and difficult to turn, especially at low speeds (such as when parking or turning into a driveway), often accompanied by fluid leaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the power steering pump, reservoir, or fluid lines.
- **Cost:** $271.77
- **Evidence:** `nhtsa-complaint-11428432`, `nhtsa-complaint-11054165`, `nhtsa-complaint-10726726`, `nhtsa-complaint-10714707`, `nhtsa-complaint-10680028`, `nhtsa-complaint-10622647`, `nhtsa-complaint-10618734`

## Exterior

### Structure

**Sliding Door Detachment from Tracks**
- **Symptoms:** Rear sliding doors completely popping off their tracks and detaching or hanging by the control cable during manual or automatic operation, occasionally causing vehicle body scratches or physical injury to the operator.
- **Solution:** Repairing or replacing the sliding door track system, rollers, or cables.
- **Evidence:** `nhtsa-complaint-11471646`, `nhtsa-complaint-11360665`, `nhtsa-complaint-11337465`, `nhtsa-complaint-11234310`, `nhtsa-complaint-11232184`, `nhtsa-complaint-11232208`, `nhtsa-complaint-11194494`, `nhtsa-complaint-11194260`, `nhtsa-complaint-11191682`, `nhtsa-complaint-11163186`, `nhtsa-complaint-11144602`, `nhtsa-complaint-11071889`

## Recall Check

**Recall 14E049000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump**
- **Issue:** Delphi Automotive Systems, LLC (Delphi) is recalling certain replacement fuel pump modules, part number FG1155-11B1, sold for use in 2005-2010 Honda Odyssey vehicles.  The affected fuel pump may send an incorrect fuel reading to the fuel gauge possibly causing the vehicle to run out of fuel.  The fuel gauge will always read 3/4 full to full regardless of the actual level. A vehicle that runs out of fuel and stalls has an increased risk of a crash. Delphi has begun notifying dealers and distributors, and will replace the fuel pump modules, free of charge.  Owners may contact Delphi customer service at 1-877-411-8770.  Delphi's number for this recall is  GL14-004.
- **Remedy:** Check recall completion status with the manufacturer or NHTSA.
- **Evidence:** `nhtsa-recall-14E049000`

**Recall 14V112000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump**
- **Issue:** American Honda Motor Co., Inc. (Honda) is recalling certain 2005-2010 Honda Odyssey vehicles manufactured June 23, 2004, through September 4, 2010.  In the affected vehicles, the fuel pump strainer cover may deteriorate allowing fuel to leak out. A fuel leak increases the risk of a fire. Parts to permanently repair the affected vehicles are not currently available.  Honda will notify owners with an interim letter during May 2014.  A second notice was mailed to owners October 6, 2014 when the remedy became available.  Any vehicles that are currently leaking from the fuel pump cover will get a replacement, original cover.  All vehicles, including those receiving the interim repair, will get an improved cover when they are available.  Owners may contact Honda at 1-800-999-1009. The recall numbers associated with this campaign are JD9 (inspection and/or preliminary part replacement) and JE0 (final parts replacement).
- **Remedy:** Check recall completion status with the manufacturer or NHTSA.

### Summary Verdict

While the 2005 Honda Odyssey offers standard family utility, its long-term reliability is significantly compromised by high-frequency issues. The sliding door structural failures, expensive transmission replacements, persistent airbag warnings, and sudden steering stiffening present notable safety concerns and potentially high out-of-pocket costs for used-car buyers.

## Warnings

- Multiple owners report that the heavy rear sliding doors can completely detach from their tracks and fall off, posing severe safety and physical injury hazards.
- Numerous complaints document sudden transmission failure or severe slipping and shuddering, often resulting in expensive replacement bills ranging up to $6,000.
- There are highly concerning reports of spontaneous engine surging to extremely high RPMs (5,000-6,000) while stopping or reversing.
