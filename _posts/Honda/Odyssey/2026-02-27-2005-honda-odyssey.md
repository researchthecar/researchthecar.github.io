# 2005 Honda Odyssey Reliability & Common Issues

### Overview

The 2005 Honda Odyssey is a highly popular and spacious family minivan. While it offers strong utility and a comfortable cabin, used-car buyers should be aware of several commonly reported mechanical and interior problem areas. The following reliability summary is compiled from owner feedback and National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) records. These complaints are a sample of reported issues and do not mean every vehicle will experience these problems, but they highlight key components that merit close inspection prior to purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

The 2005 Odyssey is powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 255 horsepower. Higher-tier trims (EX-L and Touring) feature Honda's Variable Cylinder Management (VCM) technology, which deactivates three cylinders under light loads to save fuel. While generally durable, this early iteration of VCM is known for accelerating wear on spark plugs and engine mounts.

**VCM System Malfunction and Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Reports indicate excess oil consumption, engine misfires, spark plug fouling, and an illuminated check engine light, often accompanied by distinct vibrations felt inside the cabin.
- **Solution:** Replacing fouled spark plugs and damaged catalytic converters. Many owners also install aftermarket VCM disabling devices to keep all six cylinders permanently active.

**Engine Mount Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Degraded engine mounts can cause harsh cabin vibrations, a noticeable shift of the engine, clunking noises, or accelerated wear on adjacent transmission mounts.
- **Solution:** Replacing the failed engine mounts, particularly the active mounts used on VCM-equipped models.
- **Cost:** Up to $2,000

**Fuel Pump Module Failures and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Reports of unexpected engine stalling while driving, failure to restart until the vehicle has cooled down, and strong gasoline odors.
- **Solution:** Replacing the fuel pump module, seals, or related wiring.

**Sudden Engine Surging**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported instances where the engine unexpectedly surges to high RPMs (up to 5000-6000 RPM) while slowing down or coming to a stop with the brakes applied.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential cleaning or replacement of the throttle body assembly, vacuum lines, or timing components.

## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic Transmission

Standard on all 2005 Odyssey models is a 5-speed automatic transmission. This gearbox is frequently cited for running hot and experiencing mechanical fatigue under heavy towing or hill-climbing conditions. Regular fluid changes and aftermarket auxiliary coolers are highly recommended by enthusiasts to prolong its lifespan.

**Shifting Slippage, Shudder, and Gear Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission may hesitate, fail to shift into higher gears, slip under acceleration (particularly up inclines), or emit a loud metal clunk when shifted from Park into Reverse or Drive.
- **Solution:** A complete rebuild or replacement of the automatic transmission unit.
- **Cost:** $3,000 to $4,000

**Transmission Overheating and Solenoid Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Fluid blowing out of the transmission breather vent, a burning smell, immediate loss of motive power, and trouble codes related to stuck solenoids or pressure switches (P0746, P0777, P0741).
- **Solution:** Replacing failed shift or pressure control solenoids, flushing transmission lines, and installing an aftermarket transmission cooler.

## Brakes

### Brake System

**Sudden Braking Pressure Loss**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal may feel extremely spongy or sink to the floor, requiring significant pumping or excessive force to bring the vehicle to a halt.
- **Solution:** Replacing the brake master cylinder or testing and replacing a faulty ABS / VSA modulator.

**Summer Brake Grinding and Shudder**
- **Symptoms:** A loud grinding or scratching sound occurs when applying brakes on downgrades during hot weather, often resulting in reduced braking performance.
- **Solution:** Resurfacing or replacing front rotors and pads with premium, heat-resistant components, and keeping the assembly free of heavy brake dust.

## Steering

### Power Steering Assembly

**Heavy or Difficult Steering at Low Speeds**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel becomes extremely stiff or nearly impossible to turn during low-speed maneuvers, such as parking or turning into a driveway, particularly in cold weather.
- **Solution:** Replacing the power steering pump, fluid lines, and flushing the steering system.
- **Cost:** Around $271 for pump replacement

## Exterior

### Body and Sliding Doors

**Sliding Doors Detaching from Track**
- **Symptoms:** Heavy rear sliding doors can bind, jump their tracks, or detach completely from the vehicle's body when opened, sometimes hanging solely by the control cable.
- **Solution:** Replacing the worn or disintegrated plastic rollers and bracket assemblies on the sliding door hinges.

## Interior

### Cabin Components

**Power Sliding Door Latch and Spontaneous Opening**
- **Symptoms:** Sliding doors fail to latch shut properly—especially in cold or freezing weather—or unexpectedly slide open on their own while the vehicle is in motion.
- **Solution:** Replacing the door lock actuators, cleaning latch assemblies to remove friction, or replacing the rear power door latches.

**Front Passenger Door Latch Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The front passenger door becomes permanently stuck closed and cannot be opened from either the interior or exterior handles, despite the lock mechanism appearing functional.
- **Solution:** The interior door card must be carefully removed or cut to access the inner mechanics, followed by replacing the actuator and latch assembly.
- **Cost:** Approximately $1,092

**Third-Row Seat Latching Issue**
- **Symptoms:** When pulling the 60/40 split folding third-row seats back up from their flat position, they may fail to lock securely into place, creating a hazard if they fall forward during driving.
- **Solution:** Inspecting, cleaning, or replacing the floor latch and guide mechanisms.

## Safety

### Airbags and Restraints

**Persistent Airbag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** The SRS or airbag warning light illuminates and stays on permanently on the dashboard, which typically disables the airbag safety system.
- **Solution:** Replacing faulty driver or passenger airbag modules, seatbelt buckle tensioners, or seat sensors.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**Recall 14E049000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump**
- **Issue:** Delphi Automotive Systems, LLC (Delphi) is recalling certain replacement fuel pump modules, part number FG1155-11B1, sold for use in 2005-2010 Honda Odyssey vehicles.  The affected fuel pump may send an incorrect fuel reading to the fuel gauge possibly causing the vehicle to run out of fuel.  The fuel gauge will always read 3/4 full to full regardless of the actual level. A vehicle that runs out of fuel and stalls has an increased risk of a crash. Delphi has begun notifying dealers and distributors, and will replace the fuel pump modules, free of charge.  Owners may contact Delphi customer service at 1-877-411-8770.  Delphi's number for this recall is  GL14-004.
- **Remedy:** Check recall completion status with the manufacturer or NHTSA.

**Recall 14V112000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump**
- **Issue:** American Honda Motor Co., Inc. (Honda) is recalling certain 2005-2010 Honda Odyssey vehicles manufactured June 23, 2004, through September 4, 2010.  In the affected vehicles, the fuel pump strainer cover may deteriorate allowing fuel to leak out. A fuel leak increases the risk of a fire. Parts to permanently repair the affected vehicles are not currently available.  Honda will notify owners with an interim letter during May 2014.  A second notice was mailed to owners October 6, 2014 when the remedy became available.  Any vehicles that are currently leaking from the fuel pump cover will get a replacement, original cover.  All vehicles, including those receiving the interim repair, will get an improved cover when they are available.  Owners may contact Honda at 1-800-999-1009. The recall numbers associated with this campaign are JD9 (inspection and/or preliminary part replacement) and JE0 (final parts replacement).
- **Remedy:** Check recall completion status with the manufacturer or NHTSA.

IF REPLACEMENT BRAKE FLUID IS USED THAT DOES NOT CONTAIN SUCH POLYMERS, OR THAT CONTAINS ONLY SMALL AMOUNTS, A PART OF THE RUBBER SEAL LOCATED AT THE REAR OF THE BRAKE MASTER CYLINDER MAY BECOME DRY, AND THE SEAL MAY CURL DURING MOVEMENT OF THE PISTON.  IF THIS OCCURS, A SMALL AMOUNT OF BRAKE FLUID COULD SLOWLY LEAK FROM THE SEAL INTO THE BRAKE BOOSTER. A LEAK OF BRAKE FLUID COULD LEAD TO A CHANGE IN BRAKE PEDAL FEEL AND OVERTIME A DEGRADATION IN BRAKING PERFORMANCE INCREASING THE RISK OF A CRASH. DEALERS WILL REPLACE THE BRAKE MASTER CYLINDER SEAL FREE OF CHARGE.  THE RECALL IS EXPECTED TO BEGIN DURING DECEMBER 2010.  OWNERS MAY CONTACT HONDA AT 1-800-999-1009.

### Summary Verdict

The 2005 Honda Odyssey remains a highly functional vehicle for families on a budget, but its high volume of reported owner issues means buyers must tread carefully. Particular attention should be paid to the automatic transmission, power steering system, and power sliding doors, all of which are common failure points for this model year. A thorough pre-purchase inspection by a mechanic familiar with Honda V6 engine mounts and sliding door mechanisms is highly recommended to avoid costly post-purchase repairs.
