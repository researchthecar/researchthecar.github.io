# 2023 Honda Odyssey Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Honda Odyssey remains a staple in the minivan segment, praised for its practical interior and smooth ride. However, owners have reported various issues spanning powertrain operation, electrical accessories, and safety tech. Prospective buyers should inspect these areas carefully during a test drive to ensure everything is in proper working order.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6

Powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 280 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, this powerplant offers brisk acceleration and solid highway performance. Overall reliability is generally strong, though isolated owner reports note intermittent rough running and hesitation.

**Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may hesitate, jerk, or experience a sudden loss of motive power, occasionally stalling without prior warning.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic testing by a qualified technician is required to check fuel delivery, ignition components, and electronic sensor performance.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic

Paired exclusively with the V6 engine, this 10-speed automatic transmission delivers power smoothly to the front wheels. It is well-matched to the vehicle's gearing needs, with very few widespread mechanical complaints noted by owners.

## Exterior

### Tailgate Control Unit

Electrical complaints focus primarily on accessory modules and power-operated features like the rear liftgate.

**Power Liftgate Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The power tailgate fails to operate completely due to water intrusion and subsequent corrosion in the control unit.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty tailgate control module and ensure the housing seal is secure to prevent future moisture entry.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Advanced safety features and occupant restraints generally perform well, though minor complaints highlight occasional issues with electronic driver aids and belt retractors.

**Seatbelt Retractor Locking**
- **Symptoms:** The seatbelt mechanism can lock excessively tight around a passenger and fail to unbuckle or release tension normally.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seatbelt assembly and replace the retractor mechanism if locking components fail to operate properly.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V431000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Due to a faulty Media Oriented Systems Transport (MOST) communication coaxial cable connector, the rearview camera image may not appear on the display. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not display an image can reduce the driver's.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the MOST cable harness and install a straightening cover over the vehicle cable connector, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V114000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The steering gear box assembly may not have been tightened properly, allowing water to enter the assembly and cause corrosion and binding gears. Corroded and binding steering gears can result in a loss of steering control and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the electric power steering rack, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V877000: Equipment Adaptive/Mobility:Wheelchair Restraints/Securement:Latch/Anchor:](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The retractors may not lock, preventing the wheelchair from being properly secured. An unsecured wheelchair can move during transit, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Honda Odyssey is a well-rounded and versatile family hauler, but shoppers should verify the proper operation of the power liftgate, seatbelts, and powertrain electronics prior to purchase. A thorough pre-purchase inspection can catch any lingering intermittent faults before they become costly out-of-pocket repairs.
