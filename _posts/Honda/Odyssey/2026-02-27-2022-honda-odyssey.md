# 2022 Honda Odyssey Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Honda Odyssey continues to be a top choice for families, offering a spacious cabin, refined ride quality, and a robust V6 engine. However, used-car buyers should review owner complaints regarding occasional electrical glitches, power sliding door or liftgate operation, and advanced safety system sensitivities.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

Equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 280 horsepower, providing strong and smooth acceleration for daily driving. This powertrain is generally reliable, though owners have occasionally reported isolated issues regarding idle stability and unexpected stalls.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may experience unexpected stalling or rough idling during stops.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic check by a qualified technician to inspect fuel delivery and software calibrations.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with a 10-speed automatic transmission that delivers smooth power delivery and highway efficiency across all trims. While mostly trouble-free, buyers should verify proper shifting behavior during a test drive.

**Unexpected Shifting to Neutral**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission may unexpectedly shift into neutral while driving at highway speeds, causing a sudden loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Dealer inspection for transmission control module updates or internal component wear.

## Suspension

### Rear Suspension

The suspension provides a comfortable, compliant ride suited for road trips, though rear components should be inspected during routine maintenance for premature wear.

**Suspension Squeaks and Component Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Abnormal squeaking noises when driving over bumps or premature fracturing of rear coil springs and shocks.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace worn rear shocks or coil springs.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Power Accessories

The electrical system powers a wide array of convenience features, though certain owners have encountered intermittent touchscreen resets and power door sensor glitches.

**Infotainment Screen Reboot**
- **Symptoms:** Center display screen or backup camera may suddenly go blank or reboot while driving.
- **Solution:** Software update or infotainment system module replacement.

**Power Tailgate and Sliding Door Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Power tailgate may fail to open via key fob, sag over time, or automatic sliding door sensors may fail to detect obstacles.
- **Solution:** Inspect liftgate struts, water infiltration seals, and power door control units.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety features provide added reassurance for families, but sensor calibration issues can occasionally trigger false warnings or unexpected braking.

**Erroneous Collision Braking**
- **Symptoms:** Collision mitigation braking system may unexpectedly engage on the highway or near intersections without an actual obstruction.
- **Solution:** Reprogramming or calibration of front-facing radar and camera sensors by a dealer.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V768000: Tires:Sidewall](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these tires fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 139, "New Pneumatic Radial Tires for Light Vehicles." A slow air leak can affect tire performance, and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect all four tires and replace the tire if a pinhole is found, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V432000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The second-row outboard seats may have deformed seat rail locking mechanisms, which could prevent the seat rails from locking into place. A seat that is not locked into place can move unexpectedly, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both second-row outboard seat frames, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V174000: Visibility:Rearview Mirrors/Devices:Exterior](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The heating pads behind both side-view mirrors may not be bonded properly, allowing the mirror glass to detach. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." Detached mirror glass can reduce driver visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both left and right side-view mirrors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V431000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Due to a faulty Media Oriented Systems Transport (MOST) communication coaxial cable connector, the rearview camera image may not appear on the display. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not display an image can reduce the driver's.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the MOST cable harness and install a straightening cover over the vehicle cable connector, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V064000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, failing to suppress the air bag as intended. An air bag that deploys unintentionally during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V877000: Equipment Adaptive/Mobility:Wheelchair Restraints/Securement:Latch/Anchor:](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The retractors may not lock, preventing the wheelchair from being properly secured. An unsecured wheelchair can move during transit, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V227000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A software programming error can cause the side and side curtain air bags to deploy unexpectedly. Air bags that deploy unexpectedly can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the supplemental restraint system (SRS) electronic control unit (ECU) software or replace the ECU, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Honda Odyssey remains a highly capable and family-friendly minivan with strong performance and versatile interior space. While most owners experience dependable operation, prospective buyers should thoroughly test drive the vehicle to check transmission responsiveness, inspect the operation of power sliding doors and the tailgate, and verify that all electronic safety aids and infotainment features function correctly.
