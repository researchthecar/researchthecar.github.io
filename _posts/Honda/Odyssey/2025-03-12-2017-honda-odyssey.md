Here is a breakdown of common issues and complaints for the 2017 Honda Odyssey, organized by category and engine/transmission configuration, following your specified format.

The 2017 Honda Odyssey is a popular minivan known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and family-friendly features. It comes equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing 280 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission. Owners appreciate the Odyssey's practicality, smooth ride, and advanced safety features. Common dislikes include the infotainment system's complexity and some reported reliability issues.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6**

*   **Variable Timing System (VTS) Actuator Noise**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A rattling or grinding noise from the engine, especially during cold starts. This may indicate a problem with the VTS actuator/phaser. The noise is caused by oil pressure changes in the engine.
    *   Solution: Replacing the VTS actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Misfires/Poor Performance**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, rough idling, loss of power, and the check engine light illuminating. Often caused by faulty spark plugs, ignition coils, or fuel injectors.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing the cause of the misfire and replacing the faulty component (spark plug, coil, injector).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ depending on the component(s) needing replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Poor fuel economy, rough idling, and potential misfires. Fuel injectors may become clogged or fail to deliver the proper amount of fuel.
    *   Solution: Cleaning or replacing the fuel injectors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**9-Speed Automatic Transmission**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Jerking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or jerky shifting between gears, particularly at lower speeds. Hesitation during acceleration.
    *   Solution: Updating the transmission control module (TCM) software, replacing valve bodies, or a complete transmission replacement if the issue is severe.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (software update) - $4000+ (transmission replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The engine revving up without a corresponding increase in vehicle speed. The transmission struggles to engage gears.
    *   Solution: Typically requires replacing the transmission.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3000 - $5000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The battery drains overnight or within a few days, preventing the vehicle from starting.
    *   Solution: Diagnosing the source of the parasitic draw (faulty module, aftermarket accessory) and repairing or replacing the offending component.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system freezes, reboots unexpectedly, or experiences lag. Bluetooth connectivity problems and navigation errors.
    *   Solution: Updating the infotainment system software, replacing the infotainment unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1500+ (unit replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sliding Door Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The power sliding doors failing to open or close properly. Issues with the door motor, latch, or sensors.
    *   Solution: Replacing the door motor, latch assembly, or sensors. Recalibrating the sliding door system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per door.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rear Entertainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The rear entertainment screen freezes, doesn't turn on, or has distorted video or audio.
    *   Solution: Replacing the screen or the rear entertainment system unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Seat Motor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power seats failing to adjust properly, motor noise without movement.
    *   Solution: Replacing the seat motor or switch.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*No Issues Found*

**Summary**

The 2017 Honda Odyssey is a generally reliable minivan, but some owners have reported issues with the 9-speed transmission, VTS actuator, electrical system, and infotainment system. The severity of these issues varies, with transmission problems potentially leading to costly repairs. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to any unusual symptoms can help minimize potential problems.
