# 2021 Honda Odyssey Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Honda Odyssey remains a top-tier choice in the minivan segment, praised for its spacious interior, refined ride, and strong V6 performance. However, used car buyers should note a series of recurring owner complaints reported to safety regulators. These include issues ranging from engine idle-stop failures and infotainment glitches to suspension wear and power tailgate malfunctions.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6

Equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 280 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, paired with a smooth-shifting automatic transmission. This powertrain provides brisk acceleration and decent efficiency for a family hauler, though the stop-start system can occasionally present reliability concerns.

**Engine Stalling / Idle-Stop Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may fail to restart after the Auto Idle-Stop system engages at a stoplight or stop sign, particularly during warm weather.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic testing by a dealer or qualified mechanic is required; replacement of faulty starter components, battery sensors, or software updates may be necessary.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic

The standard 10-speed automatic transmission delivers power smoothly to the front wheels and generally maintains a strong reputation for durability, with few systemic complaints reported by owners.

## Suspension

### Rear Suspension

The suspension provides a comfortable, compliant ride suited for family road trips, though a small number of owners have encountered premature component wear in the rear.

**Premature Shock or Spring Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Unusual metal-on-metal noises, a rough ride, or rear shock absorbers and coil springs snapping during normal driving conditions.
- **Solution:** Inspect rear springs and shock absorbers for premature wear or cracking, and replace damaged components immediately.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Display System

Electrical architecture governs the cabin infotainment, driver displays, and convenience accessories, with occasional owner reports highlighting screen lockups and audio unit glitches.

**Infotainment Screen Freezing and Rebooting**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display may freeze, display error messages such as system crashes, crackle through the speakers, or reboot randomly.
- **Solution:** Software updates performed by a dealership or a system reset can sometimes resolve communication errors on the fiber-optic network.

## Exterior

### Tailgate and Door Latches

Interior convenience features, including power sliding doors and a power liftgate, add everyday utility but can occasionally experience latch or motor binding issues over time.

**Power Tailgate Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The power liftgate fails to respond to key fob, dashboard, or exterior buttons, leaving it stuck open or closed.
- **Solution:** Inspect the tailgate wiring harness, actuators, and fuses; replace faulty latch mechanisms or control modules as needed.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Driver-assist technologies offer valuable protection on the highway, though sensor sensitivity in construction zones or poor weather can occasionally trigger false warnings.

**Unexpected Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The forward collision prevention or lane-keeping assist system may unexpectedly engage the brakes when no immediate road hazard is present.
- **Solution:** Check camera and radar sensor alignment or consult a dealership for potential ADAS calibration and software updates.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V010000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source may increase the risk of fire.
- **What to do:** Braun will notify owners, and dealers will inspect vehicles. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V174000: Visibility:Rearview Mirrors/Devices:Exterior](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The heating pads behind both side-view mirrors may not be bonded properly, allowing the mirror glass to detach. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." Detached mirror glass can reduce driver visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both left and right side-view mirrors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V431000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Due to a faulty Media Oriented Systems Transport (MOST) communication coaxial cable connector, the rearview camera image may not appear on the display. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not display an image can reduce the driver's.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the MOST cable harness and install a straightening cover over the vehicle cable connector, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V064000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, failing to suppress the air bag as intended. An air bag that deploys unintentionally during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V877000: Equipment Adaptive/Mobility:Wheelchair Restraints/Securement:Latch/Anchor:](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The retractors may not lock, preventing the wheelchair from being properly secured. An unsecured wheelchair can move during transit, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the retractors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V227000: Air Bags: Air Bag/Restraint Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A software programming error can cause the side and side curtain air bags to deploy unexpectedly. Air bags that deploy unexpectedly can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the supplemental restraint system (SRS) electronic control unit (ECU) software or replace the ECU, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Odyssey&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Honda Odyssey is a highly versatile and comfortable family vehicle, but prospective buyers should inspect potential trouble spots thoroughly during a pre-purchase evaluation. Pay close attention to the functionality of the power liftgate, infotainment system responsiveness, and the condition of the rear suspension. Ensuring all software updates and routine maintenance are up to date will help secure a more dependable ownership experience.
