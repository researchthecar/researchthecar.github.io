**Introduction**

The 1991 Honda Prelude is a sports coupe known for its sleek design, responsive handling, and reliable performance. It was available with two engine options: a 2.0-liter inline-4 producing 135 horsepower and 133 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.1-liter inline-4 producing 140 horsepower and 142 lb-ft of torque. Both engines came with a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission.

Drivers appreciate the Prelude's sporty character, comfortable interior, and impressive fuel economy. However, some issues have been reported with certain components over the years.

**Engine**

* **2.0L Inline-4**
    * **Cylinder Head Gasket Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, loss of power
        * Solution: Replace cylinder head gasket
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * **Timing Belt Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Noisy engine, stalling, loss of power
        * Solution: Replace timing belt and tensioner
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * **Water Pump Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating
        * Solution: Replace water pump
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

* **2.1L Inline-4**
    * **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Manual**
    * **Clutch Master Cylinder Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, soft or spongy clutch pedal
        * Solution: Replace clutch master cylinder
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **4-Speed Automatic**
    * **Transmission Fluid Leak**
        * Description/Symptoms: Transmission fluid leaks, slipping gears, harsh shifting
        * Solution: Inspect and repair leak, replace transmission fluid
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Suspension**

* **Front Lower Control Arm Bushing Wear**
    * Description/Symptoms: Squealing noises, uneven tire wear, instability during cornering
    * Solution: Replace lower control arm bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, difficulty starting, battery light on
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Rust Prone**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust spots on body panels, wheel wells, and exhaust system
    * Solution: Regular cleaning and rust prevention treatment
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $Varies depending on severity

**Interior**

* **Power Window Motor Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Slow or inoperative power windows
    * Solution: Replace power window motor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Sunroof Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin through the sunroof
    * Solution: Inspect seals, adjust sunroof alignment, or replace seals
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Summary**

Overall, the 1991 Honda Prelude is a generally reliable vehicle. However, it is not immune to certain issues that can arise with age and mileage. The most common issues include cylinder head gasket failure, timing belt failure, and rust on the body panels. The cost of repairs can vary depending on the severity of the issue. Regular maintenance and proper care can help extend the lifespan of the vehicle and minimize unexpected expenses.
