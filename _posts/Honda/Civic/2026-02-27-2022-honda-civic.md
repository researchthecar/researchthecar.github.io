# 2022 Honda Civic Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Honda Civic arrives as a fully redesigned, well-received compact vehicle known for its modern styling, improved interior quality, and balanced driving dynamics. However, prospective buyers should look closely at steering reliability and a few powertrain-specific concerns, particularly regarding electronic power steering stickiness and manual transmission clutch wear reported by some owners.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The naturally aspirated 2.0-liter 4-cylinder engine produces approximately 158 horsepower and 138 lb-ft of torque, serving as the dependable standard powerplant for lower trims. It generally offers solid long-term mechanical reliability with few widespread complaint-backed issues.

### 1.5L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine

The 1.5-liter turbocharged 4-cylinder engine bumps output to roughly 180 horsepower and 177 lb-ft of torque, delivering a more spirited driving experience for higher trims. It is generally robust, though turbocharged systems require diligent routine maintenance from prior owners.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The continuously variable transmission is paired primarily with both mainstream engine options to maximize fuel efficiency and smooth power delivery. It operates reliably under normal conditions provided fluid changes are maintained on schedule.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The available 6-speed manual transmission caters to enthusiasts wanting a more engaging drive, though select owners have reported premature clutch wear.

**Clutch Failure and Slippage**
- **Symptoms:** A burning smell inside the cabin, difficulty shifting into gear, and severe clutch slippage while accelerating or driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect the clutch assembly and replace worn clutch plates, discs, or related manual drivetrain components.

## Steering

### Electronic Power Steering Rack

**Steering Assist Failure and Sticky Steering**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel feels sticky, stiff, or notched around the center position at highway speeds, requiring increased steering effort and making micro-adjustments difficult.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the electric power steering (EPS) gearbox and replace the steering rack assembly if internal binding or worm wheel swelling is diagnosed.

### Steering Gearbox

The electric power assist mechanism controls steering effort, though the steering rack and EPS gearbox assemblies represent a notable area of concern due to recurring stickiness complaints.

## Electrical

### Body Electrical and Locks

Electrical accessories generally function well, though intermittent faults with power locks and exterior lighting can occasionally require minor troubleshooting.

**Intermittent Door Lock Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The driver-side door intermittently fails to unlock or automatically lock when leaving the vehicle with the key fob.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the door lock actuator or electronic control module and replace faulty latch components as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V704000: Steering:Rack And Pinion](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Civic&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** A damaged tire can fail and increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the electric power steering rack, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V064000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Civic&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, failing to suppress the air bag as intended. An air bag that deploys unintentionally during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V744000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Civic&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Difficulty steering can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the worm gear spring and redistribute or add grease as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Civic&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Honda Civic is a compelling used compact car option that excels in comfort, efficiency, and modern design. While most mechanical systems prove durable, used car shoppers should thoroughly check the steering feel during a test drive to ensure there is no abnormal stickiness or heavy spots. Additionally, manual transmission models should be inspected for any signs of premature clutch wear.
