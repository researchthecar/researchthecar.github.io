# 2017 Honda Civic Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Honda Civic offers an engaging drive, impressive fuel economy, and a spacious cabin, making it a popular choice on the used market. However, prospective buyers should review certain known mechanical and cosmetic tendencies, including steering feel concerns, paint durability issues on specific finishes, and climate control component failures.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder

Naturally aspirated 2.0-liter 4-cylinder engine producing around 158 horsepower, providing dependable daily driving performance and strong baseline fuel efficiency.

### 1.5L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

Turbocharged 1.5-liter 4-cylinder engine delivering approximately 174 horsepower with lively acceleration, though some owners have reported intermittent propulsion and stalling concerns.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences sudden loss of power or stalls while driving, occasionally accompanied by diagnostic trouble codes or warning lights.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician scan for fault codes, check fuel delivery components, and inspect the throttle system.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Smooth continuously variable transmission paired with front-wheel drive, optimized for highway efficiency and seamless power delivery.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

Engaging 6-speed manual transmission option available on select trims, offering direct gear selection and a traditional driving experience.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Electric Power Steering

Electric power steering system provides light and responsive handling, but some drivers experience premature rack wear or stiffness.

**Sticky or Stiff Steering**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel develops a sticky or notched feeling during minor adjustments, requiring deliberate overcorrection to maintain lane position.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electric power steering assembly and steering gear components; replacement of the steering rack may be required in severe cases.

## Exterior

### Body Control Module and Accessories

Centralized electrical architecture handles cabin electronics and security features, though occasional module anomalies can cause parasitic battery drains.

**Battery Drain and Glitching Accessories**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected battery drain occurs alongside random alarm activations, flickering instrument cluster lights, or failure to recognize the key fob.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the body control module (BCM) and test electrical draws to identify faulty modules or relays.

### Paint and Body Structure

Body panels and exterior finishes generally hold up well, though certain paint colors have documented peeling vulnerabilities.

**Paint Peeling and Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Clear coat or paint begins to peel and flake off, particularly around the roof and rear windshield areas, exposing bare metal to potential rust.
- **Solution:** Inspect affected panels for paint degradation and apply professional refinishing or protective sealants to prevent corrosion.

## Recall Check

**[Recall 18V663000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Civic&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The magnet that controls the torque sensor output signal for the electronic power steering system may not be properly secured, allowing the magnet to become dislodged. During a full lock turn, the dislodged magnet may cause steering assist to be applied in the opposite direction.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the steering gearbox assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Honda Civic remains a compelling and practical used car choice, praised for its driving dynamics and efficiency. Buyers should thoroughly inspect the steering feel during a test drive, verify the condition of the paint and climate control system, and ensure all electrical accessories operate properly before purchasing.
