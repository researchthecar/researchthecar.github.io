# 2021 Honda Civic Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Honda Civic remains a popular and generally reliable compact car, known for its efficiency, practical layout, and nimble driving dynamics. However, prospective buyers should carefully review specific known trouble spots reported by owners, particularly related to steering feel and advanced driver-assistance features. Taking a thorough test drive can help catch many of these concerns before purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Naturally Aspirated 4-Cylinder

This base 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine produces around 158 horsepower and serves as a dependable, low-maintenance option for daily commuting.

### 1.5L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 1.5-liter turbocharged engine boosts output to 174 or 205 horsepower depending on the trim, offering a more spirited drive while requiring attentive maintenance.

**High-Pressure Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may experience sudden engine performance issues or starting difficulties due to fuel delivery failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the faulty high-pressure fuel pump.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The continuously variable transmission is paired with most trims to maximize fuel economy, delivering smooth acceleration under normal driving conditions.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

Available on select sportier trims, the 6-speed manual transmission offers a more engaging driving experience with solid mechanical durability.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Electric Power Steering

Steering complaints are among the most notable issues for this model year, with drivers experiencing sticky spots or resistance during minor course corrections.

**Steering Wheel Stickiness and Binding**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel feels as though it sticks or binds at highway speeds, requiring extra effort to return to center and potentially causing overcorrection.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the electronic power steering gearbox and steering rack for internal binding.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety features generally enhance protection, though intermittent glitches with collision avoidance sensors can cause abrupt vehicle slowing.

**Unexpected Automatic Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle suddenly applies the brakes without warning or in the absence of an immediate front-end obstacle while using cruise control or driving normally.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates related to the Honda Sensing suite and have sensor alignment verified by a dealership.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V458000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Master Cylinder](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Civic&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Brake master cylinder separation can cause a loss of brake function and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the brake booster assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V064000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Civic&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, failing to suppress the air bag as intended. An air bag that deploys unintentionally during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Civic&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Civic&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Honda Civic offers strong fuel economy and everyday usability, but used-car buyers should pay close attention during a test drive. Specifically, test the steering responsiveness at highway speeds to check for any unnatural sticking, and verify that all driver-assistance systems operate smoothly without phantom braking events.
