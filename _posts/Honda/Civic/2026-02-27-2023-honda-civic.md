# 2023 Honda Civic Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Honda Civic continues to be a strong contender in the compact car segment, offering sharp handling, modern technology, and efficient powertrain choices. As a relatively recent model year, it maintains a solid reputation among used car buyers looking for modern safety features and everyday practicality. While overall owner satisfaction remains high, prospective buyers should review available powertrain options and monitor minor early-production quirks reported by drivers.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder

This naturally aspirated 2.0-liter 4-cylinder engine produces approximately 158 horsepower and 138 lb-ft of torque, serving as the dependable base powerplant for lower trims. It offers straightforward mechanical simplicity and generally strong long-term reliability for daily commuting.

### 1.5L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

This turbocharged 1.5-liter 4-cylinder engine generates around 180 horsepower and 177 lb-ft of torque, providing a more spirited acceleration experience for higher trims. Buyers should maintain regular oil change intervals to support long-term turbocharger longevity.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

This continuously variable transmission is paired with most Civic trims to optimize fuel efficiency and smooth power delivery. Regular fluid maintenance is recommended to ensure trouble-free operation over high mileage.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

Available on select performance-oriented trims, this 6-speed manual transmission offers an engaging driving experience for enthusiasts. It features a traditional clutch setup with generally robust mechanical durability.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V430000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Civic&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Unintended vehicle movement or an unexpected increase in brake pedal travel can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the VSA modulator, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V704000: Steering:Rack And Pinion](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Civic&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A damaged tire can fail and increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the electric power steering rack, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V744000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Civic&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Difficulty steering can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the worm gear spring and redistribute or add grease as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V859000: Seats:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Civic&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The driver's seat cushion frame may not have been tightened properly, which can result in an unsecured seat. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 207, "Seating Systems." An unsecured driver's seat may not adequately restrain the driver during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver's seat cushion frame, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Honda Civic remains a compelling and sensible choice for used car shoppers, balancing efficiency, safety, and modern styling. Its established powertrain options offer reliable performance when properly maintained. Buyers are encouraged to perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection to verify overall vehicle condition before finalizing a purchase.
