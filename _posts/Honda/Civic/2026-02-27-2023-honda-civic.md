# 2023 Honda Civic Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Honda Civic continues to be a popular compact car choice, offering refined road manners, solid fuel economy, and up-to-date tech features. However, used car buyers should pay close attention to potential problem areas—most notably steering complaints regarding sticky steering racks, as well as occasional electrical and driver-assist system glitches.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Naturally Aspirated I4

The base 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces approximately 158 horsepower, providing dependable everyday performance and solid baseline efficiency for commuters.

**Premature Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks and unexpected engine power loss while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect the engine bay for coolant leaks and replace the head gasket if failure is confirmed.

### 1.5L Turbocharged I4

The available 1.5-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates around 180 horsepower, offering stronger acceleration and a more engaging driving experience while maintaining good fuel economy.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The continuously variable transmission routes power smoothly to the front wheels, though owners should monitor for unusual shifting or slipping behavior during test drives.

**Clutch and Powertrain Slip**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle slipping out of gear or unexpected loss of motive power during operation.
- **Solution:** Inspect the transmission assembly and replace worn components or fluid as necessary.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The enthusiast-oriented 6-speed manual transmission provides crisp shift action and direct driver engagement on select trims.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Column

The electric power steering system provides light, maneuverable handling at low speeds, but a notable number of drivers have reported sticky steering sensations and power assist issues.

**Sticky Steering and Rack Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel feels sticky or stuck, requiring extra force to keep centered at highway speeds and causing overcorrection.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician inspect the steering rack assembly; replacement of the steering rack may be required.

**Steering Assist Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power steering assistance accompanied by electrical warning lights on the instrument cluster.
- **Solution:** Check diagnostic trouble codes and update or replace power steering control modules as needed.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Lighting Systems

Onboard electronics govern modern safety suites and convenience features, though intermittent sensor warnings and blown accessory fuses have been noted by some owners.

**Electrical System and Safety Warning Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Multiple warning lights illuminate simultaneously on the dashboard, affecting systems like cruise control and collision mitigation.
- **Solution:** Scan for electrical fault codes, inspect battery health, and verify software updates with a dealer.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V430000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip:Control Unit/Module](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Civic&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Unintended vehicle movement or an unexpected increase in brake pedal travel can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the VSA modulator, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V704000: Steering:Rack And Pinion](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Civic&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A damaged tire can fail and increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the electric power steering rack, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V744000: Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Civic&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Difficulty steering can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the worm gear spring and redistribute or add grease as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V859000: Seats:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Civic&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The driver's seat cushion frame may not have been tightened properly, which can result in an unsecured seat. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 207, "Seating Systems." An unsecured driver's seat may not adequately restrain the driver during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver's seat cushion frame, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2023 Honda Civic, pay close attention to the steering feel during a test drive to ensure there is no abnormal stickiness or play in the wheel. Verify that all electronic driver-assistance systems and dashboard warning lights function normally, and check the engine bay for any signs of coolant leaks.
