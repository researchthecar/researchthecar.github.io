# 2012 Honda Civic

### Overview
The 2012 Honda Civic is generally considered a reliable vehicle, building on Honda's reputation for durability. It offers a range of engine and transmission options, catering to different driving preferences and fuel economy needs. While most owners report a positive experience with minimal unexpected repairs, like any vehicle, certain components can occasionally present issues. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems reported by owners and technicians, providing insights into potential areas of concern for the 2012 model year across its various configurations.

## Electrical

### Standard Electrical System
**Intermittent Power Window Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows may operate slowly, intermittently, or stop working altogether.
- **Solution:** Replacement of window regulator or motor.
- **Cost:** $250 - $600

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Unexplained battery drain leading to starting issues.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair of parasitic draw, potentially involving faulty modules or accessories.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 Honda Civic stands out as a dependable compact car with a strong track record for reliability. The powertrain, including both the 1.8L and 2.4L engines and their associated automatic and manual transmissions, generally performs without significant widespread issues. The suspension system also appears robust. The most commonly cited non-wear-and-tear concerns revolve around the electrical system, specifically intermittent power window operation and occasional battery drain issues, which can be resolved with component replacement or diagnosis. Some reports also indicate potential paint defects on exterior panels. Overall, the 2012 Civic is a solid choice for those seeking a practical and reliable vehicle, with most potential issues being relatively minor and manageable.
