# 2016 Honda Civic Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Honda Civic marks the debut of the highly acclaimed tenth-generation model, bringing bold styling, improved driving dynamics, and a modernized cabin to the compact car segment. Available in sedan, coupe, and later hatchback body styles, this generation introduced turbocharged power to the mainstream Civic lineup for the first time. For used-car buyers, the 2016 model remains a highly attractive target thanks to its excellent fuel economy, spacious interior, and strong resale value. However, being the first model year of a complete redesign, it does exhibit some notable first-year teething issues. While many owners enjoy trouble-free daily commuting, buyer reports highlight specific areas of concern—particularly in the steering, climate control, and braking systems—that require careful inspection before purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Naturally Aspirated 4-Cylinder

This base engine offers 158 horsepower and 138 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting. It is paired with either a 6-speed manual or a CVT and is generally regarded as a highly durable, low-maintenance choice for budget-conscious buyers.

**Piston Misset and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden loss of power or entering 'limp mode' while driving, followed by quick deceleration and a failure to restart the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Dealers may need to inspect the internal engine assembly for manufacturing defects related to the pistons; in severe cases, a complete engine replacement may be necessary.

### 1.5L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

This optional turbocharged engine produces 174 horsepower and 162 lb-ft of torque, delivering a more spirited driving experience. While offering great fuel economy and performance, it has faced scrutiny over oil dilution and head gasket issues in early model years.

**Coolant Leak and Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Disappearing engine coolant with no visible external leaks on the ground, engine overheating warnings on the dashboard, and internal coolant consumption.
- **Solution:** Mechanics typically diagnose this as a head gasket failure, requiring replacement of the head gasket and potentially resurfacing or replacing the cylinder head.

### General Fuel System Components

This category covers fuel delivery, delivery lines, and ignition electronics shared across different trims to ensure consistent engine combustion and operation.

**Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Engine stalling while driving, loss of power during long trips, or a complete failure of the engine to start.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the fuel pump assembly and cleaning of the fuel delivery lines.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The 6-speed manual transmission is available on lower trims, providing crisp shifts and highly reliable mechanical operation. It appeals to buyers looking for a more engaging driving experience and long-term durability without CVT-related concerns.

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The CVT is designed to optimize fuel efficiency and smooth power delivery across both engine options. While generally reliable with regular fluid changes, CVTs require strict adherence to maintenance schedules to avoid premature belt or pulley wear.

## Brakes

### Electronic Parking Brake & Brake Booster

**Electronic Parking Brake (EPB) and VSA Modulator Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Warning lights for 'Brake System' or 'EPB' illuminating on the dashboard, vehicle shuddering at speed, and the parking brake failing to engage or release.
- **Solution:** Replacing the VSA/ABS modulator or performing a software update to prevent module corruption.
- **Cost:** Software updates typically cost around $150 to $262, while VSA module replacement can cost up to $2,500.

**Brake Booster Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The brake pedal becomes extremely stiff ('brick-like') and difficult to depress, significantly reducing stopping power.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the defective brake booster assembly.

### Airbags and Vehicle Stability Assist (VSA)

**Airbag Non-Deployment or Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning module illuminates on the dashboard, or airbags fail to deploy during a collision.
- **Solution:** Diagnosing the airbag sensor or control module and replacing defective components.

**VSA Module Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Simultaneous illumination of multiple safety warning lights (Brake, Steering, Uphill Start, VSA) and numbness in steering feel.
- **Solution:** Replacing or overhauling the VSA modulator unit.

## Steering

### Electric Power Steering (EPS)

**Sticky Steering Rack and EPS Gear Swelling**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel feels 'sticky' or stiff, particularly during highway driving or in warm weather. Small corrective inputs require excessive force, leading to sudden, jerky movements or over-correction.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the steering rack and pinion assembly. This is a highly common issue where internal gears swell, and out-of-pocket costs are very high.
- **Cost:** Replacement quotes for the steering rack and pinion assembly generally range between $4,000 and $4,571.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Body Control Module (BCM)

**Infotainment Screen Glitches and Reboots**
- **Symptoms:** The display randomly resets, backs out of navigation apps, turns on the blind-spot camera without input, or freezes, causing driver distraction.
- **Solution:** Performing system software updates, hard resets, or replacing the infotainment unit.

**Body Control Module (BCM) Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical malfunctions, including keys failing to program or vehicle refusing to recognize keys after a battery replacement.
- **Solution:** Replacing the failing Body Control Module.
- **Cost:** Replacing the Body Control Module typically costs around $1,300.

**Trunk Water Leaks and Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Water accumulating in the trunk area, damp trunk lining, and premature battery degradation or failure to crank.
- **Solution:** Resealing the tail light gaskets to stop water intrusion and replacing the damaged battery.

## Exterior

### A/C Condenser, Evaporator Core & Windshield

**A/C Condenser and Evaporator Core Leakage**
- **Symptoms:** A/C system blowing warm or hot air. In early models, leaks in the evaporator core and compressor shaft seal cause complete system failure and eliminate the windshield defogger/defrost capability, leading to sudden fogging.
- **Solution:** Replacing the evaporator core, condenser, or compressor shaft seal. While Honda issued warranty extensions for the condenser and shaft seal, the evaporator core is often excluded.
- **Cost:** Evaporator replacement costs range from $2,000 to $3,000 out of pocket. Diagnoses and system recharges cost around $320 to $553.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Glass Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** A loud popping sound followed by the sunroof glass exploding outward without any external impact.
- **Solution:** Replacing the sunroof glass panel.
- **Cost:** Sunroof replacement costs approximately $786.

**Front Windshield Adhesive Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive wind noise while driving, and the windshield glass easily pushing outward due to factory glue failure.
- **Solution:** Re-installing and resealing the front windshield with new adhesive.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V526000: Exterior Lighting](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Civic&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Equipment." If a side marker light does not function, vehicle visibility can be decreased to other drivers, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will inspect both the taillight assemblies, replacing them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V725000: Parking Brake](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Civic&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The electric parking brake (EPB) may not engage if it is applied immediately after turning the ignition off. If the EPB is not applied, the vehicle may roll away, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will update the vehicle's software to allow the EPB to engage immediately after the ignition is turned off, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V074000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Civic&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If a circlip is missing or incorrectly installed, the piston wrist pin may not be secure and may drift and damage the engine cylinder causing the engine to seize, and increasing the risk of a crash or a fire.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and replace the piston assemblies and damaged engine components, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Civic&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Civic&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Honda Civic remains a standout compact car that offers an appealing blend of style, efficiency, and modern technology. For buyers looking for a dependable daily commuter, a well-maintained model can represent excellent value. To make the most of your purchase, look for examples where the steering rack and air conditioning components have already been serviced or replaced under owner goodwill or extended programs. When test driving a prospective vehicle, pay close attention to how the steering feels at highway speeds and ensure the air conditioning blows cold consistently on both sides of the cabin. A thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the suspension, electronics, and under-hood fluid levels will go a long way toward securing a highly reliable, rewarding vehicle.
