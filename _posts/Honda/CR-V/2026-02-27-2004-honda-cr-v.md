# 2004 Honda CR-V

### Overview
The 2004 Honda CR-V is generally considered a reliable compact SUV, known for its practicality and longevity. This generation (second generation) built upon the reputation of its predecessor, offering a comfortable ride and decent fuel economy for its class. While most owners report a positive experience, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen more attention from mechanics and owners regarding potential issues. This analysis focuses on documented common problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential repair costs and solutions for the 2004 CR-V.

---


## Engine

### 2.4L K24A1 (Inline-4)
**VTEC Solenoid Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Oil leaks around the VTEC solenoid, potential for engine oil level to drop.
- **Solution:** Replace the VTEC solenoid gasket or the entire solenoid unit.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

**Oil Dilution (Rare)**
- **Symptoms:** Engine oil may appear milky or smell of gasoline, especially in colder climates with frequent short trips.
- **Solution:** Regular oil changes with correct viscosity, ensuring the engine reaches operating temperature. In severe cases, engine internal cleaning or rebuild may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $50 - $200 (for oil change/flush), $1000+ (for engine work)


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic Transmission
**Harsh Shifting / Delayed Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission may hesitate to engage gears or shift roughly between gears.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid level and condition. Flush and refill transmission fluid. May require internal transmission repair or replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500 (fluid service), $1500 - $3000 (rebuild/replacement)

## Suspension

### Standard Independent Suspension
**Worn Control Arm Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, uneven tire wear, vague steering feel.
- **Solution:** Replace worn control arm bushings or the entire control arm assembly.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600 per axle

**Premature Strut/Shock Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Bouncy ride, reduced handling, visible leaks from struts/shocks.
- **Solution:** Replace worn struts or shocks.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800 per set


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Power Window Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows stop working or operate slowly.
- **Solution:** Replace the power window motor and regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400 per door

**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light illuminates, dimming headlights, difficulty starting the engine.
- **Solution:** Replace the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Clear Coat Peeling (Sun Exposure)**
- **Symptoms:** Clear coat begins to flake or peel, particularly on horizontal surfaces like the roof and hood, due to UV damage.

### HVAC System
**Blower Motor Resistor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** HVAC fan only works on the highest setting, or not at all.
- **Solution:** Replace the blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250

### Summary Verdict
The 2004 Honda CR-V is a dependable vehicle with a solid reputation for reliability. The most common issues tend to be related to wear and tear on suspension components and occasional electrical gremlins like power window motors and alternators. Engine and transmission issues are less common but can be costly if they arise, particularly with the automatic transmission. Proactive maintenance, especially fluid changes for the transmission, can help mitigate potential problems. Overall, it remains a good choice for those seeking a practical and durable compact SUV, provided it has been reasonably maintained.
