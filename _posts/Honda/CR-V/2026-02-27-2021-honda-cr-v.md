# 2021 Honda CR-V Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Honda CR-V remains a popular compact crossover choice, offering a roomy cabin, efficient powertrain choices, and a smooth ride. However, prospective buyers should review recurring owner complaints regarding driver-assist anomalies, steering feel, and select powertrain concerns. While many drivers enjoy its everyday utility, checking service records for past steering or electrical repairs can help ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged Inline-4

The standard 1.5-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces 190 horsepower and provides a good balance of pep and fuel efficiency for daily driving, though a small number of owners have reported coolant leaks and fuel injector issues.

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant levels, warning lights, or head gasket failure indications.
- **Solution:** Inspect cooling system components and head gasket integrity; replace failed seals or gaskets as necessary.

**Fuel Injector Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light illumination, rough idle, hesitation, or engine misfires.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fuel trim codes and replace faulty fuel injectors.

### 2.0L Hybrid Inline-4

The 2.0-liter Atkinson-cycle hybrid powertrain pairs with dual electric motors to deliver 212 total horsepower with excellent fuel economy, though occasional electrical and stalling complaints have been logged.

**Engine Stalling and Acceleration Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected loss of acceleration power, vehicle stalling, or electrical system warnings.
- **Solution:** Have dealership technicians inspect hybrid control modules, software calibrations, and electrical connections.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The continuously variable transmission transfers power smoothly to front-wheel or all-wheel-drive configurations, generally operating quietly without major mechanical failure patterns reported in standard use.

### E-CVT (Hybrid Transmission)

The electronic continuously variable transmission integrated into the hybrid model coordinates power delivery between the gasoline engine and electric motors seamlessly.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Column

Owners have reported issues with sticky steering wheels and mechanical wear in the steering rack, which can impact highway handling and driver comfort.

**Steering Sticky and Rack Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Steering wheel sticking or not returning to center smoothly during highway driving, or clunking noises when turning.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering rack assembly; replacement of the steering rack may be required to resolve sticking.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

The Honda Sensing suite provides active safety features like forward collision mitigation, though some drivers experience false-positive automatic braking events.

**Unexpected Braking**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle suddenly applies the brakes automatically without an obstacle present in front.
- **Solution:** Check for sensor misalignment, clear debris from camera/radar areas, and apply relevant software updates.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V900000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=CR-V&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the second-row center seat belt assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V064000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=CR-V&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, failing to suppress the air bag as intended. An air bag that deploys unintentionally during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=CR-V&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=CR-V&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Honda CR-V is a practical, spacious, and efficient choice for used compact SUV buyers, backed by strong overall design. However, buyers should take a thorough test drive to check for steering stickiness and ensure that advanced driver-assistance systems function without phantom braking. A pre-purchase inspection that checks the steering rack, coolant levels, and electrical systems is highly recommended.
