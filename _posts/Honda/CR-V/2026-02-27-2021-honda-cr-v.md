# 2021 Honda CR-V

### Overview
The 2021 Honda CR-V is a popular compact SUV known for its practicality, fuel efficiency, and comfortable ride. It generally exhibits strong reliability, reflecting Honda's reputation for building durable vehicles. This model year offers a choice between a naturally aspirated and a turbocharged engine, both paired with a Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT). Owners typically report satisfaction with the CR-V's performance and longevity, with common issues being relatively minor and infrequent. The vehicle's design prioritizes everyday usability, making it a solid choice for families and commuters seeking a dependable mode of transportation.

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Honda CR-V demonstrates excellent overall reliability, with very few reported significant mechanical or electrical faults. The most notable potential issue is oil dilution in the 1.5L turbocharged engine, which is often addressed by software updates and is not typically a major concern for most owners. Other components across the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior systems are generally robust and perform as expected. Routine maintenance is key, but major component failures are uncommon, making the 2021 CR-V a dependable choice in its segment.
