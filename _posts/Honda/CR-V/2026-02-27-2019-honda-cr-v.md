# 2019 Honda CR-V Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Honda CR-V remains a popular compact crossover choice, prized for its exceptionally spacious interior, comfortable ride, and solid fuel efficiency. However, used-car shoppers should be aware of several recurring owner complaints tracked by safety authorities. Prominent trouble spots include advanced safety system glitches resulting in unexpected braking, steering mechanism complaints regarding stiffness or binding, and powertrain-related fuel system concerns.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 1.5-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces 190 horsepower and provides lively performance paired with great fuel economy. While generally efficient, this powerplant is associated with owner reports concerning fuel pump failures, injector issues, and occasional stalling.

**Engine Stalling and Fuel System Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may experience sudden engine stutters, extended cranking times, or complete engine stalling while driving or stopped at lights, often accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the fuel pump, injectors, or related fuel system components, and replace failed parts or update software as needed.

### 2.4L Naturally Aspirated 4-Cylinder

The 2.4-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine delivers 184 horsepower, offering simple, traditional reliability for lower trim levels.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The continuously variable transmission routes power smoothly to front-wheel or all-wheel drive models while optimizing fuel economy. It displays relatively few widespread mechanical complaint trends compared to the engine and safety systems.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Column

Issues involving steering resistance, binding, or unexpected stiffness can affect driver confidence and control at various speeds.

**Steering Rack Binding and Sticky Steering**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a stiff, sticky, or ratcheting feeling when turning the steering wheel, requiring constant minor corrections at highway speeds and accompanied by occasional creaking noises.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering gear and replace the steering rack assembly if internal binding or component failure is confirmed.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Electrical architecture handles infotainment, convenience features, and vehicle sensors, though occasional power drain concerns have been reported by owners.

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle battery drains unexpectedly while parked, leaving the car unable to start after sitting for short periods.
- **Solution:** Inspect electrical parasitic draws, test the battery health, and apply any applicable software updates to modules that fail to 'sleep' properly.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Driver-assistance and collision avoidance technologies add modern security layers but can occasionally misbehave due to sensor errors or software calibration faults.

**Unexpected Automatic Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The collision mitigation system may erroneously detect phantom obstacles, causing the vehicle to suddenly and aggressively slam on the brakes while traveling at moderate or high speeds.
- **Solution:** Have the radar sensors and camera alignment checked by a qualified dealer or technician, and ensure all relevant collision system software updates are applied.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V865000: Structure:Frame And Members](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=CR-V&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A separated rear subframe reduces vehicle handling and can suddenly disable the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rear subframe bolts and retorque them or replace them, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V215000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=CR-V&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V569000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=CR-V&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The failed weld may allow fuel to leak, increasing the risk of a fire in the presence of an ignition source.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel tank, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V694000: Equipment:Other:Owners/Service/Other Manual](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=CR-V&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." If the front passenger does not correctly understand how the airbag functions, they may be at an increased risk of personal injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 19V383000: Air Bags:Frontal:Driver Clockspring/Spiral Cassette](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=CR-V&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The metal core of the steering wheel may have burrs which can damage the harnesses routed within, potentially disabling the driver's air bag or causing it to deploy without warning. In the event of a crash, a disabled air bag would increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will install a protective cover on the steering wheel core and replace clockspring and the harnesses within, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V158000: Seat Belts:Front:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=CR-V&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A manufacturing issue with the front seat belts may cause the seat belt buckle channel to interfere with the release button, preventing the seat belt buckle from latching. An unlatched seat belt cannot properly restrain the seat occupant during a crash, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver and front passenger seat belt buckle release buttons or the buckle assemblies as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=CR-V&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=CR-V&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Honda CR-V is a practical, comfortable, and efficient used compact SUV option, but it requires careful inspection before purchase. Prospective buyers should thoroughly test drive the vehicle to check for any steering binding, verify that driver-assistance sensors operate properly without phantom braking, and ensure the engine idles and accelerates smoothly without fuel system hesitations.
