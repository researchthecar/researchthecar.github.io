# 2018 Honda CR-V Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Honda CR-V remains a popular compact SUV known for its practical interior space, comfortable ride, and fuel efficiency. However, used car buyers should approach this model year with a degree of caution. Owner complaints logged with federal safety regulators point to a few recurring mechanical and electrical pain points—most notably oil dilution concerns on early turbo engines, steering gear stickiness, and unexpected activation of driver-assistance features.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 1.5-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 190 horsepower and 179 lb-ft of torque, offering a great balance of pep and fuel economy. While generally efficient, this powerplant is associated with owner reports regarding fuel mixing into engine oil and occasional stalling incidents.

**Fuel Dilution / Oil Dilution**
- **Symptoms:** Strong gasoline odors coming from the engine bay or cabin, and rising engine oil levels observed on the dipstick.
- **Solution:** Check service history for manufacturer software updates, oil changes, and fuel injector inspections.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Engine loses power, stalls unexpectedly while driving, or displays dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fuel system components, software calibrations, or potential fuel pump operation.

### 2.4L Naturally Aspirated 4-Cylinder

The 2.4-liter naturally aspirated inline-4 engine generates roughly 184 horsepower and 180 lb-ft of torque, found standard on lower trims. It generally avoids the complex turbo fuel-dilution quirks, though isolated reports indicate potential head gasket vulnerabilities.

**Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, overheating, white exhaust smoke, or early signs of internal engine leaks.
- **Solution:** Inspect coolant levels and engine block integrity; replace head gaskets if coolant contamination or failure is diagnosed.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The continuously variable transmission (CVT) pairs with both engine options to maximize fuel efficiency across front-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations. It delivers smooth acceleration under normal operation without widespread mechanical complaint patterns in this model year.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and EPS

Issues involving the electric power steering system can compromise driver feedback and handling predictability.

**Steering Rack Binding / Stickiness**
- **Symptoms:** A noticeable dead spot, sticking feeling, or increased steering effort required during slight lane corrections on the highway.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic power steering (EPS) rack and gearbox assembly; replacement of the steering rack is frequently required.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance System

The Honda Sensing suite provides advanced safety features, but sensitive collision avoidance sensors can trigger false positive braking events.

**Unexpected Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle automatically applies the brakes suddenly or activates collision warnings while driving normally without obstacles.
- **Solution:** Check for software updates, recalibrate sensor cameras and radar units, or inspect brake light switches.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V215000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=CR-V&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V663000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=CR-V&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The magnet that controls the torque sensor output signal for the electronic power steering system may not be properly secured, allowing the magnet to become dislodged. During a full lock turn, the dislodged magnet may cause steering assist to be applied in the opposite direction.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the steering gearbox assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V158000: Seat Belts:Front:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=CR-V&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A manufacturing issue with the front seat belts may cause the seat belt buckle channel to interfere with the release button, preventing the seat belt buckle from latching. An unlatched seat belt cannot properly restrain the seat occupant during a crash, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver and front passenger seat belt buckle release buttons or the buckle assemblies as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=CR-V&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=CR-V&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Honda CR-V remains a practical and functional choice for used car buyers who prioritize cabin room and everyday drivability. However, prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect vehicle history reports for unresolved turbo engine oil dilution symptoms, test drive the vehicle to check for steering rack stickiness, and ensure all safety system recalls or software updates have been completed by a certified dealer.
