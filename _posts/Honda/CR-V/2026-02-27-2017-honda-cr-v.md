# 2017 Honda CR-V

### Overview
The 2017 Honda CR-V, a popular compact SUV, generally boasts a strong reputation for reliability, building on Honda's legacy of dependable vehicles. This generation, the fifth, introduced a more refined interior, updated styling, and a more potent engine option. Owners often praise its practicality, fuel efficiency, and comfortable ride, making it a solid choice for families and daily commuting. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the CR-V typically performs well in long-term ownership, with most reported problems being minor or related to specific components rather than widespread systemic failures. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns.

### 1.5L L15B7 (Turbocharged)
**Oil Dilution**
- **Symptoms:** Fuel may mix with engine oil, leading to a higher oil level and potential lubrication issues over time. This is more commonly reported in colder climates or during short-trip driving.
- **Solution:** Software updates to the engine control module (ECM) have been released to mitigate this. Regular oil changes and ensuring the engine reaches operating temperature are also recommended.
- **Cost:** $0 - $150 (for software update/diagnosis)

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Honda CR-V is a highly reliable vehicle with a strong track record. The naturally aspirated 2.4L engine and the CVT transmission have proven to be robust with no significant widespread issues reported. The turbocharged 1.5L engine, while generally good, has seen some reports of oil dilution, particularly in specific driving conditions, though Honda has addressed this with software updates. Electrical components, especially the infotainment system, may occasionally exhibit minor glitches, but these are typically resolved with software updates. Overall, the 2017 CR-V offers excellent dependability for its class, making it a sound investment for buyers prioritizing reliability and low ownership costs.
