# 2017 Honda CR-V Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Honda CR-V remains a popular compact crossover choice, praised for its spacious interior, fuel economy, and practical everyday usability. However, used car buyers should approach this model year with care, as owner complaints point to several recurring mechanical and electronic hiccups. Knowing what to watch for can help you identify a well-maintained vehicle or spot potential trouble before signing on the dotted line.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 1.5-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 190 horsepower and 179 lb-ft of torque, offering a peppy drive and strong fuel efficiency. While generally capable, this engine option is tied to notable owner complaints regarding fuel system odors and injector wear.

**Fuel Leak or Odor**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report a strong smell of gasoline in and around the vehicle, sometimes accompanied by rough cold starts and illuminating dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Have the fuel system and injectors inspected by a qualified mechanic to identify and replace leaking components.

**Fuel Injector Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine lights, rough idling, engine misfires, and reduced motive power leading to limp mode.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty fuel injectors and clear diagnostic trouble codes.

### 2.4L Naturally Aspirated 4-Cylinder

The 2.4-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine generates around 184 horsepower and 180 lb-ft of torque, providing a simpler, traditional powertrain option for base trims. It is generally considered a lower-complexity alternative to the direct-injection turbo setup.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Paired with both engine choices, the continuously variable transmission delivers smooth power delivery and optimized fuel economy across various driving conditions. Owner feedback specifically targeting internal mechanical transmission failures is comparatively low.

## Steering

### Electronic Power Steering

Steering reliability is vital, and some owners have experienced premature wear or sticky feedback within the electronic power steering setup.

**Sticky or Stiff Steering**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel momentarily becomes difficult to turn or develops a sticky, binding sensation at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic power steering gearbox and steering rack assembly for internal mechanical wear.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Electrical system concerns generally center around intermittent battery drain issues and infotainment screen glitches.

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to start after sitting parked, frequently accompanied by random dashboard warning lights illuminating simultaneously.
- **Solution:** Test the battery and charging system, and inspect for parasitic electrical draws from infotainment or body control modules.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Safety technology suites can enhance everyday driving security, but sensor calibration issues can sometimes lead to unexpected system behaviors.

**Unexpected Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The collision mitigation braking system activates abruptly and without warning while driving at highway or street speeds, even when no obstacles are present.
- **Solution:** Have dealership technicians inspect sensor calibration and software updates for the Honda Sensing suite.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V663000: Steering:Electric Power Assist System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=CR-V&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The magnet that controls the torque sensor output signal for the electronic power steering system may not be properly secured, allowing the magnet to become dislodged. During a full lock turn, the dislodged magnet may cause steering assist to be applied in the opposite direction.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the steering gearbox assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V442000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=CR-V&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the pipe disconnects, the engine will stall, increasing the risk of a crash. Additionally, the disconnected pipe may leak fuel, which, in the presence of an ignition source, can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel supply pipe, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V256000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=CR-V&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The Certification Labels on the affected vehicles were printed with ink that may be inadvertently wiped away with an alcohol solvent. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of 49 CFR Part 567, "Certification." If the label information can be wiped away, the operator may not be able to refer to the information, possibly resulting in the.
- **What to do:** Honda and Acura will notify owners, and dealers will replace the Certification Label, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V158000: Seat Belts:Front:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=CR-V&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** A manufacturing issue with the front seat belts may cause the seat belt buckle channel to interfere with the release button, preventing the seat belt buckle from latching. An unlatched seat belt cannot properly restrain the seat occupant during a crash, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver and front passenger seat belt buckle release buttons or the buckle assemblies as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V524000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=CR-V&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." An incorrectly sized tire may cause premature tread wear, or a tire blow out, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will install a new corrected label, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=CR-V&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=CR-V&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used model, a pre-purchase inspection by a qualified technician is essential to verify the condition of the fuel injectors, battery system, and steering gear. Checking service history for any completed recall work or warranty extensions will also help ensure a more dependable ownership experience.
