# 2014 Honda Crosstour

### Overview
The 2014 Honda Crosstour is a crossover vehicle that blends the practicality of an SUV with the driving dynamics of a sedan. It was known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and available all-wheel drive. Reliability for the 2014 model year is generally considered good, with most owners reporting few significant mechanical problems. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen more attention from mechanics and owners. This report details common issues reported for the 2014 Honda Crosstour, focusing on specific components and their potential problems, solutions, and estimated repair costs.

---


## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder (K24Z7)
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil spots under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking seals or gaskets (e.g., valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket).
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

**Variable Timing Control (VTC) Actuator**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise on startup, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the VTC actuator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Worn Sway Bar Links**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking or rattling noise over bumps, especially at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn sway bar links.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


## Electrical

### Standard System
**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, dead battery, electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to find parasitic draw, repair or replace faulty component causing drain.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500 (depending on cause)

**Faulty Power Window Motors**
- **Symptoms:** Power windows not operating, slow operation.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the power window motor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400 per window

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Honda Crosstour generally exhibits good reliability, aligning with Honda's reputation. The 2.4L four-cylinder engine has a few reported issues like oil leaks and VTC actuator problems, which are typically manageable repairs. The 3.5L V6 engine and both transmission options (5-speed and 6-speed automatic) are quite robust with no widespread significant issues. Suspension components like sway bar links can wear over time, leading to noise. Electrical gremlins, such as battery drain and power window motor failures, are not uncommon but usually have identifiable causes. Exterior and interior components are generally durable, though headlight clouding and occasional infotainment connectivity glitches can occur. Overall, the Crosstour is a dependable vehicle with a few predictable wear items and minor electrical quirks.
