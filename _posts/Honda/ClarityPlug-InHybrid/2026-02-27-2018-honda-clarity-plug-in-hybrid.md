# 2018 Honda Clarity Plug-In Hybrid Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The Honda Clarity Plug-In Hybrid offers an efficient electrified driving experience, combining an electric motor with a gasoline engine for everyday commuting and longer trips. While many owners appreciate its comfort and efficiency, used-car buyers should review potential trouble spots reported to federal safety regulators, particularly involving fuel system components, advanced driver assistance electronics, and climate control longevity.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L 4-Cylinder Plug-In Hybrid

The Clarity relies on a 1.5-liter Atkinson-cycle four-cylinder engine paired with an electric motor and battery pack, producing a combined 212 horsepower. This powertrain prioritizes high efficiency and smooth transition between electric and hybrid operation, though buyers should watch for fuel system concerns.

**Fuel Pump Failure and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may experience sudden engine stalling, loss of motive power, or fuel pump failure while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the faulty fuel pump assembly as necessary.

## Transmission

### E-CVT Electric Continuously Variable Transmission

An electronic continuously variable transmission manages power delivery smoothly between the electric motor and gasoline engine. The transmission architecture is generally robust, with few recurring mechanical complaint clusters noted.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Exterior lighting systems provide good illumination but can experience intermittent failures with running lights or turn signals.

**Headlight and Daytime Running Light Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Daytime running lights or headlights stop working prematurely, or turn signals may flicker erratically.
- **Solution:** Inspect lighting assemblies and replace failed bulbs or control modules.

## Interior

### HVAC and Climate Control

Climate control features maintain cabin comfort and clear visibility, though refrigerant leaks from AC evaporators or condensers are known failure points.

**AC Condenser and Evaporator Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of air conditioning cooling capability and inability to properly defog or defrost the windshield.
- **Solution:** Inspect AC components for refrigerant leaks and replace faulty condensers or evaporators.
- **Cost:** $300

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Honda Sensing safety technologies assist with collision mitigation and lane keeping, but forward camera and sensor glitches have been noted by multiple owners.

**Unexpected Braking and Camera Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warnings indicate the collision mitigation system is unavailable, or the vehicle may experience unexpected braking events.
- **Solution:** Clean the windshield sensor area, check camera alignment, and have dealer diagnostic scans performed if electronic faults persist.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Clarity+Plug-In+Hybrid&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The Honda Clarity Plug-In Hybrid is an appealing choice for shoppers seeking high fuel economy and usable electric-only range. Prospective buyers should thoroughly test drive the vehicle to verify that the air conditioning, safety cameras, and powertrain operate without warning lights or hesitation. A pre-purchase inspection focusing on the fuel system and climate control components will help ensure a reliable ownership experience.
