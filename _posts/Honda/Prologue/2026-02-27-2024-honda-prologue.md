# 2024 Honda Prologue Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Honda Prologue enters the electric SUV market as a joint venture with General Motors, utilizing the Ultium platform. While buyers praise its comfortable ride, roomy interior, and modern tech, early models have accumulated notable owner complaints centered around drivetrain axle noises, advanced driver-assistance system glitches, and electrical display issues.

---

## Engine

### Single-Motor Electric (FWD)

Produces approximately 212 horsepower and 236 lb-ft of torque, driving the front wheels. This standard configuration offers efficient everyday commuting and adequate power for city and highway driving.

### Dual-Motor Electric (AWD)

Generates around 288 horsepower and 333 lb-ft of torque across all four wheels. It delivers brisk acceleration and improved all-weather traction, serving as the performance choice for the lineup.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Direct Drive Automatic

A single-speed reduction gear transmission standard on all front-wheel-drive and all-wheel-drive trims. It provides seamless, shift-free power delivery characteristic of modern electric vehicles.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Instrument Cluster

Screen blanking and software reboots are recurring electrical complaints impacting the primary vehicle displays.

**Random Screen Blanking and Reboots**
- **Symptoms:** Both the central infotainment screen and the driver gauge cluster go completely blank or reboot while driving, temporarily eliminating visibility of speed, navigation, and safety aids.
- **Solution:** Software updates issued by the manufacturer aim to resolve stability issues, though some owners experience recurring display resets.

## Safety

### CV Joints and Axles

Owner reports frequently highlight premature clicking or knocking from the constant velocity joints during low-speed maneuvers.

**Clicking and Grinding Noise During Turns**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report a distinct clicking, knocking, or ratcheting sound from the front axles and CV joints when turning the steering wheel sharply at low speeds or while reversing.
- **Solution:** Dealership inspection is required; some owners report being told no fix is immediately available or that the noise does not currently impact structural safety, while others await component redesigns.

### Collision Mitigation Braking

Advanced safety suite features can occasionally exhibit false positive collision warnings or abrupt braking interventions.

**Unexpected Phantom Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The forward collision avoidance system or emergency braking occasionally triggers abruptly without obstacles present, or regenerative braking faults out unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Have dealership technicians check for software updates related to the collision mitigation and driver-assistance modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V540000: Suspension:Front:Control Arm:Lower Arm](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Prologue&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A right front lower control arm fracture can cause loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the right front lower control arm as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V112000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Prologue&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** Additionally, a software error may cause the rearview camera to display a blank screen when the vehicle is in reverse. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) numbers 101, "Control and Displays," 111, "Rear Visibility," and 305, "Electric-Powered Vehicles." An instrument panel display.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the vehicle radio control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V306000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Prologue&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A rearview image that does not display correctly reduces the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the rearview camera, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Honda Prologue presents a compelling package for used electric SUV buyers seeking comfort and range, but prospective owners should thoroughly check for early-production quirks. Key areas to inspect during a test drive include listening for clicking noises from the front axles during tight turns and verifying that all dashboard and infotainment screens remain stable without random reboots.
