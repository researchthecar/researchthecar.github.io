The 2022 Honda BR-V is a subcompact crossover SUV primarily sold in Asian markets. It features a practical design with seating for up to seven passengers. The 2022 BR-V is powered by a 1.5-liter i-VTEC inline-four engine, producing around 119 horsepower and 107 lb-ft of torque. The engine is mated to either a 6-speed manual transmission or a CVT. Owners generally appreciate its fuel efficiency, spacious interior for its class, and reliability. Common dislikes include its somewhat underpowered engine, basic interior materials, and limited availability of advanced features compared to competitors.

**Engine**

**1.5L i-VTEC Inline-Four (L15ZF)**

*   **Excessive Engine Noise**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report excessive engine noise, especially during acceleration. The noise is often described as a rattling or knocking sound.
    *   Solution: Diagnosis by a mechanic is crucial to pinpoint the source of the noise. Potential causes include issues with the valve train, timing chain, or piston slap. Repair may involve replacing worn components or adjusting clearances.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+, depending on the cause and repair needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Idle issue**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine idles too high.
    *   Solution: Check and replace idle air control valve. Reset computer and idle.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)**

*   **CVT Hesitation/Jerking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report hesitation or jerking during acceleration, especially at lower speeds. This can be caused by the CVT not smoothly adjusting the gear ratio.
    *   Solution: A software update or a CVT fluid flush might resolve the issue. In more severe cases, the CVT may need to be rebuilt or replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (fluid flush) - $4000+ (CVT replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may freeze, crash, or become unresponsive. This can affect navigation, audio, and other connected features.
    *   Solution: Attempting a system reset may resolve the issue. If the problem persists, a software update from the dealer may be necessary. In some cases, the infotainment unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (reset) - $500+ (unit replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain, especially if the vehicle is left unused for a few days.
    *   Solution: Diagnose parasitic drain. Check aftermarket accessories that could cause a parasitic draw. Replace battery if necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Tail Light Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture or condensation builds up inside the tail light housings.
    *   Solution: Some owners drill small drain holes in the bottom of the tail light housing. The tail light may need to be replaced if moisture causes damage to the bulb sockets or wiring.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (drilling) - $200+ (tail light replacement).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling Noises from Dashboard**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling or squeaking noises emanating from the dashboard area, especially when driving on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Locating the source of the rattle is key. Tightening loose screws or using foam padding to insulate components may resolve the issue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (DIY) - $100+ (professional repair).
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2022 Honda BR-V, while generally considered a reliable vehicle, does have some reported issues. The most common complaints revolve around the engine noise and CVT transmission performance, which can be relatively expensive to repair if serious problems arise. Electrical issues like infotainment problems and battery drain are also worth noting. While less critical, exterior issues like tail light condensation and interior rattles can be annoying for owners. The severity and cost of these issues can vary greatly depending on the specific problem and the extent of the required repairs. It's always wise to have a qualified mechanic thoroughly inspect any used vehicle before purchase.
