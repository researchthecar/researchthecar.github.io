# 2024 Honda Pilot Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2024 Honda Pilot is a spacious and versatile three-row midsize SUV known for family-friendly comfort and capable handling. While it offers strong utility and a robust engine setup, used-car buyers should review emerging owner complaints regarding steering rack noises, electrical quirks related to the auto-idle stop system, and intermittent safety sensor alerts. Being aware of these areas can help you thoroughly inspect a prospective vehicle before purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

Equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 285 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, providing confident acceleration and towing capability for daily family driving. Overall powertrain performance is generally well-regarded, though occasional electronic integration glitches can arise.

**Auto-Idle Stop System Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The auto-idle stop indicator light flashes continuously, and the engine may fail to restart smoothly or unexpectedly shut off electrical accessories when coming to a stop.
- **Solution:** Have a certified mechanic or dealership inspect the idle stop module, battery health, and associated software calibrations.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

Features a 10-speed automatic transmission paired with front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive, designed to optimize highway efficiency and smooth power delivery.

**Shift Hesitation and Rough Lower Gears**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience high revs, hesitation, or a jerky shifting sensation when accelerating through lower gears.
- **Solution:** Check for available transmission control module (TCM) software updates at a dealership.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Pinion

The electric power steering system provides responsive handling, though recurring mechanical noise complaints involving the steering rack warrant careful pre-purchase evaluation.

**Steering Rack Clicking and Popping Noises**
- **Symptoms:** A noticeable popping, crackling, or clicking sound develops when turning the steering wheel after the vehicle has warmed up, especially during low-speed maneuvers and parking.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electric steering rack assembly; replacement of the steering rack may be required under warranty or through a qualified specialist.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Manages extensive cabin technology, driver aids, and vehicle electronics, though isolated battery and power-state glitches have been reported by owners.

**Intermittent Power Loss During Idle Stop**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may completely lose system power or stall out when coming to a complete stop while the auto-idle stop feature is engaged.
- **Solution:** Test battery voltage, charging system output, and check for software updates addressing electrical power management.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Includes comprehensive active and passive safety features, though minor sensor calibration issues can cause nuisance warnings.

**False Seat Belt Warning Chimes**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard displays warning alerts or constant chimes indicating that rear or middle-row passengers are unbuckled even when their seatbelts are securely fastened.
- **Solution:** Inspect seatbelt buckle sensor wiring and replace faulty buckle switches if false triggers persist.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V735000: Steering:Rack And Pinion:Rack](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A loss of steering control or steering lock-up can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the electric power steering rack, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V859000: Seats:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The driver's seat cushion frame may not have been tightened properly, which can result in an unsecured seat. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 207, "Seating Systems." An unsecured driver's seat may not adequately restrain the driver during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver's seat cushion frame, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V900000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly:Filler Pipe And Cap](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the fuel filler neck tube and pipe as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V031000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Fuel Injection System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** A software error in the fuel injection electronic control unit (FI-ECU) may cause an engine stall or a loss of power. An engine stall or loss of power can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the FI-ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V391000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Pedals And Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment" and 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." A brake pedal that shifts out of position can prevent the driver from applying the brakes as intended, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the brake pedal assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V054000: Seats:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2024)**
- **Issue:** The driver's seat cushion frame may not have been tightened properly, which can result in a loose seat. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 207, "Seating Systems." A loose driver's seat may not adequately restrain the driver during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver's seat cushion frame, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2024 Honda Pilot remains a compelling choice for families seeking a spacious, well-appointed SUV with strong V6 performance. While most owners enjoy trouble-free driving, prospective buyers should pay close attention during a test drive to check for steering rack noises, verify smooth transmission shifting in lower gears, and ensure all electrical features and safety sensors operate correctly.
