# 2021 Honda Pilot Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Honda Pilot is a capable and spacious three-row midsize crossover well-suited for family duty, offering solid utility and comfort. However, used car buyers should pay close attention to reported owner complaints regarding electronic issues, driver-assistance system glitches, and occasional engine stop-start behavior. While most mechanical systems prove durable, verifying that safety recalls and software updates have been completed is essential before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

Powered by a 3.5-liter V6 producing 280 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, this engine provides smooth and reliable power for daily driving and towing. Routine maintenance is straightforward, though some owners have experienced electronic integration quirks tied to the automatic idle stop system.

**Engine Stalling During Auto Stop-Start**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle stalls or fails to restart automatically when releasing the brake pedal after an idle stop, sometimes throwing dashboard warnings or shifting into neutral.
- **Solution:** Software updates or electrical diagnostics performed by a dealer can often address intermittent stop-start control module glitches.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 9-speed automatic transmission delivers smooth gear shifts and maximizes highway efficiency across front-wheel and all-wheel-drive configurations. It generally exhibits good mechanical durability with minimal widespread complaint trends.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System

The cabin technology suite provides ample connectivity options, though software glitches and intermittent display blackouts remain a common frustration among owners.

**Screen Freezing and Reboots**
- **Symptoms:** Center touchscreen goes completely blank, displays static or popping noises from speakers, or randomly reboots during operation.
- **Solution:** Install the latest factory software updates or inspect wiring harnesses under the dash for loose connections.

### Seats and Restraints

The cabin offers flexible seating arrangements and generous cargo space, though minor mechanical sticking in folding seat mechanisms can occasionally occur.

**Headrest and Sensor Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Third-row seat headrests fail to lock in the upright position or weight sensor circuits trigger warning alerts.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat mechanisms and ensure relevant seat sensor recalls have been serviced by an authorized dealer.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

The suite of safety features is comprehensive, but sensitive electronic sensors can occasionally cause false collision warnings or unexpected automatic braking inputs.

**Unexpected Braking and Sensor Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Collision mitigation braking system activates unexpectedly, or radar and camera sensors trigger warning lights and disable driver assistance features.
- **Solution:** Have a dealership inspect radar sensors, check for diagnostic trouble codes, and calibrate the camera and ADAS systems.

- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 20V439000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) numbers 101, "Control and Displays" and 111, "Rearview Mirrors." Operating a vehicle without a functioning instrument panel or rearview camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the instrument panel control module software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V725000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Over-cured tires may develop a break in the sidewall resulting in sudden air loss or a belt edge separation which could lead to a tread/belt loss. Either condition can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will inspect all four tires and replace the tires that have the affected DOT serial number and mold number, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V174000: Visibility:Rearview Mirrors/Devices:Exterior](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The heating pads behind both side-view mirrors may not be bonded properly, allowing the mirror glass to detach. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." Detached mirror glass can reduce driver visibility, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace both left and right side-view mirrors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V431000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Due to a faulty Media Oriented Systems Transport (MOST) communication coaxial cable connector, the rearview camera image may not appear on the display. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not display an image can reduce the driver's.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the MOST cable harness and install a straightening cover over the vehicle cable connector, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V458000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Master Cylinder](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Brake master cylinder separation can cause a loss of brake function and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the brake booster assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V064000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, failing to suppress the air bag as intended. An air bag that deploys unintentionally during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Honda Pilot remains a practical, comfortable choice for families needing dependable three-row transportation. While mechanical fundamentals are strong, prospective buyers should thoroughly test electronics, driver-assistance features, and the engine stop-start system during a pre-purchase test drive. Ensuring all open factory recalls have been completed will help secure a trouble-free ownership experience.
