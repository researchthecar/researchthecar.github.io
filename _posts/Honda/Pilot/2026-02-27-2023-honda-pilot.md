# 2023 Honda Pilot Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Honda Pilot brings a rugged redesign, spacious three-row cabin, and refined road manners to the midsize SUV segment. While most owners enjoy its utility and comfortable ride, early production models have attracted notable complaints regarding steering rack noises, unexpected electrical or safety system behaviors, and seatbelt warning sensor glitches. Used car buyers should inspect these specific areas thoroughly during a test drive to ensure everything operates smoothly.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

The Pilot features a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 285 horsepower, paired with smooth power delivery for daily driving and towing. Overall, the powertrain is generally robust, though minor startup noises have been reported by some owners.

**Engine Ticking / Noise**
- **Symptoms:** A loud ticking or knocking noise from the engine compartment upon startup.
- **Solution:** Have a certified mechanic or dealership inspect the valvetrain to verify normal operation.

## Transmission

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

A 10-speed automatic transmission handles gear selection across front-wheel and all-wheel drive configurations. While it generally delivers seamless gear changes, sporadic hesitation during low-speed maneuvers has been documented.

**Acceleration Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Random hesitation or delayed shifting response when attempting to accelerate or decelerate in normal driving mode.
- **Solution:** Check for available manufacturer software updates or powertrain control module recalibrations.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Column

The electronic power steering system provides light, maneuverable handling at low speeds. However, wear or internal faults within the steering rack have caused premature noises and steering stiffness for some drivers.

**Steering Rack Popping and Stiffness**
- **Symptoms:** Popping or knocking noises heard and felt in the steering wheel during low-speed turns, or occasional heavy steering resistance.
- **Solution:** Inspect the steering gear assembly; replacement of the steering rack may be required under warranty or service guidelines.

## Electrical

### Electrical Wiring and Tailgate Latch

Onboard electronics manage convenience features like power tailgates and auto idle-stop systems. Intermittent wiring harness faults or sensor glitches have caused occasional accessory malfunctions.

**Power Tailgate Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rear tailgate attempts to open slightly before beeping and closing automatically without fully lifting.
- **Solution:** Check tailgate latch alignment, switch contacts, and associated wiring harnesses for proper continuity.

## Safety

### Seat Belt Sensors

Safety equipment generally performs well, but sensor calibration issues in the rear seating rows can trigger annoying false warnings.

**False Unbuckled Alarms**
- **Symptoms:** Continuous audible alarms and warning lights indicating that rear passengers are unbuckled even when properly secured.
- **Solution:** Inspect second-row seatbelt buckle assemblies and occupant detection wiring harnesses for faulty sensors.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V735000: Steering:Rack And Pinion:Rack](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A loss of steering control or steering lock-up can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the electric power steering rack, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V859000: Seats:Critical Fasteners](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** The driver's seat cushion frame may not have been tightened properly, which can result in an unsecured seat. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 207, "Seating Systems." An unsecured driver's seat may not adequately restrain the driver during a crash, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver's seat cushion frame, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V900000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly:Filler Pipe And Cap](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the fuel filler neck tube and pipe as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V031000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Fuel Injection System](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A software error in the fuel injection electronic control unit (FI-ECU) may cause an engine stall or a loss of power. An engine stall or loss of power can increase the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the FI-ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V391000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Pedals And Linkages](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard numbers 108, "Lamps, Reflective Devices, and Associated Equipment" and 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." A brake pedal that shifts out of position can prevent the driver from applying the brakes as intended, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the brake pedal assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Honda Pilot is a capable and family-friendly three-row SUV, but used car buyers should approach early production models with a healthy level of diligence. Be sure to test the steering thoroughly for any popping noises or stiffness, verify that all seatbelt sensors and power accessories function correctly, and check for any history of transmission hesitation during a test drive.
