# 2022 Honda Pilot Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Honda Pilot is a capable and popular midsize three-row crossover SUV known for family-friendly packaging and comfortable road manners. While generally regarded as a solid daily driver, used car buyers should review owner-reported complaints regarding advanced safety driver aids, electrical glitches, and mechanical wear items. Understanding these common trouble areas will help prospective owners inspect prospective vehicles more thoroughly.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

Powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 280 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, this powertrain provides confident acceleration and towing capability. Overall reliability is strong, though isolated reports highlight unexpected stalling and power delivery interruptions.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported instances where the engine suddenly stalls or experiences a temporary loss of motive power while driving at highway speeds or in heavy traffic.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic scanning is required to check for fault codes related to fuel delivery, electrical sensors, or powertrain control modules.

## Transmission

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a 9-speed automatic transmission paired with front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive, offering smooth gear changes. While generally dependable, electronic shift controls can occasionally display operational quirks.

**Shifter or Park Defect**
- **Symptoms:** Some operators note issues with gear engagement, such as the vehicle failing to properly register or maintain drive or park modes, leading to unintended movement.
- **Solution:** Inspect electronic shifter modules, linkages, and update transmission control software if TSBs or manufacturer updates apply.

## Steering

### Steering Rack and Column

The steering system provides responsive handling, but higher-mileage models or vehicles exposed to rough road conditions can develop premature wear within the steering rack and linkage joints.

**Steering Rack Failure and Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Owners experience premature rattling, vibrations, clanking noises when turning or going over bumps, and occasional stiffness in the steering wheel.
- **Solution:** Have a certified mechanic inspect the steering rack, tie rods, and suspension links; worn components may require steering rack replacement.

## Electrical

### Starting and Charging System

Electrical architecture relies heavily on constant power management, making battery health critical for supporting accessory loads and the automatic idle-stop system.

**Auto Start-Stop Failure to Restart**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may fail to restart automatically after shutting off at traffic lights via the idle-stop feature, occasionally draining the battery.
- **Solution:** Test battery health, starter relay operation, and charging system components; replace weak batteries with heavy-duty AGM units specified for start-stop systems.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance and ADAS

Advanced safety features enhance driver awareness, but sensitive camera and radar hardware can trigger false alerts or unexpected corrective actions if disrupted by weather, windshield chips, or calibration errors.

**Unexpected Braking and Sensor Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note unexpected automatic braking events or multiple warning lights appearing on the dashboard due to windshield camera or front sensor alignment failures.
- **Solution:** Recalibrate the forward-facing camera system, check sensor cleanliness, and inspect windshield replacements for proper ADAS integration.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V431000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Due to a faulty Media Oriented Systems Transport (MOST) communication coaxial cable connector, the rearview camera image may not appear on the display. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not display an image can reduce the driver's.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the MOST cable harness and install a straightening cover over the vehicle cable connector, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V458000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Master Cylinder](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Brake master cylinder separation can cause a loss of brake function and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair the brake booster assembly as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V064000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, failing to suppress the air bag as intended. An air bag that deploys unintentionally during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V365000: Structure:Frame And Members](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Failure of rear suspension components, such as the rear control arm, can cause a loss of vehicle handling and control, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear subframe and install a rear subframe reinforcement kit and, as necessary, repair or replace the rear subframe components, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Honda Pilot remains a practical, versatile option for families seeking three-row utility. Prospective buyers should thoroughly test-drive the vehicle to check for steering noises, verify proper operation of driver-assistance sensors, and ensure the electrical and start-stop systems function without hesitation.
