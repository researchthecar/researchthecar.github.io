# 2019 Honda Pilot Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Honda Pilot is a spacious and capable three-row midsize SUV that offers strong family utility and solid performance. However, used car buyers should carefully review potential issues related to its powertrain, electrical systems, and driver-assistance features. While many owners enjoy its comfort and versatility, keeping an eye out for known mechanical and electrical quirks is recommended before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6

Powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 280 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, providing robust acceleration and towing capability. Overall performance is strong, though buyers should verify proper maintenance history regarding fuel and ignition components.

**Engine Stalling and Auto-Stop Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may stall unexpectedly while driving or fail to restart properly after utilizing the automatic stop-start feature at traffic lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic inspection of electrical connections, starter components, and software updates; replacement of faulty components as needed.

**Fuel Pump Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Engine sputtering, hesitation under acceleration, sudden loss of power, or difficulty starting the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Replace the defective fuel pump assembly.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic

Paired with base trims, this traditional automatic transmission delivers dependable daily operation, though some drivers experience hesitation or shudder over time.

**Transmission Shudder and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable hesitation, jerking, or shuddering during acceleration, accompanied by dashboard warning lights or sudden loss of drive power.
- **Solution:** Perform transmission fluid flushes, software updates, or complete mechanical overhaul/replacement if internal wear is severe.

### 9-Speed Automatic

Found on higher trims, this push-button electronic automatic transmission offers highway efficiency but can exhibit occasional electronic shifting faults.

**Unintended Neutral Shift and Slippage**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle unexpectedly drops into neutral while driving or stopped, accompanied by battery or transmission warning lights.
- **Solution:** Reprogram transmission control modules or replace faulty valve bodies and internal sensors.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Infotainment Network

The central electronics control infotainment, audio, and connected displays. Glitches are relatively common but often correctable with software patches.

**Screen Freezing and Rebooting**
- **Symptoms:** Infotainment display goes black, freezes, or reboots repeatedly while driving, sometimes impacting the backup camera display.
- **Solution:** Apply manufacturer software updates or inspect and replace faulty wiring harnesses and coaxial cables.

## Safety

### Honda Sensing Suite

Advanced driver assistance features enhance safety on the road, though sensitive radar and camera components can occasionally trigger false warnings or unexpected braking events.

**Unexpected Braking and Sensor Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Collision mitigation braking system activates falsely without obstacles, or dashboard displays multiple simultaneous safety system error warnings.
- **Solution:** Inspect front radar sensors and camera alignments, clean windshield areas, and perform system recalibrations at a dealership.

**[Recall 21V932000: Structure:Body:Hood](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The hood latch striker may become damaged and separate from the hood, which can result in the hood opening while driving. A hood that opens while driving can obstruct the driver's view and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will either repair the hood latch striker, or replace the hood if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V165000: Tires](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Overcured tires may develop a break in the sidewall, resulting in sudden air loss or belt edge separation, which could lead to tread/belt loss. Either condition can cause a loss of vehicle control, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will inspect all four tires and replace them as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V215000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V784000: Structure:Body](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The front frame left and right side upper members may not have been welded completely to the unibody. As such, these vehicles may fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) numbers 208, "Occupant Crash Protection" and 219, "Windshield Zone Intrusion." In the event of a crash, incomplete body welding may provide.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the vehicle for missing welds, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V298000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Gasoline:Belts And Associated Pulleys](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2019)**

- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the vehicle and replace any affected timing belt free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V848000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2019)**

- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will install a bracket on the right center pillar that will securely hold the center pillar trim clip, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Honda Pilot remains a practical, family-friendly choice with ample space and strong V6 performance. Prospective buyers should thoroughly test drive the vehicle to check for transmission smoothness, verify that all electronic displays and safety sensors operate correctly, and confirm that any previous fuel system or electrical recalls have been properly addressed.
