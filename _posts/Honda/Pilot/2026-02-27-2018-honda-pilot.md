# 2018 Honda Pilot Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Honda Pilot is a spacious and generally well-regarded three-row midsize SUV known for family friendliness and comfortable road manners. However, used-car buyers should review its mechanical history closely, as owner complaints highlight a few notable trouble spots regarding powertrain components, electrical behavior, and fuel systems. Being aware of these recurring patterns can help you spot potential issues during a test drive or pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

Equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 280 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, offering strong acceleration and everyday usability. While generally robust, a subset of owners have reported fuel pump and stalling concerns.

**Engine Stalling and Starting Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may suddenly stall, fail to restart after auto-idle stop, or display multiple electrical warning lights on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic testing by a qualified technician; may require software updates, electrical sensor replacements, or ignition component repairs.
- **Cost:** $1500

**Fuel Pump Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences fuel delivery failures, loss of motive power, or engine hesitation while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace faulty fuel pumps or related fuel system assemblies.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed / 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Depending on the trim level, the vehicle utilizes either a 6-speed or 9-speed automatic transmission paired with front-wheel or all-wheel drive. Transmission hesitation and torque converter concerns represent some of the more serious reported mechanical complaints.

**Torque Converter and Pressure Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission system error codes (such as P0741), sudden loss of hydraulic pressure, delayed engagement, or entry into limp mode.
- **Solution:** Have a transmission specialist inspect the torque converter and valve body; replacement or rebuild may be necessary for severe mechanical failures.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety equipment generally performs well in crash tests, though isolated owner complaints note unexpected component wear or failure.

**Seat Belt Damage**
- **Symptoms:** Front passenger seat belt unexpectedly snapping or separating into pieces near the lower anchor points.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt assemblies for structural compromise and replace damaged units immediately.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V932000: Structure:Body:Hood](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The hood latch striker may become damaged and separate from the hood, which can result in the hood opening while driving. A hood that opens while driving can obstruct the driver's view and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will either repair the hood latch striker, or replace the hood if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V298000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Gasoline:Belts And Associated Pulleys](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Teeth separation from the timing belt may result in an engine stall, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the vehicle and replace any affected timing belt free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V221000: Seats](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash, the rivets can break causing the seats not to be secured to the floor. As such, these vehicles may fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 207, "Seating Systems," and 210, "Seat Belt Assembly Anchorages." In the event of a crash, if the seat does not remain secured to the floor.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the driver seat frame assembly in Acura MDX vehicles, and replace the driver and front passenger seat slide rail frames in Honda Pilot and Ridgeline vehicles, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V751000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A damaged engine may run improperly or stall while driving, increasing the risk of a fire, crash, or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair, or replace the engine as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V365000: Structure:Frame And Members](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Failure of rear suspension components, such as the rear control arm, can cause a loss of vehicle handling and control, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear subframe and install a rear subframe reinforcement kit and, as necessary, repair or replace the rear subframe components, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Honda Pilot remains a practical choice for families seeking comfort and utility, but prospective buyers should proceed with due diligence. Ensure that a thorough pre-purchase inspection checks the condition of the transmission, fuel delivery components, and electrical systems. Verifying service records and confirming that relevant recall work has been performed will significantly reduce the risk of unexpected repair bills.
