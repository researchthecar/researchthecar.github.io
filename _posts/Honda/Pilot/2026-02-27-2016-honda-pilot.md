# 2016 Honda Pilot Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Honda Pilot is a popular three-row midsize crossover known for its spacious interior, comfortable ride, and family-friendly versatility. However, buyers should approach the 2016 model year with caution due to a higher-than-average volume of owner complaints regarding powertrain performance, electrical glitches, and engine reliability. Thoroughly inspecting service records and verifying whether powertrain or software updates have been performed can help mitigate potential ownership headaches.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Earth Dreams Engine

Equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 280 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, providing strong acceleration for a family SUV. While generally capable, this model year has seen notable complaints regarding engine knocking, stalling, and fuel system concerns.

**Engine Stalling and Rod Bearing Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Loud rattling or knocking noises from the engine compartment, illumination of dashboard warning lights, sudden loss of motive power, and engine stalling.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic inspection by a qualified mechanic; affected engines may require extensive repairs or component replacements depending on internal wear.

**Fuel Injector Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine lights, rough idling, engine misfires, and reduced performance or emissions-related trouble codes.
- **Solution:** Replace failing fuel injectors and update engine management software if necessary.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired with lower trims, this conventional 6-speed automatic offers straightforward shifting, though some owners have reported rough engagement or slipping.

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Found on upper trims (Touring and Elite), this 9-speed automatic transmission improves fuel economy but has received complaints regarding rough shifting and hesitation, often addressed through software updates.

**Hard Shifting and Transmission Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Jerking, lurching, hard shifting between first and second gears, or transmission slipping during acceleration, particularly when driving uphill.
- **Solution:** Perform a transmission fluid service and apply factory Electronic Control Unit (ECU) software updates designed to recalibrate shift points.

## Electrical

### Keyless Start and Electrical System

The electrical architecture supports modern cabin electronics and safety tech, but owners frequently report battery drains and keyless start recognition errors.

**Keyless Ignition and Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Failure of the vehicle to start, error messages regarding the keyless start system, and unexpected battery drain while the vehicle is parked.
- **Solution:** Test the battery and charging system, inspect keyless ignition modules, and replace faulty switches or drained batteries.

## Safety

### Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS)

Honda Sensing features provide valuable active safety protection on equipped trims, though sensor sensitivity and camera-related warnings are common points of owner frustration.

**Forward Collision and Lane Departure Warnings Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Warning lights illuminating for the collision mitigation system, lane keeping assist, and adaptive cruise control, often triggered by camera overheating or calibration issues.
- **Solution:** Inspect the windshield-mounted camera module, clear any obstructions, and recalibrate or replace faulty camera sensors.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V219000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel tank, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V417000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Storage:Tank Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source may result in a fire.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel tanks, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V424000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the third row seatbelt is trapped, the occupants may not be restrained properly, increasing the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will inspect and repair the rear third row seat belt, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V668000: Electrical System: Instrument Cluster/Panel](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If a safety system cannot immediately warn the driver when needed, the driver may be at increased risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will update the instrument cluster software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V932000: Structure:Body:Hood](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The hood latch striker may become damaged and separate from the hood, which can result in the hood opening while driving. A hood that opens while driving can obstruct the driver's view and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will either repair the hood latch striker, or replace the hood if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V751000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** A damaged engine may run improperly or stall while driving, increasing the risk of a fire, crash, or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and repair, or replace the engine as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V365000: Structure:Frame And Members](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Failure of rear suspension components, such as the rear control arm, can cause a loss of vehicle handling and control, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear subframe and install a rear subframe reinforcement kit and, as necessary, repair or replace the rear subframe components, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Honda Pilot offers impressive comfort, space, and utility, making it a tempting choice on the used market. However, prospective buyers should exercise caution due to reported issues with engine reliability, transmission refinement on the 9-speed automatic, and electrical glitches. Before finalizing a purchase, ensure a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection is performed and verify that all software updates and open recalls have been properly addressed.
