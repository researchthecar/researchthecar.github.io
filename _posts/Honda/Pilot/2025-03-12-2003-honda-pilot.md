The 2003 Honda Pilot marked the introduction of Honda's foray into the mid-size SUV market. It came equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing 240 horsepower and 242 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally praised the Pilot for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and Honda reliability. However, some common complaints revolved around transmission issues, particularly at higher mileage, as well as some electrical glitches and suspension wear.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6 (J35A4)**

*   **Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the valve cover gaskets onto the exhaust manifold, causing a burning oil smell. Visible oil accumulation around the valve covers may also be present.
    *   Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

* **Engine Mount Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive engine vibration felt inside the cabin, clunking noises during acceleration or deceleration. Visible cracking or separation of the rubber in the engine mounts.
    * Solution: Replace worn or damaged engine mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic (BYBA)**

*   **Transmission Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifting, slipping between gears, or complete failure to shift. Can occur around 100,000-150,000 miles.
    *   Solution: Replacement or rebuild of the transmission.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2500 - $4500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

* **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibration at highway speeds, especially under light acceleration. Stalling at low speeds.
    *   Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Shift Solenoid Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Erratic shifting, failure to shift into certain gears, or "check engine" light with transmission-related codes.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty shift solenoids.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Trailing Arm Bushing Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or squeaking noises from the rear suspension, especially when going over bumps. Uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replace the rear trailing arm bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Strut Mount Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noises from the front suspension. Poor handling and alignment issues.
    *   Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Driver's side Window Master Switch Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or complete failure of one or more windows to operate from the driver's side switch panel.
    *   Solution: Replace the driver's side master window switch.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Rear Hatch Latch Actuator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rear hatch failing to latch properly or not opening at all.
    *   Solution: Replace the rear hatch latch actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **ABS Module Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: ABS light illuminated, brake malfunction, and ABS system inoperative.
    *   Solution: Replace the ABS Module.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1200+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust (Rear Quarter Panels/Wheel Wells)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust forming on the lower rear quarter panels, particularly around the wheel wells.
    *   Solution: Rust repair and repainting, or replacement of the affected panels.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Climate Control (Blend Door Actuator Failure)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Inability to control the temperature of the air coming from the vents. Air only blowing hot or cold. Clicking noises from the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replace the blend door actuator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof motor failure. Leaks around the sunroof seal.
    *   Solution: Replace the sunroof motor or sunroof seal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2003 Honda Pilot, while generally reliable, is prone to some notable issues, particularly as it ages. Transmission problems can be severe and costly, while electrical issues and suspension wear are also common. Rust can be a significant concern depending on the vehicle's location. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues and carefully inspect any used 2003 Pilot before purchasing.
