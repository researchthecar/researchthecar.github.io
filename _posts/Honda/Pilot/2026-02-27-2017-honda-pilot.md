# 2017 Honda Pilot Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Honda Pilot is a spacious and versatile midsize three-row SUV that has long been a favorite for family hauling. While it offers a comfortable ride, strong safety features, and a practical interior layout, used-car buyers should pay close attention to powertrain and electrical concerns reported by owners. Addressing these common trouble spots early can save owners unexpected headaches down the road.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

The 2017 Honda Pilot comes equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing approximately 280 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque. It provides smooth acceleration and sufficient towing capability for daily family driving duties, though some owners have noted drivability glitches.

**Engine Stalling and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report unexpected engine stalling while operating at various speeds or while stopped at traffic lights, sometimes accompanied by warning lights.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic testing by a qualified technician to check fuel pressure, software calibrations, and electrical connections.

**Fuel Injector Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illumination of emission or check engine warning lights, rough idling, engine misfires, and decreased vehicle performance.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty fuel injectors and clearing diagnostic trouble codes.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 6-speed automatic transmission is paired with specific trims of the Pilot. While generally adequate for fuel economy, it has been associated with torque converter shudder and harsh engagement issues.

**Torque Converter Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle jerking, shuddering, harsh shifting in lower gears, and illumination of error codes such as P0741.
- **Solution:** Software updates or mechanical repair/replacement of the torque converter component.
- **Cost:** $700+

### 9-Speed Automatic Transmission

Higher trims feature a 9-speed automatic transmission offering smoother highway cruising potential, though owners occasionally cite electronic shift hesitation and erratic behavior.

**Shifting Irregularities and Unintended Neutral Shifts**
- **Symptoms:** Hesitation during gear changes, rough engagement, and unexpected drops into neutral while driving.
- **Solution:** Reprogramming transmission control modules or inspecting internal hydraulic components.

## Electrical

### Keyless Start and Instrument Cluster

The electrical architecture supports complex driver aids and infotainment features, but owners frequently report glitches involving warning messages and digital display failures.

**Keyless Start System Error**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warnings reading 'keyless start system problem' preventing the vehicle from starting properly.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of ignition switches, software modules, or starter relays, which can require costly out-of-pocket repairs.
- **Cost:** $1000

**Blank Speedometer Display**
- **Symptoms:** The digital speedometer or instrument cluster goes completely blank intermittently while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential replacement of the instrument cluster panel or associated wiring harnesses.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Glass

Exterior body components are generally durable, though isolated reports indicate vulnerability in factory glass panels such as panoramic sunroofs.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** Loud popping noise followed by the moonroof glass cracking or shattering completely without external impact.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the sunroof assembly and clearing glass debris from the cabin.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V932000: Structure:Body:Hood](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The hood latch striker may become damaged and separate from the hood, which can result in the hood opening while driving. A hood that opens while driving can obstruct the driver's view and increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will either repair the hood latch striker, or replace the hood if necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V365000: Structure:Frame And Members](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Pilot&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Failure of rear suspension components, such as the rear control arm, can cause a loss of vehicle handling and control, increasing the risk of a crash or injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect the rear subframe and install a rear subframe reinforcement kit and, as necessary, repair or replace the rear subframe components, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Honda Pilot is a capable and family-friendly crossover, but prospective buyers should inspect it thoroughly before purchase. Pay close attention to transmission shift smoothness, check for any history of torque converter or fuel injector repairs, and verify that all electrical features and dashboard displays operate reliably.
