The 2024 Honda HR-V is a subcompact SUV known for its fuel efficiency, spacious interior for its class, and comfortable ride. It comes standard with a 2.0-liter inline-4 engine producing 158 horsepower and 138 lb-ft of torque, paired with a Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT). Drivers appreciate the HR-V's nimble handling and Honda's reputation for reliability. Some criticisms include the engine being underpowered for some drivers, and the CVT can sometimes drone under heavy acceleration.

**Engine**

**2.0L Inline-4**

* **Engine Jerking/Stalling**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported instances of the engine jerking or stalling, particularly at lower speeds or during acceleration. This can be intermittent and difficult to diagnose.
    * Solution: There is not a definitive solution at this time. Some owners have had success with software updates to the engine control unit (ECU). However, if the issue is a mechanical failure, the part may need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT**

*   **CVT Hesitation/Lag**
    *   Description/Symptoms: A common complaint involves a noticeable hesitation or lag when accelerating, especially from a standstill. This can create a feeling of sluggishness.
    *   Solution: Some dealers perform a CVT fluid drain and refill. In more severe cases, CVT replacement may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $6,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Rebooting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system, including the touchscreen display, may freeze, become unresponsive, or spontaneously reboot. This can affect navigation, audio, and other vehicle functions.
    *   Solution: Check to ensure your software is up to date. If the issue persists, replace the infotainment system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Backup Camera Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The backup camera may display a distorted image, flicker, or fail to activate entirely when the vehicle is in reverse.
    * Solution: Replacement of the camera and recalibration of the system.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Rattling Noises from Dashboard/Panels**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported rattling noises emanating from the dashboard or interior panels, particularly when driving on uneven surfaces. The exact source can be difficult to pinpoint.
    *   Solution: The specific solution depends on the source of the rattle. It may involve tightening loose fasteners, adding felt or foam padding to reduce vibration, or replacing damaged trim pieces.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2024 Honda HR-V generally appears to be a reliable vehicle, but some owners have reported issues with the CVT transmission and infotainment system. Engine stalling and interior rattles also appear to be more common complaints. The severity and cost of repairs vary, with some issues requiring only software updates or minor adjustments, while others may necessitate component replacements. Overall, these issues do not seem widespread, but potential buyers should be aware of them and perform a thorough inspection before purchasing.
