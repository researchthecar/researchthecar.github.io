# 2016 Honda HR-V Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Honda HR-V offers buyers a practical and spacious subcompact crossover option with flexible cargo configurations. However, used-car shoppers should carefully review its reliability history, as owners have reported notable mechanical and electrical concerns. Potential buyers should prioritize a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the transmission and electrical systems before finalizing a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 1.8L I4 Engine

The 2016 Honda HR-V is powered by a 1.8-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine producing 141 horsepower and 127 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate power for daily commuting with competitive fuel efficiency, though standard maintenance and electrical upkeep are crucial.

**Electrical Warning Lights and Starting Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience unexpected dashboard warning lights—such as oil pressure or parking brake alerts—accompanied by difficulty starting the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Diagnose electrical codes, test the battery sensor, and replace faulty components or push-start switches as needed.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Equipped with a Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) available in front-wheel drive or Real Time AWD configurations, this transmission requires attentive monitoring. Buyers should verify whether previous service bulletin repairs or warranty extensions for belt wear have been completed.

**CVT Belt Deterioration and Limp Mode**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated dashboard warning lights, sudden loss of acceleration or inability to exceed lower speeds, and diagnostic trouble codes indicating transmission failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission operation; repairs may involve replacing the automatic transmission assembly or servicing internal components under extended manufacturer warranty coverage where applicable.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Service Brakes

Brake system complaints highlight potential ABS module malfunctions and unexpected warning lights that warrant professional verification during an inspection.

**ABS Module and Brake Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Multiple warning lights illuminate on the instrument panel (including ABS, traction control, and brake system lights), occasionally preceding diminished braking response or failure.
- **Solution:** Inspect the anti-lock braking system module, wheel speed sensors, and hydraulic lines; replace faulty modules or switches.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Lighting Systems

Electrical accessories and exterior lighting systems can experience intermittent glitches, ranging from failing battery sensors and keyless control modules to moisture accumulation in rear lamp housings.

**Tail Light Water Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Water accumulates inside rear tail light assemblies, leading to moisture buildup, mold, and inoperable exterior lighting.
- **Solution:** Inspect tail light seals and housing gaskets; replace damaged light assemblies or reseal housings to prevent water entry.
- **Cost:** $617 - $1,100

## Exterior

### Body Structure and Paint

Exterior body complaints predominantly center around cosmetic paint degradation on specific factory color options and occasional structural window anomalies.

**Paint Peeling and Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Paint begins to bubble, flake, and peel away from body panels, most notably on vehicles finished in Orchid White Pearl.
- **Solution:** Consult a professional body shop for repainting or panel refinishing where paint defects are present.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V777000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Foundation Components:Disc:Caliper](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=HR-V&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 135, "Light Vehicle Brake Systems." A reduction of braking performance increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Owners will be notified, and Honda or Acura dealers will bleed the brakes, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 15V472000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=HR-V&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles may be missing the required tire placard indicating "Tire and Loading Information." These vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) 110, "Tire Selection and Rims and Motor Home/Recreation Vehicle Trailer Load Carrying Capacity Information for Motor Vehicles with a GVWR of 4,536.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the vehicle, and apply a new label, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Honda HR-V remains an appealing choice for used car buyers seeking versatility and efficiency, but it demands careful evaluation. Prospective owners should verify the service history of the CVT transmission and check for any unresolved electrical or paint concerns before purchasing.
