# 2016 Honda CR-Z Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Honda CR-Z is a distinctive hybrid coupe that blends sporty styling with efficiency. When shopping for a used model, understanding the available powertrain options and typical owner-reported quirks can help guide your purchase decision.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L i-VTEC 4-Cylinder Hybrid

This 1.5-liter four-cylinder engine is paired with Honda's Integrated Motor Assist (IMA) hybrid system, producing around 130 total horsepower. It offers a frugal fuel economy profile and generally dependable mechanical performance for daily commuting.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

The standard 6-speed manual transmission pairs well with the CR-Z's sporty handling dynamics and offers an engaging driving experience. Routine fluid changes are key to maintaining long-term shift quality.

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The optional Continuously Variable Transmission features steering-wheel-mounted paddle shifters to simulate stepped gear ratios. It prioritizes fuel efficiency and smooth power delivery during city driving.

## Exterior

### Door Handles

Owner reports indicate that exterior door handles may become sticky and prone to failure under warm weather conditions.

**Outside Door Handle Binding and Breakage**
- **Symptoms:** In warm or hot weather, the door opening mechanism can bind up, placing excess stress on the handle until it eventually snaps.
- **Solution:** Replace the broken exterior door handle assembly with an updated part.
