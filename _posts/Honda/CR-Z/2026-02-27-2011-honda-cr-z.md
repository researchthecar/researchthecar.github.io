# 2011 Honda CR-Z

### Overview
The 2011 Honda CR-Z is a unique compact hybrid sports car known for its distinctive styling and fuel efficiency. As a relatively niche vehicle, its long-term reliability data is less extensive than more mainstream models. However, based on available owner reports and technical analyses, the CR-Z generally exhibits good reliability, particularly in its powertrain. Common concerns tend to be minor and related to specific components rather than widespread systemic failures. Owners often praise its engaging driving dynamics for a hybrid, but like any vehicle, it's important to be aware of potential issues that may arise over time.

---


## Engine

### 1.5L I-VTEC Hybrid
**Hybrid Battery Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced electric-only range, decreased overall fuel economy, potential for check engine light.
- **Solution:** Hybrid battery replacement.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $4,000

**Accessory Belt Tensioner**
- **Symptoms:** Squealing noise from the engine bay, especially on startup or acceleration.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the belt tensioner and potentially the accessory belt.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2011 Honda CR-Z generally presents a good reliability record, especially considering its hybrid powertrain. The primary concerns revolve around the longevity of the hybrid battery and potential issues with the inverter cooling fan, which are common to many hybrid vehicles. The CVT transmission can occasionally develop leaks, while the manual transmission is typically robust. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment system glitches and premature 12V battery failure, are also noted but not overwhelmingly prevalent. Overall, the CR-Z is a reliable choice for those seeking a sporty and efficient compact car, provided regular maintenance is performed and potential hybrid-specific components are monitored.
