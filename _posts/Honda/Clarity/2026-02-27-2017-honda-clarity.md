# 2017 Honda Clarity

### Overview
The 2017 Honda Clarity, primarily known for its Plug-in Hybrid (PHEV) and Electric Vehicle (EV) variants, aimed to offer an eco-friendly and technologically advanced driving experience. While generally well-received for its innovative powertrain and comfortable interior, like any vehicle, it has specific areas where owners have reported concerns. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability issues beyond standard maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for prospective buyers and current owners. The data presented is based on aggregated owner reports and technical service bulletins, highlighting common issues that may require attention.

---


## Engine

### 1.5L Atkinson-Cycle 4-Cylinder (PHEV)
**Hybrid System Malfunction Indicator**
- **Symptoms:** Check engine light illuminated, reduced electric-only range, or issues with the gasoline engine engaging.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of hybrid system components, including battery, inverter, or engine control module. May require software updates or replacement of faulty parts.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500+


### Electric Motor (EV & PHEV)
**Reduced Power Output**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle acceleration is sluggish, or the electric motor does not provide expected power.
- **Solution:** Inspection of motor controller, battery pack, and associated wiring. Potential need for motor or controller replacement.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $5000+

## Electrical

### High-Voltage Battery Pack
**Degradation or Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Significant decrease in electric range, inability to hold a charge, or complete battery failure.
- **Solution:** Battery diagnostics and potential replacement. Honda typically offers a long warranty on these components.
- **Cost:** $3000 - $10000+


### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing or Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, freezes, or requires a system reset.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the infotainment system, or in some cases, replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1000

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Honda Clarity, particularly its hybrid and electric variants, demonstrates a generally good reliability record, with most reported issues concentrated in the advanced hybrid and electrical systems. The high-voltage battery pack and the infotainment system are the most common areas for concern, though often covered by warranty. The gasoline engine and e-CVT transmission have shown robust performance. Owners should be aware of potential hybrid system malfunctions and ensure regular software updates for the infotainment. Beyond these specific points, the Clarity is considered a reliable vehicle for its segment, especially for those prioritizing fuel efficiency and advanced technology.
