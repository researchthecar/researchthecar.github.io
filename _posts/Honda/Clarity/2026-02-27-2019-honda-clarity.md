# 2019 Honda Clarity

### Overview
The 2019 Honda Clarity is a unique offering in the automotive market, primarily known for its electrified powertrains. It was available in Plug-in Hybrid (PHEV) and Electric (EV) variants, with a Fuel Cell (FCV) version also in production but less common. This analysis focuses on the reliability of the components typically associated with these advanced powertrains and the vehicle's general systems. While Honda generally has a strong reputation for reliability, the advanced nature of the Clarity's systems means specific considerations are important for owners. This data aims to provide a technical overview of potential reliability concerns based on available information.

**Degradation over time**

- **Cost:** $3,000 - $10,000+


### 12V Auxiliary Battery
**Premature failure**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical system malfunctions, difficulty starting, infotainment system issues.

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Honda Clarity, particularly its PHEV and EV variants, generally exhibits good reliability for its core mechanical and electric powertrain components, aligning with Honda's reputation. However, the advanced nature of its systems introduces specific potential concerns. The high-voltage battery pack and, for FCV models, the fuel cell stack are significant components with the potential for degradation over time, leading to costly replacements. Electrical systems, including the 12V auxiliary battery and charging system, can experience issues. Infotainment software glitches are also noted. Owners should be aware of these specific areas, especially as the vehicle ages, and factor in potential maintenance costs related to these advanced technologies.
