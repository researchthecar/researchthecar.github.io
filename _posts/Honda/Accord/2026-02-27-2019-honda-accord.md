# 2019 Honda Accord Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Honda Accord is widely celebrated for its spacious cabin, refined ride quality, and engaging handling. However, prospective buyers should review some recurring mechanical and electrical concerns that have been reported by owners, particularly related to the powertrain and driver-assistance features.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 1.5-liter turbocharged inline-4 produces 192 horsepower and 192 lb-ft of torque, offering an excellent blend of efficiency and everyday pep. It is paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT) on most trims. While popular for its fuel economy, used buyers should carefully inspect this engine for potential coolant and head gasket concerns.

**Head Gasket Failure and Coolant Intrusion**
- **Symptoms:** Engine misfires, coolant loss, overheating, and white smoke from the exhaust.
- **Solution:** Replace the head gasket and inspect the cylinder head; in severe cases, engine replacement may be necessary.

**Fuel Pump Failure and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Engine stalls unexpectedly while driving, rough idling, or failure to start.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty fuel pump module under manufacturer guidance.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The larger 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 generates 252 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, delivering swift acceleration and a more athletic driving dynamic. It is typically mated to a responsive 10-speed automatic transmission or a 6-speed manual on Sport trims. This powertrain generally enjoys a stronger reputation for mechanical durability compared to its smaller displacement sibling.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The CVT is standard on 1.5L models, providing seamless power delivery and optimized fuel efficiency. Regular fluid changes are vital to ensure long-term reliability.

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

Found on 2.0L models, this traditional geared automatic transmission offers quick, smooth shifts and complements the spirited performance of the larger engine.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides confident stopping power, though electrical glitches related to the parking brake module have been noted by some owners.

**Electronic Parking Brake and ABS Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Intermittent warning lights for the parking brake, ABS, and stability control systems appear on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Diagnose wheel speed sensors, wiring harnesses, or the parking brake actuator for faults.

## Exterior

### Structure and Exterior Paint

Body panels are generally well-constructed, but owners have reported cosmetic paint degradation and peeling on specific exterior surfaces.

**Paint Peeling and Corrosion**
- **Symptoms:** Clear coat failure, paint flaking off in chunks, and localized corrosion on the roof, hood, or door jambs.
- **Solution:** Repaint affected body panels and apply protective clear coat layers.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety features enhance protection, though sensors can occasionally trigger false alerts or unwarranted braking events.

**Unexpected Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle suddenly brakes hard on clear roads without immediate obstacles, accompanied by dashboard warnings.
- **Solution:** Have the Honda Sensing radar and camera sensors recalibrated or inspected by a qualified technician.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V314000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Accord&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel pump fails, the engine can stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V771000: Electrical System:Body Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Accord&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause intermittent or continuous disruptions in communication between the Body Control Module (BCM) and other components. Various system malfunctions such as inoperative windshield wipers, defroster, rearview camera, or exterior lighting can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will update the BCM software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V215000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Accord&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V158000: Seat Belts:Front:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Accord&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A manufacturing issue with the front seat belts may cause the seat belt buckle channel to interfere with the release button, preventing the seat belt buckle from latching. An unlatched seat belt cannot properly restrain the seat occupant during a crash, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver and front passenger seat belt buckle release buttons or the buckle assemblies as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Accord&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Accord&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Honda Accord remains a top contender in the midsize sedan market due to its driving dynamics and feature-packed interior. When shopping for a used model, prioritize service history records, especially regarding the 1.5L engine's cooling system and fuel pump updates. Opting for a pre-purchase inspection can help catch potential mechanical or electrical issues before finalizing your purchase.
