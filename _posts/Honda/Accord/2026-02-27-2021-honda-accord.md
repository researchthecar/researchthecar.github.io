# 2021 Honda Accord

### Overview
The 2021 Honda Accord is a well-regarded mid-size sedan known for its blend of performance, comfort, and fuel efficiency. Across its various trims and powertrain options, it generally exhibits strong reliability, a hallmark of the Honda brand. Owners typically report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected mechanical issues. The Accord's engineering focuses on durability and a refined driving experience, making it a competitive choice in its segment. While no vehicle is entirely immune to problems, the 2021 Accord demonstrates a robust build quality and well-integrated systems, contributing to its favorable reputation for long-term dependability.

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged Inline-4
**Oil Dilution**
- **Symptoms:** Fuel smell in engine oil, increased oil level, potential for reduced lubrication.
- **Solution:** Software updates to engine control module (ECM) to optimize fuel mixture and combustion. In some cases, oil changes may be recommended more frequently.
- **Cost:** $0 - $150 (for software update/diagnosis)

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Honda Accord demonstrates strong overall reliability, with most major components performing as expected. The primary documented concern is oil dilution in the 1.5L turbocharged engine, which Honda has addressed with software updates. Other reported issues, such as minor infotainment glitches, are generally resolved with software updates or simple resets and do not indicate systemic failures. Both the CVT and 10-speed automatic transmissions have proven durable. The suspension, electrical systems (excluding minor infotainment quirks), exterior, and interior components are largely free from widespread complaints. For prospective buyers, the 2021 Accord represents a dependable choice with a low probability of significant mechanical issues beyond typical maintenance.
