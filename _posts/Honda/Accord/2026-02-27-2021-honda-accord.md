# 2021 Honda Accord Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Honda Accord continues to be a top choice in the midsize sedan segment, offering agile handling, a spacious cabin, and efficient powertrain choices. However, used car buyers should approach with awareness of certain recurring owner complaints, particularly concerning engine head gasket durability on some units, unexpected automated braking incidents, and persistent infotainment glitches.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged Inline-4

A 1.5-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine producing approximately 192 horsepower, paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). It offers a strong balance of everyday pep and fuel efficiency, though some owners have reported major cooling and head gasket issues.

**Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report coolant loss, white smoke from the exhaust, and eventual engine power loss due to head gasket failure.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis and replacement of the head gasket, which can be an intensive engine repair.

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

A punchy 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine delivering 252 horsepower, mated to a responsive 10-speed automatic transmission. It provides brisk acceleration and spirited performance for driving enthusiasts.

### 2.0L Hybrid Powertrain

A hybrid system combining a 2.0-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine with electric motors for a total system output of 212 horsepower, paired with an electronic continuously variable transmission. It delivers exceptional fuel economy for daily commuting.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

A smooth-shifting continuously variable transmission standard on the 1.5L engine, optimized for seamless power delivery and high fuel efficiency during standard highway and city driving.

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission

A traditional 10-speed automatic transmission paired with the 2.0L turbocharged engine, providing refined gear selection and quick responses compared to typical CVTs.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System

Controls audio, navigation, and phone integration, prone to software glitches and intermittent display unresponsiveness.

**Infotainment Reboot and Ghost Touches**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen display intermittently registers unguided touch inputs, beeps randomly, freezes, or reboots while driving.
- **Solution:** Perform software updates or replace the head unit if hardware failure is diagnosed.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Driver assistance features designed to prevent collisions, though select sensors can trigger false alarms or unexpected vehicle braking.

**Unexpected Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle's collision mitigation system may suddenly engage the brakes without an imminent obstacle, causing abrupt slowing while driving.
- **Solution:** Have the driver-assist sensors and software calibrated or inspected by a qualified service center.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V900000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Accord&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the second-row center seat belt assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V064000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Accord&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, failing to suppress the air bag as intended. An air bag that deploys unintentionally during a crash can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Accord&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Accord&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Honda Accord is a well-rounded and generally reliable midsize sedan with strong efficiency and safety credentials. However, used car shoppers should carefully inspect the vehicle history and watch out for potential engine head gasket concerns on the 1.5L turbo, as well as operational quirks like ghost touches on the infotainment screen and occasional unexpected automatic braking.
