# 2018 Honda Accord Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Honda Accord marks the introduction of the highly praised tenth-generation design, offering a stylish profile, advanced safety driver-assist features, and punchy engine options. It is widely considered one of the most well-rounded midsize sedans on the market, blending excellent fuel economy with a spacious and modern cabin. However, used-car buyers should be aware that owners have reported several recurring issues, particularly regarding the turbocharged engines, steering gearboxes, and driver-assist sensors. While these owner complaints represent real experiences, they do not mean every single vehicle will face these problems.

---

## Engine

### 1.5-Liter Turbocharged Inline-4 (L15BE)

This engine produces 192 horsepower and 192 lb-ft of torque, offering a great balance of fuel economy and daily drivability. However, it is highly prone to premature head gasket and fuel injector failures, requiring meticulous pre-purchase inspection.

**Head Gasket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Symptoms include engine stuttering under acceleration, low coolant levels, engine misfires (often felt on cold starts), dash warnings, and the vehicle unexpectedly entering 'limp mode' while driving.
- **Solution:** Replacing the head gasket, along with resurfacing or replacing the cylinder head if it has become warped. In some severe cases, a complete replacement of the engine assembly is recommended.
- **Cost:** $6,000 to $10,000

**Fuel Injector and Fuel Pump Failures**
- **Symptoms:** A dashboard display showing an 'emissions problem' warning light, engine sputtering, hard starting, stalling, and a loss of propulsion.
- **Solution:** Replacing the faulty high-pressure fuel injectors and the fuel pump unit.
- **Cost:** Up to $1,600 for fuel injector replacement

### 2.0-Liter Turbocharged Inline-4 (K20C4)

Generating 252 horsepower and 273 lb-ft of torque, this engine offers robust performance similar to the Civic Type R's powerplant. While generally regarded as more robust than the smaller 1.5-liter engine, some owners have reported similar head gasket failures at higher mileages.

**Premature Head Gasket Blow**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, cold-start engine misfires, low coolant level without any external leaks, and check engine light illumination.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the cylinder head gasket and pressure testing of the cooling system.
- **Cost:** Approximately $6,000

### 2.0-Liter Naturally Aspirated Inline-4 Hybrid

This hybrid powertrain combines a 2.0-liter Atkinson-cycle engine with electric motors for a total output of 212 horsepower. It has established a reputation for solid efficiency and relatively fewer mechanical complaints compared to the turbocharged options.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Paired with the 1.5-liter engine and hybrid models, this continuously variable transmission provides smooth, step-less gear ratio changes optimized for fuel economy. It has shown average durability with proper, timely fluid service.

### 10-Speed Automatic Transmission (10AT)

Exclusively paired with the 2.0-liter turbocharged engine, this planetary automatic offers rapid, positive gear changes and enhances high-speed cruising efficiency. It is generally robust but benefits from routine maintenance.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission (6MT)

Available on Sport trims with both the 1.5-liter and 2.0-liter engines, this manual transmission offers precise shifting and a highly engaging driving experience. It remains a reliable and sought-after option for enthusiasts.

## Brakes

### General / multiple configurations

Issues that occur across multiple configurations, often relating to electrical communications or sensor networks that feed back into engine-management displays.

**Dashboard Warning Light Cascade**
- **Symptoms:** Multiple warning lights (Tire Pressure, ABS, Brake System, Lane Keeping Assist, Traction Control, and Power Steering) illuminating simultaneously while driving, often accompanied by the vehicle losing some power or disabling driver-assist features.
- **Solution:** Diagnosing underlying diagnostic trouble codes, which can range from sensor circuit faults (such as a wheel speed sensor) to electronic control unit communication glitches.

### Brake Hold System

The braking system features electronic enhancements including an Electric Parking Brake and a Brake Hold convenience feature, which are controlled by electronic modules and sensors.

**Brake Hold Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The 'Brake Hold' system fails to keep the vehicle stopped at traffic lights, allowing the vehicle to creep or lurch forward after releasing the brake pedal.
- **Solution:** Software/firmware updates or physical sensor adjustments within the electronic braking system.

**Unintended Parking Brake Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** The electronic parking brake independently engages while the vehicle is at a complete stop in traffic, requiring the driver to manually disengage it to drive off.
- **Solution:** Dealer diagnostics to inspect the parking brake switch or electronic brake booster module.

## Steering

### Steering Gearbox and Rack Assembly

The electronic power steering assembly uses a rack and pinion gearbox that communicates with the vehicle's electronic modules to deliver variable steering assist.

**Sticky Electronic Steering Wheel**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel 'sticks' or resists micro-adjustments near dead center on straight highways, requiring significant driver force to break free, which causes the vehicle to jerk suddenly.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the electronic steering gearbox or rack and pinion unit.

**Complete Power Steering Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden, unexpected loss of power steering assist while driving, making the steering wheel extremely heavy and difficult to turn.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the faulty steering gearbox and rack and pinion assembly.
- **Cost:** Up to $6,233

## Electrical

### Infotainment System

The Accord's multimedia screen controls system preferences, navigation, and displays feeds for safety systems like the backup camera.

**Infotainment Screen Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** The center touchscreen randomly selects buttons on its own ('ghost touching'), freezes during navigation, goes completely blank, or fails to display the backup camera feed when shifting into reverse.
- **Solution:** Replacing the infotainment unit screen or updating the system firmware.

## Exterior

### Door Lock Actuators

The power door locking system relies on electric actuators inside each door panel to lock or unlock the latch mechanism.

**Premature Door Lock Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Door locks become unresponsive to the key fob and interior door switches, often accompanied by grinding noises from the doors, forcing owners to manually lock and unlock each door.
- **Solution:** Replacing the faulty door lock actuator motors.

### Sunroof and Glass Assemblies

The structural glass elements of the cabin include an acoustic windshield and an available tilting tempered glass sunroof.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The tempered glass sunroof spontaneously 'explodes' or shatters outward while driving at speed without any impact from road debris.
- **Solution:** Replacing the sunroof glass panel.

**Windshield Stress Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** A large crack (often several inches long) suddenly appearing on the lower windshield while parked or driving, with no evidence of impact chip marks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the acoustic windshield.

## Safety

### Collision Mitigation and Sensing Systems

The Honda Sensing suite includes active safety electronics like adaptive cruise control, collision mitigation, lane-keep assist, and traditional airbag passive safety components.

**Unintended Braking and Lane Corrections**
- **Symptoms:** The Collision Mitigation Braking System (CMBS) unexpectedly flashes 'BRAKE' on the dashboard, emits warning beeps, and slams on the brakes or jerks the steering wheel to correct a perceived road departure when no actual obstacle is present.
- **Solution:** Calibrating or replacing the forward-facing radar sensor, windshield camera, or updating the driving assist software.

**Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) Faults**
- **Symptoms:** A persistent 'Supplemental Restraint System' error message on the dash, or in rare reported instances, the spontaneous deployment of side airbags without a collision.
- **Solution:** Diagnosing the SRS error codes; replacing faulty seat weight sensors, airbag modules, or related wiring.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V314000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Accord&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the fuel pump fails, the engine can stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners and dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V771000: Electrical System:Body Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Accord&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A software error may cause intermittent or continuous disruptions in communication between the Body Control Module (BCM) and other components. Various system malfunctions such as inoperative windshield wipers, defroster, rearview camera, or exterior lighting can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will update the BCM software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V629000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Accord&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** In certain scenarios, the back-up camera center display may not function properly. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rearview Mirrors." If the rearview camera display does not show what is behind the vehicle, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the display audio unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V158000: Seat Belts:Front:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Accord&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** A manufacturing issue with the front seat belts may cause the seat belt buckle channel to interfere with the release button, preventing the seat belt buckle from latching. An unlatched seat belt cannot properly restrain the seat occupant during a crash, increasing their risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driver and front passenger seat belt buckle release buttons or the buckle assemblies as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Accord&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Accord&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Honda Accord remains an extremely appealing used-car choice for shoppers who prioritize contemporary style, crisp driving dynamics, and excellent fuel efficiency. However, because this was the first model year of a complete redesign, it has experienced some highly concentrated mechanical issues. To shop smart, look for models where a previous owner has documented head gasket and fuel injector replacements, particularly on the highly affected 1.5-liter turbocharged engine. Buying a model equipped with the 2.0-liter turbocharged engine or the naturally aspirated Hybrid option may help minimize the risk of premature engine problems. Regardless of the engine choice, a thorough pre-purchase inspection should always include checking the steering for any sticking sensations and ensuring that the safety sensors and infotainment screens work flawlessly.
