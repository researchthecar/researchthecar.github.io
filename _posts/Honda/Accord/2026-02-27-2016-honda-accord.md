# 2016 Honda Accord Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2016 Honda Accord is widely praised for its engaging driving dynamics, spacious cabin, and strong fuel economy, making it a compelling choice on the used car market. However, prospective buyers should review specific recurring owner complaints regarding electrical glitches, audio system failures, and occasional powertrain hiccups.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder

The 2.4-liter direct-injection four-cylinder engine produces approximately 185 horsepower and offers a balanced blend of efficiency and daily drivability. It is generally robust, though some owners report low-RPM hesitation and stalling.

**Engine Stalling and Low RPM Sputtering**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may stutter, hesitate, or feel like the engine is choking at low RPMs (between 1,000 and 2,000 RPM) or experience unexpected stalling.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel delivery components, throttle body, and update engine control software if applicable.

### 3.5L V6

The 3.5-liter V6 engine delivers a smooth 278 horsepower, providing brisk acceleration for higher trims. It is a long-lasting powerplant, though proper maintenance of its fuel and ignition systems is essential.

**Fuel Pump and Pressure Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated warning lights on the dashboard, difficulty starting, and potential engine stalling related to fuel pressure delivery.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fuel pressure levels and replace failing fuel pumps or related high-pressure components.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Paired with the 4-cylinder engine, this CVT optimizes fuel economy and provides seamless acceleration. Regular fluid changes are vital to maintain its longevity.

### 6-Speed Manual Transmission

Available on select 4-cylinder trims, the 6-speed manual offers a direct, engaging shifting experience for enthusiasts with few mechanical complaints reported.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

Coupled with the V6 engine, this traditional 6-speed automatic provides swift, smooth gear changes and has a solid reputation for durability.

## Brakes

### ABS and VSA Module

The braking system provides confident stopping power under normal conditions, but module failures can trigger multiple warning lights and require costly repairs.

**Vehicle Stability Assist / ABS Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Multiple warning lights illuminate on the dashboard (ABS, traction control, brake, and steering lights), and the anti-lock braking system may temporarily disengage.
- **Solution:** Scan diagnostic codes for VSA solenoid malfunction; dealership repair may involve replacing the hydraulic control module.
- **Cost:** Over $3,000 for module replacement

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Audio System

The electronic architecture supports advanced tech features, but the audio head unit is susceptible to disruptive static feedback and freezing.

**Loud Static and Speaker Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Speakers suddenly blast deafening static noise even when the audio unit is powered off, potentially masking critical exterior sounds.
- **Solution:** Reset or replace the infotainment head unit or audio amplifier module.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Exterior lighting complaints frequently center around premature burnout or failure of daytime running lamps and headlight assemblies.

**Headlight and Daytime Running Light Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Daytime running lights (DRLs) or headlights fail prematurely or flicker intermittently on one or both sides.
- **Solution:** Replace the affected bulb assemblies or integrated LED modules.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V060000: Fuel System, Other:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Accord&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Reduced fuel pump performance can result in an engine stall, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will update the fuel injection engine control unit (FI-ECU) software and, if necessary, replace the fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V418000: Electrical System:12V/24V/48V Battery](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Accord&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** An electrical short increases the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will replace the sensor, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Accord&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Accord&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Honda Accord remains a top contender in the midsize sedan segment due to its refined performance, comfort, and safety ratings. Used car buyers should thoroughly check the infotainment system for static issues, test all exterior lights, and ensure there are no active dashboard warning lights related to the stability control or braking modules.
