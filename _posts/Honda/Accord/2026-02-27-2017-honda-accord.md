# 2017 Honda Accord Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used Honda Accord, prospective buyers generally find a well-regarded sedan known for its everyday comfort, strong resale value, and composed handling. However, owners have flagged several recurring concerns that require careful attention during a pre-purchase inspection. Key areas to monitor include potential powertrain hesitations, electrical quirks such as infotainment audio distortion, and advanced driver-assistance system glitches.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder

A naturally aspirated 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine producing approximately 185 horsepower, paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT) or manual shifter. It offers solid daily efficiency and generally dependable mechanical performance.

### 3.5L V6

A smooth 3.5-liter V6 engine generating around 278 horsepower, typically mated to a 6-speed automatic transmission. It delivers strong acceleration but demands attention to fuel system reliability.

**Engine Stalling and Fuel System Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences sudden engine hesitation, loss of power, or unexpected stalling while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect fuel pump pressure and replace faulty fuel system components or injectors as needed.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

A smooth-shifting automatic transmission designed to maximize fuel economy on four-cylinder models. Regular fluid changes are vital to maintain long-term reliability.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

A traditional geared automatic transmission paired with V6 engine variants. It provides responsive gear changes and robust durability under normal driving conditions.

## Brakes

### Anti-lock Braking System (ABS)

Ensures stable braking performance and vehicle control. Failures in the ABS module can trigger dashboard warning grids and expensive repairs.

**ABS Module Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Multiple warning lights illuminate on the dashboard simultaneously, accompanied by low-voltage or module communication error codes.
- **Solution:** Test the hydraulic control unit and replace the malfunctioning ABS module.
- **Cost:** $2,200 to $3,500

## Steering

### Power Steering Rack

Provides directional control and steering feedback. System binding or component failure poses significant safety risks.

**Steering Lockup**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel suddenly becomes rigid or difficult to turn while driving at speed.
- **Solution:** Inspect the power steering rack and pinion assembly and replace defective internal components.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Audio System

Manages cabin entertainment, navigation, and vehicle settings. Owners report occasional audio anomalies that can be jarring while driving.

**Loud Static Interference**
- **Symptoms:** The factory audio system or infotainment screen suddenly emits a piercing, high-amplitude static noise through the speakers.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the radio control head unit and update or replace faulty audio components.
- **Cost:** Expensive

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Integrates cameras and sensors to assist with emergency braking and lane keeping. Malfunctions can lead to unwarranted system interventions.

**Unexpected Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The collision mitigation braking system activates abruptly without obstacles, or camera modules register internal errors.
- **Solution:** Inspect the multi-function ADAS camera module and recalibrate or replace sensors experiencing internal faults.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V060000: Fuel System, Other:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Accord&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Reduced fuel pump performance can result in an engine stall, increasing the risk of crash.
- **What to do:** Honda will notify owners, and dealers will update the fuel injection engine control unit (FI-ECU) software and, if necessary, replace the fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V858000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Accord&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall while driving, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V332000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification:Front Passenger](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Honda&model=Accord&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The front passenger seat weight sensor may crack and short circuit, which can cause the air bags to deploy unintentionally during a crash. Air bags that deploy unintentionally during a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the seat weight sensors, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

Overall, this vehicle remains a solid choice in the used midsize sedan market, offering an appealing blend of everyday drivability, comfort, and safety. Prospective buyers should verify that all open recalls have been addressed and thoroughly test the electrical systems, brakes, and driver-assistance features during a pre-purchase inspection to avoid unexpected repair costs.
