# 2017 Honda Accord

### Overview
The 2017 Honda Accord is generally regarded as a reliable vehicle, continuing Honda's reputation for durability and quality. It was offered with two main powertrain options: a 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine and a 3.5-liter V6 engine, both paired with either a continuously variable transmission (CVT) or a 6-speed automatic transmission. The Accord is known for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and good fuel economy. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected repairs, like any vehicle, some specific components have seen isolated reports of issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance.

---


## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder (K24W1)
**Oil Dilution**
- **Symptoms:** Potential for oil dilution with fuel, especially in colder climates or during short trips. This can lead to increased oil level and a gasoline smell in the oil.
- **Solution:** Software updates for engine control module (ECM) have been released by Honda to mitigate this. Regular oil level checks and appropriate oil changes are recommended.
- **Cost:** $0 - $150 (for software update/inspection)

**Variable Timing Control (VTC) Actuator**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the engine on startup, particularly when cold. This is due to a faulty VTC actuator.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the VTC actuator.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

## Transmission

### CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)
**Hesitation or Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners have reported occasional hesitation or a slight shudder during acceleration, particularly at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the transmission control module (TCM) or fluid changes may resolve this. In some cases, internal transmission issues might require repair or replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500 (for software/fluid), $2,000 - $4,000 (for replacement)

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Software Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional freezing, unresponsiveness, or issues with Bluetooth connectivity.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the infotainment system are often available. A system reset can sometimes resolve temporary glitches.
- **Cost:** $0 - $200 (for software update/diagnostic)


### Battery
**Premature Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Some reports of batteries failing sooner than expected, potentially linked to parasitic drain or specific usage patterns.
- **Solution:** Battery testing and replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Honda Accord stands out as a highly reliable vehicle, with the majority of its systems performing without significant issues. The most commonly cited concerns revolve around minor engine-related issues like oil dilution in the 2.4L engine and VTC actuator noise, as well as occasional infotainment system glitches. The CVT transmission has seen some reports of hesitation, though often resolvable with software updates. Overall, the Accord offers a dependable ownership experience, and potential buyers can be confident in its long-term reliability, provided routine maintenance is performed and any minor reported issues are addressed promptly.
