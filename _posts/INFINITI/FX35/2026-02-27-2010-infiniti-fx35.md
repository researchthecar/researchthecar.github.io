# 2010 INFINITI FX35

### Overview
The 2010 INFINITI FX35, a luxury crossover SUV, is generally considered a reliable vehicle, particularly for its powertrain. It was offered with a potent V6 engine and a smooth automatic transmission, contributing to a positive ownership experience for many. While most owners report satisfaction, like any vehicle, some common areas have seen occasional reports of issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective buyers or current owners.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L VQ35HR V6
**Oil Leaks (Valve Covers/Gaskets)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil on the engine, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets or other seals.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Coolant Leaks (Hoses/Radiator)**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant leaks under the vehicle, sweet smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking hoses, radiator, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

## Electrical

### Standard System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, navigation issues.
- **Solution:** Software updates or replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

**Keyless Entry/Start Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the vehicle, key fob not recognized.
- **Solution:** Battery replacement in key fob, reprogramming, or replacement of the intelligent key system components.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2010 INFINITI FX35 demonstrates good overall reliability, with its robust 3.5L V6 engine and transmission being strong points. Owners should be aware of potential minor oil and coolant leaks, which are not uncommon for vehicles of this age and mileage. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system and keyless entry, are the most frequently cited non-routine issues. However, these are generally manageable with repairs or updates. The suspension, exterior, and interior components are largely free from widespread problems, suggesting a well-built vehicle that, with proper maintenance, can offer dependable service.
