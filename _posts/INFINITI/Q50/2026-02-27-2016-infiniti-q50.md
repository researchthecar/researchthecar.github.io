# 2016 INFINITI Q50 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2016 Infiniti Q50, buyers will find a stylish luxury sport sedan offering a variety of potent engine choices and rear-wheel or all-wheel drive configurations. While many owners enjoy its performance and cabin comfort, owner feedback and safety reports point to a few recurring trouble spots worth noting. Prospective buyers should carefully evaluate vehicle history, maintenance records, and specific electronic and restraint systems prior to purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

The entry-level 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates around 208 horsepower and offers efficient daily driving performance. Buyers should listen for unusual turbo noises and verify consistent boost pressure during test drives.

**Power Loss and Turbocharger Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected engine power loss, hesitation, or smoke, sometimes accompanied by turbocharger failure.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the turbocharger assembly, boost pressure, and engine management codes for proper operation.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbocharged V6

The 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 is available in higher trims, producing either 300 or 400 horsepower for brisk acceleration. Maintenance history and oil change frequency are critical when evaluating this complex powerplant.

### 3.5L Hybrid V6

The 3.5-liter hybrid powertrain pairs a V6 engine with an electric motor to deliver around 360 combined horsepower with improved fuel economy. Routine checks of both traditional engine components and the hybrid battery system are recommended.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 7-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly across rear-wheel and all-wheel drive layouts. Ensuring fluid changes have been performed on schedule helps maintain long-term shifting reliability.

**Valve Body and Shifting Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported rough shifting, unexpected gear selection issues, or sudden loss of drive functions linked to internal transmission components like the valve body.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition and run diagnostic scans for stored transmission control module codes.

## Exterior

### Sunroof and Body Structure

Cabin features and trim elements generally hold up well, though occasional body and glass anomalies have been documented by owners.

**Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The glass sunroof panel may spontaneously crack or explode while driving under normal conditions without outside impact.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof glass and frame assembly for structural stress or alignment issues.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Occupant Detection System

The airbag and restraint systems are designed to protect occupants during collisions, though electronic sensor faults can trigger dashboard warnings.

**Passenger Airbag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** The airbag warning light or passenger seat indicator illuminates persistently on the instrument cluster, indicating a fault within the occupant classification system.
- **Solution:** Have a dealership or specialist inspect the passenger seat sensor wiring harness and occupant detection module.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V430000: Steering:Automated/Adaptive Steering](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=INFINITI&model=Q50&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the vehicle does not steer as the driver anticipates, there is an increased the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the Direct Adaptive Steering ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V244000: Air Bags:Sensor:Occupant Classification](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=INFINITI&model=Q50&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** As a result, the passenger frontal air bag may be turned off and not deploy in the event of a crash. If the passenger frontal air bag does not deploy as intended in the event of a crash, the passenger is at an increased risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the Air Bag Control Unit (ACU) and OCS Electronic Control Unit (ECU) in Altima, Maxima, Murano, Rogue, and Sentra vehicles, and replace the OCS ECU in LEAF, NV200, NV200 Taxi, Pathfinder, Infiniti Q50, JX35, and QX60. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V476000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=INFINITI&model=Q50&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and Infiniti dealers will reprogram the Fuel Pump Control Module (FPCM) with corrected software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V470000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=INFINITI&model=Q50&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** Both scenarios can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driveshaft, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Infiniti Q50 delivers appealing luxury performance, but used-car buyers should perform thorough pre-purchase inspections. Pay close attention to powertrain operation, turbocharger health, transmission shift quality, and electrical warning lights regarding the airbag system to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
