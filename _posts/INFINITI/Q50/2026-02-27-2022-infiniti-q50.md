# 2022 INFINITI Q50 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

Evaluating the 2022 Infiniti Q50 involves weighing its luxury sport sedan appeal against limited owner-reported reliability data. While official owner feedback for this model year is sparse, prospective buyers should still inspect standard luxury vehicle systems and security features closely during a pre-purchase evaluation.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6

This 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine produces roughly 300 horsepower in standard trims and up to 400 horsepower in Red Sport models, delivering strong acceleration. It is generally robust, though turbocharged engines require consistent oil change intervals to maintain long-term reliability.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic

Paired with a responsive 7-speed automatic transmission available in rear-wheel or all-wheel drive, this setup offers smooth highway cruising. Routine fluid checks are recommended to ensure longevity.

## Electrical

### Keyless Entry and Security

Electrical and security systems generally function well, though advanced keyless entry systems can occasionally be susceptible to external signal relay manipulation.

**Remote Relay Vulnerability**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle accessed and driven away without a key fob present or physical signs of a break-in, potentially via remote relay devices.
- **Solution:** Park the vehicle securely, store key fobs in signal-blocking Faraday pouches when not in use, and consult a dealer regarding available security updates.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Infiniti Q50 remains an engaging luxury sport sedan choice with potent twin-turbo performance. Because available owner data is minimal, buyers should prioritize a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection and verify all electronic security features before finalizing a purchase.
