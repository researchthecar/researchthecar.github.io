**Introduction to the 2021 INFINITI Q50:**

The 2021 INFINITI Q50 is a luxury sport sedan that offers a thrilling driving experience. It boasts a powerful 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine that delivers 300 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque. The Q50 also comes with an available 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 that produces an impressive 400 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque. These engines are paired with either a 7-speed automatic transmission or an 8-speed automatic transmission. Drivers appreciate the Q50's agile handling, responsive steering, and comfortable interior. However, some owners have reported issues related to its engine, transmission, suspension, and other components.

**Engine**

**3.0L Twin-Turbocharged V6 (300 hp)**

* **Oil consumption:** Excessive oil consumption due to faulty piston rings or valve seals. Symptoms include low oil levels, blue smoke from the exhaust, and poor engine performance. Solution: Replace the piston rings or valve seals. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
* **Timing chain problems:** The timing chain may stretch prematurely, causing the engine to lose power and stall. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, reduced performance, and increased fuel consumption. Solution: Replace the timing chain and related components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

**3.0L Twin-Turbocharged V6 (400 hp)**

* **Turbocharger failure:** The turbochargers may fail due to oil starvation or other issues. Symptoms include loss of power, decreased boost pressure, and increased exhaust emissions. Solution: Replace the turbochargers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $5,000+
* **Fuel injector issues:** The fuel injectors may become clogged or malfunction, causing engine misfires and poor performance. Symptoms include rough idling, difficulty starting, and increased fuel consumption. Solution: Replace the affected fuel injector(s). Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Shifting problems:** Transmission may hesitate or refuse to shift into certain gears due to faulty sensors or solenoids. Symptoms include hard shifting, delayed gear engagement, and loss of power. Solution: Replace the faulty components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**8-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Clunking noise:** Transmission may make a clunking noise during acceleration or shifts due to worn transmission gears. Symptoms include a noticeable clunk when shifting, a rough transmission engagement, and vibration. Solution: Replace the worn gears. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Suspension**

* **Strut mount failure:** The strut mounts may wear out prematurely, causing the suspension to become noisy and unstable. Symptoms include a clunking noise from the suspension, uneven tire wear, and reduced handling. Solution: Replace the strut mounts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $700+

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** The vehicle's battery may drain prematurely due to a faulty alternator or other electrical components. Symptoms include difficulty starting or starting the vehicle, dimming headlights, and dead battery. Solution: Diagnose and repair the underlying electrical issue. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
* **Electrical glitches:** Occasional electrical glitches may occur, causing various issues such as malfunctioning gauges, inoperative lights, or intermittent system failures. Solution: Update software or repair faulty electrical components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Headlight condensation:** The headlights may experience condensation buildup inside the lens assembly due to poor sealing. Symptoms include reduced light output, fogging inside the lens, and potential electrical damage. Solution: Replace the affected headlight assembly. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Interior**

* **Infotainment system glitches:** The infotainment system may experience glitches, causing the screen to freeze, lag, or display errors. Symptoms include intermittent system failures, unresponsive touchscreen, and distorted audio. Solution: Update software or replace the infotainment module. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Climate control issues:** Climate control system may malfunction, causing inconsistent temperatures, air conditioning failure, or fan noise. Symptoms include poor air distribution, ineffective climate control, or distracting fan noises. Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty climate control components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+

**Summary**

The 2021 INFINITI Q50 is a performance-oriented luxury sedan with potential issues in various areas. Some of the more serious concerns include excessive oil consumption, timing chain problems, and turbocharger failure. The transmission, suspension, and electrical systems may also encounter issues. While many of these problems can be addressed through repairs, the cost of fixes can be significant. Prospective buyers should carefully consider these potential issues before investing in this vehicle.
