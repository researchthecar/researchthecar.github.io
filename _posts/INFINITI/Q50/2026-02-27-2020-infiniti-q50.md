# 2020 INFINITI Q50 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 Infiniti Q50, buyers will find a sporty luxury sedan known for its striking design and punchy turbocharged engine options. While many owners enjoy its engaging driving dynamics and comfortable cabin, used car shoppers should review potential owner-reported concerns regarding powertrain responsiveness and engine longevity. Conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help ensure any prospective vehicle has been well-maintained and is free from hidden mechanical troubles.

---

## Engine

### 3.0t Twin-Turbo V6

The 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine produces around 300 horsepower in standard trims and up to 400 horsepower in Red Sport models, delivering strong acceleration. Buyers should listen closely for unusual turbo noises and verify consistent maintenance history to mitigate the risk of premature component failure.

**Premature Turbocharger and Engine Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported premature turbocharger failure followed by catastrophic engine failure under 60,000 miles while driving.
- **Solution:** Requires professional inspection, potential turbocharger replacement, or complete engine replacement depending on the severity of the internal damage.

### 2.0t Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates roughly 208 horsepower, offering a more efficient entry point into the luxury sedan lineup. This powerplant provides adequate daily performance, though overall availability is more limited in later model years.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic

The 7-speed automatic transmission is paired with both rear-wheel drive and intelligent all-wheel drive configurations across the Q50 lineup. While generally smooth, some drivers have reported shifting sluggishness that warrants a careful test drive.

**Delayed Shifting and High RPM Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers note that the transmission takes too long to shift gears and requires higher engine revolutions per minute (RPM) than expected to change ratios.
- **Solution:** Perform a transmission control module (TCM) software update if available, or inspect internal hydraulic and mechanical components for wear.

## Electrical

### Forward Emergency Braking System

The standard safety suite includes modern driver assist technologies designed to enhance road awareness. Buyers should test these automated systems during a test drive to ensure sensor calibration is functioning correctly.

**Inadvertent Forward Emergency Braking Activation**
- **Symptoms:** The forward emergency braking system activates unexpectedly while driving under normal conditions without any immediate obstructions present.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect and recalibrate the radar and camera sensors responsible for advanced driver assistance features.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Infiniti Q50 offers an appealing blend of luxury and performance for used car buyers, but it demands careful evaluation before purchase. While drivetrain options deliver strong power, isolated reports of severe engine distress and shifting hesitations mean shoppers should prioritize vehicles with documented service records. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by an independent mechanic is strongly recommended to catch any hidden issues.
