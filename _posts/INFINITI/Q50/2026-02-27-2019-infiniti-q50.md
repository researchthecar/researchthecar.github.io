# 2019 INFINITI Q50 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury sport sedan, the 2019 Infiniti Q50 delivers striking styling and strong performance options. However, prospective buyers should carefully review potential mechanical and electrical trouble spots. While many owners enjoy the punchy acceleration and sleek cabin, owner-reported complaints highlight specific areas—such as turbocharger performance, steering alignment, and minor electrical quirks—that warrant a thorough pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6

The 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine comes in varying output tunes, offering robust horsepower and torque that provide energetic acceleration. While generally powerful, this powerplant has been associated in owner reports with early turbocharger wear and hesitation issues.

**Turbocharger Failure and Loss of Power**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a sudden loss of motive power, hesitation under acceleration, engine knocking, ticking noises, and rough idling.
- **Solution:** Requires diagnostic testing of the turbocharger units and potential replacement of failed turbo components.

### 2.0L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine provides baseline power and improved fuel efficiency for entry-level trims. It is a less common option but generally mirrors standard four-cylinder turbo maintenance requirements.

### 3.5L Hybrid V6

The 3.5-liter hybrid V6 combines a traditional internal combustion engine with an electric motor to boost efficiency and low-end torque. It offers a smooth power delivery profile though adds electronic complexity.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

The 7-speed automatic transmission is paired across most trims and rear-wheel or all-wheel-drive layouts. While designed for smooth highway cruising, some owners report rough shifting behaviors.

**Hard Shifting and Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may jerk violently or hesitate when shifting gears while driving in Drive or when pressing the gas pedal after a complete stop.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission control module software, check fluid levels, and evaluate internal valve body operation.

## Steering

### Steering System

The steering setup includes electronic power steering or Infiniti's Direct Adaptive Steering system on select models. Alignment sensitivity and persistent pulling have been noted as recurring owner complaints.

**Drifting and Alignment Instability**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle chronically drifts to the right or left, requiring constant steering correction, and can cause premature tire wear.
- **Solution:** Perform a comprehensive wheel alignment check and inspect steering linkage components for premature wear.

## Exterior

### Sunroof

Exterior glass and body panels generally hold up well, though isolated reports highlight unexpected sunroof failures.

**Spontaneous Sunroof Shattering**
- **Symptoms:** The glass sunroof panel suddenly shatters or explodes outward while driving at highway speeds without any apparent impact from debris.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged glass panel and inspect the sunroof frame for structural stress or alignment issues.

## Interior

### Driver Assistance and Cabin Electronics

Infiniti's technology suite handles advanced safety features, infotainment, and core vehicle electronics. Owners occasionally experience frustrating glitches with driver-assist sensors and auxiliary controls.

**Sensor and Horn Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Features such as the horn, blind spot monitoring, and lane departure warning systems intermittently fail to function.
- **Solution:** Diagnose wiring harnesses, reset or replace faulty radar/camera sensors, and inspect steering wheel clockspring connections.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=INFINITI&model=Q50&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

When evaluating a used model, prioritizing a detailed inspection of the turbochargers, steering alignment, and electronic driver aids is essential. While the vehicle offers compelling luxury and performance, verifying service records and conducting a comprehensive pre-purchase test drive will help guard against unexpected repair costs.
