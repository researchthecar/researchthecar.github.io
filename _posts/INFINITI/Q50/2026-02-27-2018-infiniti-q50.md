# 2018 INFINITI Q50 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used luxury sport sedan, the 2018 Infiniti Q50 delivers striking styling, competitive performance, and an upscale cabin. However, prospective buyers should look closely at its mechanical history and electronics. While many owners enjoy their daily drives, recurring complaints point to specific trouble areas—particularly concerning turbocharger durability, accessory drive belts, and passenger safety sensors—that warrant a thorough pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6

The 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 comes in varying output tiers ranging from 300 to 400 horsepower, offering brisk acceleration. Buyers should review service records carefully, as turbocharger longevity and related oil leaks are known pain points.

**Turbocharger Failure and Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** A leak in the turbocharger can cause oil to burn, creating visible smoke from the exhaust and a sudden loss of motive power.
- **Solution:** Requires professional inspection and replacement of the failing turbocharger assembly.

**Serpentine Belt Contamination and Shredding**
- **Symptoms:** Oil leaks can contaminate the serpentine belt, causing it to slip, shred, and potentially get pulled into the engine, leading to a sudden loss of essential drive functions.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged serpentine belt and address the root oil leak source immediately.

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4

The 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine generates around 208 horsepower and prioritizes efficiency. It provides adequate power for daily commuting, though maintenance history remains key.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

This 7-speed automatic transmission routes power smoothly to the rear wheels or intelligent all-wheel-drive system. It generally operates reliably when properly maintained.

## Safety

### Air Bag and Occupant Classification System

Safety electronics generally protect occupants well, but sensor failures in the front passenger seating can trigger warning lights and deactivate supplemental restraints.

**Air Bag Warning Light and OCS Sensor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The air bag warning light illuminates unexpectedly on the dashboard due to an inoperable front passenger Occupant Classification System (OCS) sensor or seatbelt component.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to a dealer or qualified technician to diagnose and replace the faulty OCS sensor or seat wiring.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=INFINITI&model=Q50&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 17V476000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=INFINITI&model=Q50&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and Infiniti dealers will reprogram the Fuel Pump Control Module (FPCM) with corrected software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V470000: Power Train:Driveline:Driveshaft](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=INFINITI&model=Q50&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Both scenarios can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the driveshaft, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Infiniti Q50 remains an appealing and budget-friendly entry into luxury performance sedans, but it requires careful vetting before purchase. Buyers should prioritize models with comprehensive service histories, paying special attention to turbocharger health, accessory belts, and functioning cabin electronics. A pre-purchase inspection by a knowledgeable mechanic is strongly recommended to catch any hidden leaks or sensor faults early.
