**Introduction:**

The 2014 INFINITI Q50 is a luxury sport sedan powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine that produces 328 horsepower and 269 lb-ft of torque. It is available with a 7-speed automatic transmission or a 6-speed manual transmission. The Q50 offers a stylish exterior, a well-equipped interior, and a comfortable ride. However, it has been criticized for its lack of rear seat space and some reliability concerns.

**Engine**

**3.5L V6 Engine**

* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner may fail, causing the timing chain to skip or break. This can result in catastrophic engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Torque Converter Shudder**
    * Description/Symptoms: The torque converter may shudder during acceleration or cruising.
    * Solution: Replace the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Keyless Entry System Malfunction**
    * Description/Symptoms: The keyless entry system may fail to unlock or lock the doors.
    * Solution: Replace the keyless entry module.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Panel Rattle**
    * Description/Symptoms: The sunroof panel may rattle while driving.
    * Solution: Adjust or lubricate the sunroof tracks.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Navigation System Screen Black Out**
    * Description/Symptoms: The navigation system screen may black out while driving.
    * Solution: Update the navigation system software.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2014 INFINITI Q50 is a stylish and comfortable luxury sport sedan, but it has some reliability concerns. The most severe issue is the timing chain tensioner failure, which can lead to catastrophic engine damage. The torque converter shudder and sunroof panel rattle are also significant, but more common problems. Overall, the cost of repairs can be substantial, making the long-term ownership of a Q50 potentially expensive.
