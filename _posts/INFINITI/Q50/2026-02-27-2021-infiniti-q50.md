# 2021 INFINITI Q50 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Infiniti Q50 delivers strong twin-turbo V6 performance and a luxury-focused cabin for used-car shoppers. While NHTSA complaints for this model year are limited, prospective buyers should still inspect the vehicle thoroughly, focusing on electronics, vehicle speed control, and safety systems.

---

## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6 (Luxe, Sensory)

This 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine produces 300 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration for daily driving. It is generally regarded as a potent powerplant, though proper maintenance is essential for longevity.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6 High Output (Red Sport 400)

Tuned to deliver 400 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque, this high-output version powers the performance-oriented Red Sport 400 trim. Buyers should verify proper electronic throttle operation during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Unexpected Acceleration**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experienced unintended acceleration while parked or operating at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Have the electronic throttle control system and accelerator pedal sensor inspected by a qualified technician.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

Equipped with a responsive 7-speed automatic transmission paired with rear-wheel or all-wheel drive, this unit offers smooth gear changes. No widespread recurring complaint patterns have been highlighted for this model year.

## Exterior

### Body Structure

Isolated reports note sudden exterior noises at highway speeds, though structural integrity remains generally sound.

**Unexpected Exterior Noise**
- **Symptoms:** A sudden loud noise, resembling a gunshot, occurred while driving at highway speeds, causing driver alarm.
- **Solution:** Inspect exterior glass, panels, and trim for stress fractures or unbonded sections.

## Safety

### Air Bags

Owner reports indicate occasional illumination of the airbag warning indicator, requiring diagnostic scanning.

**Air Bag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** The supplemental restraint system (SRS) warning light illuminates unexpectedly while driving.
- **Solution:** Scan the vehicle's computer for diagnostic trouble codes to identify the faulty sensor or module and replace as necessary.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V402000: Suspension:Rear:Axle:Spindle](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=INFINITI&model=Q50&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The steering knuckle or rear axle could deform upon impact, such as from hitting a curb, resulting in a loss of steering control, or possible wheel separation. Both of these conditions can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the front steering knuckle and rear axle housing, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V599000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=INFINITI&model=Q50&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rear-view camera that does not display an image reduces the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the control unit software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V234000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Engine Control Module (Ecu/Ecm):Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=INFINITI&model=Q50&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A lack of fuel may cause an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** INFINITI will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the ECM software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Infiniti Q50 remains an appealing sport sedan option on the used market thanks to its powerful engine lineup and upscale features. Because NHTSA owner reports indicate minor concerns ranging from electronic throttle behavior to airbag warning lights, a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection is strongly recommended before finalizing your purchase.
