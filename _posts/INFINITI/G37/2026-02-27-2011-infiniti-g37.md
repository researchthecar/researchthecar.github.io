# 2011 INFINITI G37

### Overview
The 2011 INFINITI G37, a popular luxury sport sedan and coupe, is generally regarded as a reliable vehicle, particularly known for its potent V6 engine and engaging driving dynamics. Built on the robust Nissan FM platform, it offers a compelling blend of performance and comfort. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected repairs, like any vehicle, certain components can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented, non-wear-and-tear related problems that may arise, providing insights into potential areas of concern for prospective buyers and current owners. The G37's strong mechanical foundation contributes significantly to its overall dependability.

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional freezing or unresponsiveness of the infotainment screen, navigation system errors.
- **Solution:** Software updates, potential replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

**Key Fob Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Premature battery depletion in the intelligent key fob.
- **Solution:** Replacement of key fob battery, checking for parasitic drain.
- **Cost:** $10 - $50

### Summary Verdict
The 2011 INFINITI G37 stands out as a highly reliable luxury vehicle, with its core mechanical components, including the powerful 3.7L V6 engine and various transmission options (5-speed auto, 7-speed auto, 6-speed manual), demonstrating excellent durability. Major issues are infrequent, and most reported problems are minor and related to specific electronic features. The infotainment system can occasionally exhibit glitches, and some owners have noted issues with seat heaters. Key fob battery drain is also a minor inconvenience. Overall, the G37 offers a dependable driving experience with a low probability of significant mechanical failures, making it a solid choice for those seeking performance and reliability.
