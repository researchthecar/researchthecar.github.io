**2010 INFINITI FX50**

The 2010 INFINITI FX50 is a luxury SUV powered by a 390-horsepower, 5.0-liter V8 engine paired with a seven-speed automatic transmission. It comes standard with all-wheel drive, a premium audio system, and a host of advanced safety features. The FX50 is praised for its powerful performance, comfortable interior, and sharp handling. However, it has also been criticized for its high fuel consumption and somewhat cramped rear seat.

**Engine**

**5.0L V8 Engine**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:** Oil or coolant leaks from the intake manifold gaskets. Symptoms include a drop in coolant level, oil leaks, and a rough idle. The solution is to replace the intake manifold gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Transmission**

**Seven-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Fluid Leaks:** Fluid leaks from the transmission pan or lines. Symptoms include low transmission fluid levels and slipping gears. The solution is to replace the leaking seals or lines. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
* **Shift Solenoid Failure:** Electrical issues within the transmission control module or a failure of the shift solenoid. Symptoms include harsh or delayed shifting or a complete loss of gears. The solution is to replace the faulty shift solenoid or transmission control module. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Suspension**

* **Rear Suspension Sag:** The rear suspension can sag over time due to worn or broken springs. Symptoms include a lower ride height and reduced stability. The solution is to replace the worn springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** The battery can drain prematurely due to a parasitic draw from the electrical system. Symptoms include difficulty starting the vehicle or a dead battery. The solution is to identify and correct the source of the electrical drain. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the source of the drain
* **Navigation System Failure:** The navigation system may malfunction or become unresponsive. Symptoms include a blank or frozen screen, incorrect directions, or a loss of GPS signal. The solution is to update the navigation software or replace the navigation module. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof Leaks:** Water leaks from the sunroof during heavy rain or car washes. Symptoms include water stains on the headliner or interior trim. The solution is to adjust or replace the sunroof seals or drain lines. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:** The climate control system may malfunction, leading to inadequate heating or cooling. Symptoms include inconsistent temperature control, fan issues, or unusual noises. The solution is to diagnose and repair the faulty component, which may include replacing the climate control module or actuator motors. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,200+
* **Instrument Cluster Failure:** The instrument cluster may malfunction, causing dim or inoperative gauges, warning lights, or a complete loss of display. The solution is to replace the faulty instrument cluster or its components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Summary**

The 2010 INFINITI FX50 is generally a reliable vehicle, but it has some potential issues that can be costly to repair. The most common problems are related to the engine, transmission, and electrical system. Some of these issues, such as the intake manifold gasket leak and transmission fluid leaks, are considered major repairs. However, overall, the FX50 is a well-rounded vehicle with strong performance and a comfortable interior.
