## 2023 INFINITI QX60

The 2023 INFINITI QX60 is a mid-size, three-row luxury SUV that offers a comfortable ride, a spacious and well-equipped interior, and powerful engine options. It is powered by a standard 3.5-liter V6 engine that produces 295 horsepower and 270 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission. It also offers an optional 2.5-liter turbocharged 4-cylinder engine that produces 268 horsepower and 300 lb-ft of torque with the same transmission. While the QX60 is known for its reliability and high build quality, there have been some issues reported by owners.

### Engine

**3.5-liter V6 Engine**
- **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Some owners have reported experiencing excessive oil consumption, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage. The issue is believed to be related to piston ring problems, which allow oil to leak into the combustion chamber.
    - Solution: The solution involves replacing the faulty piston rings, which can cost around $2,000-$3,000+.
    - Recalls: None Found

**2.5-liter Turbocharged 4-Cylinder Engine**
- **No Issues Found**

### Transmission

**9-Speed Automatic Transmission**
- **Harsh Shifting:** Some owners have reported experiencing harsh shifting, especially when shifting between gears 1 and 2. The issue is believed to be caused by a faulty transmission control module or solenoids.
    - Solution: The solution involves reprogramming the transmission control module or replacing the faulty solenoids. The cost can range from $500-$1,500+.
    - Recalls: None Found

### Suspension

- **No Issues Found**

### Electrical

- **Battery Drain:** Some owners have reported experiencing rapid battery drain, even when the vehicle is parked. The issue is believed to be related to a faulty parasitic draw or a weak battery.
    - Solution: Identifying and fixing the parasitic draw or replacing the battery can cost around $100-$500.
    - Recalls: None Found

- **Faulty Sensors:** Some owners have reported issues with faulty sensors, such as the TPMS (Tire Pressure Monitoring System) or ABS (Anti-Lock Braking System) sensors, which can lead to incorrect readings and potential safety concerns.
    - Solution: Replacing the faulty sensors can cost around $200-$800 per sensor.
    - Recalls: None Found

### Exterior

- **No Issues Found**

### Interior

- **Infotainment System Issues:** Some owners have reported experiencing issues with the infotainment system, such as freezing, slow response, or connectivity problems.
    - Solution: Software updates or replacing the infotainment unit can cost around $500-$1,500.
    - Recalls: None Found

### Summary

Overall, the 2023 INFINITI QX60 is a reliable and well-received SUV with few reported issues. The most significant issue is excessive oil consumption in the 3.5-liter V6 engine, which can be costly to repair. However, this issue is not widespread, and the majority of QX60 owners have not experienced any major problems.
