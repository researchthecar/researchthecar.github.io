# 2019 INFINITI QX60 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Infiniti QX60 is a luxury three-row midsize crossover that balances a comfortable ride with a spacious, family-friendly cabin. While many owners enjoy its smooth performance and feature set, used-car buyers should review potential trouble spots. Driver-reported complaints point to occasional powertrain complications, electrical sensors, and visibility or camera issues that warrant a thorough inspection prior to purchase.

---

## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Engine

The QX60 is powered by a 3.5-liter V6 engine producing around 295 horsepower and 270 lb-ft of torque, offering confident acceleration for daily driving. Overall, the powerplant is reasonably robust, though isolated owner reports note timing chain and tensioner wear requiring professional mechanical attention.

**Timing Chain and Tensioner Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicles may fail to start, exhibit a loss of acceleration response, illuminate the check engine light, or produce an audible rattling noise.
- **Solution:** Inspect the timing chain, tensioner, and guide assembly for premature wear; replace worn components as necessary.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Equipped with an Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission paired with front-wheel drive or intelligent all-wheel drive, this setup prioritizes fuel efficiency. However, buyers should inspect the transmission and differential areas carefully, as CVT durability and fluid leaks are known points of concern for this model.

**CVT Failure and Solenoid Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden transmission failure, inability to shift into gear, vehicle becoming undriveable, or diagnostic trouble codes such as P2813 indicating pressure control solenoid faults.
- **Solution:** Have a certified technician diagnose internal transmission pressures and replace failing solenoids or the entire CVT unit if mechanical damage has occurred.

**Transfer Case and Differential Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Fluid leaks, whining noises, or premature failure of side seals and bearings in the transfer case and differential components.
- **Solution:** Reseal the transfer case or replace damaged seals and bearings to prevent secondary drive system damage.

## Steering

### Electronic Power Steering

The steering system generally provides light, easy maneuverability, though rare reports of electronic power steering lockups require immediate diagnostic evaluation.

**Steering Wheel Binding**
- **Symptoms:** The steering wheel locks or binds while driving straight or turning at low speeds, accompanied by a green EPS warning light.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic power steering module, motor, and associated steering column components for faults.

## Electrical

### Back-Up Camera

The backup camera system is critical for parking safety, but owner feedback highlights intermittent display blackouts and visibility concerns.

**Backup Camera Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** A black screen appears on the display or visibility becomes poor when reversing, creating a safety hazard.
- **Solution:** Inspect camera wiring harnesses, software modules, and display units; replace faulty camera components as needed.

## Safety

### Advanced Driver Assistance Sensors

Electrical and sensor systems manage modern driver aids, though minor environmental factors or sensor sensitivity can trigger false warnings.

**Forward Collision Avoidance Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** Warning sensor lights randomly illuminate to indicate that the forward collision avoidance system has turned off.
- **Solution:** Clean debris from sensor areas and check sensor calibration or module connections.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=INFINITI&model=QX60&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 19V807000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic:Antilock/Traction Control/Electronic Limited Slip](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=INFINITI&model=QX60&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** A brake fluid leak on the circuit board may result in an electrical short, increasing the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Nissan will notify owners, and dealers will replace the ABS actuator, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2019 Infiniti QX60, prospective buyers should prioritize a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection focusing heavily on the transmission, transfer case, and drivetrain seals. While the vehicle offers great comfort and utility, ensuring the CVT operates smoothly and that safety cameras and steering components function without error will help protect your investment.
