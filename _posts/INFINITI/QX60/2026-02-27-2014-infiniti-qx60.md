# 2014 INFINITI QX60

### Overview
The 2014 INFINITI QX60, a luxury three-row SUV, generally offers a comfortable and refined ownership experience. It is powered by a robust V6 engine paired with a Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT). While the QX60 is known for its smooth ride and family-friendly features, like any vehicle, it has had some reported areas of concern. This analysis focuses on common technical issues reported by owners and repair data, excluding routine maintenance. Understanding these potential problems can help owners prepare for and address them proactively, ensuring the longevity and reliability of their QX60.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (VQ35DD)
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil spots under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking gaskets (e.g., valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket).
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant puddles, sweet smell, low coolant level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of hoses, radiator, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


## Transmission

### CVT (JR710E/JR711E)
**Hesitation/Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle hesitates during acceleration, jerky shifts, or feels like it's slipping.
- **Solution:** Software update, transmission fluid flush, or transmission replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $4,000+

**Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission warning light, reduced performance, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Transmission cooler flush and replacement, or transmission rebuild/replacement.
- **Cost:** $400 - $3,500+

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Malfunctions**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen unresponsive, freezing, or displaying errors.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500


### Battery Drain
**Premature Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, dead battery, electrical components not working.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, replacement of battery or faulty module.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

## Interior

### Climate Control System
**AC/Heater Performance**
- **Symptoms:** Weak airflow, inconsistent temperatures, unusual noises from vents.
- **Solution:** Replacement of blower motor, blend door actuators, or refrigerant recharge.
- **Cost:** $250 - $700


### Seat Actuators
**Power Seat Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Power seats not moving forward/backward or up/down.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seat motor or control module.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 INFINITI QX60 is a generally reliable luxury SUV, particularly praised for its comfortable ride and spacious interior. The most frequently cited concerns revolve around the Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT), which can experience hesitation, jerking, or overheating issues, sometimes requiring costly repairs. Engine-related problems like oil and coolant leaks are also reported, though often less severe. Electrical issues, primarily with the infotainment system and occasional battery drain, are present but not overwhelmingly common. While the suspension, exterior, and interior components are largely dependable, owners should be aware of potential CVT and minor engine leak concerns. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to any warning signs are recommended to ensure optimal performance and longevity.
