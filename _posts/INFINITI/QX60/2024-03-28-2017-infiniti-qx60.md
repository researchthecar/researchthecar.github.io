**2017 INFINITI QX60**

The 2017 INFINITI QX60 is a midsize luxury SUV that offers a spacious cabin, a powerful engine, and a comfortable ride. It is available with a 3.5-liter V6 engine that produces 295 horsepower and 270 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission. The QX60 is also available with a hybrid powertrain that combines a 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine with an electric motor for a combined output of 250 horsepower and 243 lb-ft of torque.

**Engine**

* **3.5-liter V6**
    * **Timing chain tensioner failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner can fail, causing the timing chain to skip and damage the engine. Symptoms include an engine rattle, reduced power, and a check engine light.
        * Solution: The timing chain tensioner and timing chain need to be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $3,000 - $4,000+
        * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 18V393000
    * **Valve cover gasket leak**
        * Description/Symptoms: The valve cover gasket can leak, causing oil to seep out of the engine. Symptoms include an oil leak, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels.
        * Solution: The valve cover gasket needs to be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found

* **2.5-liter Four-Cylinder (Hybrid)**
    * No Issues Found

**Transmission**

* **7-speed Automatic**
    * **Torque converter shudder**
        * Description/Symptoms: The torque converter can shudder during acceleration or deceleration. Symptoms include a vibration or shuddering sensation.
        * Solution: The torque converter needs to be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number 17V428000
    * **Shift solenoid failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The shift solenoid can fail, causing the transmission to shift improperly. Symptoms include harsh or delayed shifting, and a check engine light.
        * Solution: The shift solenoid needs to be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front lower control arm bushings wear out**
        * Description/Symptoms: The front lower control arm bushings can wear out, causing the suspension to become loose and noisy. Symptoms include clunking or rattling noises, excessive tire wear, and a misalignment.
        * Solution: The front lower control arm bushings need to be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain**
        * Description/Symptoms: The battery can drain prematurely, leaving the vehicle unable to start. Symptoms include a dead battery, dim headlights, and a clicking sound when trying to start the vehicle.
        * Solution: The battery needs to be replaced or the electrical system needs to be diagnosed for a parasitic drain.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight condensation**
        * Description/Symptoms: Moisture can accumulate inside the headlights, causing condensation to form. This can impair visibility and damage the headlights.
        * Solution: The headlights need to be resealed or replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Infotainment system screen failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system screen can fail, causing it to become unresponsive or display a blank screen. Symptoms include a frozen or unresponsive screen, flickering, or a loss of display.
        * Solution: The infotainment system screen needs to be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Panoramic sunroof shade failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The panoramic sunroof shade can fail to open or close properly. Symptoms include a jammed or noisy sunroof shade.
        * Solution: The panoramic sunroof shade needs to be repaired or replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2017 INFINITI QX60 is a reliable and comfortable SUV, but it is not without its issues. The most common problems reported include timing chain tensioner failure, torque converter shudder, and shift solenoid failure. The electrical and interior systems have also had some issues, such as battery drain, infotainment system screen failure, and panoramic sunroof shade failure. Overall, the QX60 has a moderate level of reliability with issues ranging in severity from minor to major, and repair costs from relatively affordable to expensive.
