# 2021 INFINITI QX60

### Overview
The 2021 INFINITI QX60, a luxury three-row SUV, generally presents a solid reliability profile, reflecting INFINITI's commitment to quality and driver comfort. While no vehicle is entirely immune to potential issues, the QX60 for this model year has demonstrated a commendable track record. Owners often praise its refined interior, smooth ride, and the performance of its powertrain. However, as with any complex automotive system, specific components can occasionally experience problems. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern and their associated repair considerations for the 2021 QX60.

## Transmission

### CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional hesitation during acceleration or a slight jerkiness in gear changes.
- **Solution:** Software update or transmission fluid flush and replacement. In severe cases, transmission internal repair or replacement may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600 for fluid service; $1,500 - $4,000 for transmission repair/replacement.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing or Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment touchscreen may occasionally freeze, become unresponsive, or restart unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Software update for the infotainment system. A hard reset of the system may also resolve temporary glitches.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300 for diagnostic and software update.


### Battery
**Premature Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Battery may drain faster than expected, leading to starting issues, especially after the vehicle has been sitting.
- **Solution:** Check for parasitic draws from electrical components. Battery testing and replacement if faulty.
- **Cost:** $150 - $350 for battery replacement.

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 INFINITI QX60 generally demonstrates good reliability, with its 3.5L V6 engine and standard suspension components showing no widespread issues. The most commonly reported technical concerns revolve around the CVT transmission, which can occasionally exhibit hesitation or jerking, often resolvable with software updates or fluid services. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment screen freezing and premature battery drain, are also noted but typically manageable. Interior issues are infrequent, with some reports of reduced AC performance. Overall, the QX60 is a dependable luxury SUV, but potential buyers should be aware of the minor transmission and electrical quirks that may arise.
