**Introduction:**

The 2021 INFINITI QX50 is a mid-size luxury SUV that impresses with its striking design, premium interior, and advanced technology. It's powered by a 2.0L turbocharged inline-four engine that generates 268 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT).

**Suspension:**

* **Rear Suspension Strut Upper Mounting Point**
Description/Symptoms: The rear suspension strut upper mounting point may corrode excessively, leading to a loss of structural integrity and potential safety issues.
Solution: Inspect the suspension strut upper mounting points and replace any affected parts.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 21V858000

**Electrical:**

* **Transmission Control Module (TCM)**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission control module (TCM) may malfunction and cause the transmission to shift erratically or not engage in gear at all.
Solution: Inspect the TCM and replace it if necessary.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Numbers: 20V326000, 20V631000

**Exterior:**

* **Taillights**
Description/Symptoms: The taillights may fail prematurely, affecting visibility and safety.
Solution: Replace the affected taillights.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Interior:**

* **Center Console**
Description/Symptoms: The center console may crack when subjected to excessive force, creating an unsightly defect.
Solution: Replace the center console.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Summary:**

The 2021 INFINITI QX50 has some reported issues that range in severity from minor cosmetic defects to more serious mechanical problems. The rear suspension strut upper mounting point corrosion, transmission control module malfunction, and taillight failures are the most concerning issues. Overall, while the QX50 offers a luxurious and enjoyable driving experience, potential buyers should be aware of these issues and consider the potential repair costs before making a purchase.
