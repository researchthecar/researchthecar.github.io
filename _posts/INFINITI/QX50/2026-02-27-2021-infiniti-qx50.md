# 2021 INFINITI QX50 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2021 Infiniti QX50, buyers will find a stylish compact luxury crossover powered by an innovative variable-compression turbocharged engine. While offering strong features and distinctive styling, prospective owners should review owner-reported complaints regarding electrical anomalies, powertrain behavior, and advanced driver assistance systems carefully during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L VC-Turbo 4-Cylinder

The 2.0-liter variable-compression turbocharged (VC-Turbo) 4-cylinder engine produces approximately 268 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque. It balances power and efficiency through dynamic compression ratio adjustments, though buyers should verify all mechanical health during an inspection.

**Electrical Warning Lights and System Faults**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report sudden vehicle jerking accompanied by multiple warning lights and system fault messages on the dashboard while driving at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified dealer or technician scan computer modules for pending diagnostic trouble codes and check for active software updates or related service campaigns.

**Loss of Motive Power and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Unexplained loss of power response, unusual mechanical noises from under the hood, or engine stalling incidents reported by drivers.
- **Solution:** Inspect engine mechanical components, variable compression actuator systems, and fuel delivery components for proper operation.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Equipped with an Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) paired with either front-wheel drive or intelligent all-wheel drive, this setup delivers smooth highway cruising. Buyers should test drive thoroughly to ensure responsive acceleration without hesitation or unusual transmission whine.

## Electrical

### Tire Pressure Monitoring System

Suspension and wheel hardware ride comfortably, though peripheral electronics like tire pressure monitors require standard upkeep.

**TPMS Sensor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Tire pressure warning light illuminates on the dash while individual tire pressure readings fail to display on the screen.
- **Solution:** Scan TPMS sensors using diagnostic tools to identify faulty wheel sensors or receiver modules and replace as necessary.

## Exterior

### Door Latches and Sensors

Interior cabin amenities are well-appointed, though electronic convenience sensors and latches should be tested during a used car evaluation.

**Door Ajar Warning Fault**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle repeatedly displays a door open or door ajar warning on the instrument cluster even when the door is fully shut.
- **Solution:** Inspect the door latch mechanism, striker alignment, and integrated door ajar sensor for proper electrical continuity and mechanical adjustment.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced safety systems provide driver-assist features but require verified sensor calibration to minimize false trigger risks.

**Phantom Braking Incidents**
- **Symptoms:** The emergency braking system unexpectedly engages and slows the vehicle down abruptly while driving at highway or moderate speeds with no obstructions.
- **Solution:** Check forward-facing camera and radar sensor alignment, and consult a dealer regarding any available sensor recalibration or software updates.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V402000: Suspension:Rear:Axle:Spindle](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=INFINITI&model=QX50&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** The steering knuckle or rear axle could deform upon impact, such as from hitting a curb, resulting in a loss of steering control, or possible wheel separation. Both of these conditions can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the front steering knuckle and rear axle housing, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V437000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=INFINITI&model=QX50&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Engine failure increases the risk of a crash. A bearing failure may cause a breach in the engine block, allowing hot oil to be discharged, increasing the risk of an engine fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the engine control module (ECM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2021 Infiniti QX50 offers attractive luxury packaging and an advanced variable-compression engine, but potential buyers should exercise diligence. Review vehicle history records carefully to confirm that all open safety campaigns and software updates have been completed by a certified dealer before finalizing a purchase.
