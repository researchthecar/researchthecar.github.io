The 2020 INFINITI QX50 is a compact luxury SUV known for its distinctive styling and innovative engine technology. The sole engine option for this model year is a 2.0-liter VC-Turbo inline-4 engine, producing 268 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque. This engine is paired with a Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT). Some owners praise the QX50 for its comfortable ride, spacious interior, and advanced driver-assistance features. However, some common complaints include the CVT's performance and the complexity of the infotainment system.

**Engine**

**2.0L VC-Turbo Inline-4**

*   **Engine Mount Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive engine vibration, especially at idle or during acceleration. Unusual noises coming from the engine bay.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty engine mount(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)**

*   **CVT Hesitation/Jerking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Hesitation or jerky acceleration from a stop or during low-speed driving. This can feel like the transmission is struggling to find the right gear ratio.
    *   Solution: Software recalibration, CVT fluid change. In severe cases, CVT replacement may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (fluid change) - $500 (recalibration) - $3,000+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **CVT Whining Noise**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noticeable whining noise coming from the transmission, particularly during acceleration.
    *   Solution: CVT fluid change or, in severe cases, CVT replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (fluid change) - $3,000+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system freezes, becomes unresponsive, or crashes, requiring a restart. Issues can affect navigation, audio, and climate control functions.
    *   Solution: Software update. Replacement of the infotainment unit in severe cases.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1,000+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Battery drains overnight or within a short period, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle.
    *   Solution: Diagnose and replace the faulty component causing the drain. Could be a faulty module or connection.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Panoramic Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof may squeak, stick, or fail to open or close properly.
    *   Solution: Lubrication, repair, or replacement of sunroof motor or components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various rattling or squeaking noises coming from the dashboard, door panels, or other interior trim pieces.
    *   Solution: Locate and tighten loose components, apply sound-deadening material, or replace damaged trim pieces.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+ (depending on source and severity)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2020 INFINITI QX50 presents a mixed bag of potential issues. The CVT transmission seems to be a common source of complaints, with hesitation, jerking, and noise issues reported. Electrical issues related to the infotainment system and battery drain are also concerns. Engine mount failures can also be an issue. While many issues are minor, the potential for costly CVT repairs and infotainment system replacements are definitely something to keep in mind when shopping for a QX50.
