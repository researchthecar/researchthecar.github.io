# 2016 INFINITI QX50

### Overview
The 2016 INFINITI QX50, a luxury compact SUV, generally presents a solid reliability record, particularly concerning its powertrain. Owners often praise its refined driving experience and upscale interior. While most components perform as expected, like any vehicle, there are specific areas where minor concerns have been noted by some owners. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of attention for prospective buyers or current owners. The QX50's design emphasizes comfort and performance, and its reliability data reflects a commitment to quality in its core mechanical and electrical systems.

## Engine

### 3.7L V6 (VQ37VHR)
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Higher than expected oil consumption, potentially leading to low oil levels if not monitored.
- **Solution:** Regular oil level checks and top-offs. In some cases, PCV valve replacement or internal engine inspection may be recommended.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500 (for PCV valve replacement and oil)

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 INFINITI QX50 demonstrates good overall reliability, with its V6 engine and automatic transmission being particularly robust. The most commonly cited technical concern is a tendency for the 3.7L V6 engine to consume oil, necessitating regular checks. Electrical systems, specifically the infotainment's Bluetooth, can occasionally exhibit minor glitches, usually resolved with software updates. Other areas like suspension, exterior, and interior components are generally free from widespread issues. For potential buyers, focusing on vehicles with a well-documented maintenance history, especially regarding oil levels, is advisable. The QX50 offers a dependable luxury SUV experience with a few minor points to monitor.
