# 2019 INFINITI QX50 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Infiniti QX50 introduces an innovative variable-compression engine design, offering a stylish exterior and a comfortable cabin for used-car buyers. However, owner complaints and technical data highlight notable reliability concerns regarding the powertrain and electronic safety systems. Buyers should carefully evaluate mechanical history, particularly concerning engine performance and automatic braking systems, before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L VC-Turbo 4-Cylinder

The 2.0-liter variable-compression turbocharged four-cylinder engine produces approximately 268 horsepower and 280 lb-ft of torque, providing an innovative balance of efficiency and power. While technologically advanced, this specific powerplant has been associated with critical engine bearing and actuator durability concerns.

**Engine Bearing and VCR Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report sudden loss of motive power, engine hesitation, severe stalling, and the appearance of metal shavings or warning lights [nhtsa-complaint-11728318, nhtsa-complaint-11745300].
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential engine component replacement or adherence to manufacturer recall repairs.

## Transmission

### Xtronic Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The continuously variable transmission pairs with front-wheel drive or intelligent all-wheel drive to maximize fuel economy. Owners have occasionally noted unexpected gear selection anomalies, such as dropping into neutral during operation.

**Unintended Neutral Shift**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle unexpectedly shifts into neutral while driving or idling at intersections, or experiences difficulty shifting into neutral [nhtsa-complaint-11579772, nhtsa-complaint-11338265, nhtsa-complaint-11184158].
- **Solution:** Diagnostic inspection of the shifter assembly and transmission control module.

## Interior

### Power Seats

Cabin convenience features like power-adjustable seats offer luxury appeal, but mechanical wear within the tracks and motors can cause premature failure.

**Seat Track and Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Driver seats become loose, rock during transit, or completely fail to move forward and backward, preventing proper driver positioning [nhtsa-complaint-11690094, nhtsa-complaint-11629513, nhtsa-complaint-11466239].
- **Solution:** Replacement of seat track mechanisms or electric adjustment motors.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Forward collision and automatic emergency braking systems are designed to enhance occupant safety, though sensor calibration faults can trigger unprompted hard stops.

**Unexpected Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The automatic emergency braking system abruptly engages without warning or obstacles present, increasing the risk of a rear-end collision [nhtsa-complaint-11487506, nhtsa-complaint-11707903, nhtsa-complaint-11506389].
- **Solution:** Reprogramming or recalibration of radar and camera sensors by a qualified service center.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V593000: Air Bags:Knee Bolster](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=INFINITI&model=QX50&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** In the event of a crash in very cold temperatures, the trim cover may separate unexpectedly from the passenger-side knee air bag module instead of splitting at the trim cover tear seam as designed. If the passenger-side knee air bag cover detaches during deployment, it can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** INFINITI will notify owners, and retailers will replace the passenger-side knee air bag module, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=INFINITI&model=QX50&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 25V437000: Engine And Engine Cooling:Engine:Hard Parts Internal/Mechanical](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=INFINITI&model=QX50&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** Engine failure increases the risk of a crash. A bearing failure may cause a breach in the engine block, allowing hot oil to be discharged, increasing the risk of an engine fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will reprogram the engine control module (ECM) software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used model, prospective buyers should verify that all major engine recalls and powertrain updates have been fully addressed by an authorized dealer. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection focusing on the variable-compression motor, transmission responsiveness, and electronic safety suites is strongly advised to avoid costly out-of-pocket repairs.
