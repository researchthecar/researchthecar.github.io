# 2013 INFINITI M35h

### Overview
The 2013 INFINITI M35h, a luxury hybrid sedan, aimed to blend performance with fuel efficiency. It featured a sophisticated hybrid powertrain combining a V6 gasoline engine with an electric motor. While generally well-regarded for its comfortable ride and premium interior, like any vehicle, it has specific areas that potential owners should be aware of regarding long-term reliability. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential repair costs and common problems across its key systems.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 Hybrid
**Hybrid Battery Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced electric-only range, decreased overall fuel economy, potential loss of power assist.
- **Solution:** Hybrid battery pack replacement.
- **Cost:** $3,000 - $6,000

**Inverter Cooling System Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating warnings, reduced hybrid system performance, potential system shutdown.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace leaking hoses, pumps, or radiator for the inverter cooling system.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500


## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic Hybrid Transmission
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid, potential for rough shifting, overheating, or transmission failure if not addressed.
- **Solution:** Replace seals, gaskets, or transmission lines.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

**Hesitation or Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable delay or vibration during acceleration or gear changes.
- **Solution:** Software updates, transmission fluid flush and fill, or internal component repair.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1,200

## Electrical

### Hybrid System Electronics
**12V Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the vehicle, electrical system malfunctions, warning lights.
- **Solution:** Replace the 12V auxiliary battery.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsive touch controls, navigation errors.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system reset, or replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2013 INFINITI M35h is a generally reliable luxury hybrid, with its primary concerns revolving around the longevity and cost of its hybrid-specific components, particularly the hybrid battery pack and inverter cooling system. Transmission issues, while not rampant, can be costly to repair. Electrical gremlins, such as 12V battery failures and infotainment glitches, are also noted. However, the chassis, suspension, exterior, and interior components tend to hold up well. Prospective buyers should factor in the potential for significant hybrid battery replacement costs as the vehicle ages, but otherwise, it offers a solid ownership experience if well-maintained.
