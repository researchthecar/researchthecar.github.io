# 2019 INFINITI Q60 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 Infiniti Q60, buyers are treated to striking coupe styling and strong twin-turbo performance. However, owner reports indicate that prospective buyers should pay close attention to onboard electronics, advanced safety features, and basic lighting components during pre-purchase inspections. While major mechanical failures are rarely highlighted in initial owner complaints, electrical glitches can impact daily drivability and safety.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

This base 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 produces around 208 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, offering efficient cruising for daily commutes. It provides an entry point to the Q60 lineup, though shoppers seeking traditional Infiniti performance often look toward the V6 options.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6 (300 hp)

This 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 delivers 300 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, balancing strong acceleration with everyday refinement. It serves as a popular mid-tier option for buyers wanting spirited performance without stepping up to the top-tier Red Sport trim.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6 (400 hp)

Reserved for the Red Sport 400 trim, this high-output 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 pushes 400 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque. It transforms the coupe into a serious performance machine, though buyers should verify maintenance records carefully on higher-output models.

## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic Transmission

This 7-speed automatic transmission is paired across the Q60 lineup, routing power to either the rear wheels or an intelligent all-wheel-drive system. It generally delivers smooth gear changes, though prospective owners should test-shift thoroughly across all modes.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Display Screen

Owner feedback highlights potential vulnerabilities in the dual-screen infotainment setup and associated backup camera systems, which can occasionally fail or display distorted feeds.

**Blank Display Screen**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle's main display screen goes completely black while driving or at a stop, disabling rearview camera visibility.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle's infotainment software and display module inspected by an Infiniti specialist; replacement of the screen or control module may be required if persistent.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Lighting systems require standard checks to ensure all exterior bulbs and complex headlight assemblies operate reliably during night driving.

**Headlight Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights may suddenly stop working while driving at night, creating a significant visibility hazard.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight bulbs, fuses, and assembly wiring harnesses, and replace faulty components as necessary.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced driver assistance systems can occasionally exhibit erratic behavior, such as unwarranted emergency braking events based on owner reports.

**Phantom Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** The automatic emergency braking system activates independently while driving at various speeds without any vehicles or obstacles in the immediate vicinity.
- **Solution:** Check collision sensor alignment and have an authorized service center inspect the radar and camera modules for calibration or software updates.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V654000: Back Over Prevention: Sensing System: Camera](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=INFINITI&model=Q60&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." The lack of an image in the back-up camera display increases the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Infiniti Q60 remains an eye-catching luxury coupe with robust engine choices, but potential buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle's electronics, infotainment displays, and advanced driver assistance systems. Ensuring that all cameras, screens, and safety sensors operate correctly during a test drive will help prevent unexpected post-purchase headaches.
