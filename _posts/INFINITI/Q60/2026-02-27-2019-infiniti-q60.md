# 2019 INFINITI Q60

### Overview
The 2019 INFINITI Q60 is a luxury sports coupe known for its stylish design and potent performance, particularly with its V6 engine options. While generally considered a reliable vehicle, like any car, it has specific areas where owners have reported issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective buyers and current owners. We will examine the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, exterior, and interior systems to offer a comprehensive reliability picture.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6 (VR30DDTT)
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, whistling noises, oil leaks from turbo seals.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement or repair.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,500

**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Low oil level warnings, blue smoke from exhaust, engine misfires.
- **Solution:** Engine internal inspection, potential rebuild or component replacement.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $5,000+


## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic (JR710E/JR711E)
**Harsh Shifting / Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden jolts during gear changes, delayed engagement, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, software update, or transmission repair/rebuild.
- **Cost:** $300 - $2,500

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 INFINITI Q60, particularly with its powerful 3.0L twin-turbo V6 engine, offers an engaging driving experience. However, potential buyers should be aware of reported issues concerning turbocharger reliability and oil consumption, which can be costly to repair. The 7-speed automatic transmission can sometimes exhibit harsh shifting. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment system glitches and battery drain, are also noted concerns. While the suspension, exterior, and interior generally hold up well, these specific engine and electrical issues are the primary areas to scrutinize when considering a used 2019 Q60.
