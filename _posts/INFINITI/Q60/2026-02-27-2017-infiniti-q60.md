# 2017 INFINITI Q60

### Overview
The 2017 INFINITI Q60 is a luxury sports coupe that offers a blend of performance and style. While generally well-received for its design and driving dynamics, like any vehicle, it has specific areas where owners have reported issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problems with its powertrain, electrical systems, and other key components. Understanding these potential issues can help prospective buyers and current owners make informed decisions regarding maintenance and repairs.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6 (VR30DDTT)
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, whistling noises from the engine bay, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement or repair.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $5,000

**Oil Pressure Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Oil warning light illumination, potential engine damage if not addressed.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential replacement of oil pump or related sensors.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1,500


## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic (JR710E/JR711E)
**Harsh Shifting / Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable jolts or rough shifts between gears, especially at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, software update, or internal transmission repair.
- **Cost:** $300 - $2,500

**Transmission Slipping**
- **Symptoms:** Engine revs increase without corresponding acceleration, difficulty engaging gears.
- **Solution:** Internal transmission repair or replacement.
- **Cost:** $2,500 - $6,000

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, system freezes or restarts randomly.
- **Solution:** Software updates, infotainment unit reset, or replacement of the unit.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1,500


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle fails to start, battery dies prematurely.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw from electrical components, battery replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 INFINITI Q60, particularly with the potent 3.0L twin-turbo V6 engine, presents some potential reliability concerns primarily related to its turbochargers and oil pressure systems. The 7-speed automatic transmission can also experience issues such as harsh shifting or slipping, requiring attention. While the suspension, exterior, and interior generally hold up well, electrical gremlins, especially with the infotainment system and intermittent battery drain, have been noted. Owners should be aware of these potential issues and ensure regular maintenance, particularly for the powertrain, to mitigate risks and maintain optimal performance.
