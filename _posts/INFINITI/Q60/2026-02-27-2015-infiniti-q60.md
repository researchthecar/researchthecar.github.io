# 2015 INFINITI Q60

### Overview
The 2015 INFINITI Q60, a luxury sport coupe, generally offers a solid ownership experience with a focus on performance and premium features. While it shares its platform with the Nissan 370Z, INFINITI's refinements aim for a more sophisticated feel. Reliability for this model year is largely positive, with owners reporting satisfaction with its powertrain and overall build quality. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen occasional reports of issues, primarily related to certain electronic components and the adaptive suspension system in some trims. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring the longevity and optimal performance of the Q60.

### Sport Suspension (with Continuous Damping Control - CDC)
**CDC System Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warning lights related to suspension, uneven ride height, or a harsh/uncomfortable ride.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and replacement of faulty sensors, actuators, or the control module. Fluid flush and refill may also be necessary.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing/Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen becomes unresponsive, freezes, or displays errors. Navigation system glitches.
- **Solution:** Software update or reset. In some cases, replacement of the head unit may be required.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1200


### Keyless Entry/Start System
**Intermittent Operation**
- **Symptoms:** Key fob not recognized, difficulty starting the car, doors not locking/unlocking.
- **Solution:** Battery replacement in key fob, reprogramming of fobs, or inspection/replacement of the intelligent key antenna or control module.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2015 INFINITI Q60 is a reliable luxury coupe, particularly with its robust 3.7L V6 engine and well-regarded transmissions. Most owners will experience a trouble-free ownership period, with the primary concerns revolving around the optional sport suspension's electronic components and occasional glitches within the infotainment system. Electrical issues, such as with the keyless entry, are also noted but not widespread. The interior and exterior generally hold up well. Addressing potential issues promptly, especially those related to the adaptive suspension and electronics, will ensure the Q60 continues to provide a premium driving experience.
