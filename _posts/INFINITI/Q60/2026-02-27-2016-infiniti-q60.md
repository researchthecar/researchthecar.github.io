# 2016 INFINITI Q60

### Overview
The 2016 INFINITI Q60, a luxury sport coupe, generally offers a solid ownership experience with a focus on performance and premium features. While it shares its platform with the Nissan 370Z, it aims for a more refined and comfortable ride. Reliability for this model year is considered good, with most owners reporting satisfaction. However, as with any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen more attention from owners and technicians. This analysis focuses on documented mechanical and electrical concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential issues and their associated costs for the 2016 Q60.

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6 (VR30DDTT)
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil stains under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking seals or gaskets, potentially turbocharger components if affected.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, check engine light.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of turbocharger assemblies.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 INFINITI Q60 is a generally reliable luxury coupe, particularly with the naturally aspirated 3.7L V6 engine. Owners of models equipped with the newer 3.0L twin-turbo V6 should be aware of potential oil leaks and turbocharger issues, which can be costly to repair. Transmission reliability is good, though some automatic transmission models may experience occasional shifting anomalies. Electrical components, such as the infotainment system and LED headlights, have seen isolated reports of malfunctions. Overall, the Q60 offers a compelling blend of performance and luxury, with most common issues being manageable and not indicative of systemic failure across the model range.
