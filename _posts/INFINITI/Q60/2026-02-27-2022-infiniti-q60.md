# 2022 INFINITI Q60

### Overview
The 2022 INFINITI Q60 is a luxury sports coupe that offers a blend of performance and style. While generally considered a reliable vehicle, like any car, it can experience certain issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns, excluding routine maintenance and common wear-and-tear items. The Q60 is known for its potent V6 engine and engaging driving dynamics, but potential buyers should be aware of specific areas that have seen reported problems. This data aims to provide a clear picture of the Q60's technical resilience across its key systems.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6 VR30DDTT
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, whistling noises, oil leaks from turbo seals.
- **Solution:** Turbocharger replacement or repair.
- **Cost:** $2,000 - $4,500

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Overheating, visible coolant puddles under the vehicle, sweet smell.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace leaking hoses, gaskets, or water pump.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic (JR710E/JR711E)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Rough shifting, delayed engagement, or sudden jolts during gear changes.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, or transmission control module (TCM) reprogramming. In severe cases, internal transmission repair or replacement may be needed.
- **Cost:** $250 - $700 (for fluid service/reprogram), $2,500 - $5,000 (for major repair/replacement)

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Glitches/Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen unresponsive, display freezing, or intermittent system reboots.
- **Solution:** Software update for the infotainment unit, or replacement of the unit if hardware failure is confirmed.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300 (for software update), $1,000 - $2,500 (for unit replacement)


### Battery Drain
**Premature Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting, electrical components malfunctioning intermittently, battery warning light.
- **Solution:** Testing and replacement of the battery. Investigation into parasitic draw if the issue persists.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 INFINITI Q60, powered by its robust 3.0L twin-turbo V6, generally presents a solid reliability record. The most frequently cited technical concerns revolve around the turbochargers, with potential for issues like reduced power and noise, and coolant leaks that can lead to overheating. The 7-speed automatic transmission, while generally smooth, has seen reports of hesitation or jerking, often resolvable with fluid services or software updates, though more significant repairs can be costly. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system's responsiveness and occasional battery drain, are also noted. However, the suspension, exterior, and interior components are largely free from widespread reported defects. Overall, the Q60 offers a compelling performance package with manageable reliability concerns, provided potential buyers are aware of and prepared for these specific potential issues.
