The 2020 INFINITI Q60 is a luxury sports coupe known for its stylish design and powerful engine options. Available with a 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 engine in various states of tune, producing either 300 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque or 400 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque in the Red Sport trim. All trims are equipped with a 7-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the Q60 for its attractive styling and strong acceleration, particularly in the Red Sport trim. However, some criticisms include the infotainment system, which is considered dated, and the steering feel, which can lack feedback.

**Engine**

**3.0L Twin-Turbo V6 (VR30DDTT) - 300/400 HP**

*   **Turbo Lag**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Noticeable delay in power delivery when accelerating, especially at lower RPMs. The engine feels sluggish until the turbos spool up, leading to a less responsive driving experience.
    *   Solution: Tuning/ECU recalibration can sometimes reduce lag. Aftermarket turbo upgrades may also be considered.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Fuel Injector Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, misfires, decreased fuel economy, and a check engine light. Diagnosed with a scan tool revealing codes related to fuel injector malfunction.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty fuel injector(s).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Belt Tensioner Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Squealing noise coming from the engine bay, especially during startup or acceleration. The belt may appear loose or vibrating excessively.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the belt tensioner and potentially the drive belt.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Automatic**

*   **Rough Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or jerky shifts, especially between certain gears. May be more noticeable when the transmission is cold.
    *   Solution: Some owners report improvement after a transmission fluid flush and filter replacement. In more severe cases, valve body replacement or transmission rebuild may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (fluid flush) - $3000+ (rebuild)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Transmission Slipping**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine RPMs increase without a corresponding increase in vehicle speed. Hesitation or delay in acceleration, especially when trying to pass or merge onto the highway.
    *   Solution: Diagnose the cause of the slippage. Common causes include low transmission fluid, worn clutch packs, or valve body issues. Repair options range from fluid replacement to transmission rebuild or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (fluid flush) - $4000+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Lagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen freezes, becomes unresponsive, or experiences significant lag when navigating menus or using features.
    *   Solution: Software updates may resolve some issues. In more severe cases, the infotainment unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) - $1500+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Backup Camera Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Backup camera displays a distorted image, black screen, or no image at all when the vehicle is in reverse.
    *   Solution: Check the camera connections. If the connections are fine, the camera or screen may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof motor failing causing the sunroof to open or close very slowly, or not at all.
    *   Solution: Replacing the sunroof motor
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500-1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Seat Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Power seats failing to adjust forward, backwards, up, or down
    *   Solution: Replacing the seat motor
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300-$800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2020 INFINITI Q60 exhibits a mix of performance and comfort, but potential buyers should be aware of some common issues. Engine-related concerns, such as turbo lag and fuel injector failures, can affect performance and require moderate investment to resolve. Transmission issues, like rough shifting and slippage, can range from simple fluid replacements to more costly rebuilds. Electrical issues with the infotainment system and backup camera can detract from the overall driving experience. While some issues may be minor, others can be more severe and expensive to address. Pre-purchase inspections and careful consideration of vehicle history are recommended.
