# 2021 INFINITI Q60

### Overview
The 2021 INFINITI Q60 is a luxury sports coupe that offers a blend of performance and premium features. While generally considered a reliable vehicle, like any car, it has specific areas where owners have reported issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential concerns for prospective buyers and current owners. We will examine the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical systems, exterior, and interior components to offer a comprehensive reliability picture.

---


## Engine

### 3.0L Twin-Turbo V6 (VR30DDTT)
**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine power, whistling noises from the engine bay, increased oil consumption.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential replacement of turbocharger seals or the entire unit.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,500

**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil under the vehicle, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Tightening or replacing gaskets and seals, particularly around the oil pan and valve covers.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic (JR710E/JR711E)
**Hesitation/Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Rough shifting, delayed engagement, noticeable jerks during acceleration or deceleration.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement, software update, or internal transmission repair.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1,200

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 INFINITI Q60, powered by its robust 3.0L twin-turbo V6, generally offers a solid ownership experience. However, potential buyers should be aware of reported issues concerning the turbochargers, which can manifest as power loss or unusual noises, and occasional oil leaks. The 7-speed automatic transmission, while smooth for the most part, has seen some reports of hesitation or rough shifting, often addressable with maintenance or software updates. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system's responsiveness and occasional battery drain, are also noted. The suspension, exterior, and interior components have shown good resilience, with no widespread critical failures reported in these areas. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to early signs of the aforementioned issues are key to ensuring long-term reliability for the Q60.
