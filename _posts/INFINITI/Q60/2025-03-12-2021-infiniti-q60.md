Here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints for the 2021 INFINITI Q60, categorized for clarity. The 2021 INFINITI Q60 is a stylish sports coupe known for its sleek design, powerful engine options, and comfortable interior. It's available with a 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 engine in various states of tune. The base models produce 300 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, while the Red Sport 400 trim boasts 400 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque. All Q60 models are equipped with a 7-speed automatic transmission. Owners often praise the Q60's attractive styling and engaging driving dynamics. However, some common complaints revolve around the infotainment system, which can feel dated compared to competitors, and the lack of driver feedback from the steering.

**Engine**

**3.0L Twin-Turbo V6 (VR30DDTT) - 300hp & 400hp Versions**

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining noise from the engine bay, loss of power, potential check engine light. Some owners have reported premature turbo failure, especially in higher mileage vehicles or those that have been heavily modified.
    *   Solution: Turbocharger replacement. Addressing any underlying oil supply issues that may have contributed to the failure.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,500+ depending on whether one or both turbos need replacement, and labor rates.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Coolant level dropping, visible coolant leaks near the engine, potential overheating. Some owners have reported coolant leaks from the water pump or the coolant reservoir.
    *   Solution: Identifying and replacing the leaking component (water pump, reservoir, hoses, etc.).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+ depending on the component and labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**7-Speed Automatic (RE7R01A)**

*   **Rough Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Harsh or delayed shifts, particularly between certain gears. Hesitation during acceleration. Some owners have reported this issue becoming more pronounced over time.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush and fill with Nissan Matic S fluid. In some cases, a valve body replacement or transmission rebuild may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $4,000+ depending on the severity of the issue and the required repair.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Electronic Steering System Issues (DAS - Direct Adaptive Steering)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Vague or artificial steering feel, lack of feedback from the road, potential steering malfunction warning lights.
    *   Solution: Software update for the DAS system. Replacement of faulty DAS components (sensors, actuators, etc.) may be required.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+ depending on the issue and whether parts need to be replaced.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment screen freezes, becomes unresponsive, or crashes. The system may reboot itself.
    *   Solution: Software update. In some cases, the entire infotainment unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $1,500+ depending on whether the issue can be resolved with a software update or requires hardware replacement. Software updates are usually free if performed at the dealer.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Malfunctions (Parking Sensors, Blind Spot Monitoring)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: False alarms or failure to detect objects, error messages on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Inspect and clean the sensors. Replace faulty sensors.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+ per sensor, depending on location and labor.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof/Moonroof Problems**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof/moonroof may get stuck, make excessive noise during operation, or leak.
    *   Solution: Lubricate sunroof tracks. Adjust sunroof alignment. Replace faulty sunroof motor or components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+ depending on the issue and parts needed.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 INFINITI Q60, while praised for its styling and performance, can present some common issues. Engine problems like turbocharger failures can be quite costly. Transmission issues can range from minor inconveniences to more severe problems requiring significant repairs. Electronic issues with the steering and infotainment systems are common and can be frustrating. Overall, while the Q60 offers an enjoyable driving experience, potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and factor in the potential repair costs when making their purchase decision.
