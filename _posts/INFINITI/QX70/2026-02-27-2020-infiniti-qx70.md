# 2020 INFINITI QX70

### Overview
The 2020 INFINITI QX70, while a stylish and capable luxury SUV, presents a mixed bag in terms of long-term reliability. As a model that was nearing the end of its lifecycle in 2020, many of its core components were well-established. However, like many vehicles in its class, certain areas can be prone to specific issues that may arise with mileage. This analysis focuses on documented technical failures, excluding routine maintenance and normal wear and tear, to provide a clear picture of potential reliability concerns for owners and prospective buyers. Understanding these potential weak points can help in proactive maintenance and budgeting for repairs.

**Screen Glitches/Freezing**

### Battery Drain
**Premature Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery may fail prematurely, leading to starting issues. Can sometimes be related to parasitic draw from modules.
- **Solution:** Battery replacement, diagnosis of parasitic draw.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2020 INFINITI QX70 demonstrates solid mechanical reliability, with its V6 engine and automatic transmission generally performing without widespread issues. The chassis and suspension also appear robust. However, potential owners should be aware of some electrical gremlins, particularly concerning the infotainment system's responsiveness and occasional premature battery failures. These electrical issues, while not critical to the vehicle's core function, can be frustrating and incur repair costs. Overall, the QX70 is a reliable choice for its powertrain and chassis, but vigilance regarding the electrical system is advised.
