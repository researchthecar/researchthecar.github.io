**Introduction:**

The 2001 INFINITI G20 is a compact luxury sports sedan known for its agile handling, spacious interior, and premium amenities. It's powered by a 2.0-liter inline-4 engine that produces 145 horsepower and 165 lb-ft of torque when paired with the 5-speed manual transmission or 140 horsepower and 160 lb-ft of torque with the 4-speed automatic transmission.

Owners appreciate the G20's sporty performance, comfortable interior, and sophisticated styling, while some have noted concerns regarding fuel economy and reliability issues.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Inline-4**

**Valve Cover Gasket Leak**
Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the valve cover gasket, causing an unpleasant odor, smoke from the engine, and potential engine damage.
Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Front Lower Control Arms**
Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged lower control arms can result in excessive play in the suspension, uneven tire wear, and a decrease in handling stability.
Solution: Replace the lower control arms.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per side
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Alternator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: A faulty alternator can lead to battery issues, dim headlights, and other electrical problems.
Solution: Replace the alternator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
* No Issues Found

**Interior**

**Climate Control Panel Malfunction**
Description/Symptoms: The climate control panel may become unresponsive, fail to adjust temperature or airflow, or display incorrect information.
Solution: Replace the climate control panel.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2001 INFINITI G20 generally receives positive reviews for its handling and luxury features. However, it is not exempt from potential issues, primarily related to engine oil leaks, suspension wear, and electrical problems. The severity of these issues can vary, and repair costs can range from affordable to moderately expensive.
