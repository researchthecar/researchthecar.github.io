# 2011 INFINITI EX35

### Overview
The 2011 INFINITI EX35, a luxury crossover, generally presents a solid reliability record, particularly concerning its powertrain. It is equipped with a robust V6 engine known for its smooth performance and durability. While most owners report a positive ownership experience with minimal unexpected repairs, like any vehicle, certain components can experience issues over time. This analysis focuses on documented common problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for prospective buyers or current owners. The EX35's design prioritizes a blend of comfort and sportiness, and its reliability largely reflects this focus.

### General Electrical System
**Infotainment System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional freezing or unresponsiveness of the infotainment screen, navigation system errors.
- **Solution:** Software updates, system reset, or replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500

**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Premature battery failure or unexplained battery drain, potentially related to parasitic draw.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis of parasitic draw, replacement of faulty modules or battery.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2011 INFINITI EX35 is generally considered a reliable vehicle, with its VQ35HR engine and automatic transmission being particularly robust and rarely presenting significant issues. The primary areas where owners might encounter problems are within the electrical system, specifically related to infotainment glitches and potential battery drain issues. These are not typically catastrophic failures but can be inconvenient and costly to repair. Exterior and interior components, as well as the suspension, tend to hold up well, with no widespread common failures reported. Overall, the EX35 offers a dependable ownership experience, provided potential electrical quirks are addressed.
