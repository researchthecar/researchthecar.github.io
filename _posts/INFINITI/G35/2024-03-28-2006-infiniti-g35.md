**Introduction**

The 2006 INFINITI G35 is a compact luxury sedan that offers a sporty driving experience. It is available with a choice of a 3.5-liter V6 engine or a 5.6-liter V8 engine, and either a 5-speed automatic or 6-speed manual transmission. The G35 is praised for its performance, handling, and luxurious interior, but it is criticized for its stiff ride and high price.

**Engine**

**3.5-liter V6**

* **Intake manifold failure:** The intake manifold can crack, causing a loss of power and a check engine light. The fix involves replacing the intake manifold. $1,000 - $1,500+
* **Timing chain stretch:** The timing chain can stretch over time, causing a rattling noise and a loss of power. The fix involves replacing the timing chain, guides, and tensioners. $1,500 - $2,000+
* **Oil leaks:** Oil leaks can occur from various locations, including the valve covers, oil pan, and rear main seal. The fix involves replacing the leaking gaskets or seals. $200 - $600+
* **Cam sensor failure:** The camshaft position sensor can fail, causing a check engine light and a loss of power. The fix involves replacing the cam sensor. $100 - $200+

**5.6-liter V8**

* **Valve seat recession:** The valve seats can recess into the cylinder head, causing a loss of compression and a decrease in power. The fix involves replacing the cylinder head. $3,000 - $5,000+
* **Connecting rod bearing failure:** The connecting rod bearings can fail, causing a knocking noise and a loss of power. The fix involves replacing the connecting rod bearings. $2,000 - $3,000+
* **Oil consumption:** The engine may consume excessive oil, which can lead to low oil levels and engine damage. The fix involves replacing the piston rings or valve seals. $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Fuel injector failure:** The fuel injectors can fail, causing a loss of power or a rough idle. The fix involves replacing the fuel injectors. $600 - $1,000+

**Transmission**

**5-speed automatic**

* **Transmission slipping:** The transmission may slip between gears, causing a loss of power and a rough ride. The fix involves rebuilding or replacing the transmission. $2,000 - $3,000+
* **Solenoid failure:** The transmission solenoids can fail, causing a loss of power or a rough shift. The fix involves replacing the solenoids. $200 - $500+

**6-speed manual**

* **Clutch problems:** The clutch may wear out prematurely, causing difficulty shifting gears or a loss of power. The fix involves replacing the clutch. $500 - $1,000+
* **Synchronizer failure:** The synchronizers can fail, causing difficulty shifting gears. The fix involves rebuilding or replacing the transmission. $1,500 - $2,500+

**Suspension**

* **Strut failure:** The struts can fail, causing a rough ride and poor handling. The fix involves replacing the struts. $800 - $1,200+
* **Sway bar link failure:** The sway bar links can fail, causing a clunking noise and poor handling. The fix involves replacing the sway bar links. $200 - $400+
* **Ball joint failure:** The ball joints can fail, causing a clunking noise and poor handling. The fix involves replacing the ball joints. $300 - $600+

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** The battery may drain prematurely, causing the vehicle to lose power. The fix involves replacing the battery or repairing the electrical system. $100 - $600+
* **Alternator failure:** The alternator can fail, causing the battery to lose power. The fix involves replacing the alternator. $500 - $800+
* **Starter failure:** The starter can fail, preventing the engine from starting. The fix involves replacing the starter. $300 - $500+
* **BCM failure:** The body control module (BCM) can fail, causing a variety of electrical problems, such as inoperative windows, door locks, or lights. The fix involves replacing the BCM. $500 - $1,000+

**Exterior**

* **Headlight condensation:** The headlights may accumulate condensation, causing poor visibility at night. The fix involves resealing the headlights. $200 - $400+
* **Taillight failure:** The taillights may fail, reducing visibility at night. The fix involves replacing the taillights. $100 - $200+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard rattle:** The dashboard may rattle over bumps, causing a buzzing or rattling noise. The fix involves tightening or replacing the dashboard components. $200 - $500+
* **HVAC problems:** The heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) system may fail, causing poor heating or cooling. The fix involves repairing or replacing the HVAC components. $300 - $1,000+
* **Seat heater failure:** The seat heaters may fail to heat up, providing no warmth during cold weather. The fix involves replacing the seat heater elements. $200 - $400+

**Summary**

The 2006 INFINITI G35 has a number of potential issues, including engine, transmission, suspension, electrical, and interior problems. The severity of the issues varies, but some, such as valve seat recession and connecting rod bearing failure, can be quite costly to repair. Overall, the G35 is a reliable vehicle, but it is not without its potential problems.
