# 2007 INFINITI QX56

### Overview
The 2007 INFINITI QX56, a full-size luxury SUV, is generally considered a robust vehicle, particularly for its powertrain. Built on a sturdy platform, it offers a comfortable ride and ample power for towing and daily driving. However, like any vehicle of this age and class, certain areas may require attention. This analysis focuses on common reported issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential reliability concerns for owners and prospective buyers. The focus is on specific components and their typical failure points, offering estimated repair costs and solutions.

---


## Engine

### 5.6L VK56DE V8
**Timing Chain Rattle**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup or at idle.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
- **Cost:** $1,200 - $2,500

**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil leaks from valve covers, oil pan, or front crankshaft seal.
- **Solution:** Replacement of gaskets, seals, or crankshaft seal.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic (RE5R05A)
**Harsh Shifting / Delayed Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** Jerking or rough shifts between gears, delayed engagement when shifting into drive or reverse.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid and filter change, solenoid replacement, or full transmission rebuild.
- **Cost:** $500 - $3,500


## Suspension

### Standard Chassis
**Air Suspension Compressor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle sagging on one side, compressor not engaging, 'Service Suspension System' warning light.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
- **Cost:** $700 - $1,500

**Worn Ball Joints / Tie Rod Ends**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints and/or tie rod ends.
- **Cost:** $400 - $900


## Electrical

### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Electrical Gremlins**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical components malfunctioning randomly (e.g., power windows, interior lights, infotainment system).
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1,200


### Alternator
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light illuminated, dimming headlights, electrical system issues.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900


## Exterior

### Standard Body
**Rear Hatch Struts**
- **Symptoms:** Rear hatch not staying open or closing unexpectedly.
- **Solution:** Replacement of rear hatch support struts.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


## Interior

### Infotainment System
**Navigation System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Navigation screen freezing, not loading maps, or inaccurate GPS location.
- **Solution:** Software update or replacement of the navigation unit.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1,000


### Climate Control
**Blend Door Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Inability to control temperature or airflow direction, clicking noises from dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blend door actuator(s).
- **Cost:** $300 - $600


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2007 INFINITI QX56 is a capable and generally reliable large SUV, with its V8 engine and robust transmission being strong points. The most common issues tend to be related to the air suspension system, electrical components like the alternator and BCM, and some interior electronics such as the navigation system. Addressing potential timing chain noise and oil leaks early can prevent more significant engine problems. While some repairs can be costly, particularly transmission or suspension work, proactive maintenance and addressing minor issues promptly can ensure the longevity and continued enjoyment of this luxurious and powerful vehicle.
