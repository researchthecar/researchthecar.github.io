# 2012 INFINITI QX56

### Overview
The 2012 INFINITI QX56, a full-size luxury SUV, is generally regarded as a robust vehicle with a strong emphasis on comfort and capability. It shares its platform with the Nissan Armada, benefiting from a proven powertrain. While it offers a commanding presence and a well-appointed interior, like any vehicle, it has specific areas where owners have reported issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for prospective buyers or current owners.

---


## Engine

### 5.6L V8 VK56VD
**Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Excessive oil burning, potentially leading to low oil levels and engine damage if not monitored.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis may involve checking PCV system, piston rings, or valve seals. Repairs can be extensive.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000+

**Timing Chain Rattle**
- **Symptoms:** Rattling or knocking noise from the front of the engine, especially on startup.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing chain, tensioners, and guides.
- **Cost:** $1000 - $2500+


## Transmission

### 7-Speed Automatic (RE7R01A)
**Harsh Shifting / Delayed Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky gear changes, hesitation when shifting into drive or reverse.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid and filter change, solenoid replacement, or valve body repair.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500+


## Suspension

### Hydraulic Body Motion Control (HBMC)
**Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking hydraulic fluid from suspension lines or actuators, potentially causing a rougher ride.
- **Solution:** Replacement of leaking lines, seals, or actuators.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1200+

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Malfunctions / Freezing**
- **Symptoms:** Navigation screen or other infotainment displays freezing, becoming unresponsive, or showing errors.
- **Solution:** Software updates, or replacement of the head unit.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1000+


### Body Control Module (BCM)
**Intermittent Electrical Gremlins**
- **Symptoms:** Various electrical components (lights, power windows, locks) acting erratically or failing intermittently.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis and potential reprogramming or replacement of the BCM.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500+

### Summary Verdict
The 2012 INFINITI QX56 is a capable luxury SUV, but potential buyers should be aware of a few common technical concerns. The 5.6L V8 engine can experience oil consumption and timing chain noise, which can be costly to repair. The 7-speed automatic transmission may develop issues with harsh shifting. For models equipped with the Hydraulic Body Motion Control (HBMC) suspension, fluid leaks are a possibility. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system and Body Control Module, can also arise. While many owners report satisfaction, these specific areas warrant careful inspection and consideration during purchase or ownership.
