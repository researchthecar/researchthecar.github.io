# 2013 INFINITI QX56

### Overview
The 2013 INFINITI QX56, a full-size luxury SUV, is generally regarded as a robust and reliable vehicle, particularly given its size and capabilities. It shares its platform with the Nissan Armada, benefiting from proven Nissan engineering. The QX56 is known for its powerful V8 engine and comfortable, well-appointed interior, making it a strong contender for families or those needing substantial towing and passenger capacity. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the QX56 tends to perform well in terms of long-term durability, with most reported problems being relatively minor or related to age and mileage rather than inherent design flaws. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring its continued reliability.

## Suspension

### Independent Front Suspension / Multi-link Rear Suspension
**Hydraulic Body Motion Control (HBMC) Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Fluid leaks from HBMC system, potential loss of active suspension control, clunking noises.
- **Solution:** Repair or replace leaking HBMC lines, actuators, or pump.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500


## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Navigation System Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Screen freezing, unresponsiveness, inaccurate GPS location.
- **Solution:** Software update, system reset, or replacement of head unit.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1200


### Battery Drain
**Intermittent Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Dead battery after sitting for a short period, warning lights on dashboard.
- **Solution:** Diagnose parasitic draw from modules or accessories, replace faulty component.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

**Dual Climate Control Malfunction**

### Summary Verdict
The 2013 INFINITI QX56 demonstrates strong overall reliability, with its core powertrain components (engine and transmission) showing minimal reported issues. The most common areas for potential concern revolve around the advanced Hydraulic Body Motion Control (HBMC) system, which can develop leaks, and some electrical gremlins such as infotainment system glitches and intermittent battery drain. Interior climate control issues are also occasionally noted. Despite these points, the QX56 is a dependable large SUV, and addressing these potential problems proactively can ensure a positive ownership experience. Its robust build and powerful V8 engine contribute to its reputation for durability.
