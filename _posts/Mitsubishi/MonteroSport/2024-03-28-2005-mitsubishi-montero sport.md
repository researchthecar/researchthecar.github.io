**Introduction:**
The 2005 Mitsubishi Montero Sport is a mid-size SUV that offers a rugged design and capable off-road performance. It comes with a standard 3.8L V6 engine that produces 215 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque. The Montero Sport also has an available 3.0L V6 engine with 173 horsepower and 203 lb-ft of torque. Both engines can be paired with either a 4-speed automatic transmission or a 5-speed manual transmission. Owners appreciate the Montero Sport's spacious interior, off-road capabilities, and comfortable ride quality. However, some have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, and other components.

**Engine**
**3.8L V6**
**Timing Belt Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The timing belt may break or wear out prematurely, leading to significant engine damage and costly repairs. Symptoms include a rattling noise from the engine, difficulty starting, and reduced engine power.
Solution: Replace the timing belt, timing chain, and tensioners.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

**3.0L V6**
**No Issues Found**

**Transmission**
**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**
**Transmission Slipping**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip or hesitate when shifting gears, making acceleration difficult. This can also lead to increased fuel consumption and decreased performance.
Solution: Repair or replace the transmission.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**
**Leaking Clutch**
Description/Symptoms: The clutch may leak fluid, causing difficulty shifting gears and a burning smell.
Solution: Replace the clutch.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
**Front Suspension Springs**
Description/Symptoms: The front suspension springs may weaken or break, resulting in a lowered ride height and poor handling.
Solution: Replace the front suspension springs.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
**Battery**
Description/Symptoms: The battery may fail prematurely, leaving the vehicle unable to start. Symptoms include dimming headlights, electrical system malfunctions, and difficulty starting.
Solution: Replace the battery.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
**No Issues Found**

**Interior**
**HVAC System**
Description/Symptoms: The HVAC system may not circulate air properly, leading to ineffective heating or cooling. Symptoms include weak airflow, uneven cabin temperatures, and strange noises.
Solution: Inspect and repair or replace the HVAC system components, such as the blower motor, evaporator core, or control unit.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**
The 2005 Mitsubishi Montero Sport is a capable SUV with potential problems such as timing belt failure (3.8L V6), transmission slipping (4-speed automatic), and a leaking clutch (5-speed manual). Some electrical issues and HVAC system malfunctions have also been reported. The severity and cost of these issues vary, so it's important to have regular maintenance and inspections performed to address any concerns promptly.
