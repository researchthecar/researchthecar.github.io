The 2000 Mitsubishi Montero Sport is a mid-size SUV known for its off-road capability and relatively affordable price point. Engine options included a 2.4L inline-4, a 3.0L V6, and a 3.5L V6. The 3.0L V6 produced around 173 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque. Drivers often appreciate the Montero Sport for its ruggedness and truck-like feel, while common complaints revolve around fuel economy and some reliability concerns, particularly with age.

**Engine**

**3.0L V6**

* **Oil Leaks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from valve cover gaskets, oil pan gasket, and rear main seal are common as the vehicle ages. You may notice oil spots under the vehicle or a burning smell.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking gasket or seal.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+ depending on the location and severity of the leak.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Timing Belt Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing belt is a critical component and, if not replaced according to schedule, can break. This can cause significant engine damage, including bent valves.
    * Solution: Replace the timing belt and water pump at the recommended interval (typically 60,000 miles). If the belt breaks, extensive engine repair or replacement is required.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800 for timing belt and water pump replacement. Engine repair after belt failure can cost $1,500+.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Automatic Transmission**

*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report harsh or erratic shifting, especially between gears. This can be caused by worn-out transmission fluid, a faulty shift solenoid, or internal damage.
    *   Solution: Start by checking and replacing the transmission fluid. If the problem persists, further diagnosis and repair may be needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$200 for fluid change. $200-$600+ for shift solenoid replacement or more for internal repairs.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Ball Joint Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The lower ball joints are prone to wear and failure. Symptoms include clunking noises from the front suspension, loose steering, and uneven tire wear.
    * Solution: Replace the worn ball joints. It's often recommended to replace both upper and lower ball joints at the same time.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+ per side, depending on parts and labor.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Sway Bar Link Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sway bar links are also common wear items. Owners report clunking noises when going over bumps.
    * Solution: Replace the sway bar links.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ depending on labor costs.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Power Window Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Power windows can fail, often due to a faulty window motor or switch.
    * Solution: Replace the faulty motor or switch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ per window.
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: The alternator can fail, leading to a dead battery and inability to start the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rust is common, especially in areas with road salt. Common spots include the rocker panels, wheel wells, and undercarriage.
    * Solution: Repairing rust can be expensive and may involve cutting out and welding in new metal. Prevention is key.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies greatly depending on severity, from $200 for small repairs to $1000+ for significant rust.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **No Issues Found**

**Summary**

The 2000 Mitsubishi Montero Sport, while appreciated for its ruggedness, presents several common issues that potential buyers should be aware of. Engine and transmission problems, particularly oil leaks and harsh shifting, can lead to moderate repair costs. Suspension issues such as ball joint failure and sway bar link issues contribute to safety and drivability concerns. Electrical gremlins and rust formation also contribute to the overall ownership experience. While some fixes are relatively inexpensive, others can become quite costly, making a thorough inspection essential before purchasing this vehicle.
