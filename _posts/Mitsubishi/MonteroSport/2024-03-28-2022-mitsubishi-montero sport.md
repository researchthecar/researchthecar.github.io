**Introduction:**

The 2022 Mitsubishi Montero Sport is a mid-size SUV that offers a combination of rugged capability and everyday comfort. It is available with a single engine option: a 3.0L V6, paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. The Montero Sport is known for its spacious interior, off-road performance, and comfortable ride, but it also has some drawbacks, including limited towing capacity and fuel economy.

**Engine**

**3.0L V6**

* **Timing chain issue:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The timing chain on some models can stretch or break, resulting in engine damage and costly repairs. Symptoms include rattling noises from the engine, reduced power, and potentially a check engine light.
    * Solution: Replacing the timing chain and related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $4,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 22V274
* **Oil leaks:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks can occur from various gaskets and seals in the engine. Symptoms include a visible oil leak under the vehicle, low oil levels, and a burning oil smell.
    * Solution: Replacing the affected gaskets or seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **Intake manifold issues:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold on some models can develop cracks or leaks, leading to engine performance problems, such as rough idling or loss of power.
    * Solution: Repairing or replacing the intake manifold.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+

**Transmission**

**8-speed Automatic**

* **Transmission slipping:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip or hesitate when shifting gears, causing a loss of power or acceleration.
    * Solution: Repairing or replacing the transmission clutches or other components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
* **Transmission fluid leaks:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission fluid leaks can occur from various seals or gaskets. Symptoms include a visible fluid leak under the vehicle, low transmission fluid levels, and difficulty shifting gears.
    * Solution: Replacing the affected seals or gaskets.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

**Suspension**

* **Front strut mount failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The front strut mounts can prematurely fail, leading to a clunking or rattling noise when driving over bumps or uneven surfaces.
    * Solution: Replacing the strut mounts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
* **Rear leaf spring corrosion:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear leaf springs can develop corrosion, which can weaken the suspension and lead to a rough ride or potential failure.
    * Solution: Replacing the affected leaf springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The battery can drain prematurely, leading to difficulty starting the vehicle.
    * Solution: Identifying and addressing the underlying electrical issue, such as a parasitic draw or faulty component.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the cause

**Exterior**

* **Rear bumper scuffing:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The rear bumper can easily get scuffed or scratched when loading or unloading cargo.
    * Solution: Installing a rear bumper protector or applying touch-up paint.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+

**Interior**

* **Climate control issues:**
    * Description/Symptoms: The climate control system may not function properly, such as not blowing cold or hot air.
    * Solution: Diagnosing and repairing the faulty component, such as the compressor or blower motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **Dashboard rattles:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some interior panels or components can rattle or vibrate, particularly over rough roads.
    * Solution: Identifying and fixing the source of the rattle, such as tightening screws or applying sound-dampening material.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Summary:**

The 2022 Mitsubishi Montero Sport has a mix of serious and minor issues. While the engine and transmission are generally reliable, potential timing chain issues and transmission slipping are important to consider. The suspension components are prone to some corrosion and premature failure. Electrical issues and interior rattles are relatively common but typically not major concerns. Overall, the severity and cost of the issues vary, and each individual vehicle's experience may differ.
