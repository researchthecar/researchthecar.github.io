## 2019 Mitsubishi Montero Sport: Common Issues

The 2019 Mitsubishi Montero Sport is a mid-size SUV powered by a 3.0-liter V6 engine mated to an 8-speed automatic transmission. With 215 horsepower and 209 lb-ft of torque, the Montero Sport offers a spacious interior, off-road capabilities, and a comfortable ride. However, like many vehicles, the Montero Sport has experienced some issues.

### Engine

#### 3.0-liter V6

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
Description/Symptoms: Abnormal oil consumption, resulting in frequent oil level checks and top-ups.
Solution: Replace piston rings and engine seals.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
Recalls: None Found

### Transmission

#### 8-Speed Automatic

**Shifting Hesitation**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission hesitates or delays when shifting gears, especially during acceleration or deceleration.
Solution: Update transmission control unit (TCU) software and perform transmission fluid exchange.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

### Suspension

**Rear Leaf Spring Squeaking**
Description/Symptoms: Squeaking or creaking noise coming from the rear leaf springs, especially when driving over uneven surfaces.
Solution: Lubricate or replace leaf springs.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
Recalls: None Found

### Electrical

**Faulty Alternator**
Description/Symptoms: Battery fails to charge or discharges quickly, leading to starting problems or electrical system failures.
Solution: Replace alternator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

### Exterior

**Fog Light Condensation**
Description/Symptoms: Condensation or moisture buildup inside the fog lights, impairing visibility.
Solution: Replace fog light assemblies with improved seals.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

### Interior

**Windshield Wiper Motor Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Windshield wipers stop working intermittently or fail to operate altogether.
Solution: Replace wiper motor.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

### Summary

Overall, the 2019 Mitsubishi Montero Sport has received mixed reviews regarding reliability. The reported issues range from minor annoyances to more significant problems that may require costly repairs. Owners have also expressed concerns about excessive oil consumption and transmission issues with the V6 engine and 8-speed automatic transmission. While some issues have been addressed through recalls and software updates, others remain unresolved, making it important for prospective buyers to be aware of these potential problems before purchasing.
