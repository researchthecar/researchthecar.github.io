The 2011 Mitsubishi Montero Sport is a mid-size SUV known for its ruggedness and off-road capabilities. It comes with a 3.0-liter V6 engine rated at 192 horsepower and 207 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. With its powerful engine and capable four-wheel drive system, the Montero Sport offers impressive performance on various terrains. However, like any vehicle, it has some known issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

**3.0L V6**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Issue:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Affects vehicles produced before 2019. Can cause engine misfires, rough idling, and decreased fuel efficiency.
    * Solution: Replacing the intake manifold gasket is necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Occurs in older, high-mileage vehicles. May result in engine rattling, reduced power, and eventually engine failure.
    * Solution: Replacing the timing chain and tensioner is essential to prevent further damage.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Slippage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Occurs during gear changes and can lead to delayed acceleration, hesitation, and jerking.
    * Solution: Transmission fluid flush and filter replacement may resolve the issue. In severe cases, transmission rebuild or replacement might be necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Bearing Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Affects vehicles with high mileage. Can cause clunking noises over bumps and reduced steering stability.
    * Solution: Replacing the strut mount bearings is necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800 per axle
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Leaf Spring Breakage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Occurs in older vehicles or those subjected to heavy loads. Can result in a sagging rear end and reduced stability.
    * Solution: Replacing the broken leaf springs with upgraded or reinforced ones is recommended.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Affects vehicles with faulty alternator or battery. Can lead to a dead battery and difficulty starting the vehicle.
    * Solution: Testing and replacing the alternator or battery is necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical Gremlins:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Various electrical issues have been reported, including malfunctioning gauges, intermittent lights, and power window problems.
    * Solution: Identifying the specific electrical fault and repairing or replacing the affected components is required.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust and Corrosion:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Can occur on the undercarriage, frame, or body panels. Moisture and road salts accelerate corrosion.
    * Solution: Regular cleaning, rustproofing, and touch-up paint can help prevent or slow down the issue.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of damage.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattle:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Affects vehicles with loose or poorly fitted dashboard components. Can produce annoying rattles and squeaks.
    * Solution: Identifying the source of the rattle and tightening or replacing the affected parts is necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2011 Mitsubishi Montero Sport is a capable SUV with its powerful engine and rugged construction. However, it has some known issues that owners should be aware of, including engine misfires, transmission slippage, suspension problems, and electrical gremlins. While some of these issues are minor and can be resolved with relatively low costs, others can be more severe and require substantial repairs. Therefore, it is crucial to consider these potential issues before purchasing a used Montero Sport and factor them into the overall ownership cost.
