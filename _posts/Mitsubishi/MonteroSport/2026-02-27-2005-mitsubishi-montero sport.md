# 2005 Mitsubishi Montero Sport

### Overview
The 2005 Mitsubishi Montero Sport, a body-on-frame SUV, offered a blend of rugged capability and practicality. Known for its off-road prowess and durable construction, it appealed to those seeking an adventurous vehicle. While generally considered reliable, like any vehicle of its age, certain components can experience issues. This analysis focuses on documented common problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and prospective buyers. The data presented aims to offer a clear picture of the Montero Sport's technical reliability.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (6G74)
**Intake Manifold Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, engine misfires, rough idle, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Timing Belt Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden engine shutdown, no start, significant internal engine damage if belt breaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing belt, tensioners, and idler pulleys. If failure occurred, engine rebuild or replacement may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1200 (preventative), $3000+ (post-failure)


### 3.8L V6 (6G75)
**Oil Leaks (Valve Covers/Oil Pan)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil drips, burning oil smell, low oil level.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets or oil pan gasket.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500

**Coolant Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Low coolant level, visible coolant puddles, engine overheating, sweet smell.
- **Solution:** Inspection and repair/replacement of hoses, radiator, water pump, or head gaskets.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1500+


## Transmission

### 4-Speed Automatic (4G64)
**Slipping Gears**
- **Symptoms:** Delayed engagement, engine revving without acceleration, harsh shifting.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid and filter change, solenoid replacement, or full transmission rebuild.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800 (fluid/filter), $800 - $2500+ (rebuild)


### 5-Speed Automatic (4G64/6G74/6G75)
**Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Jerky gear changes, delayed shifts, transmission warning light.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush, sensor replacement, or transmission control module (TCM) issues.
- **Cost:** $200 - $700


### 5-Speed Manual
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises.
- **Solution:** Clutch kit replacement (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $600 - $1000


## Suspension

### Independent Front Suspension / Solid Rear Axle
**Worn Ball Joints and Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, poor handling, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints and suspension bushings.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

**Leaking Shock Absorbers/Struts**
- **Symptoms:** Bouncy ride, reduced handling stability, visible fluid leaks on components.
- **Solution:** Replacement of shock absorbers or struts.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, dead battery, electrical system malfunctions.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Power Window Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Windows not operating, slow window operation.
- **Solution:** Replacement of individual power window motors.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400 per window


## Exterior

### Body and Trim
**Rust on Frame and Body Panels**
- **Symptoms:** Visible corrosion, bubbling paint, structural weakness.
- **Solution:** Rust treatment, panel replacement, or frame repair (depending on severity).
- **Cost:** $500 - $3000+

**Headlight Lens Hazing/Yellowing**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced headlight visibility, aesthetic degradation.
- **Solution:** Headlight restoration kit or replacement of headlight assemblies.
- **Cost:** $50 - $200 (restoration), $300 - $600 (replacement)


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air coming from vents, or air only on high setting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the HVAC blower motor and resistor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

**Seat Fabric Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Tearing, fraying, or excessive wear on seat upholstery.
- **Solution:** Seat cover installation or professional upholstery repair/replacement.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500+


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2005 Mitsubishi Montero Sport is a robust SUV with a generally good reliability record, particularly for its powertrain when maintained properly. Common issues tend to be related to age and mileage, such as suspension wear, minor oil and coolant leaks from V6 engines, and potential electrical component failures like alternators or power window motors. Rust can be a concern in certain climates. Addressing issues like timing belt replacement proactively is crucial for engine longevity. Overall, with diligent maintenance and attention to common problem areas, the Montero Sport can remain a dependable vehicle.
