# 2007 Mitsubishi Montero Sport

### Overview
The 2007 Mitsubishi Montero Sport, a capable mid-size SUV, generally presents a respectable reliability record for its era. While not without its potential issues, many owners report a durable vehicle when properly maintained. Common areas of concern tend to be related to the powertrain and some electrical components, though these are not universally experienced. The robust chassis and off-road prowess are often highlighted as strengths. This analysis focuses on documented common faults beyond standard maintenance, providing insights into potential repair costs and solutions for owners.

---


## Engine

### 3.5L V6 (6G74)
**Intake Manifold Gasket Leak**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant loss, engine misfires, rough idle, sweet smell from exhaust.
- **Solution:** Replacement of intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Timing Belt Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Sudden engine stoppage, no start, potential catastrophic engine damage if belt breaks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of timing belt, tensioners, and water pump.
- **Cost:** $600 - $1000


### 3.8L V6 (6G75)
**Oil Leaks (Valve Cover Gaskets)**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil on engine block, burning oil smell, potential for oil to drip onto exhaust manifold.
- **Solution:** Replacement of valve cover gaskets.
- **Cost:** $300 - $550


## Transmission

### 4-Speed Automatic (4G64)
**Slipping Gears**
- **Symptoms:** Delayed engagement, engine revs without vehicle acceleration, harsh shifting.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid and filter change, potential rebuild or replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $2500+


### 5-Speed Automatic (6G74/6G75)
**Harsh Shifting**
- **Symptoms:** Jerking or clunking during gear changes, especially between 2nd and 3rd.
- **Solution:** Transmission fluid flush and relearn procedure, potential solenoid replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600


## Suspension

### Independent Front Suspension / Multi-link Rear
**Worn Ball Joints / Control Arm Bushings**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, vague steering, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints and/or control arm bushings.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Power Window Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Window not operating, slow movement, grinding noise.
- **Solution:** Replacement of power window motor and regulator assembly.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400

**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light illuminated, difficulty starting, dead battery.
- **Solution:** Replacement of alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700


## Exterior

### Body and Trim
**Rear Hatch Strut Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rear hatch not staying open, difficulty lifting.
- **Solution:** Replacement of rear hatch support struts.
- **Cost:** $100 - $200


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Resistor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Fan only works on highest setting, or not at all.
- **Solution:** Replacement of blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $100 - $250


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Mitsubishi Montero Sport offers a generally robust platform, but like many vehicles of its age, it is susceptible to specific component failures. The V6 engines, while powerful, can experience issues like intake manifold leaks or, more critically, timing belt problems if maintenance is neglected. Automatic transmissions may develop shifting issues. Suspension components, particularly ball joints and bushings, are common wear items that can lead to noise and handling problems. Electrical gremlins, such as failing power window motors and alternators, are also reported. Addressing these potential issues proactively, especially regular maintenance of the timing belt and transmission fluid, can significantly enhance the long-term reliability of the Montero Sport.
