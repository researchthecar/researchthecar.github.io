**Introduction**

The 2009 Mitsubishi Montero Sport is a mid-size SUV powered by a 3.8L V6 engine paired with a 6-speed automatic transmission. It delivers 215 horsepower and 250 lb-ft of torque. The Montero Sport is praised for its spacious interior, impressive towing capacity, and off-road capabilities. However, there are some reported issues that potential owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

3.8L V6 Engine

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Engine burns excessive oil, leading to low oil levels and engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace piston rings, valve seals, or the entire engine.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $5,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

6-Speed Automatic Transmission

* **Erratic Shifting:** Transmission hesitates, slips, or jerks during gear changes.
    * Solution: Replace solenoids, valve body, or the entire transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Leaking Shock Absorbers:** Shock absorbers leak fluid, causing reduced ride quality and handling issues.
    * Solution: Replace shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

* **Sagging Rear Springs:** Rear springs sag, affecting the vehicle's stance and handling.
    * Solution: Replace rear springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Battery drains excessively while parked, causing difficulty starting the vehicle.
    * Solution: Replace the battery, alternator, or starter motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+

* **Electrical Shorts:** Electrical components experience shorts, resulting in flickering lights, faulty gauges, or even engine stalls.
    * Solution: Repair or replace faulty wiring, switches, or sensors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Exterior**

* **Rusty Frame:** The frame can develop rust, especially in areas exposed to road salt or moisture.
    * Solution: Apply rustproofing treatment, or replace the frame.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $5,000+

* **Cracked Headlights:** Headlights can crack or develop condensation, affecting visibility.
    * Solution: Replace headlights.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Interior**

* **Fading Instrument Panel:** Instrument panel can fade or discolor over time.
    * Solution: Replace the instrument panel.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Summary**

The 2009 Mitsubishi Montero Sport has some reported issues that can be costly to repair. The severity and frequency of these problems vary, and not all Montero Sports will experience them. It is recommended to have the vehicle inspected by a qualified mechanic before purchasing and to keep up with regular maintenance to minimize the risk of future problems.
