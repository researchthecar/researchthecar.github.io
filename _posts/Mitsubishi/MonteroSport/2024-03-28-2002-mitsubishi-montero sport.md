**Introduction:**

The 2002 Mitsubishi Montero Sport is a mid-size SUV that is powered by a 3.5L V6 engine that produces 197 horsepower and 235 lb-ft of torque. The Montero Sport was available with a 4-speed automatic transmission or a 5-speed manual transmission. It received positive reviews for its off-road capabilities, spacious interior, and reliable engine. However, it also has some notable issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine**

* **3.5L V6 Engine:**
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, engine misfires
        * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **4-Speed Automatic Transmission:**
    * **Transmission Slippage:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, slipping out of gear
        * Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **5-Speed Manual Transmission:**
    * **No major issues found**

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension:**
    * **Lower Ball Joint Failure:**
        * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises, steering instability
        * Solution: Replace ball joints
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Suspension:**
    * **No major issues found**

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, battery discharge
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Front Bumper Cracking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracks or breaks in the front bumper
    * Solution: Replace front bumper
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Instrument Cluster Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Gauges or warning lights not working
    * Solution: Repair or replace instrument cluster
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2002 Mitsubishi Montero Sport is a capable SUV with some notable issues. The most severe issues are related to the transmission, which can be expensive to repair or replace. The other issues are generally less severe and less costly to fix. Overall, the Montero Sport is a reliable vehicle, but it's important to be aware of its potential problems before purchasing one.
