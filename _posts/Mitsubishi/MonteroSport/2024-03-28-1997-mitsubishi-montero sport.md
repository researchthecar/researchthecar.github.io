**Introduction:**

The 1997 Mitsubishi Montero Sport is a mid-size SUV that offers rugged off-road capabilities thanks to its powerful engine options and sophisticated 4WD system. It comes with two engine choices: a 3.0L V6 producing 173 horsepower and 188 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.5L V6 delivering 190 horsepower and 220 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 4-speed automatic and a 5-speed manual. Despite its off-road prowess, the Montero Sport also offers a comfortable and well-equipped cabin. However, like any vehicle, it has its share of reported issues.

**Engine**

* **3.0L V6**
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure:** Leaks coolant and causes overheating. Requires replacement of the intake manifold gasket. $500 - $800+
    * **EGR valve clogging:** Reduced power and increased emissions. Cleaning or replacing the EGR valve is necessary. $200 - $400+
* **3.5L V6**
    * **Head gasket failure:** Leaks coolant and oil into the combustion chambers. Requires replacement of the head gasket. $1000 - $2000+
    * **Timing belt failure:** Can cause catastrophic engine damage if not replaced at the recommended intervals. Replacement of the timing belt and related components is essential. $500 - $1000+

**Transmission**

* **4-speed automatic**
    * **Slipping gears:** Hesitation or failure to engage gears. May require rebuilding or replacement of the transmission. $1500 - $3000+
* **5-speed manual**
    * **Grinding noises:** Difficulty shifting gears. Replacement of worn clutch components or transmission synchronization may be necessary. $500 - $1200+

**Suspension**

* **Front lower control arms:** Premature wear or failure can lead to poor handling and tire alignment issues. Replacing the control arms and related components is recommended. $600 - $1000+
* **Rear leaf springs:** Sagging or breakage can affect ride quality and load-carrying capabilities. Replacement of the leaf springs is necessary. $400 - $800+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:** Loss of electrical power and battery drain. Replacement of the alternator is required. $500 - $800+
* **Starter motor problems:** Difficulty starting the engine. Replacement of the starter motor may be necessary. $300 - $600+

**Exterior**

* **Rust on the frame and body:** Premature rusting can compromise the structural integrity of the vehicle. Rustproofing treatment or replacement of affected parts may be required. $500 - $2000+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracking:** Aging and sun exposure can cause the dashboard to crack. Replacement of the dashboard is typically required. $500 - $1000+
* **Air conditioning problems:** Malfunctions can result in poor cooling or heating. Repairs or replacement of AC components may be necessary. $300 - $1000+

**Recalls:**

* None Found

**Summary:**

The 1997 Mitsubishi Montero Sport offers a combination of off-road capabilities and comfort, but it is not immune to issues. Common problems include engine gaskets and timing belts, transmission slipping or grinding, suspension component wear, electrical failures, and rust. Some of these issues can be costly to repair, especially if they are not addressed promptly. Overall, the severity and cost of the potential issues should be carefully considered before purchasing or owning a Montero Sport.
