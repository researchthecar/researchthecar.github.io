# 2018 Mitsubishi L200

### Overview
The 2018 Mitsubishi L200, also known as the Triton in some markets, is a robust and capable pickup truck designed for both work and leisure. It is generally regarded as a reliable vehicle, particularly in its core mechanical components. Mitsubishi's long history with pickup trucks means the L200 benefits from proven engineering and durable construction. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the L200 typically performs well in terms of long-term reliability, with owners often praising its sturdy build and ability to withstand demanding conditions. Common areas of attention, if any, tend to be minor and related to specific component wear rather than systemic design flaws. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond standard maintenance.

---


## Engine

### 2.4L 4D56 Turbo Diesel
**Glow Plug Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting in cold weather, rough idling, engine warning light illumination.
- **Solution:** Replacement of faulty glow plugs.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, black smoke from the exhaust.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential repair or replacement of the turbocharger.
- **Cost:** $800 - $2000


### 2.5L 4D56 Turbo Diesel
**DPF (Diesel Particulate Filter) Clogging**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced engine performance, increased fuel consumption, DPF warning light.
- **Solution:** Forced regeneration or DPF cleaning/replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1200


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty engaging gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Clutch kit replacement (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $500 - $1000


### 5-Speed Automatic
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Leaking fluid under the vehicle, rough shifting, overheating.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of seals and gaskets, transmission fluid flush.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting after sitting, dimming lights, electrical components malfunctioning intermittently.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis to find parasitic draw, repair or replace faulty component, battery check.
- **Cost:** $100 - $500

**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery warning light illuminated, dimming headlights, electrical issues, engine stalling.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 Mitsubishi L200 demonstrates strong reliability, especially concerning its powertrain and chassis. The diesel engines, while generally robust, can experience issues like DPF clogging or turbocharger problems, particularly if not maintained or driven appropriately for diesel operation. Transmission reliability is good across both manual and automatic options, with clutch wear being a common, albeit expected, maintenance item for manuals. Electrical gremlins, such as battery drain or alternator failure, are not uncommon in vehicles of this age and type but are typically repairable. Suspension components like ball joints may require attention over time. Overall, the L200 is a dependable workhorse with a solid reputation for durability, provided regular maintenance is performed.
