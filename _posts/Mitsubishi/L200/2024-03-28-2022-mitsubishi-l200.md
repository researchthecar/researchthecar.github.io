**Introduction:**

The 2022 Mitsubishi L200 is a rugged and versatile pickup truck known for its off-road capabilities. It comes standard with a 2.4-liter diesel engine that produces 129 hp and 241 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options include a 2.5-liter diesel engine with 143 hp and 324 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.8-liter gasoline engine with 257 hp and 269 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 6-speed manual, a 6-speed automatic, and a 5-speed automatic.

Owners praise the L200 for its impressive off-road performance, reliable engine, and spacious interior. However, they also report some issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**2.4-Liter Diesel Engine**

* **Fuel Injector Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, loss of power, rough idling
    * Solution: Replace fuel injectors
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Turbocharger Issues:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke from exhaust, whistling sound
    * Solution: Replace turbocharger
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1200 - $2000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.5-Liter Diesel Engine**

* **EGR Valve Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine stalls, reduced fuel efficiency, rough idling
    * Solution: Replace EGR valve
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Head Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, white smoke from exhaust
    * Solution: Replace head gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $2000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Gear Grinding:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noise
    * Solution: Replace gears or synchronizers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Slippage:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vehicle hesitates or slips when shifting gears
    * Solution: Replace transmission filter and fluid, or overhaul transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rear end of vehicle sags, bumpy ride, handling issues
    * Solution: Replace leaf springs
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Electrical Short:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Blown fuses, flickering lights, battery drain
    * Solution: Repair or replace damaged wiring
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: AC or heater not working properly
    * Solution: Repair or replace climate control module
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Sunroof Leak:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into cabin through sunroof
    * Solution: Replace or repair sunroof seals
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2022 Mitsubishi L200 is a reliable and capable pickup truck. However, it has some potential issues that can be costly to repair. The most severe issues include head gasket failure, turbocharger problems, and transmission slippage. These issues can impact vehicle performance and safety, and should be addressed promptly. Other issues, such as fuel injector failure, EGR valve failure, and sunroof leaks, are less severe but can still be inconvenient and expensive to fix.
