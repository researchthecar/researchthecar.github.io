**Introduction:**

The 2020 Mitsubishi L200 is a mid-size pickup truck available in both single and double cab configurations. It features a 2.4L diesel engine paired with either a 6-speed manual or 6-speed automatic transmission. The L200 boasts a payload capacity of 1,110 kg and a towing capacity of 3,100 kg. It is known for its ruggedness, off-road capabilities, and affordable price point. However, there are some reported issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

* **2.4L Diesel Engine**
    * **Excessive Oil Consumption**
        * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption requiring frequent top-ups.
        * Solution: Piston ring replacement.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **Difficult Gear Shifting**
        * Description/Symptoms: Hardness in shifting gears, particularly into reverse.
        * Solution: Replacement of gear linkage components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Rear Leaf Springs**
    * **Sagging**
        * Description/Symptoms: Loss of suspension performance due to sagging rear leaf springs.
        * Solution: Replacement of leaf springs.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: Electrical system malfunction, battery not charging properly.
    * Solution: Alternator replacement.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Latch Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Tailgate not opening or closing properly due to faulty latch.
    * Solution: Replacement of tailgate latch.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Panel Cracks**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cracking or warping of dashboard panel.
    * Solution: Replacement of dashboard panel.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

While the Mitsubishi L200 offers value and capability, it is not without its issues. The most severe issues are the excessive oil consumption and difficulty shifting gears. The cost of these repairs can be significant, ranging from $1,000 to over $2,000. Other issues like sagging rear leaf springs, electrical problems, tailgate latch issues, and dashboard panel cracks are less severe but can still be costly to fix. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these reported issues and factor in the potential cost of repairs when considering the L200.
