## 2016 Mitsubishi L200

The 2016 Mitsubishi L200 is a mid-size pickup truck that offers a combination of ruggedness and practicality. Available in both single and double-cab configurations, it comes equipped with a 2.4-liter turbodiesel engine producing 181 horsepower and 317 lb-ft of torque. Other engine options include a 2.5-liter turbodiesel with 178 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.2-liter turbodiesel with 200 horsepower and 317 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual, a 6-speed manual, and a 5-speed automatic.

Owners of the 2016 Mitsubishi L200 generally praise its off-road capabilities, spacious cabin, and reliable performance. However, some have reported issues with the vehicle's engine, suspension, and electrical systems.

**Engine**

**2.4-Liter Turbodiesel**

* **Turbocharger Failure:** Failure of the turbocharger can lead to reduced engine power, excessive smoke, and increased oil consumption. Requires replacement of the turbocharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
* **Fuel Injector Malfunction:** Faulty fuel injectors can cause hard starting, rough idling, and decreased fuel efficiency. Requires replacement of the affected fuel injectors. Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+

**2.5-Liter Turbodiesel**

* **EGR Valve Problems:** Issues with the EGR valve can result in reduced engine power, increased emissions, and illuminated check engine light. Requires cleaning or replacement of the EGR valve. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**3.2-Liter Turbodiesel**

* **Intake Manifold Cracking:** Cracking of the intake manifold can cause air leaks, engine misfires, and poor performance. Requires replacement of the intake manifold. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,400+

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Gear Grinding:** Difficult or noisy shifting can indicate gear grinding. Requires inspection and possible replacement of worn gears or synchronizers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+

**6-Speed Manual**

* **Transmission Mount Failure:** Broken or worn transmission mounts can cause excessive vibration and noise. Requires replacement of the affected mounts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**5-Speed Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Shudder:** Vibrations when shifting gears can be a sign of torque converter shudder. Requires replacement of the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,800+
* **Transmission Fluid Leaks:** Leaks from the transmission can lead to reduced fluid levels and transmission damage. Requires inspection and repair of the leak source. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+

**Suspension**

* **Leaf Spring Failures:** Broken or cracked leaf springs can result in a rough ride, reduced handling, and uneven tire wear. Requires replacement of the affected leaf springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+ per side
* **Shock Absorber Wear:** Worn shock absorbers can lead to a bouncy ride, reduced stability, and increased braking distances. Requires replacement of the affected shock absorbers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per axle

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** A faulty alternator can prevent proper battery charging, leading to electrical system failures. Requires replacement of the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $900+
* **Wiring Harness Issues:** Electrical shorts or open circuits in the wiring harness can cause various problems, including malfunctioning lights, gauges, or other electrical components. Requires inspection and repair of the affected wiring. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
* **Battery Drain:** Excessive battery drain can be caused by parasitic draws or faulty electrical components. Requires inspection and repair of the drain source. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $400+

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Rust:** Corrosion of the tailgate can lead to structural weakness and cosmetic issues. Requires repair or replacement of the tailgate. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,200+

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Plastic Cracking:** Cracking of the dashboard plastic can occur due to exposure to heat or UV radiation. Requires replacement of the affected dashboard panel. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
* **Climate Control Malfunctions:** Issues with the climate control system can include ineffective heating or cooling, or malfunctioning fan speeds. Requires inspection and repair of the affected components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+

**Summary**

The 2016 Mitsubishi L200 is a capable and reliable pickup truck, but it is not without its fair share of issues. While some reported problems are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others can be more severe and costly. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues and factor in the potential repair expenses when considering purchasing this vehicle.
