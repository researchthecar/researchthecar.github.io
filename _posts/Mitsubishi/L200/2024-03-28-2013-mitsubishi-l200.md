**2013 Mitsubishi L200 Vehicle Overview**

The 2013 Mitsubishi L200 is a mid-size pickup truck that offers versatility and ruggedness for both personal and commercial use. It is powered by a 2.5-liter inline-four diesel engine that produces 178 horsepower and 317 lb-ft of torque. The L200 is available with both a 6-speed manual and a 5-speed automatic transmission. It has a payload capacity of up to 2,200 lbs and a towing capacity of up to 6,600 lbs.

Owners appreciate the L200's off-road capabilities, spacious cabin, and low ownership costs. However, some have encountered issues with its engine, transmission, and other components.

**Engine**

* **2.5L Inline-Four Diesel**
    * **Turbocharger Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke, whistling or rattling noises. Solution: Replace turbocharger. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800+. Recalls: None Found.
    * **Injector Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Rough idling, poor fuel economy, black smoke from exhaust. Solution: Replace faulty injectors. Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $900+. Recalls: None Found.

**Transmission**

* **6-Speed Manual**
    * **Synchro Issues:** Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises. Solution: Replace synchronizer rings or assembly. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+. Recalls: None Found.
* **5-Speed Automatic**
    * **Transmission Slipping:** Description/Symptoms: Loss of power during acceleration, delayed shifting. Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+. Recalls: None Found.

**Suspension**

* **Rear Leaf Springs:** Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, decreased ride quality. Solution: Replace leaf springs. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+. Recalls: None Found.
* **Front Control Arms:** Description/Symptoms: Clunking or knocking noises during turns, poor handling. Solution: Replace faulty control arms. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+. Recalls: None Found.

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Dimming lights, weak battery, charging system warning light. Solution: Replace alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $800+. Recalls: None Found.
* **Battery Issues:** Description/Symptoms: Electrical system malfunctions, difficulty starting the vehicle. Solution: Replace faulty battery. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250+. Recalls: None Found.

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Condensation:** Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside headlights. Solution: Replace headlights. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+. Recalls: None Found.
* **Tailgate Corrosion:** Description/Symptoms: Rust and corrosion on tailgate panel. Solution: Replace or repair tailgate. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+. Recalls: None Found.

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattle:** Description/Symptoms: Noises or vibrations from the dashboard. Solution: Tighten or replace loose components. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+. Recalls: None Found.
* **Power Window Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Windows not operating or intermittent operation. Solution: Replace window motor or switch. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+. Recalls: None Found.

**Summary**

The 2013 Mitsubishi L200 has mixed reviews among owners. While it offers a capable and budget-friendly option for a mid-size pickup truck, it is not without its issues. The engine and transmission are prone to some significant and costly failures. Other issues, such as suspension and electrical problems, may arise as well. Overall, the severity and expense of these issues can significantly impact the L200's ownership experience.
