# 2022 Mitsubishi L200

### Overview
The 2022 Mitsubishi L200, also known as the Triton in some markets, is a robust and capable pickup truck designed for both work and leisure. It generally exhibits good reliability, a hallmark of Mitsubishi's engineering. Owners often praise its durability and off-road prowess. While no vehicle is entirely immune to issues, the L200 typically performs well, with most reported problems being minor or specific to certain configurations. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability, excluding routine maintenance and common wear-and-tear items, to provide a clear picture of potential mechanical and electrical concerns across its various engine and transmission options.

---


## Engine

### 2.4L MIVEC Turbo Diesel (4N15)
**Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Regeneration Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated DPF warning light, reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption, potential engine stalling.
- **Solution:** Forced DPF regeneration via diagnostic tool, or manual cleaning/replacement of the DPF if heavily clogged.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800 (cleaning/regeneration), $1000 - $2500 (replacement)

**Turbocharger Actuator Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, 'check engine' light, whistling or whining noises from the turbo.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger actuator or the entire turbocharger assembly.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500


## Transmission

### 6-Speed Automatic
**Hesitation or Jerking during gear changes**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable delays or abruptness when shifting between gears, especially at lower speeds.
- **Solution:** Software update for the transmission control module (TCM), fluid flush and replacement, or internal transmission repair if necessary.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400 (software/fluid), $1500 - $3500 (internal repair)

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Mitsubishi L200 demonstrates a strong reliability record, particularly concerning its core mechanical components. The 2.4L turbo diesel engine, while generally dependable, can occasionally encounter issues with its Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) regeneration system or turbocharger actuator, especially under specific driving conditions. The automatic transmission may sometimes exhibit minor shifting anomalies, often rectifiable with software updates or fluid maintenance. Manual transmissions and the vehicle's suspension, electrical systems, exterior, and interior components are largely free from widespread, significant reliability concerns. Overall, the L200 is a solid choice for those seeking a durable and dependable pickup truck, with most potential issues being manageable and not indicative of systemic flaws.
