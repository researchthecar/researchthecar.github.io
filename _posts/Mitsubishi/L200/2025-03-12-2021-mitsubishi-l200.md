The 2021 Mitsubishi L200 is a popular pickup truck known for its ruggedness, practicality, and off-road capabilities. It's often praised for its comfortable ride, spacious cabin, and strong towing capacity. However, some owners have reported issues with various components. Engine options include a 2.4L Turbo Diesel engine producing around 178 horsepower and 317 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.5L diesel engine, with power outputs varying depending on the market. Transmission options usually consist of a 6-speed manual and a 6-speed automatic. Some common complaints involve build quality issues.

**Engine**

**2.4L Turbo Diesel**

* **Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: DPF clogging frequently, leading to reduced engine performance, increased fuel consumption, and warning lights on the dashboard. Often occurs with short trips and city driving where the DPF cannot regenerate properly.
    * Solution: Forced DPF regeneration by a mechanic, changing driving habits to include longer trips at higher speeds, or DPF replacement in severe cases.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

* **EGR Valve Problems**
    * Description/Symptoms: The Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) valve may become clogged with carbon deposits, causing rough idling, poor acceleration, and engine stalling.
    * Solution: Cleaning or replacing the EGR valve.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**2.5L Diesel**
* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Automatic**

* **Rough Shifting**
    * Description/Symptoms: Harsh or jerky gear changes, especially between lower gears. May also experience hesitation when accelerating.
    * Solution: Transmission fluid flush and replacement, software update (if available), or valve body repair/replacement in severe cases.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**6-Speed Manual**
*   **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Rear Leaf Spring Squeaking**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive squeaking noise coming from the rear leaf springs, especially when driving over bumps or uneven surfaces.
    * Solution: Lubricating the leaf springs with a silicone-based lubricant, or replacing the leaf spring bushings.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    * Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may freeze, crash, or become unresponsive. Can also experience issues with Bluetooth connectivity.
    * Solution: Performing a system reset, updating the software (if available), or replacing the head unit.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (reset) - $1000+ (replacement)
    * Recalls: None Found

* **Sensor Malfunctions (ABS, TPMS)**
    * Description/Symptoms: Faulty ABS (Anti-lock Braking System) or TPMS (Tire Pressure Monitoring System) sensors, triggering warning lights on the dashboard.
    * Solution: Replacing the faulty sensor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ per sensor
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Undercarriage**
    * Description/Symptoms: Premature rust development on the vehicle's undercarriage, especially in areas with harsh winters or coastal environments.
    * Solution: Applying rustproofing treatments, cleaning and repairing affected areas, or replacing corroded components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $1000+ (depending on severity)
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattles**
    * Description/Symptoms: Rattling or buzzing noises coming from the dashboard, particularly on rough roads.
    * Solution: Identifying the source of the rattle and applying foam padding or felt to dampen the noise.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (DIY) - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2021 Mitsubishi L200, while generally reliable, can experience a range of issues. The most common problems appear to relate to the DPF in the 2.4L turbo diesel engine, the infotainment system, and suspension noises. Electrical issues and rust are also concerns. The severity of these issues varies, with some being relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, while others, such as DPF replacement or major transmission repairs, can be more costly. Prospective buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle, pay attention to any warning lights or unusual noises, and consider having a mechanic perform a pre-purchase inspection.
