# 2021 Mitsubishi Outlander Sport Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used compact crossover, evaluating common owner feedback helps highlight areas needing closer inspection during a test drive. While many drivers enjoy daily commuting in this affordable utility vehicle, owner reports submitted to federal safety regulators point to specific mechanical and electrical areas to watch out for, particularly concerning powertrain performance and cabin electronics.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces 148 horsepower and 145 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for basic commuting. It generally offers straightforward mechanical reliability, though routine maintenance of ignition and fuel systems is essential.

**Engine Stalling and Misfires**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected engine stalling while driving, accompanied by random cylinder misfires and erratic revving.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis typically involves checking ignition components, fuel delivery systems, and engine control modules for fault codes.

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine

The optional 2.4-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine generates 168 horsepower and 167 lb-ft of torque, offering slightly stronger acceleration for highway passing. It shares a similar mechanical architecture to the base engine, benefiting from regular fluid and filter changes.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Equipped with a continuously variable transmission paired with front-wheel drive or all-wheel drive, this setup prioritizes fuel efficiency. Buyers should carefully test drive the transmission across various speeds to ensure smooth operation without hesitation or slipping.

**CVT Hesitation and Limp Mode**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note transmission slipping, failure to accelerate properly past moderate speeds, flashing gear indicators, and sudden entry into limp mode.
- **Solution:** Repairs may range from software updates and fluid services to complete transmission replacement in severe cases.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System

Cabin electronics manage connectivity and convenience features, though touchscreen glitches have been reported by early owners.

**Touchscreen and Infotainment Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen display becomes completely unresponsive or unusable, hindering access to navigation, audio controls, and smartphone integration.
- **Solution:** Software resets or replacing the infotainment head unit may be required to restore full functionality.

## Safety

### Advanced Driver Assistance Systems

Active safety features provide added peace of mind on the road, but sensor calibration issues can occasionally lead to false alerts.

**Unintended Collision Warning Activation**
- **Symptoms:** The forward collision warning system may trigger falsely, occasionally causing the vehicle to unexpectedly lose power and slow down.
- **Solution:** Dealer inspection and recalibration of radar and camera sensors can help address erratic safety system behaviors.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V563000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander+Sport&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the CVT-ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

When considering a used model, conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection can save you from unexpected repair bills. Pay close attention to how the transmission performs during acceleration and verify that all infotainment and safety sensors operate correctly before finalizing your purchase.
