# 2017 Mitsubishi Outlander Sport

### Overview
The 2017 Mitsubishi Outlander Sport is a compact SUV known for its value and practicality. Reliability data suggests a generally solid performance for its model year, with most reported issues being relatively minor or specific to certain components rather than widespread systemic failures. Owners often praise its straightforward operation and decent fuel economy. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen more attention from repair shops, particularly concerning the powertrain and some electrical components. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential long-term ownership considerations.

**Oil Leaks**

- **Solution:** Replacement of seals or gaskets (e.g., valve cover gasket, oil pan gasket).
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may hesitate during acceleration or exhibit jerky movements.
- **Solution:** Software update for the transmission control module, or in severe cases, transmission fluid flush or replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $800

**Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission warning light illuminated, reduced performance when hot.
- **Solution:** Inspection and potential repair or replacement of transmission cooler, or transmission fluid service.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1000

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Mitsubishi Outlander Sport generally presents a reliable profile, especially with the 2.0L engine option. The 2.4L engine has seen some reports of oil leaks, which are typically manageable repairs. The CVT transmission, while common in this segment, can occasionally exhibit issues like hesitation or overheating, necessitating attention. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system and occasional battery drain, are also noted but not universally prevalent. Overall, for its price point, the Outlander Sport offers a reasonable level of dependability, with most potential issues being addressable without extreme cost, provided they are caught early.
