**Introduction:**

The 2021 Mitsubishi Outlander Sport is a compact crossover SUV known for its affordability, fuel efficiency, and spacious interior. It offers a 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). The vehicle boasts 168 horsepower and 167 lb-ft of torque. Owners appreciate its comfortable ride, user-friendly technology features, and ample cargo space. However, it has received some criticism for its lackluster performance and outdated design.

**No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

**Suspension**

* **Front Lower Control Arms**
Description/Symptoms: Premature wear in the ball joints, leading to clunking or rattling noises, excessive vibration, and difficulty maintaining alignment.
Solution: Replacement of the front lower control arms, typically including the ball joints.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

* **Rear Sway Bar Links**
Description/Symptoms: Worn or broken sway bar links can cause excessive body roll during cornering, unstable handling, and rattling noises.
Solution: Replacement of the rear sway bar links.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery**
Description/Symptoms: Premature failure of the battery, resulting in difficulty starting the vehicle, electrical system malfunctions, or a loss of power.
Solution: Replacement of the battery.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
Recalls: None Found

* **Alternator**
Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning alternator can lead to undercharging or overcharging the battery, causing electrical system issues, dimming lights, or difficulty starting the vehicle.
Solution: Replacement of the alternator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlights**
Description/Symptoms: Condensation or fogging inside the headlights, reducing visibility and potentially leading to electrical issues.
Solution: Replacement of the affected headlights.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+ per headlight
Recalls: None Found

* **Door Handles**
Description/Symptoms: Sticking or malfunctioning door handles can make it difficult to open the doors, especially in cold weather.
Solution: Replacement of the affected door handles.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200 per door handle
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control System**
Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning climate control system can result in insufficient heating or cooling, fan speed issues, or temperature control problems.
Solution: Diagnosis and repair or replacement of the affected components, such as the blower motor, evaporator, or condenser.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

* **Infotainment System**
Description/Symptoms: Glitches or freezing of the infotainment system, affecting navigation, audio, or other functionalities.
Solution: Software updates or replacement of the infotainment module.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 2021 Mitsubishi Outlander Sport has received relatively few complaints about major issues. While some owners have experienced problems with the suspension, electrical system, or exterior components, these issues are generally not widespread and can vary in severity and cost to repair. The engine and transmission have not reported many significant issues. However, potential buyers should be aware of the possibility of encountering these problems and factor them into their decision-making.
