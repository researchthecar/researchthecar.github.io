# 2018 Mitsubishi Outlander Sport Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Mitsubishi Outlander Sport offers an affordable entry point into the compact crossover segment, but prospective buyers should review its historical complaint record carefully. While many owners enjoy its everyday usability and warranty coverage, owner reports filed with safety agencies highlight potential problem areas ranging from CVT transmission failures to electrical glitches and airbag warning lights. Performing a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help uncover lingering mechanical or electrical issues before signing on the dotted line.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine produces 148 horsepower and 145 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting. Overall engine reliability is generally solid, though occasional timing or sensor faults can surface.

**Variable Valve Timing Code**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated check engine light and stored permanent fault codes related to the intake VVT system.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the VVT solenoid and actuator components, and replace faulty parts or clear oil passages as needed.

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine

The available 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine bumps output to 168 horsepower and 167 lb-ft of torque for punchier acceleration. It shares a similar mechanical architecture to the base engine with reasonable long-term durability.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The continuously variable transmission routes power to either the front wheels or an available all-wheel-drive system. CVT longevity varies significantly, making a pre-purchase test drive and fluid check vital.

**Transmission Slippage and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated transmission warning lights, sudden loss of forward acceleration, surging, or complete internal component failure.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified transmission specialist inspect the unit; severe internal mechanical wear typically requires rebuilding or replacing the transmission.

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission

A 5-speed manual transmission was available on select base trims, offering a more direct mechanical connection. Manual gearboxes are generally robust and lack the complex hydraulic failure modes of automatic units.

## Brakes

### ABS and Wheel Speed Sensors

Brake system complaints are minimal, though wheel speed sensors can occasionally break during adjacent suspension or hub repairs.

**Fractured ABS Speed Sensor**
- **Symptoms:** ABS or stability control warning lights appearing on the dashboard, often discovered during routine hub or bearing service.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged wheel speed sensor and clean the surrounding hub mounting surfaces.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Starting System

electrical issues in the cabin and lighting systems can occasionally pop up, requiring careful diagnostic work by an auto electrician.

**Push-Button Start and Power Failures**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warning messages indicating electrical failure, clicking sounds behind the dashboard, or sudden loss of vehicle electronics while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect the push-button start switch, battery terminals, main ignition relays, and grounding points for corrosion or loose connections.

**Headlight Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Both low beam and high beam headlights failing simultaneously while driving at night.
- **Solution:** Check the headlight bulbs, fuses, and multi-function switch assembly for electrical faults.

## Safety

### Airbags and Seat Belts

Safety system alerts, particularly concerning airbag warning lights and seat belt hardware, require immediate professional attention to ensure proper occupant protection.

**Persistent Airbag Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** Airbag warning symbol remains illuminated on the instrument cluster dashboard.
- **Solution:** Scan the Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) module for diagnostic trouble codes and replace faulty sensors, wiring harnesses, or clock springs.

**Seat Belt Retractor Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** Center seat belt fails to retract properly, rendering it loose and unusable.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat belt assembly and replace the faulty retractor mechanism.

**[Recall 18V620000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander+Sport&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The resulting rapid deceleration of the vehicle can increase the risk of a rear-end collision.
- **What to do:** MMNA will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the FCM-ECU software with updated software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V621000: Forward Collision Avoidance: Adaptive Cruise Control](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander+Sport&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the safety systems do not function as intended, it can increase the risk of a crash.

## Summary Verdict

When shopping for a used 2018 Mitsubishi Outlander Sport, prioritize vehicles with comprehensive service records and a healthy transmission. Pay close attention to the operation of the CVT, verify that all dashboard warning lights clear properly during startup, and check that electrical accessories and headlights function as intended. A dedicated pre-purchase mechanical inspection will go a long way toward ensuring a reliable ownership experience.
