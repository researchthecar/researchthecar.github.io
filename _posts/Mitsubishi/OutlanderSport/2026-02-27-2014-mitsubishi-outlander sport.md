# 2014 Mitsubishi Outlander Sport

### Overview
The 2014 Mitsubishi Outlander Sport, a compact crossover, generally presents a decent reliability record for its class. While not without its quirks, most owners report a satisfactory ownership experience with fewer major mechanical concerns compared to some competitors. The vehicle's straightforward design contributes to its overall dependability. However, like any vehicle, specific components can experience issues, particularly as mileage increases. This analysis focuses on documented common problems beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for prospective buyers and current owners.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** The CVT may exhibit hesitation during acceleration or feel jerky when shifting between simulated gears.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the transmission control module, or in severe cases, transmission fluid flush or replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

**Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Under heavy load or prolonged use, the CVT can overheat, leading to reduced performance or warning lights.
- **Solution:** Ensure proper CVT fluid levels and condition. In some cases, an auxiliary transmission cooler may be beneficial.
- **Cost:** $200 - $600

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Bluetooth Connectivity Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty pairing devices or intermittent loss of Bluetooth connection.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the infotainment system, or resetting the system.
- **Cost:** $50 - $200


### Battery
**Premature Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The battery may fail sooner than expected, leading to starting problems.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the battery.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2014 Mitsubishi Outlander Sport is generally considered a reliable vehicle, especially for its price point. The powertrain, particularly the 2.0L engine, is robust. The main areas to be mindful of are the CVT transmission, which can occasionally exhibit hesitation or overheating issues, and some minor electrical gremlins like Bluetooth connectivity problems or premature battery failure. Exterior and interior components are largely free from widespread complaints, though headlight condensation has been noted. Overall, with regular maintenance, the Outlander Sport should provide dependable service, but potential buyers should be aware of the specific transmission concerns.
