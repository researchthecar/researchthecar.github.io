# 2020 Mitsubishi Outlander Sport Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a pre-owned 2020 Mitsubishi Outlander Sport, prospective buyers will find a budget-friendly compact crossover that offers decent everyday utility, though owner complaints highlight a few recurring pain points. Most concerns center around electrical functionality, powertrain hesitation, and minor interior usability issues. Reviewing these known problem areas can help you inspect a prospective vehicle more thoroughly before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces 148 horsepower and 145 lb-ft of torque, providing basic commuter performance. While mechanically simple, owners have occasionally noted hesitation and ignition component failures.

**Engine Hesitation and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported occasional acceleration hesitation, brief engine hesitation while underway, or intermittent stalling accompanied by an illuminated check engine light.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the ignition system, throttle body, and diagnostic trouble codes to pinpoint faulty coils or sensors.

**Ignition Coil and Misfire Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Recurring ignition coil failures can cause engine misfires, catalyst-efficiency faults, and intermittent loss of power during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Replace failing ignition coils and spark plugs, and check the catalytic converter for secondary damage.

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine

The available 2.4-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine generates 168 horsepower and 167 lb-ft of torque, offering slightly stronger acceleration for highway driving. It shares a similar overall reliability profile to the base 2.0L powerplant.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The automatic CVT routes power to either the front wheels or the available all-wheel-drive system. Routine fluid maintenance is essential to ensure long-term durability and smooth operation.

**Powertrain Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle experiences momentary power drops or near-stalling sensations while driving at steady speeds.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition and software calibration; consult a specialist if mechanical slipping is suspected.

## Electrical

### Infotainment Touchscreen

Electrical complaints for this model frequently involve software stability and touchscreen display failures that can distract the driver.

**Unresponsive Display Screen**
- **Symptoms:** The main infotainment touchscreen freezes or becomes entirely unresponsive, preventing use of Bluetooth hands-free functions and vehicle settings.
- **Solution:** Software updates may temporarily resolve glitches, but hardware digitizer failure often requires replacing the head unit.
- **Cost:** $3,000.00

## Safety

### Rear Seat Belts

Cabin safety features generally perform as expected, though rear passenger seat belt ergonomics have drawn notable owner feedback.

**Hidden or Short Seat Belt Buckles**
- **Symptoms:** Rear passenger seat belt buckles are positioned deep within the seat cushion or feel too short, making them difficult for adult passengers to locate and fasten.
- **Solution:** Ensure belt webbing is fully pulled out and un-jammed from the seat base; aftermarket extensions may be considered if approved.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V563000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander+Sport&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the CVT-ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Mitsubishi Outlander Sport offers an affordable entry point into the compact SUV market with acceptable powertrain choices. However, buyers should thoroughly test the infotainment electronics, check for any engine hesitation or misfires during a test drive, and verify that all seat belts are easily accessible. A pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic is recommended to catch any lingering electrical or ignition issues.
