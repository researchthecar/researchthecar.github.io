# 2019 Mitsubishi Outlander Sport Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2019 Mitsubishi Outlander Sport is a compact crossover that offers budget-friendly styling and an attractive warranty for used-car shoppers. However, prospective buyers should carefully evaluate powertrain options and known mechanical grievances reported by owners. While many drivers appreciate its utility and pricing, reviewing specific component performance—particularly concerning the continuously variable transmission and electrical systems—is essential before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine produces approximately 148 horsepower and 145 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate fuel economy for daily commuting, though some owners report hesitation and stalling issues.

**Engine Stalling and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle suddenly stalls while driving, accompanied by erratic battery warning lights or sluggish acceleration.
- **Solution:** Diagnose the charging/fuel system and replace faulty sensors or alternators as needed.

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine

The available 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine bumps output to 168 horsepower and 167 lb-ft of torque, offering slightly more eager acceleration for highway passing and heavier loads.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Paired with front-wheel drive or all-wheel control systems, the CVT handles power delivery for both engine options. Owners should verify regular fluid maintenance has been performed to promote long-term durability.

**Transmission Overheating and Shuddering**
- **Symptoms:** Dashboard warning messages regarding transmission overheating, sudden loss of motive power, or violent RPM surges and hesitation.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition, update control modules, or service/replace transmission components if mechanical failure occurs.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes and Parking Brake

Brake components generally provide stopping power as expected, but sticking calipers and parking brake module faults have been noted by owners.

**Sticking Calipers and Parking Brake Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Rear brake calipers sticking constantly, unusual rattling noises from front brakes over bumps, or the electronic parking brake locking up or losing tension.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace worn brake calipers, slide pins, or the parking brake switch/module.
- **Cost:** $400 for part replacement

## Safety

### Seat Belts and Air Bags

Safety equipment is critical for occupant protection; buyers should verify that all seat belts and airbag readiness indicators operate correctly during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Seat Belt Latch and Air Bag Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Seat belts failing to lock properly or airbags failing to deploy during severe impacts.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt assemblies and airbag control modules, replacing malfunctioning hardware immediately.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V563000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander+Sport&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An engine stall can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the CVT-ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2019 Mitsubishi Outlander Sport can be an affordable entry point into the compact SUV market, but careful vetting is recommended. Prospective buyers should prioritize a thorough test drive to check for CVT overheating symptoms, engine hesitation, and proper brake operation. Ensuring a comprehensive pre-purchase mechanical inspection will help mitigate the risk of encountering unexpected repair bills.
