# 2016 Mitsubishi Outlander Sport

### Overview
The 2016 Mitsubishi Outlander Sport is a compact SUV known for its value and practicality. Reliability data suggests a generally solid performance, with most reported issues being relatively minor and not widespread across the model. Owners often praise its fuel efficiency and maneuverability, especially in urban environments. However, like many vehicles in its class, some specific components have seen occasional reports of concern, particularly related to the powertrain and certain electrical systems. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond standard maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of attention for owners and prospective buyers.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may hesitate during acceleration or exhibit jerky movements, especially at lower speeds or when shifting between simulated gears.
- **Solution:** Software update for the transmission control module, or in severe cases, transmission fluid flush and replacement. Internal component repair or replacement may be necessary.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1500

**Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission warning light may illuminate, accompanied by a burning smell or reduced performance. This is more common in heavy-duty use or hot climates.
- **Solution:** Check and top off transmission fluid. Ensure proper cooling system function. Transmission cooler may need cleaning or replacement. Internal transmission repair might be required.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1200

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Screen Freezing or Unresponsive**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen display may freeze, become unresponsive to touch inputs, or randomly reboot.
- **Solution:** Software update for the infotainment system. Battery terminal cleaning or replacement if voltage issues are suspected. Unit replacement if hardware failure occurs.
- **Cost:** $150 - $800


### Battery
**Premature Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the vehicle, dimming headlights, or electrical components malfunctioning intermittently.
- **Solution:** Test battery and charging system. Replace battery if found to be faulty. Ensure all connections are clean and secure.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300

### Sunroof
**Leaking**
- **Symptoms:** Water ingress around the sunroof seal, especially during heavy rain or car washes, leading to interior dampness.
- **Solution:** Clean sunroof drains. Inspect and reseal or replace sunroof weatherstripping. Check for cracks in the glass or frame.
- **Cost:** $100 - $400

### HVAC System
**Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Lack of airflow from vents, or intermittent operation of the fan at different speeds.
- **Solution:** Replace blower motor resistor or the blower motor itself. Check cabin air filter for obstructions.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Mitsubishi Outlander Sport generally presents a reliable profile, with most owners not encountering significant mechanical or electrical failures. The powertrain, particularly the 2.0L and 2.4L engines, appears robust. The CVT transmission, while generally dependable, has seen some reports of hesitation or overheating under specific conditions, suggesting that regular maintenance and mindful driving are beneficial. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment system glitches and occasional battery issues, are present but not endemic. Exterior and interior components are largely free of widespread complaints. Overall, the Outlander Sport is a sensible choice for budget-conscious buyers seeking a dependable compact SUV, provided routine checks are performed, especially on the CVT.
