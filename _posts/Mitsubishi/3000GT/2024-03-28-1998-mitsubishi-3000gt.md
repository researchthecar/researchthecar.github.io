**Introduction:**

The 1998 Mitsubishi 3000GT is a high-performance sports car powered by a twin-turbocharged 3.0L V6 engine. It boasts an impressive output of 320 horsepower and 315 lb-ft of torque. The 3000GT was available with a 5-speed manual or a 4-speed automatic transmission. While praised for its sleek design and exhilarating performance, the 3000GT is not without its fair share of issues.

**Engine**

**3.0L V6 Twin Turbo**

* **Blown Head Gaskets:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, white smoke from exhaust
    * Solution: Replace head gaskets
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vacuum leaks, rough idle, decreased power
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
* **Timing Belt Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sudden engine failure, catastrophic internal engine damage
    * Solution: Replace timing belt and associated components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Synchros Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Solenoid Malfunctions:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission slipping or not engaging properly
    * Solution: Replace solenoids
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Torque Converter Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, overheating
    * Solution: Replace torque converter
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Noises from suspension, poor handling
    * Solution: Replace strut mounts
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800
* **Control Arm Bushings Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking or squeaking sounds, poor alignment
    * Solution: Replace control arm bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Loss of electrical power, charging system warning light
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
* **Starter Motor Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine fails to start
    * Solution: Replace starter motor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Rear Quarter Panels:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Corrosion and bubbling paint on rear quarter panels
    * Solution: Bodywork and repainting
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**Interior**

* **Instrument Cluster Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Gauges not working properly
    * Solution: Replace instrument cluster
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000

**Summary:**

The 1998 Mitsubishi 3000GT is a thrilling sports car with a potent engine and impressive handling. However, it is not without its problems. The engine is prone to head gasket and timing belt failures, while both transmissions can experience issues. Suspension components wear prematurely, leading to handling and noise problems. Electrical issues like alternator and starter failure are also common. Additionally, the exterior can rust on the rear quarter panels, and the instrument cluster may fail. The severity and cost of these issues can vary, making it important for potential buyers to be aware of the potential pitfalls associated with this vehicle.
