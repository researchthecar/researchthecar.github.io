**Introduction:**

The 1996 Mitsubishi 3000GT is a high-performance sports coupe powered by a 3.0L V6 engine. It offers handling and acceleration that rivals more expensive models, making it a popular choice among enthusiasts. However, like all vehicles, the 3000GT has its share of common issues to be aware of.

**Engine**

**3.0L V6**

* **Valve Guide Seals Failing:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, blue smoke from exhaust
    * Solution: Replace valve guide seals
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing Belt Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Catastrophic engine damage if not replaced at recommended intervals
    * Solution: Replace timing belt and components as per maintenance schedule
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **5th Gear Synchro Issue:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting into 5th gear, grinding noise
    * Solution: Replace 5th gear synchronizer
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Shudder:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Vibration during acceleration or deceleration
    * Solution: Replace or rebuild torque converter
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Lower Control Arm Bushings Worn:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking noise when going over bumps, loose or unstable handling
    * Solution: Replace lower control arm bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Shock Absorbers Leaking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Poor ride quality, reduced handling
    * Solution: Replace rear shock absorbers
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Battery not charging, electrical components not working
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Starter Solenoid Sticking:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent starting issues, starter clicking but not engaging
    * Solution: Replace starter solenoid
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Lens Fogging:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Cloudy or hazy headlight lenses
    * Solution: Replace headlight lenses or polish them
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Gauges Malfunctioning:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Intermittent or inaccurate readings on dashboard gauges
    * Solution: Replace faulty gauge cluster
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Power Window Motors Failing:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Windows not rolling up or down, or moving slowly
    * Solution: Replace power window motors
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per window
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1996 Mitsubishi 3000GT is a capable and exciting sports coupe, but it is not without its potential issues. While the majority of problems are relatively minor and affordable to fix, some can be more severe and expensive. It is important to be aware of these potential issues when considering a used 3000GT, and factor the potential costs of repairs into your decision-making process.
