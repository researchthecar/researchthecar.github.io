**Introduction:**

The 1995 Mitsubishi 3000GT was a high-performance sports car available with two engine options: a 3.0L V6 and a twin-turbocharged 3.0L V6. The 3.0L V6 produced 222 horsepower and 205 lb-ft of torque, while the twin-turbocharged version produced 320 horsepower and 315 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were paired with either a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission. The 3000GT was known for its sleek styling, potent engines, and advanced technology. However, like any vehicle, it has its share of issues.

**Engine**

**3.0L V6**
- **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Leakage of coolant or oil, overheating, loss of power
    - Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Timing Belt Tensioner Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Noisy engine, possible timing belt slip, engine damage
    - Solution: Replace timing belt tensioner
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Oil leakage from valve cover
    - Solution: Replace valve cover gasket
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    - Recalls: None Found

**Twin-Turbocharged 3.0L V6**
- **Turbocharger Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke from exhaust, turbocharger noises
    - Solution: Replace turbocharger
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Intercooler Piping Leak:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Loss of boost, reduced engine performance
    - Solution: Replace intercooler piping
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
    - Recalls: None Found
- **EGR Valve Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Rough idle, poor engine performance, increased emissions
    - Solution: Replace EGR valve
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**
- **Clutch Master Cylinder Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, loss of clutch pressure
    - Solution: Replace clutch master cylinder
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Synchronizer Wear:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Grinding noises when shifting gears, difficulty shifting into certain gears
    - Solution: Replace worn synchronizers
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Input Shaft Bearing Failure:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Noisy transmission, loss of power, transmission damage
    - Solution: Replace input shaft bearing

    - Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Transmission Fluid Leak:**
    - Description/Symptoms: Leakage of transmission fluid, slipping gears

    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
    - Recalls: None Found
- **Valve Body Failure:**

    - Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1995 Mitsubishi 3000GT is a high-performance sports car with a long list of potential issues. Some of the most common problems include intake manifold gasket failure, turbocharger failure, clutch master cylinder failure, and strut mount failure. While some of these issues are relatively inexpensive to fix, others can be quite costly. It is important for potential owners to be aware of these potential problems before purchasing a 3000GT.
