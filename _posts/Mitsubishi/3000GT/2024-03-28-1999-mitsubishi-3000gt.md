**Introduction:**

The 1999 Mitsubishi 3000GT is an iconic sports car known for its sleek design, powerful engine, and advanced technology. It is powered by a 3.0L V6 engine producing 320 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual or a 4-speed automatic. Drivers appreciate its handling, performance, and unique styling; however, some issues have been reported over the years.

**Engine**

* **3.0L V6 Engine**
    * **Valve Spring Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Premature valve spring failure can occur, causing engine misfires, loss of power, and potential engine damage. Solution: Replacing the affected valve springs is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $2,500+
    * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner may fail, resulting in excessive timing chain slack, skipped timing, and significant engine damage. Solution: Replacement of the timing chain tensioner is required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
    * **Oil Leaks from Rear Main Seal:** Description/Symptoms: Leaks can occur from the rear main seal of the engine, leading to oil loss and potential engine damage if not addressed. Solution: Replacing the rear main seal is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
* **Recalls:**
    * **Valve Spring Failure:** NHTSA Recall #00V294000
    * **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:** NHTSA Recall #00V187000

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **Shifter Bushing Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Shifter bushings can wear out, causing excessive play in the shifter and making shifting difficult. Solution: Replacing the worn shifter bushings is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
* **4-Speed Automatic Transmission**
    * **Transmission Fluid Leak:** Description/Symptoms: Leaks can develop in the transmission, causing low fluid levels, premature wear, and potential transmission failure. Solution: Identifying and replacing the source of the leak is required. Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
* **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Mounts Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The front strut mounts can deteriorate, causing a clunking noise when turning or hitting bumps, and impaired handling. Solution: Replacing the worn strut mounts is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The alternator may fail, causing insufficient power generation and eventually leading to a dead battery. Solution: Replacing the alternator is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1,000+
* **Ignition Coil Failure:** Description/Symptoms: Failure of the ignition coils can result in engine misfires, difficulty starting, and reduced performance. Solution: Replacing the failed ignition coils is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $350+ per coil

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Switch Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The headlight switch may malfunction, preventing the headlights from turning on or off. Solution: Replacing the headlight switch is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Unit Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The climate control unit may malfunction, causing failure of heating, cooling, or fan functions. Solution: Replacing the faulty climate control unit is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
* **Door Lock Actuator Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The door lock actuators may fail, causing difficulty locking or unlocking the doors, or automatic locking/unlocking systems to malfunction. Solution: Replacing the failed actuator is necessary. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $350+ per actuator

**Summary:**

The 1999 Mitsubishi 3000GT is a powerful and stylish sports car with occasional issues. However, the severity and cost of these problems can vary widely. Regular maintenance and prompt repairs are essential to ensure the vehicle remains in good condition.
