**Introduction to the 2011 Mitsubishi Outlander**

The 2011 Mitsubishi Outlander is a midsize SUV that offers a comfortable and spacious interior, along with a variety of standard features. It is powered by a 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine with 168 horsepower and 167 lb-ft of torque, paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). Other available engine options include a 3.0-liter V6 with 230 horsepower and 215 lb-ft of torque, mated to a six-speed automatic transmission.

Owners of the Mitsubishi Outlander typically praise its comfortable seating, ample cargo space, and affordable price. However, some complaints have been reported regarding its sluggish performance, lackluster fuel economy, and dated interior design.

**Engine**

* **2.4-liter Four-Cylinder Engine with CVT**

    * **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    Description/Symptoms: Abnormal oil loss, low oil level warnings, smoke from the exhaust
    Solution: Replace piston rings or engine block
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    Recalls: None Found

* **3.0-liter V6 Engine with Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

    * No Issues Found

**Transmission**

* **CVT**

    * **Hesitation or Shuddering**
    Description/Symptoms: Rough shifting, loss of power, whining or grinding noises
    Solution: Update transmission software or replace CVT unit
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $2,000+
    Recalls: None Found

* **Six-Speed Automatic Transmission**

    * No Issues Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Strut Assembly**
    Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises, uneven tire wear, poor handling
    Solution: Replace strut assembly
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $800 per side
    Recalls: None Found

* **Rear Shock Absorbers**
    Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, excessive body roll, premature tire wear
    Solution: Replace shock absorbers
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per pair
    Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Failure**
    Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, difficulty starting, dim headlights
    Solution: Replace battery
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    Recalls: None Found

* **Starter Motor Issues**
    Description/Symptoms: Clicking or grinding noises, engine not starting
    Solution: Replace starter motor
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Roof Rack Crossbars**
    Description/Symptoms: Corrosion or rust on crossbars
    Solution: Replace crossbars
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $250
    Recalls: None Found

* **Liftgate Struts**
    Description/Symptoms: Difficulty opening or closing liftgate
    Solution: Replace struts
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200 per pair
    Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Climate Control Panel**
    Description/Symptoms: Buttons not responding, inaccurate temperature readings
    Solution: Replace climate control panel
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
    Recalls: None Found

* **Center Console Lid**
    Description/Symptoms: Lid rattles or does not open smoothly
    Solution: Replace center console lid
    Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
    Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2011 Mitsubishi Outlander offers a comfortable and spacious interior, but it has been reported to have several issues with its engine, transmission, and suspension. While some of these issues may be minor, others can be more serious and expensive to fix. Overall, the severity and cost of the issues vary, with some repairs costing as little as $100, while others can exceed $3,000.
