**Introduction**

The 2013 Mitsubishi Outlander is a compact SUV known for its affordability, spacious interior, and available all-wheel drive. It offers two engine options: a 2.4-liter four-cylinder and a 3.0-liter V6. Transmission choices include a continuously variable transmission (CVT) and a six-speed automatic. Drivers appreciate the Outlander's comfortable seating and user-friendly technology, but some criticize its lackluster performance and fuel economy.

**Engine**

**2.4-liter Four-Cylinder**

* **Excessive Oil Consumption:** Leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
: Solution: Replace defective piston rings.
: Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
: Recalls: None Found

**3.0-liter V6**

* **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)**

* **Hesitation and Jerking:** Rough shifting or delays in acceleration.
: Solution: Update transmission software or replace CVT.
: Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
: Recalls: None Found

**Six-Speed Automatic**

* **No Issues Found**

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Noise:** Clunking or rattling sounds over bumps or uneven surfaces.
: Solution: Replace faulty strut mounts.
: Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
: Recalls: None Found

* **Rear Shock Failure:** Loss of damping and handling issues.
: Solution: Replace failed rear shocks.
: Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
: Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain:** Rapid battery discharge even when parked.
: Solution: Replace defective battery or diagnose and repair electrical system.
: Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
: Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Tailgate Seal Leaks:** Water leaking into the rear cargo area.
: Solution: Replace faulty tailgate seal.
: Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
: Recalls: None Found

* **Sunroof Malfunction:** Sunroof failing to open or close properly.
: Solution: Repair or replace sunroof motor or switch.
: Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
: Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattle:** Noises coming from the dashboard or center console.
: Solution: Locate and tighten loose components or apply sound deadening material.
: Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
: Recalls: None Found

* **Air Conditioning Malfunction:** AC system not cooling effectively or blowing warm air.
: Solution: Diagnose and repair refrigerant leak, compressor issue, or other component failure.
: Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
: Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2013 Mitsubishi Outlander has some significant issues with its engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical system. These issues can be costly to repair and may require major component replacements. While the Outlander offers a spacious interior and affordable price point, potential buyers should be aware of these potential problems before making a purchase.
