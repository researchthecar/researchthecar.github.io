# 2017 Mitsubishi Outlander Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Mitsubishi Outlander offers budget-friendly family seating and available all-wheel drive, but potential buyers should thoroughly check its mechanical and electrical history. While it provides decent utility, owner reports highlight several recurring problem areas ranging from transmission performance to electrical issues and braking concerns. Keeping a close eye on maintenance records, particularly regarding powertrain health, can help buyers avoid costly surprises.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine

The standard 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine produces 166 horsepower and 162 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate power for daily commuting and basic family transport. It is generally dependable, though some owners have reported peripheral issues like oil pan gasket leaks.

### 3.0L V6 Engine

The larger 3.0-liter V6 engine delivers 224 horsepower and 215 lb-ft of torque, paired exclusively with a conventional 6-speed automatic transmission and all-wheel drive. This option offers smoother power delivery and higher towing capacity compared to the base four-cylinder setup.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The continuously variable transmission (CVT) is paired with the 2.4-liter engine and front-wheel or all-wheel drive. It requires attentive fluid maintenance and is a frequent point of owner complaints regarding premature wear and hesitation.

**CVT Hesitation and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience hesitation during acceleration, rough shifting, delays, or complete transmission failure requiring full replacement at higher mileages.
- **Solution:** Inspect transmission fluid condition and test drive thoroughly for smooth operation; failing units require professional replacement.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The conventional 6-speed automatic transmission is coupled with the V6 engine and all-wheel drive models. It avoids many of the mechanical complaints associated with the CVT variants, offering a more traditional driving feel.

## Brakes

### Brake Calipers and Parking Brake

The braking system generally stops the vehicle well, but owners have reported occasional issues with rear brake caliper failures and sticking electronic parking brake mechanisms.

**Caliper Seizure and Electronic Parking Brake Sticking**
- **Symptoms:** Premature wear on brake rotors and pads due to stuck rear calipers, or electronic parking brakes failing to disengage properly while parked on hills.
- **Solution:** Check brake pads, rotors, and calipers for uneven wear during inspections; replace seized components and service parking brake actuators.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Charging System

electrical issues in the Outlander can manifest as sudden battery drains, warning lights, and occasional powertrain drivability disruptions.

**Battery Drain and Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Unexpected battery drain leading to starting failures, accompanied by intermittent engine stalling and 4WD system service warning messages.
- **Solution:** Test battery health, check for parasitic electrical draws, and update control modules or replace faulty sensors if required.

## Exterior

### Seats and Door Locks

Cabin comfort and convenience items are generally functional, though cabin fixtures like front seats and door lock mechanisms can develop annoying mechanical play over time.

**Loose Driver Seat and Inoperable Door Locks**
- **Symptoms:** The driver's seat rocks back and forth during acceleration or braking, and power door locks intermittently fail to respond.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat mounting hardware and tracks for wear or looseness; diagnose door lock actuators and replace faulty switches or motors.

### Windshield and Wipers

Visibility components require regular checking, particularly wiper arm linkages and windshield durability against road debris and stress fractures.

**Windshield Wiper Failure and Cracking**
- **Symptoms:** Windshield wiper arms become stripped and stop working entirely, or front windshields show susceptibility to unexpected cracking.
- **Solution:** Replace stripped wiper linkage arms or motors, and inspect replacement glass quality when addressing windshield damage.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V403000: Seat Belts: Rear/Other:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The interference may cause the right-side seat belt buckle cover to come off, making the buckle latch inoperable and unable to restrain an occupant. An unrestrained occupant has an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** MMNA will notify owners, and dealers will modify the second-row center seat belt buckle cover, install a new warning label to the second-row seat support base, and provide a new warning label insert for the owner's manual, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V621000: Forward Collision Avoidance: Adaptive Cruise Control](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the safety systems do not function as intended, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MMNA will notify owners, and dealers will update the Hydraulic Unit-ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V620000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** The resulting rapid deceleration of the vehicle can increase the risk of a rear-end collision.
- **What to do:** MMNA will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the FCM-ECU software with updated software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V609000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly:Relay](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2017)**
- **Issue:** If the engine stalls, there is an increased the risk of a crash.

- **Issue:** The liftgate gas spring cylinders may corrode and lose pressure, which can result in a gas spring cylinder rupture or the liftgate falling unexpectedly. A gas spring cylinder rupture or a liftgate falling unexpectedly can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MMNA will replace the left and right liftgate gas springs, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Mitsubishi Outlander presents a mixed ownership picture. While its flexible seating and affordable pricing attract budget-conscious buyers, prospective owners should pay close attention to potential CVT transmission hesitation, electrical battery drains, and minor interior/exterior glitches. A comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a qualified mechanic is strongly advised to verify the health of the powertrain and electronics before buying.
