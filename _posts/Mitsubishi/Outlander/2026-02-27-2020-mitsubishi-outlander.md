# 2020 Mitsubishi Outlander Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Mitsubishi Outlander offers practical three-row seating and an affordable entry point into the compact crossover segment, but prospective buyers should review known owner concerns. While powertrain options are generally conventional, complaints highlight occasional issues ranging from infotainment glitches to safety sensor sensitivity.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder

The 2.4-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces 166 horsepower paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT), serving as the standard powerplant for base trims. It provides adequate efficiency for daily commuting, though routine maintenance of fluid changes is essential for longevity.

### 3.0L V6

The 3.0-liter V6 engine generates 224 horsepower and uses a traditional 6-speed automatic transmission, available on higher trims with all-wheel drive. This option offers smoother power delivery and higher towing capacity compared to the base four-cylinder.

## Transmission

### CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)

The continuously variable transmission is paired with the base four-cylinder engine to maximize fuel economy. Buyers should test drive thoroughly to ensure smooth acceleration without slipping or shuddering.

**Transmission Hesitation and Warning Lights**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may experience knocking, bucking, and illumination of the service transmission light with a blinking gear indicator.
- **Solution:** Have the transmission evaluated by a qualified technician for diagnostic trouble codes and potential fluid or component service.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The traditional 6-speed automatic transmission is paired exclusively with the V6 engine. It provides conventional shifting behavior and generally proves durable with regular fluid maintenance.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System

Electrical complaints frequently involve touchscreen responsiveness and auxiliary electronics.

**Infotainment Screen Freezing and Reboots**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen display may freeze, become unresponsive, or continually reboot while driving.
- **Solution:** Check for available software updates from a dealership or restart the system.

## Exterior

### Door Locks

Power accessory components like door locks and seat motors warrant careful inspection during a pre-purchase test.

**Door Lock Actuator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Power door locks fail to engage or unlock via the key fob or interior switches, often requiring multiple replacements.
- **Solution:** Replace the faulty door lock actuator assemblies.

## Safety

### Forward Collision Avoidance

Advanced driver assistance systems can occasionally trigger phantom warnings or require sensor calibration.

**False Collision Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** The dashboard may display unexpected brake warnings, error messages regarding icy roads in warm weather, or trigger sudden alerts without obstacles.
- **Solution:** Ensure camera and radar sensors are clean and free of obstruction; consult a dealer if sensor recalibration is required.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V280000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** An incorrectly assembled seat belt may not properly restrain the occupant, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** MMNA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the right side second-row seat belt and replace it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V507000: Structure:Body:Hatchback/Liftgate:Support Device/Strut](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The liftgate gas spring cylinders may corrode and lose pressure, which can result in a gas spring cylinder rupture or the liftgate falling unexpectedly. A gas spring cylinder rupture or a liftgate falling unexpectedly can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MMNA will replace the left and right liftgate gas springs, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V252000: Structure:Body:Hatchback/Liftgate:Support Device/Strut](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2020)**

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Mitsubishi Outlander is an affordable choice for buyers seeking three-row seating in a compact footprint. While major mechanical failures are relatively uncommon, used car shoppers should thoroughly test the infotainment system, power locks, and driver-assistance sensors to ensure everything operates as expected.
