# 2018 Mitsubishi Outlander Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2018 Mitsubishi Outlander, buyers will find a practical and affordable three-row crossover that offers flexible seating and available all-wheel drive. While many owners enjoy its value and utility, review of owner-reported data highlights several recurring areas of concern—particularly regarding exterior lighting, engine drivability, and electrical accessories. Prospective buyers should carefully inspect these specific systems during a test drive to ensure everything operates as intended.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder

The base 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine produces 166 horsepower and 162 lb-ft of torque, providing adequate efficiency for daily commuting. It is generally dependable, though owners have reported occasional stalling and sensor-related drivability issues.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may suddenly stall after starting on warm days or while coming to a stop, accompanied by illuminated check engine lights and mass airflow or throttle sensor codes.
- **Solution:** Diagnose fault codes, inspect the mass airflow or throttle position sensors, and replace faulty components as necessary.

### 3.0L V6

The available 3.0-liter V6 generates 224 horsepower and 215 lb-ft of torque, offering stronger acceleration and towing capability. It is paired with a traditional six-speed automatic transmission.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The continuously variable transmission is paired with the 2.4L engine and front-wheel or all-wheel drive systems. Routine fluid maintenance is critical to ensure long-term durability.

### 6-Speed Automatic Transmission

The six-speed automatic transmission comes exclusively with the V6 engine and all-wheel drive. It offers traditional shift points and solid mechanical reliability.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting

Issues with exterior lighting components, particularly recurring headlight outages, have been reported by multiple vehicle owners.

**Headlight Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights intermittently or permanently stop working on one or both sides shortly after replacement.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight bulbs, wiring harnesses, and control modules, and replace failed assemblies.

## Safety

### Seat Belt

Owner complaints regarding seat belts highlight occasional operational snags that should be checked for proper locking and retraction.

**Seat Belt Retractor or Latch Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulties with seat belt latching mechanisms or retractor operation during normal use.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt assemblies for mechanical wear or obstruction and replace faulty hardware.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V591000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." The incorrect rim size information on the label can cause the operator to replace the rim with an incorrect rim size, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 20V403000: Seat Belts: Rear/Other:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The interference may cause the right-side seat belt buckle cover to come off, making the buckle latch inoperable and unable to restrain an occupant. An unrestrained occupant has an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** MMNA will notify owners, and dealers will modify the second-row center seat belt buckle cover, install a new warning label to the second-row seat support base, and provide a new warning label insert for the owner's manual, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V620000: Electrical System:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The resulting rapid deceleration of the vehicle can increase the risk of a rear-end collision.
- **What to do:** MMNA will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the FCM-ECU software with updated software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V621000: Forward Collision Avoidance: Adaptive Cruise Control](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the safety systems do not function as intended, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MMNA will notify owners, and dealers will update the Hydraulic Unit-ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V507000: Structure:Body:Hatchback/Liftgate:Support Device/Strut](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The liftgate gas spring cylinders may corrode and lose pressure, which can result in a gas spring cylinder rupture or the liftgate falling unexpectedly. A gas spring cylinder rupture or a liftgate falling unexpectedly can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MMNA will replace the left and right liftgate gas springs, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V252000: Structure:Body:Hatchback/Liftgate:Support Device/Strut](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2018)**

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Mitsubishi Outlander presents a budget-friendly option for families needing three rows of seating and all-wheel drive capability. While it provides good utility, buyers should perform a thorough inspection of the vehicle's electrical systems, exterior lights, and engine idle behavior. Verifying service records and ensuring all recalls have been addressed will help secure a more dependable used vehicle purchase.
