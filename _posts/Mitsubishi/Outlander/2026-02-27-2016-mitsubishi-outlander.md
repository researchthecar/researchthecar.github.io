# 2016 Mitsubishi Outlander Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When considering a used 2016 Mitsubishi Outlander, buyers will find a budget-friendly compact crossover that offers available three-row seating and decent fuel economy. However, owner reports and complaint data highlight a few recurring mechanical and electrical pain points that require careful inspection before purchase. Prospective buyers should thoroughly check the powertrain operation and electrical accessories to avoid inheriting costly post-purchase repairs.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder

The base 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine produces approximately 166 horsepower and is paired with a continuously variable transmission, offering adequate efficiency for daily commuting.

**Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may experience unexpected engine stalling while idling or operating at low speeds, sometimes accompanied by a sudden drop in acceleration.
- **Solution:** Inspect throttle body cleanliness, fuel delivery components, and related powertrain control modules for fault codes.

### 3.0L V6

The available 3.0-liter V6 engine generates around 224 horsepower and uses a traditional 6-speed automatic transmission, providing stronger towing capability and smoother highway passing power.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The continuously variable transmission mated to the 4-cylinder engine requires diligent fluid maintenance, as some owners report premature wear and driveability concerns.

**Transmission Slipping and Shudder**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may notice hesitation, rough shuddering during acceleration, or a complete failure of the transmission to engage gears properly.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid condition and software calibration; severely worn units may require professional overhaul or replacement.

## Brakes

### Parking and Service Brakes

Braking system complaints center around mechanical parking brake operation and tension loss, making a physical brake inspection essential.

**Parking Brake Malfunction**
- **Symptoms:** The parking brake lever may lose tension, fail to hold the vehicle securely on inclines, or stick while driving.
- **Solution:** Inspect parking brake cables, actuators, and associated mechanical linkage components for wear or corrosion.

## Exterior

### Exterior Lighting and Accessories

Electrical system concerns primarily involve exterior lighting failures and occasional accessory fuse issues.

**Headlight Assembly Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights may repeatedly burn out prematurely, or headlight housings can exhibit melting around the bulb sockets.
- **Solution:** Inspect bulb connectors, wiring harnesses, and headlight housings, replacing damaged assemblies as needed.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V609000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly:Relay](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the engine stalls, there is an increased the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MMNA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the affected relays, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V233000: Latches/Locks/Linkages:Doors:Latch](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The inside door latch mechanisms may not latch securely in high temperatures, allowing the doors to open while vehicle is in motion. If a door opens while the vehicle is being driven, it can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MMNA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the door latches, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V070000: Parking Brake](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the parking brake actuator shaft binds, the parking brake may not engage or disengage fully. If the parking brake does not engage properly, the vehicle may move unexpectedly if it is parked on a slope, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MMNA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the rear brake calipers and replace the parking brake boot and lever or the brake caliper body, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V563000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles are equipped with a constant velocity transmission (CVT) that, in certain driving conditions, may cause a delay in the vehicle accelerating. A hesitation during acceleration can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MMNA has notified owners, and dealers will reprogram the CVT control unit with modified software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V403000: Seat Belts: Rear/Other:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The interference may cause the right-side seat belt buckle cover to come off, making the buckle latch inoperable and unable to restrain an occupant. An unrestrained occupant has an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** MMNA will notify owners, and dealers will modify the second-row center seat belt buckle cover, install a new warning label to the second-row seat support base, and provide a new warning label insert for the owner's manual, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V741000: Parking Brake](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The rust prevention coating on the rear brake operating shafts could have been peeled off during the assembly process. As a result, water entering the rear brake caliper boot may cause the parking brake operating shafts to corrode and possibly bind.
- **What to do:** MMNA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the rear brake caliper assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V507000: Structure:Body:Hatchback/Liftgate:Support Device/Strut](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The liftgate gas spring cylinders may corrode and lose pressure, which can result in a gas spring cylinder rupture or the liftgate falling unexpectedly. A gas spring cylinder rupture or a liftgate falling unexpectedly can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MMNA will replace the left and right liftgate gas springs, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V252000: Structure:Body:Hatchback/Liftgate:Support Device/Strut](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The liftgate gas spring cylinders may corrode and lose pressure, which can result in a gas spring cylinder rupture or the liftgate falling unexpectedly. A gas spring cylinder rupture or a liftgate falling unexpectedly can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MMNA will replace the left and right liftgate gas springs, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2016 Mitsubishi Outlander remains an affordable choice for buyers seeking practical features like third-row seating, but it demands careful pre-purchase vetting. Test drive models meticulously to detect any CVT shudder, transmission slipping, or parking brake abnormalities, and ensure all electrical accessories and headlights function reliably.
