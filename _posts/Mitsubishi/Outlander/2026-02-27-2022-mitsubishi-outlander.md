# 2022 Mitsubishi Outlander

### Overview
The 2022 Mitsubishi Outlander, a significant redesign for the model, aims to blend practicality with a more upscale feel. It offers a choice between a standard gasoline powertrain and a plug-in hybrid variant. Early reliability data suggests a generally positive reception, with the new platform and updated technology performing well. However, as with any new generation, some minor initial concerns have surfaced, primarily related to infotainment and specific electronic features. The powertrain options, particularly the PHEV system, have shown promising efficiency and performance, though long-term durability is still being assessed. Overall, the Outlander appears to be a competitive offering in its segment, with a focus on family-friendly features and modern amenities.

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Intermittent screen freezing/unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** Touchscreen may become unresponsive or freeze during operation.
- **Solution:** Software update or system reset. In rare cases, unit replacement.
- **Cost:** $100 - $1500 (Software update typically free, unit replacement can be costly)

**Bluetooth connectivity issues**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty pairing devices or intermittent connection drops.
- **Solution:** Device restart, phone software update, or infotainment system reset.
- **Cost:** $0 - $100 (If phone issues, otherwise potentially free)


### Driver Assistance Systems
**False alerts from safety systems**
- **Symptoms:** Forward collision mitigation or lane keeping assist may trigger without apparent cause.
- **Solution:** Sensor cleaning, recalibration, or software update.
- **Cost:** $100 - $300

### Summary Verdict
The 2022 Mitsubishi Outlander demonstrates a solid start in terms of reliability, particularly with its core powertrain components. The gasoline and PHEV systems have shown no significant widespread issues. The primary areas of concern are concentrated within the electrical system, specifically the infotainment screen's occasional unresponsiveness and Bluetooth connectivity glitches, along with some minor false alerts from driver assistance features. These issues are generally addressable through software updates or resets. The suspension, exterior, and interior components appear to be holding up well. Overall, the Outlander is a reliable choice for its segment, with most reported issues being minor and related to modern electronic features rather than fundamental mechanical flaws.
