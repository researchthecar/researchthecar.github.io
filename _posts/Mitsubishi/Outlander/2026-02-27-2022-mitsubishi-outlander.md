# 2022 Mitsubishi Outlander Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Mitsubishi Outlander introduced a complete redesign sharing a platform with the Nissan Rogue, offering sharp styling and a much-improved interior. While it has proven reasonably popular on the used market, owners have logged notable recurring complaints regarding specific structural quirks and mechanical vulnerabilities. Buyers evaluating a used model should inspect the vehicle thoroughly for common trouble spots before finalizing a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

Powered by a 2.5-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine generating approximately 181 horsepower and 181 lb-ft of torque, this powerplant provides adequate daily motivation. Overall reliability is generally standard for the class, though isolated fuel delivery and stalling concerns have been reported.

**Fuel Pump Failure & Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may fail to start, experience sudden engine stalling while driving, or display dashboard warning lights related to fuel delivery.
- **Solution:** Have the fuel pump inspected by a qualified technician and replaced under warranty or with an updated service part if failure occurs.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Equipped with an electronically controlled continuously variable transmission paired with front-wheel drive or available all-wheel drive. The transmission delivers smooth power delivery and optimized fuel economy, with fewer widespread mechanical complaints than previous generations.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides stopping power suited for a midsize SUV, but some owners report accelerated wear on rear brake components.

**Premature Brake Wear and Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience loud squealing noises during braking or maneuvering, accompanied by rapid wear of brake pads and rotors.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and calipers for uneven wear, and replace with upgraded aftermarket or revised OEM components if necessary.

## Electrical

### Electrical and Infotainment System

Advanced electronics enhance the cabin experience but can occasionally exhibit software glitches, backup camera display blackouts, and electrical accessory quirks.

**Infotainment Screen Freezing and Battery Drain**
- **Symptoms:** The infotainment display screen may go completely black, especially when shifting into reverse, or electrical system draws may cause premature battery failure.
- **Solution:** Perform software resets, update firmware through a dealer, or test and replace weak batteries and parasitic power draws.

## Exterior

### Hood Structure

Body panels are generally well-assembled, but the hood structure is subject to well-documented aerodynamic flutter at higher speeds.

**Hood Flutter and Vibration**
- **Symptoms:** At highway speeds or during windy conditions, the hood visibly shakes, vibrates, or flutters, causing driver distraction and concern that the latch might fail.
- **Solution:** Adjust or replace the hood stoppers and latch mechanisms, or consult a dealer regarding available technical service bulletins.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V027000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Fuel Pump](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Fuel pump failure can cause an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel pump, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V596000: Seat Belts:Front:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect, and replace as necessary, the right front seat belt assembly, and the right and left second-row seat belt assemblies, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V821000: Structure:Body:Roof And Pillars](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) numbers 214, "Side Impact Protection" 210, "Seat Belt Assembly Anchorages" and 216, "Roof Crush Resistance." In the event of a crash, the reduced strength of the vehicle body may increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 22V335000: Seat Belts:Front:Retractor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 208, "Occupant Crash Protection." An unsecured child restraint system can increase the risk of injury during a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the seat belt assemblies, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 22V470000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** The rearview camera may not display the rearview image due to a software error. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." An inoperable rearview camera display reduces the driver's rear view, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the rearview camera software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V345000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** Due to a software error in the in-vehicle infotainment system (A-IVI), the backup camera rearview image may not appear on the display. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not display an image can reduce the driver's rear view.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the A-IVI software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V369000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2022)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not display properly reduces the driver's view of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the A-IVI software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2022 Mitsubishi Outlander is a modern, feature-rich compact SUV that represents solid value on the used market. While its powertrain is generally dependable, buyers should carefully check for the prominent hood flutter at highway speeds, inspect brake pad and rotor wear, and verify that all electrical and infotainment systems function correctly. A pre-purchase inspection by an independent mechanic is recommended to catch any hidden issues before buying.
