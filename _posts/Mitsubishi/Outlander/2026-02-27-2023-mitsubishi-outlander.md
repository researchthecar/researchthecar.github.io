# 2023 Mitsubishi Outlander Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Mitsubishi Outlander offers a bold design, a comfortable interior, and contemporary technology, alongside both standard gasoline and plug-in hybrid powertrain options. However, early adopters and owners have reported several recurring issues ranging from minor software glitches to more concerning mechanical and steering complaints. When shopping for a used model, a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the suspension, brakes, and electrical systems is strongly advised.

---

## Engine

### 2.5L 4-Cylinder Engine

A 2.5-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine producing around 181 horsepower, serving as the standard power plant for gasoline models. It provides adequate daily driving capability, though buyers should watch for routine maintenance history and cooling system health.

### 2.4L Plug-In Hybrid (PHEV) Powertrain

A 2.4-liter 4-cylinder engine paired with dual electric motors and a lithium-ion battery pack for the Plug-In Hybrid variant. It delivers responsive electrified performance and efficiency, though complex hybrid electronics warrant careful verification during a test drive.

**Power Delivery Interruption**
- **Symptoms:** The EV portion of the hybrid system intermittently fails to provide power to the motors despite adequate battery charge, occasionally triggering a 'Power Reduced' warning.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic software updates or inverter checks by a certified dealership may be required to resolve communication errors within the hybrid control modules.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

A continuously variable transmission paired with front-wheel or all-wheel-drive configurations. Regular fluid service is crucial to ensuring longevity and smooth operation across varying temperatures.

**Delayed Upshifting When Cold**
- **Symptoms:** The transmission may fail to shift out of first gear until the vehicle has reached normal operating temperatures.
- **Solution:** Check transmission fluid levels and software calibration; consult a dealer if hesitation persists.

## Suspension

### Struts and Rear Suspension

The suspension setup delivers a compliant ride, though early wear on strut bearings and minor rear-end shifting sensations have been reported by owners navigating rough roads.

**Strut Bearing Clunking and Rear Sway**
- **Symptoms:** Loud knocking or clunking sounds from the front and rear when turning or hitting bumps, accompanied by a feeling of the rear end swaying or moving independently.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace worn strut bearings or damaged rear suspension components under available service coverage.

## Brakes

### Brake Calipers and Pads

The braking hardware generally provides adequate stopping power, but owners have noted isolated instances of sticking calipers and transient sensor warnings requiring attention.

**Premature Pad Wear and Sticking Calipers**
- **Symptoms:** Uneven brake pad wear, pulling to one side, or seizing brake components leading to drastically shortened pad lifespan at low mileage.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake calipers for proper sliding function, and replace seized calipers alongside fresh pads and rotors.

**Parking Brake Sensor Warnings**
- **Symptoms:** An intermittent parking brake sensor failure warning light flashes briefly on the dashboard during stops.
- **Solution:** Have the electronic parking brake actuator and sensor connections inspected and reset by a technician.

## Steering

### Steering Assembly

The electronic power steering system provides light maneuverability at low speeds, though highway feedback issues have been documented by some drivers.

**Heavy Steering and Return-to-Center Resistance**
- **Symptoms:** Extremely heavy steering feel at highway speeds, with the steering wheel slow to return to center or temporarily staying locked in a turn.
- **Solution:** Inspect the electronic power steering module, steering shaft universal joints, and alignment specifications.

## Electrical

### Infotainment and Instrument Cluster

Modern digital displays and infotainment tech power the cabin interface, but software stability issues can occasionally disrupt critical instrumentation and backup camera views.

**Infotainment Freeze and Blank Instrument Panel**
- **Symptoms:** The digital driver display or the infotainment screen goes completely blank or freezes during operation, occasionally causing rearview camera feeds to fail while reversing.
- **Solution:** Update the in-vehicle infotainment software or perform a system reset/module replacement as advised by a dealer.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 23V345000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** Due to a software error in the in-vehicle infotainment system (A-IVI), the backup camera rearview image may not appear on the display. As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview camera that does not display an image can reduce the driver's rear view.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the A-IVI software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V369000: Back Over Prevention:Software](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not display properly reduces the driver's view of what is behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will update the A-IVI software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Mitsubishi Outlander presents a mixed reliability picture during its inaugural production year. While many owners enjoy its comfort and styling, potential buyers should carefully verify the condition of the suspension, brakes, and electrical systems during a pre-purchase inspection. Checking for completed software updates and ensuring the hybrid and infotainment features operate smoothly will help secure a trouble-free ownership experience.
