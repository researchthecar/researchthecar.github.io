**Introduction**

The 2023 Mitsubishi Outlander is a mid-size SUV that offers a spacious cabin, ample cargo space, and a variety of advanced safety features. It comes standard with a 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine that produces 181 horsepower and 181 lb-ft of torque. A more powerful 3.0-liter V6 engine is available, producing 294 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with a continuously variable automatic transmission (CVT).

Owners appreciate the Outlander's comfortable ride, user-friendly infotainment system, and generous warranty. However, some have experienced issues with the vehicle's engine, transmission, and other components.

**Engine**

* **2.5-liter four-cylinder**

    * **Premature timing chain failure:** The timing chain can stretch or break prematurely, leading to catastrophic engine damage.
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine noise, rough idling, loss of power, and check engine light
    * Solution: Replace the timing chain and related components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000-$4,000+
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign ID 23V010000

* **3.0-liter V6**

    * **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

* **Continuously variable automatic transmission (CVT)**

    * **Hesitation or shuddering during acceleration:** The CVT may hesitate or shudder during acceleration, especially when the vehicle is cold.
    * Description/Symptoms: Rough or jerky acceleration, lack of power
    * Solution: Update the transmission software or replace the CVT
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500-$3,000
    * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign ID 23V035000

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** The vehicle's battery may drain prematurely due to a faulty alternator or other electrical component.
* Description/Symptoms: Difficulty starting the vehicle, electrical system malfunction warnings
* Solution: Replace the faulty battery or alternator
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$500
* Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Excessive body panel vibration:** Body panels, such as the doors and fenders, may vibrate excessively at certain speeds.
* Description/Symptoms: Rattling or buzzing noises
* Solution: Adjust or replace the body panel mounts
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $100-$200
* Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Steering wheel trim peeling or cracking:** The leather or plastic trim on the steering wheel may peel or crack prematurely.
* Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged steering wheel trim
* Solution: Replace the steering wheel trim
* Estimated Cost to Fix: $200-$300
* Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

While the 2023 Mitsubishi Outlander offers several positive attributes, it has also faced a number of issues. The most severe and expensive issue is premature timing chain failure in the 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine. Other issues include transmission hesitation or shuddering, electrical battery drain, excessive body panel vibration, and peeling or cracking steering wheel trim. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but some can be costly to repair.
