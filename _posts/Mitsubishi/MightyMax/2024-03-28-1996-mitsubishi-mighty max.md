**Introduction:**

The 1996 Mitsubishi Mighty Max is a compact pickup truck available with a range of engine and transmission options. The standard engine is a 2.4-liter inline-four producing 142 horsepower and 162 lb-ft of torque. A 3.0-liter V6 producing 150 horsepower and 180 lb-ft of torque is optional. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual transmission for all engine configurations and a 4-speed automatic transmission for the V6 engine. Owners praise the Mighty Max for its durability, reliability, and off-road capabilities, but criticize its lack of modern features and unrefined interior.

**Engine**

**2.4-Liter Inline-Four**

**Cylinder Head Gasket Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Coolant and oil leaking from the engine, overheating, white smoke from the exhaust, loss of power.
Solution: Replace cylinder head gaskets.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**3.0-Liter V6**

**Exhaust Manifold Cracking**
Description/Symptoms: Loud ticking noise from the engine, reduced power, increased fuel consumption.
Solution: Replace exhaust manifold.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

**Clutch Master Cylinder Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, no clutch engagement.
Solution: Replace clutch master cylinder.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300
Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

**Internal Transmission Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Slipping gears, delayed engagement, grinding noises.
Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Front Suspension Bushing Wear**
Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises when driving over bumps, uneven tire wear.
Solution: Replace suspension bushings.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
Recalls: None Found

**Rear Suspension Leaf Spring Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Sagging rear end, poor handling, noisy suspension.
Solution: Replace leaf springs.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Alternator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, flickering dashboard lights, electrical system issues.
Solution: Replace alternator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
Recalls: None Found

**Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: Battery drains overnight, slow starting, electrical system issues.
Solution: Diagnose and repair electrical system component causing drain, replace battery if necessary.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Tailgate Rust**
Description/Symptoms: Rust and corrosion on the tailgate panel.
Solution: Repair or replace tailgate panel.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Dashboard Cracking**
Description/Symptoms: Cracks appearing on the plastic dashboard.
Solution: Replace dashboard.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 1996 Mitsubishi Mighty Max is a reliable and durable pickup truck with some common issues that vary in severity and repair costs. Engine and transmission issues can be expensive to fix, while suspension and electrical problems are typically more minor repairs.
