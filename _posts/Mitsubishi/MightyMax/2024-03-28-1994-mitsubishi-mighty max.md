**Introduction:**
The 1994 Mitsubishi Mighty Max is a compact pickup truck available in various trim levels. It offers a 2.4L inline-four engine paired with either a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission. The Mighty Max is known for its ruggedness, affordability, and maneuverability. While praised for its reliability, it has encountered some specific issues over time.

**Engine**

**2.4L Inline-Four**
* **Intake Valve Guide Wear:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, blue smoke from the exhaust, rough idling, and misfires.
    * Solution: Replace the intake valve guides.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**
* No Issues Found

**4-Speed Automatic**
* **Transmission Slipping:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission hesitates or slips when shifting, difficulty in shifting, or loss of power.
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission or specific components (e.g., solenoid pack, torque converter).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
* **Rear Leaf Springs:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Sagging or broken leaf springs, resulting in poor handling and ride quality.
    * Solution: Replace the leaf springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per spring
* **Front Ball Joints:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Knocking or grinding noises, loss of steering control, or uneven tire wear.
    * Solution: Replace the ball joints.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400 per joint

**Electrical**
* **Alternator Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Dead battery, dim lights, or flickering electrical components.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
* **Starter Solenoid:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Starter does not engage or spins without starting the engine.
    * Solution: Replace the starter solenoid.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $350

**Exterior**
* **Rust:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Corrosion on body panels, frame, or suspension components.
    * Solution: Treat and repair the rust, or replace severely affected parts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3,000+ depending on the extent of damage

**Interior**
* **HVAC System Failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: AC or heater not working, weak airflow, or strange noises.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair specific components (e.g., compressor, blower motor, hoses).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
* **Seat Recline Mechanism:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Seat recliner does not lock or is difficult to adjust.
    * Solution: Replace or repair the seat recliner mechanism.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250

**Summary:**
The 1994 Mitsubishi Mighty Max is generally a reliable vehicle, but it does have its fair share of issues. The most severe and expensive problems include transmission slipping and rust damage. Other common issues, such as intake valve guide wear and alternator failure, are more moderate in cost. The estimated repair costs provided here are approximate and may vary depending on factors like labor rates and the availability of parts. Overall, the Mighty Max is a budget-friendly workhorse that may require some occasional maintenance and repairs to keep it in good condition.
