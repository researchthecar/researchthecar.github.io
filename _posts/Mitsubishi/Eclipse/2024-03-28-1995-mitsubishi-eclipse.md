**Introduction:**

The 1995 Mitsubishi Eclipse is a popular sports coupe that was praised for its sleek design, handling, and performance. It was offered with two engine options: a 2.0L inline-4 and a 3.0L V6. Transmission options included a 5-speed manual and a 4-speed automatic. Despite its strengths, the Eclipse has several known issues that can affect different components of the vehicle.

**Engine**

**2.0L Inline-4**

* **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, leading to vacuum leaks and engine performance issues.
  * Symptoms: Rough idle, loss of power, increased fuel consumption
  * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
  * Recalls: None Found

**3.0L V6**

* **Timing Belt Tensioner Failure:** The timing belt tensioner can fail, causing the timing belt to slip or break, potentially leading to catastrophic engine damage.
  * Symptoms: Noisy engine, rough idle, loss of power
  * Solution: Replace timing belt tensioner and timing belt
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Synchronizer Wear:** The synchronizers in the transmission can wear out, making shifting difficult or impossible.
  * Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises
  * Solution: Rebuild or replace transmission
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Transmission Solenoid Failure:** The transmission solenoids can fail, leading to shifting problems and transmission failure.
  * Symptoms: Harsh shifting, delayed engagement, slipping gears
  * Solution: Replace transmission solenoids
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure:** The strut mounts can fail, causing excessive noise, vibration, and poor handling.
  * Symptoms: Clunking noises, vibration, uneven tire wear
  * Solution: Replace strut mounts
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Starter Issues:** The starter can fail, making it difficult or impossible to start the vehicle.
  * Symptoms: No clicking or grinding when turning the key, slow cranking
  * Solution: Replace starter
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
  * Recalls: None Found

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator can fail, leading to a loss of electrical power and potentially draining the battery.
  * Symptoms: Dim headlights, dead battery, warning lights
  * Solution: Replace alternator
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Door Handle Failure:** The door handles can break or become loose, making it difficult to open the doors.
  * Symptoms: Loose or broken door handle
  * Solution: Replace door handle
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Window Regulator Failure:** The window regulators can fail, causing the windows to become stuck or fall down.
  * Symptoms: Inoperable windows, window rattling
  * Solution: Replace window regulator
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ per window
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 1995 Mitsubishi Eclipse has some known issues that can affect various components of the vehicle. While some issues are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix, others, such as engine or transmission issues, can be more severe and costly. Owners should be aware of these potential issues and take proactive steps to maintain and repair their vehicles as needed.
