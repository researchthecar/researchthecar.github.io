**Introduction:**

The 1992 Mitsubishi Eclipse was a popular sports coupe that offered a combination of performance and affordability. It came with a choice of two engines: a 2.0L 4-cylinder and a 3.0L V6. The 4-cylinder produced 135 horsepower and 142 lb-ft of torque, while the V6 generated 164 horsepower and 183 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options included a 5-speed manual or a 4-speed automatic. While the Eclipse was praised for its handling and performance, it also had some common issues that buyers should be aware of.

**Engine**

**2.0L 4-Cylinder**

- **Timing Belt Failure:** The timing belt can fail prematurely, leading to major engine damage. Symptoms include engine stalling, rough idling, and reduced power. Solution: Replace the timing belt according to the manufacturer's recommended schedule. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
- **Cylinder Head Gasket Failure:** The cylinder head gasket can fail, causing coolant leaks and engine overheating. Symptoms include white smoke from the exhaust, coolant loss, and overheating. Solution: Replace the cylinder head gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

**3.0L V6**

- **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** The valve cover gasket can leak oil. Symptoms include an oil leak from the top of the engine. Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
- **Power Steering Pump Failure:** The power steering pump can fail, resulting in a loss of power steering. Symptoms include heavy steering and a whining noise from the engine bay. Solution: Replace the power steering pump. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

- **Gear Synchronization Wear:** The gears can become difficult to engage or grind when shifting. Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises, and popping out of gear. Solution: Replace the worn synchronizers. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**4-Speed Automatic**

- **Transmission Slipping:** The transmission may slip or hesitate when shifting. Symptoms include delayed or erratic shifting, slipping gears, and a loss of power. Solution: Replace the transmission or rebuild the existing one. Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+

**Suspension**

- **Strut Failure:** The front struts can fail prematurely, leading to a loss of stability and handling. Symptoms include excessive bouncing, noise from the suspension, and uneven tire wear. Solution: Replace the worn struts. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+ per pair
- **Ball Joint Wear:** The ball joints can wear out, causing a loss of steering precision and stability. Symptoms include noise from the front suspension, uneven tire wear, and a loose steering feel. Solution: Replace the worn ball joints. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+ per joint

**Electrical**

- **Alternator Failure:** The alternator may fail, resulting in a loss of electrical power. Symptoms include flickering lights, difficulty starting the car, and a dead battery. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
- **Starter Failure:** The starter may fail, preventing the car from starting. Symptoms include a clicking noise when trying to start the car or no response at all. Solution: Replace the starter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Exterior**

- **Sunroof Leaks:** The sunroof may leak, allowing water to enter the interior. Symptoms include water stains on the headliner or carpets. Solution: Replace the sunroof seal or adjust the sunroof alignment. Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Interior**

- **Climate Control Malfunction:** The climate control system may malfunction, resulting in poor heating or cooling. Symptoms include uneven temperatures, lack of air flow, and excessive noise. Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty components within the climate control system. Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the issue

**Summary**

The 1992 Mitsubishi Eclipse is a fun and affordable sports coupe, but it does have some common issues that buyers should be aware of. While the severity of these issues can vary, some of the most expensive repairs can include timing belt failure, cylinder head gasket failure, transmission replacement, and suspension repairs. It's important to consider these potential costs when purchasing a used Eclipse and to have the vehicle inspected by a qualified mechanic before making a decision.
