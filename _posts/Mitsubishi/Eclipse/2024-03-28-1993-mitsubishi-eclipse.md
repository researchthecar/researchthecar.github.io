The 1993 Mitsubishi Eclipse is a sports coupe that was available with two engine options: a 2.0-liter 4-cylinder engine rated at 140 horsepower and 132 lb-ft of torque, and a 3.0-liter V6 engine rated at 210 horsepower and 204 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options included a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Eclipse was praised for its stylish design, performance, and handling, but it was also criticized for its small interior and limited cargo space.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter 4-cylinder Engine**

* **Piston Ring Failure:** Premature wear of the piston rings can cause excessive oil consumption and reduced engine performance.
  * Solution: Replace the piston rings and hone the cylinder walls.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800+
  * Recalls: None Found

**3.0-liter V6 Engine**

* **Head Gasket Failure:** Failure of the head gasket can lead to coolant leaks, overheating, and engine damage.
  * Solution: Replace the head gasket and perform a valve job.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-speed Manual Transmission**

* **Synchronizer Wear:** Worn synchronizers can cause difficulty shifting gears and grinding noises.
  * Solution: Replace the worn synchronizers.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**4-speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Transmission Solenoid Failure:** Failure of a transmission solenoid can lead to erratic shifting or transmission failure.
  * Solution: Replace the faulty solenoid.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Mount Failure:** Worn strut mounts can cause clunking noises and impaired handling.
  * Solution: Replace the strut mounts.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** A failing alternator can lead to electrical problems and a dead battery.
  * Solution: Replace the alternator.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300+
  * Recalls: None Found

* **Starter Motor Failure:** A faulty starter motor can prevent the engine from starting.
  * Solution: Replace the starter motor.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Hood Struts Failure:** Weak or broken hood struts can cause the hood to fall or come loose.
  * Solution: Replace the hood struts.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:** The dashboard can develop cracks over time, especially in hot climates.
  * Solution: Replace the dashboard or repair the cracks.
  * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500+
  * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1993 Mitsubishi Eclipse has a number of potential issues that can affect performance, reliability, and comfort. The severity of these issues can vary, and some may be more common than others. The estimated cost of repairs can be significant, but it is important to weigh the potential costs against the benefits of owning this vehicle.
