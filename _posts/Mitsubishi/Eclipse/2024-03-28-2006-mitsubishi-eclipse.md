**Introduction to the 2006 Mitsubishi Eclipse**

The 2006 Mitsubishi Eclipse is a sporty coupe that offers a thrilling driving experience with its responsive handling and powerful engine options. It is powered by a 2.4-liter inline-four engine that produces 160 horsepower and 162 lb-ft of torque, paired with a five-speed manual or four-speed automatic transmission. Enthusiasts can opt for the GT trim, which boasts a 3.8-liter V6 engine that generates 263 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, mated to a six-speed manual or five-speed automatic transmission.

Despite its performance prowess, the Eclipse has garnered mixed reviews. Some owners praise its sporty handling and comfortable interior, while others express concerns over its reliability and high maintenance costs.

**Engine**

**2.4-liter Inline-Four**
- **Intake Valve Contamination**
  - Description/Symptoms: Carbon buildup on the intake valves restricts airflow, causing poor engine performance, hesitation, and decreased fuel efficiency.
  - Solution: Regular cleaning of the intake valves.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: A faulty timing chain tensioner can lead to a rattling noise from the engine, loss of power, and potentially catastrophic engine damage if the chain fails.
  - Solution: Replacement of the timing chain tensioner and associated components.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

**3.8-liter V6**
- **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak**
  - Description/Symptoms: A leaking intake manifold gasket can cause a vacuum leak, leading to rough idling, poor performance, and increased emissions.
  - Solution: Replacement of the intake manifold gasket.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Camshaft Position Sensor Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: A faulty camshaft position sensor can disrupt the engine's ignition timing, resulting in difficulty starting, stalling, and reduced engine performance.
  - Solution: Replacement of the camshaft position sensor.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**Five-Speed Manual**
- **Synchronizer Wear**
  - Description/Symptoms: Worn synchronizers can cause difficulty shifting gears, especially when downshifting, and may produce grinding noises.
  - Solution: Replacement of the worn synchronizers.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Four-Speed Automatic**
- **Shift Solenoid Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: A faulty shift solenoid can prevent the transmission from shifting properly, leading to delayed or harsh shifts, and potential transmission damage if left unchecked.
  - Solution: Replacement of the shift solenoid.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Six-Speed Manual**
- **Clutch Malfunction**
  - Description/Symptoms: A worn or damaged clutch can cause slippage, difficulty shifting gears, and a burning smell.
  - Solution: Replacement of the clutch and associated components.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,200+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **Strut Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: Worn struts can cause the vehicle to ride rough, handle poorly, and lead to premature tire wear.
  - Solution: Replacement of the struts and associated components.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Alternator Failure**
  - Description/Symptoms: A failing alternator can lead to a loss of electrical power, dim headlights, and difficulty starting the vehicle.
  - Solution: Replacement of the alternator.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
  - Recalls: None Found
- **Battery Drain**
  - Description/Symptoms: A parasitic battery drain can occur due to faulty electrical components, causing the battery to discharge prematurely.
  - Solution: Troubleshooting and replacing the faulty component(s) causing the drain.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Headlight Housing Condensation**
  - Description/Symptoms: Moisture can accumulate inside the headlight housing, creating condensation and impairing visibility at night.
  - Solution: Resealing the headlight housing or replacing the entire assembly.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Airbag Warning Light Activation**
  - Description/Symptoms: A faulty airbag sensor can trigger the airbag warning light, potentially disabling the airbag system.
  - Solution: Diagnostic testing and replacement of the faulty sensor or component.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2006 Mitsubishi Eclipse offers a thrilling driving experience, but it comes with a history of potential reliability issues. The engine and transmission components can be prone to various problems, which can lead to costly repairs. The vehicle's electrical system and suspension can also encounter issues, further adding to the maintenance burden. While the Eclipse's performance may appeal to some, potential buyers should weigh the risks and consider the associated repair costs before making a purchase.
