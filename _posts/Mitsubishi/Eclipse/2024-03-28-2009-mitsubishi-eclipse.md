**2009 Mitsubishi Eclipse**

The 2009 Mitsubishi Eclipse is a sporty coupe that was offered with three different engine options: a 2.4L inline-4, a 3.8L V6, and a turbocharged 2.0L inline-4. Transmission options included a 5-speed manual, a 4-speed automatic, and a continuously variable transmission (CVT). The Eclipse was praised for its stylish exterior, but criticized for its cramped interior and lackluster performance.

**Engine**
- **2.4L Inline-4**
    - **Piston Ring Failure**
        - Description/Symptoms: Excessive oil consumption, blue smoke from exhaust
        - Solution: Replace piston rings
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    - **Timing Chain Noise**
        - Description/Symptoms: Rattling or ticking noises from engine
        - Solution: Replace timing chain
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,500+
- **3.8L V6**
    - **Head Gasket Failure**
        - Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating
        - Solution: Replace head gasket
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    - **EGR Valve Failure**
        - Description/Symptoms: Engine idling issues, performance loss
        - Solution: Clean or replace EGR valve
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
- **2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**
    - **Turbocharger Failure**
        - Description/Symptoms: Loss of boost pressure, reduced engine performance
        - Solution: Replace turbocharger
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
    - **Intake Valve Carbon Buildup**
        - Description/Symptoms: Reduced engine power, misfires
        - Solution: Clean intake valves
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Transmission**
- **5-Speed Manual**
    - **Clutch Failure**
        - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, slipping clutch
        - Solution: Replace clutch
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
- **4-Speed Automatic**
    - **Transmission Slippage**
        - Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, erratic shifting
        - Solution: Replace transmission
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,500 - $3,500+
- **CVT**
    - **Belt Failure**
        - Description/Symptoms: Slipping or shuddering during gear changes
        - Solution: Replace CVT belt
        - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+

**Suspension**
- **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**
- **Alternator Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Electrical system problems, battery not charging
    - Solution: Replace alternator
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
- **Stator Wiring Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Electrical system problems, charging system warning light on
    - Solution: Repair or replace stator wiring
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Exterior**
- **Trunk Lid Release Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Trunk lid cannot be opened
    - Solution: Replace trunk lid release mechanism
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+

**Interior**
- **Power Window Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Power windows become inoperable
    - Solution: Replace power window motor or switch
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
- **Sunroof Motor Failure**
    - Description/Symptoms: Sunroof does not open or close properly
    - Solution: Replace sunroof motor
    - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Summary**

The 2009 Mitsubishi Eclipse has several potential issues that can range in severity and cost. Engine problems can be particularly costly to repair, especially for the turbocharged 2.0L inline-4 engine. Transmission issues can also be expensive, with CVT failures being the most common. Electrical problems and interior issues are generally less severe and less expensive to fix. Overall, potential buyers should be aware of these issues and factor in the potential cost of repairs when considering purchasing this vehicle.
