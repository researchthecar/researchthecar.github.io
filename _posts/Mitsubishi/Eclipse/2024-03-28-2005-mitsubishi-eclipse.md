**Introduction:**
The 2005 Mitsubishi Eclipse is a stylish and sporty compact coupe available in GS and GT trims. The GS trim features a 2.4-liter inline-four engine, while the GT trim offers a powerful 3.8-liter V6 engine. Both trims are available with either a 5-speed manual or a 4-speed automatic transmission. Despite its performance-oriented design, the Eclipse has been plagued by numerous issues that have dampened the overall ownership experience.

**Engine**

**2.4L Inline-Four**

**Exhaust Manifold Crack**
Description/Symptoms: A crack in the exhaust manifold can lead to a loss of power, reduced fuel efficiency, and a loud exhaust noise.
Solution: Replacing the exhaust manifold.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
Recalls: None Found

**3.8L V6**

**Intake Valve Seat Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Over time, the intake valve seats can wear and loosen, leading to a loss of compression, misfires, and engine damage.
Solution: Replacing the affected valve seats.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $4000+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

**Synchronizer Wear**
Description/Symptoms: Excessive shifting effort, grinding or crunching noises, and difficulty shifting gears are indications of worn synchronizers.
Solution: Replacing the worn synchronizers.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1500+
Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic**

**Transmission Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Loss of gears, slipping, and complete transmission failure are all potential signs of transmission problems.
Solution: Overhauling or replacing the transmission.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $2000 - $5000+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Strut Mount Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Worn strut mounts can cause clunking noises, poor handling, and premature tire wear.
Solution: Replacing the strut mounts.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+ per axle
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: The vehicle battery drains prematurely, often within hours of parking.
Solution: Finding and fixing the electrical component responsible for the drain.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Fog Light Malfunction**
Description/Symptoms: Fog lights may not turn on or may turn off intermittently due to faulty wiring or connectors.
Solution: Replacing the affected wiring or connectors.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Dash Panel Rattle**
Description/Symptoms: The dashboard panel may rattle or vibrate, especially over rough roads.
Solution: Locating and securing the loose area.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2005 Mitsubishi Eclipse has significant issues that can impact reliability and ownership costs. Engine and transmission problems are among the most severe and expensive to fix. While some interior and exterior issues may be minor annoyances, persistent electrical problems can be frustrating. Potential buyers should be aware of these potential issues before purchasing an Eclipse.
