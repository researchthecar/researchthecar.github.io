# 2019 Mitsubishi Outlander PHEV

### Overview
The 2019 Mitsubishi Outlander PHEV is a plug-in hybrid SUV that aims to blend electric efficiency with the practicality of a traditional SUV. It features a unique powertrain combining a gasoline engine with electric motors, offering all-wheel-drive capability and a decent electric-only range for its class. Reliability data suggests a generally solid performance, with most reported issues being minor or related to specific component failures rather than widespread systemic problems. Owners often praise its fuel economy and comfortable ride, though some find the infotainment system and interior materials to be less premium than competitors. Overall, the Outlander PHEV presents a compelling option for those seeking an eco-friendly and versatile family vehicle.

### Hybrid Battery System
**Battery Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced electric-only range, decreased overall efficiency.
- **Solution:** Battery replacement (typically covered under warranty for a specified period/mileage).

### Summary Verdict
The 2019 Mitsubishi Outlander PHEV demonstrates good reliability, particularly with its gasoline engine and eCVT transmission, which have few reported issues. The primary concerns revolve around the hybrid battery system, where degradation over time or charging system faults can occur, though these are often addressed under warranty. Electrical issues, such as infotainment screen unresponsiveness, are also noted but not considered widespread. Exterior and interior components generally hold up well. For potential buyers, it's advisable to check the battery health and warranty status, but overall, the Outlander PHEV is a dependable choice in the plug-in hybrid segment.
