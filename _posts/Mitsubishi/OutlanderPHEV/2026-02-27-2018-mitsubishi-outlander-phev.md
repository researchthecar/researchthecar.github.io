# 2018 Mitsubishi Outlander PHEV Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Mitsubishi Outlander PHEV offers plug-in hybrid versatility and standard all-wheel drive, making it an appealing option for efficiency-minded buyers. While it provides practical daily driving capability, prospective buyers should review available owner feedback regarding hybrid propulsion behavior, parking mechanism functions, and minor electrical glitches when evaluating a used model.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Plug-In Hybrid

The 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine is paired with electric motors to produce approximately 190 total system horsepower, featuring a plug-in hybrid drivetrain. It delivers solid fuel efficiency for daily commutes, though drivers should verify hybrid system software health during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Limited Propulsion Warning and Speed Reduction**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported that while driving uphill or under heavy loads, a 'Limited Propulsion' warning light illuminates, and vehicle speed drops significantly even with adequate fuel levels.
- **Solution:** Diagnostic checks by a qualified technician are recommended to inspect the hybrid control system and battery temperature management.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Fixed Gear Transaxle

The single-speed transaxle works seamlessly with the dual-motor hybrid system to distribute power smoothly to all four wheels. It generally operates quietly, though buyers should ensure all gear selection functions respond properly during a test drive.

### Forward Collision and Automatic Braking

The regenerative and friction brake systems provide dependable stopping power, though forward collision warning sensors can occasionally trigger false interventions.

**False Positive Automatic Emergency Braking**
- **Symptoms:** On multiple occasions, owners have noted the vehicle issuing false collision alerts followed by sudden, unintended activation of the automatic emergency braking system.
- **Solution:** Clean the windshield camera and front radar sensor areas, and consult a technician to check for software updates related to the collision mitigation system.

## Electrical

### Parking Sensors and Driver Assistance

Electrical and sensor components manage various driver aids, though occasional glitches such as false proximity warnings have been noted by owners.

**Erroneous Parking Sensor Activation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience front parking sensors sounding alerts and beeping intermittently even when no obstacles are present in front of the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Inspect sensor cleanliness, check for loose wiring connections, or have a dealer recalibrate the sensor system.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

- **Issue:** The resulting rapid deceleration of the vehicle can increase the risk of a rear-end collision.
- **What to do:** MMNA will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the FCM-ECU software with updated software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 18V621000: Forward Collision Avoidance: Adaptive Cruise Control](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander+PHEV&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** If the safety systems do not function as intended, it can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MMNA will notify owners, and dealers will update the Hydraulic Unit-ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V403000: Seat Belts: Rear/Other:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander+PHEV&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The interference may cause the right-side seat belt buckle cover to come off, making the buckle latch inoperable and unable to restrain an occupant. An unrestrained occupant has an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** MMNA will notify owners, and dealers will modify the second-row center seat belt buckle cover, install a new warning label to the second-row seat support base, and provide a new warning label insert for the owner's manual, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V507000: Structure:Body:Hatchback/Liftgate:Support Device/Strut](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander+PHEV&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The liftgate gas spring cylinders may corrode and lose pressure, which can result in a gas spring cylinder rupture or the liftgate falling unexpectedly. A gas spring cylinder rupture or a liftgate falling unexpectedly can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MMNA will replace the left and right liftgate gas springs, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V252000: Structure:Body:Hatchback/Liftgate:Support Device/Strut](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander+PHEV&modelYear=2018)**

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Mitsubishi Outlander PHEV is a practical entry into the used plug-in hybrid market, offering everyday electric driving capability combined with traditional gasoline backup. While most systems perform reliably, buyers should carefully test the hybrid powertrain under various loads, check all parking and shifting functions, and inspect safety sensors to ensure trouble-free ownership.
