# 2019 Mitsubishi Outlander PHEV Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 Mitsubishi Outlander PHEV, buyers will find a practical plug-in hybrid crossover that pairs electric efficiency with traditional utility. While many owners enjoy daily commuting efficiency, review owner reports carefully for isolated electrical glitches, brake hardware concerns, and occasional powertrain hesitation. Prospective buyers should perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection focusing on the brakes, parking brake functionality, and electrical systems.

---

## Engine

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine (Plug-In Hybrid)

The 2.4-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine works alongside electric motors in the plug-in hybrid system to deliver efficiency and daily driving capability. Overall reliability is generally standard for modern hybrids, though owners should watch for minor electronic and throttle response quirks.

**Acceleration Hesitation and Power Lag**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report that pressing the gas pedal can produce a noticeable drag or delayed response before the vehicle accelerates up to speed.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect throttle body operation, fuel delivery, and software calibration.

**Intermittent Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly shut off completely while operating and restart a few seconds later.
- **Solution:** Inspect electrical connections, ignition components, and powertrain control modules for fault codes.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Fixed Gear / Hybrid Transaxle

The specialized hybrid transaxle routes power seamlessly between the gasoline engine and electric drive units in all-wheel-drive configurations. While generally dependable, owners have noted occasional hesitation and rare overheating reports.

**Transmission Overheating and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may display an overheating warning message and suddenly stop driving while in motion.
- **Solution:** Check transmission cooling systems, fluid levels, and diagnostic trouble codes immediately.

**Drive Engagement Hesitation and RPM Fluctuation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience hesitation when shifting into drive or reverse, accompanied by stuttering during acceleration and fluctuating RPMs.
- **Solution:** Update powertrain software or inspect hybrid transaxle control components.

## Brakes

### Parking Brake System

Braking components handle both regenerative braking and traditional friction stops, but certain mechanical and electronic parking brake parts can fail prematurely.

**Parking Brake Lockup**
- **Symptoms:** The electronic parking brake can engage or lock up unexpectedly, requiring professional diagnosis.
- **Solution:** Replace faulty parking brake modules or switches as necessary.
- **Cost:** $400 for the part

## Electrical

### Accessory Electrical and Windows

Accessory electronics generally function well, though isolated cabin electrical or window mechanism complaints have been logged by owners.

**Power Window Track Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The driver's window may become stuck in the tract and bend, preventing it from rolling up properly.
- **Solution:** Replace the damaged window regulator and track assembly.
- **Cost:** $200

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V403000: Seat Belts: Rear/Other:Buckle Assembly](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander+PHEV&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The interference may cause the right-side seat belt buckle cover to come off, making the buckle latch inoperable and unable to restrain an occupant. An unrestrained occupant has an increased risk of injury in the event of a crash.
- **What to do:** MMNA will notify owners, and dealers will modify the second-row center seat belt buckle cover, install a new warning label to the second-row seat support base, and provide a new warning label insert for the owner's manual, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 20V280000: Seat Belts:Rear/Other](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander+PHEV&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** An incorrectly assembled seat belt may not properly restrain the occupant, increasing the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** MMNA will notify owners, and dealers will inspect the right side second-row seat belt and replace it as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V507000: Structure:Body:Hatchback/Liftgate:Support Device/Strut](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander+PHEV&modelYear=2019)**
- **Issue:** The liftgate gas spring cylinders may corrode and lose pressure, which can result in a gas spring cylinder rupture or the liftgate falling unexpectedly. A gas spring cylinder rupture or a liftgate falling unexpectedly can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MMNA will replace the left and right liftgate gas springs, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 26V252000: Structure:Body:Hatchback/Liftgate:Support Device/Strut](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander+PHEV&modelYear=2019)**

- **What to do:** MMNA will replace the left and right liftgate gas springs, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
