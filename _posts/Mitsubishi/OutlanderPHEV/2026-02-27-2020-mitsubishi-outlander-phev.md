# 2020 Mitsubishi Outlander PHEV Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2020 Mitsubishi Outlander PHEV offers buyers an accessible entry point into the plug-in hybrid SUV market, combining a traditional gasoline engine with electric drive capability. While overall owner feedback highlights the efficiency and practicality of its hybrid system, prospective buyers should review available powertrain options and keep an eye on potential electrical and accessory concerns typical of early plug-in hybrid implementations.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Plug-In Hybrid

The 2.0-liter naturally aspirated 4-cylinder engine works in tandem with dual electric motors to produce approximately 190 total system horsepower. This conventional powerplant is generally robust, though buyers should verify the health of the high-voltage battery system and hybrid components.

## Transmission

### Single-Speed Fixed Gear Transaxle

The dedicated plug-in hybrid EV transaxle coordinates power delivery between the gasoline engine and electric motors with seamless power flow. It is generally reliable, with few widespread mechanical complaints reported by owners.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V507000: Structure:Body:Hatchback/Liftgate:Support Device/Strut](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Outlander+PHEV&modelYear=2020)**
- **Issue:** The liftgate gas spring cylinders may corrode and lose pressure, which can result in a gas spring cylinder rupture or the liftgate falling unexpectedly. A gas spring cylinder rupture or a liftgate falling unexpectedly can increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MMNA will replace the left and right liftgate gas springs, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Mitsubishi Outlander PHEV is a compelling choice for shoppers seeking plug-in efficiency in a crossover package. While the core mechanical and hybrid powertrains tend to be dependable, buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle's electrical systems, charging equipment, and overall battery performance prior to purchase.
