**Introduction:**

The 1998 Mitsubishi Mirage was offered with two engine and transmission options:

* **Engine:**
    * 1.3L Inline-4 (73 hp, 77 lb-ft)
* **Transmission:**
    * 5-speed Manual
    * 4-speed Automatic

The Mirage was known for its affordability, fuel efficiency, and maneuverability. However, it was also criticized for its lack of power, poor sound insulation, and cramped interior.

**Engine**

**1.3L Inline-4**

**Timing Belt Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The timing belt can fail, causing catastrophic engine damage. Symptoms include a squealing noise from the engine and a loss of power.
Solution: Replace the timing belt according to the manufacturer's recommended schedule.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-speed Manual**

**Shifter Cable Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The shifter cable can fail, making it difficult or impossible to shift gears. Symptoms include a loose or sloppy feel when shifting and difficulty getting the vehicle into gear.
Solution: Replace the shifter cable.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
Recalls: None Found

**4-speed Automatic**

**Transmission Slippage**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip or hesitate when shifting gears. Symptoms include a delay in gear engagement, erratic shifting, and a loss of power.
Solution: Replace or rebuild the transmission.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

**Strut Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The front struts can fail, causing the vehicle to bounce or sway excessively. Symptoms include a rough ride, uneven tire wear, and difficulty controlling the vehicle.
Solution: Replace the struts.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Alternator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The alternator can fail, causing the battery to drain and the vehicle to stall. Symptoms include dim lights, difficulty starting the vehicle, and a warning light on the dashboard.
Solution: Replace the alternator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Rust**
Description/Symptoms: The body can develop rust, especially in areas such as the fenders, rocker panels, and trunk lid. Symptoms include bubbling or peeling paint, rust spots, and holes in the metal.
Solution: Repair or replace the rusted parts.
Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of the damage
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**Door Panel Rattle**
Description/Symptoms: The door panels may rattle or make noise when driving. Symptoms include a buzzing or rattling sound coming from the doors.
Solution: Tighten the door panel clips or apply sound deadening material.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
Recalls: None Found

**Seat Adjustment Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The seat adjustment mechanisms may fail, making it difficult or impossible to adjust the seat. Symptoms include a loose or wobbly seat, difficulty moving the seat forward or backward, and an uneven seat position.
Solution: Repair or replace the seat adjustment mechanisms.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 1998 Mitsubishi Mirage has some reliability issues that can be costly to repair. The most severe and expensive issues include engine timing belt failure, transmission slippage, and suspension strut failure. However, the Mirage is still a relatively affordable and fuel-efficient vehicle that may be suitable for some drivers on a budget.
