**Introduction**

The 1991 Mitsubishi Mirage is a subcompact hatchback known for its affordability, fuel efficiency, and maneuverability. It was offered with two engine options: a 1.3-liter inline-four with 64 horsepower and 71 lb-ft of torque, and a 1.5-liter inline-four with 79 horsepower and 88 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options included a 4-speed automatic and a 5-speed manual. The Mirage was praised for its spacious interior, but criticized for its lack of power and basic features.

**Engine**

**1.3-Liter Engine**

* **Oil Consumption:** The engine may consume excessive oil, leading to low oil levels and potential engine damage.
    * Solution: Replace piston rings or perform a cylinder head rebuild.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**1.5-Liter Engine**

* **Head Gasket Failure:** The head gasket may fail, causing coolant and oil to leak. This can lead to engine overheating and further damage.
    * Solution: Replace the head gasket and related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Slipping:** The transmission may slip out of gear while driving, causing loss of power.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**5-Speed Manual**

* **Clutch Failure:** The clutch may wear out or fail, making it difficult or impossible to shift gears.
    * Solution: Replace the clutch and related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Strut Failure:** The front struts may fail, causing a loss of stability and handling.
    * Solution: Replace the struts and related components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator may fail, leading to a loss of power and battery drainage.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** The body of the Mirage may be prone to rust, especially in areas exposed to moisture.
    * Solution: Repair or replace rusted panels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:** The dashboard may develop cracks due to sun exposure or age.
    * Solution: Replace the dashboard.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1991 Mitsubishi Mirage is a reliable and affordable vehicle, but it is not without its issues. The most severe and costly problems include engine oil consumption, head gasket failure, and transmission slipping. However, many of these issues can be prevented with proper maintenance and repairs.
