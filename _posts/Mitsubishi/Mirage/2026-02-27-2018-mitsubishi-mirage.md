# 2018 Mitsubishi Mirage Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Mitsubishi Mirage is a budget-focused subcompact car known primarily for its exceptional fuel efficiency and affordability. While it serves as practical, low-cost transportation for city driving, used-car buyers should note a variety of owner-reported concerns ranging from occasional powertrain stalls and steering wandering to interior trim and seat belt complaints.

---

## Engine

### 1.2L 3-Cylinder

The 1.2-liter three-cylinder engine produces around 78 horsepower, prioritizing ultra-high fuel economy over brisk acceleration. It provides adequate power for urban commutes, though owners have occasionally reported unexpected stalling incidents.

**Vehicle Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly stall while driving at various speeds, during turns, or immediately after refueling.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis by a qualified technician is required to check fuel delivery, sensors, and throttle body components.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The continuously variable transmission transfers power smoothly to the front wheels while maximizing fuel efficiency. Routine fluid changes are essential to maintain long-term reliability and minimize potential drone or wear.

## Steering

### Steering and Alignment

Steering components generally feel light for easy city maneuvering, but some drivers experience high-speed tracking irregularities.

**Vehicle Pulling and Swaying**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may pull to the passenger side unexpectedly or exhibit swaying behavior at highway speeds above 60 mph.
- **Solution:** Inspect front suspension components, check wheel alignment, and verify tire wear patterns.

## Exterior

### Side Windows

Exterior panels and trim are generally durable, though occasional mechanical failures inside the door assemblies have been documented.

**Window Regulator Bracket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The driver's window may make clicking noises when operated before the internal bracket breaks, causing the glass to drop into the door jamb.
- **Solution:** Replace the broken window regulator mechanism inside the door panel.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Standard safety equipment includes multiple airbags and active restraint systems, though owner feedback highlights minor mechanical nuisances with seat belt positioning.

**Seat Belt Trapping and Warning Light**
- **Symptoms:** The seat belt can become stuck underneath the vehicle seat, triggering a persistent warning light on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Inspect the seat belt anchor area, free any trapped webbing, and have the seat belt sensor checked if the light remains illuminated.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V686000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Mirage&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The internal acceleration sensor in the Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) Electronic Control Unit (ECU) can misinterpret vibrations such as those from poor road conditions or a flat tire to be a sensor error thereby disabling the air bags from deploying in a crash. Air bags that do not deploy as designed in a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MMNA will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the SRS-ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Mitsubishi Mirage is a straightforward, budget-friendly commuter vehicle that excels at saving fuel. While its powertrain and mechanical layout are simple to maintain, prospective buyers should thoroughly inspect the vehicle for steering alignment, window regulator operation, and proper seat belt functionality before purchasing.
