# 2018 Mitsubishi Mirage Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2018 Mitsubishi Mirage is a budget-focused subcompact car designed primarily for high fuel efficiency and affordable city commuting. As a used car purchase, it appeals to budget-conscious drivers, though owners have reported occasional mechanical and electrical quirks. Reviewing specific components and maintenance history can help prospective buyers ensure reliable performance.

---

## Engine

### 1.2L 3-cylinder Engine

The 2018 Mitsubishi Mirage comes equipped with a 1.2-liter three-cylinder engine producing approximately 78 horsepower and 74 lb-ft of torque. It prioritizes fuel economy over performance and generally offers simple mechanical reliability, though occasional owner reports note stalling incidents.

**Unexpected Engine Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may experience sudden engine stalls while driving at various speeds or after refueling.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the fuel system, throttle body, and engine sensors for faults.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The continuously variable transmission transfers power smoothly to the front wheels while optimizing fuel efficiency. No widespread recurring failure trends are highlighted in current owner complaints.

### 5-speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 5-speed manual transmission offers a direct shifting experience for base trim models. It maintains a straightforward mechanical layout with minimal reported drivetrain issues.

## Steering

### Steering and Alignment

Steering and alignment issues can occasionally lead to uneven tire wear and vehicle pulling if suspension components fall out of spec.

**Vehicle Pulling and Rapid Tire Wear**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may pull to one side unexpectedly, accompanied by rapid or uneven front tire wear.
- **Solution:** Check front-end alignment, steering components, and suspension geometry, and replace worn tires as needed.

## Exterior

### Headlights

Electrical concerns are generally minor, though owners have noted limited headlight performance during nighttime driving.

**Dim Headlight Illumination**
- **Symptoms:** Headlights fail to illuminate the road brightly at night, with limited visibility and no simple adjustment mechanism.
- **Solution:** Inspect headlight bulb condition and housing alignment; upgrading to authorized brighter bulbs may improve visibility.

## Safety

### Airbags and SRS

Safety systems generally function as intended, though sporadic sensor warnings can require professional diagnostic attention.

**SRS Warning Light Illumination**
- **Symptoms:** The Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) warning light illuminates on the dashboard upon starting the vehicle.
- **Solution:** Have a dealer or specialist scan the airbag control module for diagnostic trouble codes to identify faulty sensors or wiring.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V686000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Mirage&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** The internal acceleration sensor in the Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) Electronic Control Unit (ECU) can misinterpret vibrations such as those from poor road conditions or a flat tire to be a sensor error thereby disabling the air bags from deploying in a crash. Air bags that do not deploy as designed in a crash increase the risk of injury.
- **What to do:** MMNA will notify owners, and dealers will reprogram the SRS-ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Mitsubishi Mirage is a straightforward, highly economical choice for buyers prioritizing low fuel costs and simple transportation. While most owners experience trouble-free daily driving, used-car shoppers should carefully inspect steering alignment, tire wear history, and electrical components before purchasing.
