# 2020 Mitsubishi Mirage Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 Mitsubishi Mirage, buyers are looking at an exceptionally budget-friendly and fuel-efficient subcompact car. Powered by a modest three-cylinder engine, it prioritizes gas mileage over brisk performance. While owner complaints in public databases remain sparse for this model year, prospective buyers should still inspect basic wear items like brakes during a pre-purchase test drive.

---

## Engine

### 1.2L 3-Cylinder Engine

The 2020 Mirage features a 1.2-liter three-cylinder engine producing approximately 78 horsepower and 74 lb-ft of torque. This naturally aspirated motor is simple in design and generally reliable, though acceleration is leisurely.

## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission

A 5-speed manual transmission is available on base trims, offering a traditional shifting experience and straightforward mechanical durability.

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

A continuously variable transmission (CVT) is paired with the 1.2L engine on most trims to maximize fuel efficiency. Buyers should listen for unusual whining or hesitation during test drives.

## Brakes

### Hydraulic Service Brakes

Basic hydraulic service brakes handle stopping duties, though isolated owner reports indicate potential friction wear concerns.

**Grinding Noise and Burning Odor**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers may experience an abnormal burning odor and grinding noises emanating from the brakes during normal operation at various speeds.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the brake pads, rotors, and calipers for premature wear, friction material binding, or component failure.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Mitsubishi Mirage is a straightforward, highly efficient commuter car with very few reported owner complaints in early service life. While public databases show isolated friction concerns regarding the braking system, overall mechanical trouble spots appear minimal. Buyers should perform a standard used-car inspection, paying close attention to brake wear and transmission operation before finalizing a purchase.
