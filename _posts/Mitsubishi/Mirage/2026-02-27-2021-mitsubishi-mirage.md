# 2021 Mitsubishi Mirage Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Mitsubishi Mirage offers buyers an ultra-affordable and highly fuel-efficient subcompact option, making it popular for budget-conscious commuters. While basic transportation is its primary strong suit, prospective buyers should inspect individual vehicle history and ensure all standard safety and electrical systems function properly before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 1.2L 3-Cylinder Engine

The 1.2-liter three-cylinder engine produces approximately 78 horsepower and 74 lb-ft of torque. It prioritizes fuel economy over performance and generally proves dependable for daily driving if routine maintenance is kept up.

## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 5-speed manual transmission is paired with base trims and offers a straightforward, mechanical shifting experience. It tends to be mechanically robust with fewer complex failure points than automatic alternatives.

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The Continuously Variable Transmission is designed to maximize fuel efficiency across the model lineup. Buyers should test drive CVT-equipped models thoroughly to ensure smooth power delivery and responsive acceleration.

## Safety

### Air Bag Deployment

Owner complaints concerning safety restraint systems highlight the importance of verifying airbag functionality and checking vehicle collision history.

**Front Air Bags Failed to Deploy**
- **Symptoms:** An owner reported that the front of the vehicle sustained significant impact during a collision, but the front airbags failed to deploy.
- **Solution:** Have the vehicle's Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) inspected and diagnosed by a qualified repair facility to ensure sensor and module integrity.
