The 2024 Mitsubishi Mirage is a subcompact hatchback known for its affordability and fuel efficiency. It is powered by a 1.2-liter 3-cylinder engine producing 76 horsepower and 74 lb-ft of torque, paired with a Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT). People generally appreciate the Mirage's low price point, excellent fuel economy, and maneuverability in urban environments. Common dislikes include its limited power, basic interior, and relatively noisy engine.

**Engine**

**1.2L 3-Cylinder Engine**

*   **Excessive Engine Vibration**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported experiencing excessive engine vibration, particularly at idle and low speeds. This can sometimes be accompanied by unusual noises.
    *   Solution: The fix can range from replacing worn engine mounts to more complex engine repairs or adjustments. Start by inspecting the engine mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Engine Knocking or Pinging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have noted an engine knocking or pinging sound, especially under acceleration or when using lower-grade fuel.
    *   Solution: Try using a higher-octane fuel or having the engine timing checked. In some cases, it may require a software update or more in-depth engine diagnosis.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)**

*   **Hesitation or Jerking During Acceleration**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported hesitation or jerking, particularly during initial acceleration or when trying to pass.
    *   Solution: A CVT fluid change is often recommended as a first step. In more severe cases, the CVT may require recalibration or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Unusual Noises from Transmission**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Whining or humming sounds from the transmission, especially at certain speeds, have been reported.
    *   Solution: This may indicate a problem with the CVT's internal components and usually requires professional diagnosis and repair, potentially including a CVT rebuild or replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing or Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Reports of the infotainment system freezing, crashing, or becoming unresponsive.
    *   Solution: Try a system reset. If the problem persists, a software update from the dealer or replacement of the infotainment unit may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature battery drain, especially if the vehicle sits for extended periods.
    *   Solution: Check for parasitic draws by aftermarket accessories or faulty electrical components. A new battery might also be needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2024 Mitsubishi Mirage, while appealing for its affordability, does have some reported issues. The most notable concerns involve the engine and transmission, with reports of vibrations, knocking, hesitation, and unusual noises. Electrical issues with the infotainment system and battery drain also surface occasionally. The severity of these issues can range from minor annoyances to potentially costly repairs. While issues do exist, it is important to remember that these are simply reported issues. Not every vehicle will have issues, and some may have other unreported issues.
