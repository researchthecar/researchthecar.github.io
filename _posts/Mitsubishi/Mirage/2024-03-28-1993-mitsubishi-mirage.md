**Introduction:**

The 1993 Mitsubishi Mirage is a compact hatchback known for its fuel efficiency and affordability. It comes with a 1.5-liter engine and a 5-speed manual or 3-speed automatic transmission. Despite its positive qualities, the Mirage has encountered several issues that owners have reported.

**Engine**

**1.5-liter Engine**

* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** Oil leakage from the joint between the valve cover and the cylinder head. It can cause low oil levels and engine overheating.
    * Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Spark Plugs Fouling:** Premature failure of spark plugs due to oil buildup. It can lead to engine misfires and reduced performance.
    * Solution: Inspect and replace spark plugs as necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $30 - $100+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual Transmission**

* **Gearbox Noise:** Excessive noise from the gearbox, particularly during shifts or acceleration.
    * Solution: Inspect and repair or replace the gearbox as necessary.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found

**3-Speed Automatic Transmission**

* **Slipping Gears:** Inability of the transmission to engage or hold gears properly. It can lead to loss of power and difficulty driving.
    * Solution: Rebuild or replace the transmission.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Weak Front Struts:** Premature wear or failure of the front struts. It can cause poor handling and alignment issues.
    * Solution: Replace the front struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Premature failure of the alternator. It can lead to a loss of electrical power and a dead battery.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery Drain:** Excessive battery drain, even when the vehicle is turned off. It can be caused by electrical shorts or faulty components.
    * Solution: Inspect and repair or replace the affected components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust on Body Panels:** Corrosion and rust on the body panels, particularly around the fenders and wheel wells.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the rusted panels.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of the damage
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Knobs and Controls Failure:** Malfunctioning or sticking dashboard knobs and controls, such as climate control or radio buttons.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the affected components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Seat Fabric Tearing:** Premature tearing or fraying of the seat fabric.
    * Solution: Repair or replace the seat upholstery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Overall Summary:**

The 1993 Mitsubishi Mirage has encountered several issues that can be costly to fix. The most severe and expensive issues include transmission problems, such as slipping gears with the automatic transmission and excessive gearbox noise with the manual transmission. While the engine issues are generally less severe, they can still lead to reduced performance and increased maintenance costs. Other common issues include suspension wear, electrical problems, rust on body panels, and interior component failures.
