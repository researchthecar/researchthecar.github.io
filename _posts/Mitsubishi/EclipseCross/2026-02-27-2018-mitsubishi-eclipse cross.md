# 2018 Mitsubishi Eclipse Cross

### Overview
The 2018 Mitsubishi Eclipse Cross is a compact crossover SUV that offers a distinctive styling and a comfortable ride. It generally presents a decent reliability record for its class, with most owners reporting satisfaction. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas where potential issues have been noted. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns, excluding routine maintenance and normal wear and tear, to provide a clear picture for potential owners and current users. We will examine the engine, transmission, suspension, electrical systems, exterior, and interior components.

---


## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged 4-Cylinder (4B40)
**Oil Dilution**
- **Symptoms:** Fuel smell in the engine oil, increased oil level, potential for reduced lubrication effectiveness.
- **Solution:** Software updates to optimize fuel injection and combustion, or in some cases, more frequent oil changes may be recommended.
- **Cost:** $0 - $150 (Software update/inspection)


## Transmission

### CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Slight delay in acceleration, occasional jerky movements during gear changes or at low speeds.
- **Solution:** Software recalibration of the transmission control module, or in severe cases, transmission fluid flush or replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400 (Software update/fluid service)

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Connectivity Glitches**
- **Symptoms:** Bluetooth connectivity issues, touchscreen unresponsiveness, Apple CarPlay/Android Auto intermittently failing.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the infotainment unit, or a system reset.
- **Cost:** $0 - $200 (Software update/reset)


### Battery

### Summary Verdict
The 2018 Mitsubishi Eclipse Cross generally offers a reliable ownership experience, with the most frequently cited technical concerns revolving around the 1.5L turbocharged engine's oil dilution and occasional hesitations with the CVT transmission. Electrical issues, particularly with the infotainment system's connectivity, have also been noted. However, these issues are often addressable through software updates or routine servicing. The suspension, exterior, and interior components have shown good durability. Overall, while not entirely immune to minor technical quirks, the Eclipse Cross is considered a reasonably dependable vehicle in its segment, provided potential buyers are aware of and prepared for these specific potential issues.
