# 2023 Mitsubishi Eclipse Cross Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2023 Mitsubishi Eclipse Cross, understanding its powertrain setup and owner-reported experiences can help guide your inspection. While owner feedback is limited, reviewing specific mechanical systems and potential electrical or safety anomalies will ensure you make an informed purchase.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 1.5-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 152 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate power for daily commuting, though buyers should verify proper turbo maintenance history during pre-purchase inspections.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

This continuously variable transmission channels power to either the front wheels or an available all-wheel-drive system. Smooth operation and responsive acceleration are typical, but a test drive is recommended to check for any hesitation or unusual noise.

### Front and Rear Differentials

Drivetrain components like differentials require periodic fluid checks to ensure long-term durability.

**Differential Wear and Failure**
- **Symptoms:** An independent mechanic identified failing front and rear differentials during a routine service appointment.
- **Solution:** Inspect differential fluid levels, look for leaks, and listen for whining or grinding noises coming from the axles during a test drive.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Electrical complaints are infrequent, but intermittent accessory-related glitches should be checked closely.

**Motive Power Loss During Accessory Use**
- **Symptoms:** An owner reported that activating the air conditioning while driving led to an unexpected loss of motive power without any dashboard warning lights.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the electrical charging system, battery connections, and engine control modules for faults.

## Safety

### Air Bags

Safety system anomalies are rare owner reports, but a thorough diagnostic scan is always recommended before buying.

**Inadvertent Air Bag Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** An owner experienced an unexpected deployment of the passenger-side air bag while driving under normal conditions, resulting in loss of vehicle control.
- **Solution:** Ensure the Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) diagnostic trouble codes are scanned and that the airbag control module has no active faults.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Mitsubishi Eclipse Cross generally offers a straightforward ownership experience, though isolated owner reports highlight potential concerns regarding electrical accessories and differential wear. Prospective buyers should prioritize a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection, checking the powertrain, differentials, and electronic systems thoroughly.
