# 2018 Mitsubishi Eclipse Cross Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2018 Mitsubishi Eclipse Cross, prospective buyers will find a stylish compact crossover backed by standard powertrain configurations. While most owners enjoy a comfortable ride and modern amenities, driver feedback submitted to safety agencies highlights several electrical and handling anomalies to watch out for during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 1.5-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 152 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque, offering adequate power for daily commuting. Owners generally report standard operating reliability, though occasional electronic management system glitches have been logged.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The continuously variable transmission routes power to either the front wheels or the available Super All-Wheel Control (SAWC) system. It delivers smooth acceleration and respectable fuel economy under normal driving conditions.

## Steering

### Steering and Stability Control

Steering and stability control components generally perform adequately, but intermittent electronic sensor errors can temporarily impact driver assist features.

**Stability Control Service Warnings and Steering Lockups**
- **Symptoms:** Owners note that the Active Stability Control (ASC) service indicator can illuminate, potentially disabling vehicle operation temporarily, with rare owner reports of sudden steering stiffness.
- **Solution:** Check for stored diagnostic trouble codes in the stability and steering modules and verify software updates are current.

## Electrical

### Battery and Charging System

Electrical system complaints primarily center around battery drain, accessory malfunctions, and intermittent dashboard warning lights.

**Premature Battery Drain and Stalling**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported unexpected battery drainage requiring multiple attempts to start the vehicle, alongside rare instances of sudden stalling while driving.
- **Solution:** Have the battery, alternator, and electrical parasitic draw tested by a qualified mechanic to ensure proper charging system function.

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Mitsubishi Eclipse Cross offers distinctive styling and practical utility for used car buyers, but it is wise to thoroughly test all electrical accessories and safety systems before purchasing. Ensuring that the battery, charging system, and stability control sensors are in top working order will help mitigate common owner-reported headaches.
