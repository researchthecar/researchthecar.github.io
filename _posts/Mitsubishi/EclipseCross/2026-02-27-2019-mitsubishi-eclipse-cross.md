# 2019 Mitsubishi Eclipse Cross Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2019 Mitsubishi Eclipse Cross, buyers will find a stylish compact crossover with modern features and distinctive styling. While many owners enjoy their vehicles, review of driver reports submitted to safety authorities highlights a few areas worth noting, particularly concerning powertrain operation and electrical system stability.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2019 Eclipse Cross is powered by a 1.5-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine producing approximately 152 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate daily power, though buyers should monitor fluid levels and check for proper turbo performance during a pre-purchase inspection.

**Excessive Oil Consumption**
- **Symptoms:** Some owners report that the engine consumes notable amounts of oil between routine service intervals.
- **Solution:** Monitor oil levels regularly between changes and consult a mechanic if consumption rates exceed normal manufacturer thresholds.

**Motive Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have experienced unexpected losses in acceleration or engine power while attempting to merge or drive at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Have the powertrain control module, turbocharger wastegate, and fuel delivery systems inspected by a qualified technician.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) with 8-Step Sport Mode

Equipped with a continuously variable transmission paired with front-wheel drive or all-wheel control, this setup offers smooth cruising. Prospective buyers should thoroughly test drive the transmission for hesitation, slipping, or unusual noises.

**Sudden Transmission Failure**
- **Symptoms:** In rare instances, owners have reported that the vehicle fails to engage gears or move after being parked, sometimes without prior warning lights.
- **Solution:** Requires professional diagnosis, which may involve comprehensive transmission rebuild or replacement.

## Brakes

### Service Brakes

The braking system provides standard stopping power, but regular inspections are recommended to catch premature wear and electronic sensor faults early.

**Premature Brake Wear and ABS Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Some drivers report early brake component wear or intermittent failures of the anti-lock braking system, brake assist, and hill start assist features.
- **Solution:** Inspect brake pads, rotors, and ABS module sensors regularly, and replace worn friction materials promptly.
- **Cost:** Varies by parts and labor

## Steering

### Steering and Alignment

The electric power steering system delivers light handling suitable for city driving; ensuring proper alignment during a test drive helps confirm straight-line stability.

**Pulling and Swaying**
- **Symptoms:** Some drivers have noted the vehicle pulling to one side or swaying slightly from left to right while driving straight.
- **Solution:** Perform a professional wheel alignment check and inspect steering components for premature wear or improper adjustment.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

The vehicle relies heavily on integrated electronics for powertrain management and cabin accessories, making a thorough electrical diagnostic scan wise before purchase.

**Electrical Shutdown**
- **Symptoms:** Owner reports note rare incidents where the vehicle's electrical system shut down during operation, potentially affecting steering and engine function.
- **Solution:** Check the battery, alternator connections, and main wiring harnesses for loose grounds or water intrusion.

## Summary Verdict

Overall, the 2019 Mitsubishi Eclipse Cross offers a comfortable ride and modern amenities, but used car shoppers should perform a rigorous inspection. Pay close attention to engine health, fluid consumption, transmission responsiveness, and electronic systems to ensure a trouble-free ownership experience.
