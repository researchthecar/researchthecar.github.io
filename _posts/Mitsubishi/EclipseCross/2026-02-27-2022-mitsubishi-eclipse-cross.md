# 2022 Mitsubishi Eclipse Cross Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2022 Mitsubishi Eclipse Cross offers a distinctive coupe-like stance and standard all-wheel drive, appealing to shoppers looking for a budget-friendly compact crossover. While most mechanical systems prove generally dependable during routine commutes, prospective buyers should review known owner complaints regarding intermittent electrical issues and occasional powertrain hesitation before making a purchase.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged Inline-4

This 1.5-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 152 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate power for daily driving and balanced efficiency, though owners should maintain regular servicing to ensure long-term turbocharger health.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

Paired with standard all-wheel drive, this continuously variable transmission delivers seamless gear ratios for efficiency. Buyers should thoroughly test drive the vehicle to verify smooth acceleration response.

**Transmission Hesitation and Slippage**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report occasional jerking or a feeling of slipping when pressing the accelerator pedal during acceleration.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified technician inspect the transmission fluid condition and update the powertrain control module software if necessary.

## Electrical

### Infotainment Touchscreen

Electrical owner feedback primarily highlights center console display concerns and occasional accessory anomalies.

**Unresponsive Touchscreen Display**
- **Symptoms:** The center infotainment screen develops dead zones or unresponsive areas, making it difficult to control climate and navigation features.
- **Solution:** Take the vehicle to a dealership to check for software updates or replace the touchscreen display unit if hardware failure is confirmed.
