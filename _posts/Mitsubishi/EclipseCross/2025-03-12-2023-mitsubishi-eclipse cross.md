The 2023 Mitsubishi Eclipse Cross is a compact SUV that offers a blend of style and practicality. It comes standard with a 1.5-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine producing 152 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque, paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). Some owners appreciate the Eclipse Cross's distinctive design and comfortable ride, while others find the engine performance lacking and the infotainment system dated. Common criticisms often revolve around the CVT's responsiveness and the vehicle's overall fuel economy.

**Engine**

**1.5L Turbocharged Four-Cylinder**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported higher-than-expected oil consumption, requiring more frequent oil top-offs between scheduled oil changes. Symptoms include low oil level warnings and the need to add oil every few thousand miles.
    *   Solution: Closely monitor oil levels and follow recommended oil change intervals. If consumption is excessive, consult a mechanic for potential issues such as worn piston rings or valve seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies greatly depending on the cause and repair needed. Could range from a few hundred dollars for minor repairs to several thousand for a major engine overhaul or replacement.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission)**

*   **Hesitation/Jerky Acceleration**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners experience hesitation or jerky acceleration, especially at lower speeds. This can manifest as a delay in response when pressing the accelerator or an uneven feeling during acceleration.
    *   Solution: Some dealerships may offer software updates to improve CVT performance. In more severe cases, CVT replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $4,000 - $8,000+ for a CVT replacement.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may occasionally freeze, become unresponsive, or crash, requiring a restart. This can affect navigation, audio, and other connected features.
    *   Solution: Try performing a system reset. Check for software updates and install them if available. If the problem persists, the head unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $1200+ depending on the complexity.
    * Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the battery draining quickly, especially if the vehicle is not driven for extended periods.
    *   Solution: Ensure all accessories are turned off when the vehicle is not in use. Have the battery and charging system tested. Consider a battery tender if the vehicle sits for long periods.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+ to replace battery
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Creaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported rattling or creaking noises emanating from the dashboard, door panels, or other interior trim pieces. These noises can be intermittent and are often more noticeable on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Identify the source of the noise and try tightening any loose screws or fasteners. Applying felt tape or foam padding to contact points can help dampen the noise.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 - $200+ depending on complexity.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks around the sunroof area, especially during heavy rain.
    *   Solution: Check the sunroof drains for clogs and clear them if necessary. Inspect the sunroof seal for damage and replace if needed.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

No Issues Found

**Summary**

The 2023 Mitsubishi Eclipse Cross has some common issues, mostly centered around the CVT transmission and electrical system. Owners have reported some instances of excessive oil consumption with the 1.5L engine. The electrical gremlins tend to be with the Infotainment system and battery drain. Some interior rattling is also common. Most of the issues seem to be minor, and the potential costs for the issues are varied.
