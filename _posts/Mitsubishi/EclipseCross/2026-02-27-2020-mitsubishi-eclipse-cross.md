# 2020 Mitsubishi Eclipse Cross Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 Mitsubishi Eclipse Cross, buyers will find a stylish compact SUV equipped with a standard turbocharged four-cylinder engine and a continuous variable transmission. While owner-reported complaints for this model year are relatively limited, prospective buyers should review available vehicle history and perform a thorough pre-purchase inspection to check for potential powertrain anomalies or electrical quirks reported by drivers.

---

## Engine

### 1.5L Turbocharged Inline-4

The 2020 Eclipse Cross features a 1.5-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine producing approximately 152 horsepower and 184 lb-ft of torque. It provides adequate power for daily commuting, though buyers should verify smooth throttle response during a test drive.

**Engine Stalling and Acceleration Hesitation**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers have reported instances where the vehicle hesitates, makes abnormal thumping noises, and unexpectedly loses motive power while driving at highway speeds.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis by a qualified technician is required to check fuel delivery, turbo operation, and electronic engine management systems.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

A continuously variable transmission channels power to either front-wheel drive or the available All-Wheel Control (AWC) system. Routine fluid inspections are recommended to ensure long-term operational health.

## Brakes

### Electronic Shifter and Parking Brake

Controls inside the cabin generally operate as expected, but shifters and electronic parking brakes should be tested carefully during evaluation.

**Unintended Shifter Movement and Brake Engagement**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have noted that the electronic parking brake can engage unexpectedly during transit, and the gear selector may slip easily out of drive.
- **Solution:** Have the shifter linkage and electronic parking brake switch inspected and adjusted by a professional mechanic.

## Exterior

### Panoramic Sunroof

Interior appointments and comfort features offer modern convenience, though cabin electronics and sunroof mechanisms should be tested prior to purchase.

**Sunroof Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The sunroof motor may run while the glass panel itself remains stuck and fails to open or close properly.
- **Solution:** Inspect the sunroof tracks for obstructions and have the motor or track assembly serviced or replaced as needed.

## Safety

### Air Bag System

Safety equipment includes standard airbags and driver assistance technologies, though buyers should verify that all occupant protection systems are fully functional.

**Failure to Deploy in Frontal Impact**
- **Symptoms:** An owner reported that the frontal airbags failed to deploy during a high-speed frontal collision.
- **Solution:** Ensure that the vehicle has a clean history without unresolved collision damage or diagnostic trouble codes within the Supplemental Restraint System (SRS).

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Mitsubishi Eclipse Cross presents a reasonable used vehicle option for buyers seeking an affordable compact crossover with modern styling. While isolated owner complaints highlight potential concerns regarding engine hesitation, shifter operation, and sunroof mechanisms, a comprehensive test drive and pre-purchase inspection remain the best ways to ensure a dependable ownership experience.
