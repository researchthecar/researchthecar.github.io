The 2019 Mitsubishi Mirage G4 is a subcompact sedan known for its fuel efficiency and affordability. It comes equipped with a 1.2-liter three-cylinder engine producing 78 horsepower and 74 lb-ft of torque. This engine is available with either a 5-speed manual transmission or a Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT). Owners often appreciate the car's low price and excellent gas mileage, making it an economical choice for city driving. However, common complaints include its underpowered engine and basic interior features.

**Engine**

**1.2L 3-Cylinder Engine**

*   **Engine Mount Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive vibration, noise, and movement from the engine. The engine mounts may appear cracked or broken.
    *   Solution: Replace the damaged engine mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Low oil levels requiring frequent top-offs, blue smoke from the exhaust, and potential engine damage.
    *   Solution: Investigate for leaks, perform a compression test, and potentially replace piston rings or the entire engine if wear is severe.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT Transmission**

*   **CVT Transmission Jerking/Shuddering**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The vehicle jerks or shudders during acceleration, especially at lower speeds. This can be intermittent and worsen over time.
    *   Solution: Perform a CVT fluid change. If the issue persists, the CVT valve body may need to be replaced, or in severe cases, the entire transmission.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 (fluid change) - $3000+ (transmission replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **CVT Transmission Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Complete loss of power, inability to shift, or loud grinding noises coming from the transmission. Warning lights may illuminate on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replace the CVT transmission.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3000 - $5000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Suspension Components**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises from the suspension, especially when going over bumps. The vehicle may handle poorly, with excessive body roll. Shocks/Struts may have fluid leaking.
    *   Solution: Replace worn shocks, struts, bushings, or sway bar links.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Dead Battery**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Car won't start, clicking sound when trying to start, dim lights.
    *   Solution: Jump start, replace battery, check for parasitic draw
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Interior**

*   **Flimsy Interior Trim**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loose or easily broken interior trim pieces, especially around the dashboard and door panels.
    *   Solution: Replace or repair the broken trim pieces.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $300+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2019 Mitsubishi Mirage G4, while economical, can suffer from a range of issues. The most concerning are related to the CVT transmission, which can experience jerking or complete failure. Engine mounts and excessive oil consumption can also be potential problems. Suspension components wear is typical for most cars but can lead to poor handling if not addressed. Interior trim can be flimsy. The cost of repairs can vary significantly, with transmission replacements being the most expensive.
