# 2020 Mitsubishi Mirage G4 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2020 Mitsubishi Mirage G4, buyers are typically looking for maximum fuel efficiency and straightforward daily transportation. While owner-reported complaints are relatively sparse, reviewing specific mechanical and electrical feedback helps paint a clearer picture of what to look out for during a pre-purchase inspection.

---

## Engine

### 1.2L 3-Cylinder Engine

This 1.2-liter three-cylinder engine produces approximately 78 horsepower and 74 lb-ft of torque. It prioritizes fuel economy over rapid acceleration, making it well-suited for city commuting.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The continuously variable transmission is paired with front-wheel drive to maximize efficiency across various trims. Buyers should thoroughly test drive this unit to ensure smooth power delivery.

**Transmission Hard Pulling**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may exhibit heavy resistance or feel like it is struggling to pull forward while driving.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the transmission fluid condition, pressure, and internal components for wear.

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission

The traditional 5-speed manual transmission offers a simpler mechanical alternative to the CVT on base trims. It generally provides reliable operation if the clutch is maintained properly.

## Electrical

### Electrical System

Owner reports highlight occasional electrical issues, though widespread systemic failures remain uncommon.

**Unexpected Engine Stall**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may unexpectedly stall while driving at low speeds, such as when making a turn, without any prior warning lights illuminating.
- **Solution:** Check the battery connections, alternator output, and electronic control modules for diagnostic trouble codes or software updates.

## Safety

### Seat Belts

Safety equipment should be carefully inspected to ensure proper operation, as owner feedback notes isolated concerns during impacts.

**Seat Belt Restraint Failure**
- **Symptoms:** A driver or passenger may report that the seat belt failed to adequately restrain them during a collision.
- **Solution:** Inspect seat belt pretensioners, latch mechanisms, and webbing for proper function and signs of wear.

## Summary Verdict

The 2020 Mitsubishi Mirage G4 is an economical choice for budget-conscious drivers, though prospective buyers should conduct a thorough test drive to check for CVT hesitation and electrical stability. Always perform a pre-purchase inspection to verify the condition of the powertrain and safety systems before finalizing your purchase.
