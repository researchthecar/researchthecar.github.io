The 2024 Mitsubishi Mirage G4 is a subcompact sedan known for its affordability and fuel efficiency. It comes equipped with a 1.2-liter 3-cylinder engine producing 78 horsepower and 74 lb-ft of torque, paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). Owners often appreciate the Mirage G4's low price and excellent gas mileage, making it a practical choice for budget-conscious buyers. However, common complaints include its underpowered engine, basic interior, and limited features compared to competitors.

**Engine**

**1.2L 3-Cylinder**

*   **Excessive Engine Vibration**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report excessive engine vibration, particularly at idle or low speeds. This can be felt through the steering wheel, seats, and floor.
    *   Solution: In some cases, this has been resolved by replacing the engine mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**CVT**

*   **CVT Hesitation/Jerking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported hesitation or jerking during acceleration, particularly from a stop. The CVT may feel sluggish or unresponsive.
    *   Solution: A software update might address the issue. In more severe cases, CVT replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 (Software Update), $3000 - $5000 (Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rough Ride Quality**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Many owners complain about the harsh ride quality, especially over bumps and uneven road surfaces. The suspension struggles to absorb impacts.
    *   Solution: While not a direct fix, aftermarket suspension upgrades (shocks/struts) might slightly improve the ride.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system (if equipped) may freeze, crash, or become unresponsive.
    *   Solution: Performing a system reset or software update may resolve the issue.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (Reset/Update), $200 - $500+ (Replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Failing Backup Camera**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported issues with the backup camera, including it not turning on, showing a distorted image, or failing intermittently.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the backup camera.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Weak Headlights**
    * Description/Symptoms: Owners complain about the poor illumination of the headlights at night.
    * Solution: Replacement with aftermarket headlights can improve visibility.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Rattling/Squeaking Noises**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners often report various rattling and squeaking noises emanating from the dashboard, door panels, or other interior components.
    *   Solution: Locating and securing the source of the noise. (Difficult to pinpoint and often not permanently resolved)
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200+ (Labor, if a mechanic attempts to fix)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2024 Mitsubishi Mirage G4 offers an affordable entry into the car market, but it's not without its flaws. While engine and transmission issues are relatively limited, the potential for CVT problems should be considered. Suspension, electrical, and interior issues are more common and can impact the overall driving experience. The severity of these issues ranges from minor annoyances (rattles) to potentially costly repairs (CVT replacement). Prospective buyers should carefully inspect the vehicle and be aware of these potential problems.
