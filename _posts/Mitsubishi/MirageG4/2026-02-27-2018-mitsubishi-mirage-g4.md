# 2018 Mitsubishi Mirage G4 Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2018 Mitsubishi Mirage G4, buyers will find an ultra-compact sedan designed primarily for maximum fuel efficiency and budget-friendly daily driving. While its modest powertrain keeps operating costs low, prospective owners should review owner-reported feedback regarding electronics, body hardware, and safety systems before purchasing.

---

## Engine

### 1.2L 3-Cylinder Engine

The 1.2-liter three-cylinder engine produces 78 horsepower and 74 lb-ft of torque, paired with a lightweight chassis to prioritize fuel economy over brisk acceleration. Owners occasionally note vibration or performance hesitation when auxiliary loads like the air conditioning are engaged.

## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual Transmission

The standard five-speed manual transmission offers a direct mechanical connection and straightforward operation for drivers who prefer shifting their own gears. It generally provides dependable service when normal clutch maintenance is observed.

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The available continuously variable transmission optimizes gear ratios for fuel efficiency during city and highway commuting. Buyers should test-drive CVT models thoroughly to ensure smooth operation without excessive noise or hesitation.

## Exterior

### Window Regulator and Brackets

Exterior body hardware is generally durable, though isolated mechanical issues with window regulators have been reported by owners.

**Driver Window Bracket Failure**
- **Symptoms:** The driver's window may make clicking noises during operation, eventually causing the bracket inside the door to break and the glass to drop into the door panel.
- **Solution:** Inspect and replace the broken window regulator bracket and secure the door glass properly.

## Safety

### Seat Belts and Airbags

Safety equipment is critical to verify during a pre-purchase inspection, as a small number of owner complaints highlight potential seat belt and airbag operational faults.

**Seat Belt and Airbag Operation Concerns**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported instances where seat belts became stuck under seats or failed to lock during sudden impacts, alongside complaints of airbags failing to deploy in collisions.
- **Solution:** Have a certified professional inspect the seat belt latch mechanisms, anchorage points, and Supplemental Restraint System (SRS) diagnostics.

---

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 19V330000: Air Bags](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Mirage+G4&modelYear=2018)**
- **Issue:** Air bags that do not deploy increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** MMNA notified owners, and dealers will reprogram the SRS-ECU software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

The 2018 Mitsubishi Mirage G4 serves as an economical commuter car with straightforward mechanical systems, but used-car buyers should perform a thorough hands-on inspection. Pay close attention to window operation, electrical accessory stability, and safety restraint components before finalizing a purchase.
