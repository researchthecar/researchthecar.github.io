**1994 Mitsubishi Precis**

The 1994 Mitsubishi Precis is a compact car that was available with a 1.8-liter engine and a 4-speed automatic transmission or a 5-speed manual transmission. The 1.8-liter engine produces 116 horsepower and 128 lb-ft of torque.

The Precis is known for its reliability, fuel efficiency, and sporty handling. However, it has also been reported to have some issues.

**Engine**

* 1.8-liter Engine

**Timing Belt Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The timing belt can fail, causing the engine to stop running. This can be a catastrophic failure, as it can damage the valves and pistons. The symptoms of a failing timing belt include a ticking or rattling noise, difficulty starting the engine, and a loss of power.
Solution: The timing belt should be replaced according to the manufacturer's recommended maintenance schedule.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Intake Valve Stem Seals Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The intake valve stem seals can fail, causing oil to leak into the combustion chamber. This can lead to smoking from the exhaust, increased oil consumption, and a decrease in power. The symptoms of failed intake valve stem seals include blue smoke from the exhaust, a burning smell from the engine, and a decrease in oil pressure.
Solution: The intake valve stem seals should be replaced.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* 4-speed Automatic Transmission

**Transmission Slipping**
Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip out of gear, especially when shifting under load. This can be a dangerous issue, as it can lead to the vehicle losing power and control. The symptoms of transmission slipping include difficulty shifting gears, a delay in acceleration, and a loss of power.
Solution: The transmission may need to be rebuilt or replaced.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $3,000+
Recalls: None Found

* 5-speed Manual Transmission

**No notable issues found**

**Suspension**

**Strut Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The struts can fail, causing the vehicle to bounce and handle poorly. The symptoms of failed struts include a clunking noise when going over bumps, a decrease in ride quality, and a loss of control.
Solution: The struts should be replaced.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

**Alternator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The alternator can fail, causing the battery to drain and the vehicle to stall. The symptoms of a failing alternator include dimming lights, difficulty starting the engine, and a warning light on the dashboard.
Solution: The alternator should be replaced.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

**Rust**
Description/Symptoms: The Precis is prone to rust, especially in the rear wheel wells and around the fuel filler door. The symptoms of rust include bubbling paint, flaking paint, and holes in the metal.
Solution: The rust can be repaired by sanding, priming, and painting the affected areas.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

**No notable issues found**

**Summary**

The 1994 Mitsubishi Precis is a reliable and fuel-efficient car, but it has been reported to have some issues. The most common issues include timing belt failure, intake valve stem seals failure, transmission slipping, strut failure, alternator failure, and rust. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific problem and the extent of the damage.
