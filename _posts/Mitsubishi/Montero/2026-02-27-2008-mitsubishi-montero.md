# 2008 Mitsubishi Montero

### Overview
The 2008 Mitsubishi Montero, also known as the Pajero in many markets, is a capable and rugged SUV that has generally proven to be reliable for its intended purpose of off-road adventure and family hauling. While it offers robust construction and a powerful engine, like any vehicle, it can experience specific issues. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability concerns beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential problem areas for owners and prospective buyers. Understanding these common issues can help in proactive maintenance and informed repair decisions, ensuring the longevity of this capable vehicle.

---


## Engine

### 3.8L V6 (6G75)
**Intake Manifold Gasket Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Coolant leaks, engine misfires, rough idle, potential overheating.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gaskets.
- **Cost:** $300 - $600

**Timing Belt Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Engine noise, potential catastrophic engine failure if belt breaks.
- **Solution:** Scheduled replacement of the timing belt, tensioners, and idler pulleys.
- **Cost:** $500 - $900


## Transmission

### 5-Speed Automatic
**Transmission Fluid Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping gears, delayed shifting, low fluid level warning.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of leaking seals or lines, fluid flush and refill.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500


### 5-Speed Manual
**Clutch Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting gears, burning smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the clutch kit (clutch disc, pressure plate, release bearing).
- **Cost:** $700 - $1200


## Suspension

### Independent Front Suspension / Multi-link Rear
**Ball Joint Wear**
- **Symptoms:** Clunking noises over bumps, uneven tire wear, steering looseness.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn ball joints.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Strut/Shock Absorber Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Bouncy ride, poor handling, visible leaks from shocks.
- **Solution:** Replacement of worn struts or shock absorbers.
- **Cost:** $400 - $800


## Electrical

### General Electrical System
**Alternator Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Dimming headlights, battery warning light, dead battery.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the alternator.
- **Cost:** $400 - $700

**Power Window Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Windows not operating, slow window operation.
- **Solution:** Replacement of individual power window motors.
- **Cost:** $200 - $400


## Exterior

### Body and Trim
**Rust on Frame and Body Panels**
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust, corrosion, potential structural compromise.
- **Solution:** Rust treatment, panel replacement, or undercoating.
- **Cost:** $200 - $1500+


## Interior

### Cabin Components
**HVAC Blower Motor Issues**
- **Symptoms:** No airflow from vents, intermittent fan speed.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the HVAC blower motor or resistor.
- **Cost:** $250 - $500


--- 
### Summary Verdict
The 2008 Mitsubishi Montero is a generally robust vehicle, but like many SUVs of its era, it is not immune to specific mechanical and electrical issues. The 3.8L V6 engine can experience intake manifold gasket leaks and requires timely timing belt replacement. Automatic transmissions may develop fluid leaks, while manual transmissions can suffer from clutch wear. Suspension components like ball joints and shocks are common wear items that can require replacement. Electrical issues, particularly with the alternator and power window motors, are also noted. Rust can be a concern depending on climate. Overall, with diligent maintenance and attention to these potential problem areas, the 2008 Montero can remain a reliable vehicle.
