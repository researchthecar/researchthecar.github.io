**Introduction**

The 2003 Mitsubishi Montero is a mid-size SUV that offers rugged performance and a luxurious interior. It comes with a 3.8L V6 engine that produces 202 horsepower and 250 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Montero is known for its off-road capabilities, spacious cabin, and comfortable ride. However, it has also received some criticism for its poor fuel economy and dated technology.

**Engine**

* **3.8L V6**
    * **Timing Chain Failure:** The timing chain can stretch or break over time, causing the engine to lose power or stall.
        * Solution: Replace the timing chain and related components.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: NHTSA Campaign Number: 13V173000
    * **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing a vacuum leak and loss of engine power.
        * Solution: Replace the intake manifold gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+

**Transmission**

* **4-Speed Automatic**
    * **Transmission Slipping:** The transmission may slip or hesitate when shifting gears.
        * Solution: Replace or rebuild the transmission.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,500+
    * **Overheating:** The transmission may overheat during heavy towing or in hot weather.
        * Solution: Flush the transmission fluid and replace the filter.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+

**Suspension**

* **Leaking Shock Absorbers:** The shock absorbers may leak fluid, causing a loss of suspension damping and reduced handling.
    * Solution: Replace the leaking shock absorbers.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
* **Sagging Springs:** The rear leaf springs may sag over time, compromising ride height and handling.
    * Solution: Replace the sagging springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** The alternator may fail, causing the battery to discharge and the vehicle to lose power.
    * Solution: Replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
* **Battery Drain:** The battery may discharge excessively when the vehicle is parked.
    * Solution: Test the electrical system for parasitic drain and repair or replace the affected components.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Exterior**

* **Rust:** The Montero may be prone to rust on the frame, body panels, and suspension components.
    * Solution: Apply rust-proofing treatments and repair existing rust.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the severity of the rust.
* **Sunroof Leak:** The sunroof may leak during heavy rain or car washes.
    * Solution: Replace the sunroof seals.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Interior**

* **HVAC Malfunction:** The heating, ventilation, and air conditioning system may fail to function properly, resulting in inadequate heating or cooling.
    * Solution: Diagnose and repair the affected HVAC component (blower motor, evaporator core, etc.).
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,500+
* **Power Window Failure:** The power windows may fail to operate or become inoperable.
    * Solution: Replace the power window switches or motors.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+

**Summary**

The 2003 Mitsubishi Montero offers a capable combination of off-road performance and interior comfort. However, it has several potential issues that can be costly to repair, such as timing chain failure, transmission problems, and rust. It's important for buyers to be aware of these potential issues and factor them into their purchasing decision.
