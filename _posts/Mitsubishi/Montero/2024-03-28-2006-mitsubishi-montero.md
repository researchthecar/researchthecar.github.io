**2006 Mitsubishi Montero**

The 2006 Mitsubishi Montero is a mid-size SUV known for its ruggedness and off-road capabilities. It came with a 3.8-liter V6 engine, producing 218 horsepower and 246 lb-ft of torque, paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission.

People enjoy the Montero's powerful engine, spacious interior, and excellent off-road performance. However, it has been criticized for its dated interior, subpar fuel economy, and lack of advanced safety features.

**Engine**

**3.8-liter V6**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Coolant leaks, overheating, poor engine performance
    * Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Timing chain tensioner failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine rattle, loss of power, engine failure
    * Solution: Replace timing chain tensioner and related components
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $1,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-speed automatic transmission**

* **Transmission slipping:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, vehicle stalling
    * Solution: Repair or replace transmission
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Shift solenoids failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Transmission does not shift properly, engine performance issues
    * Solution: Replace shift solenoids
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front control arm bushings wear out:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Clunking sounds, poor handling, alignment issues
    * Solution: Replace control arm bushings
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Electrical system issues, battery not charging
    * Solution: Replace alternator
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Starter motor failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Engine will not crank or start
    * Solution: Replace starter motor
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rear hatch latch failure:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Hatch will not latch or open
    * Solution: Replace latch assembly
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Power window switches malfunction:**
    * Description/Symptoms: Windows do not roll up or down
    * Solution: Replace window switches
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2006 Mitsubishi Montero is a reliable and capable SUV with a powerful engine and excellent off-road capabilities. However, there are some common issues that owners should be aware of, particularly with the intake manifold gasket, timing chain tensioner, and transmission. The severity of these issues varies, with some being minor and inexpensive to fix, while others can be more costly and time-consuming to repair. Overall, the Montero remains a solid choice for those who prioritize off-road performance and durability.
