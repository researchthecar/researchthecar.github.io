**Introduction:**

The 1998 Mitsubishi Montero is a mid-size SUV renowned for its rugged off-road capabilities, spacious interior, and powerful engine options. It is available with either a 3.0L 172 horsepower V6 or a 3.5L 190 horsepower V6 engine, coupled with a 4-speed automatic or a 5-speed manual transmission. The Montero has received positive feedback for its reliability, comfort, and overall value. However, like many vehicles, it is not without its share of reported issues.

**Engine**

**3.0L V6**

* **Timing Belt Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The timing belt can break prematurely, causing catastrophic engine damage. Symptoms include stalling, misfires, and lack of power. Solution: Replace the timing belt and tensioners. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1000 - $1500+ Recalls: None Found
* **Intake Manifold Gasket Leak:** Description/Symptoms: The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing vacuum leaks. Symptoms include rough idling, poor fuel economy, and a whistling noise. Solution: Replace the intake manifold gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+ Recalls: None Found

**3.5L V6**

* **Valve Cover Gasket Leak:** Description/Symptoms: The valve cover gaskets can develop leaks, resulting in oil seepage. Symptoms include visible oil leaks, a burning oil smell, and decreased engine performance. Solution: Replace the valve cover gaskets. Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**4-Speed Automatic**

* **Torque Converter Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The torque converter can fail, causing shuddering, slipping, and loss of power. Symptoms include delayed engagement, overheating, and a burning smell. Solution: Replace the torque converter. Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $2500+ Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Ball Joint Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The front ball joints can wear out prematurely, causing a clunking or rattling noise when turning or going over bumps. Symptoms include excessive front-end play and difficulty steering. Solution: Replace the front ball joints. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+ Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure:** Description/Symptoms: The alternator can fail, resulting in a loss of battery power. Symptoms include dim headlights, difficulty starting, and a warning light on the dashboard. Solution: Replace the alternator. Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1000+ Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Headlight Oxidation:** Description/Symptoms: The headlight lenses can become oxidized over time, causing them to turn cloudy and reduce light output. Symptoms include poor night visibility and difficulty passing inspections. Solution: Restore or replace the headlight lenses. Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $500+ Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Cracking:** Description/Symptoms: The dashboard can develop cracks over time due to exposure to UV rays and heat. Symptoms include unsightly cracks and potential glare. Solution: Replace the dashboard. Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+ Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

Overall, the 1998 Mitsubishi Montero is a reliable and capable SUV, but it is not without its potential issues. The most common issues reported by owners include timing belt failure, intake manifold gasket leaks, valve cover gasket leaks, torque converter failure, front ball joint failure, alternator failure, headlight oxidation, and dashboard cracking. The severity and cost of these issues can vary depending on the specific issue, but many of them can be addressed with relatively minor repairs. Regular maintenance and proper care can help mitigate some of these issues and extend the lifespan of the Montero.
