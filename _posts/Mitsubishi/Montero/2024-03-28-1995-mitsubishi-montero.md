**Introduction**

The 1995 Mitsubishi Montero is a mid-size SUV known for its ruggedness and off-road capability. It is powered by a 3.0L V6 engine mated to a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Montero received praise for its powerful engine, spacious interior, and comfortable ride. However, it has also been criticized for its poor fuel economy and lack of modern features.

**Engine**

* **3.0L V6**

**Transmission**

* **4-Speed Automatic**

**Suspension**

* **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

* **Alternator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The alternator fails, causing the battery to not charge and the vehicle to eventually stall.
Solution: Replace the alternator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

* **Starter Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The starter fails, causing the engine to not turn over.
Solution: Replace the starter.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

* **Battery Drain**
Description/Symptoms: The battery drains quickly, even when the vehicle is parked and turned off.
Solution: Test the battery and charging system. Replace the battery or repair the charging system as necessary.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Rust**
Description/Symptoms: Rust develops on the body panels, especially around the wheel arches and lower doors.
Solution: Treat the rust with a rust converter and touch up the paint. In severe cases, body panel replacement may be necessary.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $2,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Power Window Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The power windows fail to operate, either due to a faulty switch or a broken window motor.
Solution: Replace the faulty switch or window motor.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+
Recalls: None Found

* **Air Conditioner Failure**
Description/Symptoms: The air conditioner blows warm air or does not work at all.
Solution: Recharge the refrigerant, repair or replace the compressor, or replace other faulty components in the air conditioning system.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 1995 Mitsubishi Montero is a reliable vehicle overall, with only a few common issues to be aware of. Electrical problems, such as alternator and starter failures, are the most prevalent. Rust can also be a concern, especially in areas with high humidity or salt exposure. Interior issues, such as power window and air conditioner failures, are less common but still worth considering. The cost of repairs can vary depending on the severity of the issue and the location of the repair.
