**Introduction:**

The 2019 Mitsubishi Montero is a mid-size SUV available with a single engine and transmission option. It offers 228 horsepower and 250 lb-ft of torque from its 3.0L V6 engine paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. The Montero is known for its off-road capabilities, spacious interior, and reliable performance. However, there have been several reported issues with the vehicle.

**Engine**

**3.0L V6**

- **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: The timing chain tensioner can fail, leading to a loss of engine power and stalling. Other symptoms can include a rattling sound coming from the engine and a check engine light.
  - Solution: Replace the timing chain tensioner and related components.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,200 - $1,800+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

- **Transmission Slipping:**
  - Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip gears or hesitate when shifting. This can cause a lack of power or difficulty accelerating.
  - Solution: Overhaul or replace the transmission.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **Rear Axle Shaft Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: The rear axle shaft can snap or break, causing a loss of power to the rear wheels. This can make the vehicle difficult or impossible to drive.
  - Solution: Replace the rear axle shaft assembly.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Alternator Failure:**
  - Description/Symptoms: The alternator may fail, resulting in a loss of electrical power and a dead battery. This can cause the vehicle to stall or fail to start.
  - Solution: Replace the alternator and check for any underlying electrical issues.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Rust on Frame and Underbody:**
  - Description/Symptoms: Excessive rust can form on the vehicle's frame and underbody components. This can weaken the frame and lead to premature failure.
  - Solution: Inspect the vehicle for rust and apply rust-resistant coatings or treatments to prevent further damage.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies depending on the extent of rust damage
  - Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **Non-Functional Climate Control:**
  - Description/Symptoms: The climate control system may fail, resulting in a lack of heating or cooling in the cabin.
  - Solution: Diagnose and repair the faulty component(s) in the climate control system.
  - Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
  - Recalls: None Found

**Summary:**

The 2019 Mitsubishi Montero has several potential issues, including engine timing chain tensioner failure, transmission slipping, rear axle shaft failure, alternator failure, rust on frame and underbody, and non-functional climate control. The severity and cost of these issues vary, but some can be quite costly to repair. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues and consider the cost of repairs before purchasing a Montero.
