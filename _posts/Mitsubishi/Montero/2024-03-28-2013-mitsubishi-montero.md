**2013 Mitsubishi Montero**

The 2013 Mitsubishi Montero is a midsize SUV that offers a rugged and capable off-road experience. It is powered by a standard 3.8L V6 engine paired with a 5-speed automatic transmission. The Montero is praised for its comfortable interior, spacious cargo capacity, and decent fuel economy for its size. However, it is criticized for its outdated design, lackluster performance, and poor reliability.

**Engine**

* **3.8L V6**

**Issues Found:**

* **Intake manifold gasket failure:** The intake manifold gasket can fail, causing a vacuum leak that leads to rough idling, stalling, and decreased engine power. **Solution:** Replacement of the intake manifold gasket. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $600 - $800+. **Recalls:** None Found
* **Throttle body failure:** The throttle body can fail, causing the engine to stall or idle erratically. **Solution:** Replacement of the throttle body. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $400 - $600+. **Recalls:** None Found
* **Valve cover gasket leak:** The valve cover gasket can leak oil, causing oil consumption and potential engine damage if left unchecked. **Solution:** Replacement of the valve cover gasket. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $400+. **Recalls:** None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-speed automatic**

**Issues Found:**

* **Transmission slipping:** The transmission may slip when shifting gears, causing loss of power and drivability issues. **Solution:** Overhaul or replacement of the transmission. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2,000 - $4,000+. **Recalls:** None Found
* **Transmission fluid leaks:** The transmission fluid lines or seals can develop leaks, causing low fluid levels and potential transmission failure if not addressed promptly. **Solution:** Replacement of the leaking parts and refill of transmission fluid. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500+. **Recalls:** None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front lower control arm bushings:** The front lower control arm bushings can wear out or fail, causing clunking noises, vibration, and reduced handling stability. **Solution:** Replacement of the control arm bushings. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $400 - $600+. **Recalls:** None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery drain:** The battery may drain prematurely due to a faulty alternator or parasitic draw from other components. **Solution:** Testing of the electrical system and replacement of the faulty alternator or diagnosis and repair of the parasitic draw. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $800+. **Recalls:** None Found
* **Electrical gremlins:** Various electrical components, such as switches, relays, and sensors, may malfunction, causing intermittent problems or complete system failures. **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair or replacement of the faulty electrical components. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $100 - $500+ per component. **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior**

* **Sunroof leaks:** The sunroof seals may deteriorate or fail, allowing water to leak into the cabin. **Solution:** Replacement of the sunroof seals. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $600 - $1,000+. **Recalls:** None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracks:** The dashboard material may develop cracks over time, especially in areas exposed to direct sunlight. **Solution:** Replacement of the dashboard or repair using a dashboard cover. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $800 - $1,200+ for dashboard replacement, $50 - $200 for a dashboard cover. **Recalls:** None Found
* **Climate control issues:** The climate control system may malfunction, causing improper heating or cooling. **Solution:** Diagnosis and repair or replacement of the faulty climate control components. **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $1,000+. **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary**

The 2013 Mitsubishi Montero has several potential issues that can affect its engine, transmission, suspension, electrical system, exterior, and interior. While some issues are minor and inexpensive to repair, others can be more severe and costly. The overall severity of the Montero's reliability issues is moderate, but the cost of repairs can be relatively high. It is important to consider these potential issues when researching or purchasing a used Montero.
