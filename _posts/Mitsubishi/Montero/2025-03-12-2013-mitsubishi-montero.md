The 2013 Mitsubishi Montero, also known as the Pajero in some markets, is a full-size SUV known for its off-road capabilities and spacious interior. The engine options are limited, with a common choice being a 3.8L V6 gasoline engine producing approximately 250 horsepower and 243 lb-ft of torque. It is paired to a 5-speed automatic transmission. People like the Montero for its ruggedness, off-road performance, and spaciousness. Common dislikes include its fuel economy, outdated technology, and relatively unrefined driving experience compared to more modern SUVs.

**Engine**

**3.8L V6**

*   **Timing Chain Tensioner Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rattling noise upon startup, especially when cold. Can lead to more severe engine damage if left unresolved.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the timing chain tensioner.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Valve Stem Seal Leakage**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Blue smoke from the exhaust, especially upon starting the engine after it has been sitting for a while.  Oil consumption may increase.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the valve stem seals.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1200 - $2500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Automatic**

*   **Torque Converter Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Shuddering during gear changes, especially at lower speeds, or the engine revving high without acceleration. Can lead to complete transmission failure.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the torque converter, fluid flush, and inspection of the transmission for further damage.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Rear Air Suspension Compressor Failure (if equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The rear of the vehicle sags, the ride becomes bouncy, and a warning light illuminates on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the air suspension compressor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Upper Control Arm Bushing Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking noises coming from the front suspension, especially over bumps.  Can lead to uneven tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the upper control arm bushings or the entire upper control arm.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per side
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Central Door Locking System Malfunction**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Doors failing to lock or unlock with the remote or the interior switch.
    *   Solution: Inspection of the door lock actuators, wiring harness, and the central locking module.  Replacement of faulty components.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+ per door
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Alternator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Battery light on the dashboard, dimming headlights, difficulty starting the engine.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the alternator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $700+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Power Mirror Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The power mirrors no longer adjust or only adjust intermittently.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the power mirror motor or the entire mirror assembly.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+ per mirror
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof fails to open or close, or only operates intermittently.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the sunroof motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2013 Mitsubishi Montero can present owners with a range of issues, from relatively minor electrical glitches to more significant engine and transmission problems. Engine issues like timing chain tensioner and valve stem seal issues can lead to severe issues. Suspension issues, such as rear air suspension and upper control arms can result in reduced ride quality and affect handling. Electrical problems related to the door locking system and alternator can affect the vehicles functionality. Addressing these issues promptly can prevent further damage and ensure the vehicle's longevity. The cost of repairs can vary greatly, with some issues costing several thousand dollars to fix.
