# 2016 Mitsubishi i-MiEV

### Overview
The 2016 Mitsubishi i-MiEV is a subcompact electric vehicle known for its urban maneuverability and zero-emission operation. As an electric vehicle, its powertrain differs significantly from traditional internal combustion engine cars, focusing on battery and electric motor performance. Reliability data for the i-MiEV generally indicates a solid performance for its class, with owners reporting fewer mechanical issues compared to many gasoline-powered counterparts. However, like any vehicle, specific components can experience problems. This analysis focuses on documented technical issues beyond routine maintenance, providing insights into potential areas of concern for owners and prospective buyers.

### High-Voltage Battery
**Battery Degradation**
- **Symptoms:** Reduced range, slower charging times, loss of overall capacity.
- **Solution:** Battery replacement (often covered under warranty for a period).
- **Cost:** $5,000 - $10,000+

**Charging System Faults**

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Mitsubishi i-MiEV demonstrates a generally reliable profile, particularly in its core electric powertrain components. The electric motor and single-speed transmission are robust and rarely present significant issues. The primary areas of concern revolve around the high-voltage battery, which can experience degradation over time leading to reduced range and capacity, and potential charging system faults. The 12V auxiliary battery can also be a point of failure. Interior electronics, specifically the infotainment system, may encounter minor software glitches. Exterior and suspension components are typically problem-free. Overall, for an electric vehicle of its era, the i-MiEV offers a dependable experience, with the main long-term cost consideration being the eventual replacement of the high-voltage battery.
