The 2012 Mitsubishi Lancer is a compact car known for its sporty handling and affordability. Available engines include a 2.0L inline-4 producing 148 horsepower and 145 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.4L inline-4 producing 168 horsepower and 167 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options include a 5-speed manual and a CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission). Owners often appreciate the Lancer's sharp styling and responsive steering, but some common complaints revolve around interior material quality, road noise, and fuel economy.

**Engine**

**2.0L Inline-4**
*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report the 2.0L engine consuming excessive amounts of oil, leading to the need for frequent top-offs. This can manifest as blue smoke from the exhaust, a burning oil smell, and low oil levels indicated on the dipstick.
    *   Solution: The most common fix is to replace the piston rings. In severe cases, engine replacement may be necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $4000+

**2.4L Inline-4**
*   **No Issues Found**

**Transmission**

**CVT**
*   **CVT Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The CVT is prone to failures and strange noises. This can be caused by overheating or a faulty valve body. Symptoms include slipping, jerking, hesitation upon acceleration, and unusual whining or humming noises from the transmission.
    *   Solution: The CVT may require replacement or rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $3000 - $5000+

**Suspension**

*   **Worn Struts/Shocks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Premature wear of the struts and shocks, leading to a bouncy ride, poor handling, and excessive tire wear.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the worn struts and/or shocks.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+

**Electrical**

*   **Alternator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Alternator failure, leading to a dead battery, dimming lights, and eventual stalling.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the alternator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
*   **Faulty Blower Motor Resistor**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Malfunctioning blower motor resistor, causing the climate control fan to only work on certain speeds or not at all.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the blower motor resistor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200

**Exterior**

*   **Rust**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust forming in the wheel wells, rocker panels, and around the fuel filler door.
    *   Solution: Repair and repaint the rusted areas. Prevention with rustproofing.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2000+

**Interior**

*   **Dashboard Cracking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Cracking in the dashboard, particularly in warmer climates or with prolonged sun exposure.
    *   Solution: Dashboard replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1500+
*   **Headliner Sagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The headliner can sag, particularly in warmer climates.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the headliner.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500

**Recalls:**

*   **None Found**

**Summary**

The 2012 Mitsubishi Lancer can be a reliable and fun-to-drive car, but potential buyers should be aware of some common issues. The most serious concerns involve the CVT transmission and the 2.0L engine's potential for excessive oil consumption, both of which can lead to significant repair costs. Rust is also a potential problem, especially in regions with harsh winters. Addressing these issues early on can help maintain the vehicle's reliability and extend its lifespan.
