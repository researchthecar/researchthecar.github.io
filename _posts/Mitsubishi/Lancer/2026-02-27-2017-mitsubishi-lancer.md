# 2017 Mitsubishi Lancer Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used 2017 Mitsubishi Lancer, buyers will find a familiar compact sedan that offers sporty styling and available all-wheel control. However, reviewing owner-submitted feedback highlights a few key areas that require careful attention during a pre-purchase inspection, particularly regarding powertrain behavior and safety systems.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder

The 2.0-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine produces approximately 148 horsepower and 145 lb-ft of torque, serving as the base powerplant for lower trims. It is generally a conventional and straightforward design, though buyers should still verify routine maintenance history.

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder

The larger 2.4-liter naturally aspirated four-cylinder engine generates around 168 horsepower and 167 lb-ft of torque, powering higher trims and all-wheel-drive variants. It provides adequate daily performance, though it operates under similar mechanical expectations as the base engine.

## Transmission

### 5-Speed Manual

The 5-speed manual transmission is paired with select base configurations, offering a more direct mechanical connection. It tends to be simpler and less prone to the complex sensor or pressure issues seen in automatic alternatives.

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The continuously variable automatic transmission manages power delivery for most mainstream trims. CVT variants require close scrutiny during a test drive, as owner reports indicate potential shuddering and pressure sensor concerns.

**Transmission Shudder and Surge**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers report severe vehicle shuddering, surging, and a rubber-banding sensation while driving, sometimes accompanied by a loss of power during acceleration or turns.
- **Solution:** Diagnosis by a qualified technician may reveal fluid pressure sensor codes, potentially requiring professional transmission repair or replacement.

## Exterior

### Windshield Defroster

Exterior and body accessory complaints are minimal, though minor cabin and glass climate control noises have been noted by owners.

**Defroster Noise**
- **Symptoms:** Activating the window defroster can produce an audible clicking or popping noise from the dashboard area during morning operations.
- **Solution:** Inspect the blend door actuators and heating system housing for mechanical binding or electrical glitches.

## Safety

### Air Bags

Safety system complaints primarily involve airbag warning indicators and deployment concerns, making a thorough pre-purchase diagnostic scan essential.

**Airbag Warning Light and Non-Deployment**
- **Symptoms:** Owners have reported illuminated airbag warning lights indicating system faults, as well as instances where frontal airbags failed to deploy during collisions.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the airbag control module, wiring harnesses, and sensor connections to diagnose underlying fault codes.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Mitsubishi Lancer can serve as an affordable commuter option, but prospective buyers should conduct a rigorous test drive to check for CVT shudder or surging. Ensuring that all safety warning lights function correctly and verifying the health of the supplemental restraint system will help avoid unexpected repair expenses.
