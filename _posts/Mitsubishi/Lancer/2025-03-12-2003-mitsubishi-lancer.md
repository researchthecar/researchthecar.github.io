The 2003 Mitsubishi Lancer was a compact car known for its sporty styling and affordability. Engine options included a 2.0L 4G94 SOHC 16-valve inline-4 engine producing around 120 horsepower and 130 lb-ft of torque. Transmission options were a 5-speed manual and a 4-speed automatic. Owners often appreciated the Lancer's fuel efficiency and handling, while some criticized its basic interior and engine noise.

**Engine**

**2.0L 4G94 SOHC 16-valve Inline-4**
*   **Valve Cover Gasket Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Oil leaks from the valve cover gasket, leading to oil consumption and potential smoke.
    *   Solution: Replace the valve cover gasket.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $150
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Timing Belt Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The timing belt can degrade and eventually break, leading to catastrophic engine damage.
    *   Solution: Replace the timing belt according to the manufacturer's recommended service interval (usually every 60,000 miles).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Engine Mount Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Excessive engine vibration, clunking noises, and rough shifting.
    *   Solution: Replace the worn or broken engine mounts.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300 per mount
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Oxygen Sensor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, poor fuel economy, rough idling.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty oxygen sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $250
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**
*   **Clutch Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Slipping clutch, difficulty shifting, high clutch engagement point.
    *   Solution: Replace the clutch disc, pressure plate, and potentially the flywheel.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Shift Linkage Bushing Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sloppy or difficult shifting.
    *   Solution: Replace the worn shift linkage bushings.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200
    *   Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic**
*   **Harsh Shifting**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or jerky shifts between gears, especially when cold.
    *   Solution: Transmission fluid flush, and inspection of valve body.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+ for a flush, $300-$800+ for valve body work.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Strut/Shock Absorber Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Bouncy ride, poor handling, excessive body roll.
    *   Solution: Replace the worn struts/shock absorbers.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500 per axle
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Sway Bar Link Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the suspension, especially when going over bumps.
    *   Solution: Replace the worn sway bar links.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $50 - $200
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Power Window Motor Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Window not working.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty power window motor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300 per window
    *   Recalls: None Found
*   **Alternator Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, battery warning light, difficulty starting.
    *   Solution: Replace the faulty alternator.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Rust**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rust on the rocker panels, quarter panels, and wheel wells.
    *   Solution: Cut out the rust and weld in new metal.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: Varies greatly depending on the extent of the rust, but typically $500 - $2000+ per panel

**Interior**

*   **Headliner Sagging**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The headliner fabric detaches from the backing and sags down.
    *   Solution: Replace the headliner fabric or the entire headliner board.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $400+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Summary:** The 2003 Mitsubishi Lancer can be a reliable and affordable vehicle, but owners should be aware of common issues, particularly concerning engine maintenance (timing belt, valve cover gaskets) and suspension components. Rust can also be a significant concern, especially in regions with harsh winters. Repair costs can range from relatively inexpensive fixes like sway bar links to more substantial expenses for rust repair, clutch replacement, or engine work.
