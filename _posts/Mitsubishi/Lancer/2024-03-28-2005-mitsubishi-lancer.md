**Introduction**

The 2005 Mitsubishi Lancer is a compact sedan offered in various trims with a choice of two engine and transmission options. The 2.0-liter inline-4 engine produces 120 horsepower and 146 lb-ft of torque, paired with either a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission. The 2.4-liter inline-4 engine generates 160 horsepower and 158 lb-ft of torque, coupled with a 5-speed manual or 4-speed automatic transmission. Despite its modest performance, the Lancer is praised for its handling and affordability but criticized for its lack of interior space and dated exterior design.

**Engine**

**2.0-liter Inline-4**

- **Leaking Valve Cover Gasket**
Description/Symptoms: Oil leak from the top of the engine, visible as a black or brown residue.
Solution: Replace valve cover gasket.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
Recalls: None Found

**2.4-liter Inline-4**

- **Timing Belt Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Engine won't start or stalls, accompanied by a loud noise.
Solution: Replace timing belt and associated components.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $600 - $1,000+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**5-Speed Manual**

- **Gear Grinding**
Description/Symptoms: Difficulty shifting gears, especially into second gear.
Solution: Replace worn synchronizers.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $700+
Recalls: None Found

**4-Speed Automatic**

- **Slipping Gears**
Description/Symptoms: Transmission hesitates or fails to engage gears smoothly.
Solution: Replace or rebuild transmission.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

- **Strut Mount Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Noise and vibration when driving over uneven surfaces.
Solution: Replace strut mounts.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

- **Alternator Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Dimming headlights, battery not charging properly.
Solution: Replace alternator.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

- **Starter Relay Failure**
Description/Symptoms: Engine fails to start, accompanied by a clicking noise.
Solution: Replace starter relay.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

- **Fog Lamp Condensation**
Description/Symptoms: Water droplets inside the fog lamps.
Solution: Replace fog lamps or improve ventilation system.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

- **HVAC Blower Motor Failure**
Description/Symptoms: No air flow from the vents, accompanied by a burning smell.
Solution: Replace blower motor.
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $300+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2005 Mitsubishi Lancer presents a range of issues primarily related to its engine, transmission, and electrical systems. The severity and cost of repairs can vary depending on the specific problem. Overall, potential buyers should carefully consider the potential repair expenses before purchasing this model.
