# 2016 Mitsubishi Lancer Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

When shopping for a used compact sedan, the model offers a sharp exterior and distinctive styling. However, prospective buyers should carefully evaluate the mechanical condition, particularly concerning the powertrain and electrical systems, as owner reports point to several recurring areas of concern.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L 4-Cylinder Engine

The base 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine produces approximately 148 horsepower and is typically paired with a manual transmission or a continuously variable transmission (CVT) in lower trims. Regular maintenance is essential, as overall mechanical durability depends heavily on consistent fluid changes and upkeep.

### 2.4L 4-Cylinder Engine

The larger 2.4-liter four-cylinder engine generates around 168 horsepower and provides power to higher trims equipped with all-wheel control. It offers a bit more pep for daily driving, though buyers should watch for signs of hesitation or sudden power loss during test drives.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)

The continuously variable transmission handles power delivery for most automatic configurations. It is one of the most frequently criticized areas by owners, making a thorough pre-purchase inspection vital.

**Transmission Shudder and Lag**
- **Symptoms:** Drivers experience a noticeable shudder or bucking sensation when accelerating from a stop, accompanied by transmission lag and fluctuating RPMs.
- **Solution:** Have a qualified mechanic inspect the transmission fluid condition and scan for diagnostic trouble codes such as P084A to determine if internal wear or valve body issues are present.

**Transmission Slipping and Loss of Motive Power**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may fail to accelerate properly, slip out of simulated gears, or trigger a service transmission warning light while underway.
- **Solution:** Perform a diagnostic inspection of the transmission control module and fluid levels; severe internal mechanical wear may require professional transmission overhaul or replacement.

## Electrical

### Starting and Power Control System

electrical issues can occasionally plague starting and charging functions, leading to frustrating no-start conditions or unexpected power interruptions.

**Intermittent No-Crank and Power Loss**
- **Symptoms:** The vehicle may occasionally fail to crank when attempting to start, or experience sudden hesitation and loss of electrical motive power while driving.
- **Solution:** Test the battery, starter motor, alternator connections, and associated wiring harnesses for corrosion or intermittent failures.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 17V609000: Electrical System:Starter Assembly:Relay](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Lancer&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** If the engine stalls, there is an increased the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MMNA will notify owners, and dealers will replace the affected relays, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 16V563000: Power Train:Automatic Transmission:Control Module (Tcm/Pcm/Tecm)](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Mitsubishi&model=Lancer&modelYear=2016)**
- **Issue:** The affected vehicles are equipped with a constant velocity transmission (CVT) that, in certain driving conditions, may cause a delay in the vehicle accelerating. A hesitation during acceleration can increase the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** MMNA has notified owners, and dealers will reprogram the CVT control unit with modified software, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

---

## Summary Verdict

Before finalizing a purchase, ensure you conduct a thorough test drive paying close attention to transmission engagement, acceleration smoothness, and starting behavior. A professional pre-purchase inspection by a trusted mechanic is highly recommended to catch potential CVT wear or hidden subframe issues.
