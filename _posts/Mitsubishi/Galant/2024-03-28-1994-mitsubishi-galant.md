**Introduction**
The 1994 Mitsubishi Galant is a midsize sedan that has been praised for its impressive performance and handling. It came with two engine options: a 2.0L SOHC I4 and a 2.5L DOHC V6. The 2.0L engine produces 135 horsepower and 134 lb-ft of torque, while the 2.5L engine offers 164 horsepower and 156 lb-ft of torque. Both engines can be paired with a 5-speed manual transmission or a 4-speed automatic transmission. The Galant was appreciated by many for its sporty performance, though it was criticized for its uninspiring interior and lack of features.

**Engine**
**2.0L SOHC I4**
- **Intake manifold gasket failure**
Description/Symptoms: Coolant or oil leaks from the intake manifold, leading to overheating or engine damage
Solution: Replace intake manifold gasket
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500+
Recalls: None Found

**2.5L DOHC V6**
- **Timing belt failure**
Description/Symptoms: The timing belt can break or slip, causing the engine to stop suddenly and requiring a rebuild
Solution: Replace timing belt and water pump (recommended)
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1000+
Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**
**5-speed Manual Transmission**
- **Grinding or difficulty shifting gears**
Description/Symptoms: Transmission may grind when shifting gears or become difficult to shift, especially when cold
Solution: Replace synchronizers or transmission fluid
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

**4-speed Automatic Transmission**
- **Slipping or delayed gear engagement**
Description/Symptoms: Transmission may slip or hesitate when shifting gears, leading to decreased performance
Solution: Replace or rebuild transmission
Estimated Cost to Fix: $1500 - $3000+
Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**
- **Strut failure**
Description/Symptoms: Worn or damaged struts can lead to a rough ride, decreased handling, and uneven tire wear
Solution: Replace struts
Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**
- **Alternator failure**
Description/Symptoms: The alternator may stop charging the battery, leading to electrical problems and potential engine failure
Solution: Replace alternator
Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $600+
Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**
- **Door lock failure**
Description/Symptoms: Power door locks may become inoperable or malfunction, making it difficult to enter or exit the vehicle
Solution: Replace door lock actuators
Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
Recalls: None Found

**Interior**
- **Climate control failure**
Description/Symptoms: HVAC system may not blow air, or blow only cold or hot air
Solution: Repair or replace climate control unit
Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $800+
Recalls: None Found

**Summary**
The 1994 Mitsubishi Galant has several potential issues, some of which can be severe and expensive to fix. Engine issues can include intake manifold gasket failure and timing belt failure. Transmission problems may involve grinding or difficult shifting, as well as slipping or delayed gear engagement. Suspension issues, such as strut failure, can affect the ride quality and handling. Electrical problems can include alternator failure, leading to potential engine failure if not addressed promptly. Some exterior issues include door lock failure, while interior issues may involve climate control failure. Overall, while the Galant offers impressive performance, its reliability and cost of ownership may be a concern for potential owners.
