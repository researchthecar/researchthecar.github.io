# 2017 Mitsubishi RVR

### Overview
The 2017 Mitsubishi RVR, also known as the Outlander Sport in some markets, is a compact SUV that generally offers a straightforward and relatively reliable ownership experience. It's designed for urban commuting and light recreational use, prioritizing affordability and practicality over high-performance or luxury features. While not without its minor quirks, the RVR typically avoids major widespread mechanical failures, making it a sensible choice for budget-conscious buyers. Owners often report satisfaction with its maneuverability and ease of use, though some find the interior materials and technology to be a bit dated compared to competitors. Overall, the 2017 RVR presents a solid, if unexciting, option in its segment.

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional hesitation during acceleration or slight jerking motions, particularly at lower speeds. Some users report a whining noise.
- **Solution:** Software updates from the dealer can sometimes resolve minor hesitation. In more severe cases, transmission fluid changes or internal component inspection may be required.
- **Cost:** $150 - $500 (for fluid change/software update); $1,500 - $4,000 (for major repairs/replacement)

### Summary Verdict
The 2017 Mitsubishi RVR demonstrates a generally good reliability record, particularly concerning its powertrain and chassis components. The most frequently cited issue, though not widespread, relates to the Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) experiencing occasional hesitation or minor jerking. The 2.0L and 2.4L engines are robust, and the suspension systems are typically trouble-free. Electrical gremlins are minimal, with some users reporting minor Bluetooth connectivity hiccups with the infotainment system. Exterior and interior components are also largely free from significant defects. Overall, the RVR is a dependable choice for daily driving, with potential buyers advised to be aware of the CVT's characteristics.
