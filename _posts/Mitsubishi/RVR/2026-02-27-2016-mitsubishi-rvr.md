# 2016 Mitsubishi RVR

### Overview
The 2016 Mitsubishi RVR, also known as the Outlander Sport in some markets, is a compact crossover SUV that generally offers a straightforward and relatively reliable ownership experience. It's designed for urban commuting and light recreational use, prioritizing practicality and affordability over outright performance or luxury. While not without its minor quirks, the RVR has demonstrated a decent track record for its model year, with most owners reporting satisfaction with its day-to-day usability. The vehicle's mechanical components are largely proven, contributing to its overall dependability for its intended purpose. However, like any vehicle, specific areas can experience issues, particularly as mileage increases.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Occasional hesitation during acceleration or slight jerking sensations, particularly at lower speeds or when the transmission is cold.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the transmission control module, fluid changes, or in severe cases, transmission replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500

**Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission warning light illuminated, reduced engine power, or a burning smell, especially under heavy load or prolonged driving.
- **Solution:** Check and top up transmission fluid, inspect for leaks, and ensure proper cooling system operation. Transmission cooler may need cleaning or replacement.
- **Cost:** $200 - $800

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Bluetooth Connectivity Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty pairing devices, dropped connections, or audio playback problems.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the infotainment system, resetting the system, or checking device compatibility.
- **Cost:** $50 - $200


### Battery
**Premature Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty starting the engine, dimming headlights, or electrical components malfunctioning.
- **Solution:** Battery testing and replacement.
- **Cost:** $150 - $300


## Exterior

### Paint and Body Panels
**Clear Coat Peeling**
- **Symptoms:** The clear coat on the paint may start to peel or delaminate, particularly on horizontal surfaces exposed to harsh weather.
- **Solution:** Professional repainting or touch-up work.
- **Cost:** $500 - $2000+


### Sunroof
**Leaking**
- **Symptoms:** Water ingress around the sunroof seal, leading to interior water damage or mold.
- **Solution:** Cleaning and resealing the sunroof drain channels and seals. Replacement of seals if damaged.
- **Cost:** $150 - $400


## Interior

### HVAC System
**Blower Motor Failure**
- **Symptoms:** No air coming from the vents, or intermittent fan speed operation.
- **Solution:** Replacement of the blower motor or blower motor resistor.
- **Cost:** $200 - $500


### Seat Upholstery
**Wear and Tear**
- **Symptoms:** Fabric or leather upholstery may show signs of wear, such as thinning, tearing, or fading, especially on the driver's seat.
- **Solution:** Professional cleaning, repair, or replacement of seat covers.
- **Cost:** $100 - $600

### Summary Verdict
The 2016 Mitsubishi RVR generally presents as a reliable compact SUV, particularly for its powertrain components. The engines are robust, and while the CVT can occasionally exhibit minor issues like hesitation or overheating, these are often addressable with maintenance or software updates. The suspension is typically trouble-free. Electrical gremlins are infrequent, though infotainment connectivity and battery life can be points of concern. Exterior paint, especially the clear coat, and sunroof seals are the most commonly cited areas for potential issues. Interior components like the HVAC blower motor and seat wear are also noted. Overall, the RVR is a sensible choice for budget-conscious buyers seeking a dependable vehicle for daily use, provided regular maintenance is performed.
