# 2015 Mitsubishi RVR

### Overview
The 2015 Mitsubishi RVR, also known as the Outlander Sport in some markets, is a compact crossover SUV that generally offers a decent balance of practicality and value. Reliability for this model year is considered average, with most owners reporting satisfaction. However, like any vehicle, there are specific areas that have seen more attention from repair shops. Common concerns tend to be concentrated in areas such as the electrical system and occasional powertrain issues, though widespread catastrophic failures are not prevalent. Regular maintenance is key to ensuring the longevity of the RVR.

## Transmission

### Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT)
**Hesitation or Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Vehicle may hesitate during acceleration or exhibit jerky movements, particularly at lower speeds. Some users report a whining noise.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the transmission control module, fluid flush and replacement, or in severe cases, transmission replacement.
- **Cost:** $300 - $1500

**Overheating**
- **Symptoms:** Transmission warning light may illuminate, accompanied by a burning smell or reduced performance.
- **Solution:** Check and top off transmission fluid, inspect for leaks, and ensure proper cooling system function. May require transmission cooler service or replacement.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1200

## Electrical

### Infotainment System
**Bluetooth Connectivity Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty pairing devices, dropped connections, or audio playback problems.
- **Solution:** Software updates for the infotainment system, resetting the system, or checking device compatibility.
- **Cost:** $50 - $200

**Touchscreen Unresponsiveness**
- **Symptoms:** The touchscreen may become slow to respond or completely unresponsive.
- **Solution:** System reboot, software update, or replacement of the infotainment unit.
- **Cost:** $100 - $800


### Battery Drain
**Premature Battery Failure**
- **Symptoms:** Battery drains quickly, leading to starting issues. May be related to parasitic draw from modules.
- **Solution:** Test battery and charging system, identify and repair parasitic draw (e.g., faulty module, aftermarket accessory).
- **Cost:** $150 - $500

### Summary Verdict
The 2015 Mitsubishi RVR is a generally reliable compact SUV, particularly with its standard engine options. The most frequently reported issues tend to be related to the Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT), which can sometimes exhibit hesitation or overheating. Electrical gremlins, such as infotainment system glitches and battery drain, are also noted but not to a degree that suggests a systemic flaw. The suspension, exterior, and interior components are largely free of widespread complaints. With diligent maintenance, especially concerning the CVT fluid, the RVR should provide dependable service for its owners.
