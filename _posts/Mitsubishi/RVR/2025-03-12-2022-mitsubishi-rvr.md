The 2022 Mitsubishi RVR, also known as the ASX or Outlander Sport in some markets, is a subcompact crossover SUV known for its affordability and fuel efficiency. It comes standard with a 2.0L inline-4 engine producing 148 horsepower and 145 lb-ft of torque, paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT). Some find the RVR appealing for its compact size, maneuverability, and available all-wheel drive. Common criticisms include the engine's lack of power, dated interior, and limited cargo space.

**Engine**

**2.0L Inline-4**

**Transmission**

**CVT**

*   **CVT Juddering/Shaking**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners have reported a juddering or shaking sensation, especially during acceleration from a stop or at low speeds.
    *   Solution: In some cases, a CVT fluid change resolves the issue. In other cases, a valve body replacement or complete CVT replacement might be needed.

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Freezing/Crashing**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The infotainment system may freeze, crash, or become unresponsive.
    *   Solution: Try resetting the system or updating the software. If the problem persists, the head unit may need to be replaced.

**Exterior**

*   **Sunroof Leakage**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Water leaks into the cabin through the sunroof during rain or car washes.
    *   Solution: Check the sunroof drain tubes for clogs and clear them. The sunroof seal may also need replacement.

**Interior**

*   **Rattling Noises from Dashboard**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Annoying rattling or buzzing noises emanating from the dashboard, especially when driving on rough roads.
    *   Solution: Identifying the source of the rattle and securing loose components within the dashboard. This may involve adding padding or tightening fasteners.

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Wear of Rear Shocks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The rear shocks wear out faster than expected, leading to a bouncy ride and reduced handling.

**Summary**

The 2022 Mitsubishi RVR has several potential issues, with concerns about the CVT transmission being the most serious and potentially costly. Other problems like electrical glitches and interior rattles are less severe but can still be irritating for owners. Oil consumption and suspension wear can lead to unplanned maintenance. Prospective buyers should be aware of these potential problems and consider a pre-purchase inspection.
