**Introduction**

The 2022 Mitsubishi RVR is a compact crossover SUV. It is powered by a 2.0L 4-cylinder engine that produces 148 horsepower and 145 lb-ft of torque. The RVR is available with either a 5-speed manual transmission or a CVT automatic transmission. The RVR has been praised for its spacious interior, fuel efficiency, and affordable price. However, like all vehicles, the RVR has its share of problems.

**Engine**

* **2.0L Engine**
    * **Intake Valve Sticking**
        * Description/Symptoms: The intake valves can become stuck due to carbon buildup. This can cause the engine to run rough, idle erratically, and stall.
        * Solution: The intake valves must be cleaned of the carbon buildup.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found
    * **Piston Ring Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The piston rings can fail, allowing oil to leak into the combustion chamber. This can cause the engine to burn oil, smoke, and lose power.
        * Solution: The piston rings must be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $1,200
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-Speed Manual Transmission**
    * **Grinding Gears**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may grind gears when shifting. This can be caused by worn synchronizers or gears.
        * Solution: The transmission must be rebuilt or replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **CVT Automatic Transmission**
    * **Shuddering**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may shudder when shifting gears or accelerating. This can be caused by a faulty torque converter or transmission fluid.
        * Solution: The torque converter or transmission fluid must be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found

    * **Slipping**
        * Description/Symptoms: The transmission may slip when shifting gears. This can be caused by worn clutch packs or a faulty valve body.
        * Solution: The transmission must be rebuilt or replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

* **Front Suspension**
    * **Strut Failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: The front struts can fail, causing the vehicle to bounce or sway excessively.
        * Solution: The front struts must be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $400 - $600
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear Suspension**
    * **Spring Breakage**
        * Description/Symptoms: The rear springs can break, causing the vehicle to sag in the rear.
        * Solution: The rear springs must be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
        * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Battery Drain**
        * Description/Symptoms: The battery may drain prematurely, leaving the vehicle unable to start. This can be caused by a faulty alternator or a parasitic electrical drain.
        * Solution: The alternator or the parasitic electrical drain must be repaired.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Electrical Harness Issues**
        * Description/Symptoms: The electrical harness may have faulty wiring, causing various electrical problems such as flickering lights, inoperative gauges, or even starting issues.
        * Solution: The faulty wiring must be repaired or replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800
        * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Body Panel Gaps**
        * Description/Symptoms: The body panels may have uneven gaps between them. This can be a cosmetic issue or a sign of a manufacturing defect.
        * Solution: The body panels must be adjusted or replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $500
        * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard Rattles**
        * Description/Symptoms: The dashboard may rattle or creak when driving over rough roads. This can be caused by loose screws or clips.
        * Solution: The loose screws or clips must be tightened or replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200
        * Recalls: None Found
* **Windshield Windshield Noise**
        * Description/Symptoms: Wind noise may be excessive around the windshield. This can be caused by a faulty windshield seal.
        * Solution: The windshield seal must be replaced.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400
        * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The Mitsubishi RVR is a generally reliable vehicle, but it does have some common problems. The most serious issues are the engine and transmission problems, which can be expensive to repair. However, most of the other problems are relatively minor and inexpensive to fix. Overall, the RVR is a good value for the money, but buyers should be aware of the potential problems before purchasing.
