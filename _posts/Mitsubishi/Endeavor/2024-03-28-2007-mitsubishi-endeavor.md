**Introduction:**

The 2007 Mitsubishi Endeavor is a mid-size SUV known for its spacious interior and comfortable ride. It offers a single engine option with two transmission choices. The V6 engine produces 225 horsepower and 242 lb-ft of torque. The 4-speed automatic transmission is available on all trims, while the 5-speed automatic transmission is available only on the Limited trim.

Despite its strengths, the Endeavor has several reported issues that owners should be aware of.

**Engine:**

**3.8L V6**

- **Intake Manifold Gasket Failure:** This issue occurs due to defective intake manifold gaskets, leading to coolant and oil leaks.
- **Symptoms:** Coolant smell, reduced engine power, overheating.
- **Solution:** Replace the intake manifold gaskets.
- **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000
- **Recalls:** None Found

**Transmission:**

**4-Speed Automatic Transmission**

- **Gear Slipping:** The transmission may slip out of gears, causing loss of power or abrupt shifts.
- **Symptoms:** Jerking or hesitation while shifting, delayed engagement, loss of speed.
- **Solution:** Replace or rebuild the transmission.
- **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $2,000 - $4,000+
- **Recalls:** None Found

**5-Speed Automatic Transmission**

- **No Major Issues Found**

**Suspension:**

- **Strut Mount Failure:** The strut mounts may fail, causing clunking noises and poor handling.
- **Symptoms:** Clunking sound when going over bumps, uneven tire wear.
- **Solution:** Replace the strut mounts.
- **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $200 - $500 per corner
- **Recalls:** None Found

**Electrical:**

- **Alternator Failure:** The alternator may fail, causing charging issues and loss of power.
- **Symptoms:** Dimming lights, difficulty starting, battery warning light.
- **Solution:** Replace the alternator.
- **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000
- **Recalls:** None Found

**Exterior:**

- **Rust on Undercarriage:** The undercarriage of the vehicle may be prone to rust.
- **Symptoms:** Visible rust on components such as the frame and exhaust system.
- **Solution:** Apply rust-prevention measures or replace rusted components.
- **Estimated Cost to Fix:** Varies depending on extent of rust
- **Recalls:** None Found

**Interior:**

- **Dashboard Panel Peeling:** The dashboard panel may peel or crack over time.
- **Symptoms:** Peeling or cracking material on the dashboard.
- **Solution:** Replace the dashboard panel.
- **Estimated Cost to Fix:** $500 - $1,000
- **Recalls:** None Found

**Summary:**

The 2007 Mitsubishi Endeavor has a range of reported issues that vary in severity and cost to fix. Some issues, such as intake manifold gasket failure and gear slipping, can be expensive to repair. Other issues, like rust on the undercarriage, may be less severe but still require attention. Potential buyers should be aware of these issues and consider the potential costs of repairs when evaluating this vehicle.
