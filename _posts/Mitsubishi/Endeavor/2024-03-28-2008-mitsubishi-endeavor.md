The 2008 Mitsubishi Endeavor is a mid-size SUV that was offered with a choice of two engines and two transmissions. The base engine was a 3.8L V6 that produced 225 horsepower and 250 lb-ft of torque. The optional engine was a 4.7L V8 that produced 302 horsepower and 329 lb-ft of torque. Both engines were available with either a 5-speed automatic transmission or a 6-speed manual transmission.

Overall, the 2008 Mitsubishi Endeavor is a reliable and affordable SUV that offers a comfortable ride and a spacious interior. However, there are a few issues that have been reported with the vehicle.

**Engine**

* **3.8L V6**
    * **Intake manifold gasket failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause a variety of symptoms, including a rough idle, decreased engine power, and a check engine light.
        * Solution: The solution to this issue is to replace the intake manifold gasket.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **4.7L V8**
    * **Timing chain failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause the timing chain to skip or break, which can lead to catastrophic engine damage.
        * Solution: The solution to this issue is to replace the timing chain and tensioner.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $3,000+
        * Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

* **5-speed automatic transmission**
    * **Torque converter failure**
        * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause the transmission to slip or shudder, and it can also lead to a complete loss of power.
        * Solution: The solution to this issue is to replace the torque converter.
        * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $2,500+
        * Recalls: None Found
* **6-speed manual transmission**
    * **No issues found**

**Suspension**

* **Front suspension strut failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause the front end of the vehicle to sag or bounce excessively.
    * Solution: The solution to this issue is to replace the front suspension struts.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Rear suspension spring failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause the rear end of the vehicle to sag or bounce excessively.
    * Solution: The solution to this issue is to replace the rear suspension springs.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $250 - $500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

* **Alternator failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause the vehicle to lose power or to have difficulty starting.
    * Solution: The solution to this issue is to replace the alternator.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Battery failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause the vehicle to lose power or to have difficulty starting.
    * Solution: The solution to this issue is to replace the battery.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

* **Roof rack crossbars peeling**
    * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause the roof rack crossbars to peel or flake.
    * Solution: The solution to this issue is to replace the roof rack crossbars.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Tailgate handle failure**
    * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause the tailgate handle to break or become difficult to operate.
    * Solution: The solution to this issue is to replace the tailgate handle.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $200+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

* **Dashboard cracking**
    * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause the dashboard to crack or become distorted.
    * Solution: The solution to this issue is to replace the dashboard.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $1,000+
    * Recalls: None Found
* **Door panel trim peeling**
    * Description/Symptoms: This issue can cause the door panel trim to peel or flake.
    * Solution: The solution to this issue is to replace the door panel trim.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $400+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

Overall, the 2008 Mitsubishi Endeavor is a reliable and affordable SUV, but it does have a few issues that can be costly to repair. The most severe issues include the timing chain failure on the 4.7L V8 engine, the torque converter failure on the 5-speed automatic transmission, and the dashboard cracking. However, these issues are relatively rare, and most Endeavor owners will not experience any major problems with their vehicle.
