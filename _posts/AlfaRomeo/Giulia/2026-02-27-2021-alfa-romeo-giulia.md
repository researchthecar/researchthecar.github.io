# 2021 Alfa Romeo Giulia Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2021 Alfa Romeo Giulia delivers engaging driving dynamics, sharp Italian styling, and a driver-focused cabin. For used car buyers exploring this luxury sport sedan, understanding the mechanical makeup and potential owner-reported quirks helps ensure a satisfying ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

This 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 280 horsepower and 306 lb-ft of torque, providing brisk acceleration for daily driving. It serves as the standard powerplant across most trims, combining lively performance with balanced efficiency.

### 2.9L Twin-Turbocharged V6

Exclusive to the high-performance Quadrifoglio model, this 2.9-liter twin-turbo V6 unleashes 505 horsepower and 443 lb-ft of torque. It offers exotic-car thrills and track-ready capability at the expense of higher maintenance considerations.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic Transmission

Paired across the lineup, this ZF 8-speed automatic transmission delivers quick, smooth shifts via column-mounted aluminum paddle shifters. It is generally regarded as a robust and responsive unit in modern luxury applications.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V878000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Pressure Sensor](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Alfa+Romeo&model=Giulia&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak may cause an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash. A fuel leak also increases the risk of fire in the presence of an ignition source.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the fuel line sensor housing, as necessary, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 21V309000: Service Brakes, Hydraulic](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Alfa+Romeo&model=Giulia&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** Hard braking may cause the Anti-Lock Brake System (ABS) Hydraulic Control Unit (HCU) rotor shaft to separate, a reduction of braking assist, and a loss of ABS. The loss of braking assistance and ABS functionality can reduce overall braking performance, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the ABS HCU, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V306000: Fuel System, Gasoline:Delivery:Hoses, Lines/Piping, And Fittings](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Alfa+Romeo&model=Giulia&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A fuel leak may cause an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash. A fuel leak in the presence of an ignition source can increase the risk of a fire.
- **What to do:** Dealers will replace the fuel line sensor housing, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V512000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Alfa+Romeo&model=Giulia&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." Incorrect tire information can result in under-inflated tires or installing the incorrect-size tires, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V510000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Alfa+Romeo&model=Giulia&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** A seat belt buckle switch sensor may be improperly connected, preventing the front seat air bag from deploying as intended. A front seat air bag that does not deploy as intended can increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will repair the connection, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V667000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Alfa+Romeo&model=Giulia&modelYear=2021)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not display properly reduces the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the radio, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.
