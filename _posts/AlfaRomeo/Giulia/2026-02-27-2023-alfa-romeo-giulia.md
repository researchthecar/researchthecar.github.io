# 2023 Alfa Romeo Giulia Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2023 Alfa Romeo Giulia delivers engaging driving dynamics, sharp Italian styling, and a driver-focused cabin that sets it apart from traditional luxury sport sedans. For used car buyers, understanding the powertrain options and monitoring common owner-reported quirks are essential steps before making a purchase. While NHTSA owner feedback highlights a few electrical and exterior trouble spots, a thorough pre-purchase inspection can help ensure a rewarding ownership experience.

---

## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4

This 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 280 horsepower and 306 lb-ft of torque, serving as the standard power plant for the Giulia lineup. It offers a lively and responsive driving experience, though buyers should maintain regular service intervals to ensure long-term reliability.

### 2.9L Twin-Turbocharged V6

Exclusive to the high-performance Quadrifoglio model, this 2.9-liter twin-turbo V6 pushes out 505 horsepower and 443 lb-ft of torque. It provides exhilarating track-ready performance, requiring meticulous maintenance and attentive mechanical oversight from prospective buyers.

## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic

Paired across the Giulia lineup, this ZF 8-speed automatic transmission delivers swift, refined shifts and power delivery to either the rear wheels or available all-wheel-drive system. It is generally regarded as a robust unit in modern luxury applications when properly maintained.

## Recall Check

Recalls are listed separately because used vehicles may already have had recall work completed. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 24V512000: Equipment:Other:Labels](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Alfa+Romeo&model=Giulia&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard number 110, "Tire Selection and Rims." Incorrect tire information can result in under-inflated tires or installing the incorrect-size tires, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Verify recall completion by VIN with NHTSA, the manufacturer, or a dealer before purchase.

**[Recall 24V510000: Air Bags:Frontal](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Alfa+Romeo&model=Giulia&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** A seat belt buckle switch sensor may be improperly connected, preventing the front seat air bag from deploying as intended. A front seat air bag that does not deploy as intended can increase the risk of injury in a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will repair the connection, free of charge. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

**[Recall 25V667000: Back Over Prevention:Display Function](https://api.nhtsa.gov/recalls/recallsByVehicle?make=Alfa+Romeo&model=Giulia&modelYear=2023)**
- **Issue:** As such, these vehicles fail to comply with the requirements of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) number 111, "Rear Visibility." A rearview image that does not display properly reduces the driver's view behind the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash.
- **What to do:** Dealers will inspect and replace the radio, as necessary. Verify completion by VIN before purchase.

## Summary Verdict

The 2023 Alfa Romeo Giulia remains a compelling choice for buyers seeking distinctive style and athletic handling in a luxury sport sedan. Prospective owners should prioritize a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection and verify service history, keeping in mind that owner-reported grievances represent individual experiences rather than guaranteed systemic flaws.
