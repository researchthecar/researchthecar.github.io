# 2007 Alfa Romeo Brera Reliability Guide

### Overview
The 2007 Alfa Romeo Brera, a striking Italian sports coupe, offers a blend of distinctive styling and engaging driving dynamics. While its aesthetic appeal is undeniable, potential owners should be aware of its reliability profile. This analysis focuses on documented common issues beyond standard maintenance, providing insights into specific engine, transmission, and other system vulnerabilities. The Brera, particularly with its more potent engine options, can be a rewarding ownership experience, but understanding its potential weak points is crucial for managing expectations and maintenance costs.

## Engine

### 1.9L JTDm Diesel
**Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Illuminated DPF warning light, reduced engine power, increased fuel consumption.
- **Solution:** Forced regeneration or DPF replacement.
- **Cost:** $500 - $1500

### Summary Verdict
The 2007 Alfa Romeo Brera is a stylish coupe with a generally acceptable reliability record for its class and age, though some specific areas warrant attention. Diesel variants are more prone to DPF and EGR issues, common in modern diesels. The automatic transmission can occasionally exhibit selection problems. Electrical gremlins, particularly with the infotainment system and window regulators, are also noted. The V6 petrol engine and manual transmission are generally more robust. Prospective buyers should factor in potential costs for these specific issues, especially if purchasing a higher-mileage example. Regular maintenance and prompt attention to warning lights are key to ensuring a positive ownership experience.
