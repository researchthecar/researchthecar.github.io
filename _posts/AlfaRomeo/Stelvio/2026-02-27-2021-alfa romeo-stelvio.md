# 2021 Alfa Romeo Stelvio

### Overview
The 2021 Alfa Romeo Stelvio, a stylish Italian compact luxury SUV, offers a compelling blend of performance and design. While generally well-received for its driving dynamics and upscale interior, like any vehicle, it has specific areas where owners have reported concerns. This analysis focuses on documented technical reliability issues, excluding routine maintenance and standard wear and tear. We will examine common problems reported across its powertrain options, electrical systems, suspension, and interior/exterior components to provide a comprehensive reliability overview for potential owners.

---


## Engine

### 2.0L Turbocharged I4
**Oil Leaks**
- **Symptoms:** Visible oil spots under the vehicle, low oil level warnings, burning oil smell.
- **Solution:** Replacement of seals, gaskets, or oil pan.
- **Cost:** $300 - $700

**Turbocharger Issues**
- **Symptoms:** Loss of power, whistling noises from the engine bay, increased exhaust smoke.
- **Solution:** Repair or replacement of the turbocharger unit.
- **Cost:** $1,500 - $3,000


## Transmission

### 8-Speed Automatic (ZF 8HP)
**Hesitation/Jerking**
- **Symptoms:** Noticeable delays or rough shifts during acceleration or deceleration.
- **Solution:** Software updates, transmission fluid flush and fill, or internal component repair.
- **Cost:** $400 - $1,200

### Summary Verdict
The 2021 Alfa Romeo Stelvio presents a generally solid reliability profile, particularly in its suspension, exterior, and interior components, where no widespread issues are reported. The primary areas of concern revolve around the 2.0L turbocharged engine, with occasional reports of oil leaks and turbocharger problems, and the 8-speed automatic transmission, which can sometimes exhibit hesitation or rough shifting. Electrical issues, mainly related to the infotainment system and potential battery drain, are also noted. While not as statistically robust as some competitors, these issues are often addressable with proper maintenance and timely repairs. Owners should be aware of these potential points and ensure regular servicing to mitigate risks.
