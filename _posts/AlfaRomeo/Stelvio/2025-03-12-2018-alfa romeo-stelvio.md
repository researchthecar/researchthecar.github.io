Okay, here's a breakdown of common issues and complaints for the 2018 Alfa Romeo Stelvio, formatted as requested.

The 2018 Alfa Romeo Stelvio is a compact luxury SUV known for its sporty handling and Italian styling. It offers a compelling alternative to more established competitors. Engine options include a 2.0-liter turbocharged four-cylinder producing 280 horsepower and 306 lb-ft of torque, and a 2.9-liter twin-turbocharged V6 in the Quadrifoglio model, making 505 horsepower and 443 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission. Owners generally appreciate the Stelvio's engaging driving dynamics and distinctive design, but often cite concerns about reliability and electronic gremlins as downsides.

**Engine**

**2.0L Turbocharged Inline-4**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report higher than expected oil consumption, requiring frequent top-offs between oil changes. This may manifest as a low oil level warning light.
    *   Solution: Monitor oil levels regularly. If consumption is excessive (more than 1 quart per 1,000 miles), consult a qualified mechanic. Some cases may require piston ring replacement.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $5,000+ (depending on the repair needed, piston ring replacement is expensive)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Turbocharger Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Loss of power, unusual noises (whining, hissing) from the turbocharger area, check engine light.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the turbocharger.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

**2.9L Twin-Turbocharged V6 (Quadrifoglio)**

*   **High-Pressure Fuel Pump Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Engine misfires, rough idling, stalling, check engine light. Diagnostic codes related to fuel pressure.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the high-pressure fuel pump.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,500+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Coolant Leaks**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Low coolant levels, overheating, coolant smell, visible leaks near the engine.
    *   Solution: Identify and repair the source of the leak (hoses, water pump, radiator).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,500+ depending on the source
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Transmission**

**ZF 8-Speed Automatic (Both Engines)**

*   **Harsh Shifting/Hesitation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Rough or jerky gear changes, particularly at low speeds. Hesitation when accelerating, especially from a stop.
    *   Solution: Some cases can be resolved with a transmission software update or adaptive learning reset. More severe cases may require valve body replacement or transmission rebuild.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 (software update) to $5,000+ (rebuild)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Torque Converter Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Shuddering or vibrations at certain speeds, especially under light acceleration. Diagnostic trouble codes (DTCs) related to torque converter clutch performance.
    * Solution: Replacement of the torque converter.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,500+
    * Recalls: None Found

**Suspension**

*   **Premature Bushing Wear**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Clunking or rattling noises from the suspension, particularly when going over bumps. Reduced handling precision.
    *   Solution: Replacement of worn bushings (control arm bushings, sway bar bushings, etc.).
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+ per corner, depending on the bushings needing replacement.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Adaptive Suspension Malfunctions (If Equipped)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Warning lights on the dashboard related to the suspension. Uneven ride quality, with one or more corners feeling stiffer or softer than others.
    *   Solution: Diagnostic testing to identify the faulty component (shock absorber, sensor, control module). Replacement of the defective part.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $500 - $2,000+ per shock absorber, plus labor and diagnostics.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Electrical**

*   **Infotainment System Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Freezing, crashing, slow response, connectivity problems (Bluetooth, Apple CarPlay/Android Auto), screen glitches.
    *   Solution: Software updates are often released to address these issues. In some cases, the infotainment unit may need to be replaced.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $0 (software update) to $1,500+ (replacement)
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Sensor Failures (Various)**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Check engine light, ABS light, TPMS light, or other warning lights. These can be triggered by a variety of faulty sensors (oxygen sensors, wheel speed sensors, tire pressure sensors, etc.).
    *   Solution: Diagnostic testing to identify the faulty sensor. Replacement of the sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per sensor, including labor and diagnostics.
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Battery Drain**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The car's battery frequently dies, especially after being parked for a few days.
    *   Solution: Test battery and charging system. Check for parasitic drains. Possible replacement of battery.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $150 - $300+ (battery replacement) to $100 - $500+ (diagnose and fix parasitic drain)
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Exterior**

*   **Tail Light Condensation**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Moisture buildup inside the tail light housings.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the tail light assembly, or resealing of the existing unit.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $300 - $800+ per tail light.
    *   Recalls: None Found

**Interior**

*   **Sunroof Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Sunroof motor failure, difficulty opening or closing, leaks.
    *   Solution: Repair or replacement of the sunroof motor or mechanism. Resealing of the sunroof.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $1,000+
    *   Recalls: None Found

*   **Power Window Malfunctions**
    * Description/Symptoms: Windows not operating properly, sticking or binding when going up or down.
    * Solution: Repair or replacement of the power window motor.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+ per window.
    * Recalls: None Found

**Summary**

The 2018 Alfa Romeo Stelvio, while offering a sporty and stylish driving experience, is prone to several issues. Engine problems, particularly oil consumption and turbocharger failures on the 2.0L and fuel pump issues on the Quadrifoglio, can be costly. Electrical gremlins and infotainment glitches are also common. Suspension issues and transmission quirks can impact ride quality and reliability. The severity of these issues ranges from minor inconveniences to major repairs, with potential repair costs spanning from a few hundred to several thousand dollars, making a thorough pre-purchase inspection crucial.
