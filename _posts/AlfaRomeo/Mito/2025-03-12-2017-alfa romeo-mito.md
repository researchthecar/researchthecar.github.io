The 2017 Alfa Romeo Mito is a stylish and sporty subcompact hatchback, appreciated for its Italian design and engaging driving dynamics. Engine options for the 2017 Mito typically included various turbocharged gasoline engines. A popular choice was the 1.4-liter MultiAir Turbo, producing around 135-170 horsepower and 150-184 lb-ft of torque, depending on the specific variant. Transmission options usually consisted of a 5- or 6-speed manual or a 6-speed dual-clutch automatic (TCT). Owners generally praised the Mito's distinctive styling, agile handling, and responsive engines. However, common complaints often revolved around reliability issues, particularly with the electrical system and some engine components, as well as concerns about parts availability and higher maintenance costs.

**Engine**

**1.4L MultiAir Turbo**

*   **MultiAir Unit Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The MultiAir system, responsible for controlling intake valve timing, can experience failures. Symptoms include rough idling, misfires, a noticeable loss of power, and the engine warning light illuminating. The car might struggle to accelerate or exhibit jerky behavior.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty MultiAir unit is usually required. A diagnostic scan can confirm the issue by identifying specific error codes related to the MultiAir system.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,000 - $2,000+

*   **Turbocharger Issues**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Owners have reported issues with the turbocharger, including failures and leaks. Symptoms can include reduced engine power, whistling or screeching noises from the turbocharger, and the engine warning light activating. Oil leaks around the turbocharger area may also be visible.
    *   Solution: Depending on the severity, the turbocharger may need to be repaired or replaced. Checking and replacing any damaged hoses or fittings is also crucial.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $800 - $2,500+

**Transmission**

**6-Speed Dual-Clutch Automatic (TCT)**

*   **Clutch Wear/Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The dual-clutch transmission can experience premature clutch wear, especially with frequent stop-and-go driving. Symptoms include jerky gear changes, slipping during acceleration, and difficulty engaging gears.
    *   Solution: Replacing the clutch pack is the typical solution. Recalibrating the transmission control unit (TCU) after replacement is also necessary.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $1,500 - $3,000+

*   **Mechatronic Unit Failure**
    *   Description/Symptoms: The mechatronic unit, responsible for controlling the transmission's shifting, can fail. Symptoms include difficulty shifting gears, erratic shifting behavior, and the transmission getting stuck in a particular gear. Error messages related to the transmission might also appear on the dashboard.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the mechatronic unit is usually necessary. The unit often needs to be programmed to the specific vehicle.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $2,000 - $4,000+

**Suspension**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Electrical**

*   **Blue&Me System Issues**
    * Description/Symptoms: Some owners report problems with the Blue&Me infotainment system, including connectivity issues with Bluetooth devices, system freezes, and failure to recognize USB drives. The system may also become unresponsive or display error messages.
    * Solution: Software updates can sometimes resolve these issues. However, in some cases, the Blue&Me module may need to be replaced.
    * Estimated Cost to Fix: $200 - $800+

*   **Sensor Malfunctions**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Various sensors, such as ABS sensors, and oxygen sensors, can malfunction. Symptoms will be related to the faulty system, such as ABS system fault, Traction control failure, or even catalytic convertor efficiency.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty sensor is required. A diagnostic scan can confirm the issue by identifying specific error codes related to the sensor.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $500+

**Exterior**

*   **Door Handle Failures**
    *   Description/Symptoms: Some owners report that the door handles can become difficult to operate or break entirely. This may be due to the quality of the plastic.
    *   Solution: Replacement of the faulty door handle is the typical solution.
    *   Estimated Cost to Fix: $100 - $300+

**Interior**

*   **No Issues Found**

**Summary:**

The 2017 Alfa Romeo Mito, while offering a stylish and engaging driving experience, appears to have some notable reliability concerns. The most commonly reported issues relate to the engine, particularly the MultiAir unit and turbocharger on the 1.4L engine, as well as the dual-clutch transmission's clutch wear and mechatronic unit failures. Electrical issues with the Blue&Me system and sensor malfunctions are also reported. These issues can range in severity from moderate inconvenience to major component failure, with repair costs potentially reaching several thousand dollars. Potential buyers should be aware of these common problems and factor them into their purchasing decision, including the possibility of higher maintenance costs and potential downtime.
