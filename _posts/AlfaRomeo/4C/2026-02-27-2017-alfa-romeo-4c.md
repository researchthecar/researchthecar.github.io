# 2017 Alfa Romeo 4C Reliability & Common Issues

## Overview

The 2017 Alfa Romeo 4C is an exotic, track-focused sports car celebrated for its lightweight carbon-fiber monocoque and exhilarating driving dynamics. Because these cars are produced in limited numbers and driven recreationally, overall owner complaint volume tends to be low. However, prospective buyers should carefully evaluate specialized maintenance records, engine bay inspections, and vehicle history prior to purchase.

---

## Engine

### 1.75L Turbocharged Inline-4

This 1.75-liter direct-injected turbocharged inline-4 engine produces approximately 237 horsepower and 258 lb-ft of torque, mounted mid-ship. It delivers blistering performance for the 4C's light weight, though routine professional inspection is recommended due to its high-strung nature and tight packaging.

**Engine Compartment Smoke**
- **Symptoms:** An owner reported smoke appearing from underneath the front hood area while driving, requiring a safe pull-over to the road shoulder.
- **Solution:** Inspect the engine bay thoroughly for fluid leaks, wiring harness chafing, or turbocharger component issues.

## Transmission

### 6-Speed Dual-Dry-Clutch Automatic

The 6-speed dual-dry-clutch transmission features paddle shifters and provides lightning-fast gear changes suited for spirited driving. While generally robust under normal operating conditions, clutch wear and software calibrations should be verified during a pre-purchase inspection.

## Summary Verdict

The 2017 Alfa Romeo 4C remains an uncompromising weekend toy that delivers exotic car presence and performance. While major recurring mechanical failure reports are minimal in available owner data, prospective buyers should prioritize a comprehensive pre-purchase inspection by a knowledgeable technician to ensure all powertrain and auxiliary systems are in top condition.
